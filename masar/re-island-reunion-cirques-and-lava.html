☀️ الطقس

موسم الشتاء/الموسم الجاف (مايو-نوفمبر): ظروف جوية مثالية (18-26°C) مع هطول أمطار قليل، وظروف ممتازة للمشي لمسافات طويلة، ودرجات حرارة مثالية للأنشطة الخارجية. موسم الذروة السياحية مع أعلى الأسعار ولكن مناخ أكثر راحة لاستكشاف المناظر الطبيعية البركانية.

موسم الصيف/الموسم الممطر (ديسمبر-أبريل): ظروف حارة ورطبة (23-30°C) مع هطول أمطار غزيرة، وخطر الأعاصير، وظروف المشي لمسافات طويلة صعبة. أسعار أقل ولكن قيود الطقس، ومناظر طبيعية خضراء خصبة، وتجربة استوائية أصيلة.

مواسم الانتقال (أبريل-مايو، أكتوبر-نوفمبر): فترات انتقالية ممتازة بدرجات حرارة معتدلة وهطول أمطار متناقص وأسعار ذات قيمة جيدة. مثالية للأنشطة الخارجية مع حشود أقل وأنماط طقس مستقرة.

الغوص على مدار السنة: درجات حرارة المياه الثابتة (24-27 درجة مئوية) تجعل الغوص والأنشطة المائية ممكنة طوال العام. تتنوع الحياة البحرية موسمياً مع كون مراقبة الحيتان مثلى من يونيو حتى سبتمبر.

🌍 اكتشاف

جنة المحيط الهندي مع المناظر الطبيعية البركانية والثقافة الفرنسية والجمال الاستوائي. استمتع بالمأكولات Creole وأنشطة المغامرة والبيئات الطبيعية الجميلة. تقدم هذه المنطقة التنوع الثقافي مع الجمال الطبيعي والأناقة الفرنسية. توفر Réunion تجارب فرنسية مع الجنة الاستوائية والأصالة الثقافية.

Saint Denis

1

Saint-Denis — Cannons along the Barachois seafront and 19th-century Creole houses on Rue de Paris.

⏱ 75 min
2

Start at the Barachois—these cannons aimed seaward are the city's icon. They were installed during the Napoleonic Wars to repel a British attack from the sea. The British eventually landed at La Grande Chaloupe, to the west, in July 1810. The cannons stayed, scorched by sun and rusted by spray, lending the waterfront an unexpected charm. The neighbouring prefecture, former residence of the French East India Company governors, has been a listed historic monument since 1970—it only opens to the public during Heritage Days.

⏱ 45 min
3

Jardin de l'État — Botanical collections and natural history museum in a colonial garden.

⏱ 75 min
4

Walk up Rue de Paris—the island's most history-laden artery. Called Grande rue in 1725, Rue Royale in 1777, Rue Impériale in 1810, it only got its definitive name in 1848. Stop at number 14: Maison Carrère, built in the early 19th century, now houses the tourist office (listed historic monument since 1998). Its permanent exhibition on the street's Creole families is worth 30 minutes. Spot the guétalis at property corners—these raised kiosks allowed ladies to observe the street without being seen.

⏱ 60 min
5

Rue de Paris ends at the Jardin de l'État: 4 hectares laid out between 1767 and 1773 for the French East India Company, with trees and spices brought back by botanist Pierre Poivre. At the back of the garden, push open the door to the Natural History Museum—the island's first museum, opened in 1855 in the former Legislative Palace of 1834. It holds 560,000 specimens. Don't miss the coelacanth from the Mozambique Channel: this living fossil fish, thought extinct for 65 million years, is the collection's star piece.

⏱ 90 min

أنشطة

Evening tasting of cari curry and arranged rum; book a table on Rue Maréchal Leclerc.

⏱ 60 min

In the evening, settle on Rue Maréchal Leclerc, the pedestrian centre artery, for Creole dinner: goat cari, rougail sausages, homemade arranged rum. If you arrive on a Saturday evening in season, watch for the night market that sets up certain months at the Barachois.

⏱ 75 min

Light jog along the seafront promenade at the Barachois at sunrise.

⏱ 60 min

The Barachois is the morning jogging spot for locals—if you're staying nearby, a lap of the waterfront at sunrise, before the heat sets in, is a good way to start the island.

⏱ 30 min

Saint Gilles Les Bains

1

Saint-Paul market (Fri–Sat) — Spices, fruit and Creole street food.

⏱ 75 min
2

Saint-Paul market is the island's biggest—some 300 vendors spread along the seafront on Fridays (6am–3pm) and Saturdays (6am–noon). Friday morning is when the stalls are fullest. You'll find spices, homemade arranged rums, lychee honey, tropical fruits and local crafts. Bring change and park in the town centre: nearby car parks fill fast.

⏱ 90 min
3

Saint-Gilles-les-Bains — Aquarium and marina promenade.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Maïdo viewpoint is a visual knockout. At 2,190 m altitude, you tower over the Mafate cirque by more than 1,000 m—the only cirque accessible solely on foot or by helicopter. The site draws 400,000 visitors a year and is the island's 2nd most visited. Real advice: leave before 6am from the coast. The sky clouds over between 9am and 10am almost systematically, and if you arrive too late, you'll see a wall of white clouds. At sunrise in clear weather, the view stretches to the island's east coast.

⏱ 75 min
5

Maïdo road — Viewpoint over the Mafate cirque; panoramas at 2,190 m (arrive at sunrise).

⏱ 75 min
6

Savanna distillery — Rum tasting and botanical garden (check opening hours).

⏱ 75 min

أنشطة

Snorkelling in the Hermitage lagoon (inside the reef only; respect lifeguard flags).

⏱ 60 min

The Hermitage lagoon is one of the finest on Réunion's coast—turquoise water, shallow, fish visible without special gear. For snorkelling, stay inside the reef and strictly respect lifeguard flags. Don't cross the marked zone.

⏱ 90 min

Sunset on the Boucan Canot promenade.

⏱ 60 min

For sunset, head to Boucan Canot beach. Local operators offer tandem paragliding over the lagoon from the heights—a memorable experience if the weather's with you. Ask your accommodation for a certified operator.

⏱ 60 min

Paragliding over the lagoon (weather-dependent; certified operator only).

⏱ 60 min

Saint-Gilles-les-Bains has an aquarium dedicated to Indian Ocean marine life—a good option if the sky clouds over late afternoon. The marina promenade is also worth a stroll to pick a restaurant with a view of the boats.

⏱ 60 min

Hell Bourg

1

Salazie — Voile de la Mariée waterfall and fern canyons; viewpoints along the RD48.

⏱ 75 min
2

Driving up the D48 to Salazie, park at the viewing areas to observe the Voile de la Mariée waterfall from the road—the view is perfect from below, and you'll see a white sheet bursting from the cliff that splits into small cascades. The waterfall is far more spectacular after rains. Local legend tells of Amanda, a young woman who fell into the precipice on her wedding night trying to flee her father. Practical note: the approach trail has been closed by decree since 2014—don't take it.

⏱ 30 min
3

Hell-Bourg — Restored Creole villas (Maison Folio) and village museum.

⏱ 75 min
4

Hell-Bourg takes its name from Baron de Hell, governor of Madagascar, during whose mandate wild goat hunters discovered hot springs in 1832. The spa opened in 1852 drew all the island's high society: governors, foreign consuls, Malagasy princes. A cyclone swallowed the springs in 1948 and ended the golden era. The village kept its colourful Creole houses and earned the Plus Beaux Villages de France label—the only one in overseas France. Wander its lanes—every house has a plaque telling its story.

⏱ 60 min
5

Push open the wrought-iron gate of Maison Folio, rue Amiral Lacaze. This Creole dwelling built in the 1860s, listed as a Historic Monument since 1989, is a perfectly preserved 19th-century time capsule. The guided tour (around €5, slots at 10am and 2:30pm) shows you period-furnished rooms, the lambrequin-trimmed veranda and the garden planted with geranium, vetiver, patchouli and orchids. It's one of the only bourgeois Creole houses open to the public across the whole island.

⏱ 75 min

أنشطة

Easy loop hike around Hell-Bourg (2–3 hrs, early start to avoid clouds).

⏱ 60 min

The Trois Cascades trail starts from the village centre toward Îlet à Vidot. Allow 1hr return on a moderate slope path. Leave early morning—clouds descend on the Salazie cirque around 10am and would block the view. Closed shoes essential: the forest path is slippery.

⏱ 75 min

Tasting cassava cake and bonbon piment at a village snack bar.

⏱ 60 min

Two kilometres from the village, the Mare à Poule d'Eau merits a detour—a small body of water of improbable calm in the heart of the cirque, frequented by Sunday fishermen and a few local families. Stop at a village snack bar to try cassava cake or bonbon piment, highland specialities.

⏱ 45 min

Cilaos

1

RN5 to Cilaos — Road of 400 bends, basalt cliffs and tunnels; panoramic stops at Îlet à Cordes viewpoint.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Cilaos road is an experience before you even reach the village. Construction began in 1927 under engineer Mallet—inaugurated in 1932, it crosses three tunnels carved through crumbly rock and passes a double-level bridge built to fix a construction misalignment. Over 35 km from Saint-Louis, you chain more than 400 bends above the Bras de Cilaos. Stop at the viewpoints: the views of the UNESCO-listed ramparts and pitons are the best of the whole journey. The road can be cut by rockfalls in the rainy season—check before you go.

⏱ 60 min
3

Cilaos — Thermal heritage, embroidery workshop and Cirque panoramas.

⏱ 75 min
4

Cilaos comes from the Malagasy 'tsilaosa'—'the place you never leave'. You'll understand why on arrival. Perched at 1,200 m altitude, ringed by Piton des Neiges (3,071 m, Indian Ocean's highest point), Gros Morne (3,019 m) and Grand Bénare (2,898 m), it's the sunniest and driest of the three cirques. Stroll the main street to the Mare à Joncs, a peaceful pond ideal for a break facing the ramparts.

⏱ 60 min
5

Cilaos cirque — Full-day hike in the Bras Rouge sector; basalt pools and waterfalls.

⏱ 75 min
6

Stop at the Maison de la broderie to see the 'jours de Cilaos'—a lace-on-fabric technique created in 1900 by Angèle Mac Auliffe, multiple Meilleur Ouvrier de France award winner. Just next door, the winemakers' cellars offer tastings of the only wine produced on Réunion, Vin de Pays labelled since 2004: red, rosé and white grown on altitude terraces since the 1860s.

⏱ 60 min
7

Village craft centre — Lace products and plateau lentils.

⏱ 75 min
8

If you have energy, the Bras Rouge waterfall trail leaves west of the village and descends into a basalt canyon with pools and successive falls. Allow half a day return—one of the cirque's most photogenic routes.

⏱ 60 min

أنشطة

Short hike to La Roche Merveilleuse viewpoint (late afternoon light).

⏱ 60 min

Late afternoon, take the Roche Merveilleuse trail from the village—1hr return to a viewpoint over the whole cirque. Get there in the two hours before sunset: the oblique light on the ramparts is particularly beautiful.

⏱ 75 min

Thermal bath or wine tasting (local micro-production of chenin/viognier).

⏱ 60 min

The Cilaos Thermes have exploited volcanic-origin springs that gush between 31°C and 38°C since 1939. A thermal bath after the day's drive and hike is a smart move. The first installations date to 1882. Book ahead in high season.

⏱ 90 min

Optional attempt to climb Piton des Neiges (very early start; experienced hikers).

⏱ 60 min

For experienced hikers, the ascent of Piton des Neiges (3,071 m) takes 2 days with a night at Caverne Dufour gîte. Leave before 4am to reach the summit at sunrise before the clouds arrive. It often freezes at the summit even in austral summer—bring equipment accordingly and book the gîte well in advance.

⏱ 60 min

Saint Pierre

1

Étang-Salé — Black sand beach and petrified lava cliffs.

⏱ 75 min
2

Étang-Salé beach is the island's largest black sand beach—1.5 km lined with casuarina trees. The sand, a mix of basalt, olivine and coral eroded by the ocean, is deep black contrasting with turquoise water. Two zones: to the south, the Pirogue basin is protected by a coral barrier and suits families; to the north, swimming is forbidden (strong currents and shark risk). Caution: black sand heats fast—protect your feet or risk burns.

⏱ 60 min
3

Saint-Pierre — Seafront market and Creole temple facades on Rue des Bons-Enfants.

⏱ 75 min
4

Saint-Pierre is the South's capital and the island's only town with both a monitored beach and lagoon. The Saturday morning market on the seafront (Ravine Blanche side) came 3rd in the TV show Votre plus beau marché on TF1 in 2018 and 2019. Also stroll the historic centre: the colourful Tamil temples and facades marked by the French East India Company era merit a detour.

⏱ 75 min

أنشطة

Snorkelling in the protected lagoon (when the sea is calm; never swim outside the marked zone).

⏱ 60 min

Saint-Pierre's lagoon is one of the rare southern ones where swimming is secure and monitored. Stay in the marked zone (watch for sea urchins). Snorkelling reveals quite remarkable seabeds in calm weather. Never swim outside the marked zone.

⏱ 75 min

Bourg Murat

1

Plaine des Cafres — Volcano museum (Cité du Volcan) presenting Piton de la Fournaise.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Cité du Volcan, on the RN3 at Bourg-Murat, is the right place to understand what you'll see tomorrow. The museum was born in 1992 from an idea by volcanologist couple Maurice and Katia Krafft—the same ones who died filming the Pinatubo eruption in 1991. Entirely renovated and reopened in August 2014, the 6,000 m² building offers a reconstructed lava tunnel, 4D cinema, 270° panoramic screen and bathyscaphe to explore underwater flows. The museum welcomes 100,000 to 120,000 visitors a year. Allow 2hrs minimum.

⏱ 120 min
3

Plaine des Sables — Lunar ash plains on the volcano access road.

⏱ 75 min
4

On the road to Pas de Bellecombe, stop at the Nez de Bœuf viewpoint for the view of Piton des Neiges and the Plaine des Cafres grasslands. Further on, the Plaine des Sables is a landscape of red and black ash totally unreal—a vast flat colourful expanse you drive through as if on another planet. Step out a few minutes to walk on this cold ash.

⏱ 60 min

أنشطة

Stargazing session (clear nights) on the high plateau; bring warm clothes.

⏱ 60 min

Night on the Plaine des Cafres plateau, above 1,500 m altitude, is one of the island's best for astronomy: no light pollution, sky often clear in the dry season. Bring a thick fleece—it regularly drops between 5°C and 10°C at night, even in dry season.

⏱ 60 min

Sainte Rose

1

Piton de la Fournaise — Hike from Pas de Bellecombe to the caldera rim with views of Dolomieu crater.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Pas de Bellecombe-Jacob hike is the circuit's highlight. From the car park at 2,311 m altitude, the trail descends into the Enclos Fouqué via a steep staircase, skirts Formica Léo—a small red cone dating from 1753—and climbs to the edge of Dolomieu crater: 1 km wide, over 300 m deep, a gaping chasm over a lunar landscape. Allow 12 km return, about 5hrs, 500 m elevation gain. Leave before 7am: fog sets in mid-morning and can erase everything. SPF 50+ sunscreen, hat and 2L water minimum—no shade in the enclos. ⚠️ Check OVPF the day before: in case of eruption or alert, the enclos is closed to the public.

⏱ 300 min
3

Grand Brûlé — Coastal lava flows and segments of N2 road rebuilt over recent eruptions.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Route des Laves (RN2 section between Sainte-Rose and Saint-Philippe) crosses the Grand Brûlé, the Enclos Fouqué's coastal zone. Signs along the route date each flow. The most memorable remains April 2007—the eruption of the century—which covered the road in places under 60 metres of lava. It took 7 months of work to reopen it. ⚠️ Check RN2 status that morning: the road may close during active eruptions.

⏱ 60 min

أنشطة

Stop at Notre-Dame des Laves chapel near Piton-Sainte-Rose.

⏱ 60 min

Stop at the Notre-Dame des Laves church in Piton-Sainte-Rose. Built in 1927, it miraculously survived the off-enclos eruption of 8 April 1977: the flow split into two arms that skirted the building, burning everything around, stopping at the nave entrance. The access staircase was carved directly into the solidified lava. The neighbouring former gendarmerie houses an interpretation space detailing the eruption.

⏱ 45 min
