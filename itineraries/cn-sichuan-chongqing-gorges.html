Chengdu

Where bamboo forests shelter the world's last giant pandas

★★★★☆
📍 Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding📍 Wuhou Memorial Temple Chengdu📍 Jinli Ancient Street Chengdu📍 Chengdu Panda Nursery📍 Three Kingdoms Statues Wuhou📍 Jinli Night Market Chengdu
1

The Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding, founded in 1987 from six rescued, sick and starving pandas, now spans 3 km² of forested hills and bamboo groves just 10 km from downtown. It's home to the world's largest captive giant panda population—over 200 individuals living in enclosures that recreate their natural habitat. Arrive early: pandas are most active during morning feeding and nap through the afternoon. The nursery lets you watch newborns in incubators (best in autumn-winter). The Giant Panda Museum, opened in 1993, displays more than 800 documents, fossils and models—it's the world's only museum entirely dedicated to this species.

⏱ 180 min
2

Wuhou Temple (Wǔhóu Cí), originally built in 223 CE, is the only temple in China that jointly honors an emperor and his prime minister: Liu Bei, founder of the Shu Kingdom, and Zhuge Liang, the legendary strategist of the Three Kingdoms period (220-280). The current complex, rebuilt in 1672 under Emperor Kangxi, covers 37,000 m². Liu Bei Hall houses his 3-meter gilt statue flanked by his generals Guan Yu and Zhang Fei. The Stele of Three Perfections, erected in 809, stands 3.67 m tall—it combines text by Minister Pei Du, calligraphy by Liu Gongquan, and a eulogy to Zhuge Liang. Behind the temple, the Huiling Mausoleum (Liu Bei's tomb) has remained intact for over 1,700 years, never violated or excavated.

⏱ 120 min
3

Jinli Ancient Street, adjacent to Wuhou Temple, recreates Qing Dynasty commercial architecture across roughly 550 meters. Its wooden houses with gray tiles and red eaves shelter shops selling Sichuan handicrafts (Shu embroidery, shadow puppets, Sichuan opera masks) and street stalls serving local classics: dan dan noodles (sesame noodles with chili oil), mapo tofu, and mala skewers. After dark, red lanterns light up and the atmosphere transforms—this is when to try san da pao, glutinous rice balls launched against a metal plate that bounce into soybean flour.

Chengdu

Where bamboo forests shelter the world's last giant pandas

★★★★☆
📍 Kuanzhai Alley Chengdu📍 People's Park Chengdu📍 Qingyang Palace Chengdu📍 Wide and Narrow Lanes Chengdu📍 Heming Teahouse People's Park📍 Bronze Goats Qingyang
1

The Kuanzhai Alleys (Kuānzhǎi Xiàngzi), right in downtown Chengdu, are the last remnants of the Manchu city of Shaocheng, built in 1718 under Emperor Kangxi to house soldiers of the Eight Banners after the campaign against the Dzungars in Tibet. Of the original 42 lanes, only three survive: Wide Lane (Kuan, 7-8 m wide, reserved for officers who traveled by carriage), Narrow Lane (Zhai, 5 m, a Sino-Western mix tied to late-Qing foreign residents), and Well Lane (Jing, named after a well dug under Kangxi). The 45 interior courtyards (sìhéyuàn) were restored between 2004 and 2008. Here you sip covered-bowl tea (gàiwǎn chá) from bamboo chairs while watching Sichuan opera face-changing performances (biànliǎn).

⏱ 120 min
2

People's Park (Rénmín Gōngyuán), a few minutes from Kuanzhai Alleys, is the heart of everyday Chengdu life. Under bamboo and ginkgo trees, retirees practice tai chi, play cards and mahjong, dance in couples, or fly kites. Open-air teahouses set up tables along the artificial lake—order jasmine tea and watch the ballet of hot-water pourers with their long-spouted kettles. On Sundays, an improvised "marriage corner" sees parents display profiles of their single children on placards.

⏱ 75 min
3

Qingyang Palace (Qīngyáng Gōng), 1.2 km from People's Park, is one of Sichuan's oldest and most important Taoist temples. Founded during the Tang Dynasty, it takes its name from two bronze goats (qīngyáng) placed before the main hall—tradition says touching them brings good luck. The main building, the Hall of Three Purities (Sānqīng Diàn), is a nail-free wooden structure supported by massive columns.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Sichuan cooking class in a downtown workshop. Learn to balance Sichuan spices—numbing Sichuan pepper (huājiāo) that tingles the tongue, dried chili, and fermented broad bean paste (dòubàn jiàng) from Pixian. Dishes taught vary: kung pao chicken, mapo tofu, or steamed dumplings. Allow 2 to 3 hours, tasting included.

Chengdu

Where bamboo forests shelter the world's last giant pandas

★★★★☆
📍 Leshan Giant Buddha📍 Lingyun Temple Leshan📍 Nine Bends Plank Road Leshan📍 Min River Confluence Leshan📍 Giant Buddha Scenic Area📍 Leshan Buddha Head
1

The Leshan Giant Buddha (Lèshān Dàfó), 126 km south of Chengdu, is the world's tallest stone Buddha statue: 71 meters high, carved into the red sandstone cliff of Mount Lingyun at the confluence of the Min, Dadu, and Qingyi rivers. Construction began in 713 during the Tang Dynasty, initiated by monk Haitong who wanted to calm the dangerous waters of the confluence. When funds were threatened by corrupt officials, Haitong gouged out his own eyes to prove his sincerity—the extortionists fled. The monk died before completion; his disciples continued the work for 90 years until 803. Rock debris thrown into the river during construction actually altered the currents, making navigation safer. The head measures 14.7 m high, the ears 7 m, the fingers 8.3 m, and the feet can accommodate a hundred people. The hair consists of 1,021 spiral buns, each individually set into the rock. Descend via the Nine Bends Plank Road (Jiǔqū Zhàndào), a 278-step path carved into the cliff that leads from the top of the head to the feet of the colossus. UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1996.

★★★★☆
📍 Mount Qingcheng📍 Jianfu Temple Mount Qingcheng📍 Dujiangyan Irrigation System📍 Erwang Temple Dujiangyan📍 Yuzui Fish Mouth Dujiangyan📍 Anlan Suspension Bridge Dujiangyan
1

Mount Qingcheng (Qīngchéng Shān), 68 km west of Chengdu, is one of the birthplaces of Taoism in China. This is where Zhang Daoling supposedly founded the Taoist School of Five Pecks of Rice in the 2nd century CE. The main path climbs through dense forest of cedars and bamboo, connecting about ten Taoist temples and pavilions up to the 1,260-meter summit. A cable car can shorten the ascent. Jianfu Temple at the mountain's base dates to the Tang Dynasty (built in 730). The site is a UNESCO World Heritage Site along with the Dujiangyan Irrigation System.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Dujiangyan Irrigation System (Dūjiāngyàn), 3 km from the town of the same name, is the world's oldest irrigation system still in operation. Built in 256 BCE by Governor Li Bing and his son under the State of Qin, it diverts the waters of the Min River without a dam, through three complementary structures: a levee (Yuzui) that divides the current, a diversion channel (Baopingkou) tunneled through the mountain, and a spillway (Feishayan) that regulates floods. This system still irrigates over 5,300 km² of agricultural plains today and enabled Sichuan to become China's "rice basket." UNESCO listed in 2000.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Hike Mount Qingcheng via the main trail (ascent about 2h, descent 1h30). The path crosses suspension bridges, tunnels carved into rock, and clearings where Taoist monks sell green tea grown on the slopes. Option to shorten via cable car and ferry across Yuecheng Lake.

Chongqing

Where stilt houses cling to cliffs above the meeting of two rivers

★★★★☆
📍 Hongyadong Chongqing📍 Ciqikou Ancient Town Chongqing📍 Hongya Cave Stilts📍 Jialing River Hongyadong📍 Ciqikou Porcelain Port📍 Baolun Temple Ciqikou
1

Hongyadong (Hóngyádòng), clinging to the cliff overlooking the Jialing River, is an 11-story, 75-meter-high complex built in the style of traditional stilt houses (diàojiǎolóu) of Bayu culture. The site dates back over 2,300 years—it was originally a fortified gate and major commercial wharf on the river. The current complex, completed in 2006, reproduces this characteristic wooden stilt architecture of southwestern China, adapted to mountainous terrain and floods. Peculiarity: the 1st floor and the 11th floor each open onto different streets at different elevations. In the evening, red lanterns illuminate the structure and its reflection in the river—an image often compared to the spirit world in the film Spirited Away.

⏱ 90 min
2

Ciqikou (Cíqìkǒu), "the porcelain port," is a town over 1,000 years old on the bank of the Jialing River in Shapingba District. During the Ming and Qing dynasties, it was a center of porcelain production and trade. Its cobbled lanes bordered by two-story wooden houses preserve the atmosphere of an old riverside town. You'll find hand-pulled caramel candy workshops (máhuā), traditional teahouses, and handicraft shops. On weekends, artists play traditional Sichuan music in interior courtyards.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Chongqing hot pot (huǒguō) tasting, the city's absolute specialty. The bright red broth, saturated with chili, Sichuan pepper, and beef tallow, boils in a pot at the center of the table. Each diner plunges thin meats, tripe, vegetables, and tofu into it. The local version is reputed spicier and fattier than Chengdu's. In the Jiefangbei or Nanbin Lu districts, specialized restaurants serve until late into the night.

⏱ 90 min

Chongqing

★★★★☆
📍 Liziba Monorail Station Chongqing📍 Yangtze River Cableway Chongqing📍 Chaotianmen Square Chongqing📍 Yangtze Jialing Confluence📍 Line 2 Monorail Building📍 Chaotianmen Cruise Port
1

Liziba (Lǐzǐbà) monorail station became famous for a simple reason: Line 2 of the monorail passes through a 19-story residential building between the 6th and 8th floors. This isn't an accident—the building was designed simultaneously with the line, opened in 2005, to adapt to Chongqing's extreme topography, a city built on steep hills between two rivers. The monorail enters and exits the building every few minutes with surprisingly little noise thanks to soundproofing systems.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Yangtze River Cableway (Chángjiāng Suǒdào), opened in 1987, is the only urban cableway still in service crossing the Yangtze. The cabin soars over the river for about 1,166 meters, linking Yuzhong District to the south bank (Nan'an). The crossing takes 4 minutes and offers a bird's-eye view of the river, bridges, and Chongqing's skyline. It's a relic from the era when Chongqing, "the mountain city," used cableways as daily public transport before the metro was built.

⏱ 30 min
3

Chaotianmen Dock (Cháotiānmén), at the tip of the Yuzhong Peninsula, marks the exact confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing rivers. This is the wharf where Three Gorges cruises depart. The square offers a panoramic view of the confluence where the Jialing's green waters meet the Yangtze's brown waters—the demarcation line is visible in clear weather. At night, illuminated boats and Jiangbei's skyscrapers compose Chongqing's nocturnal panorama.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Late afternoon or evening boarding at Chaotianmen wharf for the Three Gorges cruise toward Yichang (4 days / 3 nights downstream). The ship typically casts off around 9 PM. From the upper deck, Chongqing's nighttime skyline scrolls past as the vessel enters the Yangtze.

Yangtze Cruise

Four days threading between 1,500-meter gorge walls that swallow the sky

★★★★☆
📍 Fengdu Ghost City📍 Mount Ming Fengdu📍 Naihe Bridge Fengdu📍 Shibaozhai Pagoda📍 Yangtze River Cruise📍 Ghost King Palace Fengdu
1

The "Ghost City" of Fengdu (Fēngdū Guǐchéng), on Mount Ming along the Yangtze, is a vast complex of temples, shrines, and monasteries dedicated to the afterlife in Chinese beliefs. Blending Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism, the site features sculptures and dioramas depicting the judgment of souls, the tortures of hell, and the path to reincarnation. You cross the "Nothing-to-Be-Done Bridge" (Nàihé Qiáo), the "Ghost Torture Passage," and the "River of Blood." Despite these names, the complex is colorful and lively—Chinese sometimes make pilgrimages here to pray for their ancestors.

⏱ 120 min
2

Shibaozhai Pagoda (Shíbǎozhài), an alternative stop to Fengdu depending on cruise companies, is a 400-year-old temple built against a 200-meter vertical cliff on the Yangtze's edge. Its 12-story wooden tower, painted red, leans against the rock wall without touching it. After the Three Gorges Dam flooded the area, the promontory became an island connected to the bank by a causeway—the temple was barely saved from rising waters.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Navigation on the Yangtze between Chongqing and Fengjie. The ship traverses wide sections of the river bordered by karst cliffs and rural villages. Onboard: lectures on Yangtze history, calligraphy demonstrations, and morning tai chi classes on the upper deck.

Yangtze Cruise

Four days threading between 1,500-meter gorge walls that swallow the sky

★★★★☆
📍 Qutang Gorge📍 Kuimen Gate Qutang📍 Wu Gorge📍 Goddess Peak Wu Gorge📍 Shennong Stream📍 Lesser Three Gorges
1

Qutang Gorge (Qútáng Xiá), the shortest of the Three Gorges (8 km), is also the most spectacular. Cliffs rise over 1,500 meters on each side, narrowing the river to just a few dozen meters at its tightest point. The entrance, framed by the Kuimen Gates, is the most famous image of the Three Gorges—it appears on the 10-yuan banknote. Poet Li Bai set his celebrated verse here: "In the morning I left Baidi amid colorful clouds; a thousand li to Jiangling, covered in one day."

⏱ 60 min
2

Wu Gorge (Wū Xiá), 46 km long, is known for its twelve peaks whose summits vanish into clouds. The forest-covered cliffs are so vertical that sunlight barely penetrates. Goddess Peak (Shénnǚ Fēng), a rock formation shaped like a female silhouette atop a ridge, is associated with the legend of Yao Ji, daughter of the Queen Mother of the West, who supposedly helped the mythical Emperor Yu tame the Yangtze's waters.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Excursion by sampan on Shennong Stream (Shénnóng Xī) or the Lesser Three Gorges (Xiǎo Sānxiá), depending on cruise company. You leave the main vessel for a smaller boat that ascends a narrow tributary between rock walls. On Shennong Stream, teams of boatmen pole the sampan forward through gorges so tight the cliffs seem to close overhead. You sometimes spot hanging coffins clinging to the walls—a burial practice of the Ba people over 2,000 years old.

Yichang

Gateway to the Three Gorges beneath the world's mightiest dam

★★★★☆
📍 Three Gorges Dam📍 Three Gorges Ship Lift📍 Five-Stage Lock Yichang📍 Xiling Gorge📍 Three Gorges Dam Observation Deck📍 Maoping Port Yichang
1

The Three Gorges Dam (Sānxiá Dàbà), completed in 2006, is the world's largest hydroelectric dam: 2,335 meters long and 185 meters high. Its power station produces about 100 billion kWh annually. The project, launched in 1994, required relocating over one million people and submerging entire cities beneath a 660-km reservoir. The five-stage ship lock allows vessels to traverse a 113-meter elevation change—the complete passage takes about 4 hours. The ship lift, the world's largest, hoists 3,000-ton vessels 113 meters in roughly 40 minutes.

⏱ 120 min
2

Disembarkation at Maoping port in Xiling Gorge (Xīlíng Xiá), the longest of the Three Gorges at 120 km. Its waters were once the most dangerous on the Yangtze, scattered with rapids and reefs—the dam's flooding calmed this section. The gorge remains bordered by steep cliffs and successive narrow defiles (Dengying Gorge, Bingshu Baojian Gorge). The bus transfer to downtown Yichang takes about an hour.

⏱ 60 min

Yichang

Gateway to the Three Gorges beneath the world's mightiest dam

★★★★☆
📍 Gezhouba Dam Yichang📍 Three Gorges Visitor Center📍 Yichang Museum📍 Binjiang Park Yichang📍 Yichang Railway Station📍 Three Kingdoms Yiling Battlefield
1

Gezhouba Dam (Gězhōubà), 38 km downstream from the Three Gorges Dam, was the first major dam built on the main course of the Yangtze, completed in 1988. Spanning 2,606 meters, it served as a "dress rehearsal" before the Three Gorges project. Its lock still allows passage of large-tonnage vessels. From Yichang's developed embankments, you can observe the structure and the passage of boats.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Free time in Yichang before departure (flight or train). Yichang, with a history spanning over 4,000 years, was a strategic point during the Three Kingdoms period—the famous Battle of Yiling (222) took place here. The Three Gorges Tourist Center offers a summary of the river's and region's history.
