☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Perfect weather across most regions (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, moderate crowds, and excellent conditions for national parks and cities. Ideal for diverse activities nationwide.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with spectacular fall foliage, harvest festivals, and comfortable weather. Perfect for outdoor activities, cultural events, fewer crowds than summer.

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warmest weather but highest crowds and prices. Excellent for northern regions, challenging in southwestern deserts, perfect for beaches and national parks.

Winter (December-February): Variable conditions from tropical Florida to snowy Colorado. Perfect for skiing, southern destinations, indoor attractions, lowest prices outside holiday periods.

Continental diversity with iconic landmarks, cultural melting pot, and endless possibilities. Experience natural wonders, vibrant cities, and authentic American dreams from coast to coast. 📋 Important notes

Open-loop circuit crossing Iowa from west to east. Can start from Sioux Falls (SD) or Des Moines (IA) airports. Return via Rochester (MN) or La Crosse (WI). Paved roads throughout. The Loess Hills Scenic Byway includes some gravel sections. Book accommodations in Okoboji and Decorah ahead for summer—demand runs high.

Okoboji

Midwest resort town where glacial lakes meet a century-old amusement park

★★★★★
📍 Arnolds Park Amusement Park Iowa📍 Iowa Great Lakes Maritime Museum📍 West Lake Okoboji📍 Abbie Gardner Cabin Arnolds Park
1

Arnolds Park Amusement Park—This 20-acre park on the shore of West Lake Okoboji has been operating since 1889. Its wooden roller coaster, The Legend, dates to 1927 and ranks as the 13th-oldest operating coaster in the world. Park admission is free—you pay only for the rides. Climb aboard the Ferris wheel for sweeping views across the lake.

⏱ 90 min
2

Iowa Great Lakes Maritime Museum—Right next to the amusement park, this small museum showcases antique wooden boats and the region's lake history. If you have time, walk one block west to the Abbie Gardner Cabin, an 1856 log structure tied to the Spirit Lake Massacre, a violent chapter in pioneer history.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Boat cruise on West Lake Okoboji—The Queen II excursion boat runs 75-minute narrated tours around the lake's bays and shoreline estates. West Lake Okoboji is one of only three lakes in the world with such intensely blue water (the others being Lake Geneva and Bavaria's Königssee, according to locals). True or not, the water is genuinely deep blue thanks to the lake's 135-foot depth and spring-fed clarity.

⏱ 75 min

📍 Gull Point State Park Okoboji📍 Spine Trail Okoboji📍 Spirit Lake Iowa📍 Mini Wakan State Park

Gull Point State Park—On the north shore of West Lake, this park offers a sandy beach, shaded trails, and picnic areas under the oaks. It's quieter than Arnolds Park—perfect for a calm morning by the water.

Biking the Spine Trail—This 14-mile bike path links the communities around the lakes. The stretch between Arnolds Park and Milford hugs the shoreline with open water views. Bike rentals available on-site.

In the evening, catch sunset from the west shore of the lake—locals gather with coolers and folding chairs. The vibe recalls old-time resort towns from another era.

Stop 3

Missouri Valley

Gateway to the Loess Hills, glacial-dust formations found nowhere else on Earth

★★★★★
📍 Loess Hills Scenic Byway Iowa📍 Preparation Canyon State Park Iowa📍 Loess Hills State Forest
1

Loess Hills Scenic Byway—This scenic route follows the loess hills, geological formations found in only two places on Earth: here in western Iowa, and in China's Yellow River valley. Loess is wind-deposited glacial dust from 14,000 to 24,000 years ago. The hills rise up to 200 feet above the plain—rolling prairie landscapes you won't see anywhere else in the United States.

⏱ 90 min
2

Preparation Canyon State Park—A wild park with no electricity or running water, tucked into the loess hills. The name comes from a Mormon splinter group that settled here in the 1850s before dispersing. Trails wind through tallgrass prairie and wooded ravines—you'll likely have the place to yourself.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Hiking in Loess Hills State Forest—Several marked trails cross the forest and prairies. The contrast between the steep Missouri River-facing slopes and the gentle eastern valleys is striking. Bring water—no resupply points along the way.

⏱ 75 min

Stop 4

Des Moines

Iowa's capital crowned with a gold dome and contemporary sculpture park

★★★★★
📍 Iowa State Capitol Des Moines📍 Pappajohn Sculpture Park Des Moines📍 East Village Des Moines📍 Greater Des Moines Botanical Garden
1

Iowa State Capitol—The 23-karat gold-leaf dome rises 275 feet above ground. Completed in 1886, it's the only state capitol in the U.S. with five domes. Inside, don't miss the carved wood law library, marble floors, and the collection of porcelain figurines representing Iowa's First Ladies. Admission and guided tours are free.

⏱ 60 min
2

Pappajohn Sculpture Park—Right in the heart of downtown, this 4.4-acre park displays about 30 contemporary sculptures by world-renowned artists: Ai Weiwei, Louise Bourgeois, Jaume Plensa. Plensa's Nomade—a 26-foot human silhouette made of steel letters—has become Des Moines' iconic symbol. Free admission, open dawn to dusk.

⏱ 45 min
3

Stroll through the East Village, the trendy district east of the Capitol. Independent shops, cafés, and galleries occupy old brick buildings. This is where you'll find the city's liveliest atmosphere.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

If visiting between May and October, stop by the Greater Des Moines Botanical Garden right on the river. The tropical conservatory offers a striking contrast to Iowa's prairie landscape outside.

Stop 5

Pella

Dutch village with North America's tallest working windmill

★★★☆☆
📍 Vermeer Windmill Pella Iowa📍 Pella Historical Village📍 Scholte House Pella📍 Klokkenspel Pella📍 Sunken Gardens Pella
1

Vermeer Windmill—At 124 feet tall, this is the tallest working windmill in North America. Built in the Netherlands by Lukas Verbij using an 1850s design, it was disassembled, shipped by boat, and reassembled in Pella in 2002. The guided tour takes you up five floors to the observation deck—from there, you see the blades turning just a few feet away. Flour milled on-site is used by local bakeries.

⏱ 60 min
2

Pella Historical Village—Right next to the windmill, this recreated village depicts the life of the first Dutch settlers who arrived in 1847. Among the buildings: Wyatt Earp's childhood home. Yes, the famous Wild West marshal grew up in Pella before heading to Dodge City. An interactive exhibit traces the Earp family history. You'll also see collections of Delft and Gouda pottery.

⏱ 60 min
3

Scholte House—The home of Hendrik Scholte, the pastor who founded Pella in 1847 by bringing 800 Dutch religious dissenters. The mansion and its Dutch-style gardens are self-guided.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll the central square to see the Klokkenspel, a mechanical clock with animated figures that tell Pella's story. The show runs a few minutes and performs at fixed times. Then walk through the Sunken Gardens—if you come in May during Tulip Time Festival, the town explodes with color.

⏱ 30 min

Try a Dutch letter at one of the bakeries on the square—this S-shaped puff pastry filled with almond paste is the local specialty. You can't leave Pella without tasting one.

Stop 6

Amana

Seven German pietist villages preserving communal craft traditions since 1855

★★★★☆
📍 Amana Colonies Iowa📍 Amana Heritage Museum📍 Millstream Brewery Amana📍 Amana Woolen Mill
1

Amana Colonies—Seven villages founded in 1855 by German Inspirationists, who lived communally until 1932. Everything was shared: land, meals, housing. Start at the Amana Heritage Museum to understand this unique history—photos, everyday objects, and a recreated communal kitchen.

⏱ 60 min
2

Amana's craft shops are worth exploring: handmade solid wood furniture, quilts, cuckoo clocks. This isn't tourist-trap kitsch—the artisans work on-site. The Amana Woolen Mill still weaves wool using 19th-century techniques.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Lunch at one of the colonies' family-style restaurants—German cuisine runs hearty: Sauerbraten (vinegar-marinated beef), Kartoffelsalat, Apfelstrudel. Portions are generous and the atmosphere feels like a big family gathering.

⏱ 60 min

End the day with a tasting at the local brewery or one of the area's wineries. Iowa produces wine—surprising but true—and cold-hardy hybrid grapes yield fruity whites.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 7

Iowa

★★★★★
📍 Old Capitol Museum Iowa City📍 University of Iowa campus📍 Iowa City Pedestrian Mall📍 Lake Macbride State Park
1

Old Capitol Museum—At the center of the University of Iowa campus, this former territorial capitol built in 1842 is topped by a gold dome. An accidental fire in 2001 destroyed the dome and cupola—it took four and a half years to fully restore. Climb to the second floor to see the 660-pound crystal and brass chandelier installed in the 1920s.

⏱ 45 min
2

Wander Iowa City Pedestrian Mall, a pedestrian zone lined with independent bookstores, cafés, and restaurants. Iowa City was designated a UNESCO City of Literature in 2008—the city is home to the legendary Iowa Writers' Workshop, which has launched dozens of major American writers.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Lake Macbride State Park—Twenty minutes north of Iowa City, this forested lake offers beach access, hiking trails, and kayak rentals. The waterfall spillway at the dam's edge makes for a good photo stop. Plan a half-day if you combine swimming and hiking.

West Branch

Birthplace of President Herbert Hoover with recreated tallgrass prairie

★★★★★
📍 Herbert Hoover National Historic Site West Branch📍 Herbert Hoover Presidential Library📍 Hoover Birthplace Cottage West Branch📍 Herbert Hoover grave West Branch
1

Herbert Hoover National Historic Site—The 31st U.S. President was born in this tiny two-room house in 1874. His father was a blacksmith. The original cottage, remarkably small, gives a tangible sense of the modest beginnings. Around it, a tallgrass prairie has been recreated to show the landscape Hoover saw as a child.

⏱ 30 min
2

Herbert Hoover Presidential Library & Museum—The museum traces the life of a man who lived many lives: mining engineer in Australia and China, humanitarian relief coordinator during World War I, then president during the Great Depression. The Depression exhibit is especially well done.

⏱ 60 min
3

Walk the site's loop trail—the blacksmith shop, Quaker meetinghouse, and the graves of Herbert and Lou Hoover, on a wooded hilltop overlooking the site, can be seen in 30 minutes. The grounds are peaceful and well-maintained.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll through the recreated tallgrass prairie—In summer, the grasses reach shoulder height and wildflowers attract butterflies and birds. A marked trail loops around in 30 minutes.

⏱ 30 min

Stop 9

Dubuque

Victorian Mississippi port with an 1882 funicular and towering bluffs

★★★★★
📍 Fenelon Place Elevator Dubuque📍 National Mississippi River Museum Dubuque📍 Dubuque Main Street Historic District📍 Shot Tower Dubuque
1

Fenelon Place Elevator—Locals call it "the world's shortest and steepest scenic railway": a 296-foot funicular connecting downtown to the bluff-top residential district. Built in 1882 by a banker who wanted to get home for lunch without taking the long road route. The view from the top sweeps across the Mississippi, three states (Iowa, Illinois, Wisconsin), and the Victorian city below.

⏱ 25 min
2

National Mississippi River Museum & Aquarium—This riverside museum traces the natural and human history of the Mississippi from its Minnesota headwaters to the Gulf of Mexico. Freshwater aquariums, historic boats, and interactive exhibits. Plan 2 hours to cover it all.

⏱ 90 min
3

Stroll the Main Street Historic District—red-brick Victorian buildings recall Dubuque's golden age as a river port and mining town in the 19th century. The Shot Tower, a 120-foot structure where lead shot was made by dropping molten lead from the top, is visible from the street.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Walk the Riverwalk at sunset—the golden light on the Mississippi and the Illinois bluffs across the water is one of Iowa's most beautiful sights.

Dubuque

Victorian Mississippi port with an 1882 funicular and towering bluffs

★★★★★
📍 Mines of Spain Recreation Area Dubuque📍 Julien Dubuque Monument📍 Crystal Lake Cave Dubuque
1

Mines of Spain Recreation Area—Five minutes south of Dubuque, this 1,500-acre natural area overlooks the Mississippi. The name comes from Julien Dubuque, a French Canadian who mined lead here starting in 1788 with Meskwaki approval. His stone tower monument, perched on a bluff, offers a sweeping view of the river. Several marked trails wind through forest and prairie.

⏱ 90 min
2

Crystal Lake Cave—Six miles south, this public cave displays stalactites, stalagmites, and calcite formations in underground chambers. Guided tour runs about 45 minutes. It stays 55°F inside year-round—bring a jacket.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

Hiking the Mines of Spain trails—The Catfish Creek Trail descends to the Mississippi shore through oak and walnut forest. You might spot white-tailed deer or a bald eagle—they nest along the bluffs.

Stop 11

Mcgregor

Antique village at the foot of Iowa's only national monument

★★★★☆
📍 Effigy Mounds National Monument Iowa📍 Fire Point Trail Effigy Mounds📍 Pikes Peak State Park Iowa📍 McGregor Iowa Main Street
1

Effigy Mounds National Monument—Iowa's only national monument. Across 2,500 acres along the Mississippi, 206 Native American mounds are preserved, including 31 shaped like animals: bears and birds, all oriented head-downriver. The oldest date to 500 BC. Start at the visitor center, then take the Fire Point Trail (3-mile round-trip)—it passes the Little Bear Mound and leads to a viewpoint 300 feet above the Mississippi. The view is breathtaking.

⏱ 120 min
2

Pikes Peak State Park—Nothing to do with Colorado's Pikes Peak. This state park offers Iowa's highest overlook above the Mississippi: 500 feet of vertical bluff. A boardwalk trail leads to Bridal Veil Falls, a cascade tumbling into a wooded ravine. From the main overlook, you see the confluence of the Wisconsin and Mississippi Rivers—a panorama stretching for miles.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Late in the day, head down to McGregor—this tiny riverside village lines up antique shops and cafés in 19th-century buildings. The atmosphere feels timeless.

⏱ 30 min

★★★★☆
📍 River Bluffs Scenic Byway Iowa📍 Guttenberg Iowa Mississippi📍 Motor Mill Historic Site Iowa📍 Spook Cave McGregor
1

River Bluffs Scenic Byway—This scenic route follows the bluffs above the Mississippi between McGregor and Guttenberg. Views of the river and deciduous forests rank among the Midwest's finest, especially in fall when the maples turn red.

⏱ 60 min
2

Guttenberg—This riverside village was founded by German immigrants who named it after Gutenberg (with one T, later corrected). The town overlook offers clear views of the river and lock system. Walk along the levee—it's peaceful.

⏱ 40 min
3

Motor Mill Historic Site—Ten miles west of Guttenberg, this 1869 limestone mill stands beside the Turkey River. Six stories, 3-foot-thick walls—one of the largest stone mills in the Midwest. It was never profitable and closed after just two years. The ruins are photogenic.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you're curious, stop at Spook Cave—a cave you tour by rowboat on an underground river. The entrance is a hole in the bluff just wide enough for the boat. Kitschy but fun.

Stop 13

Decorah

Norwegian valley with a downtown waterfall and natural year-round ice cave

★★★☆☆
📍 Dunnings Spring Decorah Iowa📍 Vesterheim Norwegian-American Museum Decorah📍 Decorah Fish Hatchery📍 Phelps Park Decorah
1

Vesterheim Norwegian-American Museum—The largest museum of Norwegian heritage outside Norway. Across 12 buildings, it traces the history of Norwegian immigrants who settled this valley starting in the 1850s. Collections of rosemaling (decorative painting), textiles, farm tools, and a recreated farmstead. Plan 90 minutes.

⏱ 75 min
2

Dunnings Spring—Right in downtown Decorah, this 200-foot waterfall drops from a limestone cliff into a shaded ravine. A staircase and walkway bring you to the base of the falls. It's a 5-minute walk from downtown—perfect for a cool-off break.

⏱ 25 min
3

Decorah Fish Hatchery—This state trout hatchery, nestled in a valley, raises rainbow and brown trout. The spring water feeding the pools is crystal-clear. Admission is free and kids love watching the fish. Right next door, a bald eagle nest is streamed live by webcam—Decorah became famous thanks to these eagles.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll downtown Decorah—Water Street is lined with independent shops, cafés, and craft breweries. The town has youthful energy despite its small size (8,000 people), thanks to Luther College, which has been here since 1861.

Stop 14

Decorah

Norwegian valley with a downtown waterfall and natural year-round ice cave

★★★☆☆
📍 Ice Cave Hill Decorah Iowa📍 Upper Iowa River Decorah📍 Phelps Park Decorah📍 Trout Run Trail Decorah
1

Ice Cave—Just outside Decorah, this natural rock cavity holds ice year-round thanks to a natural ventilation system. Cold air descends into the cave in winter and stays trapped. In mid-summer, you'll see ice formations while it's 85°F outside. A short trail leads from the parking area.

⏱ 30 min
2

Phelps Park—On the heights above Decorah, this park offers views across the Upper Iowa River valley and its limestone bluffs. A trail descends to a rustic stone arch bridge, photogenic and surrounded by ferns.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Biking or walking the Trout Run Trail—This 11-mile loop circles Decorah following the Upper Iowa River, passing bluffs, prairies, and woods. It's one of Iowa's most beautiful bike trails. Bike rentals available in town.

⏱ 90 min

Canoeing or kayaking the Upper Iowa River—The river winds between 100-foot forested limestone bluffs. Outfitters offer rentals with shuttle service. The Decorah-to-Bluffton section (9 miles) is the most popular—plan 3 to 4 hours at a relaxed pace.
