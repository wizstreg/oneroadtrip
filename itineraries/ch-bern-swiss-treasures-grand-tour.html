Bern

★★★★☆
1

Discover Bern, the federal capital of Switzerland and a UNESCO World Heritage treasure. Stroll through the perfectly preserved medieval old town featuring over 6 kilometers of covered arcades, Renaissance fountains adorned with colorful statues, and vaulted cellars housing boutiques and eateries. This captivating city founded in 1191 transports you through centuries of history.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the Zentrum Paul Klee, an architectural masterpiece designed by Renzo Piano housing the world's largest collection of Paul Klee's works. This undulating museum, harmoniously integrated into the rolling landscape, displays over 4,000 paintings, watercolors, and drawings by the Swiss-German artist, offering a fascinating journey through his revolutionary artistic evolution.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Obtain the Bern Museum Card for access to all city museums, including the Swiss Alpine Museum, the History Museum, and the Museum of Communication. This card allows you to explore the capital's rich cultural and historical collections at your own pace.

Activities

Cross the iconic covered wooden bridge spanning the Aare, offering picturesque views of the old town and surrounding hills.

Basel

★★★★☆
1

Discover Basel, Switzerland's third-largest city and cultural capital with over 40 museums. Explore the medieval old town dominated by the majestic red sandstone Münster cathedral, whose panoramic terrace offers spectacular views of the Rhine and city rooftops. The cobblestone alleys reveal architectural treasures at every corner.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the Marktplatz, the vibrant heart of the city, dominated by the impressive Rathaus with its vivid red painted facades. This 16th-century town hall, adorned with remarkable frescoes and an elegant tower, testifies to the historic prosperity of this merchant city at the crossroads of three countries.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Cross the Rhine aboard one of the traditional ferries powered solely by the river's current. These unique vessels, connected to a cable, use the water's force to glide from one bank to the other, offering an authentic and ecological experience.

Head to the Dreiländereck, the meeting point of Swiss, German, and French borders. This symbolic monument on the Rhine marks the only place where you can contemplate three nations simultaneously.

Schaffhausen

★★★★☆
1

Explore Schaffhausen, a historic town whose medieval center houses magnificent buildings adorned with frescoes and sculpted oriels. Climb to the circular Munot fortress, a unique 16th-century defensive structure, to enjoy panoramic views over the town, surrounding vineyards, and the Rhine winding through the valley.

⏱ 75 min
2

Marvel at the Rhine Falls, Europe's largest waterfalls at 150 meters wide and 23 meters high. This grand natural spectacle sees 600,000 liters of water per second plunging with a deafening roar. Boats take you to the base of the falls and to the central rock for an unforgettable immersive experience.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Explore the terraced vineyards overlooking the Rhine during a local wine tasting. The Schaffhausen region produces excellent Pinot Noir and Riesling-Sylvaner wines, witnesses to a centuries-old viticultural tradition favored by the valley's mild microclimate.

Zurich

★★★★☆
1

Discover Zurich, Switzerland's largest city and economic center. Stroll along Bahnhofstrasse, one of the world's most prestigious shopping avenues, lined with luxury boutiques and major brands. This elegant 1.4-kilometer artery connects the main station to the lake, crossing the historic heart of the metropolis.

⏱ 75 min
2

Explore the Altstadt, the old town with winding medieval lanes on both sides of the Limmat River. Admire the Grossmünster, the iconic Romanesque cathedral with its characteristic twin towers, whose construction began in the 12th century. Legend has it that Charlemagne himself discovered the tombs of Zurich's patron saints at this location.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Climb to Lindenhof, a linden tree-shaded hill offering panoramic views of the Limmat River and old town rooftops. This historic site, a former Roman fort, is now a peaceful haven in the heart of urban bustle, ideal for contemplating the Zurich landscape.

⏱ 60 min

Walk along Lake Zurich and enjoy splendid views of the snow-capped Alps in the background. The landscaped quays invite leisurely strolling, with green spaces, sculptures, and terraces overlooking the crystal-clear waters.

⏱ 60 min

Visit the Kunsthaus Zurich, one of Switzerland's most important art museums, housing an exceptional collection spanning medieval art to contemporary works, with masterpieces by Monet, Picasso, Giacometti, and numerous Swiss artists.

Lucerne

★★★★☆
1

Discover Lucerne, the pearl of Central Switzerland nestled on the shores of its namesake lake. Cross the Kapellbrücke, Europe's oldest covered wooden bridge dating from 1333, adorned with 111 triangular 17th-century paintings illustrating the city's history and saints' lives. The accompanying octagonal Water Tower completes this iconic picturesque tableau.

⏱ 75 min
2

Admire the Lion Monument, a poignant sculpture carved into natural rock commemorating the Swiss Guards who fell during the French Revolution. Mark Twain called this 1821 work, depicting a dying lion, 'the saddest and most moving piece of stone in the world'.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Wander through the cobblestone alleys of the old town, admiring facades painted with colorful frescoes and lively squares. The historic buildings with their carved oriels tell centuries of commercial and cultural history of this prosperous city.

⏱ 60 min

Don't miss the Lilu Light Festival if visiting in winter, when the city illuminates with spectacular artistic projections transforming historic monuments into magical luminous canvases.

Ascend Mount Pilatus, the legendary mountain rising to 2,132 meters, accessible by the world's steepest cogwheel railway with a 48% gradient. This engineering marvel inaugurated in 1889 hoists you through vertiginous Alpine landscapes to the summit offering 360° panoramas over the Alps and Swiss Plateau.

⏱ 75 min
2

Take the Drachenweg, the dragon path carved through the rock according to legends of dragons that once inhabited these caves. This mystical trail winds between rock walls and crevices, evoking the fantastical medieval tales associated with this mythical mountain.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Enjoy panoramic views from the summit's various viewpoints, embracing Lake Lucerne, the cantons of Nidwalden and Obwalden, and surrounding Alpine peaks. On clear days, views extend to the Jura Mountains and Black Forest.

⏱ 60 min

Descend by red gondolas and cable car to Kriens or by boat from Alpnachstad for a complete 'Golden Round Trip' experience, combining different panoramic transport modes.

Interlaken

★★★★☆
1

Arrive in Interlaken, spectacularly situated between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz in the heart of the Bernese Oberland. This charming resort town serves as the gateway to the Jungfrau region and offers stunning views of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau peaks. The town's unique position between two glacial lakes creates an unforgettable Alpine panorama.

Ascend to Harder Kulm by funicular to the peak at 1,322 meters above the town. Activities

Explore the St. Beatus Caves, a fascinating underground world of stalactites, stalagmites, and subterranean lakes. According to legend, the Irish monk Beatus drove a dragon from these caves in the 6th century. The illuminated walkways lead through chambers carved by water over millennia.

⏱ 60 min

Experience adventure activities that Interlaken is famous for, from paragliding over the lakes to kayaking on the crystal-clear waters. The town is Switzerland's adventure capital, offering unique experiences amidst spectacular scenery.

Grindelwald

★★★★☆
1

Explore Grindelwald, a stunning Alpine village nestled in a valley surrounded by the towering peaks of Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau. Journey to Jungfraujoch - Top of Europe, home to Europe's highest railway station at 3,454 meters. This remarkable destination offers an otherworldly experience among eternal ice and snow. Activities

Saas Fee

★★★★☆
1

Arrive in Saas-Fee, the 'Pearl of the Alps' and one of Switzerland's most spectacular car-free resort villages. Surrounded by 13 peaks over 4,000 meters, this glacier village sits at 1,800 meters in a dramatic mountain amphitheater. Electric vehicles and horse-drawn carriages maintain the peaceful atmosphere of this traffic-free paradise.

Dine at the Drehrestaurant Allalin, the world's highest revolving restaurant at 3,500 meters. The journey to the restaurant via the Metro Alpin, the world's highest underground funicular, is an experience in itself.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Experience year-round skiing and snowboarding on the Fee Glacier. The resort's high altitude guarantees excellent snow conditions even in summer, making it a training destination for professional athletes and a unique opportunity for visitors to ski in any season.

⏱ 60 min

Relax at one of the village's spa facilities offering mountain-inspired treatments. The combination of fresh Alpine air, stunning scenery, and wellness traditions creates a rejuvenating retreat in this romantic mountain setting.

Zermatt

Experience Zermatt, the iconic car-free village at the foot of the legendary Matterhorn.
