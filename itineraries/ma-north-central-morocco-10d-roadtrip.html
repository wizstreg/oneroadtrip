Grand Tour of Morocco: From Tangier to the Sahara Dunes

📅 20 days📍 20 stops🚗 2290 km⏱ 34h10

Tangier → Chefchaouen → Meknes → Fez → Merzouga → Ouarzazate → Marrakesh → Essaouira → Agadir → Casablanca

Tangier → Chefchaouen → Meknes → Fez → Merzouga → Ouarzazate → Marrakesh → Essaouira → Agadir → Casablanca

From Tangier and its mythical caves to the 150 m dunes of Erg Chebbi, this 20-day grand tour covers Morocco in all its diversity: millennial medinas of Fes and Marrakech, pisé kasbahs of the pre-Saharan south, Atlantic coast of Essaouira and Agadir, and natural parks where the world's last northern bald ibises nest. The route follows a logical loop from north to desert, then back up the coast to Casablanca.

Tangier

★★☆☆☆
1

Arrival in Tangier and first immersion in the medina: the Grand Socco, former marketplace, marks the crossroads between the colonial town and the old city. The Kasbah, perched atop the medina, houses the Dar el Makhzen - the former sultan's palace converted into a museum, displaying Roman mosaics and ethnographic collections. Climb to the terrace: the view sweeps across the Strait of Gibraltar and, on clear days, the Spanish coastline.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Wander the medina's alleys in late afternoon: the souks come alive, the scents of spices mingle with those of warm bread, and shopkeepers close up for the day. This is when Tangier reveals its true character, far from organized tours.

⏱ 60 min

Mint tea at Café Hafa, perched on a cliff overlooking the strait since 1921: generations of writers and artists have set down their notebooks here facing the horizon. Settle on the terrace to watch ferries pass between two continents.

⏱ 45 min

Tangier

★★☆☆☆
1

14 km west of Tangier, Cap Spartel is the extreme northwestern point of continental Africa: this is where the Mediterranean and Atlantic meet before your eyes. The lighthouse dates from 1864 - climb to the platform to watch the two seas merge into one. The sight is more impressive than you'd imagine.

⏱ 45 min
2

5 km from the cape, the Caves of Hercules open onto the Atlantic through a fissure whose shape eerily resembles the map of Africa. The site has been occupied for over 7,000 years BCE - archaeologists found stone tools here. According to legend, Hercules rested here before completing his eleventh labor. Come early morning to avoid the crowds; entry opens at 9am.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

After the caves, walk along Achakkar Beach at the foot of the cliffs: it's a wild, uncrowded beach where you can stroll along the rocks. A good contrast to the bustle of Tangier's port.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 130 km · 2h30

Chefchaouen

★★★★☆
1

Road from Tangier to Chefchaouen through the slopes of the Rif: the first mountain hairpins reveal the red tile roofs and first blue facades long before entering town. Late afternoon arrival recommended to catch the light in the medina.

⏱ 30 min
2

Place Uta el-Hammam is the heart of Chefchaouen: sidewalk cafés, the Great Mosque with its 15th-century octagonal minaret, and the ochre-walled Kasbah built by Moulay Ali Ben Rachid, founder of the city in 1471. Inside the Kasbah, an ethnographic museum displays costumes, musical instruments, and pottery - climb the tower for views over the medina's blue rooftops from above.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

First stroll through the blue alleys at dusk. Chefchaouen's blue traces back to the Sephardic Jewish community that arrived from Spain after 1492 - they painted the walls to symbolize sky and the divine. Get lost in dead-end streets without trying to see everything: that's the best plan.

⏱ 90 min

Chefchaouen

★★★★☆
1

The Spanish Mosque (Bouzaafar) is THE viewpoint of Chefchaouen: built in the 1920s during the protectorate but never used for worship, it's now the photographers' essential spot. Allow 25 to 30 minutes walking from Ras el-Maa. Arrive an hour before sunset - the medina shifts from brilliant blue to golden and orange tones as the sun descends behind the Rif ridges.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Ras el-Maa spring, a few minutes from the medina, is Chefchaouen's natural breathing point: water descends from the mountains, women come to wash laundry, children play on the rocks. It's a scene of daily life worth more than many monuments.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

For those with energy, the excursion to Akchour waterfalls (30 km from Chefchaouen) is one of the region's most beautiful: natural pools, cedar forest, and the famous Bridge of God, a natural rock formation. Local operators organize shared taxi transfers from the main square.

⏱ 240 min

Tour the artisan souks early morning - wool textiles with Berber patterns, rugs, and the town's signature blue pottery deserve a visit. Monday and Thursday, the local market sets up with farm products at local prices.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 200 km · 3h20

Meknes

★★★☆☆
1

Road from Chefchaouen to Meknes with a stop at Volubilis, Morocco's largest Roman site: 42 hectares of UNESCO-listed ruins, founded in the 3rd century BCE. At its peak, the city had between 10,000 and 20,000 inhabitants. The Arch of Caracalla (early 3rd century CE), the Villa of Venus mosaics, and the forum are the centerpieces. Go before 10am - after that, the heat is punishing and there's no shade on site.

⏱ 120 min
2

3 km from Volubilis, the holy village of Moulay Idriss Zerhoun clings to two hills shaped like camel humps. This is where Idris I rests, founder of Morocco's first Muslim dynasty and the country's first Islamic king. The village hosts annually, on the last Thursday of August, Morocco's largest Moussem (pilgrimage).

⏱ 60 min
3

Meknes, nicknamed the city of the 'Moroccan Louis XIV': Sultan Moulay Ismail made it his capital from 1672 and endowed it with 40 km of ramparts pierced by 20 monumental gates. Bab Mansour, main gate to the imperial city, is one of Morocco's most imposing. The Mausoleum of Moulay Ismail, open to non-Muslims, is a masterpiece of zellige and stucco.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Evening stroll through Meknes' medina: less touristy than Fes or Marrakech, it has retained an authentic character with its butchers, grocers, and artisans working in their shops.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 1h

Fez

★★★★★
1

The Chouara Tanneries of Fes are the largest and oldest artisanal tanneries in the world still operating - they function using methods unchanged since the 11th century. You view them from the terraces of leather shops overlooking the vats: bring a sprig of fresh mint that shopkeepers offer at the entrance to soften the smells. Morning between 8am and 10am, dyers are at work and colors are most vivid.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Bou Inania Madrasa, built between 1350 and 1357, is the only religious building in Fes open to non-Muslims. Its zelliges, cedar wood carvings, and moucharabiehs are among the finest examples of Marinid art in Morocco. Allow 45 minutes to explore without rushing.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Nejjarine Fountain and its square are the artisan heart of Fes: cabinetmakers work cedar wood in workshops opening onto the alley, and the Wood Museum housed in the adjacent fondouk displays regional crafts across four levels. The museum's terrace offers a plunging view over the medina's rooftops.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Get lost in Fes el-Bali in late afternoon - with its 9,000 alleys, the medina of Fes has been a UNESCO World Heritage site since 1981 and remains one of the largest pedestrian medinas in the Arab world. A local guide for two hours is recommended for the first day: landmarks are virtually nonexistent for uninitiated visitors.

⏱ 120 min

Fez

★★★★★
1

The Al-Qarawiyyin Mosque, founded in 859, is considered the world's oldest university still in operation. Not accessible to non-Muslims, but open doors onto the courtyard allow glimpses of the interior from certain adjacent alleys - a fleeting view sufficient to gauge the place's proportions.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Mellah, Fes' historic Jewish quarter, contrasts with the rest of the medina through its projecting balconies and different facades. The Ibn Danan Synagogue, restored, can be visited. From the Mellah, the gilded Royal Palace Gates are visible at the end of the grand esplanade - the palace itself is not open, but the entry gates are an essential photo subject.

⏱ 75 min
3

Borj Nord, a 16th-century fortress perched on the hill above the medina, houses the Arms Museum and above all offers the best panorama over Fes el-Bali - the medina sprawls across both valley slopes, with its rooftops, minarets, and the green mass of the Al-Qarawiyyin Mosque at center. See it in late afternoon for the light.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Moroccan cooking class: operators offer morning classes in medina riads - tagine, pastilla, briouats, and mint tea. A 3-hour morning that lets you leave with verified recipes.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 470 km · 6h30

Merzouga

★★★★☆
1

Road from Fes to Merzouga via the Ziz Valley and Midelt: the route crossing the Tizi n'Talrhmt pass (1,907 m) is one of the most spectacular passages in the Middle Atlas. As altitude descends, vegetation disappears and the ochre and red tones of the pre-Sahara take over. Arrival in Merzouga early afternoon.

⏱ 60 min
2

First contact with Erg Chebbi in late afternoon: the dunes stretch 22 km long and 5 km wide, with peaks reaching 150 m. It's one of the most imposing dune systems in the Moroccan Sahara. Sunset transforms the golden sand into a palette of red, orange, and violet - climb the nearest dune to the village to watch the transformation.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Camel ride to a bivouac in the open desert: local operators offer 1.5 to 2-hour round-trip packages by camel to reach a camp at the foot of the great dunes. Berber dinner, percussion music around the fire, and night under the stars. It's an experience that requires advance planning - book through your Merzouga riad or guesthouse upon arrival.

⏱ 180 min

Merzouga

★★★★☆
1

Sunrise from the summit of a dune: wake before dawn (allow 20 minutes minimum climbing) to be in place when light begins grazing the dune crests. The contrast between shadow and light on the waves of sand is the most photographed moment in the Moroccan Sahara. After sunrise, the desert's silence reclaims its dominion.

⏱ 90 min
2

Lake Dayet Srij, at the dunes' edge, is a seasonal lake that forms after rains and attracts flamingo colonies. Depending on season, birds are visible to the naked eye from the shore. The village of Khamlia, a few kilometers from Merzouga, is inhabited by a Gnawa community whose percussion music is played evenings.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Quad or 4x4 in the dunes during morning: operators offer one- or two-hour circuits through Erg Chebbi's secondary dunes, with access to zones usually inaccessible on foot. The experience is radically different from camel riding.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 310 km · 5h

Ouarzazate

★★★☆☆
1

Stop in the Dadès Gorges en route from Merzouga: the pink limestone cliffs close in on the road until forming a vertiginous corridor. At the gorges' entrance, the 'monkey fingers' - rock formations eroded into grotesque bulbs - are one of the strangest landscapes in pre-Saharan Morocco. Allow 30 to 40 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
2

The ksar of Aït Ben Haddou, 30 km from Ouarzazate, has been a UNESCO World Heritage site since 1987. This fortified village in pisé and raw earth, built from the 17th century on the trade route between Sudan and Marrakech, has served as a backdrop for some twenty Hollywood films - Gladiator, Game of Thrones, Lawrence of Arabia. Climb to the collective granary (agadir) atop the ksar for panorama over the Ounila Valley.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Dinner on a terrace in Ouarzazate facing the Atlas: the city, at 1,160 m altitude, marks the frontier between mountains and desert. The Draa Valley palm groves begin here.

⏱ 60 min

Ouarzazate

★★★☆☆
1

The Kasbah Taourirt, built in the 19th century for the powerful Glaoui tribe, dominates Ouarzazate from downtown. A section is open for visits: stucco-decorated apartments, interior courtyards, and terraces offer a glimpse of Berber luxury of the era. Allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 60 min
2

Excursion into the Draa Valley southward: from Ouarzazate, the valley stretches 200 km through palm groves, pisé villages, and kasbahs. The first 50 km, to Agdez, concentrate the most beautiful landscapes - palms, vegetable crops, and kasbahs that surge at every bend in the road.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Visit the Atlas Studios of Ouarzazate: one of the world's largest open-air filming complexes, used since the 1960s for productions from Lawrence of Arabia to Babel. Guided tours access sets still standing - Jerusalem, Rome, or Egyptian ruins in papier-mâché amid the desert. It's kitschy but fascinating.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 205 km · 3h30

Marrakesh

★★★★★
1

Road from Ouarzazate to Marrakech via the Tichka Pass (2,260 m): it's the highest passage on Morocco's national road network. The climb is long and hairpins numerous - landscapes change dramatically at each altitude, from pre-desert to cedar forests. At the summit, stop for views over both Atlas slopes. Allow 3.5 to 4 hours driving.

⏱ 30 min
2

Arrival in Marrakech mid-afternoon. First approach to the medina's ochre ramparts, 19 km long and dating from the 12th century - they give the city that characteristic tint earning it the nickname 'red city'. Settle into a medina riad for total immersion.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

First evening on Jemaa el-Fna square: at nightfall, the square transforms into a permanent spectacle - storytellers, snake charmers, Gnawa musicians, orange juice stands, and smells of grilled skewers. It's been UNESCO-listed as intangible cultural heritage since 2001. Arrive at 7pm to watch the square gradually come alive.

⏱ 120 min

Marrakesh

★★★★★
1

The Koutoubia Mosque, built in the 12th century by the Almohads, is one of North Africa's largest mosques with its 70 m minaret - the model for Seville's Giralda. Not accessible to non-Muslims, but the surrounding gardens offer an unobstructed view of the entire tower. Morning recommended for light.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Bahia Palace, built in the late 19th century by Grand Vizier Si Moussa, extends across 8 hectares of gardens, interior courtyards, and decorated apartments. Its name means 'the brilliant' - the painted cedar ceilings, mosaics, and moucharabiehs justify the appellation. Visit is self-guided, allow 1 hour.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Saadian Tombs, rediscovered in 1917 beneath a layer of plaster, house the sepulchers of 66 members of the Saadian dynasty including Sultan Ahmad al-Mansour (died 1603). The main hall, decorated with 12 Carrara marble columns, is a jewel of Hispano-Moorish art. Arrive at opening (9am) to avoid the line.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Dive into Marrakech's souks: each street has its specialty - leather, spices, babouches, brass, carpets. The dyers' souk (Souk des Teinturiers) is most spectacular, with wool skeins hanging from facades in red, yellow, and violet. Bargaining obligatory and advised with a smile.

⏱ 120 min

Marrakesh

★★★★★
1

The Majorelle Garden, created from 1923 by a French painter and purchased in 1980 by a couturier who saved it from destruction, is Morocco's most visited garden. The intense cobalt blue of the structures - christened 'Majorelle blue' - contrasts with the green of bamboos and cacti. Book online: capacity is limited and queues without reservation are long from 10am. Arrival at opening recommended.

⏱ 75 min
2

The fashion and art museum adjacent to Jardin Majorelle traces the history of a great French couture house through its archives, sketches, and a selection of iconic pieces. The building's architecture in ribbed red brick is itself remarkable.

⏱ 60 min
3

Marrakech's Mellah, historic Jewish quarter adjacent to the royal palace, preserves ancient synagogues and distinctive architecture with its overhanging balconies. The Lazama Synagogue, from the 16th century, is the most accessible and best preserved.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Traditional hammam in late afternoon: medina hammams offer the complete package - steam, scrubbing with kessa glove, black beldi soap, and argan oil massage. Allow 1.5 hours. A way to close two days of walking through the souks.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 185 km · 3h20

Essaouira

★★★☆☆
1

Arrival in Essaouira and first walk on the ramparts: the former Mogador was built in the 18th century following plans inspired by Vauban's military architecture, at the request of Sultan Mohammed ben Abdellah. The Skala de la Ville, a 200 m crenellated esplanade facing the Atlantic, is armed with some twenty 18th-century Spanish bronze cannons. Atlantic wind is permanent - bring a windbreaker even in summer.

⏱ 60 min
2

Essaouira's medina, a UNESCO World Heritage site since 2001, is laid out in a grid - unlike the labyrinth of Fes or Marrakech, you can navigate easily. Streets smell of thuya sawdust: artisans carve this fragrant wood into objects, boxes, and furniture. The fishing port remains active: go at noon to choose your fish from the stalls and have it grilled on the spot.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Sunset on Essaouira beach from the ramparts: the beach extends several kilometers south of town and powerful winds make it a world-renowned windsurf and kitesurf site. Even without practicing, watching kitesurfers evolve at sunset with the walls behind is a spectacle in itself.

⏱ 90 min

Essaouira

★★★☆☆
1

The Skala de la Kasbah, the medina's oldest quarter (18th century), is accessed through a small door leading to a walkway overlooking the port. From here the bronze cannons protected the port entrance - artillery pieces over 3 m long could launch cannonballs 1,000 m. A unique view over the Purpuraires Islands and fishing boats.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Bayt Dakira Museum traces Essaouira's Judeo-Moroccan history, a city that was long a center of coexistence between Muslims, Jews, and Christians. Essaouira's Jewish community played a vital commercial role - the recently restored and opened museum is one of the town's most interesting visits.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Horseback or camel ride on the beach south of Essaouira: local operators offer one-hour walks at water's edge, with the town's ramparts as backdrop. A way to see Essaouira from the beach.

⏱ 60 min

Grilled fish lunch at the port: choose your fresh fish from the ice-filled stalls - sea bream, sole, shrimp, or sardines - and it's grilled immediately before you. A typical port experience, in an atmosphere of fish market and charcoal smoke.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 175 km · 2h45

Agadir

★★★☆☆
1

Road from Essaouira to Agadir along the Atlantic coast: argan trees appear shortly after Essaouira - it's one of the rare places in the world where these trees grow, and goats climbing their branches to eat the fruit are an authentic spectacle (not staged, unlike certain tourist spots). The argan forest is a UNESCO biosphere reserve.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Kasbah of Agadir Oufella, perched on a hill 236 m above the bay, is the only vestige from before the 1960 earthquake that destroyed the city in 15 seconds. The view from the summit sweeps the entire Bay of Agadir, its 9 km beach, and the Souss mountains behind. The drive or walk up is worth the trip.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll through Souk El Had, Morocco's largest covered market: 6,000 shops across multiple levels - spices, textiles, leathers, fruits, and vegetables. It's a local market, not a tourist market - prices are posted and pressure is less than in Marrakech.

⏱ 90 min

Agadir

★★★☆☆
1

Souss Massa National Park, created in 1991 between Agadir and Tiznit, extends across 33,800 hectares of coastal cliffs, dunes, wetlands, and steppes. The Oued Massa estuary is a Ramsar site of international importance for migratory birds: on the 5 km walking circuit, you'll observe flamingos, spoonbills, grey herons, cormorants, and - if you're lucky - the northern bald ibis, one of the world's most threatened species with 95% of the wild population nesting here. Binoculars recommended.

⏱ 120 min
2

The town of Tiznit, 90 km south of Agadir, is known for its medina ringed by 7 km of pink ramparts and for its silversmiths - the town is Morocco's main center of Berber jewelry. Place du Méchouar and its blue spring are the quiet heart of the old town.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

4x4 circuit in the park's animal reserves (Rokein and Arrouis): addax with long horns, dorcas gazelles, scimitar oryx, and red-necked ostrich - species extinct in the wild in Morocco and reintroduced here since the 1990s. Visits are mandatory with a park guide.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 465 km · 5h

Casablanca

★★★★☆
1

Road from Agadir to Casablanca with a stop in Rabat: the Hassan Tower, an unfinished 12th-century minaret, was to be the minaret of the world's largest mosque according to Almohad Sultan Yacoub al-Mansour. His death in 1199 stopped work at 44 m - half the planned height. The 200 columns of the never-completed mosque today form a silent field of stones around the tower.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Mausoleum of Mohammed V, facing the Hassan Tower, houses the tombs of King Mohammed V, architect of Morocco's independence, his son Hassan II, and Prince Moulay Abdallah. Built between 1961 and 1971 by 400 Moroccan artisans, its green-tiled pyramidal roof, white Italian marble, and zelliges make it a masterpiece of contemporary Moroccan art. Entry is free and open to non-Muslims.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Kasbah des Oudayas, a 12th-century Almohad fortress on the Bouregreg estuary, dominates the river and Atlantic. Its blue and white alleys resemble a miniature Chefchaouen. Café Maure, inside the kasbah, is the place for mint tea facing the estuary.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Chellah, 2 km from central Rabat, combines Roman vestiges of the ancient city of Sala Colonia (1st century) and a 14th-century Marinid necropolis in a garden invaded by storks nesting on the minarets. A historic monument since 1920 and UNESCO heritage since 2012, it's one of Morocco's most unusual sites.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 1h15

Casablanca

★★★★☆
1

The Hassan II Mosque, built on the sea between 1987 and 1993, is Africa's largest mosque and the third in the Islamic world with its 210 m minaret. Its prayer hall can accommodate 25,000 worshippers, and 80,000 additional people on its esplanade. Unique feature: part of the glass floor allows viewing the Atlantic Ocean from inside.

⏱ 90 min
2

Casablanca's downtown Art Deco quarter, built during the French protectorate (1920s-1950s), concentrates one of the world's most remarkable Art Deco architecture collections outside Miami. Place Mohammed V, with its fountains and arcades, is the center. Walk among facades ornamented with geometric forms and Moroccan motifs reinterpreted in concrete.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Interior guided tour of Hassan II Mosque for non-Muslims: official operators offer organized visits morning and afternoon. It's one of the rare great mosques in the Islamic world accessible to visitors of all faiths - book in advance, places are limited.

⏱ 90 min

Seafood lunch on the Corniche of Casablanca before departure: the waterfront, stretching from the port to Ain Diab, is lined with fish restaurants with terraces on the Atlantic. A final image of Morocco facing the ocean.

⏱ 90 min
