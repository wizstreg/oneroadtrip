  • Midnight sun in the Lofoten and Lapland from late May to mid-July: 24 hours of continuous light

📆 Best period

June, July, August, September, October, February, March

⏳ How many days

To make the most of it, plan between 8 and 12 days.

☀️ Weather

Summer (June-August): Perfect weather conditions (18-25°C) with midnight sun in north, white nights, and ideal conditions for outdoor activities. Peak tourist season with highest prices but most comfortable temperatures and endless daylight hours.

Late Spring (May): Excellent weather emergence with blooming landscapes, fewer crowds, and moderate prices. Traditional Midsummer celebrations and optimal conditions for hiking and cycling.

Early Autumn (September-October): Beautiful fall colors with comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, and moderate prices. Excellent for cultural activities and northern lights begin appearing.

Winter (December-March): Cold temperatures (-5 to -15°C) with snow, northern lights, and winter activities. Lower prices, authentic Nordic winter experience, and excellent conditions for winter sports.

🌍 Discover

Nordic paradise with pristine nature, progressive society, and rich cultural heritage. Experience midnight sun, traditional culture, and incredible natural beauty. The country offers cultural authenticity with natural preservation and progressive values. Sweden provides Nordic experiences with cultural depth and environmental leadership.

📋 Notes

The Moskenes→Bodø ferry is essential to book in high season (6+ hour wait without reservation). Pick up your rental car in Narvik, return in Bodø or Tromsø depending on availability.

Stockholm

fourteen islands weave the Swedish capital between Lake Mälaren and the Baltic Sea

★★★★★
1

Welcome to Gamla Stan, the birthplace of Stockholm: the old town spreads across three islands - Stadsholmen, Riddarholmen and Helgeandsholmen - between Lake Mälaren and the Baltic Sea, exactly as it was in the 13th century when Birger Jarl founded the city here in 1252. The ochre and red facades dominating Stortorget, Stockholm's oldest square, once belonged to a medieval fortress. This same square was also the site of the 1520 Stockholm Bloodbath, one of Scandinavia's most infamous political massacres. Arrive early in the morning before the tour groups - the silence of the cobbled streets at dawn is worth as much as the monuments themselves.

⏱ 90 min
2

At the heart of Gamla Stan, the Royal Palace (Kungliga Slottet) contains over 600 rooms and remains one of the official residences of the Swedish monarch. Built in the 17th and 18th centuries in baroque style, it houses several museums including the Royal Treasury and the Tre Kronor Museum. If you're here in summer, don't miss the changing of the guard - it takes place daily at 12:15pm and is accompanied in July-August by the military mounted band.

⏱ 75 min
3

Get lost in Mårten Trotzigs Gränd, Stockholm's narrowest alley at just 90 centimeters at its tightest point, then stroll along Österlånggatan to wander past the Hanseatic houses that recall how German merchants controlled much of Baltic trade from these walls in the Middle Ages. In the evening, Stortorget becomes one of Stockholm's best spots to dine on a terrace facing the colorful facades.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Settle in and enjoy the evening for dinner in Gamla Stan or Södermalm - this is your first night in Scandinavia, take your time. Tomorrow you'll have a full day in Stockholm before boarding the overnight train.

Stockholm

fourteen islands weave the Swedish capital between Lake Mälaren and the Baltic Sea

★★★★★
1

Dedicate your morning to the Vasa Museum on the island of Djurgården. The Vasa was the most impressive warship of its era: 64 cannons, 700 sculptures, 1,200 tons. It sank on August 10, 1628, twenty minutes after leaving Stockholm harbor, after sailing just 1,300 meters. Salvaged 98% intact in 1961 after 333 years on the harbor floor, it's now the only 17th-century vessel preserved in the world. Allow at least 2 hours and arrive at opening (8:30am in summer) to see the ship without the tour group crowds.

⏱ 120 min
2

After the Vasa, stay on Djurgården for Skansen, the world's largest open-air museum: 160 historic buildings dismantled throughout Sweden and reconstructed here, forming a miniature 19th-century Sweden. It's also a zoo with moose, lynx and reindeer. If the weather cooperates, the island deserves a long walk along the quays.

⏱ 90 min
3

In late afternoon, head to Södermalm, Stockholm's bohemian district south of Gamla Stan. Climb to Monteliusvägen for the best panoramic view of the old town at sunset - locals gather here in the evening for exactly this spectacle. The neighborhood is filled with vintage shops, galleries and restaurants.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

To understand why Stockholm is built on 14 islands, take a ferry from Nybroviken to Vaxholm (45 min) or the archipelago islands. In summer, some Stockholmers swim directly from the rocks. It's the best way to grasp that the city and water are one.

⏱ 240 min

Tonight, board the SJ Nattåg 94 overnight train from Stockholm Central. Departs around 6pm, arrives at Abisko Östra the next day around 11am - 17 hours of travel crossing Sweden from south to north. Book in advance: seats go fast in aurora season and summer. You can choose between a seat in an open car, a 6-berth sleeper compartment, or a private 2-3 person cabin. This train is a journey in itself - the landscapes between Kiruna and Abisko in the last hour are among the most beautiful of the crossing.

Abisko

the blue hole microclimate offers the finest northern lights in Lapland

★★★☆☆
1

Arrival at Abisko Östra around 11am after 17 hours by train. Abisko National Park, created in 1909, covers 77 km² of birch valleys, Arctic mountains and tundra along Lake Torneträsk. Start at the Naturum Abisko visitor center to orient your stay and pick up a trail map.

⏱ 45 min
2

In the afternoon, follow the Abiskojåkka canyon trail - allow 1 hour round trip for the short version, longer if you also climb upstream below the railway bridge. The river has carved a deep gorge into the rock. Tip: head up toward the mountains from Abisko Turiststation, not just toward the lake - the most impressive viewpoints are on this side.

⏱ 90 min
3

Late in the day, descend to the shore of Lake Torneträsk, Scandinavia's largest mountain lake with its 330 km² of glacial water framed by peaks. On clear days, you'll spot the famous U-shaped Lapporten pass (Čuonjávággi in Sami), a glacial valley carved by glaciers that remains one of Lapland's iconic symbols. The wood-fired sauna right on the shore can be reserved at Abisko Turiststation the same morning.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

If you're here from September to March, the Aurora Sky Station on Mount Nuolja is the evening rendezvous. The chairlift - Sweden's longest - takes you up to 900 meters altitude. At the top, a panoramic terrace and heated café. Abisko benefits from an exceptional microclimate: the mountains create a 'blue hole' that regularly clears the sky even when it's raining in Kiruna 100 km away. Book ahead, spaces are limited.

⏱ 180 min

1

⏱ 180 min

Activities

If you prefer to stay in the valley, the easy trail along both banks of the Abiskojokk to the Torneträsk shore (3.5 km round trip, accessible to all) is perfect for observing Arctic birds: Arctic loon, ptarmigan, northern warbler. The park is also home to moose and lynx, discreet but present.

Narvik

ore port at the fjord's end, theater of the first Allied victory in 1940

★★★☆☆
1

The Abisko-Narvik road (85 km, 1h15) crosses the Norwegian border at Riksgränsen and descends toward the fjords. In Narvik, begin at the War Museum (Narviksenteret). The Battle of Narvik from April 9 to June 8, 1940 lasted 62 days and cost 8,500 lives. It was at Narvik that Allied forces - British, French, Polish and Norwegian - won their very first land victory against Nazi Germany. The museum recounts these 62 days battle by battle, through photos, films and audio testimonies. Allow 1.5 hours.

⏱ 100 min
2

Narvik's harbor deserves a stroll: it's why the battle took place. Narvik was the export port for iron ore from Kiruna (Sweden) - an ice-free port year-round, connected to the Swedish mines by the Ofotbanen, the railway line built in 1902. Hitler needed this ore for his war industry: 90% of Swedish iron passed through Narvik in winter. The city still lives to the rhythm of this rail traffic.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take the cable car up Narvikfjellet for a panoramic view of the city, Ofotfjord and mountains from 656 meters. In summer under the midnight sun, the panorama is striking; in winter, it's one of the best vantage points for northern lights away from city lights.

Svolvaer

capital of Lofoten perched between fjords and granite rock needles

★★★★☆
1

Welcome to the Lofoten. The Narvik-Svolvær route via the E10 (3-4h) is an experience in itself: it runs along fjords, passes through tunnels carved into rock and crosses suspension bridges between islands. Svolvær, the de facto capital of Lofoten with 5,000 inhabitants, lives to the rhythm of its harbor. Head down to the quays: fishing boats, cod drying racks, rorbuer on stilts reflecting in the water. Skrei fishing (Arctic cod) remains the archipelago's engine - between January and April, Svolvær sees the world's largest cod catches.

⏱ 60 min
2

Look up from the harbor: the Svolværgeita dominates the city from the flank of Mount Fløyfjellet. These two 150-meter rock pinnacles, nicknamed 'the Svolvær Goat', are the city's emblem. Climbers from around the world come to scale them. For the view without ropes, climb to Fløya (590 m, 2h from the center) - guaranteed 360° panorama over the archipelago.

⏱ 75 min
3

Push open the door of the Gunnar Berg Gallery, dedicated to the painter born here in 1863. His canvases capture the light of the Lofoten in the 19th century - stormy skies, reflections on water, fishermen's silhouettes. A way to understand these landscapes differently than through photos.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take a speedboat excursion to Trollfjord, a 2 km fjord arm squeezed between vertical granite walls - so narrow that large boats can barely maneuver there. This is also where white-tailed eagles, with their wingspan of over 2 meters, dive to catch fish. Operators offer this outing departing from the harbor.

⏱ 180 min

Henningsvaer

the football pitch suspended between rocks symbolizes Henningsvær

★★★☆☆
1

Henningsvær, nicknamed the 'Venice of Lofoten', spreads across several small islands connected by bridges - from Google Maps, you can clearly see the shape of the miniature archipelago. Park at the entrance and explore on foot: the streets are too narrow for cars. The harbor and its old converted fishing warehouses are classified as Kulturminne (Norwegian cultural heritage). Cod fishing remains the main activity - in February-March, the cod season transforms the quays into a scene of continuous work.

⏱ 60 min
2

Henningsvær's football field is probably the most photographed in the world: built on a rocky islet between two arms of sea, without stands - spectators bring their own chairs. The local club uses it for amateur matches, and the site also hosts concerts and festivals.

⏱ 30 min
3

The KaviarFactory is one of the most unexpected cultural surprises in Northern Norway. Opened in 2013 in a former 1950s caviar factory rehabilitated into 500 m² of exhibition space, it presents internationally renowned artists - Ai Weiwei, Marina Abramovic, Yoko Ono have all exhibited here. Next door, Galleri Lofoten displays the largest collection of painting from the 'golden age of North Norwegian art' (1870-1930). Two world-class museums in a village of 500 inhabitants.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

For a short but striking hike, climb Festvågtind (541 m). The ascent is steep and short, but the 360° view over Henningsvær, the archipelago and the open sea is one of the best in the eastern Lofoten. Allow 2h30 round trip. Operators also offer sea kayaking in the islets around the village.

Reine

Reine's red cabins on stilts facing the peaks are Norway's most photographed

★★★★☆
1

Reine is often described as Norway's most beautiful village - hard to dispute when you arrive by road and the red cabins on stilts suddenly appear facing the peaks plunging into Reinefjord. Reine's rorbuer, painted red, were once the lodgings of fishermen who came from the mainland to work the cod from January to April. Many have been converted into accommodations - book well in advance.

⏱ 60 min
2

A few kilometers away, the village of Å (pronounced 'Aw') is the last in the archipelago - the road ends there. This end of the world is visited like an open-air museum: a boathouse, a cod liver oil factory, a forge, an old post office, rorbuer. The Norwegian Fishing Village Museum traces the history of cod fishing in 7 buildings. Halfway, don't miss Hamnøy: the red cabins seen from the bridge are perhaps the most famous photo of the Lofoten.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

The Reinebringen hike (448 m) is the most famous in the Lofoten. The ascent is on a staircase of 1,600 stone steps laid in 2019 by Nepalese workers accustomed to Himalayan trails. Allow 1h30 to 2h climbing. At the summit, the plunging view over the Reinefjord, Kjerkfjord and Vorfjord with the red cabins below is the most photographed panorama in the Lofoten. Leave early - the parking lot fills up quickly in season.

Bodo

gateway to the Lofoten and site of Saltstraumen, the world's strongest tidal current

★☆☆☆☆
1

Moskenes→Bodø ferry: absolutely must book in advance in summer - without reservation, wait time can exceed 6h at the Moskenes dock. The crossing lasts 3h15 to 3h45 across Vestfjord, sometimes choppy. It's also on this route that you can spot eagles and, in season, whales.

⏱ 225 min
2

From Bodø, head to Saltstraumen 30 km southeast. In this 150-meter-wide strait, 400 million cubic meters of water rush through every 6 hours when the tide reverses, reaching 22 knots (40 km/h) and forming whirlpools 10 meters in diameter and 5 meters deep. It's officially the world's strongest tidal current, a phenomenon 2,000 to 3,000 years old. The spectacle is most intense two to three days before or after the full and new moon - check tide times before going.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If you have energy, climb Mount Keiservarden from Bodø for a panoramic view over the city, the sea and the silhouettes of the Lofoten on the horizon. It's also the evening in Bodø that you organize what's next: the flight to Tromsø the following morning.

⏱ 120 min

Tromso

capital of Arctic Norway nicknamed the Paris of the North for its cultural life since the 19th century

★★★★☆
1

Bodø→Tromsø flight (~1h). Tromsø: an island connected to the mainland by a one-kilometer bridge, surrounded by fjords and mountains 350 km north of the Arctic Circle. With 77,000 inhabitants, it's Norway's eighth largest city, nicknamed the 'Paris of the North' - a nickname from the 19th century, when upper-class women had their dresses imported directly from Paris and nightlife was surprisingly lively for such a northern city. Start with the Arctic Cathedral (Ishavskatedralen), built in 1964-1965 by architect Jan Inge Hovig: its triangular silhouette symbolizes icebergs, the long darkness and the northern lights. The eastern stained glass window - 140 m² of glass - is one of Europe's largest.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Polar Museum (Polarmuseet) occupies a former 1833 customs warehouse, classified as national heritage since 2000. It traces whaling, sealing, and the great polar expeditions. Roald Amundsen - first man to the South Pole in 1911 - has an entire room, as does Fridtjof Nansen. Allow 1h30.

⏱ 90 min
3

In late afternoon, stroll through Tromsø's old town: the colorful wooden houses from the 19th and 20th centuries are among the best preserved in Northern Norway. The wooden church in the center (Tromsø Domkirke, 1861) is Norway's only Lutheran wooden cathedral.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the dark season (September-March), a nighttime northern lights excursion from Tromsø is essential. Operators offer outings by minibus or boat to the surrounding fjords. Tromsø is one of Europe's best bases for this: the sky is statistically clearer more often than at equivalent latitudes.

Tromso

capital of Arctic Norway nicknamed the Paris of the North for its cultural life since the 19th century

★★★★☆
1

Start with the Fjellheisen cable car, in service since 1961. In four minutes, it takes you up to 420 meters on Mount Storsteinen. The panorama embraces the city on its island, the one-kilometer bridge, the fjords and mountains on the horizon. In winter, it's one of Tromsø's best spots to observe the auroras. In summer, it's the starting point for hikers heading to Tromsdalstinden (1,238 m).

⏱ 120 min
2

Stop by Mack Brewery, founded in 1877 by Ludwig Markus Mack, son of a German immigrant. Mack long remained the world's northernmost brewery. Its historic pub, Ølhallen, open since 1928, is Tromsø's oldest brewery-bar. It's the perfect spot for an Arctic beer facing the old docks.

⏱ 60 min
3

A detour through Polaria, the Arctic aquarium whose building evokes white concrete dominoes toppled by a wave. Inside, bearded seals, Arctic fish and an exhibition on phenomena of the Far North.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

If you have a car, head out to Kvaløya island or the Lyngen Alps for wild fjords less than an hour away. Local hikers go there year-round. In the evening, prepare for the return flight to Stockholm the next day.

Stockholm

fourteen islands weave the Swedish capital between Lake Mälaren and the Baltic Sea

★★★★★
1

Tromsø→Stockholm flight (~2h30). Back in the Swedish capital with a fresh perspective after 13 days in the Arctic. Make the most of these final hours with a canal cruise from Nybrokajen or Slussen - the best way to understand that Stockholm is built on 14 islands.

⏱ 120 min
2

The City Hall (Stadshuset, 1923) on Kungsholmen island hosts the Nobel Prize banquet every December 10 since 1930 - in the Blue Hall, which isn't blue: architect Ragnar Östberg changed his mind at the last minute and kept the red bricks exposed, but the name was already printed on the plans. The guided tour tells this anecdote.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Last evening in Stockholm: climb to Monteliusvägen or Fjällgatan in Södermalm for one last panoramic view of the old town lights at twilight - the view that sums up Stockholm better than any postcard.
