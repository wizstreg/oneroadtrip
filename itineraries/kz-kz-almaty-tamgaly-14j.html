Stop 1

Almaty

Former capital backed by the snow-capped Tian Shan at 900 metres

★★★★☆
📍 Zenkov Cathedral Almaty📍 Panfilov Park Almaty📍 Museum of Folk Musical Instruments Almaty📍 Green Bazaar Almaty
1

Begin with Zenkov Cathedral, at the heart of Panfilov Park. This 56-metre-tall Orthodox church was built between 1904 and 1907 from Tian Shan spruce timber - practically without nails, using an ingenious system of interlocking beams with rolling hooks. When the devastating 1911 Kebin earthquake struck the region, it remained standing while countless buildings collapsed. Architect Zenkov, who had overseen construction and added earthquake-resistant modifications, wrote that the bell tower swayed like a treetop in wind, bent but intact. Step inside to see Khludov's gilded iconostasis - you won't find anything comparable in Central Asia.

⏱ 45 min
2

Right next door, the Museum of Folk Musical Instruments occupies an elegant 1908 wooden building designed by that same Zenkov. You'll find over 1,000 instruments representing 60 types, including dombras and kobyzes that belonged to celebrated composers. Some date back to the 17th century. If you catch the Sazgen ensemble, stay - they perform live.

⏱ 45 min
3

Head to the Central State Museum of Kazakhstan, the country's finest museum. In the entrance hall, a life-size replica of the Golden Man greets you - this 5th-4th century BC Saka warrior discovered near Issyk in 1969, covered in gold plaques, has become a national symbol. Don't miss the Gold Hall with its 2,500-year-old Scythian ornaments. Allow at least 90 minutes.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

End the day at the Green Bazaar - the two-level covered market exudes authentic Central Asian atmosphere. Sample dried fruits, kurt (dried cheese balls), mountain honey and loose spices. Even if you don't buy anything, it's worth visiting for the ambience and colours alone.

⏱ 60 min

Stroll along pedestrian Jibek Joly street (Almaty's Arbat) - café terraces and craft shops line the way. In the evening, locals gather here for a leisurely walk.

Almaty

Former capital backed by the snow-capped Tian Shan at 900 metres

★★★★☆
📍 Medeu Ice Rink Almaty📍 Shymbulak Ski Resort Almaty📍 Kok Tobe Hill Almaty📍 Arasan Baths Almaty
1

Head for Medeu, 15 km southeast of the centre. This open-air ice rink is the world's highest - 1,691 metres altitude. The skating venue has existed since 1951, but the current stadium dates from 1972. Over 120 world speed skating records have been set here thanks to exceptional ice quality at altitude. Even outside skating season, the site merits the trip for the panoramic view of the snow-capped Zailiysky Alatau peaks.

⏱ 60 min
2

From Medeu, take the cable car up to Shymbulak resort at 2,200-2,500 m altitude. The gondola offers plunging views over the valley. At the summit, even in summer, you can hike the ridges and observe peaks over 4,000 m surrounding Almaty. The contrast between the modern city below and the glaciers above is striking.

⏱ 75 min
3

Late afternoon, ascend Kok Tobe by cable car from Dostyk Avenue - six minutes above Almaty's rooftops to reach 1,100 m. At the top, the 372 m TV tower (the world's tallest tubular steel structure, designed to withstand the most powerful earthquakes) dominates the park. Stay for sunset - the view of the city lighting up with mountains as backdrop is the finest in Almaty.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Treat yourself to a session at the Arasan Baths, one of the last great Soviet public baths still operating. The circular building offers Russian, Finnish and Oriental sections. Allow about 2 hours to enjoy the hammam, sauna and cold plunge - a local ritual not to be missed.

Almaty

Former capital backed by the snow-capped Tian Shan at 900 metres

★★★★☆
📍 Big Almaty Lake Kazakhstan📍 Ile-Alatau National Park Almaty
1

Today, head for Big Almaty Lake - a glacial lake at 2,511 m altitude, just 15 km south of the centre. The lake stretches 1.6 km long and changes colour with the seasons: vivid turquoise in July-August when glacial melt peaks, deep green in autumn, pale grey in spring. The hike from the checkpoint runs about 7 km one way (300 m elevation gain) - allow 2.5 hours going up. Warning: swimming is forbidden, as this is Almaty's drinking water reservoir. Bring water and snacks, there's nothing en route.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike round-trip to Big Almaty Lake via the pipeline trail or the winding road. Start early - before 8am - to avoid heat and enjoy the perfect reflection of peaks in the still morning water. Sovetov Peak (4,317 m), Ozyorny (4,110 m) and Tourist (3,954 m) frame the lake. Pack a warm layer even in summer - it's 10-15°C cooler than in the city.

Stop 4

Altyn Emel

Kazakhstan's largest national park with its 150-metre singing dune

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Singing Dune Altyn Emel📍 Besshatyr Burial Mounds Altyn Emel📍 Oshaktas Steles Altyn Emel
1

Drive to Altyn-Emel National Park - inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage list in 2023 as part of the "Cold Winter Deserts of Turan". At over 535,000 hectares, it's one of Kazakhstan's largest parks. Stop first at the park office in Basshi to purchase your permit and collect route keys. Head for the Besshatyr burial mounds: this Saka necropolis from the 6th-4th century BC contains 31 kurgans, the largest measuring 104 m in diameter and 17 m high. Around them, circles of standing stones recall Stonehenge - their function remains a mystery.

⏱ 60 min
2

Continue to the Singing Dune - a barchan 150 m high and 3 km long, formed by wind carrying sand from the Ili River banks. In dry weather, when the sand slides, the dune emits a deep hum resembling an organ or distant bomber. Only about thirty "singing" dunes exist worldwide. Climb to the summit for the view over the Ili valley - but know the slope is steep and the sand burns in summer.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Watch for Przewalski's horses on the park's plains - Altyn-Emel harbours one of the last populations living in natural habitat. You might also spot Persian gazelles (dzheiran) or onagers (kulan). The park concentrates 40% of Kazakhstan's fauna.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 5

Charyncanyon

150 km red sandstone canyon often compared to the Grand Canyon

★★★★★
📍 Aktau Mountains Altyn Emel📍 Katutau Mountains Altyn Emel📍 Charyn Canyon Valley of Castles
1

Before leaving Altyn-Emel, explore the Aktau and Katutau mountains. The Aktau ("White Mountains") are an open-air geological museum - layers of sedimentary rock 25 to 30 million years old, deposited on the floor of the ancient Tethys Ocean, form bands of white, pink, orange and red. Remains of giant rhinoceroses, crocodiles and turtles have been discovered here. The Katutau, right nearby, preserve eroded volcanic lava formations sculpted into natural forms.

⏱ 120 min
2

Drive to Charyn Canyon - 150 km long, up to 300 m deep, carved by the Charyn River over 12 million years. Arrive late afternoon for sunset: the red sandstone walls take on absolutely spectacular scarlet and orange hues. Settle in at the canyon rim for the night - accommodation is basic but the silence of the steppe at dusk is worth its weight in gold.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If time permits, descend into the Valley of Castles late afternoon - the red sandstone columns sculpted by wind and water resemble medieval towers. The descent covers 2 km, allow 1 hour one way including photo stops. The low evening light glorifies the formations.

⏱ 75 min

Kolsai

Three turquoise alpine lakes nestled in the Tian Shan forests

★★☆☆☆
📍 Charyn Canyon Valley of Castles Kazakhstan📍 Charyn River oasis📍 Saty village Kazakhstan
1

Rise early to descend into the Valley of Castles before organized tours arrive (around 10-11am in summer). The 3.5 km trail winds between 100 m tall red sandstone pillars sculpted into tower and keep shapes. At the bottom, the Charyn River flows among Sogdian ash trees - a relict species from the Tertiary era, remnant of a forest that once covered the Tian Shan foothills. If you search the rock for fossils, you might find 12-million-year-old shells.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Drive to Saty village and the Kolsai Lakes (about 2-2.5 hours from Charyn). Settle into a family guesthouse - the home-cooked dinners with local produce are one of the pleasures of staying here. Saty is an authentic mountain village without a single tourist restaurant.

Kolsai

Three turquoise alpine lakes nestled in the Tian Shan forests

★★☆☆☆
📍 Kolsai Lake 1 Kazakhstan📍 Kolsai Lake 2 Kazakhstan📍 Kolsai Lakes National Park
1

The Kolsai Lakes are three alpine lakes stacked at 1,870 m, 2,250 m and 2,650 m altitude in the Tian Shan mountains. The first lake, the most accessible, lies 9 km from Saty on a paved road. Its turquoise waters reflect fir trees and snow-capped peaks - it's one of Kazakhstan's most beautiful images. Take time to sit by the shore, the silence is total.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike from Kolsai Lake 1 to Kolsai Lake 2 - the trail climbs through fir forests and alpine meadows for about 6 km and 400 m elevation gain. Allow 5 to 7 hours round-trip. The second lake, wilder and less frequented, amply rewards the effort. If you're fit, continue to the third lake - but plan for a full day.

Almaty

Former capital backed by the snow-capped Tian Shan at 900 metres

★★★★☆
📍 Kaindy Lake sunken forest Kazakhstan📍 Lake Kaindy Saty
1

Head for Kaindy Lake - the "sunken forest". This 400 m long lake formed in 1911 after an earthquake triggered a landslide, creating a natural dam at 2,000 m altitude. Tian Shan spruce trunks still protrude from the emerald water like ghostly masts. It's Kazakhstan's most photographed image. Warning: the access track (12 km from Saty) requires 4x4 - there's a river crossing. Otherwise, rent a vehicle with driver in Saty.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Return to Almaty (about 4-5 hours driving via the M-39). En route, if time allows, stop at Black Canyon or Moon Canyon - two lesser-known but photogenic secondary gorges along the road.

Almaty

Former capital backed by the snow-capped Tian Shan at 900 metres

★★★★☆
📍 Tamgaly Petroglyphs UNESCO Kazakhstan📍 Tamgaly Gorge rock art
1

Day excursion to the Tamgaly petroglyphs - a UNESCO site since 2004, 160 km northwest of Almaty (about 2.5 hours driving). In Tamgaly Gorge, some 5,000 rock carvings cover the dark rocks - the oldest date back 3,500 years to the Bronze Age. You'll see solar deities with rays around their heads, dancing shamans, mounted warriors and animal herds. The most recent carvings date from the 20th century - Kazakh shepherds drew Soviet vehicles. The contrast between the dark Bronze Age engravings and the lighter medieval ones is striking.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Explore the 48 petroglyph groups spread across different side canyons. The main canyon concentrates the densest carvings and what archaeologists believe are sacrificial altars. Bronze Age and Iron Age burial mounds also dot the site. An on-site guardian can direct you - the trail marked with arrows takes about 1.5 hours to complete the circuit. Bring water and a hat, there's no shade whatsoever.

Stop 10

Astana

Futuristic capital risen from the steppe in less than 25 years

★★★★☆
📍 Bayterek Tower Astana📍 Nurzhol Boulevard Astana📍 Nur-Astana Mosque Astana📍 National Museum Kazakhstan Astana
1

Morning flight Almaty → Astana (about 1.5 hours). Begin with Bayterek Tower, the capital's symbol - 97 m tall, crowned with a 22 m diameter golden sphere representing the egg of Samruk, the sacred bird of Kazakh mythology. Take the lift to 86 m for panoramic views over the left bank. Inside, you'll find President Nazarbayev's handprint in a wooden globe.

⏱ 60 min
2

Walk down Nurzhol Boulevard - the monumental axis of new Astana. Each building competes with futuristic forms. Visit the National Museum of Kazakhstan, the country's largest: the exhibition covers history from the Bronze Age to independence, with a poignant section on the Stalinist Great Famine. The Gold Hall presents spectacular nomadic ornaments.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Stroll Nurzhol Boulevard in the evening - the fountains and illuminated buildings transform the avenue into a light show. Sample beshbarmak (boiled meat over flat noodles) at a Kazakh restaurant - it's the national dish, and Astana is the best place to discover it.

Astana

Futuristic capital risen from the steppe in less than 25 years

★★★★☆
📍 Hazrat Sultan Mosque Astana📍 Khan Shatyr Astana📍 Palace of Peace and Reconciliation Astana📍 EXPO Astana
1

Hazrat Sultan Mosque is Kazakhstan's and Central Asia's largest - it can accommodate up to 10,000 worshippers. Inaugurated in 2012, its main dome rises 51 m high and 28 m in diameter, and its four minarets reach 77 m. The classical Islamic style is enhanced with traditional Kazakh ornaments. The interior is luminous and peaceful - visit in the morning for the best light.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Palace of Peace and Reconciliation - a glass and steel pyramid 62 m tall designed by Norman Foster, inaugurated in 2006. It houses a 1,500-seat opera house, conference rooms and a winter garden. Ascend to the summit for the view and circular stained glass windows.

⏱ 60 min
3

Khan Shatyr is a giant translucent tent 150 m tall, also designed by Foster. Under its ETFE dome, a shopping centre houses boutiques, restaurants and - surprise - a tropical beach with pool on the top floor. The idea of an artificial beach in the midst of the Kazakh steppes rather sums up Astana's ambition.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit the EXPO 2017 complex, converted into a cultural and scientific centre. The giant Nur Alem sphere (80 m diameter) houses an interactive museum on future energies. The EXPO district gives a good sense of how rapidly Astana has transformed in just 25 years.

Karaganda

Mining city housing the KarLag museum dedicated to the Soviet gulag system

★★★☆☆
📍 KarLag Museum Dolinka Kazakhstan📍 Karaganda city center📍 Ecomuseum Karaganda
1

Drive to Karaganda (about 3 hours from Astana), then continue 36 km southwest to Dolinka village. The KarLag Museum occupies the former administrative building - a deceptively elegant Stalinist neoclassical façade. Between 1931 and 1959, up to one million prisoners passed through this network of labour camps covering 17,600 km² - equivalent to France's area, according to local guides. The two-floor exhibition shows reconstructed cells, prisoner identity photos and declassified documents. Allow at least 2 hours - it's Kazakhstan's finest gulag museum.

⏱ 120 min
2

Back in Karaganda, visit the Ecomuseum - it traces the history of this city born from coal and camps. The blend of massive Soviet architecture and green parks gives Karaganda a strange atmosphere, between memory and renewal.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll Karaganda's centre in the evening. The city has retained an unapologetically Soviet character - broad avenues, massive buildings, communist-era statues. Kazakhs have a saying: "Have you been to Karaganda?" - a question one didn't ask, because the answer often implied the gulag.

Burabay

Oasis of lakes and pines dubbed the Switzerland of Kazakhstan

★★★★★
📍 Burabay National Park Kazakhstan📍 Lake Borovoye Kazakhstan📍 Zhumbaktas Rock Burabay📍 Okzhetpes Mountain Burabay
1

Long drive to Burabay (about 5-6 hours via Astana, or directly from Karaganda). But arrival rewards you: after hundreds of kilometres of scorched steppe, a wall of pine trees suddenly rises. Kazakhs call Burabay "the pearl of Kazakhstan" or "Kazakh Switzerland". The national park of 129,935 hectares harbours 14 lakes, pine and birch forests, and granite rock formations that appeared 5 million years ago.

⏱ 45 min
2

Walk around Lake Borovoye - the park's largest. In its midst, Zhumbaktas rock ("mysterious stone") rises from the water like an island. Depending on the angle, it resembles a young girl's face, a woman's, then an old lady's. Locals rent kayaks to approach it. To the south, Mount Okzhetpes ("that the arrow cannot reach") dominates the lake - its name comes from a legend of a princess and suitors.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Climb to the Blue Bay viewpoint via the wooden staircase - 15 minutes suffices and the view embraces Lake Borovoye, Zhumbaktas rock, Mount Okzhetpes and Lakes Bolshoye and Maloye Chebache. It's the park's finest panorama.

⏱ 45 min

Wander through the dancing birches forest - trunks twisted by wind that seem to sketch a choreography. According to legend, young girls dancing in a clearing were transformed into trees when the khan surprised them. Magical atmosphere late in the day.

Astana

★★★★☆
📍 Burabay hiking trails Kazakhstan📍 Lake Shchuchye Burabay📍 Astana airport
1

Enjoy the morning at Burabay for a final walk - Lake Shchuchye, in the park's north, is less crowded and offers fine sandy beaches surrounded by pines. The water is fresh but clean - locals swim here all summer. Marked hiking trails crisscross the surrounding hills, with panoramas over the infinite steppe beyond the forests.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Return to Astana (about 3 hours by road, or 2.5 hours by modern train). End of road trip - departure flight from Astana International Airport. If you have time before your flight, visit the ALZHIR memorial 35 km west of Astana - a former camp for wives of political prisoners, now transformed into a place of remembrance.
