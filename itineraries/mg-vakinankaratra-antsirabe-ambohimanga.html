Antananarivo

★★★★☆
1

Head up to Rova Manjakamiadana first thing in the morning - perched 200 metres above Tana on Analamanga hill, it's the only spot where you can take in the entire capital in a single sweep. Jean Laborde, a French adventurer, wrapped it in stone between 1839 and 1841 for Queen Ranavalona I; a 1995 fire gutted the interiors, which have since been restored and reopened. Allow 90 minutes - and ignore the hawkers at the entrance.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Upper Town (Tanàna Ambony) is classified as a protected heritage zone - unique in Madagascar. Its sloping cobbled lanes, red-brick houses and cascading staircases feel like an old European quarter dropped into the Indian Ocean. Climb up to the viewpoint late afternoon: the golden light on the rooftops is worth the climb.

⏱ 75 min
3

Drop by Andravoahangy market - the largest and most chaotic in the capital. Spices, filigree silver jewellery, spare parts, live poultry. Go in the morning before 10am, when the stalls are full.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wander the lanes of the Upper Town on foot in early evening - locals heading home, children playing on the stairs, colonial houses lighting their windows. The best time to feel Tana's rhythm without a guide.

⏱ 60 min

Antananarivo

★★★★☆
1

Make the drive to Ambohimanga, 21 km north of Tana - allow an hour in traffic. This isn't your average tourist site: Malagasy people still come here on pilgrimage to make wishes. Inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2001, the royal city is sealed by a 12-tonne stone disc. The Mahandrihono palace, built of rosewood, was home to King Andrianampoinimerina - the one who dreamed of a kingdom bounded only by the sea.

⏱ 90 min
2

Look for the two trees entwined on a rock beside the sacred lake - legend has it they shelter the souls of two star-crossed royal-era lovers and would bleed if severed. Don't point your finger at the sacred sites: fold your index finger down, as the locals will explain.

⏱ 45 min
3

Late afternoon, push open the door of the Museum of Art and Archaeology in the Isoraka neighbourhood in Tana - collections of beads, ceramics and ritual objects that tell Madagascar's story better than any guidebook. Often empty of tourists, which is a bonus.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Walk the trail through the residual forest around Ambohimanga's Rova - one of the last fragments of primary deciduous forest in inland Madagascar. The local guide will explain the traditional uses of endemic plant species.

⏱ 45 min

Antsirabe

★★☆☆☆
1

Welcome to Antsirabe - the town that smells of sulphur and minerals. Perched at 1,500 m on a volcanic plateau, it draws its thermal water from ground so salt-laden it earned the nickname 'where there's a lot of salt'. Its colonial avenues swarm with brightly painted pousse-pousse - hop into one to cross town: it's the local transport of choice.

⏱ 60 min
2

Lake Tritriva deserves the detour: this 160-metre-deep volcanic crater with dark blue-green waters is sacred. Don't eat pork before you go - villagers strongly advise against it and the legend of a tourist drowned for ignoring this taboo still circulates. From above, the lake is shaped exactly like Madagascar. Allow 20 minutes to walk around it.

⏱ 90 min
3

On the way, stop at Lake Andraikiba, 7 km from town - more accessible and livelier: fishermen striking the water with bundles of sticks to stun the fish, women washing clothes on the banks. A peaceful spot to catch your breath.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit a semi-precious stone workshop in central Antsirabe - the artisans work in front of you and sell directly, at prices far lower than in Tana. Antsirabe is known across the island for its surrounding quarries.

⏱ 45 min

Take a pousse-pousse ride through the town centre streets - negotiate directly with the driver. It's a tangible gesture for the local economy.

⏱ 30 min

Ambositra

★★☆☆☆
1

Step into a workshop in Ambositra - you're entering the world of the Zafimaniry, whose woodcraft is inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Heritage list. No nails, no screws: the panels carved with geometric motifs fit together by hand, following rules passed orally from father to son for centuries. The same patterns cover walls, doors and furniture in the houses - it's a visual language.

⏱ 90 min
2

Take the track to the village of Antoetra - there you'll see Zafimaniry work in its real context: in an actual house, with the actual tools, pieces that aren't made for tourists. The track is bumpy but passable by 4x4.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Tour Ambositra's craft markets and buy directly from the makers - marquetry, wood carvings, textiles. Prices are far lower than in Tana for the same pieces.

⏱ 60 min

Fianarantsoa

★★☆☆☆
1

Climb into Fianarantsoa's upper town late afternoon - its red-brick lanes stacked across several hills are classified as a protected zone, unique in Madagascar. Head up to the upper viewpoint: the view over the terraced rice paddies tumbling into the valley is among the finest in the highlands. Bring good shoes - the cobblestones are slippery after rain.

⏱ 90 min
2

Don't leave without tasting the local wine - Fianarantsoa is home to Madagascar's only vineyard, grown on the surrounding slopes. The red and white have a very particular profile, far from European standards: that's exactly what makes them interesting. Look for the cellar in town.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stroll the upper town's lanes in the evening when the low light colours the red bricks and schoolchildren head home. It's free, quiet and a hundred times more genuine than any guided tour.

⏱ 60 min

Ambalavao

★★☆☆☆
1

Twelve kilometres south of Ambalavao on the RN7, stop at Anja Reserve - run directly by the villagers since 1999. They protect around 400 ring-tailed lemurs here, the ones with the long black-and-white striped tail. Arrive early: in the morning the lemurs are active and unafraid - they sometimes come within a metre. The proceeds have funded the village school. Allow 2 hours for the small loop, accessible to families.

⏱ 120 min
2

Drop into an Antemoro paper workshop in Ambalavao - they make a pulp from the bark of the avoha shrub, into which they embed dried flowers and leaves. The result looks like floral parchment. You can watch every stage and leave with sheets made on site.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Guided circuit in Anja Reserve - the local guide will also show you chameleons, boas and endemic birds tucked in the rocks that you'd never have spotted alone.

⏱ 120 min

Ranomafana

★★★☆☆
1

Ranomafana means 'hot water' in Malagasy - the town existed since colonial times for its thermal springs, before the 1986 discovery of a new lemur species changed everything. Patricia Wright, an American primatologist, identified the golden bamboo lemur (Hapalemur aureus) here, a species that feeds almost exclusively on young bamboo shoots containing enough cyanide to kill ten humans. The park created in 1991 to protect this habitat covers 41,600 hectares of tropical rainforest, inscribed as UNESCO World Heritage in 2007.

⏱ 30 min
2

Enter the park with your compulsory guide first thing in the morning - the lemurs are active early. The Park Centre trail is the best adapted for observing the golden bamboo lemur and Milne-Edwards' sifaka. The park is home to a total of 12 lemur species, 115 bird species and the giraffe weevil - Trachelophorus giraffa - whose disproportionate neck is a curiosity in its own right.

⏱ 150 min

Activities

Night circuit in the park after dinner - mouse lemurs with shining eyes, woolly lemurs and multicoloured frogs become active once darkness falls. Bring a head torch, long trousers and repellent. The outing lasts 2 to 3 hours depending on the circuit. Book at the park office the same morning.

⏱ 150 min

Antsirabe

★★☆☆☆
1

If you're here on a Wednesday, don't miss Antsirabe's zebu market - hundreds of cattle pass through every week. The herdsmen draped in their coloured blankets, the bargaining ritual and the auctions are a spectacle in themselves. It's one of the largest zebu markets in the highlands.

⏱ 75 min
2

Antsirabe's colonial railway station is a handsome ochre building constructed under French administration, which served as the railhead southwards. It hasn't seen much traffic for a long time, but the building itself warrants a stop.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Visit a zebu horn workshop - Antsirabe artisans carve and polish the horn to make jewellery, combs and decorative objects. It's a local speciality you won't find anywhere else at this level of quality. Buy directly from the workshop.

⏱ 60 min

Take one last pousse-pousse ride through the lanes behind the station - the neighbourhoods outside the centre are less visited and more authentic.

⏱ 45 min

Last day in Tana. If you have a few hours before your flight, tour the Digue craft market (Lisy) - silver jewellery, zebu horn lamps, embroidery, basketwork. It's your last chance to leave with something made in Madagascar rather than imported.

⏱ 75 min
2
