India's East Coast: Chennai to Visakhapatnam, 13 Days

Chennai

★★★★☆
1

Built as early as 1640, Fort St. George stands as England's first fortress in India—and it's still here, intact, at the heart of the modern city. The fort houses a museum gathering weapons, uniforms, coins, and manuscripts from the East India Company era. Arrive before 10 a.m. to dodge school groups; on Fridays, the Legislative Assembly is in session and certain wings close.

⏱ 90 min
2

The scent of incense and jasmine drifts from the stalls before you even glimpse the gopuram of Kapaleeshwarar Temple. This Shiva temple in the Mylapore quarter is said to have been founded two millennia ago, then considerably expanded under the Vijayanagara kings in the 16th century. The entrance tower, covered in multicolored sculptures, ranks among Chennai's finest examples of Dravidian architecture. Come out shoes in hand: the ground burns at midday.

⏱ 75 min
3

Not glamorous at first glance, the Government Museum of Chennai turns out to be one of India's oldest, with collections spanning the Chola, Hoysala, and Vijayanagara dynasties. The 9th–11th-century Chola bronzes alone justify the trip. Allow three to four hours; separate ticket for the National Art Gallery.

⏱ 120 min
4

You walk thirteen kilometers of golden sand on Marina Beach, the world's second-longest urban beach according to most sources. Crowded late in the day with families, snack vendors, and cricket players. The stretch between the lighthouse and Foreshore Estate is calmer. Skip swimming in any season: the currents are treacherous.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you reach Mylapore's flower market before 7 a.m., you'll see Chennai at its best: jasmine and marigold wholesalers, ox-drawn carts, tea served in steel tumblers. The market empties fast once the sun rises. Plan forty-five minutes on-site.

⏱ 45 min

Chaotic on the surface, the Marina Beach fishing harbor reveals its own rhythm at dawn: catamarans returning laden, fish sorted on the sand, the smell of salt and diesel. Free spectacle and raw—wear sturdy shoes and don't be squeamish.

⏱ 40 min
$$$9.2The Leela Palace Chennai

Chennai

★★★★☆
1

Syrian Christians call it the world's third basilica built over an apostle's tomb—alongside St. Peter's in Rome and Santiago de Compostela. San Thome Cathedral Basilica was erected by the Portuguese in 1504 over the burial site of Thomas the Apostle, who arrived in India in 52 A.D. according to tradition. The current neo-Gothic edifice dates from 1893–1896. Descend into the crypt to see the tomb; the adjacent small museum displays relics. Closed weekday early afternoons.

⏱ 75 min
2

You enter Kapaleeshwarar Temple through the great side gate to see the inner courtyard, often less photographed than the gopuram. This is where the morning ceremonies unfold between 5 a.m. and 10 a.m.: drumming, incense smoke, devotees in white saris. Arrive early if you want to witness the rituals without the afternoon crowds.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Mylapore on foot in the evening is the real Chennai: flower vendors, cafés serving filter coffee in steel tumblers, Kanjivaram silk shops open until 10 p.m. The neighborhood has kept its 17th-century street fabric around the temple tank. Start at the Kapali tank and wander without a map.

⏱ 90 min

Sriharikota

★★★★☆
1

Pink silhouettes stand out against the calm water long before you make out the flamingos. Pulicat Lake, India's second-largest brackish lagoon according to Wikipedia, stretches between Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh along the road to the space center. In winter, migratory birds from Siberia settle here for the season. The best viewing point sits on the Sriharikota causeway: no boat needed. Visit between November and February.

⏱ 60 min
2

Here, 105 kilometers north of Chennai, India launches its rockets into space. The Satish Dhawan Space Centre is ISRO's main spaceport, installed on Sriharikota Island since the 1970s. The Visitor Complex opens weekdays from 9 a.m. to 1 p.m.; a 1:1 scale model of several launchers stands in the Rocket Garden. Regulated access: bring ID.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

You drive the Sriharikota causeway at sunrise, windows down: the Pulicat stretches on either side are crossed by flights of pelicans and painted storks at this hour. Free stops, no ticket required. Perfect before the Visitor Complex opens.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 105 km · 2h

Tirupati

★★★☆☆
1

You don't need to be Hindu to be struck by Tirumala: the Venkateswara temple, perched on the Seven Peaks hills at 853 meters elevation, receives according to Britannica tens of thousands of pilgrims every day. Dedicated to Venkateswara, an avatar of Vishnu, it's reputed to be the world's richest and most visited Hindu temple. The complex covers 16.2 acres. Book your darshan ahead through the TTD website or take the paid 'Sudarshana' ticket to bypass queues of several hours.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

You take the free pilgrims' bus from the base of Tirupati up to Tirumala: nineteen kilometers climbing in hairpins through the Seshachalam hills, with brief glimpses of forest. An alternative to private-car darshan, and often faster at peak hours. Last departure generally around 10 p.m.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.6Courtyard By Marriott Tirupati
🚗Drive to next stop: 36 km · 50min

Srikalahasti

★★★★☆
1

Thirty-six kilometers from Tirupati, Srikalahasti often comes after Tirumala—wrongly. This temple dedicated to Shiva, whose gopuram was enlarged in 1516 under Krishnadevaraya of the Vijayanagara kingdom, is one of the five Pancha Bhoota Sthalas representing the air element (Vayu). The name comes from Sri (spider), Kala (serpent), and Hasti (elephant)—three legendary devotees of Shiva. Closed roughly between 1 p.m. and 4 p.m.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.1The Lotus Park
🚗Drive to next stop: 135 km · 2h20

Bhairavakona

★★★★☆
1

Eight sanctuaries cut into the rock about a thousand years ago, each dedicated to a different form of Shiva: the Bhairavakona temples are hewn straight into the Nallamala hills cliffside. Local legend holds that no crow ever enters the valley—and visitors confirm it. A rare detail: on Karthika Pournami (November full moon), moonlight falls directly onto the deity in the main cave.

⏱ 75 min
2

Water falls into a natural pool ringed by jungle: the Bhairavakona waterfall flows year-round, and pilgrims bathe here before entering the temples. The water passes through medicinal hill plants according to local tradition. The view is better from October to January when the flow runs strong. Free entry.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 148 km · 2h40

Ongole

★☆☆☆☆
1

You walk on Vodarevu Beach, a quiet beach near Chirala little known to foreign travelers. The sand is clean, the sea less agitated than at Vizag, and fishing nets drying on the shore set the tone. Perfect stop between Tirupati and Vijayawada to get some air. No major tourist facilities—that's the appeal.

⏱ 60 min
$9Sithara Grand
🚗Drive to next stop: 148 km · 2h40

Vijayawada

★★★☆☆
1

Modest in appearance, the Undavalli Caves hide a treasure of Indian rock-cut architecture ten kilometers from Vijayawada. These sandstone caves, carved between the 4th and 7th centuries, house on the second floor a monumental statue of reclining Vishnu sculpted from a single granite block. The ensemble reflects the successive influence of Jain, Buddhist, and Hindu worship. Open 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.; bring sturdy shoes for the stairs.

⏱ 90 min
2

Andhrites simply call her 'Kanaka Durgamma'—the golden goddess of Vijayawada. The Kanaka Durga temple crowns the summit of Indrakeeladri hill, on the banks of the Krishna. Its current construction dates back at least to the 10th century. The eight-armed goddess, standing atop the demon Mahishasura, is dressed daily in different ornaments according to the ritual calendar. Queues can run long on weekends.

⏱ 90 min
3

Industrial and dense, Vijayawada hides this barrage-promenade on the Krishna: the Prakasam Barrage, built in 1957 and stretching several kilometers, links the Krishna and Guntur districts. In the evening, lighting illuminates the currents. Clear view of the river and the temple hill in the background.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

You cross the Prakasam Barrage on foot at sunset, between the two banks of the Krishna. Wind comes off the river, fishermen cast their lines from the parapet, a few families have spread mats. The city seems distant during these twenty minutes of walking.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.1Vivaan Inn

Vijayawada

★★★☆☆
1

In 1360, the Reddi kings built Kondapalli Fort on a hill overlooking the plain: the main entrance gate, carved from a single granite block, still stands. Passed to the Qutubshahis in the 16th century, then to the British, this eighteen-square-kilometer fort twenty-four kilometers from Vijayawada remains uncrowded despite its interest. The Tanisha Mahal, a palace perched on a ridge between two peaks, justifies the climb. The neighboring village is also known for its colorful wooden toys.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

If you have time, the artisans of Kondapalli village carve their toys from Tella Poniki softwood in their homes opening onto the street. The style—vivid, colorful mythological figures—goes back several generations. Purchases possible directly from families, no middleman.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.1Vivaan Inn
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 1h10

Amaravati

★★★★☆
1

A few broken slabs in the grass—and yet the Amaravati site carries one of South Asia's longest histories. The Amaravati Stupa, built in several phases between the 3rd century B.C.E. and around 250 C.E. according to Wikipedia, was one of ancient India's greatest Buddhist centers. Little remains in place on the site itself: the major sculptures are in the British Museum and Chennai museum. The on-site local museum displays casts and fragments.

⏱ 90 min
$$9.7Vacation Rental Home

Eluru

★☆☆☆☆
1

Not many travelers stop at Eluru—wrongly. The Kolleru Bird Sanctuary covers 673 square kilometers between the Krishna and Godavari deltas and ranks as one of India's largest freshwater lakes. The site has been a Ramsar wetland since 2002. Between October and March, migratory birds arrive from Siberia: painted storks, glossy ibises, waterfowl. Walk-in access from the shores; boat available at Atapaka.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

You board a boat at sunrise on Kolleru: herons and cormorants take off at your approach, water lilies still closing their blooms in the calm water. The ride lasts about an hour from the fishing village of Atapaka. Rate negotiated directly with local fishermen.

⏱ 60 min
$$7.6BSR plaza
🚗Drive to next stop: 121 km · 2h10

Kakinada

★★☆☆☆
1

India's second-largest mangrove forest at 235 square kilometers, Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary sprawls across the Godavari estuary about twenty kilometers from Kakinada. More than 120 bird species are recorded here, plus saltwater crocodiles and the rare fishing cat. Open 9 a.m. to 5 p.m., closed Tuesdays. Modest entry fee.

⏱ 120 min
2

River and sea merge into a blurred horizon at the Godavari mouth, visible from Coringa sanctuary. This estuary landscape—brown river waters meeting the blue-gray Bay of Bengal—looks like nothing else on the east coast. Best light late afternoon.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

You walk the 600-meter wooden boardwalk snaking through the mangrove canopy: the plunging roots of Rhizophora frame the path, kingfishers cross the green shade. The boardwalk leads to a viewpoint over the estuary. Bring sturdy shoes; the wood can be slippery.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.1Grand Kakinada by GRT Hotels

Kakinada

★★☆☆☆
1

If weather permits, rent a boat at Kakinada port to reach Hope Island, an hour's crossing in the bay. This barrier island is a nesting site for Olive Ridley turtles and a bird refuge. No lodging on-site; return is in the afternoon. Verify boat service is operational before leaving—it's sometimes suspended.

⏱ 120 min
2

Uppada Beach doesn't look like much from the road, but the traditional fishing nets stretched between wooden poles make it one of the delta coast's signature sights. Fishermen leave before dawn; by 7 a.m., the nets are already retrieved. The beach remains uncrowded by tourists.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.1Grand Kakinada by GRT Hotels
🚗Drive to next stop: 145 km · 2h30

Visakhapatnam

★★★☆☆
1

Built in 1969 by the Soviets, INS Kursura is a 91.3-meter S20-class submarine beached on Ramakrishna Beach since 2002—India's first submarine museum. You visit the crew quarters, torpedo room, and command post in corridors so narrow you measure concretely what living under the sea means. She served in the 1971 Indo-Pakistani War. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 75 min
2

From the summit of Kailasagiri, sea and city open 360° from 110 meters elevation. This 380-acre park shelters two giant statues of Shiva and Parvati in brilliant white, visible from the coastal highway. Access is on foot or by cable car. Best Vizag sunset, according to regulars.

⏱ 90 min
3

RK Beach is Vizag's liveliest city beach—and at the same time one of the most accessible. The Victory at Sea Memorial, Visakha Museum, and promenade run directly along the waterfront. Avoid weekend peak hours if you're seeking tranquility.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

You take the cable car to the top of Kailasagiri: the gondola rises slowly above the coconut palms and Vizag's coastline unfolds in an arc. The walk down by the park trail takes an extra hour and rewards the detour.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.3Hotel SVA

Visakhapatnam

★★★☆☆
1

Rushikonda is Vizag's best beach—this is where regulars come midweek to dodge the crowds. Golden sand, wooded hills in the background, Bay of Bengal waters swimmable under lifeguard watch. Blue Flag certified (with ups and downs per audits), it's one of Andhra Pradesh's best-maintained beaches. Water sports available all day.

⏱ 120 min
2

Fishermen still come before dawn to Yarada Beach, which has kept its fishing village soul fifteen kilometers from Vizag center. The beach is wedged between the Dolphin's Nose hills and the sea: its isolation protects it from commerce. Currents run strong—no swimming. Sensitive military zone: drones strictly forbidden.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

If you enjoy water sports, Rushikonda offers water skiing, windsurfing, and jet ski outings directly on the beach. Operators are present from 9 a.m. Rates vary by season; negotiate in the morning before the afternoon rush.

⏱ 90 min
