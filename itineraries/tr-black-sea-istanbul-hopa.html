📋 Notes▾

The Black Sea coast is remarkably rainy, even in summer. Pack a waterproof jacket and quick-dry shoes. This itinerary pairs perfectly with a Georgia trip: Batumi is just 25 km from the Sarp border crossing.

Turkish Black Sea Road Trip: 8 Days from Istanbul to Hopa

📅 8 days📍 12 stops🚗 1665 km⏱ 29h20

Istanbul → Safranbolu → Amasra → Sinop → Samsun → Ordu → Trabzon → Sumela → Uzungol → Rize → Ayder → Hopa

Istanbul → Safranbolu → Amasra → Sinop → Samsun → Ordu → Trabzon → Sumela → Uzungol → Rize → Ayder → Hopa

Turkey's Black Sea coast remains one of the great overlooked routes of classic itineraries. In 8 days, this road trip links Istanbul to Hopa via Safranbolu, Sinop, Trabzon, Sumela Monastery, and Ayder Plateau. Twelve stops to cross UNESCO Ottoman houses, beaches, tea plantations, and the misty highlands of the Kaçkar, all the way to the Georgian border.

Day 1

Istanbul

1

Before heading north, keep a full day in Istanbul. Topkapı Palace housed the Ottoman sultans for nearly four centuries, and its treasury remains one of the world's most concentrated collections. Plan at least two hours, more if you add the harem (separate ticket). Closed Tuesdays.

⏱ 150 min
2

Hagia Sophia divides opinion—you'll decide on the spot. A 6th-century Byzantine basilica, it's been a mosque since 2020; entry is free but follows prayer times. Look up: massive black calligraphy medallions hang alongside fragments of Christian mosaics. Avoid Friday midday.

⏱ 60 min
3

The smell of spices and grilled leather at the Grand Bazaar hasn't budged since Ottoman times. More than 4,000 shops spread across 61 covered streets: yes, it's touristy, but the guild-based organization is still readable. Bargain without excess; start at a third of the asking price.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Catch the ferry to Kadıköy late afternoon—a 20-minute crossing for a few lira. The Asian side runs younger, less crowded, and the sunset return over the Bosphorus offers Istanbul's best bang-for-your-time ratio. Ferries leave from Eminönü or Karaköy.

⏱ 90 min

If you have two hours before setting off for Safranbolu, head to the Basilica Cistern. Built by Justinian in the 6th century, 336 columns plunge into the gloom, two Medusa heads placed upside-down as pedestals. Recently restored with new lighting, buy tickets online to skip the queue.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 400 km · 5h30
Day 2

Safranbolu

1

Listed as a UNESCO site in 1994, Safranbolu preserves an extraordinarily dense fabric of Ottoman timber houses—the konaks. Kaymakamlar House, open to the public, gives a precise snapshot of 19th-century merchant life, mannequins included. Visit before 11 a.m. to dodge the tour buses.

⏱ 60 min
2

The dark timber and white plaster of the konaks form Çukur's visual signature, the lower town. Allow two hours to loop on foot through the merchant quarter, the Incekaya Bridge, and Köprülü Mehmet Paşa Mosque. Cobbles get slippery in the rain.

⏱ 120 min
3

Cinci Han deserves more than a glance. This 17th-century caravanserai, now converted into a boutique hotel, welcomes you for tea even if you're not staying. The neighboring hammam of the same name still operates—separate sessions for men and women.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Climb Hıdırlık Hill at sunset. The view frames the entire lower town, its red-tile roofs and the 1797 clock tower. A small Ottoman cemetery sits on the plateau—quiet, free, and five minutes on foot from the bazaar.

⏱ 60 min

Locals call their city the place where time stood still, and the saffron trade still thrives. Sample saffron lokum at a confectioner in the bazaar, and try saffron tea: deep orange color, a flavor far from the clichés. Skip the shops with façades too aligned with the bus route.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 1h40
Day 3

Amasra

1

Amasra Castle barely has a castle left. Sections of gray walls remain, embedded in the houses, along with three Roman gates and the Kemere Bridge linking the town's two promontories. The 14th-century Genoese layer shows in carved coats of arms visible at the museum.

⏱ 75 min
2

Amasra Museum occupies a former 19th-century naval school near the small harbor. The collection is small but dense: Roman statuary, coins, Byzantine liturgical objects, and Ottoman pieces. An hour is enough. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

On Kemere Bridge, the north wind arrives straight from Crimea, 300 km across the water. Walk to the northern point via the Hisar Peçe path, following the Byzantine ramparts. Four kilometers round-trip; small cafés near the main harbor for refreshments before or after.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 220 km · 4h
Day 4

Sinop

1

Sinop's residents claim Diogenes, the Cynic philosopher born here around 412 BC. His 5.5-meter statue, erected in 2006, shows him with his lantern and dog, standing on his famous barrel. The spot, at the peninsula's entrance, also makes the best landmark to start your visit.

⏱ 30 min
2

Sinop Fortress Prison operated from 1887 to 1997. Nicknamed the Alcatraz of Anatolia, it once held writers like Sabahattin Ali and Nazım Hikmet. The windowless solitary cells set the tone. Allow 1.5 hours to cover all 28 wings. Signage in English and Turkish.

⏱ 90 min
3

Sinop Archaeological Museum displays Neolithic pottery, Hellenistic amphorae, and statuary from the Kingdom of Pontus. Small, well done, located near the harbor. Perfect for understanding how this peninsula spent 2,500 years changing hands.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you're arriving by sea, Sinop's Byzantine fortress still commands the northern tip. Walk the ramparts from Lonca Gate to the cape, about 3 km, with views of the Black Sea on both sides. The path runs above the prison.

⏱ 90 min

The fish market is worth the stop around 10 a.m. Hamsi (anchovy), barbun (red mullet), kalkan (turbot). Several small restaurants around the small harbor will grill what you choose. Expect 200–300 TL per person in 2026, drink included.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 165 km · 2h40
Day 5

Samsun

1

This is where Atatürk landed on May 19, 1919, the official starting point of Turkey's War of Independence. The Bandırma ferry, reconstructed at full scale, now serves as a museum. Small, informative, free. Allow 45 minutes if you want to read the panels.

⏱ 45 min
2

Samsun's waterfront lines up 5 km of paved promenade, popular late afternoon. The Onur Monument, a massive 1932 equestrian statue of Atatürk, marks the center. A simple stop, with little old heritage; the city works mainly as a staging point.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll along the corniche west to Atakum Park. Black volcanic-sand beaches, popular in summer. You'll find pide salonu in the Atakum neighborhood—quieter than downtown. Half a day at most.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 165 km · 2h45
Day 6

Ordu

1

From the Boztepe cable car, Ordu's coastline unfolds across 30 km. The cabin climbs 450 meters in six minutes; round-trip ticket around 50 TL. At the summit: panoramic café, paragliding for enthusiasts, and noticeably cooler air than below. Skip foggy days—common in spring.

⏱ 75 min
2

Paşaoğlu Mansion houses a small ethnographic museum in a properly restored 19th-century timber house. Period furniture, Pontic costumes, and an antique kitchen give a clear picture of merchant life before 1923. Allow 45 minutes; right in the center.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Here, people live on hazelnuts, and Ordu is one of Turkey's main producers. Stop at a downtown roaster to taste freshly roasted hazelnuts. September is harvest season—you'll see sacks piling up everywhere on the roads.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 165 km · 2h35
Day 7

Trabzon

1

Built in the 13th century under the Empire of Trebizond, Trabzon's Hagia Sophia mixes Byzantine architecture, a Georgian dome, and Seljuk stone. Converted to a mosque in 1511, then a museum, it recently returned to worship. The frescoes from the late Byzantine period are remarkably preserved.

⏱ 60 min
2

Atatürk Pavilion in Trabzon, a late 19th-century neoclassical villa designed by a Greek architect, overlooks the city. Atatürk stayed here during his Black Sea visits. Original furniture, manicured garden, clear views of the harbor. An hour is enough; exterior photos recommended.

⏱ 60 min
3

From Boztepe's viewing terrace, Trabzon reveals itself all at once: harbor to the west, valleys southward, the Black Sea on the horizon. The promenade runs 1,200 meters with three glass-cantilevered platforms. Take a taxi (15 min from center); walking back down the stairs is possible.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Russian Bazaar has nothing Russian left in it anymore. Stalls of knock-offs, clothes, cheap utensils. Worth a quick pass for the atmosphere, not for shopping. More interesting: the neighboring Bedesten Bazaar, a 16th-century Ottoman hall with goldsmiths and copper work.

⏱ 60 min

Try kuymak in a neighborhood lokanta. It's the local fondue: cornmeal, butter, cheese. Served piping hot for breakfast or as a main. Paired with Trabzon black olives and fresh bread. Hamsiköy Sütlaç, 60 km south, is the most famous village for this specialty, but it's good in town too.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 1h
Day 8

Sumela

1

Clinging at 1,200 meters to the cliff face of Mount Mela, Sumela Monastery was founded around 386 by two Athenian monks, Barnabas and Sophronios. Most of today's buildings date from the 13th–14th centuries, the Empire of Trebizond period. Reopened in 2019 after four years of rockfall prevention work.

⏱ 90 min
2

Inside Sumela Monastery, Byzantine frescoes layer over each other across centuries, sometimes covered with 19th-century graffiti. The troglodyte chapel, the historical starting point, holds the oldest layers. Allow an hour on-site after the shuttle arrives.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you come in winter, the road is often closed for snow and the monastery itself can suspend visits. Safe period: May to October. Parking is 4 km away; mandatory shuttles since 2019, combined ticket. Expect a 30-minute uphill walk.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 95 km · 2h10
Day 9

Uzungol

1

Uzungöl Lake, at 1,090 meters altitude, formed after a landslide dammed the Haldizen stream. One kilometer long, 500 meters wide in places. The white-façade mosque reflects in the water—the postcard image of the place. A landscaped walking path loops the entire lake.

⏱ 60 min
2

Uzungöl Mosque mirrors in the lake water at first light in the morning, before the buses arrive. A modest building, but it's the setting that makes the photo. The surrounding village is touristy but manageable outside weekends. Avoid July–August for guaranteed crowds.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Head for the nearby Sultan Murat yaylas at 2,000 meters, a scenic 25-km drive. Summer pastures, wooden chalets, clear views of the Pontic peaks. Allow two hours on-site. Road open June to October, sometimes earlier depending on snow.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 125 km · 2h40
Day 10

Rize

1

In Rize, they say the rain never truly stops. The province produces nearly all of Turkey's tea, and the terraced slopes are part of the landscape. Çaykur plantation, the state operator, is open for tours with tastings. Downtown, several panoramic teahouses.

⏱ 60 min
2

Rize Castle, perched on the hill, is what remains of a Byzantine fortress. Little architectural interest, but the café built into the walls offers a 180° view over the harbor and coast. Twenty minutes is enough. Walking access from downtown, steep climb.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stop at a Çaykur factory on the road. Free guided tour (in Turkish, but the process is visual), explanation of sorting by leaf, picking from April to October. Exit shop with vacuum-sealed bags at unbeatable prices. Choose leaf tea over tea bags.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 80 km · 1h50
Day 11

Ayder

1

Ayder Plateau smells of damp fog and wet wood. Timber chalets, green meadows, thermal springs. The village has turned touristy, sometimes excessively, but remains the best base for exploring Kaçkar Mountains National Park. Gelintülü Waterfall is a 10-minute walk.

⏱ 90 min
2

Fırtına Valley ranks among the WWF's 200 ecological regions to preserve. The eponymous river tumbles between dense forests and Ottoman arched bridges, notably the one at Şenyuva. Zilkale Castle, perched at 1,300 meters, is worth the 25-km detour from Ayder. Narrow but paved road.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Ayder's thermal baths are functional, not glamorous. Sulfurous water at 56°C, communal pool or private cabins. Modest fee, family atmosphere. More interesting than beautiful; you go for the heat after a day in the rain. Avoid weekends.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 110 km · 2h30
Day 12

Hopa

1

Hopa marks the end of Turkey's coastal road, 18 km from the Sarp border crossing. A small port town with a mixed population of Turks, Laz, and border communities from the Caucasus. No major architectural interest, but strategically positioned for anyone continuing into Georgia. Two hours on-site, no more.

⏱ 90 min
2

Hopa's harbor, built for trade with Georgia, livened up after the Sarp crossing opened on August 31, 1988. Russian and Georgian cargo ships still unload regularly. A short waterfront walk, working-class atmosphere, not touristy. Good tea terraces along the quay.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Try anchovies in Hopa, the king dish of the eastern Black Sea coast. Hamsi pilavı (rice with anchovies), hamsi tava (pan-fried), anchovy bread: several lokantas along the waterfront serve these specialties at modest prices. Avoid high summer season—stocks collapse in summer.

⏱ 60 min
