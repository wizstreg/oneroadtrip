Trip info

✨ Highlights▾
  • The piano concerts at Żelazowa Wola are held every Saturday and Sunday from May to late September, at noon and 3 PM. They are included in the park admission ticket. The site is closed on Mondays - if your D4 falls on this day, swap D4 and D5.
  • The Warsaw Uprising Museum (Muzeum Powstania Warszawskiego) and the POLIN Museum require online reservation in July-August - queues without prepaid tickets can reach 1h30. The Royal Castle is free on Wednesdays.
  • The Płock → Kazimierz Dolny journey (~195 km) can go either via the Warsaw and Radom bypass (highway and main road, faster) or via Sochaczew and Warka (more direct on the map but secondary roads). Check traffic on D5 morning.
  • Kazimierz Dolny is saturated on July-August weekends and some May-June weekends. Book accommodation several weeks in advance. For a Warsaw-only stay (3-day variant), the metro, trams and buses cover all sites without a car.
⏳ How many days▾

We spread this itinerary over 6 days rather than the 3-day minimum, so you're not rushing from one stop to the next.

📋 Notes▾

This itinerary corrects the classic Warsaw trip mistake: staying in the city all six days. Three days are enough for the capital - the reconstructed Old Town, 20th-century museums, baroque palaces. The following three days venture beyond the metropolitan area: to Chopin's birthplace on the Masovian plain, to Płock which has overlooked the Vistula since the 11th century, and to Kazimierz Dolny, a Renaissance town of painters on the riverbank.

Day 1

Warsaw

1

Warsaw's Old Town (Stare Miasto) was 85% destroyed during World War II and rebuilt brick by brick using the 18th-century vedute paintings of Bernardo Bellotto. This feat earned it UNESCO inscription in 1980. The Royal Castle, reconstructed between 1971 and 1984 thanks to donations from Polish citizens, houses the Bellotto Room - 23 paintings that served as blueprints for the rebuilders. The apartments of Stanisław August Poniatowski, Poland's last king, are recreated identically. The castle is free on Wednesdays.

⏱ 150 min
2

The Royal Route (Krakowskie Przedmieście then Nowy Świat) connects the castle to baroque residences: the Presidential Palace, the university founded in 1816, St. Anne's Church. On Nowy Świat, the Blikle patisserie (no. 33) has served pączki since 1869. Charles de Gaulle, who lived in the neighboring building during his 1919-1920 stay in Warsaw, was a customer of the establishment.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Łazienki Park (76 hectares) is Warsaw's main royal park. The Palace on the Isle, built for King Stanisław August in the 18th century, reflects in an artificial lake. The statue of Chopin, sculpted by Henryk Kuna and unveiled in 1926, stands at the southern entrance - destroyed by the Nazis in 1940, it was recreated identically in 1958. Free Chopin concerts are held there every Sunday from May to September, at noon and 4 PM.

⏱ 90 min
Day 2

Warsaw

1

The Warsaw Uprising Museum opened on July 31, 2004 for the 60th anniversary of the uprising. On August 1, 1944, some 20,000 members of the Home Army attacked the German garrison; the city held out for 63 days. The museum recreates these weeks of urban combat through original objects, sound recordings, period films and multimedia installations. The uprising and its aftermath - 200,000 deaths, deliberate destruction of the city by the Nazis - were long censored under the communist regime.

⏱ 120 min
2

The POLIN Museum (Museum of the History of Polish Jews) opened on April 19, 2013 for the 70th anniversary of the Warsaw Ghetto Uprising. The building, designed by Finnish architects Rainer Mahlamäki and Ilmari Lahdelma, is built on the site of the ghetto. The permanent exhibition covers 1,000 years of Jewish history in Poland, from the Middle Ages to the present. POLIN was voted best European museum in 2016.

⏱ 150 min

Activities

The Palace of Culture and Science was built in 1952-1955, Stalin's gift to Poland. At 237 meters and 42 floors, it long dominated central Warsaw. The observation deck on the 30th floor offers views of the city and its architectural paradoxes: reconstructed Old Town, Złote Tarasy, Soviet-era blocks and contemporary skyscrapers. Free on Wednesdays.

⏱ 60 min
Day 3

Warsaw

1

Wilanów Palace was built between 1677 and 1696 as the summer residence of John III Sobieski, victor over the Turks at Vienna in 1683. The architecture combines French baroque and Polish ornamentation - facades covered in stucco bas-reliefs. The French gardens, terraces and baroque orchard extend over several hectares behind the palace. The palace houses collections of Polish and European paintings, including royal portraits.

⏱ 150 min
2

The Praga district occupies the east bank of the Vistula, facing the Old Town. The only part of Warsaw preserved from Nazi destruction, it retains its pre-war buildings, cobbled courtyards and graffiti. The Neon Museum gathers illuminated signs from the communist era (1950-1989) - a collection of 200 neons recovered from Polish streets. Former factories have been converted into galleries, workshops and alternative cafes.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Dinner at a new Polish cuisine restaurant in Warsaw. The capital's gastronomic scene has transformed since 2015: chefs like those at Nolita or Senses revisit tradition - barszcz in different textures, pierogi with foie gras, carp gravlax-style. Tasting menus between 180 and 280 PLN without wine pairings. Reservation required.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 1h10
Day 4

Plock

1

Żelazowa Wola lies 46 km west of Warsaw, on the Masovian plain. Frédéric Chopin was born there on March 1, 1810 - his parents left the village that same autumn. The birthplace, a former outbuilding of the Skarbek manor, has been transformed into a museum. The park, crossed by a stream and planted with willows, houses five Chopin monuments. Open-air piano recitals are held every Saturday and Sunday from May to September, at noon and 3 PM, included in the ticket. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 120 min
2

Płock overlooks the Vistula from a 50-meter cliff. Its diocese was founded in 1075; from 1079 to 1138, the Dukes of Poland resided there, making it the country's political center. The Romanesque cathedral, built between 1130 and 1144 by Bishop Alexander of Malonne (from the Namur region), houses the tombs of Vladislaus I Herman and Bolesław III Wrymouth. Tumskie Hill (Wzgórze Tumskie) offers panoramic views over the Vistula and the flat Masovian lands.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Kampinos National Park, adjacent to Żelazowa Wola, covers 385 km² of lowland forests between Warsaw and Żelazowa Wola - one of the rare national parks in the immediate vicinity of a European capital. Lynx populations have been reintroduced there. The time available between Warsaw and Żelazowa Wola allows for a brief walk on marked trails along the inland dunes.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 195 km · 3h
Day 5

Kazimierz Dolny

1

Kazimierz Dolny has 3,600 inhabitants. Its early 17th-century Renaissance Rynek has been preserved almost intact for 400 years. The Przybyłów brothers' houses, commissioned in 1615 by two grain merchants, feature sculpted facades characteristic of the so-called "Lublin" Renaissance - a local style with Mannerist ornamentation. The town owes its medieval and Renaissance prosperity to the grain trade between Kraków and Gdańsk via the Vistula. Decline began when the river changed course in the 18th century.

⏱ 120 min
2

The ruins of the Gothic castle, built in the 14th century under Casimir the Great, rise on the hill overlooking the town. The view from the battlements extends over the Rynek, the 16th-century granaries by the river and the Vistula meander. A cylindrical watchtower, older than the castle, completes the defensive ensemble.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Hill of Three Crosses (Góra Trzech Krzyży) offers the best panorama of the town and the Vistula - the crosses were erected in 1708 during a cholera epidemic. The local bakery specializing in koguty - rooster-shaped breads, a town specialty since the 19th century - is located on the Rynek or in adjacent streets. Kazimierz's art gallery brings together a hundred studios and exhibition spaces: painters and sculptors have been settling there since the 1920s.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 170 km · 2h30
Day 6

Warsaw

1

Explore Warsaw's meticulously reconstructed Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage site that stands as a remarkable testament to Polish resilience. After suffering more than 85% destruction during World War II, the city was patiently rebuilt by determined citizens. The charming market square and surrounding streets now showcase the architectural beauty that once characterized this historic capital.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the Royal Castle, the beautifully restored former official residence of Polish monarchs in its baroque splendor. This grand palace overlooking Castle Square contains sumptuously decorated state rooms, royal apartments and an impressive collection of paintings and decorative arts. The castle serves both as a museum and a symbol of Poland's royal heritage.

⏱ 75 min
3

Discover the Warsaw Uprising Museum, an innovative and deeply moving tribute to the 1944 uprising against Nazi occupation. Through immersive exhibits, authentic artifacts and multimedia presentations, this award-winning museum tells the story of the 63-day struggle that shaped modern Polish identity. The experience provides essential context for understanding Warsaw's wartime destruction and subsequent rebirth.

⏱ 75 min
4

Stroll through Łazienki Park, Warsaw's largest and most beautiful park, with the enchanting Palace on the Isle reflecting in tranquil waters. This 18th-century royal summer residence, surrounded by manicured gardens, peacocks and classical sculptures, offers a serene escape from urban exploration. The park's romantic landscapes and neoclassical architecture make it a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

⏱ 75 min
5

Visit Wilanów Palace, often called the Polish Versailles, an exquisite baroque royal residence set amid formal gardens on the city's outskirts. Built as a summer retreat for King John III Sobieski, the palace showcases opulent interiors, period furniture and a remarkable collection of European art. The surrounding Italian and English gardens provide a picturesque setting for peaceful walks.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

On the way back to Warsaw, Puławy (30 km after Kazimierz) merits an hour's stop. The Czartoryski Palace park (early 19th century) extends in terraces above the Vistula. Princess Izabela Czartoryska had created there one of the first museums open to the public in Poland - part of the collections is now in the National Museum of Kraków.

⏱ 60 min

Return to Warsaw in the morning. Mirowska Hall (a late 19th-century covered market) offers regional charcuterie, cheeses, dried mushrooms and vodka specialties for last-minute purchases. Nowy Świat and Grodzka Street have the capital's best-stocked Polish design shops.

⏱ 90 min
