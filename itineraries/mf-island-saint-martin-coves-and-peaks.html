Saint-Martin Journey: Beaches & Pic Paradis

📅 7 days📍 7 stops🚗 22 km⏱ 45min

Marigot → Grand Case → Orient Bay

French Saint-Martin unfolds in seven days, from the Creole capital of Marigot to the turquoise waters of Orient Bay. Between ascending Pic Paradis at 424 meters, the gourmet lolos of Grand-Case, and the reefs of Îlet Pinel in the Nature Reserve, the island of 37 beaches condenses everything best about the Caribbean into 53 km².

✨ Highlights
  • Fort Louis from 1789 and Creole market in Marigot
  • Hike through Loterie Farm tropical forest to Pic Paradis (424 m)
  • Lolos and fine dining along Grand-Case boulevard
  • Îlet Pinel and snorkeling in the Nature Reserve from Cul-de-Sac
📆 Best period

December, January, February, March, April

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 1 and 4 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (December-April): Peak season with perfect weather (24-28°C), minimal rainfall, and trade winds. Highest prices and crowds, but ideal conditions for beaches, water sports, and outdoor activities.

Shoulder Season (May-June, November): Good weather with brief showers, moderate prices, and fewer crowds. Excellent value while maintaining comfortable conditions for most activities.

Hurricane Season (July-October): Hot, humid weather (26-32°C) with potential storms, lowest prices, and minimal crowds. Weather monitoring essential, but many enjoy the lush landscapes and bargain rates.

Holiday Periods (Christmas, New Year, Spring Break): Premium pricing and maximum crowds during peak vacation times. Book well in advance, expect busy beaches and restaurants throughout these periods.

🌍 What to discover in MF?

Caribbean paradise with French sophistication, pristine beaches, and duty-free shopping. Experience multicultural heritage, excellent cuisine, and authentic Caribbean culture. The island offers cultural diversity with natural beauty and modern amenities. Saint Martin provides sophisticated Caribbean experiences with cultural richness and natural wonders.

📋 Important notes

Circuit covering French Saint-Martin: Marigot and its 1789 colonial fort, wild beaches of Terres Basses, hiking to Pic Paradis (424 m), gastronomic capital Grand-Case, beaches and Îlet Pinel at Orient Bay.

Day 1

Marigot

Where colonial ramparts meet colorful Creole houses and Caribbean waters

★★★★☆
📍 Fort Louis Marigot Saint-Martin📍 Marché de Marigot Saint-Martin📍 Rue de la République Marigot Saint-Martin📍 Marina Fort Louis Marigot Saint-Martin📍 Plage Galisbay Marigot Saint-Martin📍 Pont de Durat Marigot Saint-Martin
1

Start with Fort Louis: climb 91 steps from the parking lot behind the old prefecture building and church, and you'll reach the summit of the promontory overlooking the entire bay. The fort was built in 1789 under orders from Governor Jean-Sébastien de Durat to protect the port warehouses—salt, coffee, sugar cane, rum—from English raids launched from Anguilla. At the top, the 360° view sweeps across Marigot, Simpson Bay, and on clear days, Anguilla to the north. Plan for an hour, and go in the morning before the heat sets in.

⏱ 75 min
2

Walk down to Rue de la République, the historic artery from the 19th century. The Creole houses line up with their colorful facades following an unchanging formula: ground floor in lime-mortared stone, wooden upper floor with gingerbread-trimmed balconies. It's one of the best-preserved streets on the island.

⏱ 30 min
3

Just a short walk away, the prison built at the same time as the fort in 1789—still under Durat—has kept its original facade on Rue Perrinon. It operated until 1968, when it was converted into a fire station. A historical detail many visitors walk past without noticing.

⏱ 15 min
4

The Pont de Durat, contemporary with the fort, sits at the exit of Marigot toward Grand-Case. This stone bridge was named in honor of the governor by locals who wanted to pay him tribute. Discreet but steeped in history.

⏱ 15 min
5

Head down to Marina Fort Louis, opened in 1998. Sailboats anchor facing the duty-free shops along the quay. From here, you'll see the ferries heading to Anguilla—the crossing takes just about twenty minutes. It's also the best vantage point to look back at the Fort above.

⏱ 40 min
6

End the day at Galisbay Beach, right below the fort. Locals sometimes call it Baie de la Potence. The atmosphere is relaxed, far from the tourist beaches, and the late afternoon light on the fort's promontory is worth the trip.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Marigot market sets up every morning except Sunday in the white and blue Creole pavilions along the waterfront. Tropical fruits, bulk spices, rum punches, and local crafts. Wednesday and Saturday mornings, a fish auction starts at 6 a.m.—if you're an early riser, don't miss it.

⏱ 60 min

Have lunch at a lolo on the waterfront, next to the ferry terminal. Salt cod fritters, chicken colombo, barbecue ribs served on plastic tables facing the bay. No reservations, first come first served—arrive before noon to get your pick.

⏱ 60 min

Climb back up to Fort Louis in late afternoon with something to snack on. The slanting light sets the bay ablaze, and Anguilla takes shape on the horizon. It's one of the best sunset spots you can reach on foot from Marigot.

⏱ 60 min

Marigot

Where colonial ramparts meet colorful Creole houses and Caribbean waters

★★★★☆
📍 Sandy Ground Saint-Martin📍 Baie Rouge Terres Basses Saint-Martin📍 Baie Longue Saint-Martin📍 Baie aux Prunes Saint-Martin📍 Terres Basses Saint-Martin📍 Baie Nettlé Saint-Martin
1

Cross Sandy Ground, the sand spit connecting Marigot to Terres Basses. This narrow strip of land separates Simpson Bay lagoon from the Caribbean Sea—you truly have water on both sides. Fishermen prepare their nets here in the morning.

⏱ 20 min
2

Baie Rouge is worth the stop. The beach gets its name from the slight pinkish hue of its sand, which turns red at sunset. It stretches between Falaise aux Oiseaux and Pointe des Canonniers. The rocky bottoms are excellent for mask and snorkel—bring your own, there's no rental on site.

⏱ 120 min
3

Continue to Baie Longue, at the western tip of Terres Basses. Two kilometers of white sand in a still-wild setting, with no development. On clear days from the beach, Saba cuts across the horizon about 40 km away. A sight few visitors take the time to look for.

⏱ 90 min
4

Make a detour to Baie aux Prunes, a few hundred meters farther along the same coast. Less known than Baie Rouge, quiet and uncrowded, it offers the same wild atmosphere with waters that are often crystal clear.

⏱ 60 min
5

On the way back, stop at Baie Nettlé, five minutes from Marigot. The beach faces both the sea and the lagoon—a rare configuration. Caressed by the trade winds, uncrowded, it's favored by locals on weekdays.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Beach day and snorkeling on the west coast of Terres Basses. Baie Rouge is the best spot: the rocky bottoms around the rocks of Falaise aux Oiseaux shelter moray eels, parrotfish, and a few turtles. Bring mask and snorkel—few amenities here.

⏱ 90 min

Settle in facing the sunset at Baie Longue late in the day. It's one of the most open vantage points on the island's Caribbean side—the light is excellent from 5 p.m. onward.

Day 3

Marigot

Where colonial ramparts meet colorful Creole houses and Caribbean waters

★★★★☆
📍 Pic Paradis Saint-Martin📍 Loterie Farm Rambaud Saint-Martin📍 Friar's Bay Saint-Martin📍 Happy Bay Saint-Martin📍 Sentier Pic Paradis Saint-Martin
1

Pic Paradis peaks at 424 meters—the highest point on the island. From the summit, the panorama sweeps across Marigot and Fort Louis to the west, Grand-Case and Rocher Créole to the north, Baie Orientale to the east, and on clear days Anguilla, Saba, and Saint-Barthélemy. The view of both sides of the island simultaneously is striking.

⏱ 45 min
2

At the foot of Pic Paradis, Loterie Farm spreads across 55 hectares of tropical rainforest. It's a former sugar cane plantation created in 1721 under the name "Loterie Plantation," whose sugar operations ceased after the abolition of slavery. It has since been converted into a private nature reserve. On the trails, you'll encounter iguanas, green monkeys, and hummingbirds in the canopy.

⏱ 60 min
3

Head down to Friar's Bay (Anse des Pères), the locals' favorite beach in this area. Calm sea, white sand, two beach restaurants. On full moon evenings, barbecues with music spring up spontaneously.

⏱ 90 min
4

From Friar's Bay, a coastal trail leads to Happy Bay in about ten minutes' walk among iguanas and coconut palms. The beach is entirely wild, without any development—one of the most beautiful and least crowded on the island. Make sure you have water before setting out.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike to the summit of Pic Paradis from Loterie Farm. The trail loops about 4 km through dense tropical forest, with some sections equipped with ropes on the steeper areas. Allow 1.5 to 2 hours round trip depending on your pace. Guided hikes run Saturdays and Sundays from 10 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.—reservation recommended the day before.

⏱ 120 min

Treetop adventure course and ziplines in the canopy at Loterie Farm. Two circuits available: a standard course (FlyZone) and an extreme version for thrill seekers. No reservations for treetop adventure, first come first served. Closed shoes required.

⏱ 75 min

Swimming and lounging at Friar's Bay late in the day. This is when the beach regains its local atmosphere, with island workers coming to unwind after their day.

Day 4

Grand Case

A fishing village reborn as the gourmet heart of the Caribbean

★★★☆☆
📍 Boulevard Grand-Case Saint-Martin📍 Plage de Grand-Case Saint-Martin📍 Rocher Créole Grand-Case Saint-Martin📍 Petite Plage Grand-Case Saint-Martin📍 Lolos Grand-Case Saint-Martin📍 Street art Grand-Case Saint-Martin
1

Grand-Case is a single one-way main street running along the Caribbean Sea for barely 800 meters. Colorful gaulette houses sit alongside some thirty gourmet restaurants—some listed in the Gault et Millau guide. The village has kept its fishing village soul, with families of Anguillian origin who settled here as fishermen and salt workers in the last century.

⏱ 30 min
2

Walk along Grand-Case beach to the wooden pier extending into the bay. Facing you, Rocher Créole emerges a few hundred meters out—it's a protected nature reserve where pelicans and brown boobies have taken up residence. You can't land there, but the sight from the beach is already beautiful.

⏱ 45 min
3

At the end of the boulevard, where the road dead-ends, hides Petite Plage. You can only reach it on foot. This discreet cove offers crystal-clear waters and rocks for snorkeling—much calmer than the main beach.

⏱ 75 min
4

After Hurricane Irma passed through in 2017, street art covered the village walls. Colorful murals celebrate the island's historical figures and the resilience spirit of the residents. Look for them by wandering a bit away from the main boulevard.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Snorkeling outing to Rocher Créole. Operators offer glass-bottom boats or kayaks from Grand-Case beach. Below the surface, the reef shelters moray eels, parrotfish, porcupinefish, and sometimes turtles. Landing on the rock is prohibited—stay in the water.

⏱ 120 min

Dine at the Grand-Case lolos from 6 p.m. Four lolos line up in the center of the boulevard, each with its grill smoking in the middle of the street. Choose your lobster or ribs directly from the coals. Johnny Cake—local bread fried or baked—accompanies everything.

⏱ 90 min

Post-dinner walk along the illuminated boulevard. The Mardis de Grand-Case, from January to March, transform the village into a celebration: concerts, crafts, carnival with music. Even off-season, the boulevard atmosphere in the evening is pleasant.

⏱ 45 min

Day 5

Grand Case

A fishing village reborn as the gourmet heart of the Caribbean

★★★☆☆
📍 Anse Marcel Saint-Martin📍 Marina Anse Marcel Saint-Martin📍 Sentier des Froussards Saint-Martin📍 Petites Cayes Saint-Martin📍 Plage Grand-Case Saint-Martin
1

Anse Marcel, north of Grand-Case, is a horseshoe-shaped beach nestled in a bay sheltered from winds. The marina hosts a few sailboats in a verdant setting, and the sea is remarkably calm here—ideal for children or for swimming without constraint.

⏱ 90 min
2

From Anse Marcel, the Sentier des Froussards trail leads to the bay of Petites Cayes: a wild beach battered by the Atlantic, at the island's northern tip. The path runs along the cliffs with plunging views of the sea—the contrast with Anse Marcel's protected bay is striking.

⏱ 60 min
3

Return to the Tijon artisanal perfumery, at the intersection of the boulevard and the airport road. All products are handmade on site from essential oils of locally grown flowers: jasmine, gardenia, frangipani. A laboratory tour is offered.

⏱ 60 min
4

Finish the afternoon on the main beach of Grand-Case, the calm waters of the bay being protected by Rocher Créole. It's a chance to see the village from a different angle—from the sea rather than from the boulevard.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Coastal hike on the Sentier des Froussards trail between Anse Marcel and the bay of Petites Cayes. The path is easy to moderate, well-marked, with views of the Atlantic. Round trip or loop via the road.

⏱ 90 min

Kayak or Hawaiian outrigger canoe in Grand-Case bay. The calm waters allow you to paddle along the coast to Rocher Créole. Operators offer rentals directly on the beach.

Day 6

Orient Bay

White sands and turquoise shallows stretching toward the Atlantic horizon

★★★★☆
📍 Baie Orientale Orient Bay Saint-Martin📍 Îlet Pinel Saint-Martin📍 Embarcadère Cul-de-Sac Saint-Martin📍 Caye Verte Saint-Martin📍 Village Orient Bay Saint-Martin
1

Orient Bay, nicknamed the Saint-Tropez of the Caribbean, stretches for 3 km of white sand facing the Atlantic. The bay is partly protected by a coral reef and the deserted islets of Caye Verte, Pinel, and Tintamarre. The southern end of the beach is traditionally naturist.

⏱ 60 min
2

Îlet Pinel is a speck of land covered with cacti and palm trees, five minutes by shuttle from the Cul-de-Sac pier. Two beaches: one sheltered, with shallow water ideal for swimming, the other wild on the Atlantic side. Legend has it that the islet owes its name to the buccaneer Pinel, who ran aground here after a failed attempt to seize the island of Saba.

⏱ 180 min
3

A 1.5 km botanical trail crosses Îlet Pinel from beach to beach, with panels identifying species of dry coastal forest and birds. Iguanas bask on the rocks in complete indifference.

⏱ 40 min
4

Set back from the beach, the village of Orient Bay offers a small Creole square that comes alive in the evening, with restaurants, shops, and occasional night markets. Colorful architecture in a setting much calmer than the beachfront.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Shuttle to Îlet Pinel from the Cul-de-Sac pier. The crossing takes 5 minutes, boats depart all day. Devote a full day to it: swimming, botanical trail, lunch with your feet in the sand. Large free parking at the pier.

⏱ 240 min

Snorkeling around the marked underwater trail of Îlet Pinel. The nature reserve shelters turtles, rays, barracudas, and parrotfish between 5 and 10 meters depth. Bring your equipment—operators also offer rental at Cul-de-Sac.

⏱ 75 min

Reach Îlet Pinel by kayak or paddleboard from Cul-de-Sac for those who prefer the athletic approach. The crossing takes about twenty minutes in a generally calm bay. Operators offer rental on site.

⏱ 60 min

Day 7

Orient Bay

White sands and turquoise shallows stretching toward the Atlantic horizon

★★★★☆
📍 Plage du Galion Saint-Martin📍 Étang aux Poissons Saint-Martin📍 Quartier d'Orléans Saint-Martin📍 Baie de l'Embouchure Saint-Martin📍 Baie Orientale Saint-Martin
1

Galion Beach, in the southern extension of Orient Bay, is a paradise for families and windsurfers. The coral barrier protects the bay and maintains calm, shallow waters—it's where locals gather for island celebrations. The constant trade winds also make it a playground for windsurfing and kitesurfing.

⏱ 120 min
2

Étang aux Poissons borders the road between Galion and Orient Bay. This protected wetland of the Nature Reserve is a refuge for herons, pelicans, and egrets. Take the time to stop for a few minutes—it's one of the few preserved wetlands on the island.

⏱ 30 min
3

Quartier d'Orléans, inland from Orient Bay, is one of the oldest hamlets in Saint-Martin—Europeans settled here in the 17th century. A few Creole houses survive in a quiet rural setting, far from the coastal bustle.

⏱ 30 min
4

Return to the main beach of Orient Bay for one last afternoon. Settle in, enjoy the beach clubs' loungers, and watch the parade of kitesurfers doing tricks in the trade winds. Beach bars serve cocktails facing the sea until evening.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Horseback riding along the Nature Reserve between the Galion mangrove and the coastal heathland. Operators offer guided tours with an instructor. The landscape changes a lot depending on the tide—inquire about schedules before booking.

⏱ 120 min

Windsurfing or kitesurfing at Galion. The constant trade winds and coral barrier create ideal conditions for beginners and advanced alike. Operators offer lessons and equipment rental directly on the beach.

⏱ 120 min

Last evening at a restaurant in Orient Bay facing the sea. The village square comes alive at nightfall, with varied musical and culinary programming depending on the season.

⏱ 90 min
