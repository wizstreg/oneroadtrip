Stop 1

Cagliari

Where Sardinian Baroque rises above medieval ramparts and Mediterranean light

★★★★☆
📍 Bastione di San Remy Cagliari📍 Cattedrale di Santa Maria Cagliari📍 Quartiere Castello Cagliari📍 Terrazza Umberto I Cagliari📍 Parco di Monte Urpinu Cagliari📍 Spiaggia del Poetto Cagliari
1

The Castello quarter, founded in 1217 when Pisan nobleman Lamberto Visconti received the hilltop overlooking the city as a gift, forms Cagliari's fortified heart. Its winding lanes embrace seven churches, Aragonese palaces, and the Palazzo Regio, former residence of Spanish viceroys.

⏱ 75 min
2

Santa Maria Cathedral, built by the Pisans in the 13th century (115 feet long, 111 feet wide, 105 feet high), houses two pulpits carved in the 12th century by Master Guglielmo for Pisa Cathedral, then transferred to Cagliari in 1312. Its crypt preserves the relics of 179 martyrs in individual niches.

⏱ 60 min
3

Terrazza Umberto I, the former Saint Remy bastion, offers a sweeping 180-degree view from its esplanade across Cagliari's bay, lagoons, and surrounding hills. It's the evening promenade spot for locals.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stroll through Parco di Monte Urpinu, a wooded hill in the heart of the city where peacocks and parrots roam semi-wild, with panoramic views over Cagliari's rooftops.

⏱ 60 min

Swim at Poetto Beach, a five-mile ribbon of sand stretching east of the city between the sea and Molentargius Lagoon, frequented by pink flamingos.

⏱ 90 min

Visit the National Archaeological Museum in the Cittadella dei Musei, which holds the world's largest collection of nuragic artifacts, including the famous bronzetti—bronze figurines dating from the 8th century BC.

⏱ 75 min

Stop 2

Villasimius

★★★☆☆
📍 Capo Carbonara Villasimius📍 Spiaggia di Simius Villasimius📍 Porto Giunco Villasimius📍 Museo Archeologico Villasimius📍 Stagno di Notteri Villasimius📍 Torre di Porto Giunco Villasimius
1

Capo Carbonara, a limestone promontory at Sardinia's southeastern tip, has been a protected marine area since 1998. Its waters shelter posidonia meadows, dolphins, and sea turtles. The 16th-century Aragonese tower crowning the cape served to signal Barbary pirate raids.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Archaeological Museum of Villasimius preserves artifacts recovered from ancient shipwrecks in the gulf, including a 4th-century BC Punic anchor and Roman amphorae discovered in shallow waters.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Swim at Simius Beach, a long stretch of white sand with shallow waters, bordered to the north by Notteri Lagoon where flamingos nest.

⏱ 120 min

Boat excursion around Capo Carbonara to explore the marine reserve's coves and observe some of the Mediterranean's richest seabeds.

⏱ 180 min

Swim at Porto Giunco, a beach separated from Notteri Lagoon by a narrow tongue of sand, with direct views of the Aragonese tower.

Stop 3

Barumini

Guardian village of Su Nuraxi, inscribed as a UNESCO site in 1997

★★★★☆
📍 Su Nuraxi Barumini📍 Casa Zapata Barumini📍 Giara di Gesturi Gesturi📍 Parco della Giara Gesturi
1

Su Nuraxi is Sardinia's most complete nuragic complex, inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1997. Built from the 17th century BC using basalt blocks, its central tower originally stood 61 feet high. Archaeologist Giovanni Lilliu excavated it between 1950 and 1957 from beneath an earth mound.

⏱ 90 min
2

The nuragic village surrounding the tower contains over 50 circular huts inhabited from the 13th to 6th centuries BC. The only entrance still accessible is a doorway about 23 feet high, making the fortress virtually impregnable.

⏱ 45 min
3

Casa Zapata, a 16th-century Aragonese manor built directly atop a second nuraghe, now houses a museum where the ancient remains are visible through glass floors from the very rooms above.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Excursion to the Giara di Gesturi, a basalt plateau at 1,640 feet covered with cork oaks and seasonal ponds—one of the last refuges of Sardinia's wild horse, the cavallino della Giara.

⏱ 120 min
Affitti Brevi Casa Principessa Maria9.8

Stop 4

Oristano

Former capital of the Judicature of Arborea, facing the ruins of Tharros

★★★★☆
📍 Torre di Mariano II Oristano📍 Antiquarium Arborense Oristano📍 Area archeologica di Tharros Cabras📍 Spiaggia Is Arutas Cabras📍 Piazza Eleonora d'Arborea Oristano📍 Penisola del Sinis Cabras
1

The Tower of Mariano II, standing 62 feet high, is one of two surviving remnants of the city's medieval fortifications. Built in the 13th century under King Mariano II of Arborea, it guarded the Porta Manna, the city's main gate. The bell at its summit dates from 1430.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Antiquarium Arborense preserves archaeological treasures looted from the Tharros site in the 19th century by private collectors, including Lord Vernon in 1851. Part of the haul ended up in the British Museum and the Louvre; what remained in Sardinia is displayed here.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Tharros site, on the Sinis Peninsula, layers traces of a nuragic village, an 8th-century BC Phoenician town, a Carthaginian fortress, and a Roman city with its basalt-paved streets, thermal baths, and castellum aquae. Abandoned around the year 1000 in the face of Saracen raids, its stones were used to build Oristano.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Stroll along Corso Umberto I and Piazza Eleonora d'Arborea, where a statue honors Eleanor, the 14th-century judge who promulgated the Carta de Logu, one of Europe's earliest legal codes.

⏱ 45 min

Swim at Is Arutas, a beach on the Sinis Peninsula whose sand is formed from grains of white and pink quartz, the result of erosion from the surrounding granite cliffs.

⏱ 90 min

Taste Vernaccia di Oristano, a DOC white wine produced from ancient grape varieties—one of Sardinia's oldest wines, with an oxidative character similar to Sherry.

⏱ 45 min
B&B Da Marcella9.7

Stop 5

Bosa

Where pastel facades mirror in Sardinia's only navigable river

★★★☆☆
📍 Castello Malaspina Bosa📍 Quartiere Sa Costa Bosa📍 Chiesa San Pietro Extramuros Bosa📍 Ponte Vecchio Bosa📍 Bosa Marina Bosa📍 Cala Managu Bosa
1

Castello Malaspina has stood since the late 13th century on Serravalle hill, 260 feet above the Temo River. It contains the chapel of Nostra Signora de Sos Regnos Altos, whose 14th-century frescoes illustrate the rare scene of "The Meeting of the Three Living and the Three Dead," the only known example of this Franciscan theme in Sardinia.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Sa Costa quarter is Bosa's medieval heart, with ochre, yellow, and rose-colored houses pressed together on the slopes above the Temo. The Ponte Vecchio, built from red trachyte, links this quarter to the quays.

⏱ 45 min
3

The church of San Pietro extra muros, a 12th-century Romanesque structure, stands apart from the center on the far bank of the Temo, where ancient Roman Bosa once stood. It's one of Sardinia's oldest Christian churches.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Boat ride on the Temo, Sardinia's only navigable river, between the city quays and the river mouth at Bosa Marina. Every first Sunday in August, a procession of decorated boats carries the Virgin's statue upriver.

⏱ 60 min

Taste Malvasia di Bosa DOC in one of the wine cellars in the nearby villages of Magomadas or Modolo. This sweet, amber white wine, produced from a grape variety of Greek origin, is one of Italy's rarest wine productions.

⏱ 60 min

Swim at Cala Managu or Bosa Marina beach, about a mile from the center, on a coastline still uncrowded and bordered by basalt cliffs.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 6

Alghero

Fortified city where Catalan still echoes through streets since 1353

★★★★☆
📍 Grotta di Nettuno Alghero📍 Centro Storico Alghero📍 Cattedrale Santa Maria Alghero📍 Bastioni di Alghero Alghero📍 Capo Caccia Alghero📍 Escala del Cabirol Alghero
1

Alghero's ramparts are among the best preserved in Italy: over 70% of the original defensive walls survive, along with seven 16th-century towers. The Aragonese presence, established in 1353 when Peter IV of Aragon brought in settlers from Majorca and Catalonia, remains so strong that Catalan is recognized as an official minority language.

⏱ 60 min
2

Santa Maria Cathedral, built in the mid-16th century, blends Catalan Gothic and Baroque. Its octagonal bell tower is the most recognizable feature. Steps away, the Church of San Francesco, from the late 14th century, is the most important Catalan monument in all of Sardinia.

⏱ 45 min
3

Neptune's Grotto, discovered by a local fisherman in the 18th century, extends nearly a mile into the cliff of Capo Caccia, 15 miles from Alghero. Its stalactites and stalagmites form shapes nicknamed "the organ" and "the church dome." The guided tour lasts about 45 minutes.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Boat crossing from Alghero harbor to Neptune's Grotto, with views of coastal vineyards and Porto Conte bay. In bad weather, access is via the Escala del Cabirol staircase.

⏱ 180 min

Descend the Escala del Cabirol, a 654-step staircase carved into the cliff in 1959, plunging 360 feet down to the grotto entrance. The climb back up is strenuous, but the sea views are worth it.

⏱ 90 min

Try lobster à l'algherese, prepared according to a traditional Catalan recipe with tomatoes, onion, olive oil, and vinegar—a culinary specialty distinct from the rest of Sardinia.

⏱ 90 min
Dimora Sella Rooms9.9

Stop 7

Sassari

Sardinia's second city, where Spanish Baroque meets island soul

★★★☆☆
📍 Piazza d'Italia Sassari📍 Cattedrale San Nicola Sassari📍 Fontana di Rosello Sassari📍 Museo Sanna Sassari📍 Monte d'Accoddi Sassari📍 Corso Vittorio Emanuele Sassari
1

Piazza d'Italia is Sassari's monumental heart, bordered by the neoclassical Palazzo della Provincia (1872) and the Teatro Civico. Each year on the second-to-last Sunday in May, the Cavalcata Sarda unfolds here—a parade on foot and horseback born in 1899 during the visit of King Umberto I and Queen Margherita of Savoy.

⏱ 30 min
2

San Nicola Cathedral layers five centuries of styles: the 13th-century Romanesque bell tower is the sole vestige of the original church, first mentioned in 1135. Fifteenth-century Catalan Gothic coexists with an 18th-century Baroque façade adorned with statues of Sassari's martyrs. Writer Elio Vittorini noted that "one would not want to remove a thing."

⏱ 45 min
3

The Fontana di Rosello, built by the Genoese in 1606 on an older medieval fountain, is Sassari's most symbolic monument. Its bas-reliefs represent the four seasons and the four winds; water flows from twelve marble dolphins.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Visit the G. A. Sanna Archaeological and Ethnographic Museum, one of Sardinia's most important, preserving nuragic objects, bronzes, Punic and Roman ceramics, and discoveries from the megalithic site of Monte d'Accoddi.

⏱ 75 min

Excursion to the megalithic site of Monte d'Accoddi, 4 miles from Sassari: a great stepped altar dating from 2400 BC, without equivalent in Western Europe, whose form evokes Mesopotamian ziggurats.

⏱ 60 min

Wander through the historic center along Corso Vittorio Emanuele, the main artery lined with Baroque palaces and 16th-century houses showing Spanish influence.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 8

Castelsardo

Medieval eyrie perched on volcanic rock facing Corsica

★★★☆☆
📍 Castello dei Doria Castelsardo📍 Cattedrale Sant'Antonio Abate Castelsardo📍 Centro Storico Castelsardo📍 Spiaggia Lu Bagnu Castelsardo
1

Castelsardo perches on a rocky promontory overlooking the Gulf of Asinara since the 12th century. The Castello dei Doria, built by the powerful Genoese Doria family, now houses the Museum of Mediterranean Basket Weaving, tracing in nine rooms the art of basket making—a craft still alive in the town.

⏱ 75 min
2

Sant'Antonio Abate Cathedral, perched at the village summit, is recognizable by its blue-green dome visible from the sea. Inside, it preserves the Black Christ of Castelsardo, a 14th-century polychrome wood statue carried in procession on Easter Monday during the Lunissanti.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Climb through the labyrinth of cobbled lanes to the promontory's summit, with successive views over the sea, colorful houses, and the Doria castle—one of northern Sardinia's best-preserved villages.

⏱ 60 min

Shop for local crafts in boutiques specializing in traditional basketry made from asphodel, dwarf fan palm, and willow—a technique passed from mother to daughter for centuries.

⏱ 45 min

Swim at Lu Bagnu beach, a few miles from the medieval center, with clear waters facing the Gulf of Asinara.

⏱ 90 min

Dine on seafood in the harbor restaurants, facing the sea that has been the village's main economic activity for generations.

Stop 9

Santa Teresa Gallura

Northern outpost founded in 1808, facing Bonifacio's white cliffs

★★★★☆
📍 Torre di Longosardo Santa Teresa Gallura📍 Capo Testa Santa Teresa Gallura📍 Spiaggia Rena Bianca Santa Teresa Gallura📍 Lu Brandali Santa Teresa Gallura📍 Porto di Santa Teresa Gallura Santa Teresa Gallura
1

The Longosardo Tower, built under Philip II of Spain in the 16th century, is Sardinia's largest Spanish tower. From its summit, the view embraces Bonifacio's white cliffs 7.5 miles away, the Maddalena archipelago, and on clear days, all of Corsica.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Lu Brandali archaeological site, dated from the 14th to 10th centuries BC, comprises a nuraghe, two towers, a giants' tomb, and a village. Excavations conducted in the 1980s unearthed ceramics and funerary objects that help reconstruct daily life on the Gallura coast during the Bronze Age.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Capo Testa promontory, connected to the mainland by a narrow isthmus, is the ancient granite quarry where Romans extracted stone for the colonnades of their monuments. You can still see roughly hewn column shafts abandoned in the rock.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swim at Rena Bianca, a beach of fine white sand framed by granite boulders, right in the town center. It has held Blue Flag status since 1987.

⏱ 90 min

Ferry crossing to Bonifacio in Corsica (50 minutes), a town perched on white limestone cliffs directly across from Santa Teresa. A day trip is possible.

⏱ 300 min

Taste Vermentino di Gallura DOCG, a crisp, aromatic white wine produced on Gallura's granite soils, paired with local seafood.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 10

La Maddalena

Main island of a 62-island national park carved from granite

★★★☆☆
📍 Compendio Garibaldino La Maddalena📍 Chiesa Santa Maria Maddalena La Maddalena📍 Isola di Caprera La Maddalena📍 Cala Coticcio Caprera📍 Isola di Spargi La Maddalena📍 Isola di Budelli La Maddalena
1

The town of La Maddalena, the archipelago's only settlement, preserves in the Church of Santa Maria Maddalena a crucifix and two candlesticks offered by Admiral Nelson in 1804, who called this harbor "the most beautiful in the world" and used it as a fleet base while pursuing the French.

⏱ 30 min
2

On Caprera Island, the Compendio Garibaldino is the house where Giuseppe Garibaldi spent the last 26 years of his life, from 1856 until his death on June 2, 1882 at 6:21 PM. The bedroom clock remains stopped at that hour. His red shirt, bed, wheelchair, and the rough granite tomb where he's buried are on display.

⏱ 75 min
3

La Maddalena Archipelago National Park, created in 1994, groups 62 islands and islets along 112 miles of granite coastline. Spiaggia Rosa on Budelli Island, famous for its pink sand made from coral and shell fragments, is now protected and accessible only by sea.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Ferry crossing from Palau (15 minutes) to the main island of La Maddalena.

⏱ 30 min

Boat excursion around Spargi, Budelli, and Santa Maria islands, accessible only by sea. Spargi Island preserves the wreck of a 2nd-century BC Roman ship whose cargo of amphorae has been partially recovered.

⏱ 240 min

Swim at Cala Coticcio on Caprera Island, nicknamed "Sardinian Tahiti" for its transparent waters in shades of turquoise and deep green. Access is on foot via a trail or by sea.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 11

Olbia

Gateway to Gallura, watched over by the Romanesque Basilica of San Simplicio

★★☆☆☆
📍 Basilica San Simplicio Olbia📍 Chiesa San Paolo Apostolo Olbia📍 Museo Archeologico Olbia📍 Corso Umberto I Olbia📍 Spiaggia Pittulongu Olbia📍 Porto Istana Olbia
1

The Basilica of San Simplicio, built from gray granite between the late 11th and early 12th centuries on a hill that served as a necropolis since Carthaginian times, is Gallura's most important Romanesque monument. It's dedicated to Simplicio, Olbia's first bishop, martyred under Emperor Diocletian. His relics were discovered in the crypt in 1614.

⏱ 45 min
2

The San Simplicio necropolis, visible beneath glass panels in front of the basilica, has yielded some 450 tombs spanning 2,000 years of Olbia's history, from the Phoenicians through the Middle Ages.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Church of San Paolo Apostolo, in the historic center, is recognizable by its 1939 dome entirely clad in multicolored majolica tiles. It was built over the remains of a Roman temple.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Visit the Olbia Archaeological Museum, tracing the port city's history from nuragic times through Rome, with a section dedicated to Roman shipwrecks discovered in the harbor.

⏱ 60 min

Swim at Porto Istana or Pittulongu beaches, located a few miles north of Olbia, with shallow waters and fine sand—the gateway to the Costa Smeralda.

⏱ 120 min

Stroll along Corso Umberto I, the main artery of the historic center lined with palaces and café terraces, to Piazza Matteotti.

Stop 12

Orosei

Coastal town opening onto the Gulf of Orosei's untamed coves

★★☆☆☆
📍 Chiesa San Giacomo Maggiore Orosei📍 Centro Storico Orosei📍 Cala Gonone Dorgali📍 Cala Luna Dorgali📍 Grotta del Bue Marino Dorgali📍 Gola di Gorropu Urzulei
1

The historic center of Orosei, perched on a hill 1.2 miles from the coast, is a well-preserved ensemble of stone houses, churches, and interior courtyards. The Church of San Giacomo Maggiore, whose bell tower dominates the town, dates from the 16th century.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Gulf of Orosei stretches for 25 miles of limestone cliffs plunging into the sea, punctuated by coves accessible only by boat. Cala Gonone, a seaside resort at the foot of Supramonte belonging to the municipality of Dorgali, is the departure port for all excursions.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Boat excursion from Cala Gonone to the gulf's coves: Cala Luna (blonde sand and caves in the cliff), Cala Sisine, Cala Mariolu with white and pink pebbles, and Cala Goloritzé whose 469-foot limestone pinnacle is classified as natural heritage.

⏱ 360 min

Hike through the Su Gorropu gorge, a canyon with limestone walls reaching 1,640 feet high—one of Europe's deepest. The trail starts from the Ghenna Silana pass (3,280 feet elevation) and descends 2.5 miles to the gorge entrance.

⏱ 300 min

Visit the Grotta del Bue Marino, a sea cave accessible by boat from Cala Gonone. Its walls preserve Neolithic engravings depicting a "Dance of the Sun," dated to 1800 BC. The name refers to the monk seal, which lived here until the 1970s.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 13

Orgosolo

★★★★☆
📍 Murales di Orgosolo Orgosolo📍 Museo Sa Dommo e sos Corraine Orgosolo📍 Centro Storico Orgosolo📍 Altopiano del Supramonte Orgosolo
1

Orgosolo, a village of 5,000 inhabitants perched at 2,000 feet in Barbagia, has been covered since 1969 with 250 murals painted on building facades. The movement began with an art teacher, Francesco Del Casino, who had posters commemorating the anti-fascist Resistance pasted up—then painted them directly on the walls. Each mural tells a local or global episode: the repression of Sardinian shepherds, May 1968, Pinochet's Chile.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Sa Dommo e sos Corraine House-Museum preserves ten period rooms intact—kitchen, master bedroom, granary, weaving workshop—frozen in the peasant and pastoral life of the early 20th century.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hike on the Montes plateau, a vast forest of holm oaks and pines above Orgosolo, with views over the Supramonte massif and Monte Corrasi (4,800 feet).

⏱ 120 min

Lunch at a Barbagia farm with plateau specialties: wood-roasted porceddu, aged sheep's cheese, pane carasau, and red Cannonau wine.

Stop 14

Nuoro

Cultural capital of Barbagia, birthplace of Nobel laureate Grazia Deledda

★★☆☆☆
📍 Museo della Vita e delle Tradizioni Popolari Sarde Nuoro📍 Casa Natale Grazia Deledda Nuoro📍 Monte Ortobene Nuoro📍 Statua Cristo Redentore Nuoro📍 MAN Museo d'Arte Nuoro📍 Quartiere Seuna Nuoro
1

The Museum of Sardinian Life and Folk Traditions, built in the 1950s on Sant'Onofrio hill, is the island's most complete ethnographic collection. Its six spaces display embroidered costumes, coral and filigree jewelry, carnival masks, ceremonial breads, and musical instruments including the launeddas, a triple-reed flute with a single reed.

⏱ 75 min
2

The birthplace of Grazia Deledda, in the historic Seuna quarter, became a museum in 1983. This writer born in Nuoro in 1871 received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1926—the first Italian woman to do so. Her diploma, Nobel medal, and the kitchen described in her autobiographical novel Cosima are on display.

⏱ 45 min
3

Monte Ortobene rises 3,133 feet above the city. A bronze statue of Christ the Redeemer has stood there since 1901. Every last Sunday in August, the Feast of the Redeemer gathers thousands in traditional costumes from all over Sardinia.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit MAN, the Art Museum of the Province of Nuoro, preserving over 600 works by Sardinian artists from the 19th century to today, including sculptures by Francesco Ciusa, awarded at the 1907 Venice Biennale.

⏱ 60 min

Taste Cannonau di Sardegna DOC, a red wine with notes of dark fruits and spices produced in the Barbagia region from one of the island's most ancient grape varieties.
