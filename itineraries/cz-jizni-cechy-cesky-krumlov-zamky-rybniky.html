✨ Highlights▾
  • Český Krumlov Castle, Czechia's second-largest castle complex after Prague
  • Český Krumlov's baroque theater, among the world's best-preserved with original 1682 machinery
  • Holašovice, UNESCO-listed village since 1998, 23 South Bohemian rustic baroque farmhouses
  • Třeboň's Svět pond and medieval fishpond network, heart of Czech carp country

☀️ Weather▾

Best period: May-June and September, temperatures 15-23°C, ideal for castles and gardens. Avoid July-August (heavy tourist crowds in Český Krumlov, frequent thunderstorms) and January-February (freezing conditions, frozen ponds, -5 to 2°C).

📋 Notes▾

Many castles and cloisters close from November 1st to March 31st; the prime window runs from April to October. The draining of Třeboň's fishponds takes place in late October or November.

Ceske Budejovice

★★★★★
1

A square spanning nearly 1.7 hectares with a layout so precise it's held up as a model: Přemysl Otakar II Square is one of the largest in Central Europe. At its heart stands the Samson Fountain and its baroque sculptures, whose originals now rest inside the town hall. Arcades ring the square, sheltering cafés and terraces. Come in late afternoon when the light gilds the façades and the tour buses have moved on.

⏱ 45 min
2

Climb the 225 steps of the Black Tower and seven ancient bells await you at the top, each with its own name: Budvar, Maria, Marta. Standing 72 meters tall, the 16th-century Černá věž served as watchtower and belfry. The panorama over the rooftops and the Vltava is worth the effort, even if wire mesh gets in the way of your photos a little. Tickets are purchased at the summit; closed in winter, so check before you climb.

⏱ 40 min
3

More discreet than the square, St. Nicholas Cathedral deserves a look for its baroque interior. More surprising still: the old Solnice, a 16th-century Gothic salt granary, now houses a motorcycle museum with dozens of vintage machines. The contrast is absolute. Allow twenty minutes for each; both lie at the heart of the old town, steps from the main square.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

This is where the true Budweiser was born, and the distinction matters: the Budějovický Budvar brewery offers tours in České Budějovice against the backdrop of a century-long trademark dispute with the American brand. The visit passes through the fermentation cellars and ends with an unfiltered lager drawn straight from the source. Book online—English-language slots fill up fast.

⏱ 60 min

Walk the ring of canals that encircle the old town: České Budějovice is wrapped in waterways, remnants of its fortifications. A peaceful half-hour loop, lovely at sunset. No ticket required, just good shoes.

⏱ 40 min

Hluboka Nad Vltavou

★★★★☆
1

Nicknamed the most beautiful castle in Bohemia, and the acknowledged comparison is to Windsor: Hluboká Castle owes its white neo-Gothic appearance to the Schwarzenbergs, who remodeled a 13th-century royal fortress in the 19th century. 140 rooms, 11 towers, carved woodwork, and crystal chandeliers. The 52-meter tower can be climbed via 245 steps but closes in bad weather. Parking near the Penny Market, then a 900-meter uphill walk.

⏱ 90 min
2

In the castle's former riding hall, the Aleš South Bohemian Gallery displays South Bohemian Gothic masters and Dutch painters. A short but dense stop, ideal if the line for the castle is long. Separate ticket from the castle tour.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Stroll through the English-style park surrounding Hluboká Castle, designed by the Schwarzenbergs in the mid-19th century. Lawns, centuries-old trees, and views over the Vltava. Free, perfect before or after touring the interiors.

Holasovice

★★★☆☆
1

Twenty-three painted farmhouses lined up around a long central green, not one more since the 19th century: Holašovice is the only Czech village inscribed on the UNESCO list, since 1998. The colorful gables in South Bohemian rustic baroque reveal themselves as you wander; count on fifteen to thirty minutes. The village was nearly emptied by plague in the 16th century, then repopulated by settlers. Come early: a single tour bus is enough to fill the square.

⏱ 45 min
2

Push open the door of farmhouse No. 6, the Selský dvůr: the exhibition of South Bohemian peasant life gathers hundreds of objects from rural daily life. Small, but it brings the façades to life. Modest entrance fee, closed off-season.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.2Boutique Hotel U Solné brány
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Netolice

★★☆☆☆
1

An Italian villa set amid Czech fishponds, ringed by moats: Kratochvíle Castle was built at the end of the 16th century by William of Rožmberk as a pleasure pavilion. The showpiece remains the Golden Hall, with its lunette vault and mythological stuccowork by Antonio Melana, still used for concerts thanks to its acoustics. Open April to October, parking 200 meters from the gate.

⏱ 60 min
2

Peacocks roam free and swans glide in the moat: Kratochvíle's geometric Renaissance garden lends itself to strolling. Inside, an exhibition on Czech animation and puppet cinema (Trnka, Zeman) surprises in this ancient setting. Count on a good hour in all.

⏱ 30 min
$$9.5Residence Tvrz Skočice
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min

Prachatice

★★☆☆☆
1

The façades on Prachatice's main square are so finely etched with sgraffito you'd think them fresh: the Renaissance old town, wrapped in its ramparts, ranks among the best-preserved urban ensembles in the country. Its prosperity came from the Bavarian salt trade, brought from Passau via the Zlatá stezka, the Golden Trail. Protected zone since 1981. Wander the cobbled lanes, terraces around the square.

⏱ 50 min
2

Climb the 157 steps of St. James Church tower (kostel sv. Jakuba), 53 meters high, for the view over Prachatice and the Šumava. The wooden staircase is steep and creaky, the safety netting interferes a bit with photos. Small fee paid on the way up, bat colony included.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Pivovar Prachatice, the town brewery, deserves a fresh beer break after the climb. No-frills atmosphere, gentle prices, terrace when the weather's fine. Good stopping point before getting back on the road.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.2Hotel Schreiner
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 45min

Zlata Koruna

★★★★☆
1

On a spur the Vltava wraps around on three sides, the Cistercian monastery of Zlatá Koruna has kept its medieval atmosphere intact. Founded in 1263 by Přemysl Otakar II, it shelters the rare two-level Gothic Chapel of the Guardian Angels and the largest church in South Bohemia. Guided tour April to October, last one starts an hour before closing. French text available.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk along the Vltava at the foot of the monastery: the valley floor, calm and wooded, lends itself to a short walk between Zlatá Koruna and the meanders. A few easy kilometers, perfect for digesting the road. Spot the ferry and jetties depending on the season.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.4Ultra premium apartments
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 15min

Cesky Krumlov

★★★★★
1

Czechia's second-largest castle complex after Prague, Český Krumlov Castle layers Gothic, Renaissance, and baroque across five courtyards and eleven hectares of gardens. The Renaissance tower with its trompe-l'oeil frescoes can be climbed for the classic shot of the Vltava's meander. Start with the courtyards, free and already spectacular, before choosing a guided circuit. Get the Český Krumlov Card if you're chaining museums.

⏱ 120 min
2

Three stories of covered galleries perched on tall arches: the Cloak Bridge links the castle to the baroque theater by spanning the old moat. You cross it, but it's from below that it impresses most. It often closes at sunset; time your passage beforehand.

⏱ 30 min
3

Book early for the Castle Baroque Theatre: it's one of the best-preserved in the world, with stage, machinery, sets, and original costumes from 1682. It operates only a few times a year for baroque opera. Guided tour by slots, limited places, separate ticket from the castle.

⏱ 50 min
4

Set in a former 16th-century brewery, the Egon Schiele Art Centrum traces the life of the Expressionist painter, whose mother was born in Český Krumlov. Rotating exhibitions of modern art as a bonus. The building is worth as much as the canvases. Allow one to two hours, included in the Český Krumlov Card.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Embark on a wooden raft at Parkán harbor to descend the Vltava: the castle, St. Vitus Church, and the old town glide past from the water, commentary from two raftsmen included. Stop at "Krumlov's Venice" with a complimentary drink. In summer you can hear the weirs roaring up close.

⏱ 60 min

Pivovar Eggenberg has been brewing in town since the 16th century, established there by the Rožmberks. The tour crosses the historic installations and ends with a Czech lager. Tours generally at 11 a.m., steps from the center. Good food stop in the evening.

⏱ 60 min

If the crowds weary you, slip away late in the day to the castle's baroque gardens, free, all the way to the revolving theater at the back of the park and its waterfall. Summer house, fountains, views over the rooftops. The moment when the buses ebb is the quietest.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.3Ultra premium apartments
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 35min

Rozmberk Nad Vltavou

★★★☆☆
1

Perched on a promontory the Vltava wraps around on three sides, Rožmberk Castle ranks among the oldest in Bohemia, mentioned as early as 1253. Cradle of the powerful Rožmberk family, it passed to the Buquoys in 1620. The widest view opens from the English Tower, at the cost of more than 200 steps. Open April to October, guided tours in English.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The borough of Rožmberk nad Vltavou deserves a descent to wander after the castle: 400 inhabitants, picturesque houses, a few cafés by the water. A former residence now houses a hotel-restaurant. Small, peaceful, quickly explored.

⏱ 40 min
$$9.5Apartmány Srub Výtoňka Lipno
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 15min

Vyssi Brod

★★☆☆☆
1

Founded in 1259 by Vok of Rožmberk, reportedly in gratitude for escaping drowning in the Vltava, the Cistercian monastery of Vyšší Brod still houses monks. Its rococo library of 70,000 volumes is the third-largest in Czechia. The Záviš Cross is kept here—70 cm of gold and gems—and the Rožmberk tomb beneath the choir. Guided tour, sometimes an organ resounding beneath the vaults.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

At the edge of the Šumava, the monastery opens onto the trails: hiking and cycling paths lead toward Lake Lipno, and the Vltava can be descended by canoe to Český Krumlov. Café, shop, and postal museum on-site before setting off.

⏱ 45 min
$$9.5Apartmány Srub Výtoňka Lipno
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h05

Nove Hrady

★★☆☆☆
1

Two drawbridges and one of the deepest moats in the country guard Nové Hrady Castle, founded in the 13th century by the Vítkovec family. The courtyard is free to visit; the interior and its Buquoy portraits require a guide. The tower was blown away in 1573 by lightning and a gunpowder explosion. Open year-round, parking around the square.

⏱ 60 min
2

East of the square, the Empire-style château of Nové Hrady was completed in 1806 by Theresa Buquoy. Three-wing building, grand staircase on the garden side. It now houses a branch of the Academy of Sciences; the exterior can be seen in passing.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Descend into Terčino údolí, Theresa's Valley, a landscape park laid out in the 18th century at Countess Buquoy's request. Artificial waterfall, romantic follies, 7-km educational trail marked with 12 stations. National natural monument since 1949, ideal for stretching your legs.

⏱ 60 min
$$9.6Residence Klažary u Žumberka - Terasové apartmány
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 35min

Trebon

★★★★☆
1

One of the largest castle complexes in the country, Třeboň Castle unfolds the Rožmberks' Renaissance apartments and courtiers' chambers. You even learn the rules of period table etiquette. The park borders the Svět pond. Guided tour, vast estate that grew over centuries.

⏱ 60 min
2

Masaryk Square lines up Renaissance and baroque gables, including the House at the White Stallion and the residence of Štěpánek Netolický, the engineer of the fishponds. Climb the town hall tower, 31 meters with an onion dome, for the view over the town and Svět pond. Modest ticket.

⏱ 45 min
3

Hidden in the trees by the Svět, in Domanín, the neo-Gothic mausoleum of the Schwarzenbergs holds 26 family tombs from the line that ruled the region from 1660. Stained glass, crypt open on guided tour. Three kilometers from the center, on foot along the dike or by bike.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Circle the world without leaving Třeboň: the Svět pond, 200 hectares, is ringed by a nature trail, beach to the north, Ostende tavern to the south. On foot or by bike, count on half a day for the big loop. The surrounding fishponds are a masterpiece of medieval hydraulics.

⏱ 120 min

The Regent Brewery, founded in 1379, ranks among the oldest in the world and can be visited between two stone gate-towers on the south side of town. Tour of the historic rooms and tasting of unfiltered beer. Pair it with a portion of carp fries, local specialty.

⏱ 60 min

If the road has compressed you, the peat bath at Třeboň Spa soothes the musculoskeletal system, a tradition over a century old. The spa also offers sauna and pools. Treatments must be booked in advance, often by prescription on the Czech side.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.4RELAX PARK Třeboň III
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Jindrichuv Hradec

★★★☆☆
1

Czechia's third-largest castle complex, 3.5 hectares, Jindřichův Hradec Castle was born on a 10th-century Slavic fort that became a Renaissance manor. Must-sees: the Knights' Hall and its 1338 frescoes on the legend of St. George, the 15th-century black kitchen, and the rare Mannerist music pavilion, the Rondel, with ingenious acoustics. Several guided circuits of about 50 minutes.

⏱ 75 min
2

Beneath the covered galleries, the Laubengänge, the pastel-hued old town stretches around an oblong square by the Vajgar pond. A Jewish quarter and its Holocaust memorial hide within. Much quieter than Krumlov, you'll mostly encounter locals here.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hop aboard the narrow-gauge train from Jindřichův Hradec, in service for over a century, for an escape through the wooded "Czech Canada." Slow and old-fashioned ride, perfect with children. Check seasonal schedules before coming.

⏱ 60 min
$$9.5Amenity Hotel & Resort Česká Kanada
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 55min

Tabor

★★★☆☆
1

Sgraffito, arcaded gables, and a fountain from 1567: Žižka Square is one of the finest Renaissance squares in Bohemia, under the gaze of Hussite leader Jan Žižka's statue. Everything radiates from here. The old Gothic town hall on the west side houses the Hussite Museum.

⏱ 30 min
2

Descend into the Hussite underground beneath the square: half a kilometer of 15th-century galleries, up to 16 meters below ground, dug for storage and shelter. The Hussite Museum above tells the story of "Tábor, fortress of the righteous" and displays a treasure of some 4,000 silver coins. Closed Mondays, combined ticket with Kotnov Tower.

⏱ 60 min
3

The oldest remnants of Tábor are here: Kotnov Tower and Bechyně Gate, from the 13th century, predating the Hussites. 155 steps lead to the top of the tower, 25 meters up, with views over the rooftops, Jordán lake, and the hills. Modest entrance fee, the neighboring gate holds an exhibit on the medieval town.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Tábor's real pleasure is getting lost in the tangle of narrow alleys, deliberately laid out to hinder attackers. For a playful break, the Chocolate and Marzipan Museum delights children. Some good terraces around the square.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.2Boutique Hotel Nautilus
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Bechyne

★★★☆☆
1

Perched on a promontory between the Lužnice and Smutná rivers, Bechyně Castle was transformed into a Renaissance residence at the end of the 16th century for Petr Vok of Rožmberk. The tour, in the spirit of his life, passes through the Palm Hall and its central pillar shaped like a tree. Curiosity: a gallery of children's portraits, painted in the belief that the child was doomed to die within the year.

⏱ 60 min
2

Bechyně has lived from ceramics since the 15th century and is home to the country's oldest pottery school. The International Museum of Ceramics, at the castle entrance, and the Aleš Gallery in the former brewery display world-class pieces. Never crowded, the town has a drowsy rhythm.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Cross the Bechyňská duha, Bechyně's Rainbow, a concrete bridge 50 meters high over the Lužnice, by car or historic train. Panorama over the whole town, and viewpoints below along the river. Beautiful walk on the Zářečí side.

⏱ 45 min
$$9.9Duškova vila
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Pisek

★★★☆☆
1

The oldest preserved bridge in Czechia, predating Prague's Charles Bridge, Písek's Stone Bridge has spanned the Otava for 110 meters since the 13th century. Pedestrian, classified as a national monument, it has survived floods, submerged in 2002 then quickly restored. You can cross it freely year-round. Ideal light in late afternoon from the riverbank.

⏱ 30 min
2

Písek's royal castle, founded by Přemysl Otakar II on gold-bearing sands, houses the Museum of the Prácheň Region. King's Square lines up pastel houses and the town hall under arcades. Písek was, in 1887, the first town in Bohemia to be permanently electrically lit. Allow half a day for the old town.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Follow the Otava's bank from the stone bridge: the "rich river" carried the gold that made Písek's fortune. You can still try gold-panning for fun, and in summer a sand-sculpture exhibition sets up on the west bank. Easy walk, ideal for closing out the circuit.

⏱ 50 min
$$$9.1Hotel U Kaplicky
