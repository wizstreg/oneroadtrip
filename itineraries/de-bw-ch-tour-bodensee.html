Konstanz

★★★★☆
1

The Imperia turns slowly on her pedestal at the end of the jetty, a 9-meter statue mocking the powerful men of the 1414-1418 Council. Across from her, the 1388 Konzilgebäude hosted the conclave that elected Pope Martin V. Two centuries of history, fifty meters apart. Free access.

⏱ 20 min
2

At 78 meters, the tower of Konstanz Minster is the visual landmark of the entire old town. Climb the 190 steps (€4): the view embraces the lake, the Alps, and Switzerland. Originally Romanesque, Gothic in its additions, the crypt and Konradi Chapel are worth the detour.

⏱ 45 min
3

If you're looking for intact medieval lanes, head to the Niederburg, Konstanz's oldest quarter. Two world wars spared it thanks to the nearby Swiss border. Half-timbered houses, painted facades, five minutes from the Minster.

⏱ 20 min
4

Cross the Marktstätte to the Konzilgebäude, a 14th-century commercial warehouse transformed into a concert hall. The building is massive, sober, planted by the lake. Next door, the Seestraße promenade lines up its patrician houses.

⏱ 15 min

Activities

If it's Tuesday or Friday, the Wochenmarkt at Stephansplatz spreads its stalls of lake produce and regional specialties. The Stephanskirche behind it has a leaning tower visible from the square. Allow 30 minutes to browse.

⏱ 30 min

Konstanz locals cross into Switzerland on foot to buy their cheese in Kreuzlingen, a five-minute walk. Do as they do: the crossing is smooth and disorienting. Return via the lakeside promenade on the German side.

⏱ 30 min

Insel Mainau

★★★★☆
1

Twenty-seven euros to see flowers is steep. But Mainau Island delivers what it promises: 45 hectares of gardens, a tropical Schmetterlingshaus (Germany's second-largest butterfly house), a Bernadotte baroque palace, and lake views at every turn. Children free up to age 12.

⏱ 120 min
2

Take the boat from Konstanz to arrive by lake: the approach is more beautiful than the road bridge. The 18th-century Deutschordensschloss stands at the center, private residence of Count Björn Bernadotte. The Palmenhaus shelters more than 20 palm species.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

In May, one million tulips carpet Mainau's slopes. In summer, roses take over in the Italienischer Rosengarten. In autumn, 12,000 dahlias. The Schlosscafé, between palms and exhibition space, is the best spot for a break.

⏱ 30 min

Ueberlingen

★★★☆☆
1

Less crowded than Meersburg, Überlingen is the spa town of Lake Constance that the masses ignore. So much the better. The historic center is compact, well preserved, with fountains on every corner. Allow an hour for a stroll.

⏱ 45 min
2

Five stories of linden-wood sculpture, 15 meters high: Jörg Zürn's 1613-1616 altarpiece in the Münster Sankt Nikolaus of Überlingen is worth the stop alone. Late Gothic, sober, luminous. Free entry.

⏱ 30 min
3

Settle into Überlingen's Stadtgarten, a terraced garden descending to the lake: lawns, old trees, benches facing the Swiss Alps. It's the quietest spot on the north shore. Free, open year-round.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Head down to the Bodensee-Therme Überlingen for an hour of swimming with lake views. Water at 34°C, Finnish sauna, outdoor pool. Allow €15 for 2 hours. In summer, the public beach right next door is free.

⏱ 60 min

Meersburg

★★★☆☆
1

The first stones of Burg Meersburg reportedly date back to the 7th century, under Merovingian King Dagobert I. It's Germany's oldest still-inhabited castle: not a ruin, not a frozen museum, a living fortress. More than 30 rooms to explore, from dungeon to knights' hall.

⏱ 60 min
2

Poet Annette von Droste-Hülshoff died here on May 24, 1848. Her apartments and death chamber are part of the circuit. Guides offer special Droste tours by reservation, with access to normally closed rooms.

⏱ 20 min
3

Climb to the Neues Schloss for the view from the baroque terrace: the lake, the vineyards, the Alps in the background. The palace, built in the 18th century for the prince-bishops of Konstanz, houses the Dornier Museum and temporary exhibitions.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The terraced vineyards descend to the lake between the two castles. The Winzerverein Meersburg produces dry whites typical of the Bodensee. Buy directly from the cellar, modest prices for the quality.

⏱ 30 min

If you're visiting on a Thursday evening, the Burg hosts a tasting of four Winzerverein wines within its medieval walls. Lake view included. Book at burg-meersburg.de.

⏱ 45 min

Friedrichshafen

★★★☆☆
1

An airship four times larger than an Airbus, yet lighter than air: the Zeppelin Museum in Friedrichshafen tells this engineering madness across 4,000 square meters. The Bauhaus building of the former Hafenbahnhof is a perfect setting. More than 1,500 pieces on display.

⏱ 90 min
2

Step inside the life-size reconstruction of the LZ 129 Hindenburg passenger cabins: 33 meters long, original carpeting, Art Deco dining room. You understand why the wealthy of the 1930s paid a fortune to cross the Atlantic in three days.

⏱ 30 min
3

You don't expect to find Otto Dix in a dirigible museum, and yet. The Zeppelin Museum's art collection brings together Baumeister, Ackermann, and other artists of the "inner migration," refugees at Lake Constance during Nazism. An unexpected counterpoint.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

If you have the budget, a Zeppelin NT flight over Lake Constance is still possible: Deutsche Zeppelin-Reederei offers tours from 30 to 120 minutes departing from Friedrichshafen. Steep price, unique experience in the world. Reservation required.

⏱ 30 min

Tettnang

★★☆☆☆
1

The smell of hops floats over Tettnang in summer. The small town is one of Europe's great hop terroirs: Tettnanger Hopfen goes into the composition of beers worldwide. The Hopfenmuseum explains everything, from cultivation to harvest.

⏱ 30 min
2

Less known than the lake castles, the Neues Schloss of Tettnang is visited in peace. Baroque residence of the Counts of Montfort, 18th century, state rooms with stucco and frescoes. Open April to October.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Visit the Hopfenmuseum to understand why this small town feeds breweries around the planet. Models, tools, tasting possible. In September, the Hopfenernte (harvest) transforms the surrounding fields into a green, fragrant spectacle.

⏱ 30 min

Lindau

★★★★☆
1

The Kelheim sandstone lion, 6 meters high and 50 tons, gazes at the lake since 1856. Facing it, the Leuchtturm (36 meters) is Bavaria's only lighthouse and Germany's southernmost. Together they form the most beautiful harbor entrance on Lake Constance. 139 steps for the panoramic view.

⏱ 30 min
2

A lighthouse in Bavaria, the only one in the state: the geographic absurdity makes you smile, but the view from the platform justifies every step. On clear days, the panorama covers the Dreiländereck (Germany, Austria, Switzerland).

⏱ 20 min
3

Wander the lanes of the Altstadt on Lindau island: patrician houses, Altes Rathaus with Renaissance frescoes, shops in interior courtyards. Everything walkable in 30 minutes.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

It's Lake Constance's postcard: lion on the left, lighthouse on the right, Alps in the background. Lindau's Hafeneinfahrt draws photographers at all hours, but the golden sunset light on the sandstone remains unbeatable.

⏱ 20 min

If the weather is clear from Lindau, Austrian and Swiss peaks stand out above the water. Take a boat to Bregenz (15 min) to change countries without leaving the lake.

⏱ 30 min

Bregenz

1

Up to 6,659 spectators per night, a floating stage that changes with each production: Bregenz's Seebühne is the world's largest lake stage. In 2026, it's Verdi's La Traviata, premiere on July 22. Even outside festival season, the stage can be viewed from the promenade.

⏱ 30 min
2

Take the Pfänder cable car to climb to 1,064 meters: the view plunges over the entire lake, from Konstanz to Lindau, with the Alps as backdrop. A panoramic trail circles the summit in 30 minutes. Allow €14 round-trip.

⏱ 60 min
3

1

The painted facades of Stein am Rhein's Rathausplatz explode with color: biblical scenes, allegories, floral motifs. You'd think you were inside a life-size medieval picture book. Each house bears a name: Sonne, Weisser Adler, Roter Ochsen.

2

3

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Painters continued to decorate Stein am Rhein's facades for five centuries, and the inhabitants still care. Look for hidden details: animals, coats of arms, mottoes. The entire town is an open-air museum.

Activities

Reichenau

★★★★☆
1

Founded in 724 by itinerant bishop Pirmin, Reichenau Abbey was one of Carolingian and Ottonian Europe's major cultural centers. UNESCO since 2000, Lake Constance's largest island keeps its three Romanesque churches, its vegetable fields, and its calm.

⏱ 30 min
2

Benedictine monks returned to the island in 2001, two and a half centuries after the last ones left. The abbey also serves as town hall. The Minster's Schatzkammer houses reliquaries and illuminated manuscripts of considerable value.

⏱ 20 min
3

Visit Sankt Georg church for its 10th-century murals: the most complete Ottonian cycle preserved north of the Alps. The colors have survived a thousand years. Free entry.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Small but erudite, the reconstructed Kräutergarten behind the cloister follows the prescriptions of Walahfrid Strabo, 9th-century abbot and author of the botanical treatise Hortulus. Medicinal herbs, aromatic plants, flowers. Guided tours in summer.

⏱ 20 min
