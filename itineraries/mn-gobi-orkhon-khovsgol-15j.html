Mongolia's Great Wilderness: Gobi Desert to Taiga Forest Nomadic Journey

📅 22 days📍 15 stops🚗 3490 km⏱ 70h30

Follow Khongoryn Els' singing dunes, then explore desert canyons where ruined monasteries hide in rock. Cross the Orkhon Valley and its meadows, reach Lake Khövsgöl with its cold waters, then enter forests where the Tsaatan people raise reindeer. From Gobi to taiga, the contrast strikes.

✨ Highlights
  • Khongoryn Els: singing dunes golden
  • Yolyn Am: ice canyon desert marvel
  • Khövsgöl: dark blue pearl lake
  • Tsaatan: reindeer herders taiga home
⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 7 and 11 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warmest weather (15-25°C), accessible remote areas, and traditional festivals including Naadam. All tourist infrastructure operational but highest prices and crowds.

Spring (April-May): Mild temperatures (5-20°C), fewer tourists, and reasonable prices. Some remote areas may still be inaccessible due to weather, but cities and main attractions fully operational.

Autumn (September-October): Comfortable temperatures (8-18°C), stunning fall colors, and fewer crowds. Excellent photography weather with clear skies, though nights become quite cold.

Winter (November-March): Extremely cold (-20 to -10°C) but unique experiences like ice festivals and winter nomadic life. Lowest prices but limited accessibility to remote areas and harsh conditions.

🌍 What to discover in MN?

Nomadic paradise with vast steppes, traditional culture, and incredible hospitality. Experience yurt living, horseback adventures, and authentic nomadic lifestyle. The country offers cultural immersion with natural beauty and adventure activities. Mongolia provides life-changing cultural experiences with natural wonders and cultural authenticity.
