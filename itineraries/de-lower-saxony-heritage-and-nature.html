Goslar

★★★★☆
1

Explore the Medieval Historic Town of Goslar, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that ranks among Germany's best-preserved medieval cities. Wander through cobblestone streets lined with over 1,500 half-timbered houses spanning five centuries, and discover the Imperial Palace where German emperors once held court. This architectural treasure trove offers an authentic glimpse into medieval Germanic life.

⏱ 75 min
2

Descend into the legendary Rammelsberg Mine, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with over 1,000 years of continuous mining history. This extraordinary site, once a source of copper, zinc, silver, and lead, features fascinating underground galleries and innovative mining technology that shaped the region's prosperity. The museum showcases the remarkable engineering achievements of medieval miners.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Discover the Upper Harz Water Management System, an ingenious UNESCO-listed network of dams, reservoirs, and channels built to power the historic mines. This 800-year-old hydraulic engineering marvel stretches across the Harz mountains, demonstrating how medieval miners harnessed water power with remarkable sophistication.

⏱ 60 min

Hildesheim

★★★★☆
1

Marvel at St. Mary's Cathedral in Hildesheim, a UNESCO World Heritage Site representing the pinnacle of Romanesque architecture in Germany. This 11th-century masterpiece houses the famous bronze doors and the thousand-year-old rose bush, considered the oldest living rose in the world. The cathedral's treasures include exceptional medieval artworks and the renowned Bernward Column.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the magnificent St. Michael's Church, another UNESCO-listed Romanesque gem that showcases exceptional early medieval craftsmanship. This architectural wonder features a unique painted wooden ceiling depicting the Tree of Jesse and perfectly proportioned double choir design. The church stands as one of the finest examples of Ottonian architecture in Europe.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Explore the Fagus Factory in nearby Alfeld, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and pioneering example of modern industrial architecture. Designed by Walter Gropius in 1911, this revolutionary building with its innovative glass curtain walls became a cornerstone of Bauhaus design principles. The factory remains operational while offering fascinating tours of its groundbreaking architecture.

★★★★☆
1

Explore the German Wadden Sea National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site ranked alongside the Grand Canyon and Great Barrier Reef as a natural wonder of global significance. This unique tidal ecosystem stretches along Lower Saxony's North Sea coast, hosting millions of migratory birds and remarkable marine life. The dramatic interplay of ebb and flow creates ever-changing landscapes of mudflats, sandbanks, and salt marshes.

⏱ 75 min
2

Discover the seven East Frisian Islands, one of Germany's most beautiful coastal treasures offering pristine beaches and unique island atmospheres. These car-free havens feature sandy shores, towering dunes, and endless skies where the rhythm of life follows the tides. Each island has its own distinct character, from bustling resorts to tranquil nature reserves.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Embark on a guided mudflat walking tour across the exposed seabed during low tide, an experience unique to the Wadden Sea. Wade through this lunar-like landscape teeming with shellfish, crabs, and marine worms while expert guides reveal the secrets of this extraordinary ecosystem. The sensation of walking on the ocean floor as seabirds wheel overhead is truly unforgettable.

⏱ 60 min

Stroll through the atmospheric Am Sande square, surrounded by magnificent gabled houses showcasing the finest examples of North German brick architecture. The lively student atmosphere-Lüneburg is a university town-adds vibrant energy to the historic streets filled with cafes, boutiques, and cultural venues.
