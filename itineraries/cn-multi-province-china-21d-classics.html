Beijing

★★★★★
1

The Forbidden City, built between 1406 and 1420 under Emperor Yongle, sprawls across 72 hectares and encompasses 980 buildings connected by courtyards paved with 12 million bricks. For five centuries it served as the residence of 24 emperors from the Ming and Qing dynasties. UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1987. Begin at the Gate of Heavenly Peace, a monumental entrance crowned with Mao's portrait since 1949, then proceed through the three great ceremonial halls.

⏱ 180 min
2

Climb Coal Hill (Jingshan), an artificial mound rising 108 meters high, constructed from earth excavated when the Forbidden City's moat was dug. From the summit, sweeping panorama over the entire expanse of golden rooftops. This is where the last Ming emperor hanged himself in 1644.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Temple of Heaven, erected in 1420 and expanded during the 16th century, occupies 273 hectares. The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (38 m tall) rests on 28 pillars assembled without nails or cement. UNESCO since 1998.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Morning observation of locals in the Temple of Heaven park: tai chi, dancing, card games beneath ancient cypresses.

⏱ 30 min

Beijing

★★★★★
1

The Mutianyu section of the Great Wall, 73 km from Beijing, rebuilt in granite in 1569 by General Qi Jiguang. Twenty-two watchtowers spanning 2,250 meters, walls 7-8 m high, crenellations on both sides—an exceptional configuration. The Mutianyu Pass with its three interconnected towers (Zhengguantai) is unique along the entire Wall.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Nanluoguxiang hutongs, over 800 years of history. Narrow passages from the Yuan dynasty (1271-1368). Courtyard houses (siheyuan) arranged around small courtyards planted with pomegranate trees. Fewer than 1,000 hutongs survive today.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Cable car ascent to tower no. 14, hiking between towers 14 and 20, toboggan descent from tower no. 6.

⏱ 120 min
$$$9.8Happy Dragon Courtyard Hotel Beijing Forbidden City Lama Temple

Beijing

★★★★★
1

The Summer Palace, designed in 1750 by Emperor Qianlong, covers 290 hectares. Kunming Lake excavated by 100,000 laborers. Destroyed in 1860, rebuilt in 1886 by Empress Cixi. The Long Corridor (728 m), the Marble Boat (36 m). UNESCO since 1998.

⏱ 150 min
2

The 798 Art District, a former industrial complex of 640,000 m² built in the 1950s by East German architects (Bauhaus style). Over 200 galleries today, including the UCCA (10,000 m²).

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Crossing the Seventeen-Arch Bridge (150 m, 1750), adorned with over 500 stone lions, each one different.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.8Happy Dragon Courtyard Hotel Beijing Forbidden City Lama Temple

Datong

★★★☆☆
1

High-speed train Beijing→Datong (~2h). The Yungang Grottoes, 16 km from Datong: 252 caves and 51,000 Buddhist statues carved into sandstone between 460 and 525 under the Northern Wei. The complex stretches 1 km along the cliff face of Mount Wuzhou. The five Tan Yao caves (nos. 16-20) each shelter a colossal Buddha representing a Tuoba emperor—an act of repentance following anti-Buddhist persecutions. Cave no. 20: 13.7 m Buddha in meditation. Cave no. 5: largest statue at the site (17 m). The style blends Persian, Greco-Buddhist Gandharan, Indian and Chinese influences. UNESCO since 2001.

⏱ 180 min
2

Caves nos. 5 and 6, the best preserved. Cave no. 6 features a central pagoda adorned with scenes from the life of Buddha. After the capital relocated to Luoyang in 494, work gradually ceased.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.6五福居 Five Blessings Hotel

Datong

★★★☆☆
1

The Hanging Monastery Xuankong, 65 km from Datong, built in 491 under the Northern Wei. Plastered 50 m high on the Jinlong canyon cliff face, opposite Mount Hengshan (sacred Taoist mountain). The structure is held by horizontal beams driven into the rock—the red wooden pillars are purely aesthetic. Forty pavilions linked by vertiginous walkways, 80 statues. The only monastery in China honoring Buddhism, Taoism and Confucianism—Sakyamuni, Lao Tzu and Confucius side by side.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Nine Dragons Screen of Datong (1392), in front of the palace of Emperor Ming Hongwu's thirteenth son. Measuring 45.5 m long and 8 m high, covered in glazed tiles. The oldest and largest of China's three existing Nine Dragons screens.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Ascent into the monastery via stone stairway carved into the cliff, crossing the Changxian bridge between the north and south sections—plunging view over the canyon.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.6五福居 Five Blessings Hotel

Pingyao

★★★☆☆
1

High-speed train Datong→Pingyao (~3h). Fortified city, UNESCO since 1997, China's last intact walled city. Ramparts erected in rammed earth as early as 827 BC, then reinforced in stone in 1370 under the Ming. Six kilometers in perimeter, 12 m high, 6 gates, 72 bastions. Turtle silhouette seen from the sky.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Rishengchang Bank (1823), China's first bank. Invention of interbank transfers. Martial arts-trained escorts for money transport.

⏱ 60 min
3

The old county yamen (26,000 m², rebuilt in 1346). Daily reenactments of ancient trials.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Strolling the lanes parallel to Ming Qing Jie. Sample mao'erduo (Shanxi 'cat's ear' noodles).

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.6Shanxi Merchant Mansion

Pingyao

★★★☆☆
1

Shuanglin Temple, 7 km away, 1,500 years old. Over 1,000 colored clay Buddhist statues, including a 26-armed Guanyin. UNESCO with Pingyao.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Confucius Temple, the oldest preserved in China. Dacheng Hall from 1163 (Jin dynasty).

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Sunset from the ramparts—north section, view over the rooftops and Shanxi mountains.

⏱ 45 min

Chengdu

★★★★☆
1

Flight to Chengdu. Giant Panda Research Center, founded in 1987 (6 pandas initially), today houses over 150 giant pandas across 238 hectares (after the extension completed in 2022). The giant panda has lived on Earth for 8 million years, surviving in the wild only in Sichuan, Shaanxi and Gansu. Arrive before 10am—pandas fall asleep afterward.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Observation of red pandas in the upper section of the park. Documentary about breeding at the center's cinema.

⏱ 60 min

Chengdu

★★★★☆
1

Wuhou Temple, dedicated to Zhuge Liang (3rd century). Gallery of 47 terracotta statues of heroes from the Three Kingdoms.

⏱ 75 min
2

Jinli Street (550 m), a Qing-era merchant street lined with red lanterns and Chinese shadow puppet theaters.

⏱ 45 min
3

Wenshu Monastery, founded under the Tang, in Chengdu's Tibetan quarter—the historic gateway to Tibet.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Sichuanese tasting: hotpot with Sichuan pepper, mapo tofu, dan dan mian. The pepper produces a 'ma' numbing sensation on the tongue.

⏱ 60 min

Teahouse at People's Park: jasmine tea in gaiwan, mahjong and ear cleaning—a Sichuanese ritual.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.4Grand ParcVue Hotel Residence Chengdu

Chengdu

★★★★☆
1

Excursion to Dujiangyan (60 km, 30 min by high-speed rail). Dam-free irrigation system built in 256 BC by Li Bing, governor of Sichuan under the Qin. Still operating after 2,200 years—the world's oldest large-scale hydraulic work still in service. The Yuzui levee divides the Minjiang River, the Feishayan spillway evacuates floods and silt, the Baopingkou channel regulates flow. Li Bing heated then cooled the rock to carve a 20 m channel through Mount Yulei—8 years of labor, tens of thousands of workers. UNESCO since 2000.

⏱ 150 min
2

Erwang Temple (Temple of the Two Kings) above the dam, dedicated to Li Bing and his son. Stone statue of Li Bing excavated from the river in 1974, Han dynasty.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Crossing the Anlan suspension bridge (500 m), one of China's five most famous ancient bridges, built from woven bamboo rope under the Song dynasty.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.4Grand ParcVue Hotel Residence Chengdu

Zhangjiajie

★★★★★
1

Flight to Zhangjiajie. National Forest Park, China's first (1982), UNESCO since 1992, 130+ km². Over 3,000 quartzite sandstone pillars, some exceeding 200 m. The Southern Sky Column (150 m) renamed 'Avatar Hallelujah Mountain'. Bailong Elevator (2002, Guinness): 326 m in under 2 min in glass cabins.

⏱ 300 min

Activities

Golden Whip Stream hike (5.7 km, ~2h) at the foot of the pillars. Wild monkeys along the route.

⏱ 120 min

★★★★★

Tianzi Peak, highest point in the park. Panorama over a 'sea of pillars' to the horizon. Legend: a peasant named Xiang Dakun proclaimed himself emperor here during the Taiping Rebellion.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Yangjiajie sector, less crowded, peaceful trails and spectacular views of pillars and forested gorges.

Tianmen Mountain: 7.5 km cable car (1,200 m elevation gain) soaring over the 99 hairpin bends. Natural arch 131 m × 57 m accessible by 999 steps. Coiling Dragon Cliff glass walkway at 1,400 m altitude.

Grand Canyon glass bridge (430 m long, 300 m above the void). Sways slightly during crossing—by design.

Fenghuang

★★★★☆
1

Drive to Fenghuang (~4h). Old town ('Phoenix'), built under the Tang along the Tuojiang River, inhabited by the Miao and Tujia. Stilt houses (diaojiaolou) in wood and bamboo assembled without nails or bolts. Ming ramparts.

⏱ 60 min
2

Hongqiao Bridge (red lanterns, Miao shops), North Gate tower, Wanming Pagoda.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Boat on the Tuojiang River at sunset—lanterns lit, reflections of stilt houses on the water.

⏱ 30 min

Crossing on the stepping stones between the two banks.

Longsheng

★★☆☆☆
1

Drive from Fenghuang to Longsheng (~5h). Longji rice terraces ('Dragon's Backbone'), 100 km north of Guilin. Sculpted by the Zhuang and Yao starting from the Yuan dynasty (13th century), 650 years of labor. 66 km², altitudes from 300 to 1,100 m. In spring the flooded terraces reflect the sky; in autumn, they turn golden.

⏱ 60 min
2

Zhuang village of Ping'an: stilt houses, 'Nine Dragons and Five Tigers' and 'Seven Stars Around the Moon' viewpoints.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike between Ping'an viewpoints (~1h30) through terraces and villages. At Huangluo village, encounter with Yao women whose hair exceeds 1.80 m in length.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.9Rice View Lodge

Yangshuo

★★★★☆
1

Drive Longsheng→Guilin (~2h) then Li River. The landscape of karst peaks and rice paddies appears on the reverse of the 20 yuan note—the panorama precisely at Xingping. Limestone formations sculpted by erosion over millions of years.

⏱ 120 min
2

Xingping village: old Ming and Qing street. Climb to the summit of Laozhai Hill (20 min steep climb) for the region's most photographed panorama.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Bamboo raft cruise (Xingping section, ~1h) between karst peaks, water buffalo and cormorant fishermen.

Yangshuo

★★★★☆
1

Yangshuo countryside and the Yulong River (Jade Dragon). Dragon Bridge (Ming dynasty) spans the river amid rice paddies framed by karst peaks. The scenic Ten-Mile Gallery road lines up rock formations and villages.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Bicycle ride through the Yulong rice paddies (15-20 km) between stone villages, water buffalo and karst peaks. Flat trails along the river.

⏱ 120 min

Bamboo raft descent on the Yulong River—calmer than the Li River. Passing beneath ancient stone bridges.
