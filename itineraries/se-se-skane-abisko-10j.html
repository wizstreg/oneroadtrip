📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 6 and 10 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (June-August): Perfect weather conditions (18-25°C) with midnight sun in north, white nights, and ideal conditions for outdoor activities. Peak tourist season with highest prices but most comfortable temperatures and endless daylight hours.

Late Spring (May): Excellent weather emergence with blooming landscapes, fewer crowds, and moderate prices. Traditional Midsummer celebrations and optimal conditions for hiking and cycling.

Early Autumn (September-October): Beautiful fall colors with comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, and moderate prices. Excellent for cultural activities and northern lights begin appearing.

Winter (December-March): Cold temperatures (-5 to -15°C) with snow, northern lights, and winter activities. Lower prices, authentic Nordic winter experience, and excellent conditions for winter sports.

🌍 What to discover in SE?

Nordic paradise with pristine nature, progressive society, and rich cultural heritage. Experience midnight sun, traditional culture, and incredible natural beauty. The country offers cultural authenticity with natural preservation and progressive values. Sweden provides Nordic experiences with cultural depth and environmental leadership.

📋 Important notes

Two ferry crossings to Gotland require advance booking in summer (Destination Gotland company). Öland is connected by a free 6 km bridge. The route can be completed by rental car or combined transport (trains + buses + ferries).

The Grand South: From Malmö to Stockholm via the Islands

📅 10 days📍 10 stops🚗 675 km⏱ 11h25

Malmo → Ystad → Karlskrona → Kalmar → Borgholm → Visby → Stockholm

Malmo → Ystad → Karlskrona → Kalmar → Borgholm → Visby → Stockholm

This 10-day route links Malmö to Stockholm by following Sweden's Baltic coastline and crossing two islands of contrasting character: Öland, flat and windswept, UNESCO-listed for its limestone steppes, and Gotland, bristling with medieval ramparts and 11th-century rural churches. Between them, Karlskrona and its 17th-century naval base, Kalmar and its Renaissance castle, then Stockholm and its 30,000-island archipelago.

Stop 1

Malmo

Where twisted concrete meets medieval cobblestones across the Øresund

★★★★☆
📍 Turning Torso Malmö📍 Västra Hamnen Malmö📍 Stortorget Malmö📍 Lilla Torg Malmö📍 Malmöhus Castle📍 Ribersborgsstranden Malmö
1

Begin with the Västra Hamnen district, the former industrial port redeveloped into a model residential quarter in the 2000s. Here rises the Turning Torso: 190 meters of twisted white concrete designed by Santiago Calatrava and completed in 2005. It remains Scandinavia's tallest residential tower.

⏱ 45 min
2

Stortorget, Malmö's main square, is framed by the 16th-century city hall and the governor's residence. Head down to Lilla Torg, the cobbled small square lined with half-timbered houses where locals gather in the evenings—it's one of the city's liveliest spots.

⏱ 60 min
3

Malmöhus Castle dates from 1434—it's the oldest surviving Renaissance castle in Scandinavia. Today it houses several museums: natural history, art, and a section devoted to the city's history. Allow at least 90 minutes.

⏱ 90 min
4

The medieval old town, centered around Stortorget and Sankt Petri Cathedral (14th century), deserves an hour of wandering. Behind its austere facade, the church conceals a 16th-century Flemish altarpiece and a burial crypt.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Late in the day, head to the Ribersborg baths: a long wooden pier extending into the sea with gender-separated bathing cabins dating from 1898. Even out of season, regulars plunge into the cold waters of the Øresund.

⏱ 60 min

Walk through Möllevångstorget, the working-class southern district's market, to see Malmö away from tourist circuits: vegetable stalls, spice aromas, neighborhood cafés.

Stop 2

Ystad

Half-timbered streets where Wallander walked and Vikings raised stones

★★★★★
📍 Smygehuk Trelleborg📍 Ystad medieval center📍 Ystad Klostret📍 Ales Stenar Kåseberga📍 Kåseberga cliff
1

Between Malmö and Ystad, detour to Smygehuk: Sweden's southernmost point, marked by a small red-and-white striped lighthouse on a rocky coast. Nothing spectacular—but it's the geographic extremity of the country.

⏱ 30 min
2

Ystad is a well-preserved medieval town with cobbled lanes, tightly packed half-timbered houses, and a 13th-century Franciscan monastery, Klostret, now converted into a regional museum. Fans of the Wallander crime series will recognize the settings—exteriors were filmed here.

⏱ 90 min
3

Don't miss Ales Stenar at Kåseberga, 10 km east of Ystad. This megalithic monument of 59 standing stones forms a 67-meter ship-shaped ellipse on a grassy clifftop facing the Baltic Sea. Built around 600 AD, its exact purpose remains debated—solar observatory, burial site, or commemorative monument. The sea view at sunset is among the finest in Scania.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Drive down to the fishing village of Kåseberga below the cliff to see boats pulled up on the shore and artisanal eel smokehouses—smoked eel is a local specialty.

⏱ 45 min

In the evening at Ystad, walk along Stora Östergatan, the best-preserved medieval street, and look for a café in one of the hidden courtyards behind the facades.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 3

Karlskrona

A baroque admiral's dream built on islands, still guarding the Baltic

★★★★☆
📍 Marinmuseum Karlskrona📍 HMS Neptun submarine Karlskrona📍 Stortorget Karlskrona📍 Admiralty Church Karlskrona📍 Karlskrona archipelago
1

Karlskrona was founded in 1680 by King Charles XI as the main naval base for Sweden's Baltic fleet—the entire city was planned around its shipyards. It's the world's only still-operational baroque naval base, earning UNESCO World Heritage status in 1998. Start at the grand Stortorget, one of Sweden's largest baroque squares, flanked by two twin circular churches.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Maritime Museum (Marinmuseum) traces 350 years of Swedish naval history with models, cannons, uniforms, and a section on Baltic battles. Outside, you can board the HMS Neptun submarine, a real Cold War-era submarine open for tours—the passageways are narrow, the atmosphere claustrophobic, and it's fascinating.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Admiralty Church (Amiralitetskyrkan), built in 1685, is the largest surviving wooden church in Sweden. Before its entrance stands a wooden statue of an old lighthouse keeper who has been begging since the 18th century—tradition calls for dropping a coin in his bowl.

⏱ 45 min
4

Karlskrona's archipelago extends across dozens of islands and islets around the city. Local ferries reach the main inhabited islands from the central harbor—some shelter 18th-century fortifications.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rent a kayak from the harbor to paddle along the fortifications from water level—the view of the city and bastions from sea level differs dramatically from the quayside perspective.

⏱ 120 min

Karlskrona's covered market (Saluhallen) offers smoked Baltic fish, Swedish cheeses, and Scanian gingerbread—a good option for a quick lunch.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 4

Kalmar

Where three kingdoms united beneath Renaissance towers in 1397

★★★☆☆
📍 Kalmar Castle📍 Kalmar Cathedral📍 Öland Bridge Kalmar📍 Old Kalmar historic center
1

Kalmar Castle is one of Sweden's best-preserved Renaissance castles. Here in 1397 the Kalmar Union was signed, a treaty uniting Sweden, Denmark, and Norway under a single crown—a union that lasted until 1523. The castle was remodeled in the 16th century by the Vasa kings, and its restored interior halls rank among Scandinavia's finest.

⏱ 90 min
2

Kalmar's baroque cathedral dates from the 17th century—built to plans by Nicodemus Tessin the Elder, the royal court architect. Its yellow facade and dome are visible from across the lower town.

⏱ 45 min
3

Old Kalmar, rebuilt after a 17th-century fire, follows a regular grid plan with low wooden houses painted red, ochre, and yellow. Walk the Kalmarsund waterfront to see traditional boats moored facing Öland island.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the afternoon, cross the Öland Bridge—6 km of concrete over the sea, toll-free, opened in 1972 and still Sweden's longest bridge. The drive across with views of the Kalmarsund on a clear day is a journey highlight in itself. You can stop on Öland for an initial reconnaissance before returning to sleep in Kalmar.

⏱ 60 min

Kalmar harbor fisheries still serve fresh Baltic catch—perch, pike, and smoked eel are the local essentials.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 5

Borgholm

Windmills and limestone steppes on an island shaped by wind and sea

★★★★★
📍 Borgholm Castle Öland📍 Solliden Palace Öland📍 Stora Alvaret Öland📍 Windmill Öland📍 Ottenby Nature Reserve Öland📍 Långe Jan lighthouse Öland
1

Borgholm Castle is an imposing ruin atop a rocky plateau overlooking the sea. Begun in the 16th century and expanded until the 18th, it burned in 1806 and was never rebuilt. Its ochre sandstone walls and gutted towers have a romantic allure. The interior is open to visitors, and summer concerts are held here.

⏱ 60 min
2

Solliden Royal Palace, the Swedish royal family's summer residence since 1906, is partially open on the garden side. The private apartments remain closed, but the formal French-style parterres and flowered terraces are worth visiting.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Stora Alvaret is a limestone steppe extending across 260 km² in the island's center and south. This almost lunar landscape—low grasses, exposed bedrock, wild orchids in spring—is one of Europe's rare examples of this ecosystem. It earned Öland UNESCO World Heritage status in 2000.

⏱ 90 min
4

Ottenby bird reserve, at the island's southern tip, is one of Scandinavia's most important migration sites. Over 300 species have been recorded here. At the cape's extremity, Långe Jan—"Tall John"—is Sweden's tallest lighthouse at 41 meters.

⏱ 75 min
5

Öland has several hundred windmills, about forty still standing along the east coast. The most accessible cluster is near Lerkaka, with five mills side by side in a meadow.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Öland is perfectly flat—ideal cycling territory. Rental shops in Borgholm offer bikes for touring southward. The coastal cycle path follows the east shore for dozens of kilometers with views toward the mainland.

⏱ 180 min

Birdwatching at dawn in Ottenby: in spring and autumn, migrations of cranes, geese, and raptors are spectacular from sunrise onward.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 6

Visby

Medieval ramparts encircling church ruins where roses bloom in cobbled lanes

★★★★☆
📍 Visby city walls Gotland📍 Stortorget Visby📍 St. Catherine's Church ruins Visby📍 Fiskargränd Visby📍 Gotland Museum Visby
1

This morning, cross back over the Öland Bridge to the mainland, then drive north to Oskarshamn (~80 km from the bridge, about 1 hour). This is where ferries depart for Gotland. Board with Destination Gotland, crossing takes about 3 hours—book ahead in July-August as spaces sell out. Arriving in Visby by ferry from Oskarshamn, the first thing you'll see entering the harbor: the ramparts. These 3.4 km of medieval walls, built between the 12th and 14th centuries, rank among Northern Europe's best-preserved. UNESCO-listed since 1995, they feature 44 towers and have never been demolished. Walk the complete circuit—allow 90 minutes.

⏱ 90 min
2

Visby's ruined medieval churches are among the town's most distinctive features. Gotland once had 16 churches for a population of just a few thousand in the Middle Ages—most were abandoned after 1361 when Danish King Valdemar Atterdag captured the city. The ruins of St. Catherine's (Franciscan, 13th century) and St. Nicholas are the most photogenic.

⏱ 60 min
3

Fiskargränd, the "Fisherman's Lane," is covered with blooming roses from June to August. It's one of Gotland's most photographed streets—narrow, cobbled, with low houses whose gardens overflow onto the lane.

⏱ 30 min
4

Gotland Museum (Gotlands Museum) holds the world's largest collection of painted rune stones—these slabs illustrated with mythological scenes date from the 5th to 11th centuries. The treasury section displays thousands of Viking-era coins found in the island's fields.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

In the evening, Visby comes alive around Stortorget and the old town lanes. Restaurant terraces set up in medieval house courtyards—it's an atmosphere quite different from mainland Sweden.

⏱ 90 min

If you're arriving in summer (late July - early August), check dates for Visby Medieval Week (Medeltidsveckan): one of Europe's largest medieval festivals, with costumes, jousting, and markets throughout the old town.

⏱ 120 min

Visby

Medieval ramparts encircling church ruins where roses bloom in cobbled lanes

★★★★☆
📍 Fårö island Gotland📍 Raukar Fårö Gotland📍 Ingmar Bergman house Fårö📍 Gothem medieval church Gotland📍 Lärbro church Gotland
1

Take the ferry to Fårö, the small island north of Gotland (free crossing, 5 minutes). This is where Ingmar Bergman lived and worked from 1967 until his death in 2007—he filmed several movies here including Persona and Scenes from a Marriage. The house is visible from outside; a center devoted to his work screens films in summer.

⏱ 60 min
2

Fårö's raukar are limestone pillars sculpted by marine erosion over thousands of years. These strange 3-6 meter formations rise from beaches and moorlands like petrified figures. The most accessible are at Langhammars and Digerhuvud.

⏱ 90 min
3

Gotland has over 90 rural medieval churches, some dating to the 11th century. They look similar from outside—limestone, square tower, interior paintings—but each conceals different details. The churches at Gothem, Garde, and Roma are among the most remarkable and freely open to visitors.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Rent a bicycle on Fårö to link wild beaches and raukar—the island is small (about 30 km long) and lightly trafficked. Sudersand beach is the finest, with white sand and water that can reach 20°C in July.

⏱ 180 min

Back in Visby in the evening, climb the northern ramparts to watch the sun descend over the Baltic Sea—in summer, light lingers until 10 PM.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 8

Stockholm

Fourteen islands woven together by bridges, history, and northern light

★★★★★
📍 Gamla Stan Stockholm📍 Stortorget Stockholm📍 Royal Palace Stockholm📍 Storkyrkan Cathedral Stockholm📍 Järntorget Stockholm
1

Arrive in Nynäshamn by ferry, then drive to Stockholm (60 km north). Drop your bags and head straight for Gamla Stan, the old town on its central island. This is medieval Stockholm's heart, with cobbled lanes dating to the 13th century. Stortorget, the central square with red, ochre, and yellow houses, is one of the city's oldest squares.

⏱ 90 min
2

Stockholm's Royal Palace is the Swedish royal family's official residence, with 608 rooms—one of Europe's largest royal palaces still in use. The changing of the guard takes place daily at noon in summer. Several state apartments, the royal apartment museum, and the crown treasury are open to the public.

⏱ 90 min
3

Storkyrkan Cathedral, adjacent to the Royal Palace, is Stockholm's oldest church (13th century). It houses the statue of St. George slaying the dragon, carved in oak in 1489, and the 1535 Sundog Painting—the first Nordic painting to depict a city in realistic perspective.

⏱ 45 min
4

Leave Gamla Stan via Slussen bridge and climb up to Södermalm. The Monteliusvägen viewpoint offers the best panoramic view over the water, islands, and Gamla Stan's spire—it's free and accessible on foot from the district's lower reaches.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Descend into Stockholm's metro to see its decorated stations. The blue line in particular (Rådhuset, Kungsträdgården, T-Centralen stations) is often called the world's longest art gallery: over 90 stations decorated by artists since the 1950s.

⏱ 60 min

Gamla Stan's market and the shops along Västerlånggatan are lively in the evening—you'll find Swedish handicrafts, Nordic design, and the inevitable Dala figurines (painted wooden horses, a Swedish folk symbol).

⏱ 60 min

Stockholm

Fourteen islands woven together by bridges, history, and northern light

★★★★★
📍 Vasa Museum Stockholm Djurgården📍 Skansen Stockholm📍 ABBA The Museum Stockholm📍 Södermalm Stockholm📍 Hornstull Stockholm
1

The Vasa Museum is Stockholm's must-see. The warship Vasa sank in 1628, twenty minutes after launching in Stockholm harbor, before the eyes of King Gustav II Adolf and thousands of spectators. Raised in 1961 after 333 years in the mud, it survives 95% original. It's the world's only 17th-century ship this complete. Allow at least 2 hours.

⏱ 120 min
2

Skansen, founded in 1891, is the world's oldest open-air museum. It brings together on a Djurgården hillside over 150 historic buildings relocated from all Swedish regions: Sami farms, manor houses, charcoal kilns, artisan workshops. A zoo presents Scandinavian wildlife—moose, brown bears, lynx, reindeer. Allow half a day.

⏱ 180 min
3

ABBA The Museum on Djurgården is an interactive exhibition about Swedish pop history's best-selling group. Original costumes, recordings, holograms of the four members—even non-fans will appreciate the immersive scenography.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

In the evening, head up to Södermalm to see hip Stockholm: the Hornstull district with weekend designer markets, Götgatan cafés, and water views from the heights of Skinnarviksberget—locals' favorite viewpoint.

⏱ 120 min

Try Swedish smörgåsbord: the traditional Scandinavian buffet with marinated herring (in six different preparations), gravlax, meatballs, cheeses, and crispbread (knäckebröd). Several restaurants around Djurgården and downtown offer this format at lunchtime.

⏱ 75 min

Stockholm

Fourteen islands woven together by bridges, history, and northern light

★★★★★
📍 Stockholm archipelago excursion📍 Vaxholm Stockholm archipelago📍 Östermalm Stockholm📍 Kungsträdgården Stockholm📍 Stockholm City Library
1

If taking the archipelago excursion (recommended): Stockholm's archipelago extends 80 km between the city and open sea, with roughly 30,000 islands, islets, and skerries. Vaxholm is the first island accessible by regular ferry from downtown (45 minutes)—a small fortified town with a 16th-century citadel and yellow and red painted wooden houses.

⏱ 120 min
2

If staying in the city: Östermalm is Stockholm's bourgeois residential district, with Östermalmshallen market (1888)—a red-brick hall with counters of smoked salmon, Swedish charcuterie, and cheeses. Two steps away, Kungsträdgården park, the former king's garden, is the city's gathering center in all seasons.

⏱ 90 min
3

Stockholm City Library (Stadsbiblioteket, 1928) is a masterpiece of Nordic classical architecture: a red-orange brick cylinder pierced with vertical windows, designed by Gunnar Asplund. The circular interior with floor-to-ceiling bookshelves across three levels is striking.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Archipelago cruise: regular ferries and organized excursions depart from Strömkajen pier, in front of the Grand Hotel. The simplest option is the public ferry to Vaxholm—ordinary transport ticket, island views, flexible return.

⏱ 240 min

Final Swedish meal: try almond bread (mandelbullar) or cardamom buns (kardemummabullar) in one of the artisan bakeries in Södermalm or Gamla Stan—coffee accompanied by a pastry (fika) is a Swedish social ritual in its own right.
