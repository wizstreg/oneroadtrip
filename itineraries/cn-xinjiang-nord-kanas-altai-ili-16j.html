Day 1

Karamay

China's oil capital ringed by yardangs sculpted by Dzungarian winds
Wuerhe Ghost City KaramayKaramay Petroleum ParkWucai Tan Karamay
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

First major leg from Urumqi: 350 km straight north to Karamay, China's oil capital that sprang from the desert in the 1950s. You'll arrive in early afternoon and head straight to the Devil's City, 100 km northwest. The site, called Wuerhe Yardang, is a vast field of yardangs - rock formations sculpted by millennia of Dzungarian winds. The scenery is Martian, ochre, riddled with canyons. Budget at least 2.5 hours for your visit, and bring water: there's not a shred of shade.

⏱ 150 min
2

On the way back to Karamay, stop at the Five-Colored Beach (Wucai Tan) along the Irtysh River. Sedimentary layers create red, ochre, green, and gray bands according to the geology. Short but photogenic - allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Karamay Petroleum Park in town traces the history of the oil field discovered here in 1955 - China's first. You'll see restored original derricks and a didactic exhibition on the transformation of a desert corner into a city of half a million people. Not essential, but interesting if you arrive early.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Sample Uyghur cuisine at Karamay's night market: charcoal-grilled lamb skewers with cumin, dapanji (big plate chicken with potatoes and hand-pulled noodles), naan bread fresh from the tandoor. On summer evenings, the atmosphere buzzes until midnight.

⏱ 90 min

Sunset walk among the Wuerhe yardangs: the low-angle shadows reveal the rock formations' relief, and golden light transforms the desert into a movie set. Photographers set up around 7:30 PM in July-August.

⏱ 60 min

Day 2

Beitun

Bingtuan pivot town on the Irtysh, founded 1954 by demobilized farmer-soldiers
Beitun ReservoirBeitun Bingtuan MuseumIrtysh River Beitun
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Stopover in Beitun, a pivot town built by the Bingtuan - the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps, a paramilitary organization founded in 1954 to clear and colonize these lands. The small local Bingtuan museum tells this singular history: demobilized soldiers from the People's Liberation Army turned farmer-soldiers, massive irrigation projects, birth of state farms. It's a little-known page of Chinese history, and Beitun is one of the ten Bingtuan administrative divisions still active today.

⏱ 60 min
2

Beitun Reservoir, north of the city, is fed by the Irtysh. Quick stroll along the banks, opportunity to spot migratory birds in spring and autumn. Good break to stretch your legs before continuing on to Burqin.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Lunch in a Beitun workers' canteen - the surrounding state farms bring fresh produce: watermelons, Manas tomatoes, Altai lamb. Simple, cheap meal (30-50 yuan), authentic to Bingtuan culture.

⏱ 60 min

Day 3

Burqin

Gateway to northern Xinjiang on the Irtysh, China's only river flowing to the Arctic
Five Colored Beach BurqinBurqin Night MarketIrtysh River Burqin
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Burqin marks the gateway to the north, the antechamber to Kanas. You'll arrive mid-afternoon from Beitun. Head for the Five-Colored River (Wucai Tan of Burqin), 24 km west, along the Irtysh. This is China's only river that flows into the Arctic Ocean. The clay cliffs display seven hues at sunset, and the contrast with the turquoise water is spectacular. Allow 1.5 hours on site.

⏱ 90 min
2

Make a detour via the Irtysh bridge at the edge of town to see the river flowing north - a unique geographic fact in China, as all other major rivers flow east or south. Brief 20-minute stop, explanatory signs in Chinese and English.

⏱ 20 min
3

Burqin's night market is one of the best in northern Xinjiang. Fresh fish caught from the Irtysh grilled whole (the pikeperch is delicious), lamb skewers, Kazakh cheese, kvas (Russian fermented drink). Noisy, bright, lively atmosphere.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If you arrive early enough, walk down to the Irtysh bank for a peaceful stroll. Local fishermen cast their lines in the evening - strike up a conversation, they're curious about foreigners and often offer tea.

⏱ 45 min

Day 4

Kanas

China's deepest alpine lake in the heart of the Altai, 188 m beneath Friendship Peak
Kanas LakeWolong Bay KanasMoon Bay KanasGuanyu Pavilion Kanas
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Today you enter the sanctuary. Kanas Lake, at 1,374 m altitude and 188.5 m deep - China's deepest freshwater lake, formed 200,000 years ago by glaciers from Friendship Peak (4,374 m). The water changes color with the season and weather: turquoise in June, emerald in August, blue-gray in September. The Mongols call it 'God's palette'. First approach via Wolong Bay, where the Kanas River draws an omega loop around a dragon-shaped islet.

⏱ 90 min
2

One kilometer upstream, Moon Bay forms a perfect crescent in the valley - this is the postcard image of Xinjiang. The color literally changes hour by hour with the light. Wooden walkways let you descend to the water. Avoid midday when the light is flat.

⏱ 60 min
3

Then climb to Guanyu Pavilion, perched at 2,030 m on Camel Peak, accessible by 1,068 steps or electric shuttle. The viewpoint, built in 1987, overlooks the lake by 660 m. Panoramic view of Kanas's seven curves and, on clear days, the snow-capped Friendship Peak. Allow 2 hours for the ascent and photo stop.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Embark on a 1-hour cruise on the lake from the main dock. Boats pass near the driftwood dam - a kilometer of logs accumulated by valley winds - and run along the birch and spruce forest. Local legend speaks of a lake monster spotted in 1980, nicknamed 'the Chinese Nessie'; fishermen say it's actually giant Hucho taimen, salmon that can reach two meters.

⏱ 60 min

Late afternoon, walk along the Three Bays trail (Wolong, Moon, Fairy Bay) on 7 km of wooden walkways through the larch forest. Doable in 2 hours, little elevation gain, ideal for recovering from jet lag and altitude.

⏱ 120 min

Day 5

Kanas

China's deepest alpine lake in the heart of the Altai, 188 m beneath Friendship Peak
Kanas Lake hikingFish Watching Pavilion KanasKanas birch forest
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Second day at Kanas, the good one - the photographers' day. Wake up before 6 AM for the morning mist over Moon Bay: a cotton cloud floats at water level, pierced by the first rays. A nearly daily phenomenon in June and September, rarer in midsummer.

⏱ 90 min
2

In the afternoon, take the shuttle to Fish Watching Pavilion, different from Guanyu. This is where the Tuvas allegedly first spotted 'the monster' in 1980. Plunging view over the main bay, and walkways along the west shore to escape the crowds.

⏱ 90 min
3

If the season is right (late September-early October), explore the northern park's birch forest. The trees yellow in barely two weeks, and the ground disappears under golden leaves. September is the absolute best month for Kanas.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike in Heihu Bay valley, wilder than the tourist sections. 5 km round-trip trail through the taiga, with a chance to spot Siberian squirrels and, more rarely, elk. Ask your local guide if they'll take you - the area isn't marked.

⏱ 180 min

Tuva dinner at a farm near the park: boiled lamb with marrow bones, dried yak cheese, buttered milk tea, and throat singing. A few families open their table to visitors by reservation through your accommodation.

⏱ 120 min

Sayram Lake

Xinjiang's highest alpine lake, the Atlantic's last tear at 2,070 m

✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Sayram Lake, perched at 2,070 m, is nicknamed 'the Atlantic's last tear' - it's the easternmost point reached by moist winds from the ocean, after a 4,000 km journey. At 458 km² and 92 m deep, it's Xinjiang's largest and highest alpine lake. The color, in June-July, oscillates between sapphire and turquoise depending on light. Kazakhs say it was born from the tears of two lovers separated by the steppe.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Guozigou Bridge, just before the lake's south entrance, is an engineering feat: Xinjiang's highest cable-stayed bridge, opened in 2011, spanning a dizzying valley. Spectacular view while crossing, and also from the trail descending northward. Photographers fly their drones here (prior declaration required).

⏱ 60 min
3

The 'Genghis Khan Command Point' (Dianjiangtai) is a rocky platform where, according to Mongol tradition, the Khan reviewed his troops during the western campaign of 1219. Small commemorative stele, 360° view over the lake and peaks. Allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Bike around the lake: the 79 km circular road was completed in 2005 and hosts an international race each year. Ride over two days for athletes, or in 15-20 km sections for half-day trips. Rental at the main entrance (50 yuan/hour for an electric bike).

⏱ 240 min

Picnic on the flowery meadows of the less crowded west shore: daisies, yellow poppies, gentians in June. Kazakh yurts open to the public offer milk tea, cheese, and fresh bread.

Bayanbulak

China's second alpine grassland, refuge of the Torghut Mongols since 1771
Bayanbulak GrasslandNine Bends Kaidu RiverSwan Lake Bayanbulak
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Bayanbulak means 'rich spring' in Mongolian - and the grassland delivers on that promise. China's second-largest, at 2,500 m altitude, it extends over 23,000 km² in the heart of the Tianshan. The Torghut people, a branch of the Oirat Mongols, have lived here since 1771, after an epic migration: leaving the Volga in January, 170,000 of them marched for 8 months to Xinjiang, only 70,000 arrived, the rest dead from cold, hunger, or Cossack attacks. Emperor Qianlong granted them these lands.

⏱ 90 min
2

Swan Lake (Tianer Hu) is the world's highest swan reserve, at 2,400 m. Established in 1980, it's the only one in China. More than 6,000 whooper swans nest here from April to November, along with 120 bird species. The observation walkway is 200 m from the marsh. Bring binoculars, swans keep their distance.

⏱ 90 min
3

Balun Kure Temple is a mobile Tibetan Buddhist sanctuary, vestige of the seven portable temples of the Torghuts during their migration. It's now the only one still preserved in China. Small but fascinating, it testifies to a Buddhism-shamanism syncretism unique to the Mongols.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The Nine Meanders and Eighteen Bends of the Kaidu River are THE emblematic photo of Xinjiang. At sunset, nine reflections of the sun appear simultaneously in the river's curves - hence its nickname. The Basirik platform is the best viewpoint. Arrive 1.5 hours early to get a spot. Cult filming location for the 2019 movie 'Pegasus'.

⏱ 180 min

Horseback ride on the grassland with a Torghut herder, about 80 yuan for 1 hour. You'll ride along the river, pass flocks of Bayanbulak black-headed sheep - a breed adapted to -45°C, recognized as a 'living fossil' of local pastoralism.

⏱ 60 min

Kuqa

Former Tocharian kingdom of Kucha, Kizil caves UNESCO-listed in 2014
Kizil Caves KuqaSubashi Ruins KuqaKuqa Friday Mosque
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Major stage of this itinerary: the Kizil Buddhist caves, 60 km from Kuqa. China's oldest Buddhist complex - predating Dunhuang by several centuries - carved between the 3rd and 8th centuries in the Tocharian kingdom of Kucha. 339 known caves, 135 still in good condition, and 5,000 m² of preserved frescoes. UNESCO-listed in 2014. The style mixes Greco-Indian (Gandhara), Persian Sassanid, and Indian Buddhist influences - a unique syncretism on the Silk Road.

⏱ 180 min
2

Tocharian Buddhism spoke an Indo-European language now extinct (Tocharian B), close to Celtic and Italic - an astonishing fact 6,000 km from Europe. The frescoes of the 'Painters' Pavilion' (cave 207, carbon-14 dated 478-536) show painters signing their work in Sanskrit, extremely rare in Buddhist art.

⏱ 60 min
3

23 km northwest of Kuqa, the Subashi temple ruins testify to the same Buddhist golden age. Built in the 1st century, at its peak under the Tang (618-907), it housed up to 10,000 monks. The Chinese pilgrim Xuanzang, en route to India, stayed here for two months in 630. Today, mud-brick stupas and adobe walls stand in the desert. Melancholic atmosphere, few visitors.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Kuqa's Uyghur market on Friday is one of Xinjiang's most traditional. You'll find embroidered hats (doppa), atlas silk fabrics, musical instruments (rawap, dutar), spices, dried fruits. The atmosphere is more authentic than Kashgar (more touristy). Go between 10 AM and noon.

⏱ 120 min

Sample Kuqa polo (pilaf rice), reputed as one of Xinjiang's best: rice cooked in lamb broth with carrots, raisins, and fatty lamb pieces. Traditionally served by hand, but spoons available in tourist restaurants.

⏱ 60 min
