⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 10 and 14 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming flowers, ideal weather, moderate crowds, excellent for most regions, comfortable travel.

Fall (September-October): Perfect weather, clear skies, comfortable temperatures, peak tourist season, higher prices, autumn colors beautiful.

Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, rainy season, crowded tourist sites, highest prices, but best for Tibet and western regions.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, fewer crowds, lower prices, perfect for southern regions, harsh in north, unique winter experiences.

Regional Variations: Vast climate diversity, southern subtropical, northern continental, western plateau, plan according to regions visited.

🌍 What to discover in CN?

Ancient civilization meets modern superpower with Great Wall, forbidden cities, and cutting-edge technology. Experience diverse landscapes, incredible cuisine, and 5000-year cultural heritage. The country offers bustling megacities, pristine nature, and traditional arts. China provides overwhelming cultural richness and geographical diversity in rapidly modernizing nation.

📋 Important notes

Border permit required for Baihaba and certain Kanas zones (Russian and Kazakh borders), request 3 months in advance via an accredited agency. Kanas accessible May to October only, snow blocks passes off-season. Frequent police checks throughout Xinjiang: passport scanned several times daily. Private vehicle + driver essential, domestic flights Urumqi-Kanas and Urumqi-Yining strongly recommended to save time.

Karamay

China's oil capital ringed by yardangs sculpted by Dzungarian winds

📍 Wuerhe Ghost City Karamay📍 Karamay Petroleum Park📍 Wucai Tan Karamay
1

First major leg from Urumqi: 350 km straight north to Karamay, China's oil capital that sprang from the desert in the 1950s. You'll arrive in early afternoon and head straight to the Devil's City, 100 km northwest. The site, called Wuerhe Yardang, is a vast field of yardangs - rock formations sculpted by millennia of Dzungarian winds. The scenery is Martian, ochre, riddled with canyons. Budget at least 2.5 hours for your visit, and bring water: there's not a shred of shade.

⏱ 150 min
2

On the way back to Karamay, stop at the Five-Colored Beach (Wucai Tan) along the Irtysh River. Sedimentary layers create red, ochre, green, and gray bands according to the geology. Short but photogenic - allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Karamay Petroleum Park in town traces the history of the oil field discovered here in 1955 - China's first. You'll see restored original derricks and a didactic exhibition on the transformation of a desert corner into a city of half a million people. Not essential, but interesting if you arrive early.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Sample Uyghur cuisine at Karamay's night market: charcoal-grilled lamb skewers with cumin, dapanji (big plate chicken with potatoes and hand-pulled noodles), naan bread fresh from the tandoor. On summer evenings, the atmosphere buzzes until midnight.

⏱ 90 min

Sunset walk among the Wuerhe yardangs: the low-angle shadows reveal the rock formations' relief, and golden light transforms the desert into a movie set. Photographers set up around 7:30 PM in July-August.

⏱ 60 min

Beitun

Bingtuan pivot town on the Irtysh, founded 1954 by demobilized farmer-soldiers

📍 Beitun Reservoir📍 Beitun Bingtuan Museum📍 Irtysh River Beitun
1

Stopover in Beitun, a pivot town built by the Bingtuan - the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps, a paramilitary organization founded in 1954 to clear and colonize these lands. The small local Bingtuan museum tells this singular history: demobilized soldiers from the People's Liberation Army turned farmer-soldiers, massive irrigation projects, birth of state farms. It's a little-known page of Chinese history, and Beitun is one of the ten Bingtuan administrative divisions still active today.

⏱ 60 min
2

Beitun Reservoir, north of the city, is fed by the Irtysh. Quick stroll along the banks, opportunity to spot migratory birds in spring and autumn. Good break to stretch your legs before continuing on to Burqin.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Lunch in a Beitun workers' canteen - the surrounding state farms bring fresh produce: watermelons, Manas tomatoes, Altai lamb. Simple, cheap meal (30-50 yuan), authentic to Bingtuan culture.

Stop 3

Burqin

Gateway to northern Xinjiang on the Irtysh, China's only river flowing to the Arctic

📍 Five Colored Beach Burqin📍 Burqin Night Market📍 Irtysh River Burqin
1

Burqin marks the gateway to the north, the antechamber to Kanas. You'll arrive mid-afternoon from Beitun. Head for the Five-Colored River (Wucai Tan of Burqin), 24 km west, along the Irtysh. This is China's only river that flows into the Arctic Ocean. The clay cliffs display seven hues at sunset, and the contrast with the turquoise water is spectacular. Allow 1.5 hours on site.

⏱ 90 min
2

Make a detour via the Irtysh bridge at the edge of town to see the river flowing north - a unique geographic fact in China, as all other major rivers flow east or south. Brief 20-minute stop, explanatory signs in Chinese and English.

⏱ 20 min
3

Burqin's night market is one of the best in northern Xinjiang. Fresh fish caught from the Irtysh grilled whole (the pikeperch is delicious), lamb skewers, Kazakh cheese, kvas (Russian fermented drink). Noisy, bright, lively atmosphere.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If you arrive early enough, walk down to the Irtysh bank for a peaceful stroll. Local fishermen cast their lines in the evening - strike up a conversation, they're curious about foreigners and often offer tea.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 4

Kanas

China's deepest alpine lake in the heart of the Altai, 188 m beneath Friendship Peak

📍 Kanas Lake📍 Wolong Bay Kanas📍 Moon Bay Kanas📍 Guanyu Pavilion Kanas
1

Today you enter the sanctuary. Kanas Lake, at 1,374 m altitude and 188.5 m deep - China's deepest freshwater lake, formed 200,000 years ago by glaciers from Friendship Peak (4,374 m). The water changes color with the season and weather: turquoise in June, emerald in August, blue-gray in September. The Mongols call it 'God's palette'. First approach via Wolong Bay, where the Kanas River draws an omega loop around a dragon-shaped islet.

⏱ 90 min
2

One kilometer upstream, Moon Bay forms a perfect crescent in the valley - this is the postcard image of Xinjiang. The color literally changes hour by hour with the light. Wooden walkways let you descend to the water. Avoid midday when the light is flat.

⏱ 60 min
3

Then climb to Guanyu Pavilion, perched at 2,030 m on Camel Peak, accessible by 1,068 steps or electric shuttle. The viewpoint, built in 1987, overlooks the lake by 660 m. Panoramic view of Kanas's seven curves and, on clear days, the snow-capped Friendship Peak. Allow 2 hours for the ascent and photo stop.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Embark on a 1-hour cruise on the lake from the main dock. Boats pass near the driftwood dam - a kilometer of logs accumulated by valley winds - and run along the birch and spruce forest. Local legend speaks of a lake monster spotted in 1980, nicknamed 'the Chinese Nessie'; fishermen say it's actually giant Hucho taimen, salmon that can reach two meters.

⏱ 60 min

Late afternoon, walk along the Three Bays trail (Wolong, Moon, Fairy Bay) on 7 km of wooden walkways through the larch forest. Doable in 2 hours, little elevation gain, ideal for recovering from jet lag and altitude.

⏱ 120 min
Stop 5

Kanas

China's deepest alpine lake in the heart of the Altai, 188 m beneath Friendship Peak

📍 Kanas Lake hiking📍 Fish Watching Pavilion Kanas📍 Kanas birch forest
1

Second day at Kanas, the good one - the photographers' day. Wake up before 6 AM for the morning mist over Moon Bay: a cotton cloud floats at water level, pierced by the first rays. A nearly daily phenomenon in June and September, rarer in midsummer.

⏱ 90 min
2

In the afternoon, take the shuttle to Fish Watching Pavilion, different from Guanyu. This is where the Tuvas allegedly first spotted 'the monster' in 1980. Plunging view over the main bay, and walkways along the west shore to escape the crowds.

⏱ 90 min
3

If the season is right (late September-early October), explore the northern park's birch forest. The trees yellow in barely two weeks, and the ground disappears under golden leaves. September is the absolute best month for Kanas.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike in Heihu Bay valley, wilder than the tourist sections. 5 km round-trip trail through the taiga, with a chance to spot Siberian squirrels and, more rarely, elk. Ask your local guide if they'll take you - the area isn't marked.

⏱ 180 min

Tuva dinner at a farm near the park: boiled lamb with marrow bones, dried yak cheese, buttered milk tea, and throat singing. A few families open their table to visitors by reservation through your accommodation.

Stop 6

Hemu

Tuva village of log cabins nestled in a forest of golden birches

📍 Hemu Village📍 Hemu viewpoint📍 Tuva log cabin Hemu
1

Hemu is the other jewel of Kanas. A Tuva village of log cabins, nestled in a birch valley, 60 km from the lake. The Tuvas - a Turko-Mongol people, 2,500 individuals total in the region - claim descent from Genghis Khan's soldiers left here in 1219 during the western campaign. Their cabins, unique in China, resemble those of Siberia: steep roofs, smoke curling above chimneys. Stroll early morning through the lanes, before the buses arrive at 10 AM.

⏱ 120 min
2

Climb to the east hill viewpoint behind the village. Thirty minutes of steep ascent, but the reward is worth the effort: plunging view over all the roofs, the Hemu River winding through birches, and snowy peaks in the background. This is the iconic shot of northern Xinjiang.

⏱ 90 min
3

Visit the small Tuva ethnographic museum in the village center. Traditional costumes, throat-singing instruments (the khoomei), archival photos from the 1950s when the region was definitively attached to China. Half an hour suffices, but it provides context.

⏱ 30 min
4

Walk upstream along the Hemu River for 2-3 km. The trail follows the water, passes meadows of wildflowers in June (columbines, gentians, edelweiss), and opens onto deserted banks. Perfect picnic spot with naan bread, local cheese, and fruit bought in the village.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

If you're here in October, you'll catch the 'golden forest': the birches yellow all at once, the cabins vanish in a yellow tide. This is the season when Chinese photographers flock here - book accommodation six months in advance.

⏱ 180 min

Horseback ride with a Tuva herder, from 1 hour to half a day. Altay horses are small, sturdy, perfectly adapted to the terrain. Expect 100-150 yuan per hour. Ask if you can see the reindeer - a few families still raise them, extremely rare in China.

⏱ 90 min

Evening of Tuva throat singing in a yurt or cabin open to visitors. Khoomei produces two simultaneous notes - a deep drone, a high whistled melody. Spectacular and very ancient. Some local singers give demonstrations for 50-100 yuan. Inquire in the village.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 80 km · 2h30
Stop 7

Baihaba

China's most beautiful village according to NatGeo, perched on the Kazakh border

📍 Baihaba Village📍 Baihaba border China Kazakhstan📍 Baihaba willow trees
1

Baihaba is arguably Xinjiang's most beautiful village - National Geographic China ranked it number one in 2005. Perched on the Kazakhstan border, only 1.5 km from the line, it's off-limits to foreigners without a border permit (request 3 months in advance). A hundred Tuva and Kazakh cabins, ancient weeping willows along the river, pastures stretching forever. The atmosphere is even more pristine than Hemu - fewer tourists, few shops, horses grazing in the streets.

⏱ 180 min
2

Don't miss the thousand-year-old willow in the village center, under which elders still gather. Locals say it was planted by a Tuva ancestor seven centuries ago; dendrochronological dating suggests rather 400-500 years, which remains exceptional.

⏱ 30 min
3

Take the trail leading to the west hill for the overview. The village appears as a wooden nest wrapped in meadows, with the first Kazakh foothills as backdrop. At sunset, light gilds the roofs and the river glitters orange.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Stay with a Tuva family - a few houses open one or two rooms, with wooden bed, wool quilt, and wood stove. Spartan comfort but strong human experience. In the morning, you share salted milk tea and fried doughnuts.

⏱ 480 min

Walk early toward the border post (without crossing it, obviously): 1.5 km from the village, easy trail. You'll see the barbed wire fence marking the limit with Kazakhstan, and watchtowers on each side. Strange feeling of being at the end of Chinese territory.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 8

Altay

Birthplace of skiing worldwide, 5,000-year-old skis found in a nearby cave

📍 Altay General Park📍 Altay ski museum📍 Altay Keketuohai
1

Return toward Burqin then Altay, capital of the prefecture of the same name. The city is modern without great architectural interest, but it harbors an extraordinary fact: the birthplace of skiing worldwide. In 2005, 5,000-year-old skis were found in a cave in the neighboring mountains - predating Scandinavian Nordic skis by several millennia. Visit the small Altay Ski Museum, which presents replicas and the history of this discovery.

⏱ 90 min
2

Stroll through General Park along the Kelan River: monument to pioneers, swans in season, locals practicing tai chi in the morning. A quiet hour to catch your breath after the mountain roads.

⏱ 60 min
3

If you have time, Altay's central market is worth a visit: fragrant Hami melons, dried fruits (Turpan raisins, apricots, jujubes), Uyghur spices. Good place to stock up before the long road to the Ili valley.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Try grilled whole Irtysh River fish, a regional specialty - often pike or pikeperch, locally caught. Several popular restaurants line the south bank. Expect 80-150 yuan for a good-sized fish.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 9

Vallee Ili

China's vastest steppes and 4,000 hectares of lavender at Huocheng

📍 Huocheng lavender fields📍 Ili Valley grasslands📍 Yili horses Xinyuan
1

You're now in another world: the Ili valley, an immense fertile plain in southwestern Xinjiang. Flight Altay-Yining (2h) or drive via Urumqi for those with time. If you arrive in June, rush to Huocheng and its lavender fields - the world's second-largest production area after Provence, about 4,000 hectares cultivated by state farms since the 1960s. Flowering runs from June 10 to July 10, peaking around June 25.

⏱ 150 min
2

The steppes of Xinyuan district, farther east, are among China's vastest. Cavalcades of Ili horses (famous breed since the Han dynasty, raised for the imperial cavalry), Kazakh yurts, sheep flocks as far as the eye can see. Stop at Naraty Grassland (250 km from Yining) for half a day if the itinerary allows.

⏱ 180 min
3

Along the route, the Ili River (Yili) flows in wide meanders between poplars. It's one of the rare Chinese rivers that empties abroad - in Kazakhstan, into Lake Balkhash. The bridges make for beautiful sunset photos.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Lavender distillery in Huocheng: tour the fields and factory, sample essential oils, soaps, and infusions. Most Chinese lavender cosmetics come from here. Allow 1.5 hours for a complete guided tour.

⏱ 90 min

If you stay with a Kazakh family in a yurt (offered by several base camps around Naraty), share the evening meal: besh barmak (flat noodles with mutton), tangy fresh cheese, butter tea. Kazakhs still largely live from seasonal herding in the valley.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 10

Sayram Lake

Xinjiang's highest alpine lake, the Atlantic's last tear at 2,070 m

📍 Sayram Lake📍 Guozigou Bridge Sayram📍 Sayram Lake cycling
1

Sayram Lake, perched at 2,070 m, is nicknamed 'the Atlantic's last tear' - it's the easternmost point reached by moist winds from the ocean, after a 4,000 km journey. At 458 km² and 92 m deep, it's Xinjiang's largest and highest alpine lake. The color, in June-July, oscillates between sapphire and turquoise depending on light. Kazakhs say it was born from the tears of two lovers separated by the steppe.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Guozigou Bridge, just before the lake's south entrance, is an engineering feat: Xinjiang's highest cable-stayed bridge, opened in 2011, spanning a dizzying valley. Spectacular view while crossing, and also from the trail descending northward. Photographers fly their drones here (prior declaration required).

⏱ 60 min
3

The 'Genghis Khan Command Point' (Dianjiangtai) is a rocky platform where, according to Mongol tradition, the Khan reviewed his troops during the western campaign of 1219. Small commemorative stele, 360° view over the lake and peaks. Allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Bike around the lake: the 79 km circular road was completed in 2005 and hosts an international race each year. Ride over two days for athletes, or in 15-20 km sections for half-day trips. Rental at the main entrance (50 yuan/hour for an electric bike).

⏱ 240 min

Picnic on the flowery meadows of the less crowded west shore: daisies, yellow poppies, gentians in June. Kazakh yurts open to the public offer milk tea, cheese, and fresh bread.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 380 km · 6h

Yining

Kazakh capital of Ili, Baytulla Mosque from 1773 and Hami melon markets

📍 Baytulla Mosque Yining📍 Yining old town📍 Hanbin Park Yining
1

Yining (or Ghulja in Uyghur) is the capital of the Ili Kazakh Autonomous Prefecture. Start at the Baytulla Mosque, built in 1773 and rebuilt in 1999, one of Xinjiang's principal mosques. Carved wood architecture, white minaret, paved courtyard. Men at prayer remain discreet toward respectful visitors. Modest dress required, no photos during prayers.

⏱ 60 min
2

Yining's old Uyghur quarter is organized around the mosque. Courtyard houses, Ili poplar framework, doors painted turquoise and green. Wander at random through the lanes: children playing soccer, elders playing Uyghur chess, smell of bread emerging from the tandoor.

⏱ 90 min
3

Hanbin Park, along the Ili River, welcomes residents each evening: spontaneous Kazakh and Uyghur dances, rose ice cream vendors, carnival rides. View of illuminated bridges at nightfall. Good place to understand the city's ethnic diversity (Uyghurs, Kazakhs, Han, Hui, Sibe).

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Hami melon market near the Kazakh border (50 km, Khorgos sector) is one of China's most colorful. 15 kg watermelons, honey-flavored yellow melons, white peaches, seedless Turpan grapes. Kazakh truck drivers stop here between cross-border convoys. Allow 2 hours round trip from Yining.

⏱ 120 min

Traditional Uyghur dinner in a mehmankhana (guesthouse): laghman (pulled noodles with lamb sauté), polo (pilaf rice), samsa (flaky meat pastries), sweet milk black tea. A few old quarter addresses offer festive menus with musicians.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 12

Qiaoerma

Waystation on the Duku road, panorama over Bogda's snowy peaks

📍 Qiaoerma Tianshan📍 Duku Highway viewpoint📍 Tianshan pass
1

The Duku Road - 561 km across the Tianshan - is a stage in itself. Built by 168 soldiers sacrificed over 10 years (1974-1983), it crosses five passes over 3,000 m and traverses glaciers, spruce forests, gorges, and high plateaus. Stop at Qiaoerma, a small high-altitude relay town that serves as a bivouac point for drivers along the Duku, with its panorama over the Bogda's snowy mountains. Plan the whole day by car, with several breaks. Road closed October to May due to snow.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Tianshan Pass, the road's highest point, peaks at 3,200 m. Thin air, alpine meadows, marmots whistling as you pass. Don't linger if you feel dizzy - descending 200 m is enough to dissipate symptoms.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 13

Bayanbulak

China's second alpine grassland, refuge of the Torghut Mongols since 1771

📍 Bayanbulak Grassland📍 Nine Bends Kaidu River📍 Swan Lake Bayanbulak
1

Bayanbulak means 'rich spring' in Mongolian - and the grassland delivers on that promise. China's second-largest, at 2,500 m altitude, it extends over 23,000 km² in the heart of the Tianshan. The Torghut people, a branch of the Oirat Mongols, have lived here since 1771, after an epic migration: leaving the Volga in January, 170,000 of them marched for 8 months to Xinjiang, only 70,000 arrived, the rest dead from cold, hunger, or Cossack attacks. Emperor Qianlong granted them these lands.

⏱ 90 min
2

Swan Lake (Tianer Hu) is the world's highest swan reserve, at 2,400 m. Established in 1980, it's the only one in China. More than 6,000 whooper swans nest here from April to November, along with 120 bird species. The observation walkway is 200 m from the marsh. Bring binoculars, swans keep their distance.

⏱ 90 min
3

Balun Kure Temple is a mobile Tibetan Buddhist sanctuary, vestige of the seven portable temples of the Torghuts during their migration. It's now the only one still preserved in China. Small but fascinating, it testifies to a Buddhism-shamanism syncretism unique to the Mongols.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The Nine Meanders and Eighteen Bends of the Kaidu River are THE emblematic photo of Xinjiang. At sunset, nine reflections of the sun appear simultaneously in the river's curves - hence its nickname. The Basirik platform is the best viewpoint. Arrive 1.5 hours early to get a spot. Cult filming location for the 2019 movie 'Pegasus'.

⏱ 180 min

Horseback ride on the grassland with a Torghut herder, about 80 yuan for 1 hour. You'll ride along the river, pass flocks of Bayanbulak black-headed sheep - a breed adapted to -45°C, recognized as a 'living fossil' of local pastoralism.

⏱ 60 min
Stop 14

Bayanbulak

China's second alpine grassland, refuge of the Torghut Mongols since 1771

📍 Bayanbulak yurt camp📍 Mongolian culture Bayanbulak
1

Second day at Bayanbulak to enjoy the grassland during hours without tour buses. Wake before 6 AM to watch sunrise over the Kaidu: mist at water level, first orange rays, herds heading out to pasture. It's a different spectacle, quieter than sunset.

⏱ 90 min
2

Visit a Torghut camp off the tourist trail. The family welcomes you into the yurt - 5 meters diameter, wooden lattice, sheep felt, top opening for smoke. Salted milk tea, fresh butter, dried cheese. Conversation happens in Torghut language, translated by your guide.

⏱ 90 min
3

If the season permits, attend the Nadam Fair, annual Mongol festival held on the 4th day of the lunar calendar's 6th month (generally late June-early July). Traditional wrestling, horse races with 6-12 year old children, archery, dances. The best Torghut riders come from throughout the region.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Taste Bayanbulak lamb - a breed raised above 3,000 m altitude, fine meat, very lean. Traditional Torghut cooking: whole on embers for 4 hours, then carved at table. Several yurt camps offer this feast by reservation for 6-8 people. Expect 800-1,200 yuan for a whole sheep.

⏱ 180 min

Night in a yurt under the Milky Way. At this altitude and with zero light pollution, the sky is extraordinary. Nights stay cool even in July (5-10°C), yurts are heated with yak dung stoves. Unforgettable experience.

⏱ 480 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 280 km · 5h
Stop 15

Kuqa

Former Tocharian kingdom of Kucha, Kizil caves UNESCO-listed in 2014

📍 Kizil Caves Kuqa📍 Subashi Ruins Kuqa📍 Kuqa Friday Mosque
1

Major stage of this itinerary: the Kizil Buddhist caves, 60 km from Kuqa. China's oldest Buddhist complex - predating Dunhuang by several centuries - carved between the 3rd and 8th centuries in the Tocharian kingdom of Kucha. 339 known caves, 135 still in good condition, and 5,000 m² of preserved frescoes. UNESCO-listed in 2014. The style mixes Greco-Indian (Gandhara), Persian Sassanid, and Indian Buddhist influences - a unique syncretism on the Silk Road.

⏱ 180 min
2

Tocharian Buddhism spoke an Indo-European language now extinct (Tocharian B), close to Celtic and Italic - an astonishing fact 6,000 km from Europe. The frescoes of the 'Painters' Pavilion' (cave 207, carbon-14 dated 478-536) show painters signing their work in Sanskrit, extremely rare in Buddhist art.

⏱ 60 min
3

23 km northwest of Kuqa, the Subashi temple ruins testify to the same Buddhist golden age. Built in the 1st century, at its peak under the Tang (618-907), it housed up to 10,000 monks. The Chinese pilgrim Xuanzang, en route to India, stayed here for two months in 630. Today, mud-brick stupas and adobe walls stand in the desert. Melancholic atmosphere, few visitors.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Kuqa's Uyghur market on Friday is one of Xinjiang's most traditional. You'll find embroidered hats (doppa), atlas silk fabrics, musical instruments (rawap, dutar), spices, dried fruits. The atmosphere is more authentic than Kashgar (more touristy). Go between 10 AM and noon.

⏱ 120 min

Sample Kuqa polo (pilaf rice), reputed as one of Xinjiang's best: rice cooked in lamb broth with carrots, raisins, and fatty lamb pieces. Traditionally served by hand, but spoons available in tourist restaurants.

⏱ 60 min

If you arrive in Urumqi late morning, use the opportunity to close the loop at the Grand Bazaar - you may have passed through at the start of your trip, but seeing it with a 'post-Xinjiang' eye is a different experience.
