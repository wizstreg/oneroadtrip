📆 Best period

late June, July, August, early September

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 7 and 11 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (June-August): Midnight sun, warmest weather, lupine flowers, highland access, peak tourist season, highest prices, advance booking essential.

Winter (December-February): Northern Lights season, ice caves, winter activities, darker days, lowest prices, fewer crowds, magical atmosphere.

Shoulder Season (April-May & September-November): Aurora viewing possible, fewer crowds, moderate prices, transitional weather, excellent value, variable conditions.

Spring (March-May): Lengthening days, awakening nature, moderate temperatures, bird nesting, good weather, fewer tourists, excellent photography.

Fall (September-November): Aurora season begins, autumn colors, comfortable weather, harvest season, good value, stunning landscapes.

🌍 What to discover in IS?

Nordic island with dramatic landscapes, geothermal wonders, and unique culture. Experience Northern Lights, pristine nature, and authentic Icelandic lifestyle. The country offers natural phenomena with cultural authenticity and progressive values. Iceland provides life-changing natural experiences with cultural depth and environmental leadership.

📋 Important notes

Summer-only itinerary (late June - early September). 4x4 mandatory for days 3-5 (Highlands F-roads). Nights at Landmannalaugar are in campsites/mountain huts - no hotels, no shops. Book camping in advance. In return, it requires a 4x4 and is only accessible in summer (late June - early September). The 12-day circuit covers the same major sites but stays on paved roads with a standard vehicle.

Selfoss

gateway town at the crossroads of the Golden Circle and Highlands

★★★★☆
📍 Þingvellir National Park📍 Strokkur Geysir Haukadalur📍 Gullfoss Waterfall📍 Kerið Crater Lake
1

Golden Circle day. Start with Þingvellir: UNESCO-listed since 2004, this is where the Althing was founded in 930 - the world's oldest parliament. You're walking through the rift between the Eurasian and North American tectonic plates, which are drifting apart at 0.8 inches per year.

⏱ 60 min
2

At Haukadalur, Strokkur geyser erupts every 5-10 minutes, shooting 50-65 feet into the air. Its neighbor Geysir - the one that gave its name to the phenomenon - is now almost dormant. Position yourself downwind to avoid scalding spray.

⏱ 40 min
3

Gullfoss: the Hvítá river plunges in two tiers, 105 feet into a 230-foot-deep canyon. On clear days, a permanent rainbow forms in the spray. Take the path down to get closest to the lower falls.

⏱ 45 min
4

On your way back to Selfoss, detour to Kerið crater: a 3,000-year-old volcanic crater filled with a blue-green lake 45 feet deep. The contrast between the red crater rock and turquoise water is striking.

⏱ 25 min

Activities

In the evening, explore the small town of Selfoss, a practical supply point. If you want a more exclusive bath, the Secret Lagoon in Flúðir is a natural hot spring at 100-104°F in a rural setting - Iceland's oldest pool, open since 1891. Important: Fill up in Selfoss or Hella - these are the last gas stations before the Highlands F-roads.

Stop 3

Landmannalaugar

geothermal oasis surrounded by multicolored peaks in the wild Highlands

★★★★★
📍 Hjálparfoss Waterfall📍 Háifoss Waterfall Þjórsárdalur📍 Landmannalaugar Campsite📍 Laugahraun Lava Field
1

Head into the Highlands via Route 32 then F208 north. First stop: Hjálparfoss, a twin waterfall tumbling into a pool surrounded by basalt columns. Then Háifoss, one of Iceland's tallest waterfalls (400 ft), visible from a viewpoint right on the cliff edge - vertigo guaranteed.

⏱ 60 min
2

Arrive at Landmannalaugar by midday. This geothermal site in the heart of the Fjallabak Nature Reserve is surrounded by rhyolite mountains in mind-bending colors - yellow, green, pink, red, orange. The Laugahraun lava field, formed during a 1477 eruption, borders the campsite. This is the starting point for the famous Laugavegur trek (34 miles to Þórsmörk).

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Bathe in Landmannalaugar's natural hot spring - a hot stream mixes with a cold stream at the foot of the lava field, creating a natural pool at perfect temperature. Free and open to all. Warning: The only accommodation is the mountain hut and campsite (no hotels). Bring your own food - there are no shops here.

⏱ 60 min
Stop 4

Landmannalaugar

geothermal oasis surrounded by multicolored peaks in the wild Highlands

★★★★★
📍 Brennisteinsalda Volcano Landmannalaugar📍 Bláhnjúkur Blue Peak Landmannalaugar📍 Ljótipollur Crater Lake
1

Hiking day. The Brennisteinsalda loop ('sulphur wave') is the most popular: this mountain is considered Iceland's most colorful - layers of sulphur yellow, moss green, iron red, and rhyolite pink. The loop is about 3.7 miles and takes 2-3 hours.

⏱ 120 min
2

For the more athletic, Bláhnjúkur ('blue peak', 3,094 ft) offers 360° panoramas of the Highlands from its summit. The ascent is steep (about 1 hour) on black and blue volcanic ash - the contrast with the rhyolite mountains below is spectacular.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If you have a 4x4 and time, detour to Ljótipollur, a blood-red crater filled with intensely blue water - 20 minutes on the track. The contrast is surreal. In the evening, return to the hot springs to soothe your muscles after hiking.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 5

Kirkjubaejarklaustur

between volcanic craters and Europe's mightiest glacier

★★☆☆☆
📍 Fjaðrárgljúfur Canyon📍 Kirkjugólf Basalt Floor📍 Systrafoss Waterfall Kirkjubæjarklaustur
1

Drive south via F208 then return to the Ring Road. Stop at Fjaðrárgljúfur canyon: 330 feet deep and 1.2 miles long, carved 9,000 years ago by glacial erosion. A path runs along the rim with viewing platforms. The site had to temporarily close after a Justin Bieber music video brought too many visitors.

⏱ 45 min
2

In Kirkjubæjarklaustur (which Icelanders shorten to 'Klaustur'), visit Kirkjugólf, the 'church floor' - a flat surface of hexagonal basalt columns that looks like natural tiling. Medieval monks thought it was the floor of a vanished church. Systrafoss waterfall (82 ft) is a short walk away.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Klaustur is a good supply point - gas station, grocery store, restaurants. Take advantage because between here and Höfn, services are very scarce. If you have a robust 4x4, the Laki craters (Lakagígar) are accessible on a day trip from Klaustur - 130 craters aligned over 15.5 miles, remnants of the 1783 eruption that killed a quarter of Iceland's population and affected the global climate.

langoustine harbor facing Europe's largest ice cap

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Skaftafell Svartifoss Waterfall📍 Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon📍 Diamond Beach Jökulsárlón
1

At Skaftafell, take the trail to Svartifoss (3.4 miles round trip, 394 ft elevation gain). This 66-foot waterfall tumbles in front of an amphitheater of black basalt columns - it inspired the architect of Hallgrímskirkja church.

Jökulsárlón: this 6.9-square-mile lagoon didn't exist before 1935. At 932 feet deep (Iceland's deepest lake), it's filled with blue, white, and black icebergs made from ice over 1,000 years old. Walk along the west shore to escape the crowds.

⏱ 60 min
3

Diamond Beach, just across Route 1: wave-polished icebergs wash up on the black sand. Come at sunset for the best colors.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Operators offer zodiac tours on the lagoon to navigate between icebergs and approach the glacier front. Book ahead in summer.

langoustine harbor facing Europe's largest ice cap

📍 Höfn Harbour Iceland📍 Vatnajökull Glacier View📍 Fjallsárlón Glacier Lagoon

Morning at Höfn harbor, the capital of humar (Icelandic langoustine). The docks offer stunning views of Vatnajökull in clear weather - Europe's largest ice cap appears to tumble straight into the sea. The Vatnajökull National Park visitor center is worth a stop.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Operators offer guided glacier hikes (crampons provided). It's your chance to walk on the Vatnajökull ice, among blue crevasses and glacial moulins. Allow 3-4 hours for a full excursion.

📍 East Fjords Iceland📍 Djúpivogur Eggs Sculpture📍 Fardagafoss Waterfall Egilsstaðir

Drive along the East Fjords, one of Iceland's least visited regions. In Djúpivogur (population 450), giant stone eggs line the harbor - reproductions of eggs from 34 local bird species.

At Egilsstaðir, the hike to Fardagafoss (2.5 miles round trip, 30-minute climb) leads to a waterfall in a rocky amphitheater with valley views.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk along Lake Lagarfljót (15.5 miles long, 367 feet deep), home according to legend to the Lagarfljótsormur, a giant serpent cousin of the Loch Ness monster. In 2012, a video showing a serpentine shape went viral worldwide.

Seydisfjordur

where rainbow streets meet blue churches at the fjord's end

★★☆☆☆
📍 Hengifoss Waterfall Egilsstaðir📍 Seyðisfjörður Blue Church📍 Rainbow Street Seyðisfjörður📍 Seyðisfjörður Hiking Trails
1

Before leaving the area, detour to Hengifoss, Iceland's third-highest waterfall (420 ft). The 1.5-mile trail from the parking lot follows Lake Lagarfljót and passes Litlanesfoss first, framed by basalt columns. The red clay layers striping the cliff around Hengifoss are spectacular. Allow 1.5 hours round trip.

⏱ 90 min
2

The 16.8-mile road between Egilsstaðir and Seyðisfjörður crosses a mountain pass before plunging toward the fjord - one of Iceland's most beautiful descents. Along the way, a series of waterfalls lines the road.

⏱ 25 min
3

Seyðisfjörður is the most beautiful village in the East Fjords. At the head of the fjord, colorful houses line a rainbow-paved path leading to Bláa kirkjan, the little blue church. The village attracts artists and musicians - several galleries and workshops dot the main street. It's also the ferry arrival port from Denmark.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Several hiking trails start from the village. The trail to Gufufoss waterfall (1.2 miles) offers a plunging view of the fjord and village below. If you have the full day, the coastal trail to Skálanes (about 3 miles) crosses wild landscapes with chances of seeing seals and bird colonies.

Myvatn

volcanic wonderland of craters, fumaroles and steaming earth

★★★★☆
📍 Dettifoss Waterfall Iceland📍 Ásbyrgi Canyon Iceland📍 Grjótagjá Cave Mývatn
1

Drive north. Dettifoss first: 144 ft high, 328 ft wide, average flow of 7,063 cubic feet per second - Europe's most powerful waterfall. You hear the roar before you see it. Approach from the west bank (Route 862, paved). Opening scene of Prometheus.

⏱ 60 min
2

18.6 miles north, Ásbyrgi: a horseshoe canyon 2.2 miles long, 0.6 miles wide, with 328-foot cliffs. According to mythology, it's the hoofprint of Sleipnir, Odin's horse. A birch forest thrives at the bottom.

⏱ 45 min
3

Arrive at Mývatn: detour to Grjótagjá cave (hot water lake, Game of Thrones filming location, bathing forbidden >122°F).

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Drive around the lake (22.4 miles) to scout tomorrow's sites. The midge lake hosts 13 species of nesting ducks in summer.

Myvatn

volcanic wonderland of craters, fumaroles and steaming earth

★★★★☆
📍 Dimmuborgir Lava Formations📍 Hverfjall Crater📍 Hverir Námaskarð📍 Krafla Víti Crater📍 Mývatn Nature Baths
1

Dimmuborgir ('dark castles'): 2,300-year-old lava field, pillars, arches and caves sculpted by steam. The Kirkjan cave resembles a natural Gothic cathedral. Game of Thrones filming location.

⏱ 60 min
2

Hverfjall crater: 0.6 miles in diameter, 460 ft deep, formed 2,500 years ago. Steep climb (820 ft, 20-30 min) but exceptional 360° panorama. Rim circuit in 1 hour.

⏱ 90 min
3

Hverir (Námaskarð): geothermal field - mud pots, fumaroles, sulphur smell, ochre to red ground. You'd think you were on Mars. Free, right by Route 1. Stay on the trails - the ground is scalding.

⏱ 40 min
4

Krafla volcano: Víti crater ('hell') holds a blue-green lake formed in 1724. At Leirhnjúkur, still-steaming lava flows date from the 1975-1984 eruptions.

⏱ 55 min

Activities

Bathe at Mývatn Nature Baths (Jarðböðin): milky blue-green water between 97-104°F, cheaper and less crowded than the Blue Lagoon. Open until midnight in summer - in June, bathe under the midnight sun.

Husavik

where humpback whales breach under the midnight sun

★★★★★
📍 Húsavík Whale Museum📍 GeoSea Geothermal Sea Baths Húsavík📍 Húsavík Church
1

Húsavík is Iceland's whale-watching capital. The Whale Museum (Hvalasafnið) is Iceland's most comprehensive - it displays several complete skeletons, including an 82-foot blue whale. The Eurovision Song Contest movie (2020) with Will Ferrell was partly filmed here.

⏱ 60 min
2

Húsavík Church (Húsavíkurkirkja), built in 1907 from Norwegian timber, is one of the country's most charming with its red facade and pointed steeple facing the harbor.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Operators offer whale-watching excursions (2-3h). Skjálfandi Bay is one of the world's best spots: humpback whales, minke whales, white-beaked dolphins, and sometimes blue whales. The observation rate approaches 99% in summer.

⏱ 150 min

End the day at GeoSea baths, perched on a cliff above the harbor. The geothermal seawater at 100-104°F comes from a 6,560-foot-deep borehole. The view of the bay, mountains, and possibly whales from the pool is remarkable.

📍 Goðafoss Waterfall📍 Akureyrarkirkja Akureyri📍 Lystigarðurinn Botanical Garden📍 Akureyri Sundlaug

Goðafoss en route: 98 ft wide, 39 ft high, 'waterfall of the gods'. In the year 1000, chieftain Þorgeir allegedly threw his statues of Norse gods into it when Iceland adopted Christianity. East bank for the best view.

Akureyri (pop. 19,000): Akureyrarkirkja (same architect as Hallgrímskirkja, stained glass from Coventry), Lystigarðurinn botanical gardens (the world's northernmost, 7,000 species, free admission), heart-shaped traffic lights (2008 initiative).

Activities

Akureyri municipal swimming pool: several heated outdoor pools, slides, steam room. The quintessential Icelandic social institution - locals gather here to chat.

Departure day. If your flight is in the evening, spend the morning exploring the Reykjanes peninsula.
