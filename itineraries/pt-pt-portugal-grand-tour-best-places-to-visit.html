Portugal Grand Tour: Algarve Coast to Northern Mountain Peaks

📅 16 days📍 14 stops🚗 1297 km⏱ 18h03

Dive into the Algarve between golden coves, then follow Lagos and its sculpted cliffs. Live Lisbon to fado rhythm, head up to Porto and its azulejos, then follow the Douro and its vine terraces. End in Gerês, where waterfalls and ancient forests offer a wilder Portugal.

✨ Highlights
  • Lagos: sea caves golden cliffs
  • Lisbon: fado hills tram lines
  • Porto: azulejo wine cellars bridges
  • Gerês: wild mountains granite pools
⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 13 and 17 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Late Spring (April-June): Perfect weather conditions (18-25°C) with mild temperatures, moderate rainfall, and blooming landscapes. Ideal for exploring cities, coastal areas, and countryside with comfortable conditions and fewer crowds than summer.

Early Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures after summer heat, warm ocean waters, and stable weather patterns. Best time for beach activities combined with cultural exploration, moderate prices and pleasant conditions.

Winter (November-March): Mild temperatures (10-18°C) with increased rainfall but lowest prices and authentic experiences. Good for cultural immersion, indoor attractions, and experiencing Portuguese daily life without tourist crowds.

Summer (July-August): Peak season with hottest weather (25-35°C), highest prices, and largest crowds. Perfect for beach holidays but challenging for extensive sightseeing in inland areas due to heat intensity.

🌍 What to discover in PT?

Atlantic charm with stunning coastlines, rich history, and authentic culture. Experience traditional fado music, beautiful cities, and genuine Portuguese hospitality. The country offers cultural depth with natural beauty and culinary excellence. Portugal provides authentic European experiences with natural wonders and cultural richness.

Day 1

Tavira

moorish charm gilded churches calm lagoon

★★★☆☆
1

Discover Faro, the Algarve's capital city offering a compact historic center with charming narrow streets and cozy atmosphere. The old town features Faro Cathedral where you can climb the tower for aerial views, the Bone Chapel at Igreja do Carmo, and the excellent Municipal Museum. The Marina area and Jardim Manuel Bivar garden host weekend craft markets and provide pleasant waterfront strolling.

⏱ 75 min
2

Explore Ria Formosa Nature Park, a series of barrier islands stretching over 60 kilometers along the eastern Algarve. This protected landscape of lagoons, wetlands, saltpans, and white sand dunes creates scenery completely different from the famous cliff-backed beaches further west. Traditional fishing villages on the islands offer glimpses of authentic Algarve life largely unchanged for generations.

⏱ 75 min
3

Visit Tavira, one of the most authentic larger towns in the Algarve region built around its medieval center along the river. Don't miss the Castelo remains, the Roman Bridge featured in all Tavira postcards, and the Igreja da Misericordia with its impressive 18th-century azulejo portal. Camera Obscura inside an old water tower provides unique perspectives over this charming historic town.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Take a boat trip through Ria Formosa visiting islands like Ilha de Tavira, Culatra, and Armona to experience pristine beaches and traditional fishing communities. Sunset boat tours from Faro provide a magical way to experience the nature park as the sky transforms over the lagoons. The birdwatching opportunities here are exceptional, with the wetlands providing habitat for numerous species.

⏱ 60 min

Day 2

Albufeira

beach resort nightlife old town contrast

★★★★☆
1

Discover Albufeira, the most popular tourist resort in southern Portugal offering something for every type of traveler. From scenic coastlines and long sandy beaches to a charming old town with traditional architecture, this central Algarve hub provides excellent access to attractions throughout the region. The old fishermen's neighborhood and clifftop old town are particularly atmospheric areas to explore.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit Praia da Falésia, one of the world's most unique beaches stretching over 6 kilometers between Vilamoura and Albufeira. The impressive red sandstone cliffs change colors depending on weather and light, creating breathtaking scenery worth visiting in any season. The nicest stretch near Olhos de Agua features particularly dramatic cliff formations that photograph beautifully.

⏱ 75 min
3

Explore the picturesque Chapel of Nossa Senhora da Rocha in Porches, sitting dramatically on coastal cliffs above the ocean. While the little chapel itself is charming, the main attraction is the spectacular coastal scenery visible from paths leading westward. This popular sunset location rewards visitors with stunning views of the Algarve coastline.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Experience kayaking from St Rafael Beach in Albufeira, paddling along the beautiful coastline to discover hidden caves and secluded coves. Water sports enthusiasts will find parasailing, jet skiing, and countless other activities available throughout the summer season. The beaches around Albufeira like Praia dos Arrifes and Praia São Rafael offer some of the most scenic coastal swimming in central Algarve.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 35min
Day 3

Silves

moorish castle orange groves red walls

★★★☆☆
1

Explore Silves, the ancient capital of the Algarve usually overlooked by first-time visitors yet offering fascinating glimpses into the region's Moorish past. This typical medieval hilltop town is crowned by a partially-restored castle that once rivaled Lisbon in importance before the devastating 1755 earthquake. The Silves Cathedral and Archaeological Museum just off the main square complement the castle experience.

⏱ 75 min
2

Discover the charming village of Alte, one of the most picturesque traditional villages in the Algarve countryside where time seems to stand still. Narrow cobbled streets wind between traditional white buildings adorned with colorful flowers and some of the region's most beautiful street art. The natural springs at Fonte Grande de Alte offer refreshing swimming in crystal-clear water-an unexpected inland Algarve experience.

⏱ 75 min
3

Visit the beaches of Alvor including Praia dos Três Irmãos and tiny Praia da Prainha, interconnected via tunnels and passages through the cliffs. Walking barefoot through tunnels, under natural arches, and along narrow cliff passages reveals surprise after surprise in this spectacular coastal landscape. The cliffside restaurant at Prainha perched between the rocks offers an absolutely unique dining location.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Explore the Costa Vicentina, the less-visited western Algarve coastline offering wild, majestic scenery completely different from the popular eastern beaches. Praia da Bordeira and Praia da Cordoama are favorites among surfers, but any small road leading to the coast rewards visitors with magnificent views. This undeveloped natural coastline provides authentic Atlantic beauty without the crowds.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 50min
Day 4

Carvoeiro

sea caves fishing village golden beauty

★★★★☆
1

Explore Algar Seco in picturesque Carvoeiro, a unique coastal area featuring remarkable caves, grottos, and limestone cliffs sculpted by rain and sea over millions of years. Boardwalks provide accessible viewing, but adventurous visitors can explore pathways and staircases carved between the rocks that feel like navigating a natural maze. The most beautiful cave features two natural windows overlooking the coast, discovered by descending stairs at the car park's end.

⏱ 75 min
2

Hike the famous Seven Hanging Valleys Trail, the most popular coastal walk in the Algarve following breathtaking scenery between Praia da Marinha and Praia do Vale de Centeanes. This 5.7-kilometer route passes Benagil Cave, Benagil Beach, Carvalho Beach, and Alfazina Lighthouse among the highlights. The trail provides easy access to one of the most scenic stretches of coastline in all of Portugal.

⏱ 75 min
3

Photograph Praia da Marinha, where postcard-perfect views have made this one of the most recognizable beaches in Portugal. The clifftop paths overlooking the beach reveal the typical Algarve coastline featured in guidebooks worldwide. Walking westward from the viewpoint near the parking area reveals the most spectacular scenery along this stunning stretch of coast.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Take a boat trip to the famous Benagil Cave, the largest and best-known sea cave in the Algarve accessible only from the water. This cathedral-like cavern is so massive that several boats can enter simultaneously, with sunlight streaming through its collapsed roof. The entire coastline here is dotted with sea caves creating spectacular scenery whether explored by speedboat or kayak.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min
Day 5

Lagos

dramatic cliffs ponta piedade grottoes kayak

★★★★☆
1

Discover Lagos, one of Portugal's most charming historic towns with white-washed buildings, cobbled streets, and a cozy traditional atmosphere. The compact old town offers historic landmarks, interesting museums, and a lively local market perfect for experiencing authentic Portuguese culture. This lovely western Algarve town combines heritage with easy access to spectacular coastal scenery.

⏱ 75 min
2

Marvel at Ponta da Piedade, one of Portugal's most famous natural landmarks featuring stunning coastal rock formations, sea caves, and grottos. The breathtaking scenery includes towering cliffs accessible via coastal paths and a steep staircase descending to sea level for closer views of the grottos. This extraordinary landscape is even more magical at sunset when golden light illuminates the sculptural formations.

⏱ 75 min
3

Visit Praia do Camilo, a tiny beach accessed via steep stairs where the views from above are equally stunning as the beach itself. The beach divides into two areas connected by a short tunnel carved through the rocks, creating an incredibly scenic experience. Nearby Praia de Dona Ana is surrounded by steep colorful cliffs and crystal-clear blue water, ranking among the Algarve's most beautiful beaches.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Explore Ponta da Piedade by kayak, paddling through sea caves and along the spectacular coastline for an unforgettable perspective on these natural wonders. Small boat tours also navigate into the grottos, offering intimate access to the sculpted rock formations. The combination of golden cliffs, turquoise water, and hidden caves creates one of Europe's most photogenic coastal landscapes.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 45min
Day 6

Sagres

edge of world fortress henry navigator

★★☆☆☆
1

Arrive at Cabo de São Vicente, the southwesternmost point of continental Europe where towering cliffs surrounded by water on three sides create a mesmerizing spectacle. In the past, Europeans believed this was the end of the world, and standing here watching massive waves crash against the rocks below, that feeling remains palpable. The lighthouse and fortress of Santo António do Beliche nearby offer equally stunning coastal panoramas.

⏱ 75 min
2

Explore Ponta de Sagres, a rugged windswept headland steeped in history where Portugal's first great explorers began their voyages in search of the New World. Visit the 16th-century Fortaleza de Sagres encompassing an old fort with museum, the Igreja de Nossa Senhora da Graça, and a lighthouse with unique sound installation. Walk around the entire cape following paths offering breathtaking views of the endless Atlantic.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Experience world-class surfing conditions around Sagres, where consistent waves and strong winds attract water sports enthusiasts from around the globe. The beaches in this area offer some of the best surfing in Portugal, with numerous surf schools providing lessons for all levels. The rugged Atlantic coastline here feels wild and untamed compared to the sheltered beaches further east.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 40min
Day 7

Lisbon

seven hills fado trams belem tower

★★★★★
1

Jour 1: Begin your Portuguese adventure at Praça do Comércio, Lisbon's grandest square opening dramatically onto the Tagus River. This monumental waterfront plaza rebuilt after the 1755 earthquake served as the ceremonial gateway to Portugal's capital. The elegant arcaded buildings and triumphal arch create one of Europe's most impressive urban spaces.

⏱ 75 min
2

Jour 1: Stroll along Rua Augusta to the historic Baixa district, climbing the Arco da Rua Augusta for sweeping views over downtown and the glittering Tagus. Ascend in the Elevador de Santa Justa, an extraordinary neo-Gothic iron lift designed by a student of Gustave Eiffel. Explore the haunting ruins of Convento do Carmo, left roofless since the catastrophic 1755 earthquake.

⏱ 75 min
3

Jour 2: Lose yourself in Alfama's labyrinthine streets, Lisbon's oldest neighborhood that survived the great earthquake intact. Narrow alleyways wind between ancient houses adorned with colorful azulejo tiles while melancholic fado drifts from hidden taverns. Conquer the heights of Castelo de São Jorge, the hilltop fortress offering the most spectacular panoramic views in the capital.

⏱ 75 min
4

Jour 2: Visit the Sé Cathedral, Lisbon's oldest church built on a former mosque site in the 12th century. Journey to Belém where Portugal's Age of Discovery left magnificent monuments along the Tagus. Marvel at the Mosteiro dos Jerónimos, an extraordinary Manueline masterpiece commemorating Vasco da Gama's voyage to India.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Jour 1: Wander through the elegant Chiado district, Lisbon's traditional quarter of intellectuals and literary cafés where Fernando Pessoa once held court. This sophisticated neighborhood bridges the lower city with bohemian Bairro Alto offering excellent shopping and charming squares.

⏱ 60 min

Jour 2: Stand before the iconic Torre de Belém, the fortified tower that has become Lisbon's definitive symbol. Photograph the Padrão dos Descobrimentos celebrating Portuguese explorers. Ride the legendary Tram 28 rattling through picturesque neighborhoods on its winding route across the city.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 32 km · 38min
Day 8

Cascais

coastal elegance surf beaches seaside sophistication

★★★★★
1

Journey to Sintra, a UNESCO World Heritage cultural landscape where romantic palaces rise from forested hillsides. Explore the Palácio Nacional with its distinctive conical kitchen chimneys and remarkable azulejo-covered interiors. Ascend to the flamboyant Palácio da Pena, a 19th-century fantasy combining Gothic, Manueline, Moorish, and Renaissance elements in an explosion of color.

⏱ 75 min
2

Stand at Cabo da Roca, the westernmost point of continental Europe where dramatic cliffs plunge into the Atlantic beneath a historic lighthouse. This windswept headland marks where Europe meets the ocean, creating a profound sense of standing at the edge of the world.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Conclude in elegant Cascais, a beach resort attracting aristocrats since Portuguese royalty made it their summer playground. The charming old town features cobbled streets, whitewashed buildings, and a picturesque fishing harbor still in use. Sandy beaches and relaxed atmosphere provide the perfect transition before heading south.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 280 km · 3h
Day 9

Porto

ribeira rabelo boats port wine cellars

★★★★★
1

Arrive in Porto, Portugal's vibrant northern capital where the UNESCO World Heritage Ribeira district cascades down hillsides to the Douro River. This atmospheric city reveals layers of history through medieval streets, baroque churches, and iconic azulejo-covered buildings. Porto serves as the gateway to magnificent wine country and wild landscapes of the north.

⏱ 75 min
2

Cross the iconic Dom Luís I Bridge, the double-deck iron arch spanning the Douro that has become Porto's most recognizable landmark. The upper level offers spectacular views over the historic center and the port wine cellars of Vila Nova de Gaia opposite. This engineering marvel connects the old city with centuries of winemaking tradition.

⏱ 75 min
3

Stroll from the Dom Luís I Bridge along the Douro to the Felgueiras Lighthouse at Foz, where the river meets the Atlantic. This scenic waterfront walk passes historic neighborhoods, riverside promenades, and traditional fishing communities. The lighthouse marks the dramatic meeting point of river and sea.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Experience surfing at Matosinhos beach just outside Porto, an excellent destination with gentle waves and sandy bottom perfect for beginners. Local surf schools offer lessons on this accessible beach break with consistent Atlantic swells. Alternatively, paddle along the Douro by kayak or SUP for unique perspectives on the city from the water.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 65 km · 1h
Day 10

Arouca

suspension bridge paiva walkways mountain adventure

★★☆☆☆
1

Explore the Arouca UNESCO Global Geopark, renowned for extraordinary geological heritage including the famous Giant Trilobites of Canelas. This territory surrounded by the Freita, Montemuro, and Arada mountain ranges represents millions of years of Earth's history. The geopark offers adventure activities amid dramatic natural scenery.

⏱ 75 min
2

Walk the Passadiços do Paiva, a spectacular 8-kilometer wooden boardwalk zigzagging along the Paiva River gorge. This engineering marvel passes thundering waterfalls, sheer cliffs, and pristine swimming holes carved into bedrock. The walkways provide access to one of Portugal's most dramatic river landscapes.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Cross the 516 Arouca, one of the longest pedestrian suspension bridges in the world stretching 516 meters at 175 meters height. This thrilling walkway offers breathtaking views of the valley far below and surrounding mountains. White water rafting, kayaking, and canyoning on the Paiva River provide additional adventure options.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 55min
Day 11

Sao Pedro Do Sul

thermal springs roman baths healing waters

★★★☆☆
1

Discover São Pedro do Sul, known for picturesque scenery of thick green forests, crystal-clear rivers, and charming villages inhabited since Roman times. Natural thermal baths used for healing since antiquity offer relaxation with sulfur-rich mineral waters. The sweet-scented eucalyptus forests and peaceful atmosphere make this region ideal for nature lovers.

⏱ 75 min
2

Drive the thrilling Estrada do Portal do Inferno (Road to Hell's Gate), a dramatic mountain road with steep gradients and vertical cliff edges. This spectacular route passes through northern Portugal's most dramatic highland scenery with viewpoints offering sweeping vistas. The narrow winding road demands attention but rewards with unforgettable landscapes.

⏱ 75 min
3

Ascend Serra de São Macário for some of the finest panoramic views in northern Portugal, accessible by car to the summit. The winding drive climbs through dramatic landscapes to sweeping vistas extending across valleys and distant mountain ranges. Visit the isolated village of Aldeia da Pena where goats roam cobbled streets and stone houses seem grown from the landscape.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Hike through the forests to discover hidden waterfalls and scenic viewpoints along networks of paths winding through fragrant woodland. The trails follow rushing streams and climb to panoramic ridgelines offering perfect opportunities to experience natural beauty in solitude. Sunset from Serra de São Macário creates magical moments with landscapes bathed in golden light.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 80 km · 1h30
Day 12

Pinhao

douro heart terraced vines wine estates

★★★★☆
1

Enter the Douro Valley, a UNESCO World Heritage landscape of steeply terraced vineyards cascading to one of Europe's most scenic rivers. This oldest demarcated wine region in the world has produced port wine for centuries using traditional methods. The dramatic scenery of vine-covered slopes and whitewashed quintas creates Portugal's most iconic landscape.

⏱ 75 min
2

Explore the Alto Douro Wine Region on foot, hiking through working vineyards and peaceful villages perched on hillsides. Walking trails wind between terraced slopes cultivated since Roman times, passing ancient olive groves and almond orchards. Natural beauty, agricultural heritage, and warm hospitality create unforgettable experiences.

⏱ 75 min
3

Travel along the historic Douro railway line opened in 1887, considered one of the world's most scenic train journeys. The tracks follow the river through tunnels and over bridges spanning tributary valleys. This engineering marvel provides a relaxing way to absorb magnificent valley scenery.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Cycle through vineyard-covered hillsides and traditional villages along riverside trails and mountain climbs-e-bikes highly recommended for the steep terrain. Paddle the slow-flowing Douro by kayak, gliding beneath cliffs and past terraced vineyards reflected in calm waters. The river perspective reveals the full grandeur of this UNESCO-protected landscape.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 140 km · 2h20
Day 13

Geres

national park granite peaks wild swimming

★★★☆☆
1

Enter Peneda-Gerês National Park, Portugal's only national park protecting a wilderness of mountains, ancient forests, and cascading waterfalls. The park encompasses the Peneda, Soajo, Amarela, and Gerês mountain ranges reaching 1,545 meters at their highest point. This remarkable landscape harbors diverse wildlife in pristine environments earning Portugal's highest protection level.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit Soajo, a traditional mountain village known for its unique espigueiros-historic stone granaries raised on granite pillars creating one of the park's most photographed scenes. Stone houses and ancient churches reflect enduring traditions of this remote community. The Mourela Plateau rising to 1,000 meters offers exceptional birdwatching in heather and grassland habitat.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Hike the trails of Peneda-Gerês including the Grande Rota traversing spectacular mountain scenery with options from gentle walks to challenging treks. Seek adventure at waterfalls where canyoning and cliff jumping provide adrenaline-fueled experiences in dramatic settings. Relax in the natural thermal waters of Caldas do Gerês, a spa village famous for hot springs since Roman times.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 1h20
Day 14

Viana Do Castelo

lima estuary hilltop sanctuary pilgrimage views

★★★★☆
1

Explore the Minho region, Portugal's greenest area where the River Minho forms a natural border with Spain. The Lima Valley stretches from Peneda-Gerês to the Atlantic at Viana do Castelo through a corridor of lush landscapes. This peaceful region rewards visitors with authentic culture, excellent hiking, and refreshing Vinho Verde wines.

⏱ 75 min
2

Discover the Costa Verde stretching along the Atlantic with quiet unspoilt beaches and traditional fishing villages offering authentic seaside experiences. Unlike southern resorts, this coastline preserves fishing traditions and creates landscapes quite different from famous Algarve beaches. The dramatic meeting of green hills and Atlantic surf defines this northern seaboard.

⏱ 75 min
3

Visit Viana do Castelo, an elegant port town with rich maritime heritage dating to the Age of Discovery. The historic center features remarkable Manueline architecture, traditional markets, and the spectacular hilltop Santa Luzia sanctuary offering panoramic coastal views. This cultured city serves as the Minho's gateway to the sea.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Walk the Rio Minho Trail following the river along an old railway line through tranquil riverside landscapes and traditional villages. Canoe or kayak through lush valleys where calm waters wind past historic towns-excellent conditions for families and beginners. Sample Vinho Verde, the light slightly sparkling local wine perfectly refreshing after outdoor activities.

⏱ 60 min
