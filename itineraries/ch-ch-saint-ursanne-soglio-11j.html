Saint Ursanne

★★★☆☆
1

Saint-Ursanne takes its name from the Irish monk Ursicinus, companion of Saint Columban, who settled around the year 600 in a cave overlooking the Doubs to live as a hermit. He died there around 620. A monastery grew around his tomb, then a settlement that preserves its medieval character almost intact today. You enter the town through three monumental gates: the Porte Saint-Pierre to the east, the Porte Saint-Paul to the west (rebuilt in 1664), and the Porte Saint-Jean to the south, which serves as the bridgehead over the Doubs. Bourgeois houses from the 14th to 16th centuries line the narrow lanes. The village has been a member of 'Switzerland's Most Beautiful Villages' association since 2017.

⏱ 60 min
2

The collegiate church, built in the late 12th century, blends late Burgundian Romanesque with early Gothic. The choir and crypt were completed before 1210, the vaults of the main nave between 1259 and 1307. The south portal, dating from the second half of the 12th century, is the most important Romanesque portal in Burgundian style in Switzerland: its tympanum depicts Christ surrounded by the apostles Peter and Paul and seven angels. In the side niches, the Virgin and Child and Saint Ursicinus. The capitals of the columns feature the four evangelists, monsters, sirens, and a wolf going to school. The crypt houses Saint Ursicinus's sarcophagus, made of stone, dating from the Gallo-Roman era. The saint's bust-reliquary, created in 1519, is preserved in the cloister.

⏱ 45 min
3

The cloister, flanking the north side of the collegiate church, was renovated several times. Its verdant inner courtyard is surrounded by a gallery of ogival arcades from the 14th century. Excavations conducted between 1965 and 1974 uncovered some fifty monolithic sarcophagi dating from the 7th to 9th centuries—the most significant discovery of this type in the Swiss Jura.

⏱ 30 min
4

Climb the roughly 190 stone steps north of the collegiate church to reach the hermitage. The entrance door, in late Renaissance style, bears a pediment dated 1688. Inside the enclosed cave stands a life-size statue of the sleeping hermit, accompanied by a young bear—his legendary companion. The adjoining chapel, in post-Gothic style, was rebuilt in 1621. From the hermitage, a passage through the rock opens onto a panoramic view of the Doubs valley.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Cross the Pont Saint-Jean, built in stone in 1728 by master mason Brunet. It replaced a wooden bridge from the 1670s. Its four arches span the Doubs, and a statue of Saint John Nepomuk, patron saint of bridges, watches over the crossing. From the bridge, the view of the medieval town, its rooftops, and the cliff crowned by the ruins of the 12th-century castle composes Saint-Ursanne's most iconic image.

⏱ 30 min

Walk along the Doubs from the Pont Saint-Jean. The river winds through the Clos du Doubs between wooded hills and limestone cliffs. The path follows the riverbank for several kilometers through preserved landscape, prized by fishermen for its trout.

⏱ 60 min

Thun

★★★☆☆
1

Thun Castle (Schloss Thun), built around 1190 by the Dukes of Zähringen, dominates the town from atop the Schlossberg. Its Knights' Hall (Rittersaal), measuring 30 by 15 meters, is one of the largest medieval castle halls in Switzerland. Four corner towers from the 12th century frame the Romanesque keep. The historical museum it houses displays weapons, ceramics, and a 15th-century tapestry.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Obere Hauptgasse, Thun's upper main street, features a unique characteristic in Switzerland: raised sidewalks (Hochtrottoirs) that allow circulation on two levels—shops occupy the ground floor at street level, while pedestrians walk on the roofs of these businesses, accessing the stores above. This medieval system is preserved for several hundred meters.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Stadtkirche, on the Schlossberg, is flanked by an octagonal tower erected around 1330. The current nave, in late Baroque style, dates from 1738. Paintings from 1430 decorate the tower hall. From the church forecourt, the panorama embraces the rooftops of Thun, the Aare flowing through the town, the lake, and the peaks of the Bernese Oberland—Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau—in the background.

⏱ 30 min
4

The Rathausplatz (Town Hall Square) is dominated by the Rathaus, built between 1500 and 1520 in late Gothic style. Its facade bears sculptures representing figures of justice and local family coats of arms. Around the square, well-preserved traditional Bernese houses shelter shops and cafes.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Walk along the Aare from the lake to the historic center: this 2 km stroll follows the emerald-green river, passes under covered bridges, and emerges at Rathausplatz. An old wooden bridge dating from 1726 spans the Aare between the two banks of the old town.

⏱ 45 min

Lauterbrunnen

★★★☆☆
1

The Lauterbrunnen Valley, a deep U-shaped glacial valley bordered by nearly 300-meter vertical cliffs, contains 72 waterfalls over 8 kilometers. In 1911, the young J.R.R. Tolkien, then 19 years old, traveled through this valley during a trip to Switzerland—he drew inspiration here for the landscapes of Middle-earth and notably Rivendell in 'The Lord of the Rings.'

⏱ 30 min
2

The Staubbach Falls, at the entrance to the village, drops in free fall for 297 meters—Switzerland's highest free-falling waterfall. Its name ('dust stream') comes from the thermal updraft phenomenon that disperses the water into mist before it reaches the ground. In 1779, Goethe, fascinated by the spectacle, composed his poem 'Gesang der Geister über den Wassern' (Song of the Spirits over the Waters), helping to make the valley famous throughout Europe. In summer, an arranged gallery allows you to walk behind the falls.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Trümmelbach Falls, 3 km from the village, constitute Europe's largest underground waterfalls. Ten cascades fed by meltwater from the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau glaciers thunder inside the mountain, accessible since 1877 by elevator (100 m ascent), then a network of 600 meters of galleries, tunnels, and platforms spread over 140 meters of elevation gain. The flow can reach 20,000 liters per second, carrying more than 20,000 tons of gravel per year.

⏱ 90 min
4

The Mürrenbachfall, toward Stechelberg, plunges 417 meters: it's Switzerland's highest waterfall (in multiple tiers). Less known than Staubbach because partially hidden between rocks, it's the most spectacular in spring after snowmelt.

⏱ 30 min
5

Lauterbrunnen's church, in Reformed style, stands in the village center facing the Staubbach Falls. The old wooden farms and chalets along the main street, some dating from the 18th century, preserve the traditional alpine village appearance.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Valley floor hike from Lauterbrunnen to Stechelberg (8 km, nearly flat) along the Weisse Lütschine: this family-friendly trail passes several major waterfalls and offers immersion in the glacial valley. Information panels along the way.

⏱ 150 min

Grindelwald

★★★★☆
1

The village of Grindelwald, mentioned as early as 1146, occupies a verdant hollow surrounded by the Eiger (3,967 m), the Mönch, and the Wetterhorn. Nicknamed the 'glacier village,' it welcomed its first tourists—mostly English—from the late 18th century, attracted by glaciers that then descended to the valley floor. The first hotel opened in 1820, and the first carriage road followed in 1860.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Gletscherschlucht (glacier gorge) opens between 300-meter-high rock walls, carved over 250 million years. A network of walkways extends 870 meters into the gorge, where the Lütschine river roars below. The Spiderweb net, 170 m² stretched above the canyon, allows you to cross the void. The lower glacier that sculpted it has now disappeared, but meltwater continues to shape the rock.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Eiger's north face, 1,800 meters of nearly vertical wall, was long nicknamed 'Mordwand' (murder wall). The first attempts in 1935 and 1936 cost the lives of all engaged climbers. In 1936, the drama of the Kurz-Hinterstoisser rope team left its mark: trapped 15 meters from rescuers, a knot jammed in the carabiner, Toni Kurz spoke his last words: 'Ich kann nicht mehr' (I can't go on). The first successful ascent took place on July 24, 1938, by the Austro-German rope team Heckmair, Vörg, Harrer, and Kasparek.

⏱ 45 min
4

Local curiosity: the Martinsloch (Martin's hole), a natural window in the rock at the eastern end of the Eiger, lets sunlight pass through to Grindelwald's church on November 27-28 and January 13-16. This optical phenomenon, observable from the village, attracts curious visitors each year.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Stroll through Grindelwald's old village: traditional wooden chalets, some centuries old, line the lanes below the church. The contrast between these low blackened larch constructions and the mass of the Eiger rising just behind composes one of the most striking images in the Bernese Oberland.

⏱ 45 min

Lucerne

★★★★☆
1

The Kapellbrücke (Chapel Bridge), built around 1333, crosses the Reuss diagonally over 205 meters. Originally conceived as part of the city's fortifications, it bore 158 triangular painted panels starting in 1614, illustrating Swiss history and the lives of patron saints Leodegar and Maurice. The fire of August 18, 1993, probably caused by a cigarette butt, destroyed two-thirds of the structure and 86 of the 111 remaining panels. Rebuilt in eight months for 3.4 million francs, the bridge reopened on April 14, 1994. About 13,800 people cross it daily.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Wasserturm (Water Tower), octagonal in shape, predates the bridge: built after 1262, it served in turn as a prison, torture chamber, treasury, and municipal archives over its 700-year existence.

⏱ 20 min
3

The Löwendenkmal (Lion Monument), carved into the wall of a former sandstone quarry, measures 10 meters long by 6 meters high. Designed by Danish sculptor Bertel Thorvaldsen and executed between 1820 and 1821, it commemorates the approximately 760 Swiss Guards killed on August 10, 1792, defending the Tuileries in Paris during the French Revolution. The Latin inscription 'Helvetiorum Fidei ac Virtuti' pays tribute to their loyalty. Mark Twain described it as 'the saddest and most moving piece of stone in the world.' About 1.4 million visitors come each year to pay their respects before the dying lion.

⏱ 30 min
4

The Spreuerbrücke (Mill Bridge), Lucerne's second covered bridge, built in 1408, houses a cycle of 67 'Dance of Death' paintings by Kaspar Meglinger between 1626 and 1635. Unlike the Kapellbrücke, these panels survived intact.

⏱ 30 min
5

The Museggmauer, medieval ramparts from the 13th century, preserves nine of the original thirty towers. Four are open to the public. The Zytturm (Clock Tower) has the privilege of running one minute ahead of all other clocks in the city—a right granted since the 15th century.

⏱ 45 min
6

The Jesuit Church (Jesuitenkirche), consecrated in 1677, is the first major sacred Baroque building constructed in Switzerland. Its single nave in Austrian Baroque style, adorned with stucco and gilding, contrasts with the austere exterior.

⏱ 30 min
7

The Glacier Garden (Gletschergarten), discovered in 1872 during construction of a cellar, displays glacial potholes carved about 20 million years ago, as well as a fossilized palm leaf relief proving the region enjoyed a subtropical climate. A mirror maze from 1896, inspired by the Alhambra, completes the visit.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk along the quays from the Seebrücke to the Hofkirche (St. Leodegar's Church), a Renaissance basilica built in 1633-1639 on the foundations of an 8th-century Benedictine monastery. Its organ with 4,950 pipes, installed in 1648, is one of the oldest in Switzerland still in operation.

⏱ 60 min

Wander through the lanes of the old town (Altstadt): facades painted with frescoes tell the story of guilds and merchant families. The squares Weinmarkt, Hirschenplatz, and Kornmarkt preserve Renaissance fountains and houses with carved bay windows.

⏱ 75 min

Bremgarten

★★☆☆☆
1

Bremgarten is built within a loop of the Reuss, giving it a very distinctive layout. The upper town stands on a rocky spur at the tightest point of the meander, while the lower town runs along the river below. Founded after 1230 in the shadow of a tower mentioned as early as 1238, it obtained market rights in 1258 from King Rudolf I. Its name comes from Middle High German 'Breme,' meaning 'riverbanks.' Bremgarten belonged to the Habsburg sphere of influence before being placed under Confederate authority during the conquest of Aargau in 1415.

⏱ 60 min
2

Cross the covered wooden bridge spanning the Reuss. This 16th-century bridge, protected by two oratories including one dedicated to Saint John Nepomuk, patron saint of bridges, was once the only bridge between Lucerne and Mellingen. From the bridge, the view of the colorful facades of the lower town plunging into the Reuss is Bremgarten's most famous postcard image.

⏱ 30 min
3

The old town, entirely pedestrian, preserves its ramparts, upper gate (Obertor), and cobbled lanes of the Marktgasse. The parish church of Saint Nicholas, of medieval origin, dominates the upper town. Reformer Heinrich Bullinger, Zwingli's successor in Zurich, lived in Bremgarten before distinguishing himself. In the lower town, the former convent of Saint Clare, founded in 1406, testifies to medieval monastic life—the nuns followed the rule of the Franciscan Third Order.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk along the Reuss from the covered bridge upstream. The path follows the river through a landscape of floodplain forests and meanders. The Flachsee, a nearby protected natural area, shelters rich birdlife.

Ascona

★★★☆☆
1

Ascona is a former fishing village on the northern shore of Lake Maggiore, at 196 meters elevation—Switzerland's lowest point. The borgo (historic center) preserves its narrow lanes, ochre and pink-hued houses, and loggias facing the lake. The lakeside promenade (Lungolago), lined with terraces and plane trees, offers views of the Brissago Islands and Ticino mountains.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Collegio Papio, built in 1585 at the instigation of Charles Borromeo, owes its name to Bartolomeo Papio, a villager from Ascona who bequeathed his fortune to found a college. Its Renaissance cloister, with two levels of arcades, is one of Ticino's most harmonious.

⏱ 30 min
3

Monte Verità (332 m), the hill overlooking Ascona, was the birthplace of one of Europe's first alternative communities. In 1900, a group of seven pioneers led by Henri Oedenkoven, a wealthy Belgian heir, and pianist Ida Hofmann, purchased the hill then called Monte Monescia and renamed it the 'hill of Truth.' The colony practiced strict vegetarianism, nudism, and rejected private property. It later attracted personalities such as dancer Rudolf Laban, writers Hermann Hesse and Erich Maria Remarque, psychiatrist Carl Gustav Jung, and painter Paul Klee. In 1926, Baron Eduard von der Heydt purchased the site and built a Bauhaus-style hotel. The Casa Anatta museum traces this history, and the park preserves a tea garden and Japanese tea house.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Wander through Ascona's borgo: the pedestrian lanes of the historic center open onto small squares adorned with fountains. The church of Saints Peter and Paul, remodeled in the 16th century, preserves a painting attributed to Giovanni Serodine. The harbor, with its colorful boats, sets the Mediterranean tone of the village.

Morcote

★★★☆☆
1

Morcote, nicknamed 'the pearl of Ceresio,' is a former fishing village clinging to a hillside on the southern shore of Lake Lugano. Elected 'Switzerland's Most Beautiful Village' in 2016, it preserves its arcades, patrician houses with frescoed facades, and subtropical vegetation. The Palazzo Paleari, on the quays, displays a beautiful decorated facade and a Captain's Tower (Torre del Capitano) adorned with frescoes and a twin window.

⏱ 45 min
2

Climb the 404 steps of the monumental Way of the Cross leading to the church of Santa Maria del Sasso. Built in several stages between 1470 and 1758, it blends Renaissance and Baroque styles. Inside, frescoes from different periods, an illusionistic trompe-l'oeil dome, and a 17th-century organ. The adjacent octagonal chapel, dedicated to Saint Anthony of Padua, displays stucco work and frescoes by local artists. The terraced monumental cemetery, just beside it, houses tombstones and chapels of great artistic value, with a sweeping view over the lake.

⏱ 75 min
3

Parco Scherrer, laid out in the 1930s by a St. Gallen textile merchant, combines botanical rarities (cedars, Mexican pines, camphor trees, eucalyptus, palms, bamboos) and architectural reproductions from different countries and eras. A garden where you move from a Greek temple to an oriental pavilion in a few steps.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stroll along Morcote's quays, between the arcades of former fishermen's houses and the small harbor. The cobbled lanes zigzagging up toward the church pass through covered passages and run alongside seigneurial dwellings whose walls retain traces of frescoes.

Chur

★★★☆☆
1

Chur is Switzerland's oldest town, with over 5,000 years of attested human occupation and traces of hunter-gatherers dating back 13,000 years. The Romans established the camp of Curia Raetorum here, and the town became an important episcopal seat from the 5th century. The old town, entirely pedestrian, is one of the best preserved in Switzerland. Red signs guide visitors through its winding lanes, fountain-adorned squares, and painted-facade houses.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Cathedral of the Assumption, built between 1154 and 1272, dominates the episcopal court perched above the old town. The main portal is flanked by six slender columns linked by colored arches. Inside, the late Gothic high altar, created by Jakob Russ between 1486 and 1492, gleams with gilding—it's one of the masterpieces of Swabian sculpture. Romanesque capitals, carved choir stalls, and the arched window, the largest medieval window in Graubünden, complete the visit. The site has housed a religious building since the 5th century.

⏱ 45 min
3

The episcopal court (Bischöflicher Hof), established on a rocky spur occupied since Roman times, forms a fortified ensemble with its canons' houses and gate tower. The adjacent church of St. Lucius preserves a circular Carolingian crypt from the first half of the 8th century and a late Gothic carved altar (around 1520).

⏱ 30 min
4

Descend toward Obere Gasse, the main pedestrian street, and Arcas Platz, probably the old town's most beautiful square, surrounded by painted historic houses. The church of St. Martin, rebuilt between 1470 and 1492, stands nearby. The great fire of 1464 destroyed much of the town, forcing reconstruction of the town hall and numerous buildings—their tilted walls still testify to their antiquity.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb to the viewpoint above the episcopal court, where the panorama embraces Chur, vineyards in the foreground, and the Calanda massif mountains in the background. The Bündner Kunstmuseum (Graubünden Art Museum) presents a collection of Grisons works, including pieces by Alberto Giacometti.

Soglio

★★★☆☆
1

Soglio, perched on a natural terrace at 1,090 meters elevation in the Val Bregaglia, faces the needles of Sciora, Pizzo Badile (3,308 m), and the Bondasca glacier. Painter Giovanni Segantini, who spent several winters here in the late 19th century, exclaimed: 'Soglio è la soglia del Paradiso!' (Soglio is the threshold of paradise). Mentioned as early as 1219, the village preserves a labyrinth of cobbled lanes, granite houses, and old stables that local architect Armando Ruinelli has skillfully rehabilitated, blending contemporary restraint with tradition.

⏱ 60 min
2

The central square is dominated by the palaces of the von Salis family, present in Soglio since the 13th century. The oldest dates from the 14th century. Casa Battista (1701), transformed into a hotel since 1876 under the name Palazzo Salis, presents a Baroque facade where heavy granite ornaments stand out against the white facade. Behind, a Baroque garden aligns boxwood hedges, a rose garden, tree peonies, and two sequoias planted in the 19th century. Poet Rainer Maria Rilke stayed here, convinced he had found the 'Promised Land.'

⏱ 45 min
3

The evangelical church of San Lorenzo, rebuilt in the 18th century in Baroque style after a fire, anchors the village on the cemetery side. This balcony cemetery, facing the peaks of Sciora, Cengalo, and Badile, is both a place of contemplation and a striking viewpoint over the Bondasca glacier.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk down from Soglio to Castasegna (last village before the Italian border) through the chestnut groves. Chestnuts were introduced by the Romans and have been cultivated throughout the Val Bregaglia ever since. A nature trail explains the diversity of varieties and cultivation techniques. The walk takes about 1.5 hours downhill, with continuous views over the valley.

⏱ 90 min

Panoramic hike on the Sentiero Panoramico Val Bregaglia, an old high-altitude path that traverses the valley's northern flank. The trail passes between mixed forests, granite slabs, and viewpoints over the Bondasca ridges. Allow a full day from Maloja to Soglio.

⏱ 360 min
