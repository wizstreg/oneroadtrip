Auckland

★★★★☆
1

Mount Eden (Maungawhau) is one of Auckland's 53 volcanic cones - its crater measures 50 metres deep and it peaks at 196 metres. The view from the summit takes in Auckland's skyline on one side, the Hauraki Gulf on the other. It's a sacred site to the Māori of the Ngāti Whātua tribe: stick to the marked paths around the crater rim.

⏱ 75 min
2

Viaduct Basin is the former industrial harbour converted into a lively waterfront after the 2000 America's Cup. Walk along the wharves to see racing yachts moored up, then head towards Wynyard Quarter to watch the Urban Loft - the rotating footbridge that swings open to let boats through. A relaxed introduction to the city without breaking a sweat.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Arrival day: shake off the jet lag with a gentle wander through the Ponsonby neighbourhood, a Victorian shopping street 10 minutes from downtown. The cafés stay open late and the walking is flat - perfect for recovering from a long flight.

⏱ 90 min

Hahei

★★★★☆
1

Cathedral Cove (Te Whanganui-A-Hei) is reached on foot from the Grange Road car park - allow 35 minutes' walk each way on a bush trail that opens onto a white volcanic rock archway 10 metres high. The beach below is sheltered from the wind and the water is a milky blue-green. In summer, the track is accessible only on foot or by shuttle from Hahei - no cars allowed all the way.

⏱ 120 min
2

Hot Water Beach is an ordinary stretch of sand except for two hours either side of low tide: hot springs at 64°C bubble up straight through the sand. Local operators rent spades to dig your own beach pool. Check the low tide times before leaving Auckland - this is the one thing not to miss.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Sea kayaking from Hahei Beach to Cathedral Cove: roughly 3 km one way, passing sea caves and several rock arches. Operators offer half-day rentals from Hahei Beach. It's the best way to approach Cathedral Cove while avoiding the trail crowds in peak summer.

⏱ 180 min

Rotorua

★★★★☆
1

Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland is 27 km south of Rotorua: it's the most spectacular geothermal park on the North Island. The Champagne Pool is a 65-metre lake at 74°C, coloured orange by arsenic and antimony deposited over 700 years. The Lady Knox Geyser erupts every day at 10:15 am - not spontaneously, but with the help of soap poured in to break the surface tension. Allow 2 hours to walk the boardwalks.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Whakarewarewa Forest redwoods were planted in 1899 as an experiment to test 170 tree species. The Californian coastal redwoods have reached 70 metres in a century - twice the growth rate of California thanks to the volcanic soil. Suspended walkways 12 metres up have been installed in the trees since 2017 for a canopy-level visit.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Māori cultural evening at Te Puia: the reconstructed traditional village houses the Pohutu geyser (the largest in the southern hemisphere, erupting 30 metres), wood carving and weaving workshops, and an evening haka with hāngī - food cooked in an underground geothermal oven. It's the tourist showcase of Te Arawa culture but the demonstrations are authentic and led by local artisans.

⏱ 150 min

Whakapapa Village

★★★★☆
1

Huka Falls are on the Waikato River, 3 km north of Taupo: in 10 seconds, 220,000 litres of water funnel through a 15-metre canyon before dropping 11 metres. The water is an almost artificial turquoise blue. The viewing platform is a 5-minute walk from the car park - an easy stop en route to Tongariro.

⏱ 30 min
2

Tawhai Falls - nicknamed 'Gollum's Pool' since filming Lord of the Rings in 2001 - are 3 km south of Whakapapa Village on SH48: a 13-metre waterfall drops into a circular basin surrounded by tree ferns. This is where Gollum's fishing scene was filmed. The track from the car park takes 20 minutes return - perfect for late afternoon before heading back to the village.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

The Tongariro Alpine Crossing is considered New Zealand's finest one-day hike: 19.4 km linear traverse across Mount Ngauruhoe volcano (2,291 m), with the Emerald Lakes - three craters filled with water coloured by hydrothermal minerals - as the highlight. Start from Mangatepopo (end of the shuttle bus) early morning, finish at Ketetahi late afternoon. The shuttle is mandatory: cars cannot be left at the start. Allow 6 to 8 hours depending on your pace, bring waterproof clothing and hiking boots - the terrain is volcanic with loose scree between 1,500 and 1,967 metres.

⏱ 420 min

Wellington

★★★★☆
1

Te Papa Tongarewa is New Zealand's national museum, open since 1998 on the waterfront: 6 floors spanning 36,000 m², with permanent collections on the country's natural history, the 1840 Treaty of Waitangi (first version of the Constitution), and Māori culture. Entry is free - allow at least 2 hours for the main galleries.

⏱ 120 min
2

Mount Victoria (Matairangi) rises 196 metres above Wellington harbour. It's the best viewpoint in the city: on a clear day you can see the South Island 90 km to the south. The summit is reachable by car or on foot from the Mt Victoria neighbourhood in 20 minutes. Film buffs' note: some scenes from The Fellowship of the Ring were shot in the hills above.

⏱ 60 min
3

Cuba Street is Wellington's bohemian artery, classified as a Heritage Precinct: brick buildings from the 1900s, independent shops, cafés and the Bucket Fountain with its buckets that tip out in rhythm. The city has the highest number of cafés per capita in New Zealand - a chance to understand why.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Wellington Cable Car has linked downtown to the Kelburn neighbourhood since 1902 - the country's oldest funicular railway. From the top, the terraced botanic gardens descend across 26 hectares to the Beehive (Parliament). The walk down through the gardens takes 30 minutes and offers fine views over the harbour.

⏱ 75 min

Picton

★★★☆☆
1

The Cook Strait crossing aboard the Interislander ferry takes 3 hours 30 minutes between Wellington and Picton. The strait is one of the roughest in the world due to currents between the Pacific Ocean and the Tasman Sea. The final 30 minutes through the Marlborough Sounds - the drowned valley system carved into hundreds of forested inlets - are the most spectacular. Grab a spot on the outer deck as you approach Picton.

⏱ 210 min
2

The Queen Charlotte Drive links Picton to Havelock along 35 km of winding road above the Sounds: the viewpoint at Ngākuta Bay, halfway along, is the best photo stop. The road passes through Sauvignon Blanc vineyards - Marlborough represents 77% of New Zealand's production of this grape variety.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Kayaking in the Marlborough Sounds from Picton: operators offer half-day rentals to explore the calm inlets around Ship Cove, the historic landing point where James Cook anchored during his three visits to New Zealand between 1770 and 1777. The water is crystal clear and boat traffic rare in the smaller arms.

⏱ 180 min

Kaikoura

★★★☆☆
1

The Peninsula Walkway circles the Kaikoura peninsula in 2 hours 30 minutes: the coast is lined with seal-covered reefs and colonies of little blue penguins (kororā). The reason whales are here year-round is geological: the Kaikoura Canyon drops to 1,000 metres depth just 500 metres from shore, bringing cold krill-rich water to the surface. It's a unique ecosystem.

⏱ 150 min
2

The fur seal colony at Ohau Point is 25 km north of Kaikoura on SH1: a waterfall in the forest just behind the road shelters hundreds of young seals playing in the pools. You can see them from the track in 20 minutes - a brief stop but hard to forget.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Whale watching trip by boat or plane: Kaikoura is one of the few places in the world where sperm whales are visible year-round, as they feed in the nearby submarine canyon. Sperm whales can measure 18 metres and weigh 57 tonnes. Operators offer 2.5-hour boat trips from the harbour. Book the evening before - trips depend on weather conditions.

⏱ 150 min

Christchurch

★★★★☆
1

Christchurch Botanic Gardens stretch across 21 hectares in the loop of the Avon River, founded in 1863: the grand Victorian glasshouse (Cuningham House) shelters tropical plants and a greenhouse with giant Victoria amazonica water lilies. ⏱ 90 min
2

The Cardboard Cathedral was built in 2013 to temporarily replace the neo-Gothic cathedral damaged in the 2011 earthquake: its 98 cardboard tubes, 60 cm in diameter, form a structure capable of withstanding a magnitude 8 earthquake. ⏱ 45 min
3

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Lake Tekapo

★★★★☆
1

The Church of the Good Shepherd is a small stone chapel built in 1935 as a tribute to the pioneers of the Mackenzie region. Its chancel window opens directly onto the lake - the effect is striking, with the Southern Alps as backdrop. It's one of New Zealand's most reproduced photographs. Arrive before 8am or after 5pm to avoid tour groups.

⏱ 30 min
2

Lake Tekapo is coloured milky turquoise by glacial flour: particles of rock ground by Southern Alps glaciers remain suspended in the water and reflect the light. The colour is most intense in summer when melt is active. The northern shore offers the best viewpoint from the road.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stargazing at Mt John Observatory: the Mackenzie Basin has been classified as an International Dark Sky Reserve since 2012, one of the largest in the world at 4,300 km². Mount John rises 1,031 metres above the lake. Operators offer evening guided tours with telescopes - the southern hemisphere offers a direct view of the Milky Way's core invisible from Europe.

Mount Cook

★★★★☆
1

Lake Pukaki lies on the road between Tekapo and Mount Cook: the view from the lookout on SH80 with Aoraki/Mount Cook (3,724 m) in the background is one of the country's postcard images. The water is fed directly by the Tasman Glacier - the same glacial flour as Tekapo but more concentrated. Stop for 20 minutes at the official viewpoint.

⏱ 25 min
2

The Hooker Valley Track is the most accessible walk in Aoraki/Mount Cook National Park: 10 km return, three swing bridges over glacial torrents, and at the end, the terminal lake of Hooker Glacier where icebergs float. The elevation gain is minimal (100 m) but the setting is alpine - Aoraki is visible the whole way. Allow 3 hours at a relaxed pace.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Light aircraft or helicopter flight over the Tasman Glacier: New Zealand's largest glacier extends 27 km long and 3 km wide - it has lost 3 km of length since 1990. Operators offer 40 to 60-minute tours with landing on the névé above 2,000 metres. It's the only way to see the glacier system from within without a multi-day expedition.

Wanaka

★★★★☆
1

Lindis Pass (SH8) at 971 metres is the route between Mount Cook and Wanaka: the bare golden hills resemble savannah in summer. It's a typical New Zealand high-country landscape - near-desert with sheep and tussocks (tall grasses), not a tree for dozens of kilometres. Stop at the summit of the pass.

⏱ 20 min
2

Lake Wanaka is New Zealand's fourth largest lake (192 km²), fed by glaciers from the Harris Mountains. The solitary willow planted in the water at the shore has become the country's most famous photo subject - it features on thousands of Instagram posts. The lakefront promenade between the village and Roys Bay park is flat and pleasant.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Rob Roy Glacier Track in the Matukituki Valley: 10 km return through a glacial valley with a direct view of the Rob Roy Glacier hanging at 2,644 metres. The track passes through beech forest then opens onto a glacial cirque - avalanches regularly break off the facing walls. Allow 4 hours total. The access road (SH6 then Matukituki track) sometimes requires a vehicle with sufficient ground clearance.

Queenstown

★★★★★
1

Arrowtown is 21 km from Queenstown on the Arrow River: the village preserves 60 stone buildings from the 1862 gold rush era, including Chinese miners' cabins on the riverbank. The Lakes District Museum traces the history of the prospectors who flocked here after the discovery of gold seams in 1862. In autumn (April-May), the aspens planted by pioneers blanket the streets in golden leaves.

⏱ 90 min
2

Queenstown's lakefront (Steamer Wharf, Wakatipu shores) is the heart of the town. Lake Wakatipu has a 83-km Z-shape carved by Pleistocene glaciers - its level rises and falls 10 cm every 5 minutes due to atmospheric pressure differences, a local phenomenon called a seiche. The Queenstown Gardens on the neighbouring peninsula offer relative calm facing the lake.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Skyline gondola up Bob's Peak (450 m above the lake): panoramic view over Queenstown, Lake Wakatipu and the Remarkables - the flat-topped mountain range that served as backdrop for Middle-earth. Luge tracks descend 800 metres from the summit - a family activity but fast-paced. The summit restaurant is open evenings for dinner with the view.

Te Anau

★★★★☆
1

The Mirror Lakes are a 10-minute stop on SH94, 58 km from Te Anau: a wooden boardwalk runs alongside two ponds where the Eglinton Mountains reflect perfectly when there's no wind. Arrive early - the surface is glassy before the wind picks up in late morning.

⏱ 20 min
2

The Chasm is a 20-minute stop 10 km before Milford Sound: the Cleddau River has carved a spiral canyon through the granite over millions of years. A 500-metre forest track leads to footbridges above the gorge - the water swirls 3 metres below with impressive force even in summer. Don't miss it if time allows.

⏱ 25 min
3

Milford Sound (Piopiotahi): granite walls plunge 1,200 metres sheer, including Mitre Peak (1,692 m) rising straight from the water. The fjord receives 6,000 to 8,000 mm of rain annually - this feeds a 3-metre freshwater layer floating on the saltwater and allows cold-water corals normally found at depth to live here at 40 metres. The SH94 road from Te Anau passes through the Homer Tunnel (1,270 m, hand-carved from 1935, completed 1954) beneath the Darran Mountains - the entrance on the Milford side is vertiginous.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Cruise on Milford Sound: boats navigate 16 km to the mouth on the Tasman Sea, passing Stirling Falls (151 m) and Bowen Falls (162 m), and approaching seal colonies on the rocks. Hector's dolphins (the world's smallest dolphins, endemic to New Zealand) often accompany the boats. Allow 2 hours for the cruise. Operators offer overnight kayaking trips with camping for the more adventurous.

⏱ 120 min
