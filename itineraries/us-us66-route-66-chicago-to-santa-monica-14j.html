☀️ Weather

Spring (April-May): Perfect weather across most regions (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, moderate crowds, and excellent conditions for national parks and cities. Ideal for diverse activities nationwide.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with spectacular fall foliage, harvest festivals, and comfortable weather. Perfect for outdoor activities, cultural events, fewer crowds than summer.

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warmest weather but highest crowds and prices. Excellent for northern regions, challenging in southwestern deserts, perfect for beaches and national parks.

Winter (December-February): Variable conditions from tropical Florida to snowy Colorado. Perfect for skiing, southern destinations, indoor attractions, lowest prices outside holiday periods.

📋 Notes

Route 66 was decommissioned in 1985 - the historic route alternates between preserved sections and Interstate stretches. A GPS with Route 66 waypoints is essential. Some gas stations are scarce in the desert between Needles and Barstow - fill up beforehand. Extreme heat in Arizona and California from June to September.

Chicago Il

where the Begin Route 66 sign launches the journey from Michigan Avenue

★★★★★
1

Begin Historic Route 66 Sign - Corner of Adams Street and Michigan Avenue: this is where it all starts. The brown sign is understated - you'll have to look for it. Get your photo, this is Mile 0 of the 2,448 miles stretching ahead to the Pacific. Route 66 was established in 1926 and decommissioned in 1985, but its legend has never faded.

⏱ 15 min
2

Cloud Gate (Millennium Park) - The 110-ton mirror-polished bean-shaped sculpture by Anish Kapoor reflects Chicago's skyline. It's 200 meters from the Route 66 sign. Go early in the morning to beat the crowds and have the reflection all to yourself.

⏱ 30 min
3

Art Institute of Chicago - If you've got an afternoon to spare, this museum houses Grant Wood's American Gothic, Edward Hopper's Nighthawks, and an Impressionist collection that rivals the Musée d'Orsay. Allow at least 2 hours, though you could easily spend the whole day.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Try deep-dish pizza - Chicago invented the thick-crust pizza in the 1940s. Giordano's, Lou Malnati's, or Pequod's - every Chicagoan has their allegiance. Order 45 minutes before you want to eat: it bakes slow.

⏱ 60 min

Stroll the Riverwalk - Along the Chicago River, this pedestrian path runs beneath Art Deco bridges and skyscrapers. In the evening, the view of the illuminated towers is spectacular. If you have time, an architecture cruise on the river (1h30) is the best way to understand the skyline.

Gemini Giant (Wilmington, IL) - This 28-foot fiberglass giant holding a Gemini rocket is the first of the 'Muffler Men' you'll encounter on the Route. Built in the 1960s to attract motorists, it stands in front of a former restaurant. Quick photo stop.

3

Abraham Lincoln Home (Springfield) - The only house Lincoln ever owned, preserved by the National Park Service. Pick up a free ticket at the visitor center for the guided tour - first come, first served. The entire neighborhood is preserved as it was in the 1860s.

Activities

Gateway Arch (St. Louis) - This 630-foot stainless steel arch, the tallest structure in the United States when it opened in 1965, marks the Gateway to the West. 3

Meramec Caverns (Stanton, MO) - These caves, 60 miles southwest of St. Louis, were supposedly used as a hideout by outlaw Jesse James according to local legend. The guided tour lasts 1h20 and descends 60 feet underground. Temperature stays constant at 60°F - bring a jacket.

Activities

Amarillo Tx

where the 72-ounce steak and buried Cadillacs draw road-trippers to Texas

★★★★☆
1

Rock Cafe (Stroud, OK) - Stop for breakfast at this cafe built in 1939 with local stone - it inspired the character Sally in Cars. Burned down in 2008, it was rebuilt stone by stone by the community.

2

Round Barn (Arcadia, OK) - Take a look at this 1898 round wooden barn, the only one of its kind on Route 66. It nearly got demolished in the 1980s before being saved by a retired carpenter volunteer who restored it alone over four years.

⏱ 20 min
3

POPS 66 (Arcadia, OK) - You can't miss the 66-foot soda bottle sculpture marking the entrance - this gas station-restaurant offers over 700 varieties of sodas from around the world. Try something weird, you won't find it anywhere else.

⏱ 25 min
4

Oklahoma Route 66 Museum (Clinton) - The best museum dedicated to Route 66 along the entire route. The exhibit is organized by decades, from the 1920s to the 1980s, with reconstructions of period diners, gas stations, and motels. Allow 1h30.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Dinner and atmosphere in Amarillo - The historic downtown on 6th Street has antique shops, bars, and restaurants far better than the tourist zone along the Interstate. If you like challenges, a famous steakhouse offers a free 72-ounce steak if you finish it in an hour.

Tucumcari Nm

where the Blue Swallow Motel and its neon are Route 66's beating heart

★★☆☆☆
1

Cadillac Ranch (Amarillo) - Cross the field (400 meters from the parking lot) to see ten Cadillacs planted nose-first in the ground, all covered in multicolored graffiti. This 1974 installation, financed by eccentric millionaire Stanley Marsh, pays tribute to the evolution of the Cadillac tail fin. Bring spray paint - this is one of the few places in the world where graffiti is encouraged. You'll need to walk 400 meters across the field from the parking area.

⏱ 30 min
2

MidPoint Cafe (Adrian, TX) - You're exactly halfway between Chicago and Los Angeles: 1,139 miles on each side. The sign in front of the cafe confirms it. Try the Ugly Crust Pie - the house specialty.

⏱ 20 min
3

Blue Swallow Motel (Tucumcari) - Arrive at dusk: the blue neon swallow promising '100% Refrigerated Air' is the most beautiful lighted sign on all of Route 66. This 1939 motor court, listed on the National Register, has garages attached to each room - standard back when every traveler wanted to keep an eye on their car. If you can book a room (reserve 2 months ahead in summer), it's the ultimate Route 66 experience.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk through Tucumcari's neon lights - Over two dozen vintage neon signs illuminate the main street after dark. The Blue Swallow is the star, but also look for the Palomino Motel, Safari Motel, and Teepee Curios. Walk slowly - it's an open-air museum.

⏱ 45 min

Albuquerque Nm

where Central Avenue follows the Mother Road's original alignment

★★★★☆
1

Santa Fe (60-mile detour) - Take the detour - New Mexico's capital is worth the 60-mile side trip. The Plaza, surrounded by adobe buildings, has been the city center since 1610 - it's the oldest state capital in the United States. The art galleries on Canyon Road (over 80 in one mile) are free to browse.

⏱ 90 min
2

Meow Wolf (Santa Fe) - A two-story Victorian house where every door, drawer, and passage leads into a parallel universe: bioluminescent forest, spaceship, crystal cave. It's an immersive art experience created by a local collective. Book online - it often sells out.

⏱ 75 min
3

Albuquerque - Central Avenue - Drive down Central Avenue - Route 66 runs straight through Albuquerque here. Stop in the Nob Hill district, with its shops in 1940s buildings. In the evening, the vintage motel neons light up along the avenue.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Sunset from the Sandia Peak Tramway - The longest aerial tramway in North America (2.7 miles) takes you to 10,378 feet. The view over Albuquerque and the desert at sunset is extraordinary - the city lights up below while the sky turns red. Bring a jacket, it's 20-25°F cooler at the top.

⏱ 90 min

Petroglyph National Monument - Over 24,000 petroglyphs carved into volcanic rock by ancestors of the Pueblos and early Spanish settlers. The Boca Negra Canyon trail (3 short loops, ~100 petroglyphs) is the most accessible. Arrive early - there's no shade and it heats up fast.

⏱ 60 min
3

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Winslow Az

where Standing on the Corner immortalizes the Eagles song in Arizona

★★☆☆☆
1

El Rancho Hotel (Gallup, NM) - This 1937 motel, nicknamed the 'Playground of the Stars,' hosted Hollywood stars during western film shoots in the 1940s-50s. Each room is named after a star - John Wayne, Katharine Hepburn, Ronald Reagan. Even if you don't stay overnight, the lobby and its hunting trophies are worth a look.

⏱ 20 min
2

Petrified Forest National Park - Don't miss this park protecting the world's largest concentration of petrified wood: 225-million-year-old tree trunks transformed into colorful quartz crystals. The 28-mile scenic drive also crosses the Painted Desert - hills striped with red, purple, and white. Don't take anything: it's illegal and the park estimates it loses 12 tons of petrified wood annually to visitors.

⏱ 120 min
3

Standing on the Corner Park (Winslow, AZ) - 'Take it easy, take it easy / Standing on the corner in Winslow, Arizona...' - the 1972 Eagles song saved this town from decline. A bronze statue and mural mark the street corner. Five-minute photo stop, but the song will stick in your head all day.

⏱ 10 min

Activities

Stay at La Posada (Winslow) - If you can book a room at this 1929 former Harvey House hotel, designed by architect Mary Colter, it's the most beautiful lodging on the entire Route 66. The Turquoise Room restaurant serves refined Southwest cuisine. Even without staying overnight, visit the gardens and lobby.

⏱ 45 min

Flagstaff Az

college town at 7,000 feet serving as base for the Grand Canyon

★★★☆☆
1

Grand Canyon - Mather Point - Even though the Grand Canyon isn't on Route 66, it's 80 miles north of Flagstaff and skipping it would be absurd. Mather Point, right next to the visitor center, offers the first view into the canyon. 277 miles long, 6,000 feet deep, 2 billion years of geological strata. Nothing prepares you for what you see.

⏱ 45 min
2

Bright Angel Trail - You don't need to descend to the bottom (2-day round trip). The hike down to the first rest house (1.5 miles round trip, 1h30) already gives you a dizzying perspective. Warning: it's always hotter at the bottom than the top, and the climb back up takes twice as long as going down. Bring water.

⏱ 90 min
3

Yavapai Point - The best spot for sunset. The strata shift from red to purple as shadows descend into the canyon. Arrive 30 minutes before sunset - spots fill up quickly.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Evening in Flagstaff - This college town at 7,000 feet elevation is cool and pleasant even in summer. Route 66 runs right through the historic downtown. Craft breweries are plentiful and downtown is pedestrian-friendly and lively in the evening.

⏱ 60 min

Kingman Az

where the Seligman-Kingman stretch is Route 66's best-preserved section

★★☆☆☆
1

Seligman - Stop here - this is where Route 66 was saved. In 1987, Angel Delgadillo, a local barber, founded the Route 66 Association of Arizona to prevent the Mother Road from fading into obscurity after its decommissioning. The few-block village inspired Radiator Springs in the movie Cars. The Snow Cap Drive-In, founded by Angel's brother in 1953, is a temple of kitsch with gags on the menu ('dead chicken' for chicken) and squirting mustard.

⏱ 45 min
2

Seligman - Kingman: the most beautiful stretch - The 90 miles between these two towns is the best-preserved section of all of Route 66. Two lanes, no traffic, the same road as in 1955 through sagebrush and ranches. Take your time.

⏱ 90 min
3

Hackberry General Store - Make the stop 30 miles east of Kingman - this 1930s former gas station is frozen in time: period pumps, rusted signs, red Corvette parked out front. It's as authentic as Route 66 gets.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Kingman neon lights - If you arrive after dark, Andy Devine Avenue (the local Route 66) lights up with vintage neon. Kingman is also the gateway to the Historic Route 66 toward Oatman - a winding mountain road with wild burros on the pavement.

⏱ 30 min

Barstow Ca

where the Mojave Desert delivers the route's most desolate and spectacular crossing

★★★☆☆
1

Needles, CA - You enter California through the Mojave Desert. Needles, named after the rock spires overlooking the town, is the last stop before 150 miles of desert. Fill up on gas and water here - stations are scarce until Barstow.

⏱ 15 min
2

Roy's Motel & Café (Amboy) - In the middle of nowhere, literally. This 1930s motel in the heart of the Mojave is being renovated for Route 66's centennial in 2026. The Googie-style neon sign is one of the most photographed on the route. It often exceeds 110°F here in summer - stay in your car if you're not prepared.

⏱ 15 min
3

Elmer's Bottle Tree Ranch (Oro Grande) - Stop to see these 200 'trees' made of metal pipes topped with colored glass bottles that tinkle in the desert wind. You've never seen anything like it - this folk art installation, created by one man on his property, is mesmerizing.

⏱ 20 min
4

Calico Ghost Town - If you're traveling with family, stop at this 1880s former silver mining town. Restored in the 1950s, it's a historic park with period buildings, a visitable mine, and a small train. Allow 1 hour for the visit.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Crossing the Mojave - The most desolate and spectacular part of the Route. Lunar landscapes, railroad tracks stretching to the horizon, purple mountains. Plan on having music - radio doesn't pick up much between Needles and Barstow.

⏱ 120 min

Santa Monica Ca

where the End of the Trail sign on the pier marks the end of 2,448 miles

★★★★☆
1

Wigwam Motel (Rialto/San Bernardino) - 19 concrete teepee-shaped rooms, built in 1949, surrounded by vintage cars. One of Route 66's most photographed motels. If you're not staying overnight, a photo from the parking lot is enough.

⏱ 15 min
2

Santa Monica Pier - The 'End of the Trail' sign (installed in 2009) marks the official end of Route 66. You've driven 2,448 miles from Chicago. The Pacific is before you. Get your photo near the sign, then turn around: the Pacific Park Ferris wheel, the historic 1922 carousel, and arcade are there. It's the end of the journey and the beginning of California.

⏱ 45 min
3

Venice Beach - Walk 2 miles south of the pier to Venice Beach, California's most eccentric boardwalk: bodybuilders working out outdoors, street performers, skaters, vendors selling everything and anything. The original Muscle Beach has been here since 1934.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Pacific sunset - You've crossed the Illinois plains, Texas, the New Mexico and Arizona deserts, the Mojave. Sit on the Santa Monica sand and watch the sun descend into the Pacific. It's the perfect ending.

⏱ 45 min
