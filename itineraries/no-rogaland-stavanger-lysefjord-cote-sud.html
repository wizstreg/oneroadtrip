✨ Highlights
  • Preikestolen (604 m) and Kjeragbolten (984 m) are accessible from May to October - outside this season, special equipment and a guide are mandatory.
  • Lysevegen (27 switchbacks) and the access road to Kjerag via Sirdal are closed from November to May.
  • Flørli is accessible only by ferry - 4 departures daily on weekdays, check Kolumbus schedules before setting out.
  • Stavanger (SVG) is the main gateway; Kristiansand (KRS) is the ideal airport for ending the circuit.
📆 Best period

June, July, August, September

☀️ Weather

Summer (June-August): Peak season with midnight sun, warmest weather (15-25°C), and all attractions accessible. Highest prices but ideal conditions for hiking, fjord cruises, and outdoor activities.

Northern Lights Season (September-March): Best aurora viewing with long dark nights, winter activities, and snow-covered landscapes. Cold temperatures (-5 to 5°C) but magical winter experiences.

Shoulder Season (May, September): Excellent weather with fewer crowds and moderate prices. Spring blooms or autumn colors, comfortable temperatures, most activities available.

Winter Sports Season (December-March): Cold weather (-10 to 0°C) perfect for skiing, dog sledding, and winter adventures. Aurora season with cozy cabin culture and winter festivals.

Stavanger

★★★★☆
1

Step into Gamle Stavanger, Norway's best-preserved old quarter: 173 white wooden houses from the 18th and 19th centuries, flower-filled facades, and cobbled lanes. Allow a good hour to explore - this is a living residential neighborhood, not a frozen museum piece.

⏱ 60 min
2

Stavanger Cathedral (Stavanger domkirke), founded around 1125, is Norway's oldest cathedral still in active use. The original Romanesque nave contrasts with the Gothic choir rebuilt after the 1272 fire - a striking example of two-era architecture.

⏱ 45 min
3

Øvre Holmegate, nicknamed the "street of colors" (Fargegaten), brings together brightly painted facades and a handful of trendy cafes. A striking contrast to the white houses of Gamle Stavanger, just a stone's throw away.

⏱ 30 min
4

The Norwegian Petroleum Museum traces the oil saga from the discovery of the first North Sea fields in the 1960s. The building itself, opened in 1999, resembles an oil platform planted on the harbor - you can't miss it.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stroll from Vågen harbor to the fish market (Fisketorget): fresh shrimp, smoked salmon, and the maritime atmosphere of Stavanger's waterfront. The trick here is to arrive early for the best shrimp.

★★★★☆
1

The road from Sirdal to Lysebotn crosses a mountain plateau where you might spot wild reindeer - keep your eyes on the roadside. The landscape changes dramatically as you descend toward the fjord.

Kristiansand

1

Posebyen, Kristiansand's historic quarter, survived the great fire of 1892 - the other neighborhoods burned, this one didn't. Founded in 1641 by King Christian IV, the city preserves its grid layout and single-story white wooden houses here. A peaceful atmosphere a stone's throw from the harbor.
