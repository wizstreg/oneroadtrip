Costa Verde Brazil: São Paulo, Paraty, Ilha Grande & Rio de Janeiro - 13-Day Circuit

📅 13 days📍 13 stops🚗 740 km⏱ 13h20

Sao Paulo → Cunha → Paraty → Ilha Grande → Angra Dos Reis → Petropolis → Teresopolis → Rio De Janeiro Leme

From São Paulo's sprawling metropolis to Ilha Grande's deserted beaches, this 13-day circuit unfolds Brazil's Costa Verde in all its diversity. São Paulo and its museums, the ceramics village of Cunha in the mists of Serra da Bocaina, the UNESCO-listed colonial town of Paraty, car-free Ilha Grande and its 100 pristine beaches, then the imperial mountains of Petrópolis and Teresópolis before Rio de Janeiro's dazzling finale.

✨ Highlights
  • Paraty and Ilha Grande jointly UNESCO World Heritage-listed in 2019
  • Ilha Grande: car-free island, 150 km of trails and 100 beaches in Atlantic forest
  • Angra dos Reis and its 365-island archipelago with wreck diving
  • Petrópolis, former summer capital of the Brazilian Empire, crown with 639 diamonds
  • Serra dos Órgãos: Dedo de Deus appears on Rio de Janeiro State's flag
📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 10 and 14 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 What to discover in BR?

Carnival energy, Amazon rainforest, and pristine beaches create the ultimate South American adventure. Experience vibrant cities, incredible biodiversity, and passionate culture from Rio's beaches to Pantanal wetlands. Samba music, capoeira martial arts, and incredible football culture showcase national spirit. Brazil offers unparalleled natural wonders with infectious cultural energy.

📋 Important notes

D3: São Paulo → Cunha (170 km, 3h) via Serra da Bocaina, early departure advised. D6→D7: ferry crossing Paraty or Angra → Ilha Grande (1h), schedules vary by season. D8: return ferry Abraão → Angra (1h30) then mainland road. Ilha Grande logistics: car-free island, manage luggage before boarding, one missed ferry delays the entire day. D9-D10: Petrópolis and Teresópolis can be done as excursions from Rio if the itinerary is shortened. Climate: humid Atlantic forest zone, plan alternatives outside May–September especially for Ilha Grande and Serra dos Órgãos.

Sao Paulo

Where South America's boldest skyline meets world-class museums and electric nightlife

1

MASP (São Paulo Museum of Art) hovers above Avenida Paulista on four red pillars. Lina Bo Bardi designed it in 1968 so the space beneath would remain open to the public. The collection spans Van Gogh, Rembrandt, Picasso, Monet, and Brazilian masters.

⏱ 120 min
2

Ibirapuera Park, designed by Oscar Niemeyer and Roberto Burle Marx for São Paulo's 400th anniversary in 1954, sprawls across 1.6 km². Lakes, cycling paths, the Ibirapuera Auditorium, Afro Brasil Museum, and Museum of Modern Art share this sprawling green lung.

⏱ 180 min
3

Mercado Municipal (Mercadão), a covered market hall from 1933, shelters remarkable stained-glass mosaics. Spices, tropical fruits, smoked meats, cheeses, and the legendary mortadella sandwich—São Paulo's gastronomic icon.

⏱ 90 min
4

Liberdade, São Paulo's Japanese quarter, is home to the largest Japanese community outside Japan. Red lanterns, specialty groceries, authentic Japanese restaurants, and a Sunday street market.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

On Sundays, Avenida Paulista closes to traffic. Under MASP, an antiques market springs up; across the street, Bixiga's artisan market offers street food and live music.

⏱ 180 min

Street art walk through Vila Madalena: Beco do Batman concentrates spectacular murals. Independent galleries, designer boutiques, and trendy bars fill this bohemian neighborhood.

⏱ 150 min

Dinner at a neighborhood churrascaria: all-you-can-eat grilled meats rodízio-style, caipirinhas, and Brazilian music. Skip the touristy downtown spots; head for Jardins or Itaim instead.

⏱ 120 min

Climb to Terraço Itália (41st floor of Edifício Itália) to watch São Paulo unfurl endlessly. The world's fourth-largest metropolis reveals its dizzying scale at sunset.

Day 2

Sao Paulo

Where South America's boldest skyline meets world-class museums and electric nightlife

★★★★☆
📍 Pinacoteca do Estado São Paulo📍 Praça da Sé cathédrale São Paulo📍 Museu Arte Sacra couvent São Paulo📍 Quartier Bixiga Bela Vista italo São Paulo📍 Edifício Martinelli Copan centre São Paulo📍 Museu do Futebol Pacaembu São Paulo📍 Quartier Pinheiros Jardins São Paulo
1

Pinacoteca do Estado, founded in 1905, is São Paulo's oldest art museum. Its neoclassical building renovated by Paulo Mendes da Rocha holds Brazil's largest collection of 19th and 20th-century Brazilian art: paintings, sculptures, and works by Paulista modernists.

⏱ 90 min
2

Praça da Sé is Brazil's kilometer zero—all distances in the country radiate from here. The neo-Gothic cathedral inaugurated in 1954, with its twin 92-meter towers, dominates the largest square in the historic center.

⏱ 45 min
3

Museu de Arte Sacra de São Paulo occupies an 18th-century Benedictine convent. Its collection of 18,000 pieces—ex-votos, baroque sculptures, colonial silverwork—is Latin America's largest in sacred art.

⏱ 75 min
4

Bairro da Bixiga (Bela Vista) is São Paulo's Italian-Brazilian quarter. Cobblestone streets, century-old cantinas, and independent theaters tell the story of 19th-century Italian immigration. On Sunday mornings, the market at Praça Dom Orione blends secondhand finds and street food.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Walking tour of the historic center: Edifício Martinelli (Brazil's first skyscraper, 1929), Oscar Niemeyer's Edifício Copan, Viaduto do Chá and its views over the old downtown. Architecture that charts the city's waves of modernization.

⏱ 150 min

Visit the Museu do Futebol beneath the stands of Pacaembu stadium: Brazilian football history told through sound archives, legendary jerseys, and immersive installations. Pelé, Garrincha, and World Cup glory on display.

⏱ 90 min

Evening in Pinheiros or Jardins: designer cocktail bars, celebrated chef restaurants, intense cultural and music scene. São Paulo is one of the world's gastronomic capitals.

⏱ 120 min

Day 3

Cunha

Cloud-forest village where Japanese kilns flame and lavender blooms in Brazilian hills

★★★☆☆
📍 Casa do Artesão céramique Cunha São Paulo📍 Pedra da Macela randonnée Cunha📍 Parque Estadual Serra do Mar Núcleo Cunha📍 Route céramistes Cunha São Paulo📍 Domaine lavande Lavandário Cunha📍 Cachoeira do Pimenta cascade Cunha📍 Centre historique Cunha São Paulo
1

Cunha emerges from the mists of Serra da Bocaina at 850 meters elevation. Founded as Vila do Falcão in 1730 on the old gold route linking the mines of Minas Gerais to the port of Paraty, the town retains its character as a preserved Brazilian rural village deep in the Atlantic forest.

⏱ 60 min
2

Casa do Artesão brings together local ceramicists around a collection of handcrafted pieces. Since the 1970s, Cunha has become Brazil's ceramics capital: a Pottery Route crosses the countryside, dotted with workshops where artisans still use the Japanese noborigama kiln technique, whose free flames create unpredictable color effects.

⏱ 90 min
3

Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar - Núcleo Cunha protects one of the last remnants of cloud forest in Brazil, above 1,000 meters elevation. Its trails traverse century-old trees, springs, and waterfalls including Cachoeira do Barracão, nestled in a cirque of rocks and natural pools.

⏱ 120 min
4

Cunha's lavender farm astonishes: rows of lavender, rosemary, and geranium spread beneath a Provençal-style house lost in the Brazilian hills. Artisan products sold on-site.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hike to Pedra da Macela (1,840 m): 2.3 km climb through forest, with 360° panoramic views over Serra da Bocaina, the coast of Paraty, and Ilha Grande in the distance. The border between São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro States stretches beneath your feet.

⏱ 150 min

Visit ceramics workshops along the rural road. Rental shops offer bikes to tour the Pottery Route and stop at active noborigama kilns, where wood-firing produces unique pieces with each firing.

⏱ 180 min

Swim at Cachoeira do Pimenta: a waterfall accessible on foot from Cunha's rural zone, surrounded by dense Atlantic forest. Natural pools carved by the current offer welcome refreshment after the hike.

⏱ 90 min

Day 4

Paraty

Where gold-rush cobblestones meet turquoise tides beneath Atlantic rainforest

★★★★☆
📍 Centre historique colonial Paraty UNESCO📍 Igreja Santa Rita Paraty📍 Igreja Nossa Senhora dos Remédios Paraty📍 Praça da Matriz Paraty📍 Distillerie cachaça alambic Paraty📍 Baie kayak Jabaquara Paraty📍 Rues pavées centre colonial Paraty
1

Paraty's historic center has been UNESCO-listed since 2019. Founded in 1667 as an export port for gold from Minas Gerais, it holds Brazil's largest concentration of Portuguese colonial architecture. Four 18th-century churches, whitewashed houses with colorful shutters, cobblestone streets that flood at high tide.

⏱ 90 min
2

Igreja Santa Rita, built in 1722 by freed Blacks, is Paraty's oldest church. Its white baroque facade stands out against palms and mountains. It now houses the Museum of Sacred Art.

⏱ 30 min
3

Igreja Nossa Senhora dos Remédios (1787), Paraty's main church, overlooks Praça da Matriz, the heart of colonial social life. Its sober facade, twin bell towers, and baroque interior with gilded altarpieces speak to the city's opulence during the gold era.

⏱ 30 min
4

The historic center's streets are paved with polished schist that reflects light. The colonial grid layout, flood drainage channels, and absence of motor vehicles make Paraty one of the continent's best-preserved historic centers.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit artisanal cachaça distilleries on foot or by bike from the center. Paraty was the cachaça capital in the 18th century; some copper stills still run on local sugarcane. The cinnamon and clove variety is the region's signature.

⏱ 90 min

Kayak Jabaquara Bay in late afternoon: golden light on the islands and mangroves is particularly beautiful. Circle tropical islets, navigate mangrove corridors, reach deserted beaches accessible only by sea.

⏱ 180 min

Dinner in the historic center at sunset. Tables spill onto cobblestone streets, candles glow in colonial windows. Fresh fish of the day, coconut moqueca, artisanal caipirinha.

⏱ 90 min

Paraty Bay counts over 50 islands and 100 beaches accessible only by sea. Schooner excursions last 5 hours and stop at deserted islands with turquoise waters, notably Piscina Natural Caixa d'Aço, a natural rock formation that creates a protected lagoon.

⏱ 300 min
2

Trindade, a bohemian village 35 minutes by road, is protected by Serra da Bocaina Park. A single road accesses it. Praia Cepilho is renowned for surfing, Praia Cachadaço for its calm waters bordered by dense forest.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Schooner excursion in the bay: early morning departure from the port, five hours among deserted islands. The captain anchors at snorkeling spots, where the seabed reveals turtles and tropical fish. Onboard: caipirinhas and fresh fruit.

⏱ 300 min

Natural slide at Penha, 15 km from the center: a rock polished smooth by the current where you glide into a fresh-water pool deep in the Atlantic forest.

⏱ 120 min

Hike in Serra da Bocaina from Paraty's heights: one of Brazil's best-preserved Atlantic forests. Trails lead to hidden waterfalls and offer ocean and island views in the distance.

Ilha Grande

A roadless island where jaguar tracks cross jungle trails to hundred secret beaches

★★★★★
📍 Vila do Abraão Ilha Grande📍 Plage Lopes Mendes Ilha Grande📍 Cachoeira da Feiticeira cascade Ilha Grande📍 Ruines Lazareto prison Ilha Grande📍 Kayak baie Abraão Ilha Grande📍 Pico do Abraão point de vue Ilha Grande📍 Parc État Ilha Grande forêt atlantique
1

Vila do Abraão, Ilha Grande's only real village, opens onto a cove surrounded by jungle-covered peaks. No cars: dirt lanes, colorful pousadas, moored boats. The entire island has been a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve since 1992 and inscribed as a World Heritage Site in 2019.

⏱ 60 min
2

Praia de Lopes Mendes stretches for 3 km of immaculate white sand, ranked among the world's most beautiful beaches. Count on 2 hours of walking round-trip from Abraão through Atlantic forest to reach it—the trail is part of the experience. Offshore winds and waves also make it the island's surf spot.

⏱ 180 min
3

The Lazareto ruins testify to the island's troubled past: a 19th-century colonial lazaretto for sick passengers landing in Brazil, then a maximum-security prison closed in 1994. The ruins scattered in the vegetation are accessible on foot from Abraão.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike to Cachoeira da Feiticeira: 1h30 walk from Abraão through rainforest, crossing clearings and clear-water streams. The waterfall plunges 15 meters into a natural pool ringed by boulders and dense vegetation.

⏱ 180 min

Kayak or paddle from Abraão: 20 minutes' paddling to reach Praia de Crena, where restaurants serve seafood caught that morning. The bay's interior waters stay calm all day.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset from Pico do Abraão, a hill accessible in 30 minutes' walk from the village: panoramic view over the bay, forested peaks, and the lights of Vila do Abraão glowing at twilight.

⏱ 60 min

Ilha Grande

A roadless island where jaguar tracks cross jungle trails to hundred secret beaches

★★★★★
📍 Plage Aventureiro réserve biologique Ilha Grande📍 Lagoa Azul piscine naturelle snorkeling Ilha Grande📍 Saco do Céu baie Ilha Grande📍 Praia de Dois Rios ancienne prison Ilha Grande📍 Pico do Papagaio randonnée Ilha Grande📍 Excursion bateau archipel Ilha Grande📍 Plongée épaves récifs Ilha Grande
1

Praia do Aventureiro, protected by an integral biological reserve, is one of the island's most remote beaches. Accessible only by boat or a 4-hour trail from Abraão, its calm waters, white sand, and forest plunging into the sea make it timeless.

⏱ 180 min
2

Ilha Grande's Lagoa Azul—not to be confused with the namesake site in Angra dos Reis—is a natural pool formed between two rocks, with translucent blue waters, accessible by boat from Abraão in 20 minutes. One of the island's best snorkeling spots.

⏱ 90 min
3

Saco do Céu—the Bay of Heaven—is an enclosed cove whose name comes from the sky's reflection in its perfectly calm waters. Surrounded by forested mountains and nearly closed upon itself, this bay is renowned for blazing sunsets.

⏱ 60 min
4

Praia de Dois Rios is accessible on foot from Abraão via a 2-hour forest trail. This long isolated arc of sand borders the site of the former prison—foundations and some walls survive in the vegetation, recalling the island's decades of penitentiary isolation.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Ascent of Pico do Papagaio (987 m): 4h30 round-trip from Abraão, 956 m elevation gain through jungle. Panoramic view over the entire island, Angra dos Reis Bay, and the coast all the way to Paraty. One of the Costa Verde's most spectacular treks.

⏱ 270 min

Boat excursion around the island: stops at Ilha Grande's Lagoa Azul for snorkeling on coral reefs, swimming in the emerald waters of Saco do Céu, stops at deserted beaches. Sea turtles, multicolored fish, and dolphins offshore on good days.

⏱ 300 min

Scuba diving from Abraão: Ilha Grande's seabeds shelter coral reefs, wrecks, and exceptional fauna. Jorge Grego and Lagoa Azul dive sites are accessible from the village in under 30 minutes by boat.

⏱ 180 min

Day 8

Angra Dos Reis

Where 365 islands scatter across emerald waters like jewels beneath forested peaks

★★★★☆
📍 Baie 365 îles Angra dos Reis📍 Port historique centre Angra dos Reis📍 Lagoa Azul piscine naturelle Angra dos Reis📍 Île Gipóia Praia das Flechas Angra dos Reis📍 Excursion bateau archipel Angra dos Reis📍 Forêt atlantique cascades Angra dos Reis📍 Igreja Nossa Senhora Carmo Angra dos Reis
1

Angra dos Reis Bay is dotted with 365 islands—according to legend, one for each day of the year. This colonial port founded in 1502 by navigator Gonçalo Coelho was one of the most active on the Brazilian coast for trade routes between Europe and South America.

⏱ 45 min
2

Igreja Nossa Senhora da Lapa da Boa Morte (1632) and Igreja Nossa Senhora do Carmo (1758) dot Angra's historic center. Praça Nilo Peçanha and its old buildings recall the era when the city served as a stopover for caravels restocking fresh water before the great crossing.

⏱ 60 min
3

Ilha da Gipóia, the archipelago's second island, harbors beaches popular with families and a festive atmosphere around the floating bars of Praia do Dentista. Further south, Praia das Flechas is calmer, ideal for swimming.

⏱ 180 min
4

Angra dos Reis Bay harbors one of Brazil's largest concentrations of shipwrecks, making its seabeds an exceptional dive site. Jorge Grego and Angra's Lagoa Azul dive spots allow you to observe multicolored fish, corals, and wrecks side by side.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

The ilhatour—a day-long boat excursion through the archipelago—is Angra dos Reis' signature activity. Swimming stops in the natural pools of Angra's Lagoa Azul (distinct from Ilha Grande's namesake site, deeper and flanked by cliffs), snorkeling on reefs, lunch stop on a white-sand beach, tour of private islands and their villas.

⏱ 360 min

Hike to Banqueta waterfalls in Angra's mainland Atlantic forest: Véu de Noiva and Borboleta Azul falls succeed each other in a corridor of dense vegetation. Natural pools allow swimming along the way.

⏱ 180 min

Hang-gliding flight over the bay: from Angra's heights, an aerial view of the island chain, the forest plunging into the sea, and Ilha Grande in the background reveals this archipelago's extraordinary scale.

⏱ 60 min

Day 9

Petropolis

An emperor's mountain retreat where European palaces rise from Brazilian cloud forest

★★★★☆
📍 Musée Impérial palais Dom Pedro II Petrópolis📍 Cathédrale São Pedro Alcântara Petrópolis📍 Palácio de Cristal Petrópolis📍 Avenida Koeler villas impériales Petrópolis📍 Centre historique Petrópolis📍 Brasserie artisanale Petrópolis📍 Palácio Amarelo Petrópolis
1

The Imperial Museum occupies Dom Pedro II's summer palace, built in 1845 in neoclassical style at the heart of the city he founded by decree in 1843. Polished parquet floors, music salons, Sèvres porcelain, and the imperial crown adorned with 639 diamonds are presented as they were in Empire times. The visit is conducted in slippers provided at the entrance.

⏱ 120 min
2

Cathedral São Pedro de Alcântara is a neo-Gothic edifice completed in 1925. Its stained glass colors the nave with golden light. The tombs of Dom Pedro II, Empress Teresa Cristina, and Princess Isabel rest in the side chapel.

⏱ 45 min
3

Palácio de Cristal is a glass-and-metal greenhouse prefabricated in France and assembled in Petrópolis in 1884. Princess Isabel organized exhibitions and high-society receptions here. Its transparent structure, with some panes tinted green and blue, now hosts exhibitions and concerts.

⏱ 30 min
4

Avenida Koeler and Rua do Imperador concentrate the finest villas from the imperial era: neoclassical, neo-Gothic, Anglo-Norman, and Alpine chalets succeed each other along canals where the Quitandinha River flows. A Brazilian Paris lost in the mountains.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll through Petrópolis' historic center from the Imperial Museum to the Cathedral, passing Palácio Amarelo and the canals bordered by preserved Victorian villas.

⏱ 120 min

Visit a craft brewery: Petrópolis, colonized by German immigrants in the 19th century, is Brazil's beer capital. The city's craft breweries produce lagers and ales following Germanic tradition, offering vat tours and tastings.

⏱ 90 min

Sound and Light show at the Imperial Museum Thursday–Saturday evenings: the palace's pink facade lights up, projections evoke the imperial family's life and 19th-century Brazilian history.

⏱ 60 min

Teresopolis

Where granite spires pierce the sky and the Finger of God points to heaven

★★★★☆
📍 Parc national Serra dos Órgãos Teresópolis📍 Dedo de Deus pic granit Serra dos Órgãos📍 Mirante do Soberbo point de vue Teresópolis📍 Trilha Cartão Postal randonnée Serra dos Órgãos📍 Pedra do Sino sommet Serra dos Órgãos📍 Cascade Véu da Noiva Serra dos Órgãos📍 Igreja Santa Teresa Teresópolis
1

Serra dos Órgãos National Park, created in 1939, spans 20,000 hectares between Teresópolis and Petrópolis. Its name comes from its granite peaks that evoke a cathedral's organ pipes. Dedo de Deus—the Finger of God—rises to 1,692 m and appears on Rio de Janeiro State's flag.

⏱ 60 min
2

Mirante do Soberbo is the park's iconic viewpoint: a panoramic terrace accessible by car from Teresópolis headquarters, with a direct view of Dedo de Deus and the entire Serra dos Órgãos range.

⏱ 60 min
3

The park's lower section from the Teresópolis entrance offers manicured gardens, accessible waterfalls, natural swimming pools, and the Trilha Suspensa (1.3 km elevated boardwalk trail, wheelchair-accessible).

⏱ 120 min
4

Igreja Santa Teresa d'Ávila, a neo-Gothic church overlooking Teresópolis' central square, gives the city its name—named after Teresa Léoni who acquired it in the 19th century. It marks the starting point for any downtown visit.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Trilha Cartão Postal hike (1.2 km) from park entrance: climb to viewpoint at 1,320 m elevation, spectacular view of Dedo de Deus and the entire range. Easy, round-trip in 2 hours.

⏱ 120 min

Trilha Pedra do Sino hike for seasoned hikers: 8h round-trip, passing Véu da Noiva waterfall. From the summit at 2,263 m, Guanabara Bay and Rio de Janeiro spread across the horizon.

⏱ 480 min

Climbing on the park's granite walls: Dedo de Deus is considered the birthplace of sport climbing in Brazil. Local operators offer guided outings for all levels.

⏱ 240 min

Day 11

Rio De Janeiro Leme

★★★★☆

1

Corcovado rises to 710 m. Christ the Redeemer, inaugurated in 1931, stands 28 m tall, arms outstretched. From the summit platform: Copacabana, Ipanema, Guanabara Bay, Sugarloaf, and Tijuca Forest spread 360°.

⏱ 90 min
2

Sugarloaf (396 m) is reached by two cable cars from Praia Vermelha. The view embraces the entire city, spectacular in late afternoon when golden light illuminates the bay.

⏱ 120 min
3

Escadaria Selarón counts 215 steps covered with over 2,000 ceramic tiles collected worldwide. Begun in 1990 and completed in 2013, this stairway links Lapa to Santa Teresa.

⏱ 30 min
4

Santa Teresa is Rio's Montmartre: steep cobbled lanes, colorful colonial houses, artists' workshops. The historic streetcar (bonde) still connects downtown to the neighborhood.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike from Parque Lage to Corcovado: 3h through Tijuca's rainforest, +700 m elevation gain. Arrive directly beneath Christ.

⏱ 180 min

Sunset cruise in Guanabara Bay: schooners depart from Urca. Sugarloaf and Corcovado seen from the water.

⏱ 120 min

Evening in Lapa: the arches of the 18th-century colonial aqueduct dominate Rio's largest off-season street carnival. Live samba in bars open until dawn.

Rio De Janeiro Leme

★★★★☆
📍 Plage Copacabana Rio de Janeiro📍 Plage Ipanema Arpoador Rio de Janeiro📍 Forêt Tijuca Parc Lage Rio de Janeiro📍 Museu do Amanhã Praça Mauá port Rio de Janeiro📍 Praça XV Paço Imperial centre historique Rio📍 MAC Niterói Oscar Niemeyer Rio de Janeiro📍 Cathédrale São Sebastião Rio de Janeiro
1

Tijuca Forest covers 33 km²—the world's largest urban tropical forest. Ocelots, monkeys, 300 bird species. Parque Lage, at its entrance, combines gardens, waterfalls, a 19th-century mansion, and contemporary art gallery.

⏱ 120 min
2

Cathedral São Sebastião, built between 1964 and 1979 by Edgar Fonseca, is a conical pyramid 96 m high and 104 m in diameter. Four giant stained-glass windows run from floor to ceiling.

⏱ 30 min
3

Museu do Amanhã (Museum of Tomorrow), inaugurated in 2015 on the renovated port at Praça Mauá, is an architectural feat by Santiago Calatrava. Its interactive exhibits explore humanity's great environmental and climate challenges.

⏱ 90 min
4

Praça XV and its surroundings form Rio's historic colonial heart. The Paço Imperial (1743), former royal residence, Igreja Nossa Senhora do Carmo, and lanes of the old Portuguese city tell Rio before the beaches.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Early morning walk on Copacabana's paved boardwalk (4.5 km) to Ipanema: jogging, beach volleyball, surfing. Leave valuables at the hotel.

⏱ 90 min

Head to Arpoador, between Copacabana and Ipanema, 30 minutes before sunset. Cariocas applaud when the sun disappears. Free, authentic, unmissable.

⏱ 30 min

Visit Niterói's Museum of Contemporary Art (MAC) across the bay: Oscar Niemeyer's white flying saucer perched on a cliff, with panoramic views of Rio and the bay. Ferry crossing from downtown Rio.

⏱ 180 min

Rio's Jardim Botânico, founded in 1808 by Dom João VI, sprawls across 54 hectares at Corcovado's foot. Its imperial avenue of royal palms planted in 1842 is one of Brazil's most famous. Orchids, bromeliads, Victoria Amazonica, and a butterfly greenhouse.

⏱ 90 min
2

Ipanema and Leblon, Rio's two chicest beaches, stretch 4 km facing the Atlantic. Numbered kiosks (postos) structure beach social life: each posto has its vibe, clientele, and roaming vendors of mate and açaí.

⏱ 120 min
3

Museu Nacional de Belas Artes, founded in 1937 in a neoclassical palace downtown, holds Brazil's largest collection of 19th-century Brazilian painting plus European works brought by the royal court in 1808.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Saturday feijoada lunch in Santa Teresa or Lapa: black bean and smoked meat stew, caipirinhas, and live samba. The carioca weekend ritual.

⏱ 120 min

Ipanema hippie market (Praça General Osório) on Sunday mornings: crafts, jewelry, paintings, semi-precious stones, and Brazilian culinary specialties. Rio's largest street market.

⏱ 120 min

Dois Irmãos hike from Vidigal favela: 45 minutes climbing the two iconic peaks overlooking Ipanema. 360° view over the coast, lagoons, Christ, and bay. The trailhead starts from Vidigal's summit, accessible by collective moto-taxi.

⏱ 120 min
