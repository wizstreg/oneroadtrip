✨ Highlights
  • Isla del Sol: north-south trek across the Incas' sacred island on Lake Titicaca
  • Salar de Uyuni: mirror effects during rainy season on the world's largest salt desert
  • Laguna Colorada: thousands of pink flamingos in a blood-red lake at 4,278 m
  • La Paz: urban cable car, Witches' Market, and Cholitas Wrestling
  • Death Road: 64 km cycling descent into the cloud forest of the Yungas
📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September

⏳ How many days

To make the most of it, plan between 4 and 8 days.

☀️ Weather

Dry Season (May-October): Best weather overall, minimal rainfall, clearer skies, ideal for Salar de Uyuni, peak tourist season.

Wet Season (November-April): Heavy rains, challenging road conditions, Salar de Uyuni becomes mirror-like, lush landscapes, fewer tourists.

Shoulder Seasons (April-May, October-November): Good weather transitions, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for most activities.

High Altitude Considerations: La Paz and Altiplano best visited during dry season, less altitude sickness risk with clear weather.

🌍 Discover

World's highest capital and salt flats create otherworldly landscapes alongside ancient Incan heritage. Experience authentic indigenous culture in dramatic Andean settings with incredible value for money. Lake Titicaca's floating islands and death road adventures thrill visitors. Bolivia offers authentic South American experiences with stunning natural phenomena.

📋 Notes

14-day itinerary. La Paz: 3 days outbound (D4-D6), 2 days return (D11-D12). 4x4 Salar + Sud Lipez circuit: 3 days included in D8-D10 (Colchani, Laguna Colorada, return to Uyuni). Progressive acclimatization: Copacabana 3,812m → La Paz 3,600m → Uyuni 3,653m → Sud Lipez 4,000-4,850m.

Copacabana

Where pilgrims gather on the shores of the world's highest navigable lake

★★★☆☆
1

The brilliant white Basilica of Our Lady of Copacabana stands proudly on Plaza 2 de Febrero. Built in 1601 in Mannerist style, it houses the Black Virgin of Copacabana, Bolivia's patron saint, enshrined since 1583 in a lavishly decorated altar.

⏱ 45 min
2

Head to the basilica forecourt to witness the blessing of cars. Bolivian families decorate their vehicles and have them sprinkled with holy water to invoke the Virgin's protection—a daily ritual unique in the world.

⏱ 20 min
3

Climb Cerro El Calvario: 120 meters of elevation gain lined with crosses and votive altars. From the summit at 3,960 m altitude, Copacabana Bay and the vast expanse of Lake Titicaca spread before you, with the snow-capped peaks of the Cordillera Real as backdrop.

⏱ 60 min
4

Stroll along the lakefront and its wooden piers. Local fishermen unload their trout; beachfront restaurants cook them in garlic, butter, or as ceviche. Here Lake Titicaca sits at 3,812 m altitude—the world's highest navigable lake.

⏱ 30 min
5

Copacabana's market occupies the corner of Pando and Jauregui streets. Aymara Cholitas, in layered skirts and bowler hats, sell fresh produce, coca leaves, and local handicrafts. An essential stop before heading to the docks.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Kayak or pedal boat outing on Lake Titicaca from the port. The water, an intense blue at this altitude, offers a unique perspective on the bay and mountains.

⏱ 60 min

Hike along the Yampupata Peninsula: take a taxi to Sampaya (20 min), then walk the ridges overlooking the lake for 2 to 3 hours to Yampupata village, where boats depart for Isla del Sol.

⏱ 60 min

Sample lake trout at the small waterfront restaurants. Trout in garlic, butter, or ceviche—the fish are caught that same morning. The ispis, tiny fried fish sold on the Tiquina docks during the journey, are also worth trying.

⏱ 60 min

Isla Del Sol

The sacred birthplace of the Inca empire, rising from Titicaca's turquoise waters

★★★★★
1

At the island's north end, the Chinkana Labyrinth is Isla del Sol's grand ceremonial complex. Its stone corridors supposedly served as a training center for Inca priests. Nearby, the puma-shaped Sacred Rock is the legendary spot where, according to Inca mythology, Manco Cápac and Mama Ocllo emerged to found the empire.

⏱ 50 min
2

In Yumani, the Inca Stairway counts 206 steps carved into rock. It leads to the Sacred Fountain, a three-spouted water source the Incas considered the source of eternal youth and spiritual purity.

⏱ 30 min
3

The island holds about a hundred ruins scattered among Inca agricultural terraces, pastures, and eucalyptus-scented trails. The silence is absolute: no cars, no motors—just wind and lapping waves.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

North-south trek: take the morning boat to Challapampa in the north, then walk to Yumani in the south—10 to 12 km along ridgelines. The route passes ruins and offers 360° panoramas of the lake and snow-capped Andean peaks. Allow 3 to 5 hours depending on your pace.

⏱ 60 min

Sunset from the heights of Yumani. At 3,800 m, the low light turns the lake to copper and the Andean peaks to violet silhouettes. Arrive at least 30 minutes early.

⏱ 60 min

Overnight on the island in simple accommodation in Yumani or Challapampa. At 3,800 m, the night sky is ink-black. The southern hemisphere stars, without light pollution, are stunning.

⏱ 60 min

Isla Del Sol

The sacred birthplace of the Inca empire, rising from Titicaca's turquoise waters

★★★★★
1

The Pilkokaina Palace clings to the island's southern tip. Built by Inca Tupac Yupanqui, only one floor of this two-level palace remains—but the trapezoidal doorways and wall niches characteristic of Inca architecture are intact. The terraces plunge toward the lake.

⏱ 40 min
2

The island holds about a hundred ruins scattered among Inca agricultural terraces, pastures, and eucalyptus-scented trails. The silence is absolute: no cars, no motors—just wind and lapping waves.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Swim at Challapampa beach, one of Lake Titicaca's rare sandy beaches. The water is cold but crystal-clear.

⏱ 60 min

Return by boat to Copacabana late morning, then bus to La Paz (3.5h). The route crosses the Tiquina isthmus where buses and passengers cross the strait separately on flat-bottomed boats.

⏱ 60 min

La Paz

A gravity-defying capital carved into canyon walls beneath eternal snows

★★★★★
1

The Killi Killi viewpoint, perched on Santa Bárbara hill, offers the most complete panorama of La Paz. Below, red roofs cascade down the canyon to El Alto on the plateau above. In the distance, the snow-capped peaks of the Cordillera Real close the horizon.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Mercado de las Brujas (Witches' Market) stretches along Calle Linares between Sagarnaga and Santa Cruz streets. Stalls overflow with dried llama fetuses, magic stones, medicinal herbs, and mysterious powders: offerings to Pachamama and Aymara remedies. The yatiris, in their black hats, read fortunes in coca leaves.

⏱ 50 min
3

The San Francisco Church dominates the plaza of the same name. Its 18th-century mestizo baroque façade blends indigenous motifs with Spanish ornamentation. The interior nave houses gilded sculptures and religious paintings. The bell tower offers stunning views of La Paz.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Ride the Mi Teleférico network upon arrival: the red line connects the bus terminal to downtown in 20 minutes, offering a striking first aerial view of the La Paz canyon and the colorful neighborhoods packed within.

⏱ 60 min

La Paz

A gravity-defying capital carved into canyon walls beneath eternal snows

★★★★★
1

Plaza Murillo is Bolivia's political heart. The neoclassical Government Palace, the Legislative Palace, and the 19th-century Metropolitan Cathedral frame it. Illuminated at night, the plaza bustles with newspaper vendors, masked shoe shiners, and pigeons.

⏱ 30 min
2

Valle de la Luna lies 10 km from downtown. Erosion has sculpted clay and sandstone into spires, miniature canyons, and pinnacles that resemble a lunar surface. Marked trails allow a 45-minute circuit. Afterward, head to the Sopocachi neighborhood for an almuerzo completo at one of its restaurants: soup, main course, dessert, and chicha for a few bolivianos—this is La Paz's bohemian and gastronomic quarter.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

The Feria 16 de Julio in El Alto, every Thursday and Sunday, is one of South America's largest open-air markets. Hundreds of thousands of vendors line several kilometers of streets: smuggled electronics, auto parts, textiles, food, medicinal herbs. A sensory experience and slightly chaotic—keep your belongings close.

⏱ 90 min

Cholitas Wrestling show in El Alto every Sunday afternoon. These women in layered skirts and long braids battle in a wrestling ring in a wildly colorful spectacle. Beyond the entertainment, it's a powerful message of Bolivian women's empowerment.

⏱ 60 min

1

The Parque Urbano Central in the Miraflores neighborhood is La Paz's main green space. Its shaded paths along the Choqueyapu River offer a peaceful counterpoint to downtown's hustle. This is where Paceños picnic on Sundays between football matches on the grass fields.

Hike in Valle de las Ánimas, 30 minutes from downtown. This eerie canyon holds rock formations in obelisks and pre-Columbian worship sites. According to local beliefs, the protective spirits Achachilas reside there.

Uyuni

Gateway to the world's most surreal landscape, where earth and sky become one

★★★☆☆
1

The train cemetery is 3 km from Uyuni. About fifty locomotives and wagons from the early 20th century rust on abandoned tracks. These trains carried ore from Andean mines to Chilean ports—the dream of a grand Bolivian rail network that never came to be.

⏱ 40 min
2

In Colchani, a village at the Salar's edge, artisans work salt extracted from the flats: decorative objects, salt blocks, raw furniture. A small artisanal refinery explains the salt transformation process. Just outside, the Ojos de Sal—underground water springs piercing the crust—reveal the geothermal activity beneath the desert.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Salar de Uyuni stretches across 10,582 km², the world's largest salt desert, at 3,663 m altitude. Its white crust, formed about 10,000 years ago when an inland sea dried up, conceals beneath its halite polygons 40% of the world's lithium reserves.

⏱ 90 min
4

Late afternoon, departure meeting with the guide and 4x4 team: route briefing, equipment check (warm sleeping bag required), vehicle assignments. The 3-day circuit officially starts the next morning at 10:30 AM from downtown Uyuni.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

3-day 4x4 circuit in the Salar and Sud Lipez. Day 1: train cemetery, Colchani, Incahuasi, night in salt hotel. Day 2: Laguna Colorada and its pink flamingos, Arbol de Piedra, Sol de Mañana geysers, Polques hot springs. Day 3: Laguna Verde and Dali Desert, return to Uyuni.

⏱ 60 min

Perspective photos on the Salar. The perfectly flat surface tricks the eye: play with miniature objects in the background to create optical illusions. The best angles come in late afternoon when the low light tints the white with pink.

⏱ 60 min

Stargazing from the Salar on a moonless night. At 3,663 m altitude with no light pollution, the Milky Way is visible to the naked eye. New moon nights between May and September offer the best conditions.

⏱ 60 min

Colchani

Where salt workers extract white gold from the edge of an endless desert

★☆☆☆☆
1

Colchani, population 600, lives entirely to the rhythm of salt. The local cooperative extracts and processes some 25,000 tons annually—exported throughout Bolivia and to Brazil. In the small artisanal refinery, workers demonstrate the transformation stages: grinding, iodization, bagging. The visit lasts 10 to 15 minutes but provides a complete understanding of the Salar as an economic resource.

⏱ 20 min
2

The Salt Museum in Colchani, built entirely of salt bricks, displays sculptures, models, and objects illustrating the history of salt trade on the Altiplano. For centuries, llama caravans traveled up to 560 km to carry salt to Tarija, returning loaded with coca and corn.

⏱ 15 min
3

The Ojos del Salar: these are small underground water springs that pierce the salt crust in perfect circles. Water surges from the depths, revealing that beneath 10 billion tons of salt lie immense brine reserves—and half the world's lithium reserves.

⏱ 20 min
4

Incahuasi Island emerges in the middle of the Salar like a rocky mirage. Its cardón cacti reach 10 meters tall and can be 1,000 years old. From the island's summit, accessible by a marked trail, the Salar extends as far as the eye can see in all directions—absolute white to the horizon.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Playa Blanca salt hotel, the first hotel built entirely from salt blocks extracted from the Salar, is now a quirky photo stop in the white desert. Its walls, floor, and furniture are made of the same material as the landscape surrounding it.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Perspective games on the Salar: the extraordinarily flat surface—less than 50 cm elevation change over 120 km—creates perfect optical illusions. Giant bottles, tiny travel companions, trompe-l'oeil scenes... The 4x4 guide knows the best poses.

⏱ 60 min

Sunset over the Salar. The low light transforms the white to gold and pink. In the rainy season (December-March), a thin layer of water creates the mirror effect: the sky reflects identically over several hundred kilometers.

⏱ 60 min

Night in a salt hotel at the Salar's edge. The thick walls maintain a stable temperature despite the nighttime cold that can drop below zero between April and October.

⏱ 60 min

Laguna Colorada

A crimson mirror at the roof of the Andes, where flamingos wade through fire

★☆☆☆☆
1

Laguna Colorada sits at 4,278 m altitude and covers 60 km² with a depth of only 35 cm. Its blood-red color—shifting from brown to deep red depending on time and wind—comes from Dunaliella salina algae and mineral sediments accumulated on the bottom. On its shores whitened by borax, sodium, and gypsum, thousands of flamingos wade in silence.

⏱ 90 min
2

Three flamingo species share the lagoon: the Andean Flamingo (110 cm, yellow legs), the Chilean Flamingo (105 cm, gray-blue legs with red knees), and the James's Flamingo (90 cm, most common, yellow beak with black tip). Born white, their plumage turns pink thanks to pigments from the Dunaliella salina they filter. The lagoon has been a Ramsar site since 1990.

⏱ 60 min
3

In the Siloli Desert, the Árbol de Piedra (Stone Tree) is a volcanic formation sculpted by millennia of sand-laden winds. This 7-meter-high rock shaped like a tree, with a base narrower than its top, is one of the Sud Lipez's iconic symbols.

⏱ 30 min
4

Laguna Hedionda—"stinking lagoon" in Spanish, due to sulfurous fumes—is a first ornithological stop before Colorada. Its calm waters reflect the surrounding volcanoes and shelter a permanent flamingo population. Laguna Cañapa, farther north, also offers striking Andean reflections.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Birdwatching at dawn on the shores of Laguna Colorada. Flamingos stir with the first light, wading and filtering mud. The morning's low light heightens the contrast between the lake's red, the borax's white, and the birds' pink.

⏱ 60 min

Photo stops at the circuit's intermediate lagoons: Laguna Cañapa (turquoise), Laguna Hedionda (perfect reflections), Laguna Honda. Each body of water has its own color depending on mineral composition.

⏱ 60 min

Night in a refuge in San Juan or near Laguna Colorada. Above 4,000 m, nighttime temperatures regularly drop below -10°C between May and October. The night sky, without any light pollution, has a star density rarely matched.

⏱ 60 min

Uyuni

Gateway to the world's most surreal landscape, where earth and sky become one

★★★☆☆
1

The Sol de Mañana geysers are visited at dawn from the refuge: at 4,850 m altitude, the geothermal field ejects jets of steam and boiling mud over 10 meters high. The sulfurous fumaroles dissipate into the freezing morning air—a spectacle both beautiful and inhospitable.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Polques hot springs (4,400 m) offer the ultimate contrast: after a night at -10°C, the 30°C hot spring pools allow a bath in the Andean desert, facing Laguna Chalviri and its flamingos.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Dali Desert takes its name from the surrealist painter. Multicolored rocks, improbable volcanic formations, llamas crossing the track in the middle of nowhere: the landscape looks straight from a canvas. In the background, the Licancabur volcano (5,916 m) marks the border with Chile.

⏱ 30 min
4

Laguna Verde closes the circuit. At 4,400 m, its electric green waters—tinted by concentrations of copper, arsenic, and sulfur—change color depending on wind speed stirring the sediments. The Licancabur volcano reflects in its waters at early morning.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Morning bath in the Polques hot springs at dawn, after the geysers. The 30°C water warms bodies after a freezing night. Flamingos from Laguna Chalviri are active just a few dozen meters away.

⏱ 60 min

Return to Uyuni by 4x4 in the afternoon, via a different track along the Andes foothills. The journey offers views of the Cordillera Occidental volcanoes and vicuña herds.

⏱ 60 min

La Paz

A gravity-defying capital carved into canyon walls beneath eternal snows

★★★★★
1

The Coca Museum (Museo de la Coca) at 906 Linares traces the millennial history of the sacred leaf: its ritual role among the Tiwanaku, its medicinal use by the Aymara, and the political debate on the difference between coca and cocaine. Essential insight for understanding Andean culture before leaving Bolivia.

⏱ 45 min
2

The National Museum of Ethnography and Folklore (MUSEF) occupies an 18th-century baroque palace on Calle Ingavi. Its collections trace Tiwanaku, Aymara, and Quechua cultures through ritual textiles, carnival masks, and pre-Columbian ceramics.

⏱ 60 min
3

Calle Jaén, La Paz's best-preserved colonial street, houses several small museums: the Museo del Litoral (War of the Pacific and the lost sea), the Museo de Metales Preciosos with its pre-Inca gold pieces. Its ochre and blue façades offer a colonial interlude in the urban whirlwind.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Artisan market on Sagarnaga Street: alpaca wool textiles, Cholita bowler hats, charangos and zampoñas. Perfect for last-minute purchases—prices are better than at the airport.

⏱ 60 min

Traditional Andean music evening in the peñas of San Francisco neighborhood: charango, siku, wankara, and Andean voices in a low-ceilinged room. Peñas start around 9 PM.

⏱ 60 min
