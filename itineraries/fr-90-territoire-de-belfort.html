Territoire de Belfort Road Trip: The Citadel and the Lion

📅 2 days📍 6 stops🚗 97 km⏱ 3h13

Belfort → Giromagny → Rougemont Le Chateau → Delle → Beaucourt

Belfort → Giromagny → Rougemont Le Chateau → Delle → Beaucourt

The Territoire de Belfort packs Vauban fortifications, the heroic resistance of 1870, and Vosges mountain peaks into six unforgettable days. Belfort asserts itself with Bartholdi's 22-meter Lion and the citadel dominating the strategic Gap. The Ballon d'Alsace (1,247 m) opens the Vosges massif to GR5 hikers. Beaucourt tells the industrial epic of the Japy dynasty, from artisanal clockmaking (1777) to France's second-largest industrial concentration (5,000 workers by 1880). Château de Rougemont crowns the itinerary with medieval ruins and sweeping Vosges-Jura-Alps panoramas.

Belfort

Where Bartholdi's mighty Lion guards the Vauban citadel

★★★★☆
📍 Lion de Bartholdi Belfort📍 Citadelle de Belfort📍 Porte de Brisach Belfort📍 Cathédrale Saint-Christophe Belfort📍 Vieille ville Belfort
1

The Lion of Belfort measures 22 meters long and 11 meters high. Carved from pink Vosges sandstone between 1875 and 1880 by Bartholdi, it commemorates the town's legendary 103-day resistance during the siege of 1870-1871.

⏱ 75 min
2

Pass through the Porte de Brisach, the only remaining gate from Vauban's fortifications. Carved fleurs-de-lis and the Sun King's motto adorn its stone facade.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Citadel has dominated the town since the 17th century. Vauban designed this fortress to control the Belfort Gap between the Vosges and Jura mountains.

⏱ 90 min
4

The Grand Souterrain offers an audiovisual journey beneath the ramparts. Projections and scenography trace the military history of this strategic stronghold.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb to the Tour de la Miotte, a medieval tower perched on the heights above town. The panorama sweeps across Belfort, the Savoureuse valley, and the southern Vosges.

⏱ 90 min

Follow the banks of the Savoureuse on foot or by bike. The cycling path winds through residential neighborhoods and green spaces.

Belfort

Where Bartholdi's mighty Lion guards the Vauban citadel

★★★★☆
📍 Musée d'Histoire Belfort📍 Étang des Forges Belfort📍 Cathédrale Saint-Christophe Belfort📍 Place de la Grande Fontaine Belfort

The Citadel's keep houses the History Museum. Collections on the 1870 siege, scale models of fortifications, and military memorabilia testify to Belfort's strategic past.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Cathédrale Saint-Christophe dates from the 18th century. Its sober neoclassical architecture shelters a pipe organ classified as a historic monument.

Wander through the Place de la Grande Fontaine and the lanes of the old town. Colorful Vosges sandstone facades line Vauban-era squares.

Activities

The Étang des Forges spreads across 7 hectares on the eastern edge of town. Walking paths, playgrounds, and birdwatching await in this urban park.

⏱ 90 min

Follow the Hauts de Belfort circuit (7 km) from La Miotte down to the Forts of Justice and Salbert. Sweeping views over the town and southern Vosges ridges.

Stop 3

Giromagny

Mountain village at the foot of the Ballon d'Alsace and the GR5

★★★★★
📍 Ballon d'Alsace sommet📍 Statue Jeanne d'Arc Ballon d'Alsace📍 Fort Dorsner Giromagny📍 Église Saint-Jean-Baptiste Giromagny📍 Sentier minier Giromagny
1

At the summit of the Ballon d'Alsace (1,247 m), the statue of Jeanne d'Arc erected in 1909 marks the border between three departments. The orientation table built in 1888 by the Alpine Club offers views stretching to the Alps on clear days.

⏱ 90 min
2

Fort Dorsner was built in 1875 as part of the Séré de Rivières system to guard the Savoureuse valley. This pink sandstone fortress towers over Giromagny and the Ballon d'Alsace road.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Église Saint-Jean-Baptiste in Giromagny houses a remarkable Verschneider organ. Its 19th-century neoclassical architecture is worth a visit.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hike the GR5 from the Ballon d'Alsace down to Giromagny. This 13-km stage with 224 m descent follows the red rectangle blazes of the Club Vosgien. Pass through the mountain farmhouse inns of La Chaumière and Wissgrut.

⏱ 270 min

The mining trail winds through former lead-silver extraction sites. Discover the industrial history of the Upper Savoureuse valley from the Middle Ages to the 20th century.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 4

Rougemont Le Chateau

Medieval ruins crowning a summit with views to the Alps

★★★★☆
📍 Château de Rougemont ruines📍 Chapelle Sainte-Catherine Rougemont📍 Mont des Boules panorama 736m📍 Sentier botanique Rougemont📍 Parc naturel Ballons Vosges
1

The ruins of Château de Rougemont crown the Montagne des Boules at 736 meters altitude. Built in the late 12th century by Count Frédéric II of Ferrette, it guarded the southern gateway to Alsace and the Roman road from Langres to Basel. Besieged in 1375 by Enguerrand de Coucy's mercenaries, it was abandoned and later demolished on Louis XIV's orders in 1673. Restored between 1977 and 1980, the ruins have been a classified historic monument since 1996.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Chapelle Sainte-Catherine, rebuilt in 1876, stands beside a spring once believed to cure eye ailments. In the 18th and 19th centuries, pilgrimages here were so popular that offerings collected were 75 times greater than those at the parish church.

⏱ 60 min
3

From the summit, the panorama stretches from the Vosges to the Jura with views of the Swiss Alps on clear days. Rougemont nestles against the mountain at an average elevation of 450 meters.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb to the castle through forest trails from the Plainot picnic area. This 3-hour circuit traverses 1,249 hectares of communal forest integrated into the Natura 2000 site and the Ballons des Vosges Regional Natural Park.

⏱ 180 min

Follow the botanical trail at Mont Marty. Discover Vosges flora with valley views. This 247-hectare Natura 2000 site preserves forest habitats.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 5

Delle

Where Franco-Swiss railways bridge borders and histories

★★★☆☆
📍 Gare internationale Delle📍 Frontière franco-suisse Delle📍 Ligne Belfort-Bienne Delle
1

The international station at Delle, reopened in 2018, serves the Belfort-Bienne line via Delémont. Just 1,600 meters from Boncourt in Switzerland, it marks the terminus for CFF trains and provides connections to TGV services from Belfort-Montbéliard.

⏱ 45 min
2

The railway heritage testifies to Delle's strategic role since 1868. After the annexation of Alsace in 1870, the line was rerouted around German territories to maintain the Franco-Swiss connection without passing through Mulhouse.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Cross the border on foot or by bike to Boncourt. Explore the Swiss canton of Jura: Porrentruy lies 15 km away with its medieval old town and castle.

⏱ 240 min

The Malsaucy leisure base (30 minutes from Delle) offers sailing, kayaking, and stand-up paddleboarding from April to October. A free 6,000 m² supervised beach opens from June to August. The Eurockéennes festival draws 100,000 spectators in early July.

⏱ 180 min

Stop 6

Beaucourt

Birthplace of France's clockmaking revolution and the Japy dynasty

★★★★☆
📍 Musée Japy Beaucourt📍 Pendulerie Japy Beaucourt📍 Cités ouvrières Japy Beaucourt📍 Demeures patronales Beaucourt📍 Temple protestant Beaucourt
1

The Musée Japy chronicles the industrial dynasty's epic story. Housed in the former 1892 clockworks, it displays clocks, typewriters, pumps, and the family's diverse production lines. From a simple workshop in 1777, the enterprise grew to become France's second-largest industrial concentration under the Second Empire, employing 5,000 workers.

⏱ 90 min
2

Seven Japy châteaux survive in Beaucourt out of the thirteen the family built. These opulent industrialist mansions were erected during the company's golden age in the 1880s.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Japy workers' housing estates exemplify 19th-century industrial paternalism. Six complexes have been studied by heritage surveys: workers' lodgings, communal kitchens, and private schools.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Protestant temple built by the Japy family recalls founder Frédéric Japy's Anabaptist origins. The Protestant community shaped Beaucourt's social life.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk the Japy heritage circuit (5 km): clockworks, industrialist châteaux, workers' estates, temple. Pick up a brochure at the museum to discover the Japy imprint on the town's urban landscape.

⏱ 150 min

Cycle the FrancoVéloSuisse route toward Delle and Porrentruy. This 37-km cross-border itinerary follows the Allaine valley into the Swiss canton of Jura.

⏱ 180 min
