Barcelona and Costa Brava Road Trip: Coves and Villages

📅 8 days📍 7 stops🚗 352 km⏱ 5h36

Barcelona → Girona → Cadaques → Figueres → Tossa De Mar

From the forest of columns at the Sagrada Família to the medieval ramparts of Tossa de Mar, passing through Dalí's surrealist house at Portlligat and the Game of Thrones streets of Girona - Catalonia is a region where every stop has a powerful story to tell.

✨ Highlights
  • Gaudí's Sagrada Família, under construction since 1882 and the world's largest unfinished Catholic church
  • The Vila Vella of Tossa de Mar, the only intact medieval fortified town on the Catalan coast
  • The Dalí House-Museum at Portlligat, the artist's residence for 52 years facing the sea
  • The Dalí Theatre-Museum in Figueres, Spain's second most visited museum
📆 Best period

May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 5 and 9 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather, mild temperatures, blooming landscapes, ideal for sightseeing, moderate crowds, excellent value.

Fall (September-October): Comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, harvest season, perfect beach weather, cultural activities, good prices.

Summer (July-August): Hottest weather, peak beach season, highest prices, very crowded, festival season, intense heat inland.

Winter (November-March): Mild temperatures south, cooler north, fewer crowds, lowest prices, cultural attractions, ski season Pyrenees.

Day 1

Barcelona

Where Gaudí transformed stone into living nature

★★★★★
📍 Sagrada Família Barcelona📍 Casa Batlló Passeig de Gràcia Barcelona📍 Casa Milà La Pedrera Barcelona📍 Barcelona Cathedral Gothic Quarter📍 Park Güell Barcelona
1

The Sagrada Família tells the story of a construction begun in 1882 and still unfinished - making it the world's largest incomplete Catholic church. Gaudí took over the project in 1883 and devoted the final 43 years of his life to it. In October 2025, the central tower reached 162 meters, finally surpassing Montjuïc mountain by just one meter - Gaudí believed no human creation should exceed nature. Book your tickets online well in advance, even off-season: 4.5 million visitors flock here annually.

⏱ 90 min
2

Just steps away, Passeig de Gràcia showcases Gaudí's two wildest houses. Casa Batlló, renovated between 1904 and 1906, is nicknamed the 'house of bones' - its limestone pillars evoke skeletons and its balconies, skulls. Casa Milà (La Pedrera), built between 1906 and 1912, was the last house Gaudí designed before dedicating himself entirely to the Sagrada Família. Its rooftop, with twisted chimneys, offers one of the city's finest vantage points - the classic spot for photographing the Sagrada Família through the arches.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic) stands on the ruins of the Roman colony of Barcino, founded around 15 BC. The Cathedral of Saint Eulalia, begun in 1298, houses 13 white geese in its cloister - a tradition linked to the legend of Saint Eulalia, co-patron of Barcelona, who reportedly suffered 13 tortures in Roman times. Get lost in the narrow streets: the Pont del Bisbe, a neo-Gothic footbridge built in 1928 between the Palau de la Generalitat and Casa dels Canonges, is one of the city's most photographed corners.

⏱ 60 min
4

Park Güell is a 17-hectare park that Gaudí designed from 1900 as a private garden-city for 60 families - a project that never materialized for lack of buyers. The main terrace, covered in multicolored trencadís mosaics and shaped like a winding serpent, offers panoramic views across the entire city to the sea. Arrive early in the morning: the monumental zone is ticketed and subject to visitor quotas.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Start the day with coffee and a coca de recapte (Catalan flatbread with toppings) in the Eixample, the modernist district built according to Cerdà's 1860 plan - its perfect grid with chamfered corners is visible from the air. The markets at Sant Antoni or La Boqueria are ideal for a standing breakfast.

⏱ 45 min

In late afternoon, take the cable car or funicular up to Montjuïc to catch the sunset over the port and Barcelona's skyline. The castle and Laribal gardens are worth exploring - the view embraces the entire bay.

⏱ 90 min

In the evening, head to the Gràcia neighborhood for an aperitif vermouth in one of its old-school bars. This neighborhood, formerly an independent village annexed to Barcelona in 1897, has preserved its lively little squares and village atmosphere. Continue with creative tapas in the Eixample or El Born.

⏱ 90 min

Day 2

Barcelona

Where Gaudí transformed stone into living nature

★★★★★
📍 Barceloneta beach Barcelona📍 Palau de la Música Catalana Barcelona📍 Picasso Museum Barcelona El Born📍 Santa Maria del Mar Barcelona📍 La Rambla Barcelona
1

The Palau de la Música Catalana, built between 1905 and 1908 by Lluís Domènech i Montaner, is one of the world's most beautiful concert halls and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Its red-brick facade and interior skylight of multicolored stained glass filter unique light during concerts. If you're not attending a performance, the guided tour of the hall comes highly recommended.

⏱ 60 min
2

The El Born neighborhood houses the Picasso Museum, installed in five medieval palaces from the 13th and 15th centuries connected to one another. The collection traces the painter's formative years in Barcelona, notably featuring the complete Las Meninas series (58 variations on Velázquez, created in 1957). The visit requires at least 90 minutes - book your tickets online.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Basilica of Santa Maria del Mar, built between 1329 and 1383 in Catalan Gothic style, is often preferred to the cathedral by Barcelonans themselves. Its nave is taller, brighter, more austere. It was collectively financed by the port's stevedores - each neighborhood resident brought a stone. It's 'the cathedral of the sea' described by Ildefonso Falcones in his novel of the same name.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

In the morning, stroll down La Rambla to the port, then follow the waterfront promenade to Barceloneta beach. This neighborhood, built in the 18th century on a grid plan to house families expropriated from the Ciutadella, retains its characteristic narrow streets. The beach stretches nearly a kilometer - perfect for a morning swim before the crowds arrive.

⏱ 90 min

In the afternoon, explore the markets and boutiques of the El Born neighborhood, one of the city's most creative areas. Don't miss the medieval archaeological remains of the Born market, visible beneath the 1876 iron hall - an entire neighborhood preserved under glass after its destruction in 1714.

⏱ 60 min

Finish with dinner in the Poble-sec neighborhood, at the foot of Montjuïc, where Carrer de Blai avenue is famous for its pintxos bars. It's the local alternative to tourist tapas: cheaper, more authentic, and the portions are generous.

⏱ 90 min

Day 3

Girona

Where Carolingian ramparts meet Game of Thrones filmography

★★★☆☆
📍 Girona Cathedral📍 City Walls Passeig de la Muralla Girona📍 Jewish Quarter El Call Girona📍 Colorful houses Onyar River Girona📍 Arab Baths Girona
1

Girona Cathedral dominates the city from its monumental staircase of 91 steps. Begun in the 11th century and completed in the 18th, it boasts the world's widest Gothic nave: 23 meters, surpassed only by St. Peter's Basilica in the Vatican. Inside, don't miss the Tapestry of Creation, an 11th-century Romanesque embroidery depicting the medieval calendar - a unique textile masterpiece in Europe. Game of Thrones fans will immediately recognize this staircase: Jaime Lannister rode up it in season 6.

⏱ 60 min
2

From the ramparts of the Passeig de la Muralla - a 3 km free walk along 9th-century Carolingian walls - you'll have plunging views over the old town's rooftops and, on clear days, the Pyrenees ridges on the horizon. It's Girona's best vantage point, and entry is free.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Jewish Quarter of El Call is one of the best preserved in Europe. The Jewish community lived here for over 600 years before being expelled in 1492. The stepped alleyways, stone arches, and tightly packed houses have changed function but retained their exact medieval morphology. The Museum of Jewish History, installed in one of the Call houses, presents Hebrew inscriptions and ritual objects recovered during excavations.

⏱ 60 min
4

The colorful houses along the Onyar River are Girona's most photographed image. These ochre, pink, and yellow facades reflecting in the river have been painted in the tones mandated by local tradition. For the best angle, position yourself on the Pont de Ferro - yes, the one designed by Eiffel's workshops, the same Gustave Eiffel, delivered in 1877 before his Parisian tower was built.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

In the morning, visit the Arab Baths (built in 1194 following the model of Roman baths, despite their name) and the 12th-century Benedictine monastery of Sant Pere de Galligants - two stops that are unremarkable from the outside but whose interiors are truly worth seeing. The Baths were transformed into a Jewish mikveh in the 13th and 14th centuries.

⏱ 60 min

For Game of Thrones fans, free guided tours of the season 6 filming locations are organized from the tourist office. More than 11 series sets were filmed in Girona between the cathedral (Great Sept of Baelor), the Jewish quarter, and the Arab Baths (House of Black and White). Even if you've never watched the series, the tour is a good excuse to explore the old town thematically.

⏱ 90 min

In the evening, Plaça de la Independència and its terraces are Girona residents' headquarters for aperitifs. Order a glass of Catalan cava and xuixos - fried cream pastries, an absolutely local specialty you won't find anywhere else. Don't leave without eating at least one.

⏱ 60 min
Little Home Girona9.4

Day 4

Cadaques

White-washed village where Dalí lived and created for fifty years

★★☆☆☆
📍 Salvador Dalí House Museum Portlligat Cadaqués📍 Santa Maria Church Cadaqués📍 Cap de Creus Natural Park📍 Cadaqués Port📍 Cadaqués Museum
1

Cadaqués is accessible only by a winding road from Roses - which long preserved it from mass tourism and attracted Dalí. The village of white houses by the water has changed very little since the early 20th century. Start with the 16th-century Church of Santa Maria, whose gilded baroque altarpiece was painted by local artists. The port with its colorful boats moored facing the white facades is one of the most copied views of the Costa Brava.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Salvador Dalí House-Museum in Portlligat, 2 km north of the village, is the only permanent residence the artist ever had. In 1930, he bought a 22 m² fisherman's hut - penniless, estranged from his family - and expanded it over 40 years by buying neighboring cottages, piece by piece. He lived and worked there until Gala's death in 1982, then never returned. Reservations are mandatory and groups are limited to 8 people - book at least 2 to 3 weeks in advance in summer.

⏱ 90 min
3

Cap de Creus, 15 km from Cadaqués, is the easternmost point of the Iberian Peninsula and one of Catalonia's wildest natural parks. The granite rocks sculpted by the tramontana - the north wind that blows here up to 150 km/h - directly inspired Dalí's lunar landscapes. The lighthouse restaurant offers a 180-degree view of the sea. Bring a jacket even in summer: the tramontana can pick up without warning.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Hike to Cap Norfeu, between Roses and Cadaqués: from the viewpoint, the view plunges over a succession of wild coves accessible only on foot or by boat. The well-marked 6 km round-trip trail from the parking lot takes about 3 hours.

⏱ 180 min

In late afternoon, settle into one of the tapas bars on Cadaqués' waterfront. The low light on the white facades and boats at this hour is extraordinary - Dalí himself said that Cadaqués had taught him aesthetics. Grilled cuttlefish and Empordà anchovies are the local specialties to order.

⏱ 60 min
Hotel Playa Sol9.3

Day 5

Figueres

Where Dalí was born and built his greatest surrealist masterpiece

★★☆☆☆
📍 Dalí Theatre-Museum Figueres📍 Sant Ferran Fortress Castle Figueres📍 Salvador Dalí birthplace Figueres📍 Rambla Figueres📍 Empordà Museum Figueres
1

The Dalí Theatre-Museum is the only architectural work that Dalí designed himself, from A to Z. He built it on the ruins of Figueres' municipal theatre, burned at the end of the civil war in 1939. Dalí wanted to make it 'the world's largest surrealist object' - and he's not wrong: giant eggs crown the walls, loaves adorn the facade, and a glass geodesic dome tops the central hall. The museum opened on October 28, 1974, and has become Spain's second most visited museum after the Prado. Dalí is buried in its crypt, beneath the stage, against his will - he wished to rest at Púbol alongside Gala.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Sant Ferran fortress is 18th-century Europe's largest bastioned fortification: 32 hectares, 3.2 km perimeter, 9 million liters of water capacity in its underground cisterns. Built from 1751 under the reign of Fernando VI to block a potential French invasion, it nevertheless capitulated twice without fighting - to French troops in 1793 and again in 1808. Salvador Dalí did his military service here. Allow at least 2 hours, including the cistern tour if available.

⏱ 120 min
3

Salvador Dalí's birthplace, at 6 Carrer Monturiol, is open to the public with an audio-visual tour tracing his childhood in Figueres. It's the intimate complement to the Theatre-Museum spectacle: here, you understand where the man came from before measuring the myth.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The Rambla de Figueres, with its cafes and neoclassical and modernist buildings, is the city's commercial heart. Stop here for lunch with Empordà products: Alt Empordà anchovies are among Spain's finest. The markets on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday at Plaça del Gra are an opportunity to buy olives, Empordà AOC oils, and local cheeses.

⏱ 60 min

Wine tasting of AOC Empordà wines at one of the downtown bodegas or in the surroundings: Empordà vineyards produce powerful reds and mineral whites influenced by the tramontana. The region also produces an excellent sweet fortified wine, Garnatxa d'Empordà.

⏱ 60 min

Day 6

Tossa De Mar

The only intact medieval fortress town on Catalonia's shore

★★★☆☆
📍 Vila Vella Tossa de Mar📍 Platja Gran Beach Tossa de Mar📍 Lighthouse Tossa de Mar📍 Cala Pola Tossa de Mar📍 Municipal Museum Tossa de Mar
1

The Vila Vella of Tossa de Mar is the only intact medieval fortified town on the entire Catalan coast, listed as a historic monument in 1931. Its walls were built from 1186 to defend against Barbary pirates - Ramon de Berga, the abbot who governed the town, ordered their construction after centuries of raids. The perimeter includes three cylindrical towers and four keeps, including the Clock Tower (which housed the town's only public clock) and the Tower of Homage overlooking Codolar beach. Climb up to the lighthouse - built in 1917 on the site of the old castle - for the best coastal view.

⏱ 75 min
2

Platja Gran stretches 400 meters at the foot of the medieval ramparts - it's one of the rare beaches in the world where you sunbathe beneath the walls of a 12th-century fortress. The waters are clear and sheltered. For more tranquility, walk 15 minutes to Cala Codolar, a small rocky nook nestled beneath the Vila Vella ramparts, ideal for snorkeling.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Tossa Municipal Museum, founded in 1935 in the former governor's residence, is Spain's first contemporary art museum. It holds a painting by Marc Chagall - who stayed in Tossa and dubbed it 'blue paradise' - and Roman mosaics from the 1st-century BC villa dels Ametllers found on the hillside.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Sea kayaking along the cliffs and coves between Tossa and Sant Feliu de Guíxols: the coast is carved with caves and small beaches accessible only by water. Local operators offer 2 to 4-hour guided outings. Allow 2 hours to paddle to Cala Giverola.

⏱ 120 min

Snorkeling at Es Codolar cove or Cala Pola: the transparent waters of the Costa Brava allow you to see the bottom several meters deep. Posidonia (seagrass meadows) and sea urchins are the most common species. Bring your mask if you have one.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner at the port with suquet de peix, a local fish stew simmered with potatoes and saffron - the house specialty in almost every waterfront restaurant. The region's white wine (Alella or Empordà) pairs perfectly with seafood.

⏱ 90 min

Day 7

Barcelona

Where Gaudí transformed stone into living nature

★★★★★
📍 Palau Güell Barcelona El Raval📍 Boqueria Market Barcelona📍 Santa Maria del Mar El Born Barcelona📍 Barceloneta beach promenade📍 Tibidabo Barcelona
1

If you missed sites during days 1 and 2, this final day in Barcelona is your chance to catch up. Palau Güell, in the El Raval neighborhood, is the first major commission Barcelona gave to Gaudí (1886-1890) - much less known than the Sagrada Família or Casa Batlló, but fascinating for its dark interiors and sculptural chimneys on the roof. The El Raval neighborhood has changed dramatically since the 1990s: it now mixes museums, galleries, and restaurants from around the world.

⏱ 60 min
2

La Boqueria market, on La Rambla, is one of Europe's most photographed markets. Arrive before 9 am to avoid tour group crowds and find the freshest stalls. Freshly pressed fruit juices are worth the stop. However, the tapas in the front rows are often prepared for tourists - go eat a bit further into El Raval for a more local address.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Final swim at Barceloneta or stroll along the waterfront to Port Olímpic, built for the 1992 Olympic Games. The transformation of Barcelona's waterfront after 1992 is one of Europe's most studied urban projects: 4 km of beaches created from scratch where there had been only warehouses and railway tracks.

⏱ 90 min

To finish the trip on a high note, climb to Tibidabo by blue tram or funicular: the hill at 512 meters dominates the entire bay of Barcelona. On clear days, you can see all the way to Majorca. The amusement park at the summit, opened in 1901, is one of Europe's oldest still operating.

⏱ 120 min
