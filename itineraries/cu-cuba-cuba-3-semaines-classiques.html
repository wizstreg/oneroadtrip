Havana

★★★★★
1

Havana was founded in 1519 by the Spanish, and its historic center has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1982. Start at Plaza de la Catedral: the San Cristóbal Cathedral, completed in 1777 by the Jesuits, features an asymmetrical baroque facade - its two towers aren't the same width, a rare occurrence in colonial architecture. The square is framed by the Palacio del Marqués de Aguas Claras and the Palacio del Conde de Lombillo, both from the 18th century.

⏱ 60 min
2

Walk down Calle Obispo, a 460-meter pedestrian artery connecting Plaza de Armas to Parque Central. Along the way, step inside the Taquechel pharmacy, preserved in its 19th-century state with porcelain jars and mahogany furniture. Plaza de Armas, Havana's oldest square (late 16th century), hosts the Palacio de los Capitanes Generales (1776) - its wooden paving stones were laid to muffle the noise of carts that disturbed the governor.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Castillo de la Real Fuerza, built between 1558 and 1577 after the sacking of Havana by French corsair Jacques de Sores in 1555, is the oldest stone fortress in the Americas. Atop its tower, the bronze weathervane La Giraldilla (1632) has become the symbol of Havana.

⏱ 45 min
4

El Capitolio, constructed from 1926 to 1929 by architect Eugenio Rayneri Piedra, measures 207 meters long. Its dome rises to 92 meters - the world's third-highest at the time. Inside, the bronze Statue of the Republic (14.6 m, 30 tons) was then the world's third-largest covered statue. Beneath the dome, a 25-carat diamond set in the floor marks kilometer zero for all Cuban roads. The Paseo del Prado, laid out in 1772 and redesigned in 1928 by French urban planner Forestier, connects the Capitol to the Malecón.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

The drive from the airport area takes about 30 minutes. Take your time strolling toward the historic center at your own pace.

⏱ 40 min

Late afternoon, walk along the Malecón, an 8-km promenade built from 1901 to 1958. Locals call it "the world's longest sofa": at sunset, hundreds of Habaneros sit on the seawall facing the ocean. Every evening at 9 PM, a cannon shot rings out from La Cabaña fortress - a tradition perpetuating the ancient closing of the city walls.

⏱ 60 min

Panoramic tour in a 1950s American car through the Vedado and Miramar districts. These vehicles, kept running by Cuban mechanics since the 1962 embargo, have become a symbol of the country.

⏱ 60 min

Havana

★★★★★
1

Plaza de la Revolución, designed in the 1920s by French urban planner Forestier, covers 72,000 square meters. The José Martí Memorial (1953-1958), shaped like a five-pointed star carved from gray marble from the Isle of Youth, rises to 109 meters - the highest point in Havana. Take the elevator or climb 567 steps to the observation deck. At its base, the white marble statue of a seated, pensive Martí measures 18 meters. On the Interior Ministry facade, the giant portrait of Che reproduces Korda's 1960 photograph.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Museum of the Revolution occupies the former presidential palace (1913), used by Cuban presidents until 1959. The Hall of Mirrors, inspired by Versailles, contrasts sharply with the guerrilla warfare displays. At the rear, the Granma Pavilion houses the 18-meter yacht that carried 82 revolutionaries from Mexico in 1956.

⏱ 60 min
3

Plaza Vieja, laid out in 1559, served as a marketplace, a bullfighting arena in the 18th century, then a parking lot in the 1950s - before being restored from the 1990s onward. The surrounding houses span four centuries of architecture. The Camera Obscura installed in the former Palacio Gómez Vila offers a 360° view of the city.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Rum Museum, housed in an 18th-century colonial mansion, traces the production of Cuban rum: molasses fermentation, distillation in copper stills, aging in white oak barrels. An animated scale model reproduces the workings of a 19th-century sugar mill.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Craft market at the Almacenes de San José, a 19th-century port warehouse converted into a covered hall beside the bay.

⏱ 45 min

Street art walk through Callejón de Hamel, a 200-meter alley transformed into an open-air gallery since 1990 by muralist Salvador González. Afro-Cuban frescoes, scrap metal sculptures - on Sundays around noon, percussionists play rumba there.

⏱ 60 min

Vinales

★★★★☆
1

The Viñales Valley (UNESCO since 1999) extends over 132 square kilometers in the Sierra de los Órganos. The mogotes - limestone buttes from the Upper Jurassic (~150 million years ago) - reach 300 meters high. Their formation is unique: underground rivers carved immense caves whose vaults collapsed, leaving the walls standing. The name "Sierra de los Órganos" comes from Spanish navigators who saw in the mogotes the silhouette of organ pipes.

⏱ 60 min
2

Visit a tobacco plantation. Pinar del Río produces ~80% of Cuban tobacco. The veguero explains each stage: sowing in September, harvesting leaf by leaf from January to March, drying 45 to 60 days in casas de tabaco - those ubiquitous wooden and palm-thatch barns. Methods remain manual: ox-drawn plows, no machinery or pesticides.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Cueva del Indio, rediscovered in 1920, served as shelter for indigenous people before the Spanish arrival. Electrified in 1952, it's visited on foot for 300 meters (stalactites and cave paintings), then by motorboat for 400 meters along the San Vicente underground river that cuts through the mogote.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Horseback ride among mogotes and tobacco fields. The trails cross tropical vegetation - royal palms, kapok trees, and Microcycas calocoma, a living fossil cork palm from the Cretaceous declared a national monument.

⏱ 120 min

Vinales

★★★★☆
1

The Mural de la Prehistoria, painted on the face of Pita mogote, measures 120 m high by 180 m wide. Commissioned in 1961 by Fidel Castro, it took four years to complete by Leovigildo González Morillo, director of cartography at the Academy of Sciences. The fresco depicts the evolution of life in the Sierra: ammonites, dinosaurs, giant mammals, Guanahatabey Indians.

⏱ 45 min
2

Climb up to the Mirador de Los Jazmines (4 km south of Viñales). The panorama embraces the entire valley: tobacco fields in the foreground, dark green mogotes with sheer flanks, Sierra de los Órganos in the background.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hike through the Valle del Silencio, a rural trail among the mogotes away from tourism. The path crosses plantations of tobacco, cassava, bananas and coffee. In season (January-March), pickers work the fields.

⏱ 150 min

Cuban coffee tasting and cigar-rolling demonstration at a farmer's home. The torcedor assembles the leaf by hand: wrapper, binder and filler, without glue. An experienced roller produces 100 to 150 cigars per day.

⏱ 60 min

Cayo Jutías, an islet in the Colorados archipelago, is linked to the mainland by a pedraplén - a causeway road built over the sea, bordered by mangroves. Cienfuegos

★★★★☆
1

Cienfuegos (UNESCO since 2005), nicknamed "The Pearl of the South," is the only Latin American city founded by the French under Spanish rule. On April 22, 1819, Louis de Clouet and 36 Bordeaux settlers laid out the first streets around Parque José Martí. The perfect grid plan, inspired by the Enlightenment, remains intact. Around the square: the Teatro Tomás Terry (1890), inspired by Italian theaters, the neoclassical cathedral with French stained glass, and the Palacio Ferrer (1918) whose dome offers a bay view.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Palacio de Valle, in the Punta Gorda district, was built between 1913 and 1917 by Italian architect Alfredo Colli for Asturian sugar magnate Acisclo del Valle. The building blends Moorish, Gothic, Byzantine and Baroque styles - like a Moroccan kasbah transplanted to the Caribbean. Spanish, Italian and Cuban artisans worked together on it. After the 1959 Revolution, plans to convert it into a casino were abandoned. Climb to the rooftop terrace: panoramic view over the entire Cienfuegos bay.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll along the Paseo del Prado of Cienfuegos - the longest in Cuba at 900 meters - then along the Malecón facing the bay. Cienfuegos Bay is one of the world's largest natural bays.

⏱ 60 min

Trinidad

★★★★☆
1

Trinidad (UNESCO since 1988), founded in 1514 by conquistador Diego Velázquez, is Cuba's best-preserved colonial town. Plaza Mayor, paved with irregular cobblestones, is surrounded by 18th and 19th-century mansions built by the sugar barons. The Museo Romántico (Palacio Brunet, 1812) preserves period furnishings: mahogany furniture, Bohemian crystal chandeliers, Sèvres porcelain.

⏱ 75 min
2

The San Francisco de Asís convent, built in 1813, houses the Museo de la Lucha Contra Bandidos (Museum of the Fight Against Bandits). Its yellow and green bell tower, visible from all over town, is Trinidad's main landmark. Climb the steps for a 360° view over the red-tiled roofs, the Escambray mountains and the Caribbean Sea to the south.

⏱ 60 min

Santiago De Cuba

★★★☆☆
1

The Castillo de San Pedro de la Roca (El Morro), a UNESCO site since 1997, was designed in 1637 by Italian engineer Giovanni Battista Antonelli to protect the bay from pirates. Built on a steep rocky promontory, the fortress unfolds over several terrace levels connected by flights of steps. It's the best-preserved example of 17th-century Spanish-American military architecture. At sunset, the view of the narrow bay entrance channel is striking.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Moncada Barracks, the country's second-strongest military installation under Batista, was attacked on July 26, 1953 by a group of 93 young revolutionaries led by Fidel Castro - a failed attack that cost most of them their lives but marked the beginning of the Cuban Revolution. Bullet impacts are still visible on the facade. Converted into a school after 1959, it houses the Museo Histórico 26 de Julio: photos, weapons, uniforms and period documents.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Santa Ifigenia Cemetery, Cuba's second-most important cemetery after Havana's, houses José Martí's mausoleum (1951) - a 23-meter octagonal tower oriented so sunlight illuminates the coffin draped in the Cuban flag. The changing of the guard takes place every 30 minutes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit the Museo Emilio Bacardí, founded in 1899 - Cuba's oldest museum. Archaeological collections (Egyptian mummies, Taíno artifacts), Cuban paintings and independence war memorabilia.
