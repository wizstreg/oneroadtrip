Ionian Islands Road Trip: Corfu, Paxos & Lefkada

📅 6 days📍 5 stops🚗 180 km⏱ 5h10

Corfu unfolds four centuries of Venetian architecture through its labyrinthine UNESCO-listed lanes, before the ferries carry you to Paxos — a 25 km² island blanketed in olive groves and carved into coves accessible only by sea. Lefkada rounds off the journey with its 72-metre white cliffs plunging into impossibly blue water, and Vassiliki where windsurfing sails fill the bay every afternoon.

✨ Highlights
  • Corfu's UNESCO-listed old town with its Venetian fortresses and labyrinthine lanes
  • Blue caves of Paxos and Antipaxos beaches accessible only by boat
  • Porto Katsiki and Egremni cliffs, ranked among the most beautiful beaches in the Mediterranean
  • Vassiliki, a European benchmark for windsurfing and kitesurfing
📆 Best period

May, June, September, October

To make the most of it, plan between 5 and 9 days.

Day 1

Corfou

Where Venetian kantounia, British cricket and Ottoman-defying fortresses share one spellbinding UNESCO old town

★★★★★
1

Corfu's old town has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2007 — a maze of narrow cobblestone lanes known as kantounia, lined with neoclassical facades in pastel hues inherited from centuries of Venetian rule. Venice held the island almost uninterrupted from 1386 to 1797, and it's the Republic that shaped this unique urban fabric, which neither the French nor the British managed to erase. Get up early: the lanes are stunning before 9am, empty and gilded by the morning light.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Old Fortress (Palaio Frourio) sits on a rocky headland separated from the town by a sea moat — the Contrafossa — which the Venetians dug to turn the citadel into an artificial island. It successfully repelled three major Ottoman sieges: 1537, 1571 and 1716. Inside, don't miss the Church of St George, whose golden facade resembles an ancient temple — the contrast is striking. Climb to the lighthouse at the top for the best panoramic view over the sea and the town.

⏱ 90 min
3

The New Fortress (Fortezza Nuova), built by the Venetians from 1577 on the hill overlooking the harbour, draws fewer visitors than the Old one — take advantage of that. Its underground galleries, bastions and tunnels carved into the rock form a fascinating labyrinth. The view over the harbour and the Ionian Sea from the battlements alone makes it worth the visit.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Palace of St Michael and St George, built by the British between 1819 and 1824 in neoclassical style, now houses the Museum of Asian Art — a collection of 11,000 pieces from China, Japan and India, bequeathed by a Greek diplomat. An unexpected combination for a Greek island. The entrance from the People's Garden also offers a fine angle on the Spianada.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Spianada Esplanade is the largest green public square in Greece. The arcaded Liston that lines its western edge was built during the French occupation, directly inspired by the Rue de Rivoli in Paris. This is where the Corfiots still play cricket, a tradition inherited from the British — one of the few places in Greece where the sport is practised. Late in the afternoon, grab a table on the terrace and watch local life unfold.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Lose yourself in the Campiello, the oldest neighbourhood in the old town, north of the Old Fortress. It's a tangle of kantounia so narrow that two people can barely pass, with carved Venetian wells popping up in tiny squares. Laundry dries between buildings, bougainvillea spills over ochre walls — the atmosphere is light-years away from mass tourism.

⏱ 60 min

On Orthodox Easter evening (in April), the Corfiots celebrate the Resurrection by hurling ceramic jars from their windows at 11pm sharp — a loud, festive spectacle unique to Greece. If you're not here for Easter, the religious processions around the relics of Saint Spyridon, the island's patron saint, are still well worth stopping for.

⏱ 45 min

Pastitsada is Corfu's signature dish: rooster (or veal) braised in a tomato sauce with Venetian spices, served over thick macaroni. The recipe is a direct trace of four centuries of Italian influence. Try it in an old-town taverna — the spots around the Campiello are the most authentic.

⏱ 60 min

Bourdeto is Corfu's other pride: fish (scorpionfish by preference) cooked in a tomato sauce fired up with hot paprika. It's a fisherman's recipe — rustic and bold. Pair it with a glass of Robola, the dry, mineral white from neighbouring Cephalonia, which cuts through the acidity of the sauce.

⏱ 60 min
Day 2

Corfou

Where Venetian kantounia, British cricket and Ottoman-defying fortresses share one spellbinding UNESCO old town

★★★★★
1

The Achilleion is one of the most romantic — and most tragic — palaces in the Mediterranean. Empress Sisi had it built between 1889 and 1891 in a neo-Pompeian style, 10 km south of the capital, on a 200,000 m² plot overlooking the Ionian Sea. She had taught herself Ancient Greek to read Homer in the original and wanted to pay tribute to Achilles, whom she saw as the embodiment of the Greek soul. After her assassination by an anarchist in 1898, Kaiser Wilhelm II bought the palace in 1907 and spent every spring there until World War I. In 1981, the first floor served as the casino set for the James Bond film For Your Eyes Only. Allow at least 1h30 for the gardens and the apartments.

⏱ 90 min
2

The peninsula of Kanoni, 4 km south of the capital, offers Corfu's most iconic view: in the foreground, Vlacherna Monastery perched on its tiny islet connected to the shore by a causeway, and behind it, the islet of Pontikonisi ('Mouse Island') crowned with dark cypresses. Homeric legend says it's the Phaeacian ship turned to stone by Poseidon after bringing Odysseus home to Ithaca. Arrive in the late afternoon for the best light.

⏱ 45 min
3

Paleokastritsa, 25 km to the northwest, is one of the most beautiful natural sites in the Ionian Islands: six interlocking bays, crystal-clear turquoise waters and limestone cliffs rising 30 to 50 metres. The Monastery of Panagia Theotokos, founded in 1225 on a rocky headland above the main bay, was according to tradition established after the discovery of an icon of the Virgin. The present buildings date from the 18th century. Modest dress is required to enter — cover-up garments are provided at the door. The view from the monastery gardens over the sea is among the finest on the island.

⏱ 90 min
4

The hilltop villages of Lakones and Makrades, above Paleokastritsa, offer plunging views over the six coves of the bay — a panorama the locals call the 'Balcony of Corfu'. These olive-grove villages have kept their traditional architecture and their unpretentious tavernas. Stop for a coffee or a simple terrace lunch.

⏱ 45 min
5

Corfu's Archaeological Museum, in town, houses the pediment of the Artemision, a 6th-century BC temple of Artemis discovered on the island: the central Gorgon stands nearly 3 metres tall and ranks among the best-preserved archaic sculptures in Greece. Allow about 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Book a boat trip from Paleokastritsa to explore the sea caves along the northwest coast. Small boats slip into limestone cavities where sunlight creates blue and green reflections on the water — local guides call one of them the 'Blue Light Cave'. The experience is at its best in the morning, when the sea is calm.

⏱ 90 min

Take a dip in Agios Spyridon bay at Paleokastritsa — it's the most accessible beach in the resort, a mix of sand and pebbles with shallow waters. Several outfitters rent sea kayaks to explore the coves that can't be reached on foot. Expect to pay around €25–35 for a half-day kayak rental.

⏱ 120 min

Sofrito is Corfu's quintessential Venetian dish: thin slices of veal pan-fried in a sauce of garlic, parsley, white wine and vinegar. The recipe dates back to 16th-century Venetian cooks. Pair it with a glass of Verdea, the dry white from Zakynthos — slightly oxidised and full-bodied, it holds its own against the bold flavours of the sauce.

⏱ 60 min

Mantolato, a hard nougat made with roasted almonds and thyme honey, is Corfu's traditional confection — old-town delicatessens have stocked it since the 19th century. It's the ideal edible souvenir to bring home: it travels well and keeps for several weeks.

⏱ 20 min

Day 3

Paxos

A pocket-sized island of twisted olive groves and turquoise coves that no road can reach

★★☆☆☆
1

Gaios, the capital of Paxos, stretches along a natural channel sheltered by two islets: Agios Nikolaos, which bears the ruins of a Venetian fortress built between 1424 and 1510, and Panagia, home to a small monastery. The pastel-coloured waterfront with its Venetian-influenced buildings is one of the most charming in the Ionian Islands. According to Greek mythology, Poseidon struck off the southern tip of Corfu with his trident to create Paxos as a love retreat for himself and Amphitrite. Standing here, you can believe it.

⏱ 60 min
2

The blue caves along Paxos' west coast are accessible only by sea — no road, no stairway. Local boats depart from Gaios or Lakka to cruise along the limestone cliffs, where sunlight filtered through the water casts blue and green reflections inside the cavities. Set aside half a day for the excursion and book ahead in July–August.

⏱ 120 min
3

Lakka, at the northern tip of the island, nestles at the head of a horseshoe-shaped bay with perfectly still waters — sailing boats moor here by the dozen in summer. The village is tiny (a handful of tavernas, flower-lined lanes) but the waterfront atmosphere at sunset is hard to beat. If you have access to a small boat, rent one and explore the pebble beaches along the north coast, unreachable any other way.

⏱ 60 min
4

Loggos is the most discreet of Paxos' three villages — a fishing port of a few dozen houses, with tavernas right on the water. It's the best address for a lunch of grilled fish in an authentic setting, well away from the organised excursions that flood Lakka in high season.

⏱ 45 min
5

The Tripitos Arch, on the west coast of Paxos, is a natural limestone arch several metres high jutting out above the sea. It's reachable on foot from the village of Magazia (about 20 minutes). Few tourists make this detour — it's one of the most dramatic landscapes on the island.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Book a boat trip to Antipaxos as soon as you arrive — the little sister island sits just 15 minutes to the south. Its beaches of Vrika and Voutoumi regularly rank among the finest in the Mediterranean — fine white sand and turquoise water that could pass for the Caribbean. The excursion typically takes a full day and includes a stop at the blue caves.

⏱ 360 min

Set off on the footpaths that wind through the centuries-old olive groves in the island's interior. The Republic of Venice actively promoted olive cultivation on Paxos from the 15th century to secure its oil supply — the gnarled trees you'll see are often several hundred years old. The coastal trail from Loggos to Lakka (roughly 2 hours) offers magnificent views.

⏱ 120 min

Paxos' bourdeto is a local twist on the Corfu classic: fish (scorpionfish or gurnard) simmered in a spicy tomato sauce, with a more pronounced kick than the Corfu version. Enjoy it at a harbour-side taverna in Gaios with a glass of tsipouro, the anise-scented grape marc spirit of the Ionians, served ice-cold.

⏱ 60 min

Amygdalota from Paxos are small, soft almond-and-icing-sugar biscuits traditionally baked in the island's monasteries for religious feast days. You'll find them in the artisanal grocery shops of Gaios — pick up a box for the ferry ride the next day.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 82 km · 3h
Day 4

Leucade

Where Sappho leapt and white cliffs drop 72 metres into impossible shades of blue

★★★☆☆
1

The castle of Agia Mavra (Santa Maura), standing at the edge of the channel that separates the island from the mainland, is the first thing you'll see as you cross the bridge. Built in the 14th century by the Orsini counts, it gave its medieval name to the entire island — Lefkada was known as Santa Maura until the 19th century. The Turks held it from 1479 to 1684, before the Venetians took it back. The grounds are freely accessible and the view over the lagoon and salt flats is beautiful in the late afternoon.

⏱ 45 min
2

Cape Lefkatas, at the island's south-western tip, is one of the most myth-laden sites in all of Greece. The white cliff drops 72 metres straight into the Ionian Sea. In antiquity, tradition held that those suffering from unrequited love would leap from this promontory into the sea — surviving the jump meant being cured of their passion. The poet Sappho (c. 630–580 BC) is said to have died here for the love of Phaon, according to legend. A lighthouse built in 1913 now stands on the site of the ancient temple of Apollo. On a clear day, you can see the coast of Epirus.

⏱ 60 min
3

Nydri, on the east coast, is the launching pad for boat trips to the small islands of the archipelago: Meganisi, Madouri, Sparti and, most famously, Skorpios — formerly owned by Aristotle Onassis, where he married Jackie Kennedy in 1968. Water taxis and organised excursions leave from the harbour throughout the day in summer. The marina is lively, with plenty of waterfront tavernas.

⏱ 45 min
4

Faneromeni Monastery, perched on a wooded hill 3 km north of the capital, offers the finest bird's-eye view of Lefkada town and the channel separating it from the mainland. The present buildings date from the 17th–18th centuries. Allow 20 minutes on foot from the car park.

⏱ 45 min
5

The town of Lefkada itself is worth an hour's wander through its lanes: the facades are built of painted corrugated iron on wooden frames — an earthquake-resistant technique honed over centuries of seismic experience, which gives the houses a Caribbean feel unique in the Ionians. The archaeological museum, in town, displays finds from the excavations of Dörpfeld, the German archaeologist who believed he had found the homeland of Odysseus here.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rent a boat from Nydri and explore the islets off the east coast at your own pace. Skorpios remains private property, but you can anchor nearby; Meganisi has several superb coves accessible only by sea. The east coast of Lefkada is sheltered from the prevailing winds — ideal for a relaxed day on the water, even without sailing experience.

⏱ 240 min

Eglouvi lentils are Lefkada's most prized agricultural product — this small mountain village at 700 metres altitude grows tiny, flavourful lentils that carry a Protected Designation of Origin (PDO). In soup with local olive oil or as a cold salad, they're simple and outstanding. Look for them in the grocery shops of the capital.

⏱ 60 min

Ladopita is a rustic Lefkadian cake made with olive oil, flour and raisins — no butter, no milk. It's a Lenten recipe handed down from the Venetian centuries. Dense and mildly sweet, it pairs perfectly with a Greek coffee on a terrace. You'll find it in the traditional bakeries of the old town.

⏱ 20 min

Agios Nikitas, 12 km northwest of the capital, is the most charming seaside village on the coast: its pedestrian lanes lead straight down to a small sandy beach, the tavernas sit at the water's edge and the crowds are more manageable than in Nydri. It's a good option for dinner or a late-afternoon swim.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 33 km · 1h05
Day 5

Vassiliki

Morning glass, afternoon thermals: the bay where Europe comes to windsurf beneath Lefkada's cliffs

★★☆☆☆
1

Porto Katsiki ('Goat Port') is one of the most photographed beaches in the Mediterranean. A concave limestone cliff drops sheer onto a strip of white pebbles and sand, lapped by water of an almost unreal blue-turquoise hue — locals attribute the colour to the geological composition of the seabed. From the car park at the top of the cliff, roughly a hundred steps cut into the rock lead down to the beach. Arrive before 10am in July–August: after that, the beach fills quickly and the staircase becomes a queue.

⏱ 120 min
2

Egremni, a few kilometres north of Porto Katsiki, stretches for nearly 2.5 km of golden pebbles at the foot of 150-metre cliffs. The earthquake of 17 November 2015 destroyed the old stairway of 347 steps and buried part of the beach under a landslide. Since 2022, a new stairway has been rebuilt, but the beach is still mainly reached by boat from Vassiliki — which keeps it wilder and less crowded than Porto Katsiki. The waves can be powerful: exercise caution with children.

⏱ 90 min
3

Vassiliki is a world-class windsurfing destination — the reliable afternoon thermals that blow across the bay from the open sea draw competitors and enthusiasts from around the globe. In the morning, the bay is a mirror-flat playground for beginners; from 2pm, the wind picks up and sails multiply. Dozens of windsurf and kitesurf schools cater to all levels.

⏱ 45 min
4

Karya, a mountain village in the centre of the island at 550 metres altitude, is famous throughout Greece for its handmade embroidery — the village women carry on a tradition dating back to the 19th century, producing intricate floral patterns. The local folklore museum tells the story. In summer, an artisan market livens up the central square. The view from the heights takes in both coasts of the island.

⏱ 60 min
5

Kathisma beach, halfway between the capital and Porto Katsiki on the west coast, is the largest organised beach on the island: golden sand, sunbeds, beach bars and occasionally paragliding sessions from the neighbouring hill. If Porto Katsiki feels too chaotic, Kathisma offers the same turquoise water with a little more comfort.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take a windsurfing lesson in Vassiliki — it's one of Europe's most renowned spots to learn or improve. Conditions are close to ideal: calm mornings for the basics, steady thermal winds in the afternoon for practice. Book your slot the day before — schools fill up fast in high season.

⏱ 180 min

Head out early by boat from Vassiliki to Egremni and Porto Katsiki — the perspective from the sea on those white cliffs plunging straight down is completely different from the view at the top. Water taxis and small excursion boats leave from the harbour from 9am.

⏱ 180 min

Savoro is a traditional preservation dish of Corfiot and Lefkadian fishermen: fish (sardines, mackerel) is fried, then marinated in vinegar, raisins and rosemary. The result is a surprisingly tangy-sweet preparation served cold — perfect for a light lunch after the beach.

⏱ 60 min

Pasteli, a sesame-and-honey bar, is the traditional beach snack of the Ionian Islands — people have been eating it since ancient Greece. Simple, energising and utterly unprocessed. Pick up a few from a grocery shop in Vassiliki before heading down to Porto Katsiki: you'll need the fuel for the climb back up.

⏱ 15 min
