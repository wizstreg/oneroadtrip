Salzburg

★★★★★
1

Hohensalzburg Fortress, one of the best-preserved castles in Europe, dominates the city from a rocky outcrop 120 metres high. Built in 1077 by Archbishop Gebhard, enlarged until the 16th century. The princely apartments, armoury and panoramic views across the Salzburg Alps from the ramparts justify the climb by funicular or on foot.

⏱ 120 min
2

Salzburg Old Town (Altstadt), a UNESCO-listed baroque ensemble built under the prince-archbishops of the 17th century. The Getreidegasse with its wrought-iron guild signs, Residenzplatz with its baroque fountain, and countless art galleries and boutiques create a unique urban stage set.

⏱ 90 min
3

Salzburg Cathedral (Dom), a northern Italian baroque masterpiece with three naves completed in 1628. The dome frescoes, monumental organs and bronze portals make this one of the finest religious buildings in the Alps. Mozart was baptised here on 28 January 1756.

⏱ 60 min
4

Mirabell Gardens, a 17th-century formal garden designed by Fischer von Erlach, with Hohensalzburg Fortress as backdrop. The geometric parterres, mythological statues, central basin and fountains create Salzburg's most famous photographic composition.

⏱ 60 min
5

Mozart's Birthplace (Mozarts Geburtshaus), Getreidegasse 9 - the third-floor apartment where Wolfgang Amadeus was born on 27 January 1756. The child's harpsichord, miniature violin and original manuscript scores are among the most moving pieces in this biographical museum.

⏱ 60 min
6

Prince-Archbishops' Residence (Residenz), a baroque palace of 180 rooms occupying an entire side of Residenzplatz. The Princes' Gallery houses a remarkable collection of Rembrandt, Rubens and Flemish paintings. The great reception hall with its trompe-l'oeil frescoes is one of the most impressive baroque spaces in Austria.

⏱ 75 min
7

Hellbrunn Palace, a 17th-century pleasure palace 4 km from the centre with its famous Wasserspiele (mechanical water features). Archbishop Markus Sittikus installed 265 hidden water jets to surprise his banquet guests. Still working, these hydraulic automata are unique in Europe.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Walk on the Mönchsberg, a wooded hill accessible by lift from the old town (€3). Aerial views over the entire city and castle from the terrace of Café Winkler. Museum of Modern Art (Museum der Moderne) at the summit with contemporary collection.

⏱ 60 min

Mozart concert or recital in one of Salzburg's historic settings: Mozarteum, Residence or Mirabell Palace. Daily chamber concerts in period costume in 18th-century salons are an authentic experience, not a tourist gimmick.

⏱ 60 min

Tour of the Salzburg lakes - excursion to nearby Salzkammergut (St Wolfgang, Mondsee, Wolfgangsee) 40-50 km from Salzburg. Mondsee with its baroque church (Sound of Music setting) and St Wolfgang with its Pacher altarpiece (1481) can be done in half a day.

⏱ 60 min

Market and Salzburg gastronomy at the Grünmarkt (daily market) near the cathedral. Specialities: authentic Mozartkugeln from Fürst (not the foil-wrapped version), farmhouse bread, alpine cheeses and Stiegl Goldbräu on tap in the Mönchsberg beer gardens.

⏱ 60 min

Gosau

★★★☆☆
1

The waters of Vorderer Gosausee shimmer emerald green - the Dachstein massif (2,995 m) reflects in it like an eye. Gosau locals call this lake 'the eye of God' and, seen from here, you understand why. The lake can be walked around in 45 minutes on a flat path, accessible May to October. Park at Parkplatz Vorderer Gosausee and arrive early morning to beat the tour buses.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Gosaukammbahn cable car climbs from the lake to Zwieselalm, a panoramic alpine pasture on the Gosaukamm ridge. At the top, the ridge trail offers 360° views over the Dachstein and Gosaukamm (2,458 m at Große Bischofsmütze). Times vary by season - generally open May to October.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Walk around the lake in 45 minutes on the Rundwanderweg, then if you have time continue on foot to Hinterer Gosausee - 30 minutes extra on a forest path. This second lake is wilder and far less crowded than its sibling.

⏱ 75 min

Hallstatt

★★★★★
1

Seven thousand years of salt mining: the Salzwelten Hallstatt is the oldest known salt mine in the world. The wooden staircase discovered inside dates from 1344 BCE - the oldest in Europe. Note: the mine is closed for renovation until 30 June 2026; a free shuttle to Salzwelten Altaussee is offered as an alternative. Book early.

⏱ 120 min
2

Tiny village, massive crowds - and yet Hallstatt remains beautiful. The Marktplatz and its houses clinging to the cliff face, squeezed between lake and rock, have been UNESCO-listed since 1997. Arrive by boat from Obertraun for the most cinematic entrance.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take the funicular early to avoid queues - the view from the Welterbeblick, 350 metres above the lake, is dizzying. Coming back down, walk along the east side of Hallstätter See: the raking morning light on the houses is worth the detour.

⏱ 90 min

Bad Aussee

★★★☆☆
1

Locals call it the 'capital of Trachten' - the dirndl and Tyrolean costume are still worn here daily, not for tourists. Bad Aussee is also the town of the famous Fasching carnival, first documented in 1768. The Marktplatz with its fountains and 19th-century bourgeois houses deserves an hour.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Kammerhofmuseum, housed in a late-Gothic building first mentioned in 1395, was the former administrative seat of the salt trade until 1926. Across 800 m² of exhibition space, it covers salt history, folk traditions and the Aussee carnival. The Emperors' Hall with its frescoes from 1740-65 is the highlight of the visit.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Kurpromenade follows the Ausseer Traun for several hundred metres among willows. The 'Sommerfrische' atmosphere of the Viennese bourgeoisie of the 19th century is still palpable - Archduke Johann and writer Adalbert Stifter stayed here. Alpine gardens on the opposite bank.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Browse the weekly market on the Marktplatz for cheeses and Aufschnitt (cold cuts) from the region - authentic atmosphere, no tourist performance. The vendors speak loud and fast. It's on Tuesdays.

⏱ 45 min

If the sun holds, climb to Tressenstein on the marked trail from the town centre - the Tressensteinalm and its panorama over the three lakes of Ausseerland is the region's reference viewpoint. Allow 1h30 return from the base.

⏱ 120 min

Altaussee

★★★☆☆
1

The best WWII story you've never heard - and it takes place under the Altaussee mountain. The Nazis had hidden Michelangelo's Bruges Madonna, the Ghent Altarpiece by the Van Eyck brothers and hundreds of other works here. The miners sabotaged the explosives to save them. This story, immortalised in the film The Monuments Men, can be visited at Salzwelten Altaussee, Austria's largest active salt mine - operating since the 12th century. The special guided tour 'Bomben auf Michelangelo' takes place every Friday at 4pm, booking required.

⏱ 90 min
2

Lake Altaussee gleams deep blue beneath the Totes Gebirge. The Seenrundweg circles it on 7 km of flat path - ideal for digesting the mine visit. The fishermen's houses and their colourful boats remind you that this lake is still living, not museumified.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Complete the Altausseer Seenrundweg in 2 hours at your own pace - the path follows both shores, the north bank in full sun in the afternoon, the south bank in shade. Stop at the Fischerhütte for grilled lake trout.

⏱ 120 min

Grundlsee

★★★☆☆
1

Three lakes, one journey - simple in theory, except that Toplitzsee can only be reached by traditional flat-bottomed boat (Plätte) or on foot. The Kammersee, right at the end, is the source of the River Traun. Schifffahrt Grundlsee offers the '3-Seen-Tour' from May to late October: nostalgic motor boat on Grundlsee, walk (1.6 km) to Toplitzsee, then Plätte.

⏱ 240 min
2

Toplitzsee has no dissolved oxygen below 20 metres depth - a particular chemistry that fuelled decades of rumours about Nazi treasure hidden in its waters. Divers did find crates, but no gold. Real relics are on display at the Fischerhütte on the lake shore.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Board the Schifffahrt Grundlsee from opening time - the crossing of Grundlsee takes 30 minutes, time enough to see the reflections of the Totes Gebirge. Allow minimum 3h30 for the three-lake loop. Departures several times daily May to October, tickets online or at the pier.

⏱ 210 min

Admont

★★★☆☆
1

No monastic library in the world approaches the hall at Stift Admont: 70 metres long, 14 wide, 11 high, 70,000 old books and frescoes by Bartolomeo Altomonte painted when he was over 80 (1775-1776). The sculptures by Josef Stammel - 'The Four Last Things' - are disturbingly modern. The monastery, founded in 1074, is the oldest in Styria. Reopened after restoration in 2008. Allow 1h30.

⏱ 90 min
2

Behind the Stift, the wild Enns rushes through the limestone gorges of the Gesäuse in permanent thunder. The suspension bridge at Haindlkar offers the best plunge into this landscape. The walls of Hochtor (2,369 m) drop almost vertically into the river.

⏱ 60 min
3

Gesäuse National Park, created in 2002, covers 120 km² of gorges and primary forest. It's Styria's only national park. The visitor centre in Admont presents the geology and wildlife - lynx and golden eagle are present, discreet.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you only do one hike in the Gesäuse, choose the Haindlkar from the Enns car park - 2 hours return, high glacial valley landscape with the Haller Mauern range as backdrop. Marked trail, intermediate level.

⏱ 120 min

Lunch at the Stiftskeller before leaving - the monastic tavern's kitchen serves regional specialities and the Stift's own wine. Terrace on the Stiftsgarten facing the pond.

⏱ 60 min

Bad Blumau

★★★☆☆
1

Hard to believe you're swimming in a painting, yet that's exactly the feeling at Rogner Bad Blumau. Hundertwasser conceived the world's largest habitable work of art here: green roofs, asymmetrical windows, coloured columns, golden domes - inaugurated 10 May 1997 across 40 hectares. More than 2,400 different windows and 330 columns.

⏱ 60 min
2

The water from the Vulkania spring emerges at 110°C from 2,843 metres depth, cooled before feeding the thermal lake. The natural salt content is such that you float effortlessly - the sensation of swimming in the sea, according to regulars. It's the most mineralised spring in Styria's thermal region.

⏱ 30 min
3

Rogner Bad Blumau has been voted 'Austria's most popular spa' several times by Thermencheck readers. The geothermal installation has produced electricity since 2001 - the spring serves simultaneously for heating, bathing and power generation. A circular logic that would have delighted Hundertwasser.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Treat yourself to the Vulkania Heilquelle, the outdoor thermal lake - bathing lasts between 1h and half a day depending on your mood. The canopied rest areas and indoor galleries are designed for staying put, not circulating. Resist trying to see everything.

⏱ 180 min

If you're staying overnight, book the sauna on arrival - slots fill fast at weekends. Under-16s not permitted and practised naked as in Austria. Aufgüsse (steam infusions) scheduled every hour.

⏱ 90 min
