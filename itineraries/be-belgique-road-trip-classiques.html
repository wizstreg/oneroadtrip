📆 Best period

April, May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 12 and 16 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Mild temperatures, blooming flowers, perfect for city walking and cycling, moderate crowds, pleasant weather.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather, outdoor festivals, peak tourist season, higher prices, ideal for outdoor dining and beer gardens.

Fall (September-October): Comfortable temperatures, autumn colors, fewer crowds, excellent for museums and cultural activities.

Winter (November-March): Christmas markets, cozy atmosphere, indoor attractions, shorter days, lowest prices except holiday period.

🌍 What to discover in BE?

Medieval cities, world-famous chocolates, and exceptional beer culture in compact, easily explored country. Gothic architecture in Bruges and modern EU institutions in Brussels create diverse experiences. Art nouveau heritage and diamond trade add cultural depth while waffles delight visitors. Belgium perfectly combines history, culture, and culinary excellence.

Belgium Road Trip: Art Cities, Citadels & the Ardennes

📅 11 days📍 11 stops🚗 574 km⏱ 8h

Bruxelles → Gent → Brugge → Anvers → Leuven → Namur → Dinant → Liege

Brussels and its UNESCO Grand-Place, Ghent and Van Eyck's Ghent Altarpiece, Bruges and its medieval canals, Antwerp and Rubens, Leuven and its beguinage—then Wallonia: Namur the citadel, Dinant on the Meuse, Liège the ardent city, and the peat bogs of the Hautes Fagnes. Eleven days to cross all of Belgium.

Day 1

Bruxelles

Where Europe's institutions meet Art Nouveau masters and the world's finest chocolatiers

★★★★★
📍 Grand-Place Bruxelles📍 Manneken-Pis Bruxelles📍 Centre belge de la Bande Dessinée Bruxelles📍 Musée Magritte Bruxelles📍 Musées royaux des Beaux-Arts Bruxelles📍 Atomium Bruxelles
1

Brussels' Grand-Place is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The 15th-century Gothic City Hall dominates the square; facing it, the King's House holds the City Museum. All around, the guild houses rebuilt after the 1695 bombardment form an exceptionally cohesive Baroque ensemble.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Manneken-Pis, a 17th-century bronze fountain, is the city's most irreverent icon. From there, follow the comic book mural trail that has adorned some thirty walls across the center since the 1990s.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Belgian Comic Strip Center occupies an Art Nouveau building by Victor Horta. Its collections trace a hundred years of Belgian comic art, from Tintin to contemporary creators.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Royal Museums of Fine Arts bring together ancient and modern art under one roof. Don't miss the Flemish Primitives department and the rooms devoted to Bruegel the Elder.

⏱ 90 min
5

Step inside the Magritte Museum next door. Over 250 works by the Brussels surrealist painter are gathered here—the world's largest collection of this artist.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

The Atomium, a relic of the 1958 World's Fair, represents an iron crystal magnified 165 billion times. Its nine 18-meter-diameter spheres rise to 102 meters. The permanent exhibition traces the adventure of Expo 58, and the panorama from the top sphere takes in all of Brussels.

⏱ 75 min

Stroll through the Galeries Saint-Hubert, Europe's first covered shopping arcade (1847). The cast-iron glass roof and shop windows of artisan chocolatiers create a timeless setting.

⏱ 45 min

Day 2

Bruxelles

Where Europe's institutions meet Art Nouveau masters and the world's finest chocolatiers

★★★★★
📍 Mont des Arts Bruxelles📍 Parc de Bruxelles📍 Parc du Cinquantenaire Bruxelles📍 Maison de l'Histoire européenne Bruxelles📍 Parlementarium Bruxelles📍 Quartier Royal Bruxelles
1

The Mont des Arts is a terraced esplanade linking the lower town to the upper town. The gardens offer a panorama over the rooftops of the historic center, with the City Hall belfry as the focal point.

⏱ 45 min
2

Cross the Brussels Park, laid out in the 18th century, and reach the Royal Palace whose facade runs along the south side. The palace is open to the public in summer.

⏱ 45 min
3

Cinquantenaire Park was laid out for the 1880 National Exhibition. Its triumphal arches frame three museums: Art & History, Autoworld, and the Royal Museum of the Armed Forces. The neoclassical architecture alone is worth the detour.

⏱ 75 min
4

Choose between the Parlamentarium and the House of European History depending on your interests. The former plunges you behind the scenes of the European Parliament through interactive displays; the latter traces two centuries of continental history through collections of outstanding museographic quality.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Belgian chocolate tasting in one of the artisan workshops of the Sablon quarter. Master chocolatiers explain cocoa origins, tempering techniques, and the peculiarities of the Brussels praline invented in the late 19th century.

⏱ 60 min

Introduction to Belgian beer styles in a specialized café in the center. Trappists, gueuzes, saisons, and lambics form a brewing heritage recognized by UNESCO in 2016.

⏱ 60 min

Day 3

Gent

Three medieval towers rise above canals where Van Eyck's masterpiece glows eternal

★★★★☆
📍 STAM Stadsmuseum Gand📍 Sint-Michielsbrug Gand📍 Cathédrale Saint-Bavon Gand📍 Gravensteen Château des Comtes Gand📍 Beffroi de Gand📍 Graslei Korenlei Gand
1

STAM—the Ghent City Museum—occupies a 17th-century abbey and traces ten centuries of Ghent's history. Relief plans and models help you understand how the city developed around the confluence of the Leie and Scheldt rivers.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Sint-Michielsbrug bridge is the city's best viewpoint. From here, your gaze takes in Ghent's three medieval towers all at once: Saint Nicholas, the Belfry, and Saint Bavo, aligned over a few hundred meters.

⏱ 20 min
3

Enter Saint Bavo's Cathedral to see the Ghent Altarpiece. This masterpiece by Jan and Hubert van Eyck, completed in 1432, is one of the most important paintings in Western art history. The central panel, recovered after a 1934 theft, is visible in a side chapel with dedicated museography.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Belfry of Ghent, a 14th-century tower listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, once stood guard over the city's commercial freedoms. Its bells still punctuate daily life in Ghent; an interior exhibition tells the story of the guilds.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Gravensteen—Castle of the Counts of Flanders—has stood since the 12th century at the heart of the city. Its moats, battlements, and reconstructed torture chamber make it one of the best-preserved medieval castles in Belgium.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk along the Graslei and Korenlei, the two quays that bordered the medieval river port's warehouse. The gabled facades of 12th- to 17th-century guild houses reflect in the waters of the Leie.

⏱ 60 min

Wander through the Patershol quarter, the former tanners' district with cobbled lanes, now lively in the evening. The atmosphere contrasts with the architectural rigor of the historic center.

⏱ 45 min

Day 4

Brugge

Cobblestones and bell towers reflected in silent waters untouched by time

★★★★☆
📍 Beffroi de Bruges Belfort📍 Musée Groeninge Bruges📍 Béguinage Bruges📍 Minnewater Bruges📍 Markt Grand-Place Bruges📍 Église Notre-Dame Bruges
1

Climb the 366 steps of the Belfry of Bruges to look out over the city. This 13th-century tower, listed with the belfries of Belgium and France as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, houses a 47-bell carillon that chimes every quarter hour.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Groeninge Museum holds the finest collection of Flemish Primitives outside the major national museums. Jan van Eyck, Hans Memling, Gerard David, Hieronymus Bosch—15th-century panels hang alongside works of 19th-century Bruges symbolism.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Church of Our Lady houses a Madonna and Child by Michelangelo, a marble sculpture commissioned by a Bruges merchant in the early 16th century. It's one of the few works by the Florentine sculptor to have left Italy during his lifetime.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Princely Beguinage of the Vineyard, founded in 1245, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its white houses encircle a silent lawn where daffodils bloom in spring. A Benedictine community still lives there today.

⏱ 40 min
5

The Minnewater—Lake of Love—borders the beguinage to the south. This 14th-century basin served as a lock and water reserve for the inner port. The shaded promenade around it is one of the city's most photographed spots.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Board a cruise on the canals of Bruges. In half an hour, the boatmen trace the network of waterways that made the medieval city's fortune and pass under a series of low bridges of centuries-old brick.

⏱ 35 min

Photo walk at sunset along the canals. The Rozenhoedkaai, steps from the Belfry, offers the classic reflection of Bruges rooftops in the waters of the Dijver.

⏱ 45 min

Day 5

Brugge

Cobblestones and bell towers reflected in silent waters untouched by time

★★★★☆
📍 Huis van Alijn Bruges📍 Musée Groeninge Bruges📍 Église Saint-Sauveur Bruges📍 Markt Bruges📍 Sint-Janshospitaal Memling Bruges📍 Minnewater Bruges
1

The Huis van Alijn occupies a former 14th-century almshouse in the Vrijdagmarkt quarter. This museum of Belgian daily life in the 20th century brings together toys, clothing, furniture, and household objects that recreate ordinary Belgium from 1900 to 1980.

⏱ 75 min
2

Saint John's Hospital—Sint-Janshospitaal—is one of the oldest medieval hospitals in Europe, operating from the 12th to the 19th century. Its halls preserve six altarpieces by Hans Memling commissioned in the 15th century, including the celebrated Reliquary of Saint Ursula with its gold-ground miniatures.

⏱ 75 min
3

Saint Saviour's Cathedral is the oldest in Bruges, built between the 13th and 15th centuries. Its treasury brings together 16th-century Flemish tapestries, paintings by Dieric Bouts and Pieter Pourbus, and a series of portraits of the Brotherhood of Saint Sebastian Archers.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Free stroll through the UNESCO historic center: follow the canals of the Dijver to the Rozenhoedkaai, the city's most photographed viewpoint with the Belfry in the background.

⏱ 60 min

Tasting at one of the artisan breweries in the center. Bruges is an active brewing city; several workshops offer tours of the fermentation tanks before the tasting.

⏱ 60 min

Day 6

Anvers

Where Rubens painted, diamonds sparkle, and Hadid's glass penthouse crowns the docks

★★★★☆
📍 Grote Markt Hôtel de Ville Anvers📍 Cathédrale Notre-Dame Anvers📍 Rubenshuis Anvers📍 Musée Plantin-Moretus Anvers📍 MAS Museum aan de Stroom Anvers📍 Port House Zaha Hadid Anvers
1

Antwerp's Grote Markt is dominated by the 16th-century Renaissance Town Hall, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The central fountain depicts Brabo throwing the severed hand of the giant Antigonus into the Scheldt—the city's founding legend.

⏱ 45 min
2

Antwerp's Cathedral of Our Lady, a Brabantine Gothic masterpiece built from 1352 to 1521, is the largest church in Belgium. Inside, four monumental paintings by Rubens occupy the transepts and high altar.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Rubenshuis is the house-studio Rubens built in 1610 modeled on Italian palaces. The collections bring together several paintings by the master, his personal belongings, and prints from his workshop.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Plantin-Moretus Museum is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This 16th-century family printing house is the only one in the world to be listed by UNESCO. Its original hand presses, collections of matrices, and commercial archives have remained intact.

⏱ 75 min
5

MAS—Museum aan de Stroom—occupies a ten-story tower on the old port docks. Each floor tackles a theme: global trade, daily life, Antwerp-port. The rooftop terrace offers an unprecedented view of the Scheldt and the Port House.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

From the quays of the Eilandje—the converted former docks quarter north of the center—observe the Port House designed by architect Zaha Hadid. Inaugurated in 2016, this extension of the Port Authority headquarters places a diamond-shaped glass penthouse on the shell of a former fire station.

⏱ 30 min

Stroll through the diamond quarter around the central station. Antwerp still handles about 80% of the world's rough diamonds. The workshop shop windows and the diversity of the international community make this a world unto itself.

⏱ 45 min
Huis Dujardin Bed&Breakfast9.8

Day 7

Leuven

A living Gothic poem written in red brick and student laughter

★★★☆☆
📍 Hôtel de Ville gothique Louvain📍 Grand Béguinage Louvain📍 Bibliothèque universitaire KU Leuven📍 Église Saint-Pierre Louvain📍 M Leuven musée📍 Abbaye de Parc Louvain
1

Leuven's Town Hall is one of the finest Gothic buildings in Belgium. Its facade features 236 statues of illustrious figures, and its construction, begun in 1439, took thirty years. It faces St. Peter's Collegiate Church on the Grote Markt.

⏱ 50 min
2

The Great Beguinage of Leuven, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the largest in Europe. Its cobbled lanes, 17th-century red-brick houses, and Dijle canals form a neighborhood frozen in time. Today it's home to students and researchers from KU Leuven.

⏱ 60 min
3

The University Library was destroyed in both world wars and rebuilt each time. Its Art Deco reading room is sumptuous. Climb the 300 steps of the tower for a panorama across the city.

⏱ 60 min
4

St. Peter's Collegiate Church houses several masterpieces by the Flemish Primitives, including The Last Supper by Dieric Bouts. A visit with an augmented reality headset brings to life the paintings and Gothic recumbent figures.

⏱ 50 min
5

M Leuven museum blends medieval, modern, and contemporary art in an audacious building. Its rooftop, open in summer, offers an unexpected vantage point over the historic center.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Architectural stroll on the Oude Markt, a long square surrounded by 16th- to 19th-century facades. In the evening, the terraces come alive, and students call this square "the longest bar in the world."

⏱ 45 min

Park Abbey, founded in 1129, is the best-preserved in Belgium. Three kilometers from the center, its unspoiled estate brings together the abbey church, cloisters, a stucco-ceilinged library, a watermill, and a kitchen garden. Park entry is free.

⏱ 60 min

Day 8

Namur

Ramparts and tunnels command the river confluence where two kingdoms once collided

★★☆☆☆
📍 Citadelle de Namur📍 Souterrains Terra Nova Citadelle Namur📍 Quais de Meuse Namur📍 Centre historique piéton Namur📍 Téléphérique Citadelle Namur
1

Head to Namur's pedestrian historic center. Between the Baroque Saint-Aubin Cathedral and the lanes of the old market gardeners' quarter, the Walloon city reveals dense civic and religious architecture, less crowded than the major Flemish cities.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Citadel of Namur occupies the promontory overlooking the confluence of the Meuse and Sambre rivers. Napoleon called it Europe's greatest "anthill": it harbors 495 meters of military tunnels dug from the 15th to the 19th century.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Terra Nova underground galleries offer an immersive 90-minute guided tour through the restored passages. Sounds, lights, and 3D reconstructions trace two thousand years of fortifications superimposed on this same rock.

⏱ 90 min
4

The Meuse quay promenade runs along the river between the two bridges of the center. The terraces overlook the confluence, and the silhouette of the citadel stands out on the wooded hill.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Take the cable car up to the citadel from the city center. In eight minutes, the cabin climbs 100 meters and offers an aerial view of the Meuse-Sambre confluence. Open seasonally.

⏱ 25 min
Island in Island, B&B boutique, Design et Vintage9.6

Day 9

Dinant

Where limestone cliffs cradle the bulbous spire and Sax's brass dreams were born

★★☆☆☆
📍 Citadelle de Dinant📍 Collégiale Notre-Dame de Dinant📍 Maison Adolphe Sax Dinant📍 Rocher Bayard Dinant📍 Pont Charles de Gaulle saxophones Dinant
1

The Citadel of Dinant clings 100 meters above the Meuse, behind the collegiate church. Access by cable car or by the 408 steps carved into the rock. The panorama over the valley justifies the effort.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Collegiate Church of Our Lady of Dinant is a remarkable example of Mosan Gothic architecture. Its bulbous 1556 bell tower—a unique silhouette in Belgium—rises directly from the limestone cliff face.

⏱ 40 min
3

Adolphe Sax's birthplace presents the history of the saxophone invented in Dinant in the mid-19th century. Outside, sixty colorful saxophones adorn the Charles de Gaulle Bridge, each representing a member country of the European Union.

⏱ 40 min
4

The Bayard Rock is a 40-meter rock needle rising on the roadside south of Dinant. Legend has it that the magic horse Bayard split it with a hoof blow to enable the escape of the four sons of Aymon. Louis XIV widened the road in 1698 by cutting into the rock.

⏱ 20 min
5

Care to taste the couques of Dinant? These hard honey-and-flour biscuits, molded in carved wooden plates, are a local specialty documented since the 15th century. Artisan bakers in the town center still make them the old-fashioned way.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Narrated Meuse cruise departing from the landing stage opposite the collegiate church. The ride follows the limestone cliffs and perched medieval castles dotting the valley. Available April to October.

⏱ 60 min
'Nulle Part Ailleurs'9.7

Day 10

Liege

374 steps climb from riverside bohemia to citadel orchards above the ardent city

★★★★★
📍 Palais des Princes-Évêques Liège📍 Grand Curtius Liège📍 Montagne de Bueren Liège📍 Cathédrale Saint-Paul Liège📍 Coteaux de la Citadelle Liège📍 Quais de Meuse Liège
1

The Prince-Bishops' Palace extends across the entire Place Saint-Lambert, right in the center of Liège. Its great courtyard, built from 1526 by Érard de La Marck, is surrounded by 60 different Gothic columns, adorned with grotesque masks and fantastic figures. The building now houses the courthouse.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Grand Curtius, housed in a 17th-century red-brick Mosan palace on the Meuse riverfront, is the largest museum in the Walloon Region with over 5,000 m² of exhibition space. Hunting weapons, Mosan glasswork, medieval Mosan art, and local archaeology coexist across six floors.

⏱ 90 min
3

Climb the 374 steps of the Montagne de Bueren. This monumental 19th-century staircase, carved into the citadel hill, allowed soldiers to reach the barracks from the city center. From the top, the panorama over the Meuse meanders is your reward.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Citadel Slopes span 86 hectares above the Montagne de Bueren. This three-star Michelin Green Guide site blends vestiges of 13th-century medieval fortifications, a former Teutonic commandery, meadows, and orchards.

⏱ 60 min
5

Saint Paul's Cathedral houses the Treasury of Saint Lambert's Cathedral, a collection of reliquaries, metalwork, and medieval sculptures recovered when the old cathedral was destroyed in 1794. The reliquary of Charles the Bold is a rare piece of Gothic goldsmithing.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Urban walk along the Meuse and its pedestrian footbridges. The quays have been entirely redesigned; on the Outremeuse side, you discover the working-class quarter of the Liège dialect, attached to its traditions and its August 15 festival.

⏱ 60 min

The Batte market, one of the oldest and largest markets in Europe, takes place every Sunday morning on the Meuse quays. Fish, cheeses, antiques, and crafts spread over several kilometers.

⏱ 90 min

Day 11

Liege

374 steps climb from riverside bohemia to citadel orchards above the ardent city

★★★★★
📍 Signal de Botrange Hautes Fagnes📍 Centre Nature Botrange Maison du Parc📍 Baraque Michel Hautes Fagnes📍 Fagne Wallonne tourbières📍 Cascade du Bayehon Hautes Fagnes
1

The Signal de Botrange, at 694 meters, is Belgium's highest point. The Baltia mound, a small 1923 structure accessible by a few steps, lets you symbolically reach 700 meters. The panorama takes in the moorlands and peat bogs of the country's largest nature reserve.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Botrange Nature Center is the welcome center for the Hautes Fagnes-Eifel Natural Park. Its permanent exhibition "Fania" interactively presents the secrets of the Fagnes ecosystem—peat bogs, moorlands, fauna, and flora—with a sensory tunnel suited to children and adults alike.

⏱ 60 min
3

Take the boardwalks of the Fagne Wallonne. These wooden walkways float on stilts above the peat bogs and heather moors. The peat layer reaches eight meters thick in places; off-trail access is strictly forbidden to preserve the environment.

⏱ 90 min
4

The Baraque Michel marks the crossroads of old smuggling routes between Wallonia and Prussia. The site at 672 meters offers clear views over the Fagnes plateau and marks the starting point for the loop hike around the Poleur-Venn.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Hike the Botrange-Fagne Wallonne loop (about 8 km, 2h30). The trail follows wooden walkways above the peat bog, runs along pine forests planted around 1850, and passes the Cascade du Bayehon before returning to the Signal.

⏱ 150 min

Naturalist observation of wetland wildlife: black woodpeckers, migrating common cranes, black grouse depending on the season. Guides from the Nature Center organize accompanied outings that allow entry into zone C, normally closed to the public.

⏱ 120 min
