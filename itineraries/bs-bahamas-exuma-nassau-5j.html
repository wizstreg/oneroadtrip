Bahamas Journey: Exuma & Nassau

📅 9 days📍 8 stops🚗 250 km⏱ 45min

George Town Exuma → Nassau

George Town Exuma → Nassau

Discover Nassau's colonial charm, swim with the famous pigs of Exuma, dive with sharks, and lounge on deserted sandbars. The Bahamas deliver Caribbean turquoise in its purest form.

✨ Highlights
  • Thunderball Grotto: snorkeling in the James Bond film cave
  • Big Major Cay: swimming with the Bahamas' famous pigs
  • Coco Plum Beach: sandbars stretching as far as the eye can see at low tide
  • Queen's Staircase: 66 steps carved from limestone by 600 enslaved people in 1793
📆 Best period

December, January, February, March, April

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 5 and 9 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 What to discover in BS?

Crystal-clear waters and swimming pigs create unique Caribbean adventures across 700 islands. Experience Atlantis resort excitement, conch fritters, and perfect beaches just miles from Florida. The islands offer incredible diving, fishing, and luxury resorts with authentic Bahamian culture. Perfect for tropical getaways with easy accessibility and diverse experiences.

George Town Exuma

Where pastel harbor villages meet endless turquoise sandbars

★★★☆☆
1

Coco Plum Beach stretches along the northeast coast of Great Exuma. At low tide, sandbars emerge for hundreds of meters and the water barely reaches your ankles. The powdery white sand conceals sand dollars waiting to be spotted.

⏱ 90 min
2

Head to Exuma Point Beach on the northern tip of the island. This expansive beach lined with coconut palms offers a wooden pier and unobstructed views across Elizabeth Harbour.

⏱ 75 min
3

The small village of Rokers Point overlooks the eastern shore. From the heights, shades of blue unfold from turquoise to cobalt, and the ocean beach below stretches endlessly.

⏱ 75 min
4

Three Sisters Beach takes its name from three rocks jutting offshore. The sand displays pink undertones and nearby reefs teem with tropical fish.

⏱ 60 min
5

Walk the loop around Lake Victoria, a two-acre lake in the heart of George Town. The pink-and-white government building, inspired by Nassau's Government House, has watched over the harbor since colonial times.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swimming and barefoot walks across Coco Plum Beach's sandbars. Arrive at low tide to fully enjoy the shallow lagoons.

⏱ 75 min

Coastal drive between Exuma Point and Rokers Point via Queen's Highway. Photo stops at promontories to capture the gradient of blues.

⏱ 60 min

Light snorkeling from Three Sisters Beach around the coral reefs close to shore.

George Town Exuma

Where pastel harbor villages meet endless turquoise sandbars

★★★☆☆
1

Catch a water taxi from George Town dock. The fifteen-minute crossing delivers passengers to Stocking Island, a natural 4-mile barrier protecting Elizabeth Harbour.

⏱ 30 min
2

The lagoon at Chat 'N' Chill welcomes stingrays and starfish in its shallow waters. This sailor's gathering place has been operating since 1998.

⏱ 75 min
3

Climb to the Monument, a former navigation marker perched on the highest point in the Exuma chain. The 360° view embraces Elizabeth Harbour, the Exuma Sound, and hundreds of boat names spelled out in stones on the ground.

⏱ 60 min
4

The ocean-side beach unfurls a long ribbon of white sand bordered by low cliffs and dunes. Waves here are stronger than on the sheltered harbor side.

⏱ 75 min
5

Moriah Harbour Cay National Park covers over 22,000 acres of marine and terrestrial ecosystems. At low tide, a sandbar appears at Stocking Island's southern tip.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Water taxi crossing between George Town and Stocking Island. Boats depart regularly from George Town dock.

⏱ 30 min

Hiking the marked trails of Stocking Island. Paths wind through dry tropical forest, salt marshes, dunes, and hidden coves. Stone cairns mark the route.

⏱ 90 min

Snorkeling at Stocking Island's Blue Hole, identifiable by an orange buoy. Tropical fish congregate here in abundance.

⏱ 60 min

Day 3

George Town Exuma

Where pastel harbor villages meet endless turquoise sandbars

★★★☆☆
1

Depart by speedboat from Barraterre, north of Great Exuma, for a full-day expedition through the Exuma Cays—a necklace of 365 islands stretching 130 miles. Count on roughly 1 hour 20 minutes to reach the first cays.

⏱ 45 min
2

Allen's Cay harbors a colony of endemic Bahamian iguanas (Cyclura cychlura), a protected species. These reptiles approach visitors on the beach.

⏱ 45 min
3

Off Norman's Cay, the fuselage of a Curtiss C-46 aircraft rests in 15 feet of water. The wreck, tied to 1980s drug-trafficking history, is now colonized by corals and fish. About 20 minutes' boat ride from Allen's Cay.

⏱ 60 min
4

Compass Cay hosts a nurse shark sanctuary. These docile sharks glide around the docks and allow close encounters in crystal-clear water.

⏱ 45 min
5

Pipe Creek, one of the Caribbean's most stunning sandbars, emerges between two islets. The water is so transparent you can see the bottom several meters down.

⏱ 45 min
6

Big Major Cay, nicknamed Pig Beach, is home to a colony of swimming pigs that have become world-famous. They swim out to greet boats as soon as they approach.

⏱ 60 min
7

Thunderball Grotto, west of Staniel Cay, is an underwater cave made famous by two James Bond films (Thunderball in 1965 and Never Say Never Again in 1983). The submerged entrance leads to a cavern lit by natural openings where angelfish, sergeant majors, and snappers swim.

⏱ 75 min
8

Staniel Cay's dock and tiny village offer a welcome break. Photos from the Thunderball shoot still hang behind the yacht club bar.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Full-day speedboat excursion through the Exuma Cays. The complete circuit takes approximately 8 hours door-to-door from George Town, including roughly 3 cumulative hours navigating between stops.

⏱ 480 min

Snorkeling at Thunderball Grotto. Enter through the submerged opening at low tide to admire the play of light inside the cave.

⏱ 45 min

Day 4

George Town Exuma

Where pastel harbor villages meet endless turquoise sandbars

★★★☆☆
1

Hoopers Bay, just minutes north of George Town, is a sheltered bay with fine sand. Sea turtles still frequent the waters around private docks—approach slowly without feeding or touching them.

⏱ 75 min
2

Jolly Hall Beach spreads out in a long strip of sand where you're often alone. The shallow, calm water makes it ideal for a peaceful swim.

⏱ 60 min
3

Rolle Town, on Great Exuma's southern coast, offers panoramas over lagoons of intense hues. The village preserves a few 18th-century Loyalist graves.

⏱ 45 min
4

Cross the one-lane bridge linking Great Exuma to Little Exuma. Tropic of Cancer Beach (also called Pelican Beach) is the island's longest: a mile-long crescent of white sand traced exactly along the Tropic of Cancer line, painted on the access steps.

⏱ 90 min
5

The Salt Beacon near Williams Town is an old stone marker from the salt marshes dating to the 19th-century salt industry. It testifies to Little Exuma's economic past.

⏱ 45 min
6

Forbes Hill Beach, one of the first beaches past the bridge, offers rock formations to explore and typically calm, limpid water.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Road trip along Queen's Highway between Great Exuma and Little Exuma, with beach stops at every cove that presents itself.

⏱ 60 min

Extended swimming at Tropic of Cancer Beach. The shallow water and gently sloping sandy bottom allow for serene swimming.

⏱ 90 min

Sea turtle watching from Hoopers Bay docks. Keep a respectful distance to avoid startling them.

⏱ 45 min

George Town Exuma

Where pastel harbor villages meet endless turquoise sandbars

★★★☆☆
1

George Town Fish Fry gathers a handful of colorful shacks about a half-mile west of downtown. In the evenings, music fills the air and platters of fried fish circulate between tables under the trees.

⏱ 75 min
2

George Town Straw Market offers bags, hats, and jewelry woven from straw or shells, legacy of a Bahamian craft tradition established in the 1960s.

⏱ 45 min
3

Stroll along Elizabeth Harbour. The natural harbor, protected by Stocking Island, is dotted with sailboats and catamarans. Annual regattas make it the sailing capital of the Bahamas.

⏱ 60 min
4

Moss Town Beach, between George Town and Rokers Point, is a quiet beach with calm waters, ideal for an unhurried morning with no agenda.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Rest day with free beach time. Choose between Jolly Hall, Moss Town, or a return to Coco Plum depending on the day's tides.

⏱ 180 min

Late-afternoon walk through George Town: market, harbor, and Fish Fry shacks for a farewell Exuma dinner.

Day 6

Nassau

A colonial capital painted in Caribbean pastels and maritime history

★★★☆☆
1

Nassau Straw Market brings together roughly 500 vendors under one roof, steps from Bay Street. Bags, hats, and jewelry woven from straw perpetuate a Bahamian craft once used to make fish traps and carrying baskets.

⏱ 60 min
2

Prince George Wharf is the cruise ship dock. The waterfront offers a direct view of towering hulls and the capital's port activity.

⏱ 45 min
3

Bay Street, Nassau's main artery, lines up pastel facades and colonial buildings. The shops' vivid colors recall Georgian architecture inherited from 18th-century American Loyalists.

⏱ 60 min
4

Arawak Cay, nicknamed the Fish Fry, is an artificial island built in 1969 with sand dredged from Nassau harbor. Its colorful shacks serve conch salad, fried fish, and cracked lobster alongside Kalik beer.

⏱ 75 min
5

The Queen's Staircase, carved from solid limestone by 600 enslaved people between 1793 and 1794, counts 66 steps (65 visible). It connected Fort Fincastle to central Nassau and was named in honor of Queen Victoria. Waterfalls and dense tropical vegetation now frame the staircase.

⏱ 60 min
6

Fort Fincastle, built in 1793 by Lord Dunmore atop Bennet's Hill (New Providence's highest point), takes the shape of a ship. Constructed from limestone to defend the port against pirates, it never saw battle and was later converted into a lighthouse.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walking tour through Nassau's historic center: from the Straw Market to Fort Fincastle via Bay Street and Parliament Square.

⏱ 120 min

Tasting conch salad and cracked conch at Arawak Cay stalls. Sunday evenings, locals gather in numbers at the Fish Fry.

⏱ 60 min

Day 7

Nassau

A colonial capital painted in Caribbean pastels and maritime history

★★★☆☆
1

Cross the bridge linking Nassau to Paradise Island. The panoramic view from the walkway embraces Nassau Harbour, sailboats at anchor, and the skyline of grand hotels.

⏱ 30 min
2

Versailles Gardens unfold in symmetrical terraces punctuated by fountains and statues—an unexpected calm in the middle of the resort island. The gardens are freely accessible.

⏱ 60 min
3

Cabbage Beach extends for 2 miles along Paradise Island's north shore. Bordered by dunes and casuarina trees, it's one of New Providence's finest beaches. Walk west to quickly escape the crowds.

⏱ 90 min
4

Marina Village, at the foot of Atlantis, lines up duty-free shops and restaurant terraces around a yacht harbor. Entry to the commercial zone is free.

⏱ 60 min
5

Under the Paradise Island bridge, Potter's Cay is Nassau's fish market. Fishermen unload conch and fresh fish, and stalls prepare conch salads to order.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Swimming and lounging on Cabbage Beach. Rent a beach chair or spread your towel toward the quieter western end.

⏱ 120 min

Walking tour of Paradise Island: gardens, beach, and Marina Village, without needing entry tickets to the resort complexes.

Day 8

Nassau

A colonial capital painted in Caribbean pastels and maritime history

★★★☆☆
1

Fort Charlotte, built between 1787 and 1790 by Lord Dunmore, is the Bahamas' largest fort. Perched 80 feet above sea level, it overlooks the western entrance to Nassau Harbour. Its dry moat and underground tunnels are freely accessible.

⏱ 60 min
2

Cable Beach unfurls 3 miles of white sand west of downtown. Named after the undersea telegraph cable that arrived here in 1892, this beach is New Providence's longest and most popular.

⏱ 90 min
3

Junkanoo Beach, between downtown and Arawak Cay, pulses with the rhythm of beach bars and volleyball. It's the closest beach to the cruise port, reachable in 15 minutes on foot.

⏱ 60 min
4

Saunders Beach, just east of Cable Beach, is quieter and less crowded than its neighbors. Locals come to picnic here on weekends.

⏱ 60 min
5

Western Esplanade Beach, an urban beach between downtown and Arawak Cay, allows a final swim in turquoise waters before departure.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Half-day at Cable Beach. Enjoy the fine sand and water sports offered on site.

⏱ 180 min

Visit Fort Charlotte in the morning, before the heat. Admission is free and the panoramic view over the harbor and Paradise Island is worth the trip.

⏱ 45 min

Final swim at Western Esplanade or Junkanoo Beach late in the day, just steps from downtown.

⏱ 60 min
