South India Circuit: Hampi, Goa and the Nilgiri Hills

📅 12 days📍 12 stops🚗 2000 km⏱ 36h

Bengaluru → Anegundi → Palolem → Fort Kochi → Coimbatore → Coonoor → Puducherry

From Bengaluru, plunge into the majestic ruins of Hampi - the world's second-largest city in 1500, today 1,600 monuments across 42 km² of granite chaos classified as UNESCO World Heritage. Descend to the secret beaches of southern Goa, explore the colonial lanes of Fort Kochi where Chinese fishing nets and Portuguese churches coexist, then climb to the Nilgiri tea plantations before looping back to Puducherry and its French Quarter.

✨ Highlights
  • Hampi: over 1,600 UNESCO-listed Vijayanagara monuments across 42 km² of granite boulder fields
  • Palolem and Patnem: secret beaches of southern Goa far from mass tourism
  • Fort Kochi: 14th-century Chinese fishing nets and India's oldest European church
  • Adiyogi at Coimbatore: world's largest sculpted bust (112 feet) with sound and light show
📆 Best period

October, November, December, January, February

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 12 and 16 days.

Day 1

Bengaluru

Karnataka's tech capital and Cubbon Park's green lung, gateway to the south

★★★★★
📍 Cubbon Park Bengaluru📍 MG Road Bengaluru📍 Vidhana Soudha Bengaluru📍 Attara Kacheri Bengaluru
1

If you're arriving from Puducherry by overnight bus or car (around 5-6 hours via NH77/NH44), you'll arrive at Majestic bus terminal right in the heart of Bengaluru. Take advantage of this stop to arrange your bus or train tickets to Hampi - Kempegowda bus station is Karnataka's main hub, with daily KSRTC buses departing for Hospet (7-8 hours by road). Book the day before if you're traveling during peak season (October-February).

⏱ 45 min
2

Cubbon Park is Bengaluru's green lung: 120 hectares of gardens created in 1870 by General Sankey, with over 6,000 trees - from gulmohar with brilliant red flowers to imposing silver oaks. You'll also find the State Central Library, a superb red-painted Gothic building, and the Government Museum, one of India's oldest (founded in 1876). If you're there on a Sunday, the park is closed to cars and transforms into a venue for yoga, concerts and a small farmers' market.

⏱ 60 min
3

Right next to the park, don't miss Vidhana Soudha: this massive neo-Dravidian building, constructed in 1954, houses Karnataka's parliament. You can't go inside, but the illuminated granite facade in the evening is worth a detour. Opposite, Attara Kacheri (1864) with its Corinthian columns houses the High Court - a striking contrast between colonial architecture and Indian style.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stroll down MG Road, Bengaluru's historic shopping artery. It's less about the shopping than the atmosphere - trendy cafes, bookshops and old-school stores coexist in a distinctly Bangalorean energy. If you're hungry, head to VV Puram Food Street (3 km south) to sample crispy dosas and fluffy bondas.

⏱ 75 min

Preparing for departure to Hampi: from Majestic, overnight KSRTC buses depart between 8pm and 10pm for Hospet (arriving around 6-7am). If you prefer the train, the Hampi Express (#16592) leaves Bengaluru at 10pm and arrives at Hospet Junction around 7:10am. From there, it's 13 km by auto-rickshaw to the ruins. Take a sleeper bus or train berth to sleep en route and save a day.

Day 2

Anegundi

Ancient village facing Hampi's ruins, mythological birthplace of Hanuman

★★★★☆
📍 Virupaksha Temple Hampi📍 Hemakuta Hill Temples Hampi📍 Hampi Bazaar📍 Tungabhadra River Hampi
1

Welcome to Hampi - a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1986, this is India's largest field of ruins. In 1500, Vijayanagara was the world's second-largest city after Beijing, with 500,000 inhabitants and merchants from Persia and Portugal. More than 1,600 monuments spread across 42 km² amid a landscape of granite boulders and rice paddies. Allow at least 2.5 days to explore the site.

⏱ 30 min
2

Start with Virupaksha Temple, the living heart of Hampi. Dedicated to Shiva, this 7th-century sanctuary is the only temple still in active worship on the site. The 50-meter gopuram dominates the entire bazaar. Inside, look for the ceiling paintings dating from the 14th and 16th centuries - they depict scenes from the Ramayana and Mahabharata. King Krishnadevaraya added the great pillared hall in 1510 to mark his coronation. Arrive early in the morning: the light is beautiful and the monkeys are still calm.

⏱ 75 min
3

Next, climb Hemakuta Hill, dotted with small Jain and Hindu temples from the 14th-15th centuries, including Ganagitti (consecrated in 1386). The real draw is the view: from the summit, you overlook the entire site on one side and the Tungabhadra on the other. Late in the day, the granite boulders take on an almost surreal golden hue. Settle on the rocks with water - this is the ultimate sunset spot.

⏱ 60 min
4

Descend through Hampi Bazaar, the ancient market street in front of Virupaksha. In the 16th century, Portuguese travelers described stalls overflowing with precious stones, spices and Arabian horses. Today, the granite columns that lined the shops still stand - imagine the bustle of this artery at its peak.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Cross the Tungabhadra in a coracle - these round bamboo boats covered in tar have been used here for centuries. The journey takes 5-10 minutes and drops you on the Anegundi side. The service runs from roughly 7am to 6pm, but depends on water levels (avoid monsoon season). On the Anegundi side, you'll find homestays with views over the rice fields - far more authentic than Hospet.

⏱ 45 min

Set yourself up on the Hemakuta rocks or the north bank for sunset. The granite blocks naturally stacked over billions of years take on orange tones as the sun sinks behind the hills. If you're staying on the Anegundi side, the peace is total - no roads, just the sound of the river.

⏱ 60 min

Day 3

Anegundi

Ancient village facing Hampi's ruins, mythological birthplace of Hanuman

★★★★☆
📍 Vijaya Vittala Temple Hampi📍 Stone Chariot Hampi📍 Elephant Stables Hampi📍 Zenana Enclosure Hampi📍 Lotus Mahal Hampi📍 Lakshmi Narasimha Hampi📍 Krishna Temple Hampi
1

Head to Vijaya Vittala Temple, the absolute jewel of Hampi. Begun under Devaraya II (1422-1446) and enriched by Krishnadevaraya (1509-1529), it occupies an enclosure measuring 164 m by 94 m. The star attraction is the Stone Chariot - a stone chariot dedicated to Garuda, inspired by Konark's Sun Temple after Krishnadevaraya discovered it during a campaign in Odisha. The wheels were once mobile, now cemented in place. The complex is open from 8:30am to 5:30pm.

⏱ 30 min
2

In the Ranga Mantapa, you'll discover the 56 musical pillars nicknamed 'SaReGaMa'. By gently tapping each pillar, you could obtain sounds of mridangam, cymbals or veena. The British, intrigued, sawed two of them open to unlock the secret - finding nothing inside. The two cut pillars are still visible. Today, touching the pillars is forbidden, but a guard sometimes gives a demonstration.

⏱ 75 min
3

Continue to the Elephant Stables: 11 aligned domes - some round, others with Islamic cupolas - which housed the royal elephants. It's one of the most striking examples of the empire's Indo-Islamic style. Right next door, the Zenana Enclosure houses the Lotus Mahal, a two-story pavilion whose arches recall an opening lotus flower - a cool retreat designed for the queens.

⏱ 75 min
4

Make a detour to the Lakshmi Narasimha monolith (6.7 m high), Vishnu in his man-lion form. Push on to Krishna Temple and the Sasivekalu Ganesha - a 2.4 m monolith carved from a single block, named 'mustard seed' for the shape of his belly.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the afternoon, rent a bicycle or scooter on the Anegundi side and explore the north bank. You'll pass Gagan Mahal palace (16th-century royal residence with Indo-Islamic balconies), Ranganatha Temple and the fort ruins. Artisans still make objects from banana fiber - a unique local tradition.

⏱ 120 min

Late in the day, descend to Sanapur Lake (4 km from Anegundi): turquoise water between granite blocks, with the possibility of a coracle tour. In the evening, Anegundi is very quiet - perfect for digesting the kilometers of ruins.

⏱ 60 min

Day 4

Anegundi

Ancient village facing Hampi's ruins, mythological birthplace of Hanuman

★★★★☆
📍 Anjanadri Hill Anegundi📍 Pampa Sarovar Anegundi📍 Kodandarama Temple Hampi
1

Rise early to climb Anjanadri Hill - the presumed birthplace of the god Hanuman according to the Ramayana. Count on 575 steps (30-40 minutes) between granite blocks. At the summit, a small temple houses a Hanuman idol carved from rock. The real reward is the 360° view: checkered rice paddies, coconut groves, the serpentine Tungabhadra, and all of Hampi spread across the horizon. Go at sunrise. Watch out for monkeys: don't show any food.

⏱ 90 min
2

Once down, push on to Pampa Sarovar, a sacred basin covered with lotuses - one of Hinduism's five holy lakes. According to legend, this is where the goddess Pampa did penance to marry Shiva. Two small temples border the basin. The place is peaceful and rarely crowded.

⏱ 45 min
3

Visit Kodandarama Temple on the banks of the Tungabhadra, where Rama is said to have crowned Sugriva king of the monkeys. The bas-reliefs tell the scene with finesse. The setting is magnificent: the river flows just below, lined with water-polished granite blocks.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Explore the prehistoric rock paintings of Onake Kindi, a few kilometers from Anegundi. These Neolithic paintings testify to human occupation well before the Vijayanagara empire. By scooter, the road through rice paddies and granite chaos is a pleasure.

⏱ 60 min

Organizing departure to Goa: from Hospet Junction (13 km), the Howrah Express departs 4 days/week to Madgaon (7-8 hours). Private overnight buses reach Margao in 8-10 hours. From Margao, count on 40 minutes by bus or taxi to Palolem. Book in advance - berths go fast.

Day 5

Palolem

Coconut-fringed crescent cove in peaceful southern Goa

★★★★★
📍 Palolem Beach Canacona Goa📍 Patnem Beach Canacona Goa📍 Butterfly Beach Canacona Goa
1

Arrival in Palolem, southern Goa - and the contrast with Hampi is striking. It's a 1.6 km crescent bay of white sand lined with coconut palms, with shallow water ideal for swimming. A former fishing village turned traveler spot, Palolem maintains a laid-back atmosphere compared to north Goa. Settle into one of the beach huts and enjoy this first day with your toes in the sand.

⏱ 60 min
2

Late afternoon, walk south along the beach to Patnem Beach - an even quieter cove, separated from Palolem by a rocky headland (10 minutes' walk). Patnem is smaller, with fewer shacks but an almost meditative atmosphere. The water is clear and the sandy bottom slopes gently.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take a kayak from the northern end of Palolem to reach Butterfly Beach - a cove accessible only by sea or jungle trail. You can see butterflies between October and March, and the beach is rarely crowded. The kayak crossing takes 15-20 minutes - it's also a chance to spot dolphins early in the morning.

⏱ 90 min

Sunset at Palolem faces west: the sun descends between the bay's two rocky headlands. This is when the shacks light candles and fishermen pull up their colorful boats. Stay for dinner - the grilled catch of the day with kokum curry (local sour fruit) is the local specialty.

⏱ 60 min

Day 6

Palolem

Coconut-fringed crescent cove in peaceful southern Goa

★★★★★
📍 Cabo de Rama Fort Canacona Goa📍 Cola Beach Canacona Goa📍 Agonda Beach Goa
1

Excursion to Cabo de Rama Fort, perched on a headland overlooking the Arabian Sea. Its name comes from the Ramayana - Rama and Sita are said to have stayed here during their exile. The fort existed well before the Portuguese, who took it in 1763 from the Raja of Soonda and fortified it with a chapel dedicated to Saint Anthony. It served as a prison from 1935 to 1955. Today in ruins, there remain rusted cannons on the bastions and the small white church. Entry is free - push to the western edge for a vertiginous view over the cliffs and ocean.

⏱ 90 min
2

Coming down from the fort, detour to Cola Beach - an isolated beach accessible by dirt track. Cola's special feature is its freshwater lagoon just behind the beach, separated from the sea by a sand spit. The contrast between the lagoon's blue water and the Arabian Sea waves is striking. Few people, no noisy shacks - this is secret Goa.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The south Goa coastal road between Palolem and Cabo de Rama (about 20 km) passes through fishing villages, coconut groves and viewpoints over coves. By scooter, it's a treat - stop whenever a view tempts you. Fill up with petrol before leaving, there are no stations between the two.

⏱ 90 min

If you have time on the return, make a detour to Agonda Beach - a long beach less crowded than Palolem, lined with palms and a few wooden cabanas. The vibe is very relaxed, with possibilities for yoga and ayurveda in centers set up along the beach.

⏱ 60 min

Day 7

Palolem

Coconut-fringed crescent cove in peaceful southern Goa

★★★★★
📍 Margao Municipal Market Goa📍 Church of the Holy Spirit Margao Goa📍 Colva Beach Goa
1

Take a local bus or taxi to Margao (Madgaon), the capital of south Goa, 40 km away. The town retains a strong Portuguese imprint: colonial houses in pastel facades line the old quarter. Step through the door of the Church of the Holy Spirit, built in 1675 - its white baroque facade dominates the central square, and the interior contains a remarkable gilded altarpiece.

⏱ 60 min
2

Margao Municipal Market is a festival for the senses: mountains of spices (pepper, cinnamon, cardamom), fresh fish spread on ice, unknown tropical fruits. It's the busiest market in south Goa - go in the morning when the stalls are full. Count on a good hour to tour it without rushing.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If timing allows, head down to Colva Beach - a huge white sand beach very popular with Goans on weekends. The contrast with the southern coves is clear: here it's livelier, with shacks lined up and a family atmosphere. Count on 15 minutes by taxi from Margao.

⏱ 60 min

Prepare departure to Kochi: from Madgaon (Margao station), the Mangala Lakshadweep Express or Netravati Express connect to Ernakulam Junction (Kochi) in 10-14 hours depending on the train. Overnight journeys are ideal - you save a day. Book your berth in advance via IRCTC.

⏱ 45 min

Day 8

Fort Kochi

Historic port with Chinese nets, Portuguese churches and 16th-century synagogue

★★★★☆
📍 Chinese Fishing Nets Fort Kochi📍 St Francis Church Fort Kochi📍 Santa Cruz Basilica Fort Kochi📍 Jew Town Mattancherry Kochi📍 Dutch Palace Mattancherry Kochi
1

Arrival at Ernakulam Junction after the night train. Take the local ferry (15 minutes) to Fort Kochi - it's the best way to arrive, with the silhouette of Chinese fishing nets appearing from the boat. These enormous cantilever nets, called 'Cheena Vala' in Malayalam, were allegedly introduced by Chinese merchants in the 14th century, possibly by explorer Zheng He's fleet. You won't find them anywhere else in India.

⏱ 45 min
2

Step through the door of St. Francis Church, the oldest European church in India. Built in wood by Portuguese Franciscans in 1503, then rebuilt in stone in 1516, it housed Vasco da Gama's burial after his death in Kochi in 1524 - his remains were later repatriated to Lisbon in 1539. Portuguese tombstones are on the north wall, Dutch ones on the south. The church is open from 9am to 5pm (except Sunday morning).

⏱ 60 min
3

Continue to Mattancherry for the Dutch Palace, built by the Portuguese around 1555 then renovated by the Dutch in 1663. Don't be fooled by the modest exterior: inside, the 17th-century murals illustrating the Ramayana are among Kerala's finest. Right next door, Jew Town and the Paradesi Synagogue (1568) - one of the Commonwealth's oldest active synagogues - are worth the detour for their hand-painted floor tiles imported from China.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Fort Kochi is best explored on foot - the lanes are lined with Portuguese, Dutch and British colonial houses, interspersed with contemporary art galleries and cafes in former spice warehouses. If the Kochi Biennale (every 2 years, December to March) is on, art installations take over historic buildings - Aspinwall House, the old harbor warehouse, is the main site.

⏱ 75 min

In the evening, attend a Kathakali performance (Kerala's dance-theater with spectacular makeup) at one of Fort Kochi's cultural centers - shows generally start around 6:30pm with a makeup demonstration 1 hour before. Otherwise, return to the Chinese nets at sunset: fishermen operate the wooden counterweights, and the low light on the nets creates Kerala's most photographed cliché.

⏱ 75 min

Day 9

Coimbatore

Gateway to the Nilgiris and Isha Yoga Center, at the foot of the Western Ghats

★★★☆☆
📍 Isha Yoga Center Coimbatore📍 Adiyogi Shiva Statue Coimbatore📍 Dhyanalinga Temple Coimbatore
1

Train from Ernakulam Junction to Coimbatore Junction - about 3.5 hours, with departures roughly every 4 hours. The journey runs along the Western Ghats with beautiful views of tropical vegetation. On arrival, Coimbatore is an industrial city with little tourist charm, but it's the gateway to the Nilgiris and Isha Yoga Center.

⏱ 30 min
2

Head to Isha Yoga Center, 30 km west of Coimbatore, at the foot of the Velliangiri mountains. The Adiyogi statue greets you: 34 meters high (112 feet), 45 meters long - it's the world's largest sculpted bust according to Guinness. Inaugurated in 2017, its height symbolizes the 112 pathways to liberation in yogic tradition. Entry is free.

⏱ 60 min
3

Inside the complex, the Dhyanalinga is a unique meditation space - a dome of 250,000 bricks built without steel or cement, housing a consecrated linga. Whatever your beliefs, sit for a few minutes in the dome's silence. Sound offerings (chanting and Tibetan bowls) take place at 11:50am and 5:50pm. The Linga Bhairavi Temple, dedicated to the divine feminine aspect, is right next door.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Every evening at 7pm, the Adiyogi Divya Darshanam sound and light show projects a 14-minute light fresco onto the giant statue, telling the story of yoga - it's visually impressive. Note: mobile phones are not allowed in the complex (lockers at the entrance).

⏱ 45 min

If you want to go deeper, the center offers guided meditation sessions and baths in the Theerthakunds - Surya Kund (men) and Chandra Kund (women), consecrated water pools. The complex also has accommodation (cottages, dormitories) if you want to spend the night - but book ahead.

Day 10

Coonoor

Colonial hill station at 1,800 m in the heart of the Nilgiri tea plantations

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Dolphin's Nose Viewpoint Coonoor📍 Highfield Tea Factory Coonoor📍 Lamb's Rock Coonoor📍 Ketti Valley Coonoor
1

Day in the Nilgiris: climb to Coonoor (70 km, 2 hours of winding road from Coimbatore) - the second-largest hill station in the Nilgiris after Ooty, perched at 1,800 m altitude. The road climbs through eucalyptus and shola forests (high-altitude tropical forests) with hairpin bends. If you can, take the Nilgiri Mountain Railway (UNESCO heritage) from Mettupalayam - the rack railway climbs through tea plantations. Book ahead.

⏱ 30 min
2

Dolphin's Nose is a natural viewpoint perched at 1,550 meters - a rocky spur resembling a dolphin's nose. In clear weather, the view embraces the valleys, tea plantations as far as the eye can see and, in the distance, Catherine Falls. The access road passes through kilometers of tea gardens - stop to photograph the pickers in colorful saris among the rows of tea bushes.

⏱ 60 min
3

Highfield Tea Factory offers a guided tour of the tea-making process, from picking to drying. You'll see the rolling machines, oxidation vats and dryers. At the end, a tasting lets you compare white, green and black teas from the Nilgiris. The shop also sells eucalyptus oil and artisan chocolate. Open 9am to 7pm.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Multiple photo stops along the Nilgiris road - Lamb's Rock offers a plunging view over the Coimbatore plains and forests below. Monkeys are numerous and bold: keep your belongings in hand. Sim's Park (12 hectares of botanical garden) is also worth a visit if you have time.

⏱ 60 min

Late in the day, instead of descending to Coimbatore, stay at altitude in Coonoor - accommodation is plentiful, from colonial homestays to resorts with valley views. The nighttime coolness (15°C) is a luxury after days in the southern heat. Tomorrow, you'll explore Ooty by rack railway before descending.

Day 11

Coonoor

Colonial hill station at 1,800 m in the heart of the Nilgiri tea plantations

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Nilgiri Mountain Railway Coonoor📍 Botanical Gardens Ooty📍 Sim's Park Coonoor📍 Droog Fort Coonoor📍 St George's Church Coonoor
1

Today you're sleeping at altitude. From Coonoor (where you were yesterday for Dolphin's Nose and Highfield Tea Factory), take the Nilgiri Mountain Railway between Coonoor and Ooty - 19 km of UNESCO-listed rack railway, pulled by diesel locomotive through tea plantations and shola forests. The train passes through Wellington, Lovedale and Ketti before arriving in Ooty (Udhagamandalam) at 2,200 m altitude. The full Mettupalayam-Ooty route (46 km) takes 5 hours and crosses 16 tunnels and 250 bridges - it's India's only rack railway, built by the British and opened in 1899. Book your tickets in advance, especially in season (October-June).

⏱ 90 min
2

In Ooty, visit the Government Botanical Gardens - 22 hectares of gardens founded in 1848 by the British, with a collection of over 1,000 plant species including fossilized trees 20 million years old. The terraced garden descends along a hillside, with a rose section, fern house and massive tropical trees. Allow 1-1.5 hours to visit. Entry is modest.

⏱ 75 min
3

Descend late in the day toward Coonoor (20 minutes by train or 30 minutes by road). On the way or once there, stop at Sim's Park - a 12-hectare botanical garden nestled in a natural ravine, with species of pines, magnolias and trees endemic to the Nilgiris. The park is less crowded than Ooty's gardens and offers a very pleasant walk in a hilly setting.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the morning, trek to Droog Fort - an old military outpost of Tipu Sultan (18th century) perched at 1,800 m altitude, 15 km from Coonoor. Only a wall and watchtower remain, but the path crossing Nonsuch Estate's tea plantations (3-4 km walk) is superb. From the summit, the view plunges over the entire Coonoor valley. Watch for wild bison in the area - keep your distance. Bring good shoes and water, there's nothing at the top.

⏱ 120 min

Evening in Coonoor: the cool altitude (15-20°C at night) is a welcome relief after the plains' heat. Enjoy a masala chai in one of the small shops in Coonoor bazaar, or try the artisan chocolates and local fruit jams - the region is known for its high-altitude products. Bring a sweater for the evening.

Day 12

Puducherry

Former French outpost with grid streets and Sri Aurobindo's ashram

★★★★☆
📍 Promenade Beach Puducherry📍 French Quarter Puducherry📍 Aurobindo Ashram Puducherry📍 Auroville Puducherry
1

Arrival in Puducherry - the former French trading post retains a unique charm in India. The French Quarter (White Town), laid out in a grid of straight streets, lines up colonial houses in ochre and pale yellow walls, with bougainvillea spilling from wrought-iron balconies. Stroll along Promenade Beach by the Bay of Bengal - it's where the whole town gathers at sunset. No swimming here (the rocks), but the atmosphere is unique.

⏱ 60 min
2

Sri Aurobindo Ashram, in the heart of the French Quarter, is a place of contemplation open to all. Founded in 1926 by philosopher Sri Aurobindo and Mirra Alfassa ('The Mother'), it still welcomes a resident community. The samadhi (tomb) of Sri Aurobindo and The Mother is the central point - covered in flowers, in total silence. Free, open 8am to noon and 2pm to 6pm.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

End of itinerary and free time in Puducherry. If you have half a day, push on to Auroville (12 km north) - the 'city of dawn' founded in 1968, with its golden Matrimandir (access by reservation the day before). Otherwise, enjoy the French Quarter cafes, rent a bike to explore the quiet streets, and savor a final Franco-Tamil meal before departure.

⏱ 90 min

Puducherry is well connected onward: Chennai is 150 km away (3 hours by road) with its international airport, and buses leave every 30 minutes. If you're continuing your journey in South India, itineraries to Kerala or deep Tamil Nadu depart easily from here.
