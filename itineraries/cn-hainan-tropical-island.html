✨ Highlights
  • The Nanshan Guanyin dominates the South China Sea from 108 meters high - it's one of the world's tallest statues, inaugurated in 2005
  • Hainan has the planet's only circular high-speed railway: 653 km around the island, complete circuit in 4h30
  • Li brocade weaving techniques, found in Wuzhishan, are UNESCO-listed since 2009 and date back 3,000 years
  • Haikou's Qilou Street counts over 600 Sino-European arcade houses, a legacy of early 20th-century overseas Chinese returnees
📆 Best period

November, December, January, February, March, April

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 1 and 5 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming flowers, ideal weather, moderate crowds, excellent for most regions, comfortable travel.

Fall (September-October): Perfect weather, clear skies, comfortable temperatures, peak tourist season, higher prices, autumn colors beautiful.

Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, rainy season, crowded tourist sites, highest prices, but best for Tibet and western regions.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, fewer crowds, lower prices, perfect for southern regions, harsh in north, unique winter experiences.

Regional Variations: Vast climate diversity, southern subtropical, northern continental, western plateau, plan according to regions visited.

🌍 What to discover in CN?

Ancient civilization meets modern superpower with Great Wall, forbidden cities, and cutting-edge technology. Experience diverse landscapes, incredible cuisine, and 5000-year cultural heritage. The country offers bustling megacities, pristine nature, and traditional arts. China provides overwhelming cultural richness and geographical diversity in rapidly modernizing nation.

📋 Important notes

Hainan is a free port: French citizens get visa-free entry for up to 30 days. Avoid typhoon season (May to October) and visit November through April (sea at 26°C, clear skies). Note: Wenchang Space Launch Center is off-limits to foreigners - use the public Yaoguang viewing platform in Dongjiao Town to watch launches instead.

Stop 1

Haikou

Where tropical trade winds meet 600 Sino-European arcade houses

📍 Qilou Old Street Haikou📍 Hai Rui Tomb Haikou📍 Five Officials Temple Haikou📍 Hainan Provincial Museum
1

Welcome to Haikou, Hainan's capital and your gateway to the tropical island. First stop: Qilou Street in the historic quarter. Over 600 arcade houses in Sino-European style, built between 1820 and 1940 by overseas Chinese returning from Singapore, Penang, and Saigon. The Sipai Building, the oldest, dates back to the Southern Song dynasty (12th century). Baroque, Indian, Arab, and Asian architecture blended together - unique in China.

⏱ 90 min
2

Five kilometers west of downtown, don't miss Hai Rui's Tomb, honoring a Ming dynasty mandarin born in Haikou in 1514 who became a symbol of Confucian integrity. Hai Rui dared criticize Emperor Jiajing in a famous 1566 letter, was imprisoned, then rehabilitated. Mao Zedong made him an official hero. The stone mausoleum dates from 1589, restored under the Qing and again in 1996. Quiet, shaded, rarely crowded.

⏱ 60 min
3

Stop by the Temple of the Five Officials (Wugong Ci), 2 km southeast, honoring five mandarins exiled to Hainan under the Tang and Song dynasties for standing up to the imperial court. The current temple dates from 1889, built of red lacquered wood and framed by century-old banyans. It's the island's oldest temple. Combined with Hai Rui's Tomb, allow half a day for the historical sites.

⏱ 60 min
4

Visit the Hainan Provincial Museum, free admission, opened in 2008. You'll find 60,000 pieces covering the island's history - shipwrecks loaded with ceramics from the Maritime Silk Road, Li and Miao minority costumes, tropical forest fauna. Good context before the rest of your journey. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Dive into Haikou cuisine at lunch. Head to Bo'ai Street for authentic Hainan noodle soup (Hainan fen) or chicken rice (Wenchang chicken rice), the island's most iconic dish, exported all the way to Singapore. Next door, a tropical fruit market: mangosteens, rambutans, dragon fruit. Also try the iced qing bu liang tea, a mix of beans, taro, and shaved ice.

⏱ 90 min

In the evening, stroll through Qilou Old Street after dark, when the arcades light up. Craft shops, café terraces, contemporary art exhibitions in former consulates. Slow atmosphere, somewhere between Macau and Singapore. Perfect for a cocktail or iced tea on a terrace.

⏱ 90 min
Stop 2

Haikou

Where tropical trade winds meet 600 Sino-European arcade houses

📍 Haikou Volcanic Cluster Geopark📍 Holiday Beach Haikou📍 Dongzhai Mangrove Forest Haikou
1

Head to Shishan Volcanic Geopark, 15 km southwest of Haikou. You'll find over 40 inactive craters, including Mahushan, formed roughly 13,000 years ago - geologically recent. You can descend into the caldera via basalt stairs, among ferns and strangler figs. UNESCO Global Geopark site. Allow 2 hours on-site, early morning before the heat hits.

⏱ 120 min
2

Don't miss Dongzhai Mangrove Reserve, east of Haikou: China's largest mangrove forest, covering over 3,300 hectares, with more than 200 species of migratory birds. Boat tour through the aerial roots, stop at the observation station. Fragile ecosystem: no touching shellfish or crabs. Very few foreign tourists, unlike the rest of the island.

⏱ 120 min
3

Holiday Beach, Haikou's urban beach, is free and open to all. You'll find blonde sand, warm water (24-26°C in winter), coconut palms, street vendors selling fresh coconuts in the background. Not the island's most beautiful coastline - save Sanya for that - but ideal for a late afternoon rest before continuing your journey. Sunset over the Qiongzhou Strait.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Book a Hainanese cooking class with a local family (via schools like Cookly or Haikou Cooking Class). On the menu: Wenchang chicken, three-sauce lamb, sago dessert. Wenchang chicken requires slow poaching, crispy skin and white meat, served with ginger-garlic sauce. It's Hainan's national dish and the basis for Singapore's famous Hainan Chicken Rice.

⏱ 180 min

Spend the evening on Haidian Island, connected to downtown by three bridges. You'll find the student and professional quarter, with rooftop bars, food trucks, and vibrant alleys. Sample Hainan coffee (Singapore style, strong, sweet, condensed milk) and watch cruise ships enter the harbor.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 50min
Stop 3

Wenchang

Where coconut groves frame China's coastal gateway to space

📍 Dongjiao Coconut Plantation Wenchang📍 Yaoguang Rocket Viewing Platform Wenchang📍 Tongguling Nature Reserve📍 Wenchang Confucius Temple
1

Take the express train to Wenchang (50 min from Haikou East), birthplace of the Chinese diaspora to Southeast Asia. First stop: Dongjiao coconut grove, China's largest. Over 20 km of coastline planted with coconut palms, some 30-50 years old, alongside shaded bike paths. Free entry. Rent a bike at the entrance, allow 2 hours for the ride. Caribbean atmosphere, flip-flops and fresh coconut mandatory.

⏱ 120 min
2

Head to Longlou Town, 30 minutes by car, where the Yaoguang viewing platform lets you watch rocket launches from Wenchang Space Launch Center - off-limits to foreigners. Inaugurated in May 2025, the platform covers 19,000 m² and accommodates up to 2,000 spectators during Long March 5 and 7 liftoffs. The center, operational since 2016, is China's only coastal spaceport (just 19° north latitude, ideal for launches). Check the launch schedule before going.

⏱ 90 min
3

Ten kilometers north, you'll find Tongguling Nature Reserve, home to "eastern Hainan's first peak," an ancient volcano collapsed into the sea. Trails through subtropical forest, sweeping views over the east coast bays. Nearby Wenchang Stone Park features spectacular marine erosion formations along 2 km of coastline. Bonus: it's one of eight recommended sites for watching official launches.

⏱ 90 min
4

Downtown, detour to Wenchang Confucius Temple, founded under the Song and rebuilt in 1042, one of the best-preserved in southern China. You'll find red courtyards, lion statues, and red lacquered wooden gates that still host spring examination rituals today. Wenchang historically produced more imperial officials than any other Hainan city.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Lunch on Wenchang chicken in its home village. The chicken is free-range raised in coconut groves, fed on fermented coconut and small fish - hence its particularly rich meat. A typical Tianci district restaurant (part of Wenchang) has served the dish for three generations.

⏱ 90 min

If luck is with you, witness a rocket launch from Yaoguang platform. A Long March 5 liftoff - roughly 869 tons at lift-off - produces a deep rumble felt for several kilometers. White light, orange trail, vibrations in your chest according to eyewitnesses. Rare spectacle in China, not to be missed if your visit coincides with a launch.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 65 km · 50min
Stop 4

Xinglong

A pocket of Java in China, where Creole coffee meets Malay-Chinese flavors

📍 Xinglong Tropical Botanical Garden📍 Xinglong Coffee Plantation📍 Boao Asia Forum Conference Center📍 Yudai Beach Boao
1

Welcome to Boao, 60 km southeast of Wenchang, transformed in two decades from a small fishing village to headquarters of the Boao Forum for Asia - the "Asian Davos" founded in 2001. You'll discover the Yongchang Pavilion conference center, designed by architect Zhang Yonghe, which hosts regional heads of state every spring. The peninsula where three rivers meet (Wanquan, Jiuqu, Longgun) before flowing into the sea offers a landscape unique in China.

⏱ 90 min
2

Walk along Yudai Beach ("jade belt"), 5 km from Boao center, which separates the river estuary from the open sea. It's one of the world's longest narrow sand strips - 8 km long and just a few meters wide. Clear water, palm trees, few people on weekdays. The sunset walk between river and sea is stunning.

⏱ 60 min
3

Head to Xinglong, 50 km south, a former village created in the 1950s-70s to welcome overseas Chinese repatriated from Indonesia, Vietnam, Malaysia, and Burma. Today, you'll still hear Javanese, Vietnamese, and Malay spoken in some families. The Tropical Botanical Garden, founded in 1957, houses over 1,200 plant species of Southeast Asian origin.

⏱ 90 min
4

Head to Xinglong coffee village, planted in the 1950s by Indonesian repatriates, where Hainanese arabica robusta is cultivated. You'll see old artisans still roasting over wood fires in earthen ovens. Coffee is served very strong, sweet, sometimes with butter - a local tradition derived from Javanese cafés. Small tasting and beans to take home.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Coffee roasting workshop in Xinglong, at a family farm. You'll see the complete process: sorting green beans, roasting over open fire, hand grinding, Singapore-style tasting (arm extended, sock filter). Allow about 90 minutes. Leave with a bag of your own roast.

⏱ 90 min

Taste the Malay-Chinese meal in Xinglong, a dish unique in mainland China. On the menu: Hainanese nasi lemak, satay, sambal, coconut curry rice. This Sino-Malay Creole cuisine exists only on this narrow strip of land. A legacy of the 1960s repatriates. Ice-cold Tsingtao beer to accompany.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 50min
Stop 5

Wanning

Where turquoise waves roll into China's premier surfing paradise

📍 Shimei Bay Wanning📍 Riyue Bay surfing Wanning📍 Nanwan Monkey Island📍 Sun Moon Bay surf
1

Welcome to Wanning, China's surf capital. You arrive at Riyue Bay ("Sun-Moon"), a Chinese stop on the World Surf League Qualifying Series - 2025 edition December 11-17, over 160 surfers from more than ten countries. Consistent waves, sandy bottom, local surf school. Waves are best November through March, when the northeast swell is consistent.

⏱ 60 min
2

Thirty kilometers north, you'll discover Shimei Bay ("stone beauty"), which draws a perfect crescent of white sand for 9 km. It's one of the island's most beautiful beaches, still relatively unspoiled. Several luxury hotels (Banyan Tree, Le Méridien) have placed their villas here, but beach access remains public. Turquoise water as far as the eye can see, ideal for a morning at the beach before sightseeing.

⏱ 120 min
3

Nanwan Monkey Island, 50 km south, is home to roughly 1,500 Chinese macaques. You access it via a 2,138 m cable car over the sea, inaugurated in 1996, which was long the longest of its kind in China. The monkeys are semi-wild, very curious, even aggressive if you brandish food. Don't wear sunglasses on your head, they'll steal them. Scientific reserve since 1965.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Take a surf lesson at Riyue Bay (2 hours, board and wetsuit provided). Several PADI-certified schools on the beach, English-speaking instructors. Beginner to intermediate level - waves break gently, perfect for learning to stand up. The best session for you is 7:30 am, before wind and crowds.

⏱ 120 min

Late afternoon, walk along Shimei Bay seawall at sunset. Light shifts from gold to pink in fifteen minutes, the sky reflects in the shallow water. A few small grilled fish restaurants set up tables on the sand - order local grouper with salt and lime.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 110 km · 55min
Stop 6

Sanya

Where a 108-meter goddess watches over crystalline bays

📍 Xincun Tanka Floating Village Lingshui📍 Yalong Bay Sanya📍 Sanya Bay coastline
1

Before Sanya, stop in Lingshui for Xincun Bay. You'll see the large floating village of the Tanka - a fishing people who have lived on water for 700 years, descendants of Song dynasty coastal migrants. Houses sit on bamboo rafts moored in neighborhoods. You reach it by boat, visit fish farms, lunch with locals. Remains one of China's last authentic Tanka communities.

⏱ 120 min
2

Sanya, the island's premier beach resort, awaits you. First discovery: Yalong Bay, considered the island's most beautiful beach - 7 km of fine white sand, translucent turquoise water up to 30 meters from shore. Dubbed "China's first bay." Luxury hotels line their villas along the waterfront, but the beach is free access. Sea at 26°C in winter, ideal for swimming.

⏱ 90 min
3

Stroll along Sanya Bay, the urban beach in the heart of the city. 22 km of coastline lined with coconut palms, dotted with small family beaches. More lively than Yalong Bay, more accessible. Spectacular sunset over the South China Sea - light shifts from orange to mauve quickly. Drink huts and bars on the sand.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Boat in Xincun Bay to visit the Tanka floating village. Allow 1 hour navigation, plus a stop at a floating restaurant for lunch - raw fish with chili, live shrimp, ginger clams. Served on bamboo tables directly connected to the pontoons. Fair price, around 100-150 yuan per person.

⏱ 180 min

Evening at Sanya night market at Diyilou ("First Market"), downtown. Skewers of grilled seafood, ginger fish, chili crabs, green mangoes with salt. Locals eat here, it's packed, lively, perfect after a long day. Ice-cold Pearl River beer at 5 yuan a large bottle.

⏱ 90 min
Stop 7

Sanya

Where a 108-meter goddess watches over crystalline bays

📍 Nanshan Cultural Tourism Zone Sanya📍 Guanyin of Nanshan statue📍 Tianya Haijiao Sanya📍 Big Buddha Hill
1

Head southwest of Sanya to Nanshan Cultural Park, founded in 1988 for the 2,000th anniversary of Buddhism in China. You'll find across 30 km² several Tang-style temples, gardens, statues. The Guanyin of Nanshan statue, standing on an artificial island connected to the coast by a 280 m causeway, rises 108 meters high - one of the world's tallest statues. It was inaugurated on April 24, 2005, after 6 years of construction, in the presence of 108 monks from across Asia.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Guanyin has three faces: one looks inland to bless China, the other two look to sea to protect the world. One face holds a sutra, another a rosary, the third a lotus. Mixed metal structure coated with anti-salt fluoropolymer, roughly 2,600 tons total. Inside, in Yuantong Hall, you'll find a Guanyin statue in gold and jade inlaid with precious stones (the "small" Guanyin set with 100 kg of gold and 120 carats of diamonds). You can climb to the goddess's feet for the view.

⏱ 60 min
3

Twenty kilometers away, you arrive at Tianya Haijiao ("Edge of Sky, Corner of Sea"), a site that owes its name to large rocks inscribed with Song dynasty calligraphy. Under the Tang, it was an exile site for disgraced officials - they said it was "the end of the world." Chinese couples come en masse to bless their love, it's become an iconic wedding site in southern China.

⏱ 90 min
4

Luhuitou Park ("the deer turns back"), on the eponymous hill, offers the best panoramic view of Sanya Bay. The bronze deer statue, erected in 1987, stands 12 m high. Li legend tells of a hunter who chased a deer to the cliff edge - the animal stopped, turned around and transformed into a young girl, who became his wife. It's the mythical origin of the Li people.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Vegetarian meal at Nanshan Temple, famous throughout China. Refined Buddhist cuisine, no garlic or onion, based on tofu, mushrooms, vegetables and meat imitations. Served in a large quiet hall with sea views. Allow 80-150 yuan per person, it's one of Asia's finest vegetarian meals.

⏱ 90 min

Relaxing evening at a Yalong Bay hotel spa. Traditional Hainanese massage with coconut oil and local aloe, 90 minutes for 400-600 yuan depending on hotels. Most resorts accept non-guests for their spas. Take advantage to recover after this big cultural day.

⏱ 90 min
Stop 8

Sanya

Where a 108-meter goddess watches over crystalline bays

📍 Wuzhizhou Island Sanya📍 Haitang Bay Sanya📍 Atlantis Sanya📍 Yalong Bay snorkeling
1

Head to Wuzhizhou Island, 30 km east of Sanya. Despite its name often mistranslated as "Monkey Island," there are no macaques here - common confusion with Nanwan, don't make that mistake. Wuzhizhou is one of the island's best diving and snorkeling spots. Visibility up to 25 m in clear weather, corals, clownfish, turtles. The island covers 1.5 km², ferry from Houhai port (10 minute crossing).

⏱ 240 min
2

Head to Haitang Bay, northeast of Sanya, which became the island's new chic bay since 2010. You'll see the Atlantis Sanya complex (opened 2018), with its giant aquarium, water park, and 1,314 rooms. Beach stretching 22 km, still wild in places - but urbanization is advancing quickly. Worth seeing for the contrast between preserved nature and XXL hotels.

⏱ 90 min
3

If you still have energy late in the day, climb Phoenix Mountain for the panoramic view of the three bays (Yalong, Haitang and Sanya Bay). Cable car in 8 minutes, observation platform at 480 m. It's an opportunity to locate all the beaches you've seen these two days, and see the South China Sea at a glance.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Snorkeling or introductory dive at Wuzhizhou. Several PADI-certified centers on the island, complete equipment. Diving at -8/-12 m, 30 minutes underwater, allow 600-800 yuan with a dedicated instructor. Water at 25°C even in winter, so no need for thick wetsuit.

⏱ 180 min

Duty-free shopping at Sanya International Duty-Free. Hainan is China's only total free port, and mainland Chinese fly in by the planeload to buy cosmetics, perfumes, watches, alcohol. Annual quotas of 100,000 yuan duty-free per person. For foreigners, it's less advantageous than Hong Kong, but simpler if you're already here.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 80 km · 1h30
Stop 9

Wuzhishan

Where ancestral Li threads weave stories among misty peaks

📍 Wuzhishan National Nature Reserve📍 Shuiman Township Li Miao village📍 Maona Village Wuzhishan📍 Yahu Terraced Fields
1

Head to Wuzhishan, the "Five Finger Mountain," sacred mountain of the Li, an aboriginal people present here for over 3,000 years. You'll cross China's last primary tropical forest, in a national reserve opened in 1985. The main peak reaches 1,867 m. Marked hiking trail, rare wildlife (Hainan gibbon, among the world's most endangered primates, fewer than 40 surviving individuals).

⏱ 180 min
2

Shuiman village, nestled in the valley at 600 m altitude, is a Li cultural center. You'll see traditional "upturned boat-shaped" houses (chuanxing wu), thatched roofs. A few rare Li grandmothers still wear ancestral facial tattoos - a practice banned since 1949, dying out with them. The last tattooed generation is now 80-95 years old.

⏱ 90 min
3

Fifteen kilometers away, Maona village offers a dive into Li daily life: brocade weaving, drying medicinal herbs, raising black-footed pigs. Li brocade (Li jin) has 3,000 years of history. Inscribed in 2009 on the UNESCO List of Intangible Cultural Heritage in Need of Urgent Safeguarding (not the Representative List), it has since paraded at Paris Fashion Week in 2024 and 2025 thanks to Dalishen patterns and Gangong bird motifs.

⏱ 60 min
4

Rise early for the Yahu terraced rice fields, in Maoyang township, considered Hainan's most beautiful. They climb the mountain slopes, water mirrors in spring, golden waves in autumn. More modest than Yuanyang's in Yunnan, but much more accessible and less crowded. Morning light is finest.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Li brocade weaving lesson with a local artisan in Maona or Shuiman. You learn the basics - foot loom, shuttle, cotton threads dyed with bark and roots. Li women traditionally weave sitting on the ground. Allow 2-3 hours to make a small sample (bracelet or bookmark) that you'll take home. Fair rates, around 200-300 yuan per session.

⏱ 180 min

Li meal in a Shuiman family: ant chicken (the "ant chicken" is a local breed), pandanus leaf rice, black Shanlan rice wine (sacred wine served in bamboo), wild forest vegetables. Taste the "jianlan jiu," sweet cardamom wine. A cuisine very different from mainland China.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 430 km · 3h
Stop 10

Haikou

Where tropical trade winds meet 600 Sino-European arcade houses

📍 Haikou Meilan Airport📍 Haikou Mission Hills📍 Haikou duty-free shopping
1

Return to Haikou in two stages. First 80 km by road from Wuzhishan to Sanya HSR station (about 1h30), as Wuzhishan isn't served by the circular rail line. Then 1h30 express train to Haikou East, along the southeast coast. The complete Hainan loop totals 653 km - the world's only circular high-speed railway, western section completed in 2015. Views of rice paddies and coconut groves from the carriage.

⏱ 60 min
2

Upon arrival, end your stay with a stop at Haikou International Duty-Free Shopping Complex. It's the world's largest duty-free store since opening in 2022 - 280,000 m², over 800 international brands. For foreigners, it's a chance to buy alcohol, perfumes and cosmetics tax-free before the return flight.

⏱ 120 min
3

Before Meilan airport (25 minutes from downtown), take a final walk through Qilou arcade street, grab a last Hainan coffee on a terrace. Wenchang chicken rice, noodle soup, green mango with chili - the classics for a last meal. Meilan airport has direct connections to most major Chinese cities, plus Hong Kong, Singapore, Bangkok and Seoul.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If your flight leaves in the evening, stop at Mission Hills Haikou, the world's largest golf complex with ten courses designed by Jack Nicklaus, Greg Norman, Annika Sörenstam and others. Greens at 600-1,200 yuan on weekdays, booking required. Spa and hot springs on-site for non-golfers.

⏱ 180 min
