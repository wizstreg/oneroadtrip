Island-Hopping the Exuma Cays: From Nassau to the Swimming Pigs

📅 8 days📍 8 stops🚗 276 km⏱ 15h05

Set sail from Nassau and island-hop through the Exuma Cays. Mangrove creeks of Shroud Cay, the pristine marine park of Warderick Wells, nurse sharks at Compass Cay, Thunderball Grotto, and the famous swimming pigs of Pig Beach: an archipelago of 365 islets waiting to be explored by boat.

✨ Highlights
  • Warderick Wells: hike Boo Boo Hill for panoramic views over the entire archipelago
  • Thunderball Grotto: snorkel inside the James Bond cave at low tide
  • Compass Cay: swim alongside tame nurse sharks in crystal-clear water
  • Shroud Cay: drift through Sanctuary Creek by kayak and ride nature's water slide
📆 Best period

December, January, February, March, April

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 5 and 9 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 What to discover in BS?

Crystal-clear waters and swimming pigs create unique Caribbean adventures across 700 islands. Experience Atlantis resort excitement, conch fritters, and perfect beaches just miles from Florida. The islands offer incredible diving, fishing, and luxury resorts with authentic Bahamian culture. Perfect for tropical getaways with easy accessibility and diverse experiences.

📋 Important notes

Island-hopping itinerary by boat (charter, sailboat, or yacht) with nights at anchor or on moorings. For a land-based stay in George Town with day excursions, see BS::bahamas::exuma-nassau-5j.

Day 1

Nassau Paradise Island Anchorage

Where colonial charm meets the gateway to paradise

★★★★★
1

Bay Street lines Nassau's main waterfront strip with pastel-painted colonial facades and duty-free shops. Just inland, Parliament Square showcases three pink Georgian buildings that have housed the Bahamian government since the 19th century.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Queen's Staircase consists of 66 steps hewn from solid limestone by 600 enslaved laborers between 1793 and 1794. Waterfalls and dense tropical vegetation frame this historic passage that climbs to Fort Fincastle.

⏱ 60 min
3

Fort Fincastle, built in 1793 by Lord Dunmore atop Bennet's Hill, was designed in the shape of a paddle-wheel steamer. From New Providence's highest point, panoramic views sweep across the harbor and Paradise Island.

⏱ 45 min
4

Cross the bridge to Paradise Island for sweeping harbor views from the pedestrian walkway. Below, Potter's Cay hosts the fish market where vendors prepare conch salad to order right at their stalls.

⏱ 45 min
5

Prince George Wharf, the cruise ship terminal, offers front-row views of massive vessels and the constant pulse of the capital's waterfront.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walking tour through Nassau's historic center: from the Straw Market to Fort Fincastle via Parliament Square.

⏱ 120 min

Pre-passage preparation for the Exuma Cays: check weather windows, verify tides, and finalize the route to Shroud Cay (~50 nautical miles).

Day 2

Shroud Cay

A wild maze of mangrove creeks and nature's own water slide

★★☆☆☆
1

Shroud Cay is an archipelago of small islets linked by a labyrinth of mangrove creeks at the northern entrance to Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park. The uninhabited island operates under strict 'take nothing, leave nothing' protection.

⏱ 30 min
2

Sanctuary Creek cuts through Shroud Cay from west to east through dense mangroves. On mid-rising tide, the gentle current carries you toward the Exuma Sound exit. Green turtles, stingrays, and small nurse sharks cruise these crystal-clear channels.

⏱ 75 min
3

At Sanctuary Creek's ocean outlet, the 'Washing Machine' creates a natural rapid where tidal current squeezes between rocks. The fine-sand beach beyond is sheltered by a crescent sandbar.

⏱ 60 min
4

Climb the trail to Camp Driftwood, an observation post built in the 1960s. From this vantage point, 360° views encompass mangrove channels, sandbars, and the Exuma Sound. On clear days, Norman's Cay's airstrip is visible to the south.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Drift through Sanctuary Creek by dinghy, kayak, or paddleboard on mid-rising tide. The current naturally carries you toward the ocean beach - time your return before the tide turns.

⏱ 90 min

Swim in the Washing Machine's natural rapid at the creek outlet. The current pushes you toward a shallow sandbar - it feels like a wild water slide carved by nature.

⏱ 45 min

Snorkeling on seagrass beds and sandy bottoms around the anchorage. Starfish, conchs, and juvenile fish hide in the underwater meadows.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 28 km · 1h30
Day 3

Warderick Wells Cay Hq Moorings

The beating heart of the world's first marine sanctuary

★★☆☆☆
1

Warderick Wells is the heart of Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park, established in 1958 as the world's first marine park. The northern anchorage forms a horseshoe of turquoise water bordered by beaches and sandbars that emerge at low tide.

⏱ 45 min
2

The park headquarters on the main beach displays the skeleton of a beached sperm whale. Interpretive panels along the trails explain the island's flora, fauna, and limestone geology.

⏱ 45 min
3

Boo Boo Hill, the highest point in the Exuma Cays, offers spectacular views over the Exuma Sound on one side and the turquoise shallows of the Bahama Bank on the other. Tradition holds that every sailor should leave a piece of driftwood carved with their boat's name. Legend says the hill is haunted by souls from a shipwreck - on full moon nights, hymns supposedly carry on the wind.

⏱ 60 min
4

The northern beaches of Warderick Wells, accessible by marked trails, are lined with limestone formations pierced by blowholes. When Atlantic swells run strong, water erupts in natural geysers.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike the network of marked trails on Warderick Wells. The complete circuit passes Boo Boo Hill, the blowholes, the ruins of Davis Plantation, and the eastern beaches.

⏱ 120 min

Drift snorkeling over coral heads in the north mooring field. Tidal current gently carries you over the corals - nurse sharks, turtles, and spotted eagle rays regularly pass through.

⏱ 60 min

Free kayaks provided by the park - explore secluded coves and beaches around the mooring field at sunset.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 1h20
Day 4

Staniel Cay Main Anchorage

A remote fishing village anchoring the middle of nowhere

★★☆☆☆
1

Compass Cay shelters a sanctuary of nurse sharks in its private marina. These docile sharks, accustomed to humans over generations, glide around the docks and allow close approaches in crystal-clear water.

⏱ 75 min
2

Crescent Beach, on Compass Cay's eastern shore, curves in a long arc of pristine sand facing the Exuma Sound. A trail crosses the island from the marina to reach it.

⏱ 60 min
3

Pipe Creek reveals one of the Caribbean's most beautiful sandbars between two islets. The water is so transparent that the sandy bottom appears within reach even in two meters of depth.

⏱ 60 min
4

Staniel Cay, a small fishing village with an airstrip and yacht club, serves as the logistical hub of the Exuma Cays. It's one of the few cays where you'll find fuel, fresh water, and a grocery store.

⏱ 45 min
5

Rocky Dundas, two underwater caves accessible by dinghy from Staniel Cay, shelter submerged stalactites and stalagmites. Sunlight penetrates through natural openings and illuminates the formations.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swim with the nurse sharks at Compass Cay. Walk down the dock steps and let them approach - they've been accustomed to visitors for decades.

⏱ 60 min

Dinghy exploration of channels between Pipe Cay, Compass Cay, and surrounding islets. Stop for swimming on emerging sandbars.

⏱ 90 min

Snorkeling in the caves of Rocky Dundas. Swim through the submerged entrance to admire limestone formations illuminated by natural light.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 15min
Day 5

Big Major Cay Pig Beach Anchorage

Where wild pigs swim out to greet every passing boat

★★★★★
1

Thunderball Grotto, west of Staniel Cay, is an underwater cave made famous by two James Bond films (Thunderball in 1965 and Never Say Never Again in 1983). The submerged entrance leads to a cavity lit by natural openings where angelfish, sergeant majors, and snappers swim in shafts of sunlight.

⏱ 75 min
2

Big Major Cay, nicknamed Pig Beach, is inhabited by a colony of swimming pigs that have become world-famous. They swim out to greet approaching boats. The island is uninhabited by humans - only the pigs reside here.

⏱ 75 min
3

O'Brien's Cay, nicknamed 'the Aquarium,' offers one of the best snorkeling spots in the Exuma Cays. Dozens of tropical fish species concentrate in its shallow waters protected by the marine park.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Big Major Cay anchorage, sheltered between islets, bathes in champagne-colored waters. At sunset, the raking light transforms the shallows into a gradient from turquoise to gold.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Snorkeling in Thunderball Grotto at low tide slack. Enter through the submerged opening - inside, sunbeams pierce the vault and illuminate schools of fish.

⏱ 60 min

Snorkeling at O'Brien's Cay (the Aquarium). The fish, protected by park regulations, are so numerous and unafraid they surround swimmers.

⏱ 60 min

Observe the swimming pigs of Pig Beach from the dinghy or beach. Do not feed or touch them - respect the wildlife.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 57 km · 3h
Day 6

Normans Cay Anchorage

An island with a notorious past and an underwater plane to prove it

★☆☆☆☆
1

Norman's Cay remains infamous as the base of operations for a drug cartel in the 1980s. The island was later purchased and transformed into a private paradise, then opened to cruisers. Its sheltered lagoon makes it a choice anchorage.

⏱ 45 min
2

The wreck of a Curtiss C-46 aircraft rests in 5 meters of crystal-clear water south of the airstrip. The fuselage, colonized by corals and sponges, has become an artificial reef teeming with tropical fish.

⏱ 75 min
3

The northern beaches of Norman's Cay stretch in ribbons of white sand bordered by coconut palms. Sandbars exposed at low tide create natural lagoons ideal for swimming.

⏱ 60 min
4

The old airstrip crosses the island from north to south. Now overgrown with vegetation, it remains a visible landmark for navigators.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Snorkeling around the plane wreck. The water is shallow enough to observe the fuselage from the surface. Parrotfish, juvenile barracudas, and rays frequent the site.

⏱ 60 min

Beachcombing on sandbars exposed at low tide. Water colors shift from jade green to deep blue depending on depth.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 17 km · 1h
Day 7

Allen Cays Iguana Reserve Anchorage

Where prehistoric dragons bask on sugar-white sand

★★★★★
1

Allen Cays (also called Leaf Cay) shelter a colony of Bahamian rock iguanas (Cyclura cychlura), a protected endemic species. These massive reptiles, which can reach 60 cm, approach visitors on the beach without fear.

⏱ 75 min
2

Small sandy coves between Allen Cays' rocks offer calm anchorages and waters of intense turquoise. The total absence of habitation reinforces the feeling of being at the edge of the world.

⏱ 60 min
3

Highbourne Cay, just to the north, has a marina and small store. It's the last provisioning point before the crossing back to Nassau.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Observe endemic iguanas on Allen Cay beach. Do not feed or touch them - species protected by Bahamian law.

⏱ 60 min

Snorkeling on seagrass beds and sandy bottoms around the anchorage. The shallow waters are ideal for observing turtles, rays, and juvenile fish.

⏱ 60 min

Prepare for the return crossing to Nassau: check weather, provision at Highbourne Cay if needed. Plan for a dawn departure tomorrow.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 64 km · 3h40
Day 8

Nassau Paradise Island Anchorage

Where colonial charm meets the gateway to paradise

★★★★★
1

Cabbage Beach stretches for 3 km along the northern shore of Paradise Island. Bordered by dunes and casuarina trees, it's one of New Providence's most beautiful beaches.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Nassau Straw Market gathers around 500 vendors. Straw bags, hats, and jewelry perpetuate a craft that once served to make fish traps and transport baskets.

⏱ 60 min
3

Arawak Cay, nicknamed the Fish Fry, is an artificial island built in 1969 with sand dredged from the harbor. Its colorful shacks serve conch salad, fried fish, and cracked lobster.

⏱ 60 min
4

Western Esplanade Beach, an urban beach between downtown and Arawak Cay, allows one last swim before leaving the Bahamas.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Swimming and relaxation on Cabbage Beach. Walking west takes you away from the crowds at the resort complexes.

⏱ 90 min

Sample conch salad and cracked conch at Arawak Cay stalls for a final Bahamian meal.

⏱ 60 min
