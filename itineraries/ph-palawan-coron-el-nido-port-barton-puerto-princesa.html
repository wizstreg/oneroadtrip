✨ Highlights
  • Coron → El Nido boat crossing: spectacular journey through limestone islands
  • Kayangan Lake: one of the Philippines' most beautiful lakes, ringed by cliffs
  • Puerto Princesa Underground River: 8 km beneath the mountain, UNESCO since 1999
  • Port Barton: Palawan's best-kept secret, between El Nido and Puerto Princesa
📆 Best period

December, January, February, March, April

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 5 and 9 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (December-May): Peak tourist season with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures (26-32°C), and excellent conditions for island hopping and beach activities. Highest prices and crowds.

Cool Dry Season (December-February): Most comfortable temperatures and lowest humidity. Perfect weather for all activities, though expect highest accommodation rates and tourist numbers.

Hot Dry Season (March-May): Hottest temperatures but still dry conditions. Good for beach activities, though midday heat can be intense. Shoulder season pricing.

Rainy Season (June-November): Monsoon season with typhoon risk, but lower prices and fewer tourists. Some islands remain accessible, afternoon rains common.

🌍 What to discover in PH?

Archipelago paradise with pristine beaches, vibrant culture, and incredible diversity. Experience traditional festivals, beautiful islands, and warm Filipino hospitality. The country offers cultural authenticity with natural beauty and adventure activities. Philippines provides diverse experiences with cultural richness and natural wonders.

📋 Important notes

Linear north-south route across Palawan. Manila-Busuanga flight on arrival, Puerto Princesa-Manila flight on departure. Factor in van transfers between stages (roads can be rough in places).

⚠️ SAFETY NOTE: The Philippines is generally safe in main tourist areas, though avoid southern Mindanao and Sulu archipelago due to terrorism and kidnapping risks. Stick to established tourist destinations like Luzon, Visayas, and northern Mindanao. Check current government advisories and use reputable transportation and accommodation.

Coron

Fishing port turned world-class dive destination, where emerald lagoons meet wartime wrecks

★★★☆☆
📍 Coron Town Palawan📍 Mount Tapyas Coron📍 Coron Market Palawan📍 Coron Port Palawan
1

Arrival at Busuanga Airport, a 30-minute drive from Coron Town. The van crosses a countryside of green hills and water buffalo before reaching the small town, wedged between the sea and towering limestone cliffs. Coron is no beach resort - it's a fishing port that's adapted to tourism without losing its soul.

⏱ 45 min
2

Coron Town's market comes alive in late afternoon. Fishermen unload the day's catch - tuna, marlin, giant prawns - which the waterfront restaurants grill to order for dinner. Try the local cashew nuts, a Palawan specialty, sold in bags on every street corner.

⏱ 40 min
3

Coron's alleyways are simple - concrete, corrugated iron and hand-painted signs. But that authenticity is the charm. No international chains, no shopping malls. A few local bakeries (panaderias) sell sweet bread still warm for a handful of pesos.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb the 721 steps of Mount Tapyas before sunset. The ascent is steep but short (20 minutes). At the summit, a white cross overlooks the entire bay of Coron - the limestone islands stand silhouetted against a sky turning orange. It's the best viewpoint in the area, and it's free.

⏱ 60 min

Grilled fish dinner on the waterfront. The restaurants lined up facing the harbor all work the same way: you choose your fish from the display, they grill it over charcoal. The price is negotiated by weight.

Coron

Fishing port turned world-class dive destination, where emerald lagoons meet wartime wrecks

★★★☆☆
📍 Kayangan Lake Coron📍 Twin Lagoon Coron📍 Barracuda Lake Coron📍 Coral Garden Coron
1

Day trip by bangka (traditional outrigger boat) around Coron Island. First stop: Kayangan Lake. After a short climb over the limestone rocks, the view of the lake below takes your breath away - a bowl of emerald water ringed by black cliffs draped in vegetation. The descent leads to a floating platform from which you dive into water so clear the bottom is visible at 15 meters.

⏱ 75 min
2

Twin Lagoon, two pools of turquoise water connected by a narrow passage between the cliffs. At low tide, you swim through an opening in the rock; at high tide, you climb a wooden ladder and descend the other side. The water in the second pool is warmer - underground springs heat it.

⏱ 50 min
3

Barracuda Lake, deeper and stranger. The water goes from 28°C at the surface to 38°C at depth - a mixture of fresh and salt water that creates convection currents visible to the naked eye. If you dive below 5 meters, the heat becomes palpable. The lake takes its name from a large solitary barracuda that used to live there.

⏱ 50 min
4

Final stop at Coral Garden, a reef accessible by snorkeling from the bangka. The corals are in good condition and the variety of tropical fish is impressive - clownfish, damselfish, parrotfish, surgeonfish.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Lunch served on the bangka between stops - rice, grilled fish, fruit. The boatmen prepare the meal on a small grill at the back of the boat while you swim. Simple and perfect.

⏱ 45 min

Free snorkeling around the reefs. If you don't have your own equipment, the bangkas provide it - quality varies, better to rent a good mask in Coron Town before the excursion.

⏱ 60 min

Coron

Fishing port turned world-class dive destination, where emerald lagoons meet wartime wrecks

★★★☆☆
📍 Japanese Wrecks Coron📍 Skeleton Wreck Coron📍 Maquinit Hot Springs Coron📍 CYC Beach Coron
1

Coron is one of the best wreck diving sites in the world. In September 1944, the US Navy sank about a dozen Japanese ships sheltering in the bay. Several of these wrecks rest between 10 and 40 meters deep, accessible by scuba diving. The Irako, a 147-meter supply ship, and the Akitsushima, a seaplane tender, are the most impressive.

⏱ 45 min
2

For non-divers, the Skeleton Wreck lies at just 5 meters depth - visible by snorkeling in calm weather. The hull overgrown with corals and sponges attracts schools of fish. It's a strange experience to float above a warship looking down through the water.

⏱ 50 min
3

CYC Beach, a small white sand beach on an islet 20 minutes by bangka, offers a break between dives. The water is calm, the bottom sandy, and the island barely 200 meters long.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Scuba diving on one or two wrecks (for certified divers). Coron's dive centers offer full-day or half-day trips. Book the day before, especially in high season (December-March).

⏱ 180 min

Late afternoon, head to Maquinit Hot Springs, 30 minutes by tuk-tuk from Coron Town. These hot saltwater springs (38-40°C) are nestled among the rocks by the sea. The main pool faces the sunset - a perfect moment of relaxation after a day of diving.

⏱ 75 min

El Nido

Towering limestone karsts plunging into turquoise lagoons, gateway to the Bacuit archipelago

★★★★☆
📍 El Nido Town Palawan📍 El Nido Beach Palawan📍 Corong-Corong Beach El Nido
1

The crossing from Coron to El Nido takes between 3.5 and 7 hours depending on the type of boat - speed boat or slow bangka. The open sea journey can be rough, especially during the monsoon (June-October). If you get seasick, take the speed boat and medication before departure.

⏱ 30 min
2

El Nido appears behind a curtain of black limestone cliffs rising above the sea. The contrast is striking: vertical rock spires 200 meters high drop straight into turquoise water. The small town stretches along a beige sand beach lined with restaurants and dive shops.

⏱ 40 min
3

Walk down El Nido's main street in late afternoon. It's lively without being overwhelming - tricycles (motorbikes with sidecars), stray dogs, children playing basketball on a concrete court, and travelers from around the world sitting at terrace tables.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

Sunset from Corong-Corong Beach, a 10-minute tricycle ride south of El Nido. The beach is quieter than the town center, and the view of the limestone cliffs illuminated by the evening light is remarkable.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner in one of the main street restaurants. Filipino cuisine is underrated - try the kare-kare (oxtail stew in peanut sauce) or sinigang (sour tamarind soup). Portions are generous and prices modest.

⏱ 60 min

El Nido

Towering limestone karsts plunging into turquoise lagoons, gateway to the Bacuit archipelago

★★★★☆
📍 Big Lagoon El Nido📍 Small Lagoon El Nido📍 Secret Lagoon El Nido📍 Shimizu Island El Nido
1

Island hopping day - Tour A, the most popular in El Nido. First stop: Big Lagoon, an arm of sea enclosed between towering cliffs. The bangka stops at the entrance and you continue by kayak through an increasingly narrow channel. The water shifts from deep blue to jade green in a matter of meters.

⏱ 60 min
2

Small Lagoon, right next door. You access it by swimming under a rock arch. On the other side, an enclosed pool surrounded by limestone walls - it feels like a movie set. Tropical fish swim between your legs in warm, transparent water.

⏱ 50 min
3

Secret Lagoon (Secret Beach), accessible through a hole in the cliff. You swim through an opening in the rock and emerge onto a tiny hidden beach, surrounded by walls on all sides - a circle of white sand and turquoise water invisible from outside.

⏱ 40 min
4

Shimizu Island, an islet planted with coconut palms and a coral reef accessible by snorkeling. Lunch is often served here - on the beach, in the shade of the trees.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Kayaking in the Big Lagoon. Kayaks are available for rent on site - expect 200-300 PHP. Paddle to the back of the lagoon where the water becomes so shallow you can see fish darting under the hull.

⏱ 60 min

Snorkeling around Shimizu Island. The corals are colorful and well-preserved, and visibility often exceeds 15 meters. Watch out for jellyfish during the wet season (June-October).

⏱ 50 min

El Nido

Towering limestone karsts plunging into turquoise lagoons, gateway to the Bacuit archipelago

★★★★☆
📍 Nacpan Beach El Nido📍 Las Cabanas Beach El Nido📍 Twin Beach Nacpan Calitang
1

Nacpan Beach, 45 minutes north of El Nido by scooter along a potholed road (improved in recent years but still bumpy in places). The detour is worth it: 4 kilometers of golden sand almost deserted, lined with coconut palms. No concrete resorts, just a few bamboo huts and hammocks. It's the beach that postcards don't show because there's nothing on it - and that's exactly the point.

⏱ 90 min
2

At the end of Nacpan, a sandbar connects to the neighboring beach of Calitang - the famous Twin Beach. One side has choppy water (ocean side), the other is flat (bay side). You walk between the two waters on a strip of sand 50 meters wide.

⏱ 40 min
3

Las Cabanas Beach, south of El Nido, is where travelers gather for sunset. A zipline connects the beach to a small islet opposite - a popular attraction but not mandatory. The beach itself is pleasant, with bars right on the sand.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

Scooter tour through El Nido's hinterland. Behind the coastal cliffs, the landscape changes: terraced rice fields, water buffalo in the paddies, villages of bamboo and corrugated iron where children wave as you pass. It's another face of Palawan, far from the turquoise lagoons.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset at Las Cabanas with a drink in a beach bar. The atmosphere is relaxed, the music mellow, and the sun drops behind the limestone islets.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 7

Port Barton

A timeless fishing village with no paved roads or ATMs, facing a pristine lagoon

★★☆☆☆
📍 Port Barton Beach Palawan📍 Port Barton Village Palawan
1

The journey from El Nido to Port Barton by van takes 3 to 4 hours on a road that cuts through Palawan's jungle. The first half is paved, the second becomes a track - bumps guaranteed. But the scenery compensates: dense tropical forest, rivers, isolated villages.

⏱ 30 min
2

Port Barton is the antithesis of El Nido. A village of 200 inhabitants, a sandy beach lined with coconut palms, no paved road, no ATM (bring cash), no nightlife. The wifi is hit-or-miss. It's exactly what those who come here are looking for.

⏱ 40 min
3

Walk along the main beach in late afternoon. Fishermen mend their nets, dogs sleep in the shade, a few travelers read in hammocks. At the end of the beach, a path leads to a small isolated cove.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

First swim in Port Barton bay. The water is calm, warm, and sea turtles regularly come to swim near the shore - especially early morning and late afternoon.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner in one of the village's small eateries. The choice is limited but fresh: grilled fish, rice, stir-fried vegetables. Some evenings, a guitarist plays Filipino ballads under the stars.

⏱ 45 min

Port Barton

A timeless fishing village with no paved roads or ATMs, facing a pristine lagoon

★★☆☆☆
📍 Island hopping Port Barton📍 Exotic Island Port Barton📍 German Island Port Barton📍 Starfish Island Port Barton
1

Bangka excursion around the Port Barton islands - the calm, intimate version of El Nido's island hopping. No crowds here: your bangka will often be the only one on site. Exotic Island, a white sandbar ringed with turquoise water, is the first stop.

⏱ 50 min
2

German Island, larger, has a shaded beach and a reef accessible in a few strokes. The clownfish are so unafraid they come to inspect you up close. The water is stunningly clear.

⏱ 50 min
3

Starfish Island takes its name from the starfish that populate the shallow waters around the islet. Don't take them out of the water (they die within minutes in the air) - observe them where they are, in 30 cm of water.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Snorkeling around the islands. Port Barton's reefs are among the best-preserved in Palawan - less frequented than El Nido's, they harbor remarkable coral diversity. Green turtles frequently spotted.

⏱ 90 min

Lunch on a deserted beach, prepared by the boatman: grilled fish, green mango, rice. Feet in the sand, alone on the island.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 9

Puerto Princesa

Palawan's laid-back capital, launching point for the UNESCO-listed underground river

★★★☆☆
📍 Underground River Puerto Princesa📍 Sabang Beach Palawan📍 Puerto Princesa Subterranean River UNESCO
1

Route heading south toward Sabang - the starting point for Puerto Princesa's underground river, a UNESCO World Heritage site since 1999. The journey from Port Barton takes 4 to 5 hours by van, with a possible stop in Sabang for the night or as a day trip from Puerto Princesa (1.5 hours drive).

⏱ 30 min
2

Puerto Princesa's underground river burrows 8.2 kilometers under a limestone mountain - it's one of the longest navigable underground rivers in the world. The visit is by paddled boat for the first 1.5 kilometers. The guide illuminates the walls with a flashlight: stalactites, stalagmites, thousands of bats, and rock formations that resemble cathedrals.

⏱ 75 min
3

Sabang Beach, right across from the boat landing, is a pleasant surprise. Fine sand, clear water, thick jungle in the background. Few people outside the river tour hours.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Underground river tour by boat. Reserve your permit in advance - the number of visitors is limited to 900 per day. The tour lasts 45 minutes in near-total darkness, lulled by the sound of water and the beating of bat wings.

⏱ 90 min

Swimming at Sabang Beach after the tour. The water is warm, the bottom sandy, and monitor lizards (large lizards) stroll calmly on the beach - harmless but impressive.

Puerto Princesa

Palawan's laid-back capital, launching point for the UNESCO-listed underground river

★★★☆☆
📍 Puerto Princesa City Center📍 Honda Bay Puerto Princesa📍 Crocodile Farm Puerto Princesa📍 Baker's Hill Puerto Princesa
1

Puerto Princesa is Palawan's capital - a sprawling provincial city with no great architectural charm, but lively and functional. The main street (Rizal Avenue) concentrates restaurants, shops and local life. This is where you return to civilization after several days in isolated villages.

⏱ 35 min
2

Puerto Princesa's central market is worth a morning visit. Tropical fruits in abundance (Palawan mangoes, considered the best in the Philippines), dried fish, spices. The atmosphere is noisy and colorful.

⏱ 40 min
3

Baker's Hill, on the outskirts of town, is a kitschy but enjoyable garden park, famous for its hopia (filled pastries) and cookies. Filipinos love it - it's an almost obligatory stop for them. The view over the valley from the terrace is pleasant.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

Excursion to Honda Bay, 30 minutes north of Puerto Princesa. Island hopping between three or four islets - Starfish Island, Luli Island, Cowrie Island. Less spectacular than Coron or El Nido, but the water is beautiful and the reefs alive.

⏱ 180 min

Dinner on Rizal Avenue. Try the crocodile sisig (yes, chopped crocodile sisig-style) - a local specialty, a bit touristy but original. Otherwise, grilled chicken inasal is a Filipino classic that's always reliable.

⏱ 60 min

Puerto Princesa

Palawan's laid-back capital, launching point for the UNESCO-listed underground river

★★★☆☆
📍 Iwahig River Puerto Princesa📍 Mangroves Puerto Princesa📍 Firefly watching Puerto Princesa📍 Immaculate Conception Cathedral Puerto Princesa
1

The Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception, in central Puerto Princesa, is a city landmark - white and blue façade, cool and quiet interior. Filipinos are deeply Catholic and the churches are rarely empty.

⏱ 25 min
2

Take a tricycle to Iwahig, 20 minutes from the center. The river winds through dense mangrove where you can travel by paddleboard or kayak. The ecosystem is rich - herons, kingfishers, mangrove crabs. It's calm, green, and the opposite of the turquoise blue of previous days.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Palawan Butterfly Eco-Garden, a small botanical garden on the outskirts of town, houses butterflies endemic to Palawan and a collection of medicinal plants used by indigenous peoples. A short but instructive visit.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Firefly watching on the Iwahig River at dusk. By boat, you go up the river in darkness - and the mangroves light up. Thousands of fireflies blink in synchrony in the trees, like living fairy lights. It's an astonishing natural spectacle, especially in the dry season.

⏱ 75 min

Last Filipino dinner. If you haven't tried lechon (spit-roasted suckling pig) yet, now's the time. The skin is crispy as glass, the meat melt-in-your-mouth. It's the ultimate celebration dish in the Philippines.

Puerto Princesa

Palawan's laid-back capital, launching point for the UNESCO-listed underground river

★★★☆☆
📍 Puerto Princesa Airport📍 Rizal Avenue Puerto Princesa
1

Last morning in Puerto Princesa. If your flight is in the afternoon, take advantage of the morning to stroll one last time along Rizal Avenue. The sidewalks are lively from 7am - vendors selling pan de sal (warm bread rolls), local coffee stands, schoolchildren in uniform.

⏱ 40 min
2

Puerto Princesa Airport is 5 minutes from the city center - one of the few Philippine airports this accessible. Direct flights to Manila (1h20), Cebu (1h30) or Clark. If you're continuing to the Visayas, the flight to Cebu avoids going back through Manila.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Last purchases: Palawan cashew nuts (the best in the country), dried mangoes, and why not a bottle of lambanog (coconut alcohol). The souvenir shops on Rizal Avenue have honest prices.
