Phnom Penh

★★★★★
1

Royal Palace and Silver Pagoda - official residence of the king, you'll discover the throne hall and the pagoda floor paved with 5,000 silver tiles (proper attire required, shoulders and knees must be covered)

⏱ 90 min
2

National Museum of Cambodia - red sandstone building in Khmer style, housing the world's largest collection of Angkorian sculptures, including the 11th-century bronze Vishnu discovered at West Mebon

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stroll along Sisowath Quay - 3 km promenade along the Tonle Sap River, bustling with terraces, street food vendors and joggers at sunset

⏱ 60 min

Sunset cruise on the Mekong - one-hour traditional boat ride at the confluence of the Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers, departing from Sisowath Quay

⏱ 60 min

Phnom Penh

★★★★★
1

Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum (S-21) - former high school converted into a prison by the Khmer Rouge between 1975 and 1979, audio guide available in multiple languages, a harrowing but essential visit to understand the country's history

⏱ 90 min
2

Choeung Ek Memorial (Killing Fields) - execution site 15 km from the center, memorial stupa containing over 8,000 skulls, poignant 45-minute audio tour

⏱ 90 min
3

Wat Phnom - 27-meter artificial hill founded in 1373 according to legend by Lady Penh, who gave the city its name; pagoda surrounded by a small shaded park

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Central Market (Phsar Thmey) - Art Deco hall from the 1930s shaped like a yellow dome, where you'll find jewelry, textiles and street food under architecture unique in Southeast Asia

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Siem Reap

★★★★★
1

Sunrise over Angkor Wat - arrive around 5 AM to watch the towers reflected in the pools; this 12th-century temple dedicated to Vishnu is the world's largest religious monument at 162 hectares

⏱ 150 min
2

Angkor Thom and Bayon - fortified city spanning 9 km², you enter through the South Gate flanked by 108 statues - 54 gods and 54 demons holding a naga; the Bayon features 216 monumental faces carved in stone, dating from the reign of Jayavarman VII in the 13th century

⏱ 90 min
3

Ta Prohm - 12th-century temple left in a semi-ruined state, giant roots of silk-cotton and strangler fig trees wrap around the galleries; made famous by the film Tomb Raider (2001)

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Tuk-tuk rides through the Angkor Archaeological Park - Small Circuit route (17 km) connecting the main temples, your driver waits for you at each stop

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Phare Ponleu Selpak circus show - troupe born in Battambang blending acrobatics, theater, live music and Cambodian social themes; performances every evening at 8 PM under a big top in Siem Reap
