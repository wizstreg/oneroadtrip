Stop 1

Hanoi

Where a thousand years of Vietnamese history unfolds through fifteenth-century alleyways

★★★★★
📍 Hoan Kiem Lake Hanoi📍 Ngoc Son Temple Hanoi📍 The Huc Bridge Hanoi📍 Old Quarter Hanoi Hoan Kiem📍 Bach Ma Temple Hanoi Hang Buom
1

Hoan Kiem Lake - Lake of the Returned Sword - takes its name from a 15th-century legend: Emperor Lê Lợi is said to have returned a magical sword to a divine tortoise here after driving out the Ming invaders. The lake's last giant turtle died in 2016; its preserved body is displayed in Ngoc Son Temple, which has occupied Jade Island since the 1865 restoration led by scholar Nguyễn Văn Siêu. You reach it via The Huc Bridge - 45 meters of red-lacquered wood whose name means where the morning light rests. Don't miss the statues dedicated to General Trần Hưng Đạo, the national hero who repelled the Mongols in the 13th century. Plan to visit in late afternoon: the illuminations around 6pm turn the red bridge's reflection in the water into a spectacle in itself.

⏱ 75 min
2

North of Hoan Kiem Lake, the Old Quarter dates back to the 15th century, when craftsmen from across the Red River Delta settled here in trade guilds. Each street still bears the name of the craft once practiced there: Hang Bac for silver, Hang Gai for silk, Hang Ma for votive paper objects. The number 36 is symbolic - the quarter actually contains more alleys. Step through the door of Bach Ma Temple on Hang Buom street, considered Hanoi's oldest temple according to local sources, founded in the 9th century to honor the white horse that supposedly guided the construction of the city's ramparts. Arrive early morning or evening - traffic is heavy from 8am onward and the pedestrian alleys around the lake come alive after 7pm.

⏱ 90 min
3

In the center of the lake, on a small inaccessible islet, stands the Turtle Tower (Thap Rua), built between 1884 and 1886 on four levels. Its roof evokes Vietnamese communal houses of the previous century. You can't access it, but the complete walk around the lake takes only 20 minutes and offers different angles on the tower depending on the light. On weekends, the shores become pedestrian and the neighborhood stays lively until midnight - if you arrive on a Friday evening, you'll catch the buzz.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Operators offer cyclo rides from the edges of Hoan Kiem Lake. It's the most effective way to navigate the alleyways without getting run over by scooters - the slow pace gives you time to observe the facades, wooden signs, and craftsmen at work. Plan to negotiate the duration and route before climbing in: the unspoken rule is to agree on the price at the start to avoid surprises at the end.

⏱ 45 min

Cà phê trứng - egg coffee - is a Hanoi invention from the 1940s: with milk scarce during wartime, a barman replaced it with egg yolks beaten with sweetened condensed milk. The result is a thick, creamy foam crowning a robust Robusta espresso. Operators serve it in cramped little cafes, often on the second floor of a tube house. In the evening, settle into Ta Hien Street and its surroundings - long communal tables under the open sky with bia hoi (fresh draft beer served in plastic cups), skewers and fried spring rolls. If you arrive on a weekend, the entire area around the lake becomes pedestrian with entertainment until midnight.

⏱ 75 min

Hanoi

Where a thousand years of Vietnamese history unfolds through fifteenth-century alleyways

★★★★★
📍 Temple of Literature Van Mieu Hanoi📍 Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum Hanoi Ba Dinh📍 Ho Chi Minh Stilt House Hanoi📍 Vietnam Museum of Ethnology Hanoi📍 Thang Long Water Puppet Theatre Hanoi
1

Founded in 1070 by Emperor Lý Thánh Tông, the Temple of Literature is Vietnam's first university - training the country's elite for 800 years, until 1802. The complex consists of five courtyards separated by brick walls, aligned north-south. Stop before the Khue Van Cac pavilion built in 1805, which has become the official symbol of Hanoi: its circular windows evoke the sun's rays spreading knowledge. In the third courtyard, 82 stone stelae resting on granite tortoises bear the engraved names of 1,307 doctorate recipients between 1442 and 1779. Arrive at opening time (7:30am in summer) to avoid the school groups that flood the pathways from 9am onward. Rent the audio guide at the entrance - available in English, it genuinely deepens the visit.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, inaugurated in 1975 on Ba Dinh Square - where the President proclaimed Vietnam's independence on September 2, 1945 - houses his embalmed body. The dress code is strict: shoulders covered, absolute silence, line moving quickly. But don't leave without visiting the stilt house in the neighboring garden: Ho Chi Minh chose to live in this modest dwelling from 1958, refusing the neo-colonial presidential palace offered to him. This decision says much about the man. Check before you go: the mausoleum closes for several weeks each year for maintenance, generally from mid-September to mid-November.

⏱ 75 min
3

Opened in 1997 in a building designed in the shape of a Dong Son drum seen from above, the Museum of Ethnology presents the 54 ethnic groups officially recognized in Vietnam. The interior collections display costumes, musical instruments, agricultural tools and ceremonial equipment. In the garden, several traditional full-size houses have been reconstructed - the Ede long house from the central highlands, the Thai stilt house from the North. Allow at least 2 hours. It's one of the best-designed museums in the country, too often skipped because it's located away from the center: don't make that mistake.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Water puppet theater is a Red River Delta tradition over a thousand years old. The puppeteers, hidden behind a bamboo curtain, maneuver their characters in a shallow pool using submerged rods - accompanied by a live traditional music orchestra. The scenes depict the legend of the lake's sword, naval battles, dragon dances and fishing scenes. Operators offer performances several times daily near Hoan Kiem Lake. Book your tickets in advance: evening shows regularly sell out in high season.

⏱ 60 min

Pho bò - beef soup with its flat rice noodles, fresh herbs and long-simmered broth - is North Vietnam's national dish. The Hanoi version is more austere than the southern one: few garnishes, a clear, concentrated broth that stands on its own. Operators throughout the Old Quarter streets serve this dish from 6am and often only until late morning - some establishments only open for as long as the pot lasts. Don't look for big signs, look for the queue.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 3

Ninh Binh

The inland Ha Long Bay, explored by rowboat between limestone peaks 259 million years old

★★★★★
📍 Tam Coc boat pier Ninh Binh📍 Mua Cave Hang Mua Ninh Binh📍 Trang An UNESCO Ninh Binh📍 Hoa Lu ancient capital Vietnam Ninh Binh
1

Ninh Binh conceals one of the most forgotten pages in Vietnamese history: Hoa Lu was the country's first unified capital from 968 to 1010, under the Đinh and early Lê dynasties. Two main temples pay homage to the founders of the unified Vietnamese state. If you have time on the way from Hanoi, stop here before heading to Tam Coc - the site is rarely crowded and the gardens are pleasant. Arrive at opening if you want to avoid the tourist coaches that arrive around 10am.

⏱ 60 min
2

Tam Coc means three caves: the landscape unfolds along the Ngô Đồng River, with its limestone peaks rising from the rice paddies like stone ghosts. In May-June, the fields are jade green; in September-October, they turn golden. It's not the most authentic spot in Ninh Binh, but it's the most photographed - the view from the pier is the one you've probably seen on all the brochures. Count about 30 minutes' drive from Hoa Lu.

⏱ 30 min
3

Inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2014 as Southeast Asia's first mixed natural and cultural site, Trang An extends over 6,226 hectares of limestone karsts shaped over more than 259 million years. Archaeological excavations in the caves have revealed traces of human occupation spanning 30,000 years. Temples are hidden in the vegetation along the water, accessible only by boat. If you only do one boat excursion in the region, choose Trang An over Tam Coc - the landscapes are more varied and the caves more numerous.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

From the Tam Coc pier, boatwomen take you in rowboats along the Ngô Đồng River through three natural caves (Hang Ca, Hang Hai, Hang Ba). What immediately surprises: they row with their feet, both hands free. Avoid the 10am-2pm slot when groups arrive en masse; early morning (8-9am) or after 3pm offer the best light and far fewer people. Plan a tip for the rower - the crossing is physically demanding in strong heat.

⏱ 120 min

Hang Mua isn't a cave in the speleological sense: it's a mountain to climb. The 500 stone steps, lined with statues of dragons and phoenixes, lead to the summit of Ngọa Long Mountain. At the top, beneath your feet lies the Ngô Đồng River winding between rice paddies and the limestone peaks of Tam Coc - this is the panorama you'll find on all Ninh Binh brochures. Wear closed shoes, the steps are sometimes slippery in wet weather. Climb early in the morning before 8am, or late afternoon for the golden light.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 4

Ha Long

Where 1,969 karst islands rise from emerald waters across 1,553 square kilometers of UNESCO wilderness

★★☆☆☆
📍 Ha Long Bay junk pier📍 Bai Tu Long Bay Ha Long📍 Thien Canh Son Cave Ha Long📍 Vung Vieng floating village Ha Long📍 Titov Island Ha Long Bay
1

Ha Long Bay covers 1,553 km² and counts 1,969 islands and karst peaks rising from the emerald waters of the Gulf of Tonkin. Classified as a UNESCO Natural World Heritage Site in 1994, it was reclassified in 2023 within an expanded ensemble incorporating the Cat Ba archipelago. Its name means bay of the descending dragon: according to legend, a dragon sent to protect the country spat out jewels that became these islands. Archaeological excavations have uncovered traces of human occupation dating back 18,000 years. To avoid the concentration of boats on classic circuits, opt for a junk in Bai Tu Long Bay to the north - protected since 2001, equally spectacular and much less frequented. Choose a junk with maximum 10 cabins: beyond that, the atmosphere becomes industrial.

⏱ 360 min
2

Junks generally stop at a cave in the afternoon. Dau Go Cave - Cave of the Wooden Stakes - takes its name from the legend that wood was hidden here in the 13th century to prepare defensive stakes against the Mongol fleet on the Bach Dang River. It extends over approximately 5,000 m² in three interior chambers. If your junk sails in Bai Tu Long Bay, request a stop at Thien Canh Son Cave - less known, it's visually just as impressive with its stalactite formations and natural light shaft. Wear good shoes: cave floors are often damp.

⏱ 60 min
3

Once the day boats have departed around 4pm, the bay regains an unexpected silence. Climb to the upper deck for sunset: the low-angle light on the limestone karsts, the silhouettes of fishermen in woven bamboo boats, the reflections on the water - this is when the bay justifies its reputation. After dinner served on board, some junks offer squid fishing by lamplight. The next morning, sunrise from the same deck often surpasses the sunset - the colors are different and the bay is still deserted.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Kayaking is the only way to access the interior lagoons hidden behind the cliffs, crossed by natural tunnels at water level. Operators on board provide kayaks in the afternoon. In silence, you paddle under damp limestone arches, into spaces where no motorized boat can enter. Plan quick-drying clothing - passages under low arches get paddlers wet. It's also a good way to distance yourself from other junks and enjoy relative calm on the emerald waters.

⏱ 90 min

Most junks include a cooking class in their program: fresh spring rolls (gỏi cuốn), nem hấp or pho. The ship's cook explains the role of each herb - mint, Thai basil, Vietnamese coriander - and the basic techniques of Northern cuisine. It's a convivial and often funny moment, even for non-cooks. If the workshop isn't spontaneously offered, request it when boarding.

⏱ 60 min

Hanoi

Where a thousand years of Vietnamese history unfolds through fifteenth-century alleyways

★★★★★
📍 Ha Long Bay sunrise junk📍 Ha Long port disembarkation📍 Noi Bai International Airport Hanoi
1

Climb to the upper deck by 5:30am - the spectacle of daybreak over the limestone karsts more than compensates for the brutal wake-up. The low-angle dawn light colors the cliffs orange and pink, and the morning mist floating on the water gives the bay an almost unreal quality. You'll probably be alone on deck at this hour - other passengers are still sleeping. Take your time: this is one of the trip's strongest moments, and it only lasts about twenty minutes before the light changes.

⏱ 60 min
2

Before returning to port, most junks offer a final stop at a cave or pocket beach. Take advantage of it - it's the day's best light for photos. Lunch is served on board while sailing back, then the boat docks for disembarkation around 12-1pm. Count approximately 3 hours' drive between the port and Noi Bai airport, depending on traffic - allow margin if your flight is in late afternoon.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Operators offer direct transfers in air-conditioned minibuses from the disembarkation wharf to Noi Bai airport - it's the simplest solution for this logistical day. The domestic Hanoi-Da Nang flight takes approximately 1h20; several Vietnamese airlines operate this route multiple times daily. Plan to be at the airport at least 1h30 before departure. Upon arrival in Da Nang, a transfer of about an hour takes you to Hue via the coastal road.

Hue

Vietnam's imperial capital from 1802 to 1945, where seven Nguyễn tombs rest beneath ancient pines

★★★★☆
📍 Imperial Citadel Hue Dai Noi📍 Thien Mu Pagoda Hue Perfume River📍 Tu Duc Tomb Hue📍 Minh Mang Tomb Hue📍 Ngo Mon Gate citadel Hue
1

Hue was Vietnam's imperial capital from 1802 to 1945, under the 13 emperors of the Nguyễn dynasty. Construction of the citadel began in 1805 and was completed in 1832 - inspired by Beijing's Forbidden City, surrounded by moats over a perimeter of more than 10 km, with brick walls 4.16 meters high. The ensemble has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1993. Enter through Ngo Mon Gate to reach the Palace of Supreme Harmony, the throne hall with red and gold lacquered columns. The Purple Forbidden City, reserved for the emperor and his family, was heavily damaged during the Tet Offensive of January 1968 - some restored pavilions coexist with gardens where only foundations remain. Allow at least 2h30 to avoid skimming the surface. Electric carts are available at the entrance for those who prefer less walking.

⏱ 150 min
2

Perched on Ha Khe hill, 3 km from the citadel on the left bank of the Perfume River, Thien Mu Pagoda is considered Vietnam's tallest pagoda with its octagonal tower of 21 meters over seven levels, built in 1844 by Emperor Thiệu Trị. Each level represents a reincarnation of Buddha. The legend of its foundation tells that an old woman appeared in a lord's dream to indicate the location of a future temple that would ensure the country's prosperity. Reach the pagoda ideally by boat from Hue's wharves - the short cruise on the Perfume River is worth the detour in itself. Entry is free.

⏱ 60 min
3

Tu Duc, 4th emperor of the Nguyễn dynasty, reigned from 1847 to 1883. His tomb - built between 1864 and 1867, 8 km south of Hue - extends over more than 120,000 m². The emperor himself inhabited it during construction, composing poems in the Xung Kiem pavilion on stilts above Luu Kiem Lake. In the court of honor, a 20-ton stele bears an epitaph entirely written by Tu Duc's own hand - extremely rare in imperial tradition. Wear comfortable shoes: the terrain is uneven and the domain vast. Count about 1h30 to tour it without rushing.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Operators offer short dragon-boat cruises on the Song Huong - Perfume River - from the city center wharves. Sunset from the deck, with Thien Mu Pagoda on its hill and the mountains in the background, is one of the most beautiful in Central Vietnam. Some operators organize these cruises with traditional Hue music - nhã nhạc, royal court music classified as UNESCO Intangible Heritage in 2003.

⏱ 90 min

Hue's cuisine is considered Vietnam's most refined, inherited from the Nguyễn imperial court cooks - the emperors reportedly demanded between 50 and 100 distinct dishes at each meal. Operators serve replicas of these banquets in restored old houses around the Perfume River. Don't miss bánh khoái (crispy pancake with shrimp and pork), bún bò Huế (spicy beef soup with lemongrass, very different from Northern pho) and rice cakes wrapped in banana leaves. It's a regional gastronomy too little known outside Central Vietnam.

⏱ 90 min

Hoi An

A 15th-century merchant port frozen in amber, where 800 historic buildings glow beneath silk lanterns

★★★★★
📍 Hai Van Pass Vietnam📍 Lang Co Beach Vietnam📍 Japanese Bridge Hoi An Chua Cau📍 Hoi An Old Town Thu Bon River📍 Hai Van Pass watchtower Vietnam
1

Hai Van Pass - Pass of the Sea and Clouds - marks the natural border between Thua Thien-Hue and Da Nang provinces at 496 meters altitude. It also divides the climatic regimes: to the north, cold winds in winter; to the south, the hot, sunny weather of Da Nang from the descent onward. Stop at the summit for the view over Da Nang Bay on one side and Lang Co Beach on the other - in clear weather, the panorama is striking. Remnants of French and American bunkers are still visible. If you have the choice, take the pass road rather than the highway tunnel: the two roads are parallel, but one crosses kilometers of concrete and the other overlooks the sea.

⏱ 45 min
2

Between Hai Van Pass and Da Nang, Lang Co stretches along a tongue of sand between the lagoon and the South China Sea, with mountains as a backdrop. It's one of the circuit's most striking landscape transitions: you descend from the pass in fresh mist and land in minutes on a sun-bathed beach. Make a stop for a fresh seafood lunch at one of the small waterfront restaurants - crabs, shrimp and clams caught that very morning. You're still 65 km from Hoi An, but this feet-in-the-sand lunch is one of the circuit's best breaks.

⏱ 75 min
3

Hoi An - formerly Faifo under French colonization - was one of Southeast Asia's most active ports from the 15th to 19th centuries, frequented by Portuguese, Spanish, Japanese and Chinese merchants. Its historic center, inscribed as UNESCO Heritage on December 4, 1999, counts 800 listed buildings. The first essential visit is the Japanese Covered Bridge (Chua Cau), built in 1593 to connect the Chinese and Japanese communities: 18 meters long, a temple dedicated to sea spirits inside, and an image so emblematic it appears on Vietnam's 20,000 dong banknote. In the evening, the old town closes to cars and silk lanterns illuminate the ochre facades - don't return before night has fully fallen.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Operators offer lantern boats on the Thu Bon River from the old town wharves. You release a floating lantern on the water - a monthly full moon festival tradition celebrated every lunar month in Hoi An. When dozens of lanterns drift together on the dark water beneath illuminated facades, the effect is genuinely beautiful. Book directly on the wharves in early evening.

Hoi An

A 15th-century merchant port frozen in amber, where 800 historic buildings glow beneath silk lanterns

★★★★★
📍 Tan Ky Old House Hoi An📍 Phuc Kien Assembly Hall Hoi An📍 Tra Que vegetable village Hoi An📍 My Son Sanctuary Quang Nam📍 Quan Cong Temple Hoi An
1

At 101 Nguyen Thai Hoc Street, Tan Ky house has been inhabited for over 200 years by the 7th generation of the same family of Chinese-origin merchants. Push open the door and you enter a space that embodies Hoi An's architectural hybridization: Japanese framework, Chinese interior decoration, Vietnamese roof. The black jackfruit wood used for the columns has held for two centuries without treatment. Look at the ceiling: suspension rings allowed furniture to be hoisted upstairs during annual floods - Hoi An floods every autumn, sometimes up to 1 meter in ground floors. Family members generally welcome visitors and answer questions.

⏱ 45 min
2

Phuc Kien Hall was built by the Chinese Fujian community settled in Hoi An since the 17th century. Its portal guarded by two dragons opens onto courtyards adorned with bonsai and lotus ponds. The central temple is dedicated to Thiên Hậu, goddess of the sea and protector of sailors - merchants came to pray here before great crossings. Incense spirals suspended from the ceiling have been smoking continuously for decades. Don't stop at the entrance: the sanctuary interior, with its mosaics and statues of marine deities, is visually much denser than the facade suggests.

⏱ 45 min
3

On Tran Phu Street, Quan Cong Temple is one of the old town's most frequented - dedicated to General Guan Yu of China's Three Kingdoms period (220-280), venerated as god of war and loyalty. Hanoi and Sino-Vietnamese merchants regularly come to leave offerings to ensure prosperity and protection. The general's statue sits enthroned beneath a red and gold canopy, surrounded by his two lieutenants. The atmosphere, with incense and worshippers in prayer, contrasts with the tourist flow in the street just outside.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The village of Tra Que, 3 km from the old town, has cultivated aromatic herbs for several centuries in soil enriched by algae from the Thu Bon River. Farmers use the same gestures as their ancestors. Operators organize bicycle rides from the old town, with the possibility of participating in the work - weeding, watering, harvesting - then cooking what you've gathered. Leave in the morning for coolness and horizontal light on the rows of basil and coriander. The terrain is perfectly flat: even Sunday cyclists are comfortable.

⏱ 120 min

40 km west of Hoi An, the My Son Sanctuary is an ensemble of Hindu temples built by the Cham Kingdom between the 4th and 14th centuries. Inscribed as UNESCO Heritage in 1999, it's Southeast Asia's most important Hindu archaeological site. Several towers were destroyed by American bombing in 1969 - the craters are still visible in the landscape. The towers still standing are dedicated to Shiva and decorated with bas-reliefs depicting apsara dancers and mythological scenes. Leave at 7am for golden light and coolness: visiting at 10am in 35°C heat is painful and groups are numerous.

⏱ 180 min

Da Nang

Gateway to Central Vietnam, where marble peaks meet the sea between past and present

★★★★☆
📍 Son Tra Peninsula Da Nang Lady Buddha📍 Linh Ung Pagoda Da Nang Son Tra📍 Marble Mountains Da Nang Ngu Hanh Son📍 Huyen Khong Cave Marble Mountains📍 Da Nang International Airport
1

The Son Tra Peninsula, 10 km from Da Nang center, shelters Vietnam's largest standing Buddha statue: the Lady Buddha (Quan Am) at Linh Ung Pagoda measures 67 meters and is visible from all of Da Nang Bay. The peninsula is also a classified nature reserve that shelters the red-shanked douc (Pygathrix nigripes), one of the world's most colorful monkeys, nearly impossible to find elsewhere. Climb to the upper viewpoint for a 180° view over Da Nang, the sea and the mountains in the background. Operators offer taxis or minibuses from the city center.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Marble Mountains are a group of five marble and limestone hills each named for an element: Water, Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal. Inside are natural caves transformed into Buddhist temples since the 17th century. In Huyen Khong Cave, a statue of Buddha Shakyamuni is illuminated by a natural light shaft passing through the rock from the summit - the effect is striking. At the base of the hills, open-air marble carving workshops are set up: you can observe artisans at work. Wear good shoes - the stairs are numerous and surfaces sometimes slippery.

⏱ 90 min
3

If timing permits before your flight, My Khe Beach stretches for 9 km along the South China Sea shore just minutes from Da Nang center. American Marines frequented it during the Vietnam War - it was then considered one of Asia's most beautiful beaches according to war correspondents. Today, it's accessible by taxi from the Marble Mountains in about ten minutes. Da Nang airport is only 3 km from the city center, which leaves you margin.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The domestic Da Nang-Ho Chi Minh City flight takes approximately 1h15 and is operated several times daily by Vietnamese airlines. Da Nang airport is 3 km from the center - access is among the most convenient in the country. Plan to be at the airport 1h30 before departure. Upon arrival at Tan Son Nhat, the transfer to Ho Chi Minh City center takes between 30 minutes and 1h30 depending on time and traffic - in the evening, the city is often congested.

Ho Chi Minh

Where French cathedrals stand beside war museums and 250 kilometers of hand-dug tunnels tell their stories

★★★★☆
📍 Notre-Dame Cathedral Saigon Ho Chi Minh City📍 Central Post Office Saigon Ho Chi Minh City📍 Reunification Palace Ho Chi Minh City📍 War Remnants Museum Ho Chi Minh City📍 Thien Hau Pagoda Cholon Ho Chi Minh City📍 Ben Thanh Market Ho Chi Minh City
1

Built between 1877 and 1880 by architect J. Bourad for a cost of 2.5 million French francs, Notre-Dame Cathedral of Saigon is entirely constructed in red bricks imported from Marseille - the builders deliberately avoided local materials to display colonial power. Its two bell towers house 6 bells. Just opposite, the Central Post Office was built between 1886 and 1891 with a metal framework; its walls preserve two large wall maps of Indochina dating from 1936, and the original wooden telephone booths have been converted into ATMs. Don't miss the main hall - it's one of Southeast Asia's most beautiful colonial halls, still operating as a post office.

⏱ 60 min
2

On April 30, 1975, at 11:30am, tank 843 of the North Vietnamese army smashed through the gates of this palace - it was the end of the Vietnam War. The current building, designed by architect Ngo Viet Thu and inaugurated on October 31, 1966, replaced the colonial Norodom Palace built between 1868 and 1873. The reception halls are frozen in 1960s style. The basement is most fascinating: war room with its operations maps, telecommunications center, liaison tunnels - everything is preserved in 1975 condition. Visit in the morning to avoid tour buses.

⏱ 90 min
3

The War Remnants Museum receives more than half a million visitors per year. It documents the Indochina and Vietnam wars through photographs, objects and testimonies - several rooms are devoted to the effects of Agent Orange on subsequent generations. Outside, tanks, aircraft and artillery pieces that actually saw service are displayed in the courtyard. Allow at least 2 hours and don't take it lightly: it's one of the world's most documented and most direct war museums, and it doesn't seek to spare the visitor.

⏱ 120 min
4

Cholon, the 5th district, is Southeast Asia's largest Chinatown - the Chinese population of Biên Hoà settled there from 1778. Thien Hau Pagoda is the neighborhood's spiritual center, dedicated to the goddess of the sea and protector of sailors. Incense spirals suspended from the ceiling smoke continuously. The street in front is lined with medicinal herb shops and worship accessories. Come on a weekday morning for the most authentic atmosphere - on weekends, guides bring groups that make the visit less intimate.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Ben Thanh Market, built in reinforced concrete in 1914, is Ho Chi Minh City's central market. During the day, it's touristy and prices reflect that. From 6pm onward, the street stalls that set up around the building form a livelier night market where locals also come to eat. Try bánh mì - baguette filled with pâté, pickled vegetables and fresh herbs, a direct legacy of French baking. If you want to make purchases, neighboring Dong Khoi Street concentrates silk, lacquer and higher-quality crafts than tourist markets.

⏱ 60 min

Operators offer tuk-tuk or open jeep tours through District 1 streets in the evening - it's a good way to see Ho Chi Minh City in motion, with the colonial opera house, illuminated grand avenues and street markets. The city takes on another dimension once night has fallen and traffic is slightly lighter. If you prefer to go at your own pace, Bui Vien Street in the backpacker quarter is pedestrian in the evening and lively until very late.

Before entering the tunnels, a short documentary film traces the history of Cu Chi district during the Vietnam War. The underground network of 250 km was dug by hand from the 1940s. At the height of combat, nearly 16,000 people lived there: hospitals, armories, kitchens, meeting rooms, dormitories - all underground. The Ben Duoc section is larger and less crowded than Ben Dinh, if you have the choice. On-site explanations are available in English.

⏱ 30 min
2

My Tho, 70 km southwest of Ho Chi Minh City, is the first Mekong Delta town. It's a radically different Vietnam from Saigon: slower, greener, more humid. The Mekong divides here into several arms loaded with reddish silt. Stilt houses line the canals, fishermen casting nets from their boats, fruit merchants navigating between islands - the river is still the real local transport network. Count about 1h drive from Cu Chi to reach the pier.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

On site, you can crawl through tunnel sections widened for tourists - the original sections are 80 cm high and 50 cm wide. They show you the camouflaged traps, the entrances hidden under dead leaves, the kitchens whose smoke ducts emerged at ground level several dozen meters away to confuse pursuers. Military personnel explain how soldiers lived and moved in total darkness. Wear clothes you won't be afraid to soil - the tunnels are damp and narrow. Operators organize round-trip transfers from Ho Chi Minh City with English-speaking guide.

⏱ 150 min

Operators offer excursions from My Tho including a boat crossing on the Mekong and a sampan ride through secondary arms shaded by coconut palms and banana trees. The typical program includes a visit to a beekeeping farm with honey tasting, a tropical fruit orchard (mangosteen, longan, dragon fruit depending on season) and a coconut candy-making workshop. It's a plunge into a pace of life that Saigon's growth hasn't yet completely absorbed. If you combine Cu Chi in the morning and the Mekong in the afternoon, a private vehicle for the full day is the most practical solution.

Tao Dan Park, in District 1, is Saigon residents' open-air living room. Early in the morning, the paths fill with tai chi groups, elderly couples playing Vietnamese chess, songbird sellers with ornate cages, dancers improvising to a portable radio set on a bench. It's the city au naturel, before heat and traffic set in. If you have time in the morning before your flight, it's a pleasant final hour with no imposed program - sit beneath the trees and watch Saigon wake up.

⏱ 60 min
2

Built in 1909 by Saigon's Cantonese community, the Jade Emperor Pagoda is one of the city's most beautiful - and one of the most unknown to rushed tourists. The interior is a theater of wood and ceramic statues representing Taoist gods, Buddhas and demons in an atmosphere of thick incense. Don't miss the sacred tortoises in the courtyard pond: believers release tortoises there to ensure good graces. A lotus pond completes the picture. The visit takes 30 to 45 minutes - sufficient to appreciate its richness.

⏱ 45 min
3

Dong Khoi Street - formerly Rue Catinat under French colonization - has been District 1's main artery since the 19th century. In a few hundred meters you'll find the Saigon Opera House (1898, baroque style), the Grand City Hall (1902, neo-baroque), colonial shopping arcades and quality craft boutiques. If you want to make final purchases - silk, lacquer, ceramics - this is where you'll find the best items at fixed prices, higher than tourist markets but for noticeably better quality. Rooftop cafes offer a final view over the city's roofs before the transfer.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Tan Son Nhat airport is only 7 km from District 1, but Ho Chi Minh City's midday traffic can transform this journey into an ordeal. Allow transfer time at least 3 hours before departure for an international flight, 2 hours for a domestic flight. Operators offer air-conditioned car transfers with departure time tracking. If you have time before leaving, cafes around Dong Khoi Street are the last comfortable places to wait.
