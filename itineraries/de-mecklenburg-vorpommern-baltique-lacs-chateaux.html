Ludwigslust

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Schwerin

★★★★☆
1

Cross the bridge to approach Schwerin Castle head-on: perched on an island between lake and Burgsee, it evokes a northern Neuschwanstein with its onion-dome towers and gilded cupola. Fortress documented in 973 by Arab merchant Ibrahim ibn Ya'qub. Its current appearance—953 rooms—dates from 1843-1857. UNESCO since 2024.

⏱ 90 min
2

The spire of Schwerin Cathedral soars to 117 meters. Climb it for sweeping views over the twelve lakes ringing the city. Gothic brickwork, begun in the 13th century. Tower access is a few euros.

⏱ 40 min
3

If Flemish painting speaks to you, the Staatliches Museum facing Alter Garten holds Rubens, Rembrandt, and Frans Hals. Neoclassical building, quiet even in summer. One of northern Germany's richest provincial collections.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Follow the lakeshore to the Schlossgarten, the baroque garden behind the castle. Locals circle it by bike. Rent one downtown and budget an hour. Finish with a Fischbrötchen—smoked herring sandwich—at the harbor.

⏱ 60 min

Wismar

★★★★☆
1

Wismar's Marktplatz covers 10,000 square meters—one of northern Germany's largest medieval squares. At its center, the Wasserkunst, a small Dutch Renaissance pavilion. UNESCO since 2002 alongside Stralsund.

⏱ 30 min
2

Of Marienkirche, only the 80-meter tower survives, a sea-marker for centuries. Nikolaikirche (consecrated 1459) impresses more: its nave reaches 37 meters high. Free admission.

⏱ 40 min
3

Spot the Alter Schwede on the square's east flank: brick façade from 1380, one of Wismar's last Gothic gabled houses. Its name recalls two centuries of Swedish occupation.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Head down to the Alter Hafen for a Fischbrötchen on the quay. Fishing boats still unload in the morning. The smell of smoked herring and the cry of gulls: Wismar in a nutshell.

Rostock

★★★★☆
1

Kröpeliner Strasse links the medieval gate to Neuer Markt: red-brick Hanseatic Gothic façades, rebuilt after bombing destroyed half the city. Rostock received Lübeck city rights on June 24, 1218.

⏱ 45 min
2

In Marienkirche, Rostock's largest church, a 15th-century astronomical clock still runs with its original mechanism. It shows the time, moon phases, and zodiac signs. Germany's only one in this condition.

⏱ 45 min
3

The University of Rostock, founded 1419, is Germany's third-oldest still-active university and northern Europe's oldest after St Andrews. The main building on Universitätsplatz sets the tone.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Head to Warnemünde, a coastal quarter 16 km north. Walk along the Alter Strom—old river arm, harbor of sailboats and fishermen—to the 1897 lighthouse, 30 meters high, clad in white-glazed brick. Beach over 3 km long with rows of Strandkörbe beach baskets.
