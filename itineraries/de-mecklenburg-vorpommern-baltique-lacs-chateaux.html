Ludwigslust

★★★☆☆
1

Dubbed the 'Versailles of the North,' Ludwigslust Palace surprises with its French airs dropped into the Mecklenburg plains. Ducal residence from 1765 to 1837, it hides a Goldener Saal whose decorations in papier-mâché mimic stucco. The 127-hectare English-style park is free to enter, with complimentary parking.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk up Schlossstrasse from the palace: this straight baroque avenue, lined with courtiers' houses, reveals a town designed entirely as the palace's setting. Stop for a craft beer at the Orangerie.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 65 km · 55min

Schwerin

★★★★☆
1

Cross the bridge to approach Schwerin Castle head-on: perched on an island between lake and Burgsee, it evokes a northern Neuschwanstein with its onion-dome towers and gilded cupola. Fortress documented in 973 by Arab merchant Ibrahim ibn Ya'qub. Its current appearance—953 rooms—dates from 1843-1857. UNESCO since 2024.

⏱ 90 min
2

The spire of Schwerin Cathedral soars to 117 meters. Climb it for sweeping views over the twelve lakes ringing the city. Gothic brickwork, begun in the 13th century. Tower access is a few euros.

⏱ 40 min
3

If Flemish painting speaks to you, the Staatliches Museum facing Alter Garten holds Rubens, Rembrandt, and Frans Hals. Neoclassical building, quiet even in summer. One of northern Germany's richest provincial collections.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Follow the lakeshore to the Schlossgarten, the baroque garden behind the castle. Locals circle it by bike. Rent one downtown and budget an hour. Finish with a Fischbrötchen—smoked herring sandwich—at the harbor.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 50min

Wismar

★★★★☆
1

Wismar's Marktplatz covers 10,000 square meters—one of northern Germany's largest medieval squares. At its center, the Wasserkunst, a small Dutch Renaissance pavilion. UNESCO since 2002 alongside Stralsund.

⏱ 30 min
2

Of Marienkirche, only the 80-meter tower survives, a sea-marker for centuries. Nikolaikirche (consecrated 1459) impresses more: its nave reaches 37 meters high. Free admission.

⏱ 40 min
3

Spot the Alter Schwede on the square's east flank: brick façade from 1380, one of Wismar's last Gothic gabled houses. Its name recalls two centuries of Swedish occupation.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Head down to the Alter Hafen for a Fischbrötchen on the quay. Fishing boats still unload in the morning. The smell of smoked herring and the cry of gulls: Wismar in a nutshell.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min

Kluetz

★★★☆☆
1

Count Bothmer lived at 10 Downing Street before it became the Prime Minister's residence. A Hanoverian diplomat, he helped George I ascend the throne. His Schloss Bothmer at Klütz (1726-1732) is northern Germany's only English country house. Thirteen brick buildings, 12 hectares of parkland. Around €8.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk the Festonallee, an avenue of linden trees pruned into garland shapes—a unique ceremonial axis in the north. Open-air concerts in the park in summer.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 45min

Bad Doberan

★★☆☆☆
1

Doberaner Münster, a 13th-century Cistercian monastery, is a pinnacle of brick Gothic. The interior preserves a monumental altarpiece and tower-shaped tabernacle. Austere and luminous atmosphere. Guided tour recommended.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Catch the Molli, an 1886 narrow-gauge train that whistles down the middle of Bad Doberan's high street. The journey to Heiligendamm takes 15 minutes. A full-size toy.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Heiligendamm

★★☆☆☆
1

Founded in 1793 by Duke Frederick Francis I, Heiligendamm is continental Europe's oldest seaside resort. White neoclassical villas line the beach, earning it the nickname Weiße Stadt am Meer (White City by the Sea). The Grand Hotel—site of the 2007 G8 summit—can be admired from outside. 200-meter pier.

⏱ 40 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 15min

Rostock

★★★★☆
1

Kröpeliner Strasse links the medieval gate to Neuer Markt: red-brick Hanseatic Gothic façades, rebuilt after bombing destroyed half the city. Rostock received Lübeck city rights on June 24, 1218.

⏱ 45 min
2

In Marienkirche, Rostock's largest church, a 15th-century astronomical clock still runs with its original mechanism. It shows the time, moon phases, and zodiac signs. Germany's only one in this condition.

⏱ 45 min
3

The University of Rostock, founded 1419, is Germany's third-oldest still-active university and northern Europe's oldest after St Andrews. The main building on Universitätsplatz sets the tone.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Head to Warnemünde, a coastal quarter 16 km north. Walk along the Alter Strom—old river arm, harbor of sailboats and fishermen—to the 1897 lighthouse, 30 meters high, clad in white-glazed brick. Beach over 3 km long with rows of Strandkörbe beach baskets.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 15min

Warnemuende

★★☆☆☆
1

On the Warnemünde side, the morning beach is empty before 9am. The Alter Strom lines up fishermen's houses converted to fish restaurants. Buy Räucherfisch—smoked fish—straight from the boat for a few euros.

⏱ 40 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 50min

Ahrenshoop

★★★☆☆
1

Around 1900, painter Paul Müller-Kaempff founded Ahrenshoop's artists' colony. Thatched-roof houses, narrow lanes, galleries everywhere. The Kunstmuseum Ahrenshoop, whose buildings echo fishermen's houses, is worth the visit. Warning: high-season prices climb.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk the steep cliff of Hohes Ufer westward: the coast crumbles into the sea, wild landscape steps from the village. Marked trail, an hour round-trip.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Prerow

★★★☆☆
1

The Darßer Ort lighthouse, at the peninsula's tip, is reached on foot or by bike through the Darß primeval forest. The Natureum, museum of the Boddenlandschaft national park, sits right beside it. The Darß's west beach is one of the Baltic's wildest.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hunt for Darßer Haustüren, painted and carved house doors—a local tradition now protected cultural heritage. Each door tells the owner's story. Prerow's Darß Museum documents them.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Zingst

★★★☆☆
1

Zingst is the last link in the Fischland-Darß-Zingst peninsula. Its Seebrücke pier and the Max Hünten Haus photography center animate the seafront. In autumn, common cranes gather by the thousands on the Bodden lagoons before migration.

⏱ 40 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 55min

Stralsund

★★★★☆
1

Stralsund's old town, UNESCO since 2002, has preserved its medieval plan intact: narrow streets, six great red-brick churches, Hanseatic merchants' houses. Swedish occupation—until 1815—left traces in the coats of arms.

⏱ 30 min
2

Alter Markt is dominated by the Gothic town hall (circa 1250), 30 by 60 meters, with five Hanseatic city shields on its north façade. Neighboring Nikolaikirche, consecrated 1276, is the city's oldest.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Ozeaneum, opened 2008 in the harbor, is Germany's largest maritime museum. Giant aquariums recreate Baltic and North Sea ecosystems. The Gorch Fock I (1933), a training tall ship, is moored at the quay just outside.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Stroll the quays in the evening: the view across the Strelasund with Gothic spires in the background sums up Pomerania's atmosphere. Short cruises to Rügen or Hiddensee in summer.

⏱ 40 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 35min

Putbus

★★★☆☆
1

Putbus is Rügen's only neoclassical town, created in the early 19th century by Prince Malte zu Putbus. Its Circus, a round square ringed with white houses, evokes Bath more than the Baltic. The Rasender Roland, a steam train chugging at 30 km/h, departs from here to Binz and Sellin.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 15min

Jagdschloss Granitz

★★★★☆
1

Perched atop Tempelberg hill, Jagdschloss Granitz overlooks Rügen's beech forests. Its central tower houses a freestanding spiral cast-iron staircase of 154 steps. The view from the top sweeps the island's east coast. Access on foot, by carriage, or via the Rasender Roland.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min

Binz

★★★☆☆
1

Binz lines up 3 km of seaside promenade bordered by white Bäderarchitektur villas (late 19th-early 20th century). The Seebrücke pier stretches into the Baltic. Refined atmosphere without stuffiness. Good base for exploring southern Rügen.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Drive 6 km north to Prora: the Koloss von Rügen, a Nazi-era Kraft durch Freude seaside complex (1936), stretches 4.5 km along the beach. Never completed. The Baumwipfelpfad—treetop walkway—offers an unexpected vantage point. 'Macht Urlaub' exhibition in Block III.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 15min

Sellin

★★★☆☆
1

Sellin's Seebrücke, 394 meters long, is Rügen's most beautiful: a white spa-style pavilion rises at the end, above the water. Restaurant inside, Tauchgondel—submarine gondola—at the tip. The approach via stairs from the clifftop is steep but photogenic.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Walk down Wilhelmstraße lined with Bäderarchitektur villas from the Rasender Roland station to the sea—10 minutes on foot. The street slopes steeply, which is why the pier also descends via monumental stairs to beach level. Below, the over-water restaurants serve fresh fish and pretzels in the sunset.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 35min

Prora

★★★★☆
1

Prora deserves a stop separate from the pass-through from Binz. The Dokumentationszentrum traces the KdF complex's history, meant to accommodate 20,000 vacationers simultaneously. The contrast between the ideology and the sandy beach just behind is stark.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 35min

Nationalpark Jasmund

★★★★★
1

The Königsstuhl, a 118-meter chalk cliff, is Rügen's symbol. The Nationalparkzentrum presents the ecosystem of ancient beech forests (UNESCO world heritage). The Königsweg, a suspended walkway, replaces the old cliff-edge access. Plan on the shuttle from Sassnitz or Hagen—cars prohibited.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike the Hochuferweg, a ridge trail along the chalk cliffs between Sassnitz and Königsstuhl. The path winds through beech forest, with openings onto the Baltic. About 6 km, two hours.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Kap Arkona

★★★★☆
1

Kap Arkona, Rügen's northern tip, lines up two lighthouses and a Peilturm on the 45-meter cliff. Schinkel's old lighthouse (1827) is the most photogenic. Below, the village of Vitt—reetgedeckte Häuser (thatched roofs) and an octagonal chapel—seems frozen in time. Access by shuttle or on foot.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Leave the car at Putgarten (paid parking) and walk the 1.3 km to the cape's tip—no traffic, sea wind, views of the 45-meter cliff. On clear days, Denmark's Møn coast is visible on the horizon, about 30 km away. The village of Vitt, 200 m below the cape, is an untouched fishing hamlet with a 19th-century octagonal chapel.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Hiddensee

★★★★☆
1

No cars on Hiddensee: you move by bike or on foot. The island stretches 17 km long and 250 meters at its narrowest between Baltic and Bodden. The Dornbusch lighthouse, in the north, offers a 360° panorama. Gerhart Hauptmann, Nobel laureate, had his summer house here (museum open).

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Rent a bike at the Vitte landing and circle the island in half a day. The west beaches are deserted; the Dornbusch heathland recalls Scotland. Return by evening ferry.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 40min

Greifswald

★★★☆☆
1

Greifswald is Caspar David Friedrich's city: the Romantic painter was born here in 1774. The Klosterruine Eldena, which he immortalized, can be visited on the city's edge. Marktplatz lines up colorful gabled houses, well restored.

⏱ 40 min
2

Wieck harbor, a former fishing village absorbed by Greifswald, preserves its wooden drawbridge (Holzklappbrücke). Peaceful atmosphere, removed from the islands' mass tourism.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Follow the Caspar-David-Friedrich-Bildweg on foot or by bike from the painter's birthplace in the Altstadt to Klosterruine Eldena, passing through the historic harbor and the fishing village of Wieck with its wooden drawbridge. The route covers about 6 km and takes 2 hours on foot. The Caspar-David-Friedrich-Zentrum in the birthplace opens onto his work and local motifs.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 50min

Peenemuende

★★★★☆
1

In 1943, the first V2 rocket was launched from Peenemünde. The Historisch-Technisches Museum traces this research site's history, the ambivalent birthplace of space exploration. The former Soviet submarine U-461, moored in the harbor, can be visited as a complement. Budget two hours minimum.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 40min

Heringsdorf

★★★☆☆
1

The three Kaiserbäder (Heringsdorf, Ahlbeck, Bansin) line up their Belle Époque villas along 12 km of promenade—Europe's longest. Bismarck, Kaiser Wilhelm II, and Fontane took the waters here. Heringsdorf's Seebrücke, 508 meters long, is Germany's largest.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk the promenade from Heringsdorf to Ahlbeck (4 km) to compare the three Kaiserbäder's styles. Each resort has its own personality. Ahlbeck possesses Germany's oldest Seebrücke (1882), the one from the film Pappa ante Portas.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min

Ahlbeck

★★☆☆☆
1

Ahlbeck's Seebrücke, with its 1882 pavilion, is the grande dame of German piers. It's a listed historic monument. Direct view of the Polish coast. At its foot, the 'Polenmarkt'—border market—offers textiles and crafts at reduced prices.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 130 km · 1h50

Neubrandenburg

★★★☆☆
1

Neubrandenburg possesses northern Germany's best-preserved medieval brick wall. Four Gothic gates (Stargarder, Treptower, Friedländer, Neues Tor) frame 25 Wiekhäuser—small half-timbered houses embedded in the wall. Now workshops, cafés, and galleries.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Konzertkirche St. Marien is a radical contrast: Gothic brick exterior, interior transformed into a modern concert hall (glass, concrete, wood). The acoustics rival the spectacle.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 30min

Ivenack

★★★★☆
1

Five oaks aged 600 to 1,000 years stand in Ivenack Park, Germany's first National Natural Monument. Their trunks exceed 14 meters in circumference. A Baumkronenpfad—canopy walkway—lets you approach them at crown height. Fallow deer roam free in the enclosure.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 35min

Guestrow

★★☆☆☆
1

Schloss Güstrow is considered northern Germany's finest Renaissance building. The Ernst Barlach workshop-museum, showcasing the expressionist sculptor who lived here, displays his tormented bronzes in a Gothic chapel. The town is calm, far from tourist circuits.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 50min

Waren Mueritz

★★★☆☆
1

Waren is the gateway to Müritz National Park. The Müritzeum, an interactive museum on lakes and forests, features a freshwater aquarium where a school of whitefish circles across two floors. Immersive effect guaranteed. Lake Müritz itself (117 km²) is Germany's second-largest.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In autumn, head to Rederangsee at dusk: up to 10,000 common cranes gather before migration. Guided observation by national park rangers. In summer, canoe trips on the canals between lakes.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 25min

Malchow

★★☆☆☆
1

Malchow is an 'island town' set between two lakes, linked by a swing bridge (Drehbrücke) that opens every hour to let boats through. The monastery converted into a DDR-Alltag museum—GDR everyday life—is a quick but revealing stop.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Mirow

★★★☆☆
1

The small Schloss Mirow, on its island in the lake, is the birthplace of Sophie Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz, future Queen of England (wife of George III). The exhibition traces her journey. The palace park overlooks the lake—absolute calm.

⏱ 40 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 25min

Rheinsberg

★★★☆☆
1

Schloss Rheinsberg was Frederick the Great's youthful residence before his accession in 1740. The palace on the Grienericksee inspired Tucholsky (Rheinsberg: Ein Bilderbuch für Verliebte, 1912). Rococo rooms, palace theater, English-style park.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Circle the Grienericksee by canoe or on foot (5 km loop). The palace reflects in the water in the morning—ideal light for photos. Canoe rental at the harbor.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 45min

Neustrelitz

★★★☆☆
1

Neustrelitz lost its palace (destroyed in 1945) but kept its baroque star plan. Eight streets radiate from the central square. The Schlosspark, with its orangery and neoclassical temple, remains the lake district's finest park. Logical final stop before Berlin.

⏱ 40 min
