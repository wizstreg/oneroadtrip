Day 1

Cuiaba

Where the Pantanal wilderness begins and the southern cross burns brightest overhead
Marechal Rondon Airport CuiabáNossa Senhora do Bom Despacho Church CuiabáFish Market CuiabáRepública Square Cuiabá
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

You arrive in Cuiabá, capital of Mato Grosso (650,000 inhabitants), gateway to the Northern Pantanal. The city is known for two things: its heat (often 38-40°C in the dry season, one of Brazil's hottest) and the geodesic center of South America, calculated in 1909 and marked by an obelisk on Praça Pascoal Moreira Cabral. The compact historic center clusters around the Metropolitan Cathedral and the Igreja Nossa Senhora do Bom Despacho. Half a day is more than enough.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Mercado do Peixe (fish market), along the Rio Cuiabá, is where pantaneiros sell pacus, pintados, and dourados caught that morning. It's also the city's beloved canteen: the waterfront restaurants serve mojica de pintado (fish stew with manioc) or peixada—you sit on plastic benches and share. Expect R$40-60 per person. Skip Sundays; most places are closed.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Museu Rondon (on the UFMT campus) houses a beautiful ethnographic collection on the indigenous peoples of Mato Grosso: Bororo, Xavante, Pareci, Rikbaktsa. Ceremonial feathers, bows, masks, historical photos of Cândido Rondon, the "general of peace" who pacified contact with indigenous peoples in the early 20th century. Free entry, closed weekends. One hour is plenty.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stock up for the journey ahead: powerful insect repellent (DEET 40-50%), SPF 50 sunscreen, wide-brimmed hat, light-colored long-sleeve clothing, binoculars if you don't have any. Supermarkets in the center (Modelo, Goiabeiras Shopping) sell everything cheaper than in Poconé. Fill up with gas if you're renting a car here—beyond Poconé, there's not a single station before Porto Jofre.

⏱ 90 min

Pantanal dinner: try Choppão (an institution since 1973) or Mahalo. Regional classics: maria-isabel (rice with charqui), pacu na brasa (whole grilled pacu), galinhada (rice with chicken and saffron), farofa de banana. Caipirinha de cumaru or jenipapo as an aperitif. Tables fill around 9 pm—that's Brazilian time.

⏱ 90 min

Day 2

Pixaim Fazenda

Where cobalt-blue macaws crack palm nuts on century-old fazenda verandas
Pouso Alegre Lodge TranspantaneiraPantanal Mato Grosso Hotel PixaimAraras Eco Lodge PantanalPiuval Lodge Transpantaneira
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Early departure toward Poconé via BR-070 (102 km, 1h30 on paved road). This small colonial town, founded in 1781, was once the region's gold capital. Quick look at the Igreja Matriz de São Bento (1781), mandatory stop at the gas station—it's the last one for 147 km. If you have time, quick lunch at Maria do Cerrado to try vatapá pantaneiro. And off you go onto the Transpantaneira.

⏱ 60 min
2

The red gate of the Transpantaneira marks the start of the adventure: 147 km of laterite dirt road to Porto Jofre, 122 wooden bridges (some being replaced with concrete since 2018). Built in 1976 to cross the Pantanal from end to end, it stopped abruptly at Porto Jofre—the ecosystem won against asphalt. The road has become a wildlife sanctuary: capybaras, caimans, jabirus, horned screamers, curassows. Drive at 30-40 km/h max—animal speed, not highway speed.

⏱ 60 min
3

First stop at Pousada Piuval (km 10) or Pousada Pouso Alegre (km 33): these century-old fazendas have become ecolodges. You may not be sleeping here, but you can stop for lunch and a guided walk. Bullet ants with their ferocious sting, cathedral-sized termite mounds, tapir tracks in dried mud—the guide reads the landscape for you. Count on 2 hours on-site.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

The Transpantaneira's star activity: spotting hyacinth macaws (Anodorhynchus hyacinthinus), the world's largest parrot, 1-meter wingspan, cobalt blue with a yellow ring around the eye. Vulnerable species, protected—you'll observe them mainly around acuri palms whose nuts they crack with their beaks. Several fazendas (Aymara, Araras, San Francisco) shelter night roosts—be there around 4:30-5 pm to watch them come home.

⏱ 90 min

At sunset, observe the jacarés (yacare caimans): over 10 million in the Pantanal, a world record. They line up by the dozens along the waterways, mouths open to regulate their temperature. Don't get out of the car without a guide. The Pantanal's evening soundscape—croaks, bird calls, distant howler monkey roars—beats any concert.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner and overnight at a fazenda in the Pixaim stretch (around km 65): Hotel Pantanal Mato Grosso, Pousada Pouso Alegre, or Araras Eco Lodge. Pantanal cuisine, hammocks on verandas, starry sky without light pollution. Half-board included, expect R$600-1200 per night depending on comfort level. Night jeep safari available (sloths, armadillos, sometimes ocelots).

⏱ 120 min

Day 3

Porto Jofre

End of the road, beginning of the hunt—jaguar country on the Cuiabá
Pantanal Norte Hotel Porto JofrePantanal Jaguar Camp Porto JofreDona Onça Jaguar Lodge Porto JofreCuiabá River Porto Jofre
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Early start for the second half of the Transpantaneira, wilder and more challenging: no electricity or phone signal, wooden bridges in worse condition. Count on 3 hours for 80 km. This section crosses the "Jaguar Ecological Reserve" (around km 100), one of the planet's highest jaguar densities. Drive very slowly—this is where you'll have the best encounters with tapirs, giant anteaters, marsh deer.

⏱ 180 min
2

Arrival at Porto Jofre, a former fishing village at the end of the road, on the banks of the Rio Cuiabá. About ten buildings, two airstrips for air-taxi arrivals from Cuiabá (1-hour flight, alternative to 6 hours by road). Check into your lodge—Hotel Pantanal Norte is the largest and most comfortable (with pool), Pantanal Jaguar Camp and Dona Onça simpler but excellent for safaris.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

First afternoon boat safari on the Rio Cuiabá. Four-hour outing (roughly 3-7 pm), flat-bottomed boat with 4-8 passengers and a tracker guide in VHF contact with other boats. When a jaguar is spotted, the info circulates in minutes. The cats rest on the riverbanks between 3 and 6 pm, sometimes 5 meters from the boat, completely indifferent. The probability of seeing a jaguar is 80-90% in July-August-September, close to 99% over 3 days of safari.

⏱ 240 min

At sunset, you head up the Rio Cuiabá toward the confluence with the Piquiri: this is the heart of Encontro das Águas State Park, created in 2004, 108,000 hectares. Hundreds of capybaras on the banks, bare-faced curassows, stork-billed kingfishers fishing, black caimans (jacaré-açu) motionless in shallow waters. The low light paints the sand orange. Return to the lodge after dark.

⏱ 60 min

Day 4

Porto Jofre

End of the road, beginning of the hunt—jaguar country on the Cuiabá
Piquiri River Porto JofreTrês Irmãos River PantanalEncontro das Águas State Park
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Today, full-day boat safari: 6 to 8 hours on the Cuiabá, Piquiri, and Três Irmãos rivers. Departure around 6 am, return around 11 am for the heat break, then out again around 3 pm. Breakfast thermos served aboard. The Piquiri is narrower, wilder than the Cuiabá: better chances of witnessing a caiman hunt (Pantanal jaguars are the world's only big cats that regularly hunt caimans, taking prey up to 70 kg).

⏱ 360 min
2

Lunch break on a white sandbank of the Rio Cuiabá. The lodge provides a cooler picnic: sandwiches, fruit, cupuaçu juice, water. Swimming not recommended (caimans, piranhas, occasional anaconda) except in areas supervised by the guide. Siesta in the shade, watching parrots come to drink at the water's edge.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Observing giant river otters (ariranhas), endemic to South American rivers, up to 1.8 meters long, in family groups of 5-10 individuals, extremely vocal—their calls carry a kilometer. They fish in teams, chase juvenile caimans. Endangered species (3,000 individuals in the entire Pantanal). Easy photography—the group doesn't hide.

⏱ 90 min

If luck is with you, it'll be the spectacular hunt: a jaguar crouched on the bank, diving into the river and grabbing a caiman. The sequence lasts 30 seconds, the fight is over in 2 minutes max. Rare but documented multiple times daily during high season in Porto Jofre. Otherwise, you might see a mother with cubs, or a dominant male marking his territory by rubbing a tree.

⏱ 60 min

Free evening at the lodge: pool if at Hotel Pantanal Norte, reading under fans, chatting with other travelers (often photographers staying a week). If you're up for it, night canoe outing with spotlight: caimans, capybaras on land, sometimes a puma caught drinking (rare).

⏱ 90 min

Day 5

Porto Jofre

End of the road, beginning of the hunt—jaguar country on the Cuiabá
Cuiabá River Porto Jofre safariPantanal birdwatching afternoon
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Third and final day of boat safari. If you haven't yet had the "perfect jaguar" (seen up close, in good light, for a long time), today's your day. Statistically, over three days of safari in Porto Jofre during the dry season, travelers see an average of 4 to 7 different jaguars—each individual is identifiable by its rosettes, like fingerprints. The Panthera project maintains a catalog of over 200 jaguars tracked since 2010.

⏱ 240 min
2

Morning variation: head up a small tributary in a silent electric-motor boat (some lodges have them). This is the photographers' option—less noise, less skittish animals, dawn light filtering through the vegetation. The price is higher (R$1500-2000 for a private half-day outing) but it's unbeatable for photography.

⏱ 240 min

Activities

Birdwatching special in the afternoon: the Pantanal counts 656 bird species. Classic targets: tuiuiú (jabiru stork, the Pantanal's symbol bird, 1.4 meters tall, 2.2-meter wingspan), herons, egrets, cormorants, Amazon kingfisher, maguari stork, razor-billed curassow. On the banks, horned screamers (Anhima cornuta)—large black birds with a single horn. Log your sightings—it's a birder's dream terrain.

⏱ 180 min

Farewell dinner around the Pantanal buffet: pacu, tambaqui ribs, saffron rice, farofa, fried bananas, doce de leite for dessert. Ask the tracker guide to share his best stories—jaguar fights, 6-meter anacondas, swimming tapirs caught mid-crossing. Lodge tradition: collective tip for the guide and pilots (10-15% of stay, in BRL or USD), given the evening before departure.

Pre-dawn wake-up to retrace the Transpantaneira in reverse: 6 hours back to Cuiabá depending on road conditions and stops. You'll see the same capybaras and caimans, but with different eyes—what seemed magical at first has become familiar. Breakfast en route in Pixaim. Arrival in Cuiabá around 1 pm. At this point, two options: return flight, or push on to the Chapada (1 hour more).

⏱ 60 min
2

Chapada dos Guimarães National Park sits 65 km northeast of Cuiabá, created in 1989, 33,000 hectares of red sandstone plateau. The Cachoeira Véu de Noiva is its postcard: 86-meter waterfall formed by the Rio Coxipó, viewed from a secure overlook after 550 meters of easy walking. No swimming at the base (descent impossible). Park open 9 am-4 pm, free entry. On clear days, 200-km visibility from the viewpoint.

⏱ 90 min
3

If you still have energy, the Cachoeiras trail (waterfall circuit, about 6 km, 3-4 hours) passes 5 or 6 falls—Cachoeira dos Namorados, Cachoeirinha (18 meters, you can stand under the water), Cachoeira do Degrau. Swimming allowed, cool water very welcome after the Pantanal. Daily visitor limit, early arrival recommended.

⏱ 240 min

Activities

Lunch in Chapada dos Guimarães, a small colonial town (1751) 45 minutes from the park. Restaurants in the center (Atmã, Trapiche) serve galo caipira (wood-fired free-range chicken) or peixe à urucum. Local specialty: bolo de arroz with coconut milk. Expect R$60-100 per person. The central square, around the Igreja de Santana, is shaded and cool.

⏱ 90 min

Late afternoon, climb to the Mirante viewpoint of South America's geodesic center. The sign isn't much to look at, but the panorama over the red cliffs at 800 meters altitude, with the Pantanal stretching endlessly in the distance, is worth the stop. Spectacular sunset. Return to Cuiabá for your flight (Aeroporto Marechal Rondon, CGB, 30 minutes from the city) or overnight in Chapada to extend.
