✨ Highlights
  • Ang Thong Marine Park: 42 limestone islands, hidden Emerald Lake and kayaking through sea caves
  • Bottle Beach in Koh Pha Ngan: accessible only by jungle or taxi-boat, zero cars
  • Sail Rock: Gulf's best dive site, vertical chimney at 18 m and seasonal whale sharks
  • Koh Nang Yuan: three islets connected by double white sandbar, panoramic views from summit
📆 Best period

December, January, February, March, April, May

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 5 and 9 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Cool Season (November-February): Perfect weather (20-30°C) with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures, and peak tourist season. Highest prices but optimal conditions for all activities and sightseeing.

Hot Season (March-May): Very hot temperatures (30-40°C) with moderate crowds and prices. Challenging for extensive outdoor activities but good for beaches and air-conditioned attractions.

Rainy Season (June-October): Heavy afternoon rains, high humidity, but lowest prices and fewer crowds. Lush landscapes, some flooding, but authentic experiences and great value.

🌍 What to discover in TH?

Southeast Asian paradise with ancient temples, incredible cuisine, and vibrant culture. Experience Buddhist traditions, beautiful beaches, and authentic Thai hospitality. The country offers cultural immersion with natural beauty and culinary excellence. Thailand provides diverse experiences with cultural authenticity and natural preservation.

📋 Important notes

Ferries between the three islands run frequently (Lomprayah and Seatran). The journey from Koh Samui to Koh Pha Ngan takes 30 minutes, Koh Pha Ngan to Koh Tao around 1h30. The dry season runs from December to April - the best time for diving (visibility 20-30 m). The windy season (June-August) can disrupt crossings and underwater visibility. Ang Thong Marine Park is closed from November to mid-December in some years.

Koh Samui

Where a 12-meter golden Buddha overlooks the bay and 42 limestone islands float offshore.

★★★★☆
📍 Wat Phra Yai Big Buddha Koh Samui📍 Wat Plai Laem Koh Samui📍 Fisherman's Village Bophut Koh Samui
1

Cross the causeway that connects Koh Samui to the islet of Koh Faan. At the end stands the Big Buddha - 12 meters tall, golden, seated in the Mara posture - staring back at you since 1972. It's often the first thing you spot when landing in Samui. Climb the bell-lined staircase. At the top, views sweep across the entire Bang Rak bay and planes taking off 3 km away.

⏱ 45 min
2

Two kilometers from Big Buddha, push on to Wat Plai Laem. This modern temple built on an artificial lake blends Chinese and Thai styles. The 18-armed white statue of Guanyin reflects in the water. Catfish in the lake fight over pellets visitors toss them. It's more kitsch than solemn - and that's precisely its charm.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Late afternoon, head down to Bophut's Fisherman's Village. This converted fishing quarter lines wooden Chinese shophouses along a calm beach. On Friday nights, the Walking Street comes alive: stalls of grilled fish, smoothies, artisan jewelry. It's the island's most pleasant market - more intimate than Chaweng, without the blasting music.

Koh Samui

Where a 12-meter golden Buddha overlooks the bay and 42 limestone islands float offshore.

★★★★☆
📍 Ang Thong National Marine Park📍 Emerald Lake Mae Koh Ang Thong📍 Wua Talap Island Ang Thong
1

Board at 7:30 am from Nathon pier for Ang Thong Marine Park - 42 limestone islands set on an emerald-green sea, 30 km northwest of Samui. The boat docks at Wua Talap, the main island. Climb the 500 steps up to the viewpoint: below, the archipelago spreads like a scale model - jungle-covered islets, turquoise lagoons, deserted white beaches.

⏱ 120 min
2

Take the boat to Mae Koh island. A 400-meter trail climbs through jungle to the Thale Nai - the Emerald Lake. This inland saltwater lagoon, ringed by limestone cliffs, shifts color with the light. In the morning, it's deep green. It's the most famous image of Ang Thong, and it doesn't disappoint.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Kayak between Ang Thong's islets. Paddle into sea caves accessible at low tide and along cliffs where sea eagles nest. Most excursions include kayaking, snorkeling and lunch on Wua Talap beach. Return around 5 pm.

Koh Samui

Where a 12-meter golden Buddha overlooks the bay and 42 limestone islands float offshore.

★★★★☆
📍 Wat Khunaram mummified monk Koh Samui📍 Na Muang Waterfall Koh Samui📍 Secret Buddha Garden Koh Samui📍 Lamai Beach Koh Samui
1

Stop by Wat Khunaram in the central hills. Monk Luong Pordaeng died in 1973 in meditation position. His mummified body is displayed in a glass case - seated, sunglasses on, barely altered after 50 years. For Thai Buddhists, it's a sign of extreme virtue. The place is understated and peaceful.

⏱ 30 min
2

Continue to Na Muang 1 waterfall, accessible in 5 minutes' walk from the parking lot. Water drops 18 meters into a natural pool of purple granite - the color gives the waterfall its name (na muang = purple water). Swimming possible during rainy season when the flow is strong.

⏱ 60 min
3

If you have a 4x4 or powerful scooter, climb to the Secret Buddha Garden, at 500 meters altitude in the jungle. This sculpture garden was created in 1976 by a farmer, Nim Thongsuk, who spent 20 years erecting Buddha and goddess statues among the trees. The road up is steep, muddy after rain, and not for scooter beginners.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

End the day at Lamai Beach, Samui's second beach. Calmer and longer than Chaweng, it stretches 4 km. At the south end, the rocks Hin Ta and Hin Yai (grandfather and grandmother) have very explicit anatomical shapes - Thais love posing there for photos. Dinner with your feet in the sand at one of the beach restaurants.

⏱ 120 min

Koh Samui

Where a 12-meter golden Buddha overlooks the bay and 42 limestone islands float offshore.

★★★★☆
📍 Chaweng Beach Koh Samui📍 Lipa Noi Beach Koh Samui sunset📍 Nathon town Koh Samui
1

Give yourself a morning at Chaweng, Samui's main beach - 5 km of white sand, shallow water, coconut palms. It's the island's busiest and makes no apologies: parasols, fruit vendors, music. Swim early morning when the beach is still calm.

⏱ 120 min
2

In the afternoon, ride to Nathon, the small administrative capital on the west coast. No tourism here - just shopping streets, a fish market, Chinese shophouses and the main ferry port. Order an iced coffee in one of the old waterfront cafés.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Sunset at Lipa Noi, the west coast beach. It faces due west, toward the mainland hills. The water is so shallow you walk 100 meters into the sea and still have water at knee level. The late-day colors - orange, pink, purple - are among Samui's finest.

Koh Pha Ngan

Hidden boat-only beaches meet jungle waterfalls carved by Thai kings.

★★★★☆
📍 Thong Sala pier Koh Phangan📍 Srithanu Beach Koh Phangan📍 Phaeng Noi Waterfall Koh Phangan
1

Thirty-minute ferry from Samui. Arrival at Thong Sala, Koh Pha Ngan's main port. Rent a scooter at the pier - it's the only practical way to explore the island. Roads are decent on the west coast, more adventurous on the east and north.

⏱ 30 min
2

Start with Phaeng Noi waterfall, in the island's center, in Than Sadet National Park. A 500-meter trail leads to a waterfall surrounded by dense jungle. Continuing 30 minutes upward, a viewpoint offers views over the entire west coast and distant islands. Entry 100 baht.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Settle into Srithanu on the west coast, the island's yoga and wellness quarter. Relaxed vibe, vegetarian cafés, beachfront yoga studios. It's not for everyone, but it's Koh Pha Ngan's alternative face, far from Haad Rin's parties.

Koh Pha Ngan

Hidden boat-only beaches meet jungle waterfalls carved by Thai kings.

★★★★☆
📍 Bottle Beach Koh Phangan📍 Chaloklum Bay Koh Phangan📍 Koh Ma sandbar Mae Haad Koh Phangan
1

Leave early for Bottle Beach (Haad Khuat), on the north coast. Two options: a 2.5 km jungle trail from Chaloklum (1h30, steep) or a taxi-boat from Chaloklum (10 min, 100 baht). The beach is a white sand crescent framed by boulders and coconut palms - no road, no cars, just a few wooden bungalows. It's the image you have in mind when you think 'Thai beach'.

⏱ 180 min
2

Late afternoon, ride to Mae Haad Beach on the northwest coast. At low tide, a sandbar connects Koh Pha Ngan to the small island of Koh Ma. Cross on foot - ankle-deep water - to the island. Decent snorkeling around rocks on the north side. Check tide times before going.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Lunch at the fishing village of Chaloklum, at the back of the north bay. Restaurants serve the morning's catch - grilled sea bass, prawns sautéed with basil, tom yum soup. Chaloklum has remained a real fishing port, with colorful boats at anchor and nets drying on the pier.

⏱ 90 min

Koh Pha Ngan

Hidden boat-only beaches meet jungle waterfalls carved by Thai kings.

★★★★☆
📍 Haad Yuan Beach Koh Phangan📍 Than Sadet Waterfall Koh Phangan📍 Thong Nai Pan Beach Koh Phangan
1

Ride toward the east coast to Than Sadet National Park. The eponymous waterfall flows over granite rocks carved by several Rama kings - Rama V came to bathe here in the late 19th century. Descending the rocks to natural pools takes 20 minutes. Continue to Haad Sadet Beach, a wild beach with two bars and deep water for swimming.

⏱ 120 min
2

Push on to Thong Nai Pan, the northeast's double bay. Two crescent beaches separated by a rocky headland - Thong Nai Pan Noi (the small one) is quieter, Thong Nai Pan Yai (the big one) has more restaurants. Access via a winding road from the island's center makes it less crowded than the west coast.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Take a taxi-boat from Haad Rin to Haad Yuan, the hidden beach on the southeast coast. No road: you arrive by boat or via a 2h trek through jungle. A few wooden bungalows, a bar perched on rocks (Eden Bar), and turquoise water. On Saturday nights, Eden Bar hosts a party that runs until Sunday morning - electronic music vibe in the middle of nature.

Koh Pha Ngan

Hidden boat-only beaches meet jungle waterfalls carved by Thai kings.

★★★★☆
📍 Haad Rin Beach Koh Phangan📍 Phantip Night Market Thong Sala📍 Kuan Yin Chinese Temple Koh Phangan
1

Head down to Haad Rin, the island's southern tip. This peninsula has two beaches: Haad Rin Nok (east side, the Full Moon Party beach) and Rin Nai (west side, sunsets). Outside party periods, Haad Rin Nok is a beautiful fine-sand beach with clear water. Walk to the small lighthouse at the end of Rin Nai for the view.

⏱ 90 min
2

Stop at the Chinese Kuan Yin temple, on the west coast between Ban Tai and Thong Sala. A large Guanyin statue gazes at the sea from a dragon-adorned terrace. The temple is more recent but photogenic - and almost always deserted.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

In the evening, dine at Thong Sala's Phantip Night Market. It's the island's real local market - pad thai, chicken skewers, sticky mango rice, fresh fruit juices. Thais eat there between 6 pm and 9 pm. Less photogenic than Fisherman's Village, but more authentic.

Stop 9

Koh Tao

The planet's dive training capital with 26 sites and passing whale sharks in season.

★★★☆☆
📍 Sairee Beach Koh Tao📍 Koh Nang Yuan viewpoint📍 John Suwan Viewpoint Koh Tao
1

Ferry from Koh Pha Ngan (1h30). Arrival at Mae Haad port. Settle into Sairee Beach, the main beach - 1.7 km of sand on the west coast. Dive schools line the beach. Koh Tao is the world's most popular place to get your PADI Open Water - prices are among the planet's lowest and conditions ideal for beginners.

⏱ 60 min
2

Take a longtail from Mae Haad to Koh Nang Yuan, 1 km northwest. Three islets connected by two white sandbars in a Y shape. Climb the steps to the viewpoint: the plunging view over the three sand strips and turquoise water is one of the most famous images of the Gulf of Thailand. Decent snorkeling on the west side. Paid entry, no plastic allowed on the island.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Late afternoon, climb to John Suwan Viewpoint at the island's southern tip. The trail starts from Chalok Baan Kao beach and climbs 20 minutes through rocks. At the top, views embrace the entire Chalok bay and Shark Bay below. It's Koh Tao's best sunset spot.

Koh Tao

The planet's dive training capital with 26 sites and passing whale sharks in season.

★★★☆☆
📍 White Rock dive site Koh Tao📍 Japanese Gardens dive site Koh Tao📍 Shark Bay Koh Tao📍 Twins dive site Koh Tao
1

Dive day. Koh Tao has 26 dive sites around the island. For beginners, Japanese Gardens offers coral gardens at 5-12 meters depth with clownfish, angelfish and stingrays. Twins, two twin pinnacles covered in corals, is perfect for a first dive. Dive schools pick up divers directly from Sairee beach.

⏱ 240 min
2

In the afternoon, head down to Shark Bay (Thian Og Bay) south of the island. The name comes from blacktip reef sharks patrolling the shallow waters - harmless, between 1 and 2 meters long. You see them from the beach, often late in the day. Easy snorkeling from shore.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If you prefer snorkeling to scuba diving, rent mask and fins at Sairee and head along the northwest coast on foot or by boat toward Mango Bay and Hin Wong Bay. Reefs there are close to shore and visibility often excellent. Hin Wong has large underwater boulders colonized by colorful corals.

Koh Tao

The planet's dive training capital with 26 sites and passing whale sharks in season.

★★★☆☆
📍 Chumphon Pinnacle dive site Koh Tao📍 Sail Rock dive site Gulf of Thailand📍 Southwest Pinnacle dive site Koh Tao
1

Second day underwater - the advanced sites. Sail Rock, halfway between Koh Tao and Koh Pha Ngan, is considered the Gulf of Thailand's best dive site. A granite pinnacle piercing the surface, with a vertical chimney divers pass through at 18 meters. Schools of barracuda, giant groupers, and in season (March-May), whale sharks. The excursion takes the entire day - breakfast and lunch included.

⏱ 360 min

Activities

Alternative if Sail Rock is too far or conditions poor: Chumphon Pinnacle, 12 km northwest. Granite pinnacles between 12 and 35 meters depth, carpeted with anemones. It's the statistically most likely spot to encounter a whale shark around Koh Tao. Southwest Pinnacle, 13 km southwest, offers walls of soft corals and schools of barracuda.

📍 Tanote Bay Koh Tao📍 Ao Leuk Bay Koh Tao📍 Sairee Beach sunset Koh Tao
1

Explore the east coast by scooter. First stop: Tanote Bay, a bay wedged between granite boulders with a natural diving rock. Water is crystal clear and reefs start 2 meters from shore. Continue to Ao Leuk, a calmer bay with a gentle slope of sand and corals - perfect for a final snorkeling session.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Last sunset on Sairee Beach. Beach bars roll out their cushions on the sand and musicians settle in. It's the end-of-trip vibe - feet in the sand, cold beer, the sun dropping into the Gulf. For the return journey, ferries to Chumphon (mainland, 2h) or back via Koh Pha Ngan and Samui.
