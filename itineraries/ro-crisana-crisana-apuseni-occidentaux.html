Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with stunning fall colors, stable weather, and harvest season celebrations. Perfect for wine tourism, cultural exploration, and photography with moderate prices and pleasant conditions.

Summer (July-August): Warm weather (20-30°C) with long daylight hours, festival season, and peak tourist activities. Highest prices and crowds but best conditions for mountain hiking and outdoor adventures.

Arad

★★★☆☆
1

Look up beneath the dome of Arad's Palatul Cultural: the fresco depicts the solar system and Halley's comet, gilded with gold leaf. Built between 1911 and 1913 by local architect Szántay Lajos, this palace blends neoclassical, Gothic and Italian Renaissance styles. Richard Strauss, Béla Bartók and George Enescu all performed here. Closed Monday mornings.

⏱ 60 min
2

Piața Avram Iancu opens wide, lined with pastel-hued Art Nouveau façades. Take time to spot the red-brick Administrative Palace and the neo-Gothic church. The covered market, a stone's throw away, sells fresh langos for a few lei.

⏱ 45 min
3

If you've got another hour, push on to the Orthodox Cathedral of St John the Baptist. Its massive proportions are surprising in a city this size. Construction lasted from 1862 to 1865, funded by the Romanian community.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk the pedestrian promenade along the Mureș riverbank, especially late in the day when the terraces come alive. Count on 2 km round trip, flat and shaded. A good way to walk off the langos.

Siria

★★☆☆☆
1

The climb to Cetatea Șiria takes 20 minutes—steep but short. At the top, the 13th-century ruins dominate the entire Arad plain. You can still make out a western wall over 20 meters high and the remains of a Romanesque keep. Destroyed by the Habsburgs in 1784, the site retains a raw beauty. Proper footwear essential.

Activities

Remetea

★★★☆☆
1

The portal of Peștera Meziad measures 16 meters high and 10 wide—you enter as into an underground Gothic cathedral. With 4,750 m of development over four levels, it's one of Romania's longest developed caves. Emil Racoviță studied it in 1921. The protected bat colony is impressive. Activities

On the road to Meziad, stop in front of the centuries-old oak of Remetea, a pedunculate oak reputed to be around 400 years old, the last survivor of a forest that once linked the two villages. It reaches about 25 m high. Photo mandatory.

Oradea

★★★☆☆
1

The Palatul Vulturul Negru overshadows everything you've seen. This Y-shaped passage, inspired by Milan's Galleria Vittorio Emanuele, dates from 1908. Its 1907 stained-glass window depicting a black eagle casts colored reflections when the sun enters. It's the largest Art Nouveau commercial complex in Transylvania. Today, cafés and bars occupy the ground floor. Free, open.

⏱ 45 min
2

Walk around Piața Unirii on foot: each façade tells a different century. Baroque, Art Nouveau, neoclassical coexist over 200 meters. Oradea has 89 listed Art Nouveau buildings—the highest concentration in Romania. The City Hall Palace, with its clock tower, closes the vista.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Cetatea Oradea, a pentagonal fortress, now hosts exhibitions and festivals. The bastioned enclosure dates from the 16th century, but the site has been fortified for much longer. In summer, an open-air bar sets up in the courtyard. Free entry to the courtyard, paid for exhibitions.

⏱ 60 min
4

Cross the bridge to reach the Palatul Baroc (Episcopal Palace), nicknamed "the palace with 365 windows." Built in the 18th century, it houses a museum and interior courtyards. The style recalls imperial Vienna, not deep Romania. Open Tuesday to Sunday.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stroll along the Crișul Repede, between the Podul Ladislau and the Union Bridge. The landscaped banks offer a fine perspective on the illuminated façades after dark. Food trucks often set up by the water in summer.

⏱ 45 min

Try the kürtőskalács (chimney cake) sold in the Vulturul Negru passage. This crispy Hungarian cinnamon dessert costs 10-15 lei and is eaten hot, on the go.

Baile Felix

★★☆☆☆
1

The thermal lakes of Băile Felix host a rare tenant: the water lily Nymphaea lotus thermalis, a subtropical species that survived from the Tertiary era. The water emerges between 20 and 49 °C depending on the spring, rich in calcium, sodium and bicarbonates. Monk Felix Helcher founded the first baths between 1711 and 1721. Outdoor pool entry around 30-50 lei depending on the complex.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

If you're traveling with children, Nymphaea Aqua Park offers slides, wave pools and a wellness area. Plan at least 3 hours. Open year-round.

In Salonta, Romanians and Hungarians have coexisted for centuries, and it shows.
