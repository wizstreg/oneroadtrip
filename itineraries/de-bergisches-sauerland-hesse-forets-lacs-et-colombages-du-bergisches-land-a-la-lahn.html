Wuppertal

★★★☆☆
1

You're floating twelve meters above the Wupper, swaying gently as you glide. The Schwebebahn, a suspended monorail inaugurated in 1901, runs 13.3 km through the city across 20 stations. Several stations preserve their original Jugendstil architecture. The full round trip takes about an hour. Grab a window seat for photos as you pass over the zoo.

⏱ 60 min
2

Founded in 1902 in the former Elberfeld town hall, the Von der Heydt Museum houses Monet, Picasso, Kirchner, and Dix. The collection comprises around 3,000 paintings and 30,000 graphic works. On the first Thursday of the month, the permanent collection is free from 5pm to 8pm. Allow a good two hours.

⏱ 120 min
3

Take the Nordbahntrasse, a former railway line converted into a 22-km pedestrian and cycling path. It threads through tunnels and viaducts above the rooftops. A 3-km stretch between Mirke and Loh is enough to capture the reclaimed industrial vibe. Free access, wheelchair accessible.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Wuppertal Zoo surprises with its hilly topography spread across 24 hectares. Lions and penguins share terraced woodland slopes. Founded in 1881, it's one of Germany's oldest zoos. Arrive at opening time to avoid weekend crowds.

⏱ 90 min

If you arrive on a Thursday evening, the Von der Heydt Museum opens its doors free for the permanent collection. Otherwise, the cafés along Friedrich-Ebert-Straße offer ideal terraces to watch the Schwebebahn glide overhead.

⏱ 45 min

Solingen

★★☆☆☆
1

Take the cable car (Seilbahn) up to Schloss Burg rather than climbing on foot. This 12th-century fortress, the largest restored burg in North Rhine-Westphalia, towers 110 meters above the Wupper. The Bergisches Museum traces the lives of the Counts of Berg. Admission €10, closed Mondays.

⏱ 90 min
2

In the lanes of Gräfrath's old town, the scent of bakeries mingles with slate-grey walls. This historic Solingen quarter clusters half-timbered houses and cobbled courtyards around the Klosterkirche. Less touristy than Freudenberg, more authentic too. Park at Gräfrather Marktplatz.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If weather permits, hike the Klingenpfad, a 7-km 'blade trail' linking Gräfrath to Schloss Burg through the forest. Solingen has been the city of knives since the Middle Ages. The path passes old forges and grinding mills.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Altenberger Dom

1

No bell tower, no town around it, yet it's definitely a Dom. Altenberg Cathedral rises from deep forest in the Dhünn valley. ⏱ 45 min

Activities

Follow the short path that runs along the Dhünn downstream from the cathedral (2 km round trip, flat). The stream flows between beeches and ferns in a silence only Cistercian monks would have known. A café-restaurant sits just opposite the portal.

Freudenberg

★★★☆☆
1

The photo is everywhere on Instagram, but nothing prepares you for the real visual shock. Freudenberg's Alter Flecken lines up over 80 black-and-white half-timbered houses, rebuilt after the 1666 fire following the 1540 plan. Climb to the Kurpark for the full panorama. Designated a monument of international significance by the State.

⏱ 60 min
2

Locals simply call their quarter the 'Flecken.' The 4fachwerk museum on Mittelstraße displays local clockmaking crafts in two restored half-timbered houses. Open Wednesday, Saturday, and Sunday 2pm-5pm. Free admission.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk the Fachwerkweg, a 12-km loop around Freudenberg. The trail passes through the Hohenhain tunnel (former railway), the wooded Gambachtal valley, and offers several viewpoints over the black-and-white ensemble. Allow three hours at a good pace.

⏱ 180 min

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Arnsberg

★★☆☆☆
1

Pass beneath the Glockenturm, a medieval keep turned baroque bell tower after two fires (1600 and 1709). It's Arnsberg's emblem. The old town coils around the Ruhr's loop, with half-timbered houses and remnants of ramparts. The Kaiserspförtchen, the last medieval gate, deserves a detour.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Sauerland Museum in the Landsberger Hof covers regional history from prehistory to the 20th century. The building itself is worth seeing: a palace built in 1605 by the Prince-Elector for his mistress. Admission €6, closed Mondays.

⏱ 60 min
3

The ruins of Arnsberg Castle overlook the Ruhr loop from their wooded promontory. Only wall fragments and a tower remain, but the valley view is wide. Free access, 15 minutes on foot from Alter Markt.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Arnsberg feels very medieval, yet its classicist quarter around Neumarkt dates from 1816, designed by architect Schinkel when the Prussians made it an administrative seat. The contrast between the two atmospheres can be walked in twenty minutes.

⏱ 30 min

⏱ 90 min

Marburg

★★★☆☆
1

Start with St. Elizabeth's Church at the foot of the hill. Built between 1235 and 1283 over the tomb of Saint Elizabeth of Thuringia, it's Germany's oldest purely Gothic church. The stained glass tells her life story. It served as the model for Cologne Cathedral.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Oberstadt's staircases climb between half-timbered facades pressed together like books on a shelf. Marburg counts over 700 historic houses, most inhabited. Student cafés spill onto the steps whenever sun breaks through.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Landgrafenschloss overlooks the Lahn valley from its promontory. The building houses the Universitätsmuseum with a 13th-century Gothic knights' hall, one of Germany's largest secular Gothic halls. Park access is free, museum is paid.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you're there on a Saturday morning, the market on Marktplatz animates the square in front of the town hall. Local cheeses, sausages, Apfelwein. The atmosphere mixes cycling students and retirees with shopping bags.

⏱ 30 min

Marburg students call the climb to the castle 'daily exercise.' The Philipps-Universität, founded in 1527, is the world's oldest Protestant university. Its campus is scattered through town, historic buildings alongside bookstores and Kneipen.

Wetzlar

★★☆☆☆
1

Wetzlar Cathedral is famous for what it isn't: finished. The second Gothic tower never rose beyond its base, for lack of money after plague and 15th-century wars. The result is a strange asymmetric façade mixing Romanesque and Gothic. Free admission.

⏱ 30 min
2

In 1772, young Goethe arrived in Wetzlar for an internship at the Reichskammergericht. He fell for Charlotte Buff, engaged to another. The Lottehaus on Pfaffengasse tells this story that became 'The Sorrows of Young Werther.' Charming small museum, 30-minute visit.

⏱ 30 min
3

Walk down to the Lahn via Pfaffengasse and Kornmarkt. Goethe lived at Kornmarkt during his stay. The half-timbered houses, small squares, and walls of the former imperial city can be covered in an hour. Six museums cluster in the old town.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

At Leitz-Park, the Leica world displays photography history since the invention of the Leica I in 1925. Galleries, workshops, boutique. The contemporary building contrasts with the Fachwerk downtown. Bus from Domplatz or 15 min by car.

Weilburg

★★☆☆☆
1

Weilburg Palace occupies half the old town, set on a rocky spur above the Lahn. The baroque residence of the Nassau-Weilburg dynasty preserves its terraced gardens with orangery, among Hesse's finest. The Renaissance Hochschloss (1530-1572) is one of the state's best preserved.

⏱ 60 min
2

Inaugurated in 1847, Weilburg's ship tunnel is Germany's oldest and longest still-navigable tunnel: 195 meters beneath the Mühlberg. Duke Adolph of Nassau had it bored to bypass the Lahnschleife rapids. Ten years later, the railway made it obsolete.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Rent a canoe to pass through the ship tunnel by river. The Lahn flows gently through the vaulted tunnel, lit by a few bulbs. The experience lasts five minutes but stamps the memory. Rental available at Weilburger Bootsanleger, at the tunnel's north entrance.

⏱ 60 min

Limburg An Der Lahn

★★★☆☆
1

Seven towers, a red-and-white façade, a limestone outcrop above the Lahn: Limburg Cathedral (1235) strikes first by its silhouette. The interior mixes massive Rhenish Romanesque with early luminous Gothic. No other church in Germany has seven towers.

⏱ 45 min
2

Limburg locals claim that Römer 2-4-6 is Germany's oldest freestanding half-timbered building, dated 1289. True or not, the Fischmarkt lane around it concentrates 13th-century façades in a tiny space. The contrast with the cathedral looming overhead is striking.

⏱ 30 min
3

Cross the old stone Lahnbrücke (replaced in 1315, rebuilt since). On the other side, the view of the cathedral rising above the slate rooftops is the city's postcard shot. The Farbmühle, an old dye mill at the foot of the rock, makes a good photo landmark.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

If you stay for dinner, restaurants on Salzgasse and Fischmarkt serve Hessian cuisine in half-timbered settings. The cathedral is illuminated after sunset. Limburg is well served by train (ICE at Limburg-Süd), convenient if you're returning the car here.
