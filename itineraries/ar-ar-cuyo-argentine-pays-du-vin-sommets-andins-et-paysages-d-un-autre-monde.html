Mendoza

★★★★☆
1

Plaza Independencia forms the heart of Mendoza, organized around gardens with fountains and sculptures. Surrounded by four secondary squares (España, Chile, Italia, San Martín), it stands as testimony to the city's rebirth after the devastating 1861 earthquake that obliterated the colonial settlement.

⏱ 45 min
2

Parque General San Martín sprawls across 307 hectares of landscaped gardens designed in 1897 by French architect Carlos Thays. At its heart, Cerro de la Gloria delivers sweeping panoramas of the city against the Andes backdrop. The monument to General San Martín commemorates his legendary crossing of the Andes into Chile.

⏱ 90 min
3

Bodega Catena Zapata in the Luján de Cuyo district, 25 km south, was crowned the world's best vineyard to visit in 2023 by World's Best Vineyards. Its truncated pyramid architecture inspired by Mayan temples is one of a kind in Argentina. Guided cellar tours and premium Malbec tastings by reservation only.

⏱ 120 min
4

The Luján de Cuyo wine zone, 30 km south of Mendoza, is revered as the birthplace of Argentine Malbec. Argentina's only Denominación de Origen Controlada (DOC) is located right here. Prestigious bodegas line poplar-shaded roads with the Andes soaring in the distance.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Full-day bike tour through Maipú wine country (17 km east of Mendoza). Bike rentals available on-site. A 16 km circuit connecting bodegas, olive groves, and chocolate factories. Tastings at your own pace at estates like Trapiche, Chandon, or Lopez.

⏱ 360 min

Mendoza

★★★★☆
1

The Uco Valley, 80 km south of Mendoza between 900 and 1,500 meters elevation, produces wines with a unique acidity and freshness. The high-altitude vineyards benefit from intense sunlight and cool nights. Bodega Salentein (Dutch-designed architecture) and Zuccardi Valle de Uco have established estates here.

⏱ 180 min
2

Bodega Chandon, a Moët Hennessy establishment, has been producing Argentina's most acclaimed sparkling wines since 1959. The Terrazas de los Andes estate showcases a Franco-Argentine collaboration blending Bordeaux expertise with Andean terroir.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Tasting of an authentic Argentine asado at a Uco Valley bodega with food and wine pairings. Argentine barbecue paired with high-altitude Malbec is an essential gastronomic experience.

⏱ 180 min

Hot air balloon ride at sunrise over the Uco Valley vineyards. Soar above the Andes and vine rows. Several operators offer this experience from Mendoza with advance reservation required.

Uspallata

★★☆☆☆
1

Uspallata is a village of 5,000 inhabitants at 1,751 meters elevation in the Andean foothills. A classic starting point for Aconcagua, it also served as the filming location for Seven Years in Tibet (1997). The Bóvedas Históricas, 18th-century smelting furnaces used during the independence wars, are the village's only historic monument.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Uspallata Valley is flanked by two mountain ranges in violet and ochre hues. RN7 running through it is the international route to Santiago, Chile via the Cristo Redentor tunnel. The shifting colors of the rock formations with changing light create an ever-evolving spectacle.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Transfer day from Mendoza to Uspallata (107 km, 1h30). Free afternoon to explore the village and immediate surroundings. Short hike on the hills around the village to acclimatize to altitude (1,750 m). Sunset over the Andes.

⏱ 120 min

Uspallata

★★☆☆☆
1

Puente del Inca is a natural rock bridge 70 meters long spanning the Rio de las Cuevas at 2,700 meters elevation. Formed by mineral deposits from hot springs, it displays unique yellow and orange tones. Ruins of the 1940s thermal spa, destroyed by an avalanche, remain beneath the arch.

⏱ 60 min
2

Aconcagua Provincial Park provides access to Laguna de los Horcones and the first viewpoints of Aconcagua (6,961 m), the highest peak in the Americas. The Plaza de Mulas base camp hosts 6,000 climbers each year. Paid entry from the visitor center.

⏱ 120 min
3

Cristo Redentor de los Andes stands at 3,832 meters at the Chilean border. Inaugurated in 1904, it commemorates the peaceful resolution of the border conflict between Argentina and Chile. The road to the pass offers vertiginous panoramas of glaciers and the cordillera. Accessible only in summer (November-April).

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike to Laguna de los Horcones in Aconcagua Park. Easy 2 km round-trip trail with direct view of Aconcagua's south face. Return to Uspallata for the night.

⏱ 90 min

Barreal

★★☆☆☆
1

Barreal Blanco is a massive dried salt flat 12 km by 6 km at 1,800 meters elevation. Constant winds enable land sailing (carrovelismo) at speeds reaching 50 km/h across its flat, white surface. Views of Mercedario (6,770 m), Argentina's fourth-highest peak, form the backdrop.

⏱ 120 min
2

El Leoncito National Park, 35 km south of Barreal, extends across 89,700 hectares at 2,250 meters elevation. More than 250 clear nights per year with zero light pollution - among Argentina's finest skies. Two international-caliber astronomical observatories: CASLEO and CESCO.

⏱ 90 min
3

CASLEO (Complejo Astronómico El Leoncito), a CONICET research observatory inaugurated in 1986, houses the Jorge Sahade telescope, Argentina's largest. Astronomers from around the world come here to observe from one of the planet's purest skies. Guided daytime visits (10am-12pm and 2:30pm-5pm) and nighttime sessions.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Transfer Uspallata-Barreal via RP149 (200 km, 3h30-4h). Mountain road partially ripio (gravel track), spectacular pre-cordillera landscapes. Arrival in Barreal mid-afternoon.

⏱ 240 min

Nighttime observation at CESCO in El Leoncito Park. Sessions every evening around 8pm, no reservation needed. Telescopes aimed at Jupiter, Saturn, nebulae, and the Milky Way. Very warm clothing essential - temperatures drop below freezing after sunset.

⏱ 90 min

Barreal

★★☆☆☆
1

The Calingasta Valley, with Barreal as its main village, is an agricultural oasis irrigated by the Rio de los Patos. Poplars, vineyards, and market gardens contrast dramatically with the surrounding desert mountains. Several artisan bodegas offer tastings of high-altitude Syrah and Torrontés.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Land sailing session on Barreal Blanco in the morning. Local operators offer one-hour introductions. Andean wind blows almost every afternoon. Unique sensation between white salt flat, intense blue sky, and snow-capped Andes.

⏱ 90 min

Excursion to El Leoncito Park: easy 40-minute hike to El Rincón waterfall. Then guided tour of CASLEO in the afternoon (2:30pm-5pm). Ideal combination of daytime visit plus evening astronomy session at CESCO.

Ischigualasto

★★★☆☆
1

Ischigualasto Provincial Park (Valle de la Luna) covers 63,000 hectares, designated a UNESCO site since 2000. In Quechua, Ischigualasto means 'place where the moon sets.' It's the only site in the world presenting the complete, chronological sequence of the Triassic period (245-208 million years ago) at the surface.

⏱ 60 min
2

El Hongo (The Mushroom) is the most emblematic formation - a sandstone ball balanced on a narrow pedestal. In 2024, Coldplay chose this icon as the backdrop for the global launch of Moon Music. El Submarino and El Gusano round out the most visited geoforms.

⏱ 90 min
3

Valle Pintado offers panoramic views from an elevated vantage point over multicolored sediments creating a fantastical landscape. The stratigraphic layers of the Triassic are readable like pages in a 245-million-year-old geological book.

⏱ 60 min
4

Cancha de Bochas displays dozens of natural sandstone spheres from 30 cm to 2 meters in diameter, formed by differential erosion. A rare geological phenomenon visible only here and in New Zealand.

⏱ 45 min
5

The natural sciences museum exhibits Eoraptor lunensis and Herrerasaurus ischigualastensis - the two oldest known dinosaurs, dating back 228 million years. Discovered in 1959 by local guide Victorino Herrera. Herrerasaurus measures 3 meters long.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Transfer Barreal-Ischigualasto (290 km, 4-5h via San Juan). Arrival late morning to catch the 9am or 12pm guided caravan. 40 km circuit in vehicle convoy with park guide leading, 5 stops in 3.5 hours.

⏱ 210 min

Villa Union

★★☆☆☆
1

Talampaya Canyon was carved by the Rio Talampaya (now dry) through red sandstone from the Lower Triassic dating back 251 million years. The walls rise 145 to 160 meters high, sculpted into giant hemicylindrical flutes 5 meters in diameter. UNESCO site since 2000.

⏱ 60 min
2

La Catedral is the most spectacular formation - a 100-meter red sandstone cliff carved into shapes evoking a Gothic cathedral. El Monje, El Rey Mago, El Tablero de Ajedrez, and Los Balcones complete the canyon's named geoforms.

⏱ 90 min
3

The petroglyphs at Puerta del Cañón feature over 100 rock engravings representing figurative, geometric, and animal forms. Created between 640 BC and 1180 AD by pre-Hispanic peoples.

⏱ 60 min
4

Ciudad Perdida presents eroded formations resembling urban ruins - towers, ramparts, streets. Rheas and maras (Patagonian hares capable of running at 80 km/h) are frequently observed here. Accessible from the canyon floor.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Main circuit by bus through Talampaya Canyon (2h30). 4 guided stops: petroglyphs, botanical garden, Cathedral, El Monje. Optional 4x4 Overland extension to Shimpa Canyon (carob forest, 4h total). Visits are mandatory guided.

⏱ 180 min

San Rafael

★★★☆☆
1

San Rafael is Mendoza's second wine city, crossed by the Diamante River. Its vineyards produce Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec, and the local Chenin Blanc grape. Bodega Jean Rivier, founded by a French immigrant in 1905, is among the region's most emblematic estates.

⏱ 120 min
2

Cañón del Atuel is a 70 km gorge carved by the Rio Atuel through sedimentary rocks in red and orange hues. The geological formations take on anthropomorphic shapes with names like 'El Castillo', 'Los Gigantes', 'La Catedral'. Five cascading dams create a succession of lakes in different colors.

⏱ 120 min
3

Valle Grande reservoir forms an artificial lake with turquoise waters framed by colorful cliffs. Home to Andean condors nesting in the walls. Pebble beaches, water sports, river fish restaurants along the lakeshore.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Rafting or kayaking on the Rio Atuel (level 2-3 depending on season). 12 km descent between the canyon's colored walls. San Rafael operators offer full-day trips with guide and equipment.

⏱ 240 min

Wine tasting in San Rafael bodegas. The region is less touristy than Mendoza, allowing more intimate visits to family estates. Specialty: low-altitude Malbec with smooth tannins.

⏱ 120 min

Malargüe is the southernmost city in Mendoza province, 330 km from the capital. It's the essential logistics hub for visiting Laguna del Diamante, Las Leñas, and La Payunia. The town also hosts the Pierre Auger Observatory, the world's largest cosmic ray detector, visitable by reservation.

Malargue

★★☆☆☆
1

Laguna del Diamante forms in a volcanic caldera at 3,300 meters elevation. The Maipo stratovolcano (5,323 m) reflects in its hyper-alkaline waters of intense blue. The lake's shape evokes a cut diamond. Accessible only between December and March, road closed the rest of the year.

⏱ 120 min
2

Colonies of pink flamingos (Phoenicopterus chilensis) frequent the lagoon's alkaline waters. Fauna also includes Andean condors, guanacos, and Darwin's rheas. The extreme isolation ensures wildlife remains undisturbed.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Full-day excursion from Malargüe in 4x4 with guide (3h drive each way, difficult track). Observe pink flamingos, photograph the volcano reflected in the lake, high-altitude panoramas. Return to Malargüe in evening. Only possible December-March.

⏱ 420 min

Malargue

★★☆☆☆
1

Las Leñas is South America's most renowned ski resort, at 2,240 meters elevation 70 km from Malargüe. 65 runs covering 65 km of skiable terrain, summit at 3,430 m. Outside ski season (June-October), the site transforms into an Andean hiking and trekking destination.

⏱ 120 min
2

Valle Hermoso, accessible from Las Leñas, is a high-altitude valley surrounded by Andean peaks between 4,000 and 5,000 meters. High-altitude flora, Andean condors, glacial melt rivers. Self-guided or guided hiking from the resort.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Full-day excursion from Malargüe to Las Leñas (70 km, 1h). In winter (June-October): skiing and snowboarding. In summer (November-May): hiking in Valle Hermoso, trekking to Cerro Torrecillas (4,145 m), horseback riding. Return to Malargüe for the night.

Malargue

★★☆☆☆
1

La Payunia Provincial Reserve covers 665,000 hectares in southern Mendoza. It harbors over 800 volcanic cones - one of the highest concentrations of volcanoes in the world. Its name comes from the Pehuenche 'payen' (place where there is copper). Basaltic lavas, ash, and volcanic bombs create an otherworldly landscape.

⏱ 60 min
2

Payún Matrú (3,700 m) and Payún Liso (3,680 m) are the reserve's emblematic volcanoes. Pampa Negra is a vast plateau of black lava with almost no vegetation. Campo de Bombas displays solidified volcanic projectiles in bizarre shapes.

⏱ 120 min
3

Over 30,000 guanacos undertake the world's longest and most numerous migration here between summer and winter zones. Andean condors, Darwin's rheas, gray foxes, and armadillos complete the fauna of this volcanic steppe.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Full-day excursion in 4x4 from Malargüe with mandatory guide (160 km each way, minimum 10h). Circuit: La Pasarela (gateway), Pampa Negra, Campo de Bombas, viewpoints over Payún Matrú. Frequent stops to observe guanacos and condors. Return to Malargüe in evening.

⏱ 600 min
