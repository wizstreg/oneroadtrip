Ulaanbaatar

★★★★☆
1

Gandantegchinlen Monastery - start with morning ceremonies (between 9 and 10 am) to see the monks at prayer. Founded in 1838, it's the only monastery that remained open during the Stalinist purges of 1937-38. Inside the Mejid Janraiseg temple stands a 26-meter-high statue of Megjid-Janraiseg, inaugurated in 1996 by the Dalai Lama: 26 tons of copper covered in gold, adorned with 2,286 precious stones.

⏱ 60 min
2

Sükhbaatar Square - in the heart of the capital, this is where Sükhbaatar proclaimed Mongolia's independence from China in July 1921. The bronze equestrian statue bearing his name stands facing the grand government palace. Allow 30 minutes to stroll this lively square, a gathering place for all Ulaanbaatar residents.

⏱ 30 min
3

Zaisan Memorial - head south of the city to climb to this Soviet memorial built to commemorate Russian-Mongolian soldiers who fell during World War II. The circular mosaic at the monument's base traces the history of relations between the two peoples. The view from the hilltop embraces the entire capital.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk through central Ulaanbaatar - after the visits, take time to explore the shopping streets around Sükhbaatar Square, stop at a local restaurant to try buuz (Mongolian dumplings) or suutei tsai (salted milk tea), and observe the city's contrasts: traditional yurts stand alongside Soviet-era buildings and contemporary towers.

⏱ 90 min

Olgii

★★☆☆☆
1

Arrival in Ölgii after approximately 2.5 hours' flight from Ulaanbaatar. Capital of Bayan-Ölgii province, this city of 29,000 inhabitants sits at 1,710 meters altitude and represents a world apart within Mongolia: here, residents speak Kazakh, mosques punctuate the urban landscape (four in total), and colorful embroideries adorn storefronts. Spend the afternoon visiting the provincial museum, which presents local wildlife, history, and Kazakh culture.

⏱ 75 min
2

Mongolian Altai panoramas - from Ölgii's heights, the Altai peaks dominate the horizon at over 4,000 meters. These mountains with permanent glaciers constitute one of Mongolia's most striking landscapes. From the city, spot the snow-capped massifs of Tsambagarav and Altai Tavan Bogd.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Exploration of Ölgii's local market - the city bazaar is the best place to grasp daily life in this Kazakh community. Embroidered fabrics, felt blankets, worked leather objects: local craftsmanship is particularly rich. It's also an opportunity to stock up for the following days before reaching more isolated areas.

⏱ 60 min

Khovd

★★★☆☆
1

Khar-Us Nuur Lake - about forty kilometers east of Khovd, this 1,153 km² freshwater lake (the country's second largest) has been a Ramsar site since 1999 for its important wetlands. Its average depth is only 2 to 4 meters, making it a privileged habitat for over 268 bird species. In summer, you can observe pelicans, great egrets, cormorants, and wild ducks. Ak-Bashi Island rises in its center, dividing the lake into two sections. Take time to walk along the marshy shores in search of birds.

⏱ 90 min
2

Khovd city - founded in 1731 by the Qing dynasty, Khovd is one of Mongolia's oldest cities. Capital of a particularly diverse province (twelve different ethnic groups coexist here), it maintains an authentic atmosphere. Stroll through the market to discover local crafts and specialties.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Scenic route Ölgii → Khovd through the Altai foothills - this driving day (about 3.5 hours) introduces you to Mongolian Altai landscapes: dry steppes, river valleys, herds of yaks and horses. Plan photo stops at mountain passes to enjoy panoramas over snow-capped massifs.

⏱ 60 min

Khyargas Nuur

★★★☆☆
1

Hyargas Nuur Lake - saltwater lake in Uvs province, designated a nature reserve since 2000 and integrated into the Great Lakes Basin UNESCO World Heritage site since 2003. At 75 km long and 31 km wide with an average depth of 50 meters, it's one of the country's four largest lakes. Its semi-desert shores, white marble cliffs to the south, and the emerging Khet-suu Khad rock - serving as a perch for migratory cormorants from April to October - make it a remarkable ornithological site. The lake's south side is particularly photogenic.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Migratory bird watching on the lake shores - Hyargas Nuur is a major stopover on the migration route between Siberia and South Asia. Herons, cranes, geese, spoonbills, and, if you're lucky, the white-headed duck (a rare species) are visible in wetland areas bordering the lake. Bring binoculars and visit the shores early morning for best observations.

⏱ 90 min

Horseback or walking excursion around the lake - local providers offer horseback rides along the saline shores. It's an opportunity to cross little-frequented semi-desert steppe zones, observe local wildlife (ibex on heights, marmots), and converse with nomadic families camping near the lake in summer.

⏱ 120 min

Uliastai

★★★☆☆
1

Uliastai, capital of Zavkhan - one of Mongolia's oldest cities, founded in 1733 as a Manchu garrison by the Qing dynasty. At its peak, the fortress housed 3,500 soldiers. Today, traces of this era remain on the Bogd River banks: ruins of the former governor's fortress are visible a few minutes' walk from the center. Uliastai is also an essential supply point before continuing east.

⏱ 45 min
2

Zavkhan People's Museum - the provincial museum presents the life and customs of Zavkhan nomads: reconstructed yurts, utensils, festival costumes, and traditional musical instruments. A useful stop for understanding this region that tourism still rarely visits.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Crossing Zavkhan - the circuit's wildest stage. The sandy or rocky track between Hyargas Nuur and Uliastai (about 300 km, 6 to 7 hours) crosses nearly deserted semi-arid steppes. There's no signage: your driver must know the cross-country tracks. A robust vehicle like a Land Cruiser is essential. Enjoy a few stops along the way to observe the landscape and, if the season permits, Mongolian gazelles on the plains.

⏱ 60 min

Terkhiin Tsagaan Lake

★★★★☆
1

Terkhiin Tsagaan Nuur, the White Lake - nestled at 2,060 meters altitude in Khorgo-Terkhiin Tsagaan Nuur National Park, this 61 km² lake was born from a lava flow from Khorgo volcano that blocked the Terkh River approximately 8,000 years ago. The water is remarkably transparent, and the shores welcome deer, wild boar, and great cormorants. Upon late afternoon arrival, circle the lake's north side to find the best viewpoints over the surrounding chain of volcanoes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Route Uliastai → Terkhiin Tsagaan Nuur - long stage of about 250 km (4 to 5 hours). The track runs along the Khangai mountains before reaching Tariat village, the national park's entry point. Plan a stop in Tariat for supplies and some photos of the first basalt formations near the lake. Arrive lakeside in late afternoon.

⏱ 60 min

Terkhiin Tsagaan Lake

★★★★☆
1

Khorgo Volcano and its lava flows - east of the lake, the Khorgo crater (2,240 m altitude) is Mongolia's last active volcano, extinct for 8,000 years. Its crater measures 200 meters in diameter and 70 to 80 meters deep, surrounded by small groves. From the summit, the panorama over Terkhiin Tsagaan Lake and the surrounding volcanic chain is one of Arkhangai's most spectacular. Around the volcano, the 'basalt yurts' - solidified lava bubbles - form a surprising geological landscape.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Ascent of Khorgo volcano - allow about 3 to 4 hours round trip to reach the crater from camp. The hike is relatively easy and follows a trail up the basalt-covered flanks. Descending into the crater bottom reveals a small central lake and dense vegetation contrasting with the black lava flows. Good walking shoes suffice.

⏱ 180 min

Bird watching on Terkhiin Tsagaan Lake - the lake is designated a wetland of international importance. In summer, swans, cranes, geese, and wild ducks land on its shores. The lake surroundings also shelter marmots and golden eagles. Bring your binoculars.

⏱ 60 min

Tsetserleg

★★☆☆☆
1

Zaya Gegeen Monastery-Museum - perched on Tsetserleg's heights, this monastery was founded by Zaya Pandita Luvsanperenlei (1642-1715), the first great Khalkh religious figure, who made it the heart of a Buddhist principality. Converted into an ethnographic and religious museum, it presents a collection of objects from nomadic life, costumes, and traditional musical instruments. The hilltop view over the city and forested mountains is worth the visit.

⏱ 60 min
2

Tsetserleg city center - its name means 'garden' in Mongolian, and the city truly deserves this reputation: capital of Arkhangai province nestled at 1,700 meters altitude, it's considered Mongolia's greenest and most pleasant provincial city. Stroll the streets, spot Soviet architecture mixed with contemporary buildings, and do some shopping at the local market.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Descent Terkhiin Tsagaan Nuur → Tsetserleg - about 180 km southeast (3 to 3.5 hours). The road crosses the Khangai foothills, alternating larch forests, passes, and verdant valleys. The landscape gradually softens approaching Tsetserleg, the country's greenest provincial capital.

⏱ 60 min

Orkhon Valley

★★★★☆
1

Ulaan Tsutgalan Falls - it's the Ulaan Tsutgalan River, 'Red Confluence,' that forms this waterfall as it plunges into the Orkhon. Standing 16 meters high and 5 meters wide, these falls formed 20,000 years ago following an earthquake coupled with volcanic eruptions. About 25 meters downstream, you can descend to the bottom of the 22-meter-deep gorge, dotted with pines. The best period to see them in full flow is July-August.

⏱ 75 min
2

Orkhon Valley - designated a UNESCO World Heritage site since 2004, the valley covers approximately 1,220 km² and includes the Karakorum site, former capital of the Mongol Empire. Along the Orkhon banks, 6th-century archaeological remains (Turkish runic inscriptions, Khar Balgas site) and yurts of nomadic herders coexist in verdant steppe.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Route Tsetserleg → Orkhon Valley - about 180 km due south (3 hours). The descent from Khangai offers beautiful panoramas over the central steppe before reaching the Orkhon River banks. Possible stop at Tsenkher hot springs (86°C waters, outdoor pools) midway if time permits.

⏱ 60 min

Orkhon Valley

★★★★☆
1

Tövkhön Monastery - at 2,312 meters altitude on Öndör Ulaan Mountain, accessible only on foot (about 1.5 hours' climb). This hermitage monastery was founded in 1648 by Zanabazar, Mongolia's first Buddhist religious leader, who composed the Soyombo alphabet there. It's from this natural balcony overlooking the valley that you'll have the best view of the Khangai range and the Orkhon's meanders below.

⏱ 150 min

Activities

Immersion with nomads of the Orkhon Valley - the valley is one of Mongolia's most populated nomadic lands in summer. Herding families set up their yurts annually along the river. Participate in milking animals, making dried cheese (aaruul), or gathering herds on horseback. This is the journey's heart.

⏱ 180 min

Horseback ride in the steppe - local providers offer horseback excursions to follow the Orkhon upstream toward the falls or reach a neighboring nomadic camp. The horse remains the best way to explore these spaces at your own pace.

⏱ 120 min

Kharkhorin

★★★☆☆
1

Erdene Zuu Monastery - founded in 1585 by Abtai Sain Khan on Karakorum's ruins, it's Mongolia's oldest Buddhist monastery of fixed structure. Its construction stones came directly from the ancient capital's ruins. The enclosure wall of 108 white stupas - 108 being the sacred number of beads in a Buddhist rosary - surrounds a vast grassy terrain punctuated by three Chinese-style temples. In 1939, leader Choibalsan's communist purge destroyed nearly everything; only three temples and the wall remained. The monastery has been active again since 1990. Inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2004.

⏱ 90 min
2

Karakorum Museum - steps from the monastery, this Japanese-co-financed museum assembles objects from archaeological excavations of Genghis Khan's imperial city. Founded in 1220, Karakorum was the Mongol Empire's capital until Kublai Khan transferred the capital to Beijing. Some vestiges of the city (including two granite tortoises that guarded the gates) are visible in the surroundings.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk around Karakorum vestiges - spot the two granite tortoises surviving from the ancient city, as well as the hill east of the monastery offering an overview of the site. If you're there in July, it's possible to attend Kharkhorin's local Naadam, which blends traditional sports (wrestling, horse racing) and Buddhist ceremonies.

⏱ 60 min

Khogno Khan

★★★★☆
1

Khogno Khan granite massif - nicknamed the 'mini-Gobi,' this natural park combines semi-desert steppes, granite rock formations, pine forests, and a long band of sand dunes (Elsen Tasarkhai) stretching 80 km. On the mountainside, Erdene Khamba Monastery (17th century) built by Zanabazar in honor of his spiritual master overlooks the rocky defile. Ruins of Ovgon Khiid Monastery, destroyed in 1939, are found lower in the canyon.

⏱ 90 min
2

Elsen Tasarkhai sand dunes - these semi-desert dunes, part of the Khugnu Khan mountain chain, constitute a geological curiosity 280 km from Ulaanbaatar. They offer a foretaste of the Gobi, and a few Bactrian camels often graze at the dunes' foot.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Horseback ride to Erdene Khamba Monastery - regional providers offer horseback rides to reach the monastery via granite massif tracks. The approach from the heights reveals beautiful views over Elsen Tasarkhai dunes below.

⏱ 150 min

★★★☆☆
1

Baga Gazriin Chuluu granite formations - 'stone of the little place' in Mongolian, this 300 km² granite massif surges from the steppe at 1,751 meters altitude. Erosion has polished cliff walls into surprising shapes. Inside the rocky labyrinth, you'll find inscriptions of two venerated 19th-century monks carved into rock, as well as ruins of a 17th-century Buddhist temple. According to legend, Genghis Khan stopped in this region during his campaigns.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Crossing the transverse steppes Khogno Khan → Baga Gazriin Chuluu - caution: this approximately 280 km journey passes through very untouristic semi-arid steppes, without signage or infrastructure. It's authentic, wild Mongolia, but your driver must absolutely know the cross-country tracks to avoid getting lost. Allow 5 to 6 hours' drive.

⏱ 60 min

Walk through the granite maze - in late afternoon, explore on foot the massif's corridors and canyons. Rock formations change color with evening light. Ibex, wild goats, and eagles are among the observable fauna. A bivouac under the stars, far from any light pollution, is one of this circuit's great experiences.

⏱ 90 min

Tsagaan Suvarga

★★★★☆
1

Tsagaan Suvarga, the White Stupa - limestone and clay cliffs 60 meters high and 400 meters long that surge from the desert in a stratified wall of ochre, pink, orange, and reddish colors. What was once the bed of a vanished sea is now an open-air geological book: sedimentary layers reveal marine fossils and seashells millions of years old. The cliff owes its name to its resemblance to a Buddhist stupa. Allow about 1 hour's walk to reach the summit and walk along the edge - watch out for wind, it can be violent. Sunset on the cliffs is particularly spectacular.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Exploration of nearby Khevtee Bosoo caves - 7 km east of Tsagaan Suvarga, two 50-meter-long caves. In some sections you can only advance by crawling, in others you can stand upright. A short but unusual excursion to complement the cliff visit.

Dalanzadgad

★★★☆☆
1

Yolyn Am Canyon - in the Zuun Saikhan massif of Gobi Gurvansaikhan National Park, this narrow canyon (at most two people abreast in the tightest passages) is famous for its perennial ice field that persists even in midsummer. Early season (June-July), the ice can reach several meters thick. The 'yol' giving the gorge its name is the bearded vulture, that great mountain raptor nesting on the walls. The contrast between Gobi heat and canyon coolness is striking.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Hike through Yolyn Am canyon - walk up the canyon bottom for 5 to 8 km from the park entrance. The walk is easy and the tight rock walls create a particular atmosphere. Observe Siberian ibex on the cliffs, and watch for bearded vultures gliding above the ridges. Bring a jacket, even in summer.

⏱ 150 min

Bayanzag

★★★★☆
1

Bayanzag, the Flaming Cliffs - these red and ochre sandstone cliffs were christened 'Flaming Cliffs' by American paleontologist Roy Chapman Andrews who visited the site in 1922. It was here that fossilized dinosaur eggs were discovered for the first time, along with specimens of Velociraptor, Protoceratops, and Oviraptor. The sedimentary strata of the Djadokhta geological formation date back over 70 million years. North of the cliffs, a saxaul forest offers an unexpected vegetative contrast in this desert environment. Sunset on the cliffs is particularly intense.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Sunset walk on the Flaming Cliffs - late afternoon's raking light sets the ochre and red cliff tones ablaze spectacularly. This is the ideal moment to photograph the site and walk along the cliff edge. Don't approach the edge too closely, the rock is crumbly in places.

⏱ 90 min

Khongoryn Els

★★★★☆
1

Khongoryn Els singing dunes - Mongolia's largest dunes stretch 180 km long and up to 27 km wide, with peaks reaching 300 meters. It's one of Asia's most spectacular sandy zones. Locals nickname them 'singing dunes': under wind effects, displaced sand produces a deep, melodious sound. At the dunes' foot, the Khongoriin Gol River traces a verdant oasis contrasting with the ambient dryness.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

First dune ascent in late afternoon - climb to the nearest ridge summit before sunset to capture the sand's changing colors (pale yellow to orange) and the desert's vastness. The climb through loose sand is physically demanding (allow 45 to 90 minutes depending on chosen height). The descent can be done in a few minutes running down the sand.

⏱ 120 min

Ongiin Khiid

★★☆☆☆
1

Ongiin Khiid Monastery ruins - founded in 1660 on both banks of the Ongi River, this monastic complex was one of the country's most important: at its peak, it comprised 30 temples, 4 Buddhist universities, and over 1,000 monks. In 1939, under Choibalsan's leadership, 200 monks were killed and buildings razed. Three monks returned in 1990 to rebuild the ruins: today, one temple has been reconstructed and two small yurt museums display objects hidden by monks before the purges. Surrounding hills offer an impressive view of the site and river.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Walk along the Ongi River - this river crossing the desert creates a true ribbon of green vegetation. Trees grow there, khatas (religious scarves) are tied to branches. It's a soothing stop after the Gobi's track days. Climb the hills north of the site for a panoramic view of both banks and ruins.

⏱ 75 min

Visit to the yurt museums - two yurts have been transformed into museums presenting objects saved from communist purges: religious articles, original architectural elements, pre-1939 monastery plans. A poignant testimony to the 1930s Buddhist repression.

⏱ 45 min
