✨ Highlights
  • Litang (4,014m): Gelugpa monastery founded in 1580, birthplace of 7th and 10th Dalai Lamas
  • Yading: three sacred peaks between 5,958m and 6,032m, three glacial lakes above 4,100m
  • Potala: 13 stories, 1,000 rooms, built from 1645 by the 5th Dalai Lama

📆 Best period

May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 7 and 11 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming flowers, ideal weather, moderate crowds, excellent for most regions, comfortable travel.

Fall (September-October): Perfect weather, clear skies, comfortable temperatures, peak tourist season, higher prices, autumn colors beautiful.

Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, rainy season, crowded tourist sites, highest prices, but best for Tibet and western regions.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, fewer crowds, lower prices, perfect for southern regions, harsh in north, unique winter experiences.

Regional Variations: Vast climate diversity, southern subtropical, northern continental, western plateau, plan according to regions visited.

🌍 What to discover in CN?

Ancient civilization meets modern superpower with Great Wall, forbidden cities, and cutting-edge technology. Experience diverse landscapes, incredible cuisine, and 5000-year cultural heritage. The country offers bustling megacities, pristine nature, and traditional arts. China provides overwhelming cultural richness and geographical diversity in rapidly modernizing nation.

📋 Important notes

Tibet Travel Permit mandatory for foreign nationals—minimum 30-day processing, must request via approved Tibetan agency. Tibet closed to foreigners in March (commemoration of 2008 riots). Sichuan section requires private 4x4 (G318 and G227 roads). Chengdu-Lhasa flight mandatory between the two sections. Official driver and guide mandatory in TAR for foreigners. Additional permit (Alien Travel Permit) for Shigatse, Gyantse, and Everest zone. Han Chinese need no special permits. Altitude sickness to be taken very seriously: Litang exceeds 4,014m, Rongbuk 5,019m. Everest north base camp closed to tourists since December 2018—furthest accessible point: Rongbuk Monastery.

Chengdu

Where giant pandas meet the gateway to the Tibetan world.

★★★★☆
📍 Giant Panda Breeding Research Base Chengdu📍 Wenshu Monastery Chengdu📍 Jinli Ancient Street Chengdu📍 Kuanzhai Xiangzi Chengdu
1

Chengdu is your launch pad—and also a city that deserves a full day. Start at the Giant Panda Breeding Research Base (熊猫基地), operational since 1987 in the northern suburbs. Arrive before 9 AM: that's feeding time, when the bears are still wandering around. About 1,900 pandas remain in the wild worldwide, most living in the Sichuan mountains right behind you. Allow two hours on-site.

⏱ 120 min
2

Wenshu Monastery (Wenshuyuan) dates back to the 7th century—one of the few things in Chengdu not rebuilt from scratch. Its inner courtyards smell of incense and jasmine tea. Orange-robed monks read sutras on wooden benches while tourists snap photos by the fountains. Free entry, genuine atmosphere. Allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
3

Kuanzhai Xiangzi, two parallel 19th-century alleyways, has become old-town Chengdu's tourist showcase. It's commercial, sure—but the Qing architecture is preserved and the fermented tofu snack they hand you at the street corner is unmissable. An hour is enough to walk both lanes and sample the food.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the evening, eat at a Sichuan hot pot restaurant. Not the most touristy—look for ones where locals queue. The red Sichuan peppercorn oil broth (花椒) produces a characteristic tongue-numbing sensation: you'll need a taste of this before the weeks of tsampa and yak butter tea awaiting you.

⏱ 90 min

Pack your bag for the road: Chengdu pharmacies (look for 药房, yaofang) stock Diamox (acetazolamide), essential for altitude sickness. Buy some before departing—you won't find it as easily in Litang or Lhasa.

Stop 2

Kangding

First Tibetan town on the G318, at 2,560m on Kham's gorge edge.

📍 Kangding city Sichuan📍 Luding Bridge Sichuan📍 Gongga Mountain Kangding
1

En route to Kangding, stop at Luding to see the Luding Chain Bridge (泸定桥), built in 1706 by order of Emperor Kangxi. Its 13 iron chains stretched across the Dadu River gorges make it one of China's oldest suspension bridges. In 1935, Mao's troops crossed it under enemy fire during the Long March—an episode that founded the Red Army legend. Allow 30 minutes.

⏱ 30 min
2

Kangding (康定) marks the frontier between lowland China and the Tibetan world of Kham. At 2,560m, it's your first night at altitude—your body begins to adapt. The town is wedged in a deep gorge crossed by two rivers. Lhamtso Ngachu Monastery, in the heights, dates to the 17th century and belongs to the Nyingma school. Climb up in late afternoon: the light on the mountains is exceptional.

⏱ 90 min
3

Kangding's old town blends Tibetan and Han architecture in commercial alleyways where monks buy yak butter alongside tourists from Chengdu. Paoma Square (跑马山), at the foot of the mountain of the same name, is the town's heart—locals gather there evenings to dance in circles, a living Khampa tradition.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Paoma Mountain (跑马山, 3,300m) is reached on foot or by cable car from downtown. Panoramic view over the city and, on clear days, of Gongga (7,556m)—Sichuan's highest peak. This is where your physical relationship with altitude truly begins. Walk slowly, drink plenty of water.

⏱ 120 min

Dinner at a Tibetan restaurant in Kangding: order momo (yak meat steamed dumplings) and salted yak butter tea. It's rich, it's hot, it's plateau fuel. Might as well get used to it tonight.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 140 km · 3h
Stop 3

Tagong

Where prayer flags dance at 3,700m beneath sacred Mount Yala.

📍 Tagong Monastery Sichuan📍 Yala Holy Mountain Tagong📍 Tagong Grasslands Sichuan
1

Tagong (塔公) sits on a plateau at 3,700m amid grasslands of an almost surreal green. Tagong Monastery dates to the 14th century and belongs to the Sakya school—it houses a replica of Lhasa's famous Jowo statue, offered according to legend by Princess Wencheng during her passage in the 7th century. A dozen white stupas encircle the main building. Monks come and go between buildings with cell phones in their pockets—21st-century Tibet.

⏱ 90 min
2

From Tagong, on clear days you can see Mount Yala (雅拉神山), a sacred peak rising to 5,820m. For the region's nomads, it's the king of Kham's mountains—no climbing expeditions are permitted. The prayer flags covering the heights around the village are dedicated to it. Sit in the grasslands and look—you probably won't need to do anything else.

⏱ 60 min
3

Xinduqiao (新都桥), 40 minutes from Tagong on the G318, is nicknamed photographers' paradise. The Tibetan village is surrounded by golden poplars (in autumn) and rolling hills. Stop for 30 minutes: the early afternoon light on the golden roofs and black stone houses is remarkable.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Horseback ride through Tagong grasslands—local Khampa guides offer 1-3 hour outings on their plateau horses. It's the only way to truly feel the immensity of these pastures where yak herds graze as far as the eye can see.

⏱ 120 min

Settle into a Tibetan guesthouse in Tagong for the night. Most are run by local families—the welcome is simple, the yak blankets thick. Step outside after dark: at 3,700m, far from any light pollution, the sky is dazzling.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 165 km · 4h
Stop 4

Litang

Holy city at 4,014m—birthplace of two Dalai Lamas and the spirit of Kham.

📍 Litang Monastery Sichuan📍 Litang Chode Gompa📍 Litang Mani Stone Wall📍 Litang Old Town Sichuan
1

Litang at 4,014m altitude—higher than the summit of Mont Blanc. The town is nicknamed "the holy city on the snowy land." Chöde Gompa Monastery (长青春科尔寺) dominates the north of town from a hill. Founded in 1580 by the 3rd Dalai Lama, Sonam Gyatso, during a passage through the region, it's Kham's largest Gelugpa monastery. At its peak, it housed 5,000 monks and oversaw 113 satellite monasteries.

⏱ 120 min
2

The birthplace of the 7th Dalai Lama (Kelzang Gyatso, 1708-1757) stands in the town center—an ordinary Tibetan dwelling with a stupa in the courtyard, surrounded by devotees circling continuously. The 10th Dalai Lama, Tsultrim Gyatso (1816), was also born in Litang. This double birth made the town a special place of devotion for Tibetan Buddhists.

⏱ 45 min
3

Litang's mani stone wall is one of Kham's longest. Thousands of stones carved with the sacred syllable Om Mani Padme Hum stretch for several hundred meters. The giant prayer wheel adjoining it is reputed to be the world's largest. Women and men in traditional costume circle it endlessly.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Wander through the lanes of Litang market in the morning. Khampa nomads from surrounding villages—women with braids adorned with coral and turquoise, men in burgundy chuba with their large knives at their belts—come to trade goods here. Don't pull out your camera immediately: observe first, earn their gaze, then ask.

⏱ 90 min

Walk slowly through town—at 4,014m, even climbing 50 steps leaves you breathless. This is your first real night at very high altitude: rest this afternoon, hydrate abundantly, don't push yourself. Your body is producing extra red blood cells—give it time.

⏱ 60 min
Stop 5

Litang

Holy city at 4,014m—birthplace of two Dalai Lamas and the spirit of Kham.

📍 Litang County nomad grasslands📍 Litang plateau horse riding📍 Chode Gompa Litang morning
1

Second morning in Litang: return to Chöde Gompa Monastery during morning services, around 7 AM. Monks chant in the great prayer hall—the sound of thighbone trumpets (kangling) and drums rises to the terraced roofs. The art collection is remarkable: saddle of the 3rd Dalai Lama, Ming Dynasty frescoes, ancient manuscripts. No explanatory panels—just the works and the silence.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Litang plateau extends for dozens of kilometers around the town. Ask your driver to take you 10-15km from the center: the nomads' black tents, herds of black yaks in the blonde grasses, and absolute silence form a tableau you won't soon forget.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If you're here in early August, the Litang Horse Festival (赛马节) is among the Tibetan plateau's largest: Khampa horsemen gallop at full speed picking up silk scarves from the ground. Outside this date, the festival atmosphere isn't there—but the town's soul remains palpable.

⏱ 180 min

Prepare for departure to Daocheng: check the G227 road condition with your driver. After June rains, some sections are cut off by landslides. The Litang-Daocheng journey takes 3-4 hours under best conditions.

⏱ 30 min

Stop 6

Daocheng

Last town before Shangri-La, where 96% of souls speak Tibetan.

📍 Daocheng Yading county Sichuan📍 Bangpu Monastery Daocheng📍 Daocheng town Sichuan
1

Daocheng (稻城, 3,800m) is the gateway town for Yading—a transit town that merits half a day. Bangpu Monastery, built in 1144 by the 1st Karmapa, Düsum Khyenpa, is the region's oldest monastery. It's 30km from town, at 3,940m altitude, amid barren hills. The building itself is damaged, half-restored, but its location facing a deep valley is striking.

⏱ 60 min
2

Daocheng's center has its decorated-facade Tibetan houses, butchers selling yak, and grocery stores at the main crossroads. 96% of the county's population is Tibetan. Stroll the town streets: it's the last real town before Yading and the next stage—buy snacks, hot water bottles, and chocolate bars for tomorrow's hikes.

⏱ 45 min
3

Daocheng Yading Airport (4,411m altitude, the world's highest commercial airport) is 50km from town. If you wish, a detour to the landing strip gives a sense of the project's magnificent absurdity: A321s landing in the clouds amid sacred mountains.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Purchase Yading Reserve entrance tickets in advance—the online ticket office is often faster. The ticket includes multi-entry access and internal tourist buses. Also arrange with your guide for accommodation in Riwa village (at the reserve entrance), far more practical than returning to Daocheng each evening.

⏱ 60 min

Sunset over Daocheng hills from the town heights—head west in late afternoon. The golden grasslands and snow-capped ridges form a panorama typical of southern Kham.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 7

Riwa Yading

📍 Yading Nature Reserve entrance Riwa village📍 Chonggu Monastery Yading📍 Pearl Lake Yading Chenrezig
1

Yading Nature Reserve was sanctified in the 8th century by Padmasambhava and described in 1928 in National Geographic by American explorer Joseph Rock—his 60-page report revealed these landscapes to the Western world. Two years later, writer James Hilton published Lost Horizon and invented Shangri-La inspired by these descriptions. From the entrance at Riwa, take the bus to Chonggu Monastery (3,880m)—starting point for all hikes.

⏱ 30 min
2

Chonggu Monastery sits at the foot of Mount Chenrezig (6,032m, compassion), the highest of the three sacred peaks. Built about 800 years ago, it's damaged and partially restored—but its location, facing Chenrezig's glaciated wall with green pastures in the foreground, compensates everything. It's one of the reserve's most beautiful viewpoints. Allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
3

Pearl Lake (珍珠海, 4,100m) is 3km from Chonggu Monastery, entirely on wooden boardwalks. In clear weather, it reflects Chenrezig with surreal clarity—the water is turquoise, the shores white as snow. It's the reserve's most accessible sacred lake. Allow 40 minutes walking from the monastery.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike to Luorong Pastures (洛绒牛场, 4,150m): it's the only place in the reserve where you see all three sacred mountains—Chenrezig (6,032m), Jambeyang (5,958m, wisdom), and Chanadorje (5,958m, power)—simultaneously. Yak herds graze in the sparse grass while snow-capped summits reflect in the Gongga River. Allow 2.5 hours round trip from Chonggu.

⏱ 150 min

Observe Tibetan pilgrims doing kora (circumambulation) around the three mountains. Some come from distant provinces, on foot for weeks. The belief holds that one complete kora equals reciting one million mantras. Walk with them, even for a short section.

⏱ 60 min
Stop 8

Riwa Yading

📍 Milk Lake Yading Niunai Hai📍 Five Color Lake Yading Wuse Hai📍 Luorong Pasture Yading three sacred mountains
1

Milk Lake (牛奶海, 4,500m) is Yading's second sacred lake—the climb from Luorong Pastures takes 1.5 hours. Its milky white color comes from the reflection of snow and surrounding limestone rocks. Its shape, seen from above, resembles a teardrop. In fine weather, Jambeyang's glaciers dominate it directly.

⏱ 60 min
2

Five Color Lake (五色海, 4,600m) sits 100m elevation above Milk Lake. It's the reserve's largest lake (0.7 hectares) and highest. Its color changes with the light—from deep green to sky blue depending on time of day. Arrive in early afternoon for the best effect. Aquatic plants on the bottom color the water from the surface.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Complete hike of the Milk Lake - Five Color Lake circuit from Luorong: this is Yading's "long" route—590m positive elevation gain, about 4-6 hours round trip. Start early (7:30 AM) to avoid groups and enjoy morning light on the glaciers. Walk slowly, drink regularly.

⏱ 360 min

Last sunset over Yading from the heights of Riwa village, facing Chenrezig. The mountain's glaciated face turns orange, then pink, then violet in 20 minutes. This is why you're here.

⏱ 60 min

Lhassa

Where the Potala rises like a prayer carved in stone and gold.

📍 Potala Palace Lhasa📍 Jokhang Temple Lhasa📍 Barkhor Street Lhasa📍 Norbulingka Lhasa
1

The Chengdu-Lhasa flight takes 2 hours 15 minutes and lands at Gonggar Airport, 60km from the city. Don't visit anything on arrival day. Lhasa is at 3,650m—after Litang and Yading, your body is better prepared than a direct traveler, but flight fatigue and pressure change demand rest. After transfer to the hotel, a short walk in the Barkhor neighborhood does no harm.

⏱ 60 min
2

Barkhor Street (八廓街) is the circumambulation route around Jokhang Temple—pilgrims have turned clockwise for centuries. Vendors offer prayer beads, thangkas, and yak butter under canvas awnings. It's lively, dense, real. The first evening in Lhasa is spent here, simply, hands in pockets.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rest—that's Day 10's main activity in Lhasa. Hydrate, sleep early, don't push. Tourists who ignore this simple rule find themselves bedridden on Day 11 with severe altitude sickness. Your body needs 12-24 hours to recalibrate its hemoglobin production.

⏱ 60 min
Stop 11

Lhassa

Where the Potala rises like a prayer carved in stone and gold.

📍 Potala Palace Lhasa📍 Jokhang Temple Lhasa Tibet📍 Drepung Monastery Lhasa📍 Sera Monastery Lhasa
1

The Potala Palace (布达拉宫) rises on the Red Hill at 3,700m, 13 stories for 110 meters height, 1,000 rooms. Begun in 1645 by the 5th Dalai Lama, Lobsang Gyatso, it took 43 years to complete—and this Dalai Lama died before the end, his death concealed for 12 years to avoid interrupting construction. It was the seat of Tibetan government until the 14th Dalai Lama's flight to India in 1959. The Red Palace houses the golden funerary stupas of eight Dalai Lamas. Tickets must be booked online with real registration—no tickets on-site.

⏱ 120 min
2

Jokhang Temple (大昭寺) is Tibet's spiritual heart. Founded in 639 by King Songtsen Gampo to house the Jowo statue—a golden Buddha brought by his Nepalese wife Bhrikuti—it rises on 4 levels. Its historic core, dendrochronologically dated to the 7th century, is the only surviving ancient Indian wooden architecture in the entire world. It houses 800 metal sculptures and thousands of thangkas. Photography prohibited inside.

⏱ 90 min
3

Drepung Monastery (哲蚌寺) was, before 1959, the world's largest monastery with 10,000 monks. Founded in 1416, it perches 5km west of Lhasa on a white hill. Only a few hundred monks remain today, but the main assembly hall, thangka chapels, and interior alleyways are intact. In the morning, monks debate in the courtyards.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Walk the Barkhor kora at sunrise (from 6 AM): pilgrims circle Jokhang with their prayer wheels, rosaries, full prostrations on the flagstones. The morning's grazing sunlight on the golden roofs, incense smoke, and mantra hum compose a scene that midday tourists won't know.

⏱ 90 min

Dinner at a Tibetan restaurant in the Barkhor quarter: thukpa (yak noodle soup), yak meat momo, chang (fermented barley beer). Some good addresses in the alleyways north of Jokhang, recognizable by red banners and kitchen sounds.

⏱ 90 min
Stop 12

Lhassa

Where the Potala rises like a prayer carved in stone and gold.

📍 Sera Monastery Lhasa debate monks📍 Norbulingka Palace Lhasa📍 Tibet Museum Lhasa
1

Sera Monastery (色拉寺) is famous for its monk debates, held each afternoon (except Sunday) in the inner courtyards between 3-5 PM. Monks clash by clapping hands, pointing fingers, laughing—it's a Buddhist dialectic learning method codified for centuries. Sera was founded in 1419 and housed 5,000 monks before 1959.

⏱ 120 min
2

Norbulingka (罗布林卡), the Dalai Lama's former summer palace, is a 36-hectare park west of the city. Built in the 18th century by the 7th Dalai Lama, it's been a UNESCO World Heritage extension of the Potala since 2001. It's less visited than Jokhang—local Tibetans come to picnic with family under the willows. A haven of calm.

⏱ 90 min
3

Chakpori Hill (药王山), facing the Potala from across the main avenue, is the best viewpoint of the Potala Palace—no queue, no ticket, no crowd. Climb on foot in 20 minutes. Dusk over the Potala from this hill is the photo everyone seeks.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Explore the old Tibetan town on foot or by rental bike: the Shöl and Ramoche neighborhoods, north of Jokhang, still have white-facade houses painted with typical black and red motifs. It's what remains of pre-1950 Lhasa, amid modern buildings.

⏱ 90 min

Travel shopping in shops around Barkhor: sutra scrolls, copper prayer wheels, carved mani stones, singing bowls. Always negotiate—displayed prices are for tourists. Shops run by Tibetans are generally more authentic than big Chinese stores.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 1h30
Stop 13

Gyantse

Medieval citadel guarding Tibet's most magnificent stupa since 1418.

📍 Pelkor Chode Monastery Gyantse📍 Kumbum Stupa Gyantse📍 Gyantse Dzong fortress📍 Yamdrok Lake Tibet
1

En route to Gyantse, Kamba La Pass (4,794m) offers the first view of Yamdrok Lake (羊卓雍错)—a 630km² sacred turquoise lake encircled by snow-capped mountains. It's one of Tibet's three great sacred lakes along with Namtso and Manasarovar. Stop for 20 minutes for photos from the pass.

⏱ 20 min
2

Pelkor Chode Monastery (白居寺, 3,900m) is unique in Tibet: it unites under one roof the three major Tibetan Buddhism schools—Gelugpa, Sakyapa, and Kadampa—a feat of inter-sect tolerance founded in 1418 by Prince Rabten Kunzang Phak. Its assembly hall houses an 8-meter bronze Sakyamuni statue. The adjoining Kumbum is its masterpiece.

⏱ 90 min
3

Gyantse's Kumbum (十万佛塔) is a 32-meter stupa built between 1418 and 1427, the only stupa of this type in all of Tibet. Its 9 stories house 77 chapels, 108 doors, and more than 10,000 Buddha images. Climb chapel by chapel clockwise: the 15th-century frescoes, in Newar-Tibetan style, are among the finest in all Himalayan Buddhist art. On the 4th floor, four pairs of eyes watch the four cardinal directions.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Ascend Gyantse Dzong (14th-century medieval fortress) dominating the town from a rocky spur. In 1904, Younghusband's troops bombarded it during the British expedition to Tibet. The museum inside relates this colonial chapter from the Tibetan viewpoint. Splendid view over the town and valley.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 100 km · 1h30
Stop 14

Shigatse

Where the Panchen Lama's throne overlooks the roof of the world.

📍 Tashilhunpo Monastery Shigatse📍 Shigatse Dzong fortress📍 Shigatse market Tibet
1

Shigatse is Tibet's second city and seat of the Panchen Lama. Tashilhunpo Monastery (扎什伦布寺) was founded in 1447 by the 1st Dalai Lama, Gendun Drup. Its red and white walls extend over 15 hectares at the foot of Tara Hill. The 10th Panchen Lama resided here until his death in 1989. The main hall houses silk thangkas from the Ming period and the 1st Panchen Lama's ceremonial saddle.

⏱ 120 min
2

Jamkhang Chenmo Chapel houses the 26-meter golden Maitreya, built in 1914 and covered with 279kg of gold. Pilgrims circle its base three times in silence—the head alone measures 4.2 meters. The building's low ceiling makes any perspective impossible: just this mass of gold rising above you.

⏱ 60 min
3

Shigatse Dzong (Samdruptse) dominates the city from a hill. Rebuilt in the 2000s on the ruins of the original fortress destroyed during the Cultural Revolution, it's now partly a museum. The view over the Yarlung Tsangpo Valley and Tashilhunpo Monastery from its terraces is remarkable.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Shigatse market is more authentic and less touristy than Lhasa's Barkhor. Tibetan merchants sell metal objects, textiles, yak butter in blocks, and spices. It's the circuit's best place to buy real souvenirs at reasonable prices.

⏱ 90 min

Prepare for tomorrow's climb: Rongbuk and Everest Base Camp are 300km away, via Tingri, with passes to cross. Verify with your guide that Alien Travel Permits for the Everest zone are in order—they won't let you go without them.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 300 km · 5h

Stop 16

Rongbuk Everest

The monastery where earth meets sky at 5,019 meters beneath Chomolungma's north wall.

📍 Rongbuk Monastery Everest Tibet📍 Everest Base Camp North Tibet📍 Rongbuk glacier Tibet
1

Rongbuk Monastery (绒布寺) stands at 5,019m altitude—the world's highest monastery according to most sources. Founded in 1902 by Nyingma lama Ngawang Tenzin Norbu, it served as starting point and shelter for all major British Everest expeditions in the 1920s. Destroyed during the Cultural Revolution in 1974, it was rebuilt. About twenty monks and nuns still live there. Behind the entrance's white stupa, Everest's north face rises directly—no foreground, no haze, just 8,848 meters of rock and ice.

⏱ 90 min
2

Rongbuk village and its immediate surroundings are worth exploring on foot from the monastery: meditation caves in the cliff, carved mani walls, prayer flags snapping in the Himalayan's icy wind. Footprints of the founder are indicated in a rock cave above the complex—legend or reality, the place is impressive.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Since the north base camp closed to tourists in December 2018 (environmental reasons), Rongbuk is the furthest accessible point. Climbers with ascent permits can go to the real base camp, 8km south. For you, the view from the monastery roof suffices—it's probably the most direct view of the world's highest mountain's north face. Stay until dusk if the sky is clear.

⏱ 120 min

Night at Rongbuk guesthouse (5,000m)—basic, unheated with minimal ventilation, but it's the only available accommodation. Bring a -20°C sleeping bag. The night at this altitude, with Everest in the darkness, is an experience unto itself.
