Cuenca

Where medieval houses cling to cliffsides between two dramatic gorges

★★★☆☆
1

The Casas Colgadas cling to the cliffside above the Huécar gorge, their wooden balconies jutting out into thin air as if floating in space. Inside, the Museum of Spanish Abstract Art showcases works by Zóbel, Tàpies and Chillida in rooms that once hung over the void.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Cathedral of Santa María y San Julián dominates Plaza Mayor with its unlikely fusion of Norman Gothic, Romanesque elements and neo-Gothic additions. Begun in the late 12th century, its main façade opens onto the arcaded square at the heart of the old town.

⏱ 75 min
3

Cross the Puente de San Pablo, a metal footbridge suspended 60 meters above the Huécar gorge. It offers the best view of the Hanging Houses, whether by day or at night when they're dramatically lit.

⏱ 30 min
4

Climb back up through the steep lanes of the UNESCO-listed Casco Antiguo. Colorful façades stack one above another, forming 'suspended favelas' over the abyss—the medieval fabric remains intact here, without the crowds of Toledo.

⏱ 45 min
5

The Fundación Antonio Pérez occupies a former 17th-century convent. Spanish and international contemporary art displayed in a setting of bare stone and exposed beams.

⏱ 45 min
6

30 km from Cuenca, the Ciudad Encantada unfolds its karst formations sculpted by 250 million years of erosion. The dolomite rocks take the shapes of animals, faces and natural arches along a marked trail of about 3 km.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Follow the trail that runs along the Huécar gorge below the old town. The path winds between limestone cliffs and vegetation, offering striking perspectives on the hanging houses seen from below.

⏱ 60 min

Stop at the Ventano del Diablo, a natural balcony carved into the rock that overlooks the Júcar gorge. The plunging view of the green waters and limestone cliffs is spectacular.

⏱ 30 min

Try the morteruelo, a warm pâté made with pork liver and spices—Cuenca's signature dish. Pair it with aged Manchego cheese and a wine from the Serranía.

⏱ 60 min

Consuegra

A ridge of twelve white windmills commanding the golden plains of the Meseta

★☆☆☆☆
1

Twelve white windmills line the ridge of Cerro Calderico. Built in the late 16th century by the Order of Saint John of Jerusalem, each bears the name of a character from Don Quixote: Sancho, Rocinante, Clavileño…

⏱ 60 min
2

Push open the door of the Sancho mill, the best preserved of the dozen. Its original mechanism still functions and grinds grain during the Fiesta de la Rosa del Azafrán on the last weekend of October.

⏱ 30 min
3

The castle of La Muela dominates the hill alongside the windmills. This 10th-century fortress of Arab origin became the seat of the Knights of the Order of Saint John. Its restored halls and ramparts offer a 360° panorama over the plains of the Meseta.

⏱ 60 min
4

Descend into the village of Consuegra. Plaza de España, a former Roman forum, hosts a Renaissance town hall and the 17th-century Los Corredores building, typical of Manchegan architecture with its wooden galleries.

⏱ 45 min
5

The church of San Juan Bautista and the cobbled lanes of the historic center reveal several Mudéjar hermitages and whitewashed houses with ochre tile roofs.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Head to Cerro Calderico in late afternoon. The golden light illuminates the white windmills against a backdrop of endless plains—the perfect moment to photograph this icon of La Mancha.

⏱ 60 min

The Consuegra region is one of the main terroirs of Spanish saffron. Local cooperatives offer tastings of olive oil and Manchego saffron.

⏱ 45 min

Walk up from the parking lot at the base of the hill (20 min). The gradual ascent reveals the windmills and castle one by one, adding suspense to the panorama.

⏱ 30 min

Almagro

Spain's theatrical soul preserved in a 17th-century corral

★★☆☆☆
1

Almagro's Plaza Mayor is bordered by arcades with colonnades and windows framed in green—legacy of the Fugger family, German bankers to Charles V. One of the finest medieval ensembles in Castile.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Corral de Comedias, built in 1628, is the only intact Golden Age Spanish theater still standing. Its 300 m² patio, two-story wooden galleries and original stage host performances year-round.

⏱ 60 min
3

Take a seat in the 'cazuela', the balcony once reserved for women, to understand the 17th-century segregation of the sexes. Beneath the stage, the 'bodega' served as the actors' dressing room.

⏱ 30 min
4

The National Theater Museum occupies the Master's Palaces of the Order of Calatrava, built in the 13th century. Permanent collections trace the history of Spanish theater from Baroque corrales to contemporary stages.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Almacén de los Fúcares, a 16th-century Renaissance warehouse that belonged to the Fugger bankers, hosts summer performances in its interior courtyard. Noble palaces dot the district: Marquis of Torremejía, Counts of Valdeparaíso.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

On weekends, attend a theatrical tour of the Corral de Comedias where costumed actors reenact scenes from the Golden Age in the original setting. In July, the International Classical Theater Festival takes over all of Almagro.

⏱ 60 min

Tapas bars around Plaza Mayor serve Manchegan specialties: pisto, migas, aged Manchego cheese. Almagro is also renowned for its pickled eggplants (berenjenas de Almagro).

⏱ 60 min

Valdepeñas is 25 km away. Local cellars offer tours and tastings of DO Valdepeñas wines—reds aged in oak barrels from Tempranillo grapes.

⏱ 90 min

Almagro is a major center for bobbin lace and blonda. Shops in the historic center display local creations.

⏱ 30 min

Campo De Criptana

Where Don Quixote's giants still turn their sails against the sky

★★☆☆☆
1

Ten white windmills stand atop the Sierra de los Molinos, remnants of the 32 that dominated the town in the 16th century. Three—Infante, Sardinero and Burleta—retain their period machinery intact and are designated national monuments.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Poyatos mill serves as both tourist office and interpretation center. Inside, models and maps explain the workings of Manchegan windmills and how the blades were oriented to face the wind.

⏱ 30 min
3

From the Cerro de la Paz, the view sweeps across the white rooftops of the village and the immensity of the Castilian plain. This is where Cervantes set Don Quixote's battle against the 'thirty outrageous giants.'

⏱ 30 min
4

Descend toward the Pósito Real, a 16th-century grain warehouse expanded under Charles III. Its façade bears two coats of arms of the Order of Santiago. It regulated the price of grain during periods of scarcity.

⏱ 30 min
5

Cave houses carved into the rock line the windmill route. The Casa Cueva de Mambrino houses a museum that recreates troglodyte life with agricultural tools and 16th-century utensils.

⏱ 45 min
6

The hermitage of the Virgen de Criptana, in Renaissance style, has dominated the hill since the 16th century. Below, the hermitage of Santa Ana and that of the Vera Cruz complete the religious heritage.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Arrive at dawn on the Sierra de los Molinos. The absence of vegetation, arid soil and low morning light create an austere, photogenic landscape—the windmills stand out one by one against the horizon.

⏱ 60 min

The Bodega Cooperativa Vinícola del Carmen, founded in 1897, is considered the oldest wine cooperative in Spain. Taste DO La Mancha wines made from Tempranillo and Airén grapes.

⏱ 60 min

Sample gachas manchegas, a flour porridge accompanied by chorizo and fried lard, and Manchegan ratatouille (pisto), typical regional dishes.

⏱ 45 min

Parque Natural De Las Lagunas De Ruidera

Fifteen turquoise lagoons cascading through travertine terraces

★☆☆☆☆
1

The Lagunas de Ruidera natural park strings together 15 lagoons over 20 km along the upper course of the Guadiana, separated by natural travertine barriers. The total elevation drop of 128 meters creates a succession of waterfalls between each basin.

⏱ 30 min
2

Start at the Cascada del Hundimiento, the park's highest waterfall, just steps from the village of Ruidera. It's the starting point of the left-bank trail, the wildest and best-preserved route.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Laguna del Rey is the park's deepest lagoon. From the Mirador de la Laguna del Rey, the view sweeps across its turquoise waters bordered by reeds. A developed beach allows swimming in summer.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Laguna Colgada ('hanging' lagoon) extends into a tongue of land that advances into the water. At its tip, small waterfalls feed the Laguna de Batana below—a bucolic landscape typical of tufa formations.

⏱ 45 min
5

The Laguna Lengua offers the park's most spectacular colors: deep blue and emerald green. The limestone rock barriers and waterfalls carving their way through create an almost surreal setting.

⏱ 45 min
6

The Cueva de Montesinos, a cave mentioned by Cervantes in Don Quixote, opens onto the plateau of Campo de Montiel. This is where the errant knight claimed to have spent three days in an enchanted palace.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Paddle a kayak on the Laguna del Rey or Laguna Colgada. The calm, crystal-clear waters allow you to glide along the travertine cliffs and access beaches and caves unreachable on foot.

⏱ 90 min

Hike the left-bank trail between the Cascada del Hundimiento and Laguna Redondilla. The path crosses shaded zones and offers plunging views over the successive lagoons.

⏱ 120 min

At dawn, the lagoons attract grey herons, coots, ducks and raptors. The marsh reeds and quiet shores of the park make excellent terrain for birdwatching.

⏱ 60 min

Set up a picnic in the designated areas by the lagoons. The turquoise waters and birdsong compose an ideal setting for a nature lunch break.

⏱ 60 min
