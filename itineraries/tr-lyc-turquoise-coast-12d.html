✨ Highlights
  • The Lycian Way (Likya Yolu), 540 km of coastal trails between Fethiye and Antalya, runs alongside the entire itinerary
  • Lycian rock-cut tombs - at Dalyan, Fethiye, Myra - carved directly into cliffs between the 4th and 2nd centuries BC
  • Kekova, ancient city partially submerged underwater after a 2nd-century earthquake, visible from glass-bottom boats
  • Kayaköy (ancient Levissi): Greek village abandoned in 1923 during the Greco-Turkish population exchange, still standing with 760 stone houses
  • The eternal flames of Yanartaş (Chimera), natural methane emissions burning for at least 2,500 years on the slopes of Mount Olympos
  • Phaselis and its three ancient harbors, with the Roman aqueduct still standing in the pine forest, facing Tahtalı (2,365 m)
📆 Best period

April, May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days

To make the most of it, plan between 7 and 11 days.

☀️ Weather

Spring (April-May): Perfect weather (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, comfortable temperatures, and excellent conditions for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Ideal for Istanbul, Cappadocia, and coastal regions.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures after summer heat with stable weather and fewer crowds. Perfect for all regions, cultural exploration, and outdoor adventures.

Summer (June-August): Hot temperatures (25-40°C) with peak tourist season and crowded attractions. Excellent for Mediterranean and Aegean coasts, challenging for inland areas.

Winter (December-February): Cool temperatures (5-15°C) with occasional snow but lowest prices and authentic experiences. Good for Istanbul and covered attractions, limited beach activities.

Marmaris

★★★★☆

1

Perched on the hill above the harbor, this castle was completely rebuilt in 1522 by Suleiman the Magnificent - he used it as a staging base for his campaign against the island of Rhodes. Arrive at opening time (8:30am) to enjoy the cool morning air: from the ramparts, you'll have an unobstructed view over the bay and wooded hills. Inside, the museum displays amphorae, coins, glassware, and ceramics from the Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine periods, including finds from the Knidos site. Allow an hour and a half to explore everything at a relaxed pace.

⏱ 90 min
2

Coming down from the castle, let yourself wander through the narrow alleys of the old town. Traditional houses with blue shutters stand alongside small artisan galleries and cafés serving black tea in small tulip glasses. This is where you'll find craftsmen's shops (ceramics, blown-glass lamps, spices) at noticeably better prices than in the covered bazaar.

⏱ 60 min
3

Adjacent to the old town, this covered market gathers dozens of stalls selling souvenirs, spices, jewelry, and textiles. If bargaining intimidates you, know that it's perfectly expected here. Bring cash and don't hesitate to refuse the first offer.

⏱ 45 min
4

Just beside the bazaar, this small two-story caravanserai was commissioned in 1545 by Hafsa Sultan, mother of Suleiman the Magnificent. The building is one of the few 16th-century Ottoman structures still standing in Marmaris. The austere stone facade is worth a quick look before heading to the waterfront.

⏱ 20 min
5

The Kordon promenade runs along the marina for several kilometers: a succession of cruise boats, wooden gulets, sidewalk cafés, and fish restaurants. It was from this waterfront that in 1798, Admiral Nelson assembled his entire fleet before departing to face Napoleon at the Battle of the Nile. In the evening, settle into a terrace table to watch the boats come in.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Numerous boats offer afternoon outings into Marmaris Bay and to nearby coves. It's a good way to grasp the scale of the coast before setting out by car. The bay is sheltered by wooded hills: the water remains calm even when the sea is rough elsewhere.

⏱ 180 min

Marmaris's main beach stretches about 4 kilometers southwest of the center. It's well-equipped (sun loungers, showers, wheelchair access) and faces due west - ideal in late afternoon.

Datca

★★★☆☆
📍 Datca hotel📍 Datca pension📍 Datca harbour accommodation📍 Eski Datca guesthouse📍 Datca Mugla Turkey stay📍 Datca boutique hotel📍 Eski Datca old village📍 Datca stone houses📍 Old Datca Mugla Turkey📍 Temple Aphrodite Knidos📍 Knidos Tholos📍 Knidos archaeological site Turkey📍 Knidos theatre📍 Knidos two harbours📍 Knidos ancient city Turkey📍 Knidos lighthouse📍 Cape Krio Turkey📍 Tekir cape viewpoint
1

Push on to Eski Datça, the old village 3 km from the harbor: about a hundred houses built from pale stone in the 18th century, lanes shaded by almond and fig trees, a few small cafés. Knidos first settled here, in the middle of the peninsula, before relocating to its western tip in the 4th century BC. The prize for tranquility goes to this village, largely spared by mass tourism.

⏱ 60 min
2

This circular temple (tholos) is one of the site's most remarkable monuments. It housed the statue of Aphrodite of Knidos sculpted by Praxiteles in 365 BC - the first female nude in Greek art, displayed from all sides thanks to doors opening around the perimeter. The original statue has disappeared; late copies are preserved in the Vatican. The pedestal is still in place: you can pause here for a few minutes wondering why kings traveled from across the Mediterranean just to see this one sculpture.

⏱ 30 min
3

Knidos's grand Hellenistic theater could accommodate up to 20,000 spectators according to some estimates. From its tiers, you have a direct view of the two natural harbors - the small harbor to the north and the large harbor to the south - that made the city wealthy: ships waited here for winds to shift before rounding Cape Krio. The Lion of Knidos, symbol of the city's naval victory, was carried off to the British Museum in 1858.

⏱ 45 min
4

A 20 to 30-minute walk from the archaeological site leads to the lighthouse at the western tip of the peninsula. This is the exact meeting point between the Aegean Sea and the Mediterranean. On clear days, you can make out the Greek island of Kos less than 30 km away. Come preferably in late afternoon: the sunset over the open sea is one of the most beautiful along the entire coast.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Datça harbor is less crowded than Marmaris: a few fishermen's restaurants, sailing boats, and a relaxed atmosphere. Datça almonds - hand-harvested on the peninsula - are sold everywhere and worth trying. Sample the local rakı too if you're out in the evening.

⏱ 90 min

Knidos's two coves are stunningly beautiful swimming spots, framed by ancient ruins. The north harbor is calmer; the south harbor, more exposed to wind, is often frequented by anchored gulets. The water clarity is remarkable.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 3

Dalyan

★★★★☆
📍 Dalyan hotel📍 Dalyan river accommodation📍 Dalyan boutique pension📍 Dalyan Muğla Turkey stay📍 Dalyan guesthouse📍 Dalyan riverside hotel📍 Dalyan rock tombs📍 Dalyan Lycian tombs cliff📍 Kaunos tombs Dalyan river📍 Kaunos ancient city📍 Caunos Dalyan ruins📍 Kaunos theatre Dalyan Turkey📍 Iztuzu Beach Dalyan📍 Turtle Beach Dalyan📍 Caretta caretta Iztuzu Turkey
1

These tombs were carved into the limestone cliff around 400 BC for the kings and nobles of the city of Caunos. According to Lycian belief, the higher the burial, the closer the deceased to the gods. The best way to admire them is from a kayık: the river offers the ideal angle, with reeds in the foreground and mausoleums in full cliff face. Several tombs display columned facades imitating contemporary temple architecture.

⏱ 45 min
2

Caunos was founded in the 9th century BC. On site, you'll find a Greco-Roman theater with a 5,000-seat capacity, whose climb to the upper tiers offers an exceptional panorama over the Dalyan delta. The Roman baths, acropolis (152 m elevation), and remains of a Byzantine basilica complete the visit. Mosquitoes made the city sadly famous in Antiquity - Strabo himself noted it - so bring repellent in summer.

⏱ 90 min
3

İztuzu stretches about 4 km between the sea and an interior lagoon. It's one of the main nesting sites for loggerhead sea turtles (Caretta caretta) in the Mediterranean: from May to September, females come to lay eggs at night. The beach closes every evening at dusk. After a long morning in the ruins, it's the ideal place to swim in blue water with no buildings on the horizon.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

The kayık is Dalyan's traditional flat-bottomed boat. A full day by boat allows you to see the tombs, disembark at Caunos, reach İztuzu Beach, and stop at Köyceğiz's thermal mud baths - whose hot sulfurous waters (38°C) are said to have therapeutic properties for skin. Several guides offer this combined circuit departing from Dalyan's central pier.

⏱ 180 min

Dalyan's mud baths are natural pools where visitors coat themselves with warm sulfurous mineral mud, then rinse in thermal springs. The experience is both absurd and joyful. The tradition dates back to Antiquity - Strabo already mentioned this region's springs.

⏱ 60 min

Fethiye

★★★☆☆
📍 Fethiye hotel📍 Fethiye marina accommodation📍 Fethiye old town pension📍 Fethiye boutique guesthouse📍 Fethiye Muğla Turkey stay📍 Paspatur Fethiye hotel📍 Amyntas rock tomb Fethiye📍 Fethiye Lycian tombs cliff📍 Fethiye Telmessos tombs Turkey📍 Paspatur Fethiye old quarter📍 Fethiye historic district📍 Fethiye old town sarcophagus📍 Fethiye fish market📍 Fethiye balik pazari📍 Fethiye harbour restaurant📍 Fethiye ancient theatre📍 Telmessos theatre📍 Fethiye Roman theatre ruins
1

The tomb of Amyntas, son of Hermagios - that's what the Greek inscription on the lintel says - was carved into the cliff in 350 BC. Its Ionic columned facade is one of the best preserved in all of Lycia. To reach it, climb about a hundred steps cut into the rock: from the top, Fethiye Bay spreads before you with its wooded islands. Come preferably in late afternoon, when the raking light gilds the limestone.

⏱ 60 min
2

The historic Paspatur quarter sits at the end of the bay, beneath the cliff tombs. Narrow lanes, overhanging houses, a few Lycian sarcophagi dropped between the cars - including a famous one sitting in the middle of a street, forcing traffic to go around it. The atmosphere is less touristy than the waterfront: this is where locals eat and drink.

⏱ 60 min
3

Fethiye's fish market operates on an original principle: you choose your fresh fish from the stalls, then surrounding restaurants prepare it for you for a set cover-and-service charge. It's one of the most pleasant dinners on the Turquoise Coast. In the evening, the stalls are illuminated and the atmosphere festive.

⏱ 30 min
4

The remains of Telmessos theater (2nd century BC) are visible near the harbor, recently restored. The restoration is controversial - many original stones were replaced - but it allows you to grasp the structure's scale. A good introduction before setting out to discover the better-preserved sites later in the journey.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Butterfly Valley is hemmed between 350-meter cliffs falling straight into the sea. It takes its name from the more than 80 butterfly species that nest there, including the Jersey Tiger. A 50-meter waterfall descends into the valley before reaching the sea. Accessible only by boat from Ölüdeniz (regular departures from the beach in season): there's no other way to get there.

⏱ 240 min

Fethiye's waterfront is one of the liveliest on the Turquoise Coast. Dozens of gulets are moored at the pier. In the evening, departures for multi-day cruises (Blue Cruise) are prepared in a contagiously festive atmosphere.

⏱ 60 min

Oludeniz

★★★★☆
📍 Oludeniz hotel📍 Oludeniz beach accommodation📍 Oludeniz boutique pension📍 Hisaronu hotel near Oludeniz📍 Oludeniz Mugla Turkey stay📍 Oludeniz guesthouse📍 Oludeniz Blue Lagoon📍 Ölüdeniz beach Turkey📍 Belcekiz beach Oludeniz📍 Kayakoy ghost village📍 Levissi abandoned village Turkey📍 Kayakoy ghost town Fethiye📍 Kayakoy churches📍 Panayia Pirgiotissa church Kayakoy📍 Kayakoy orthodox church Turkey
1

Ölüdeniz means 'dead sea' in Turkish - a reference to the absolute calm of this water protected by a tombolo. The lagoon itself is closed to swimming (protected zone) but the large Belcekız beach right next to it offers direct access to that turquoise water. Plan to arrive early in the morning before the influx of tour buses. Looking north, you'll spot paragliders descending from Mount Babadağ (1,900 m) - one of the world's most renowned paragliding sites.

⏱ 120 min
2

Kayaköy is one of the most striking testimonies to the 1923 Greco-Turkish population exchange. This village - which its inhabitants called Levissi - had up to 10,000 Orthodox Greeks living there peacefully for generations with their Muslim neighbors. After the Greek defeat in the 1919-1922 war, the Treaty of Lausanne imposed a massive exchange: Greeks left for Greece, and Turks from Greece arrived in Anatolia. No one settled in Levissi, and the village remained empty. Since the 1957 earthquake, roofs have collapsed, but walls still stand. You can visit the two large churches (Panayia Pirgiotissa and Taxiarhis), the 14 chapels, and the remains of the school. From the heights, the view over the Mediterranean is striking.

⏱ 90 min
3

The two large churches are the village's best-preserved buildings. The lower church (Panayia Pirgiotissa) even served as a mosque until the 1960s. Fragments of mosaics and painted decorations survive on the interior walls. The cemetery behind the upper church offers a silent panorama over the entire ruins.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Ölüdeniz is one of the world's top destinations for tandem paragliding. From the launch platform at 1,900 m, the flight descends 20 to 30 minutes toward the beach, soaring over the lagoon, Butterfly Valley, and the jagged coast. Thermal updrafts are stable from May to October. No experience needed: instructors handle everything.

⏱ 120 min

A marked trail connects Kayaköy to Ölüdeniz beach in 1h30 to 2h of walking. The path crosses pine forest, offers viewpoints over the coves, and ends directly at the beach. It's a section of the Lycian Way (Likya Yolu). Wear hiking shoes: the terrain is rocky.

⏱ 120 min

Fethiye

★★★☆☆
📍 Fethiye hotel second night📍 Fethiye marina accommodation📍 Fethiye guesthouse📍 Fethiye Muğla Turkey stay📍 Paspatur Fethiye pension📍 Tlos ancient city acropolis📍 Tlos Bellerophon tomb Turkey📍 Tlos Lycian ruins Fethiye📍 Tlos Roman theatre Lycian📍 Tlos baths Xanthos valley📍 Tlos stadium ruins Turkey📍 Saklikent gorge canyon Turkey📍 Saklıkent national park📍 Saklikent Gorge Fethiye day trip
1

Tlos's acropolis is a rocky spur dominating the plain. On the south cliff, look for the so-called Tomb of Bellerophon - the Lycian hero who, mounted on Pegasus, is said to have killed the Chimera (whose flames are still visible at Olympos, day 9). His tomb is a large columned burial chamber with a relief of Bellerophon riding Pegasus. On the summit, the foundations of the Lycian citadel bear the walls of an Ottoman fort built by the warlord Kanlı Ali Ağa in the 19th century. The climb is well worth the effort for the view over the Xanthos valley.

⏱ 90 min
2

At the base of the acropolis, on the main road, you'll find the Roman theater (5,600 seats according to estimates) and two bath complexes - including the Great Bath with its seven arched windows opening onto the valley. The Hellenistic stadium with 2,500 seats is still legible, with its 72-meter-long water basin. Tlos received the honorary title of 'brilliant metropolis of the Lycian nation' in Roman times.

⏱ 45 min
3

Saklıkent means 'hidden city' in Turkish. The gorge opens into the mountain for 18 km in length and reaches up to 300 m in depth with a width sometimes less than 2 m. Access is via wading through the torrent from a wooden boardwalk. The water is ice-cold even in midsummer - expect to get wet up to your waist to progress inside. The first section (1 to 2 km) is accessible to everyone. Further in, the canyon requires equipment. Restaurants on stilts above the river at the entrance offer a lunch break with your feet in the water.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Progressing through Saklıkent canyon - on foot in the water - is an intense and memorable physical experience. The torrent is fed by snowmelt from Akdağ (2,000 m); even in August, the water doesn't exceed 12°C. Water shoes are rented at the park entrance if you don't have your own.

⏱ 120 min

Kas

★★★★☆
📍 Kas hotel Turkey📍 Kas pension Mediterranean📍 Kas Antalya Turkey accommodation📍 Kas boutique hotel📍 Kas guesthouse harbour📍 Kas seafront hotel📍 Antiphellos theatre Kas📍 Kas ancient theatre Turkey📍 Kas amphitheatre sea view📍 Kas Lion Tomb Antiphellos📍 Kas sarcophagus street📍 Kas King Tomb lycian inscription📍 Kas town center Turkey📍 Kas harbour square📍 Kas old town Mediterranean
1

Antiphellos's Hellenistic theater is carved into the hillside west of the center, 500 m from the main square. Built in the 1st century BC from local limestone, its 26 rows of tiers could accommodate about 4,000 spectators - and never had a permanent stage building, to preserve the view of the sea. Free access. Sunset from the upper tiers, with Kastellorizo on the horizon, is one of the most beautiful moments of the journey. Plan a good hour here.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Lion Tomb (4th century BC) sits on a pedestal right in the middle of downtown, among restaurants and shops. Its lion heads sculpted at the roof corners and its Lycian language inscription (not yet translated) testify to its occupant's importance. Higher up on Uzun Çarşı street, the King's Tomb, also from the 4th century BC, displays a long poetic inscription in Lycian B (Milyan) - an older Lycian dialect, undeciphered. The rock-cut tombs in the cliffs northeast of town add another layer to this palimpsest.

⏱ 30 min
3

Kaş has managed to remain a human-scale town despite its success. Cobbled lanes, bougainvillea spilling from terraces, small jewelry workshops, and dive shops give the place a particular atmosphere. The main square (Cumhuriyet Meydanı) stays lively until late in the evening. If you're here on a Friday, residents from Kastellorizo cross over to sell their products at the market behind the bus station.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Greek island of Kastellorizo (Meis in Turkish) is only 6 km from Kaş harbor: the crossing takes 20 minutes. It's Greece's easternmost inhabited island. It has about 500 permanent residents, a picturesque harbor with colorful neoclassical houses, and a blue cave accessible only from the sea. Visiting Meis from Kaş also means seeing the Turkish coast from Greece - a striking shift in perspective.

⏱ 240 min

Kaş is considered one of Turkey's best diving destinations. Sites include a World War II plane wreck, ancient amphorae scattered on the seabed, coral formations, and underwater tunnels. Even snorkeling from a gulet, the water visibility is exceptional.

⏱ 180 min

Demre

★★★☆☆
📍 Demre hotel Turkey📍 Demre Kale accommodation📍 Demre Antalya Turkey pension📍 Demre guesthouse📍 Kekova area hotel📍 Üçağız pension Kekova📍 Kekova sunken city boat tour📍 Kekova underwater ruins Turkey📍 Dolichiste Kekova Lycian📍 Kaleköy Simena castle boat📍 Kaleköy village Kekova Turkey📍 Simena Byzantine fortress📍 Myra rock tombs Demre Turkey📍 Myra Lycian tombs necropolis📍 Demre Myra cliff tombs📍 Myra Roman theatre Demre📍 Myra theatre largest Lycia📍 Demre ancient theatre Turkey
1

The ancient city of Dolichiste was partially submerged in the 2nd century after an earthquake. From the boat deck, you'll see under 1 to 6 meters of water the walls of houses, staircases descending toward the sea, quays, and Roman cisterns. The area has been classified as a special protected zone since 1990: diving and swimming are prohibited. The ideal time is morning before 9am, when the water is perfectly still and the ruins stand out with surprising clarity. Polarized sunglasses make all the difference.

⏱ 60 min
2

Kaleköy ('castle village') is a village with no road access, reachable only by boat or trail. The Byzantine castle atop the hill dominates the entire Kekova bay. Inside the walls: the smallest known theater in Lycia. Lycian sarcophagi emerge everywhere among the houses and in the harbor water. Take time to climb to the castle - the 360° view of the site is exceptional, and the descent into the village offers a chance to appreciate the few small fish restaurants.

⏱ 60 min
3

Myra's tombs are among the best preserved in all of Lycia. Carved directly into the limestone cliff in the 4th and 3rd centuries BC, they imitate Lycian wooden house facades - some still bear traces of polychrome paintings. Several dozen tombs are layered on the wall, on four to five levels. The Lycian inscriptions and funerary reliefs make this site one of the most precious documents on Lycian civilization.

⏱ 60 min
4

This is Lycia's largest theater. It could accommodate around 11,000 spectators according to estimates. Built in Roman times and later enlarged, it's remarkably well preserved. From the upper tiers, the rock-cut tombs are visible just above.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

The entire day is spent by boat. Departure from Üçağız or Çayağzı (Demre) harbor. The circuit includes passing over the sunken city, a stop at Kaleköy, and several swimming bays (including Aquarium Bay and Karemlik Bay where sea turtles are often observed). Glass-bottom boats offer maximum visibility of the underwater ruins. Gulets offer lunch on board.

⏱ 300 min

Olympos

★★★☆☆
📍 Olympos treehouse Cirali📍 Cirali bungalow hotel📍 Olympos Antalya accommodation📍 Cirali pension orange grove📍 Olympos beachside guesthouse📍 Cirali Antalya Turkey hotel📍 Olympos ancient ruins Turkey📍 Olympos Lycian city forest📍 Olympos beach ruins Antalya📍 Yanartas Chimaera eternal flames Turkey📍 Chimaera fire Olympos📍 Yanartas Cirali night hike
1

Olympos is a Lycian city that, founded in the 2nd century BC, was one of the six cities with three votes in the Lycian League - the highest distinction. The ruins are scattered in dense vegetation on either side of the Akçay stream, which you cross on foot through the water to access the northern section. There's an ionic pronaos entrance gate dedicated to Marcus Aurelius (172-173 AD), a Lycian sarcophagus with a ship bas-relief (Captain Eudomus), a Byzantine basilica, and acropolis remains. The atmosphere is totally different from the concreted and signposted sites: here, you forge a path among ferns and carobs.

⏱ 120 min
2

Yanartaş ('burning rock' in Turkish) is the name of the site identified as ancient Mount Chimera. About twenty fissures in the limestone rock have been releasing non-biological methane for at least 2,500 years - ancient sailors used them as a natural lighthouse. Immediately beneath the flames, the ruins of a temple of Hephaestus (god of fire) dating from Roman times. Visit preferably at sunset or after dark: the flames, barely visible in full sun, glow strangely in the darkness. The climb from the parking lot takes 30 minutes on foot.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Olympos beach - technically Çıralı beach, 2 km away - is a long strip of fine pebbles at the stream's mouth. A few bungalows under orange trees border one side. The beach is also a nesting site for loggerhead turtles: sections are marked off in season. The water is clean and swimming pleasant.

⏱ 120 min

The nighttime visit is very different from the daytime one. The flames stand out sharply in the dark, with an oscillating light that constantly changes in intensity. Groups from Çıralı and Olympos organize evening excursions (departure around 9pm) with flashlights. It's the perfect time to brew tea on one of the flames - locals still do it today.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 10

Kemer

★★★★☆
📍 Kemer hotel Turkey📍 Kemer Antalya accommodation📍 Kemer boutique hotel📍 Kemer pension📍 Kemer Antalya Turkey📍 Kemer seaside hotel📍 Phaselis aqueduct Roman Turkey📍 Phaselis Roman ruins Kemer📍 Phaselis archaeological site Tekirova📍 Phaselis main colonnaded street📍 Phaselis Hadrian Gate 129 AD📍 Phaselis ancient city three harbours📍 Phaselis theatre Tahtali view📍 Phaselis Roman theatre Kemer📍 Phaselis ruins forest bays📍 Olympos Teleferik Tahtali cable car Turkey📍 Tahtali mountain summit 2365m📍 Olympos cable car Kemer Antalya
1

Phaselis's Roman aqueduct is the first thing you see arriving at the site. Its limestone arches rise among the pines, barely 50 meters past the entrance. Water came from a spring in the hill behind the north harbor and crossed the entire city to the agora. It's one of the best-preserved Roman hydraulic systems on the Mediterranean coast.

⏱ 30 min
2

Phaselis's grand avenue is a 10-meter-wide paved street linking the north harbor to the south harbor. It was lined with columns, shops, and fountains. In 129 AD, Emperor Hadrian visited the city and residents erected a triumphal gate at the main harbor entrance - Hadrian's Gate. Ships of 100 tons traded cedar wood and rose oil here, commodities that made Phaselis wealthy.

⏱ 45 min
3

Phaselis theater (2nd century AD, 1,500 to 2,000 seats) sits on a hillside above the central harbor. From its tiers, the view is striking: to the north, the summit of Tahtalı (2,365 m) dominates the entire landscape. It's the same mountain the Lycians called Olympos, which you can reach this afternoon. A secured wooden walkway provides access to the tiers.

⏱ 30 min
4

The Olympos cable car (Olympos Teleferik) links the lower station at 596 m to the summit in 10 minutes via a 4.3 km cable - one of Europe's longest. The arrival station is at 2,365 m. The view from the summit is 360° panoramic: the Turquoise Coast, Greek islands, and on clear days as far as Cyprus. In summer, clouds often fill the intermediate levels that the cable car passes through. Bring a jacket even in midsummer: it can be 15°C colder than below.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Phaselis's three coves are accessible from the archaeological site. The north harbor beach is the calmest; the south harbor beach (the former military cove) is the most beautiful - sandy bottom, translucent water. You swim among blocks of Roman quays visible under 1 to 2 meters of water. It's one of the most photogenic swims on the Turquoise Coast.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 11

Antalya

★★★★☆
📍 Antalya Kaleici hotel📍 Antalya old town boutique hotel📍 Antalya historic pension📍 Antalya harbour accommodation📍 Antalya Turkey hotel📍 Antalya Mediterranean coast hotel📍 Hadrian Gate Antalya📍 Uchkapilar Antalya📍 Antalya triumphal arch Turkey📍 Kaleici Antalya old town📍 Antalya historic quarter Turkey📍 Antalya Roman harbour marina📍 Yivli Minare Antalya Seljuk📍 Antalya grooved minaret Turkey📍 Alaaddin mosque Antalya📍 Antalya Archaeological Museum📍 Antalya museum Roman statues📍 Antalya muzesi Turkey
1

Hadrian's Gate is the only ancient passage still standing from Antalya's ramparts. It was erected in 130 AD on the occasion of Emperor Hadrian's visit by the local governor Quintus Veranius Nepos. Its three white marble arches, flanked by two towers from different periods (the Roman tower to the south, the Seljuk tower to the north rebuilt in the 13th century), constitute a two-thousand-year layering of history in a single monument. Entering Kaleiçi through this gate is the best possible start for exploring the quarter.

⏱ 30 min
2

Kaleiçi is a tangle of cobblestone streets mixing overhanging Ottoman houses, boutique hotels, Roman ruins, and mosques. Hıdırlık Tower (2nd century AD, 14 m), at the southwestern corner of the ramparts, overlooked the old harbor. Recent excavations have uncovered 100 meters of a columned Roman road linking the tower to Hadrian's Gate - an underground street visible from glass walkways being installed. The old Roman harbor, now a pleasure marina, is 5 minutes' walk.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Yivli Minare ('fluted minaret' in Turkish) was built around 1226-1227 under Seljuk Sultan Alaeddin Keykubad I. Its silhouette - eight engaged brick columns spiraling up 38 meters - is Antalya's symbol. It's on Turkey's UNESCO World Heritage preliminary list. The adjoining mosque is open to visitors outside prayer times.

⏱ 20 min
4

Antalya Museum is regularly cited as one of the world's ten best archaeological museums. Its collection of Roman statues - largely from the Perge excavations - is stunning. The gods hall displays deity statues (Hadrian, Artemis, Apollo, Tyche of Perge) of extraordinary technical refinement and preservation. The sarcophagus hall exhibits 2nd-century funerary chests whose bas-reliefs narrate the labors of Heracles. Allow at least two hours.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Antalya's old Roman harbor is now a marina surrounded by cafés and restaurants. Terraces look directly onto the boats and ramparts. It's the ideal place for the penultimate evening of the trip, over fresh fish meze and a glass of rakı.

⏱ 90 min

This cylindrical Roman tower from the 2nd century, 14 meters tall, stands at the southwestern tip of the ramparts in Karaalioğlu Park. It probably served as a lighthouse and watchtower. Sunset from this promontory, with the Gulf of Antalya and Taurus Mountains in the background, is one of the city's best.

⏱ 30 min

Stop 12

Antalya

★★★★☆
📍 Antalya airport hotel📍 Antalya AYT hotel📍 Antalya last night hotel📍 Antalya Kaleici stay📍 Antalya departure hotel Turkey📍 Düden waterfall sea cliffs Antalya📍 Lower Duden Falls Mediterranean📍 Duden Antalya waterfall Turkey📍 Perge stadium Roman Turkey📍 Perge ancient stadium 12000 seats📍 Perge Antalya archaeological site📍 Perge Hellenistic gates towers📍 Perge colonnaded street Roman📍 Perge Pamphylia ancient Turkey📍 Perge Roman baths ruins📍 Perge thermes Turkey📍 Perge archaeological park Antalya
1

The Lower Düden Falls are one of the few places in the world where a river plunges from a cliff directly into the sea. The Düden River descends from the Taurus Mountains and dives from a 40-meter precipice into the Mediterranean. The view from the cliff is vertiginous. From the sea, excursion boats pass within meters of the waterfall - the spray and roar are striking. You can also descend by stairway to the base of the cliff.

⏱ 45 min
2

Perge's stadium is the best-preserved Roman stadium in Turkey. Built in the 2nd century AD, it could accommodate about 12,000 spectators. The arched tiers are still largely standing, with their interior vaults that served as commercial galleries - one of history's first 'under the stands.' Climb to the top of the tribune to see the track axis along with the ramparts on the horizon.

⏱ 30 min
3

The two Hellenistic defensive towers (3rd century BC) form the heart of Perge's ramparts. In Roman times, their niches were filled with commemorative statues, several of which are now displayed in Antalya Museum. The colonnaded avenue traversing the city from north to south (500 m) was lined with shops, monumental fountains, and a central canal. Ruts worn by carts into the stone are still visible. Perge was one of Pamphylia's great cities: Saint Paul preached here in 46 AD during his first missionary journey.

⏱ 60 min
4

Perge's southern baths (1st-2nd centuries AD) are among Turkey's most extensive. Under the still-standing arches, you can distinguish the structure of different rooms (frigidarium, tepidarium, caldarium). Fragments of mosaics and marble cladding survive. Statues of exceptional quality - including the Venus of Perge and Artemis of Perge - were found here and are displayed in Antalya Museum.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Small boats depart from the base of the cliff to approach as close as possible to the falls. You'll pass under the spray, very often beneath a rainbow. It's a brief but memorable experience, different from all the other sites on the journey.

⏱ 60 min
