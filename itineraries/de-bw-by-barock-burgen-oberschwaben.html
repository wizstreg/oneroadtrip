Ulm

★★★★☆
1

768 spiral steps wind to the top of Ulm Minster, Germany's tallest church spire at 161.53 m. The viewing platform at 143 m commands views stretching to the Alps on clear days. Founded as a burgher church in 1377 and converted to Protestant worship in 1530, it survived the 1944 bombings unscathed. Church admission free, tower climb ticketed.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Fischerviertel, the old fishermen's and tanners' quarter, snakes along the Blau just before it meets the Danube. Half-timbered houses lean at improbable angles over cobbled lanes, and the Schiefes Haus ranks among the world's most tilted buildings. Five-minute walk from the Minster.

⏱ 30 min
3

Ulm's Stadtbibliothek, a glass pyramid designed by Gottfried Böhm, deliberately contrasts with the Gothic Minster it adjoins. Bold contemporary architecture in a city rebuilt after wartime destruction. Free admission.

⏱ 15 min

Activities

Sample Maultaschen at a city-center Gaststätte: these hefty Swabian dumplings filled with meat and spinach are served in broth or pan-fried. Legend has it monks invented them to hide meat during Lent.

⏱ 30 min
$$$★10Exklusive Penthouse Wohnung - XXL Terrasse - Parkplatz in Neu-Ulm

Kloster Wiblingen

★★★★☆
1

Thirty-two marble columns, a balustrade that ripples like a wave, and a ceiling painted by Franz Georg Hermann: the Bibliothekssaal at Wiblingen Abbey is a visual shock. This 23-meter-long rococo salon, built between 1740 and 1750, soars through two stories. The inscription above the door promises 'all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge.'

⏱ 45 min
2

The Benedictine abbey, founded in 1093, was secularized in 1806. Its books were scattered between Munich and Stuttgart. Only about sixty original volumes remain in the hall today. The small adjoining museum recounts this turbulent history. Around 90,000 visitors annually.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Pair your visit with the adjacent Klosterkirche: its ceiling frescoes, painted in late baroque style, rank among Württemberg's most colorful. The complex forms part of the Upper Swabian Baroque Route.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 35min

Blaubeuren

★★☆☆☆
1

The Blautopf, a 21-meter-deep karst spring, glows an otherworldly turquoise blue. Germany's second-most powerful karst spring after the Aachtopf, it discharges an average of 2,300 liters per second. Beneath the surface, a cave system explored for more than 18 km. Open access, free, year-round.

⏱ 30 min
2

The legend of the Schöne Lau tells of a mermaid dwelling at the bottom of the Blautopf who had to relearn how to laugh to break a curse. The poet Eduard Mörike popularized this tale in 1853. Local lore claims she still appears on certain days.

⏱ 10 min
3

Blaubeuren Abbey, a Benedictine monastery on the spring's edge, houses a late Gothic altarpiece and the Urgeschichtliches Museum (URMU), devoted to prehistoric finds from nearby caves. Ivory figurines carved from mammoth tusk 40,000 years ago are on display.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take coffee at Café am Blautopf with a direct view of the spring. Spring, when snowmelt swells the flow, delivers the most intense blue. Blaubeuren's half-timbered old town takes about half an hour to explore.

⏱ 30 min
$$$★9.6Sandra
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 50min

Lichtenstein

★★★★☆
1

Dubbed the 'Fairy-tale Castle of Württemberg,' Lichtenstein Castle perches at 817 m above Honau village. Count Wilhelm von Württemberg commissioned it after reading Wilhelm Hauff's novel in 1826. The neo-Gothic castle, completed in 1842, remains the private property of the ducal family of Urach.

⏱ 45 min
2

The 30-minute guided tour winds through period-furnished salons and concludes on a balcony with vertiginous views over the Echaz valley. The 2009 film Dornröschen was shot here. Closed January–February.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Combine your visit with the nearby Nebelhöhle: 141 steps descend into this stalactite cave of the Swabian Alb. Open April to November. The Maultaschenwirt at the cave entrance serves homemade Maultaschen.

⏱ 45 min
$$★9.7Historisches Postamt Lichtenstein Sachsen
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Sonnenbuehl

★★☆☆☆
1

The Bärenhöhle at Sonnenbühl takes its name from the cave bear skeletons discovered inside. Limestone formations sculpt spectacular shapes in this Swabian Alb cave. Guided tour about 30 minutes.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Swabian Alb plateau around Sonnenbühl offers easy hikes with panoramas over valleys below. The UNESCO-recognized Swabian Alb Biosphere Reserve begins here.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Walk the 'Hochgehträumt' trail linking Lichtenstein Castle to the Nebelhöhle over 10.9 km, with viewpoints overlooking the Albtrauf escarpment. Easy difficulty, moderate elevation gain.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9Prestige Apartments - Urban Lofts Reutlingen
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 35min

Bisingen

★★☆☆☆
1

Hohenzollern Castle commands its 855-meter Zeugenberg, an outlying hill of the Swabian Alb. Ancestral seat of the dynasty that gave Prussia its kings and Germany its emperors, the present castle was rebuilt between 1850 and 1867 by Friedrich August Stüler in romantic neo-Gothic style. The Schatzkammer displays Wilhelm II's crown, encrusted with diamonds.

⏱ 75 min
2

In clear weather, views reach 100 km from the ramparts. The 15th-century Gothic Michaelskapelle is the only surviving medieval element. Around 350,000 visitors annually. Adult admission 26 euros, shuttle from parking lot available.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

The castle's Biergarten, in the inner courtyard, serves regional specialties with Zollerberg views. In winter, the castle's Weihnachtsmarkt ranks among the region's most popular.

⏱ 30 min
$$$★9.7Ferienwohnung Natur
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 50min

Sigmaringen

★★★☆☆
1

From the Danube bridge, the mass of Sigmaringen Castle crushes the limestone cliff. Seat of the Hohenzollern-Sigmaringen line since 1535, it's Germany's second-largest city palace. The Waffenhalle displays some 3,000 pieces, one of Europe's largest private arms collections. Audioguides available in multiple languages.

⏱ 75 min
2

In 1944–1945, the castle briefly served as seat of the Vichy government in exile. Louis-Ferdinand Céline described the atmosphere in 'Castle to Castle.' The Marstallmuseum, in the stables, presents 17 princely carriages and sleighs.

⏱ 30 min
3

Stroll downstream along the Danube from the castle: the limestone cliffs of the Upper Danube Valley begin here, offering striking panoramas. The Naturpark Obere Donau ranks among Germany's most beautiful.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

In the evening, the Biergarten terrace facing the illuminated castle is one of the town's finest spots. The castle still belongs to the Hohenzollern family, who maintain partial residence.

⏱ 20 min
$$$★9.6Klosterblick
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Beuron

★★★☆☆
1

Attend Sunday mass at Beuron Archabbey and the monks' Gregorian chant transports you to another century. This archabbey, founded in 1077, remains an active center of monastic life. The Beuron Art School, born here in the 19th century, influenced European sacred art with a geometric style inspired by ancient Egypt.

⏱ 40 min
2

The Danube Valley cliffs around Beuron form a spectacular canyon. The trail following the Danube toward Sigmaringen or Tuttlingen ranks among Baden-Württemberg's finest hiking routes.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Paddle the Danube by canoe between Beuron and Sigmaringen: about 2 hours of relaxed paddling between limestone walls. Several Beuron outfitters offer equipment and return shuttle.

⏱ 120 min
$$$★10Schöne Ferienwohnung
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 1h05

Zwiefalten

★★☆☆☆
1

Push open the Zwiefalten Minster door and brace yourself for impact: gilded stucco, Franz Joseph Spiegler frescoes covering the entire ceiling length, monumental high altar. The Benedictine abbey, founded in 1089, was secularized in 1803. The austere exterior betrays nothing.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Stop at the Lautertal overlook on the route from Beuron: the view plunges into the sunken valley. The Alb plateau crosses through quiet forest roads, free of traffic.

⏱ 15 min
$$$★10Ferienwohnung alb.zeit
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 10min

Hayingen

★★☆☆☆
1

Germany's only cave navigable by boat: the Wimsener Höhle is explored by rowboat on an underground river that gushes from a limestone cliff. The journey lasts about ten minutes in total silence, broken only by the lapping of water. Open April to October.

⏱ 20 min
$$$★10Ferienwohnung alb.zeit
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 35min

Bad Schussenried

★★☆☆☆
1

Second baroque library of the journey: the Bibliothekssaal at Schussenried Abbey rivals Wiblingen's. Painter Franz Georg Hermann and sculptor Fidelis Sporer created another rococo summit here. The great abbeys of Upper Swabia competed for prestige through their libraries.

⏱ 40 min
2

The spa center at Bad Schussenried offers baths and a cure park. The small town provides a quiet stop between abbeys. The Bierkrugmuseum, dedicated to beer steins, is a local curiosity.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

You're at the heart of the Upper Swabian Baroque Route, a tourist trail linking some thirty baroque churches and monasteries between Ulm and Lake Constance. Map available from the tourist office.

⏱ 10 min
$$$★9.4Hotel Arthus
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min

Steinhausen An Der Rottum

★★★★★
1

Steinhausen claims the title of 'world's most beautiful village church.' The Wallfahrtskirche, built between 1728 and 1733 by Dominikus Zimmermann (the same architect as Bavaria's Wieskirche), concentrates a rare density of decoration into an oval space. Johann Baptist Zimmermann's frescoes cover the entire ceiling.

⏱ 30 min
2

The village itself comprises just a handful of houses around the church. The contrast between the place's modesty and the interior's exuberance creates the entire effect. Free admission, donation box available.

⏱ 10 min
$$$★9.9Ferienhof Greuthof
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min

Ravensburg

★★★☆☆
1

Ravensburg has preserved its medieval profile intact: towers, gates, and ramparts frame a merchant town prosperous since the 14th century. The Mehlsack, a 51-meter white tower, dominates the skyline. Climb it for views across Lake Constance to the Alps.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Veitsburg, a former Guelph fortress above the town, offers 360° panoramas and a Biergarten popular with locals. The Museum Ravensburger, devoted to the board game publisher, appeals to families.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Museum Humpis-Quartier occupies a complex of 15th-century patrician houses in the center. It traces Ravensburg's history as a member of the Great Ravensburg Trading Society, a commercial network that rivaled the Hanseatic League.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Stroll down Marktstraße under the arcades and sample a Seele, an elongated bread roll typical to Swabia, crusty and studded with salt and caraway. They're found almost exclusively in Upper Swabia.

⏱ 20 min
$$$★9Hotel & Restaurant "Am Obstgarten"
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min

Weingarten

★★★☆☆
1

With 6,666 pipes, the Gabler organ in Weingarten Basilica ranks among Germany's largest baroque instruments. Built in 1750, it resonates through a 102-meter-long nave locals call the 'Swabian St. Peter's.' The ceiling frescoes are by Cosmas Damian Asam.

⏱ 45 min
2

Every year on the Friday after Ascension, Weingarten's Blutritt gathers horsemen from across Swabia for an equestrian procession honoring a relic of Christ's Blood. It's Europe's largest mounted procession.

⏱ 15 min

Activities

Weingarten is 3 km from Ravensburg. The two towns complement each other well in a single day. The Alamannenmuseum, dedicated to the Alemanni of the early Middle Ages, displays remarkable funerary finds.

⏱ 20 min
$$$★9.5Boho vibes between mountains and lakes
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Salem

★★★★☆
1

Salem Abbey, a former Cistercian monastery founded in 1134, is one of southern Germany's largest monastic complexes. After the 1803 secularization, the Margraves of Baden made it their residence. Salem boarding school, one of Europe's most prestigious, occupies part of the buildings.

⏱ 45 min
2

The rococo stuccowork by Johann Georg Dirr in the palace salons displays exceptional finesse. The Feuerwehrmuseum, installed in the former stables, traces firefighting history with a collection of vintage vehicles. Combined ticket available.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Salem's park and gardens are freely accessible. A craft market is regularly held in the courtyard. Salem lies midway between Ravensburg and Lake Constance.

⏱ 20 min
$$$★9.3RIVA - Das Hotel am Bodensee
🚗Drive to next stop: 12 km · 15min

Uhldingen Muehlhofen

★★★★☆
1

Birnau Basilica overlooks Lake Constance from a vine-planted hillside. Completed in 1750, it concentrates the finest of Swabian rococo: Joseph Anton Feuchtmayer's stuccowork and Gottfried Bernhard Göz's frescoes. The Honigschlecker, a putto licking honey, is the most photographed figure of the entire Baroque Route.

⏱ 30 min
2

From Birnau's forecourt, the view plunges to Lake Constance and the Swiss Alps beyond. The vineyard terraces around the church produce wine sold at the monastery shop. Free admission.

⏱ 10 min

Activities

The Pfahlbauten at Uhldingen, a reconstructed lake-dwelling village with UNESCO status, is a few minutes' drive away. The Bronze Age stilt houses offer family-friendly visits.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.9Bodenseepanorama Oberuhldingen
🚗Drive to next stop: 12 km · 15min

Ueberlingen

★★★☆☆
1

Überlingen closes the journey on the shores of Lake Constance. The medieval old town slopes gently toward the lake. The Münster St. Nikolaus houses a limewood altarpiece carved by Jörg Zürn in 1613, considered among the finest late Gothic works in Germany.

⏱ 30 min
2

Überlingen's Stadtgärten, terraced gardens facing the lake, hosted the 2021 Landesgartenschau. The cactus collections and rampart gardens merit a stroll. Modest admission fee.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Städtisches Museum, housed in the 1462 Reichlin-von-Meldegg-Haus, presents a Renaissance ballroom with painted ceiling and regional art collections. The building itself is a monument.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Finish with a swim in Lake Constance from the Therme Überlingen or the public beaches below the promenade. The view across the lake to the Swiss Alps provides the perfect endpoint for the road trip.

⏱ 30 min
