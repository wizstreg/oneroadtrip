📆 Best period

December, January, February, March, April

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 12 and 16 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (December-May): Peak tourist season with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures (26-32°C), and excellent conditions for island hopping and beach activities. Highest prices and crowds.

Cool Dry Season (December-February): Most comfortable temperatures and lowest humidity. Perfect weather for all activities, though expect highest accommodation rates and tourist numbers.

Hot Dry Season (March-May): Hottest temperatures but still dry conditions. Good for beach activities, though midday heat can be intense. Shoulder season pricing.

Rainy Season (June-November): Monsoon season with typhoon risk, but lower prices and fewer tourists. Some islands remain accessible, afternoon rains common.

🌍 What to discover in PH?

Archipelago paradise with pristine beaches, vibrant culture, and incredible diversity. Experience traditional festivals, beautiful islands, and warm Filipino hospitality. The country offers cultural authenticity with natural beauty and adventure activities. Philippines provides diverse experiences with cultural richness and natural wonders.

📋 Important notes

Comprehensive circuit covering three distinct regions: Northern Luzon (rice terraces), Palawan (lagoons), and the Visayas (reefs and waterfalls). Requires 5-6 domestic flights. Fast-paced itinerary — designed for travelers who want to see the maximum in three weeks.

⚠️ SAFETY NOTE: The Philippines is generally safe in main tourist areas, though avoid southern Mindanao and Sulu archipelago due to terrorism and kidnapping risks. Stick to established tourist destinations like Luzon, Visayas, and northern Mindanao. Check current government advisories and use reputable transportation and accommodation.

Manila

Chaotic yet endearing megacity straddling colonial Intramuros and creative districts

★★★★★
📍 Intramuros Manila📍 Fort Santiago Manila📍 San Agustin Church Manila📍 Rizal Park Manila
1

Manila is a city you either embrace or flee — loud, dense, chaotic, yet full of surprises. Intramuros, the walled city fortified by the Spanish in the 16th century, makes the best starting point. Stone walls encircle a district of quiet alleys, churches, and shaded courtyards — an island of calm within the megacity.

⏱ 40 min
2

Fort Santiago, at the entrance to Intramuros, served as the Spanish citadel and later as a Japanese prison during the war. José Rizal, the Philippine national hero, was imprisoned here before his execution in 1896. The museum traces his final hours — his writings, his footsteps marked from the cell to the execution site.

⏱ 50 min
3

San Agustin Church, built in 1607, is the oldest stone church in the Philippines and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The interior is breathtaking: trompe-l'oeil ceilings, crystal chandeliers, and granite floors imported from China. It survived earthquakes, typhoons, and the 1945 bombardment.

⏱ 40 min
4

Rizal Park (Luneta Park) stretches along Manila Bay. This is where Rizal was executed by firing squad in 1896 — a granite monument marks the spot. Filipinos come here to jog in the morning, picnic with family on weekends, and watch the sunset in the evening.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

Calesa ride through Intramuros — a horse-drawn carriage tour along the cobbled streets. Touristy but pleasant for covering the district without walking in the heat. Negotiate the price before you climb aboard.

⏱ 45 min

Filipino dinner: adobo (chicken or pork braised in vinegar and soy), sinigang (sour tamarind soup), and halo-halo (shaved ice dessert with beans, jellies, and condensed milk). Halo-halo looks bizarre at first glance and becomes addictive after the first spoonful.

Manila

Chaotic yet endearing megacity straddling colonial Intramuros and creative districts

★★★★★
📍 Binondo Chinatown Manila📍 Quiapo Church Manila📍 National Museum Manila📍 Makati Manila
1

Binondo, Manila's Chinatown, is the oldest Chinatown in the world — founded in 1594. The narrow streets overflow with restaurants, apothecaries, temples, and jewelry shops. Taste the siopao (steamed buns), lumpia (Filipino spring rolls), and pancit canton (stir-fried noodles).

⏱ 60 min
2

Quiapo Church houses the Black Nazarene, a dark wooden statue of Christ carrying the cross. On January 9th, millions of barefoot devotees pull the statue through the streets in one of the world's largest religious processions. Outside the festival, the church remains a place of intense devotion.

⏱ 35 min
3

The National Museum of Fine Arts, in a neoclassical building facing Rizal Park, displays Juan Luna's Spoliarium — a monumental 7-by-4-meter canvas that won the gold medal at the 1884 Paris Salon. Admission is free.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

In the evening, explore Poblacion in Makati — the former working-class neighborhood turned nightlife hub. Rooftop bars, fusion restaurants, and art galleries in converted garages. The vibe is young and creative.

⏱ 75 min

Sunset over Manila Bay from Rizal Boulevard or a Makati rooftop bar. Manila's sky takes on spectacular orange hues — pollution has at least this advantage.

Banaue

Gateway to the UNESCO rice terraces in the Ifugao cordillera mountains

★★★★☆
📍 Banaue Rice Terraces📍 Banaue Viewpoint Ifugao📍 Banaue Museum Ifugao
1

The journey from Manila to Banaue is an adventure in itself — a 9-hour overnight bus through the Luzon cordillera. The bus leaves Manila in the evening and arrives in Banaue at dawn, in Ifugao province. You wake up at altitude, surrounded by green mountains and mist.

⏱ 25 min
2

The Banaue Rice Terraces have been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1995. Carved into the mountain slopes by the Ifugao people over 2,000 years ago, they rise 1,500 meters up the hillsides. The earth and stone walls, maintained by hand, represent an engineering feat that has endured for millennia.

⏱ 50 min
3

Banaue's main viewpoint, five minutes from town by tricycle, offers the classic panoramic view of the terraces. Early morning, when the rice paddies are shrouded in mist, the landscape feels otherworldly. If the terraces are flooded (planting season, February-March), the sky's reflection in the water adds another dimension.

⏱ 40 min
4

Banaue's small museum displays Ifugao farming tools, woven textiles, and wooden sculptures (bulul, guardians of rice granaries). It's modest but instructive for understanding the culture that created these terraces.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Rest and acclimatization after the night bus. Banaue sits at 1,200 meters elevation — the air is cool morning and evening (15-20°C), a welcome contrast after Manila's heat. Grab a coffee at one of the small downtown restaurants.

⏱ 45 min

Ifugao dinner: red rice from the terraces (local variety with a nutty taste) accompanied by pinikpikan — chicken broth prepared using the traditional method. Ifugao cuisine is simple and comforting.

Batad

Amphitheater of terraced rice paddies, roadless village accessible only on foot

★★★★☆
📍 Batad Rice Terraces Ifugao📍 Tappiya Falls Batad📍 Batad Village Ifugao
1

Batad is the hidden jewel of the rice terraces — a natural amphitheater of terraces forming a semicircle, more spectacular and better preserved than Banaue. Access is via a 45-minute downhill trail from the saddle point (jeepney drop-off). The path crosses rice paddies where farmers work knee-deep in mud.

⏱ 60 min
2

Batad village is a handful of wooden and corrugated iron houses clinging to the slope. No road, no cars — everything's done on foot. Ifugao families have lived in the same thatch-roofed houses for generations. Some offer homestays: a mat on the floor, a meal of red rice and vegetables, and mountain silence.

⏱ 45 min
3

Tappiya Falls, accessible from Batad via a steep 40-minute descent (and an equally steep ascent — prepare your legs), drops 21 meters into a cold-water pool amid the jungle. The swim after the hike is pure relief.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

Rice terrace trek with a local guide. The trail winds between terraces, crosses stone walls, and follows ancient irrigation channels. The guide explains the hydraulic system, planting rituals, and the contemporary challenges facing Ifugao farmers.

⏱ 180 min

Quiet evening in Batad. No wifi (or barely), no bars, no noise — just roosters crowing, water murmuring through the channels, and stars above the terraces.

⏱ 30 min

Climb back from Batad to the saddle point (45 minutes of steep ascent in the morning — leave early to avoid the heat), then jeepney to Banaue. If you have energy, detour to the Hapao rice terraces in Hungduan, less known but equally beautiful — and nearly deserted.

Last look at the terraces from Banaue viewpoint in late afternoon. The low-angle light carves shadows into the terraces — it's the best time for photos.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Meeting with an Ifugao family if your guide can arrange it. The elders share stories of life in the rice fields, cañao rituals (community festivals), and the fragile balance between tradition and modernity. Young people leave for Manila, and maintaining the terraces requires hands.

⏱ 60 min

Overnight bus back to Manila (departs late afternoon, arrives early morning). You'll connect directly to your flight to Palawan.

Coron

Fishing port turned world-class dive destination among lagoons and war wrecks

★★★☆☆
📍 Kayangan Lake Coron📍 Twin Lagoon Coron📍 Barracuda Lake Coron📍 Épaves Coron
1

Full-day bangka tour: Kayangan Lake (emerald lake ringed by cliffs — Coron's postcard shot), Twin Lagoon (two pools connected by a passage through the rock), Barracuda Lake (thermocline water, warm at depth). Three exceptional sites in one day.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Skeleton Wreck, a Japanese WWII shipwreck lying at 5 meters depth, is visible while snorkeling. The hull overgrown with coral attracts schools of fish. In 1944, American aviation sank a dozen Japanese ships here — Coron is now one of the world's best wreck diving destinations.

⏱ 45 min
3

Maquinit Hot Springs in late afternoon — saltwater pools at 38°C, tucked among rocks at the sea's edge. The sunset from the pools is perfect.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

Snorkeling at Coral Garden between stops. The corals are intact and the variety of tropical fish impressive.

⏱ 60 min

Lunch on the bangka between sites — rice, grilled fish, fruit. The boatmen cook on a small grill at the stern.

General Luna

★★★★☆
📍 General Luna Siargao📍 Cloud 9 Siargao📍 Tourism Road Siargao

Flight from Dumaguete or Cebu to Siargao (via Manila or Cebu — connections vary, check ahead). Siargao is the Philippines' surf capital, but it's also a beautiful island for non-surfers: lagoons, mangroves, deserted islets.

If you surf, this is the moment. Cloud 9 for advanced, neighboring beaches (Jacking Horse, Stimpy's) for intermediates. Surf lessons are offered everywhere for beginners — the inside waves are mellow and accessible.

Late afternoon surf session or free swim. Siargao's pace is slow — no one will rush you.
