✨ Highlights
  • Vale do Pati, voted among the world's best treks: 3 days in the valleys, family homestays, no cell phone signal
  • Cachoeira da Fumaça, 340-380 m free fall, one of Brazil's highest waterfalls and the tallest easily accessible, named for the smoke effect of the spray
  • Morro do Pai Inácio, 1120 m, the park's postcard: tabletop plateau offering 360° panorama at sunset
  • Poço Azul and Poço Encantado, underground lakes with fluorescent turquoise-blue waters (light effect September-March)
  • Igatu, "Brazil's Machu Picchu": stone village from the 19th-century garimpeiro era
  • Lençóis, historic diamond capital (1845), colorful colonial center classified as Brazilian national heritage
📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September, October

⏳ How many days

8 days suggested. 7 days would technically be enough, but we prefer to let each stop breathe.

☀️ Weather

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 Discover

Carnival energy, Amazon rainforest, and pristine beaches create the ultimate South American adventure. Experience vibrant cities, incredible biodiversity, and passionate culture from Rio's beaches to Pantanal wetlands. Samba music, capoeira martial arts, and incredible football culture showcase national spirit. Brazil offers unparalleled natural wonders with infectious cultural energy.

📋 Notes

Arrival and departure via Salvador (international flights via GRU or direct from Europe). Salvador → Lençóis: 415 km, 6h via BR-324 then BR-242, significantly faster by Azul regional flight (1h, 3 weekly rotations). All excursions depart from Lençóis or Vale do Capão. Pack good hiking boots, rain poncho, swimsuit and waterproof bag. Climate: dry season May to September, ideal for trekking. Poços Azul and Encantado: blue light effect only September to March (midday sun striking the cave).

Salvador

★★★★★
1

Arrive in Salvador de Bahia via Luís Eduardo Magalhães International Airport. Transfer to Pelourinho, 30 km, 45 min. Drop your bags at a pousada in the historic center. The afternoon is enough for your first immersion into Brazil's Afro-Brazilian capital.

⏱ 60 min
2

Pelourinho, a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1985, was the central square of Brazil's first capital (1549-1763) from the 16th to 18th centuries. Its name recalls the whipping post where slaves were punished. The restored pastel facades, Igreja Nossa Senhora do Rosário dos Pretos and Praça Terreiro de Jesus form South America's most cohesive colonial ensemble.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Igreja e Convento de São Francisco ranks among the world's baroque masterpieces. Austere exterior facade, interior entirely covered with 800 kilos of gold leaf on carved wood, Portuguese azulejo tiles lining the cloister walls. Built between 1708 and 1723 by Franciscan friars. Admission 10 BRL.

⏱ 60 min
4

Take the Elevador Lacerda, an Art Deco elevator from 1873 that connects the upper city to the lower city in 30 seconds (72 m drop). Obligatory postcard shot. Fare 1 BRL since the February 2025 reopening (free with SalvadorCard). Once down, head to Mercado Modelo for handicrafts (capoeira, berimbaus, hammocks) and views over Baía de Todos os Santos.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Dine in Pelourinho at a Bahian restaurant. Signature dishes: moqueca de peixe (fish in coconut milk and dendê oil), acarajé (black-eyed pea fritters fried in palm oil), vatapá. Uauá or Axego on Rua João de Deus are local favorites that avoid the tourist traps.

⏱ 120 min

On Tuesday evenings, catch free capoeira and Afro-percussion shows on Praça Pedro Archanjo. Groups like Olodum and Filhos de Gandhy perform regularly. Unique atmosphere, but keep an eye on your belongings. Avoid isolated alleys at night.

⏱ 90 min

Lencois

★★★☆☆
1

Depart Salvador → Lençóis. The Azul flight (3 weekly rotations, 1h) is highly recommended: 6h by bus via BR-242 in comparison. Horácio de Matos Airport, 20 km from downtown Lençóis, 30 min transfer.

⏱ 30 min
2

Lençóis, founded in 1845, was the capital of Brazil's diamond boom. Its name comes from the white tents of garimpeiros seen from the hilltops, which resembled sheets (lençóis). Population 10,000, but the town was Bahia's third-largest at its peak. It has preserved its colonial street plan and multicolored architectural heritage.

⏱ 60 min
3

Stroll along Rua das Pedras and Praça Otaviano Alves. Low pastel-facade houses, artisan workshops, former diamond merchants' mansions. The old French vice-consulate (1873), now the Subprefeitura, recalls when the town dealt directly with European buyers. Stop by the Museu do Garimpo for context.

⏱ 60 min
4

Afternoon at Rio Serrano, accessible on foot from the center (20 min). Natural pools carved into red sandstone, polished stone slides, 2-3 m waterfalls. The water takes on a characteristic amber tint from plant tannins. Perfect swimming spot to recover from the journey.

⏱ 120 min
5

Continue upstream to Ribeirão do Meio, famous for its 10-meter natural waterslide. The rock polished by centuries of erosion allows a cushioned slide into a large pool. Watch out for collisions with other swimmers on busy days. Municipal park entrance 5 BRL.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Dine on Rua das Pedras, the pedestrian shopping street in the center. Neco's serves a reimagined moqueca chapadeira (freshwater fish), Lampião offers a traditional Bahian menu. Count on 60-90 BRL per person with drinks.

⏱ 90 min

Book your Vale do Pati trek for D5-D7 with a trusted agency: Chapada Adventure, Raízes da Chapada, Lençóis Trekking, Extrema Expedições. A briefing the day before is typically scheduled. Count on 1800-2500 BRL for 3 days all-inclusive (guide, family homestay accommodation, meals).

⏱ 60 min

Lencois

★★★☆☆
1

Big day as a classic "caves and Pai Inácio tour," the essential must-do excursion. Depart 8:30 AM from Lençóis by van with an agency. Route of 150 km total by car, 6 km of walking spread throughout the day. Return around 6:30 PM. About 200 BRL all-inclusive with lunch.

⏱ 30 min
2

First stop at Cachoeira do Mosquito, 40 min from Lençóis. Short walk (20 min one-way) to a 25 m waterfall framed by ochre rocks. Swimming in the natural pool below. Then 800 m rocky trail to Poço do Diabo, another waterfall in a more dramatic canyon. Deep natural pool.

⏱ 120 min
3

Gruta da Lapa Doce, 80 km west, is Brazil's third-largest cave (42 km mapped, 850 m visitable). The main chamber rises 70 m high. Stalactites, stalagmites, columns, calcite curtains, cathedral formations. Visit with guide and mandatory headlamp (45 min).

⏱ 90 min
4

Fazenda Pratinha, a neighboring private property, features Gruta Azul (blue light reflecting the crystal-clear water at 2:30 PM in dry season) and Gruta da Pratinha, where a snorkeling float (40 BRL supplement) lets you explore 150 m underground. Transparent waters populated by small fish and bat nests on the ceiling.

⏱ 120 min
5

Finish at Morro do Pai Inácio. 20 min ascent, 250 m elevation gain on rocky trail. The tabletop plateau at 1120 m altitude offers 360° panorama over Serra do Sincorá, Morro do Camelo and Morrão. The low-angle sunset light sets the orange cliffs ablaze. Absolute Chapada postcard.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Lunch included in the tour, at Fazenda Pratinha. Simple but generous Mineiro-Bahian buffet (55-70 BRL). Wood-fired chicken, rice and beans, fresh salads. Good mid-route break.

⏱ 75 min

Return to Lençóis around 6:30 PM. Dinner at a downtown restaurant: Cozinha Aberta (chef who worked in São Paulo, revisited chapadeira cuisine) or the classic Picanha na Praça for grilled meat. Quiet evening, prep gear for D4.

⏱ 90 min

Lencois

★★★☆☆
1

Second major excursion: Poço Azul, Poço Encantado and Igatu village. Full day, 286 km of road, little walking but lots of driving. Depart 8 AM, return 5 PM. About 250 BRL with lunch and admission fees.

⏱ 30 min
2

Poço Encantado, 150 km from Lençóis in the municipality of Itaetê, is a limestone cavity sheltering an underground lake 61 m deep. From September to March, between 10:30 AM and 1 PM, sunlight passes through a natural crack and illuminates the water in fluorescent cobalt blue. Swimming strictly forbidden to preserve the site.

⏱ 60 min
3

Poço Azul, 20 km from Poço Encantado, shares the same light phenomenon with one major advantage: swimming is allowed. Floating with snorkel mask, mandatory vest, in 22-24°C water of absolute transparency. The golden noon light transforms the experience into a golden underwater ballet.

⏱ 75 min
4

On the return, detour to Igatu, nicknamed "Brazil's Machu Picchu." Dry-stone village built in the 19th century by garimpeiros. Ruins of houses right in the village, picturesque cemetery, Museu do Garimpo installed in a former miner's house. Current population: 350 inhabitants.

⏱ 90 min
5

In Igatu itself, the Ruínas do Garimpo, 15 min walk from the center, are the entirely abandoned former mining quarter. Dry-stone walls erected without mortar, remains of sorting workshops, cactus-lined paths. Desert-like and silent atmosphere, feels like a Western movie set.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Lunch included at Igatu in one of the simple downtown restaurants. Flor de Açucena serves typical family cooking: feijoada, galinha caipira, homemade sweets (guava, banana, umbu preserves). Stone setting, warm atmosphere.

⏱ 75 min

Return to Lençóis in the evening. Last evening before the trek: light dinner, early to bed, pack backpack for 3 days. The agency usually provides the list: technical clothing, rain poncho, headlamp, 2-3 liters of water, sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent.

⏱ 60 min

Vale Do Pati

★★★★★
1

First day of Vale do Pati trek. Wake 5 AM, 1h30 transfer to the village of Guiné (Palmeiras municipality), main starting point. Briefing with guide: safety, route, eco-responsibility. No cell phone signal for the next 3 days.

⏱ 60 min
2

Vale do Pati is regularly ranked among the world's 10 best treks. This isolated valley, formed by the Rio Pati between Palmeiras, Andaraí and Mucugê, is accessible only on foot. Eleven families still live here today (400 at the garimpeiro peak), welcoming trekkers in their family guesthouses since 1997.

⏱ 30 min
3

Trail starts via Aleixos: 1h sustained climb (300 m elevation gain). At the summit, brutal panoramic opening onto the valley. Crossing the Gerais do Rio Preto, high-altitude plateau covered with candombás (endemic plants), small streams forded. Rocky terrain, well-marked trail.

⏱ 180 min
4

Descent toward the Mirante do Vale do Pati. Plunging view over the valley framed by tabletop serras. The villages (Igrejinha, Campo das Flores, Ruinha) appear as white dots in the forest. You understand why the site is called a "hidden green utopia" by trekkers.

⏱ 45 min
5

Final descent of 1h30, 500 m negative elevation, steep and rocky trail, toward the hamlet of Igrejinha. Arrival in late afternoon at Seu Wilson and Maria's, pioneers of valley hospitality since 1997. Simple room, decent bed, cold shower, solar electricity 3 hours in the evening.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Dinner as a family at Seu Wilson's. Rice, black beans, dried beef (carne-de-sol), vegetables from the garden, doces de leite for dessert. Wood-fired cooking. Eat at a communal table with other trekkers. Chatting, singing, guitar. Unique atmosphere.

⏱ 90 min

Early to bed (9 PM), starry night without light pollution. Milky Way visible to the naked eye. Temperatures can drop below 15°C in June-July: wool and down recommended. Natural wake-up to roosters and birds at 6 AM.

⏱ 60 min

Vale Do Pati

★★★★★
1

Second trek day, the most spectacular. Depart 7 AM with picnic prepared by Maria. Head for Morro do Castelo, a 1300 m rocky peak visible from across the valley. Traverse a short cave by headlamp before the final climb.

⏱ 60 min
2

Morro do Castelo peaks at 1300 m. 360° panorama over the tabletop serras, the caatinga below, sometimes even Cachoeira da Fumaça in the distance on clear days. One of the park's best observation points. Summit picnic: pão, queijo, rapadura, fruit.

⏱ 90 min
3

Descent toward Cachoeirão, Brazil's second-highest waterfall (280 m). Observation from the top of the canyon, 260 m sheer cliff. In the rainy season, over 20 distinct water streams fall into the mist. The morning rainbows are legendary. No swimming possible at this spot.

⏱ 90 min
4

Return via alternate trail through Cachoeira dos Funis and Poço da Árvore. 50 m waterfall in three tiers, deep pool where swimming is allowed. Water at 18°C, bracing but regenerating after the effort. Lunch at the pool's edge.

⏱ 120 min
5

Return to Seu Wilson's in late afternoon, 1h30 walk. Cold shower (or icy in June-July), siesta, reading. Some try the neighboring families' tabuleiro for fresh rapadura. The valley's total silence is one of the stay's most striking elements.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Simple and hearty dinner: hot soup, galinha caipira (free-range chicken stew), rice, corn angu, cabbage salad. Many trekkers linger around the table: the absence of screens recreates forgotten conviviality. Local artisanal cachaça as digestif.

⏱ 90 min

Vale Do Capao

★★☆☆☆
1

Last trek day, exit via Vale do Capão. Wake 5:30 AM, full breakfast (pão de queijo, eggs, coffee, fruit) then farewell to Seu Wilson. Long day: 6h walking, 700 m cumulative elevation, varied landscapes.

⏱ 60 min
2

Sustained 2h climb toward Gerais do Vieira, high-altitude plateau (1200-1400 m). Low vegetation, candombás and endemic orchids. Former garimpeiro path, recognizable by stone cairns (mundéus) marking the route. Mandatory breathing break and hydration at the summit.

⏱ 150 min
3

Descent toward Mirante da Fumaça, natural overlook at 1040 m altitude. From this viewpoint, direct view of Cachoeira da Fumaça dropping 340 to 380 m below into a canyon. In dry season, winds carry the pulverized water upward creating the "smoke" effect that gives the waterfall its name. Weightless sensation guaranteed.

⏱ 60 min
4

Final descent toward Vale do Capão, 3h walk. Traverse humid Atlantic forest, crystalline streams, orchids and bromeliads. Arrival at the village of Caeté-Açu, called Vale do Capão, an alternative neo-hippie village founded in the 1970s by residents seeking counter-culture. 1500 inhabitants, unique vibe.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Late lunch at Vale do Capão in a vegetarian restaurant (Restaurante Capão, Santo Grão). Large veggie and Hare Krishna community in the village: palm soup, vegetable lasagna, fresh tropical juices. Radical change after 3 days of rustic cooking. Count on 45-60 BRL.

⏱ 75 min

4x4 transfer from Vale do Capão to Lençóis in late afternoon, 1h30 drive partly unpaved. Arrival in Lençóis around 7 PM. Extended shower, laundry, quiet evening. Light dinner downtown, early to bed. The muscle soreness will be there tomorrow morning.

⏱ 150 min

Salvador

★★★★★
1

Free morning in Lençóis. Options depending on your flight time: easy walk to Poço Halley (20 min from center, quiet swimming), visit the Museu do Garimpo we didn't have time for on D2, handicraft shopping (semi-precious stone jewelry, banana and umbu sweets).

⏱ 120 min
2

More active option: half-day excursion to Marimbus, wetland nicknamed the Chapada's "mini-Pantanal." Canoe crossing on Rio Roncador amid buritis (marsh palms), water lilies, jacaré alligators and aquatic birds. Count on 150 BRL per person, 4h all-inclusive.

⏱ 240 min
3

Transfer to Horácio de Matos Airport, 20 km, 30 min. Azul flight Lençóis-Salvador (1h) mid-afternoon. Three weekly frequencies: absolutely verify the day before booking. Alternative if no flight: Real Expresso bus Lençóis-Salvador (6h, 120 BRL).

⏱ 60 min
4

Arrive in Salvador in the evening. Depending on international return flight time, extra night downtown or direct transfer to Luís Eduardo Magalhães Airport. Flights to Europe typically depart between 10 PM and 11 PM (via GRU or direct).

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If staying overnight in Salvador, final Bahian dinner in the chic Rio Vermelho neighborhood (Boteco do França, Paraíso Tropical). Traditional moqueca, authentic dendê, passion fruit caipirinha. Last moment of Bahian spirit before returning home.

⏱ 120 min

Bring back artisanal Chapada cachaça: Seleta or Bocoy brands, produced in Vale do Paraguaçu. On sale at Lençóis Municipal Market or the airport. Count on 60-100 BRL per artisanal bottle. Checked baggage required for international flights.
