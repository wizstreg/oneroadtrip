Alghero

★★★★☆
1

Alghero's ramparts were built by the Aragonese starting in 1353 when Peter IV of Aragon settled Majorcan and Catalan colonists here, and they've retained over 70% of their original perimeter with seven 16th-century towers still intact. Catalan remains an officially recognized minority language — street signs bear bilingual names, and the Iberian accent lingers in certain families to this day.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Bastioni Marco Polo run along the sea for several hundred meters above the limestone rocks. At sunset, golden light spills across the waters of the Gulf of Alghero, drawing locals to the evening promenade as it has for centuries.

⏱ 45 min
3

Capo Caccia is a limestone promontory rising 170 meters straight out of the sea, 24 km from Alghero. At its base opens the Grotta di Nettuno, a karst cave discovered by a local fisherman in the 18th century, accessible by boat or via the 654 steps of the Escala del Cabirol carved into the cliff face in 1959.

⏱ 90 min
4

Maria Pia Beach stretches for 2 km north of Alghero, backed by a pine forest of umbrella pines planted in the 1930s to stabilize the dunes. Its fine white sand and shallow waters make it the city's most popular family beach.

⏱ 60 min
5

Le Bombarde Beach, 8 km north of Alghero in Porto Conte Bay, is framed by rocks and Mediterranean scrubland. The rocky seabed makes it an excellent snorkeling spot.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Boat crossing from Alghero port to the Grotta di Nettuno: 3-hour round trip with views of the Capo Caccia cliffs and Porto Conte Bay.

⏱ 180 min

Taste aragosta all'algherese in a harborside trattoria — a Catalan lobster recipe with tomatoes, onion, olive oil, and vinegar that's distinct from the rest of Sardinia.

⏱ 90 min

Swimming and snorkeling in the coves of Porto Conte Bay, a protected marine area with some of the clearest waters in northwest Sardinia.

⏱ 90 min

Stintino

★★☆☆☆
1

La Pelosa Beach, 2.5 km north of the village, takes its name from the posidonia seagrass that carpets its seabed — 'sa pelosa' in Sardinian. Its 10-meter Aragonese tower, built in 1578 on a small island accessible on foot through the shallow waters, has become the beach's iconic symbol. Access is now limited to 1,500 people per day to protect the fragile ecosystem.

⏱ 120 min
2

Stintino was founded in 1885 by 45 fishing families expelled from Asinara Island when the Italian state established a penal colony there. The 'Communion of the 45' — the cooperative that gave birth to the village — is commemorated by the Piazza dei 45 in the town center. The old tonnara (tuna fishery), active until the 1970s, is now a museum of tuna fishing.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Torre del Falcone, perched atop Capo Falcone promontory at 200 meters elevation, offers a 360° panorama over the Stintino peninsula, Asinara National Park, and Sardinia's west coast. The name comes from the peregrine falcons that nest in the rocky crevices.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Boat excursion to Asinara National Park, an island closed to the public for 115 years due to the presence of a maximum-security penal colony. Now a nature reserve, it's home to albino donkeys, dolphins, and pristine vegetation.

⏱ 240 min

Swimming at La Pelosetta, the neighboring beach to La Pelosa, less crowded, with the same Aragonese tower in the background and shallow waters perfect for children.

⏱ 90 min

Sample bottarga di tonno and lobster soup at the harbor trattorias — specialties drawn directly from the village's tuna-fishing heritage.

⏱ 60 min

Costa Paradiso

★☆☆☆☆
1

Costa Paradiso is an enclave of stone villas melted into a landscape of intense red-ochre granite and trachyte cliffs, formed by ancient volcanic activity. The coast between Castelsardo and Santa Teresa Gallura along provincial road SP 90 is carved into pinnacles, bays, and sea caves accessible only by trail or by sea.

⏱ 45 min
2

Li Cossi Beach, the only sandy beach at Costa Paradiso, nestles in a sheltered cove between amphitheater-like pink trachyte cliffs. A panoramic trail of about 800 meters from the parking lot winds through the maquis before revealing the beach from above the rocks. The Li Cossi stream forms a small lagoon behind the beach.

⏱ 90 min
3

Isola Rossa, 6 km south of Costa Paradiso near Trinità d'Agultu, is a red granite islet that gave its name to the locality. From the harbor jetty, the view of the rock against the surrounding emerald waters is particularly striking.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Hike the coastal trail from Li Cossi to the Tinnari natural pool — about 3 km one way from the parking lot, with successive views over the red cliffs and coves.

⏱ 180 min

Snorkeling in the rocky coves around Li Cossi, where the seabed alternates between sand and posidonia-covered rocks with excellent visibility.

⏱ 90 min

Santa Teresa Gallura

★★★★☆
1

The Longosardo Tower, built under Philip II of Spain in the 16th century, is the largest Spanish tower in Sardinia. From its summit, the view extends to the white cliffs of Bonifacio 12 km away, the La Maddalena archipelago, and on clear days, all of Corsica. The town was founded in 1808 by Victor Emmanuel I of Savoy, who named it after his wife Maria Teresa.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Capo Testa promontory, connected to the mainland by a narrow isthmus, is the ancient granite quarry from which the Romans extracted stone for their columns. Unfinished column shafts abandoned in the rock are still visible on site. The Valley of the Moon on the isthmus is a chaos of white granite blocks sculpted by wind into rounded, labyrinthine forms.

⏱ 90 min
3

Rena Bianca Beach, right in the town center, is a 700-meter cove framed by granite rocks. A Blue Flag beach since 1987, its shallow waters allow direct views of the Corsican cliffs on the horizon.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Ferry crossing to Bonifacio in Corsica (50 minutes), a town perched on white limestone cliffs directly across the strait. A day trip is possible from the port of Santa Teresa.

⏱ 300 min

Hike the trails of Capo Testa among the granite blocks and maquis, with access to Rena di Ponente and Rena di Levante beaches, sheltered from winds depending on their orientation.

⏱ 120 min

Sample Vermentino di Gallura DOCG, a dry white wine produced on the Gallura granites, at the waterfront restaurants.

⏱ 60 min

La Maddalena

★★★☆☆
1

The La Maddalena archipelago comprises 62 islands and islets across 180 km of coastline, designated a national park in 1994. In 1804, Admiral Nelson established his fleet's base here while pursuing the French and described this harbor as the most beautiful in the world. A crucifix and two candlesticks he offered in gratitude for the hospitality are still preserved in the Santa Maria Maddalena church.

⏱ 45 min
2

On Caprera Island, connected to La Maddalena by the 600-meter Ponte Moneta, the Compendio Garibaldino preserves the house where Giuseppe Garibaldi lived his final 26 years, from 1856 until his death on June 2, 1882. The clock in his bedroom remains stopped at 6:21 PM, the moment of his death.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Spiaggia Rosa (Pink Beach) on Budelli Island owes its rosy hue to fragments of coral, shells, and foraminifera mixed with white sand. Since the 1990s, the beach has been protected and accessible only by sea — setting foot on it is forbidden.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Boat excursion around the islands of Spargi, Budelli, Santa Maria, and Razzoli — inaccessible by road. Spargi Island preserves the wreck of a Roman ship from the 2nd century BC whose cargo of amphorae has been partially recovered.

⏱ 300 min

Swimming at Cala Coticcio on Caprera, nicknamed the Tahitian Sardinia for its translucent waters in shades of turquoise and green. Accessible on foot via a trail from the parking lot or by sea.

⏱ 120 min

Porto Cervo

★★☆☆☆
1

Porto Cervo was designed by architect Luigi Vietti and inaugurated in 1964, two years after the creation of the Consorzio Costa Smeralda on March 14, 1962, by Prince Karim Aga Khan IV. The Aga Khan had discovered these deserted shores in the late 1950s aboard a 12-meter sailboat and decided to create an anti-Côte d'Azur. The Consorzio still manages 3,114 hectares today, of which 96% remain undeveloped.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Stella Maris church, designed by architect Michele Busiri Vici and inaugurated in 1968, overlooks Porto Cervo's old harbor from its hilltop. Its clean white facade and square bell tower exemplify the pan-Mediterranean style the Aga Khan wanted so that each building would integrate with the granite landscape.

⏱ 30 min
3

Spiaggia del Principe (Prince's Beach) is said to have been the Aga Khan's favorite beach, where he regularly went with his architects during the site's early explorations. Accessible via a 10-minute walk from the road, its intense blue-green waters are framed by pink granite rocks.

⏱ 90 min
4

Capriccioli Beach, at the back of a sheltered bay, is a succession of small coves separated by rocks you walk along. Its shallow, clear waters make it an excellent snorkeling spot.

⏱ 75 min
5

Liscia Ruja is the longest beach on the Costa Smeralda at about 800 meters of golden sand. Bordered by juniper and mastic trees, it faces the Mortorio and Soffi islets in the open sea.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll through Porto Cervo's piazzetta, the heart of the village designed by Vietti in 1964, whose arcades and white-plastered facades reproduce the vernacular architecture of Gallura.

⏱ 45 min

Swimming and snorkeling in the Costa Smeralda's coves — Capriccioli and Spiaggia del Principe count among the clearest seabeds in the Mediterranean.

⏱ 120 min

Visit the village of San Pantaleo, 10 km inland, a pink granite village with narrow alleys known for its craft market and local artists' galleries.

⏱ 90 min

Spacious Holiday Home in Porto Cervo: Due Passi dal Centro offers a spacious holiday home in Porto Cervo. The property features two bedrooms, two bathrooms, and a comfortable living room.
