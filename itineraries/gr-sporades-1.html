Sporades Island Hop: Skiathos, Skopelos & Alonissos

📅 6 days📍 5 stops🚗 44 km⏱ 1h27

Skiathos → Skopelos → Alonissos

Start with Skiathos and its 60 beaches — Koukounaries with its golden sand under the pines, Lalaria with its polished white pebbles beneath sheer limestone cliffs, reachable only by boat. Cross to Skopelos to climb the 106 rock-hewn steps of the Agios Ioannis chapel, the iconic Mamma Mia backdrop, and to discover hill villages surrounded by PDO plum orchards. Finish on Alonissos, the wildest of the Sporades, where around 50 of the last Mediterranean monk seals live inside Europe's largest marine park.

✨ Highlights
  • Lalaria, Skiathos: boat-access only — arrive early or in the late afternoon
  • Evangelistria Monastery: birthplace of the first Greek flag (1807), monk-produced wine
  • Agios Ioannis Kastri: 106 rock-cut steps — beat the midday crowds in summer
  • Alonissos Marine Park: 6–8 hr excursion from Patitiri, booking essential in high season
📆 Best period

May, June, September, October

To make the most of it, plan between 2 and 6 days.

Day 1

Skiathos

Sixty beaches under the pines, where nightlife and history share one small island

★★★☆☆
📍 Skiathos town port📍 Bourtzi peninsula Skiathos📍 Evangelistria Monastery Skiathos📍 Kastro fortress Skiathos📍 Papadiamantis Museum Skiathos
1

Skiathos town is a compact, lively little capital whose cobbled lanes wind their way down to the double harbour. It's the most tourist-oriented island in the Sporades — in summer the atmosphere is cosmopolitan and the nightlife runs until dawn along Papadiamanti Street. If you prefer a quieter pace, avoid July–August or arrive in the morning to see the sights before the cruise-ship crowds pour in.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Bourtzi peninsula, linked to the old harbour by a short footbridge, holds a 13th-century Venetian fortification built by the Ghisi brothers to defend the island from pirates. Ancient trees cover the headland, making it a pleasant spot on a warm evening — open-air concerts are sometimes held here in summer. The view from the tip across both harbours is one of the most photogenic on the island.

⏱ 30 min
3

Evangelistria Monastery, founded in the 18th century on a hillside 4 km above town, is the most historically charged site on Skiathos. It was here, in September 1807, that the first Greek flag — a white cross on a blue field — was designed, woven, blessed and raised in the presence of the revolutionary hero Theodoros Kolokotronis. The monks who still live in the monastery produce wine, olive oil and honey. The museum housed in the old olive press displays the loom said, by tradition, to have woven that first flag. Take the marked trail from town rather than the road — it's more enjoyable and no longer.

⏱ 60 min
4

Kastro is the former fortified capital of Skiathos, perched on a rocky headland at the northern end of the island. The islanders retreated here in the 14th century to escape pirate raids and didn't return to the coast until after Greek independence in 1829. A handful of churches still cling to the cliffs, including the Chapel of the Nativity of Christ, and the views over the Aegean are striking. Reachable by hiking (3 km of track then a trail) or by boat from the harbour.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Papadiamantis Museum, at number 12 on the street of the same name near the port, occupies the birthplace of Alexandros Papadiamantis, Greece's greatest 19th-century novelist, born on Skiathos in 1851. The two-storey house retains its period interior virtually intact: original furniture, personal library, everyday objects. The cave known as the 'Murderess's Cave' near Lalaria takes its name from one of Papadiamantis's short stories. Open Tuesday to Sunday, 9:30 am to 1:30 pm and 5:15 pm to 8 pm.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Papadiamanti Street, the main artery of Skiathos town, comes alive in the evening with craft shops, tavernas spilling tables onto the pavement, and ouzo bars. Stroll down from around 7 pm when the locals start to emerge — the summer crowds don't descend until after 9 pm. It's also the best spot to order a mezze platter: taramosalata, tyrokafteri, chargrilled octopus.

⏱ 90 min

Astakomakaronada, the signature dish of the Sporades, is spaghetti with fresh lobster in a lightly spiced tomato sauce. You'll find it at almost every taverna along the old harbour — ask whether the lobster is from the morning catch, as local fishermen deliver directly at dawn. It's one of the best freshness-to-value deals on the island.

⏱ 60 min

Tsipouro, a Greek spirit distilled from grape pomace, is the Sporades' drink of choice. On Skiathos it's served cold, in small glasses, alongside mezze — chargrilled octopus, feta, herb-marinated olives. The monks at Evangelistria produce a small-batch artisanal version available from the monastery shop.

⏱ 45 min

Skiathos

Sixty beaches under the pines, where nightlife and history share one small island

★★★☆☆
📍 Koukounaries beach Skiathos📍 Lalaria beach Skiathos📍 Blue caves Lalaria Skiathos📍 Banana beach Skiathos📍 Troulos beach Skiathos
1

Koukounaries is Skiathos's postcard beach — regularly ranked among the finest in Greece, with 1,200 metres of fine golden sand backed by a protected Aleppo pine forest. Behind the beach lies the Strofilia wetland, a refuge for rare migratory birds including swans and herons. In high season the sun loungers are claimed early — arrive before 9 am or come in the late afternoon.

⏱ 120 min
2

Lalaria is Skiathos's show-stopping beach: reachable only by boat from the old harbour, it curves in a half-moon of smooth white pebbles beneath sheer limestone cliffs that plunge into water of an almost unreal blue-green. A natural rock arch sculpted by the sea is the focal point of every photograph on the island. Boats run daily from the port, but if the northerly winds pick up the crossing is cancelled. Bring water and food — there's nothing on site.

⏱ 120 min
3

The sea caves near Lalaria are every bit as worthwhile as the beach itself. The Blue Cave (Galazia) owes its name to the extraordinary colour of the water inside when the sun hits — reflections off the limestone walls produce an almost supernatural blue glow. The Dark Cave (Skotini), roughly 20 metres deep, can only be entered by small boat because the opening is so narrow. Most excursions to Lalaria include a pass through these caves.

⏱ 45 min
4

Troulos, 8 km west of town, is a family-friendly beach with shallow water and a relaxed atmosphere. Pines reach almost to the sand, and a few tavernas serve grilled fish. Facing it is the cluster of Troulonisia islets. It's the ideal beach with children if Koukounaries feels too crowded.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Book a half-day boat trip from Skiathos old harbour to Lalaria and the sea caves — it's the simplest and most enjoyable way to get there. Departures are usually at 9 am or 10 am. In July–August, book the day before: places sell out fast. Options include swimming and snorkelling stops in coves that are impossible to reach on foot.

⏱ 180 min

Revithada, a thick chickpea soup slow-baked with olive oil, onion and herbs, is a Sporades vegetarian classic often served on Sundays in tavernas. It's a warming winter recipe, though some places keep it on the menu year-round. With crusty bread and olives on the side, it makes a complete — and very affordable — lunch.

⏱ 45 min

Retsina is a white wine infused with pine resin, made according to a tradition stretching back millennia — the resin originally prevented oxidation inside clay amphorae. Its distinctive, slightly terpenic taste pairs beautifully with grilled fish and mezze. It's the cheapest and most authentically Greek bottle you'll be poured at any Sporades taverna.

⏱ 30 min

Day 3

Skopelos

The greenest island in the Sporades, famed for Mamma Mia and its 300 whitewashed chapels

★★☆☆☆
📍 Skopelos town port📍 Kastro Skopelos hilltop📍 Agios Ioannis Kastri chapel Skopelos📍 Glossa village Skopelos📍 Evangelismos Monastery Skopelos
1

Skopelos town is built amphitheatre-style around a deep bay — white houses with red-brown tile roofs tumble down toward the harbour in a setting the filming of Mamma Mia made famous worldwide. The town alone counts 123 Orthodox churches and chapels — nearly as many as days in the year — some dating back to the Byzantine era. Wander the cobbled alleys of the upper town: no two facades are alike.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Kastro of Skopelos, on the hill above the harbour, is the highest point in town. The Venetian and Byzantine ruins here offer the best panoramic view over the bay, the ferries coming and going, and — on a clear day — the outline of Alonissos on the horizon. Head up in the late afternoon: the light is ideal and the atmosphere far calmer than midday.

⏱ 45 min
3

The chapel of Agios Ioannis Kastri is perched on top of a sheer rock face plunging into the sea, a few kilometres north of Skopelos near the village of Glossa. To reach it from the car park below you must climb 106 steps carved into the rock. This is where the outdoor wedding scenes from Mamma Mia were filmed in 2007. Arrive early in the morning or after 5 pm to avoid the stream of fans who descend all day long in summer. The view over the Aegean and across to Alonissos is worth the climb even if you've never seen the film.

⏱ 60 min
4

The village of Glossa, perched in the hills of Skopelos, is one of the best-preserved mountain settlements in the Sporades. Its Macedonian architecture — overhanging roofs, stone houses, a plane-tree-shaded square — is a marked contrast to the white Cycladic-style buildings elsewhere on the island. The locals still tend olive groves and plum orchards. The central square with its cafés is the perfect stop on the way to the Agios Ioannis chapel.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The kalderimi trails — ancient paved paths that once linked Skopelos's monasteries — are the best way to explore the island's wooded interior. The route to the 18th-century Evangelismos Monastery on Mount Palouki winds through a pine forest with sweeping sea views. Set off in the morning to beat the heat and bring water.

⏱ 120 min

The tyropita on Skopelos is unlike any you'll find elsewhere: here, local cheese — a fresh, lightly salted sheep's-milk variety — is wrapped in handmade puff pastry, not factory-produced phyllo. It's the island's go-to breakfast, sold in bakeries from 7 am. Don't leave without eating one straight from the oven, still warm.

⏱ 30 min

Skopelos plums carry a protected designation of origin and are the island's agricultural pride. Family orchards produce plums preserved in syrup, sun-dried, or turned into liqueur. You'll find these products in the shops around town — they're the best edible souvenir to bring home from Skopelos.

⏱ 30 min

Day 4

Skopelos

The greenest island in the Sporades, famed for Mamma Mia and its 300 whitewashed chapels

★★☆☆☆
📍 Kastani beach Skopelos📍 Milia beach Skopelos📍 Panormos village Skopelos📍 Sendoukia tombs Skopelos📍 Hovolo beach Skopelos
1

Panormos is a sheltered seaside village tucked into a natural bay, with a fine-sand beach fringed by pines. It's a favourite anchorage for sailors, and its waterfront tavernas serve fresh fish in a peaceful setting. Less crowded than the beaches on the southern coast, it's the perfect spot for a feet-in-the-water lunch.

⏱ 90 min
2

The beaches of Kastani and Milia are two of the most recognisable Mamma Mia filming locations. Kastani, in particular, hosted the film's longest scene. Both coves — pebbles or sand depending on the section, turquoise water, a pine forest that slopes right down to the shore — rank among the most beautiful on Skopelos quite apart from their cinematic fame. Milia tends to be breezier and is ideal for snorkelling.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Sendoukia are an archaeological mystery in the hills above Skopelos: four large sarcophagi carved straight into the bedrock at the top of a ridge, linked by local legend to the Greek pirate Glaucus who is said to have ruled these waters. Their exact date and purpose are still debated by scholars. The path up is unmarked — ask in Glossa for directions. The view from the site alone justifies the effort.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The ideal day along Skopelos's west coast: head out by car in the morning to Panormos for a waterfront coffee, drive down to Milia for a swim, then move on to Kastani for the afternoon. Finish at the smaller cove of Hovolo, quieter and ringed by flat rocks perfect for snorkelling. Boat-hire outfits in Skopelos town also rent small motorboats so you can explore these coves at your own pace.

⏱ 180 min

Astakos — lobster simply grilled with lemon and olive oil — is served at the fishermen's tavernas in Panormos and at small harbours like Agnontas. Ask whether it's fresh from the morning: Skopelos fishermen have a reputation for delivering straight to the local tavernas. Pair it with garlic-rubbed toasted bread.

⏱ 60 min

Loukoumades — small, light doughnuts fried to order, drenched in honey, scattered with crushed walnuts and cinnamon — are the street dessert of the Sporades. You'll find them at the kiosks on Skopelos harbour from around 6 pm. They're crisp on the outside, airy inside, and best eaten standing up with sticky fingers, the way the locals do.

⏱ 20 min

Day 5

Alonissos

A wild island at the heart of Europe's largest marine park, where monk seals still roam

★★☆☆☆
📍 Patitiri port Alonissos📍 Chora Old Village Alonissos📍 Alonissos National Marine Park📍 MOm monk seal centre Patitiri📍 Kokkinokastro beach Alonissos
1

Patitiri, the port of Alonissos, takes its name — meaning 'wine press' — from the island's viticultural past. Rebuilt after the 1965 earthquake, it's a functional yet endearing little town with a paved waterfront, a handful of seafood tavernas, and — most importantly — the MOm Information Centre on the Mediterranean monk seal, a small but well-designed exhibition with videos and infographics about the species and the marine park's conservation work. Don't miss it if you're travelling with children or want to understand what you might see on a boat excursion.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Chora — also known as Palia Alonissos or the Old Village — sits at 200 metres above sea level on a ridge overlooking the entire island. Abandoned after the 1965 earthquake that partly destroyed it, it was gradually restored from the 1970s onward by artists and lovers of authenticity. Today its cobbled lanes lined with whitewashed, blue-shuttered houses, cascading bougainvillea and 360° panoramas over the Aegean make it the most captivating spot on Alonissos. Come in the late afternoon for sunset from the taverna terraces — it's one of the quietest spectacles in all the Sporades.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Alonissos National Marine Park, established in 1992, is Greece's first marine park and one of the largest in Europe, covering 251,440 hectares encompassing Alonissos, six smaller islands and 22 islets. It was created to protect the Mediterranean monk seal (Monachus monachus), the most endangered seal species on earth. The park's colony numbers between 50 and 60 individuals — roughly 10% of the 500 to 600 still alive worldwide. The core zone around the island of Piperi is strictly off-limits to visitors in order to safeguard breeding sites. The accessible areas, however, offer the chance to spot dolphins, sea turtles, and more than 300 fish species.

⏱ 30 min
4

Kokkinokastro is a striking spot on the east coast of Alonissos: a pebble beach in shades of rust and ochre beneath cliffs of the same hue, coloured by oxidised iron in the rock. Remains of an ancient settlement and traces of Neolithic occupation have been found here. The colour of the soil makes a dramatic contrast against the blue of the sea — it's one of the most visually arresting sights on the island.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The boat excursion through the marine park is the centrepiece of any visit to Alonissos. Departures are from Patitiri, with several options: a full-park trip (6 to 8 hours) taking in the islands of Kyra Panagia and Peristera, swimming stops in exceptionally clear water, and sometimes dolphin sightings. Spotting a monk seal remains rare and depends on luck — but even without one, the quality of the water and the beauty of the deserted islets make the trip more than worthwhile. Book ahead in July–August.

⏱ 360 min

Since 2015, Alonissos has banned single-use plastic bags across the entire island — a pioneering move in Greece that reflects the eco-conscious identity of the local community. The tap water is said to be the purest in the Aegean. If you'd like to learn more about this commitment and about monk-seal conservation, the MOm organisation occasionally runs citizen-science sessions: you can sign up as a volunteer observer for a day to help survey dolphins and seals.

⏱ 60 min

Tyrokafteri is a dip of crushed feta blended with spicy red peppers and olive oil — creamy, punchy, with a hint of sweetness. On Alonissos it's served as a starter with slices of toasted bread to open every taverna meal. Order it with a glass of ice-cold tsipouro and olives marinated in the island's wild herbs for an aperitif that's thoroughly local.
