Sankt Moritz

★★★☆☆
1

Overrated, they say, but Saint Moritz delivers on at least one promise: the view. The Engadin lake in summer, the Corviglia slopes in winter, and that altitude silence that calms everything. The resort sits at 1,856 meters. Avoid January weekends when prices double without the snow getting any better.

⏱ 60 min
2

At 1,856 meters in the Engadin valley, Saint Moritz became the birthplace of winter tourism: in 1864, hotelier Johannes Badrutt bet that British guests would appreciate the alpine winter. He was right. The Badrutt family still runs a palace hotel here. Visit the frozen lake if you're here in January.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Engadin air has that transparency that makes the ridges sparkle and gives mountain colors an almost unreal sharpness. By late November, the first snows blanket the valley before tourists arrive. Book early: Saint Moritz hotels fill up by September for ski weeks.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

You'll walk down to Lake Saint Moritz, following the shore to the Celerina district, a roughly 5 km loop. In winter, the frozen surface sometimes hosts horse races and polo. Waterproof shoes essential from November to March.

⏱ 90 min

If winter finds you here, the Cresta Run, a natural ice luge track with over a century of history, is the resort's strangest attraction. The track only admits Saint Moritz Club members or their guests. Check cresta-run.com for details.

⏱ 60 min

Davos

★★★☆☆
1

World capital of economic forums, Davos also knows how to forget the WEF the rest of the year. Its Parsenn ski slopes rank among the most extensive in eastern Switzerland, and the lake promenade offers tranquility at the opposite end of UN-style debates. Arrive outside January if queues are a problem.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Kirchner Museum, a glass cube perched on the heights of Davos, houses a major collection of works by Ernst Ludwig Kirchner, the expressionist who settled here from 1917. The Alpine paintings done from this terrace dialogue with the landscape visible through the windows. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Berghotel Schatzalp, a former sanatorium turned mountain hotel at 1,861 meters above Davos, is accessible by cable car from the center. The region's cure establishments inspired Thomas Mann's The Magic Mountain, published in 1924. Hotel lobby open to visitors during the day.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

You'll walk along the Davosersee in late afternoon, a lake with changing reflections - gold in autumn, white in winter, green in spring. The walk takes 40 minutes. In January, the lake is sometimes skateable - check conditions with the tourist office.

⏱ 60 min

If you come in summer, the Jakobshorn is accessible by cable car from Davos Dorf and opens onto the Dischma and Sertig hiking trails. The walk down takes about 2h. Closed off-season, check hours on davos.ch.

⏱ 180 min

Chur

★★★☆☆
1

For over 5,000 years, Chur has carried the label of Switzerland's oldest town - a claim well documented by archaeological digs on the Bischofsberg. The old town has kept its medieval lanes. Climb to the cathedral in the morning: the side light on the Romanesque facade differs from the afternoon.

⏱ 60 min
2

Chur Cathedral stands as one of the most surprising in the Grisons: its blend of late Romanesque and Gothic results from a multi-century construction project. The interior is more impressive than the modest exterior suggests. Free entry, donations welcome.

⏱ 45 min
3

The lanes of Chur's old town descend in tiers toward the Rhine plain, paved and narrow, lined with arcaded houses. The signs mix German and Romansh. On weekdays, tourists are rare here: it's the locals' territory.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

You'll take the Poststrasse to climb back toward the Altstadt and the stairs leading to the cathedral square from the station in 15 minutes on foot. It's the most direct route to reach the medieval lanes. Stop at the Rätisches Museum for regional collections.

⏱ 90 min

Capital of the Grisons, Chur easily goes unnoticed by tourists rushing to Saint Moritz. Wrong. The Saturday market on the Postplatz and the local specialty - Bündner Nusstorte, the canton's nut tart - alone justify a stop. Plan at least two hours.

⏱ 60 min

Innsbruck

★★★★☆
1

Tyroleans say of Innsbruck that it's 'too small for a metropolis, too large for a village' - and they see this as an asset. Capital of the Land of Tyrol, it crosses on foot in twenty minutes while offering museums, an old town, and the Karwendel Alps as backdrop. Arrive by train: the panorama on arrival.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Goldenes Dachl, a balcony with 2,657 gilded copper tiles, was built in 1500 by Maximilian I to observe tournaments from his imperial box. The excess is intentional: 2,657 tiles for a balcony. The small museum inside is worth the visit, 20 minutes suffice.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Hofburg in Innsbruck doesn't have the scope of Vienna's, but concentrates in a few rooms the essence of the Habsburgs' presence in Tyrol. The Hall of Giants gathers portraits of several generations of the imperial family. Book in the morning to avoid school groups.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

You'll walk up the Maria-Theresien-Strasse to the baroque Annasäule column, erected in 1706 to celebrate Tyrol's defense. The pedestrian street is lined with Venetian baroque facades with the Karwendel Alps visible to the north. Step back from the fountain to frame it all.

⏱ 60 min

The smell of Kiachl, Tyrolean fritters fried in lard, wafts from markets in autumn and winter. During the Christkindlmarkt, the old town smells of cinnamon and Glühwein. Outside the holidays, local bakeries serve their specialties from 7am.

⏱ 60 min

Mayrhofen

★★★★☆
1

A popular ski resort, Mayrhofen is also worth visiting in summer: the Zillertal landscapes offer some of Austria's most photographed alpine pastures. Outside peak season, the valley villages return to their slow rhythm. Arrive in May or September to avoid crowds.

⏱ 60 min
2

The milky green waters of the Ziller rush between granite banks covered in moss and rhododendrons. The alpine meadows have that smell of fresh grass and mountain manure that announces high-altitude chalets. Hike to the Ginzling Alm: 2h one-way, marked trail.

⏱ 90 min
3

Zillertal locals call their glacier 'the Hintertuxer,' like a well-known neighbor. This permanent glacier accessible from Hintertux is skiable year-round, at over 3,000 meters. Plan half a day and check rates on hintertuxer-gletscher.at.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

You'll reach the Hintertux glacier by driving up the valley from Mayrhofen to Hintertux. The cable car climbs in several sections to over 3,000 meters. Dress warm even in July: at the summit, it's several degrees below zero.

⏱ 180 min

The walk from Mayrhofen to Finkenberg follows the Zillertal valley floor in 45 minutes and opens access to several high-altitude trails. Starting point for the local via ferrata as well. Best window: late June to mid-September.

1

The air at the summit of the Hintertuxer Gletscher is thin and dry even in July: you're over 3,000 meters and skiers in shorts rub shoulders with mountaineers in down jackets. The glacier is visibly receding from one season to the next. Visit it before the picture changes again.

2

The Hintertux glacier is one of the few Alpine glaciers skiable year-round, accessible from Hintertux by cable car in several sections. National ski teams practice their off-season training here. Check conditions on hintertuxer-gletscher.at.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you come in summer, the upper glacier slopes are virtually deserted outside training groups. The climb to the Tuxer Fernerhaus at 3,250 meters takes 45 minutes. Dress warm: even in August, the summit temperature hovers around 0°C.

Brennero

★☆☆☆☆
1

You'll cross the Brenner Pass via the A22 Autostrada at 1,374 meters, without really noticing: it's one of the busiest Alpine crossings in Europe, invisible behind highway ramps. Stop to see the two customs posts side by side, witnesses to Schengen's recent history.

⏱ 20 min
2

A strategic passage since Roman times, the Brennero was the route of medieval armies and 1943 convoys. The border has been erased since Schengen, but traces of the customs post are still visible. The small chapel on the Italian side is worth a five-minute stop.

⏱ 15 min

Activities

You'll pause at Brennero to observe the Munich-Verona axis that concentrates one of the densest truck traffics in the Alps. The cafeteria on the Italian side, just after the border sign, is authentic in its own way.

⏱ 20 min

Cividale Del Friuli

★★★★☆
1

First capital of the Lombard duchy in Italy, Cividale del Friuli was founded by Julius Caesar under the name Forum Iulii - which gave the Friuli region its name. The mid-8th century Tempietto Longobardo, UNESCO-listed in 2011, is the best-preserved Lombard monument in Europe.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Tempietto Longobardo is one of the most intact works of the Lombard world: stuccos, frescoes, and 8th-century mosaics in a room barely larger than a chapel. Morning light streams through a small east-facing window. Come at 9am for this grazing light on the stuccos.

⏱ 45 min
3

Cividale's Ipogeo Celtico, a network of underground galleries whose origins remain debated, descends several meters beneath the old town. The humidity and smell of stone recall that Cividale's soil has layers. Guided tour only, reservation at the tourist office.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

You'll cross the Ponte del Diavolo, a medieval bridge over the Natisone, to reach the east bank with views of the cliff and ramparts. Legend says the devil built it for a soul. At night, the bridge is beautifully lit.

⏱ 45 min

Known for its Lombard Tempietto, Cividale also hides remarkable Friulian gastronomy: its centro storico osterie serve boreto alla graisana, white fish stew with vinegar and garlic. A local dish little known outside Friuli, ask the owner.

Udine

★★★★☆
1

Udine is often described as a 'little Venice' - a flattering but inaccurate comparison: it's actually livelier and cheaper than Venice. The Piazza Libertà and its Loggia del Lionello (1448) have rare architectural coherence for an Italian provincial square. Paid parking at the foot of the hill.

⏱ 60 min
2

Giambattista Tiepolo's frescoes at the Oratorio della Purità deploy their blues and ochres with airy lightness. Tiepolo worked in Udine from 1726 to 1729, at 30, and here he found his style. Open mornings, brief but intense visit.

⏱ 45 min
3

Friulians say Udine is 'the true capital of Friuli' - administratively, the region has Trieste, but the cultural heart beats here. The Piazza Matteotti morning market offers emblematic products: baccalà mantecato, frico, Montasio. Arrive before 10am.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

You'll climb via Mercatovecchio to Piazza Libertà from the Loggia. The climb to the castello takes 10 minutes and offers a view over the bell tower and Friuli hills. The castello museum is worth the visit for the Tiepolo collections.

⏱ 75 min

Known for its art collections, Udine also hides one of northern Italy's richest gastronomic traditions. Frico, a potato and melted Montasio cheese pancake, exists nowhere else with this texture. Ask for it at an osteria, not a tourist restaurant.

Trieste

★★★★☆
1

Neither quite Italian, nor anymore Austrian, Trieste has navigated since 1954 between two identities with particular ease. It was the Habsburgs' main port until 1918. Today, it's closer to Ljubljana than Rome in mindset. Arrive by train from Udine to see the sea emerge.

⏱ 60 min
2

James Joyce settled in Trieste in 1904 to teach English and lived there over a decade. He wrote Dubliners and much of A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man in his Trieste apartments. Several plaques recall his passage on old center streets.

⏱ 45 min
3

The saline air of the Adriatic invades Piazza Unità d'Italia, the largest square open to the sea in Italy, in late afternoon. The Bora, the northeast wind that can reach impressive speeds, refreshes the city a few days a year. When it blows, the Triestini go out anyway.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

You'll head down to the Castello di Miramare following the coast from the center, either 8 km on foot or 15 minutes by bus. The castle was built by Archduke Maximilian of Austria between 1856 and 1860. Enter early: groups arrive around 11am.

⏱ 120 min

Trieste caffè is a science apart: here, a 'caffè' isn't a simple espresso - a 'nero,' a 'capo,' a 'deca' are codes specific to Trieste. Caffè San Marco, open since 1914, remains the best place to learn the vocabulary.

⏱ 60 min
