✨ Highlights
  • Isalo Park: 81,540 ha of Jurassic sandstone, 7 lemur species and natural pools at canyon bottoms
  • Avenue of Baobabs: Endemic Adansonia grandidieri 300 to 800 years old, trunks reaching 3 m diameter
  • Andasibe: The indri-indri, world's largest lemur (70 cm, 7 kg), call carrying several km through forest
  • Sainte-Marie: Up to 198 humpback whales counted in 12 hours from shore in peak season (August)
📆 Best period

April, May, June, July, August, September, October

⏳ How many days

To make the most of it, plan between 13 and 17 days.

☀️ Weather

Dry Season (April-October): Best weather with minimal rainfall, cooler temperatures (15-25°C), and excellent wildlife viewing. Peak tourist season with higher prices but optimal conditions for national parks and outdoor activities.

Cool Dry Season (May-September): Ideal temperatures and clear skies perfect for hiking, wildlife observation, and exploring highlands. Best time for lemur watching and photography with comfortable conditions throughout.

Hot Dry Season (October-March): Warmer temperatures (20-30°C) with occasional rains, good for coastal areas and beach activities. Cyclone season (January-March) requires weather monitoring but offers lush landscapes.

Rainy Season (November-March): Heavy rains make some roads impassable, but dramatic landscapes and fewer tourists. Lower prices and breeding season for wildlife, though challenging travel conditions.

Climb up to Rova Manjakamiadana first thing in the morning - perched 200 meters above Tana on Analamanga hill, it's the only spot where you can take in the entire capital in a single sweep. ⏱ 75 min
3

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Make the drive to Ambohimanga, 21 km north of Tana - count on an hour with traffic. This isn't your ordinary tourist site: Malagasy people still come here on pilgrimage to make wishes. Activities

Antsirabe

Thermal town at 1,500m with volcanic crater lakes and rainbow-painted pousse-pousse

★★★★☆
1

Welcome to Antsirabe - the town that smells of sulfur and minerals. Perched at 1,500 m on a volcanic plateau, it draws its thermal water from soil so loaded with mineral salts it earned the nickname "where there is much salt." Its wide colonial avenues swarm with brightly painted pousse-pousse - grab one to cross town: it's the quintessential local transport.

⏱ 60 min
2

Lake Tritriva deserves the detour: this volcanic crater 160 meters deep with dark blue-green waters is considered sacred. Don't eat pork before you go - villagers strongly advise against it and the legend of a tourist who drowned for ignoring this taboo still circulates. Seen from above, the lake has exactly the shape of Madagascar. Allow 20 minutes to walk around it.

⏱ 90 min
3

On the way, stop at Lake Andraikiba, 7 km from town - more accessible and more lively: fishermen beat the water with bundles of sticks to stun fish, women wash clothes on the banks. A peaceful setting to catch your breath.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit a zebu horn or semi-precious stone workshop in the center - artisans work in front of you and sell directly. It's two to three times cheaper than in Tana.

⏱ 45 min

Take a pousse-pousse ride through downtown streets - negotiate directly with the driver. It's one of the most useful gestures for the local economy.

⏱ 30 min

Ambositra

Capital of UNESCO-listed Zafimaniry woodcraft assembled without a single nail

★★★★☆
1

Push open the door to a workshop in Ambositra - you're entering the world of the Zafimaniry, whose woodcraft is inscribed on UNESCO's intangible heritage list. No nails, no screws: panels carved with geometric motifs fit together by hand, following rules transmitted orally from father to son for centuries. The same motifs cover walls, doors and furniture of houses - it's a visual language.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Fianarantsoa

Colonial upper town in red brick and Madagascar's only vineyard terraces

★★★★☆
1

Climb into Fianarantsoa's upper town late afternoon - its red-brick lanes stacked on multiple hills are classified as a protected zone, unique in Madagascar. ⏱ 45 min

Activities

Ambalavao

Village reserve protecting 400 ring-tailed lemurs and workshops crafting floral Antemoro paper

★★★★☆
1

12 km south of Ambalavao on the RN7, stop at Anja reserve - managed directly by villagers since 1999, no national park or large structure. They protect around 400 ring-tailed lemurs here, the ones with the long black-and-white striped tail. Arrive early: in the morning, the makis are active and unafraid - they sometimes approach within a meter. The profits have funded a village school. Allow 2 hours for the small loop.

⏱ 120 min
2

Stop by an Antemoro paper workshop in Ambalavao - they make pulp from the bark of the avoha shrub, embedding dried flowers and leaves. The result resembles floral parchment. You can watch every step and leave with sheets made on site.

⏱ 60 min
3

If you're there on a Wednesday, don't miss the zebu market - 2,000 head of cattle transit through this town every week. The herders draped in colorful blankets, the bargaining ritual and the auctions are a spectacle in themselves.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Guided circuit in Anja reserve - the local guide will also show you chameleons, boas and endemic birds tucked among the rocks that you'd never have spotted alone.

⏱ 120 min
