✨ Highlights
  • Isalo Park: 81,540 ha of Jurassic sandstone, 7 lemur species and natural pools at canyon bottoms
  • Avenue of Baobabs: Endemic Adansonia grandidieri 300 to 800 years old, trunks reaching 3 m diameter
  • Andasibe: The indri-indri, world's largest lemur (70 cm, 7 kg), call carrying several km through forest
  • Sainte-Marie: Up to 198 humpback whales counted in 12 hours from shore in peak season (August)
📆 Best period

April, May, June, July, August, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 13 and 17 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (April-October): Best weather with minimal rainfall, cooler temperatures (15-25°C), and excellent wildlife viewing. Peak tourist season with higher prices but optimal conditions for national parks and outdoor activities.

Cool Dry Season (May-September): Ideal temperatures and clear skies perfect for hiking, wildlife observation, and exploring highlands. Best time for lemur watching and photography with comfortable conditions throughout.

Hot Dry Season (October-March): Warmer temperatures (20-30°C) with occasional rains, good for coastal areas and beach activities. Cyclone season (January-March) requires weather monitoring but offers lush landscapes.

Rainy Season (November-March): Heavy rains make some roads impassable, but dramatic landscapes and fewer tourists. Lower prices and breeding season for wildlife, though challenging travel conditions.

🌍 What to discover in MG?

Unique island with incredible biodiversity, endemic species, and authentic Malagasy culture. Experience lemur encounters, traditional music, and pristine natural environments. The country offers wildlife adventures with cultural authenticity and natural preservation. Madagascar provides life-changing wildlife experiences with cultural depth and natural wonders.

📋 Important notes

Domestic flights essential between Tuléar and Morondava - this cross-country connection can be unreliable, so verify schedules before booking. Return flight from Sainte-Marie to Tana at trip's end. 4x4 with driver strongly recommended on the RN7. Build in flexibility for transit days.

Climb up to Rova Manjakamiadana first thing in the morning - perched 200 meters above Tana on Analamanga hill, it's the only spot where you can take in the entire capital in a single sweep. ⏱ 75 min
3

Antsirabe

Thermal town at 1,500m with volcanic crater lakes and rainbow-painted pousse-pousse

★★★★☆
📍 Lac Tritriva Antsirabe📍 Lac Andraikiba Antsirabe📍 Gare coloniale Antsirabe
1

Welcome to Antsirabe - the town that smells of sulfur and minerals. Perched at 1,500 m on a volcanic plateau, it draws its thermal water from soil so loaded with mineral salts it earned the nickname "where there is much salt." Its wide colonial avenues swarm with brightly painted pousse-pousse - grab one to cross town: it's the quintessential local transport.

⏱ 60 min
2

Lake Tritriva deserves the detour: this volcanic crater 160 meters deep with dark blue-green waters is considered sacred. Don't eat pork before you go - villagers strongly advise against it and the legend of a tourist who drowned for ignoring this taboo still circulates. Seen from above, the lake has exactly the shape of Madagascar. Allow 20 minutes to walk around it.

⏱ 90 min
3

On the way, stop at Lake Andraikiba, 7 km from town - more accessible and more lively: fishermen beat the water with bundles of sticks to stun fish, women wash clothes on the banks. A peaceful setting to catch your breath.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit a zebu horn or semi-precious stone workshop in the center - artisans work in front of you and sell directly. It's two to three times cheaper than in Tana.

⏱ 45 min

Take a pousse-pousse ride through downtown streets - negotiate directly with the driver. It's one of the most useful gestures for the local economy.

Ambositra

Capital of UNESCO-listed Zafimaniry woodcraft assembled without a single nail

★★★★★
📍 Artisanat Zafimaniry Ambositra ateliers📍 Village Antoetra Zafimaniry Madagascar
1

Push open the door to a workshop in Ambositra - you're entering the world of the Zafimaniry, whose woodcraft is inscribed on UNESCO's intangible heritage list. No nails, no screws: panels carved with geometric motifs fit together by hand, following rules transmitted orally from father to son for centuries. The same motifs cover walls, doors and furniture of houses - it's a visual language.

⏱ 90 min
2

If you have time, take the track to the village of Antoetra - there you'll see Zafimaniry work in its real context, in an actual house, with the real tools. Not in a tourist shop.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Browse Ambositra's craft markets and buy directly from producers - marquetry, wood carvings, textiles. Prices are well below Tana's for the same pieces.

Fianarantsoa

Colonial upper town in red brick and Madagascar's only vineyard terraces

★★★★★
📍 Haute Ville Fianarantsoa belvédère📍 Cave vin Fianarantsoa Madagascar
1

Climb into Fianarantsoa's upper town late afternoon - its red-brick lanes stacked on multiple hills are classified as a protected zone, unique in Madagascar. Climb to the upper viewpoint: the vista of terraced rice paddies tumbling into the valley is one of the finest in the highlands. Bring good shoes - the cobbles are slippery after rain.

⏱ 90 min

Don't leave without tasting the local wine - Fianarantsoa is home to Madagascar's only vineyard, produced on the surrounding slopes. The red and white have a very particular profile, far from European standards: that's exactly what makes them interesting. Look for the cellar in town.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Ambalavao

Village reserve protecting 400 ring-tailed lemurs and workshops crafting floral Antemoro paper

★★★★★
📍 Réserve Communautaire Anja Ambalavao lémuriens📍 Atelier Papier Antemoro Ambalavao📍 Marché zébus Ambalavao
1

12 km south of Ambalavao on the RN7, stop at Anja reserve - managed directly by villagers since 1999, no national park or large structure. They protect around 400 ring-tailed lemurs here, the ones with the long black-and-white striped tail. Arrive early: in the morning, the makis are active and unafraid - they sometimes approach within a meter. The profits have funded a village school. Allow 2 hours for the small loop.

⏱ 120 min
2

Stop by an Antemoro paper workshop in Ambalavao - they make pulp from the bark of the avoha shrub, embedding dried flowers and leaves. The result resembles floral parchment. You can watch every step and leave with sheets made on site.

⏱ 60 min
3

If you're there on a Wednesday, don't miss the zebu market - 2,000 head of cattle transit through this town every week. The herders draped in colorful blankets, the bargaining ritual and the auctions are a spectacle in themselves.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Guided circuit in Anja reserve - the local guide will also show you chameleons, boas and endemic birds tucked among the rocks that you'd never have spotted alone.

Stop at the Maison de l'Isalo, 9 km from Ranohira on the RN7 - it's the best starting point before entering the park. In 45 minutes, you'll grasp the essentials: the massif is formed of Jurassic sandstone 250 million years old, sculpted into canyons and pinnacles by erosion. Set off early for the Canyon des Makis - the sun heats up fast and lemurs hide after 9am. You'll find seven species here including the ring-tailed maki and Verreaux's sifaka, which dances rather than walks. The canyon is deep, shaded, with dense vegetation at the bottom - a total contrast to the plateau's aridity you just crossed.

⏱ 90 min

At the canyon's end, you emerge at the natural pool - a rocky basin fed by a waterfall, fresh and transparent water. Dive in: after hours of walking under the southern sun, it's your reward. The blue pool, a bit farther, is tinted mineral blue-green and deeper. Check conditions with your guide.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Major hike Canyon des Makis → Nymphs' Waterfall → black pool → blue pool. Plan 4 to 6 hours depending on your pace, minimum 2 liters of water per person, hat and hiking boots. It's the best day's walk of the entire RN7.

Andasibe

Sanctuary of the indri-indri whose haunting call awakens the primary forest at dawn

★★☆☆☆
📍 Réserve Analamazaotra Andasibe Indri-Indri📍 Réserve Peyrieras Marozevo caméléons
1

Stop at Marozevo on the road from Tana to visit the Peyrieras reserve - a breeding center for giant butterflies, chameleons and endemic frogs. Takes 45 minutes and provides good preparation for the Andasibe forest.

⏱ 45 min
2

In Andasibe, enter the Analamazaotra reserve at opening with your local guide - mandatory and essential. You're here for the indri-indri: the world's largest lemur, 70 cm, 7 kg, capable of jumping 10 meters from tree to tree. Its call - deep and powerful, carrying several kilometers - awakens the forest at dawn. If you hear it before seeing it, follow the sound. The forest also shelters 11 other lemur species and extremely rare Eulophiella orchids.

⏱ 150 min

Activities

Night forest walk by flashlight - dwarf and mouse lemurs with shining eyes in the branches. Arrange the outing the same morning at the park entrance or through your accommodation.

Akanin Ny Nofy

Nest of dreams on the Pangalanes Canal between freshwater and dense eastern forest

★★☆☆☆
📍 Akanin'ny Nofy Canal Pangalanes bateau📍 Réserve Palmarium lémuriens Lac Ampitabe
1

From Manambato, embark on the Pangalanes Canal - 665 km of waterway running along the east coast, laid out by General Gallieni. Vanilla, coffee and clove plantations line the banks; merchant pirogues still use it daily. The journey to Akanin'ny Nofy takes 1h30: watch local life unfold from the boat.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Palmarium reserve, on the shores of Lake Ampitabe, shelters a dozen semi-free lemur species - some very tame and approaching within arm's reach. Don't feed them. The flora is equally remarkable: orchids, carnivorous plants, extremely rare endemic palms including Voanioala and Lemurophoenix, found nowhere else.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Night boat outing to Rooster Island to observe the Aye-Aye - this very rare nocturnal lemur accumulates oddities: bat ears, skeletal fingers including one very long one for extracting larvae from trunks, hallucinated stare. Departure around 5:30pm, duration about 1 hour. Arrange through your accommodation.

Ile Sainte Marie

Pirate island where humpback whales migrate from Antarctica in austral summer

★★☆☆☆
📍 Cimetière Pirates Baie des Forbans Sainte-Marie📍 Ambodifotatra village Sainte-Marie
1

Upon arrival in Sainte-Marie, start with Ambodifotatra, the main village - its markets, old church and slow, francophone atmosphere set the tone. Sainte-Marie measures 49 km long by 5 km wide: there's no possible rush here.

Go to the pirates' cemetery in Forbans Bay - accessible on foot at low tide, by pirogue at high tide. Tombstones from the 1830s, some carved with a classic skull and crossbones, mark the graves of William Kidd, Thomas Tew, Olivier Levasseur called La Buse and other pirates who made this island their lair between 1680 and 1720. It's one of the rare authentic pirate cemeteries in the world.

⏱ 60 min

Ile Sainte Marie

Pirate island where humpback whales migrate from Antarctica in austral summer

★★☆☆☆
📍 Observation baleines à bosse Canal Sainte-Marie📍 Piscines naturelles Ambodiatafana nord Sainte-Marie📍 Forêt Ikalalao lémuriens Sainte-Marie
1

If you're here between July and September, don't miss the humpback whales - they migrate from Antarctica, 5,000 km, to breed in the Sainte-Marie channel. Peak is in August: 198 individuals were counted in 12 hours from shore during one census. Off season, head to the natural pools of Ambodiatafana in the island's north: granite rocks form basins filled by tides, perfect for swimming.

⏱ 90 min
2

Make a detour through the Ikalalao forest (400 ha) - three endemic lemur species, chameleons and the Eulophiella roempleriana, the giant orchid nicknamed the Queen of Madagascar, found only on this island.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Boat outing for whale watching (July-September) - choose a local operator who respects regulatory distances. Don't dive from the boat near them. Off season, go coastline cruising on the west coast by pirogue instead.
