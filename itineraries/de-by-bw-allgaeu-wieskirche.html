Day 1

Memmingen

1

The scent of fresh bread drifts across Memmingen's Marktplatz, framed by patrician houses with carved gables. A Free Imperial City since 1268, the town built its fortune on salt and spice trading. The market runs Tuesday and Saturday mornings—perfect for sampling Allgäu cheese.

⏱ 45 min
2

Seven gates, twenty-six towers: Memmingen's Stadtmauer is the town's most extensive fortified ensemble. The 13th-century ramparts still stand at their original height in the Ulmer Vorstadt district. Walk the Stadtbach at dusk, when lights shimmer on the water.

⏱ 40 min
3

Small but mighty, the Stadtpfarrkirche St. Martin rises above the rooftops with its refined Gothic proportions, completed in 1499. The choir stalls are worth a look. Free entry, closed during services.

⏱ 30 min
4

Take the lane along the Stadtbach toward the weavers' quarter. The Frauenkirche, one of Swabia's oldest churches, hides a surprising medieval fresco: a unicorn hunt. Easy to miss—ask the tourist office for a floor plan.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If it's Tuesday or Saturday, the Wochenmarkt on Memmingen's Marktplatz lines up stalls of cheese, honey, and local schnapps. The market is compact—you can circle it in thirty minutes. Try the Allgäuer Bergkäse, the local mountain cheese.

⏱ 40 min

Head to the tanners' quarter along the Memminger Ach. Half-timbered houses mirror themselves in the stream. This is the town's most photogenic corner, especially in late afternoon light.

Day 2

Ottobeuren

1

From outside, Ottobeuren's Basilika looks like any large country church. Push the door: the interior explodes with stucco, frescoes, and gilding. Built between 1737 and 1766, it's nicknamed the Swabian Escorial. Free visit, organ concerts in summer.

⏱ 60 min
2

In the Kaisersaal, sixteen Habsburg emperors in marble stare down from their pedestals. The monastery's reception hall gives a sense of the abbey's baroque power and wealth. Accessible by guided tour only.

⏱ 30 min
3

Founded in 764, Ottobeuren's Benedictine abbey has run uninterrupted through the centuries. The Klostermuseum traces this history through the old conventual rooms. There's also a Klosterladen where the monks sell jams and liqueurs.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Attend an organ concert in the Ottobeuren Basilika during summer. The acoustics are remarkable, and concerts are often free or by donation. Check the monastery website for the schedule.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min
Day 3

Kempten

1

Kempten calls itself Germany's oldest city, and the proof lies beneath your feet. The Greek geographer Strabo mentioned Kambodounon over two thousand years ago. Today it's a lively, unpretentious town of 70,000 souls.

⏱ 30 min
2

Descend into the underground Erasmuskapelle, a vaulted space rediscovered beneath the market square. The atmosphere is striking: medieval walls and modern scenographic lighting. Open Tuesday to Sunday.

⏱ 30 min
3

The gilded stucco of the Fürstäbtliche Residenz hits you the moment you step into the Thronsaal. Bavaria's first baroque residence, built in the mid-17th century. The Prunkräume rank among the land's finest rococo interiors.

⏱ 45 min
4

Beneath your feet, the Cambodunum archaeological park reconstructs a Roman quarter with baths, forum, and temples. Open March through November, Tuesday to Sunday 10 a.m.–5 p.m. Allow an hour for the visit.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you're traveling with children, the Alpin-Museum inside the Allgäu-Museum tells the story of mountain life at eye level. Interactive and well done. Combinable with the Residenz on the same ticket.

⏱ 45 min

The people of Kempten call the Rathausplatz their living room. In the morning, cafés roll out their tables and the vibe turns Italian. Grab a Milchkaffee facing St. Mang church and its 66-meter bell tower.

⏱ 30 min

Day 4

Kaufbeuren

1

The pastel façades along Kaufbeuren's Kaiser-Max-Straße survived the war without a scratch. The town, founded as a Frankish royal court around 740, kept its medieval lanes intact. You can walk the center in an hour.

⏱ 45 min
2

Built in 1420, the Fünfknopfturm is the town's former watchtower. Its five cupolas give it a toy-like air. 92 steps with no elevator, but the panoramic rooftop view rewards the effort. Open weekends in summer.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Crescentiakloster isn't on the usual tourist circuit, and that's just as well. This 13th-century Franciscan convent still operates. You can visit the Gedenkstätte dedicated to Crescentia Höss, beatified in 2001.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you pass through in July, the Tänzelfest transforms Kaufbeuren for twelve days. It's Bavaria's oldest historical children's festival: parades in medieval costume, torchlit camps. Book accommodation well in advance.

⏱ 60 min

Day 5

Fussen

1

Touristy, yes, but Füssen's old town retains an almost Italian charm with its pastel houses, pedestrian lanes, and café terraces. The Lech flows just below, a striking turquoise green. Allow 90 minutes for a stroll.

⏱ 60 min
2

Füssen locals say the Hohes Schloss is Bavaria's finest secular Gothic castle, and they're not wrong. Trompe-l'oeil paintings on the façade mimic windows and oriels. The Rittersaal and its sculpted coffered ceiling are worth the detour.

⏱ 45 min
3

Kloster St. Mang dates back to the 8th century, when the monk Magnus founded a cell here. The municipal museum now occupies the monastery's baroque halls. You'll see a collection of lutes and violins—Füssen was long a center of luthiery.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Climb to the Kalvarienberg in late afternoon. The ascent takes thirty minutes and offers the best panorama over Füssen, the Lech, and in the distance, Neuschwanstein. The baroque stations of the cross add a spiritual touch.

⏱ 60 min

The Heilig-Geist-Spitalkirche, rebuilt after a 1733 fire, hides astonishing baroque frescoes behind its modest façade. People walk past without noticing, which is a shame. Free entry.

⏱ 20 min

If you like painting, the Staatsgalerie in the Hohes Schloss shows Gothic panels from the 15th and 16th centuries, plus the municipal gallery. Two collections for the price of one ticket.

⏱ 40 min

Day 6

Lechfall Fuessen

1

The Lech cascades down five stone steps across 7.7 meters at the Lechfall, a ten-minute walk from the old town. It's no accident: Füssen locals built these tiers in 1787 to harness hydraulic power. In spring, snowmelt makes the spectacle impressive.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

From the Maxsteg or the 2014 panoramic platform, watch the Lech's power as it rushes into the gorge. Best moment: early morning, when light hits the turquoise water. Free access.

Day 7

Schloss Neuschwanstein

1

Around the bend of the forest path, Schloss Neuschwanstein's white towers surge above the fir trees. Ludwig II launched construction in 1869 on the site of two medieval ruins. The castle mixes Romanesque Revival and Gothic Revival, inspired by Wartburg and Pierrefonds. He lived there just 172 days.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Singers' Hall, on Schloss Neuschwanstein's fourth floor, reproduces Wartburg's. Ludwig II designed it as a "worthy temple" to Richard Wagner. The castle was never finished, and this interrupted construction is part of its legend.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Book your tickets online the day before at hohenschwangau.de. Morning slots sell out first—aim for 9 a.m. The walk up from the Ticket Center takes 30 to 40 minutes. Large backpacks are forbidden inside.

⏱ 30 min

Day 8

Hohenschwangau

1

Less spectacular than Neuschwanstein, Schloss Hohenschwangau is more intimate and more authentic. This is where Ludwig II grew up, surrounded by neo-Gothic frescoes recounting Lohengrin and swan legends. Rebuilt by his father Maximilian II between 1833 and 1837.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swans glide across the Alpsee while you walk the lake loop on foot. The circuit is about 5 km—allow 90 minutes. The trail is flat and shaded, stroller-accessible.

Alpsee Hohenschwangau

1

The Alpsee, just below Hohenschwangau, is paradoxically calm despite Neuschwanstein's crowds. Crystal-clear water, alpine setting, no motors allowed. A bench and twenty minutes of silence will do.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Rent a Tretboot (pedal boat) on the Alpsee to see both castles from a different angle. The water is so clear you can see the bottom several meters down. Rentals at the kiosk on the north side.

Day 10

Wieskirche

1

From outside, a simple country church with pastel walls set in a meadow. Inside, the Wieskirche is a rococo explosion: stucco, gilding, ceiling frescoes in shades of pink and blue. A UNESCO site since 1983, it draws visitors from around the world. Free entry.

⏱ 45 min
2

In 1738, a peasant woman claimed to see tears flowing from a statue of Christ's eyes. This "miracle of tears" triggered a pilgrimage, and the Zimmermann brothers built the Wieskirche between 1745 and 1754. Dominikus Zimmermann loved his work so much he settled next door until his death.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take the time to sit for ten minutes on a bench in the Wieskirche. The light streaming through the windows changes with the hour. Early morning, before the tour groups, the silence is total. On-site parking (paid).

⏱ 20 min

Day 11

Oberammergau

1

Every façade tells a story: biblical scenes, fairy tales, peasant life. Lüftlmalerei, this art of façade frescoes, gives Oberammergau the look of an open-air museum. The village has about 5,000 inhabitants and a density of frescoes per square meter you won't see anywhere else.

⏱ 45 min
2

Locals still call the Pilatushaus the masterpiece of local Lüftlmalerei. Franz Seraph Zwinck painted it in the 18th century in architectural trompe-l'oeil, simulating columns and balconies. The 3D effect is stunning even today.

⏱ 20 min
3

The Passionstheater looks too big for a village this size. Yet every ten years since 1634, the people of Oberammergau have performed Christ's Passion, with over 2,000 local extras. Next edition: 2030. Outside Passion years, the theater hosts concerts and operas in summer.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Step into a Herrgottschnitzer workshop—Oberammergau's wood-carving ateliers. This tradition dates back to the Middle Ages, and the workshops sell directly to the public. Prices are fair for handmade work.

⏱ 30 min

Day 12

Linderhof

1

Schloss Linderhof is the only castle Ludwig II saw completed. Small in size, extravagant in décor: the bedroom drowns in gold, and the Venus Grotto recreates a Tannhäuser scene with an artificial lake and colored lighting. Online booking recommended.

⏱ 90 min
2

Linderhof's Italian-style park unfolds its terraces, fountains, and Moorish pavilion in a valley of the Ammergauer Alpen. The grand fountain spouts every hour in summer. Allow an hour to stroll the gardens.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Linderhof's Italian-style park unfolds its terraces, fountains, and Moorish pavilion in a valley of the Ammergauer Alpen. The grand fountain spouts every hour in summer. Allow an hour to stroll.

⏱ 60 min

Day 13

Hundingshuette

1

The Hundingshütte is a hunting lodge built by Ludwig II in Viking style, inspired by Wagner's Valkyrie. Raw timber structure, a century-old ash tree planted in the roof's center. Thirty-minute walk from Linderhof, gentle ascent.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk back down the forest trail to the Marokkanisches Haus, another exotic pavilion of Ludwig II's in Linderhof park. The contrast between the Viking chalet and the Moorish salon sums up the king's unbridled imagination.

Day 14

Murnau Am Staffelsee

1

It was here, in Murnau's Münter-Haus, that Kandinsky and Gabriele Münter lived between 1909 and 1914 and invented abstract art. The small house with blue shutters, nicknamed the "Russenhaus," has remained in its original state: painted furniture, Hinterglasbilder on the walls. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 45 min
2

Climb to the Schloßmuseum Murnau, whose Blaue Reiter collections have gained international stature since the 2023 KK-Stiftung donation. Kandinsky, Münter, Marc, Jawlensky: the originals are here, in the village that inspired them.

⏱ 60 min
3

From the shore of the Staffelsee, the light changes hour by hour. Franz Marc called this region the "Blaues Land," the Blue Land. You understand why at day's end, when the mountains turn gray and the lake deep green.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If weather permits, rent a Tretboot (pedal boat) on the Staffelsee. The lake is calm, shallow, and reed-lined. An hour is enough to cross and return.

Day 15

Murnauer Moos

1

The Murnauer Moos is Central Europe's largest undrained peat bog. A boardwalk trail crosses this landscape of mosses, dwarf birches, and reeds. Binoculars are useful: curlews, snipes, and harriers nest here.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Allow an hour for the boardwalk loop through the Murnauer Moos. The trail is walkable even after rain. Free access, no ticket booth.

Day 16

Kochel Am See

1

The Franz Marc Museum in Kochel is one of the world's rare museums devoted to a single expressionist. Over 150 works, from blue horses to abstract landscapes. The 2008 modern building integrates into the landscape with bay windows overlooking the lake.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Kochelsee shines emerald green when the sun beats down. From the museum café's terrace, the view of the Herzogstand is the one Franz Marc painted. The lake is swimmable in summer, though the water stays cool.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take the combined museum-boat ticket to chain a visit to the Franz Marc Museum with a Kochelsee cruise. The boat circles in an hour. Available in summer season.

⏱ 60 min

Franz Marc moved to Kochel in 1908, to the hamlet of Ried, seeking alpine light. A 5-km trail links the places he painted. Panels reproduce his paintings facing the original landscapes.

Day 17

Bad Toelz

1

The painted façades of Bad Tölz's Marktstraße line up Lüftlmalereien, alpine gables, and wrought-iron signs along 500 meters. It's postcard Bavaria, in pedestrian, living form. Tölz locals call this street the "loveliest living room of the Oberland."

⏱ 45 min
2

Gabriel von Seidl, a Munich architect, redesigned the Marktstraße façades between 1900 and 1910 in alpine style. The 15th-century Altes Rathaus and its onion-dome tower are the landmark. Thomas Mann used to vacation here.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you're traveling with children, the gravel banks of the Isar at Bad Tölz are a natural playground. The water is cold but crystal clear, and the ducks provide the show. The Taubenloch playground is right next door.

⏱ 45 min

Tölz locals say the Isar is their municipal pool. Cross the bridge and settle on the pebbles of the right bank. Turquoise water rushes between gravel bars. Bring a towel—no changing cabins.

⏱ 30 min

Day 18

Kalvarienberg Bad Toelz

1

Climb Bad Tölz's Kalvarienberg for the panorama over the Isar valley and the Karwendel range. At the top, the Heilig-Kreuz-Kirche houses a Heilige Stiege, a copy of Rome's Scala Santa. Gentle ascent, fifteen minutes.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

From the Kalvarienberg summit, the baroque stations of the cross descend past the Leonhardikapelle. The panorama stretches from Bad Tölz's rooftops to the Karwendel massif. Descent in ten minutes.

Day 19

Tegernsee

1

The former Kloster Tegernsee now houses a grammar school, a restaurant, and the Wittelsbachs' ducal brewery. The mix is improbable but works. The Bräustüberl serves beer brewed on-site, within the walls of an abbey founded in the 8th century.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Pfarrkirche St. Quirinus merits a stop: baroque interior, and the Wittelsbach crypt where Sissi's mother, Duchess Ludovika, rests. Not visitable, but mentioned on a panel at the entrance.

⏱ 20 min
3

The lake shimmers blue-green beneath the Wallberg mountains. Six kilometers long, a kilometer and a half wide, and water that's become among Europe's cleanest thanks to efforts by the lakeside communes.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Circle the Tegernsee by boat: shuttles link Tegernsee, Rottach-Egern, Bad Wiessee, and Gmund. An hour's cruise, cappuccino on deck, views of the peaks. Departures every hour in season.

⏱ 60 min

Founded in the 8th century, the Benedictine monastery was secularized in 1803 then converted into a royal residence. The Wittelsbachs' brewery still operates in the north wing. Order a Tegernseer Spezial and a Brezn at the Bräustüberl—it's a local ritual.

⏱ 45 min

Day 20

Prien Am Chiemsee

1

Prien am Chiemsee has nothing spectacular in itself, but it's the gateway to the "bayerisches Meer," Bavaria's largest lake. The small spa town and its quiet streets contrast with the bustle of the harbor where Herrenchiemsee visitors embark.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Chiemsee-Bahn has linked Prien station to Stock harbor since 1887, covering 1.8 km at a top speed of 15 km/h. It's the oldest steam tramway still in service. Eight-minute journey, guaranteed time-travel sensation.

⏱ 15 min

Activities

If you're looking for a swimming spot, the Badeplatz Schollkopf at Prien offers direct access to the Chiemsee with lawn, showers, and mountain views. Free entry, paid parking.

⏱ 60 min

Day 21

Fraueninsel Chiemsee

1

The Fraueninsel is a fishing village set on water, with a Benedictine convent active since the 8th century. You can walk around it in twenty minutes, between vegetable gardens, pottery workshops, and Tassilo's thousand-year-old linden tree. No cars, no noise.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Abtei Frauenwörth sells its own Klosterlikör and marzipan in a small shop at the entrance. The Carolingian Torhalle, a 9th-century remnant, is the island's oldest building.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Settle in at the Klosterwirt for a smoked Renke (whitefish), caught that morning from the Chiemsee. The terrace overlooks the lake and Herreninsel. One of the best quality-pleasure ratios on the whole trip.

⏱ 45 min

Day 22

Herreninsel Chiemsee

1

Leaving the boat, a forest path leads to Schloss Herrenchiemsee in fifteen minutes (or by carriage, like Ludwig II). The park fountains spout facing the Great Hall of Mirrors, a copy of Versailles'. The king slept there just ten nights before dying in 1886.

⏱ 90 min
2

Ludwig II bought the island in 1873 and launched construction in 1878 with architect Georg Dollmann. The castle was granted UNESCO status in 2025, along with Neuschwanstein and Linderhof. The ground-floor Ludwig II Museum displays his baptismal gown and death mask.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Bavarians call the Chiemsee their "sea." After visiting the castle, walk to the island's southern tip. No tourists, just trees and the lake stretching to the horizon. Allow two hours on the island in total.
