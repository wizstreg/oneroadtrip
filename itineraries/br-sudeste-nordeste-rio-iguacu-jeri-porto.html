Leme

★★★★☆
1

Corcovado towers at 710 meters above sea level. Christ the Redeemer, unveiled in 1931, stands 28 meters tall with outstretched arms spanning 28 meters. From the summit platform, a 360-degree panorama unfolds: Copacabana, Ipanema, Guanabara Bay, Sugarloaf Mountain, and the emerald rainforest of Tijuca stretching to every horizon.

⏱ 90 min
2

Sugarloaf Mountain (396 m) is reached via two cable cars from Praia Vermelha. The view sweeps across the entire city—spectacular in late afternoon when golden light floods the bay and illuminates the granite peaks rising from the Atlantic.

⏱ 120 min
3

Tijuca Forest covers 33 square kilometers—the largest urban tropical rainforest on Earth. Ocelots, monkeys, and over 300 bird species thrive beneath the canopy. Parque Lage combines manicured gardens, waterfalls, a 19th-century mansion, and a contemporary art gallery set against jungle-clad mountainsides.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Walk the iconic wave-patterned boardwalk of Copacabana early in the morning, stretching 4.5 kilometers to Ipanema. Joggers, beach volleyball players, and surfers claim the sand at dawn. Leave valuables at your hotel.

⏱ 90 min

Head to Arpoador, the rocky point between Copacabana and Ipanema, thirty minutes before sunset. Cariocas gather on the rocks and applaud as the sun sinks into the Atlantic—a daily ritual that captures the soul of Rio.

⏱ 30 min

Leme

★★★★☆
1

Escadaria Selarón climbs 215 steps covered with over 2,000 ceramic tiles collected from around the world. This vibrant staircase connects the bohemian nightlife district of Lapa to the hillside neighborhood of Santa Teresa—a living mosaic created by Chilean artist Jorge Selarón over two decades.

⏱ 30 min
2

Santa Teresa is Rio's Montmartre: steep cobblestone streets, pastel colonial houses with wrought-iron balconies, artist studios spilling onto sidewalks, and the historic yellow tram rattling through tree-lined lanes.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Metropolitan Cathedral of São Sebastião (1964-1979) rises as a conical concrete pyramid 96 meters tall and 104 meters in diameter. Four towering stained-glass windows run from floor to ceiling, flooding the interior with kaleidoscopic light.

⏱ 30 min
4

Lagoa Rodrigo de Freitas offers 7.5 kilometers of lakeside cycling path with views of the city's dramatic peaks. Pedal boats and kayaks are available for rent along the waterfront promenade.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike from Parque Lage to Corcovado: three hours through Tijuca's tropical jungle, gaining 700 meters of elevation. The trail weaves beneath canopy trees, past waterfalls, and emerges at the base of Christ the Redeemer.

⏱ 180 min

Take a sunset cruise across Guanabara Bay. Sugarloaf and Corcovado glow in the fading light as the boat glides past colonial forts and under the Rio-Niterói Bridge—the city seen from the perspective of arriving sailors.

⏱ 120 min

Foz Do Iguacu

★★★★☆
1

Iguazu Falls from the Brazilian side: 275 cascades thunder across 2.7 kilometers of tiered basalt cliffs. The 1,200-meter boardwalk trail delivers sweeping panoramic views of the entire waterfall system—80% of which lies on Argentine territory. UNESCO World Heritage since 1986.

⏱ 180 min
2

Parque das Aves, located 100 meters from the national park entrance, shelters 1,300 birds representing 130 species across 16 hectares of Atlantic rainforest. Walk-through aviaries bring you face-to-face with toucans, parrots, cassowaries, and macaws in their forest habitat.

⏱ 120 min
3

Itaipu Dam, built between 1975 and 1984 on the Paraná River between Brazil and Paraguay, ranks among the world's largest hydroelectric power stations. Its 20 turbines generate 15% of Brazil's electricity and 90% of Paraguay's—a feat of engineering visible from space.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Arrive at the Brazilian national park at dawn, right when gates open at 7am, before tour groups flood the trails. Early morning light illuminates the cascades and creates rainbows in the rising mist—the falls at their most magical.

⏱ 120 min

Macuco Safari boat ride: a zodiac navigates upriver from the Brazilian side to the base of the falls, then plunges directly through a thundering curtain of water. You will be drenched—bring a change of clothes.

⏱ 90 min

Foz Do Iguacu

★★★★☆
1

On the Argentine side, the upper and lower circuits of Iguazú National Park bring you within arm's reach of the falls—first at eye level, then at their thundering base. Boardwalks thread through veils of water so close you feel the vibration in your chest.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Devil's Throat—reached via a boardwalk extending over the Iguazú River—drops 80 meters into a U-shaped chasm that generates a permanent cloud of mist and spray. The most visceral and overwhelming experience Iguazú has to offer.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Visit the Triple Frontier landmark at the confluence of Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay. Three obelisks painted in national colors face each other across the Paraná River—the best vantage point for sunset, when the sky turns copper and the rivers merge in molten gold.

⏱ 60 min

Jijoca De Jericoacoara

★★★★☆
1

Jericoacoara—a car-free village of sand streets nestled between dunes and the Atlantic—has been protected within an 8,850-hectare national park since 2002. No asphalt, no traffic lights: feet sink into powdery white sand with every step. Low-slung houses, colorful pousadas, and cascades of bougainvillea create the atmosphere of a world's-end hideaway.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk barefoot through the village's sandy lanes. Street vendors offer açaí bowls and fresh coconut water while brightly painted jangadas (traditional sailboats) sway offshore against the turquoise Atlantic.

⏱ 60 min

After dark, the beach in front of Rua Principal transforms into an open-air celebration: caipirinha stands, DJs spinning forró and brega funk, and capoeira circles forming at sunset—Jericoacoara's nightly ritual under the stars.

⏱ 90 min

Jijoca De Jericoacoara

★★★★☆
1

Pedra Furada—a natural sandstone arch sculpted by Atlantic erosion—stands a 30-minute scramble over coastal boulders from the village. Accessible only at low tide, it frames the ocean like a portal to the horizon. From mid-June to mid-August, the sun sets precisely through the stone window.

⏱ 90 min
2

Lagoa do Paraíso, a 30-minute buggy ride from Jeri, spreads across 15 square kilometers of crystalline freshwater edged by hammocks suspended over the shallows. Floating bars serve caipirinhas and ceviche beneath palm-thatch canopies—paradise earned its name.

⏱ 120 min
3

Lagoa Azul, wilder than its neighbor Paraíso, shifts color with the angle of sunlight—from emerald green to lagoon blue. Constant trade winds make it a renowned kitesurfing and windsurfing destination.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Eastern buggy excursion: visit the Árvore da Preguiça (Lazy Tree)—a wind-sculpted mangrove with horizontal branches—then Praia do Preá, a fishing village and kitesurfing hotspot, before reaching Lagoa Azul and Lagoa do Paraíso for lunch with your feet in the water.

⏱ 300 min

1

Serrote—a rocky formation rising 95 meters above the dunes—offers 360-degree views from its lighthouse perch: the village, infinite dunes, and the Atlantic stretching to the edge of the world. The trail winds through caves, natural pools, and wind-carved rock formations.

⏱ 90 min
2

Buraco Azul—a former quarry flooded by seasonal rains—forms an intensely turquoise lake enclosed by rock walls. Jump from a three-meter ledge into translucent water below—one of the region's most photographed swimming holes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Western buggy excursion to Tatajuba: towering mobile dunes, isolated fishing villages, freshwater lagoons formed by seasonal rains, and a zipline over the lagoon. The return route via Barrinha delivers a sunset behind endless dunes rolling toward the horizon.

⏱ 240 min

Climb Sunset Dune—the massive sand mountain at the village entrance—thirty minutes before sundown. Dozens gather here every evening for the spectacle: the sun sinking into the Atlantic as the sand glows pink, then copper, then gold.

Porto De Galinhas

★★★★☆
1

The natural pools of Porto de Galinhas form at low tide among coral reefs offshore. Traditional jangada sailboats ferry swimmers ten minutes to turquoise basins where tropical fish, starfish, and sea urchins drift through less than a meter of crystalline water. The "Mapa do Brasil"—a coral formation shaped like the country—is the iconic photo spot.

⏱ 120 min
2

Pontal de Maracaípe, at the mouth of the Maracaípe River, shelters a mangrove ecosystem where seahorses reproduce. Jangada fishermen dive to show the tiny cavalos-marinhos in a jar before releasing them. Sunset from the pontal—with coconut palms and mangroves silhouetted against golden light—is the region's finest.

⏱ 120 min
3

Projeto Hippocampus, in the village center, is a research facility dedicated to seahorse reproduction and conservation. Observation aquariums and educational displays explain the life cycle of these threatened creatures.

⏱ 45 min
4

Praia de Muro Alto, to the north, boasts one of the region's largest natural pools: a coral wall traps water at low tide across more than a kilometer. Calm shallow water, white sand, and swaying palms—ideal for families.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Buggy tour from beach to beach: Cupe for surfing, Maracaípe for waves and seahorses, Muro Alto for the expansive natural pools. Each stop reveals a different coastal landscape of reefs, dunes, and coconut groves.

⏱ 180 min

Walk along the beach from Porto de Galinhas to Cupe, then onward to Muro Alto (8 kilometers one way). At low tide, Cupe's natural pools offer peaceful snorkeling away from the crowds near the village center.

Recife

★★★★☆
1

Recife Antigo—the old port district on its namesake island—centers around Praça do Marco Zero. Rua do Bom Jesus harbors the Kahal Zur Israel Synagogue, the oldest in the Americas (1636), while restored warehouses now house art galleries and cocktail bars.

⏱ 90 min
2

Olinda—seven kilometers from Recife—has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1982. Founded in 1535, burned by the Dutch in 1631, then rebuilt, the town cascades across eight hills facing the Atlantic: twenty baroque churches, convents, and pastel-painted colonial houses beneath a forest of bell towers.

⏱ 120 min
3

Alto da Sé—Olinda's highest point—combines the Cathedral da Sé, tapioca vendors, and a vertiginous lookout. From here, the view sweeps across terracotta rooftops, church steeples, a fringe of coconut palms, and in the distance, the glass towers of Recife rising from the coastal plain.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wander Olinda's cobblestone lanes on foot: artist studios open onto galleries of naïve paintings, wooden sculptures, and giant carnival masks. Taste freshly made stuffed tapioca while watching the sun set from Alto da Sé, gilding the colonial town in amber light.

⏱ 90 min
