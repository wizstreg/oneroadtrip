Klodzko

1

Perched on its rocky spur since the 10th century, Kłodzko Fortress dominates the town from atop its baroque bastions. Rebuilt in the 17th and 18th centuries by the Austrians and then the Prussians, it includes a labyrinth of galleries stretching 40 kilometers underground. The guided tour of the labyrinth lasts 40 minutes, covering 1 km of tunnels at 6–8°C year-round: bring a sweater even in summer.

⏱ 90 min
2

In the tunnels of Kłodzko Fortress, the air plummets abruptly to 6–8°C all year, ceilings drop low, some passages force you to stoop. The 600-meter Underground Tourist Route dates partly from the 13th century—cellars connecting beneath the old town and sapping galleries. You emerge on Czeska Street, at the foot of the fortress, having traversed 400 years of history without changing levels.

⏱ 45 min
3

The 13th-century Gothic bridge over the Nysa Kłodzka is often compared to Prague's Charles Bridge—in miniature. The comparison is flattering but fair: same grey stone, same medieval bearing, same chapel in the center. Kłodzko sat on the Wrocław–Prague trade route, and this bridge was its tollgate. View from the lower embankment for the best photo of the entire route.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Follow the 600-meter Underground Tourist Route from Zawiszy Czarnego Street to the exit on Czeska Street—ticket separate from the fortress, audio guide available in English. The old beer cellars and medieval warehouses take about 20 minutes to explore. Open daily except Monday off-season. Admission around 20 PLN for adults.

⏱ 30 min

If time permits, climb to the bastions of Kłodzko Fortress for the panorama over the valley encircled by Sudeten peaks. The lapidarium gathers medieval sculptures collected throughout the Kłodzko region. The fortress is also the only place in town where the Gestapo left visible traces—a detail guides rarely mention.

⏱ 60 min

Bardo

1

The 42.7-cm Romanesque beech-wood statuette displayed in Bardo's basilica is the oldest Romanesque sculpture in Lower Silesia—12th century, attributed to a Rhenish workshop. The main altarpiece painted in 1705 by Michael Willmann, dubbed the Silesian Rembrandt, forms its baroque setting. The basilica (1686–1704, architect Michal Klein) received the title of Minor Basilica in 2009.

⏱ 45 min
2

Incense and plainchant echo beneath the vaults adorned with frescoes in the Basilica of the Visitation at Bardo—an active Marian shrine, not a museum. John Paul II stopped here in 1957 and in 1978, before his papal election. Wax ex-votos cover an entire wall. Free entry; avoid Sunday mornings if you're not there for the pilgrimage.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb to the chapel on Bard Mountain—70 minutes from Bardo's center via the Rosary Stations path—to the spot where, according to tradition, the Virgin appeared weeping in 1440 and left her imprint in the rock. The view over the Nysa Kłodzka valley from the summit is striking. In dry weather, the ascent is easy.

⏱ 150 min

Srebrna Gora

1

Never conquered—even Napoleon had to give up taking Srebrna Góra Fortress in 1807. This mountain fortress, considered the largest in Europe, looms over the village from its three summits. The central Donjon could house 4,000 soldiers in 151 rooms with walls up to 12 meters thick in places. Guides in Napoleonic soldier costumes add life to the visit.

⏱ 90 min
2

Built between 1765 and 1777 by order of Frederick II of Prussia, Srebrna Góra Fortress was meant to protect Prussian Silesia against Austria. Engineer Ludwig Wilhelm Regler's plans were amended by the king himself. It has been a Polish National Monument since 2004 and is a candidate for UNESCO inscription in a transnational dossier with Germany's Königstein and Italy's Fenestrelle.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit the Donjon on a guided tour—the only way to access the bombproof casemates and the 151 rooms from the 18th century. In summer on weekends, the Fort Fan Express ski lift connects Srebrna Góra pass to the fort. Admission around 30–40 PLN; check opening times beforehand (closed outside tourist season).

⏱ 90 min

Zloty Stok

1

Water plunges 10 meters through the darkness of the Black Gallery at the Złoty Stok Gold Mine—Poland's only underground waterfall. The spectacle is amplified by the acoustics of the marble mining chamber. The gallery is crossed on a walkway at the edge of the precipice. Bring a light rain jacket: spray reaches curious visitors who venture too close.

⏱ 60 min
2

Over seven centuries of operation, the Złoty Stok mine yielded roughly 16 tonnes of pure gold. In the 16th century, production reached 150 kg per year—about 8% of European output. The mine closed in 1962, was drained, then reopened as a tourist route in 1996. The 500-meter Gertrude Gallery traces medieval mining techniques with period models and tools.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Ride the orange underground tram for the 300-meter return section—the most improbable option on the entire route. At the end of the tour, try gold panning in the stream: a few flakes guaranteed for kids. Total tour duration 1.5 hours. Online booking advised in July–August.

⏱ 90 min

Ladek Zdroj

1

Poles call it the country's oldest spa town—Lądek-Zdrój's springs are documented as early as 1325, and bath houses existed here before 1241. The sulfate-fluoride waters charged with radon are unique in Poland. Goethe ranks among the 19th-century celebrity cure-takers. The medieval town received its charter in 1282.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Wojciech building, constructed in 1680 at Plac Mariański 1, is the historic heart of Lądek's cure. Remodeled in neo-baroque style in 1880 on the model of Turkish hammams, it shelters beneath its dome a marble basin fed with thermal water directly from the spring. Water temperatures range from 22 to 44°C depending on the source. Open for day visitors who aren't taking the cure.

⏱ 90 min
3

The medieval old town of Lądek-Zdrój terraces down to the Biała Lądecka—the best reason not to stay only in the spa quarter. Renaissance arcaded facades on the central square, cobbled lanes descending to the river, the watchtower—all have existed since the 16th century in a remarkable state of preservation. On foot from the thermal park: 10 minutes.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you're here for the cure, the radon-sulfur baths at Wojciech or the Jerzy center last 15 minutes followed by a 30-minute lying rest for cutaneous absorption—a medical procedure, not an ordinary spa. Day rate around 60–120 PLN depending on treatment. For a curious dip without a prescription, Wojciech's thermal pools are also open for day use.

⏱ 120 min

Sample the Zdzisław and Maria Skłodowska-Curie waters at the Wojciech complex's pump room—a glass of each is enough to grasp their strong sulfurous taste. The Marie Curie spring has no biographical link to the physicist: the name was given after 1945. Then stroll the Spa Park on 2 km of shaded paths along the Biała Lądecka.

⏱ 60 min

Kletno

1

Discovered by accident in 1966 during an explosion in a marble quarry, the Bear Cave is today the longest cave in the Sudetes—5 kilometers of galleries on two levels, with 360 meters open to the public. Bones of cave bears, cave lions, and Pleistocene hyenas were found inside. Groups limited to 15 people: book in advance during the season.

⏱ 60 min
2

Six degrees year-round in the Bear Cave: even in the height of July, a jacket is mandatory. The stalactites and calcite columns have the distinctive tawny hue of this marble deposit. The 45-minute guided tour ends with an ascent through bare rock. Touching the concretions is strictly forbidden—a rule carefully enforced by the guides.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Visit the Kletno Uranium Mine 200 meters from the cave, in the opposite direction: a decommissioned uranium mine opened as an educational tourist route, with a spoil heap known as a mineral-collecting site. Count on 800 meters walking from the shared parking. Open in season only. The mine tells the little-known story of Soviet uranium extraction in Silesia after 1945.

⏱ 60 min

Stronie Slaskie

1

Śnieżnik at 1,425 m is the highest peak of the eastern Polish Sudetes—and the view from the observation tower confirms the obvious: Śnieżka and the Karkonosze to the northwest, the Czech Jeseníky to the south, the Kotlina Kłodzka reliefs all around. An honest effort for a rare visual reward. Ascent from Sienna via the red trail: 2.5 hours one way.

⏱ 180 min
2

From the summit of Śnieżnik, in clear weather, you can spot Śnieżka—the roof of the Sudetes at 1,602 m—more than 80 kilometers away. The massif forms the watershed between the Vistula, Oder, and Danube basins. The Polish-Czech border runs exactly through the summit. The observation tower at the top is accessible year-round.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Ride the gondola from Czarna Góra Resort to the summit of Czarna Góra (1,205 m), then continue on foot via the red trail to Śnieżnik (1,425 m)—2 hours walking through beech forest. In winter, the resort offers 20 slopes and Poland's fastest 6-person gondola. Parking at the resort, paid in high season.

⏱ 300 min

Miedzygorze

1

Wilczki Falls plunges 22 meters into the so-called American canyon—25-meter vertical walls, thunderous echo—and it's the second-highest waterfall in the Polish Sudetes, after Kamieńczyk Falls. Before the 1997 flood, a 19th-century artificial sill raised it to 27 meters. The Wilczki Waterfall Nature Reserve has been protected since 1958.

⏱ 45 min
2

The roar of water ricochets off the American canyon's walls: even in dry weather, spray soaks spectators on the lower walkways. In winter, the cascade freezes into spectacular icicle towers. Paid parking 200 m away, 10-minute walk up wooden stairs. Free access to the waterfall year-round.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take the red trail to the MB Śnieżna sanctuary on Igliczna (845 m)—1.5 hours from the village, beech forest and open views over the Śnieżnik massif. Międzygórze is the classic starting point for a hike to Śnieżnik. The return trail via Górka passes a fairy-tale sculpture garden, appreciated with children.

⏱ 180 min

Bystrzyca Klodzka

1

Nicknamed the Silesian Carcassonne—and the comparison holds: the medieval ramparts of Bystrzyca Kłodzka encircle a terraced old town whose 14th-century plan is intact. The Water Gate (Porta Aquae) and the Kłodzko Tower dominate the panorama from the river. The town is often ignored by tourists rushing to Kłodzko—which is exactly what makes it pleasant.

⏱ 60 min
2

The curator of the Philumeny Museum makes it clear from the start: this is Poland's only museum dedicated to matchboxes and lighting accessories. Founded in 1964 in a former Evangelical church, it displays thousands of matchbox labels and vintage lighters from around the world. The town had a match factory—the museum is its unexpected legacy.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Climb the Kłodzko Tower for the view over the steep valley and the ridges of the Bystrzyckie Mountains. From the Rynek, the climb takes 15 minutes. On the way, spot the Paczków Gate, remnant of the 14th-century walls financed by John of Luxembourg, King of Bohemia. The Philumeny Museum is open Tuesday to Sunday.

⏱ 60 min

Polanica Zdroj

1

Since 1906, the Spa Park of Polanica-Zdrój has spread over 13 hectares in the very center—main promenade, gazebos, protected century-old rhododendrons. The spa town's history begins in 1828 with the first captures of the Józef spring. This is where Staropolanka is produced, a mineral water sold throughout Poland since the 1970s.

⏱ 60 min
2

The water from the Wielka Pieniawa spring rises from 34 meters depth in the Art Nouveau Pump Room (1911–1913, architects Paul Rother and Felix Wild): the classicist building tinged with Jugendstil is the most elegant in the spa valley. A glass of this water charged with magnesium, calcium, and lithium leaves a persistent metallic taste. Open to all, free entry.

⏱ 45 min
3

Polanica's Mineral Water Pump Room is probably the finest spa building in the Kotlina Kłodzka: pilasters, stained glass, stone colonnade, opus sectile floor. Restored after the 1998 floods, it regained its original state thanks to archival photographs. Inside too, the Art Nouveau basins are worth a look even without thirst.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

For medical treatments, Uzdrowisko Polanica-Zdrój offers more than 110 different procedures—mineral baths, mud, dry CO2, cryotherapy. For passing travelers, the Słoneczne Wielka Pieniawa Spa (thermal water park) is accessible without prescription: two heated pools, Finnish saunas, and hammam. Water park rate around 60–80 PLN/half-day.

⏱ 120 min

Slide down the dry toboggan or lounge around the giant open-air chessboard in the park. The central promenade also hosts sculptures and a bandstand where concerts are held in summer. Bike rental available near the park's north entrance for riding along the Bystrzyca Dusznicka.

⏱ 60 min

Duszniki Zdroj

1

The young Frédéric Chopin was 16 when he played his first charity concerts outside Russian partition in Duszniki in 1826. The Chopin House (Dwór Chopina) in the Spa Park has remained an active concert venue: since 1946, the International Chopin Festival has been held there each summer—one of the oldest festivals dedicated to the composer in the world.

⏱ 45 min
2

In an 1826 letter to his friend Białobłocki, Chopin specifies that 'the paper on which I write to you comes from Reinerz'—Reinerz being the German name for Duszniki. This letter makes the Museum of Papermaking a Chopin pilgrimage site. The 17th-century paper mill, one of the few still operational in Central Europe, makes handmade paper by hand, visible during tours.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit the Museum of Papermaking, classified as a Polish National Monument in 2011 on the Bystrzyca Dusznicka: handmade papermaking workshops available for all ages on weekends. Duration about 1 hour. In August, the Paper Festival animates the site for two days. The Spa Park with the Pump Room and Chopin monument can be visited on foot from the museum in 15 minutes.

⏱ 90 min

Kudowa Zdroj

1

From outside, an ordinary baroque chapel in the Czermna district. Inside the Skull Chapel, 3,000 human skulls line walls and ceiling. In the crypt below rest 20,000 to 30,000 additional bones—victims of the Silesian wars and 18th-century epidemics. One of only three ossuaries of this kind in Europe. Photography forbidden inside.

⏱ 30 min
2

Between 1776 and 1784, parish priest Wacław Tomaszek devoted 18 years of his life to building the Czermna Skull Chapel, collecting and arranging bones from Silesian battlefields. The adjoining St. Bartholomew's Chapel dates from the 17th century. Czermna lies 2 km north of Kudowa's center. Paid parking on Kościuszki Street, free parking on Moniuszki Street.

⏱ 20 min
3

The Spa Park of Kudowa-Zdrój, laid out on the model of English gardens in the 18th century, is one of the best-maintained in the entire Kotlina Kłodzka. Exotic plants acclimatized over two centuries, the Moniuszko and Śniadecki spring pump rooms, summer concerts under the linden trees make it an ideal stop. Kudowa water is prescribed for heart and circulatory diseases.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The sweet smell of sulfur and CO2 rises from the Moniuszko spring in Kudowa's Pump Room: a sensory experience that contrasts with the usual sanitized spa. The cure building's architecture recalls Wilhelminian style. Free entry for therapeutic water tasting, paid for treatments.

⏱ 30 min

Stroll through Kudowa's Spa Park to the Wodny Świat Water Park—92-meter slide, jacuzzi, artificial river. Ping-pong tables and the giant chessboard please families. Kudowa is a few kilometers from the Czech border: the town of Náchod and local breweries merit a detour.

Szczytna

1

This 22-hectare labyrinth in the Góry Stołowe looks like nothing natural—yet it's entirely so. The Błędne Skały (Errant Rocks) are Cretaceous sandstone formations sculpted by erosion at 853 m altitude in the Table Mountains National Park. Entry ticket required, with online booking in season.

⏱ 60 min
2

In the 17th century, the Błędne Skały served as a smuggling route between Bohemia and Prussian Silesia: inscriptions carved into the smooth sandstone still commemorate arrest dates. The route is one-way over 1.5 km, impossible to turn back as the passages are too narrow. Duration 40 minutes. Bring shoes with non-slip soles.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take the one-way circuit of Błędne Skały early in the morning to avoid human traffic jams in the narrowest passages. The upper parking (limited) is only open in season; the lower parking (free) requires an additional 4 km on foot. In July–August, the wait can exceed 2 hours. Online ticket advised at pngs.gov.pl.

Karlow

1

The 665 stone steps of Szczeliniec Wielki were carved in 1814 by Franz Pabel, mayor of Karłów and Europe's first official mountain guide—he led his guests to the summit 3 to 4 times a day. These steps still lead to the highest point of the Góry Stołowe at 919 m. The ascent trail from Karłów takes 40 minutes. A PTTK mountain shelter is open year-round at the summit.

⏱ 90 min
2

From the Fotel Pradziada (Grandfather's Armchair), the highest point of Szczeliniec, the Karkonosze spread across the horizon in clear weather. The crevices of the summit labyrinth descend 18 meters and sometimes hold snow until July. An inscription carved in 1576 (IHSV—In hoc signo vinces) is the earliest known human trace on this summit.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Descend from Szczeliniec via the red trail to the shelter and continue to Pasterka for sunset over the valley. From Karłów, Błędne Skały (8 km via green trail) can be combined into a full day's hike. Site entry fee; mandatory booking in July–August at pngs.gov.pl. The Na Szczelińcu shelter serves hot meals.

Wambierzyce

1

Pilgrims have called Wambierzyce the Silesian Jerusalem since the 18th century—and the resemblance is deliberate: a stream named Cedron runs through the village, 17 gates symbolize the gates of Jerusalem, and the baroque basilica (1715–1723) was built on a model inspired by Solomon's Temple. Wambierzyce's Calvary counts 79 chapels scattered over the neighboring hills.

⏱ 45 min
2

Fifty-seven symbolic steps lead to the facade of Wambierzyce's baroque basilica—the combined age of Jesus and Mary according to tradition. The edifice was financed by Count Franz Anton von Götzen between 1715 and 1723. The 1850 mechanical Nativity represents 250 mechanically animated figures carved from linden wood—Poland's oldest mechanical crèche, the work of clockmaker Longinus Wittig.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Explore the Calvary with its 79 chapels spread over the village hills—the complete procession requires 2 hours of walking. The Wittig mechanical Nativity is displayed in the family house (paid visit, separate entry from the basilica). The basilica is open from 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. from May to September.

Radkow

1

The Radków Rocks rise above the town of Radków like a movie backdrop: sandstone needles, mushroom rocks, and tableau-shaped cliffs dominate the Góry Stołowe plateau from the heights of the national park. From Radków's Rynek, the access trail climbs in 30 minutes. Free access—unlike the paid sites in the neighboring park.

⏱ 60 min
2

Radków is modest, but its position as the northern gateway to the Góry Stołowe makes it an ideal base for hikers. The Gothic Church of St. Dorothy, one of the oldest in the region, can be visited in a few minutes. The market square surrounded by arcaded houses retains the quiet atmosphere of a border town forgotten by the main tourist routes.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

From Radków, take the Szosa Stu Zakrętów (Road of a Hundred Bends) to Karłów by car: 12 km of hairpins through the national park, with panoramas of sandstone formations at every turn. It's the most spectacular scenic route in the entire Kotlina Kłodzka. Closed to freight transport, open to cars.
