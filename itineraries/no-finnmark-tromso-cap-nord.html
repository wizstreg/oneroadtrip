✨ Highlights▾
  • The Alta River: fishing rights for non-residents reaching 30,000 NOK for three days, salmon over 25kg, highly coveted annual lottery—King Harald V has fished here more than 25 times.
  • Tromsø's Fjellheisen: cable car inaugurated in 1961, cabins named Seal and Polar Bear, 4 minutes to reach Storsteinen at 421m—one of the city's best northern lights spots.
  • Hjemmeluft rock carvings in Alta: Norway's only UNESCO prehistoric site (listed in 1985), over 6,000 carvings along a 3km boardwalk trail open from May to October.
  • North Cape: 307m cliff on Magerøya island, midnight sun visible from May 13 to July 29, accessible via a 6.8km undersea tunnel at 212m depth.

Northern Norway Road Trip: Tromsø, Alta & North Cape

📅 10 days📍 10 stops🚗 1290 km⏱ 19h45

Tromso → Lyngen → Alta → Honningsvag → Hammerfest

Tromso → Lyngen → Alta → Honningsvag → Hammerfest

This 10-day road trip takes you to the northernmost tip of Europe, from Tromsø's Arctic Cathedral to the 307m cliffs of North Cape. Along the way, you'll drive alongside the Lyngen Alps and their 140 glaciers, hike to the turquoise lake Blåisvatnet, discover the UNESCO rock carvings of Alta engraved between 4200 and 500 BC, and stop in Hammerfest to understand how an entire town rebuilt itself after being razed by the Nazis in 1944. In summer, the midnight sun bathes everything in golden light from May 13 to July 29—in winter, the northern lights take over.

The Hjemmeluft rock carvings are Norway's only UNESCO-listed prehistoric site (since 1985)—and the largest ensemble of hunter-gatherer rock art in Northern Europe. ⏱ 90 min
2

The Alta Museum, installed directly on the rock carving site since 1991, presents carving techniques, the life of Arctic hunter-gatherers, and the northern lights—the first permanent aurora observatory was built in Alta in the late 19th century.

⏱ 60 min
3

Alta's Nordlyskatedralen (Northern Lights Cathedral), inaugurated in 2013, stands out for its titanium spiral rising toward the sky. It evokes a northern light frozen in architecture—the visit is brief but the exterior is worth the detour.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The Sautso canyon on the Alta River is Northern Europe's deepest canyon with walls reaching 400m height over 22km length. Boat excursions allow you to travel alongside the cliffs from Lake Storvatnet—check local departures from Alta.

⏱ 150 min

The Alta River is one of the world's most coveted salmon rivers—Atlantic salmon regularly exceed 25kg here, and fishing rights for non-residents reach the equivalent of 30,000 NOK for three days. Each year, a lottery distributes 70 fishing days to outside anglers among 1,200 applicants. Even without a fishing rod, walking along the banks under the midnight sun is breathtaking.

2

The Sami Siida, just outside Alta, is a traditional Sami camp where you can feed reindeer, listen to stories around the fire, and taste bidos—Sami reindeer stew—in a real lavvu. It's one of the most accessible Sami cultural experiences from Alta.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

In winter, go dog sledding on the Finnmarksvidda—Norway's largest plateau stretches endlessly without a light or tree in sight. Local mushers offer 2-3 hour outings with huskies. In summer, guided hikes with the dogs are also organized.

⏱ 150 min

Alta is one of the world's northern lights capitals in winter—the surrounding countryside, far from city lights, offers a perfectly dark sky. The best nights are from September to March, between 6pm and 2am.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll along Hammerfest's waterfront: the harbor is lively, the multicolored 1950s houses tell the story of reconstruction, and you can watch seabirds from the pier in summer.

The E6 route back to Tromsø follows the Kvænangenfjord and Reisafjord—stop at viewpoints facing the sea arms that penetrate inland. The Arctic light of late afternoon tints these landscapes with a particular quality you won't see anywhere else.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Reisa, a 100km river descending from the Finnish tundra to the sea, winds through a national park with steep rock walls. A quick stop at the Storslett viewpoint lets you see the river flowing through the gorges—one of northern Norway's most beautiful salmon rivers.

⏱ 45 min
3

The ferry crossing of the Lyngenfjord on the return route lasts about twenty minutes but offers an unobstructed view of the Lyngen Alps from the water, with glaciers clearly visible above the fjords—a different image from Day 2.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

This Alta → Tromsø transit day takes about 6.5 hours with breaks and the ferry. The light changes every hour, fjords succeed one another without ever repeating, and traffic is nearly nonexistent. The real advice: start early, stop often, and save some energy for the final hour of driving.
