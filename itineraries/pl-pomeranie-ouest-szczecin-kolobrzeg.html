☀️ Weather

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather with mild temperatures (15-23°C), blooming landscapes, and longer days. Ideal for city exploration and outdoor activities without summer crowds.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather (18-25°C) with peak tourist season, highest prices, but best conditions for festivals, hiking, and outdoor experiences. Long daylight hours perfect for sightseeing.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent weather (12-20°C) with beautiful fall colors, fewer crowds, and moderate prices. Great for cultural tourism and photography.

Winter (November-March): Cold weather (-2 to 5°C) with potential snow, Christmas markets, and cozy indoor culture. Lowest prices, fewer tourists, magical winter atmosphere.

Szczecin

★★★☆☆
1

The smell of sea salt drifts over the Wały Chrobrego, the terrace built in the early 20th century above the Oder River. Neoclassical buildings, museums and a theatre line up facing the river. Come at sunset: the low light turns the façades to gold.

⏱ 45 min
2

Built from the 14th century onwards, the Zamek Książąt Pomorskich dominates Szczecin with its pale green turrets. The castle housed the Griffin dukes until the 17th century; today, two large courtyards, a museum and a panoramic terrace to the north. Reduced admission with the Szczecin Tourist Card.

⏱ 60 min
3

Underrated, Szczecin's Stare Miasto was rebuilt belatedly, in the 1990s. The Ratusz (town hall) houses a small museum on the city's history. Allow half an hour for a tour of the old quarter, much smaller than Gdańsk's.

⏱ 30 min
4

Head down to the Łasztownia district, a former port slaughterhouse converted into a red-brick cultural centre. The Craneosauruses, luminous dinosaur-crane sculptures, watch over the quays. In the evening, the lighting makes them even more striking.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you visit in summer, the Oder boulevards host the Tall Ships Race and Days of the Sea. Even outside festival season, the waterfront cafés remain lively. Check dates on the city website.

⏱ 60 min

Try the pasztecik szczeciński at the historic shop on Wojska Polskiego Street. This fried meat-filled pastry has been EU-protected since 2010. Expect 3-4 PLN each, you need to eat at least three.

⏱ 30 min

In the evening, the Craneosauruses light up the Łasztownia quays in red and blue. The district is full of food courts and terraces. Perfect for dining by the water without breaking the bank.

⏱ 45 min

Krzywy Las

★★★★☆
1

From a distance, nothing special: a patch of forest like any other. Then the trunks appear, bent at 90° to the north, all in the same direction. The Krzywy Las has about a hundred pines planted around 1930, and nobody knows why they grow this way. Free access, no barriers.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Park at the Nowe Czarnowo car park and follow the 'Krzywy Las' signs for a five-minute walk. No refreshment stand or toilets on site. Bring water.

⏱ 20 min

Stargard

★★★☆☆
1

Poles call it 'the little cathedral' even though it's a collegiate church. Stargard's Kolegiata Mariacka, begun in 1292, was inspired by Lübeck's Marienkirche. Red bricks, guild chapels and 15th-century polychromy inside. National heritage monument since 2010.

⏱ 45 min
2

Less well-known than the collegiate church, the Willa Karowów blends Art Nouveau and neo-Gothic on a cobbled street. It's not open to visitors, but the façade is worth a detour for architecture fans. Two-minute walk from the Rynek.

⏱ 15 min
3

Built in the 13th and 14th centuries, Stargard's mury obronne (defensive walls) are on the European Brick Gothic route. Several towers still stand, including the Witches' Tower. The pedestrian circuit is signposted and about 2 km long.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk around the ramparts in an hour to grasp the scale of the fortified old town. Plentiful free parking in Stargard, a rare luxury in Poland.

⏱ 60 min

Kamien Pomorski

★★★☆☆
1

The sound of the baroque organ fills the nave as soon as you push open the door of St John the Baptist Cathedral in Kamień Pomorski. The instrument dates from the 17th century, funded by Duke Ernest of Croy: 3,300 pipes, 13 m wide. The adjoining cloister is the only preserved cathedral cloister in Poland.

⏱ 45 min
2

Founded in 1176, the Kamień Pomorski cathedral complex has been listed as a Historic Monument since 2005. Next to the cathedral, the bishop's palace and the local history museum occupy the same fortified enclosure. Museum admission 10 PLN.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you pass through on a summer Friday, attend an organ concert in the cathedral. The international organ and chamber music festival has existed for over fifty years. Tickets sold on site, arrive early.

⏱ 60 min

Swinoujscie

★★★☆☆
1

The Świnoujście lighthouse is the tallest in Poland: 69 m of brick, inaugurated in 1857. Over 300 steps lead to the top. The view embraces Wolin island, the port and the German coast of Usedom. Modest entrance fee.

⏱ 45 min
2

From the outside, Fort Gerhard looks like a forgotten bunker. Push open the door: it's the best-preserved Prussian coastal fort on the Baltic, built between 1848 and 1859. A guide in soldier's costume takes you on the tour. Allow 75 minutes and a warm layer, the casemates are chilly.

⏱ 75 min
3

Push on to the Stawa Młyny mill on Świnoujście's west pier. This small red mill on stilts is the town's postcard. Free, accessible on foot from the promenade.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

The wind whistles along the east pier, several hundred metres long. Walking to the end, facing the open sea, gives an idea of the Baltic's power in autumn. Sheltered in summer, freezing in winter.

⏱ 30 min

Take advantage of the wide promenade to reach the German border on foot or by bike. You cross from one country to the other without noticing, between seaside villas and maritime pines.

⏱ 45 min

Miedzyzdroje

★★★☆☆
1

If you arrive early, the climb to Kawcza Góra is deserted. The cliff stairs, the longest on the Polish coast, drop 51 m to the beach. The view over the white cliffs of the national park is worth the effort.

⏱ 45 min
2

The water of Turkusowe lake turns bright turquoise on sunny days, an effect of chalk dissolved in the bottom. A former extraction site, it's located 6 km from Międzyzdroje, near Wapnica. Short trail to the viewpoint on Piaskowa Góra.

⏱ 30 min
3

Created in 1960, Woliński Park Narodowy protects the highest active cliffs in Poland, rising over 90 m. The white-tailed eagle is its symbol. Entry tickets at automatic terminals at each access point.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Follow the red trail along the cliffs from Międzyzdroje to the latarnia morska Kikut lighthouse. Allow 3 hours return, easy terrain. The panorama changes at every bend of the coast.

⏱ 180 min

The żubry (bison) enclosure is worth a visit, even if the pens remain modest. Access on foot only, 1.3 km from the park entrance, direction Warnowo. Free with park ticket.

⏱ 45 min

Trzesacz

★★★☆☆
1

A single wall still stands, perched on the edge of a sand cliff. The Gothic church of St Nicholas in Trzęsacz was built in the 14th-15th centuries, nearly 2 km from the sea. Erosion did the rest. In 1994, half of the south wall collapsed; what remains is stabilized on gabions. Unique case in Europe.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stop by the multimedia museum on the cliff (Muzeum na Klifie), four interactive rooms explaining the phenomenon of coastal abrasion. Small but well done, ideal in rainy weather. Modest admission.

⏱ 30 min

⏱ 30 min

Activities

⏱ 30 min

If you like smoked herring, Kołobrzeg's fishing port is the right place. Stalls are open early in the morning. Expect 10-15 PLN per piece.

Darlowo

★★★☆☆
1

Locals still call Erik 'the last Viking of the Baltic'. This dethroned king of Denmark, Sweden and Norway ended his life at the Zamek Książąt Pomorskich in Darłowo. The Gothic castle, begun in 1352 on an island in the Wieprza, is the only medieval coastal castle in Poland. 24 m tower, museum inside. Open 10am-6pm in summer.

⏱ 60 min
2

Small but endearing, Darłowo's Stare Miasto preserves its medieval layout, some half-timbered houses and St Mary's church with the mausoleum of the Pomeranian dukes. Ten minutes is enough to tour it.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Take the swing bridge at Darłówko to reach the fishing port and beach. The mechanism of the bascule bridge is a spectacle in itself. In summer, boats depart for the Danish island of Bornholm.

⏱ 30 min

Koszalin

★★★☆☆
1

Built between 1300 and 1333, Koszalin cathedral anchors its imposing red-brick Gothic silhouette amid modern buildings. Organ concerts in summer. In front of the entrance, a monument recalls John Paul II's visit.

⏱ 30 min
2

From the observation tower of Góra Chełmska (137 m), the panorama embraces the forest, lakes and coast in the distance. The hill was a pagan place of worship before hosting a sanctuary consecrated by John Paul II in 1991. Easy drive up by car.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Make a detour to the octagonal St Gertrude chapel, dated 1383, at the foot of the remains of medieval ramparts. It's one of the rare octagonal buildings in the region, inspired by Danish models. Five-minute visit.

⏱ 15 min

Borne Sulinowo

★★☆☆☆
1

Erased from maps until 1992, Borne Sulinowo housed the largest Soviet ground base in Poland. The Muzeum Militarnej Historii displays T-34 tanks, uniforms and period photos. The owner, Wiesław Bartoszek, tells the story with passion.

⏱ 45 min
2

In the corridors of the former Soviet military hospital, paint peels and vegetation reclaims its rights. A tunnel connects the basements to the railway line. Nobody knows why. Free access, but caution: some floors are fragile.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Drive to the ghost village of Kłomino, a few kilometres away. Unlike Borne Sulinowo, Kłomino was never repopulated after the Soviets left. Empty apartment blocks, broken road. Post-apocalyptic atmosphere guaranteed. Road passable by car.

⏱ 45 min

Activities
