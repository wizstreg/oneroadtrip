Istanbul

★★★★★
1

Hagia Sophia plays both sides at once: active mosque on the ground floor, museum of Byzantine mosaics in the upper gallery. Former Christian cathedral from 537 AD, converted to a mosque in 1453, reopened for worship in 2020. The upper gallery visit costs €32.50 and requires advance booking—queues stretch long from 10 AM onward.

⏱ 90 min
2

Built in 532 under Justinian I, the Basilica Cistern lines up 336 columns across 138 meters beneath the city. This underground reservoir has held its silence for fifteen centuries, indifferent to Istanbul's noise overhead. No dress code, unlike the mosques—perfect between visits.

⏱ 60 min
3

The scent of incense and gold floods Topkapi from the second courtyard onward. Residence of the Ottoman sultans from 1465 to 1853, the palace covers an entire peninsula between the Golden Horn and Bosphorus. Closed Tuesdays—and this is where unlicensed guides swarm the thickest: only follow official badges.

⏱ 120 min
4

Crowded, touristy, and yet the Blue Mosque delivers on its promise: 20,000 Iznik tiles line the interior in deep blue. Its six minarets once scandalized Mecca, which itself had only six. Free entry outside prayer times, headscarf required.

⏱ 45 min
5

Take tram T1 to Sultanahmet and walk to the Hippodrome, the ancient Byzantine chariot-racing track. Three monuments survive on the square: the Obelisk of Theodosius (erected in Egypt c. 1450 BC, re-erected here by Theodosius in 390 AD), the Serpent Column, and the Constantine Column. Free access.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you've got your sea legs, a 90-minute Bosphorus cruise frames the entrance to the Black Sea: Dolmabahçe Palace, Anadolu and Rumeli fortresses, Ortaköy Mosque beneath its bridge. Frequent departures from Eminönü, reasonable fare.

⏱ 90 min

Istanbulites call it simply 'the Hippodrome'—but it's Sultanahmet Square that serves as headquarters for watching local life between the monuments. Weekday mornings before 9 AM, the square is nearly deserted and the light on Hagia Sophia is exceptional.

⏱ 40 min

End the evening in Karaköy for the strait's best fish: the boat sandwich (balık ekmek) opposite Galata Bridge is an Istanbul institution for under €3. The neighborhood comes alive from 7 PM onward.

⏱ 60 min

Edirne

★★★★☆
1

Sinan outdid himself in Edirne, and he knew it. The Selimiye Mosque, built between 1568 and 1574, boasts a dome 31 meters in diameter—slightly wider than Hagia Sophia's—and four 71-meter minarets. UNESCO-listed in 2011, set within a külliye complex with a museum of medicine. Free entry.

⏱ 60 min
2

Built between 1402 and 1414, the Eski Cami ('old mosque') is Edirne's oldest surviving religious building, with grand Arabic calligraphy on the pillars. Contemplative atmosphere and rarely crowded. Open freely outside prayer times.

⏱ 30 min
3

Light slants obliquely into the Bayezid II Külliye Museum, housed in the former Bayezid II mosque complex—Quranic school and medieval hospital converted. Dedicated to Ottoman medical history, it displays surgical instruments and reconstructed treatment rooms. Modest fee, few visitors.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Wander the Ottoman arasta below the Selimiye: this 17th-century market gallery sells jewelry, textiles, and local olive oil. It's also the best angle to photograph the minarets in alignment.

⏱ 30 min

As locals say: 'Edirne has to be earned.' A provincial Turkish town... that was once the capital of an empire: Edirne preceded Istanbul as the Ottoman seat from 1365 to 1453. Almost no international tourists know this, which explains the preserved atmosphere. In June, wrestlers compete at the Kırkpınar Oil Wrestling Festival, ongoing since the 14th century.

⏱ 60 min

Svilengrad

★★★☆☆
1

The Mustafa Pasha Bridge spans the Maritsa River for 295 meters with 20 stone arches—built in 1529 on the orders of Vizier Çoban Mustafa Pasha, close confidant of Suleiman the Magnificent. Wikipedia and Structurae attribute the design to Mimar Sinan, though the question remains debated among historians. Declared a national monument in 2003, restored in 2016.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take time to walk the bridge's 295-meter pedestrian zone: benches allow you to pause above the current. Local legend claims the mortar was reinforced with eggs collected from all across Thrace.

⏱ 25 min

The village of Mezek, 7 km away, holds one of the finest Mycenaean tombs outside Greece and the ruins of the medieval Neutzikon fortress. A 30-minute detour from the bridge if you have time.

⏱ 30 min

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Plovdiv

★★★★☆
1

Philippopolis, Trimontium, Plovdiv: this city has changed names more often than ruling civilizations. Built on seven hills (six remain), it's one of Europe's oldest continuously inhabited cities. The old town layers 19th-century Bulgarian Revival houses, Roman remains, and an Ottoman mosque—all within a twenty-minute walk.

⏱ 60 min
2

Plovdiv's Roman theatre is still alive, not fossilized: built in the 1st or 2nd century AD, it still hosts concerts and open-air operas in summer. Check the program before visiting—if an evening show is scheduled, daytime access to the tiers may be restricted.

⏱ 45 min
3

If you only have time to visit one Old Plovdiv house, choose the Hindliyan House: decorated for a 19th-century merchant, it alternates carved-ceiling salons with fountain courtyards. The neighboring Balabanova House hosts concerts.

⏱ 45 min
4

Get lost in the Kapana quarter among the street-art murals and artisan workshops. This artists' district occupies the old craftsmen's quarter of the center. This is where Plovdiv earned its title as European Capital of Culture 2019.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

The aroma of roasted coffee drifts down Knyaz Alexander Street, Plovdiv's main pedestrian thoroughfare. This is where the 2nd-century Roman stadium is literally buried beneath the paving stones—floor windows reveal the tiers. Entry included in a combined ticket with the theatre.

⏱ 30 min

Join one of the free English-language tours offered each morning in front of the national theatre: two hours with a passionate local guide to understand why Plovdiv is complex and proud. Contribution appreciated.

⏱ 120 min

⏱ 30 min
