Best period: November to February, temperatures 25-30°C, clear skies. Avoid April-May (scorching heat up to 38°C) and June-October (monsoon season, daily rains, rough seas in the south). December-January ideal for combining north and beaches.

Essential Thailand: Temples, Jungle & Beaches in 2 Weeks

📅 14 days📍 14 stops🚗 2425 km⏱ 10h20

Bangkok → Chiang Mai → Chiang Rai → Krabi → Koh Phi Phi → Phuket

Fourteen days to see the best of Thailand without skimming. Bangkok

★★★★★
1

Enter the Grand Palace through the main gate on Na Phra Lan Road. The complex sprawls across 218,400 m² - a city within the city. Built in 1782 by King Rama I using bricks salvaged from the ruins of Ayutthaya, it comprises over 100 buildings with golden roofs and multicolored mosaics. Inside Wat Phra Kaew, look for the Emerald Buddha statue: 66 cm carved from a single block of solid jade. Only the king may change its golden costumes - three times a year, according to the season. Allow at least 2.5 hours. Dress code enforced: shoulders and knees covered. Beware of strangers telling you the palace is closed - it's the neighborhood's main tourist scam.

⏱ 150 min
2

Walk 700 m south to Wat Pho. The Reclining Buddha is there: 46 m long, 15 m high, covered in gold leaf, so large it barely fits inside its building. Look at the soles of his feet, inlaid with mother-of-pearl in 108 patterns representing the auspicious signs of the Buddha. The temple is also the official birthplace of Thai massage - a school has trained practitioners here since 1962. Before leaving, treat yourself to a foot massage session in the courtyard.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Take the ferry from Tha Tien Pier (3 baht) to cross the Chao Phraya. Wat Arun appears on the opposite bank, its 82 m central prang covered in porcelain fragments - pieces of Chinese plates used as ballast on trading junks. Climb the steep prang steps to see the mosaics up close and enjoy the view of the Grand Palace across the river. Stay for late afternoon light - the tower catches fire before sunset.

⏱ 75 min

Bangkok

★★★★★
1

Dive into Chinatown by walking up Yaowarat Road. This neighborhood has existed since 1782: King Rama I relocated the Chinese community here to clear space for the Grand Palace. Today it's a succession of gold shops, traditional medicine pharmacies and alleyways that smell of roast duck. Push open the door of Wat Mangkon Kamalawat, a Buddhist-Taoist-Confucian temple tucked in the heart of the quarter - worshippers burn incense at all hours. At night, the sidewalks become open-air kitchens: hoy tod (oyster omelet), kuay jab (rolled noodles with crispy pork), bean paste desserts. Arrive after 6 PM for the neighborhood at full throttle.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Jim Thompson House is an unexpected museum: six 17th-century teak houses dismantled and reconstructed in 1959 by an American businessman who revived Thailand's silk industry after World War II. His collection - paintings, celadons, statues - sits exactly where he left it in 1967, when he disappeared during a walk in Malaysia and was never found. The guided tour lasts 45 minutes. The shaded gardens offer refuge from Bangkok's noise.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Board a longtail boat from Tha Chang Pier to explore Thonburi's klongs. On the Chao Phraya's west bank, the city changes centuries: wooden houses on stilts, orchid vendors, temples hidden behind vegetation. Operators offer 1 to 2-hour tours through secondary canals where cars don't exist. This is Bangkok before the skyscrapers.

⏱ 90 min

Bangkok

★★★★★
1

If you're here on a Saturday or Sunday, Chatuchak is a must: 15,000 stalls across 35 hectares - the world's largest weekend market. Handicrafts, ceramics, silks, plants, vinyl, clothing: each aisle is a different neighborhood. Arrive before 10 AM - after that, heat and crowds make wandering difficult. To navigate, photograph the entrance map: sections are numbered 1 to 27.

⏱ 120 min
2

In the afternoon, head down to Charoen Krung, Bangkok's creative quarter. This is the old 19th and 20th-century trade route - European-style buildings sit alongside artist workshops, design galleries and cafés in renovated shophouses. The TCDC (Thailand Creative & Design Centre) deserves 45 minutes: exhibitions of Thai design and a library open to all. Evening falls slowly over the river from the terraces of Talat Noi.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Evening flight from Bangkok to Chiang Mai (Suvarnabhumi or Don Mueang airport, 1h20 flight). Book early - evening flights fill up fast. Upon arrival, settle in and scout the old city: you start tomorrow morning.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 700 km · 1h20

Chiang Mai

★★★★☆
1

Begin with Wat Chedi Luang in the old city center. Construction started in 1391 to house the ashes of King Saen Muang Ma's father, completed in 1475 at 82 m high - the tallest structure in the Lanna kingdom. In 1545, an earthquake claimed the top 30 meters. The truncated structure has stood ever since. In the eastern niche, a replica of the Emerald Buddha recalls that the original stayed here a century before being transferred to Bangkok. Four staircases guarded by nagas - mythical serpents - frame the four faces. Allow 45 minutes to 1 hour.

⏱ 60 min
2

Ten minutes' walk west, Wat Phra Singh is Chiang Mai's most revered temple. Founded in 1345 by King Phayu, it houses the Phra Buddha Sihing statue - its origin remains a mystery, with some chronicles tracing it from Sri Lanka via India and Ayutthaya. Enter the Viharn Lai Kham: 19th-century murals depict daily life under the Lanna kingdom. Each April during Songkran, the statue parades through Chiang Mai's streets in procession.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

On Sunday evenings, the Walking Street on Wualai Road comes alive from 5 PM. Local artisans sell hammered silver, Bo Sang umbrellas and hill tribe textiles - more local and less touristy than the standard night market. Food stalls offer khao soi (egg noodle curry, Northern specialty) and grilled Chiang Mai sausages. Plan at least 2 hours.

⏱ 120 min

For those wanting to dive deeper into local cuisine, cooking classes are offered in the old city: morning market to choose ingredients, then 3 to 4 hours in the kitchen. Khao soi, green curry and tom kha are program classics. Book the day before.

⏱ 180 min

Chiang Mai

★★★★☆
1

Leave early for Doi Suthep - 15 km from the city at 1,073 m altitude. The legend of its 1383 founding: a white elephant carrying a Buddha relic climbed the mountain, trumpeted three times and collapsed. The king saw this as a sign and built the temple. Climb the 309 steps flanked by nagas or take the funicular. At the top, the 24 m golden chedi gleams in the morning sun. The view over Chiang Mai from the terrace covers the entire plain. Arrive before 9 AM to avoid tour groups.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

In the afternoon, let yourself drift toward Nimman Road, Chiang Mai's café and creator quarter. Shophouses host local designer boutiques, galleries, coworking spaces and specialty coffee shops - Northern mountain coffee is a regional pride. One Nimman, the complex at the corner, groups craft shops, restaurants and smoothie bars. It's also the right place to find hill tribe textiles, celadon pottery and bamboo baskets.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 190 km · 3h

Chiang Rai

★★★★☆
1

13 km south of Chiang Rai, Wat Rong Khun resembles no other temple. Designed and financed by Thai artist Chalermchai Kositpipat from 1997, the project is still underway. Everything is white - encrusted with thousands of mirror fragments that make the façade shimmer like a water surface. The access bridge is lined with hands reaching up from the ground: they symbolize earthly desires the faithful leave behind. Inside, murals blend Buddhism with modern references - Superman, the Twin Towers, movie characters. Go early: morning light makes the mirrors sparkle.

⏱ 75 min
2

6 km north of town, Wat Rong Suea Ten - Dancing Tigers Temple - completed in 2016. Here everything is deep sapphire blue highlighted with gold. The architect is a former student of Kositpipat. Blue symbolizes wisdom and abandonment of material attachments. Free entry, allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
3

Baan Dam - the Black House - lies 10 km north of the center. National artist Thawan Duchanee devoted 50 years of work here until his death in 2014. Not a temple: an ensemble of 40 black-painted buildings, constructed in solid teak within a planted garden. The main building's interior is covered with animal skins, skulls and sculptures - a Buddhist aesthetic exploring death as life's hidden face. Unsettling, but without equivalent.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the evening, head to Chiang Rai's Night Bazaar. Calmer than Chiang Mai's, it's a human-scale market with hill tribe crafts, textiles and street food stalls. Order khao soi, grilled Chiang Mai sausages and mango sticky rice.

⏱ 90 min

Chiang Rai

★★★★☆
1

Drive north to the Golden Triangle at Sop Ruak (65 km, 1h15). This is the convergence point of Thailand, Laos and Burma, marked by the Mekong. The name dates from the 1960s-90s, when this region produced most of the world's heroin. The Hall of Opium, a 5,500 m² museum financed under royal patronage, traces opium history from antiquity through the Sino-British opium wars, drug lords and royal hill country crop substitution programs. The multimedia rooms are striking. Allow at least 1.5 hours.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Evening flight from Chiang Rai airport (CEI) to Bangkok, then connection to Krabi (KBV) the same evening or next morning. Check the connection when booking - some itineraries require an overnight in Bangkok. Book both flights well in advance.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 1400 km · 2h

Krabi

★★★★☆
1

Arrival at Krabi airport (KBV), then transfer to Ao Nang - the ideal base, 20 minutes by taxi. It's the region's most accessible beach: karst cliffs plunge directly into the turquoise Andaman Sea. Longtail boats at anchor, waterside vendors, coconut stands. This is a transit day - lay the groundwork and explore the waterfront.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the evening, dine on Ao Nang beach: several restaurants set up tables directly on the sand with cliff views. Order tom yum goong (spicy shrimp soup) or a whole grilled fish. Krabi Town's night market, 5 km away, also warrants the trip for a more local evening - pad thai stalls, banana sweets and fresh fruit juices.

⏱ 120 min

Krabi

★★★★☆
1

Board a longtail from Ao Nang (15-minute crossing) for Railay. This peninsula is cut off from the mainland by vertical limestone cliffs - accessible only by sea. West Railay facing the beach, East Railay facing mangroves, and at the end of the path Phra Nang beach, framed by rock pillars rising from the water. Phra Nang cave houses a shrine to the fishermen's goddess - an altar laden with floral offerings and votive phalluses, a thousand-year tradition among the region's sailors. Railay's cliffs are one of Southeast Asia's most renowned climbing spots. Operators offer day-long introductions.

⏱ 180 min
2

Late afternoon, return to Krabi Town for Tiger Cave Temple. The main temple sits at the hill's base. But the summit is what's worth it: 1,237 steep steps through jungle, monitored by macaques. At the top, a golden Buddha, a chedi and panoramic views over Krabi plain, the limestone cliffs and the Andaman Sea beyond. Go late in the day to avoid heat - and plan 1.5 hours round trip.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Kayak on the Sok River and wildlife watching

⏱ 60 min

Evening at Fisherman's Village, Bophut

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 2h

Koh Phi Phi

★★★★☆
1

Ferry from Krabi Town or Ao Nang to Koh Phi Phi Don (approximately 1h30). Upon arrival at Tonsai Village, drop your bags and climb to the viewpoint. Count 343 steps to the first belvedere - a few dozen more to the second. At the top: the view of Phi Phi's two bays, Tonsai Bay on the left, Loh Dalum Bay on the right, and the limestone cliffs enclosing them. It's the only spot on the island where you truly grasp its geography - a 150 m strip of land between two bays. Sunrise or late afternoon for the best light.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

The evening on Phi Phi Don is festive without being solely that. Tonsai Village offers restaurants on stilts, beachside cocktail bars and live music on Loh Dalum beach. Eat grilled fish by the water, drink a cocktail from a coconut shell. The vibe picks up around 10 PM - it's an island of backpackers and divers, relaxed atmosphere.

⏱ 120 min

Koh Phi Phi

★★★★☆
1

Leave before 8 AM for the Maya Bay excursion. The Phi Phi Leh beach was made famous by Danny Boyle's film The Beach (2000) with Leonardo DiCaprio. Closed four years to recover from over-tourism, it reopened in 2022 with strict rules: no swimming, stay limited to 1 hour, access via floating dock at Loh Samah Bay then 10 minutes on foot. Cliffs embrace the white sand beach - it's dizzying. The bay closes in August and September every year. Book absolutely in advance and opt for an early departure.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

The excursion generally includes Bamboo Island (Koh Mai Phai): a flat island without infrastructure, white sand strip and crystal water perfect for swimming. And Shark Point for snorkeling - blacktip reef sharks are common and harmless here, cruising 2-3 m deep above the corals. Local operators combine Maya Bay, Bamboo Island and Shark Point in one day.

⏱ 240 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 2h

Phuket

★★★★☆
1

Ferry Phi Phi → Phuket Town (about 2 hours). Drop your bags and explore the old town on foot. History lives in every façade: in the 19th century, tin mines attracted Hokkien Chinese immigrants and Portuguese settlers. Their blend produced the Sino-Portuguese style - two-story shophouses with arcades, carved shutters, pastel ochre, turquoise and powder pink façades. Thalang Road is the main showcase, Soi Romanee the most photogenic alley. Continue to Thai Hua Museum, in a 1934 former Chinese school, to understand the Baba (Peranakan) community's history in Phuket.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

On Sunday evenings, Thalang Road becomes a night market from 4 PM to 10 PM: street food stalls, local artisans, musicians. On weekdays, alternative markets take over. Taste moo hong (pork braised in soy sauce, Baba specialty), kanom jeen (fresh rice noodles) and Hokkien dim sum.

⏱ 120 min

Phuket

★★★★☆
1

Phang Nga Bay deserves a full day. Operators offer longtail or speedboat excursions to the bay's karst formations - hundreds of islets rising from the water, some hollow, others pierced with interior lagoons. Koh Panyee, a fishing village entirely built on stilts in the middle of the bay, is the usual lunch stop. Koh Tapu - the island from the film The Man with the Golden Gun (James Bond, 1974) - is the other star: a 20 m rock pinnacle planted vertically in the water. Book early in high season.

⏱ 360 min
2

If you prefer a beach day, opt for Kata or Karon: two beaches in southern Phuket, calmer than Patong. Kata is 1.5 km long, tree-lined, with water suitable for swimming. If you haven't yet seen the Big Buddha: the Phra Phuttha Ming Mongkol Akenakkiri - 45 m white marble statue - sits atop Nakkerd Hill, visible from the island's southern third.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Kayak through limestone caves around Hong Islands.

⏱ 60 min

Relax at the beachfront Bamboo Huts by Charm Churee hotel.

⏱ 60 min

Attend theoretical and safety diving briefing, followed by an open water dive.

⏱ 60 min

Phuket

★★★★☆

Activities

Last free morning. A 90-minute Thai massage in one of Phuket Town's institutes provides a good trip ending - quality often excellent in old town establishments. Take time for final purchases: silks, massage oils, Northern Thai tea, spices. Phuket International Airport (HKT) is 30 minutes north of town.

⏱ 180 min
