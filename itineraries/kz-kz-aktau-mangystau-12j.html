This road trip crosses Kazakhstan's most otherworldly landscapes. From Aktau on the Caspian Sea, head by 4x4 across the Ustyurt Plateau—once the floor of the Tethys Ocean—toward Boszhira's white cliffs, Torysh's stone spheres, and Sufi underground mosques. Boszhira Valley MangystauBoszhira Fangs formationMount Bokty Mangystau

1

You arrive at Kazakhstan's most spectacular site: Boszhira. This gorge at the base of the Ustyurt Plateau was once the floor of the Tethys Ocean—the white limestone cliffs reach 250 m high. The "Fangs" of Boszhira—two isolated rock pillars—are Mangystau's most iconic image, reproduced on the 1,000 tenge banknote. The site is immense, and you can spend hours here encountering no more than a handful of people.

⏱ 120 min
2

Climb to the viewpoint on the plateau rim—the panorama over the entire valley, with its white formations and colored streaks, looks more like another planet than Earth. The cliffs shift color with the light: beige in full daylight, pink at sunrise, golden at sunset. It's the country's best photo spot.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike to the Boszhira Fangs from the plateau—the descent is steep but manageable in 1.5 hours. Watch the cliff edges—the limestone is crumbly and cracks run across the surface. Camp at the base of the Fangs for tomorrow's sunrise.

Day trip to Turkestan (170 km, about 1.5 hours on perfect asphalt). The Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi is Kazakhstan's most important monument—UNESCO-listed. Commissioned by Timur in 1389 to replace an older 12th-century mausoleum, it remained unfinished when the conqueror died in 1405. The main dome measures 18 m in diameter—one of the largest in Central Asia. Inside, a 2-ton bronze cauldron (the tai-kazan) stands in the main hall.

⏱ 90 min
2

Explore the underground mosque of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi, accessible with the same ticket. The complex also includes a small museum of ancient baths. The entire quarter around the mausoleum has been redesigned with the Ethno-Park that recreates traditional Silk Road architecture.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wander around Turkestan near the mausoleum—Kazakh pilgrims circle the monument counterclockwise, touching the walls and murmuring prayers. It's a living place, not a frozen museum. Return to Shymkent late afternoon.
