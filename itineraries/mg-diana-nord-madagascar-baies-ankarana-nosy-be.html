✨ Highlights
  • Ankarana Special Reserve: 110 km of caves and limestone tsingy UNESCO-listed in 2023
  • Nosy Iranja: 1.2 km tombolo and 1909 Eiffel lighthouse, Madagascar's main hawksbill turtle nesting site
  • Amber Mountain: cloud forest at 1,475 m sheltering the world's smallest chameleon (Brookesia tuberculata)
  • Nosy Tanikely: marine park protected since 2010, green turtles in seagrass beds accessible snorkeling right from the beach
📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September, October, November

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 5 and 9 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (April-October): Best weather with minimal rainfall, cooler temperatures (15-25°C), and excellent wildlife viewing. Peak tourist season with higher prices but optimal conditions for national parks and outdoor activities.

Cool Dry Season (May-September): Ideal temperatures and clear skies perfect for hiking, wildlife observation, and exploring highlands. Best time for lemur watching and photography with comfortable conditions throughout.

Hot Dry Season (October-March): Warmer temperatures (20-30°C) with occasional rains, good for coastal areas and beach activities. Cyclone season (January-March) requires weather monitoring but offers lush landscapes.

Rainy Season (November-March): Heavy rains make some roads impassable, but dramatic landscapes and fewer tourists. Lower prices and breeding season for wildlife, though challenging travel conditions.

🌍 What to discover in MG?

Unique island with incredible biodiversity, endemic species, and authentic Malagasy culture. Experience lemur encounters, traditional music, and pristine natural environments. The country offers wildlife adventures with cultural authenticity and natural preservation. Madagascar provides life-changing wildlife experiences with cultural depth and natural wonders.

📋 Important notes

Avoid the rainy season (December to March): RN6 roads are sometimes cut off and there's cyclone risk. Access to Ankarana via the eastern entrance remains passable year-round. Check marine weather conditions before any island navigation. Final departure from Fascene Airport (Nosy Be), 20-30 minutes from Madirokely.

Stop 1

Antsiranana

Where a natural harbor ranks among the world's finest deep-water anchorages

★★★☆☆
📍 Baie de Sakalava Diego Suarez📍 Baie des Dunes Diego Suarez📍 Baie des Pigeons Diego Suarez📍 Lighthouse Cap Miné Antsiranana📍 Ramena Madagascar
1

Sakalava Bay is your starting point for the Three Bays hike. Reach it from Diego via a 5 km sandy track from the Ankorikely junction—you'll need a 4x4, as regular taxis won't go there. The bay is hammered by the Varatraza trade winds, making it one of the country's best kitesurfing spots. Set off early: the heat becomes intense after 10 AM on this shadeless coastline.

⏱ 75 min
2

Between Sakalava Bay and Pigeons Bay, you follow dunes and xerophilous vegetation typical of the north—achypodiums and dry species growing between sand ridges. Dunes Bay offers a swim break in crystal-clear, rarely-visited waters. From the blockhouse overlooking it, you get a simultaneous view of both bays—stop for the photo.

⏱ 75 min
3

Pigeons Bay closes the hike to the north, with whiter sand and calmer seas than Sakalava. This is where the water is most sheltered—perfect for a final swim before climbing back up to the lighthouse. Count on 3.5 to 4 hours of walking total from Sakalava, depending on your pace.

⏱ 75 min
4

At Cap Miné, you reach the remains of French fortifications built from 1894 onwards to defend the main passage into Diego Suarez Bay. Four period cannons still stand in place, and the lighthouse lit on October 1, 1900 remains active. The view from this promontory over the bay and the pass is the region's finest—here you understand why the French navy valued this anchorage so highly. Heads up: the military collect a passage fee at the exit toward Ramena.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

The Three Bays coastal hike covers about 11 km from Sakalava to Ramena, with moderate elevation and some ridge trail sections. Bring plenty of water, a hat, and sunscreen—there's no shade on the ridges. From Ramena, a taxi takes you back to Diego via 18 km of paved road.

⏱ 240 min

From Ramena late in the day, local operators offer short pirogue or boat trips to the Emerald Sea, a natural lagoon with shallow, translucent waters protected by a coral barrier. Mask and snorkel are all you need—you can stand for dozens of meters.

Stop 2

Joffreville

Gateway to Amber Mountain's mist-wrapped forest—a cloud island in the arid north

★★☆☆☆
📍 Parc National Montagne Ambre Joffreville📍 Great Waterfall Antomboka Amber Mountain📍 Lake Mahasarika Amber Mountain📍 Thousand Trees Trail Amber Mountain📍 Joffreville Ambohitra Madagascar
1

Amber Mountain National Park is an anomaly in this arid north: an 18,200-hectare volcanic massif perched between 850 and 1,475 meters elevation that receives 3,500 mm of annual rainfall while Diego Suarez gets 1,000 mm. The result—a dense, cool cloud forest draped in orchids, tree ferns, and lianas, in total contrast to the surrounding dry savannas. Created in 1958, the park shelters 8 lemur species including the crowned lemur (Eulemur coronatus) and the rufous mouse lemur, one of the world's smallest primates. Arrive at the entrance office in Joffreville by 7 AM—guides are mandatory and the early hours are best for wildlife watching.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Thousand Trees Trail crosses the park's rainforest alongside century-old trees draped in lianas and epiphytes. This circuit gives you the best chance of encountering Brookesia tuberculata, one of the planet's tiniest chameleons at under 5 cm. Your guide will spot it in the vegetation—observe and photograph, but don't touch. Furcifer timoni and Calumma vencesi, two recently scientifically described species, also live here.

⏱ 90 min
3

The great Antomboka waterfall plunges 80 meters through the heart of the forest—it's one of the park's highlights, at the end of a 3 to 4-hour circuit from the entrance. Some routes also include the sacred Ampijoroana falls, where members of the Antakarana community leave offerings for wishes granted. Respect areas marked as sacred (fady) and always follow your guide's instructions.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Choose your circuit based on your fitness: the short circuit (2h) reaches the small waterfall and Lake Mahasarika, the park's smallest lake, which supplies water to Diego Suarez city. The full circuit (5 to 6h) climbs to the great waterfall and viewpoints overlooking the Mozambique Channel. Pack waterproof shoes—the forest trails are often slippery.

⏱ 300 min

In the evening at Joffreville, head out on a night walk with your guide. Chameleons and nocturnal lemurs like the rufous mouse lemur are active after dark and far easier to spot with a headlamp. Operators based in Joffreville organize these roughly 1.5-hour outings departing from village accommodations.

Stop 3

Ankarana Mahamasina

Africa's largest cave network lies beneath limestone pinnacles sharp as blades

★★☆☆☆
📍 Tsingy Rouges Irodo Madagascar📍 Canyon Tsingy Rouge Irodo📍 RN6 Ankarana Madagascar📍 Mahamasina Ankarana
1

60 km south of Diego on the RN6, count on roughly 2 hours of driving before you spot the ochre canyon of the Red Tsingy of Irodo. This site resembles nothing else in Madagascar: unlike the limestone tsingy of Ankarana, these formations are sculpted from red sandstone, marl, and laterite by erosion from the Irodo River and rains. Hundreds of fairy chimneys in shades ranging from white to blood red, which the late-afternoon light sets ablaze—and since the site is ephemeral, each rainy season reshapes the sculptures. Two viewpoints by car overlook the canyon; a third on-foot access descends to the base of the formations. Bring water and a hat.

⏱ 120 min
2

Continuing south on the RN6, the landscape gradually shifts: the red laterite yields to the first gray limestone formations and dry deciduous forests that announce the Ankarana massif. The 'Ankarana wall,' a limestone cliff stretching 25 km from north to south, looms on the horizon—it's a geological transition visible to the naked eye, two worlds separated by a few dozen kilometers.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visiting the Red Tsingy is largely done by car between viewpoints. To descend into the heart of the fairy chimneys, count on 45 minutes on foot from the third access. The best light is late afternoon: if your timing allows, organize your departure from Diego to arrive on site around 4 PM.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 4

Ankarana Mahamasina

Africa's largest cave network lies beneath limestone pinnacles sharp as blades

★★☆☆☆
📍 Réserve Spéciale Ankarana Mahamasina📍 Tsingy Ankarana Madagascar📍 Ankarana Caves Madagascar📍 Suspended Bridge Ankarana Benavony Loop📍 Loss of Rivers Ankarana
1

The Ankarana Special Reserve (18,220 ha, created in 1956, UNESCO-listed in September 2023) shelters Africa's largest cave network: 110 km of underground cavities carved into Jurassic limestone that emerged from the seas 100 million years ago. Above ground, the tsingy—the Malagasy word means 'walking on tiptoes'—form a labyrinth of razor-sharp limestone pinnacles, deep canyons, and crevasses. Stop at the Mahamasina reception office before entering—a guide is mandatory. The site is 108 km south of Antsiranana.

⏱ 120 min
2

The lost rivers are one of Ankarana's most spectacular phenomena: a green-water river briefly crosses a canyon before disappearing under the rock to join the underground network. This circuit (about 4 km in 3 hours, with a passage through shallow water) takes you into the caves to observe cave-dwelling species—some are blind and unpigmented. Milaintety Cave at the circuit's end features a natural pool surrounded by ferns.

⏱ 90 min
3

Two suspension bridges connect the viewpoints above the great canyon of the Benavony loop, offering a plunging view over the stone forest and crevasses. From the high point at 206 m, on clear days you glimpse the islets of the Mozambique Channel. A great baobab 8 m in circumference awaits you at the circuit's end. Ankarana's dry forest shelters three endemic baobab species: Adansonia madagascariensis, Adansonia perrieri, and Adansonia suarezensis.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Eight circuits are marked in the reserve, from 3 km to 10 km, from 2 hours to a full day. The Benavony loop (5 km, 3 to 4h) is the most complete: tsingy, two suspension bridges, a cave with concretions, and a panoramic viewpoint. For the lost rivers (4 km, 3h), bring shoes that can get wet. Pack a headlamp for the caves.

⏱ 240 min

Some Ankarana caves shelter the royal tombs of the Antakarana, who took refuge in the massif in the 19th century to flee Merina armies. These caves are sacred and their access forbidden to Merina descendants—a history of conflicts between Malagasy kingdoms still very present in local memory. Ask your guide to tell you this story along the way.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 5

Madirokely

Nosy Be's main beach, where the scent of ylang-ylang drifts on every breeze

★★★★★
📍 Ambanja Market Madagascar📍 Sambirano Valley Cacao Ambanja📍 Port Ankify Madagascar📍 Hell-Ville Andoany Nosy Be📍 Madirokely Beach Nosy Be
1

Ambanja is the capital of the 'valley of perfumes': the Sambirano Valley produces cocoa, coffee, vanilla, vetiver, and ylang-ylang. If you pass through on a weekday, the local market is worth the detour—spices, tropical fruits, 100% local atmosphere. The town's cathedral hides an anecdote: its metal framework comes from the former Strasbourg train station. The town is 18 km from Ankify port.

⏱ 60 min
2

Ankify is the only embarkation point on the northern mainland coast for reaching Nosy Be. A small fishing village, it's above all a transit point. The speedboat to Nosy Be takes 45 minutes. Check schedules in advance—there may be waiting time depending on traffic. The crossing is generally calm between May and November.

⏱ 45 min
3

Hell-Ville (Andoany) is the main port and only true town on Nosy Be, named after Admiral Louis de Hell, governor of Réunion, who obtained the island's cession in 1840. From the landing dock, a first scent of ylang-ylang floats in the hot air. Don't linger too much tonight—head to Madirokely, fifteen minutes away, to settle in facing the beach.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The road between Ankarana and Ankify (about 140 km, 2.5 to 3h) first follows the dry forest then descends toward the coast passing through Ambilobe and Ambanja. The RN6's quality is uneven on some sections—leave early to have time to see Ambanja before boarding.

⏱ 180 min

The speedboat crossing Ankify → Hell-Ville lasts 45 minutes. If the weather is favorable, you'll spot Nosy Be's verdant coasts long before arriving. Outside the dry season, the sea can be rough—bring a plastic bag if you're prone to seasickness.

⏱ 45 min

Late in the day at Madirokely, enjoy Nosy Be's main beach to decompress after the long road and sea crossing. It's a wide strip of sand lively in the evening, with beachfront restaurants. You're staying here three nights—get comfortably settled.

Madirokely

Nosy Be's main beach, where the scent of ylang-ylang drifts on every breeze

★★★★★
📍 Mont Passot Nosy Be📍 Réserve Lokobe Nosy Be📍 Arbre sacré Mahatsinjo Nosy Be📍 Andilana Beach Nosy Be📍 Hell-Ville Market Nosy Be
1

Mont Passot rises to 329 meters at Nosy Be's center and offers the island's best panoramic view: eleven volcanic lakes appear below, framed by tropical vegetation, with the ocean on the horizon. Go in late afternoon—it's one of the archipelago's most beautiful sunsets.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Lokobe Nature Reserve is the last remnant of primary forest on Nosy Be. It protects the black macaco lemur, the panther chameleon (Furcifer pardalis) with its dazzling colors, and great trees like the Malagasy canarium and rosewood. Access is by pirogue from the southeast coast—count on a half-day with a mandatory guide.

⏱ 90 min
3

The sacred tree of Mahatsinjo, a several-centuries-old Ficus bengalensis with spectacular aerial roots, is a Sakalava worship site. This ancient witness to the island's history can be visited in a few minutes but merits the detour to understand local spirituality.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Andilana beach, in the island's north, is Nosy Be's most beautiful: crystalline waters, immaculate white sand, lightly frequented. Plan a half-day—count on 45 minutes' drive from Madirokely.

⏱ 180 min

In the evening, take a tour of Hell-Ville's covered market: local spices, ylang-ylang, vanilla, pepper. It's the best place to bring back olfactory souvenirs from the perfume island.

Stop 7

Tanikely

A marine park since 2010, where turtles glide through crystal shallows meters from shore

★★★★★
📍 Nosy Komba Island Lemurs Madagascar📍 Village Ampangorina Nosy Komba📍 Nosy Tanikely réserve marine📍 Lighthouse Nosy Tanikely
1

Nosy Komba, nicknamed 'lemur island,' is a volcanic island without roads or cars, 30 minutes by boat from Nosy Be. The village of Ampangorina welcomes visitors with its craft workshops: hand-stitched embroidered tablecloths sewn by village women, rosewood carvings. Don't miss the lemur park—the black macaques are easily approached and live in matriarchal groups led by females.

⏱ 90 min
2

Nosy Tanikely became a marine national park in 2010—fishing is prohibited within a 700-meter radius around the lighthouse. Result: an astonishing density of fish right from the beach edge, well-preserved corals, seagrass beds where you'll almost certainly encounter green turtles. The interesting depths start a few meters from the beach—a beginner with mask and snorkel sees as much as an experienced diver.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Full-day excursion from Madirokely: Nosy Komba in the morning (lemurs and crafts), Nosy Tanikely in the afternoon (snorkeling). Local operators offer this combo on shared or private boat. You return to sleep at Madirokely in the evening—no need to move luggage.

⏱ 90 min

Snorkeling on Nosy Tanikely's reef: the depths descend gradually from 2 to 12 meters from the beach. You'll observe snappers, clownfish, barracudas, giant clams, and green turtles in the seagrass beds. Diadema sea urchins are present—don't touch the bottom.

⏱ 120 min

Sakatia

Wild orchids cling to forest trunks above seagrass meadows where green turtles graze

★★★★★
📍 Nosy Sakatia Orchid Island Nosy Be📍 Herbiers tortues Nosy Sakatia📍 Snorkeling Nosy Sakatia Madagascar
1

Nosy Sakatia, nicknamed 'orchid island,' is a small preserved island west of Nosy Be, accessible in 5 minutes by boat from Chanty Beach. Less frequented than Tanikely, it charms with its forest trails, wild orchids, and chameleons. Antagnatrozona Bay shelters vast seagrass beds well protected from swells—this is where green turtles come to feed.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Morning snorkeling on Nosy Sakatia's seagrass beds: depth of 2 to 3 meters, clear water, green turtles almost guaranteed. Let yourself drift slowly above the seagrass—they'll approach on their own if you remain still. Return to Madirokely early afternoon.

⏱ 120 min

Coastal walk on foot around Nosy Sakatia, from cove to cove: the forest trails cross dense vegetation where wild orchids grow on tree trunks. Bring good shoes—the terrain is uneven.

⏱ 90 min

In the afternoon, prepare for tomorrow's Nosy Iranja expedition: check tide times with your operator (the 1.2 km sandbar is only accessible at low tide), confirm the bivouac, and lighten your luggage to the maximum for two nights on the island.

Stop 9

Iranja

Two islands joined by a white sand ribbon—Madagascar's premier hawksbill nesting ground

★★★★★
📍 Nosy Iranja Turtle Island Madagascar📍 Tombolo Nosy Iranja banc de sable📍 Gustave Eiffel Lighthouse Nosy Iranja📍 Village pêcheurs Nosy Iranja Be
1

Nosy Iranja is actually two islands connected by a 1.2 km white sand tombolo—at low tide, you walk between two turquoise oceans on this sand tongue that completely disappears at high tide. The large island, Nosy Iranja Be (200 ha), shelters a fishing village and a lighthouse built in 1909 by Gustave Eiffel's workshop, still visible from offshore. The small one, Nosy Iranja Kely (13 ha), is Madagascar's main hawksbill turtle nesting site.

⏱ 120 min
2

The fishing village of Nosy Iranja Be deserves a stroll: stilt houses built with mangrove wood and traveler's palm leaves, small grocery stores, fruit and fritter stands. Climb the Eiffel lighthouse (20 m, 1909) for a 360° view over both islets and the tombolo—this is where Nosy Iranja's most famous photo was taken.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Navigation from Madirokely to Nosy Iranja takes 1.5 to 2 hours depending on conditions. Leave early morning to arrive at low tide and walk the tombolo upon arrival. Your bivouac luggage awaits you on the island—you won't have to reload anything onto a boat tonight.

⏱ 120 min

Snorkeling at the foot of Nosy Iranja Kely: fish-rich reefs, surface turtles, and in season (August-November) humpback whales spotted offshore. The water is between 27 and 29°C year-round. In the evening, if you're bivouacking, local operators organize night outings to observe turtles laying eggs on Nosy Iranja Kely's beach—a rare experience.

⏱ 120 min

Iranja

Two islands joined by a white sand ribbon—Madagascar's premier hawksbill nesting ground

★★★★★
📍 Nosy Iranja tombolo marée basse📍 Nosy Iranja Kely ponte tortues📍 Nosy Iranja snorkeling bivouac
1

The second day at Nosy Iranja is the tombolo without the day-tripper crowds—who leave around 3 PM. At low tide, you have the sand tongue to yourself late afternoon and at sunrise. Nosy Iranja Kely, the small 13-ha island, can be crossed on foot in about twenty minutes between ferns and endemic species.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

If turtles nested the previous night, you may observe baby hatchlings making their way to the sea at sunrise—the island's most moving moment. Ask your operator to alert you if a nest is hatching: it's random depending on season but frequent between October and February.

⏱ 90 min

Free snorkeling on the reefs west of Nosy Iranja Kely, where tourist pressure is zero once the boats have left. Enjoy the second day's calm to explore at your own pace—that's the advantage of bivouacking over a day trip.

⏱ 150 min

Late afternoon, walk on the tombolo at sunset. The raking light on the white sand and turquoise waters is the day's most photogenic. Check the tide to be sure the tombolo is still exposed.

Stop 11

Baie Des Russes

A forgotten cove where a Russian fleet once sheltered from the tides of war

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Baie des Russes Madagascar📍 Bemanivika Ouest Madagascar
1

Russian Bay, a stopover on the return to Nosy Be, is a sheltered cove on the mainland still very lightly visited. The site takes its name from Admiral Rojdestvensky's fleet that took refuge here in 1905 during the Russo-Japanese War—the camp ruins are still visible. Near-deserted white sand beaches and turtle nests dot the shore.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Navigation from Nosy Iranja to Russian Bay (~30 km, 1h by sea) hugging the wild mainland coast. Short stopover for a swim or walk on the deserted beaches, then ascent to Nosy Be for the night. Spinner dolphins are frequent on this route.

⏱ 120 min

Return to Madirokely late afternoon—find your base hotel and luggage left on the island. Last evening on Madirokely beach before departure.

Hell-Ville (Andoany), 20 minutes from Madirokely, deserves a morning stop before the flight: the central market gathers local spices, vanilla, ylang-ylang in bottles, and handicrafts.
