☀️ Weather

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather conditions (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, mild temperatures, and excellent conditions for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Ideal for exploring castles, hiking, and experiencing Romanian culture with comfortable temperatures.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with stunning fall colors, stable weather, and harvest season celebrations. Perfect for wine tourism, cultural exploration, and photography with moderate prices and pleasant conditions.

Summer (July-August): Warm weather (20-30°C) with long daylight hours, festival season, and peak tourist activities. Highest prices and crowds but best conditions for mountain hiking and outdoor adventures.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures (-5 to 10°C) with potential snow, lowest prices, and authentic experiences. Ideal for winter sports, Christmas markets, and experiencing traditional Romanian winter culture.

📋 Notes

Brasov

★★★★☆
1

Get to Peleș Castle early—construction started in 1873, and with over 160 rooms plus electricity from day one, it was among Europe's most advanced palaces. To beat the crowds, arrive right at opening; after 10 a.m., tour groups flood in.

⏱ 90 min
2

Continue to Bran Castle, perched on a 60-meter rock outcrop. While the Dracula connection is mostly marketing, the visit is still atmospheric with its narrow staircases and hidden passages. Allow time for parking and the uphill walk.

⏱ 90 min
3

Arriving in Brașov, head straight to Piața Sfatului. The Black Church dominates the square—built in the 14th century, it's one of the largest Gothic structures in Eastern Europe.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Late afternoon, lose yourself in the old town's narrow lanes. Walk through Strada Sforii, barely 1.3 meters wide—you'll see, it's quick but fun.

⏱ 60 min

Sighisoara

★★★★☆
1

Start the morning at Libearty Sanctuary. Over 100 bears live across 60 hectares of semi-wild terrain. Tours are guided only—book several days ahead.

⏱ 90 min
2

Make a detour through Viscri, a UNESCO-listed village. Its fortified church dates from the 12th century. Climb the ramparts—the view over the village is one of the most authentic in the country.

⏱ 75 min
3

In Sighișoara, the Clock Tower has guarded the citadel entrance since the 14th century. Climb to the top for a sweeping view of the town—this is where Vlad the Impaler was supposedly born.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Evening is perfect for wandering the citadel. If you've got energy left, climb the Scholars' Stairs—175 wooden steps dating from the 17th century.
