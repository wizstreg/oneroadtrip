Romania Unveiled: Transylvania, Painted Monasteries and Carpathians

📅 12 days📍 10 stops🚗 1297 km⏱ 23h45

Discover Sinaia and Peleș Castle, then wander through Brașov at the foot of the Carpathians. Reach Sighișoara and its medieval lanes, then explore Bucovina's painted monasteries. End in Maramureș, between wooden churches and rural life. Romania blends legends, mountains and heritage, with an authenticity that hooks.

✨ Highlights
  • Brașov: Germanic squares Carpathian views
  • Sighișoara: Dracula birthplace medieval citadel
  • Bucovina: painted monasteries fresco wonder
  • Maramureș: wooden churches horse carts
⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 9 and 13 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather conditions (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, mild temperatures, and excellent conditions for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Ideal for exploring castles, hiking, and experiencing Romanian culture with comfortable temperatures.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with stunning fall colors, stable weather, and harvest season celebrations. Perfect for wine tourism, cultural exploration, and photography with moderate prices and pleasant conditions.

Summer (July-August): Warm weather (20-30°C) with long daylight hours, festival season, and peak tourist activities. Highest prices and crowds but best conditions for mountain hiking and outdoor adventures.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures (-5 to 10°C) with potential snow, lowest prices, and authentic experiences. Ideal for winter sports, Christmas markets, and experiencing traditional Romanian winter culture.

🌍 What to discover in RO?

Eastern European gem with medieval castles, traditional culture, and stunning landscapes. Experience Dracula heritage, beautiful countryside, and authentic Romanian hospitality. The country offers cultural depth with natural beauty and historical significance. Romania provides authentic European experiences with cultural richness and natural preservation.
