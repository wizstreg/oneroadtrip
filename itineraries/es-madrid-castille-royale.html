Day 1

Madrid

1

The Museo del Prado (1819) houses over 8,600 paintings in one of the world's finest collections. Velázquez's Las Meninas, Goya's The Third of May 1808, and Bosch's Garden of Earthly Delights rank among European art's greatest masterpieces.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Palacio de Cristal rises like a jewel within Parque del Retiro. This glass-and-iron pavilion (1887) now hosts rotating contemporary art exhibitions in a luminous setting.

⏱ 60 min
3

Step into Plaza Mayor (1619), where 237 meters of continuous arcades frame open-air cafés, vivid frescoes, and the bronze equestrian statue of Philip III at the square's heart.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Mercado de San Miguel (1916) tempts with Iberian ham, croquetas, tinned seafood, and Spanish wines beneath a beautifully restored iron-and-glass structure—Madrid's gourmet temple.

⏱ 75 min
5

The Palacio Real (1764) sprawls across 3,418 rooms, though only the most sumptuous are open: the gilded Throne Room, the grand State Dining Room, and the Royal Armoury's historic weaponry.

⏱ 120 min
6

The Templo de Debod (2nd century BC) was Egypt's gift to Spain in 1968. This authentic Nubian temple, painstakingly reconstructed, presides over West Park with Nile-side serenity.

⏱ 60 min
7

Gran Vía cuts through Madrid from east to west—1.3 kilometers of Belle Époque grandeur, Art Deco theaters, vintage cinemas, and flagship stores that never sleep.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Go bar-hopping through La Latina's tapas trail. Calle Cava Baja brims with centuries-old tabernas serving cañas (draft beers) and Basque-style pinchos on toothpicks.

⏱ 120 min

Catch sunset at the Templo de Debod. As the sky turns amber, the Egyptian temple and Casa de Campo beyond create Madrid's most romantic silhouette.

⏱ 75 min

Experience a tablao flamenco show. Guitar, voice, and fierce footwork unfold in vaulted cellars beneath the historic center—pure Andalusian soul in the capital's heart.

⏱ 90 min

Row a boat on Retiro's pond. Rent a rowboat and drift past the Monument to Alfonso XII (1922) and its neoclassical colonnade reflected in the water.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 88 km · 1h28
Day 2

Segovia

1

The Roman Aqueduct (1st century AD) strides across Plaza del Azoguejo in breathtaking defiance of gravity. 167 granite arches rise to 28.5 meters without a drop of mortar—818 meters of engineering genius.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Alcázar of Segovia commands the confluence of the Eresma and Clamores rivers like a ship's prow. This fairytale fortress blends Romanesque, Gothic, and Mudéjar styles and served Castilian monarchs from the 12th century onward.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Cathedral (1525–1768) was Spain's last great Gothic monument. Nicknamed the "Lady of Cathedrals," it stretches 105 meters long and shelters 18 chapels beneath soaring vaults.

⏱ 90 min
4

The Church of San Martín (12th century) anchors Plaza de Medina del Campo. Its Romanesque tower, arched galleries, and sculpted portals epitomize Segovian Romanesque elegance.

⏱ 60 min
5

Plaza Mayor pulses with Segovian life. Arcades, café terraces, and the Town Hall (1622) encircle this cobbled gathering place at the old town's heart.

⏱ 45 min
6

The medieval walls (11th century) wrap the old city in a 3-kilometer embrace. Restored Romanesque towers, fortified gates, and rampart walks invite partial exploration.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Savor cochinillo asado (roast suckling pig) in a historic mesón. This Segovian delicacy arrives in earthenware dishes traditionally smashed after serving—tender, crackling perfection.

⏱ 120 min

Walk the ramparts from Puerta de San Andrés. Panoramic views sweep over the Cathedral, Alcázar, and the blue ridges of the Guadarrama sierra.

⏱ 90 min

Climb the Cathedral Tower. 190 steps spiral up to 88 meters, rewarding you with a 360° panorama over Segovia's terracotta roofs and the Castilian plain beyond.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 75 km · 1h15
Day 3

Avila

1

The Walls of Ávila (1090–14th century) form Spain's most complete medieval enclosure. 2.5 kilometers of ramparts, 87 towers, and 9 monumental gates ring the old town—an unbroken crown of stone.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Cathedral (12th–16th century) is built directly into the city walls. Its fortified apse, the Cimorro, doubles as a defensive tower—prayer and protection intertwined.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Basilica of San Vicente (12th century) rises just outside the walls, its Romanesque galleries marking the martyrdom site of Saint Vincent of Ávila—solemn beauty in honey-colored stone.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Convent of Santa Teresa (1629) stands on the birthplace of Saint Teresa of Ávila (1515–1582). Baroque chapel, relics, and museum trace the mystic life of Spain's beloved saint.

⏱ 75 min
5

The Royal Monastery of Santo Tomás (15th century) unfolds through three Gothic cloisters. The Catholic Monarchs summered here, and their only son lies buried within.

⏱ 90 min
6

Plaza del Mercado Chico hums with local life. The Town Hall and café terraces animate this intra-muros square—Ávila's social living room.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk the ramparts at sunset. Two accessible sections (850 meters total) offer plunging views over medieval lanes and the Castilian countryside turning gold.

⏱ 90 min

Drive to the Cuatro Postes viewpoint (16th century). From 2 km away, this mirador delivers Ávila's iconic panorama: undulating walls, cathedral, and towers silhouetted against the plateau.

⏱ 60 min

Taste yemas de Santa Teresa. These convent sweets made from egg yolks and sugar have been Ávila's signature delicacy since the 16th century—melt-in-the-mouth devotion.

⏱ 45 min

Feast on chuletón de Ávila. This massive T-bone steak (600–1000g) comes from local Abulense cattle and is grilled rare in traditional asadores—carnivore heaven.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 136 km · 2h16
Day 4

Toledo

1

The Primate Cathedral (1226–1493) embodies Spanish Gothic at its zenith. Narciso Tomé's Baroque Transparente altarpiece, Renaissance choir stalls, and El Greco masterpieces dazzle in every chapel.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Alcázar dominates Toledo from the hilltop. This Roman fortress rebuilt in the 16th century now houses the Army Museum—history layered upon history.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Monastery of San Juan de los Reyes (1477–1504) marries late Gothic with Mudéjar ornament. The Catholic Monarchs commissioned this Franciscan jewel with its exquisitely carved cloisters.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Synagogue of Santa María la Blanca (12th century) unfolds in horseshoe arches and octagonal columns. This former Sephardic synagogue became a Catholic church in 1391—a testament to Toledo's layered identity.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Church of Santo Tomé safeguards El Greco's The Burial of the Count of Orgaz (1586). This masterpiece fuses earthly realism with celestial visions—Toledo's most iconic painting.

⏱ 60 min
6

The Mirador del Valle delivers Toledo's defining panorama. The Tagus River encircles the hilltop city, crowned by the Alcázar and Cathedral—a view unchanged for centuries.

⏱ 45 min
7

The Bridge of San Martín (14th century) spans the Tagus with five medieval arches. Fortified towers anchor both ends, framing views of the encircling walls.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Visit a damascene steel workshop. Artisans inlay gold and silver wire into black steel using ancestral Moorish techniques—Toledo's signature craft for over a millennium.

⏱ 75 min

Sample mazapán (marzipan) in a historic confectionery. This almond-and-sugar paste has been crafted in Toledo since the Middle Ages—sweet tradition in every bite.

⏱ 60 min

Wander the Judería at dusk. Narrow lanes, hidden patios, and medieval synagogues of the Jewish Quarter reveal their mysterious atmosphere as shadows lengthen.

⏱ 90 min

Cross the Puente de Alcántara (10th-century Roman origins, rebuilt 13th century). This fortified bridge leads to belvederes on the opposite bank—river-level views of the city's dramatic perch.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 53 km · 53min
Day 5

Aranjuez

1

The Royal Palace of Aranjuez (16th–18th centuries) served as the Bourbon monarchs' spring residence. Rococo salons, the porcelain-clad Chamber, and the gilded Throne Room unfold in opulent succession.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Jardín del Príncipe (18th century) stretches 150 hectares along the Tagus. Shaded avenues, neoclassical fountains, and the Casa del Labrador pavilion create an Enlightenment paradise.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Jardín de la Isla embraces a Tagus island in geometric Renaissance-Baroque harmony. Parterres, canals, mythological statues, and water jets compose a verdant symphony.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Royal Barges Museum displays 18th-century royal boats. These ornate vessels carried monarchs on leisurely river excursions—floating thrones on the Tagus.

⏱ 60 min
5

Plaza de San Antonio (1750) arrays Baroque façades around the town church. This regular square forms Aranjuez's urban heart—elegant geometry meets everyday life.

⏱ 45 min
6

The Casa del Labrador (1804) showcases neoclassical salons and Empire décor. This pavilion glittering with marble and bronze epitomizes Bourbon refinement at its peak.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Embark on a mini-cruise along the Tagus. Narrated tours glide past royal gardens, historic bridges, and tree-lined banks—Aranjuez from the water.

⏱ 75 min

Picnic with Aranjuez strawberries. These famed fresas are harvested April through June and sold at local markets—sweet, sun-warmed perfection.

⏱ 60 min
