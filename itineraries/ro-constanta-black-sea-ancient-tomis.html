☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather conditions (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, mild temperatures, and excellent conditions for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Ideal for exploring castles, hiking, and experiencing Romanian culture with comfortable temperatures.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with stunning fall colors, stable weather, and harvest season celebrations. Perfect for wine tourism, cultural exploration, and photography with moderate prices and pleasant conditions.

Summer (July-August): Warm weather (20-30°C) with long daylight hours, festival season, and peak tourist activities. Highest prices and crowds but best conditions for mountain hiking and outdoor adventures.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures (-5 to 10°C) with potential snow, lowest prices, and authentic experiences. Ideal for winter sports, Christmas markets, and experiencing traditional Romanian winter culture.

Eastern European gem with medieval castles, traditional culture, and stunning landscapes. Experience Dracula heritage, beautiful countryside, and authentic Romanian hospitality. Constanta

ancient tomis roman ruins port city

★★★★☆
📍 Ovid Square Constanta📍 Casino Constanta📍 Great Mosque Carol I📍 Genoese Lighthouse Constanta📍 Saint Peter and Paul Orthodox Cathedral Constanta📍 Hunkar Mosque Constanta
1

Jour 1: Explore Constanta's Old Town, historically known as Tomis, Romania's largest port on the Black Sea coast. Walk the recently restored streets from the City Hall to the Tomis Touristic Port, discovering beautiful architecture and the perfect blend between ancient history and modern life.

⏱ 75 min
2

Jour 1: Visit Ovid's Square at the center of the old town, featuring the statue of Publius Ovidius Naso, the Roman poet exiled to ancient Tomis. The statue bears lyrics written by the poet himself, with legend saying these words were also engraved on his tomb, though its location remains unknown.

⏱ 75 min
3

Jour 1: Marvel at the Constanta Casino, an amazing Art Nouveau style building located in a prime spot on the seafront. This impressive historical building is currently being rehabilitated and while you cannot enter, the exterior and surrounding pedestrian promenade offer splendid sea views.

⏱ 75 min
4

Jour 1: See the Capitoline Wolf monument where, during summer, a small Roman guard area is set up with hourly changing of guards ceremonies, bringing ancient Tomis to life.

⏱ 75 min
5

Jour 2: Visit the Grand Mosque of Constanta (Carol I Mosque), built in 1910 by King Carol I and delivering one of the most impressive sights the city has to offer. Byzantine architectural elements decorate the outside, while the interior features a large Turkish carpet gifted by Sultan Abdul Hamid.

⏱ 75 min
6

Jour 2: Climb the 47-meter minaret of the Grand Mosque for spectacular views of the harbor and historic town center. Each day, the muezzin climbs these steps to make the call to prayer for the Muslim community of Turkish and Tatar origin.

⏱ 75 min
7

Jour 2: Explore the National History and Archaeology Museum housed in the Old City Hall, featuring an impressive collection of ancient art and archaeological finds. See the famous Glykon Snake statue, statues of protective deities of Ancient Tomis, and the Sitting Woman and Thinker terracotta sculptures from the Hamangia Culture.

⏱ 75 min
8

Jour 2: Visit the Archaeological Park at the border of the old town, housing ancient vestiges of Ancient Tomis in an area with lush greenery. Walk the alleys discovering columns, amphorae, capitols, tower fragments, and remains of the Roman wall with two gateways from the Roman-Byzantine period.

⏱ 75 min
9

Jour 3: Spend a day at the Natural Sciences Museum Complex, which includes the Dolphinarium, Planetarium, Exotic Birds Exhibition, and Micro Delta. This is one of Constanta's most popular family attractions.

⏱ 75 min
10

Jour 3: Watch the incredible dolphin and sea lion shows at the Dolphinarium, one of Constanta's most popular attractions especially during summer. The facility currently houses rescue dolphins from Ukraine. Book tickets online from the official website to avoid waiting in line.

⏱ 75 min
11

Jour 3: After the Dolphinarium, explore the mini-habitat in the area - a mini-zoo with animals, a garden and a nice lake offering insight into the complexity of Dobrogea's fauna and flora.

⏱ 75 min
12

Jour 3: Visit the Constanta Aquarium, opened in 1958, housing over 100 species of fish and sea creatures. Located next to the old Casino building, don't miss the basin holding impressive sturgeons, plus sea horses and sea stars in the marine section.

⏱ 75 min
13

Jour 4: Enjoy Constanta's sandy beaches - some among the best in Romania. The beach near Tomis Marina offers amazing views and nearby restaurants, while the Modern beach is popular though more crowded during peak season.

⏱ 75 min
14

Jour 4: Ride the Tomis Eye Ferris Wheel, a brand new attraction introduced in 2023 at the Tomis Marina. At just 4 euros per ride, enjoy amazing views of the city and Black Sea during the 5-minute slow-paced journey. Open daily from 12 PM to 10 PM.

⏱ 75 min
15

Jour 4: Visit the Tomis Marina for a stroll near the water watching boats, or rent a boat to cruise on the Black Sea. Various companies also offer scuba diving lessons for those interested in underwater exploration.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Jour 1: Stroll along the Constanta Promenade near the Casino, a rehabilitated pedestrian area perfect for enjoying the sea breeze and watching seagulls dance in the salty air.

⏱ 60 min

Jour 1: Visit the Genoese Lighthouse on the promenade, an 8-meter historic monument originally built around the 1300s in honor of Genoese merchants. Rebuilt in the 1860s, it features a Genoese ship coat of arms on its walls.

⏱ 60 min

Jour 2: Visit the Hünkar Mosque, built in 1869 by an Ottoman Sultan for Turks who settled in Constanta after fleeing the Crimean War. This lesser-known mosque offers another perspective on the city's Ottoman heritage.

⏱ 60 min

Jour 2: Enter the St. Peter and Paul Orthodox Cathedral, an impressive Byzantine-style church with beautiful domes, arches, intricate carvings, and interior decorated with beautiful frescoes and mosaics. Entry is free.

⏱ 60 min

Jour 3: Explore the Romanian Navy Museum offering perspectives on both military and civil navy life. See ships, anchors, knots, lighthouse bulbs, navy uniforms, a mockup submarine, naval devices, torpedoes, guns, and submarine models.

⏱ 60 min

Jour 3: Visit the Modern Art Museum located in a beautiful Belle Époque building, showcasing works by Romanian artists and renowned international artists. Special exhibitions feature works by masters like Picasso.

⏱ 60 min

Jour 4: Spend time at Aqua Magic Waterpark, perfect for families seeking fun beyond the sea. Enjoy various waterslides, pools, and a lazy river with surprisingly tasty and reasonably priced food at the two on-site restaurants. Visit during weekdays to avoid crowds.

⏱ 60 min

Jour 4: Take the Gondola ride to Mamaia, starting near Aqua Magic Waterpark. Enjoy scenic views during the few-minute journey to Romania's pearl of beach resorts. Spend time on Mamaia's beaches, then walk back through Luna Park.

⏱ 60 min
