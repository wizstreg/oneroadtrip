Shoulder Seasons (March-May, September-November): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, moderate prices, ideal for most regions and outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Northern Australia best in dry season (May-October), southern regions pleasant year-round, consider vast distances and climate zones.

Derby

★☆☆☆☆
1

Derby is the western gateway to the Gibb River Road. Established in 1883, it's the first settlement founded in the Kimberley — its wide boab-lined streets were deliberately designed wide enough for bullock teams to turn around.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Boab Prison Tree, 7 km from Derby, is a 1,500-year-old baobab with a trunk circumference of 14.7 meters. It served as a holding cell for Aboriginal prisoners being transported to town. Now a protected cultural site.

⏱ 45 min
3

Mowanjum Aboriginal Art and Cultural Centre showcases the creative work of the Worrorra, Ngarinyin and Wunumbal peoples. The paintings and sculptures feature Wandjina figures — creator spirits with enormous eyes and no mouths.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Wharfingers House Museum chronicles Derby's maritime history. The adjacent Jetty, built in 1963, experiences Australia's highest tidal range: up to 12 meters of tidal movement makes mudflats appear and vanish with each cycle.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Head to Derby Visitor Centre to check current track conditions, river crossing depths, and stock up on water, fuel and supplies before departure. This is your last full resupply point before Kununurra.

⏱ 60 min

At sunset, walk out to Sculptures on the Marsh where metal structures up to 10 meters tall create striking silhouettes against the golden mudflats of King Sound.

⏱ 45 min

Windjana Gorge

★★★★★
1

Bandilngan (Windjana Gorge): the Lennard River has carved 3.5 km through the Napier Range, a 375-million-year-old fossilized coral reef. Limestone walls rise 100 meters high. Marine fossils are visible embedded in the rock face.

⏱ 90 min
2

Dimalurru (Tunnel Creek) is Western Australia's oldest cave system. Water has carved a 750-meter tunnel through the Napier Range — reaching 20 meters high and 15 meters wide in places. This is where Jandamarra, Bunuba resistance leader, took refuge until his death in 1897.

⏱ 90 min
3

The ruins of Lillimilura Police Station, built from local limestone in 1884 for the King Sound Pastoral Company, can be visited at the park edge. The site is intimately connected to Jandamarra's story.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk the Gorge Trail (1.9 km return) deep into the gorge along the freshwater pools. Allow an hour: around each bend, freshwater crocodiles bask on the banks, utterly indifferent to visitors.

⏱ 60 min

Enter Tunnel Creek with a powerful torch. You'll wade through calf-deep pools, brush past stalactites, and encounter several bat species including the rare Ghost Bat. The exit at the far end opens onto a sunlit clearing.

⏱ 75 min

At evening in Windjana Gorge campground, watch flying foxes and corellas settle in massive numbers in the trees along the water — a unique audio-visual spectacle.

⏱ 45 min

Bell Gorge

★★★★★
1

Dalmanyi (Bell Gorge) is the most spectacular gorge on the Gibb River Road. Bell Creek plunges 100 meters from the Wunaamin Miliwundi Ranges in a cascading waterfall before emptying into a deep pool ringed by horseshoe cliffs. The descent to the swimming hole is steep and rocky.

⏱ 120 min
2

Lennard River Gorge (Darrambal) is reached via a 3 km return walk from the carpark. The falls — impressive early in the dry season — are visible from the lookout perched above the deep gorge.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike to Bell Gorge (4 km return, 1.5–3h depending on pace). The trail follows Bell Creek to the waterfall overlook, then you can scramble down the rocks to swim in the lower pool — one of the Kimberley's finest swimming holes.

⏱ 150 min

On the way to Bell Gorge, detour to Galvans Gorge, just 1 km off the track. An easy trail follows the creek to a horseshoe pool with Aboriginal rock art, an ancient boab, and the famous rope swing. One of the Gibb's most accessible stops.

⏱ 60 min

Mount Hart

★☆☆☆☆
1

Mount Hart Wilderness Lodge occupies a former cattle station deep in Wunaamin Conservation Park. The property spans nearly a million acres across the King Leopold Ranges. The 50-km access track winds through green valleys and spectacular geological formations.

⏱ 60 min
2

Matthew Gorge, a few kilometers from the lodge, is an intimate gorge where black boulders frame dozens of pools, rapids and small cascades linked together. Perfect for peaceful aquatic exploration.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Swim at Barker Pool, a wide crystal-clear section of the Barker River that flows past the campground — the best swim of the day before the afternoon heat sets in.

⏱ 60 min

Follow the Annie Creek trail, a flat shaded path along a sheltered creek. The lodge gardens attract Gouldian Finches, kingfishers and cockatoos — an ideal birdwatching stroll at dawn.

⏱ 60 min

Late afternoon, drive up Sunset Hill via a short 4WD track for panoramic views across the Range valleys at sunset — one of the most silent and intense spectacles of the entire route.

⏱ 45 min

Manning Gorge

★★★★★
1

Manning Gorge sits 7 km from Mt Barnett Roadhouse, deep within Mt Barnett Station, owned by the Kupungarri Aboriginal community since 1988. The campground, right on the sandy bank of the Manning River, serves as the starting point for the hike to the upper falls.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

To reach the Upper Manning Falls, cross the Manning River using the provided dinghy — or the blue floating drums for backpacks. The 6 km return hike (1.5–2h each way) crosses open savanna and sandstone country before reaching the Gibb River Road's largest waterfall. Early season, water pours across the full width of the rock shelf.

⏱ 180 min

The Manning River at the campground offers a perfect sandy beach for swimming, relaxing and using the rope swing. Stay in the river to spot freshwater turtles and tropical fish.

⏱ 60 min

En route from Bell Gorge, detour to Galvans Gorge (1 km off-track): a horseshoe pool shaded by trees with Aboriginal rock art, a centuries-old boab, and the famous rope swing.

⏱ 60 min

Mornington

★☆☆☆☆
1

Mornington Wildlife Sanctuary is one of Australia's largest private non-government reserves: 565,000 hectares managed by Australian Wildlife Conservancy (AWC), spanning four river systems including the Fitzroy River. The camp protects key populations of Gouldian Finch, Purple-crowned Fairy-wren, and Northern Quoll.

⏱ 60 min
2

Dimond Gorge is the sanctuary's jewel: the Fitzroy River flows between red sandstone walls that plunge straight into the water. The gorge is accessible only by canoe from the camp.

⏱ 30 min
3

Sir John Gorge, a few kilometers from camp, offers a crystal-clear natural pool set against escarpments and savanna. Perfect for a sunset swim with views of the colorful cliffs.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rent a canoe from camp and paddle to Dimond Gorge: the sandstone walls frame a calm water corridor you explore at your own pace. A unique Kimberley experience.

⏱ 180 min

Join the morning guided birding tour led by AWC ecologists to secret spots for observing Gouldian Finches, kingfishers and raptors. One of the Kimberley's finest birdwatching experiences.

⏱ 120 min

The camp offers several self-guided walks through savanna and along gorge edges. In the evening, attend the free presentation by AWC ecologists on Kimberley wildlife conservation.

⏱ 90 min

Drysdale River

★☆☆☆☆
1

Drysdale River Station is a family-run million-acre cattle station, 59 km north of the Gibb River Road / Kalumburu Road junction. It serves as the logistics base for travelers to Mitchell Plateau: caravan storage, fuel, restaurant, and scenic flights available.

⏱ 45 min
2

Punamii-Uunpuu (Mitchell Falls): four tiers of cascades tumble over layers of sandstone across a tropical plateau held sacred by the Wunambal Gaambera people. The sheer gorge walls shelter some of the Kimberley's oldest rock art sites — Wandjina and Gwion Gwion (Bradshaw) paintings.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Hike to Mitchell Falls (8.6 km return, 3–4h). The trail crosses Mertens Creek, passes Little Mertens Falls and Big Mertens Falls — two breathtaking intermediate cascades — before reaching the main falls. Swimming possible in pools above the falls.

⏱ 240 min

Optionally, combine the hike with a helicopter return flight: lift off from the falls lookout and soar over the four-tiered cascade, gorges, and Kimberley coastline.

⏱ 60 min

En route, stop at the King Edward River crossing — a clear waterway lined with pandanus, perfect for cooling off and birdwatching.

⏱ 30 min

Home Valley

★☆☆☆☆
1

Ellenbrae Station, 64 km past the Kalumburu junction, is a legendary Gibb River Road stopover. The homemade scones served on the veranda have passed through generations — a bittersweet pause before the final long stretch.

⏱ 45 min
2

Home Valley Station sprawls across 1.4 million acres on the banks of the Pentecost River, beneath the gaze of the Cockburn Ranges. The station is owned by the Indigenous Land Corporation and operates as a training academy for Aboriginal communities of the East Kimberley.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Pentecost River crossing is one of the most photographed scenes in the Australian outback: your 4WD rolls into brick-red water with the Cockburn Ranges escarpments as backdrop. Check depth before entering — saltwater crocodiles frequent the banks.

⏱ 30 min

The Cockburn Ranges lookout, signposted on the Gibb River Road, offers sweeping views across the western face of the red sandstone escarpments and the confluence of the Durack and Pentecost Rivers. Stunning at sunset.

⏱ 45 min

Embark on a horseback riding tour from Home Valley Station. Guides lead visitors across the pastures and down to the banks of the Pentecost River — an authentic immersion in Kimberley station life.

⏱ 120 min

Bindoola Falls, accessible on foot from the Gibb River Road, offers a glimpse of what the gorge would look like in full wet season. During the dry, the residual pools are perfect for swimming.

⏱ 45 min

El Questro

★★☆☆☆
1

El Questro Wilderness Park covers 700,000 acres of the East Kimberley: sandstone gorges, thermal springs, riverine forests, and vast wild expanses. A former cattle station opened to tourism in 1991, the park offers an unmatched diversity of experiences on the Gibb River Road.

⏱ 60 min
2

Emma Gorge is framed by the sandstone cliffs of the Cockburn Range. After an hour's hike up a pandanus-lined creek, a 65-meter waterfall plunges into a turquoise pool. A warm spring trickles discreetly from the right wall — seek it out after your swim.

⏱ 120 min
3

Zebedee Springs: a series of thermal pools at 32°C linked together at the base of a gorge filled with prehistoric Livistona palms. Open to the public 7am–12pm only. A 400-meter walk from the carpark.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Embark on the Chamberlain Gorge cruise (approximately 3 km accessible only by boat). Guides point out saltwater crocodiles lurking beneath the walls, rock wallabies, and archerfish that spit jets of water to knock down insects up to 3 meters high.

⏱ 90 min

Drive up to Branco's Lookout late afternoon via the 17-km 4WD track: sunset panorama across the Elgee Cliffs and the Chamberlain River meanders from the lookout named after a former Wyndham shire president.

⏱ 90 min

Fish for barramundi on the Pentecost or Chamberlain Rivers. Guides offer 4WD trips to exclusive spots where threadfin salmon and catfish also hide.

Kununurra

★★★☆☆
1

Kununurra, meaning 'meeting of big waters' in the Miriwoong language, is the gateway to the East Kimberley. Founded in 1963 for the Ord River Scheme, the town is surrounded by vast tropical crop fields irrigated by Lake Argyle.

⏱ 60 min
2

Mirima National Park (Hidden Valley), 2 km from town center, concentrates millions-of-years-old red sandstone formations — a miniature Bungle Bungles within walking distance. Four trails reveal boabs clinging to cliffs, grinding stones, and Aboriginal petroglyphs.

⏱ 90 min
3

Lake Argyle, 70 km from Kununurra, is Australia's largest artificial reservoir: 1,000 km² in the dry season (18 times the area of Sydney Harbour). It hosts over a third of Australia's bird species and shelters 25,000 freshwater crocodiles.

⏱ 120 min
4

Wyndham, 100 km from Kununurra, is Western Australia's northernmost town. The Five Rivers Lookout overlooks the confluence of the Ord, Pentecost, King, Forrest and Durack Rivers flowing into Cambridge Gulf — a panorama of rare scale.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

As an optional excursion (250 km return), Purnululu National Park is home to the Bungle Bungles: 350 beehive towers of black-and-orange banded sandstone, UNESCO World Heritage listed. The gorges of Cathedral Gorge and Echidna Chasm look like nothing else on Earth. Scenic flights available from Kununurra.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset cruise on Lake Argyle: pink granite rocks emerge from the water, fishing eagles soar overhead, and hundreds of freshwater crocodiles glide onto the banks. Swim in the resort's infinity pool in the evening.

⏱ 150 min

Drive up to Kelly's Knob Lookout at sunset — a short climb from the carpark delivers 360° views over town, the Ord Valley, irrigated fields, and the Carr Boyd Ranges bathed in golden light.

⏱ 45 min

Visit Waringarri Aboriginal Arts to meet Miriwoong artists and discover paintings, sculptures, and objects from the traditional people of the East Kimberley. Cultural tours guided by Elders are sometimes available at sunset in Mirima National Park.

⏱ 60 min
