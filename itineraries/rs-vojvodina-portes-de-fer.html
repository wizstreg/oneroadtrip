Belgrade

★★★★☆
1

The scent of Turkish coffee drifts across Kalemegdan long before you spot the ramparts. Belgrade Fortress has commanded the confluence of the Sava and Danube since Roman times, rebuilt by Ottomans and Austrians alike. Climb to the Pobednik, the Victor monument, for the clearest view of both rivers. Park admission free, military museum closed Mondays.

⏱ 90 min
2

Built between the 1st and 18th centuries, the Roman Well at Kalemegdan is neither Roman nor a well: it's a medieval underground dungeon. The descent into the citadel's belly takes twenty minutes, but the surprise effect is worth the trip. Separate ticket from the museum, bring small change.

⏱ 30 min
3

Take pedestrian Knez Mihailova Street to reach Republic Square. Nineteenth-century facades compete with brand-name storefronts. At the end, the National Museum of Serbia displays artifacts from Vinča and Lepenski Vir that you'll see again in model form later in the journey. Free on weekends.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Skadarlija feels a bit postcard-perfect, but the grilled meats are serious and musicians still play real tamburica. The quarter measures just 400 meters: two or three terraces are enough to soak up the atmosphere. Book ahead if you're coming on Friday evening.

⏱ 90 min

If you only have one evening in Belgrade, spend it on the splavovi, those floating bar-barges moored along the Sava. The scene starts late, around 11pm. No strict dress code, but some splavovi screen at the door.

⏱ 120 min

Sremski Karlovci

★★★★☆
1

Locals call Sremski Karlovci the 'wine galaxy': fifteen cellars in a town you can cross on foot in half an hour. Bermet, a sweet spiced wine, is the local specialty, praised by European travelers since the 16th century. The Živanović cellar offers a tasting with a visit to their beekeeping museum.

⏱ 60 min
2

Push open the door to the Orthodox Cathedral of Saint Nicholas, baroque with a gilded iconostasis. Right next door, the Karlovci Gymnasium, one of the oldest secondary schools in the Balkans, recalls that this village was a Serbian intellectual center in the 18th century. Exterior visit free.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Fifteen cellars in a village you cross in thirty minutes: it's the density that makes Sremski Karlovci unique. Taste Bermet at Kiš, a cellar active since 1830, where Prince Charles once stopped to order white wine. Budget about 500 RSD for a three-wine tasting.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9.8Šomin lagum - Guest house
🚗Drive to next stop: 12 km · 20min

Novi Sad

★★★☆☆
1

Petrovaradin deserves the nickname 'Gibraltar of the Danube.' Built from 1692 to 1780, the fortress covers 112 hectares and contains 16 km of underground galleries. The intriguing detail: the clock tower has its hands reversed—the large one shows the hours. Guided tour of the tunnels recommended, about 400 RSD.

⏱ 90 min
2

Sixteen kilometers of underground galleries beneath Petrovaradin, dug on four levels, served as a communication and defense network. Temperature stays at 15°C year-round. Tours last about an hour, accompanied by a guide from the municipal museum. Booking at the Novi Sad museum, departures at fixed times.

⏱ 60 min
3

Stroll across Liberty Square (Trg Slobode) at the foot of the neo-Renaissance town hall. Cafés line their terraces beneath linden trees. Zmaj Jovina, the pedestrian street, extends the walk toward the Cathedral of the Name of Mary. No map needed, the center is walkable in twenty minutes.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Every July, the EXIT festival transforms Petrovaradin into a giant stage for electronic and rock music. Outside festival season, the fortress houses a planetarium, artists' workshops, and the world's largest informal art circle, Likovni Krug. Free admission to the upper town.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9.8Relax House NS
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 30min

Fruska Gora

★★★★☆
1

If you only see one Fruška Gora monastery, choose Krušedol. Founded in the 16th century, it preserves frescoes of remarkable finesse and the relics of the Branković family. Of the original 35 monasteries, 16 still stand. Admission is free, modest dress requested.

⏱ 45 min
2

Continue to Novo Hopovo monastery, former residence of writer Dositej Obradović. It's one of the largest of the sixteen surviving monasteries, with particularly well-preserved 16th-century frescoes. The calm of the place contrasts with the bustle of Novi Sad, just 30 minutes away.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Under the canopy of oaks and lindens, marked trails of Fruška Gora wind through gentle terrain. The park covers more than 25,000 hectares and offers over 200 km of cycling paths. Bring water, refreshment points are rare between monasteries.

⏱ 90 min
$$$★9.8Relax House NS
🚗Drive to next stop: 95 km · 1h25

Sombor

★★☆☆☆
1

The Milan Konjović Gallery houses more than 1,000 works bequeathed by the painter to his hometown. Konjović wielded an intense expressionist palette. Five rooms upstairs, two on the ground floor. Admission costs a few hundred dinars.

⏱ 45 min
2

Sombor is the town where bicycles outnumber cars. The Županija, the town hall, houses an enormous cycle of paintings on the Battle of Senta. Free access during town hall opening hours.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Rent a bike and follow the shaded canals that crisscross Sombor. Tree-lined avenues turn every street into an open-air salon. Finish with fresh kajmak bought at the covered market. Rentals are easily found near the center.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9.9Apartments Beck - Super Central
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 55min

Subotica

★★★☆☆
1

Light streams through the stained glass of Subotica Synagogue and explodes in color across the walls. Built in 1902 by Komor and Jakab in Hungarian Art Nouveau style, restored in 2018 after decades of abandonment. Eight steel columns support the central dome. Closed Mondays, admission about 250 RSD.

⏱ 45 min
2

Cross the Korzo to the Raichle Palace, a private 1904 residence converted into a gallery. The polychrome facade with Zsolnay ceramics is an Art Nouveau masterpiece. The visit takes twenty minutes, just enough to admire the floral motifs in every room.

⏱ 30 min
3

Subotica's town hall, completed in 1910, is the largest building in Vojvodina. Its 76-meter tower offers a panorama over the Pannonian plain. The same architects, Komor and Jakab, designed both the synagogue and the town hall. Guided tour available in English.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Lake Palić, 8 km away, has its own stop in this circuit. Save it for tomorrow. In late afternoon, climb to the top of the town hall tower for the panorama over the Pannonian plain at sunset.

⏱ 30 min
$$$★9.9Tara View Apartment
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 10min

Palic

★★★★☆
1

The 17-km promenade around Lake Palić runs past the Grand Terrace, the Music Pavilion, and the Water Tower, all in Viennese Secession style. The lake, Serbia's largest natural lake, covers 5.6 km² and doesn't exceed 2 meters in depth. Swimming on the north shore in summer, cafés facing the water.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Palić Zoo, founded in 1949, spreads across 10 hectares at the lake's edge. Families spend an hour or two here. The Palić European Film Festival, launched in 1993, takes place every summer in the Grand Park. Zoo admission modest.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.8Mirage rooms -Subotica,Bolmanska 31
🚗Drive to next stop: 110 km · 1h30

Zrenjanin

★★☆☆☆
1

Zrenjanin has Serbia's oldest theater building, inaugurated in 1836. Its puppet troupe, the country's most award-winning, regularly tours Europe. The theater is built on the foundations of the old Bečkerek fortress. Check the program online.

⏱ 30 min
2

Walk around Trg Slobode to appreciate the architectural eclecticism: neo-baroque town hall, Catholic cathedral, Contemporary Art Gallery founded in 1962. The latter was the first gallery dedicated to modern art in Yugoslavia. Free admission.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Locals come to the covered market for Banat products: white cheese, acacia honey, smoked paprika. The pedestrian center, developed in the 1980s, is pleasantly walkable between cafés. The terraces on Kralja Aleksandra Street are packed in the afternoon.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.8DML Apartments
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 35min

Carska Bara

★★★★☆
1

Silence first, then a beat of wings: Carska Bara shelters about 240 bird species across 1,700 hectares of marshes, reed beds, and flooded forests. Two pairs of white-tailed eagles nest in the strict protection zone. Access only with a guide, booking required.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Board the Old Begej tourist boat for an hour-long cruise through narrow reed-lined channels. The rate is about 5,000 RSD per group. The boat departs from the welcome point at fixed morning times.

⏱ 60 min

The four-kilometer fitness trail leads to a wooden observation platform above the marsh. The route is flat, walkable in sneakers. Budget a good hour round-trip with photo stops. No refreshment stand on the trail.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 1h20

Vrsac

★★☆☆☆
1

Climb up to Vršac Tower, a 15th-century vestige perched on a hill above the plain. The climb is steep but the panorama over vineyards and the Banat steppe rewards the effort. The tower is open Thursday to Saturday, 11am to 6pm.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Pharmacy on the Steps, opened in the 18th century, is part of the municipal museum. It's a baroque building that recalls when Vršac was a prosperous town under the Habsburgs. The museum also displays paintings by Paja Jovanović, a painter born here. Modest admission.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you pass through in September, the Grožđebal (Grape Festival) animates Vršac for three days. The rest of the year, cellars around town offer tastings of Kreaca, an indigenous white grape variety. The first written mention of Vršac wine dates to 1494.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9Hotel Helvecija 1880
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 45min

Bela Crkva

★★★☆☆
1

Bela Crkva is nicknamed the 'Venice of Vojvodina,' which is excessive, but its seven artificial lakes dug since 1904 for gravel extraction form an unexpected string of clear water in the plain. The main lake (Gradsko jezero) is developed with a beach and cafés. The lakes cover about 150 hectares.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Head to Vračev Gaj lake via the Kovin road: a green island accessible by a wooden bridge serves as a natural beach. Pedal boats for rent in summer. Two EuroVelo routes (6 and 13) cross the municipality, perfect for a bike tour between lakes.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9.9Apartmani Pobeda
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 1h15

Smederevo

★★★☆☆
1

The 25 towers of Smederevo Fortress rise along the Danube on 11 hectares, making it one of the largest medieval lowland fortresses in Europe. Built between 1428 and 1430 by Despot Đurađ Branković, it was heavily damaged in 1941 by an explosion. Access to the ramparts is free.

⏱ 60 min
2

Visit the Smederevo Museum to understand the history of the last medieval Serbian capital. In 2012, the tomb of an unknown noblewoman with precious jewelry was discovered there. The museum is small but the archaeological pieces are worth seeing.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you arrive in September, the Smederevska Jesen (Smederevo Autumn) is Serbia's oldest wine festival. The fortress comes alive with concerts and tastings. Off-season, the Danube quay offers a peaceful walk at the foot of the walls.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.9Serendipity
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 40min

Viminacium

★★★★☆
1

Viminacium was the capital of the Roman province of Moesia Superior, founded in the 1st century. The site extends over 450 hectares of which only 5% have been excavated. A 3rd-century triumphal arch was discovered in 2024. Baths, amphitheater, and mausoleum are visible in the open air.

⏱ 90 min
2

A mammoth skeleton named Vika rests in a pavilion next to the Roman ruins. It's one of the most complete specimens found in Serbia. The contrast between ancient vestiges and prehistoric paleontology makes Viminacium unique. The Mamut Park is visited with the same ticket.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Try the reconstructed Roman meal served in the archaeological park: two meats in sauce prepared according to a 3rd-century recipe, accompanied by wine served in an amphora. Booking necessary for groups, but individuals can try at the site restaurant.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.9Boutique Hill Hotel
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 35min

Golubac

★★★★★
1

Nine towers planted in rock, between 20 and 25 meters high, guard the entrance to the Đerdap gorges. Golubac Fortress, restored and reopened in 2019 thanks to European funds, is arguably the most beautiful castle on the Danube along its 2,850 km course. Audio guide included in the ticket.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take the path along the bank beneath the fortress for water-level photos. A small archaeological park with Roman and Byzantine remains completes the visit. Drone use is prohibited in the tourist area.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★10Golubac Bay Apartment
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 50min

Lepenski Vir

★★★★☆
1

Lepenski Vir upends everything we think we know about European Mesolithic cultures. Trapezoidal houses, concrete floors 8,000 years old, sandstone sculptures depicting half-human, half-fish faces. The site was relocated 30 meters higher to escape rising dam waters. The museum under a canopy protects the original foundations.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk through the museum under its glass canopy overlooking the Danube. Models and reproductions of sculptures help understand the social organization of this culture. Allow an hour for the complete visit. No cafeteria on site.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.7Harmony apartman
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 35min

Donji Milanovac

★★★☆☆
1

The Đerdap is Europe's longest gorge: 130 km of canyons where the Danube plunges up to 82 meters deep in the Mali Kazan defile. The national park covers nearly 64,000 hectares on the Serbian side. Donji Milanovac, at the heart of the park, serves as a base for cruises and hikes.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Board a one-hour cruise from Donji Milanovac through the Veliki and Mali Kazan. From the water, you'll see the Tabula Traiana and Decebalus's head carved on the Romanian side. Book the day before with Đerdap Travel or at the park office.

⏱ 90 min

At the Kapetan Mišin Breg viewpoint, the terrace overlooks the Danube. The restaurant serves Vlach specialties: grilled river fish, smoked cheese. Most tourists consider this viewpoint the most beautiful in eastern Serbia.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9.9Apartment Danubius
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 45min

Tekija

★★☆☆☆
1

The Tabula Traiana commemorates Trajan's victory over the Dacians and the completion of his military road along the Danube, carved around 103 AD. The original plaque was moved 50 meters higher during dam construction. It's only visible from the river.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Romanians call it Decebal Rex and are proud of it: 55 meters high, carved in rock between 1994 and 2004 on the Romanian shore, facing the Tabula on the Serbian side. It's the tallest rock relief in Europe. The face-off between the Dacian king and the Roman plaque, two thousand years apart, produces a striking effect.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Approach the Tabula only by boat from Tekija or Donji Milanovac: the coastal configuration prevents any land access. Local boatmen offer half-hour round trips. Ask at the Kladovo tourist office.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★10Casa Nuova Tekija - Kladovo
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 25min

Kladovo

★★★☆☆
1

Fetislam, built in 1524 under Suleiman the Magnificent, controlled navigation on the Danube. The fortress covers 18 hectares and includes a Great and a Small Fort. It's one of six fortresses returned to the Serbs by the Ottomans in 1867. Recently restored, modest admission ticket.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Kladovo's river beach surprises with its gentleness: sand, warm water from the Đerdap reservoir lake, dense surrounding forests. Fishing enthusiasts will find catfish and zander. The place is much less crowded than Belgrade's beaches.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9.9Danubiana Apartments
🚗Drive to next stop: 3 km · 5min

Diana

★★★☆☆
1

The Diana castrum, a 1st-century Roman camp, was part of the Danube limes. Remains of granaries, watchtowers, and civilian quarters cover several hectares downstream from Kladovo. The pillars of Trajan's Bridge, completed in 105, are steps away. Free admission, signs in English.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

From the site, spot the pillars of Trajan's Bridge surfacing in the Danube during low water. This 1,135-meter bridge, designed by Apollodorus of Damascus, was the longest arch bridge in the world for over a millennium. An explanatory sign reconstructs the structure.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 50min

Negotin

★★☆☆☆
1

The aroma of red grapes permeates the alleys of Rajačke Pimnice, a cellar-village of 270 stone houses built between the 18th and 20th centuries. Each house is a cellar: the bottom, buried two meters deep, stores wine, the top housed winemakers during harvest. The complex is classified as a cultural asset of exceptional importance, proposed for UNESCO in 2010.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Push open the door of a cellar open to visitors and taste the reds of Negotin Krajina, from vines planted in Roman times. The Rajac cemetery, with its sandstone steles carved with solar motifs, deserves a ten-minute detour. No centralized shop: buy directly from the winemaker.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★10Kizde
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 40min

Zajecar

★★☆☆☆
1

Felix Romuliana is the best-preserved Roman palace in Europe outside Italy. Inscribed on UNESCO since 2007, this 3rd-4th century fortified complex was commissioned by Emperor Galerius and named after his mother Romula. The mosaics of Dionysus and Medusa, displayed at the Zaječar museum, alone justify the visit.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Complete the visit at the National Museum of Zaječar, 11 km from the site. Galerius's porphyry head and the arch with the inscription FELIX ROMULIANA are presented there. The museum is small, an hour is enough. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★8.1Garni Hotel Hamburg
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 1h05

Bor

★★☆☆☆
1

If you love wild gorges, Lazar Canyon is the longest and deepest in eastern Serbia: 6 km long, steep limestone walls. The developed trail from Lazar Cave (the country's longest) offers dizzying viewpoints. Plan on hiking boots.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Descend to Bor Lake (Borsko jezero), nestled at 438 meters altitude at the foot of Crni Vrh. The artificial lake, created in 1959, reaches 48 meters deep. Conifer forests, meadows, water at 25°C in summer. A hotel and bungalows line the shore.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★8.1Garni Hotel Hamburg
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 55min

Despotovac

★★★☆☆
1

The eleven towers of Manasija rise in the Resava gorge like a fortified castle. Founded between 1406 and 1418 by Despot Stefan Lazarević, the monastery housed the Resava School, a center for manuscript copying and translation. The surviving frescoes rank among the most refined in Serbian medieval art.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take time to see Despotovac's miniature park on the town's edge: models of Serbian medieval monasteries, useful for situating those you've seen and those you've missed. The Veliki Buk waterfall and Veliko Vrelo spring are a few minutes away by car.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.9Miletic Villas
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min

Resava Cave

★★★★☆
1

Resava Cave, estimated at 80 million years old, is visited on a developed 800-meter route. Stalactites, stalagmites, and immense halls follow one another under a vault that reaches 25 meters in places. Constant temperature at 7°C, bring a jacket. Guided tour only, regular departures.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Veliki Buk waterfall and Veliko Vrelo spring are a few minutes by car from the cave. The spring's turquoise basin, 18 meters deep, is one of the most photogenic in eastern Serbia. Free parking, five-minute walk access.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 45min

Pozarevac

★★☆☆☆
1

The Milena Pavlović-Barili Gallery occupies the painter's birthplace, given to the city by her mother in 1962. Barili's surrealist canvases, often compared to Frida Kahlo, are a discovery for most visitors. Small gallery, great intensity.

⏱ 45 min
2

The National Museum of Požarevac, founded in 1895, is Serbia's second oldest. More than 50,000 objects, from prehistory to the modern era. The lapidary in the courtyard displays sarcophagi and Roman sculptures from nearby Viminacium.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The Danube quay in Požarevac, developed as a promenade, leads to Čačalica Park. The terraces on Tabačka Čaršija Street serve hearty local cuisine. Try the roštilj accompanied by a glass of Smederevka, the local white. The covered market is worth a morning visit.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.7Luxury Apartments 88
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min

Ljubicevo

★★☆☆☆
1

Ljubičevo Stud Farm, founded in 1853 by Prince Miloš Obrenović, raises thoroughbreds and Lipizzans on 500 hectares of meadow. At the park's center, a monument honors Ljubičevac, an undefeated racehorse in the Balkans. The stables can be visited on weekdays upon request.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Every first weekend of September, the Ljubičevo Equestrian Games draw crowds with trotting races and dressage demonstrations. Off-season, the stud farm park offers a calm walk under century-old plane trees. Free admission.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 80 km · 1h05

Belgrade

★★★★☆
1

Belgraders climb Avala Tower on Sundays for fresh air. At 204.5 meters, it's the tallest tower in the Balkans, rebuilt in 2010 after its destruction in 1999. The mountain (511 m) offers a panorama over Belgrade and the plain. Paid tower admission, parking at the summit.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

End the road trip with dinner in the Dorćol quarter, Belgrade's oldest. Trendy restaurants rub shoulders with traditional kafanas. Try a ćevapčići accompanied by a Zaječarsko beer, brewed in the city you passed through a few days earlier.

⏱ 90 min
