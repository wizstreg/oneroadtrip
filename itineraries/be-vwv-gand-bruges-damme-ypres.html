Ghent

★★★★☆
1

STAM—Ghent City Museum—occupies a 17th-century abbey and chronicles ten centuries of Ghent's evolution. Relief maps and scale models reveal how the city developed around the confluence of the Lys and Scheldt rivers, transforming from medieval cloth hub to modern metropolis.

⏱ 75 min
2

Sint-Michielsbrug offers Ghent's most iconic viewpoint. From this bridge, you'll see the city's three medieval towers—Saint Nicholas, the Belfry, and Saint Bavo—aligned in a dramatic architectural sequence spanning just a few hundred meters.

⏱ 20 min
3

Step inside Saint Bavo's Cathedral to stand before the Ghent Altarpiece. Jan and Hubert van Eyck's masterpiece, completed in 1432, ranks among the most important paintings in Western art history. The central panel is displayed in a dedicated side chapel with exceptional interpretation displays.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Belfry of Ghent, a 14th-century UNESCO World Heritage tower, once stood guard over the city's commercial freedoms. Its bells still mark the rhythm of daily life, while interior exhibits tell the story of Ghent's powerful medieval guilds.

⏱ 60 min
5

Gravensteen—the Castle of the Counts of Flanders—has dominated the city center since the 12th century. Its moat, crenellated battlements, and reconstructed torture chamber make it one of Belgium's best-preserved medieval fortresses.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk along the Graslei and Korenlei, twin quays that once flanked the medieval river port. Guild houses from the 12th to 17th centuries line both sides, their stepped gables reflected in the still waters of the Lys.

⏱ 60 min

Wander through the Patershol district, a former tanners' quarter where cobbled lanes wind past medieval houses. By evening, the neighborhood transforms into one of Ghent's liveliest dining quarters, its intimate scale contrasting with the monumental architecture downtown.

⏱ 45 min

Ghent

★★★★☆
1

Huis van Alijn occupies a 14th-century almshouse in the Vrijdagmarkt quarter. This museum of everyday Belgian life showcases toys, clothing, furniture, and domestic objects that bring 20th-century Belgium—from 1900 to 1980—vividly back to life through ordinary people's stories.

⏱ 75 min
2

Saint Nicholas' Church, a 13th-century example of Scheldt Gothic, rises opposite the Belfry on the Korenmarkt. Its massive tower and equally proportioned side aisles represent a rare style specific to the Scheldt region, distinct from the soaring verticality of Brabantine Gothic.

⏱ 40 min
3

Cross the Vrijdagmarkt, Ghent's grand market square with its working-class edge. On Saturday mornings, it hosts one of Flanders' most vibrant markets. The statue of Jacob van Artevelde, a 14th-century populist tribune who defied the French king, commands the center.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take a guided canal cruise from the Graslei embarkation points. Boatmen navigate the interior waterways, revealing medieval facades and historic bridge arches from perspectives impossible to reach on foot.

⏱ 50 min

Explore the cobbled lanes around Huis van Alijn, where vintage shops, secondhand clothing stores, and traditional brown cafés occupy stepped-gable houses in what feels like a village within the city.

⏱ 60 min

Brugge

★★★★☆
1

Climb the 366 steps of the Belfry of Bruges to survey the entire city. This 13th-century tower, part of Belgium and France's UNESCO-listed belfries, houses a 47-bell carillon that chimes every quarter hour, its sound echoing across medieval rooftops.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Groeninge Museum holds the finest collection of Flemish Primitives outside major national museums. Jan van Eyck, Hans Memling, Gerard David, Hieronymus Bosch—15th-century panels hang alongside works from Bruges' 19th-century Symbolist movement.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Church of Our Lady shelters a marble Madonna and Child by Michelangelo, commissioned by a Bruges merchant in the early 16th century. It's one of only a handful of the Florentine sculptor's works to leave Italy during his lifetime.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Princely Beguinage Ten Wijngaarde, founded in 1245, is inscribed on UNESCO's World Heritage List. White-painted houses encircle a silent lawn where daffodils bloom each spring. A Benedictine community continues to live here today.

⏱ 40 min
5

The Minnewater—Lake of Love—borders the beguinage to the south. This 14th-century basin served as both lock and water reservoir for the inner harbor. The shaded promenade around it has become one of the most photographed spots in the city.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Board a canal boat for a cruise through medieval Bruges. In half an hour, boatmen navigate the network of waterways, passing beneath a succession of low brick bridges that have spanned these canals for centuries.

⏱ 35 min

Capture sunset reflections along the canals. The Rozenhoedkaai, steps from the Belfry, offers the classic view of Bruges' rooftops mirrored in calm water—the city's most photographed vantage point.

⏱ 45 min

Damme

★★★☆☆
1

The Church of Our Lady of Damme, begun in 1225, recalls the era when this village served as Bruges' outer harbor. Its distinctive flat tower encloses 206 steps leading to panoramic views across 17th-century ramparts, the canal, and the vast polder landscape. Inside, 13th-century wooden apostle statues survived the religious upheavals of the 16th century.

⏱ 50 min
2

The 15th-century Gothic town hall dominates Damme's central square. Its stepped-gable facade, typically Flemish, was built between 1464 and 1468—even as the town's decline had already begun following the silting of the Zwin estuary. This is where Charles the Bold married Margaret of York in 1468.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Saint John's Hospital was founded in 1249 by Margaret of Constantinople, Countess of Flanders. This austere building once welcomed travelers and the sick; today it houses a collection of paintings and antique furniture, open April through September.

⏱ 40 min
4

The Schellemolen is a Dutch-style windmill purchased and restored by the Province of West Flanders in 1971. It stands at the village edge facing the canal, lined with centuries-old poplars whose gnarled trunks have become one of the region's iconic images.

⏱ 25 min
5

De Grote Sterre house contains the Till Eulenspiegel Museum, dedicated to the legendary trickster figure. According to Charles De Coster's novel, this prankster born in Damme in the 16th century became a symbol of Flemish resistance. A statue of Till greets visitors at the village entrance.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

Cycle from Bruges to Damme along the Damse Vaart canal. The towpath follows 7 kilometers of ruler-straight waterway beneath centuries-old poplars. Flat, family-friendly, car-free.

⏱ 60 min

Take the paddle steamer between Bruges and Damme. The approximately 35-minute journey aboard the boat Lamme Goedzak travels up the canal with views across the Flemish countryside and polders.

⏱ 40 min

Diksmuide

★★☆☆☆
1

The Atlantikwall Raversyde at Ostend is one of Europe's best-preserved coastal fortification sites. Bunkers, trenches, command posts, and anti-aircraft guns in situ reconstruct two layers of occupation: German defenses from 1914–1918, then those of 1940–1944 superimposed atop the earlier installations.

⏱ 90 min
2

At Nieuwpoort, the King Albert I monument marks the Ganzepoot—the point where the Yser River sluices were opened in October 1914 to flood the polders and halt the German advance. This strategic decision stabilized the Belgian front for four years.

⏱ 40 min
3

The Dodengang—Trench of Death—at Diksmuide is the last preserved section of the Belgian trench system from the Great War. These dugouts, excavated mere meters from German positions on the Yser's opposite bank, remained in use from 1914 to 1918. An interpretation center accompanies the site visit.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Drive the coastal front route between Ostend and Diksmuide. This journey across former battlefields follows the coast, passes through Nieuwpoort's dunes, and reaches the flooded polders of Ypres. Stop for photos at monuments and military cemeteries dotting the route.

⏱ 90 min

Ypres

★★★★☆
1

The In Flanders Fields Museum occupies the second floor of the Lakenhalle—Cloth Hall—on Ypres' Grote Markt. The building itself was entirely destroyed by artillery during the war and reconstructed identically. At entry, each visitor receives a Poppy bracelet containing a chip that activates throughout the journey a personalized narrative of an individual who experienced the war.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Menin Gate is the most visited Great War memorial in Belgium. This monumental arch, inaugurated in 1927, bears the engraved names of 54,896 Commonwealth soldiers who disappeared in the Ypres Salient and have no known grave. Every evening at 8 p.m., traffic stops and buglers from the Last Post Association sound the Last Post—a tradition unbroken since 1928.

⏱ 45 min
3

Tyne Cot, 9 kilometers northeast of Ypres, is the world's largest Commonwealth war cemetery. Its 11,961 graves and rows of white headstones stretching to the horizon convey the immensity of the Battle of Passchendaele (1917). The Cross of Sacrifice rises atop a captured German bunker—at King George V's request during his 1922 visit.

⏱ 60 min
4

Essex Farm Cemetery marks the spot where Canadian medical officer John McCrae wrote the poem In Flanders Fields in May 1915, after burying a friend. The advanced concrete dressing station remains visible. The grave of Private John Valentine Price, killed at age 15, is among the site's most visited.

⏱ 40 min
5

The ramparts of Ypres are among Flanders' rare medieval and early modern fortifications still intact. Ypres Ramparts Cemetery, nestled in the moat, was one of the first military cemeteries established during the war. The promenade along the parapet walk offers views across the rebuilt city.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Attend the Last Post ceremony at the Menin Gate at 8 p.m. Every evening since 1928, buglers sound beneath the arch in tribute to the missing. Representatives from associations, schools, or families lay wreaths. Arrive 20 minutes early to secure a spot.

⏱ 30 min

Drive or cycle a memory loop around the Salient: Essex Farm → Langemark (German cemetery, 44,000 soldiers) → Tyne Cot → Passchendaele → return to Ypres. The flat polder landscape contrasts starkly with the density of memorials.

⏱ 180 min
