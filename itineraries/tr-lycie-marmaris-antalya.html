Dalyan

★★☆☆☆
1

The scent of reeds rises from the Dalyan River the moment you leave the main road. This fishing village stretches along the Dalyan Çayı, which connects Lake Köyceğiz to the Mediterranean. Settle onto a riverside terrace for your first meze while the Lycian royal tombs glow in the cliff face as darkness falls.

⏱ 60 min
2

In 1986, plans for an 1,800-bed hotel threatened İztuzu Beach. British expat June Haimoff led the international campaign that saved both beach and turtles. Her cabin, now a small museum, can be visited for free at the southern end of the beach.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Saturday market is worth a visit for its fresh fruit stalls and gözleme made right in front of you. Nothing spectacular, but a genuine slice of Turkish daily life away from the resort complexes. Half an hour covers everything.

⏱ 30 min
4

Take a water taxi along the Dalyan Çayı for a close-up view of the rock-cut tombs carved into the cliff face. The crossing takes 15 minutes and costs a few lira. The best photo angle is from the opposite bank at sunset.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you arrive late afternoon, stroll the pedestrian promenade along the river. Restaurants line their tables right over the water. Order a balık ekmek (fish sandwich) and watch the boats drift past. No reservation needed.

⏱ 60 min

Head upriver by boat to the Sultaniye hot springs on Lake Köyceğiz. The thermal water emerges at 39°C, with mud baths in an adjacent pool. The half-day excursion can be combined with Kaunos.

⏱ 180 min

The Sultaniye mud baths are touristy and unglamorous, but the mud genuinely contains sulfur and minerals. You slather it on, dry in the sun, rinse off in the lake. Everyone ends up laughing.

⏱ 45 min
$$$★9.3Dna Hotel Dalyan

Kaunos

★★★★☆
1

Founded in the 9th century BC by the Carians, Kaunos was a thriving port before the delta silted it up by 5 km. Herodotus mentions the city's fierce resistance against Persian general Harpagos around 540 BC. The 5,000-seat theater and Byzantine basilica warrant the stop.

⏱ 90 min
2

Strabo described Kaunos inhabitants as 'greenish in complexion' due to malaria. When citharist Stratonicos mocked them, they sued. His defense: he hadn't called the city sick—he'd merely seen corpses walking around. The anecdote appears in Athenaeus's Deipnosophistae.

⏱ 30 min
3

Climb to the acropolis to see ramparts 4 m thick, built under Mausolus in the 4th century BC. The view sweeps across İztuzu Beach, the marshes, and the river. Allow 20 minutes for the scramble up.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

The rowboat crossing from Dalyan is the most memorable way to arrive. The boatman drops visitors at the foot of the acropolis in 10 minutes. The moment you step ashore, you're in antiquity.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 15min

Iztuzu Beach

★★★★☆
1

4.5 km of firm sand, not a building in sight: İztuzu Beach has been protected since 1988 for loggerhead turtles (Caretta caretta), which lay around 300 nests here each year. Nesting zones are marked with stakes. No lying down allowed.

⏱ 60 min
2

At the southern end, Pamukkale University's DEKAMER center cares for injured turtles (mostly boat propeller strikes). Free to visit, donations welcome. The pools let you see the animals up close.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Arrive before 10 a.m.: loggerhead turtles nest at night, and the beach closes to the public from 8 p.m. to 8 a.m. between May and October. Umbrellas and sunbeds available outside marked zones. Snack bar and toilets on site.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h

Gocek

★★★★☆
1

Six marinas in a village of 5,000 souls: Göcek is the launching point for Blue Cruises and a haven for yachts. Yet the waterfront remains pedestrian and shaded. Dalaman Airport is 20 minutes away.

⏱ 45 min
2

Göcek's twelve islands rise from the bay like a miniature archipelago. Sheltered coves, transparent water, pines down to the shore. A day-long boat tour circles the main ones.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Board a boat excursion to the islands and bays around Göcek. Public boats depart the harbor each morning with swimming stops and lunch included. Expect 150–200 TL per person.

⏱ 300 min

The waterfront is compact but pleasant. Cafés under the trees (West Cafe leads the pack) are where everyone gathers in the evening. The food is a notch above the average Turkish town. Well-prepared fish and meze.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Fethiye

★★★★☆
1

The Ionic façade of Amyntas's Tomb rises from the cliff above Fethiye like a suspended temple. Climb the 296 steps: the view over the bay and Şövalye Island is the reward. Inscription visible: 'Amyntas, son of Hermagios.'

⏱ 60 min
2

Carved from living rock in 350 BC as a single block, this Lycian tomb imitates an Ionic portico. French traveler Charles Texier, passing through in the 1830s, scratched his name into the stone. You can still spot it top left on the façade. Entry: €3 (2026).

⏱ 30 min
3

Head down to the old Paspatur quarter for its pedestrian lanes, craft shops, and Turkish cafés. The atmosphere evokes a Mediterranean souk, only quieter. Good spot for a Turkish coffee after the climb to the tomb.

⏱ 45 min
4

Fethiye's fish market runs on a clever system: you choose your fish at the stalls, then the surrounding restaurants cook it for a modest fee. Result: ultra-fresh fish, no scams, lively atmosphere.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you're a diver, Fethiye's centers offer trips to wrecks and reefs around the bay. Clear water, classic Mediterranean fauna. Expect 200–300 TL for two dives, equipment included.

⏱ 180 min

Take a dolmuş to Çalış Beach for sunset facing the islands. Feet in the sand, tea in hand. Dolmuş run easily until late evening.

⏱ 90 min

Harbor fishermen sell the day's catch as soon as the boats return, early morning. If you have a kitchen, this is your best supply. Sea bass (levrek) and red mullet (barbunya) are the local stars.

⏱ 30 min
$$$★9.6Hotel Unique-Boutique Class - Adults Only
🚗Drive to next stop: 24 km · 35min

Kadyanda

★★★★☆
1

If the site is deserted—and it almost always is—Kadyanda offers a rare moment of solitude among Lycian ruins. The ancient stadium and baths can be made out beneath the vegetation. No shop, no caretaker, just you and the stone.

⏱ 60 min
2

Lycian sarcophagi pierce the greenery like forgotten stone boxes. The theater, modest in scale, looks out over the Xanthos valley. The whole ensemble is charming but poorly signposted: follow signs from Üzümlü.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Bring closed shoes and water: no shade or kiosk on site. The visit takes an hour. Combine with Tlos if you have time.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Kayakoy

★★★★☆
1

Until 1923, Greeks and Turks lived side by side in Levissi (Kayaköy). The Greco-Turkish population exchange emptied the village of its Orthodox inhabitants. The 1957 earthquake finished off the houses. Hundreds remain, roofless, sprawled across the hillside.

⏱ 90 min
2

The faded frescoes of Taksiyarhis church (19th century) and the arches of the lower Panaghia Pyrgiotissa church remain highlights of the site. Both are closed for safety but visible from outside. Allow 1.5 hours to walk everything.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The trail to Ölüdeniz via the Lycian Way starts here and descends through pine forest in 1.5 hours. The path is marked red-white, rocky in places. Flip-flops not recommended. At the end: the beach and a dolmuş back.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 12 km · 20min

Oludeniz

★★★★☆
1

Yes, it's packed in summer. But Ölüdeniz's blue lagoon delivers on its promise: turquoise water, sandy bottom, mountains as backdrop. Access to the lagoon is paid (protected zone); the public beach of Belcekız is free just next door.

⏱ 90 min
2

Stop by Kıdrak Beach (Kidrak Beach), 2 km south of Ölüdeniz, for a less crowded version of the same scenery. Reachable on foot or by car. Umbrella pines down to the sand.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Takeoff from Babadağ at 1,960 m elevation is one of the highest commercial paragliding sites in the world. The tandem flight lasts 25–30 minutes above the lagoon. No experience required; the pilot handles everything.

⏱ 120 min

The tandem paragliding flight lasts long enough that the adrenaline gives way to contemplation. Landing happens on Belcekız beach, feet in the sand. Photos/video cost extra (around £20).

⏱ 30 min
$$$★9.5The Pearl Ölüdeniz
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 40min

Tlos

★★★★☆
1

Climb to the Ottoman fortress crowning Tlos's Lycian acropolis. The view embraces the Xanthos valley and the tomato greenhouses below. Striking contrast between antiquity and modern agriculture.

⏱ 60 min
2

Tlos's rock-cut tombs, including Bellerophon's with its winged-horse relief, are among the most photographed in Lycia. The Roman stadium and baths are worth the detour too. Allow an hour on site.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Go early morning and you'll have the site to yourself. No kiosk on site. The neighboring village of Yaka has restaurants by a stream, with trout and gözleme under the trees.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 25min

Pinara

★★★★☆
1

Site caretakers call it 'the city of a thousand tombs.' Pınara's cliff is riddled with funerary niches, aligned for dozens of meters up the rock face. The whole ensemble suggests an abandoned troglodyte apartment block.

⏱ 60 min
2

Pınara remains one of the least-visited Lycian sites. No shop, no crowds. The theater below and the agora are slowly returning to nature. Plan an hour for a peaceful visit.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Allow an hour for the complete circuit. Access road from Minare is in good condition. Site closes at sundown, no lighting.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 30min

Saklikent

★★★★☆
1

Saklıkent means 'hidden city': 18 km of canyon, up to 300 m deep, a national park since 1996. The accessible section starts with a footbridge suspended over the torrent, then you walk in the water. Entry: 250 TL (2026).

⏱ 120 min

Activities

The glacial water grips your ankles the moment you cross the torrent. The bottom is slippery, the current strong. Rent water shoes at the entrance (200 TL) or bring your own. In 30–45 minutes of walking you reach the narrowest walls (2 m wide).

⏱ 90 min

After the walk, settle into one of the restaurants on stilts above the river. Grilled trout and spinach gözleme, feet in cold water. It's kitschy but the cooling effect is real.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 15min

Sidyma

★★★☆☆
1

Lycian sarcophagi rise among the olive trees and vegetable gardens of Dodurga village. Sidyma has no fence, no ticket booth: the ruins are part of the inhabitants' everyday landscape.

⏱ 45 min
2

Dodurga village carries on its life around the ancient remains. One sarcophagus serves as a garden wall, another borders the path. It's one of the rare places where archaeology and daily life coexist this naturally.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you're after complete isolation off the tourist circuits, Sidyma is the perfect stop. The village has a small café. No official parking; pull over by the road. Visit takes 30–45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 35min

Xanthos

★★★★★
1

Capital of Lycia, Xanthos was listed as a UNESCO site in 1988. The city entered history through two mass suicides: facing the Persians (around 540 BC), then facing Brutus (42 BC). The inhabitants chose death over surrender.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Harpy Tomb pillar still dominates the site with its winged figures (copies: the originals are in the British Museum). The trilingual obelisk helped decipher Lycian. Entry: €3 (2026).

⏱ 30 min
3

The Hellenistic theater overlooks the Eşen Çayı valley. Modified in Roman times to host gladiator combats (the first six rows were removed), it could hold around 2,000 spectators.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Combine with Letoon, 4 km south by road. The two sites form a political-religious pair: Xanthos decided, Letoon sanctified. The drive takes 10 minutes.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min

Letoon

★★★★☆
1

If Xanthos was the political power, Letoon was the religious heart of Lycia. This is where the Lycian League held its assemblies. The site is modest but the atmosphere peaceful, between frogs and toppled columns.

⏱ 45 min
2

Three temples dedicated to Leto, Apollo, and Artemis date from the 5th–4th centuries BC. The Roman nymphaeum, fed by a sacred spring, is partially flooded depending on the season. The Temple of Leto was reconstructed between 2000 and 2007.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The trilingual stele from 337 BC (Lycian, Greek, Aramaic) was discovered here in 1973. It served as a Rosetta Stone for Lycian. The original is in Fethiye museum, but a reproduction stands on site.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Patara

★★★★☆
1

Patara Beach stretches for kilometers of fine sand, backed by dunes. It's also a turtle nesting site, protected and closed at night in season. Very few facilities: a car park, a café, that's all.

⏱ 90 min
2

Head up to the ruins: the bouleuterion (Lycian parliament) is remarkably preserved. This is where the Lycian League convened. The reconstructed lighthouse and city gate complete the visit. Saint Nicholas was born in Patara.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Patara remains surprisingly uncrowded compared to Ölüdeniz. The beach is huge, there's always space. Bring your picnic: the only café is at the car park.

⏱ 120 min

If you go between May and October, watch for turtle tracks in the morning. Nesting zones are marked. Swimming allowed outside protected perimeters.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 30min

Kaputas Beach

★★★★☆
1

From the road, the staircase plunges down the cliff to a sandy cove wedged between two rock walls. The water is an almost unreal turquoise. It's small, it's packed in summer, but the sight warrants the stop.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Park at the top and descend the steps (around 190). No natural shade on the beach. In high season, arrive before 10 a.m. or after 4 p.m. Off season, you'll have it to yourself.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 40min

Kas

★★★★☆
1

Kaş has kept its village scale despite its reputation. The cobbled lanes climb gently from the harbor, between bougainvillea and sleeping cats. No big seafront hotels, no garish nightclubs.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Hellenistic theater of Antiphellos, built facing the sea and the Greek island of Meis (Kastellorizo), is the only theater in Anatolia oriented toward the water. Restored in 2008, it hosts screenings and concerts in summer. Free entry.

⏱ 45 min
3

Lycian sarcophagi rise between shops and cafés in the town center. The Lion Tomb (Kral Mezarı), in the middle of a street, bears an inscription in Lycian still undeciphered. People walk past without thinking.

⏱ 30 min
4

Climb to the Doric tomb at Akdam for its 24 dancing figures carved in relief. The tomb dates from the 4th century BC and measures 4.5 × 4.5 m. Restored in 2008, it's one of the best preserved on the coast.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If diving tempts you, Kaş is Turkey's capital for the sport. Centers run trips to wrecks, reefs, and underwater caves. The water is clear, marine life abundant.

⏱ 180 min

The island of Meis is Greek but 20 minutes by boat. The ferry makes the round trip in a day. Passport required. On site: a harbor, three tavernas, absolute calm.

⏱ 240 min

Book a boat to Kekova directly at Kaş harbor. The excursion includes Üçağız, the sunken city, and Simena. Allow a full day. Alternative: rent a sea kayak to paddle the coast.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★9.4Lukka Exclusive Hotel – Adult Only +12
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 50min

Ucagiz

★★★★☆
1

Üçağız means 'three mouths': three exits to the open sea from the bay. This fishing village is the departure point for Kekova excursions. Lycian sarcophagi in the harbor water, wooden boats at anchor.

⏱ 45 min
2

Climb to Simena castle (Kaleköy), reachable only by boat or on foot from Üçağız. The medieval castle shelters Lycia's smallest ancient theater. The panoramic view over Kekova bay justifies the effort.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The artisan ice cream in Kaleköy is worth the detour—goat's milk ice cream, served in tiny shops at the castle's foot. It's become an attraction in itself.

⏱ 30 min

Lycian sarcophagi bathe in the turquoise water of Üçağız harbor, half submerged. The image is striking: stone tombs in clear water, with fish around them. Best light in the morning.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 20min

Kekova

★★★★★
1

The ancient city of Dolchiste slid underwater after a 2nd-century earthquake. From the boat you can clearly make out walls, stairs, and foundations beneath the surface. Swimming banned above the ruins (heavy fine), but swimming stops in neighboring bays.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

From the boat, spot the quay walls, cisterns, and steps descending into the water. Tersane bay (former Byzantine shipyard) allows swimming. Crystal water, ruins of a church on the beach.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 33 km · 40min

Demre

★★★☆☆
1

Santa Claus wasn't born at the North Pole but in Patara, and he was bishop here, in Myra (modern Demre). Nicholas of Myra, in the 4th century, tossed purses of gold down chimneys for poor girls. The legend was born in this town of tomato greenhouses.

⏱ 30 min
2

St. Nicholas Church, built in 520 under Justinian, is buried 5 m below today's ground level. The sarcophagus where the saint rested was looted in 1087 by merchants from Bari. Twelfth-century frescoes show his life and miracles. Entry: €15 (2024).

⏱ 60 min
3

Also visit Hadrian's granary at Andriake, Myra's ancient port (5 km), transformed into a museum of Lycian civilization. A 16 m Roman ship is displayed there. The site is calm and well presented.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The ancient port of Andriake exudes a quiet charm. Small beach, a few fish restaurants, and the ruins of a Roman synagogue discovered in 2009. You can spend a pleasant hour here.

⏱ 60 min
$$$★10Kent Villas
🚗Drive to next stop: 2 km · 10min

Myra

★★★★☆
1

Myra's rock-cut tombs riddle the cliff like a giant honeycomb, some still adorned with painted reliefs. The spectacle is striking when the morning's raking light hits the wall. It's the postcard image of Lycia.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Roman theater, Lycia's largest, could hold 11,000 to 13,000 spectators. The masks carved on the stage wall are still visible. The site is 2 km from Demre center, reachable in 5 minutes by car.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you visit in December, an Orthodox liturgy is celebrated on the 6th at St. Nicholas Church. The rest of the year, the combined visit of tombs + theater + church takes half a day.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min

Limyra

★★★☆☆
1

If Xanthos is famous, Limyra remains in the shadows. Yet this Lycian city has a monumental funerary monument (the Heroon of Perikles) and dozens of rock-cut tombs in the cliff. Little-visited site, free entry.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Allow 45 minutes to cover the main site. The rock-cut tombs are scattered across the cliff, accessible by trail. No kiosk, no permanent caretaker. Turunçova village is 10 minutes away.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Finike

★★★☆☆
1

Finike lives off its orange groves more than tourism. The small fishing harbor is authentic, prices low, the vibe local. It's a good base for Arykanda and Limyra without the bustle of Kaş.

⏱ 30 min
2

The little fishing harbor lines up its painted boats facing the mountains. A few fish restaurants on the quay, unpretentious but honest. Finike is the kind of town you stop in for no reason and end up staying.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stock up on fresh-pressed orange juice: Finike is Turkey's citrus capital. Roadside stalls sell oranges, lemons, and mandarins at bargain prices.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 40min

Arycanda

★★★★☆
1

Arykanda descends in five terraces down the mountainside facing a pine-filled valley. The setting is among Turkey's most beautiful for an ancient site. The stadium, baths, and bouleuterion are well preserved.

⏱ 90 min
2

The stadium and Roman baths occupy the upper terrace. The bath mosaics are protected by a canopy. The theater, lower down, offers a plunging view of the valley. Little-visited site, modest entry fee.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you only have time for one obscure ancient site on this coast, choose Arykanda. The beauty of the setting compensates for the lack of explanatory panels. Allow 1.5 hours for the full visit.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 45min

Elmali

★★★☆☆
1

Elmalı sits at 1,050 m elevation, far from the coast and beach tourism. Its Ottoman wooden houses, apple orchards, and fresh air make it a refreshing stop between days of scorching ruins.

⏱ 45 min
2

The name means 'orchard' in Turkish, and groves surround the town. The small local museum displays finds from Bronze Age tumuli discovered in the region. Quick but interesting visit.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stop at the local market to taste pekmez (grape molasses), walnuts, and regional apples. Elmalı is a mountain town where time slows down. Perfect for a lunch break.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 80 km · 1h30

Cirali

★★★★☆
1

Çıralı beach stretches without concrete, without lined-up sunbeds, beneath orange trees and bougainvillea. It's also a loggerhead turtle nesting site. The village has no main road: just paths between pensions and gardens.

⏱ 60 min
2

The flames of Yanartaş (the Chimaera) have burned for over 2,500 years on the mountainside. Methane escapes from fissures in the rock and ignites on contact with air. Ancient mariners used it as a natural lighthouse.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Go at night: the 3 km hike from Çıralı takes 45 minutes. The flames are more spectacular in darkness. Bring a headlamp and marshmallows—everyone roasts theirs on the flames.

⏱ 120 min

Çıralı remains a village without concrete, without apartment blocks, without beach music. Family-run pensions hide in citrus gardens. It's the perfect counterpoint after crowded archaeological sites.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 15min

Olympos

★★★☆☆
1

Turkish backpackers call Olympos 'the end of the world.' The Lycian city is buried under laurels and brambles, crossed by a river that empties into the sea. Roman temples, baths, Byzantine basilica: everything is half swallowed by nature.

⏱ 90 min
2

Follow the river under the oleanders to the beach. Ruins appear at random along the path: a bath wall here, a sarcophagus there. The city was founded by settlers from Rhodes, then occupied by Cilician pirates in the 1st century BC.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The Byzantine basilica by the river preserves fragments of floor mosaic. At the route's end, Olympos beach is wild and undeveloped. Paid entry from the parking lot.

Termessos

★★★★☆
1

In 333 BC, Alexander the Great tried to take Termessos. He failed. The city, perched at 1,050 m in the Güllük mountains, was too well defended by terrain. Alexander gave up before the landscape and left to attack Sagalassos.

⏱ 90 min
2

The theater, at 1,050 m elevation, overlooks the Antalya plain down to the sea on clear days. No other ancient theater in Turkey offers this view. The tiers are overgrown with pine forest—you have to push your way through.

⏱ 45 min
3

The necropolis of toppled sarcophagi looks like a graveyard after an earthquake—which it probably is. Lids were torn off by tomb raiders over the centuries. Some bear carved shields, hints of the Pisidians' warlike reputation.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Plan two hours walking from the parking lot to the ruins and back. Steep trail, unshaded at first, then under pines. No shop, no kiosk. Bring at least a liter of water per person.

Antalya

★★★★☆
1

Pass through Hadrian's Gate, a three-arched triumphal arch built in AD 130 for the emperor's visit. The Corinthian capitals and carved floral motifs are remarkably well preserved. It's the natural entrance to Kaleiçi, the old quarter.

⏱ 45 min
2

The fluted Yivli Minaret, 38 m high, is Antalya's symbol. Built by the Seljuks in the 13th century on the foundations of a Byzantine church, it blends red bricks and blue tiles. You can't climb it, but you see it from everywhere.

⏱ 20 min
3

Antalya Archaeological Museum deserves half a day. Hall of sarcophagi from Termessos, statues from Perge, Roman mosaics. It's one of Turkey's major museums, accessible by tram from Kaleiçi.

⏱ 120 min
4

The old Roman harbor, set between cliffs and ramparts, has become a yacht marina lined with cafés. In the evening, boat lights and illuminated cliffs create a postcard scene. Mermerli beach is accessible by stairs from a restaurant (paid entry).

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If the heat overwhelms you, descend to Mermerli beach via the wooden staircase from the restaurant of the same name. The water is limpid, the view of the harbor and cliffs impressive. It's the only beach in the historic center.

⏱ 90 min

Kaleiçi is touristy but back lanes keep their Ottoman charm. Overhanging houses, carved wooden doors, cats on walls. Get deliberately lost: the quarter is small, you'll always find the harbor again.

⏱ 60 min

Take the historic tram along Atatürk Boulevard to the archaeological museum. The ride follows Karaalioğlu Park and Hıdırlık Tower (2nd-century Roman, panoramic view). It's a pleasant urban stroll.
