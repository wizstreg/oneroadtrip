Seoul

★★★★★
1

Start at Gyeongbokgung right when it opens—100 acres sprawling across central Seoul, the largest of the five royal palaces, built in 1395. The Japanese demolished 80% of the complex between 1910 and 1945. Restoration continues to this day. Arrive for the changing of the guard at 11 AM. Wear hanbok for free admission.

⏱ 120 min
2

Head north to Bukchon—900 traditional houses from the 1920s, built as quiet resistance during Japanese occupation. The alleys of Gahoe-dong offer the classic view of curved tile roofs. Access restricted 10 AM–5 PM since November 2024—keep your voice down, people actually live here.

⏱ 75 min
3

Finish at Changdeokgung, the UNESCO-listed palace—the best-preserved of the five, and the seat of power for 250 years after Gyeongbokgung's destruction. Its Secret Garden to the north—80 acres of pavilions and forest—is accessible only by guided tour with advance reservation.

⏱ 90 min
4

Head to the Starfield Library at COEX Mall in Gangnam—a 43-foot-high hall completely lined with books, one of Seoul's most photographed spots. Right next door, Bongeunsa Temple (794 CE) forms a striking contrast with the glass towers surrounding it.

⏱ 60 min
5

The National Palace Museum, just inside Gyeongbokgung's entrance—collections of royal furniture, court attire, and everyday objects from Joseon kings. Free admission, less crowded than the palace itself.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rent hanbok around Anguk Station—free entry to all Seoul palaces and better photos in Bukchon's alleys.

⏱ 60 min

Lunch in the Bukchon alleys—the sundubu jjigae restaurants around Anguk are among Seoul's best.

⏱ 60 min

Explore Seochon west of Gyeongbokgung—painters' workshops, galleries in hanok buildings, hidden tea houses. More authentic than Insadong.

⏱ 90 min

Evening stroll along Cheonggyecheon—the urban stream reopened in 2005 over 3.6 miles, lit up and lively. A typical Seoul resident's walk.

⏱ 60 min

Seoul

★★★★★
1

Start at Jongmyo Shrine, the quietest thing in Seoul. Built in 1395, it houses the ancestral tablets of 19 Joseon kings and 30 queens. The main hall stretches 330 feet—the longest wooden structure built in a straight line in Korea. Every first Sunday in May, the royal Jongmyo Jerye ritual plays out here with costumes, music, and dance, inscribed on UNESCO's list.

⏱ 60 min
2

Head to Gwangjang Market for lunch—open since 1905, Seoul's oldest covered market still in operation. Order yukhoe (beef tartare), bindaetteok (fried mung bean pancake), or spicy mini rice rolls. Grandmothers in aprons have been at the stoves since dawn.

⏱ 75 min
3

In the evening, climb N Seoul Tower on Namsan Mountain—1,572 feet total elevation, 360° view over illuminated Seoul. Take the cable car or walk up the trail (30 minutes). Best nighttime view in the city.

⏱ 60 min
4

Dongdaemun Design Plaza by Zaha Hadid—926,000 square feet with not a single right angle, open 24/7. During construction, 1,600 artifacts from a 14th-century fortress were unearthed. Heunginjimun Gate, remnant of the old ramparts, stands right beside it.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the afternoon, wander through Insadong—antique dealers, calligraphy galleries, tea houses, craft shops. The street is closed to cars on Sundays.

⏱ 90 min

Evening in Jongno-3ga—its pojangmacha (heated street tents) welcome night owls over soju and skewers. The vibe after 9 PM is quintessentially Seoul.

⏱ 90 min

Night shopping at Dongdaemun—fashion markets open at 10 PM and close at dawn.

⏱ 120 min

Night out in Hongdae—bars, clubs, food trucks, street musicians. The liveliest student district in Seoul after midnight.

⏱ 120 min

Seoul

★★★★★
1

The Third Infiltration Tunnel, discovered in 1978 at 240 feet below the border. One mile long, capable of moving 30,000 soldiers per hour. The walls were painted black to make it look like a coal mine—there's no coal in this region.

⏱ 60 min
2

Dora Observatory, the farthest observation point toward the North. On clear days, you can spot Kijong-dong, the North Korean ghost town with automatically lit windows. Binoculars provided on-site.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Bridge of Freedom, through which 12,773 prisoners crossed south in 1953. The barbed wire is covered with colorful ribbons tied by separated families. A bullet-riddled locomotive sits on display beside it.

⏱ 45 min
4

Dorasan Station, built in 2002 in hopes of reunification. It has never hosted regular trains to the North. The departure board still displays 'Pyongyang.' The waiting room stands empty.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Organized tour from Seoul—passport required, minimum 48-hour advance reservation. Total duration about 7 hours. JSA/Panmunjom tours suspended since 2023.

⏱ 60 min

Visit the War Memorial of Korea on the way back—5 halls on the Korean War, tanks and aircraft displayed outside. Free admission.

⏱ 90 min

Dinner in Itaewon on the return—restaurants from around the world, lively bars.

⏱ 90 min

Evening stroll on Banpo Bridge—9,500 nozzles projecting illuminated colored water. Show every hour in the evening.

⏱ 60 min

Seoul

★★★★★
1

Ascend to Seoul Sky observatory at Lotte World Tower's 123rd floor—1,821 feet, the world's 5th tallest tower. 360° view over Seoul, the Han River, and mountains. The glass floor at 1,640 feet is for thrill-seekers. Built in 2017 after 18 years of construction on swampy ground beside Seokchon Lake.

⏱ 75 min
2

Finish at the National Museum of Korea, Asia's largest at 3.5 million square feet. Free admission, 12,000 years of Korean history. Don't miss the 7th-century Baekje bronze incense burner—one of Korean art's finest pieces.

⏱ 90 min
3

Bongeunsa Temple (794 CE) in Gangnam—37,000 square feet of Buddhist gardens at the foot of glass towers. The contrast between century-old pines and surrounding glass towers is one of modern Seoul's most striking images.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Insadong district—antique dealers, calligraphy galleries, tea houses, Korean craft shops. Closed to cars on Sundays.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Korean barbecue dinner in Mapo—samgyeopsal or galbi grilled over charcoal at the table, lettuce leaves, side dishes.

⏱ 90 min

Korean cooking class in Insadong or Hapjeong—kimchi, bibimbap, or tteokbokki. About 3 hours, reservation required the day before.

⏱ 180 min

Namdaemun Market for shopping—open since 1414, ginseng, artisan noodles, clothing.

⏱ 60 min

Evening in Sinchon—student bars, pojangmacha, and grilled fish restaurants. More local and cheaper than Hongdae, two subway stops away.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 200 km · 1h20

Jeonju

★★★☆☆
1

Arrive in Jeonju by train. The hanok village contains 800 traditional houses—Jeonju was the spiritual capital of the Joseon Dynasty, birthplace of founding king Taejo. Gyeonggijeon (1410) houses his official portrait. Jeondong Cathedral (1914) stands on the site of the first Korean Catholic executions in the 19th century.

⏱ 120 min
2

Omokdae Pavilion on the northern hill—Yi Seong-gye celebrated his 1380 victory here, 12 years before founding the Joseon Dynasty. Panoramic view over the hanok village below.

⏱ 45 min
3

Jeondong Cathedral (1914), in Romano-Byzantine style, built on the site of the first Korean Catholic executions. Same architect as Seoul's Myeongdong Cathedral.

⏱ 30 min
4

The Jeonju National Museum—collections of Baekje Buddhist art and Joseon artifacts. Free admission. Good complement to the village visit for understanding the region's history.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Bibimbap lunch in the village—Jeonju is designated a UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy. The rice is cooked in beef broth, not water. Try yukhoe bibimbap with beef tartare—the local specialty.

⏱ 60 min

Rent hanbok in the village for a stroll through cobbled alleys.

⏱ 90 min

Makgeolli tasting in a traditional bar in the hanok village—free side dishes (anju) with each order.

⏱ 90 min

Evening walk through the hanok village's paved alleys—the soft lantern lighting gives the district a different face. Fewer crowds after 8 PM.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 200 km · 2h10

Gyeongju

★★★★☆
1

Welcome to Gyeongju—former capital of the Silla Kingdom for a thousand years, entirely inscribed as a UNESCO 'museum without walls.' The Daereungwon site contains 23 royal tumuli right in the city center. Cheonmachong Tomb, excavated in 1973, yielded a gold crown, 11,500 objects, and the only known Silla painting—a winged white horse.

⏱ 90 min
2

Cheomseongdae Observatory (circa 632–647), East Asia's oldest astronomical observatory, assembled from 362 granite stones. Then Anapji Pond at sunset—illuminated at night, it reflects in the water. In 1975, drainage work brought up 33,000 artifacts from the bottom: royal tableware, game pieces, model boats.

⏱ 75 min
3

Woljeonggyo Bridge, reconstructed in 2018 as an identical copy of the 8th-century Silla royal bridge. Illuminated at night, it spans the Namcheon River. One of Gyeongju's most photographed nighttime views.

⏱ 30 min
4

Yangdong Village, UNESCO-listed in 2010—one of Korea's best-preserved hanok villages, founded in the 15th century by two aristocratic clans. About 60 houses still inhabited, 30 minutes from Gyeongju.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Rent a bike near the station to explore the historic center—tumuli, pagodas, and steles scattered throughout the city like a park. The terrain is flat, the loop about 9 miles.

⏱ 120 min

Sample ssambap (rice wrapped in vegetable leaves) at a traditional Gyeongju restaurant—cuisine passed down since the Silla era.

⏱ 60 min

Try hwangnam ppang—small pastry filled with red bean paste, the city's specialty since the 1920s.

⏱ 30 min

Evening walk around illuminated Anapji Pond—the reflections of pavilions in the water are Gyeongju's most photographed view.

⏱ 60 min

Gyeongju

★★★★☆
1

Take the road to Bulguksa on Mount Tohamsan—built in 528, rebuilt in 751, UNESCO-listed. The temple houses six National Treasures including the Dabotap and Seokgatap pagodas facing each other in the central courtyard for 1,250 years. According to legend, Prime Minister Kim Daeseong built it to appease his parents' souls.

⏱ 90 min
2

Then climb to Seokguram, 2.5 miles away at 2,460 feet elevation. Artificial grotto of mortarless granite, 11-foot Buddha gazing toward the East Sea. Rediscovered by chance in 1909 by a man seeking shelter from rain, after more than a thousand years of obscurity.

⏱ 90 min
3

Mount Namsan of Gyeongju—Silla sacred mountain covered with rock-carved Buddhas, pagodas, and temple ruins. Over 150 Buddhist sites on its slopes. The Sambulsa → Yongjangsaji hike (2.5 miles) passes the three most famous Buddhas.

⏱ 120 min
4

Gyeongju National Museum—the most precious objects unearthed from Silla royal tombs: gold crowns, gold belts, celadon. One of Asia's richest archaeological museums. Free admission.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Walk between Bulguksa and Seokguram via the forest trail—2.5 miles through pine forest, 1,640-foot elevation gain. The descent can be done by bus.

⏱ 90 min

Visit Gyeongju's traditional market (Jungang Market)—local vegetables, Korean snacks, artisan tofu. Open daily, liveliest in the morning.

⏱ 60 min

Forest bathing on Mount Tohamsan slopes after descending from Seokguram—the century-old pines along the trail are designated a therapeutic resource by the Korean government. Free, accessible from Bulguksa parking lot.

⏱ 60 min

Evening stroll through downtown Gyeongju—the illuminated tumuli and lit Woljeonggyo Bridge create a unique atmosphere after nightfall.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 75 km · 1h

Busan

★★★★☆
1

First stop in Busan: Gamcheon Village, dubbed 'the Machu Picchu of Busan.' This wasn't an artists' village—it was a 1950s hillside slum built by war refugees. The colorful houses resulted from a 2009 renovation project. The labyrinthine alleys retain their residential character—families still live here.

⏱ 90 min
2

Then head down to Jagalchi Market, Korea's largest seafood market. The ground floor sells by auction, upper floors cook on the spot what you just bought downstairs.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Choryang Ihwa district, Busan's oldest foreign quarter, founded after the port opened in 1876. Colonial-era Japanese houses stand alongside traditional Korean residences. A painted trail connects the hillside alleys with murals—quieter and less touristy than Gamcheon.

⏱ 60 min
4

Yeongdo Bridge and Yeongdo district—the large port island south of downtown Busan, connected by two bridges. Korea's major shipyards are based here. Yeongdo Drawbridge, built in 1934, is Korea's only historic operational drawbridge.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Dinner in Nampo-dong—octopus pancakes, fresh oysters at Gukje Market, pig's feet soup. Busan is Korea's unofficial food capital.

⏱ 90 min

Sunset at Gwangalli Beach with view of illuminated Gwangan Bridge—the favorite spot of Busan locals.

⏱ 60 min

Stroll through BIFF Square—the plaza where Korean film stars' handprints are embedded in the ground.

⏱ 45 min

Karaoke in Seomyeon after dinner—norebang (private room by the hour) is a Korean institution, open until dawn.

⏱ 90 min

Busan

★★★★☆
1

Start at Haedong Yonggungsa Temple, founded in 1376—one of Korea's few temples built right on the ocean. The 108 steps descend toward the sea past stone lanterns. The seven-story pagoda guards the entrance facing the waves. 108 is the number of human desires in Buddhism.

⏱ 75 min
2

Head to Haeundae Beach—1.1 miles of sand, Korea's most famous beach. In summer, 1.5 million people crowd it in August. Dongbaek Island at the eastern end is connected by a causeway and offers panoramic views over the entire bay.

⏱ 60 min
3

Taejongdae Park on Busan's southern tip—65-foot cliffs, pine forest, 19th-century lighthouse. On clear days, view of Tsushima, the Japanese island 30 miles away. A small scenic train (Danubi) circles the park for those who don't want to walk.

⏱ 120 min
4

Dongbaek Island at Haeundae's eastern end, connected by a causeway—panoramic view over the entire bay and skyscrapers in the background. The trail circles the island in 30 minutes.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk along Haeundae Beach boardwalk—1.1 miles of promenade lined with cafés and seafood restaurants. Lively even off-season.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner of haejangguk (Busan beef soup) at a restaurant in Jagalchi Market—the quintessential local dish, served from dawn for fishermen and night owls.

⏱ 60 min

Evening walk on Dongbaek Island causeway—view of Haeundae's lights from the sea, calm and uncrowded after 9 PM.

⏱ 45 min

Haeundae night market—grilled seafood, tteokbokki, pajeon. Lively in the evening, right on the beach.

⏱ 90 min

Busan

★★★★☆
1

Head north of Busan to Beomeosa Temple, founded in 678, on the slopes of Mount Geumjeong. Less touristy than Haedong, more authentic. Its name means 'temple of heaven's sacred carp.' The four-pillar main gate, dating from 1614, is a National Treasure.

⏱ 75 min
2

Continue to Geumjeongsan Fortress on the ridge at 2,625 feet—10.5 miles of ramparts, four gates, 360° view over Busan and the sea. Ascent by cable car from Beomeosa, descent on foot along the ramparts.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Busan Museum in Nam-gu district—collections tracing Busan's history from prehistory to the Korean War. Free admission, rarely visited by foreign tourists.

⏱ 60 min
4

Busan's North Port area in late afternoon—former warehouses converted into galleries and restaurants overlooking the bay. The port's rehabilitation draws inspiration from Hamburg and Rotterdam models.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Sunset at Gwangalli Beach with view of Gwangan Bridge—4.6 miles of illuminated suspension bridge at night. The favorite spot of Busan locals.

⏱ 60 min

Hike the Geumjeongsan ramparts from the north gate—1.2 miles with alternating views over the city and wooded hills.

⏱ 120 min

Final evening in Busan in Seomyeon—shopping centers, restaurants, bars. The city's liveliest district outside Haeundae.

⏱ 120 min

Last drink at a makgeolli bar in Busan—the local rice alcohol served cold with pajeon. Seomyeon's bars serve until dawn.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 40min

Jeju

★★★★☆
1

Start by climbing Seongsan Ilchulbong first thing in the morning—this volcanic cone burst from the sea 5,000 years ago, and it shows. Twenty minutes of ascent brings you to a bowl-shaped crater open to the sky, ringed by vertical cliffs plunging into the sea. UNESCO-listed.

⏱ 90 min
2

Continue to Manjanggul Cave, a lava tube carved 100,000 to 300,000 years ago. One kilometer of gallery with frozen lava ceilings and, at the back, a 25-foot stalagmite—the world's tallest in a lava tube. It's a constant 52°F, bring a jacket.

⏱ 75 min
3

Jeju Stone Park—open-air museum tracing the island's geological and cultural formation through basalt sculptures and gardens. The dol hareubang (big stone grandfathers) are Jeju's symbol, village protectors since the 18th century.

⏱ 60 min
4

Seongsan village—small fishing port at the foot of Ilchulbong crater, still inhabited by traditional fishing families. The morning market starts before dawn, with haenyeo returning from their dives.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

In late afternoon, set out on Olle Trail #1—a 9-mile coastal path along the sea with Seongsan Ilchulbong as backdrop. Walk as far as you like, a partial round-trip from Sinyangri works perfectly.

⏱ 180 min

Sample black pork (흑돼지) at dinner—Jeju black pork is the island's main specialty, grilled over charcoal in restaurants in Jeju City's Heuk-dwaeji district.

⏱ 90 min

Visit Seongsan village at sunset—the alleys of the small fishing port at the foot of Ilchulbong crater are quiet after 6 PM, when tour buses have left.

⏱ 60 min

Watch haenyeo at Seongsan port early morning—the female divers return with their catch before 8 AM.

⏱ 60 min

Jeju

★★★★☆
1

Hallasan, 6,398 feet—the roof of South Korea. Shield volcano with 360 parasitic cones all around, the summit crater holds a lake that disappears under snow much of the year. The ascent takes 3 to 5 hours depending on the trail chosen. Advance online reservation required before departure.

⏱ 300 min
2

Jungmun Saekdal Beach, the south coast's most beautiful beach—black volcanic sand mixed with white sand, powerful waves favored by surfers. The Teddy Bear Museum and Jeju Loveland are nearby if you want a lighthearted break.

⏱ 45 min
3

Jeju Haenyeo Research Center in Hado—exhibition on the history and diving techniques of the female divers, UNESCO-listed in 2016. Demonstrations available depending on schedule.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the evening, check out Dongmun Market in Jeju City—fresh tangerines, sweet potato pancakes, black carrot juice. The island's liveliest market in the evening.

⏱ 90 min

Hallasan hike—ascend via Eorimok trail (3 miles, 2,300-foot elevation gain) to Witseoreum plateau, or Seongpanak trail (5.6 miles) to the summit. Online reservation required.

⏱ 300 min

Stroll through Jeju City's Chilseong-ro district—lively shopping street with local boutiques, cafés, and black pork restaurants.

⏱ 60 min

Sample tangerine makgeolli at a bar in Jeju City—rice alcohol with Jeju tangerines, a specialty unavailable elsewhere. Downtown bars serve it with sweet potato pancakes.

⏱ 60 min

Jeju

★★★★☆
1

Head down to the south coast to Jusangjeolli—65- to 82-foot hexagonal basalt columns rising from the sea like a giant pipe organ. It's the result of rapid cooling of lava in seawater. A half-mile trail runs along the cliffs, the spectacle is better at low tide.

⏱ 45 min
2

Then climb to Sanbangsan, a 1,296-foot lava dome sitting alone on the west coast. Midway up, a cave houses a Buddha statue and water constantly seeps from the wall—legend says these are the tears of the mountain deity.

⏱ 60 min
3

Finish the day at Cheonjeyeon Falls—72-foot cascade at the bottom of a black basalt canyon. To reach it, cross Seonim Bridge adorned with seven stone nymphs, which spans the canyon just above the third waterfall.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Early morning, go see the haenyeo at Sehwa or Hado—these women dive without tanks in apnea to 30–65 feet to gather sea urchins, abalone, and octopus. Their practice has been UNESCO-listed since 2016. Arrive before 10 AM, that's when they're in action.

⏱ 90 min

Stroll on Jungmun Beach or in Seogwipo—Jeju's second city, calmer and greener than Jeju City, with its seaside gardens.

⏱ 90 min

Dinner of galbi-tang (beef rib soup) in Seogwipo—specialty of southern Jeju, simmered for hours. Restaurants around Seogwipo Market are the best.

⏱ 60 min

Evening walk in Seogwipo Park along the cliffs—Jeongbang Falls (75 feet directly into the sea) are illuminated in the evening. One of the few waterfalls in the world that falls directly into the ocean.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 1h

Seoul

★★★★★
1

Return to Seoul. Start with Sungnyemun Gate, built in 1398 and burned down in 2008—reconstruction took five years. Right next door, Namdaemun Market has been open since 1414, Asia's oldest covered market still in operation. Dive in for breakfast.

⏱ 60 min
2

The War Memorial of Korea if not yet visited—5 halls on the Korean War and military history, tanks and aircraft outside. Free admission.

⏱ 90 min
3

Banpo Bridge and its rainbow fountains on the Han—9,500 nozzles projecting illuminated colored water from the railings. Show every hour in the evening. The riverbanks around are lively until late.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Final shopping in Myeongdong: K-beauty cosmetics, street food in the alleys (hotteok, tteokbokki), Korean fashion boutiques. The district is pedestrian-only in the evening—that's when the atmosphere is best.

⏱ 120 min

Evening in Itaewon—restaurants from around the world, lively bars, independent designer shops. Final lively night before departure.

⏱ 90 min

Last Korean barbecue in Mapo or Hongdae before departure—samgyeopsal over charcoal, relaxed neighborhood atmosphere.

⏱ 90 min

Late ascent to N Seoul Tower on Namsan—360° night view over illuminated Seoul. Cable car until 11 PM.

⏱ 90 min

Seoul

★★★★★
1

Free day before your flight. If you haven't yet, check out Dongdaemun Design Plaza by Zaha Hadid—a steel and concrete structure without a single right angle, open 24/7. Otherwise, revisit a neighborhood you enjoyed, no agenda.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Seochon district west of Gyeongbokgung—painters' workshops, galleries in hanok buildings, hidden tea houses. More authentic than Insadong. Poet Yi Sang and painter Goryeo Ikki lived here.

⏱ 75 min
3

Seoul's ramparts around Mount Naksan—restored sections of the 1396 wall, view over old neighborhoods from the walkway. Allow 90 minutes for the walk.

⏱ 90 min
4

Deoksugung Palace in late morning—the last palace built in the Joseon era, in the heart of the modern business district. Changing of the guard three times daily in front of Daehanmun Gate. The street along its walls is called 'breakup street.'

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Settle at Han River Park for a final picnic by the water. Order ramyeon from a Korean app—delivery straight to the riverbank is a local tradition.

⏱ 120 min

Last-minute Korean cooking class if not done yet—kimchi, tteokbokki, or pajeon. Reservation possible the day before at several schools.

⏱ 180 min

Visit Gyeongbokgung early morning if not yet done—before 9 AM, almost no crowds. Free admission in hanbok.

⏱ 90 min

Last coffee in a hanok café in Bukchon or Seochon before heading to the airport.

⏱ 60 min
