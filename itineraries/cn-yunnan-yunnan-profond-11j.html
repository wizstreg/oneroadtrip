Kunming

★★★★☆
1

The Shilin Stone Forest rises 90 km south of Kunming: grey-black limestone pillars reaching 30 meters, sculpted by erosion over 270 million years. UNESCO listed it in 2007 as part of the South China Karst. Allow 3 hours for the Greater and Lesser Shilin. Leave early - tour buses arrive after 10 AM.

⏱ 180 min
2

Yuantong Temple is Kunming's oldest Buddhist temple, founded under the Tang dynasty in the 8th century. Its central pond surrounded by pavilions is peaceful on weekdays. Since 1956, a Tibetan hall and a Theravada hall have coexisted here - a rare syncretism in China.

⏱ 60 min
3

Dianchi Lake stretches across 330 km² south of Kunming. In winter (November-March), thousands of Siberian gulls overwinter here. The lakefront is accessible by bus or bicycle from downtown.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Wander through Wenlin Jie, Kunming's student quarter: cafés run by Yi minorities, secondhand bookstores, restaurants serving Yunnan stir-fried rice with vegetables. Perfect for a first taste of local cuisine before heading out on the road.

⏱ 90 min

Try clay pot rice with chicken (汽锅鸡, qiguoji) at the covered market in Jinma Biji Fang in the old town. This steam-cooked dish is a Kunming specialty - the chicken sits on a central clay cone that channels the steam. Count on 30 to 50 RMB.

⏱ 60 min

Jianshui

★★★☆☆
1

Jianshui's Confucian Temple was founded in 1285 under the Yuan dynasty. With 114 buildings spread over 7.5 hectares, it's considered China's third-largest after those in Qufu and Beijing. Unlike those two, it still functions as a school - you'll encounter high school students in the same courtyards as tourists.

⏱ 90 min
2

Chaoyang Gate, built in 1389 under the Ming dynasty, looks exactly like the Tiananmen tower in Beijing - it was constructed four years before it. From the top, you have a direct view down the old town's main axis. Free entry.

⏱ 40 min
3

The Shuanglong 17-arch bridge spans Lake Yilong over 148 meters. Built in 1839, it remains Yunnan's longest multi-arch bridge. Late in the day, the raking light on the lake's reflections is particularly beautiful.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Jianshui purple pottery is made from naturally brown-violet local clay, unglazed, dating back to the Song dynasty. Visit an active workshop in the Xichuang quarter to see throwing and sand-polishing - some offer a 2-hour introduction for 80 to 120 RMB.

⏱ 120 min

Don't leave without eating Jianshui grilled tofu - sold on skewers throughout the old town, topped with green chili and salt. It's Yunnan's most famous street specialty. The best stands open from 5 PM near Chaoyang Gate.

⏱ 45 min

Jianshui

★★★☆☆
1

Tuanshan lies 13 km west of Jianshui: a village whose Ming-Qing residences have remained intact since the 17th century. The courtyard houses (siheyuan) are in excellent condition - some have been converted to guesthouses. The guided tour included with entry lasts 45 minutes.

⏱ 120 min
2

The small Xianglu stone bridge at Tuanshan's entrance dates from the same Ming period. The water is clear and village women still come to wash laundry in the morning - a daily scene unchanged for generations.

⏱ 20 min
3

On the way back to Jianshui in the afternoon, stop at the pottery kilns in the northern quarter. These active workshops have produced unglazed purple ceramics for centuries - wood-firing in direct-flame kilns gives pieces their characteristic metallic luster.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Have lunch in a Tuanshan house-restaurant: residents serve Bai and Han dishes cooked over wood fires in their own kitchens. No menu - they bring you what's ready. Count on 50 to 80 RMB per person.

⏱ 60 min

Evening stroll through old Jianshui: Linchuan Lu pedestrian street comes alive after 7 PM with skewer stands, mahjong players, and sesame candy vendors. Less touristy than the main street.

⏱ 75 min

Yuanyang

★★★★☆
1

Yuanyang's rice terraces cover 16,000 hectares on the flanks of Mount Ailao, shaped by the Hani people over more than 1,300 years. UNESCO listed them in 2013. The Duoyishu viewpoint, at 1,940 meters altitude, is best for sunrise: flooded terraces (November-March) transform into mirrors catching the first rays. Wake by 5:30 AM minimum.

⏱ 120 min
2

In the afternoon, descend into an authentic Hani village - Pugao Laozhai or Azheke are the least touristy. The mushroom houses (conical thatched roofs) are still inhabited. Don't photograph without asking.

⏱ 90 min
3

If you're there on a market day (12-day calendar, not weekly), Xinjie market gathers Hani, Yi, and Miao vendors from dawn. Embroidered textiles, medicinal plants, and live pigs mingle in a mountain bazaar atmosphere.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike between the terraces from Duoyishu downslope: trails follow Hani irrigation canals (a 4,653 km network also listed). Count on 2 to 3 hours to descend to Qingkou village and climb back up by tuk-tuk.

⏱ 180 min

At sunset, head to Bada viewpoint, 15 km south of Xinjie: west-facing terraces glow red in the last sun. The site is less crowded than Duoyishu and offers a different angle on the terraces' curves.

⏱ 90 min

Yuanyang

★★★★☆
1

Laohuzui - Tiger's Mouth - is Yuanyang's third major viewpoint, 17 km west of Xinjie. Here terraces cascade fan-like from 1,800 meters down to the Red River valley. It's the most impressive panorama for its verticality. Best time: late afternoon.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Hani sacred forest above the villages has been protected for centuries - the Hani believe it shelters ancestral spirits. Access is free but demands respect: no noise, no litter. The dense canopy creates a particular atmosphere.

⏱ 60 min
3

Mengpin terraces, 8 km from Xinjie, are less visited and offer the same landscapes from a northern angle. Perfect for photography without crowds. The access road is unpaved - car or moto-taxi.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Join a Hani street banquet (长街宴, changjieyuan) if you're there during Kuzhaza (June or July by lunar calendar): hundreds of tables join into a single line in the main lane. Off-season, some hotels offer a reduced version for groups.

⏱ 120 min

Before leaving, buy Hani red rice at Xinjie market - a local variety cultivated in flooded terraces, unavailable outside the region. It keeps for 6 months.

⏱ 45 min

Jinghong

★★★☆☆
1

Jinghong's Tropical Botanical Garden covers 1,125 hectares and houses over 13,000 plant species. Founded in 1959, it's China's largest botanical garden. The giant fern greenhouse and the dipterocarp forest alone are worth 2 hours.

⏱ 180 min
2

Wat Manfeilong (八角亭), 65 km from Jinghong, dates from the 13th century and remains one of Yunnan's best-preserved Theravada Buddhist sites. Nine white stupas surround a central stupa - the style is clearly Thai. Shoulders and knees covered required.

⏱ 60 min
3

Jinghong's Mekong waterfront comes alive in the evening: strollers, Dai pétanque players, and grill vendors on the bank. Here you realize you're a few hundred kilometers from Laos - the Mekong flows directly toward Luang Prabang.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Explore Zhuque Street night market where Dai vendors sell fried insects, palm larvae, and smoked duck with local spices. Even if you don't eat the insects, the atmosphere is that of a Southeast Asian border market.

⏱ 90 min

Rent an electric bike (50 RMB/day) to ride along the Mekong to Manting Dai village, 3 km from downtown: coconut palms, golden temples, saffron-robed monks. The path follows the riverbank and is completely flat.

⏱ 120 min

Menghan

★★☆☆☆
1

Menghan (Ganlanba, 'olive plain' in Dai) is a Dai village on a Mekong meander 50 km from Jinghong. The Wat Luang in Ban Suan Men village is decorated with murals depicting the Ramayana in Dai version. The welcome is simple and warm.

⏱ 90 min
2

Ganlanba Tropical Rainforest Park, along the Mekong, is a 400-hectare secondary forest: wild banana trees, wine palms, teak. Signs are in Chinese and Dai - plan for a guide.

⏱ 120 min
3

If you visit in April during Dai Songkran (water festival, April 13-15), Menghan is one of the best places to experience it: the festival lasts 3 days and pirogues parade on the Mekong. Off-season, Dai dance shows are organized for groups.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Cross the Mekong by motorized boat to reach villages on the opposite bank, inaccessible by car - count on 20 RMB round-trip. Residents still cultivate sticky rice and tobacco under conditions close to those of 50 years ago.

⏱ 90 min

Lunch in a Dai stilt house: sticky rice cooked in banana leaf, pork larb, bamboo shoot soup. Xishuangbanna Dai cuisine is one of the closest to northern Thai cooking - sour, herbal, not sweet.

⏱ 90 min

Menghai

★★☆☆☆
1

The Jingmai Mountains harbor the world's largest ancient tea tree forest: 2,800 hectares of tea trees planted over a thousand years ago by the Bulang and Dai peoples. UNESCO inscribed this landscape in 2023. These tea trees grow in the shade of other trees - the agroforestry protects the soil and complexifies the tea's aromas.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Bulang village of Manpo is partially listed to preserve its bamboo and teak architecture. Bulang women still wear traditional black embroidered clothing during festivals. Outside harvest season (March-May), access is quiet.

⏱ 90 min
3

Menghai weekly market gathers Dai, Hani, Lahu, and Akha vendors every Sunday: pu'er tea cakes, rare spices, and medicinal herbs unavailable in Kunming. Arrive before 8 AM.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Join a pu'er tasting at a Jingmai producer: the comparison between a young sheng and a 10-year sheng is spectacular. Some also offer manual picking in March-May. Count on 150 to 300 RMB for a serious session.

⏱ 120 min

Hike through the ancient tea forest at dusk: light filters between 8 to 10-meter trees. Local guides know the reputed tea trees over 800 years old - some bear tracking numbers.

⏱ 120 min

Mengla

★★☆☆☆
1

Wangtianshu Park is the only place in China where Parashorea chinensis grows - a tree whose canopy reaches 70 meters. A 2.5 km suspended walkway runs 30 meters high between the crowns. In the morning, you're in the clouds.

⏱ 180 min
2

Nabanhe Nature Reserve protects a tropical forest corridor between Yunnan and Laos. White-cheeked gibbons are observed at daybreak, Asian elephants, and over 500 bird species. 4×4 access only - local guides offer outings departing from Mengla.

⏱ 240 min
3

The Mohan border crossing, 25 km from Mengla, is the northern terminus of the China-Laos train inaugurated in December 2021. If continuing to Luang Prabang, a Lao visa on arrival is available at the post.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Dawn bird outing in Nabanhe Reserve: the great hornbill (Buceros bicornis), Asia's largest hornbill with its yellow and red casque, is present in the area. Rent binoculars in Mengla (30 RMB/day) and go with a local ornithologist guide.

⏱ 180 min

Dinner in Mengla at an Akha restaurant: smoked dried pork, wild vegetables gathered from the forest, and artisanal rice wine. Akha cuisine is less known than Dai but worth the detour - look for restaurants frequented by locals.

⏱ 90 min

Jinghong

★★★☆☆
1

Return to Jinghong from Mengla (3-hour drive). Wat Pajie (勐泐大佛寺), rebuilt in 1997 on the foundations of a 14th-century Ming temple, dominates the city from a hill. The walk up takes 20 minutes and offers a view over the Mekong plain.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Dai Ethnicity Museum in the ethnocultural park traces the history of the Möng Mao kingdom (9th-17th century) and presents ancient Dai scripts, similar to Khmer and Thai. Entry 50 RMB.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Dai Thai massage in Jinghong before the departure flight: several salons on Menglong Lu street offer massages with local herbs (lemongrass, wild ginger) for 100 to 150 RMB per hour.
