Wallonia's Most Beautiful Villages: The Complete Circuit

📅 5 days📍 4 stops🚗 248 km⏱ 4h09

Sohier → Gros Fays → Our → Celles

Mirwart and its castle above the Lhomme, Sohier with its flowered facades between Famenne and Ardennes, Gros-Fays perched on its schist promontory overlooking the Semois, Our with its half-moon bridges, Celles and its 11th-century Romanesque collegiate classified as Exceptional Heritage. Four days to cross labeled rural Wallonia in all its diversity.

✨ Highlights
  • Saint-Hadelin Collegiate of Celles, masterpiece of 11th-century Mosan Romanesque art
  • Mirwart Provincial Domain: 1,350 hectares of Ardennes forest, ponds, and 19th-century ice house
  • Gros-Fays: panoramas over the Semois valley from a schist promontory
  • Our: half-moon sandstone bridges over a crystal-clear Ardennes river
  • Vêves Castle 2.5 km from Celles: medieval fortress on rocky spur, Exceptional Heritage of Wallonia
📆 Best period

April, May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 1 and 5 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Mild temperatures, blooming flowers, perfect for city walking and cycling, moderate crowds, pleasant weather.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather, outdoor festivals, peak tourist season, higher prices, ideal for outdoor dining and beer gardens.

Fall (September-October): Comfortable temperatures, autumn colors, fewer crowds, excellent for museums and cultural activities.

Winter (November-March): Christmas markets, cozy atmosphere, indoor attractions, shorter days, lowest prices except holiday period.

🌍 What to discover in BE?

Medieval cities, world-famous chocolates, and exceptional beer culture in compact, easily explored country. Gothic architecture in Bruges and modern EU institutions in Brussels create diverse experiences. Art nouveau heritage and diamond trade add cultural depth while waffles delight visitors. Belgium perfectly combines history, culture, and culinary excellence.

Day 1

Sohier

A limestone ribbon village flowering between two ancient lands

★★★★★
📍 Château de Mirwart vallée Lhomme📍 Domaine provincial Mirwart étangs📍 Église Saint-Roch Mirwart📍 Château Sohier parc Famenne📍 Église Saint-Lambert Sohier📍 Chapelle Notre-Dame Sept Douleurs Sohier
1

The village of Mirwart stretches along an Ardennes ridge between its castle and church. Its farmhouses have preserved their ancestral character: half-timbering, weathered stone, and red brick create an ensemble of rare architectural harmony in the region. The Sower statue near the church stands as the village emblem.

⏱ 30 min
2

The castle of Mirwart overlooks the Lhomme valley from a rocky spur. First mentioned in 955 in the chronicles of Saint-Hubert Abbey, it was destroyed and rebuilt several times before taking its current form between 1706 and 1734—a U-shaped pleasure residence with four cylindrical towers, now a listed monument.

⏱ 30 min
3

Seven kilometers away, the village of Sohier unfolds along the spine of a limestone ridge between Famenne and Ardennes. Its 19th-century limestone houses, beautifully flowered in the warm season, earned it the 'Most Beautiful Flowered Village' label in addition to Belgium's Most Beautiful Villages designation it has held since 1996.

⏱ 30 min
4

The Saint-Lambert church of Sohier, built in 1870, dominates the village from the main street. It faces the former school from 1879, remarkably positioned at the crossroads of the main streets—castle, church, and school once formed the hinge of the entire village's social organization.

⏱ 30 min
5

Sohier's 17th-century castle, renovated in the 19th, lies hidden beneath the foliage of a park with select species, away from the main street. The private property can be glimpsed from the path through its remarkable old trees. Beyond the village, the Chapel of Our Lady of Seven Sorrows (1860) offers a viewpoint over the plateau.

⏱ 25 min

Activities

Walk through the Mirwart Provincial Domain. This 1,350-hectare estate, freely accessible, shelters 35 fish ponds renowned for brown trout, an educational apiary, two rivers, and scattered forest heritage: a 16th-century blast furnace, a 19th-century ice house, and old stone bridges.

⏱ 90 min

Take the 2 km interpretive circuit in Sohier. Educational panels highlight over 30 points of heritage, landscape, and botanical interest. The route passes limestone farmhouses, the castle, and offers open views across the Calestienne pastures.

⏱ 45 min

Day 2

Gros Fays

Dark schist houses perched above the Semois where forest meets river

★★★☆☆
📍 Château-ferme Gros-Fays Semois📍 Église Saint-Pierre Gros-Fays📍 Ancienne brasserie Gros-Fays📍 Moulin Gros-Fays Semois📍 Panorama vallée Semois Gros-Fays📍 Séchoirs à tabac Gros-Fays Ardenne
1

Gros-Fays perches on a promontory where your gaze escapes toward the deep cut of the Semois and its beech forests. Every village house is built from dark schist quarried from the old sites below, their cavities still visible. One material, one color—the architectural unity is striking.

⏱ 30 min
2

The seigneurial domain of Gros-Fays, dating from 1684, is a double-bodied manor in schist rubble. Built in the 17th-18th century, it sits at the village edge surrounded by its agricultural outbuildings. It's the historic heart around which the village organized itself.

⏱ 20 min
3

Wander the lanes to spot traces of former village activities: tobacco drying sheds characteristic of this Ardennes region, washhouse, stone drinking troughs, the old brewery, and mill. The latter is nicknamed 'the resistance mill' for the role its inhabitants played during World War II.

⏱ 40 min
4

Saint Peter's parish church dominates the village. The 2 km walking interpretive circuit starting from the church connects the main heritage points and offers along the way the most open panoramas over the Semois valley.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Hike from Gros-Fays down to the Semois valley. Marked trails descend through dense Ardennes forest to the Gros-Fays stream, a Natura 2000 site with alluvial forests. A picnic area with barbecue, overlooking the valley and the rugged Ardennes relief, marks the circuit's low point.

⏱ 120 min

Kayak down the Semois from Alle-sur-Semois, accessible 5 km from Gros-Fays. Several routes of 7 to 25 km follow the steep meanders and forests that made the valley famous. Rental companies provide return shuttles.

⏱ 180 min
Auprès de mon arbre9.5

Day 3

Our

Where stone bridges curve like half-moons over crystal Ardennes water

★★★☆☆
📍 Église Saint-Laurent Our Paliseul classée📍 Pont en pierres Our rivière demi-lune📍 Moulin Our Ardenne restauré📍 Roche Minguet Our Lesse📍 Village Our maisons grès ardennais
1

Our nestles in a meander of the Our River, a tributary of the Lesse. Its old low houses, built from sandstone rubble and schist, represent Ardennes architecture in its purest form: multicellular farms, enclosed farmyards, small day-laborers' cottages—an entire vocabulary of intact rural building.

⏱ 30 min
2

Two bridges of schistous sandstone rubble cross the Our River upstream and downstream from the village. Their three arches and half-moon cut-stone parapets are among the village's most photographed elements. The north bridge is the most spectacular.

⏱ 20 min
3

Saint-Laurent church, a listed monument since 1983, burned in 1819 and was rebuilt the following year. It overlooks the old stone-walled cemetery. Its facade features a four-level bell tower—square base, octagonal stage, double-cone octagonal spire—and three massive north buttresses. A medieval pilgrimage object for burn victims.

⏱ 30 min
4

Follow the path downstream along the Our toward the old mill, beautifully restored. Beyond, the path runs along the small gorge carved by the river through the rocks before reaching the Minguet rock outcrop, then the confluence of the Our and Lesse at the Cochettes bridge.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk to the Our-Lesse confluence. The trail follows the Our River for about 3 km from the village, passes the restored mill and the Minguet rock before reaching the Horse Rock on the Lesse. Return via the wooded ridges with valley views.

⏱ 90 min

La Table de Maxime, the village's award-winning gastronomic restaurant. After a morning discovering the heritage, break for lunch here before continuing to Celles.

⏱ 90 min
Pouso Jardim de Assis9.5

Day 4

Celles

Home to Belgium's purest Romanesque church, untouched for a thousand years

★★★★★
📍 Collégiale Saint-Hadelin Celles romane XIe📍 Ermitage Saint-Hadelin Celles colline📍 Château de Vêves Celles éperon📍 Char Panther Celles Ardennes 1944📍 Chemin de croix Celles contemporain
1

The collegiate church of Saint-Hadelin is the masterpiece of Mosan Romanesque art. Built in the 11th century on the site where Saint Hadelin founded his hermitage in the 7th century, it has been classified as Exceptional Heritage of Wallonia since 2009. Its relative geographic isolation has preserved it from any major modification for a thousand years.

⏱ 60 min
2

Enter the collegiate to discover its whitewashed interior. The 13th-century choir with stalls, two crypts (7th and 12th century) with their Merovingian sarcophagi, the flat ceiling of edge-joined planks typical of primitive Romanesque, the 12th-century baptismal font, and the tombstones of the lords of Celles form a remarkably coherent medieval ensemble.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Way of the Cross climbs from the collegiate to Saint-Hadelin's hermitage, built in the 19th century on the village heights. The fourteen neo-Gothic stations received a contemporary art treatment by Pierre Courtois. From the summit, the view of the collegiate, limestone houses, and valley floor meadows rewards the ascent.

⏱ 40 min
4

On Celles' central square, a Panther Ausf G. tank sits meters from where it was destroyed on December 24, 1944. Celles marked the extreme limit of the German advance during the Battle of the Bulge that day—a commemorative stele accompanies the vehicle.

⏱ 15 min
5

Two and a half kilometers from the village, the castle of Vêves stands on a rocky spur. This medieval fortress from the 13th-18th centuries, classified as Exceptional Heritage of Wallonia, is one of Belgium's most photographed. Its five cylindrical towers dominate the wooded valleys of the Condroz.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take the 2 km interpretive circuit from Celles square. Educational panels mark the discovery of limestone houses, the castle hidden in its greenery, the fountain, and the Saint-Hadelin stream crossing the valley. The circuit provides the thread for a day's exploration.

⏱ 45 min

Walk to Vêves castle from the village (2.5 km on foot). The path crosses Condroz meadows before emerging at the castle's rocky spur. Interior visits possible—the medieval rooms and collections of the lords of Liedekerke-Beaufort are accessible.

⏱ 90 min
