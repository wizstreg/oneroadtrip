Shenyang

★★★☆☆
1

You start strong with the Shenyang Imperial Palace (Mukden Palace), often nicknamed the "Little Forbidden City." Built from 1625 by Nurhaci, founder of the Qing Dynasty, it housed the first three Manchu emperors before Beijing took over in 1644. The complex contains 114 buildings and roughly 300 rooms - twelve times smaller than the Forbidden City but far more intimate. Look for the Dazheng Hall, an octagonal structure inspired by a Manchu tent. Inscribed as a UNESCO site in 2004. Closed Monday mornings.

⏱ 150 min
2

Head to Beiling Park, 9 km north, which shelters the Zhaoling Tomb of Emperor Hong Taiji (died 1643). The burial mound sits within a massive park that freezes over in winter, where locals come to ice-skate on the lake and sled down hills. The avenue of carved stone animals leads to the tomb itself, guarded by snow-dusted stone lions. This is one of the Three Imperial Tombs of Manchuria classified as UNESCO heritage. Allow two hours to explore comfortably.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Marshal Zhang Zuolin Residence is worth visiting in the city center. This warlord, nicknamed the "Tiger of Manchuria," ruled the region before being assassinated by the Japanese in 1928 in a bomb attack on the railway. His mansion blends traditional Chinese architecture with European style - a rarity. The rooms have been restored with his original furniture.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Dive into Xita, Shenyang's Korean Quarter - one of the most authentic in China outside Yanbian. Storefronts are in Hangeul, Korean barbecues smoke at all hours, and vendors sell homemade kimchi. Try naengmyeon (cold noodles) or bibimbap at one of the family-run restaurants. At night, the red neon lighting creates an atmosphere you won't find anywhere else in China.

⏱ 90 min

If you've got the energy, finish on Zhongjie pedestrian street, lit up for Chinese New Year. Try tanghulu (candied fruit skewers) and bingtang chuan'er, meant to be eaten at -15°C - paradoxically, the cold keeps the caramel from sticking to your teeth. This street is home to the city's best traditional Manchu restaurants.

⏱ 60 min

Dandong

★★☆☆☆
1

The Yalu River Broken Bridge (Yalu Jiang Duan Qiao) is Dandong's star attraction. Built by the Japanese in 1911 to connect occupied Korea to the Eurasian rail network, it was bombed by over 100 American B-29s on November 8, 1950 during the Korean War. Today, only four spans remain on the Chinese side, riddled with shrapnel holes. You walk to the end, and there, the bridge stops abruptly above the river. Across: Sinuiju, North Korea. Entry 30 yuan.

⏱ 75 min
2

At 25 km northeast, the Hushan (Tiger Mountain) Great Wall marks the known eastern terminus of the wall. Rebuilt in the 1990s on Ming foundations, it climbs a steep ridge. From the summit, you see North Korean villages across an arm of the Yalu just a few meters wide - the spot is called "Yibu Kua," "one step to cross." In winter, the walk is slippery but uncrowded.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Memorial Hall of the War to Resist US Aggression and Aid Korea is worth a detour, even if the historical interpretation is very slanted. China recalls that approximately 197,000 Chinese "volunteers" died in the conflict, including Mao Anying, Mao Zedong's eldest son. Exhibits show weapons, uniforms and period photographs. Plan at least 1.5 hours.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Board a tourist boat at the foot of the Broken Bridge to cruise along the North Korean shore at close range. Guides point out official buildings, the idle Ferris wheel, sometimes a few soldiers. Drones banned, binoculars tolerated. The cruise lasts about 30 minutes and costs 40 yuan. It's probably the shortest trip abroad you'll ever take - without setting foot on land.

⏱ 45 min

In the evening, dine on the Yalu promenade at a North Korean restaurant staffed by personnel sent from Pyongyang (several exist in Dandong). The waitresses sing and dance around 8 PM. The menu features barbecue, kimchi and cold soup. It's a strange and unique experience: the smiles are genuine, the context much less so.

⏱ 90 min

Tonghua

★★☆☆☆
1

Tonghua serves as a logical stopover before ascending to Changbaishan. The city nestles in a steep valley and its mountains are covered with frozen birches. Make a detour to the Yang Jingyu Martyrs Cemetery, a Chinese resistance general against Japanese occupation killed in 1940. His story is famous throughout China: the Japanese found his stomach filled with bark and cotton.

⏱ 60 min
2

If you're curious, the Tonghua Wine Museum recounts another surprising chapter of history: the valley has produced red wines since the Qing era, and in the 1930s-40s, production was taken over by the Japanese who exported to Tokyo. Tonghua wine remains one of China's oldest labels. Tasting available.

⏱ 45 min

Changbaishan

★★★★☆
1

You've reached the gates of Changbaishan, in Manchu "Ever-White Mountain," inscribed in 2024 on the UNESCO Global Geoparks list. The massif is a volcano whose last major eruption dates to 946 - one of the most violent in the past 2,000 years, with ash found as far as Japan. Now dormant, it's under constant surveillance from the Tianchi observatory since 1999. Base yourself in Erdaobaihe, the small base town at the foot of the north slope.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Changbai Waterfall is today's attraction. Water from Heaven Lake flows through a single natural outlet, the Erdaobai River, and plunges 68 meters - one of the world's highest volcanic waterfalls. In winter, it continues to flow partially, framed by enormous blue ice formations. The access trail crosses a valley of hot springs that steam at -25°C. Allow two hours round trip from the north entrance.

⏱ 150 min
3

At the Changbai Hot Springs, near the waterfall, water gushes out at over 80°C. Vendors dunk eggs in wire baskets and sell them for 10 yuan each - the yolk cooks hard, the white stays oddly milky due to the water's mineral composition. A curiosity to taste.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Soak in the baths at the thermal spa at the mountain's base after your visit. Several establishments (Pureland Hot Spring, Wanda Resort) offer outdoor pools where steam envelops everything. Picture the scene: -25°C in the air, water at 40°C, snow on the surrounding firs. Expect 200 to 400 yuan depending on the complex.

⏱ 120 min

In the evening, sample the Manchu-Korean hotpot typical of the region: deer meat, wild mushrooms, fresh ginseng (Jilin is China's leading producer) and potato noodles. Erdaobaihe's restaurants are simple but warm. This is also where you drink the local ginseng baijiu, to be consumed in moderation.

⏱ 90 min

Changbaishan

★★★★☆
1

Decisive day: heading to Tianchi, Heaven Lake. From the north gate, you first take a shuttle then a mandatory 4x4 for the final 16 kilometers to the summit. The lake sits in a caldera 5 km in diameter, at 2,189 m altitude. Maximum depth 384 m. In winter, it's entirely frozen under a flat white ice sheet, and is visible only about 50 days per year - weather changes brutally here. If the sky is clear, the panorama is unforgettable.

⏱ 180 min
2

On the way back, stop at the Underground Forest (Dixia Senlin). A volcanic fissure 60 m deep shelters a forest of centuries-old pines and birches below the plateau. The trail descends via wooden stairs to the bottom. Surprising geological view: the collapse dates back to the 946 eruption.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Changbaishan Grand Canyon, open on the west slope but visible from certain points in the north zone, measures 70 km long and up to 100 m deep. Carved by tectonic faults and erosion, it's sculpted with towers of volcanic rock. In winter, it's whitened by snow and particularly photogenic in late afternoon.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you ski, the Wanda Changbaishan complex, on the north slope, offers about thirty runs fed by abundant natural snow. The season runs from November to March. Day pass around 400 yuan, complete rental available on-site. It's now China's most modern ski resort, quieter and less crowded than Yabuli.

⏱ 240 min

For non-skiers, head out by snowmobile through the boreal forest around Erdaobaihe. Several operators offer one-hour outings with a guide. You traverse taigas, sometimes crossing deer and wild boar tracks. Expect 300 to 500 yuan per session.

⏱ 90 min

Changbaishan

★★★★☆
1

Today, switch to Changbaishan's west slope (allow 100 km of road from the north slope). The atmosphere changes completely: fewer tourists, more austere volcanic landscapes, access to Tianchi via 1,442 stone steps. The effort is earned - the panorama here is broader than the north side, with a plunging view of the lake and, on clear days, of North Korean peaks.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Yalu Grand Canyon (not to be confused with the Changbaishan Grand Canyon further north) begins here. Over 10 km, the river has carved a gorge 200 m wide and 170 m deep in volcanic rock. The wooden trail follows the cliff. In winter, the river below marks the border with North Korea - you're just a few meters from the other side.

⏱ 90 min
3

Jinjiang Waterfall, on the west slope, plunges 50 m in free fall then cascades another 20 m. Much less known than Changbai's waterfall, it's more powerful and stretches 70 m wide. In winter, it partially freezes into impressive blue ice columns.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take a break in a traditional Manchu guesthouse in Songjianghe or Erdaobaihe. These wooden houses have kang (bed-stoves heated by the kitchen hearth) where families have slept for centuries in the northeast. They serve suancai (fermented cabbage) and pao mo (pork stew). A night here costs around 250 yuan, and it's probably the most authentic experience of the trip.

⏱ 90 min

For the brave, some local agencies organize the "Yalu River Winter Drifting" in winter: descent by inflatable boat on alternating frozen and open sections. Spectacular but reserved for thrill-seekers. Allow 2 hours and about 300 yuan.

⏱ 120 min

Jilin

★★★☆☆
1

Flight Changbaishan-Changchun then express train to Jilin (40 minutes). You come for a single phenomenon: wusong, or Jilin rime, ranked among China's Four Natural Wonders with the Three Gorges, Yunnan's Stone Forest and Guilin's landscapes. It forms because the Fengman power plant upstream releases water at 4°C that prevents the Songhua River from freezing - the water vapor it releases crystallizes on riverside trees at -20°C or below.

⏱ 60 min
2

Wusong Island (Wusongdao), 40 km north of Jilin, is the epicenter of the phenomenon. Get up early, really early: the golden light of sunrise on the frosted willows is unmatched. The villages of Hantun and Zengtong, on the banks, have traditional Manchu farms that rent heated kang beds. Around 11 AM, the frost begins to fall from the branches in a rain of crystals: it's the "rime fall."

⏱ 180 min
3

Beishan Park, in the city itself, offers a panoramic view of the Songhua and the line of frosted trees from its mixed Buddhist and Taoist temples. It's a walking spot for locals in down jackets. Short climb up icy stairs to the summit where several pavilions welcome incense offerings.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Go ice-skating on the Songhua. Several kilometers of ice are developed in season right inside the city, with skate rentals, wooden sleds for children and even bicycle-sleds. It's free or nearly so (10 yuan for rental), packed on weekends, and terribly fun. Open as soon as the ice exceeds 30 cm thickness, generally mid-December.

⏱ 90 min

Taste the frozen specialties of Dongbei in Jilin's street markets: frozen pears (dong li), frozen persimmons (dong shi), frozen tofu in broth. Locals keep fruit outside all winter in bags hung from the balcony - the freeze is their freezer. Biting into a pear at -20°C is like crunching raw sorbet.

⏱ 60 min

Jilin

★★★☆☆
1

Wula, former capital of one of the pre-Qing Manchu kingdoms, can be visited in half a day 30 km north. The ramparts of the old Manchu town are partly preserved, and a museum recounts the history of the Jurchen, ancestors of the Manchus. It's an un-touristy site where you can see still-living Manchu shamanistic traditions.

⏱ 120 min
2

Jilin's Catholic Church, built by French missionaries in 1917, surprises in the middle of a Chinese city. Neo-Gothic style, two 45 m spires, original stained glass. Still active, it welcomes the local Catholic community. Peculiarity: it survived the Cultural Revolution nearly intact, temporarily converted into a warehouse.

⏱ 45 min
3

Jilin's Confucius Temple (Wenmiao) dates to 1907. It's one of China's four great Confucian temples, unknown to foreigners. The red wooden buildings are striking under snow. Inside, an exhibition on the imperial examination system - over 1,300 years of institution.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Songhua Lake ski complex (Vanke Songhua Lake Resort), 25 km southeast, is one of the northeast's most appreciated. Voted China's best resort five times in recent years. 30 runs, abundant natural snow, family atmosphere and fair prices (day pass around 350 yuan, ski rental 150 yuan).

⏱ 240 min

To end the day, plunge into a northern hot spring. The Shengbeihu spa, in the heights north of Jilin, offers outdoor pools with views over the frozen valley. Local peculiarity: you drink warm baijiu between soaks, an unapologetic Manchu tradition. Expect 200 yuan.

⏱ 120 min

Harbin

★★★★☆
1

Welcome to Harbin, the former "Moscow of the Orient." Founded in 1898 by Russians to build the Transmanchurian Railway, the city welcomed 200,000 White Russians after the 1917 revolution. Start with Saint Sophia Cathedral, Byzantine, built in 1907 and reconstructed in red brick in 1932. Decommissioned since 1958, it now houses an architecture museum. In winter, its snow-dusted onion domes give the impression of being in Saint Petersburg.

⏱ 75 min
2

Central Street (Zhongyang Dajie) stretches 1.4 km and preserves dozens of Art Nouveau, Russian Baroque and Neoclassical buildings from the 1900s-1930s. Paved with granite blocks, it's pedestrianized. Taste Madieer ice cream (from the local Russian brand, serving stick ice cream at -25°C on the street since 1906) and Dalieba Russian bread, sold in 1 kg loaves.

⏱ 90 min
3

Zhaolin Park hosts the Ice Lantern Festival, the world's oldest ice lantern exhibition (since 1963). Smaller than Ice and Snow World, but more intimate, with about 300 sculptures lit from within. Open 9 AM to 9 PM. Ideal for a first immersion evening before the big park tomorrow.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Walk on the frozen Songhua in the evening. The river between the two banks forms a kilometer of solid ice where locals set up amusement rides, sleds pulled by Mongolian horses, inner tube slides, and even traditional ice wrestling stands. All free or nearly so. You understand why Harbin is nicknamed the "Ice City."

⏱ 120 min

Russian dinner: the Lucia or Russia Coffee & Food restaurant on Zhongyang Dajie serve borscht, piroshki, Chicken Kiev and local salmon caviar. Expect 150-250 yuan per person. Red velvet atmosphere, samovar and pianist: it's as kitsch as it is charming.

⏱ 90 min

Harbin

★★★★☆
1

The big day: Harbin Ice and Snow World, the world's largest ice theme park. The 27th edition covers 1.2 million m² with over 2,000 sculptures, some taller than 14 stories. The 2026 festival opened January 5 and runs through late February. Ice blocks are carved from the frozen Songhua. Entry 328 yuan. The park is open 10 AM to 10 PM. Arrive around 3 PM to see the sculptures in daylight, then the illumination at sunset.

⏱ 240 min
2

In the morning, cross the Songhua to Sun Island for the International Snow Sculpture Expo. Over 260 sculptures, some taller than 20 m, carved by artists from around the world. No night lighting here: must see in daylight. Open 9 AM-5 PM, entry 198 yuan.

⏱ 180 min
3

On Sun Island, detour through Volga Manor, a park-village reconstructed in Russian style with a wooden Orthodox church, izbas, troikas. Kitsch but photogenic under snow. Many Chinese weddings take photos here with Russian costumes rented on-site.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Inside Ice and Snow World, tackle the 521 m long ice slide, the park's largest. Free with admission but sometimes a long queue. Guaranteed thrills: you descend at full speed, hood flying, in a channel carved in ice. Kids and adults alike love it.

⏱ 60 min

Taste grilled lamb skewers from street stands, a Hui Muslim specialty very common in the northeast. The Daoli district around Zhongyang Dajie is full of them. With a Harbin beer (the local brand has brewed since 1900, founded by Russians), it's the ideal post-festival supper.

Yabuli

★★★★☆
1

Express train Harbin-Yabuli (1.5 hours, about 100 yuan). You arrive at the birthplace of Chinese skiing: the first resort was opened in 1974 by the provincial sports committee to train athletes. Yabuli hosted the 1996 Asian Winter Games, the 2009 Winter Universiade, and again the Harbin 2025 Asian Winter Games. The complex covers 22.5 km² at the foot of the Zhangguangcai Mountains.

⏱ 60 min
2

Mao'er Shan, the summit at 1,374.8 m, offers China's longest run: 5 km of continuous descent. Even without skiing, take the gondola up for the panorama: boreal forest as far as the eye can see, and on clear days, the snowy peaks of the northeast. Allow 150 yuan round trip.

⏱ 90 min
3

The National Training Center, legacy of the 2025 Games, is partially open to the public. You see FIS-homologated slopes (812 m drop), the K90 ski jump and biathlon facilities with their electronic targets. It's the technical matrix of Chinese winter sports.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Ski. Yabuli has 46 runs (including 15 blacks) and remains, despite competition from Wanda Changbaishan, the reference resort. Season from mid-November to mid-March, abundant natural snow. Day pass 380 yuan weekdays, 680 weekends. Complete ski-boot-pole rental 150-300 yuan. Intermediate and advanced well served; beginners have a vast magic carpet area at the base.

⏱ 360 min

At day's end, head to Yabuli's forest hot spring, rich in selenium, in a wooded 1,000 m² enclosure. The facility combines outdoor and indoor pools. Expect 118 yuan admission. Perfect relaxation after a day of skiing.

⏱ 120 min

Harbin

★★★★☆
1

Return to Harbin to close the journey with the city's darkest and most moving pages. The Unit 731 Museum, 25 km from the center, occupies the former site of the Japanese army's bacteriological experimentation laboratory (1936-1945). Over 3,000 prisoners - Chinese, Russian, Korean - were used as guinea pigs. The museum, modernized in 2015, is solemn and essential. Free admission, closed Mondays.

⏱ 150 min
2

The New Synagogue Museum tells the story of the 20,000 Jews who lived in Harbin between 1898 and 1950. Fleeing Russian pogroms then Nazi Germany, they built schools, newspapers and synagogues. The new synagogue of 1921, in yellow brick, now houses the museum. A largely forgotten history. Admission 25 yuan.

⏱ 75 min
3

If energy permits, take a final daylight tour of Zhongyang Dajie to spot details you missed: carved facades, mosaics of the Modern Hotel (1906), amber fossil souvenir shops (Manchuria is one of the world's major sources). Buy a final dalieba for the road.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Before departure, lunch at a real Harbin inn: guo bao rou (sweet and sour fried pork, invented in Harbin for Russian diplomats in 1907) and northeast jiaozi (dumplings). The Laochuanji restaurant, in the center, is an institution. Expect 100 yuan.

⏱ 90 min

Transfer to Harbin Taiping International Airport (HRB), 35 km from the center, about 45 minutes by taxi. Direct flights to Beijing, Shanghai, Seoul and several Russian cities. One last Manchu detail before takeoff: grab a Russian coffee at the airport, a still-thriving local legacy.

⏱ 60 min
