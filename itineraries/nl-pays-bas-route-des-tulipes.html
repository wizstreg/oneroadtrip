✨ Highlights
  • Keukenhof open March 19 to May 10: book your timed-entry ticket online well in advance.
  • Kinderdijk is accessible by Waterbus from Rotterdam - the river approach is the most scenic.
  • Bollenstreek tulip fields are cut in late April: plan your bike ride before April 25.
  • Maastricht: Sint Pietersberg caves stay at 9°C year-round - bring warm clothing.
📆 Best period

March, April, May

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 7 and 11 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Peak season with famous tulip blooms, mild temperatures (10-18°C), and longer daylight hours. Keukenhof Gardens and flower fields create spectacular displays.

Summer (June-August): Warmest weather (15-22°C) with long daylight hours, outdoor festivals, and peak tourist season. Highest prices but best conditions for cycling and outdoor activities.

Autumn (September-October): Pleasant temperatures (12-18°C), fewer crowds, and beautiful fall colors. Excellent value with comfortable weather and fewer tourists than summer.

Winter (November-March): Cool weather (3-8°C) with cozy indoor culture, Christmas markets, and potential canal ice skating. Museums less crowded, lower accommodation rates.

🌍 What to discover in NL?

Progressive society with beautiful canals, tulip fields, and innovative culture. Experience cycling culture, world-class museums, and authentic Dutch lifestyle. The country offers cultural sophistication with natural beauty and modern amenities. Netherlands provides European experiences with cultural innovation and natural preservation.

📋 Important notes

Keukenhof is only open from mid-March to mid-May - plan accordingly. The Maastricht detour (D7) can be skipped to shorten the trip to 8 days.

Stop 1

Den Haag

Where the Girl with a Pearl Earring meets Europe's oldest working parliament

★★★★☆
📍 The Hague📍 Den Haag📍 Hofvijver📍 Binnenhof📍 Mauritshuis📍 Scheveningen beach
1

The Mauritshuis occupies a 1644 mansion on the edge of the Hofvijver pond, built for Count Johan Maurits van Nassau. You'll see Vermeer's Girl with a Pearl Earring, Rembrandt's Anatomy Lesson of Dr. Tulp, and Fabritius's Goldfinch. Allow 90 minutes: the museum is compact but every room is a masterpiece. Beat the crowds by arriving after 3pm or on Thursday evenings.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Binnenhof is the oldest parliamentary complex still in use in Europe - its origins trace back to the 13th century under Count Floris IV. Step through the gates into the inner courtyard to discover the Ridderzaal, the Knights' Hall with 18-meter wooden beams, where the King delivers his annual Throne Speech every third Tuesday of September. The exterior is freely accessible; guided tours of the Ridderzaal add historical depth.

⏱ 45 min
3

Madurodam recreates the Netherlands at 1:25 scale: Kinderdijk windmills, Schiphol Airport, Rotterdam harbor, the Royal Palace. It's the perfect place to grasp the country's geography before experiencing it in real life. Allow an hour - it's fun for all ages.

⏱ 60 min
4

Scheveningen is The Hague's seaside resort, 5 km from the city center. Its pier extends 380 meters over the North Sea, and the sandy beach stretches 11 km. Perfect for a late afternoon break before watching the sun descend over the water.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll along Scheveningen's seafront at sunset - the Dutch bike here from downtown along the Het Haagse Beek canal. The often breezy North Sea view immediately sets the tone for the Netherlands.

Stop 2

Delft

The last surviving 17th-century Delftware factory still crafts cobalt masterpieces

★★★★☆
📍 Delft📍 Nieuwe Kerk📍 Markt Delft📍 Royal Delft factory📍 Vermeer birthplace
1

Delft's historic center unfolds on foot between canals and the Markt square. The Nieuwe Kerk houses the tomb of William of Orange, founder of the Dutch Republic, and its bell tower offers panoramic views across the city. Next door, the Renaissance town hall dates from 1618.

⏱ 75 min
2

Royal Delft - the Koninklijke Porceleyne Fles - was founded in 1653 and remains the only survivor of the original 32 Delftware factories still in operation. You'll watch artisans hand-paint blue and white patterns using cobalt oxide, a color that only appears after firing at 1,200°C. The visit lasts 90 minutes and you can observe painters at work in the studio.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Vermeer Centrum traces the life of the painter born in Delft in 1632. None of his 36 paintings are displayed here, but high-definition reproductions allow you to study his luminous technique up close. It's the best context for understanding what you saw at the Mauritshuis yesterday.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Rent a bike and meander along Delft's canals - the city is flat, bike paths wind around bridges and medieval alleyways. In an hour's ride, you'll cross neighborhoods most visitors never see.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 3

Rotterdam

A city reborn from ashes into Europe's boldest architectural experiment

★★★★☆
📍 Rotterdam📍 Kinderdijk windmills📍 Erasmusbrug📍 Markthal Rotterdam📍 Kubuswoningen📍 Delfshaven
1

Kinderdijk's 19 windmills were built between 1738 and 1740 to drain the Alblasserwaard polders - UNESCO-listed since 1997. You can enter two museum windmills and take the cruise boat included in your ticket. Arrive before 10am to avoid tour groups, or take the Waterbus from Rotterdam for the river approach.

⏱ 120 min
2

Piet Blom's Cube Houses (Kubuswoningen) were built in 1984 in Rotterdam: 38 cubes tilted 45° above the Blaak bridge vaults. You can visit one of them, the Kijk-Kubus, to understand how people live inside an impossible angle.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Markthal is a 40-meter high, 120-meter long arch inaugurated in 2014, designed by MVRDV architecture firm. Its interior vault is entirely covered with an 11,000 m² digital mural depicting a cornucopia - fruits, vegetables, giant insects. Stop here for lunch: a hundred food stalls occupy the ground floor.

⏱ 45 min
4

Delfshaven is the only historic Rotterdam neighborhood that survived the May 1940 bombings. From here, the Pilgrim Fathers departed on July 22, 1620, aboard the Speedwell before joining the Mayflower in England. The 15th-century Pilgrim Church still stands facing the canals.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Bike ride through Rotterdam along the Erasmus Bridge and waterfront - the city was rebuilt from scratch after the German bombings of May 1940, and you'll discover architecture unique in Europe, blending brutalism, high-tech, and urban experimentation.

⏱ 90 min
Rotterdam's coolest apartment9.6

Stop 4

Middelburg

Zeeland's VOC capital where medieval ramparts guard maritime memories

★★★☆☆
📍 Middelburg Zeeland📍 Oosterscheldekering📍 Neeltje Jans Delta Works📍 Veere fortified village📍 Zeeland coast North Sea
1

The Oosterscheldekering is the largest structure in the Delta Works - 9 km long between the islands of Schouwen-Duiveland and Noord-Beveland, with 62 steel gates that automatically close whenever the sea rises more than 3 meters above Amsterdam's reference level. This storm surge barrier was inaugurated in 1986 by Queen Beatrix. You can drive across it on the N57.

⏱ 45 min
2

Neeltje Jans is the artificial island built to assemble the 65 concrete pillars of the barrier. Now converted into an information center, it presents the Delta Experience - an exhibition about the catastrophic 1953 floods that killed 1,835 people and forced 70,000 evacuations, which triggered the entire Delta Plan.

⏱ 75 min
3

Middelburg is Zeeland's capital and one of the VOC cities - the Dutch East India Company had its regional headquarters here in the 17th century. The 12th-century abbey and 15th-century Gothic town hall frame the Markt square. Don't miss the abbey's Lange Jan tower, visible for miles around.

⏱ 75 min
4

Veere is a fortified village on the shores of Veerse Meer, frozen in its medieval setting. Its grand 15th-century houses on the quay belonged to Scottish wool merchants who held a monopoly on wool trade with the Netherlands. Today it's a resort town for sailors.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk along the beaches and dunes of the Zeeland coast - Domburg or Westkapelle offer wild dune landscapes opening onto the North Sea. Zeeland has one of the country's windiest and most pristine coastlines.

⏱ 90 min
Studio-Vrij9.3

Stop 5

Den Bosch

Where Gothic grandeur meets the surreal visions of Jheronimus Bosch

★★★★☆
📍 Den Bosch📍 's-Hertogenbosch📍 Sint-Janskathedraal📍 Jheronimus Bosch Art Center📍 Binnendieze canals
1

Sint-Janskathedraal is the pinnacle of Brabantine Gothic architecture in the Netherlands. Built between 1380 and 1530, it rises 73 meters high and stretches 115 meters long. Look closely at its 96 sculpted figures on the flying buttresses - dragons, angels, and fantastical creatures unique in the country. Admission is free; guided tower tours offer panoramic views.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Jheronimus Bosch Art Center occupies a former church and presents high-definition reproductions of the complete works of Jheronimus Bosch, born here around 1450. You won't travel to Madrid to see the original Garden of Earthly Delights, but here you'll study it centimeter by centimeter through enlargements.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Binnendieze is a network of partly underground canals that once extended 22 km beneath the streets of 's-Hertogenbosch. Board an electric boat and glide under houses, town halls, and historic cellars - a unique way to see a medieval city from below. The guided tour lasts about 50 minutes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Grab a bossche bol from one of the downtown bakers - this giant pastry filled with whipped cream and coated in dark chocolate has been 's-Hertogenbosch's absolute specialty for centuries. Don't miss the buzz of the market square in late afternoon either.

⏱ 30 min

Stop 6

Eindhoven

Philips factories transformed into the Netherlands' beating design heart

★★★★☆
📍 Eindhoven📍 Strijp-S Philips district📍 Van Abbemuseum📍 Philips Museum📍 Dutch Design Week
1

The Van Abbemuseum is one of the Netherlands' most important modern and contemporary art museums. Its collection includes works by Picasso, Chagall, Mondrian, and El Lissitzky. The 2003 architectural extension creates a dialogue between the original 1936 brick building and a spectacular contemporary volume.

⏱ 75 min
2

Strijp-S is the former 27-hectare Philips industrial complex, closed to the public for decades - nicknamed the 'Forbidden City.' Now converted into a creative district, it houses lofts in the Anton and Gerard factories, artist studios, galleries, and cafés in the former manufacturing workshops. This is where Dutch Design Week takes place every October.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Philips Museum traces the history of the industrial empire founded in Eindhoven in 1891 by Gerard Philips - from the first electric light bulb to razors, radios, and televisions. The interactive presentation appeals to all ages.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wander through Strijp-S in the evening - street art covers the old industrial buildings, terraces fill the interior courtyards, and local designer boutiques stay open late. It's the best place to understand how Eindhoven reinvented itself as the design capital after Philips left.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 7

Maastricht

The most European Dutch city, where Romanesque splendor lines the Meuse

★★★★☆
📍 Maastricht📍 Vrijthof square📍 Sint-Servaasbasiliek📍 Dominicanen bookshop📍 Sint Pietersberg caves📍 Meuse river
1

Sint-Servaasbasiliek is considered the oldest church in the Netherlands, built over the tomb of Saint Servatius, Bishop of Tongeren who died in 384. The current Romanesque nave dates from the 11th century. In the treasury room, don't miss Saint Servatius's 1160 reliquary shrine, a masterpiece of Mosan goldwork in gilded copper and precious stones.

⏱ 45 min
2

Boekhandel Dominicanen is a 13th-century Gothic church converted into a bookshop in 2006. A three-level steel structure was inserted into the nave for bookshelves, and a café occupies the former choir. The international press voted it the world's most beautiful bookshop - and you'll understand why the moment you walk through the door.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Vrijthof is Maastricht's grand square, framed by Sint-Servaasbasiliek on one side and Onze-Lieve-Vrouwebasiliek on the other - two Romanesque masterpieces facing each other. Terraces stretch over 200 meters facing the Baroque facades: it's one of the Netherlands' finest public spaces.

⏱ 60 min
4

Sint Pietersberg is a limestone hill south of Maastricht that's been quarried since Roman times - its tunnel network exceeds 200 km. Guided tours reveal historic inscriptions, wall paintings, and the location of a vault carved in 1944 to protect Rembrandt's Night Watch. Bring warm clothing: it's 9°C year-round.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stroll along the Meuse River and through the Wyck district - across the medieval Sint Servaasbrug, this historic neighborhood houses independent boutiques, galleries, and restaurants. The bridge itself is one of the oldest in the country.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 8

Lisse

The epicenter of spring's tulip explosion across the Bollenstreek

★★☆☆☆
📍 Lisse📍 Keukenhof gardens📍 Bollenstreek tulip fields📍 Dutch flower region📍 bulb fields cycling
1

Keukenhof is the world's largest flower garden: 32 hectares, 7 million bulbs, and over 800 tulip varieties. In 2026 it's open from March 19 to May 10 - peak bloom falls between April 10 and 30. Arrive at opening time (8am) to photograph the paths without crowds.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Bollenstreek fields stretch between Lisse, Hillegom, and Noordwijk: solid-color bands of several hectares each - red, yellow, purple, white - that growers cultivate for bulb sales. This spectacle is visible from roads or bike paths. Note: flowers are usually cut in late April to strengthen the bulbs.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rent a bike and ride the Tulip Bike Tour - a marked 10 or 25 km route through the Bollenstreek fields, completely free. The route runs along working farms, passes Keukenhof castle, and allows photo stops in the fields. Bikes can be rented at Keukenhof P1 parking or in Lisse center.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 9

Haarlem

Where Frans Hals painted, Mozart played, and the Golden Age lives on

★★★★☆
📍 Haarlem📍 Grote Markt📍 Frans Hals Museum📍 Teylers Museum📍 Spaarne river
1

Haarlem's Grote Markt is one of the Netherlands' most beautiful squares, dominated by Sint-Bavokerk - a 1370 Gothic church rising 78 meters high. The 1738 Christian Müller organ was played by a 10-year-old Mozart during his 1766 visit, and also by Handel. Admission is free.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Frans Hals Museum occupies the Oudemannenhuis, an almshouse for elderly men founded in 1609 - and it was Frans Hals himself who painted the 1664 portraits of the regents of this establishment. The collection brings together more than twelve original paintings by the master along with works by his Haarlem contemporaries like Judith Leyster and Jacob van Ruisdael.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Teylers Museum is the oldest museum in the Netherlands, continuously open to the public since 1784. Its founder, banker Pieter Teyler van der Hulst, bequeathed his fortune in 1778 to create a place dedicated to science and the arts. You'll see drawings by Michelangelo and Raphael, giant fossils, and 18th-century scientific instruments in their original neoclassical setting.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Wander through Haarlem's pedestrian streets and hofjes - these secret interior courtyards surrounded by cottages founded as early as the 14th century are a hidden treasure. The Hofje de Bakenesserkamer (1395) is the oldest in the country. Haarlem is 20 minutes by train from Amsterdam Schiphol: it's an art city without the crowds.

⏱ 60 min
