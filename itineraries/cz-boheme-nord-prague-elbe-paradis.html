⏳ How many days

Express version: 11 days. Recommended version to really enjoy: 12 days.

☀️ Weather

Best period: May-June and September-early October, temperatures 15-23°C, ideal for hiking among the rock formations. Avoid July-August (heat up to 30°C, frequent thunderstorms) and November-March (frost, slippery trails in rocky areas). Prague and the Elbe valley remain accessible year-round, but natural rock sites are hazardous in winter.

📋 Notes

Edmund's Gorge in Hřensko reopened on July 19, 2025 under limited access after the 2022 fire; check conditions before visiting. Karlštejn (30 km south of Prague) can complement the first or last day for castle enthusiasts.

Prague

★★★★★
1

Construction began in 1344 under Charles IV, but St. Vitus Cathedral inside Prague Castle wasn't completed until 1929 - a 585-year building project. Peter Parler, appointed at age 23 to lead the works, invented network vaulting of unprecedented daring. The Chapel of St. Wenceslas is encrusted with semi-precious stones; the adjoining vault with seven locks, each key held by a different state official, houses the Bohemian Crown Jewels. Arrive early in the morning to beat the tour buses.

⏱ 75 min
2

Praguers simply call it Karlův most, but the date of the first stone's laying is worth noting: July 9, 1357 at 5:31 a.m. - the palindrome sequence 1-3-5-7-9-7-5-3-1 that tradition attributes to Charles IV's astrologer's choice to ensure the bridge's longevity. Its 515 meters are flanked by 30 Baroque statues added between 1683 and 1714. Look for the bronze plaque beneath St. John of Nepomuk: touching the kneeling monk is said to bring good luck. Cross before 8 a.m. to avoid the crowds.

⏱ 60 min
3

Golden Lane (Zlatá ulička) looks like something from a fairy tale - and the legend of Rudolf II's alchemists is precisely that: false. Archives show these 16th-century cottages were inhabited by arquebusiers and craftsmen, not gold-seekers. Still, the pastel colors and low ceilings make the twenty-minute visit worthwhile. Included in the Castle ticket - no separate admission required.

⏱ 30 min
4

The scent of cold stone and incense in St. Nicholas Church in Malá Strana announces one of Central Europe's great Baroque achievements. Built between 1704 and 1755 by Christoph and then Kilian Ignaz Dientzenhofer, its 75-meter dome dominates the left-bank rooftops. Mozart played the organ here during his Prague visits. Free admission except during concerts, which are frequent and well worth attending.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk the Vltava embankments at sunset between Charles Bridge and Legion Bridge to see the illuminated Castle reflected in the river. The view from Kampa Island, accessible by stairs under Charles Bridge, is particularly striking.

⏱ 60 min

Lidice

★★★★☆
1

A village that no longer exists, yet weighs more heavily than many capitals. On June 10, 1942, the SS razed Lidice in reprisal for the assassination of Reinhard Heydrich. The 173 men over 15 present that day were shot on the spot; the women deported to Ravensbrück camp; the 105 children, mostly gassed at Chełmno. The memorial comprises the mass grave, the museum with the exhibition 'And the Innocent Were Guilty,' and Marie Uchytilová's bronze sculpture representing the 82 children murdered at Chełmno.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Take time to read the names engraved in the bronze of the Lidice children's monument and walk through the rose garden planted with rose bushes gifted by cities worldwide since 1955. The garden and outdoor site are freely accessible; the museum charges admission (about 80 Kč adult). Closed Mondays.

⏱ 30 min

Melnik

★★★☆☆
1

Perched above the confluence of the Elbe and Vltava, Mělník Castle has belonged to the Lobkowicz family since 1793 - returned in 1992 after the fall of communism. From its terrace, the view encompasses both rivers simultaneously, the legendary hill of Říp, and the extinct volcanoes of the České středohoří. The interior displays the family's Baroque collections, notably paintings by Karel Škréta and Petr Brandl. The cellars extend over 1,500 m² at a constant temperature of 8 to 12°C.

⏱ 60 min
2

In the crypt of the Church of Saints Peter and Paul, you discover with a certain vertigo one of the Czech Republic's largest ossuaries: the remains of some 15,000 people are displayed there, victims of plague and the Hussite Wars. The church itself, primarily Gothic, is mentioned as early as the 11th century. Its bell tower offers a view of the confluence. Enter through a side door, signposted - look for it.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Descend into the Lobkowicz castle cellars to taste Ludmila, Mělník's signature white wine. Vines have been planted on the slopes beneath the castle for centuries - legend attributes their introduction to Princess Ludmila. Tasting is offered as an option with the tour; book ahead in high season.

Kokorin

★★★★☆
1

Czech hikers call it the country's most romantic castle. Kokořín Castle emerges from a sandstone spur above a forested valley, its round tower dominating the eponymous nature reserve. First written mention in 1320, built in the first half of the 14th century by Hynek Berka of Dubá - ruined during the Hussite Wars, restored from 1911 to 1918. The tower offers a plunging view over the dense forest. Interiors accessible May to September (60 to 120 Kč depending on tour).

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk up from the parking lot toward Kokořín Castle on the marked trail (about 20 minutes). The crenellated walls and inner courtyard with chapel merit the full circuit. If the castle is closed off-season, the exterior view alone is compelling.

Terezin

★★★★☆
1

Founded in the 18th century for Empress Maria Theresa, Terezín was a military fortress. The Nazis turned it into a transit ghetto from 1941 to 1945 for Jews from across occupied Europe. The national memorial inaugurated in 1947 comprises the Ghetto Museum (in the former municipal school), the Small Fortress-Gestapo prison, the crematorium, and the national cemetery. Allow at least half a day for the complete visit.

⏱ 90 min
2

In the Small Fortress, the cells are so cramped and the walls so low that you physically grasp the notion of overcrowding. A former Gestapo prison, it's now visited with a guide included in the ticket. Prisoners' inscriptions and graffiti remain visible on the walls. The combined ticket (about 220 Kč) covers the Small Fortress and the Ghetto Museum and is valid for 4 consecutive days.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Ghetto Museum is essential, even for those familiar with the history. The exhibition in the former school presents drawings by deported children, saved by their teacher. The Magdeburg Barracks (open April-October) recreates a dormitory as deportees lived it. About 34,000 people died in Terezín itself - primarily from exhaustion and diseases linked to overcrowding.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Allow at least half a day: combined tickets include the Small Fortress, the Ghetto Museum, and the cemetery. A guided tour (available from Prague, about 5 hours total) adds valuable context. The memorial is open daily except December 24, 25, and 26.

⏱ 30 min

Litomerice

★★★☆☆
1

Tourists are rare here, yet Litoměřice's medieval center is among Bohemia's best preserved. Founded around 1230, a royal town with 104 buildings listed as national cultural heritage, it's surrounded by 1,800 meters of Gothic ramparts largely in their original state. Mírové náměstí square, lined with arcaded houses including some of the oldest still inhabited in the country, is the ideal starting point.

⏱ 30 min
2

Mírové náměstí square opens like an open-air Baroque theater: colorful arcades, town hall topped by its chalice-shaped tower - the symbol of the town's winemaking vocation. The tower is open to the public and offers a view of the confluence of the Elbe and Ohře. Steps away, St. Stephen's Cathedral dominates Cathedral Hill from its 50-meter bell tower.

⏱ 45 min
3

The chalice tower atop Litoměřice's town hall is both the town's symbol and a practical lookout point. The regional gallery in the medieval castle displays works by Cranach the Elder, Karel Škréta, and Matyáš Bernard Braun - a collection few foreign visitors know about. Modest admission.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Descend into Litoměřice's Gothic underground galleries, among Bohemia's most extensive. The tour route covers 366 meters and includes a lapidary and ethnographic museum. A local wine tasting is offered in the town hall cellars. Booking advised.

Usti Nad Labem

★★☆☆☆
1

Wagner came here for inspiration, fascinated by the ruins of Střekov Castle perched on a black phonolite rock 100 meters above the Elbe. Built in 1316-1319 by John of Luxembourg to monitor navigation and collect passage fees, the castle is also associated with Goethe and the poet Karel Hynek Mácha. The Knights' Hall, the Gothic cylindrical tower, and the terrace with views over the Elbe valley are the highlights.

⏱ 60 min
2

The 1998 Mariánský most bridge is beautiful but strangely discreet for a structure listed among the ten most beautiful constructions of the 1990s by Structural Engineering International magazine. From the bridge, Střekov Castle on its black rock is visible in full scope. Větruše Castle, accessible by 330-meter cable car from the Forum shopping center, offers a terrace with panoramic views over the city.

⏱ 30 min

Tisa

★★★★☆
1

The sandstone formations at Tisá crunch underfoot like candy - and the formations bear names that say it all: the Elephant, the Tortoise, the Gate of Africa. Tiské stěny (Tisá Walls) formed about 90 million years ago at the bottom of a shallow Cretaceous sea. Protected as a nature reserve since 1996, they constitute the westernmost part of the Elbe Sandstone Formations, at the edge of Bohemian Switzerland National Park.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Choose the long circuit (about 5 km) to reach Skalní náměstí, the rock square where both routes meet in a figure-eight. Both routes are doable in about 2 hours. Paid entry depending on which side you start from (about 30 Kč). Best period is April to October. Parking 100 Kč.

Decin

★★☆☆☆
1

Chopin composed his Děčín Waltz within these walls during a stay with the Counts Clary-Aldringen - the Baroque-Neoclassical Děčín Castle has overlooked the confluence of the Elbe and Ploučnice for centuries. Residence of aristocratic families and military post successively, it has been fully restored and its interiors are open from April to October. The Baroque rose garden on the north terrace is a discreet gem.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Shepherd's Wall (Pastýřská stěna) offers a view no one expects here: a 150-meter rock facing the castle, accessible by cable car or on foot, with a panoramic restaurant perched at the summit. The Elbe below, the castle opposite, the sandstone forests to the north. The surroundings abound in watchtowers accessible on foot from town.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Go up to Děčín Castle in late afternoon for the Baroque rose garden, open in season (May-September). The Baroque sala terrena, the gloriette, and the view over the Elbe valley justify admission. In the evening, a walk along the river on the right bank offers the castle's reflection in the water.

Hrensko

★★★☆☆
1

Pravčická brána measures 26.5 meters in span and 16 meters in interior height - Europe's largest natural sandstone arch. Bohemian Switzerland National Park (79 km²) created in 2000 owes its name to 18th-century Swiss artists struck by the resemblance to their Alps. In 1882, Prince Edmund of Clary-Aldringen had a hotel built near the arch, now renamed Sokolí hnízdo (the Falcon's Nest). Climbing the arch has been prohibited since 1982 to protect the rock.

⏱ 90 min
2

The inhabitants of Hřensko say that here two countries touch without seeing each other: this village of fewer than 300 inhabitants nestled at the confluence of the Kamenice and Elbe on the German border is the lowest point in the Czech Republic. Its half-timbered houses testify to Czech-German border architecture. From here radiate all the park's trails.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Descend into the Kamenice gorges for the flat-bottomed boat crossing, guided by a ferryman with a pole on translucent waters between 150-meter walls. The hike from Hřensko to Pravčická brána follows the red-marked trail (about 3 km, 1h30 uphill). Check the status of Edmund's Gorge before leaving: access still limited in 2025.

⏱ 120 min

Bezdez

★★★★☆
1

In morning fog, Bezděz Castle floats in the clouds at 604 meters altitude - a silhouette that regularly inspires photographers from across Europe. Built by Ottokar II of Bohemia from 1264 and never remodeled since, it's nicknamed 'the king of castles' for its intact primitive Gothic appearance. The 13th-century chapel, royal palace, and panoramic tower are all accessible.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

If the sky is clear from Bezděz's tower, the view encompasses a good quarter of Bohemia. From Prague, the walk up from the village takes about 30 minutes on a marked trail. Open Tuesday to Sunday from May to September, weekends in April and October. Off-season, the exterior ruins remain freely accessible.

Mnichovo Hradiste

★★☆☆☆
1

A Baroque castle that smells of the 18th century - yet its history begins with a decapitation. Václav Budovec, owner of the estate, figured among the 27 Czech lords executed in Old Town Square in 1621; Albrecht von Wallenstein recovered the castle as a reward. Becoming Baroque between 1697 and 1703, the complex houses a historic theater with original sets, a library of 22,000 volumes, and the funeral chapel where Wallenstein's remains rest.

Prague

★★★★★
1

Old Town Square (Staroměstské náměstí) has been the heart of Prague since the 10th century. The Orloj astronomical clock (1410) stands on the south wall of the town hall - admire it in the evening under the lights. Every hour, the apostles parade and the square comes alive. ⏱ 60 min

Josefov resembles a bourgeois Haussmann district - in 1389, during Prague's deadliest pogrom, over 3,000 Jews were massacred in this ghetto. The Old-New Synagogue, built around 1270 in Cistercian Gothic style, is the oldest synagogue in Central Europe still in use. It's associated with Rabbi Löw (1520-1609), to whom tradition attributes the creation of the clay Golem. The attic supposedly still houses its remains - entry forbidden.

⏱ 75 min
3

Rabbi Löw's tomb is perpetually covered with small prayer papers slipped in by visitors. Access is included in the combined ticket to the Jewish Museum in Prague.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Church of Our Lady before Týn is unjustly overshadowed by the clock opposite - its two asymmetric 80-meter towers have dominated Old Town Square since the 14th century. The main Hussite church in Prague for nearly a century, it houses the tomb of astronomer Tycho Brahe, tutor to Emperor Rudolf II who died in Prague in 1601.

⏱ 30 min

Settle onto a terrace on Old Town Square to watch the Orloj's apostles parade. The spectacle repeats every hour until 11 p.m. - one minute per hour, but the square comes alive each time. ⏱ 60 min

Praguers know: the Havelské tržiště market in the Old Town has been active since the 13th century and offers Bohemian garnets, artisan puppets, and local glassware at more reasonable prices than the tourist shops on Charles Bridge. Open daily during the day.
