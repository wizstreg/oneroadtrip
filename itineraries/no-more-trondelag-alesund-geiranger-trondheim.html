✨ Highlights
  • Ålesund is the world's only city whose entire centre was rebuilt in a single style, Art Nouveau, between 1904 and 1907 after a total fire
  • Geirangerfjord has been a UNESCO site since 2005: its walls rise to 1,700 metres and the Seven Sisters waterfall drops 410 metres
  • Trollstigen was hand-built over 8 years and inaugurated by King Haakon VII on July 31st, 1936 - 11 hairpins, 9% gradient, closed November to mid-May
  • Nidaros Cathedral in Trondheim is the world's northernmost medieval Gothic cathedral, built from 1070 on the tomb of Saint Olav
📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 3 and 7 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (June-August): Peak season with midnight sun, warmest weather (15-25°C), and all attractions accessible. Highest prices but ideal conditions for hiking, fjord cruises, and outdoor activities.

Northern Lights Season (September-March): Best aurora viewing with long dark nights, winter activities, and snow-covered landscapes. Cold temperatures (-5 to 5°C) but magical winter experiences.

Shoulder Season (May, September): Excellent weather with fewer crowds and moderate prices. Spring blooms or autumn colors, comfortable temperatures, most activities available.

Winter Sports Season (December-March): Cold weather (-10 to 0°C) perfect for skiing, dog sledding, and winter adventures. Aurora season with cozy cabin culture and winter festivals.

🌍 What to discover in NO?

Nordic paradise with dramatic fjords, Northern Lights, and progressive society. Experience midnight sun, traditional culture, and incredible natural phenomena. The country offers natural wonders with cultural authenticity and environmental leadership. Norway provides life-changing natural experiences with cultural depth and progressive values.

📋 Important notes

Trollstigen is closed from November to mid-May - check vegvesen.no before departure. The Dalsnibba road (RV63) is also closed in winter. Hellesylt-Geiranger ferries operate from May to September.

Day 1

Alesund

The world's only Art Nouveau city, rebuilt in brick after the 1904 fire.

📍 Ålesund Art Nouveau city centre Norway📍 Jugendstilsenteret Ålesund📍 Aker Brygge Ålesund harbour📍 Aksla viewpoint Ålesund
1

Ålesund's city centre is a unique case in Europe: on the night of January 23rd, 1904, a fire razed 850 houses in 16 hours and left 10,000 people homeless. The entire town was rebuilt in brick and concrete between 1904 and 1907 in the then-fashionable Art Nouveau style - hence this rare architectural coherence. Stroll along Kongens gate and St Olav square to see the turrets, ornate façades, and floral motifs.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Jugendstilsenteret is housed in the former Svaneapoteket pharmacy from 1907, whose original interior has been preserved. The museum traces the 1904 fire, the accelerated reconstruction, and the Art Nouveau influences from Germany - Kaiser Wilhelm II, a regular visitor to Sunnmøre, had sent four relief ships after the disaster.

⏱ 60 min
3

Ålesund's harbour and its quays facing the Sunnmøre Alps give the quintessential view of the town from the water. Walk to the end of Molovegen to see the old wooden warehouses - some converted into cafés - and the red lighthouse at the harbour entrance.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Climb the 418 steps to the Aksla viewpoint in Byparken park. The view over Ålesund and its islands, with the Sunnmøre Alps as backdrop, is the finest in town. The step numbers are printed on the risers if you need to keep count.

⏱ 60 min

Day 2

Alesund

The world's only Art Nouveau city, rebuilt in brick after the 1904 fire.

📍 Runde island Norway bird cliffs📍 Lundeura puffin cliff Runde📍 Runde lighthouse Norway
1

Runde island hosts up to 500,000 seabirds on its cliffs - including around 100,000 pairs of Atlantic puffins that arrive in April to nest until late July. It's Norway's southernmost bird cliff and one of the country's most important. The marked trail from Goksøyra to Lundeura takes 40-50 minutes at a normal pace.

⏱ 120 min
2

Runde Fyr lighthouse, on the western point, marks the starting point of the coastal trail along the cliffs. The best time to see puffins is in the evening around 8-9pm, when they return to their burrows after a day of fishing at sea.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Excursion to Runde to observe puffins from May to July. The island is accessible by bridge from the mainland - allow 2 hours' drive from Ålesund. Stick to marked trails: bird protection has been in force since 1957 and leaving the paths during nesting season is prohibited.

⏱ 240 min

Day 3

Geiranger

Village at the head of a UNESCO fjord, facing the Seven Sisters waterfalls.

📍 Flydalsjuvet viewpoint Geiranger📍 Ørnesvingen viewpoint Geiranger fjord📍 Norsk Fjordsenter Geiranger📍 Geirangerfjord UNESCO Norway
1

Flydalsjuvet viewpoint, accessible by car from Geiranger, gives the postcard view with the fjord below and tiny Geiranger village at the back. This is where most aerial photos of the fjord are taken - go early morning to avoid the coaches.

⏱ 30 min
2

Ørnesvingen (the Eagle's Bend), on the tourist route descending towards Geiranger, offers a direct panorama of the Seven Sisters and the fjord from the clifftop. On clear days, you can see the green water 600 metres below.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Norwegian Fjord Centre (Norsk Fjordsenter) traces the history of inhabitants who lived on the farms overlooking Geirangerfjord. The fjord has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2005 - both for its landscapes and for the traces of agricultural life in extreme conditions.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hellesylt-Geiranger cruise through the UNESCO Geirangerfjord. The Seven Sisters (De sju systre) cascade 410 metres in seven parallel streams down the fjord's north wall - opposite, the Suitor (Friaren) answers them from the other shore according to local legend. The journey takes around 1h30 depending on conditions.

⏱ 120 min
Geirangerfjorden Feriesenter9.5
Day 4

Geiranger

Village at the head of a UNESCO fjord, facing the Seven Sisters waterfalls.

📍 Dalsnibba mountain viewpoint Geiranger 1476m📍 Geiranger Skywalk Dalsnibba📍 Skageflå farm Geirangerfjord📍 Geirangerfjord kayak Norway
1

Dalsnibba (1,476 m) is Europe's highest car-accessible viewpoint above a fjord. The toll road climbs in hairpins from Geiranger - from the top, the cruise ships below look like toys. The Skywalk platform cantilevers out over the void above the fjord.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Hike to the abandoned Skageflå farm, perched 250 metres above the fjord. Take the cruise out from Geiranger to be dropped at the foot of the cliff, then climb the steep trail to the empty farm buildings. The view of the Seven Sisters from the same level is unprecedented.

⏱ 240 min

Kayak on Geirangerfjord at the foot of the waterfalls - the silence between the 1,700-metre walls is striking. Set out early morning when the cruise ships haven't yet arrived. Rental companies offer equipment and guides from Geiranger harbour.

⏱ 120 min

Day 5

Andalsnes

Norway's mountaineering capital, between Trollstigen and the Trollveggen wall.

📍 Gudbrandsjuvet gorge Norway📍 Trollstigen hairpin road Norway📍 Trollstigen viewpoint Stigfossen waterfall📍 Åndalsnes village Romsdal Norway
1

Gudbrandsjuvet, along the national tourist route, is a narrow gorge with steel walkways suspended above waterfalls. Stop for 20 minutes - it's exemplary viewpoint architecture and the place empties quickly.

⏱ 30 min
2

Trollstigen - literally 'the Troll Ladder' - climbs 11 hairpin bends at 9% gradient to 858 metres altitude, with the Stigfossen waterfall (320 m drop) right beside the road. Opened on July 31st, 1936 by King Haakon VII after 8 years of hand construction, each bend bears the name of the foreman who built it.

⏱ 60 min
3

From the panoramic platform at the top of Trollstigen, designed by Reiulf Ramstad Architects, you look down on the hairpins below and the Isterdalen valley opening towards the Atlantic. Allow 30 minutes up there - the visitor centre café is worth the stop.

⏱ 30 min
4

Åndalsnes, Norway's mountaineering capital, nestles at the head of Romsdalsfjord beneath Romsdalshorn (1,550 m). It's an ideal base for hikers - the Norsk Tindesenter on the harbour houses the mountaineering museum and Norway's tallest indoor climbing wall.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Drive the Geiranger-Trollstigen tourist route (around 104 km). Trollstigen is closed from November to mid-May depending on snow conditions - check vegvesen.no before departure. Vehicles over 12.4 metres are prohibited.

⏱ 120 min

Day 6

Andalsnes

Norway's mountaineering capital, between Trollstigen and the Trollveggen wall.

📍 Trollveggen Troll Wall Romsdalen Norway📍 Trollveggen visitor centre Åndalsnes📍 Rampestreken viewpoint Åndalsnes Norway
1

Trollveggen is Europe's highest vertical rock face - 1,100 metres of gneiss from the valley floor to the summit, with a 50-metre overhang at the top. You can see it from the E136, 10 km from Åndalsnes. The first ascents didn't happen until 1965 - a British amateur team, mocked by experts, had taken up the challenge.

⏱ 45 min
2

Trollveggen visitor centre, at the foot of the wall on the E136, offers a panoramic terrace and a film on the history of climbing attempts and BASE jumping - banned since 1986 after several fatal accidents.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Hike to Rampestreken from Åndalsnes - a cantilevered steel platform 550 metres above Romsdalsfjord. Allow 2h30 return over 4 km. The ascent is steep but the trail is marked. The view over the valley and fjords is worth every metre of elevation.

⏱ 180 min

Day 7

Kristiansund

Gateway to the Atlantic Road, 8 bridges over the Norwegian Sea.

📍 Bud fishing village Norway Møre📍 Atlantic Ocean Road Atlanterhavsveien Norway📍 Storseisundet Bridge Atlantic Road Norway
1

Bud village, at the entrance to the Atlantic Road, was the most active trading centre between Bergen and Trondheim in the 16th-17th centuries. Today a few dozen red wooden houses face the open ocean - the contrast with the raging Hustadvika is striking in stormy weather.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Atlantic Ocean Road (Atlanterhavsveien) links 8.3 km of islets via 8 bridges open to the Norwegian Sea - built between 1983 and 1989, after weathering 12 violent storms during construction. It was voted Norway's 'construction of the century' in 2005. In bad weather, waves literally wash over the bridges.

⏱ 60 min
3

Storseisundet Bridge, 260 metres long, is the most photographed on the route. Its cantilevered curve creates an optical illusion from certain angles: the road appears to plunge straight into the ocean. Stop at the viewpoint lay-by for the photo - you can't get it from the car.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Drive the Atlantic Road in rough weather if you can time it - waves washing over the bridges are part of the experience. Plan frequent stops at the four purpose-built viewpoints along the route.

⏱ 90 min

Day 8

Trondheim

Thousand-year Gothic cathedral and Bakklandet quarter along the Nidelva.

📍 Nidaros Cathedral Trondheim Norway📍 Archbishop Palace Erkebispegården Trondheim📍 Bakklandet Trondheim wooden houses📍 Gamle Bybro Old Town Bridge Trondheim
1

Nidaros Cathedral (Nidarosdomen) is the world's northernmost Gothic cathedral. Construction began in 1070 on the tomb of Saint Olav - a Viking king who died at Stiklestad in 1030 and was canonised a year later. His silver shrine was melted down for coins at the 1537 Reformation and his remains buried in a secret location within the cathedral that no one has found since.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Archbishop's Palace (Erkebispegården), right next to the cathedral, is Scandinavia's oldest secular building still standing. It houses the Norwegian crown jewels and an archaeological exhibition on medieval Trondheim.

⏱ 60 min
3

Bakklandet is Trondheim's most photogenic quarter - its colourful wooden houses and cobbled streets date from the 1600s. In 1965, the city council wanted to demolish the district for a four-lane highway; activists reclaimed the empty houses in 1971 and painted the façades to save them. Cross the Old Bridge (Gamle Bybro, 1681) to reach it from the centre.

⏱ 60 min
4

The wooden storehouses on stilts (Bryggen) lining the Nidelva are Trondheim's equivalent of Bergen's Bryggen - built partly into the river to allow boats to moor directly. The view from the Old Bridge is Trondheim's postcard shot.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Wander through Bakklandet's lanes - the district concentrates independent cafés, bookshops and craft boutiques in crooked houses. Don't miss Trampe, the world's first bicycle lift, installed in the Brubakken slope in 1993.

⏱ 60 min

Day 9

Trondheim

Thousand-year Gothic cathedral and Bakklandet quarter along the Nidelva.

📍 Kristiansten Fortress Trondheim Norway📍 Ringve Music Museum Trondheim📍 Munkholmen island Trondheim ferry
1

Kristiansten Fortress (1681), built after the great fire that devastated Trondheim the same year, dominates the town from its hilltop. From here you have the best view of the Nidelva storehouses, the cathedral and the distant fjord. Entry is free.

⏱ 45 min
2

Ringve Museum, the national museum of music, occupies an 18th-century manor surrounded by botanical gardens. The collection of historical instruments is displayed in the original rooms - guides play live during the tour.

⏱ 60 min
3

Munkholmen island, accessible in 10 minutes by boat from the harbour, has served variously as an execution site, Benedictine monastery (11th century), prison (17th century) and fortress. In summer, Trondheim locals swim from the beach there.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

The Ladestien coastal path follows 14 km of coastline north of Trondheim with views over Trondheimsfjord. You can walk part of it from Nedre Elvehavn - allow 2 to 3 hours for a one-way stretch to Lade and return by bus.

⏱ 120 min

Day 10

Trondheim

Thousand-year Gothic cathedral and Bakklandet quarter along the Nidelva.

📍 Sverresborg Trøndelag Folk Museum Trondheim📍 Trampe bicycle lift Bakklandet Trondheim📍 Dovrefjell musk ox safari Norway
1

Trøndelag Folk Museum (Trøndelag Folkemuseum) at Sverresborg gathers historic farms and buildings in an open-air setting on the hills above Trondheim. The medieval Sverresborg fortress crowns the centre of the site.

⏱ 75 min
2

Trampe, the world's only bicycle lift, has been installed since 1993 in the steep Brubakken slope in Bakklandet. You place your foot on the pedal platform, the mechanism pushes - and you ascend the steep hill effortlessly. Available in summer season.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Musk ox safari excursion in Dovrefjell National Park, 3 hours' drive south of Trondheim. Herds of musk oxen - descendants of animals reintroduced in 1932 - graze on the plateau at 1,000 metres altitude. Local operators organise guided outings with near-guaranteed sightings in summer.

⏱ 300 min
