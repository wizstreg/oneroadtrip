✨ Highlights
  • Wieliczka Salt Mine appears among the very first 12 sites inscribed on UNESCO's World Heritage List in 1978, alongside Egypt's pyramids and the Galápagos Islands. St. Kinga's Chapel, at 101m depth, measures 54m long by 12m high.
  • Visiting the Auschwitz-Birkenau memorial requires online booking at auschwitz.org. From early April to late October, slots sometimes book out several weeks in advance. Free entry, paid guided tour (approximately 100 PLN). Allow a minimum of 3.5 hours for both sites.
  • The Kasprowy Wierch cable car (kolejkalinowa.pl) closes in November and early December for annual maintenance. It also stops operating in bad weather. Check conditions before departing. Tickets book online; in summer, you need to book several days ahead.
  • The geothermal baths are at Szaflary (12km from Zakopane) or Bukowina Tatrzańska (20km), not in Zakopane itself. Expect between 40 and 80 PLN entry depending on duration. Morskie Oko: Palenica Białczańska car park often full by 9am in July-August; online booking or pre-8am arrival advised.
  • A car is essential for this circuit: distances between Kraków, Oświęcim, Wieliczka and Zakopane are short (65-155km) but public transport is insufficient or slow. Rental available at Kraków-Balice airport (KRK).

May, June, September

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 2 and 6 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather with mild temperatures (15-23°C), blooming landscapes, and longer days. Ideal for city exploration and outdoor activities without summer crowds.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather (18-25°C) with peak tourist season, highest prices, but best conditions for festivals, hiking, and outdoor experiences. Long daylight hours perfect for sightseeing.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent weather (12-20°C) with beautiful fall colors, fewer crowds, and moderate prices. Great for cultural tourism and photography.

Winter (November-March): Cold weather (-2 to 5°C) with potential snow, Christmas markets, and cozy indoor culture. Lowest prices, fewer tourists, magical winter atmosphere.

📋 Important notes

Three faces of a single region, Lesser Poland: the royal city of Kraków with its Jewish quarter, cathedrals and UNESCO-listed salt mine, the Auschwitz-Birkenau memorial an hour's drive away, and the Tatra Mountains with their cable car and oscypek cheese markets. This circuit is best covered by rental car from Kraków-Balice airport - there's no zigzagging: you head southeast to the mines, west to Auschwitz, then south to Zakopane.

Krakow

★★★★★
📍 Wawel Royal Castle Kraków📍 Wawel Cathedral Kraków📍 Main Market Square Rynek Główny Kraków📍 St. Mary's Basilica Kraków📍 Planty Park Kraków
1

Perched atop Wawel Hill, the Royal Castle served as the residence of Polish monarchs from the 11th to the 17th century. The arcaded inner courtyard was redesigned in Renaissance style during the 16th century under Sigismund I, whose architect brought master craftsmen from Italy to Kraków. You'll tour the royal apartments, state rooms adorned with Flemish tapestries, and the royal treasury. Allow at least two hours for the entire complex.

⏱ 150 min
2

Wawel Cathedral witnessed royal coronations from 1320 to 1734 and served as the final resting place for Polish sovereigns. Its crypts house the tombs of kings, heads of state, and national writers. The Sigismund Chapel, crowned by a golden dome, is considered the finest example of Renaissance funerary architecture in Central Europe. The great Sigismund Bell, cast in 1520, weighs approximately 13 tonnes.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Rynek Główny, laid out in 1257, measures 200 metres per side - making it Europe's largest medieval market square. St. Mary's Basilica on the north side houses Veit Stoss's altarpiece, carved between 1477 and 1489: 13 metres tall with 200 figures carved from linden wood. The altarpiece wings open daily at 11:50am. The Cloth Hall (Sukiennice) at the centre of the square dates from the Renaissance period.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

The Planty Park encircles the historic centre for 4km, occupying the site of medieval ramparts demolished between 1806 and 1812. An evening stroll through this tree-lined boulevard makes the perfect end to your first day, with the illuminated facades of the Old Town as your backdrop.

Krakow

Where royal Gothic towers rise above cobblestones worn by seven centuries of history

★★★★★
📍 Kazimierz Jewish Quarter Kraków📍 Remuh Synagogue Kraków📍 Schindler's Factory Museum Kraków📍 Plac Nowy Kraków📍 Old Jewish Cemetery Kraków
1

Kazimierz was founded in 1335 as an independent town by King Casimir the Great, before being incorporated into Kraków in 1791. From the 15th century onwards, the Jewish population made it one of the most important cultural and religious centres in Central Europe. Seven synagogues remain standing in the quarter. The Remuh Synagogue (1553), the smallest in the district, is one of only two still open for worship. Its adjoining cemetery, in use from 1552 to 1799, preserves Renaissance and Baroque tombstones that are among the best-preserved in Europe.

⏱ 120 min
2

Oskar Schindler's Enamel Factory (Fabryka Emalia Oskara Schindlera) on Lipowa Street has housed the museum on Nazi occupation of Kraków since 2010. Schindler employed Jewish workers here during the war, thereby protecting over 1,200 people from deportation. The permanent exhibition covers life in the occupied city between 1939 and 1945: photographs, period objects, testimonies and multimedia installations. Not to be confused with Auschwitz-Birkenau - this museum specifically addresses Kraków itself.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Plac Nowy has been the heart of Kazimierz life since the 19th century. The brick rotunda at its centre once served as a kosher chicken market; today its stalls serve zapiekanka, a toasted baguette half topped with mushrooms, cheese and various garnishes, considered by locals to be Polish pizza. The atmosphere of the square, with its bars and outdoor cafés, differs from that of the Old Town - less touristy, more alive.

📍 Wieliczka Salt Mine Kraków📍 St. Kinga's Chapel Wieliczka📍 MOCAK Museum Kraków📍 Podgórze District Kraków📍 Ghetto Wall Fragment Kraków
1

The Wieliczka Salt Mine has produced salt since the 13th century. It appears on UNESCO's very first World Heritage List, established in 1978. The standard tourist route descends to 135m depth through 3km of galleries: underground lakes, sculptures carved from salt and, on level 3, St. Kinga's Chapel. Carved from rock salt between 1896 and 1935, this chapel measures 54m long, 18m wide and 12m high - its chandeliers, altars and bas-reliefs depicting the Last Supper are entirely made of rock salt. Online booking advised during high season.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

The afternoon is free for exploring Kraków. MOCAK (Museum of Contemporary Art), installed in a building constructed on the former Schindler factory site, has showcased Polish and international art since 2011. Another option: the Podgórze district across the Vistula, where a fragment of the wall from the former Jewish ghetto created by the Nazis in 1941 still stands. The Ghetto Museum occupies Tadeusz Pankiewicz's pharmacy - he was the only non-Jew permitted to remain in the ghetto.

The Auschwitz-Birkenau memorial comprises two sites 3km apart: Auschwitz I (the former Polish camp converted into a concentration camp in 1940, now the main museum) and Auschwitz II-Birkenau (the largest extermination camp built from 1941). The Soviet Red Army liberated the site on 27 January 1945, a date since recognized as International Holocaust Remembrance Day. The guided tour lasts a minimum of 3.5 hours. Entry is free; guided tours are charged (approximately 100 PLN). Booking required at auschwitz.org, several weeks in advance during high season.

Zakopane

Where highland culture meets alpine wilderness beneath the jagged Tatra peaks

★★★★☆
📍 Krupówki Street Zakopane📍 Jaszczurówka Wooden Church Zakopane📍 Zakopane Style Architecture📍 Oscypek Market Zakopane📍 Termy Gorący Potok Szaflary
1

Krupówki Street is Zakopane's main artery. The wooden architecture in "Zakopane style" lining it was theorized in the late 19th century by Stanisław Witkiewicz, who sought a national aesthetic rooted in góral (highlander) traditions. The wooden church at Jaszczurówka (1904-1907), designed by Witkiewicz himself, stands as the finest example of this style. The adjacent market sells oscypek - smoked sheep's milk cheese from pine wood, protected by European PDO since 2008.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

The thermal baths aren't located in Zakopane itself but in the surrounding area. Termy Gorący Potok in Szaflary (12km) and the complexes at Bukowina Tatrzańska (20km) offer geothermal pools at 38-40°C, outdoors facing the Tatras. These facilities work best for late afternoon after the drive from Oświęcim. Allow 2 to 3 hours on site.

Zakopane

Where highland culture meets alpine wilderness beneath the jagged Tatra peaks

★★★★☆
📍 Kasprowy Wierch Cable Car Zakopane📍 Mount Gubałówka Zakopane📍 Morskie Oko Lake Tatras📍 Gąsienicowa Valley Trail Tatras📍 Kuźnice Station Zakopane
1

Arrive in Zakopane, Poland's premier mountain resort nestled at the foot of the spectacular Tatra Mountains. This charming town, known as Poland's winter capital, enchants visitors with its distinctive wooden architecture, vibrant highland culture and breathtaking alpine scenery. The pedestrian main street lined with traditional timber buildings showcases the unique regional style that developed in this isolated mountain community.

⏱ 75 min
2

Ride the funicular railway up Mount Gubałówka for breathtaking panoramic views across Zakopane and the dramatic Tatra peaks beyond. The summit ridge offers easy walking trails, souvenir stalls selling local crafts, and traditional mountain food to sample while taking in the mountain panoramas. On clear days, the jagged silhouette of the High Tatras creates an unforgettable alpine backdrop.

⏱ 75 min
3

Hike to Morskie Oko, Poland's largest and most beautiful mountain lake, set in a glacial cirque surrounded by towering granite peaks. The 7.7-kilometre trail through Tatra National Park passes cascading streams and virgin pine forests before revealing the crystalline waters reflecting sheer rock walls. This iconic natural wonder, whose name means 'Eye of the Sea', represents the quintessential Tatra experience.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

The Kasprowy Wierch cable car departs from Kuźnice (15 minutes from Zakopane centre) and climbs to 1,987m in two sections, changing at Myślenickie Turnie (1,354m). Total journey time is approximately 20 minutes. The summit station marks the border with Slovakia; in clear weather, the panorama encompasses the High Tatras on the Slovak side and the Podhale plain on the Polish side. From the summit, one recommended option is to walk down through Gąsienicowa Valley to Kuźnice (7.4km, 3h15, -1,000m descent, moderate trail). Cable car tickets are sold online at kolejkalinowa.pl and sell out fast in July-August. Expect temperatures 10-15°C lower than in town.

⏱ 240 min

Alternative for those preferring to avoid elevation gain: Morskie Oko, the highest lake in the Polish Tatras (1,395m). The walk from Palenica Białczańska car park takes 2.5 hours each way (9km on paved road without technical climbing). The lake is surrounded by glacial cirques and rock walls. During high season, book parking online or arrive before 8am.

Krakow

★★★★★
📍 Rynek Główny Market Square Kraków📍 Wawel Castle Kraków📍 Cloth Hall Sukiennice Kraków📍 Wieliczka Salt Mine📍 Stary Kleparz Market Kraków
1

Lose yourself in Kraków's magnificent Rynek Główny, Europe's largest medieval market square and the beating heart of this beloved former royal capital. This vast paved expanse, ringed by elegant burgher houses, Gothic churches and the Renaissance Cloth Hall, pulses with life from dawn until well past midnight. Horse-drawn carriages, street performers and café terraces create an atmosphere of timeless European elegance.

⏱ 75 min
2

Climb to Wawel Castle, the former seat of Polish kings perched dramatically on a limestone outcrop above the Vistula. This magnificent complex of Gothic and Renaissance buildings houses state rooms, the royal private apartments and an impressive collection of Flemish tapestries and armour. The adjoining Wawel Cathedral, coronation site of Polish monarchs, contains the royal tombs and the silver shrine of St. Stanislaus.

⏱ 75 min
3

Stroll through the Cloth Hall, the grand Renaissance trading arcade that has occupied the centre of the Main Square since the 14th century. The ground floor maintains its trading tradition with stalls selling amber jewellery, carved wooden crafts and Polish souvenirs, while the upper gallery houses a branch of the National Museum. This iconic structure perfectly embodies Kraków's medieval prosperity as a major trading centre.

⏱ 75 min
4

Descend into the Wieliczka Salt Mine, a UNESCO World Heritage Site just outside Kraków where salt has been extracted continuously since the 13th century. The underground chambers entirely carved from rock salt include ornate chapels, elaborate sculptures and even an underground lake, all lit by crystal chandeliers also made from salt. This subterranean marvel, reaching 327 metres deep, showcases centuries of mining history and remarkable artistic achievement.

⏱ 75 min
5

Pay solemn tribute at the Auschwitz-Birkenau Memorial and Museum, the largest Nazi concentration camp where over one million people perished during the Second World War. This harrowing site preserves physical evidence of genocide through the original barracks, guard towers and crematoria that bear witness to unimaginable human tragedy. This experience, though emotionally demanding, provides essential historical education about the Holocaust.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stary Kleparz market, a few minutes' walk from the Old Town, has operated since the Middle Ages. This is where Kraków locals still do their shopping: sheep's cheeses, dried mushrooms, artisan cold cuts, mountain honey. An ideal spot for last-minute purchases before the airport. The Kraków-Balice journey takes approximately 30 minutes by taxi or bus.
