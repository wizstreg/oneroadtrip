Armenia in 15 Days: Grand Tour from Yerevan to Gyumri

📅 15 days📍 15 stops🚗 1015 km⏱ 17h40

From Areni's ancient vineyards to Geghard's rock-hewn monasteries, from Amberd's fortress in the clouds to Akhtala's Byzantine frescoes, Armenia reveals in fifteen days a 1,700-year-old Christian civilization carved in stone.

✨ Highlights
  • Etchmiadzin: world's oldest functioning Christian cathedral since 301
  • Wings of Tatev: world's longest reversible cable car above a spectacular gorge
  • Areni: world's oldest known winery, 6,100 years of history
  • Akhtala: unique Byzantine frescoes in Armenia's 10th-century fortress-monastery
📆 Best period

May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 6 and 10 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Peak Season (May-September): Perfect weather for hiking and sightseeing, warm temperatures, minimal rainfall, best for mountain activities and festivals.

Spring (April-May): Mild temperatures, wildflowers blooming, excellent for cultural sites, fewer crowds than summer months.

Fall (September-October): Pleasant temperatures, harvest season, beautiful autumn colors, wine harvest celebrations, ideal hiking weather.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, snow in mountains, skiing season, fewer tourists, cozy indoor cultural experiences.

🌍 What to discover in AM?

Armenia boasts the world's oldest Christian sites, stunning mountain monasteries, and exceptional wine culture. Mount Ararat views and ancient khachkars create spiritual experiences while Yerevan offers vibrant nightlife. The country combines deep history with warm hospitality and incredible landscapes. Perfect for cultural enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

Yerevan

Where pink volcanic tuff meets musical fountains and world-class museums

★★★★★
1

Republic Square beats at the heart of Yerevan in neoclassical splendor. Five buildings in pink and yellow tuff frame the plaza, including the Government House crowned by a 13-foot clock dating from 1941.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Cascade Complex rises in monumental steps—572 of them—designed in 1971 and transformed into a contemporary art museum thanks to a $35 million investment from patron Gerard Cafesjian.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Cascade gardens shelter monumental sculptures by Botero, Plensa, and Flanagan. From the summit, the view sweeps across Yerevan with Mount Ararat looming in the distance.

⏱ 45 min
4

Vernissage is Armenia's largest handicraft market, open weekends near Republic Square. Hunt for carpets, stone khachkars, silver jewelry, and vintage Soviet memorabilia.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stay for the evening fountains at Republic Square—the sound-and-light spectacle begins at 9 PM from April through November.

⏱ 60 min

Take the free indoor escalators inside the Cascade all the way to the top for a nocturnal view of the illuminated city.

⏱ 60 min

Dine in the Kentron district—the terraces along Abovyan Street and Northern Avenue stay lively well into the evening.

Day 2

Yerevan

Where pink volcanic tuff meets musical fountains and world-class museums

★★★★★
1

The Matenadaran preserves over 23,000 medieval manuscripts—one of the world's most important collections. Inscribed in UNESCO's Memory of the World Register, it displays illuminated gospels from the 5th century alongside treatises on medicine, mathematics, and astronomy.

⏱ 90 min
2

On Tsitsernakaberd hill, the Armenian Genocide Memorial stands as the most solemn site in Armenia. Twelve slanted slabs encircle an eternal flame in memory of the 1.5 million victims of 1915.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Genocide Museum, underground and silent, traces through testimonies, photographs, and documents the history of the 20th century's first genocide.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Tsitsernakaberd stele rises 144 feet, split in two—a symbol of the Armenian nation divided between those who remained on their ancestral lands and those scattered across the global diaspora.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Walk the memorial's plane tree avenue, planted by visiting heads of state—each tree bears an identification plaque.

⏱ 60 min

Take the metro from Republic Square to Yeritasardakan station to reach the Matenadaran in minutes.

⏱ 60 min

In the evening, discover the contemporary art galleries along Mashtots Street, concentrated within a 500-meter radius.

⏱ 60 min

Day 3

Yerevan

Where pink volcanic tuff meets musical fountains and world-class museums

★★★★★
1

Etchmiadzin Cathedral is the oldest Christian cathedral still functioning anywhere in the world. Built between 301 and 303 by Saint Gregory the Illuminator after Armenia's conversion to Christianity—the first nation on earth to adopt it as a state religion.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Etchmiadzin Treasury Museum preserves unique relics: the Holy Lance that pierced Christ's side, Saint Gregory's right hand, and a presumed fragment of Noah's Ark.

⏱ 45 min
3

The circular ruins of Zvartnots, 3 km from Etchmiadzin, are what remains of a three-tiered cathedral built in the 7th century and destroyed by earthquake in the 10th. This rotunda's unique architecture inspired builders far beyond Armenia's borders.

⏱ 60 min
4

Saint Hripsime Church (618 AD) and Saint Gayane Church (630 AD) are both UNESCO-listed. These two edifices rank among the best-preserved Christian structures anywhere in the world.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Leave early to attend the morning ceremony at Etchmiadzin—monks in black robes and Armenian liturgical chant create an unforgettable atmosphere.

⏱ 60 min

Take minibus #23 from Kilikia station in Yerevan—frequent departures, 30–40 minutes for less than 300 drams.

⏱ 60 min

Lunch in Etchmiadzin before visiting Zvartnots—roadside restaurants serve fresh lavash, khorovats, and Armenian salads.

⏱ 60 min

Day 4

Yerevan

Where pink volcanic tuff meets musical fountains and world-class museums

★★★★★
1

Khor Virap means 'deep pit': King Tiridates III imprisoned Saint Gregory here for 13 years. Released, Gregory healed the king's madness and converted him—making Armenia the world's first Christian country in 301.

⏱ 60 min
2

Garni Temple, built in 77 AD by King Tiridates I, is the only preserved Hellenistic temple in the entire post-Soviet space. Its Ionic columns and Roman baths with Greek mosaics testify to a deeply rooted Greco-Roman culture.

⏱ 75 min
3

A kilometer from the temple, the Symphony of Stones reveals natural basalt columns shaped like a giant organ along the Azat River—a spectacular geological formation.

⏱ 45 min
4

Geghard—'the Lance'—is a 13th-century monastery partially carved into the cliff face in the Azat Gorge. UNESCO-listed in 2000, it sheltered the Holy Lance for centuries, now kept at Etchmiadzin.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Descend into Saint Gregory's underground cell at Khor Virap via the metal ladder—20 feet below ground, 15 feet in diameter, 13 years of imprisonment.

⏱ 60 min

Listen to Armenian liturgical chants in Geghard's cave-churches—the acoustics carved into the rock amplify voices supernaturally.

⏱ 60 min

Photograph Mount Ararat from the platform behind Khor Virap—one of the most iconic panoramas in the Caucasus, with the Turkish border visible 100 meters away.

⏱ 60 min

Day 5

Yeghegnadzor

Gateway to Vayots Dzor, where ancient vineyards meet limestone canyons and medieval monasteries

★★☆☆☆
1

The Areni-1 cave, discovered in 2007, is the oldest known winemaking facility in the world: 6,100 years old. Excavations have yielded a wine press, 17-gallon fermentation vats, and storage jars still stained with malvidin, red wine's pigment.

⏱ 75 min
2

Noravank nestles in the Amaghu Gorge between blood-red limestone cliffs. Founded in 1105 and developed in the 13th–14th centuries, its masterpiece is the Surb Astvatsatsin church (1339): a two-story edifice whose external staircase is so narrow you must climb it sideways.

⏱ 75 min
3

The reliefs sculpted by Momik on Noravank's facade—Christ in mandorla, angels, vegetal interlace—rank among the most refined in medieval Armenian art.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Taste wines from the Areni Noir grape in one of the village cellars—this indigenous variety has been cultivated here for at least 6,000 years without interruption.

⏱ 60 min

Photograph Noravank at sunset—the crimson cliffs encircling the monastery ignite in spectacular light.

⏱ 60 min

Visit Areni's archaeological cave with a local guide—explanations of 6,100-year-old winemaking techniques give Armenian culture an unprecedented dimension.

⏱ 60 min

Day 6

Jermuk

Where forty thermal springs bubble from mountains at 6,500 feet

★★★☆☆
1

Jermuk's Gallery of Waters lines up five public stone fountains, each at a higher temperature than the previous: from 86°F to 135°F. Rich in magnesium, calcium, and bromine, these mineral waters have been reputed curative for generations.

⏱ 45 min
2

Jermuk Waterfall plunges 230 feet into the Arpa Gorge—Armenia's second-highest. Local legend calls it 'Mermaid's Hair': a forsaken young woman threw herself from the cliff, her hair transforming into eternal cascade.

⏱ 60 min
3

Jermuk perches at 6,500 feet in the Vayots Dzor mountains. A spa town since Soviet times, its 40 natural springs supply hotels, sanatoriums, and bottled water sold throughout Armenia.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Bring a cup and taste each of the five springs at the Gallery of Waters—locals have taken their morning cure here for decades.

⏱ 60 min

Take the cable car to 8,140 feet for panoramic views over the town, Arpa Gorge, and surrounding peaks.

⏱ 60 min

Book a thermal bath at one of the hotel establishments—hydrotherapy, mineral baths at 98–108°F, and massages with local essential oils.

⏱ 60 min

Day 8

Goris

Where gray stone houses whisper of Syunik's resistance and troglodyte villages cling to canyon walls

★★★☆☆
1

Goris stands out radically from other Armenian towns: its houses are built of gray volcanic stone rather than pink tuff, with arched windows and carved wooden balconies. The surrounding rock formations shelter the 'Old Goris' caves, inhabited until the 18th century.

⏱ 60 min
2

Khndzoresk is an entire village carved into volcanic canyon cliffs, inhabited until the 1950s–70s by some 15,000 people. Four churches, three schools, and hundreds of dwellings are hewn into the rock on multiple levels.

⏱ 120 min
3

In the 18th century, Khndzoresk was a stronghold of Armenian resistance in Syunik. The tomb of hero Mkhitar Sparapet, his wife Gohar, and their son Aaron still stands there.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Cross the 525-foot suspension bridge above Khndzoresk Canyon—the vertiginous view of the caves and volcanic formations is among Armenia's most striking.

⏱ 60 min

Descend into the canyon from the new village to explore inhabited caves from the inside—some interiors are surprisingly well preserved.

⏱ 60 min

Lunch in Goris before heading to Khndzoresk—the town is the last supply point before Syunik's more isolated sites.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 40min
Day 9

Goris

Where gray stone houses whisper of Syunik's resistance and troglodyte villages cling to canyon walls

★★★☆☆
1

The Wings of Tatev cable car holds the Guinness record for the world's longest reversible aerial tramway at 3.57 miles. In 12 minutes, the cabins cross 1,050 feet above the Vorotan Gorge, revealing the monastery on its basalt plateau.

⏱ 30 min
2

Founded in the 9th century, Tatev was one of medieval Armenia's great intellectual centers. Its University, active in the 14th–15th centuries, trained generations of philosophers, theologians, and musicians who profoundly shaped Armenian culture.

⏱ 90 min
3

Tatev's Gavazan is a 26-foot stone column mounted on a swivel joint: this medieval seismograph tilted to signal the approach of earthquakes or enemy armies. No other such device is known in the medieval world.

⏱ 20 min
4

The Devil's Bridge (Satani Kamurj) is a natural travertine arch formed by thermal mineral springs over centuries. Its ochre and white hues amid the gorge create a surreal mineral landscape.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take the cable car outbound from Halidzor and descend via the road on foot—45 minutes walking through forest with different canyon views.

⏱ 60 min

Bathe in the natural hot springs near Devil's Bridge—the pleasantly warm mineral waters cap off a day's hiking perfectly.

⏱ 60 min

Explore Tatev's fortified ramparts to understand its defensive dimension—the plateau's isolation also provided strategic protection against invasions.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 240 km · 3h20
Day 10

Sevan

Where the Caucasus's largest lake cradles endemic trout and a monastery from 874

★★☆☆☆
1

Sevanavank ('black monastery') was founded in 874 by Princess Mariam Bagratuni on an island in Lake Sevan. Soviet drainage in the 1930s–60s lowered the lake by 65 feet, transforming the island into a peninsula connected to shore.

⏱ 60 min
2

Noratus cemetery, 7.5 miles from the lake, is the world's largest gathering of khachkars. Several hundred carved cross-stones—some dating from the 9th century—spread across the plain, each unique in its patterns of interlace, angels, and daily life scenes.

⏱ 60 min
3

Lake Sevan is the Caucasus's largest lake: 525 square miles at 6,200 feet altitude between Armenian mountain ranges. Formed 25,000 years ago, it shelters the Ishkhan—Sevan's endemic trout, renowned throughout Armenia.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Lunch at one of the lakeside restaurants with fresh Ishkhan trout—this endemic fish is Sevan's gastronomic essential.

⏱ 60 min

Climb the steps to Sevanavank Peninsula for panoramic views over the lake's blue immensity and surrounding mountains—ideally at sunrise or sunset.

⏱ 60 min

Photograph Noratus khachkars in raking light—the medieval sculptures gain remarkable depth in backlight.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 40min
Day 11

Dilijan

Where pine forests earned this town the nickname 'Armenia's Switzerland'

★★★★☆
1

Sharambeyan Street, restored to its 19th-century appearance, forms the heart of old Dilijan—called 'Armenia's Switzerland.' Its dark tuff houses with carved wooden balconies shelter artisan workshops and galleries.

⏱ 60 min
2

Haghartsin ('eagle's flight') is a 10th–13th-century monastery nestled in a forested bowl of Dilijan National Park. Its 13th-century refectory is the largest preserved monastic hall in Armenia.

⏱ 75 min
3

Goshavank was founded in 1188 by fabulist Mkhitar Gosh to replace Getikvank Monastery, destroyed by earthquake. Master Poghos's khachkar (1291), erected facing the basilica, is considered among Armenia's most finely sculpted.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Don't leave Haghartsin without tasting the hot gata stuffed with apricots and thyme from the bakery at the monastery entrance—an irreplaceable regional specialty.

⏱ 60 min

Pick up a trail map at the national park information center in Dilijan's municipal park before heading out to the monasteries.

⏱ 60 min

Optional: from Goshavank, the 1.5-hour trail climbs to Gosh Lich mountain lake—ideal in late afternoon if time permits.

⏱ 60 min

Dilijan

Where pine forests earned this town the nickname 'Armenia's Switzerland'

★★★★☆
1

Parz Lake ('crystal') is Dilijan National Park's natural jewel. Surrounded by pine and beech forests, its limpid waters reflect the wooded peaks with remarkable clarity.

⏱ 60 min
2

Matosavank and Jukhtakvank monasteries, accessible on foot from central Dilijan, are little-known medieval retreats buried in the woods. Their bareness and centuries-old moss make them moving in their authenticity.

⏱ 60 min

In summer, rent a kayak or rowboat to explore the lake from the water and admire the changing forest reflections.

Day 13

Alaverdi

Where Debed Canyon shelters UNESCO monasteries and Byzantium's most vivid Armenian frescoes

★★★★☆
1

Akhtala is Armenia's most surprising monastery: while Armenian churches are typically austere, its walls explode with color. Byzantine frescoes painted between 1205 and 1216 cover the entire interior in deep blues and golds—artworks among the finest preserved outside the Byzantine Empire's borders.

⏱ 75 min
2

Sanahin—'this one is older than that one'—is a 10th-century monastic complex listed by UNESCO. Its 1063 scriptorium still preserves the niches where illuminated manuscripts were stored. The collection of khachkars dotting the site is among Armenia's richest.

⏱ 75 min
3

Haghpat, founded in 976 by Queen Khosrovanush, is the Debed Canyon's other UNESCO summit. Its austerity is deliberate—monks believed any decoration distracted from prayer. Beauty resides in the perfection of proportions and harmony with the Debed landscape.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Spot in Akhtala the fresco of bearded Persians—legend says it was painted to deter enemy armies from attacking the church by giving them the impression of being watched.

⏱ 60 min

Visit the Mikoyan Brothers Museum next to Sanahin: a MiG-21 is displayed outside in homage to Artyom Mikoyan, co-designer of the famous fighter jets.

⏱ 60 min

Hike the World Heritage Trail between Sanahin and Haghpat (HikeArmenia app)—3–4 hours walking through forests and villages of Debed Canyon.

⏱ 60 min

Day 14

Gyumri

Where black volcanic tuff houses preserve 19th-century elegance and a resilient cultural spirit

★★★☆☆
1

Gyumri's historic Kumayri district is one of the Caucasus's most remarkable architectural heritages. Hundreds of 19th-century houses in black volcanic tuff, with arched windows and wrought-iron balconies, compose a cohesive ensemble unique in Armenia.

⏱ 90 min
2

Amenaprkich Church (1858–1872) is Gyumri's architectural symbol. Its neo-Gothic facade in dark tuff, ogival arches, and slender silhouette make it Armenia's most singular religious edifice—severely damaged by the 1988 earthquake, restored in the 2000s.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Dzitoghtsyan Museum, housed in a beautiful 19th-century mansion, presents the daily life, costumes, and crafts of Gyumri's bourgeoisie, plus a section documenting the 1988 earthquake that killed 25,000.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Push open the doors to Kumayri's flowering interior courtyards—residents will often welcome you hospitably to share Armenian coffee.

⏱ 60 min

Observe the contrast between meticulously restored black tuff houses and Soviet concrete buildings—Gyumri's architectural history in a single glance.

⏱ 60 min

Dine in one of the lively downtown restaurants—Gyumri is known for its cultural life and art scene more vibrant than its streets might suggest.

⏱ 60 min

Day 15

Yerevan

Where pink volcanic tuff meets musical fountains and world-class museums

★★★★★
1

Amberd Fortress ('fortress in the clouds') perches at 7,500 feet on the slopes of Mount Aragats, Armenia's highest peak. Built in the 7th–11th centuries by the Pahlavid princes, this nearly impregnable citadel protected trade routes crossing the massif.

⏱ 90 min
2

The church adjoining the fortress, from the 11th century, is one of the best-preserved from that period in Armenia. On clear days, you can see both Mount Ararat to the south and Aragats's summit (13,419 feet) above.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Explore Amberd's towers and ramparts—the site's defensive logic becomes evident from the battlements: two deep gorges protect three sides of the fortress.

⏱ 60 min

Photograph the alpine landscape around Amberd according to season—wildflowers in July, snow possible from October.

⏱ 60 min

On the return drive to Yerevan (43 miles, 1.5 hours), stop in Byurakan village to observe the Soviet Astronomical Observatory, open for visits.

⏱ 60 min

Farewell evening in Yerevan: the musical fountains at Republic Square light up the night from 9 PM. Final dinner on a terrace in the Kentron district.
