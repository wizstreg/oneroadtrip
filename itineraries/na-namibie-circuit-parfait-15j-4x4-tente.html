Windhoek

★★★☆☆
1

The Christuskirche stands with its neo-Gothic silhouette at the heart of Windhoek since 1910. This Lutheran church with golden stones remains the most photographed landmark in the Namibian capital.

⏱ 20 min
2

The Independence Memorial Museum dominates the city with its modern architecture. Climb to the top for a free panoramic view over Windhoek and the surrounding hills.

⏱ 45 min
3

Stroll through the aisles of the Namibia Craft Centre housed in the old breweries. Forty artisans display wood carvings, copper jewelry, and traditional textiles.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

4x4 vehicle rental with rooftop tent. Windhoek agencies offer fully equipped double-cab vehicles. Allow 2 hours for the complete briefing: tent assembly, compressor use, tire pressure adjustment, procedure in case of getting stuck. Check tire condition, hi-lift jack, jerry cans, and spare wheel.

⏱ 120 min

Shopping provisions. Downtown supermarkets allow you to stock up for several days. Buy enough to be self-sufficient: water (5L per person per day), canned goods, firewood, cooler. Shopping centers offer the widest selection.

⏱ 60 min

Camping near airport if late arrival. The reserve 25 km away offers first contact with Namibian wildlife: oryx, giraffes, zebras. Shaded sites with facilities, ideal for a recovery night after the flight.

⏱ 60 min

Aus

★★★☆☆
1

The wild horses of Garub graze near a water point 20 km from Aus. These feral horses, descendants of German cavalry mounts from World War I, have adapted to the desert for over a century.

⏱ 45 min
2

Aus itself is barely more than a handful of houses and a gas station. But this hamlet marks a strategic crossroads: south towards Lüderitz, west towards Sossusvlei, north towards the Naukluft Mountains.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Long drive through desert plains. The C13 and C27 cross lunar landscapes where only oryx and ostriches survive. Long straight stretches, mirages on the horizon. Refuel in Aus.

⏱ 270 min

Desert camping at Aus. Klein-Aus Vista or nearby campgrounds offer total immersion in the silence of the Namib. Night falls, stars blanket the sky. Only the calls of jackals break the desert silence.

⏱ 120 min

Sesriem

★★★★☆
1

Sesriem Canyon cuts a narrow gorge 30 meters deep into sedimentary rock. Walk through this natural corridor carved by the Tsauchab River over millennia. The name 'Sesriem' (six thongs) comes from the rope length needed to draw water from the bottom.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Route to Sesriem via the C19. The landscape transforms: flat plains give way to undulating dunes. The red sand becomes more intense. Arrival at Sesriem campground for check-in and setup.

⏱ 105 min

Afternoon visit to Sesriem Canyon. After the heat of the road, this shaded gorge offers welcome respite. Explore the narrow passages, photograph the layers of rock, spot rock hyraxes on the walls.

⏱ 90 min

Preparation for Sossusvlei dawn departure. Park gates open at sunrise (around 6am). Prepare breakfast, fill water bottles, charge cameras. Go to bed early: tomorrow's alarm rings at 5am.

⏱ 60 min

Sesriem

★★★★☆
1

Sossusvlei: the iconic white clay pan surrounded by dunes over 300 meters high. Dune 45, the most famous, offers a relatively accessible climb (30-45 min ascent). From the summit, the view sweeps over an ocean of orange dunes to the horizon.

⏱ 180 min
2

Deadvlei: the dead vlei. An ancient clay pan where skeletal camelthorn trees have stood for 900 years, killed by drought. The contrast between white clay, black trees, orange dunes, and blue sky creates a surreal landscape that photographers dream of.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Dawn departure for Sossusvlei. Leave camp at 5h30 am to be at the gate at opening. The 65 km of tar road passes Dune 45 (first stop), then reaches the 2x4 parking. Shuttle or 4x4 in deep sand for the last 5 km to Sossusvlei/Deadvlei.

⏱ 75 min

Return to camp at midday. The sun beats down, temperatures soar. Back to Sesriem for lunch in shade and siesta. The campsite pool becomes precious refuge during hottest hours.

⏱ 90 min

Optional afternoon: Elim Dune or Sesriem Canyon. Elim Dune (5 km from camp) offers a sunset climb with 360° views. Alternative: return to Sesriem Canyon in soft afternoon light for photography.

⏱ 120 min

Arrival in Swakopmund. This seaside town with Germanic architecture surprises: half-timbered houses, bakeries, beer gardens facing the Atlantic. Camp setup then stroll on the jetty. Mist rolls in, temperature drops. After desert heat, oceanic freshness feels like a gift.

Activities

Morning excursion to Cape Cross. The tar road north follows the Skeleton Coast. Seal colony visit, then possible stop at saltpans on the return. Count half a day round trip from Swakopmund.

⏱ 240 min

Free afternoon in Swakopmund. Options: sandboarding on coastal dunes, kayaking with seals at Walvis Bay, or simply stroll the promenade and enjoy a German pastry. Supermarket shopping for next leg.

1

Spitzkoppe rises like a granite fang 1,784 meters into the Namib sky. This 'Matterhorn of Namibia' displays fantastic rock formations sculpted by wind and time. Walk among rounded boulders, discover San rock paintings in rock shelters.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Route to Spitzkoppe via Henties Bay. The C34 follows the coast north before turning inland on the D1918. The massive granite peaks appear on the horizon, increasingly imposing. Arrival at community campsite for pitch selection among boulders.

Afternoon exploration. Follow marked trails to the rock arch, search for San paintings in shelters, climb onto boulders for views. Each rock formation tells millions of years of geology.

⏱ 150 min

Starry night at Spitzkoppe. This site is renowned for having one of the purest skies in the world. Once night falls and city lights are hundreds of kilometers away, the Milky Way spreads across the sky in all its glory. Photographers capture star trails above granite peaks.

Twyfelfontein

★★★★★
1

Twyfelfontein holds 2,500 rock engravings and paintings created by San hunter-gatherers 6,000 years ago. This UNESCO site displays lions, elephants, rhinos, giraffes engraved on sandstone. Guided visit mandatory to protect this exceptional heritage.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Organ Pipes are spectacular vertical basalt columns formed by volcanic cooling 120 million years ago. These geometric formations stretch over 100 meters, creating a natural pipe organ.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Burnt Mountain displays stunning colors: ochre, purple, rust, black. Volcanic rocks oxidized over time create this chromatic palette. Morning light intensifies the hues. Short stop but spectacular visual impact.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Route through Damaraland. The C39 and D2612 cross wild landscapes: red plains, scattered mountains, dry riverbeds where desert elephants sometimes wander. Long gravel section, allow time for landscapes and potential wildlife.

⏱ 210 min

Night vigil at Okaukuejo waterhole. After dinner, settle behind the wall overlooking the floodlit waterhole. Elephants arrive in silence, rhinos grunt as they drink, sometimes lions appear. Staying up until midnight is worth it – the spectacle is hypnotic.

Halali

★★★☆☆

Activities

Dawn safari from Okaukuejo. Leave at sunrise when the park gate opens. Animals are most active in morning cool. Drive slowly, scan waterholes, observe behaviors. Lions return from night hunting, elephants quench their thirst, giraffes browse acacias.

⏱ 180 min

Route to Halali camp. The 70 km crossing Etosha is pure safari. Stop at each waterhole: Ozonjuitji m'Bari, Okondeka, Salvadora. Each has its regulars. Elephants here, zebras there, sometimes a rhino in the distance.

⏱ 150 min

Arrival and installation at Halali. This camp nestled in mopane forest offers a shaded, wilder atmosphere than Okaukuejo. Its waterhole, visible from the restaurant terrace, attracts leopards at dusk – rare privilege.

⏱ 60 min

Afternoon safari loop. From Halali, explore southern tracks towards the pan. The white expanse of Etosha Pan appears in the distance – dry most of the year, transformed into a shallow lake after rains. Watch for lions in the shade of acacias.

Route to Namutoni. This third camp marks Etosha's eastern end. The landscape changes: grasslands become denser, vegetation greener. Fisher's Pan and Klein Namutoni waterholes are particularly productive for bird watching.

Installation at Namutoni. This elegant fort-camp offers colonial charm atmosphere. Its waterhole just outside the walls attracts elephants, giraffes, zebras. Pool and lawn under palm trees complete the colonial Africa setting.

⏱ 60 min

Afternoon safari towards the pan. Take the eastern track to viewpoints over Etosha Pan's immense white expanse. In dry season, mirages shimmer on the horizon. After rains, thousands of flamingos gather – surreal pink spectacle.

Waterberg

★★★☆☆
1

The Waterberg Plateau rises like a fortress of red sandstone 200 meters above surrounding plains. This massif shelters Namibia's last refuges for black rhinos and Cape vultures. The microclimate created by cliff springs feeds lush vegetation contrasting with dry surroundings.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Last dawn safari at Etosha. Make the most of final hours in the park. One last loop around Namutoni waterholes before reluctantly taking the road to Von Lindequist Gate. Exit the park around mid-morning.

⏱ 120 min

Route to Waterberg. Exit Etosha via Von Lindequist Gate, then the B1 south passes through Otjiwarongo. Stop for fuel and lunch. The C22 climbs towards Waterberg, landscape changing from flat bush to mountainous terrain.

⏱ 210 min

Afternoon at Waterberg camp. Installation at the foot of cliffs at park camp. Short guided walk to discover local vegetation and history of the 1904 Herero-German battle. Or simply rest by the pool contemplating red cliffs.

⏱ 120 min
