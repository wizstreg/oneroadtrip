Avoid July-August if possible: violent afternoon thunderstorms in the Dolomites, heavy tourist crowds. Pack warm clothing for high-mountain sections even in summer.

The Passo dello Stelvio is closed from October through late May depending on snow conditions: plan this itinerary between June and September. Lugano

★★★☆☆
1

Lugano is neither truly Italian nor truly Swiss—that's its charm. Parco Ciani follows Lake Lugano for a kilometer of shaded promenade between palm trees and rhododendrons, with Monte San Salvatore as backdrop. Views across to the Italian Alps. Free at all hours; visit early morning or late afternoon for the best light.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Cathedral of San Lorenzo dominates Lugano from Via Cattedrale: Renaissance façade from 1517 with its 15 medallions of Prophets and Sibyls, interior enriched with 15th-century frescoes and Baroque decoration. The church is Romanesque in origin, enlarged in the 13th-14th centuries. From the terrace opposite, views over the lake. Self-guided visit.

⏱ 30 min
3

Monte San Salvatore towers over Lugano like Sugarloaf over Rio: at 909 m, 360° views of the lake and the Italian Alps. The funicular from Lugano-Paradiso covers 600 m of elevation in 12 minutes. Open March through November, every 30 minutes.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Walk Via Nassa and its historic arcades to Piazza della Riforma: Lugano's commercial and pedestrian heart, lively terraces facing the lake. An hour of wandering among shops, cafés, and Renaissance façades.

⏱ 60 min

Weather permitting, boats operated by the Lake Lugano Navigation Company serve Gandria and Morcote from the central pier in under 30 minutes. Gandria, a village clinging to the mountainside with colorful lanes, is the most atmospheric. Schedules at lakelugano.ch.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.4Grand Hotel Villa Castagnola
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 40min

Como

★★★★☆
1

Gothic and Baroque on the same façade: Como Cathedral, begun in 1396 and completed in the 18th century, is one of the few buildings in Italy to pull off this marriage. The two statues flanking the main portal depict Pliny the Elder and Pliny the Younger—the two great Latin scholars born on the shores of this lake. Guided tour recommended for the Flemish tapestries in the nave.

⏱ 45 min
2

Como has been 'la città della seta' since the 15th century—the world capital of luxury silk. The Silk Museum exhibits period looms and the complete production chain: the great fashion houses have their finest scarves woven here. Two informative hours, 20-minute walk from the cathedral.

⏱ 90 min
3

Alessandro Volta was born in Como in 1745—and the city remembers him with the Tempio Voltiano on the lakeshore, dedicated to the inventor of the battery. Just 400 m away, the Casa del Fascio (1936) is a masterpiece of Rationalist architecture by Giuseppe Terragni, studied in architecture schools worldwide. Free entry to the gardens of neighboring Villa Olmo.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk along the shore of Lake Como westward from the historic center to Villa Olmo: 1.5 km waterfront promenade. The villa's gardens (free entry) open directly onto the lake. Check whether the villa itself is open before you go—restoration work occasionally affects access.

⏱ 60 min

In five minutes by funicular from lower Como, the village of Brunate opens onto a plunging view of the lake at 715 m: different light, mountain air, forest walks. Last departure from Brunate around 10:30 PM in summer.

⏱ 120 min
$$$9.8Lake Como Dream Tree House
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 1h

Varenna

★★★★★
1

Varenna may be the most beautiful village on Lake Como—and the Passeggiata degli Innamorati proves it: this walkway anchored into the rock, just inches above the water, with its famous red railing, connects the landing pier to the village center. The lake is right beneath you. On weekdays off-season, you'll have it almost to yourself.

⏱ 30 min
2

Traditionally commissioned by the Lombard Queen Theodelinda, the Castello di Vezio towers over Varenna from 375 m—20-minute walk from the village, 360° lake views. Below, Villa Monastero was founded in the 12th century as a women's monastery: today a house-museum with 14 furnished rooms and botanical gardens on the lake.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Take the ferry from Varenna's pier to Bellagio: 15-minute crossing over the intersection of the lake's three branches, the most beautiful panorama of the Lario. Return possible by the same ferry or via Menaggio on the opposite shore.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.4Hotel Du Lac & SPA
🚗Drive to next stop: 65 km · 1h35

Tirano

1

In Tirano, the bright red Bernina Express runs through the alleys like a tram before leaving Italy for Switzerland: an improbable spectacle of a UNESCO locomotive grazing the façades, triggering crossing barriers, and disappearing into the Alps. As counterpoint, the Sanctuary of Madonna di Tirano (1505) rises in Renaissance silhouette just behind the tracks. Free entry.

⏱ 30 min
2

At 429 m elevation, Tirano marks the passage from alpine to Lombard: Palazzo Salis and its wooded gardens host exhibitions and cultural events in the historic center. Via Bernina and its lanes allow a 30-40 minute circuit—first Lombard touch before the climb to Bormio.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

Have an aperitivo on a terrace before heading to Bormio: the red wines of Valtellina—Sforzato di Valtellina DOCG (passito Nebbiolo), Sassella, Grumello—are served by the glass in several bars in the historic center. Treat yourself to buckwheat pizzoccheri as an option.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.2B & B Il sorriso
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 1h

Bormio

★★★★☆
1

Piazza del Kuerc (14th century) with its Torre della Bajona—whose bell sounded the alarm for the entire County during invasions—forms Bormio's dense historic center. Spot the dragon-shaped corbels beneath the Kuerc's gutter. Off-season (June or September), Via Roma is quiet and its shops accessible.

⏱ 60 min
2

At Bormio, Cassiodorus, secretary to King Theodoric, recommended these waters as early as the 6th century. The Bagni Vecchi occupy natural caves a few kilometers from the center with water between 38 and 43°C flowing from the Ortles massif. Panoramic views over the valley from the outdoor pool. Reserve in high season.

⏱ 120 min
3

Bormio deserves the detour beyond skiing: in Palazzo de Simoni (17th century, built on the foundations of a medieval castle), the Civic Museum of Bormio traces the history of the 'Magnifica Terra'. On the carved wooden portal, look for the De Simoni coat of arms: a monkey holding a branch and an eagle. Open in summer.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk the historic center from Piazza Kuerc northward: Via Roma, Via Alberti, the Quadrilatero degli Alberti and its medieval portals. A peaceful hour in preserved lanes, with the first slopes of the Ortles visible as soon as you look up.

⏱ 60 min

If the Bagni Vecchi strain the budget (paid entry), the free thermal springs of Bormio offer a no-cost option: follow the Stelvio road 2 km from the center, park at the bridge over the Frodolfo, walk upriver 200 m. Water shoes recommended.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.7La Genzianella Bormio
🚗Drive to next stop: 22 km · 45min

Passo Dello Stelvio

1

At 2,758 m, Passo dello Stelvio is the highest paved pass in the Italian Alps: the air bites even in July, and the 48 hairpins of the Trafoi side stack up below like an engineering lesson. The Ortles massif (3,905 m) dominates the pass from the north and glaciers cover the walls. The pass is closed October through May depending on snow.

⏱ 30 min
2

Passo dello Stelvio is the second-highest paved pass in the entire Alps, after the Iseran (2,764 m), and it surpasses it in cycling notoriety: regularly the 'Cima Coppi' of the Giro d'Italia. From the pass, you can also descend into Switzerland toward Val Müstair via the Umbrail Pass—the linguistic border between Romansh and German runs through here.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

From the summit of Passo dello Stelvio, take the trail toward the ridge to the north to escape the bustle of the terraces: 15 minutes on foot is enough to find silence and a view of the Ortles-Cevedale massif. Even in August, bring a windbreaker.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.5Hotel Vedig
🚗Drive to next stop: 75 km · 1h50

Merano

★★★★☆
1

You expect a classic botanical garden—and you find yourself in a natural amphitheater of 12 hectares. The Gardens of Trauttmansdorff Castle, voted 'Most Beautiful Garden in Italy' in 2005, display 80 garden landscapes across 100 m of elevation, from lotus ponds to forest parks to sun gardens. The integrated Touriseum traces 250 years of alpine tourism. Closed in winter.

⏱ 120 min
2

The scent of cypress and Mediterranean maquis 200 m above Merano: the Tappeiner Promenade winds along the sunny slope with views over the Passirio valley. 3.8 km accessible to all, open year-round. The Powder Tower (14th century) along the route is worth a stop for the panorama.

⏱ 75 min
3

Merano is the town where Empress Elisabeth of Austria came to recover: the 'Sissi Path' connects the Kurhaus to the Trauttmansdorff gardens via the sites where she resided. This warm, dry microclimate of the Passirio valley was renowned throughout Europe for pulmonary ailments. The town's Belle Époque legacy is still legible in the façades of the Summer Promenade.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Follow the Passirio in the lower town via the Summer and Winter Promenades, the two opposing riverbank walks that frame the historic center between old cafés and Belle Époque hotels. The Ponte delle Poste leads to the Cathedral of St. Nicholas and Piazza del Duomo for an aperitivo on a terrace.

⏱ 60 min

In 1874, the Kurhaus of Merano opened at the heart of the spa town: this Belle Époque rotunda with high ceilings remains Merano's cultural center, with concerts, exhibitions, and lively terraces on the Passirio all summer. Program at meranofestival.it; free entry to common areas.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.8Villa Eden a member of Leading Hotels of the World
🚗Drive to next stop: 4 km · 15min

Tirolo

1

Built in the 11th century, Tirol Castle gave its name to the entire Tyrol region: from its ramparts, the view embraces Merano and the Adige valley in a panorama that the Counts of Tyrol dominated in the Middle Ages. The interior houses a museum of South Tyrolean cultural history and a well-preserved Romanesque chapel. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 60 min
2

View over Merano from the ramparts of Tirol Castle: the spa town spreads across the Passirio valley, framed by the first foothills of the Alps, with vineyards on the south-facing slope in the foreground. The village of Tirolo around the castle is worth a walking tour among cobbled streets and terraced gardens.

⏱ 25 min

Activities

Walk down from Tirol Castle to Merano via the panoramic trail through terraced vineyards and orchards: 45-minute descent, constant views over the valley. Free trail access; the castle is paid admission.

⏱ 50 min
$$$9.8Hotel Sonnbichl
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Bolzano

★★★★☆
1

The South Tyrol Museum of Archaeology houses Ötzi, the Iceman discovered in 1991 at 3,210 m—killed by an arrow around 3300 BC at approximately age 46, he is one of the most studied human beings in the world. The museum occupies a 1912 building (former bank) right in the pedestrian heart. Closed Mondays, allow 1 hour.

⏱ 90 min
2

Piazza Walther is a strange homage to a medieval poet in a bilingual city: Walther von der Vogelweide (c. 1170-1230), statue erected in 1889, watches over the square. The adjacent Gothic cathedral, completed in 1519, is distinguished by its green, white, and yellow diamond-patterned roof—bombed in 1943, meticulously restored. Free visit.

⏱ 45 min
3

An ordinary medieval castle would have saints and martyrs. Runkelstein Castle (13th century), perched on its rocky spur above the Talvera, is covered with entirely secular frescoes—jousts, hunts, courtly love scenes, not a single martyr. Free shuttle from Piazza Walther every 30 minutes or so. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Walk Via dei Portici and its medieval arcades to Piazza delle Erbe, where the daily market animates the stalls around the Neptune fountain. Best before 10 AM. A good hour of wandering among cafés, Speck stands, and South Tyrolean cheeses.

⏱ 60 min

If you extend your evening, the osterie on Via Argentieri serve Lagrein, a red grape unique to South Tyrol—thick, slightly bitter, perfect with Speck. The city center's enotèche also offer Schiava and Gewürztraminer by the glass, no reservation.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.1Hotel Magdalener Hof
🚗Drive to next stop: 36 km · 45min

Ortisei

★★★★☆
1

In Ortisei, the art of wood carving—'intaglio' in Ladin—has been practiced continuously since the 17th century: the School of Wood Carving perpetuates the transmission of a craft born here with the Trebinger and Vinazer families. About 85 to 90% of Ortisei residents speak Ladin as their first language—a Romance alpine language distinct from Italian and German.

⏱ 45 min
2

The parish church of Sant'Ulrich (15th century) houses some of the finest works of the local carving tradition: crucifixes, statues of saints, and a pulpit in wood worked by valley artisans. Opposite, the Museum of Ortisei traces the history of Val Gardena wood carving since the 17th century.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stroll through Ortisei's pedestrian center among colorful Tyrolean houses and craft workshops: wood carving galleries display nativity scenes, statues, and furniture made by hand using four-century-old techniques. Stop for Schlutzkrapfen (spinach and ricotta ravioli) at one of the cafés.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.7Hotel Montchalet
🚗Drive to next stop: 2 km · 20min

Seceda

1

At 2,519 m, the air is crisp and the Odle (Geislerspitzen) cut the sky like a giant saw: the Seceda plateau is one of the most photographed ridges of the Dolomites. Access by cable car from Ortisei. In summer, marked hiking trails from the upper station.

⏱ 120 min
2

It's from Seceda that the geography of the Dolomites becomes readable at a single glance: the Marmolada, the Sassolungo, and Cortina's Tofane are visible in clear weather. Orientation panels in Ladin, Italian, and German identify each peak from the panoramic terrace.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk from the Seceda upper station to Col Raiser (2,107 m) along the ridges: 2 km of well-marked panoramic trail, one-hour round trip. From the pass, views over Val Gardena below and the Sella massif to the south. Return by cable car to Ortisei.

⏱ 90 min
$$9.7Garni Karin
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 1h10

Canazei

★★★★☆
1

The region's finest trail: the Viel del Pan from the Col dei Rossi station (2,382 m) runs along a balcony beneath the walls of the Marmolada for 2 to 3 hours with continuous glacier views, no technical difficulty. One of the great classics of the Dolomites, accessible to all hikers.

⏱ 180 min
2

The only glaciated summit of the Dolomites, the Marmolada (3,343 m) reads on the horizon from Canazei: the road to Lago di Fedaia 8 km away leads to the foot of the north face and the cable car departure—covered separately tomorrow. A quick orientation from the center suffices before heading up.

⏱ 15 min
3

Canazei in summer has this rare quality: the Dolomites are in every angle of view from the terrace of the smallest café, the village keeps its human scale and Ladin animation. In summer, scent of larch resin and terraces open from 8 AM. Artisan markets and evening events in July-August; check the local program.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Leave early from Canazei for the Sella Ronda loop by car: four passes (Pordoi, Campolongo, Gardena, Sella) between 1,875 and 2,244 m, Dolomite panoramas at each descent. Allow 3 to 4 hours with stops. In July-August, traffic can be heavy at peak hours.

⏱ 180 min

If you have two extra days, the Alta Via 2 of the Dolomites passes through Canazei between Bressanone and Feltre: the section around the Pordoi is accessible without advanced technical equipment. Book refuges online from July; the best places go in a few hours.

⏱ 360 min
$$$9.6Majon Baticher
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 15min

Lago Di Fedaia

★★★★☆
1

Lago di Fedaia deserves the stop beyond a simple stage: at 2,054 m, the reservoir at the foot of the Marmolada's north face presents an intense blue-gray fed by glacial meltwater. From the dam, the gaze rises to Punta Penia (3,343 m), roof of the Dolomites, and the cable car ascends the glacier.

⏱ 45 min
2

From the Fedaia dam, comparative photographic markers document the retreat of the Marmolada glacier year after year. In July 2022, a serac collapse claimed the lives of several climbers from this face: the site has become a place of climate memory as much as a panorama.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Take the cable car from Malga Ciapela (Veneto side) to Punta Rocca (3,265 m) for a 360° view over the Dolomites. The glacier beneath the terrace is visibly shrinking. Check schedules at marmolada.com—access impossible in bad weather.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9Hotel Majon Miramonti
🚗Drive to next stop: 57 km · 1h25

Cortina Dampezzo

★★★☆☆
1

Cortina is fashionable, but not only that: the Ciasa de ra Regoles houses an ethnographic collection of Ampezzo Ladin culture and canvases by Giorgio de Chirico and Filippo de Pisis. The Ampezzans call themselves neither Italians nor Tyroleans—they are Ladins, an alpine people with their own language for centuries. Paid entry, verify hours locally.

⏱ 60 min
2

Corso Italia at dawn, before the shops open, has something strange and beautiful: the Liberty façades, the white bell tower of Santi Filippo e Giacomo in raking light, and the Tofane in the background beginning to blush pink. The 'Queen of the Dolomites' deserves to be seen before her audience.

⏱ 40 min
3

Cortina d'Ampezzo hosted the Winter Olympics in 1956—the legendary Tofane downhill—then again in 2026 with Milan. The Olympia delle Tofane course, one of the legendary women's World Cup downhills, is accessible to hikers in summer. The Palazzo del Ghiaccio (Olympic ice rink) remains open off-season.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take the Tofane lifts from lower Cortina to reach around 3,000 m: in clear weather, 360° views over the Dolomites. Check summer schedules in advance—reduced hours off ski season. Windbreaker even in August.

⏱ 120 min

Late afternoon, drive to Passo Falzarego (2,105 m) 10 km from Cortina: the Cinque Torri stand out from the Lagazuoi cable car viewpoint, and the Great War trenches accessible from the upper station restore the scale of the 1915-1918 mountain battles. Return before sunset.

⏱ 120 min
$$$9.4Grand Hotel Savoia Cortina d'Ampezzo, A Radisson Collection Hotel
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 25min

Lago Di Sorapis

1

Lago di Sorapis is a reward, not a stroll: 2 hours hiking from Passo Tre Croci via trail 215, then a final view of a cirque of orange walls and turquoise water colored by suspended glacial particles. Intermediate level, about 4 hours round trip.

⏱ 240 min
2

Even without doing the Sorapis hike, Passo Tre Croci (about 1,800 m) is worth the stop: view of the Cristallo to the northeast and the first Venetian Dolomites to the south. Starting point for several marked trails. The parking area is free but limited in high season.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Leave for Lago di Sorapis before 7:30 AM: ideal conditions and parking still available at Passo Tre Croci. Trail 215 is well marked but has some exposed passages with cables. Hiking boots and water (1.5 L minimum) required.

⏱ 240 min
$$$9.2Hotel Lajadira & Spa
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 1h10

Erto E Casso

★★★☆☆
1

The Vajont dam (265 m) is still standing—it was the dam that survived, not the mountain. On the night of October 9, 1963, a landslide of approximately 270 million m³ from Monte Toc created a wave that overtopped the dam and obliterated Longarone below, killing 1,917 people. The dam and the scar in the mountain are now a visitable memorial from Erto e Casso.

⏱ 60 min
2

From the road through Erto e Casso, the landslide scar—the bare white rock of Monte Toc—remains visible to the naked eye sixty years later. Guided tours of the Vajont site explain the circumstances of the disaster, documented without embellishment. It is established that the catastrophe could have been avoided.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Descend to the foot of the Vajont dam to measure the 265 m height from downstream: the perspective is dizzying. Information on the signage documents the disaster and political responsibilities. Free access from Erto e Casso via the canyon road.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.1Parc Hotel San Pietro
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 1h10

Spilimbergo

★★★☆☆
1

In Spilimbergo, you recognize a school's reputation by a simple sign: the corridors of the Scuola Mosaicisti del Friuli, founded in 1922, are lined with student works to the ceiling. Its alumni have laid mosaics in palaces, museums, and metro systems in dozens of countries. Free access; guided tours by reservation.

⏱ 60 min
2

In the Cathedral of Santa Maria Maggiore (built from 1284), the crypt mosaics created by the School in the 1960s dialogue with medieval frescoes. The cathedral façade is unique in Friuli for its seven stacked rose windows. Climbing toward the Castello, the arcades of Corso Roma and the Borgo Vecchio reconstruct the image of a 15th-century Friulian small town.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Reserve a tour of the Scuola Mosaicisti del Friuli workshops to see students working on real commissions: cartoons projected on light tables, stone and glass andamenti laid one by one. Duration 45 min to 1 hour. Outside guided tours, the exhibition corridor is accessible during regular hours.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.5La Casa del Gallo
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 45min

Venzone

★★★★☆
1

Venzone was razed by the 1976 earthquake—and rebuilt stone by stone, exactly as before, using the anastylosis technique: each block catalogued, numbered, replaced identically. It's the strangest and most beautiful village in Friuli: both authentically medieval and entirely modern. Elected 'Borgo dei Borghi' in 2017.

⏱ 40 min
2

The Venzonese discovered in 1647, while moving a sarcophagus, that their dead had become mummies. Forty bodies were naturally preserved thanks to a particular mold in the cemetery soil. The Crypt of San Michele, facing the Cathedral of Sant'Andrea (1308), now houses several of these mummies with paid access. 20-minute visit.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk the circuit of Venzone's ramparts: 1,060 m of wall 8 m high, flanked by 15 towers, encircling the entire medieval village. The exterior walk takes 30 minutes and gives an aerial view of the reconstructed roofs and the Julian Alps to the north. Free access through the main gates.

⏱ 35 min
$$$10LA MUELE Affittacamere
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 35min

Udine

★★★★☆
1

Udine's Piazza della Libertà is often compared to Venice's Piazza San Marco: the Loggia del Lionello (Venetian Gothic, 15th century), the Clock Tower with its two Moors, the column with the Lion of St. Mark (1539)—everything recalls four centuries of Venetian rule. The square was successively called 'piazza del vino' and 'Contarena', before taking its current name in 1945.

⏱ 30 min
2

For the earliest Tiepolos, you must climb to the castle: the Castle of Udine, perched on the only hill in the Friulian plain, houses the civic museums with Carpaccios and Tiepolos. It was here that Giambattista Tiepolo created his first major works in the 1720s, before becoming the grand master of European Rococo.

⏱ 90 min
3

After nightfall, Udine comes alive around Piazza Matteotti and its osterie serving Friulano, Ribolla Gialla, and Ramato on wooden counters. Friuli white wine is among Italy's finest—producers from Collio and Colli Orientali frequently exhibit in town. Cena around 8 PM: this is where the alpine rhythm of the journey ends.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Climb to Udine Castle from Piazza della Libertà through the Bollani Gate (1556, designed in collaboration with Palladio): 5-minute walk on cobblestones. From the castle's north terrace, view of the Julian and Carnic Alps—a line of ridges less than 100 km to the north.

Friuli, to close, is the pleasant surprise of the journey: less touristy than Tuscany, more rooted in Central Europe than Lombardy, open to Slovenia beyond the border. Udine is a town of real people, without staging—the best counterpoint to conclude an alpine arc that has crossed nine regions in 14 days.
