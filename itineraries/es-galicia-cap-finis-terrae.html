Galicia Road Trip: Santiago de Compostela and the Coast of Death

📅 7 days📍 5 stops🚗 308 km⏱ 5h36

Santiago De Compostela → A Coruna → Muxia → Pontevedra → Vigo

Santiago de Compostela, terminus of the pilgrim's way. A Coruña facing the Atlantic. Muxía and its legends. The rías of Pontevedra and Vigo. Galicia—the edge of Spain, the edge of the world.

✨ Highlights
  • Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela and the Romanesque Pórtico de la Gloria
  • Tower of Hercules—the world's only working Roman lighthouse (UNESCO)
  • Sanctuary of Nosa Señora da Barca and the legendary stones of Muxía
  • Cíes Islands—natural paradise in the Atlantic Islands National Park
📆 Best period

May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 4 and 8 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather, mild temperatures, blooming landscapes, ideal for sightseeing, moderate crowds, excellent value.

Fall (September-October): Comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, harvest season, perfect beach weather, cultural activities, good prices.

Summer (July-August): Hottest weather, peak beach season, highest prices, very crowded, festival season, intense heat inland.

Winter (November-March): Mild temperatures south, cooler north, fewer crowds, lowest prices, cultural attractions, ski season Pyrenees.

🌍 What to discover in ES?

Passionate culture with flamenco, incredible cuisine, and diverse regions from Catalonia to Andalusia. Experience Moorish architecture, world-class museums, and vibrant festivals. The country offers beautiful beaches, historic cities, and excellent transportation. Spain provides cultural richness with culinary excellence and artistic heritage in accessible European destination.

📋 Important notes

Iberian Land's End: Atlantic cities, coastal shrines, and ancient oak forests at the edge of Europe.

Day 1

Santiago De Compostela

Where pilgrims' paths end at the tomb of Saint James

★★★★☆
📍 Cathédrale de Saint-Jacques-de-Compostelle📍 Plaza del Obradoiro Santiago📍 Pórtico de la Gloria Santiago📍 Monastère San Martín Pinario Santiago📍 Musée de la Cathédrale Santiago📍 Casco Antiguo Santiago de Compostela
1

The cathedral shelters the tomb of the Apostle James. The Pórtico de la Gloria, Master Mateo's Romanesque masterpiece, displays 200 intricately carved figures frozen in stone.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Botafumeiro—a 117-pound thurible suspended 65 feet overhead—swings in dramatic arcs during solemn Friday evening masses at 7:30 PM.

⏱ 60 min
3

Climb to the cathedral rooftops for a close encounter with the Torre de la Carraca and sweeping views across the old town's slate-gray skyline.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Plaza del Obradoiro brings together four monumental façades: the baroque cathedral, the Pazo de Raxoi, the Hostal dos Reis Católicos, and the San Xerome college.

⏱ 45 min
5

The San Martín Pinario monastery—Spain's second-largest after the Escorial—showcases a baroque vault with trompe-l'œil coffering and an 18th-century altarpiece dripping with gold leaf.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Follow the bronze scallop shells embedded in the cobblestones from Rúa de Franco straight to the cathedral steps.

⏱ 45 min

The Mercado de Abastos sells Galician cheeses, octopus, percebes, and cured meats under the vaulted ceiling of this 1941 market hall.

⏱ 60 min

Rúa Raíña and Rúa de Franco pack in tapas bars and traditional taverns. Order pulpo a feira and Galician empanada washed down with cold beer.

⏱ 90 min

Day 2

A Coruna

Atlantic city crowned by a Roman lighthouse and glittering glass galleries

★★★★★
📍 Tour d'Hercule A Coruña📍 Paseo Marítimo A Coruña📍 Aquarium Finisterrae A Coruña📍 Domus A Coruña📍 Avenida de la Marina A Coruña📍 Playa de Riazor A Coruña📍 Plaza de María Pita A Coruña
1

The Tower of Hercules—the world's only Roman lighthouse still in operation—rises 180 feet above the Atlantic. Its 112-foot Roman masonry core dates to the 1st century AD.

⏱ 90 min
2

Climb the 234 steps to the summit. The UNESCO site features a sculpture park and ancient petroglyphs carved into the Monte dos Bicos rocks.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Paseo Marítimo stretches eight miles along the Atlantic coast, linking Riazor and Orzán beaches to the Tower of Hercules.

⏱ 90 min
4

The Aquarium Finisterrae showcases Atlantic sharks and seals in the Nautilus Room—a 1.2-million-gallon pool submerged in the ocean itself.

⏱ 75 min
5

The glass-enclosed balconies of the Avenida de la Marina, built between 1870 and 1884, earned A Coruña its nickname: the City of Glass.

⏱ 60 min
6

The Domus, designed by Arata Isozaki, offers interactive exhibits on the human body perched dramatically above the crashing Atlantic waves.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Swim at Playa de Orzán, a popular urban surf spot with nearly a mile of protected sand and consistent Atlantic swells.

⏱ 90 min

The seafood stalls on Rúa Pescadería sell percebes, razor clams, and scallops pulled fresh from the ocean. Pair with crisp Albariño.

⏱ 75 min

Rent a bike and cruise the seafront promenade from Riazor all the way to the sculpture park at the Tower of Hercules.

⏱ 120 min
NORO Plaza9.7

Day 3

Muxia

Where legend-soaked granite meets the fury of the open Atlantic

★★★★☆
📍 Sanctuaire Nosa Señora da Barca Muxía📍 Pedra de Abalar Muxía📍 Monte Corpiño Muxía📍 Cabo Vilán📍 Phare Cabo Vilán📍 Cimetière des Anglais Costa da Morte
1

The Sanctuary of Nosa Señora da Barca, built in the 18th century on a rocky headland, marks the spiritual terminus of the Fisterra pilgrimage route.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Pedra de Abalar rocks and sways when storms batter the coast. The Pedra dos Cadrís is said to cure back pain if you crawl beneath it nine times.

⏱ 45 min
3

The A Ferida monument commemorates the 2002 Prestige oil spill disaster. Two granite blocks separated by a fissure symbolize the wound inflicted on the coast.

⏱ 30 min
4

Cape Vilán thrusts its white lighthouse 345 feet above the pounding surf. Spain's first electrified beacon, lit in 1896, warns ships of the treacherous Costa da Morte.

⏱ 75 min
5

The English Cemetery near the cape holds the graves of 172 sailors from HMS Serpent, wrecked on these rocks in 1890.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hike the coastal trail from Muxía to Monte Corpiño for sweeping views across the Ría de Camariñas and out to Cape Vilán.

⏱ 90 min

The Atlantic-battered cliffs draw cormorants and kittiwakes. Bring binoculars to watch the seabird colonies wheel and dive.

⏱ 60 min

Sunset from the sanctuary illuminates the polished granite boulders in fiery gold. Waves explode against the Pedra da Vela in thunderous bursts.

⏱ 45 min
Apartamentos Praia do Capitàn9.4
🚗Drive to next stop: 116 km · 1h56
Day 4

Pontevedra

Car-free historic jewel nestled beside a green estuary

★★★★☆
📍 Basilique Santa María a Maior Pontevedra📍 Casco Antiguo Pontevedra📍 Plaza de la Leña Pontevedra📍 Musée de Pontevedra📍 Ruinas Santo Domingo Pontevedra📍 Paseo Lérez Pontevedra
1

Pontevedra's car-free historic center—Galicia's second-largest after Santiago—unfolds in cobbled plazas and stone mansions emblazoned with noble coats of arms.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Basilica of Santa María a Maior, funded by the mariners' guild in the 16th century, flaunts a Plateresque façade featuring carvings of Christopher Columbus and Hernán Cortés.

⏱ 75 min
3

The baroque altarpiece on the main altar, carved from chestnut and walnut, dates to the 19th century. Look for the Cristo del Buen Viaje sculpture left of the south portal.

⏱ 45 min
4

The ruins of Santo Domingo—a 14th-century Dominican monastery—preserve a Gothic transept and choir in a romantically overgrown setting.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Museum of Pontevedra exhibits Galician art, archaeology, and painting across six interconnected historic buildings.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Rías Baixas wineries offer tastings of Albariño—a crisp, mineral white wine grown on granite pergolas overlooking the estuaries.

⏱ 90 min

The Paseo del Lérez follows the river through landscaped parkland dotted with medieval bridges and shaded picnic spots.

⏱ 75 min

The terraces on Plaza de la Leña come alive after 8 PM. Order scallops, razor clams, and Padrón peppers with a glass of local white.

⏱ 90 min
Casa Cousiño Zona Monumental9.6

Day 5

Vigo

Europe's fishing capital gazing out to island paradise

★★★★☆
📍 Casco Vello Vigo📍 Monte do Castro Vigo📍 Plaza de la Constitución Vigo📍 Puerto de Vigo📍 Îles Cíes Vigo📍 Playa de Samil Vigo📍 Marché aux poissons O Berbés Vigo
1

The Casco Vello winds through cobbled lanes and traditional Galician townhouses. Plaza de la Constitución pulses at the heart of the old quarter.

⏱ 75 min
2

Monte do Castro preserves the remains of a Celtic castro settlement from the 3rd century BC alongside 17th-century fortifications. Panoramic views sweep across the ría.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Port of Vigo—Europe's largest fishing harbor—thrums with trawlers and cargo ships in constant motion along the working waterfront.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Cíes Islands, reached by ferry from the port, unfurl white-sand beaches within the Atlantic Islands of Galicia National Park—once called "the Islands of the Gods" by the Romans.

⏱ 240 min

Activities

Kayak rental outfitters offer sea kayaks to paddle the protected ría. Calm waters and island vistas make for an easy, scenic paddle.

⏱ 120 min

Calle de las Ostras in O Berbés lines up wooden tables piled with iced seafood. Slurp fresh estuary oysters with a squeeze of lemon.

⏱ 75 min

Playa de Samil stretches nearly a mile of fine sand and shallow, calm water. Sunset paints the ría in molten gold and amber.

⏱ 90 min
