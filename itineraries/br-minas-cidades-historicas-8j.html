✨ Highlights
  • Ouro Preto, former Vila Rica, UNESCO 1980, capital of Brazilian baroque and cradle of the 1789 Inconfidência Mineira independence movement
  • Igreja de São Francisco de Assis in Ouro Preto, Aleijadinho's absolute masterpiece with rococo facade and ceiling painted by Mestre Ataíde
  • Sanctuary of Bom Jesus de Matosinhos in Congonhas, UNESCO 1985, the Twelve Prophets carved in soapstone by Aleijadinho (1800-1805)
  • Tiradentes, miraculously preserved colonial center, Igreja Matriz de Santo Antônio with richly gilded interior and facade designed by Aleijadinho (1810)
  • Diamantina, UNESCO 1999, birthplace of Juscelino Kubitschek and home of Chica da Silva, 18th-century diamond capital
📆 Best period

April, May, June, July, August, September

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 3 and 7 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 What to discover in BR?

Carnival energy, Amazon rainforest, and pristine beaches create the ultimate South American adventure. Experience vibrant cities, incredible biodiversity, and passionate culture from Rio's beaches to Pantanal wetlands. Samba music, capoeira martial arts, and incredible football culture showcase national spirit. Brazil offers unparalleled natural wonders with infectious cultural energy.

📋 Important notes

Depart from Belo Horizonte (Confins airport), return to Belo Horizonte. Roads are in good condition but winding and mountainous—allow 30% extra time versus Google Maps. Ouro Preto, Mariana, and Tiradentes are entirely cobbled; proper shoes essential. Tropical highland climate (800-1200m): mild days but cool nights year-round, bring a sweater even in summer. Diamantina is 4h30 north, prioritize 2 nights there to make the journey worthwhile.

Belo Horizonte → Ouro Preto → Tiradentes → Diamantina

Eight days in the heart of Brazil's mining state, tracing Aleijadinho's legacy and the 18th-century gold rush. Ouro Preto and Mariana, cradles of mineiro baroque, Congonhas and its UNESCO Twelve Prophets, Tiradentes and its richly gilded Igreja Matriz de Santo Antônio, and finally Diamantina, diamond capital, birthplace of Juscelino Kubitschek and home of Chica da Silva. A concentrated journey through architecture, colonial history, and mineira gastronomy.

Stop 1

Belo Horizonte

Where Niemeyer's Pampulha meets the gastronomic soul of Mercado Central

★★★★☆
📍 Praça da Liberdade Belo Horizonte📍 Mercado Central Belo Horizonte📍 Pampulha Niemeyer📍 São Francisco de Assis Church Pampulha📍 Mangabeiras Viewpoint Belo Horizonte
1

Arrive in Belo Horizonte, capital of Minas Gerais founded in 1897. Transfer from Confins airport (40 km, 45 min). Drop your bags, freshen up, and walk to Praça da Liberdade. A good warm-up stroll.

⏱ 45 min
2

Praça da Liberdade, the historic administrative heart, brings together a dozen early 20th-century buildings now converted into themed museums (Mineiro, Memory, Sciences). The ensemble is known as Circuito Cultural Praça da Liberdade, one of the densest cultural circuits in Latin America.

⏱ 75 min
3

Drive to Pampulha, 15 minutes north. This modernist complex was commissioned in 1940 by the young mayor Juscelino Kubitschek from a team of artists, most at the start of their careers (Niemeyer, Burle Marx, Ceschiatti), with the notable exception of Cândido Portinari, already recognized as Brazil's greatest living painter after the 1939 New York World's Fair. UNESCO-listed in 2016.

⏱ 30 min
4

Don't miss the Igreja de São Francisco de Assis at Pampulha. Vaulted reinforced concrete roof, azulejos painted by Portinari on the rear facade, interior decorated with a monumental Stations of the Cross. The bishopric refused to consecrate it for 14 years, deeming it "too modern." Open for worship since 1959.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Dine at Mercado Central, the gastronomic soul of Belo Horizonte since 1929. Over 400 stalls, cachaça counters, mineira charcuterie, artisanal cheeses. Order the classic: pão de queijo + fígado com jiló (liver with jiló, a local bitter vegetable) + Boazinha cachaça. Lively, crowded atmosphere.

⏱ 90 min

If the evening stretches on, head up to Mirante Mangabeiras for panoramic views of the illuminated city. The Belvedere neighborhood also offers excellent rooftops.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 100 km · 1h45
Stop 2

Ouro Preto

Former Vila Rica, where gold-laden baroque churches whisper tales of conspiracy

★★★☆☆
📍 Praça Tiradentes Ouro Preto📍 São Francisco de Assis Church Ouro Preto📍 Nossa Senhora do Pilar Church Ouro Preto📍 Inconfidência Museum📍 Nossa Senhora do Rosário dos Pretos Church📍 Casa dos Contos Ouro Preto
1

Drive from Belo Horizonte to Ouro Preto via BR-356 (100 km, 1h45). The last 20 kilometers climb into the Serra do Espinhaço. Park at the Estação parking lot at the bottom of town. The center is pedestrianized and very steep: proper shoes essential.

⏱ 30 min
2

Start at Praça Tiradentes, Ouro Preto's beating heart. It's dominated by the former municipal palace and prison of 1784, now the Museu da Inconfidência. The museum chronicles the 1789 conspiracy and houses the conspirators' pantheon in its crypt. Tiradentes, the movement's central figure, was hanged then quartered in Rio de Janeiro on April 21, 1792; his head was subsequently displayed on this square as a warning.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Igreja de São Francisco de Assis, commissioned in 1766, is the absolute masterpiece of Antônio Francisco Lisboa, known as Aleijadinho. The rococo facade, soapstone medallions, and carved pulpits bear his signature. The ceiling painted by Mestre Ataíde depicts a mulatto Virgin surrounded by musician angels, an iconic work of mineiro baroque.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora do Pilar, steps away, presents Brazil's most opulent colonial interior. Over 400 kilos of gold leaf cover altars, columns, and galleries. Red velvet curtains at the side balconies create a striking theatrical effect. The country's second-most gilded church after São Francisco in Salvador.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário dos Pretos, built in the 18th century by and for freed African slaves, breaks with conventional rectangular plans. Its unique rounded form and curved lines herald a popular baroque—less gilded but profoundly alive.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Lunch on comida mineira at a pay-by-weight restaurant downtown. Tutu de feijão (bean purée with manioc flour), frango com quiabo (chicken with okra), couve (shredded collard greens), unlimited pão de queijo. Expect 50-70 BRL per person with drinks.

⏱ 75 min

Afternoon: lose yourself in the steep cobbled lanes. Every turn reveals a new perspective: twin bell towers framed by green hills, colonial facades with wrought-iron balconies, plunging views over red-tiled roofs. Ouro Preto savors best on foot, slowly.

⏱ 120 min

Late afternoon aperitif facing Igreja São Francisco with an artisanal mineira cachaça (Boazinha, Salinas, Havana). Ouro Preto is a lively university town: the repúblicas (student houses inspired by Coimbra) give weekend evenings a festive atmosphere.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 3

Ouro Preto

Former Vila Rica, where gold-laden baroque churches whisper tales of conspiracy

★★★☆☆
📍 Mariana historic center📍 Sé Cathedral Mariana📍 Praça Minas Gerais Mariana📍 Mina da Passagem📍 São Francisco Church Mariana📍 Rua Direita Mariana
1

Second day based in Ouro Preto with an excursion to Mariana, 14 km away. Leave early to visit Mina da Passagem in the morning, halfway between the two towns. It's Brazil's largest gold mine open to visitors. Worked from 1719 to 1985, it produced 35 tons of gold.

⏱ 15 min
2

A funicular trolley descends 120 meters underground into galleries carved in the 18th century by enslaved people. At the bottom, a translucent underground lake allows swimming. Guided 45-minute tour, equipment included. A striking immersion into the brutal reality of the gold cycle.

⏱ 75 min
3

Mariana, founded in 1696, was Minas Gerais's first planned capital and the seat of the first mining bishopric. Its grid plan laid out in 1745 by military engineer José Fernandes Alpoim is unique in the region. Its main streets (Rua Direita, Dom Viçoso, Dom Silvério) can be walked in two hours.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Catedral Basílica da Sé de Mariana preserves its German Arp Schnitger organ, gifted by the Portuguese crown in 1753—one of the rare instruments by this genius craftsman still in working order worldwide. Concerts on Fridays at 11:30am and Sundays at 12:15pm. The interior is stunningly rich, covered in gold.

⏱ 60 min
5

Praça Minas Gerais forms one of Brazil's finest baroque urban ensembles. The Igrejas São Francisco and Nossa Senhora do Carmo, near-twins, stand in perpendicular tension around the Pelourinho (pillory) and the Casa de Câmara e Cadeia (1798). Inside Igreja São Francisco: paintings by Mestre Ataíde (who is buried there), soapstone pulpits attributed to Aleijadinho.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Lunch in Mariana on Rua Dom Viçoso. Restaurante Lua Cheia serves pay-by-weight mineira cuisine in panelas de pedra-sabão (soapstone pots). Specialties: feijão tropeiro, frango caipira, sorvete de queijo com goiabada for dessert. Good value.

⏱ 75 min

Return to Ouro Preto late afternoon. Pleasant option: the Trem da Vale connects the two towns Friday-Sunday in panoramic carriages (1h journey, 85 BRL round-trip). Online booking required. Departure times vary by season, check schedules.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner in Ouro Preto. Try Bené da Flauta or Chafariz: revisited mineira cuisine with city views. Prices somewhat above average (90-120 BRL per person), but quality delivers. Weekend reservations recommended.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 4

Tiradentes

A colonial jewel frozen in time beneath the watchful gaze of Serra de São José

★★★★☆
📍 Bom Jesus de Matosinhos Sanctuary Congonhas📍 12 Prophets Aleijadinho📍 Santo Antônio Mother Church Tiradentes📍 Largo das Forras Tiradentes📍 São José Fountain Tiradentes
1

Drive from Ouro Preto to Congonhas via BR-040 (85 km, 1h50). Congonhas is a fairly ordinary modern mining town, but it shelters one of the twin summits of world baroque: the Santuário Bom Jesus de Matosinhos. Park at the sanctuary lot.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Santuário Bom Jesus de Matosinhos, UNESCO-listed in 1985, dominates the city from atop Monte Maranhão. Founded in 1757 by Portuguese settler Feliciano Mendes as payment for a healing vow, it brings together a basilica and six Passion chapels. You climb a monumental ramp flanked by six chapels and then encounter the Prophets.

⏱ 60 min
3

The twelve Prophets, carved in soapstone between 1800 and 1805 by an Aleijadinho already ravaged by disease, form the absolute pinnacle of his work. Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, Daniel, and eight others answer each other from a distance in theatrical poses, hands raised, scrolls unfurled—designed to be "read" from the bottom of the forecourt.

⏱ 45 min
4

In the six Passion chapels, 66 life-size polychrome cedar sculptures, created by Aleijadinho and his apprentices between 1796 and 1799, depict Christ's Passion. The polychromy, executed by Mestre Ataíde, gives each face a striking intensity. One of the world's most complete sacred sculptural ensembles.

⏱ 60 min
5

Take the road to Tiradentes, 170 km southwest via BR-265 (2h45). Arrive late afternoon. Leave the car at the town entrance parking: the center is strictly pedestrian. Drop your bags at the pousada and set out to observe the village in the low-angled light.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Lunch in Congonhas before departing. The town has no great gastronomic tradition, but Restaurante da Praça facing the sanctuary serves a decent mineiro buffet at 45 BRL. Quick and efficient.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner at Largo das Forras, Tiradentes' central square and nightlife hub. Contemporary mineira cuisine restaurants (Tragaluz, Theobroma, Uai d'Italia). Book ahead on weekends: Tiradentes is one of Brazil's major gastronomic destinations.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 14 km · 20min
Stop 5

Tiradentes

A colonial jewel frozen in time beneath the watchful gaze of Serra de São José

★★★★☆
📍 Santo Antônio Mother Church Tiradentes📍 Maria Fumaça steam train Tiradentes📍 Pilar Basilica Cathedral São João del Rei📍 Tancredo Neves Memorial📍 São Francisco de Assis Church São João del Rei📍 Serra de São José
1

Morning in Tiradentes. Begin at the Igreja Matriz de Santo Antônio on the heights. Built from 1710, it's considered one of mineiro baroque's masterpieces. The design (risco) of its rococo facade was commissioned from Aleijadinho in 1810, but execution was carried out by other craftsmen after his death in 1814. The interior is one of colonial Brazil's most richly gilded, with altarpieces and pulpits covered in gold leaf.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Portuguese organ preserved inside, installed in the late 18th century and still functional, is one of Brazil's rare baroque instruments. Regular concerts (Friday, Sunday). The church also preserves ceiling paintings attributed to Antônio de Caldas, rare representations of baroque allegorical scenes.

⏱ 30 min
3

Descend to the Chafariz de São José, built in 1749. It supplied the town with drinking water via a one-kilometer aqueduct built by enslaved laborers. Three distinct basins: washerwomen, horse trough, running water for the population. The ensemble still functions today.

⏱ 30 min
4

Allow about 1h to drive Tiradentes → São João del-Rei (12 km) and board the Maria Fumaça for the return journey to Tiradentes. Departures generally leave São João del-Rei at 10am and 1:30pm, returns from Tiradentes at 11am and 2:30pm (Friday-Sunday). The 762mm narrow-gauge line, inaugurated in 1881 in the presence of Dom Pedro II, is Brazil's oldest Maria Fumaça still in operation. Pulled by period steam locomotives. 12 km journey in 35-40 min.

⏱ 35 min
5

São João del-Rei, population 90,000, is the commercial and university town complementing Tiradentes. Visit the Catedral Basílica Nossa Senhora do Pilar (1721) on the main square. The church preserves a late 18th-century baroque organ and two bells cast in Portugal that give the city its nickname "terra onde os sinos falam" (land where the bells speak).

⏱ 45 min
6

The Igreja São Francisco de Assis (1774), near the humpback bridge, has a unique curvilinear facade attributed to Aleijadinho's school. Inside rests Tancredo Neves, president-elect in 1985 who died before his inauguration. The neighboring Memorial Tancredo Neves chronicles Brazil's 1985 democratic transition.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Lunch in São João del-Rei. Restaurante Churrascaria Ramon serves a solid meat rodízio at a gentle price (70 BRL). For a more typical option, Quinto do Ouro offers traditional mineira cuisine in a vaulted room.

⏱ 90 min

Return to Tiradentes late afternoon. If time permits, detour via the Serra de São José, the limestone massif overlooking the town. Short trails (1-3h) to Cachoeira do Mangue and viewpoints. Guide recommended (50 BRL): trails are poorly marked.

⏱ 180 min

Evening in Tiradentes. Sunset aperitif on a terrace at Largo das Forras, then gastronomic dinner. Tragaluz (contemporary mineira cuisine) and Pousada Thymus (artisanal cachaça cellar) are two benchmarks. Calm, romantic town in the evening.

⏱ 150 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 395 km · 5h30
Stop 6

Diamantina

Where diamond fortunes built a mountain town that inspired Brasília's architect

★★★★☆
📍 Diamantina historic center📍 Santo Antônio Cathedral Diamantina📍 Chica da Silva House📍 Municipal Market Diamantina📍 Passadiço da Glória Diamantina📍 Juscelino Kubitschek House Diamantina
1

Long driving day. Count on 5h30 behind the wheel for 395 km via Belo Horizonte (BR-040 then BR-135). Early departure recommended: 7am from Tiradentes to arrive in Diamantina mid-afternoon with time for a first exploration. Lunch stop in Belo Horizonte (Restaurante Xapuri or Mercado Central).

⏱ 30 min
2

Diamantina, perched at 1,114m altitude in the Serra do Espinhaço, was UNESCO-listed in 1999 as a "remarkable example of human endeavor in a hostile environment." Founded in the 18th century as Arraial do Tijuco, it was the world center of diamond extraction under the Portuguese Crown. Entirely pedestrianized historic center.

⏱ 45 min
3

First stroll through the steeply cobbled streets. White houses with colorful balconies, muxarabis (latticed balconies inherited from Maghrebi influence), and lajes de pedra pavements create a uniquely Brazilian urban landscape. Elevation varies 150 meters across the historic center alone.

⏱ 60 min
4

Don't miss the Passadiço da Glória, a small blue covered bridge spanning the street and connecting two historic casarões. Now the symbol of UNESCO candidacy, it houses UFMG's geology center. Photo essential.

⏱ 15 min
5

The Casa de Chica da Silva, Praça Lobo de Mesquita, is the former residence (1763-1771) of Francisca da Silva de Oliveira. Born enslaved around 1732, freed in 1753 by her master and companion, the immensely wealthy diamond contractor João Fernandes de Oliveira, she subsequently led a lady's life: she had her own church for Black people built, bore 13 recognized children, received a refined education. Now a museum.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Mercado Municipal, known as Mercado Velho, on Praça Barão do Guaicuí, is one of Diamantina's postcards. Wooden structure from 1835 initially built as a tropeiros (muleteers) inn. Its arches reportedly inspired Oscar Niemeyer for the Palácio da Alvorada in Brasília. Market on Friday and Saturday mornings.

⏱ 60 min

On Thursday, Friday, and Saturday evenings, the Vesperata takes place—a unique musical tradition. Musicians play from the balconies of colonial houses overlooking Rua da Quitanda while the audience dines at tables set up below. A magical moment typical of the town. Reserve a table in advance.

⏱ 120 min
Stop 7

Diamantina

Where diamond fortunes built a mountain town that inspired Brasília's architect

★★★★☆
📍 Nossa Senhora do Rosário Church Diamantina📍 São Francisco de Assis Church Diamantina📍 Juscelino Kubitschek House📍 Diamond Museum📍 Antônio Torres Library Muxarabiê📍 Biribiri Diamantina
1

Full day in Diamantina and surroundings. Begin at the Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário, the town's oldest church, built between 1728 and 1731 by the confraternity of freed African slaves. Sober interior, painted choir ceiling, bell that has withstood three centuries of weather. Unique tower on the right side, rare in the region.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Igreja de São Francisco de Assis, located on Praça Juscelino Kubitschek at slightly higher elevation, preserves in its nave the presumed burial place of Chica da Silva. Interior decorated with illusionist trompe-l'oeil paintings by José Soares de Araújo, rare in the region's mineiro baroque.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Casa de Juscelino Kubitschek, Rua São Francisco 241, is the birthplace of the president who built Brasília (1956-1960). Traditional pau-a-pique house where he lived until age 18. The museum preserves personal objects, photographs, and correspondence. JK never stopped loving his hometown: he had a hotel, school, and faculty built there by Oscar Niemeyer.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Museu do Diamante, housed in the Casa do Padre Rolim (one of the 1789 conspirators), chronicles four centuries of diamond extraction. Tools, measuring instruments, rough diamond collection, but also sacred art pieces and torture instruments used on enslaved miners. A raw testimony of colonial Brazil.

⏱ 60 min
5

After lunch, excursion to Biribiri, 14 km north via a dirt road. Former textile industrial village founded in 1876, preserved in original condition. Waterfalls and natural pools on the Rio Ribeirão Pequeno. The Igrejinha de São João Batista (1876), a tiny pink chapel, is the site's postcard.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Lunch at a mineira trattoria in downtown Diamantina. Recanto do Antônio or Tragaluz Diamantina serve iconic dishes at fair prices. The typical jequitinhonha: arinto wood with sertão vegetables. Count 50-80 BRL.

⏱ 75 min

Afternoon swimming in Biribiri's waterfalls. Multiple access points: Cachoeira dos Cristais, Cachoeira Sentinela, Cachoeira da Toca. Cool water (20°C even in summer), natural pools with polished rocks. Guide recommended from Diamantina (tour 150 BRL/person).

⏱ 180 min

Last evening in Diamantina. If you haven't yet, enjoy the Vesperata or a simple dinner at the historic center's terrace tables. The local cachaça, Havana de Salinas, is produced in the Vale do Jequitinhonha 200 km north.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 8

Belo Horizonte

Where Niemeyer's Pampulha meets the gastronomic soul of Mercado Central

★★★★☆
📍 Inhotim contemporary art📍 Brumadinho Inhotim📍 Confins Airport Belo Horizonte📍 Praça Liberdade cultural circuit📍 Pampulha Church Niemeyer
1

Drive Diamantina → Belo Horizonte via BR-040 (295 km, 4h30). Breakfast stop in Curvelo. Arrive in Belo Horizonte early afternoon. Depending on your flight time, two options: drop bags at airport luggage storage or head straight to the final stop.

⏱ 30 min
2

Late flight option: detour to Inhotim, an open-air contemporary art institute founded in 2006 by businessman Bernardo Paz. 140 hectares of botanical gardens (Roberto Burle Marx), 23 galleries dedicated to major artists (Hélio Oiticica, Cildo Meireles, Tunga, Matthew Barney). The world's largest open-air contemporary art museum.

⏱ 240 min
3

If departing early evening, simply spend the afternoon in downtown Belo Horizonte. Rua Sapucaí in the Floresta neighborhood for street art, Praça da Liberdade for museums you missed on D1, Mirante do Mangabeiras for a final view.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Lunch at Inhotim at one of its three restaurants (Oiticica, Bistrô, Tamboril). Contemporary cuisine, mineiro terroir ingredients. Count 80-120 BRL per person. Weekend reservations recommended.

⏱ 90 min

Final pão de queijo of the trip at Confins airport. The Casa do Pão de Queijo chain has a presence there—you can bring frozen ones home in carry-on (insulated bags sold on-site). True enthusiasts complete with a bottle of artisanal cachaça at duty-free.

⏱ 30 min
