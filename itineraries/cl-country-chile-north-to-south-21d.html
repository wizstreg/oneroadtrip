Chile Road Trip: From the Atacama Desert to Patagonian Glaciers

📅 21 days📍 21 stops🚗 5600 km⏱ 16h45

Santiago → San Pedro De Atacama → Pucon → Puerto Varas → Punta Arenas → Puerto Natales → Torres Del Paine

Santiago at the foot of the Andes, the absolute silence of the Atacama Desert at 4,000 meters altitude, snow-capped volcanoes and emerald lakes of the Lake District, Chiloé's colorful stilt houses, then the granite towers and blue glaciers of Torres del Paine. Chile stretches 4,300 km from north to south, offering the continent's most striking concentration of contrasts.

✨ Highlights
  • El Tatio geysers at dawn at 4,320 m altitude
  • Summit Villarrica volcano and peer into the lava crater
  • Chiloé's wooden churches and Castro's colorful palafitos
  • Base Torres trek facing three granite monoliths
  • Navigate among the icebergs of Grey Glacier
📆 Best period

November, December, January, February, March

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 9 and 13 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, warmest weather, ideal for Patagonia, highest prices, crowded attractions, perfect hiking conditions.

Fall (March-May): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, wine harvest season, moderate prices, comfortable temperatures, autumn colors.

Winter (June-August): Ski season in Andes, fewer tourists, lowest prices, perfect for deserts and cities, Patagonia challenging but beautiful.

Spring (September-November): Wildflower blooms, moderate weather, good value, excellent for most regions, shoulder season advantages.

Regional Variations: Northern desert year-round, central valley Mediterranean climate, southern regions best in summer, opposite seasons from north.

🌍 What to discover in CL?

World's longest country offers desert, wine regions, and Patagonian wilderness in narrow strip. Experience Atacama Desert, Santiago sophistication, and Easter Island mysteries. The country provides incredible geographical diversity with excellent wine culture and outdoor adventures. Chile offers adventure and culture from Pacific coast to Andes peaks.

Day 1

Santiago

Where the Andes loom over century-old markets and glass towers

★★★★★
📍 Plaza de Armas Santiago📍 Catedral Metropolitana Santiago📍 Museo Histórico Nacional Santiago📍 Palacio de la Moneda Santiago📍 Cerro Santa Lucía Santiago📍 Mercado Central Santiago
1

Plaza de Armas, Santiago's beating heart since Pedro de Valdivia founded the city on February 12, 1541. Every distance in Chile is measured from this square. At its center stands Francesco Orselino's Carrara marble allegory of Liberty, unveiled in 1836, surrounded by a hundred Chilean palm trees. An equestrian statue of Pedro de Valdivia, gifted by the Hispanic community for the 150th independence anniversary, shares the plaza with chess players, mimes, and street dancers who bring it alive throughout the day.

⏱ 45 min
2

Metropolitan Cathedral of Santiago, the fifth religious building on this site - the four predecessors fell to earthquakes and fires. Construction began in 1748 under Bishop González Melgarejo. Architect Joaquín Toesca, who arrived in 1780, designed the neoclassical facade. The building was largely completed by 1830, then modified by architect Ignacio Cremonesi who added the twin towers and Romano-Tuscan styling. Three naves organize the interior around a marble main altar and black-and-white mosaic floor. A crypt beneath houses Santiago's archbishops. Declared a National Monument in 1951, it hosts the ecumenical Te Deum each September 18th for Fiestas Patrias.

⏱ 45 min
3

National History Museum, housed in the Palacio de la Real Audiencia on Plaza de Armas. Around 70,000 pieces trace Chile's story from pre-Columbian civilizations to the 1973 coup d'état. The final room displays Salvador Allende's broken glasses. Free admission.

⏱ 60 min
4

Palacio de la Moneda, seat of Chile's presidency since 1845. Designed in 1784 by architect Joaquín Toesca, this neoclassical building originally served as the treasury where coins were minted. On September 11, 1973, it was bombed during the military coup. Now restored, the changing of the guard takes place every other day on the esplanade. The underground Palacio La Moneda cultural center hosts quality temporary exhibitions.

⏱ 60 min
5

Cerro Santa Lucía, a 69-meter hill in the city center. The Mapuche used it as a lookout point before the Spanish arrived. Pedro de Valdivia founded Santiago at the foot of this hill on February 12, 1541, using the promontory as a natural watchtower. The current park, redesigned in 1872 by mayor Benjamín Vicuña Mackenna, offers shaded walks, the Neptune fountain at the entrance, and a belvedere with views over the historic center.

⏱ 60 min
6

Barrio Lastarria, an artistic quarter born around the Veracruz church built in 1857, classified as an Atypical Zone by the Ministry of Culture in 1997. Squeezed between Cerro Santa Lucía and Parque Forestal, a few streets concentrate the Museum of Fine Arts (opened 1880), the Gabriela Mistral cultural center (built in 1972 for a UN conference), booksellers, and art galleries. El Biógrafo cinema, opened in the 1980s, screens art-house films in a red velvet setting.

⏱ 60 min
7

Mercado Central, Santiago's iconic covered market. The metal structure shelters stalls of fresh fish and seafood - congrio, locos, erizos - alongside numerous food stands. Step inside to taste caldillo de congrio, the fish soup Pablo Neruda celebrated in one of his poems.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Ascent of Cerro San Cristóbal by historic funicular, declared a Historic Monument in 2000. This 722-hectare park - Santiago's largest green space - peaks at 860 meters altitude. At the summit, a 14-meter statue of the Virgin of the Immaculate Conception (22m with pedestal) has dominated the city since 1908. Pope John Paul II celebrated mass here in 1987. On clear days, the view extends to Andean peaks exceeding 6,000 meters.

⏱ 120 min

Stroll through Barrio Bellavista at the foot of Cerro San Cristóbal. Colorful facades, murals, and bohemian atmosphere. Visit La Chascona, one of poet Pablo Neruda's three house-museums, named for his wife Matilde's wild hair.

⏱ 90 min

Santiago

Where the Andes loom over century-old markets and glass towers

★★★★★
📍 Valle de Casablanca📍 Valparaíso📍 Cerros Alegre Valparaíso📍 Plaza Sotomayor Valparaíso📍 La Sebastiana Valparaíso📍 Viña del Mar
1

Stop in the Casablanca Valley on the Santiago-Valparaíso road. This wine valley, crossed by Pacific mists, produces renowned whites - particularly sauvignon blanc and chardonnay. Several estates offer tastings.

⏱ 60 min
2

Valparaíso, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2003 for its 19th-century urban fabric adapted to the hills. The city sprawls across more than 40 cerros (hills) and once counted 31 ascensores - funiculars built from 1883 onward to connect the lower city to the heights. The first, Ascensor Concepción, dates to 1883. Seven remain operational. The port was the Pacific South's most important until the Panama Canal opened in 1914.

⏱ 45 min
3

Cerros Alegre and Concepción, the UNESCO-listed historic heart. Cobblestone streets, colorful facade houses, architecture marked by British and German immigrants of the second half of the 19th century. The Paseo Yugoslavo offers one of the finest panoramas over the bay. Access via Ascensor El Peral, inaugurated in 1901, which connects Plaza de Justicia to Cerro Alegre. The Baburizza Palace, built in 1916 on Cerro Alegre, houses the Municipal Museum of Fine Arts.

⏱ 90 min
4

Plaza Sotomayor, the civic center of the lower city. At its center stands the Monument to the Heroes of Iquique, commemorating the 1879 naval battle during the War of the Pacific. The square is surrounded by the former Navy Command building and the historic customs house.

⏱ 40 min
5

La Sebastiana, Pablo Neruda's house-museum perched on Cerro Bellavista. The poet installed his eclectic collections here: maritime objects, sculptures, paintings. From the garden viewpoint, panoramic views over the bay and Valparaíso's colorful rooftops.

⏱ 60 min
6

Viña del Mar, neighboring seaside resort nicknamed the "Garden City." The Flower Clock (Reloj de Flores), built in 1962, marks the entrance to the seafront. Stroll along Avenida Perú facing the Pacific.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Wine tasting in the Casablanca Valley (stop on the Santiago-Valparaíso road)

⏱ 60 min

Street art tour through Valparaíso's cerros. The city's walls serve as canvas for Chilean and international artists. Cerro Polanco and Cerro Bellavista, nicknamed "open-air museum," concentrate the most spectacular murals.

⏱ 60 min

Santiago

Where the Andes loom over century-old markets and glass towers

★★★★★
📍 Museo de la Memoria Santiago📍 Museo Precolombino Santiago📍 Valle del Maipo📍 Viñedos Pirque📍 Barrio Yungay Santiago📍 Iglesia Los Capuchinos Santiago
1

Museum of Memory and Human Rights, opened to document the military dictatorship's violence (1973-1990). Exhibitions trace the stages of the September 11, 1973 coup d'état and its consequences. Testimonies, audiovisual archives, and victims' personal belongings. Free admission.

⏱ 90 min
2

Chilean Museum of Pre-Columbian Art, housed in the Palacio Real Casa de Aduana (19th century) steps from Plaza de Armas. Ceramics, sculptures, textiles, and jewelry tell the story of pre-Columbian peoples from Mexico to Tierra del Fuego. The Chile room is particularly rich.

⏱ 75 min
3

Maipo Valley, Santiago's main wine region, accessible in 45 minutes' drive south. Vineyards stretch at the foot of the Andes Cordillera. This valley produces mainly cabernet sauvignon, carménère (a grape that became Chile's emblem after disappearing from Europe following phylloxera), and merlot.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wine tasting in the Maipo Valley. Several wineries in the Pirque area offer cellar visits and tastings. Carménère, long confused with merlot, was rediscovered in Chile in the 1990s - it's now the country's signature grape.

⏱ 120 min

Walk through Barrio Yungay, Santiago's historic working-class neighborhood. Plaza Yungay pays homage to the "Roto Chileno," the figure of the common soldier at the 1839 Battle of Yungay. Los Capuchinos church, built between 1853 and 1861 on a Florentine design, is worth the detour.

⏱ 60 min

Day 4

San Pedro De Atacama

An oasis at 2,400 meters in the world's driest desert

★★★☆☆
📍 Aeropuerto El Loa Calama📍 Iglesia San Pedro de Atacama📍 Valle de la Luna San Pedro📍 Cordillera de la Sal📍 Plaza San Pedro de Atacama
1

Flight Santiago → Calama then transfer to San Pedro de Atacama (about 1h30 drive, 100 km). The road crosses the Domeyko Range, a landscape of arid hills and ochre canyons. San Pedro sits at 2,400 meters altitude in the oasis of the same name, at the heart of the world's driest desert (outside polar zones).

⏱ 90 min
2

Church of San Pedro de Atacama, considered Chile's second oldest. Built during the colonial period from adobe - mud mixed with straw - it presents a sober appearance and a ceiling of cactus wood (chañar and carob). A gem of Andean vernacular architecture.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Valle de la Luna in late afternoon. This landscape sculpted by wind and erosion in the Salt Range evokes a lunar surface: sand dunes, salt ridges, and rock formations with multicolored strata. Sunset sets the minerals ablaze, transforming the relief into a gradient of ochre, red, and violet. Bring warm clothes - temperature drops rapidly after dusk.

⏱ 180 min

Day 5

San Pedro De Atacama

An oasis at 2,400 meters in the world's driest desert

★★★☆☆
📍 Géiseres del Tatio📍 Pueblo de Machuca📍 Pukará de Quitor📍 Aldea de Tulor📍 Río San Pedro
1

El Tatio Geysers, the world's third-largest geothermal field and one of the planet's highest, at around 4,280 meters altitude. Depart before dawn (around 4am) to arrive at sunrise, when freezing cold (down to -20°C) produces the most spectacular vapor columns. The site counts roughly 80 active geysers. Its name comes from the Kunza "Tata iu" - "the grandfather who weeps." Possibility to bathe in the site's natural thermal pools.

⏱ 120 min
2

Village of Machuca, on the return road from the geysers. About twenty houses of adobe and ichu grass, a small earth church, and a single extended family living from llama herding. Residents sell grilled llama meat skewers to passing visitors.

⏱ 30 min
3

Pukará de Quitor, a 12th-century pre-Incan fortress 3 km north of San Pedro. Built from liparite stone on an 80-meter promontory overlooking the San Pedro River, it served as a defensive point for the Atacameño people (Lickan Antay) against the Aymaras. In 1540, Francisco de Aguirre led a decisive battle here: after his victory, he beheaded the Atacameño chiefs and warriors - Spanish chronicles mention several hundred victims - whose heads were lined along the walls, hence the nickname "the town of heads." National Monument since 1982. The summit viewpoint offers views over the oasis, the Valley of Death, and the Andes Cordillera.

⏱ 75 min
4

Aldea de Tulor, an archaeological site roughly 3,000 years old, among the oldest evidence of sedentarization in the Atacama Desert. The circular earthen foundations have been partially reconstructed to show the organization of the original Atacameño village. The site was discovered partly buried under sand.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Bathing in El Tatio's natural thermal pools after observing the geysers. The geothermal water, hot and mineral-rich, contrasts with the freezing air of early morning at 4,300 m altitude.

⏱ 45 min
Naturalis Hotel9.7
Day 6

San Pedro De Atacama

An oasis at 2,400 meters in the world's driest desert

★★★☆☆
📍 Lagunas Miscanti y Miñiques📍 Salar de Atacama📍 Laguna Chaxa📍 Toconao📍 Valle del Arcoíris📍 Hierbas Buenas
1

Lagunas Miscanti and Miñiques, twin lagoons perched at around 4,200 meters altitude on the altiplano. Their deep blue waters are framed by volcanoes of the same names. Vicuñas (wild cousins of the llama, protected) often graze nearby. The setting is absolutely silent, broken only by the wind.

⏱ 75 min
2

Salar de Atacama, Chile's largest salt flat (roughly 3,000 km²). At Laguna Chaxa in the Los Flamencos National Reserve, observe three flamingo species - Chilean flamingo, Andean flamingo, and James's flamingo - filtering micro-algae from the brackish waters. Salt crystals form hexagonal patterns on the surface. This salt flat produces a significant portion of the world's lithium.

⏱ 75 min
3

Village of Toconao, 38 km south of San Pedro. San Lucas church and its separate bell tower are built from locally extracted white volcanic stone (liparite). The village is surrounded by a verdant quebrada (ravine) where fig trees and quínoa grow.

⏱ 40 min
4

Valle del Arcoíris (Rainbow Valley), about 90 km north of San Pedro. Mineral layers - iron, copper, sulfur, manganese - color the rock formations red, green, yellow, and violet. The site also features the Hierbas Buenas petroglyphs, carved by Atacameño caravaneers centuries ago.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Flamingo observation at Salar de Atacama. Three species coexist: Chilean flamingo (most common), Andean flamingo (endangered), and James's flamingo (rarest). Binoculars recommended to avoid disturbing the birds by approaching too close.

⏱ 45 min

Nighttime astronomical observation. The Atacama Desert offers one of the planet's purest skies - it's here that the world's largest observatories are installed, including ALMA. Several operators offer sessions with telescopes and explanations about southern hemisphere constellations.

⏱ 120 min

Pucon

Where adventure seekers stand at the rim of a living volcano

★★★★☆
📍 Lago Villarrica Pucón📍 Volcán Villarrica📍 Plaza de Armas Pucón📍 Playa Pucón📍 Avenida Bernardo O'Higgins Pucón
1

Flight Calama → Santiago → Temuco (connection). From Temuco, 112 km drive to Pucón (about 1h30). The road crosses the Araucanía countryside, historic territory of the Mapuche people, between fields and pine forests. Arrival in Pucón, a town of 20,000 inhabitants on the shores of Lake Villarrica, dominated by the volcano's nearly perfect cone.

⏱ 60 min
2

Walk along Lake Villarrica and through Pucón's center. The town has alpine resort vibes with its wooden houses and numerous adventure agencies. German settlers who arrived in the 19th century left their mark on local architecture and gastronomy (kuchenes, cakes). Villarrica volcano (2,847 m), called RukaPillán ("house of spirits" in Mapudungun), is one of South America's most active. Its last eruption was in 2015.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Settling in and free time in late afternoon. Walk on Lake Villarrica's beach with its gray volcanic sand. In good weather, the snow-capped volcano's reflection draws itself on the lake's calm waters.

Pucon

Where adventure seekers stand at the rim of a living volcano

★★★★☆
📍 Ojos del Caburgua📍 Lago Caburgua📍 Playa Blanca Caburgua📍 Volcán Villarrica📍 Crater Volcán Villarrica
1

Ojos del Caburgua, two natural pools of turquoise water fed by waterfalls, nestled in the forest about 25 km from Pucón. The emerald water comes from underground rivers filtered through volcanic rock. The contrast between transparent water and dense vegetation creates a striking tableau.

⏱ 60 min
2

Lake Caburgua, a lake with limpid waters surrounded by forests. Playa Blanca, a rare stretch of light sand in the region (sand is usually volcanic and dark), offers a swimming spot in summer. From the shores, views of snow-capped mountains plunging directly into the water.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Ascent of Villarrica volcano (2,847 m). Early morning departure from Pucón with mandatory guide. The ascent takes about 4 to 5 hours for 1,000 meters of elevation gain. The final meters cross the glacier capping the 200-meter-diameter crater - at the bottom, you can see magma. The volcano is an active stratovolcano capped by a 40 km² glacier. The descent partly involves sliding seated on the snow. Activity subject to weather conditions and volcanic alert level.

⏱ 480 min

Pucon

★★★★☆
📍 Parque Nacional Huerquehue📍 Sendero Los Lagos Huerquehue📍 Laguna Verde Huerquehue📍 Araucarias Huerquehue📍 Termas Pucón
1

Huerquehue National Park, 12,500 hectares of ancient forests 35 km from Pucón. The "Los Lagos" trail climbs through thousand-year-old araucarias - these prehistoric conifers nicknamed "monkey puzzle trees" because their sharp needle-like leaves prevent any climbing. The trail passes Nido de Águila and Truful waterfalls before reaching the crystalline Lagoons Chico, Verde, and El Toro at around 1,300 m altitude. Views of Villarrica volcano in the background.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike the Sendero Los Lagos in Huerquehue Park. Circuit of 13 to 17 km depending on the loop chosen, with about 700 to 1,200 meters of elevation gain. The ascent is sustained but the trail well-marked. Count 6 to 7 hours for the complete circuit. Bring water and picnic.

⏱ 360 min

Thermal baths in late afternoon. Several thermal centers hide in the cordillera valleys around Pucón. Natural hot water, heated by volcanic activity, flows into stone or wooden pools surrounded by native forest.

Puerto Varas

The City of Roses facing Osorno's perfect snow-capped cone

★★★★☆
📍 Frutillar📍 Teatro del Lago Frutillar📍 Puerto Varas📍 Lago Llanquihue📍 Volcán Osorno📍 Costanera Puerto Varas
1

Drive Pucón → Puerto Varas (about 4h30, 315 km). The road crosses southern Chilean countryside between volcanoes and pastures. Stop in Frutillar Bajo, a German-speaking village on Lake Llanquihue's west shore. Founded by German settlers in the mid-19th century, Frutillar has preserved its half-timbered houses, flower gardens, and tea rooms serving kuchenes (traditional German cakes). The German Colonial Museum traces this immigration's history. The Teatro del Lago, a concert hall on stilts facing the lake, hosts a classical music festival each summer.

⏱ 90 min
2

Arrival in Puerto Varas, nicknamed the "City of Roses" for the rose gardens blooming in every garden from December to March. This small town on Lake Llanquihue's shore - Chile's largest lake and South America's third - offers a direct view of Osorno volcano's snow-capped cone (2,652 m), 50 km across the lake. The colorful wooden architecture recalls the heritage of German settlers who arrived here in the early 20th century.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll along Puerto Varas' lakefront with views of Osorno and Calbuco volcanoes. The statue of Princess Licarayén, a figure from Huilliche mythology, recalls the local legend: she was allegedly sacrificed by her tribe to calm the evil spirit trapped in Osorno volcano, giving birth to the region's lakes.

⏱ 60 min

Day 11

Puerto Varas

The City of Roses facing Osorno's perfect snow-capped cone

★★★★☆
📍 Saltos del Petrohué📍 Lago Todos los Santos📍 Volcán Osorno📍 Ensenada📍 Parque Nacional Vicente Pérez Rosales
1

Saltos del Petrohué, in Vicente Pérez Rosales National Park - Chile's first national park, created in 1926. The Petrohué River crashes through basaltic lava flows from Osorno volcano, forming rapids and turquoise waterfalls against black rock. The name "Petrohué" means "place of mists" referring to vapors produced when lava once contacted water during eruptions. In season, you can observe salmon swimming upstream.

⏱ 60 min
2

Lago Todos los Santos, a glacial-origin lake with turquoise waters surrounded by dense Valdivian forest. From the Petrohué pier, views of Osorno, Puntiagudo, and Tronador volcanoes. This lake is the departure point for the lakes crossing to Argentina (Bariloche).

⏱ 60 min
3

Scenic drive to Osorno volcano's ski center. The paved road climbs in switchbacks through forest to about 1,200 meters altitude. From the viewpoints, plunging views over Lake Llanquihue on one side and Lake Todos los Santos on the other. Osorno volcano (2,652 m), a stratovolcano with a nearly perfect cone, has experienced eleven documented eruptions, the last in 1869.

⏱ 75 min
4

Village of Ensenada, on Lake Llanquihue's east shore at the foot of Osorno volcano. This small rural hamlet serves as a starting point for national park attractions. A few wooden lodges and local restaurants offer curanto (traditional dish cooked underground) and stake-grilled lamb (cordero al palo).

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Rafting on the Petrohué River amid temperate forest with Osorno volcano in the background. Class III rapids, accessible to beginners accompanied by a guide.

⏱ 180 min
B&B Puerto Varas Playa Venado9.9

Puerto Varas

The City of Roses facing Osorno's perfect snow-capped cone

★★★★☆
📍 Castro Chiloé📍 Palafitos Castro📍 Iglesia San Francisco Castro📍 Dalcahue📍 Canal de Chacao📍 Mercado Dalcahue
1

Ferry crossing of the Chacao Channel (about 30 minutes) to Chiloé Island, South America's second-largest island. The island is known for its 16 UNESCO-listed wooden churches, built by Jesuit missionaries in the 17th and 18th centuries using a unique technique blending European know-how and local materials - cypress and alerce wood, without a single nail.

⏱ 45 min
2

Castro, capital of Chiloé founded in 1567 - one of Chile's oldest cities. The palafitos, colorful stilt houses planted in the water, line Castro's fjord. Built by fishermen for direct sea access, they've become the island's symbol. San Francisco de Castro church, UNESCO-listed, stands out with its yellow and mauve facade and entirely wooden interior.

⏱ 90 min
3

Dalcahue, a small fishing port 20 km from Castro. Its Sunday artisan market is renowned for Chilote wool, rush baskets, and seafood. Dalcahue's church, also UNESCO-listed, dominates the village square.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Tasting curanto, Chiloé's traditional dish cooked in a hole dug in the ground over burning stones, covered with nalca leaves. It mixes seafood (mussels, clams), Chilota potatoes (the island cultivates over 200 potato varieties), smoked meat, and chapaleles (flour pancakes).

⏱ 60 min

Return by ferry through the Chacao Channel then drive to Puerto Varas/Puerto Montt (about 3h30 total with crossing). Night in Puerto Varas.

⏱ 210 min

Day 13

Punta Arenas

The southernmost city where three oceans converge

★★★☆☆
📍 Plaza Muñoz Gamero Punta Arenas📍 Palacio Sara Braun📍 Cerro de la Cruz Punta Arenas📍 Cementerio Municipal Punta Arenas📍 Museo Nao Victoria📍 Estrecho de Magallanes
1

Flight Puerto Montt → Punta Arenas (about 2h). Arrival in the southernmost city of the continental South American continent, founded in 1848 on the shores of the Strait of Magellan. Before the Panama Canal opened in 1914, its port was a mandatory stopover for every ship connecting the Atlantic to the Pacific.

⏱ 30 min
2

Plaza Muñoz Gamero, the central square shaded by cypress and birch trees. At its center, the bronze statue of Ferdinand Magellan, the first European to navigate the strait in 1520. North of the square, the Palacio Sara Braun, built in 1895 in French neoclassical style across two floors, testifies to the fortune of 19th-century sheep-farming magnates. Since 1983 it houses the Regional Museum of Magellan.

⏱ 60 min
3

Cerro de la Cruz, a viewpoint overlooking the city. On clear days, the view extends over Punta Arenas' colorful rooftops, the Strait of Magellan, and beyond to Argentine Tierra del Fuego.

⏱ 30 min
4

Municipal Cemetery of Punta Arenas, created in 1894, ranked among the world's most beautiful cemeteries. Grand avenues lined with centennial cypresses, opulent mausoleums of sheep-farming families enriched by wool and gold, marble chapels imported from Carrara, Italy. Also the tomb of the Unknown Indian, a memorial site for decimated indigenous peoples - Selk'nam, Tehuelches, Yaganes - after colonization.

⏱ 60 min
5

Nao Victoria Museum, a few kilometers from the center. Life-size replicas of four historic ships, including the Nao Victoria, the first vessel to complete a circumnavigation under Magellan then Elcano's command (1519-1522). The models allow you to measure the cramped navigation conditions of the 16th century.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll along the waterfront by the Strait of Magellan. With luck, you might spot Commerson's dolphins offshore. The wind often blows hard - welcome to Patagonia.

⏱ 45 min

Day 14

Puerto Natales

Gateway to glaciers that calve icebergs into turquoise fjords

★★☆☆☆
📍 Isla Magdalena📍 Pingüinos Isla Magdalena📍 Puerto Natales📍 Fiordo Última Esperanza📍 Estrecho de Magallanes
1

Isla Magdalena, 35 km northeast of Punta Arenas in the Strait of Magellan. This island of barely 1 km² shelters around 120,000 nesting Magellanic penguins. Classified as "Los Pingüinos" Natural Monument since 1982 along with neighboring Marta Island. The catamaran crossing takes 1h30. On the island, a marked path winds between burrows dug in the ground. Each year between September and March, monogamous couples return to nest in the same spot. Magellanic penguins measure 60 to 75 cm. The red and white lighthouse dominating the island serves as a landmark for sailors of the strait.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Navigation in the Strait of Magellan (round trip to Isla Magdalena). During the crossing, possible observation of imperial cormorants, black-browed albatrosses, and sea lions on neighboring Isla Marta.

⏱ 240 min

Drive Punta Arenas → Puerto Natales (about 3h, 250 km). The road crosses the Patagonian steppe, an immense grassy plain battered by wind. You regularly encounter herds of guanacos and sometimes rheas (South American cousins of the ostrich). 1

Cueva del Milodón, a 30-meter-high, 200-meter-deep cave 24 km from Puerto Natales. In 1895, a piece of mylodon skin was discovered here - a giant herbivorous ground sloth 3 meters tall, extinct about 10,000 years ago. A life-size replica of the animal stands at the cave entrance. Traces of human occupation dating back 14,000 years have also been found here.

⏱ 60 min
2

Entry into Torres del Paine National Park, created in 1959 covering 181,000 hectares. Listed as a Biosphere Reserve by UNESCO. The name "Paine" would come from the Tehuelche word "Payne" meaning "blue" - the omnipresent color of lakes and glaciers. The park borders the Southern Patagonian Ice Field, the world's third-largest ice cap after Antarctica and Greenland.

⏱ 30 min
3

Lago Pehoé, a lake with intense turquoise waters colored by "rock flour" - glacial sediments in suspension. From the shores, views of the Cuernos del Paine, two-toned peaks (black schist base, light granite summit) that evoke bull horns.

⏱ 45 min
4

Salto Grande, a powerful waterfall between Lake Nordenskjöld and Lake Pehoé. A 4 km trail (round trip) leads from the parking lot to Mirador Cuernos, viewpoint over the Cuernos del Paine with Lake Nordenskjöld in the foreground. Count 2h for the complete circuit.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wildlife observation in the park's steppe. Guanacos (wild camelids) are omnipresent - the park reportedly counts 3,000. Darwin's rheas, gray foxes, and with great luck, a puma. Andean condors patrol the sky, recognizable by their wingspan exceeding 3 meters.

Where granite towers pierce the sky above a wilderness without equal

★★☆☆☆
📍 Torres del Paine📍 Base Torres📍 Laguna Torres📍 Valle Ascencio📍 Mirador Base Torres
1

The three Torres del Paine, granite columns that gave the park its name. Torre Sur (2,850 m), Torre Central (2,800 m), and Torre Norte (2,600 m) rise above a turquoise glacial lagoon. These towers are formed from intrusive granite 12 million years old, exposed by erosion of the softer schists that surrounded them.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike to Mirador Base Torres, Patagonia's most famous trek. About 22 km round trip with 850 meters of elevation gain, count 8 to 10 hours. The trail climbs the Ascencio River valley through lenga forests. The final section, called "the moraine," is a steep climb among rocky blocks. The reward at the summit: the turquoise lagoon at the foot of the three granite towers. Recommended departure before dawn to see sunrise on the towers.

Lago Nordenskjöld, a large elongated lake with blue-green waters, bordered by the base of the Cuernos del Paine. The valley is enclosed between the walls of the Paine Grande massif and the Cuernos. You can observe hanging glaciers from which pieces sometimes break off with a dull rumble.

1

Serrano Glacier, in Bernardo O'Higgins National Park, accessible by boat from Puerto Natales via Última Esperanza Fjord ("Last Hope Fjord," named by navigator Juan Ladrillero in 1557 after months searching for a passage to the Pacific). ⏱ 60 min

Activities

Navigation on the Última Esperanza Fjord. The boat runs along cliffs where cormorants nest, passes waterfalls, and approaches Balmaceda glacier (visible but not accessible on foot) before reaching Serrano glacier. Vegetation shifts from steppe to subpolar forest as the fjord penetrates deeper into the cordillera.

⏱ 300 min
