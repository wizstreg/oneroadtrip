Grand Circuit of Crete - 15 Days

📅 15 days📍 15 stops🚗 552 km⏱ 11h05

Heraklion → Agios Nikolaos → Sitia → Ierapetra → Matala → Plakias → Rethymnon → Chania

From Knossos to the Elafonisi lagoon, this 15-day circuit makes a complete loop of Crete by car, from east to west, without backtracking. You'll cross four distinct regions: the Minoan Crete of Heraklion and Lassithi, the preserved far east of Sitia and Zakros, the wild south coast from Matala to Preveli, then the Venetian west of Rethymno and Chania. Each day alternates archaeological sites, spectacular landscapes, and isolated beaches.

✨ Highlights
  • Palace of Knossos: the largest Minoan palace in the Mediterranean, cradle of the civilization that preceded ancient Greece
  • Spinalonga: Europe's last active leper colony until 1957, in a setting of Venetian fortress on an islet off Elounda
  • Vai palm forest: 4,500 endemic palms (Phoenix theophrasti) surrounding a fine sand beach at the far east of the island
  • Matala: caves carved into the cliff, Roman tombs squatted by hippies in the 1960s-70s
  • Samaria Gorge: 16 km through the White Mountains, Greece's longest gorges, open May to October
  • Elafonisi: lagoon with turquoise waters and pink sand, protected nature reserve in the far southwest
📆 Best period

April, May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 15 and 19 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather, blooming landscapes, ideal temperatures, fewer crowds, excellent value, Easter celebrations, optimal conditions.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather, peak tourist season, highest prices, crowded attractions, perfect for islands, vibrant nightlife, festival season.

Fall (September-October): Warm seas, comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, excellent weather, good value, harvest season, ideal photography.

Winter (November-March): Mild weather, lowest prices, minimal crowds, rainy season, ski season mountains, cultural focus, cozy atmosphere.

🌍 What to discover in GR?

Ancient civilization birthplace with stunning islands, incredible history, and Mediterranean lifestyle. Experience mythology sites, beautiful beaches, and authentic Greek culture. The country offers cultural depth with natural beauty and culinary excellence. Greece provides historical significance with island paradise and cultural authenticity in Mediterranean setting.

📋 Important notes

Car rental mandatory. Samaria Gorge (D12) is open May to October only. Check boat schedules for Spinalonga (D3) and Chrissi (D6) depending on season.

Day 1

Heraklion

Capital of Crete, guardian of Knossos Palace and Greece's greatest Minoan museum.

★★★★★
📍 Knossos Palace Heraklion📍 Archaeological Museum Heraklion📍 Koules Fortress Heraklion📍 Lions Square Heraklion
1

Start right at opening time (8am) with the Palace of Knossos, 5 km south of Heraklion. This is the largest Minoan palace in Crete, occupied from 2700 to 1450 BC. British archaeologist Arthur Evans devoted 35 years of his life and much of his fortune to excavating and reconstructing the site from 1900 onwards—his painted concrete reconstructions are controversial, but without him the site would be unrecognizable. You'll see the throne room (the oldest in Europe), the royal apartments, the oil storage magazines, and the bull-leaping frescoes. Arrive early without fail: after 10am, cruise ship buses invade the site.

⏱ 120 min
2

In the afternoon, continue to the Heraklion Archaeological Museum, Greece's most important museum for the Minoan period. Its 22 halls trace 5,500 years of history. Don't miss the Phaistos Disc (17th century BC, spiral script never deciphered), the snake goddesses of Knossos, or the bee pendant from Malia in gold. Air-conditioned and quiet in the afternoon—the basic rule for Heraklion is exactly that: Knossos in the morning, museum in the afternoon.

⏱ 120 min
3

Late afternoon, wander through the old town. The Koules Fortress guards the entrance to the Venetian harbor—built in the 16th century, it resisted a 21-year Ottoman siege, the longest in history. Walk up to Lions Square (Plateia Venizelou) with the Morosini Fountain from 1628 and the 17th-century Venetian Loggia. On the ramparts, a climb leads to the tomb of Nikos Kazantzakis, author of Zorba the Greek, buried without a cross at his own request.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

In the evening, settle onto a terrace around Lions Square or the old port. The 1866 Street (covered market) is open during the day for buying herbs, cheeses, and local products. The old town is entirely pedestrianized and lively—one of the few Greek capitals that functions year-round.

Day 2

Agios Nikolaos

Where legends pool in a bottomless lake beneath the gaze of ancient gods.

★★★☆☆
📍 Lassithi Plateau Crete📍 Dikteon Cave Psychro📍 Panagia Kera Kritsa📍 Lake Voulismeni Agios Nikolaos
1

On the way to Agios Nikolaos, take the detour through the Lassithi Plateau, perched at 800 m altitude in the Dikti Mountains. A 23 km circular road links the 18 villages of this fertile plain dotted with white windmills with triangular sails. These thousands of irrigation windmills that once made the plateau famous no longer really turn, but the scenery remains striking. It's the only permanently inhabited zone above 800 m in Crete.

⏱ 45 min
2

At Psychro, climb up to the Dikteon Cave—20 minutes' walk from the parking at 1,025 m altitude. According to Greek mythology, this is where Rhea hid the infant Zeus to protect him from his father Cronos, who devoured his children. The cave reveals imposing stalactites and stalagmites and an interior lake. Arthur Evans uncovered Minoan votive objects here as early as 1896. Mules are offered for the climb—practical if the heat is strong.

⏱ 90 min
3

On the way down to the coast, stop 1 km from the village of Kritsa at the Panagia Kera church, one of the best-preserved Byzantine treasures in Crete. Founded in the 13th century, its three naves are covered with 13th-14th century frescoes among the finest on the island. Filmmaker Jules Dassin shot scenes from 'He Who Must Die' here in 1957.

⏱ 45 min
4

Arrival in Agios Nikolaos. Lake Voulismeni in the heart of the town has intrigued people for centuries: its sheer walls and deep dark waters of 64 m long made locals believe it was bottomless. Legend says Artemis and Athena bathed there. In reality it's the mouth of an underground river, connected to the sea by a canal dug by an Ottoman governor in the 19th century. The path around it (20 minutes) is ideal in late afternoon.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Agios Nikolaos really comes alive in the evening, when the terraces around the lake and harbor fill with life. Settle in for a raki and mezze—the local tsikoudia naturally accompanies the culinary introduction. The Archaeological Museum of Agios Nikolaos, steps from the lake, is worth an hour in the morning if you have time left: it houses the 'Goddess of Myrtos,' a unique Minoan anthropomorphic vase.

Day 3

Agios Nikolaos

Where legends pool in a bottomless lake beneath the gaze of ancient gods.

★★★☆☆
📍 Spinalonga Island Elounda📍 Elounda Village Crete📍 Plaka Village Spinalonga📍 Gournia Archaeological Site Crete
1

Spinalonga—take the boat from Plaka (a few minutes, €8/person) or from Elounda. This 85-hectare islet was fortified by the Venetians in the 16th century to control the entrance to the Gulf of Elounda. The Venetians held out until 1715, 46 years after the fall of the rest of Crete to the Ottomans—proof of the power of these fortifications. From 1903 to 1957, the island served as a leper colony, one of the last in Europe. The lepers, abandoned without care at first, eventually organized themselves into a community with shops and craftsmen. Victoria Hislop's novel 'The Island,' which sold 6 million copies in 35 countries, gave them justice. Walk through the 'Dante tunnel' at the entrance, wander the cobbled streets of the abandoned village, climb to the ramparts for views of the coast. Allow 3 hours.

⏱ 180 min
2

On the way back from Spinalonga, stop at the archaeological site of Gournia, halfway between Agios Nikolaos and Ierapetra. It's one of the best-preserved Minoan towns, dating from 1600 BC: its paved streets, two-story houses, and a central palatial building are still perfectly legible. Rarely crowded—ideal for an archaeo-tourist who wants authenticity without crowds.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Enjoy the afternoon swimming in Mirabello Bay, the largest of the Greek islands. The beaches around Agios Nikolaos are small but accessible on foot—Almyros beach, 2 km south, is one of the cleanest in the region. Go in late afternoon to avoid the midday heat.

⏱ 120 min

Day 4

Sitia

The quiet harbor where Europe's only palm forest meets the Libyan Sea.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Vai Palm Beach Crete📍 Toplou Monastery Sitia📍 Itanos Archaeological Site Crete📍 Kazarma Castle Sitia
1

The Vai palm forest is earned—70 km from Agios Nikolaos—but the spectacle justifies everything: 4,500 palms of the endemic species Phoenix theophrasti surround a fine sand beach caressed by translucent water. It's the only palm forest in Europe. Legend says it was planted by Egyptian soldiers in the 3rd century BC or imported by Arabs in 824; in reality, these palms are remnants of natural palm groves that covered all Cretan coasts in Minoan times. The site is classified Natura 2000. Arrive before 9am—in high season, it's packed after 10am.

⏱ 120 min
2

Twelve km southwest of Vai, Toplou Monastery (Moni Toplou) stands like a fortress in the middle of an almost desert landscape—its 33 m bell tower is visible from afar. Founded in the 15th century, it's one of the most important historical monasteries in Crete, pillaged several times by pirates. Its museum houses a remarkable collection of Byzantine icons including the great 'Megali ei Kyrie' icon from 1770, considered a masterpiece of Cretan painting.

⏱ 60 min
3

Near Vai, ancient Itanos is little-visited but interesting: an important city of Hellenistic-Roman Crete, its remains cover Minoan, Classical, and Roman periods. The adjacent beach is calm with excellent water—a good spot for a swim after the morning visits.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Arrive in Sitia in the evening. This small port town spreads its terraces facing the Aegean Sea in a pleasantly non-touristy atmosphere. Enjoy the evening dining at the harbor—tavernas offer local products including Sitia olive oil (PDO), wine, and Varvaki raki. The Kazarma castle (16th-century Venetian fortification) can be visited in 30 minutes from the center.

⏱ 90 min

Day 5

Sitia

The quiet harbor where Europe's only palm forest meets the Libyan Sea.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Kato Zakros Minoan Palace📍 Valley of the Dead Zakros Crete📍 Archaeological Museum Sitia
1

The Valley of the Dead (Nekropolis) descends from the village of Ano Zakros to Kato Zakros beach in about 2 hours (8 km). It owes its eerie name to Minoan tombs carved into its limestone walls—the Minoans believed those buried there would continue to live in the beauty of the landscape. Centuries-old plane trees, oleanders, and springs make the journey a pleasure. The gorge is passable year-round but start early in summer because of the heat.

⏱ 120 min
2

At the gorge exit, you emerge at the Minoan palace of Kato Zakros—Crete's fourth-largest, and the only one unearthed intact and unlooted by Nikolaos Platon in 1961. From 1900 to 1450 BC, its 300 rooms developed around a central courtyard of 8,000 m². Its position made it the great commercial port of eastern Crete with the Near and Middle East. Excavations yielded gold-covered swords, ivory, and an amphora depicting a temple with wild beasts.

⏱ 90 min
3

On the way back to Sitia, the Archaeological Museum of Sitia contextualizes what you've just seen. It presents pieces from Zakros, Palaikastro, and Mochlos, notably clay tablets engraved in Linear A (the still-undeciphered Minoan script) and an exceptional chryselephantine figurine—ivory and gold.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Kato Zakros beach is calm, isolated, and the water crystal clear. After the hike through the gorge and palace visit, swim before getting back in the car. To return to Sitia, take the regular road via Zakros (25 min) rather than climbing back up the gorge on foot in full sun.

⏱ 90 min

Day 6

Ierapetra

Europe's southernmost town, gateway to a desert island with Caribbean waters.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Chrissi Island Ierapetra📍 Kales Fortress Ierapetra📍 Koutsounari Beach Ierapetra
1

Ierapetra, Europe's southernmost town (continent and islands included), preserves an old Ottoman quarter with a 17th-century fountain and the Venetian fortress Kales overlooking the old port. Built in the 13th century, restored by the Venetians then modified by the Ottomans, the fortress is well-preserved and can be visited from the harbor in 30 minutes.

⏱ 40 min
2

Chrissi Island (Gaidouronisi), 10 nautical miles offshore, deserves the whole day. This uninhabited island with exceptionally pure white sand beaches shelters thousand-year-old cedars, some exceeding 200 years. The water is turquoise and shallow. Boats serve it daily from Ierapetra (about 50 minutes crossing). Bring a picnic and sufficient water—services on the island are very limited.

⏱ 240 min

Activities

Returning from Chrissi in late afternoon, follow the coastal road east toward Koutsounari—several kilometers of fine sand nearly deserted outside July-August. For the evening, Ierapetra's harbor with its fish tavernas is lively and authentic.

⏱ 480 min

Day 7

Matala

Where Roman tombs became hippie havens and cliffs blush volcanic red.

★★★☆☆
📍 Phaistos Minoan Palace Crete📍 Agia Triada Archaeological Site Crete📍 Matala Caves Crete📍 Red Beach Matala Crete
1

The Minoan palace of Phaistos, 45 km west of Ierapetra, is Crete's second-largest after Knossos. Unlike the latter, it hasn't been reconstructed—the ruins are authentic, which divides visitors but gives a truer vision of real archaeology. The first palace dates from 2000 BC; the second, rebuilt after an earthquake around 1700 BC, is the most visible. This is where the famous Phaistos Disc was discovered in 1908—the original is in the Heraklion museum.

⏱ 90 min
2

Three km from Phaistos, Agia Triada was probably a Minoan royal villa connected to Phaistos by a paved road. Its painted limestone sarcophagus (around 1400 BC, displayed at the Heraklion museum) is one of the major pieces of Minoan art. The site is little-visited, allowing you to linger in rare calm.

⏱ 60 min
3

Matala: this coastal village preserves a unique bohemian atmosphere. The caves carved into the limestone cliffs of the beach served as Roman tombs in the 1st-2nd centuries AD. In the 1960s-70s, hippies from around the world settled there—Bob Dylan and Joni Mitchell supposedly stayed here according to local legend. The caves are open for a few euros today. Red Beach, a 20-minute walk south, is an isolated cove with red-orange sand of volcanic origin—one of the most beautiful beaches in southern Crete.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Swim at Matala in late afternoon. The main beach is family-friendly with calm waters. Red Beach (accessible on foot via a 20-minute trail) is worth the effort for the more courageous—the Matala cliff seen from the water, with its caves open like honeycomb cells, is a spectacle in itself.

⏱ 150 min

Day 8

Plakias

The drowsy base for Preveli's river-fed lagoon where Africa feels a breath away.

★★★★☆
📍 Preveli Beach Crete📍 Preveli Monastery Crete📍 Kourtaliotiko Gorge Crete📍 Damnoni Beach Plakias
1

Preveli Beach (Palm Beach) is one of the most beautiful in the Mediterranean. The Megalo Potamos river descends from the Kourtaliotiko gorges and flows into the Libyan Sea after crossing a tropical palm grove—a freshwater lagoon separated from the sea by a sand bar. The result looks like the Caribbean. From the parking, count on 15-20 minutes on foot (about 300 steps). The descent is easy, the climb back in full sun much less so—leave early. In July-August, it's very crowded.

⏱ 120 min
2

Preveli Monastery, clinging to cliffs above the gorges, overlooks the Libyan Sea from a dream location. Founded in the 16th-17th century, it played a crucial role in Cretan resistance against the Ottomans in the 19th century, then helped Allied soldiers escape during the Battle of Crete in 1941. The monks sell their honey and olive oil. Cover your shoulders to enter.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Kourtaliotiko gorges, 9 km north of Preveli, are crossed by car on a road that descends between two spectacular limestone walls. An Orthodox chapel—Agios Nikolaos—is literally embedded in the rock mid-gorge. Even if you don't hike, the drive is worth taking slowly.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

The coast around Plakias is full of little-visited small beaches. Damnoni, 3 km east, is a calm family beach with very clear waters—much less known than Preveli. Enjoy the afternoon swimming peacefully before sunset.

⏱ 120 min

Day 9

Rethymnon

A Venetian dream in honey-colored stone, where minarets became bell towers.

★★★☆☆
📍 Arkadi Monastery Rethymno📍 Margarites Village Rethymno📍 Armeni Necropolis Rethymno📍 Venetian Harbor Rethymno
1

Arkadi Monastery, 23 km southeast of Rethymno, is the most emotionally charged site in all of Crete. In November 1866, during the great revolt against the Ottomans, 943 people—women, children, and fighters—took refuge there. Besieged by 2,000 soldiers for three days, they chose to blow up the gunpowder barrels rather than surrender. This heroic act drew international attention to the Cretan cause. Entry is €3. Visit the 16th-century Baroque church (sculpted facade mixing Venetian and Orthodox influences), the museum, and the powder magazine where the explosion occurred.

⏱ 90 min
2

Fifteen km from Arkadi, Margarites is one of the few villages in Crete where potters still work by hand with techniques inherited from Minoan times. The village is charming—narrow lanes, bougainvillea, and a small 14th-century church with Byzantine frescoes. Workshops open to the street let you watch potters at work.

⏱ 45 min
3

Ten km south of Rethymno, the Armeni necropolis dates from the Late Minoan period (1400-1200 BC). More than 200 tombs have been discovered since 1969, some with impressive dimensions with a long dromos (corridor) leading to a burial chamber. The site is in an oak forest, little-visited and well-explained.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Arrive in Rethymno in late afternoon. Save the evening to discover the old Venetian harbor with its Egyptian lighthouse from 1838. The pedestrian streets of the old town are best visited at nightfall when lights illuminate the facades. The Fortezza (16th-century Venetian fortress) is open until evening and offers panoramic views of the city.

⏱ 90 min

Day 10

Rethymnon

A Venetian dream in honey-colored stone, where minarets became bell towers.

★★★☆☆
📍 Fortezza Rethymno📍 Old Town Rethymno📍 Venetian Loggia Rethymno📍 Rethymno Beach
1

Devote the morning to the Fortezza of Rethymno, the 16th-century Venetian fortress perched on Paleokastro hill. Built to protect the town from pirates and Ottomans, it covers 1,400 m² and offers panoramic views from its ramparts over the Aegean Sea and Psiloritis mountains. Inside, a mosque, chapel, old dungeons, and ruined buildings let you imagine garrison life.

⏱ 75 min
2

Descend into the Venetian old town of Rethymno, one of the best-preserved in Crete. The 16th-century Venetian Loggia is considered the island's finest Renaissance building. The Rimondi Fountain from 1629, with its lion heads spouting water, marks the heart of the action. Spot the corbelled wooden houses—Ottoman additions to Venetian facades—and minarets transformed into bell towers after liberation.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Archaeological Museum of Rethymno, facing the Fortezza entrance, presents a collection from Minoan, Mycenaean, and Roman periods. The Historical and Folklore Museum traces Cretan daily life from the 17th-19th centuries with costumes, tools, and furniture. Both can be visited in 1-1.5 hours total.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Rethymno beach stretches 16 km east of town along a palm-lined promenade. Enjoy it in the afternoon before wandering Skrydlof Street (artisan leatherwork) or having coffee at sunset on the old harbor.

⏱ 150 min

Day 11

Chania

The undisputed jewel of Crete, where Byzantine grace meets Venetian grandeur on an impossible harbor.

★★★★☆
📍 Venetian Harbor Chania📍 Janissaries Mosque Chania📍 Venetian Arsenals Chania📍 Maritime Museum of Crete Chania
1

Chania is often called the most beautiful city in Crete—and the Venetian harbor alone justifies the detour. Built in the 14th century, it's dominated by the Egyptian lighthouse from 1838, symbol of the city. Walking along the harbor, you'll pass the Janissaries Mosque (1645)—the first mosque built in Crete, erected right after the Ottoman conquest, recognizable by its pink domes. The great Venetian arsenals where galleys were built are just behind: these immense vaulted halls are among the most impressive in the Mediterranean.

⏱ 75 min
2

Get lost in the old town, a maze of cobbled lanes where every facade betrays centuries of layering—Ottoman wooden balconies on Venetian facades, hidden fountains, palace doorways turned into restaurants. The Etz Hayyim synagogue, carefully restored, testifies to a Jewish community present since antiquity. Skrydlof Street is the place for artisan leather sandals and bags.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Archaeological Museum of Chania is housed in a former 13th-century Venetian Franciscan monastery. Its collections cover the Minoan period through Venetian times with a fine section on Roman mosaics. The Firkas fortress, at the harbor entrance, houses the Maritime Museum of Crete with exhibitions on Cretan maritime traditions.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Taste the Creto-Venetian gastronomy of harbor tavernas: boureki (zucchini and sheep cheese pie), kalitsounia (small cheese or herb turnovers), and aged graviera are local specialties. Chania's covered market (Agora), inspired by the Cherbourg halls, is open mornings.

⏱ 90 min

Day 12

Chania

The undisputed jewel of Crete, where Byzantine grace meets Venetian grandeur on an impossible harbor.

★★★★☆
📍 Samaria Gorge Omalos Crete📍 Agia Roumeli Village Crete📍 Hora Sfakion Crete📍 Samaria National Park Crete
1

The Samaria Gorge—Crete's iconic hike. Sixteen km from the Omalos plateau (1,200 m altitude) to Agia Roumeli on the Libyan Sea, with a 1,200 m descent. The route crosses Samaria National Park, refuge of the Kri-kri (endemic Cretan wild goat), and passes through the abandoned village of Samaria. The highlight is the 'Iron Gates': two rock walls forming only a 3 m wide gap with 300 m high walls. The hike takes 5 to 7 hours depending on pace. At the end, the only way out is by ferry to Hora Sfakion or Sougia—book ahead in July-August.

⏱ 360 min

Activities

Organize this day as a major expedition: bus from Chania to Omalos in the morning (departure around 6:30am), gorge descent, swim at Agia Roumeli, then return ferry to Hora Sfakion and bus back to Chania. Organized excursions from Chania cover this complete circuit. The gorge is open May to October.

⏱ 480 min

Day 13

Chania

The undisputed jewel of Crete, where Byzantine grace meets Venetian grandeur on an impossible harbor.

★★★★☆
📍 Gouverneto Monastery Akrotiri Crete📍 Katholiko Monastery Crete📍 Lake Kournas Crete📍 Stavros Beach Akrotiri Crete
1

The Akrotiri peninsula, 15 km northeast of Chania, is a wild plateau plunging into the Aegean Sea. Gouverneto Monastery (16th century) is the oldest active monastery in Crete still inhabited. From there, a 15-minute descent on a mule track leads to Katholiko Monastery, built directly into a limestone gorge and partially ruined—one of the strangest settings on the island. Further down, the wild beach of Stavros (accessible by car via another road) is famous for serving as the setting for the final dance scene in 'Zorba the Greek.'

⏱ 120 min
2

Lake Kournas, 45 km east of Chania, is the only natural freshwater lake in Crete, nestled at the foot of the White Mountains. The calm turquoise waters, waterside restaurant, and fearless wild ducks make for a pleasant stop—ideal for recovering after yesterday's Samaria Gorge.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

In the afternoon, enjoy one of the beaches near Chania. Nea Chora beach is walkable from the old town. If you want more space, Agii Apostoli 8 km west is quieter with better sand. Good recovery day before tomorrow's big outing to Elafonisi.

⏱ 150 min

Day 14

Chania

The undisputed jewel of Crete, where Byzantine grace meets Venetian grandeur on an impossible harbor.

★★★★☆
📍 Elafonisi Beach Crete📍 Falassarna Crete📍 Balos Lagoon Kissamos Crete
1

Elafonisi is one of the most famous beaches in Europe, 1.5 hours from Chania. Turquoise lagoon waters with shallow depths and white-pink sand, colored by fragments of red shells. The lagoon is ideal for families (you can stand for tens of meters). Wade across to the small islet of Elafonissi, a protected nature reserve sheltering rare plants and loggerhead turtles. Arrive before 9am in July-August—tourist buses arrive in droves after. Full day: limit yourself to Elafonisi + Falassarna to avoid exhaustion.

⏱ 180 min
2

On the way back to Chania, stop at Falassarna—large blonde sand beach famous for its sunsets. It's an ancient Roman port whose quays and mooring blocks are still visible on dry land several hundred meters from the current coast. The region underwent tectonic uplift in the 4th century AD that left the port out of water—a geological phenomenon visible to the naked eye.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Balos Lagoon deserves a full day on its own—if you couldn't fit it into the program, prefer the boat from Kissamos (40 km from Chania): departure around 10:30am, return around 5pm. The shallow waters between the islets of Gramvousa and Imeri are an unreal turquoise, and the 16th-century Venetian castle on Imeri Gramvousa is visible from the boat. The dirt road to access it by car (8 km) is in poor condition—SUV recommended.

⏱ 480 min

Day 15

Chania

The undisputed jewel of Crete, where Byzantine grace meets Venetian grandeur on an impossible harbor.

★★★★☆
📍 Halepa Quarter Chania📍 Municipal Garden Chania📍 Ioannis Daskalogiannis Airport Chania
1

Last morning in Chania. The Halepa district, 2 km east of the old town, is the residential quarter where great Cretan families and consulates settled in the 19th century. This is where Eleftherios Venizelos (1864-1936) was born, the great Greek statesman who played a central role in the liberation and unification of Crete with Greece. His birthplace is visible in the district, and the neighboring Chania Municipal Garden offers a final calm walk.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Last purchases in the old town delicatessens or Skrydlof Street: Cretan extra-virgin olive oil, dried wild thyme, PDO graviera, raki, and thyme honey are the best souvenirs to bring back. Chania airport (Ioannis Daskalogiannis) is 15 km from the center—count on 25 minutes without traffic.

⏱ 90 min
