1

The twin towers of the Grossmünster look stern from the bridge, but climb the 187 steps of the Karlsturm and Zurich unfolds below like a toy city. Built from the 11th century, this is where Zwingli launched the Swiss Reformation in 1519. The bronze doors by Otto Münch are easy to miss - don't.

⏱ 45 min
2

Across the river, the Fraumünster hides its best card inside: five stained-glass windows by Marc Chagall, installed in 1970 when the artist was 83. The blue-dominant palette floods the nave on sunny mornings. Go before 10 AM or you'll share the view with three tour buses.

⏱ 40 min
3

If you only visit one museum in Zurich, make it the Kunsthaus. The Chipperfield extension doubled the space in 2021, and the Giacometti collection alone is worth the detour - nowhere else displays this many of his spindly bronzes in one room. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 90 min
4

Lindenhof hill is a five-minute climb from Bahnhofstrasse but feels like another world. Locals play giant chess on the terrace while you get the best free panorama in the city - river, Old Town, university. Sunset here with a takeaway coffee from Sprüngli is hard to beat.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll the Bahnhofstrasse at your own pace - it starts at the train station and ends at the lake, 1.4 km of the most expensive retail real estate in Europe. The luxury end is toward Paradeplatz. Rumour has it the bank vaults under the tram tracks hold more gold than Fort Knox.

⏱ 60 min

🍽️ LUNCH - Kronenhalle. This Zurich institution has served artists and bankers since 1924, with original Picassos, Mirós and Chagalls on the dining room walls. Order the Zürcher Geschnetzeltes - it's the house classic. Reservations essential, even at noon.

⏱ 90 min

🍽️ DINNER - The Restaurant at The Dolder Grand. Chef Heiko Nieder holds 2 Michelin stars and 19 Gault Millau points. The 10-course tasting menu is a precision exercise in texture and restraint. Ask for a table with the city view. Wine pairing available but the sommelier's off-menu picks are better.

⏱ 150 min

🏨 NIGHT - The Dolder Grand. Perched above the city in its own park, this 1899 landmark has a 4,000 m² spa, an indoor-outdoor pool, and a private art collection (Dalí, Henry Moore, Andy Warhol). The funicular from Römerhof takes you up in 5 minutes.

⏱ 60 min

Stop in Lucerne on the drive south - the Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke) takes five minutes to cross but it's Europe's oldest covered wooden bridge, built in 1333. Look up: the ceiling panels tell the story of Swiss independence in 17th-century paintings. Half were lost in a 1993 fire, the blackened originals still visible alongside the restorations.

⏱ 20 min

Salzburg Lx

Mozart, fortress, Sachertorte - and a 2-star Michelin restaurant inside an aircraft hangar.
Hotel Sacher SalzburgFestung HohensalzburgRestaurant Ikarus Hangar-7Stiftskeller St. Peter Salzburg
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

If you see only one thing in Salzburg, make it the Hohensalzburg Fortress - it dominates the city from a cliff 120 m above the Old Town. The largest fully preserved castle in Central Europe, dating from 1077. Take the Festungsbahn funicular up, explore the state rooms, the medieval torture chamber (grimly fascinating), and the ramparts with views across the Salzach to the Bavarian Alps. Allow 2 hours; the audioguide is worth it.

⏱ 120 min
2

Mozart's Birthplace (Geburtshaus) at Getreidegasse 9 is where Wolfgang Amadeus was born in 1756 and lived until he was 17. The museum is small - three floors of original instruments, letters and family portraits. The violin he played as a child is behind glass on the second floor. Skip the souvenir shop, which is overpriced even by Salzburg standards.

⏱ 60 min
3

Salzburg Cathedral (Salzburger Dom) is a 17th-century Baroque beast - the dome is 71 m high and the interior swallows crowds whole. Mozart was baptised in the font near the entrance in 1756. The organ loft houses five organs that play together during the noon concerts. If you time it right, attend one - tickets are under €10.

⏱ 45 min
4

Mirabell Palace and Gardens were built in 1606 as a love token from an archbishop to his mistress. The gardens are pure geometric Baroque - hedges, fountains, dwarf statues and a clear sightline to the fortress on the hill opposite. Free entry. Film buffs: this is where 'Do-Re-Mi' was filmed in The Sound of Music.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Walk through the Getreidegasse - Salzburg's narrowest and most atmospheric shopping street, hung with ornate wrought-iron guild signs dating back centuries. Every luxury brand has an outpost here, but the real finds are the small independent shops in the connecting passageways (Durchhäuser). The Stiftsbäckerei at St. Peter's has been baking bread since 1160 - oldest bakery in Austria.

⏱ 60 min

🍽️ LUNCH - Stiftskeller St. Peter. Austria's oldest restaurant - documented since 803 AD (yes, the year 803). Charlemagne allegedly dined here. The vaulted rooms feel like a medieval banquet hall. Order the Tafelspitz (boiled beef in broth) or the Salzburger Nockerl for dessert - a soufflé the size of a football. Touristy? Slightly. Worth it? Absolutely.

⏱ 90 min

🍽️ DINNER - Restaurant Ikarus at Hangar-7. Two Michelin stars, and unlike any restaurant you've visited: a glass-domed aircraft hangar housing Red Bull's collection of vintage planes, helicopters and Formula 1 cars. Every month a different world-class guest chef takes over the kitchen - you literally don't know whose food you'll be eating until you arrive. Only 8 tables since the 2025 refit. Wealthy foodies fly in from across Europe for this.

⏱ 180 min

🏨 NIGHT - Hotel Sacher Salzburg. On the banks of the Salzach with direct views of the Old Town and fortress. Same crystal chandeliers and brocade as Vienna's legendary Sacher. Sachertorte waiting in your room at turndown.

Hotel Sacher Salzburg river viewGasthof Goldgasse SalzburgEsszimmer SalzburgSchloss Hellbrunn Wasserspiele

Hellbrunn Palace (Schloss Hellbrunn) is 20 minutes south of the centre and completely different from Mirabell's Baroque formality. Built in 1615 as a pleasure palace, it's famous for the Wasserspiele - trick fountains designed to soak unsuspecting guests at the dinner table. 400 years later, they still work and they still get you wet. The park is free; the fountain tour costs about €15 and is genuinely entertaining.

⏱ 90 min
2

Hangar-7 is worth visiting even if you dined at Ikarus last night. The Red Bull-owned glass dome houses a rotating collection of historic aircraft, Formula 1 cars (including Sebastian Vettel's), and Felix Baumgartner's Stratos space-jump capsule. Free entry. The architecture alone - a futuristic steel-and-glass shell near the airport runway - is worth the drive.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - Gasthof Goldgasse. Hidden in the Old Town on a tiny alley, this restaurant features in the Michelin Guide and serves modern Austrian cuisine with real personality. Almost exclusively Austrian-sourced ingredients. The lunch menu is shorter and more affordable than dinner - a smart move on your last day.

⏱ 90 min

Last walk along the Salzach riverbank toward Makartsteg (the padlock bridge). The view back toward the Old Town with the fortress lit up is Salzburg's postcard shot. If it's your last evening, the Sacher Bar's terrace has the same view with a cocktail in hand.

⏱ 45 min

🍽️ DINNER - Esszimmer. One Michelin star, family-run, and the heart of Salzburg's local fine dining scene. Chef Andreas Kaiblinger describes it as 'honouring grandmother's recipes in a modern language.' The tasting menu changes seasonally; expect game, trout, Alpine herbs, and Austrian wines you won't find outside the country. Intimate, warm, no pretension.

⏱ 120 min

🏨 NIGHT - Hotel Sacher Salzburg (second night). Before checkout tomorrow, breakfast in the winter garden - white tablecloths, silver service, best Viennoiserie in the city. Salzburg Airport is 10 minutes by taxi.
