Zurich Lx

★★★★☆
1

The twin towers of the Grossmünster look stern from the bridge, but climb the 187 steps of the Karlsturm and Zurich unfolds below like a toy city. Built from the 11th century, this is where Zwingli launched the Swiss Reformation in 1519. The bronze doors by Otto Münch are easy to miss - don't.

⏱ 45 min
2

Across the river, the Fraumünster hides its best card inside: five stained-glass windows by Marc Chagall, installed in 1970 when the artist was 83. The blue-dominant palette floods the nave on sunny mornings. Go before 10 AM or you'll share the view with three tour buses.

⏱ 40 min
3

If you only visit one museum in Zurich, make it the Kunsthaus. The Chipperfield extension doubled the space in 2021, and the Giacometti collection alone is worth the detour - nowhere else displays this many of his spindly bronzes in one room. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 90 min
4

Lindenhof hill is a five-minute climb from Bahnhofstrasse but feels like another world. Locals play giant chess on the terrace while you get the best free panorama in the city - river, Old Town, university. Sunset here with a takeaway coffee from Sprüngli is hard to beat.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll the Bahnhofstrasse at your own pace - it starts at the train station and ends at the lake, 1.4 km of the most expensive retail real estate in Europe. The luxury end is toward Paradeplatz. Rumour has it the bank vaults under the tram tracks hold more gold than Fort Knox.

⏱ 60 min

🍽️ LUNCH - Kronenhalle. This Zurich institution has served artists and bankers since 1924, with original Picassos, Mirós and Chagalls on the dining room walls. Order the Zürcher Geschnetzeltes - it's the house classic. Reservations essential, even at noon.

⏱ 90 min

🍽️ DINNER - The Restaurant at The Dolder Grand. Chef Heiko Nieder holds 2 Michelin stars and 19 Gault Millau points. The 10-course tasting menu is a precision exercise in texture and restraint. Ask for a table with the city view. Wine pairing available but the sommelier's off-menu picks are better.

⏱ 150 min

🏨 NIGHT - The Dolder Grand. Perched above the city in its own park, this 1899 landmark has a 4,000 m² spa, an indoor-outdoor pool, and a private art collection (Dalí, Henry Moore, Andy Warhol). The funicular from Römerhof takes you up in 5 minutes.

⏱ 60 min

Zurich Lx

★★★★☆
1

Take the S10 train to the top of Uetliberg (871 m) for a panorama that stretches from the city to the Bernese Alps on clear days. The hike down through the forest takes about 90 minutes and drops you near the lake. Skip the observation tower restaurant - the view is identical from the free terrace.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Old Town's right bank - Niederdorf - is Zurich's oldest nightlife quarter, but by day it's a maze of cobblestone alleys, antique shops and independent cafés. The Augustinergasse with its painted bay windows is the most photographed street in the city. Less touristy than Bahnhofstrasse and far more interesting.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Afternoon lake cruise on the Zürichsee. The short round-trip (1h30) goes to Rapperswil and back. On a clear day the Alps appear behind the water like a painted backdrop. First-class seats on the upper deck are worth the premium.

⏱ 90 min

🍽️ LUNCH - La Rôtisserie at Hotel Storchen. Terrace directly over the Limmat with the Grossmünster framed in the background. Classic French-Swiss menu - the pike-perch is local and excellent. One of the few waterfront lunch spots in Zurich that's actually good, not just scenic.

⏱ 90 min

🍽️ DINNER - IGNIV Zürich by Andreas Caminada. Sharing-style fine dining inside the Marktgasse Hotel in Niederdorf. Caminada earned 3 stars at Schloss Schauenstein; this outpost has 2 Michelin stars with a playful, sharing-plates approach. The bar downstairs was named Switzerland's best newcomer bar in 2021.

⏱ 120 min

🏨 NIGHT - The Dolder Grand (second night). Use the spa tonight - six different heat rooms and the outdoor whirlpool overlooks the city lights. Late-night room service until 1 AM.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 123 km · 1h45

Lucerne Lx

★★★☆☆
1

Stop in Lucerne on the drive south - the Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke) takes five minutes to cross but it's Europe's oldest covered wooden bridge, built in 1333. Look up: the ceiling panels tell the story of Swiss independence in 17th-century paintings. Half were lost in a 1993 fire, the blackened originals still visible alongside the restorations.

⏱ 30 min
2

Two blocks inland, the Lion Monument (Löwendenkmal) is carved directly into the sandstone cliff - a dying lion pierced by a spear, mourning the Swiss Guards massacred at the Tuileries in 1792. Mark Twain called it the saddest piece of stone in the world. It takes 10 minutes to see but stays with you longer.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

🍽️ Quick coffee stop at Café Luz on the lakefront before getting back on the road toward Andermatt. Try a Luxemburgerli if Sprüngli spoiled you - Lucerne's bakeries have their own version.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 65 km · 55min

Andermatt Lx

★★★☆☆
1

The Schöllenen Gorge feels like the earth split open. Vertical rock walls, the Reuss River roaring below, and the Devil's Bridge (Teufelsbrücke) spanning the gap since the 18th century. Legend says the villagers tricked the devil by sending a goat across first. Behind the old bridge restaurant, a monument carved into the cliff honors Russian general Suvorov's 1799 battle against the French. 30 minutes is enough for the circular walk.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Urseren Valley Museum is housed in a 1786 patrician house and covers everything from Alpine farming to the career of local ski legend Bernhard Russi. Small, well-curated, and useful context before you tackle the passes. Open June to October.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - The Restaurant at The Chedi Andermatt. Four open studio kitchens, Asian-European fusion, and a 5-metre glass cheese tower that Swiss cheese lovers will photograph more than the mountains. 14 Gault Millau points. The lunch menu is lighter and more affordable than dinner.

⏱ 90 min

🍽️ DINNER - The Japanese by The Chedi. One Michelin star, 17 Gault Millau points - officially the best Japanese restaurant in Switzerland. The Kaiseki 5-course menu is a masterclass. Or go casual at the sushi bar. Either way, the contrast of Japanese precision in a Swiss Alpine setting is surreal.

⏱ 120 min

🏨 NIGHT - The Chedi Andermatt. Asian minimalism meets Alpine chalet. Every room has a fireplace, a Hästens mattress, and a view of the Gemsstock. The 2,400 m² spa has a 35-metre pool and three hot-spring pools.

⏱ 60 min

Oberalp Pass Lx

★★★★★

Activities

Drive the Oberalp Pass (2,048 m) toward Disentis and back - 32 km of gentle bends, nine hairpins, and the photogenic Oberalpsee at the summit. A red lighthouse at the top marks the source of the Rhine. This is the warm-up pass: scenic, not scary, perfect for getting your mountain-driving confidence up. Open late May to October.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 32 km · 40min

Andermatt Lx

★★★☆☆
1

Sanner Fortress Andermatt was top secret until recently - a Cold War underground defense complex carved into the mountain. One of Switzerland's largest hidden military structures, now open for visits June through October. The guided tour lasts about an hour and reveals the paranoiac engineering of a country that planned for everything.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - Back at The Chedi for a light lunch on the terrace. Or pack a picnic from the hotel and eat at Oberalpsee - the staff will prepare one if you ask the concierge the night before.

⏱ 60 min

🏨 NIGHT - The Chedi Andermatt (second night). The Cigar Library has a strong single-malt selection. The Wine Library hosts private tastings on request.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 172 km · 2h45

Furka Pass Lx

★★★★★

Activities

Drive the Furka Pass (2,429 m) - the one from the James Bond Goldfinger chase. This is the real deal: tight hairpins, steep drops, no guardrails in places. At the top, the retreating Rhone Glacier is visible (shrinking fast - see it while you can). The old Belvédère hotel at the summit is abandoned but photogenic. Return to Andermatt the same way or continue toward the Grimsel if you want more.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 50min

Saint Moritz Lx

★★★☆☆
1

Worth the detour for one painting alone: the Segantini Museum is a purpose-built rotunda dedicated to Giovanni Segantini, the painter who made the Engadin light famous. His triptych 'Life, Nature, Death' fills the top-floor dome and is one of the most powerful things you'll see in any Swiss museum. Small enough to absorb in an hour.

⏱ 60 min
2

Walk the shoreline path around Lake St. Moritz - the full loop is about 4 km and flat, perfect after days of mountain driving. In summer the water reflects the peaks so sharply it looks digitally enhanced. In winter this same lake hosts polo matches and horse racing on ice.

⏱ 60 min
3

Via Serlas is St. Moritz's answer to the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré: Cartier, Dior, Miu Miu, Yves Saint Laurent, all within 200 metres. Even if you're not buying, the window displays are worth the stroll. The Badrutt's Palace ground floor houses several of these boutiques.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - Chesa Veglia at Badrutt's Palace. This 1658 Engadin farmhouse, now part of the hotel, serves three different restaurants under one timbered roof: pizzeria, grill, and the more formal Patrizier Stuben. The cheese fondue in the Patrizier is absurdly good - made with three local cheeses and served in the original copper pot.

⏱ 90 min

🍽️ DINNER - IGNIV by Andreas Caminada, St. Moritz. Located inside Badrutt's Palace Hotel, this is Caminada's sharing-plates concept adapted for the Engadin. Holding 2 Michelin stars. The portions arrive in waves - plan for 2.5 hours minimum. The wine list leans Swiss-Austrian and is stronger than most international fine dining.

⏱ 150 min

🏨 NIGHT - Badrutt's Palace Hotel. Since 1896, this landmark has hosted Hitchcock to Hepburn. The infinity pool overlooking the lake is the largest outdoor pool in St. Moritz. Butler service available on request.

⏱ 60 min

Saint Moritz Lx

★★★☆☆
1

Take the Muottas Muragl funicular (2,456 m) for what the Swiss consider the most beautiful view in the Engadin - three lakes, the Bernina massif, and the entire Upper Engadin valley spread out below. There's a panoramic restaurant at the top, but the view from the free terrace is identical. Early morning light is best.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - Hauser Restaurant, Via Traunter Plazzas. A St. Moritz institution since 1893 - less glitzy than the palace restaurants, more authentic. Famous for its Engadiner Nusstorte (caramelized walnut tart). Locals eat here, which tells you everything.

⏱ 75 min

🍽️ DINNER - Talvo by Dalsass. Set in a 17th-century Engadin farmhouse in Champfèr, just outside St. Moritz. Now led by chef Kevin Fernandez, who earned the restaurant its first Michelin star (Guide 2025). The kitchen blends Mediterranean and Alpine traditions using almost exclusively local and Italian ingredients. Fireplace dining in winter, garden in summer.

⏱ 120 min

🏨 NIGHT - Badrutt's Palace (second night). Hit the King's Club - the hotel's legendary basement nightclub, packing in the crowd since the 1970s. Hotel guests and members only.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 68 km · 1h15

Passo Dello Stelvio Lx

★★★★★
1

The Stelvio Pass (2,757 m) is the second-highest paved road in the Alps and the one every driving enthusiast eventually has to conquer. Approaching from the north (Prato allo Stelvio), 48 numbered hairpins stack up the mountainside like a wall of asphalt - the famous 'North Face.' The Bormio side is arguably the better drive: faster flow, long sightlines, tunnels carved into the rock. Either way, allow 2-3 hours and go early to beat the motorcycle swarms.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Stop at the summit for the obligatory photo with the 'Passo dello Stelvio 2757 m' sign. There's a handful of souvenir shops and a couple of cafés - don't expect gourmet, but the hot chocolate is earned. On a clear day you can see deep into the Ortler Alps. The retreating glacier is visible to the south - summer skiing still operates here, usually May to November.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 45min

Bormio Lx

★★★☆☆
1

Morning walk through Bormio's medieval centre. The town predates the Romans and still has a compact old quarter with stone arcades, frescoed churches, and the 13th-century Torre delle Ore. It feels genuinely lived-in - not a ski-resort museum. The Kuerc civic tower on Piazza Cavour has been the town's meeting point since the Middle Ages.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - Ristorante Kuerc in Bormio's old town centre. Valtellina cuisine done right: pizzoccheri (buckwheat pasta with potatoes, cabbage and Bitto cheese), sciatt (crispy fried cheese fritters), and bresaola from the valley. Unpretentious, local, and exactly what you want after three hours of hairpins.

⏱ 75 min

🍽️ DINNER - Ristorante Salone dei Balli at QC Grand Hotel Bagni Nuovi. Traditional Valtellina cuisine in a grand Art Nouveau ballroom with over 100 local wines on the list. The venison with polenta taragna and wild berries is the standout. Reserve when you check in - only hotel guests can dine here.

⏱ 120 min

🏨 NIGHT - QC Grand Hotel Bagni Nuovi. Art Nouveau palace from 1836, now a 5-star thermal spa hotel in the Stelvio National Park. Over 30 thermal treatments: outdoor infinity pools facing the mountains, saunas carved into rock, hot tubs fed by natural springs.

⏱ 60 min

Bormio Lx

★★★☆☆
1

Morning walk through Bormio's medieval centre. The town predates the Romans and still has a compact old quarter with stone arcades, frescoed churches, and the 13th-century Torre delle Ore. It feels genuinely lived-in - not a ski-resort museum. The Kuerc civic tower on Piazza Cavour has been the town's meeting point since the Middle Ages.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Half-day hike in the Stelvio National Park. The Valle dei Forni trail leads to the Forni Glacier viewpoint - one of the largest valley glaciers in Italy. The path is moderate (about 2h round trip from Rifugio Forni) and rewards with close-up views of crevasses and icefalls. The rifugio serves simple but solid mountain food if you want lunch up there.

⏱ 180 min

🍽️ LUNCH - Rifugio Forni if you hiked, or Osteria Belvedere at the hotel if you stayed. The Belvedere does rustic Alpine dinner food (fondue, raclette, grilled meats) with a terrace view over the valley.

⏱ 60 min

Afternoon soak in the QC Terme outdoor thermal pools. After the hike, this is non-negotiable. The pools are staggered at different temperatures, and the views of the Ortler massif from the infinity pool are absurd. Stay until sunset - the light on the peaks turns gold then pink.

⏱ 120 min

🍽️ DINNER - Enoteca Guanella in Bormio centre. Smaller, more intimate than the hotel restaurant. Strong local wine selection (Valtellina Sfursat is the region's top red - similar to an Amarone but from Nebbiolo). The tasting plates of local cheeses and cured meats are a meal in themselves.

⏱ 90 min

🏨 NIGHT - QC Grand Hotel Bagni Nuovi (second night). Try the 'emotional path' - a circuit of hot, cold, steam and salt rooms. Best done before dinner.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 185 km · 4h30

Passo Di Giau Lx

★★★★★
1

Jagged Dolomite towers on both sides, meadows so green they look Photoshopped - the approach to Cortina d'Ampezzo via the Passo di Giau (2,236 m) is one of the most photogenic drives in the Alps. The town itself earned the title 'Queen of the Dolomites' long before the 2026 Winter Olympics put it back on the front page. Allow extra time; you will stop for photos.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 35min

Cortina D Ampezzo Lx

★★★☆☆
1

Walk the Corso Italia, Cortina's pedestrianised main street - equal parts luxury shopping and people-watching. The bell tower of the Basilica dei Santi Filippo e Giacomo anchors the centre. In summer, the café terraces are the real theatre: Italian glamour meets hiking boots, fur coats next to fleece.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take the Freccia nel Cielo (Arrow in the Sky) cable car up to Tofana di Mezzo (3,244 m) - the highest point you can reach without climbing gear in the Dolomites. Three stages, the last one emerging above the clouds on most mornings. The panoramic terrace at the top offers a 360° view across the Dolomite range. Dress warm - it's often below freezing even in July.

⏱ 120 min

🍽️ LUNCH - SanBrite. Only seven tables in a room of recycled wood with a picture window onto the Ampezzo Dolomites. Chef Riccardo Gaspari grows and farms most of his ingredients at the adjoining El Brite de Larieto farmstead. One Michelin star, zero pretension. The meal starts with mountains of homemade butter and bread with extraordinary texture. Desserts are made without added sugar - and they're the best course.

⏱ 120 min

🍽️ DINNER - Ristorante Tivoli. On the road to the Falzarego Pass, at the base of the Tofane. Chef Graziano Prest has held his Michelin star since 2002 - Cortina's most decorated year-round table. The kitchen bridges Alpine mountain tradition and Venetian seafood (fresh fish arrives daily from Chioggia). The wine cellar leans French and historic Italian. Plan for 2.5 hours.

⏱ 150 min

🏨 NIGHT - Cristallo, a Luxury Collection Hotel. Cortina's original grande dame, opened in 1901. 73 rooms mixing Belle Époque bones with contemporary design. Spa with indoor pool, Turkish bath, and Dolomite-view treatment rooms. Free shuttle to town.

⏱ 60 min

Cinque Torri Lx

★★★★★
1

Drive to the Cinque Torri (Five Towers) - a cluster of Dolomite rock pillars that look like a giant's abandoned chess pieces. The short hike from the Averau chairlift (30 minutes) takes you to World War I trenches and open-air fortifications carved into the rock at 2,300 m, with interpretive panels. The contrast of war remnants and pristine mountain scenery is sobering and stays with you.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - Rifugio Averau, at the top of the Cinque Torri chairlift. Mountain rifugio cooking at its best: canederli (bread dumplings in broth), speck, polenta with deer ragù. The terrace faces the Marmolada glacier. Simple, hearty, and the views do half the work.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h05

Cortina D Ampezzo Lx

★★★☆☆

Activities

🍽️ DINNER - Il Vizietto di Cortina. Back in town, more casual than the Michelin spots but beloved locally. Wood-fired focaccia, handmade pasta, and a wine list that punches above its weight class. Good for the night when you don't want a 3-hour tasting menu but still want to eat well.

⏱ 90 min

🏨 NIGHT - Cristallo Hotel (second night). The 1224 Terrazza bar on the Grand Hotel Savoia's roof is a short drive for a nightcap with a panoramic view - Cortina's best evening spot.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h05

Lago Di Braies Lx

★★★★★
1

If you have the energy, continue to Lago di Braies (Pragser Wildsee) - about 1h15 north of Cortina. This turquoise lake surrounded by vertical Dolomite cliffs is arguably the most photographed spot in the Italian Alps. Arrive before 9 AM or after 5 PM to avoid the Instagram crowds. Boat rental (€15/30 min) is the classic shot, but the shoreline walk is free and just as beautiful.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 165 km · 3h15

Heiligenblut Lx

★★★☆☆
1

A slender Gothic church spire against a wall of snow-capped peaks - that's Heiligenblut, the southern gateway to the Grossglockner High Alpine Road and one of Austria's most photographed villages. The Pfarrkirche (Church of St. Vincent) dates from the 15th century and houses a remarkable winged altarpiece by the workshop of Michael Pacher. Five minutes to see, hard to forget.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

🍽️ Quick stop for coffee and strudel at Café Dorfstub'm in Heiligenblut's centre before tackling the Grossglockner road. Homemade apple strudel with vanilla sauce - proper Austrian fuel.

⏱ 25 min

🏨 NIGHT - Chalet Hotel Senger. Family-run 4-star at the foot of the Grossglockner. Wood-panelled rooms with mountain views, excellent half-board restaurant using local game and herbs. The terrace faces the Pfarrkirche spire.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 48 km · 1h30

Fusch An Der Grossglocknerstrasse Lx

★★★☆☆
1

The Grossglockner High Alpine Road is Austria's answer to the Stelvio - 48 km, 36 hairpins, climbing to 2,504 m at the Hochtor tunnel. But where the Stelvio is aggressive, the Grossglockner is majestic: wider, better surfaced, and with built-in viewpoints every few kilometres. Budget 4 hours minimum - not because the road demands it, but because the stops will.

⏱ 240 min

Activities

Drive the northern descent toward Ferleiten - gentler bends, greener pastures, and alpine meadows thick with wildflowers in June-July. The toll is €46.50 per car (2026 prices). If you want to drive back up and down again, your ticket is valid for the full calendar day.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 85 km · 1h15

Edelweiss Spitze Lx

★★★★★
1

Edelweiss-Spitze (2,571 m) is the highest viewpoint on the road - a short detour off the main route that most people skip. The 360° panorama from the stone tower covers over 30 peaks above 3,000 m. On a clear morning, you can see into three countries. No entrance fee beyond the road toll.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 12 km · 20min

Kaiser Franz Josefs Hoehe Lx

★★★★★
1

Kaiser-Franz-Josefs-Höhe is the grand finale: a terrace facing Austria's highest peak (Grossglockner, 3,798 m) and the Pasterze Glacier, the largest glacier in the Eastern Alps. The glacier has retreated dramatically - comparison photos from the 1900s at the visitor centre are sobering. A short trail leads closer to the ice, but watch for loose rock. The marmots here are completely fearless.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - Panoramarestaurant Kaiser-Franz-Josefs-Höhe. Not fine dining by any stretch, but the terrace has arguably the most dramatic lunch view in Austria. Stick to simple dishes: Kaiserschmarren (shredded pancake), Wiener Schnitzel, goulash soup. The Grossglockner right in front of you does the rest.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 55min

Salzburg Lx

★★★☆☆
1

If you see only one thing in Salzburg, make it the Hohensalzburg Fortress - it dominates the city from a cliff 120 m above the Old Town. The largest fully preserved castle in Central Europe, dating from 1077. Take the Festungsbahn funicular up, explore the state rooms, the medieval torture chamber (grimly fascinating), and the ramparts with views across the Salzach to the Bavarian Alps. Allow 2 hours; the audioguide is worth it.

⏱ 120 min
2

Mozart's Birthplace (Geburtshaus) at Getreidegasse 9 is where Wolfgang Amadeus was born in 1756 and lived until he was 17. The museum is small - three floors of original instruments, letters and family portraits. The violin he played as a child is behind glass on the second floor. Skip the souvenir shop, which is overpriced even by Salzburg standards.

⏱ 60 min
3

Salzburg Cathedral (Salzburger Dom) is a 17th-century Baroque beast - the dome is 71 m high and the interior swallows crowds whole. Mozart was baptised in the font near the entrance in 1756. The organ loft houses five organs that play together during the noon concerts. If you time it right, attend one - tickets are under €10.

⏱ 45 min
4

Mirabell Palace and Gardens were built in 1606 as a love token from an archbishop to his mistress. The gardens are pure geometric Baroque - hedges, fountains, dwarf statues and a clear sightline to the fortress on the hill opposite. Free entry. Film buffs: this is where 'Do-Re-Mi' was filmed in The Sound of Music.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Walk through the Getreidegasse - Salzburg's narrowest and most atmospheric shopping street, hung with ornate wrought-iron guild signs dating back centuries. Every luxury brand has an outpost here, but the real finds are the small independent shops in the connecting passageways (Durchhäuser). The Stiftsbäckerei at St. Peter's has been baking bread since 1160 - oldest bakery in Austria.

⏱ 60 min

🍽️ LUNCH - Stiftskeller St. Peter. Austria's oldest restaurant - documented since 803 AD (yes, the year 803). Charlemagne allegedly dined here. The vaulted rooms feel like a medieval banquet hall. Order the Tafelspitz (boiled beef in broth) or the Salzburger Nockerl for dessert - a soufflé the size of a football. Touristy? Slightly. Worth it? Absolutely.

⏱ 90 min

🍽️ DINNER - Restaurant Ikarus at Hangar-7. Two Michelin stars, and unlike any restaurant you've visited: a glass-domed aircraft hangar housing Red Bull's collection of vintage planes, helicopters and Formula 1 cars. Every month a different world-class guest chef takes over the kitchen - you literally don't know whose food you'll be eating until you arrive. Only 8 tables since the 2025 refit. Wealthy foodies fly in from across Europe for this.

⏱ 180 min

🏨 NIGHT - Hotel Sacher Salzburg. On the banks of the Salzach with direct views of the Old Town and fortress. Same crystal chandeliers and brocade as Vienna's legendary Sacher. Sachertorte waiting in your room at turndown.

⏱ 60 min

Salzburg Lx

★★★☆☆
1

Hellbrunn Palace (Schloss Hellbrunn) is 20 minutes south of the centre and completely different from Mirabell's Baroque formality. Built in 1615 as a pleasure palace, it's famous for the Wasserspiele - trick fountains designed to soak unsuspecting guests at the dinner table. 400 years later, they still work and they still get you wet. The park is free; the fountain tour costs about €15 and is genuinely entertaining.

⏱ 90 min
2

Hangar-7 is worth visiting even if you dined at Ikarus last night. The Red Bull-owned glass dome houses a rotating collection of historic aircraft, Formula 1 cars (including Sebastian Vettel's), and Felix Baumgartner's Stratos space-jump capsule. Free entry. The architecture alone - a futuristic steel-and-glass shell near the airport runway - is worth the drive.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

🍽️ LUNCH - Gasthof Goldgasse. Hidden in the Old Town on a tiny alley, this restaurant features in the Michelin Guide and serves modern Austrian cuisine with real personality. Almost exclusively Austrian-sourced ingredients. The lunch menu is shorter and more affordable than dinner - a smart move on your last day.

⏱ 90 min

Last walk along the Salzach riverbank toward Makartsteg (the padlock bridge). The view back toward the Old Town with the fortress lit up is Salzburg's postcard shot. If it's your last evening, the Sacher Bar's terrace has the same view with a cocktail in hand.

⏱ 45 min

🍽️ DINNER - Esszimmer. One Michelin star, family-run, and the heart of Salzburg's local fine dining scene. Chef Andreas Kaiblinger describes it as 'honouring grandmother's recipes in a modern language.' The tasting menu changes seasonally; expect game, trout, Alpine herbs, and Austrian wines you won't find outside the country. Intimate, warm, no pretension.

⏱ 120 min

🏨 NIGHT - Hotel Sacher Salzburg (second night). Before checkout tomorrow, breakfast in the winter garden - white tablecloths, silver service, best Viennoiserie in the city. Salzburg Airport is 10 minutes by taxi.
