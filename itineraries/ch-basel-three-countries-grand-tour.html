Dijon

★★★★☆
1

Discover Dijon, the historic capital of Burgundy located in the heart of France's most celebrated wine region. The city boasts a rich heritage as the seat of the powerful Dukes of Burgundy, with the Ducal Palace now housing the exceptional Musée des Beaux-Arts. Medieval and Renaissance buildings line the pedestrianized streets of the remarkably preserved center.

⏱ 75 min
2

Follow the Owl Trail (Parcours de la Chouette), a walking route marked by bronze owl plaques embedded in the pavement that leads past 22 historic landmarks. This self-guided tour reveals the city's architectural treasures, from Gothic churches to elegant hôtels particuliers.

⏱ 75 min
3

Explore the gastronomic heritage for which Dijon is famous worldwide, including its renowned mustard and exceptional Burgundy wines. The city is the birthplace of fine Burgundy cuisine, where traditional recipes have been perfected over centuries.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Sample the celebrated wines of Burgundy, from the grand crus of the Côte de Nuits to the village appellations that surround the city. The vineyards of this region produce some of the world's most sought-after Pinot Noir and Chardonnay wines.

⏱ 60 min

Ronchamp

★★★★☆
1

Visit the Notre-Dame du Haut chapel in Ronchamp, designed by legendary architect Le Corbusier and declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This revolutionary masterpiece of modern architecture, completed in 1955, breaks all conventions with its sculptural white walls, mushroom-shaped roof, and seemingly random window openings that create dramatic interior lighting.

⏱ 75 min
2

Experience the spiritual atmosphere created by Le Corbusier's masterful manipulation of light and space. The chapel's organic forms and pilgrimage setting combine modernist architecture with ancient religious traditions, creating a contemplative environment that continues to inspire architects and visitors alike.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Explore the surrounding grounds designed by Renzo Piano, including the Monastery of Saint Clare and the gatehouse. The peaceful hilltop setting offers views of the Vosges Mountains and the charming landscapes of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 180 km · 2h10

Ribeauville

★★★☆☆
1

Discover Ribeauvillé, a quintessential Alsatian village crowned by three castle ruins towering over the medieval town. These fortress remnants - Saint-Ulrich, Girsberg, and Haut-Ribeaupierre - date from the 11th to 14th centuries and offer panoramic views of the vine-covered slopes and Rhine plain beyond.

⏱ 75 min
2

Stroll through the historic Old Town along the Grand'Rue, lined with Renaissance and Gothic buildings housing artisan shops and traditional restaurants. The town's medieval heritage is visible in its fortified gates, fountain squares, and half-timbered houses decorated with wrought-iron signs.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Explore the surrounding vineyards that have produced wines here since Roman times. The Alsace Wine Route passes directly through Ribeauvillé, offering opportunities to sample exceptional Rieslings, Gewürztraminers, and Pinot Gris at local cellars.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 1h

Marlenheim

1

Begin the Alsace Wine Route at Marlenheim, the northern gateway to 170 kilometers of winding roads through charming villages and picturesque vineyards. This celebrated route leads through the foothills of the Vosges Mountains, where the unique microclimate produces some of France's finest white wines.

⏱ 75 min
2

Sample local wines including Riesling, Gewürztraminer, Pinot Gris, and Muscat - the noble grape varieties that have made Alsace famous worldwide. The roadside cellars and tasting rooms offer opportunities to discover these exceptional wines directly from the producers.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Enjoy the idyllic landscape of vine-covered hillsides, medieval villages, and distant mountain views. The warm hospitality of the winemaking families adds a personal dimension to discovering this renowned wine region.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 35min

Strasbourg

★★★★☆
1

Explore Strasbourg, the capital of Alsace and seat of the European Parliament, featuring a stunning Gothic cathedral and well-preserved Old Town designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The cathedral's 142-meter spire was the world's tallest building for over two centuries, while its intricate facade and astronomical clock continue to amaze visitors.

⏱ 75 min
2

Wander through La Petite France, the most picturesque quarter of Strasbourg, where half-timbered houses from the 16th and 17th centuries line the canals of the Ill River. Former tanners' and fishermen's homes now house restaurants and shops, their flower-bedecked balconies reflected in the still waters below.

⏱ 75 min
3

Visit the European Parliament building, where the European Union's directly elected assembly meets. Guided tours reveal the workings of this important democratic institution and the stunning hemicycle chamber where 705 members from 27 nations deliberate.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Discover Strasbourg's rich culinary heritage, from traditional winstubs to Michelin-starred restaurants. The city's cuisine reflects its unique position at the crossroads of French and German culture, producing distinctive dishes found nowhere else.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 95 km · 1h15

Rust

1

Experience Europa-Park in Rust, Germany's largest theme park and the second most visited in Europe after Disneyland Paris. With over 100 attractions and shows spread across 18 themed areas representing European countries, the park offers something for all ages. From high-speed roller coasters to fairy-tale worlds, each zone recreates the atmosphere of its respective nation.

⏱ 75 min
2

The park features 13 roller coasters, some operating since 1984, demonstrating decades of innovation in theme park thrills. The Silver Star stands as one of Europe's tallest and fastest coasters, while family-friendly attractions ensure entertainment for younger visitors.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

With capacity for 60,000 guests daily, Europa-Park offers a full day of entertainment. The convenient RailCoaster direct train from Basel makes the journey easy, with shuttle buses connecting Ringsheim station to both Europa-Park and the Rulantica water world.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 55min

Colmar

★★★★☆
1

Explore Colmar, renowned for its beautifully preserved Old Town featuring half-timbered houses and idyllic canals in the Alsace region of France. The colorful facades and flower-bedecked bridges create a fairy-tale atmosphere that has made this one of France's most photogenic towns. The medieval and Renaissance architecture has survived remarkably intact.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the Unterlinden Museum, housed in a 13th-century Dominican convent, to see the famous Isenheim Altarpiece. This masterpiece of German Renaissance art by Matthias Grünewald is considered one of the most important works of Western painting, its vivid colors and emotional intensity still captivating visitors five centuries later.

⏱ 75 min
3

Wander through La Petite Venise (Little Venice), where traditional half-timbered houses line the canals of the Lauch River. This picturesque district, best explored by boat or on foot, captures the essence of Alsatian charm with its pastel-colored buildings reflected in the still waters.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Sample the rich culinary tradition of Alsace, exploring local specialties and wines that reflect the region's unique Franco-German heritage. The town's gastronomic scene celebrates centuries of culinary tradition.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Freiburg

★★★★☆
1

Discover Freiburg im Breisgau, the vibrant university city known for its sunny climate and historic Old Town in Germany's Black Forest region. The magnificent Gothic Münster cathedral dominates the Marktplatz, its 116-meter spire a masterpiece of medieval architecture. The intricate stonework and stunning stained glass windows have survived centuries largely intact.

⏱ 75 min
2

Explore the unique Bächle, small water-filled runnels that flow through the streets and alleys of the Old Town. These medieval water channels, originally used for fire-fighting and cooling, now charm visitors as they trace their path through the historic center.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

As the gateway to the Black Forest, Freiburg offers countless nearby hiking and cycling paths. The surrounding hills, covered in dark evergreen forests, gave the region its name and provide endless opportunities for outdoor exploration.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 45min

Titisee

★★★☆☆
1

Visit Titisee-Neustadt in the heart of the Black Forest, famous for its crystal-clear lake and picturesque surroundings. The glacial lake, formed during the last ice age, stretches nearly two kilometers amid forested mountains. The charming lakeside promenade offers stunning views and access to boat rentals.

⏱ 75 min
2

Discover the region's famous cuckoo clock tradition at one of the local clock factories. The Black Forest has been crafting these intricate timepieces since the 18th century, and you can watch master craftsmen create these iconic souvenirs using traditional techniques.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Enjoy a boat trip on the lake or simply relax by the shore. Numerous hiking and cycling routes wind through the surrounding forests, while wellness facilities offer relaxation. In winter, the area transforms into a winter sports destination with skiing, cross-country skiing, and ice skating.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min

Schluchsee

1

Explore Schluchsee, the largest lake in the Baden-Württemberg region, impressing visitors with its exceptionally clear waters and surrounding natural beauty. Originally a natural lake, it was dammed in 1932 and now offers a pristine environment for water sports and lakeside relaxation. The forested shores remain largely undeveloped, preserving the wilderness character.

⏱ 75 min
2

Discover the well-maintained hiking and cycling paths that circle the lake and extend into the surrounding Black Forest. The terrain offers routes for all abilities, from gentle lakeside strolls to challenging mountain trails with rewarding views.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Enjoy swimming, sailing, and fishing in the clean waters, or rent a boat to explore the lake's quiet coves. This idyllic area attracts nature lovers and active holidaymakers throughout the year, with winter bringing cross-country skiing and skating.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 75 km · 1h10

Schaffhausen

★★★★☆
1

Explore Schaffhausen, a historic city featuring a well-preserved Old Town and the impressive Munot fortress dating to the 16th century. This unique circular fortification offers panoramic views of the city, the Rhine, and the surrounding vine-covered hills. The Old Town's ornate bay windows (Erker) and frescoed facades create a distinctive architectural heritage.

⏱ 75 min
2

Marvel at the Rhine Falls, Europe's biggest waterfall at 150 meters wide and 23 meters high. This impressive natural spectacle sees 600,000 liters of water per second cascade over the rocks with tremendous force. The fish cannot climb upstream due to the falls' power, though eels are known to make the journey.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Take a boat trip right up to the thundering mass of water, landing on the central rock for a truly immersive experience. Multiple viewing platforms on both banks offer different perspectives of this awesome natural phenomenon.

⏱ 60 min

Explore the surrounding hiking and cycling paths that wind through vineyards and along the riverbanks. The region's mild climate produces excellent wines, particularly from the Blauburgunder grape.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 1h10

Zurich

★★★★☆
1

Explore Zurich, Switzerland's largest city and an important financial hub as well as cultural metropolis. The Old Town stretches along both sides of the Limmat River, featuring landmarks including the twin-towered Grossmünster and the elegant Fraumünster with its stunning Chagall windows. Bahnhofstrasse, the main shopping street, is one of the world's most exclusive retail destinations.

⏱ 75 min
2

Discover the city's numerous museums and galleries, from the Kunsthaus Zurich with its world-class art collection to the Swiss National Museum tracing the country's cultural history. The vibrant arts scene includes theaters, opera, and countless smaller venues showcasing contemporary creativity.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Relax by Lake Zurich, perfect for swimming, sailing, and paddleboarding in summer. The lakeside promenades offer views of the Alps while the lively waterfront areas pulse with cafes, parks, and public baths.

⏱ 60 min

Experience Zurich's vibrant nightlife scene, concentrated in districts like Langstrasse and the transformed industrial areas of Zurich West. The city offers everything from traditional wine taverns to cutting-edge clubs.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 50min

Rigi

★★★☆☆
1

Experience Mount Rigi, known as the 'Queen of the Mountains' and Europe's first tourist mountain. This massif rises between three lakes - Lucerne, Zug, and Lauerz - creating stunning views in every direction. The panorama encompasses the Swiss Alps, the Jura Mountains, and the vast Swiss Plateau stretching to the horizon.

⏱ 75 min
2

Reach the summit via the historic Rigi Railway, one of Europe's first mountain railways dating to 1871. Alternatively, take the scenic cable car from Weggis, ascending through pastoral landscapes dotted with traditional chalets and grazing cattle.

⏱ 75 min
3

Relax at Rigi Kaltbad Mineral Baths and Spa, designed by renowned architect Mario Botta. The modern spa complex harmonizes with the Alpine setting, offering indoor and outdoor pools fed by natural mineral springs, with uninterrupted mountain views.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

In summer, explore hiking trails ranging from gentle paths to challenging routes. In winter, the Rigi offers skiing, sledging, and snowshoe trails. The cultural significance of the mountain in Swiss history makes it a must-visit destination.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h

Lucerne

★★★★☆
1

Discover Lucerne, a major tourist destination lying on the north end of Lake Lucerne where the River Reuss flows out. The city is famous for its Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke), the oldest standing truss bridge in the world, adorned with 17th-century paintings depicting local history and the lives of saints.

⏱ 75 min
2

Admire the iconic Water Tower (Wasserturm), an octagonal structure that has served as prison, torture chamber, treasury, and archive throughout its 700-year history. Together with the Chapel Bridge, it forms the most photographed monument in Switzerland.

⏱ 75 min
3

Visit the Swiss Museum of Transport, Switzerland's most visited museum, showcasing the country's pioneering role in Alpine transportation. From vintage locomotives to space exploration, the interactive exhibits trace the history of mobility in the mountains.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Wander through the Old Town's well-preserved historic buildings, painted with elaborate frescoes depicting historical scenes and legends. The car-free streets reveal medieval squares, Renaissance fountains, and traditional guild houses.

⏱ 60 min

If visiting toward the end of winter, experience the famous Lucerne Carnival (Fasnacht), Switzerland's largest and most colorful celebration featuring elaborate costumes, satirical floats, and raucous music.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 1h

Pilatus

★★★☆☆
1

Ascend Mount Pilatus via the world's steepest cogwheel railway, with gradients reaching 48%. This engineering marvel, inaugurated in 1889, climbs 1,629 meters through dramatic Alpine scenery to the summit at 2,132 meters. The panoramic views encompass Lake Lucerne, the Swiss Plateau, and countless Alpine peaks.

⏱ 75 min
2

Explore the summit complex featuring viewing terraces, restaurants, and the Hotel Pilatus-Kulm. On clear days, the 360-degree panorama extends from the Black Forest to the distant peaks of the Swiss Alps, with 73 Alpine peaks visible from this legendary vantage point.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Experience the Golden Round Trip: boat across Lake Lucerne, cogwheel railway up the mountain, aerial cable car descent to Kriens. This circular journey combines Switzerland's most scenic transport modes for an unforgettable day. In winter, enjoy skiing and toboggan runs on the mountain's slopes.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 1h30

Engelberg

★★★☆☆
1

Ascend Mount Titlis near Engelberg, a peak offering snow sports activities year-round thanks to its permanent glacier. The Rotair cable car - the world's first revolving gondola - provides 360-degree views during the ascent to 3,238 meters. The rotating cabin ensures every passenger enjoys the spectacular panorama.

⏱ 75 min
2

Cross the Titlis Cliff Walk, a spectacular suspension bridge at 3,041 meters that spans 100 meters across a dramatic Alpine chasm. Europe's highest suspension bridge offers vertigo-inducing views of crevassed glaciers and snow-covered peaks stretching to the horizon.

⏱ 75 min
3

Explore the Glacier Grotto, carved into the eternal ice of the Titlis glacier. Descend into the crystalline ice cave to experience the heart of a living glacier, with frozen chambers revealing the stunning blue of compressed ancient ice.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

In summer, enjoy hiking and mountain biking on trails that wind through Alpine meadows and past traditional chalets. The Engelberg valley offers activities for all abilities, from gentle walks to challenging peaks.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 45min

Brig

★★★☆☆
1

Explore Brig, officially known as Brig-Glis, a historic town of great national significance in the Valais canton. The entire Old Town is part of the official Swiss heritage sites, with the magnificent Stockalper Palace standing as the largest private building ever constructed in Switzerland. This 17th-century baroque masterpiece features distinctive onion-domed towers.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the Church of Maria Himmelfahrt (Assumption of Mary), designated a heritage site of National Significance. The baroque interior features stunning frescoes, intricate woodwork, and gilded altars that reflect the prosperity brought by the Simplon trade route passing through the town.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Discover the strategic importance of Brig as the northern gateway to the Simplon Pass, connecting Switzerland to Italy since Roman times. The town's architecture and monuments reveal centuries of history as a vital Alpine crossing point.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 115 km · 1h35

Geneva

★★★★☆
1

Discover Geneva, the city of peace and international diplomacy, home to the European headquarters of the United Nations and the International Red Cross. With 82 buildings of international significance, the city embodies global cooperation while maintaining its Swiss charm. The iconic Jet d'Eau fountain shoots water 140 meters into the air over Lake Geneva.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the Cathedral of St-Pierre, a magnificent Gothic structure that was John Calvin's church during the Protestant Reformation. Climb the tower for panoramic views across the lake to Mont Blanc on clear days. The archaeological site beneath reveals 2,000 years of religious history.

⏱ 75 min
3

Explore the Conservatory and Botanical Gardens, a peaceful oasis featuring plant collections from around the world and stunning views of the Alps. The gardens have been nurturing botanical diversity since 1817, offering themed sections from rock gardens to tropical greenhouses.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stroll through the Old Town's narrow streets, discovering artisan boutiques, antique shops, and historic squares. The elegant lakeside promenades and parks provide perfect settings for contemplating the stunning Alpine backdrop that frames this international city.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 155 km · 2h

Gruyeres

★★★☆☆
1

Explore the small medieval town of Gruyères, famous worldwide for its eponymous cheese. The imposing Gruyères Castle dominates the hilltop town, its eight centuries of history reflected in rooms decorated from medieval times through the 19th century. The ramparts offer sweeping views of the Pre-Alps and the pastoral valleys below.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit La Maison du Gruyère cheese museum to witness the traditional cheesemaking process that has defined this region for centuries. Watch artisans craft the famous wheels of Gruyère AOP using time-honored techniques, and learn about the Alpine dairy culture that sustains this tradition.

⏱ 75 min
3

Discover the HR Giger Museum, a fascinating attraction dedicated to the Swiss artist who created the iconic creatures in the Alien films. Housed in a medieval château, this surrealist collection contrasts dramatically with the town's fairy-tale atmosphere.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Wander the car-free cobblestone main street lined with traditional chalets, artisan shops, and medieval buildings. The surrounding Alpine landscape offers excellent hiking through meadows where cows graze, producing the milk that becomes world-famous cheese.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 1h15

Kandersteg

★★★★☆
1

Journey to Oeschinen Lake near Kandersteg, an idyllic mountain lake that ranks among Switzerland's most beautiful natural destinations. Surrounded by dramatic peaks rising over 3,000 meters, the turquoise waters reflect the towering cliffs and glaciers that frame this Alpine paradise.

⏱ 75 min
2

Reach the lake via gondola lift, ascending through forests to emerge at the stunning viewpoint above the water. The UNESCO-listed setting encompasses pristine wilderness that has remained largely unchanged for centuries, offering a true escape into Alpine nature.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Enjoy hiking around the lake shore, rowing across the crystal-clear waters, or swimming in the refreshing mountain lake during summer. In winter, the frozen lake transforms into a natural ice skating rink, while the surrounding slopes offer snowshoeing and cross-country skiing.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 95 km · 1h30

Lauterbrunnen

★★★☆☆
1

Explore Lauterbrunnen, a picturesque valley in the Bernese Highlands renowned for its 72 waterfalls cascading down sheer cliff faces. Staubbach Falls, one of the highest free-falling waterfalls in Europe at 297 meters, plunges dramatically into the valley floor, its mist creating rainbows on sunny days.

⏱ 75 min
2

Venture to Trümmelbach Falls, a unique series of ten glacier-fed waterfalls roaring inside the mountain. These thundering cascades, carved through the rock by the meltwater of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau glaciers, are accessible via a tunnel lift - an unforgettable experience of nature's raw power.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Hike through this glacial U-shaped valley, where towering cliffs rise 400 meters on either side. The valley's dramatic geography inspired J.R.R. Tolkien's vision of Rivendell, and its timeless beauty continues to captivate visitors from around the world.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 25min

Grindelwald

★★★★☆
1

Discover Grindelwald in the Bernese Highlands, a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts dominated by the legendary north face of the Eiger. This dramatic 1,800-meter vertical wall offers incredible climbing routes and has attracted mountaineers since the golden age of alpinism. The village's traditional chalets cluster beneath these imposing peaks.

⏱ 75 min
2

Take the Firstbahn cableway to exciting attractions including the First Cliff Walk, a dramatic walkway suspended along the cliff face at 2,168 meters. The engineering marvel extends into the void, providing heart-pounding views of the valley far below and the surrounding peaks.

⏱ 75 min
3

Hike to Bachalpsee, an idyllic Alpine lake at 2,265 meters that perfectly mirrors the Schreckhorn and Wetterhorn peaks. This relatively easy trail from First station rewards hikers with one of Switzerland's most photographed mountain panoramas.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

In winter, enjoy world-class skiing on slopes facing the iconic Eiger. In summer, cycling and hiking trails of all difficulties wind through flower-filled meadows with constant mountain views. The village offers year-round Alpine activities in a spectacular setting.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h10

Interlaken

★★★★☆
1

Arrive in Interlaken, spectacularly situated between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz - its name literally meaning 'between lakes'. This famous resort town serves as the gateway to the Jungfraujoch saddle, known as the 'Top of Europe'. A centuries-old monastery dating to the 11th century adds historical depth to this stunning natural setting.

⏱ 75 min
2

Take the Jungfrau Railway to Europe's highest railway station at 3,454 meters, where breathtaking views of surrounding glaciers and mountain peaks unfold. The journey itself through tunnels carved into the Eiger is an engineering marvel, with viewing windows offering glimpses of the dramatic north face.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Experience world-class outdoor activities including paragliding over the lakes, whitewater rafting on glacial rivers, or hiking through Alpine meadows. Interlaken's unique position in the Bernese Oberland makes it Switzerland's adventure capital.

⏱ 60 min

Cruise across Lake Brienz or Lake Thun on historic paddle steamers, admiring the dramatic mountain scenery reflected in the crystal-clear waters. The boats connect charming lakeside villages and offer unparalleled views of the surrounding peaks.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Thun

★★★☆☆
1

Discover Thun, a charming city on the shores of Lake Thun with a captivating Old Town dominated by the medieval Thun Castle. This 12th-century fortress perches dramatically above the city, its four corner towers creating one of Switzerland's most recognizable silhouettes. Inside, the historical museum traces the region's fascinating past.

⏱ 75 min
2

Explore the unique elevated sidewalks (Hochtrottoirs) of the main street, where shops occupy two levels - at street level and on raised platforms accessed by stairs. This distinctive architectural feature, found nowhere else in Switzerland, dates back to the medieval period.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Enjoy the crystal-clear waters of Lake Thun, perfect for swimming, sailing, or scenic boat trips. From the Schlossberg viewpoint, take in incredible views of the city and the surrounding Alpine peaks that frame this stunning lakeside setting.

⏱ 60 min

Stroll along the Aare promenade, a delightful riverside walk connecting the lake to the historic center. The path offers peaceful views of the water and mountains while passing charming cafes and gardens.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Bern

★★★★☆
1

Explore Bern, the capital of Switzerland, renowned for its well-preserved medieval Old Town designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Federal Palace, seat of the Swiss government, dominates the cityscape alongside the iconic Zytglogge clock tower with its famous astronomical clock and mechanical figures that perform hourly.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the Bear Pit (Bärengraben), home to the city's beloved mascots that have been kept here since 1857. The bears now enjoy a spacious riverside park where visitors can observe these magnificent animals in a more natural setting.

⏱ 75 min
3

Explore the Zentrum Paul Klee, an architectural masterpiece designed by Renzo Piano housing the world's most comprehensive collection of works by the Swiss-born artist. The undulating building harmonizes with the surrounding landscape while showcasing over 4,000 of Klee's paintings, watercolors, and drawings.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Wander beneath the six kilometers of covered arcades (Lauben), one of Europe's longest covered shopping promenades. These medieval arcades shelter boutiques, cafes, and historic cellars while protecting visitors from the elements.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 25min

Solothurn

★★★☆☆
1

Discover Solothurn, the Baroque city of Switzerland, boasting a rich history and impressive architecture that earned it this distinguished title. The Old Town by the Aare river features the magnificent Cathedral of St. Ursus with its Italian Renaissance influences, the medieval clock tower, and the elegant Waldegg Castle set in manicured gardens.

⏱ 75 min
2

Visit the Blumenstein Historical Museum to explore the region's heritage, or admire Swiss artistic achievement at the Solothurn Art Museum. These cultural institutions showcase the city's long-standing commitment to preserving and celebrating its artistic and historical legacy.

⏱ 75 min

Activities
