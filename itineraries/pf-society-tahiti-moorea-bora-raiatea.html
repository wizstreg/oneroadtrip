Tahiti, Moorea & Bora Bora: Lagoons and Mountains

📅 11 days📍 10 stops🚗 557 km⏱ 2h35

From vibrant Papeete to sacred Raiatea, this 10-day itinerary island-hops across the Society Islands through four stopovers. Bustling markets and waterfalls in Tahiti, volcanic ridges and crystalline lagoons in Moorea, Bora Bora's infinite blue, then UNESCO heritage and vanilla in Raiatea—each island adds its own color to the journey.

✨ Highlights
  • Ferry Tahiti → Moorea: 30-min crossing with views over both bays
  • Flight Moorea → Bora Bora: lagoon flyover on landing approach
  • Flight Bora Bora → Raiatea: 20 min between the Leeward Islands
  • Flight Raiatea → Tahiti: 45-min return to the main island
📆 Best period

May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 7 and 11 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (May-October): Peak season with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures (24-28°C), and trade winds. Best weather for outdoor activities but highest prices and crowds.

Shoulder Season (April, November): Good weather with brief showers, moderate accommodation rates, and fewer tourists. Excellent balance of conditions and value.

Wet Season (November-April): Hot, humid weather (26-32°C) with potential cyclones, but lower prices and lush landscapes. Afternoon rains common, mornings often clear.

Cyclone Season (December-March): Higher storm risk requiring flexible planning and comprehensive insurance, though many cyclones pass without major impact to tourism.

🌍 What to discover in PF?

Pacific paradise with overwater bungalows, pristine lagoons, and traditional culture. Experience Tahitian hospitality, incredible diving, and romantic atmosphere. The territory offers luxury experiences with cultural authenticity and natural beauty. French Polynesia provides ultimate tropical paradise with cultural richness and natural wonders.

Island-hopping across the Society Islands: markets and waterfalls on Tahiti, bays and ridges on Moorea, turquoise lagoon time on Bora Bora, and Polynesian heritage on Raiatea.

Day 1

Papeete

Where roulottes sizzle at sunset and tiare crowns perfume the morning market

★★★☆☆
1

Papeete — Municipal Market (early morning) for vanilla, monoï and island produce.

⏱ 75 min
2

Papeete's market, Mapuru a Paraita, fills a two-story covered building in the heart of downtown. Vanilla pods, handcrafted monoi oil, fresh tiare crowns, and vibrant tifaifai quilts pile high on the stalls. Sunday morning from 4 AM onwards, Tahitian families flood the aisles—it's the most authentic time to visit.

⏱ 75 min
3

Paofai Gardens — Waterfront promenade and outrigger canoes at sunset.

⏱ 75 min
4

Step inside Notre-Dame Cathedral, recognizable by its bright yellow façade and bell tower dominating the city center. Built starting in 1856 and completed in 1875, it shelters stained-glass windows filtering the tropical light and offers welcome coolness.

⏱ 40 min
5

Head to Paofai Gardens along the waterfront. This planted promenade facing the harbor offers an unobstructed view of Moorea's silhouette. Outrigger canoes (va'a) train here in the late afternoon.

⏱ 50 min
6

The Pearl Museum traces the history of pearl farming in French Polynesia. In 45 minutes, you'll learn to distinguish luster, orient, and shapes of Tahitian black pearls.

⏱ 45 min
7

Walk up Boulevard Pomaré to spot the street art murals created during the Ono'u festival. International artists have transformed several downtown façades into an open-air gallery.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Street‑food dinner at roulottes on Place Vaiete (open evenings).

⏱ 60 min

The roulottes (food trucks) on Place Vaiete set up every evening from 6 PM facing the harbor. Poisson cru with lime and coconut milk, chao mein, grilled prawns—a local institution under the stars.

⏱ 60 min

If you arrive on a Sunday, head to the Protestant temple at Paofai to hear the polyphonic religious singing. Worshippers wear hats and white outfits—a unique visual and sonic experience.

⏱ 45 min
Day 2

Papeete

Where roulottes sizzle at sunset and tiare crowns perfume the morning market

★★★☆☆
1

Tahiti east coast — Faarumai waterfalls and Arahoho blowhole; lush basalt valleys.

⏱ 75 min
2

The three Faarumai waterfalls lie at PK 22 on the east coast, 22 km from Papeete. The first, Vaimahuta, plunges 80 meters down a basalt cliff—five minutes' walk from the parking area. The other two, Haamaremare Rahi and Iti, require an additional 20 minutes on a steeper trail.

⏱ 75 min
3

Museum of Tahiti and the Islands (if open) for cultural grounding.

⏱ 75 min
4

Stop at the Arahoho Blowhole, right next to the waterfalls. The ocean rushes into a lava tube carved by erosion beneath the road and shoots a powerful jet of water out on the land side. In Tahitian, Arahoho means 'the road that roars.' The spectacle depends on the swell—the stronger it is, the more impressive the blow.

⏱ 40 min
5

Follow the coastal road to Pointe Vénus in Mahina (PK 10). The 25-meter white lighthouse, built in 1867, marks where Europeans landed in 1767. Black-sand beach with sunset views over Moorea.

⏱ 50 min
6

The Tahara'a lookout, at the start of the east coast leaving Papeete, offers from its landscaped terrace a panorama over the lagoon, the barrier reef, and the Tahiti Iti peninsula.

⏱ 30 min
7

The Museum of Tahiti and Her Islands, in Punaauia on the west coast (PK 15), traces the natural and cultural history of Polynesia. Collections of tapa cloth, traditional canoes, and basalt tools. Check opening days before visiting.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Short black‑sand beach swim; heed local currents and flags.

⏱ 60 min

Swimming at a black-sand beach along the east coast. The water stays warm year-round, but currents can be strong—respect the flags and stick to supervised areas.

⏱ 45 min

Vaiava beach (PK 18, Punaauia) offers white sand on the west side and easy snorkeling above coral heads, with a direct view of Moorea.

⏱ 60 min

Day 3

Paopao

Twin bays carved by ancient gods at the foot of sacred Mount Rotui

★★★☆☆
1

Ferry to Moorea — Opunohu and Cook’s bays landscapes and pineapple fields.

⏱ 75 min
2

The ferry crosses through Vaiare Pass and reveals Moorea, Tahiti's sister island. Right from docking, the trident silhouette of its mountains commands attention: Cook's Bay to the left, Opunohu Bay to the right, separated by the sacred Mount Rotui (899 m).

⏱ 45 min
3

Belvédère d’Opunohu — Ridgeline view over twin bays and Mount Rotui.

⏱ 75 min
4

Drive up the shaded road to the Opunohu Belvedere, at 240 meters altitude in the heart of a 9-km-diameter crater. The 360° panorama embraces both bays, eight mountain ridges, the lagoon, and pineapple fields below.

⏱ 60 min
5

Coming back down, stop at the Opunohu agricultural school. Students produce tropical fruit jams and pineapple juice from the valley. Archaeological sites (marae) lie nearby, along the marked trail.

⏱ 45 min
6

Marae Titiroa, a Polynesian stone temple nestled in Opunohu Valley's vegetation, testifies to the ancient Ma'ohi religion. Explanatory panels line the site.

⏱ 30 min
7

Ta'ahiamanu public beach, in Opunohu Bay, unfurls a strip of white sand bordered by coconut palms. Shallow, calm water makes it a family-friendly swimming spot.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Snorkel with garden fish at a public beach; mornings offer clearer water.

⏱ 60 min

Snorkeling in the lagoon on the Cook's Bay side: butterflyfish, surgeonfish, and small rays glide above corals accessible from the beach. Mornings offer optimal visibility.

⏱ 60 min

Lagoon boat tour between the two bays. You sail at the foot of Mount Rotui, encountering stingrays and small blacktip reef sharks in the shallow waters around the motus.

⏱ 90 min
Day 4

Paopao

Twin bays carved by ancient gods at the foot of sacred Mount Rotui

★★★☆☆
1

Moorea ridge — Three Coconuts Pass hike through mape forests; panoramic saddle.

⏱ 75 min
2

Te Fare Natura, Moorea's eco-museum, sits at the exit of Opunohu Bay. Interactive exhibits on reef ecosystems, endemic fauna, and the island's volcanic geology.

⏱ 50 min
3

Tiki Village (evening show) or local dance rehearsal (community schedule).

⏱ 75 min
4

Take a detour to 'Magic Mountain' for a striking viewpoint over the turquoise lagoon on the west side. Access is via a short but steep track.

⏱ 30 min
5

Temae Beach, in the northeast near the airport, ranks among the finest in French Polynesia. Immaculate white sand, transparent waters, and a direct view of Tahiti in the distance.

⏱ 50 min
6

The Manutea fruit juice distillery, located in Opunohu Bay, processes pineapples and local fruits. Free tasting of artisanal juices, liqueurs, and jams.

⏱ 40 min
7

Want a cultural show? Polynesian evenings on the island combine ma'a tahiti buffet, traditional dances, percussion, and fire performances under the stars.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Cooking class: poisson cru with lime and coconut milk.

⏱ 60 min

Three Coconuts Pass hike starting from the Opunohu Belvedere. The trail crosses dense tropical forest and emerges at a pass with views over both bays and the lagoon on the Haapiti side.

⏱ 180 min

Polynesian cooking class: preparing poisson cru with lime and coconut milk, the emblematic dish of the fenua. Some guesthouses offer this workshop.

⏱ 60 min

Day 5

Vaitape

Gateway village to the South Pacific's most mesmerizing lagoon

★★★☆☆
1

Flight to Bora Bora — Lagoon viewpoints during approach; check left‑side seating.

⏱ 75 min
2

The plane flies over Bora Bora's lagoon before landing on Motu Mute—the former airstrip built by the Americans in 1942. A boat shuttle runs to Vaitape, the island's main village.

⏱ 40 min
3

Matira area — Public beach curve and shallow turquoise shelf.

⏱ 75 min
4

Matira Beach, at the southern tip of the main island: a kilometer and a half of white sand fringed with coconut palms. It's Bora Bora's only public beach, and one of the most photographed in Polynesia. The water is shallow, the bottom sandy—ideal for swimming.

⏱ 75 min
5

From Matira, Mount Otemanu (727 m) dominates the landscape. Its basalt summit is the symbol of Bora Bora—impossible to miss, it cuts against the sky from any point on the lagoon.

⏱ 30 min
6

The small village of Vaitape stretches along the wharf. A few craft and black pearl shops line the main road. The atmosphere is calm, far from the bustle of the resorts on the motus.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Lagoon tour by boat with coral gardens and stingray viewing (respect wildlife distances).

⏱ 60 min

Lagoon boat tour: coral gardens, swimming with docile stingrays, and observing blacktip reef sharks in the shallows between the motus. Keep a respectful distance from wildlife.

⏱ 180 min

Sunset from Matira Beach: the sky turns orange and pink while Mount Otemanu stands out in silhouette. Bring something for a picnic.

⏱ 45 min
Day 6

Vaitape

Gateway village to the South Pacific's most mesmerizing lagoon

★★★☆☆
1

Mount Otemanu viewpoints — Short motu landings for classic postcard frames.

⏱ 75 min
2

Bora Bora still bears the marks of Operation Bobcat. In 1942, 4,000 GIs landed on this island of 1,000 inhabitants to turn it into a supply base in the Pacific. Eight 7-inch guns and anti-aircraft batteries were installed on the heights.

⏱ 30 min
3

WWII sites — Old coastal guns and supply base traces on the main island.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Anau guns, at Pointe Fitiu, are the island's best-preserved. Double battery on reinforced concrete mounts, bunker, ammunition shelter, and network of trenches—all restored and explained by trilingual panels installed in 2021.

⏱ 50 min
5

Continue around the island via Faanui Bay and its church at the foot of the mountain. Two more guns stand at Pointe Tereia, accessible by a short trail with views over the motus and overwater bungalows.

⏱ 45 min
6

The Popoti Lookout, a former American radar post on the west coast, offers the best panoramic view of Bora Bora. Access via a steep 4WD track—on foot or by quad.

⏱ 45 min
7

At Vaitape wharf, a commemorative plaque recalls the Seabees' presence (Navy construction battalions). At Motu Mute airport, a timeline chronicles the history of Operation Bobcat.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Sunset outrigger ride; book for golden hour.

⏱ 60 min

Island tour by bike or scooter: the circular road runs about 32 km on flat terrain. You pass luxury hotels, fishing villages, and viewpoints over the lagoon.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset outrigger canoe (va'a) trip. You glide across the lagoon while Otemanu glows red—book for the golden hour.

⏱ 60 min

Day 7

Uturoa

Where Polynesian navigators once set sail to settle the Pacific triangle

★★☆☆☆
1

Flight to Raiatea — Uturoa market and waterfront.

⏱ 75 min
2

Uturoa, Raiatea's main town, stretches along a quiet waterfront. Its small covered market sells fish, tropical fruits, and local crafts. The atmosphere is much calmer than Papeete.

⏱ 45 min
3

Marae Taputapuātea — UNESCO sacred complex of the Polynesian triangle; basalt marae facing the ocean.

⏱ 75 min
4

Marae Taputapuātea, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage list since 2017, extends along the lagoon shore at Raiatea's southeastern tip. Dedicated to the god 'Oro, this political, ceremonial, and funerary center brought together chiefs from across the Polynesian triangle. Several marae with distinct functions succeed one another on a vast site of basalt stones facing the sacred pass Te Ava Mo'a.

⏱ 90 min
5

Opposite the marae, the sacred mountain Tea'etapu dominates Opoa Valley. A trail leads to the summit for a plunging view over the site, lagoon, and ocean.

⏱ 40 min
6

Raiatea is the cradle of Ma'ohi civilization: from this island, ancient Polynesian navigators set out to colonize Hawaii, New Zealand, and Easter Island. The Taputapuātea site remains a pilgrimage destination for Polynesians worldwide.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Kayak the Faaroa River, Polynesia’s only navigable river (guided).

⏱ 60 min

Faaroa River ascent by boat or kayak—the only navigable river in French Polynesia. The motorized canoe pushes into a lush jungle of tree ferns, purau, and mape (Polynesian chestnuts). The guide tells valley legends.

⏱ 90 min

Swimming and snorkeling at Motu Iriru or Ofetaro, two public islets in Raiatea's lagoon accessible by boat. White sand, crystal-clear water, and tropical fish.

⏱ 60 min
Day 8

Uturoa

Where Polynesian navigators once set sail to settle the Pacific triangle

★★☆☆☆
1

Day trip to Taha’a — Vanilla plantations and lagoon motus shared with Raiatea.

⏱ 75 min
2

Taha'a, nicknamed the Vanilla Island, shares the same lagoon as Raiatea—30 minutes by boat is enough to reach it. The island produces about 80% of all vanilla in French Polynesia, the Vanilla tahitensis variety prized by chefs worldwide.

⏱ 40 min
3

Visit to a vanilla plantation: growers explain the manual pollination of flowers, harvesting, and the long months of preparation needed to obtain the fragrant brown pods. The sweet scent fills the greenhouse.

⏱ 50 min
4

Stop at a family pearl farm to understand the grafting of Pinctada margaritifera oysters. Live demonstration and explanations on classifying Tahitian black pearls—luster, orient, shape, and size.

⏱ 45 min
5

Taha'a's rum distillery produces organic agricole rum from sugarcane grown on former vanilla plots. Tasting available on-site.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Drift‑snorkel over coral gardens between motus (experienced swimmers only).

⏱ 60 min

Drift snorkeling in the coral garden at Motu Tau Tau, northwest of Taha'a. You let the current carry you over a natural aquarium of colorful corals, anemones, and tropical fish. Shallow, accessible to everyone.

⏱ 60 min

Lunch on a deserted motu: Polynesian barbecue of grilled fish, cooked bananas, and coconut milk, your feet in the white sand facing the turquoise lagoon.

⏱ 75 min

Observing blacktip reef sharks in the shallows on the reef side. They swim in very shallow water and pose no danger—still, keep a reasonable distance.

⏱ 30 min

1

Flight back to Tahiti — West‑coast lookout at Taharuu black‑sand beach.

⏱ 75 min
2

Back in Tahiti, explore the west coast. Taharuu's black-sand beach in Papara (PK 39) unfurls a long wild strip battered by waves—a renowned local surf spot.

⏱ 50 min
3

Arahoho or local surf break viewing (respect no‑swim zones).

4

Marae Arahurahu, in Paea (PK 22.5 west coast), is Tahiti's best-restored. Its terraced stone platform, surrounded by coconut palms, still hosts reenactments of traditional ceremonies.

⏱ 45 min
5

The Vaipahi water gardens (PK 49, Mataiea) offer a shaded botanical trail along waterfalls and pools amid dense tropical vegetation. Easy and free access.

⏱ 45 min
6

The Maraa Grotto, carved in the coastal cliff at Paea, shelters crystal-clear freshwater pools in a fern-filled setting. A quick, surprising stop.

⏱ 30 min
7

Stop at PK 18 beach (Punaauia), white sand this time, for a final easy snorkel on the coral heads with Moorea as backdrop.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

Evening roulottes round 2 — sample raw‑fish plates and chevrettes.

⏱ 60 min

Back to the roulottes at Place Vaiete for one last evening of Tahitian street food: poisson cru, chao pao, steak frites, and Nutella crepes—all under the harbor lights.

⏱ 60 min

Late-day stroll along Papeete's waterfront, from Bougainville Park to Place To'ata—the stage for Heiva i Tahiti, the major dance and song festival held every July.

Papeete

Where roulottes sizzle at sunset and tiare crowns perfume the morning market

★★★☆☆
1

Free morning for crafts and last lagoon dip; airport transfer.

⏱ 75 min
2

Last visit to Papeete market for monoi, vanilla, and pearl shopping. Vendors craft tiare crowns to take away—a Polynesian farewell symbol.

⏱ 60 min
3

King Pomare V's tomb, in Arue on the east coast, houses the sepulcher of Tahiti's last king, who died in 1891. The white coral monument topped with a funeral urn overlooks the lagoon.

⏱ 30 min
4

If time allows, push on to Mahina's pink Protestant temple, recognizable by its bright white and pink hues—one of Tahiti's most photogenic religious buildings.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Optional hike to Fautaua Valley waterfall (permit sometimes required; check conditions).

⏱ 60 min

Optional hike to Fautaua Falls, accessible from Papeete. The river plunges nearly 300 meters in a cascade into a swimming pool. A permit is sometimes required—check conditions with the town hall.

⏱ 180 min

One last dip in the lagoon or at a west-coast beach before the transfer to Faa'a airport, located 5 km from Papeete.
