May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 1 and 4 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (May-October): Peak season with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures (24-28°C), and trade winds. Best weather for outdoor activities but highest prices and crowds.

Shoulder Season (April, November): Good weather with brief showers, moderate accommodation rates, and fewer tourists. Excellent balance of conditions and value.

Wet Season (November-April): Hot, humid weather (26-32°C) with potential cyclones, but lower prices and lush landscapes. Afternoon rains common, mornings often clear.

Cyclone Season (December-March): Higher storm risk requiring flexible planning and comprehensive insurance, though many cyclones pass without major impact to tourism.

🌍 What to discover in PF?

Pacific paradise with overwater bungalows, pristine lagoons, and traditional culture. Experience Tahitian hospitality, incredible diving, and romantic atmosphere. Island‑hopping across the Society Islands: markets and waterfalls on Tahiti, bays and ridges on Moorea, turquoise lagoon time on Bora Bora, and Polynesian heritage on Raiatea.

Papeete

Where Polynesian markets meet waterfront roulottes and Moorea's silhouette rises from the harbor

★★★★★
📍 Papeete Market Tahiti📍 Notre-Dame Cathedral Papeete📍 Paofai Gardens Papeete📍 Papeete Waterfront📍 Place Vaiete roulottes Papeete📍 Paofai Protestant Temple Papeete
1

The Papeete market, Mapuru a Paraita, occupies a two-story covered building right in the city center. Vanilla pods, handcrafted monoï oil, fresh tiare flower crowns and colorful tifaifai quilts pile up on the stalls. Sunday morning from 4 am onwards, Tahitian families flood the aisles—this is when you'll see the market at its most authentic.

⏱ 75 min
2

Step through the doors of Notre-Dame Cathedral, recognizable by its bright yellow facade and bell tower dominating downtown. Built from 1856 and completed in 1875, it shelters stained-glass windows that filter the tropical light and offers welcome coolness inside.

⏱ 40 min
3

Head to the Paofai Gardens by following the waterfront. This planted promenade facing the harbor offers an unobstructed view of Moorea's silhouette. Outrigger canoes (va'a) train here in the late afternoon.

⏱ 50 min
4

The Pearl Museum traces the history of pearl farming in French Polynesia. In 45 minutes, you'll learn to distinguish luster, orient and shapes of Tahitian black pearls.

⏱ 45 min
5

Walk up Boulevard Pomaré to spot the street art murals created during the Ono'u festival. International artists have transformed several downtown facades into an open-air gallery.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

The roulottes at Place Vaiete set up every evening from 6 pm facing the harbor. Raw fish in coconut milk, chao mein, grilled chevrettes: a local institution under the stars.

⏱ 60 min

If you arrive on a Sunday, head to the Paofai Protestant temple to hear the polyphonic religious singing. Worshippers wear hats and white outfits—a unique sonic and visual spectacle.

Papeete

Where Polynesian markets meet waterfront roulottes and Moorea's silhouette rises from the harbor

★★★★★
📍 Faarumai Waterfalls Tahiti📍 Arahoho Blowhole Tahiti📍 Pointe Venus Mahina Tahiti📍 Tahara'a Viewpoint Tahiti📍 Museum of Tahiti and Her Islands Punaauia
1

The three Faarumai waterfalls lie at PK 22 on the east coast, 22 km from Papeete. The first, Vaimahuta, plunges 80 meters down a basalt cliff—five minutes' walk from the parking area. The other two, Haamaremare Rahi and Iti, require an additional 20 minutes on a steeper trail.

⏱ 75 min
2

Stop at the Arahoho Blowhole, right next to the waterfalls. The ocean rushes into a lava tube carved by erosion beneath the road and shoots a powerful jet of water up on the land side. In Tahitian, Arahoho means "the road that roars." The show depends on the swell: the stronger it is, the more impressive the spout.

⏱ 40 min
3

Take the coastal road to Pointe Venus in Mahina (PK 10). The white 25-meter lighthouse, built in 1867, marks the spot where Europeans landed in 1767. Black sand beach and views of Moorea at sunset.

⏱ 50 min
4

The Tahara'a viewpoint, at the start of the east coast leaving Papeete, offers from its landscaped terrace a panorama of the lagoon, the coral barrier reef and the Tahiti Iti peninsula.

⏱ 30 min
5

The Museum of Tahiti and Her Islands in Punaauia on the west coast (PK 15) traces the natural and cultural history of Polynesia. Collections of tapa cloth, traditional canoes and basalt tools. Check opening days before your visit.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swimming on a black sand beach along the east coast. The water is warm year-round but currents can be strong: respect the flags and stick to supervised areas.

⏱ 45 min

Vaiava Beach (PK 18, Punaauia) offers white sand on the west side and easy snorkeling above coral patches, with a direct view of Moorea.

Stop 3

Paopao

Cradled between two legendary bays at the foot of sacred Mount Rotui

★★★☆☆
📍 Cook's Bay Moorea📍 Opunohu Bay Moorea📍 Belvedere Lookout Opunohu Moorea📍 Mount Rotui Moorea📍 Agricultural School Opunohu Moorea📍 Ta'ahiamanu Beach Moorea
1

The ferry crosses the Vaiare pass and reveals Moorea, Tahiti's sister island. As soon as you dock, the trident-shaped silhouette of its mountains towers above: Cook's Bay to the left, Opunohu Bay to the right, separated by sacred Mount Rotui (899 m).

⏱ 45 min
2

Drive up the shaded little road to the Opunohu Lookout, at 240 meters altitude in the heart of a 9-km-diameter crater. The 360° panorama embraces both bays, eight mountain ranges, the lagoon and the pineapple fields below.

⏱ 60 min
3

On the way back down, stop at the Opunohu agricultural school. Students produce tropical fruit jams and pineapple juice from the valley. Archaeological sites (marae) lie nearby along the marked trail.

⏱ 45 min
4

Marae Titiroa, a Polynesian temple of basalt stones nestled in the vegetation of Opunohu Valley, bears witness to the ancient Ma'ohi religion. Explanatory panels line the site.

⏱ 30 min
5

The public beach of Ta'ahiamanu in Opunohu Bay unfurls a strip of white sand bordered by coconut trees. The shallow, calm water makes it a family-friendly swimming spot.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Snorkeling in the lagoon on the Cook's Bay side: butterflyfish, surgeonfish and small stingrays glide above corals accessible from the beach. Mornings offer optimal visibility.

⏱ 60 min

Lagoon boat tour between the two bays. You navigate at the foot of Mount Rotui, encounter stingrays and small blacktip reef sharks in the shallow waters around the motus.

⏱ 90 min

Paopao

Cradled between two legendary bays at the foot of sacred Mount Rotui

★★★☆☆
📍 Three Coconuts Pass Moorea📍 Te Fare Natura Moorea📍 Temae Beach Moorea📍 Magic Mountain Moorea📍 Manutea Distillery Moorea
1

Te Fare Natura, Moorea's eco-museum, sits at the exit of Opunohu Bay. Interactive exhibits on reef ecosystems, endemic fauna and the island's volcanic geology.

⏱ 50 min
2

Make a detour via the "Magic Mountain" for a stunning viewpoint over the turquoise lagoon on the west side. Access is via a short but steep track.

⏱ 30 min
3

Temae Beach, in the northeast near the airport, is one of the most beautiful in Polynesia. Pristine white sand, crystal-clear waters and a direct view of Tahiti in the distance.

⏱ 50 min
4

The Manutea fruit juice distillery-factory, located in Opunohu Bay, processes pineapples and local fruits. Free tasting of artisanal juices, liqueurs and jams.

⏱ 40 min
5

In the mood for a cultural show? Polynesian evenings offered on the island combine a ma'a tahiti buffet, traditional dances, percussion and fire show under the stars.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike to the Three Coconuts Pass starting from the Opunohu Lookout. The trail crosses dense tropical forest and emerges at a pass with views over both bays and the lagoon on the Haapiti side.

⏱ 180 min

Polynesian cooking class: preparing raw fish with lime and coconut milk, the fenua's signature dish. Some guesthouses offer this workshop.

Vaitape

Gateway village to the most stunning lagoon in the South Pacific

★★★★☆
📍 Matira Beach Bora Bora📍 Vaitape Bora Bora📍 Mount Otemanu Bora Bora📍 Bora Bora Lagoon📍 Motu Bora Bora
1

The plane flies over Bora Bora's lagoon before landing on Motu Mute—the former airstrip built by the Americans in 1942. A shuttle boat runs to Vaitape, the island's main village.

⏱ 40 min
2

Matira Beach, at the southern tip of the main island: a mile and a half of white sand fringed with coconut palms. It's Bora Bora's only public beach, and one of the most photographed in Polynesia. The water is shallow, the bottom sandy, ideal for swimming.

⏱ 75 min
3

From Matira, Mount Otemanu (727 m) dominates the landscape. Its basalt summit is the symbol of Bora Bora—impossible to miss, it cuts against the sky from any point on the lagoon.

⏱ 30 min
4

The small village of Vaitape stretches along the wharf. A few craft and black pearl shops line the main road. The atmosphere is calm, far from the bustle of the resorts on the motus.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Lagoon boat tour: coral gardens, swimming with friendly stingrays and observing blacktip reef sharks in the shallows between the motus. Maintain a respectful distance from wildlife.

⏱ 180 min

Sunset from Matira Beach: the sky turns orange and pink as Mount Otemanu stands out in silhouette. Bring something for a picnic.

⏱ 45 min

Vaitape

Gateway village to the most stunning lagoon in the South Pacific

★★★★☆
📍 American Guns Anau Bora Bora📍 Pointe Tereia Bora Bora📍 Popoti Lookout Bora Bora📍 Faanui Bay Bora Bora📍 Mount Pahia Bora Bora
1

Bora Bora still bears the traces of Operation Bobcat. In 1942, 4,000 GIs landed on this island of 1,000 inhabitants to turn it into a supply base in the Pacific. Eight 7-inch guns and anti-aircraft batteries were installed on the heights.

⏱ 30 min
2

The guns at Anau, at Pointe Fitiu, are the best preserved on the island. Twin battery on reinforced concrete mounts, bunker, ammunition shelter and network of trenches—all restored and explained by trilingual panels installed in 2021.

⏱ 50 min
3

Continue around the island via Faanui Bay and its church at the foot of the mountain. Two more guns sit at Pointe Tereia, accessible by a short trail with views of the motus and overwater bungalows.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Popoti Lookout, a former American radar station on the west coast, offers the best panoramic view of Bora Bora. Access via a steep uphill 4x4 track—on foot or by quad.

⏱ 45 min
5

At Vaitape wharf, a commemorative plaque recalls the presence of the Seabees (Navy construction battalions). At Motu Mute airport, a chronological frieze traces the history of Operation Bobcat.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Circle the island by bike or scooter: the ring road is about 32 km on flat terrain. You pass luxury hotels, fishing villages and viewpoints over the lagoon.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset outrigger canoe (va'a) trip. You glide across the lagoon as Otemanu glows red—book for golden hour.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 7

Uturoa

Tranquil harbor town on the sacred island, cradle of Polynesian civilization

★★★☆☆
📍 Uturoa Raiatea📍 Marae Taputapuatea Raiatea📍 Faaroa River Raiatea📍 Mount Temehani Raiatea📍 Te Ava Mo'a Sacred Pass Raiatea
1

Uturoa, Raiatea's main town, stretches along a quiet waterfront. Its small covered market sells fish, tropical fruits and local crafts. The atmosphere is much calmer than in Papeete.

⏱ 45 min
2

Marae Taputapuātea, a UNESCO World Heritage site since 2017, extends along the lagoon shore at Raiatea's southeastern tip. Dedicated to the god 'Oro, this political, ceremonial and funerary center brought together chiefs from across the Polynesian triangle. Several marae with distinct functions line a vast site of basalt stones facing the sacred pass Te Ava Mo'a.

⏱ 90 min
3

Opposite the marae, the sacred mountain Tea'etapu towers over Opoa Valley. A trail leads to the summit for a plunging view over the site, the lagoon and the ocean.

⏱ 40 min
4

Raiatea is the cradle of Ma'ohi civilization: it was from this island that ancient Polynesian navigators set out to colonize Hawaii, New Zealand and Easter Island. The Taputapuātea site remains a pilgrimage destination for Polynesians worldwide.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Boat or kayak trip up the Faaroa River, the only navigable river in French Polynesia. The motorized canoe pushes into lush jungle of tree ferns, purau and mape (Polynesian chestnuts). The guide tells legends of the valley.

⏱ 90 min

Swimming and snorkeling at Motu Iriru or Ofetaro, two public islets in Raiatea's lagoon accessible by boat. White sand, crystal water and tropical fish.

⏱ 60 min

Tranquil harbor town on the sacred island, cradle of Polynesian civilization

★★★☆☆
📍 Taha'a Vanilla Island📍 Tau Tau Coral Garden Taha'a📍 Pearl Farm Taha'a📍 Vanilla Plantation Taha'a📍 Motu Tautau Taha'a
1

Taha'a, nicknamed Vanilla Island, shares the same lagoon as Raiatea—30 minutes by boat is enough to reach it. The island produces about 80% of all French Polynesia's vanilla, the Vanilla tahitensis variety prized by chefs worldwide.

⏱ 40 min
2

Visit to a vanilla plantation: growers explain the manual pollination of the flowers, the harvest and the long months of preparation needed to obtain the brown, fragrant pods. The sweet scent fills the greenhouse.

⏱ 50 min
3

Stop at a family pearl farm to understand the grafting of the Pinctada margaritifera mother-of-pearl. Live demonstration and explanations on grading Tahitian black pearls—luster, orient, shape and size.

⏱ 45 min
4

Taha'a's rum distillery produces organic agricultural rum from sugar cane grown on former vanilla fields. Tasting available on site.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Drift snorkeling in the coral garden of Motu Tau Tau, northwest of Taha'a. You let the current carry you over a natural aquarium of colorful corals, anemones and tropical fish. Shallow, accessible to all.

⏱ 60 min

Lunch on a deserted motu: Polynesian barbecue of grilled fish, cooked bananas and coconut milk, feet in the white sand facing the turquoise lagoon.

⏱ 75 min

Observing blacktip reef sharks in the shallows on the reef side. They swim in very shallow water and pose no danger—still maintain a reasonable distance.

Back on Tahiti, explore the west coast. The black sand beach at Taharuu in Papara (PK 39) unfurls a long wild strip pounded by waves—a renowned local surf spot.

⏱ 50 min
2

Marae Arahurahu in Paea (PK 22.5 west coast) is the best restored in Tahiti. Its terraced stone platform, surrounded by coconut palms, still hosts reenactments of traditional ceremonies.

3

The Vaipahi Water Gardens (PK 49, Mataiea) offer a shaded botanical trail along waterfalls and pools amid dense tropical vegetation. Easy and free access.

4

The Maraa Caves, carved into the coastal cliff at Paea, shelter crystal-clear freshwater pools in a setting of ferns. A quick and surprising stop.

Late afternoon stroll along the Papeete waterfront, from Parc Bougainville to Place To'ata—venue for the Heiva i Tahiti, the major dance and song festival held every July.

The Tomb of King Pomare V in Arue on the east coast houses the burial site of Tahiti's last king, who died in 1891. The white coral monument topped with a funerary urn overlooks the lagoon.
