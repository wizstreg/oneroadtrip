Wisconsin's Essential Wonders

📅 20 days📍 20 stops🚗 1855 km⏱ 26h28

Superior → Mellen → Bayfield → Eagle River → Ellison Bay → Ephraim → Fish Creek → Jacksonport → Sturgeon Bay → Cedarburg → Lake Geneva → Horicon → Madison → Blue Mounds → New Glarus → Spring Green → Trempealeau → Wisconsin Dells → Baraboo

Wisconsin surprises: sea caves carved into Apostle Islands sandstone, lighthouses lining the Door County peninsula, 165-foot waterfalls plunging through billion-year-old basalt gorges. Add Taliesin, Frank Lloyd Wright's UNESCO estate, the boat-access-only canyons of Witches Gulch, and a Swiss village where the star beer is sold nowhere else. Twenty days of road-tripping between Great Lakes and Mississippi Valley.

✨ Highlights
  • Big Manitou Falls: Wisconsin's highest waterfall (165 feet), 4th tallest east of the Rockies
  • Apostle Islands: 22 islands and sea caves carved by Lake Superior
  • Door County: 11 lighthouses and 300 miles of shoreline on a peninsula between two Great Lakes
  • Taliesin: Frank Lloyd Wright's 800-acre estate, UNESCO World Heritage Site
📆 Best period

June, July, August, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 16 and 20 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Perfect weather across most regions (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, moderate crowds, and excellent conditions for national parks and cities. Ideal for diverse activities nationwide.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with spectacular fall foliage, harvest festivals, and comfortable weather. Perfect for outdoor activities, cultural events, fewer crowds than summer.

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warmest weather but highest crowds and prices. Excellent for northern regions, challenging in southwestern deserts, perfect for beaches and national parks.

Winter (December-February): Variable conditions from tropical Florida to snowy Colorado. Perfect for skiing, southern destinations, indoor attractions, lowest prices outside holiday periods.

🌍 What to discover in US?

Continental diversity with iconic landmarks, cultural melting pot, and endless possibilities. Experience natural wonders, vibrant cities, and authentic American dreams from coast to coast. The country offers incredible diversity with modern amenities and cultural richness. United States provides life-changing experiences with natural beauty and cultural authenticity.

📋 Important notes

20-day road trip crossing Wisconsin from north to south. Start in Superior (Duluth-Superior airport 15 min away). End in Baraboo (Madison 45 min drive, with airport). Car rental essential. Best seasons: summer (June-September) for water activities and parks, fall (October) for colors.

Day 1

Superior

Western gateway to Lake Superior, 20 minutes from Wisconsin's highest waterfall.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Big Manitou Falls Pattison State Park📍 Little Manitou Falls Wisconsin📍 Interfalls Lake Pattison📍 Black River gorge Superior Wisconsin
1

Big Manitou Falls - Wisconsin's highest waterfall awaits you 20 minutes south of Superior. The Black River plunges 165 feet (50 meters) into a basalt gorge carved 1.2 billion years ago by lava flows. Native Americans called it 'Gitchee Manitou' - the voice of the Great Spirit. Multiple viewing platforms offer unobstructed views from both sides of the gorge. Arrive early morning to beat the crowds and catch the best light.

⏱ 45 min
2

Little Manitou Falls - Two miles upstream, the river splits into two channels and drops 31 feet (9 meters) across a wide basalt shelf. Less dramatic than its big sister, but more accessible: you can scramble down to the pool to feel the spray. Watch for slippery rocks.

⏱ 30 min
3

Interfalls Lake and Beach - Between the two falls, the Black River slows and forms this man-made lake bordered by pines and spruces. The sandy beach (trucked in from Lake Superior shores in the 1930s by the Civilian Conservation Corps) makes for a pleasant swimming break.

⏱ 40 min
4

CCC Buildings - The Civilian Conservation Corps transformed this park between 1935 and 1941. The cut-stone shelter, nature center, and former bathhouse still stand. Interpretive signs tell the story of these young workers who came primarily from Illinois.

⏱ 25 min

Activities

Big Manitou - Little Manitou Loop Trail - This 4-mile trail follows the Black River and passes both waterfalls. Moderate elevation gain (215 feet). In fall, maples and birches frame the gorge in red and gold.

⏱ 120 min

Day 2

Mellen

Forest town at the foot of Copper Falls and its ancient lava gorges.

★★★★☆
📍 Copper Falls State Park Mellen📍 Brownstone Falls Wisconsin📍 Bad River gorge📍 Devils Gate Copper Falls📍 Loon Lake Mellen
1

Copper Falls - The tea-colored water of the Bad River (stained by tannins, not copper) plunges 29 feet down 1.2-billion-year-old black lava. The falls twist through several tiers in a canyon so narrow the walls rise 65-100 feet on either side. Wooden walkways and stone stairs bring you up close.

⏱ 45 min
2

Brownstone Falls - Just downstream, the Tyler Forks River joins the Bad River by plunging 40 feet over a red lava shelf. This is often the most photogenic of the two falls, especially after rain when the flow explodes.

⏱ 30 min
3

Devil's Gate - Further down the gorge, this massive rock formation creates a natural gateway over the river. Stone stairs (built by the CCC in the 1930s) descend to the water's edge. The place is impressive in its scale.

⏱ 35 min
4

Observation Tower - At the end of the CCC trail, this tower offers views across the forested hills of the Penokee Range to the north and, on clear days, Lake Superior on the horizon.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Doughboy's Nature Trail - This 1.7-mile loop around the gorges is the heart of the park. Budget 2 hours if you take your time at the six viewpoints. The trail is named for the WWI veterans who built the first suspension bridges here in 1922. No pets allowed on this trail.

⏱ 120 min

Swimming at Loon Lake - After the hike, head down to the lake for a swim in clear water surrounded by forest. The sandy beach is well-maintained.

Day 3

Bayfield

Harbor of 500 souls, launch point for Apostle Islands sea caves.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Apostle Islands National Lakeshore visitor center📍 Meyers Beach sea caves Wisconsin📍 Bayfield Wisconsin harbor
1

Apostle Islands National Lakeshore Visitor Center - Start here in Bayfield to understand the archipelago: 22 islands, 21 within the national park, carved by Lake Superior over billions of years. Rangers give briefings on sea conditions and visiting options. Maps and camping permits available.

⏱ 45 min
2

Meyers Beach Sea Caves (trail view) - If weather doesn't permit kayaking, take the coastal trail above the caves. Budget 1.5 miles to reach the first cavities, then another mile for the most spectacular ones. From atop the cliff, you'll see the arches, passages, and the famous 'land bridge' spanning the Fissure. The 500-million-year-old Cambrian sandstone takes on almost surreal shapes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Kayaking the Sea Caves - Bayfield's grand adventure. Outfitters offer guided trips of 2.5-3 hours from Meyers Beach. You paddle along the cliff face then enter the sandstone caverns - some large enough to glide through in your kayak. In summer, amber light from the setting sun illuminates the caves' interiors. You must be able to swim and sea conditions must be calm. Book ahead in July-August.

⏱ 180 min

Apostle Islands Cruise - If you're not a kayaker, the 2.5-3 hour cruise tours the islands, passes the lighthouses of Raspberry Island and Devils Island, and skirts the sea caves. Departs from Bayfield harbor.

⏱ 180 min

Bayfield

Harbor of 500 souls, launch point for Apostle Islands sea caves.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Bayfield Wisconsin orchards📍 Big Top Chautauqua Bayfield📍 Lake Superior Bayfield marina📍 Madeline Island ferry

Bayfield - This small Lake Superior harbor town (population under 500) was built on the lumber industry then reinvented itself as a tourist destination. Stroll the sloping streets lined with colorful Victorian houses. The marina is the launching point for all island excursions.

Bayfield Orchards - The peninsula is known for its apples, cherries, and berries. Several farms open their doors to visitors for pick-your-own (in season) or tastings of cider and preserves. It's particularly beautiful in May (apple blossoms) and October (harvest).

Lake Superior Views - Head up to the heights of Bayfield to see the Apostle Islands archipelago spread before you. On clear days, you can make out lighthouses on the nearest islands.

⏱ 120 min

Madeline Island Ferry Crossing - The largest Apostle Island (the only inhabited one) is 20 minutes by ferry. Historic village of La Pointe, Native American museum, Big Bay beach, and 10 miles of roads to explore by bike.

Day 5

Eagle River

Capital of the Northwoods and the world's longest chain of navigable lakes.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Eagle River chain of lakes Wisconsin📍 Northwoods Wisconsin📍 Eagle River fishing
1

Eagle River Chain of Lakes - Welcome to the Northwoods. Eagle River boasts the world's longest chain of navigable lakes: 28 lakes connected by natural channels spanning 28,000 acres. You can boat from one lake to another without ever touching land. The landscape is a mix of pine forests, lakefront homes, and wooden docks.

⏱ 45 min
2

Downtown Eagle River - Classic Northwoods small-town center with fishing tackle shops, burger joints, and Friday fish fries (Wisconsin's breaded fish tradition). Summer vacation Americana at its finest.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Guided Fishing - The lakes teem with largemouth bass, muskellunge, and walleye. Local guides offer half-day or full-day boat trips. The musky is the ultimate trophy - some specimens exceed 4 feet.

⏱ 240 min

Kayaking or Paddleboarding the Lakes - Quieter alternative to fishing. Rent a kayak or SUP and explore the channels between lakes. The water is clear and relatively warm in summer (72-77°F in July-August).

⏱ 120 min

Day 6

Ellison Bay

Door County peninsula's tip, gateway to Wisconsin's only Dark Sky Park.

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Newport State Park Ellison Bay📍 Dark Sky Park Wisconsin📍 Lake Michigan shoreline Door County
1

Newport State Park - Wisconsin's only wilderness park and the state's only certified Dark Sky Park. Over 2,300 acres of forest, 7 miles of Lake Michigan shoreline, and 30 miles of trails. Here, no paved roads or developed campgrounds - just walk-in campsites. The atmosphere is radically different from the more touristy rest of Door County.

⏱ 60 min
2

Lake Michigan Shoreline - The coast mixes sandy beaches and rocky shores. The water is cold (even in summer, expect 60-65°F) but remarkably clear. Sunrises over the lake are spectacular.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Newport State Park Hiking - The Europe Bay Loop Trail (5 miles) follows the coast through white cedar and birch forest before reaching Europe Bay beach - one of the peninsula's most secluded.

⏱ 150 min

Night Sky Observation - If skies are clear, return to the park after dark. Light pollution is virtually nonexistent here. The Milky Way is visible to the naked eye, and August meteor showers (Perseids) are a highlight.

⏱ 90 min

Day 7

Ephraim

Whitewashed village facing Green Bay sunsets.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Ephraim village Door County📍 Eagle Harbor sunset📍 Moravian Church Ephraim Wisconsin
1

Ephraim Village - This postcard village is a lineup of whitewashed buildings facing Eagle Harbor. Founded in 1853 by Norwegian Moravian immigrants, it has kept its character - no bars or liquor stores (the town stayed 'dry' until 2016). Browse art galleries, shops, and cafes along the main road.

⏱ 60 min
2

Eagle Harbor Sunset - In the evening, head to the village pier or beach. The sun sets behind Green Bay, illuminating the harbor's calm waters. This is Door County's most celebrated sunset spot.

⏱ 40 min
3

Moravian Church - The small white church at the village center dates from 1857. It's still active. The adjacent cemetery tells the story of the Norwegian community's early decades.

⏱ 25 min

Activities

Bonfire and Fish Boil - Door County tradition: fish boil involves boiling Lake Michigan whitefish with potatoes and onions in a huge outdoor cauldron. The show's climax: the 'boil over,' when the cook throws kerosene on the fire to make the cauldron overflow and eliminate the grease. Several Ephraim establishments offer it on summer evenings.

⏱ 90 min

Fish Creek

Base camp for Peninsula State Park and its 3,700 acres of coastal forest.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Peninsula State Park Fish Creek📍 Eagle Bluff Lighthouse Door County📍 Eagle Tower Door County📍 Nicolet Beach Fish Creek
1

Peninsula State Park - Door County's star park: 3,700 acres, one million visitors annually. Start with Eagle Tower, a 60-foot observation tower with panoramic views of Green Bay and the Strawberry Islands. Then head down to Eagle Bluff Lighthouse, built in 1868 and restored with period furnishings - guided tours May through October.

⏱ 75 min
2

Eagle Bluff Lighthouse - Perched on the Niagara Escarpment cliff (yes, the same geological formation as Niagara Falls), this stone lighthouse guided ships through the strait for over a century. The view of Green Bay waters from the lantern room is superb.

⏱ 40 min
3

Nicolet Beach - The park's main beach, facing Green Bay. Fine sand, calm shallow waters - perfect for families. Kayak rentals available on-site.

⏱ 45 min
4

White Cedar Nature Center - This small nature center in the park presents local wildlife and flora. Rangers offer guided walks and workshops in summer.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Biking in Peninsula State Park - The bike trail network (10 miles) crosses cedar forests, cliffs, and shorelines. The Sunset Trail follows the west coast with views across Green Bay. Bike rentals in Fish Creek.

⏱ 120 min

Northern Sky Theater - In the evening, catch an outdoor performance in the park's amphitheater. Original musical comedies about Wisconsin history and culture. Season runs June through September.

Day 9

Jacksonport

Calm Lake Michigan beaches and Cave Point's dolomite cliffs just minutes away.

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Cave Point County Park Door County📍 Whitefish Dunes State Park Wisconsin📍 Jacksonport Wisconsin beach
1

Cave Point County Park - Dolomite cliffs plunge straight into Lake Michigan. When wind blows offshore, waves rush into underwater caverns and explode back out through vents with thunderous noise. In calm weather, the water is so clear you can see the bottom at 20-25 feet. This is Door County's most photographed spot - and probably its most spectacular.

⏱ 60 min
2

Whitefish Dunes State Park - Right next to Cave Point. Lake Michigan's west shore's highest sand dunes (93 feet at the tallest point). The mile-long sandy beach is gorgeous for swimming. The Red Trail (2.5 miles) crosses the dunes and cedar forest.

⏱ 60 min
3

Jacksonport - Small village on the Lake Michigan side, facing sunrises. Quieter atmosphere than Fish Creek or Ephraim on the Green Bay side. A few shops and cafes along the road.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Kayaking at Cave Point - Paddle along the cliffs and glide into small caves carved by erosion. Outfitters from Sturgeon Bay or Jacksonport offer guided trips. The lake must be calm - check conditions in the morning.

⏱ 120 min

Sturgeon Bay

Shipbuilding town and gateway to the Door County peninsula.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Sturgeon Bay Door County📍 Door County Maritime Museum📍 Cana Island Lighthouse📍 Potawatomi State Park
1

Sturgeon Bay - Door County's 'gateway' is also a shipbuilding town. The canal cutting the peninsula in two connects Green Bay to Lake Michigan. Downtown is pleasant with art galleries, restaurants, and shops.

⏱ 45 min

Door County Maritime Museum - The history of shipbuilding and Great Lakes navigation. Models, tools, and shipwreck stories (Door County owes its name to the dangerous currents of 'Death's Door,' the passage between the peninsula and Washington Island).

Cana Island Lighthouse - 30 minutes north of Sturgeon Bay. Cross the gravel causeway (sometimes flooded) to this 9-acre island. The 89-foot lighthouse dates from 1869. Climb the 97 steps for the view. Door County has 11 lighthouses total - more than any other Wisconsin county.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wine and Cider Tasting - Door County has several wineries and cideries. The peninsula's cherries produce fruity wines and easy-drinking ciders. Ask to try cherry wine, the local specialty.

Cedarburg Historic Downtown - Over 200 nineteenth-century buildings line the streets of this small town north of Milwaukee. Lake Geneva

Gilded Age estates encircling a glacial lake 90 minutes from Chicago.

★★★☆☆
📍 Lake Geneva Wisconsin📍 Black Point Estate📍 Geneva Lake Shore Path📍 Riviera Ballroom Lake Geneva
1

Black Point Estate - This Queen Anne mansion from 1888 was built by beer magnate Conrad Seipp as a summer residence. Accessible only by boat (the cruise is part of the experience). Twenty rooms furnished in period style, verandas overlooking the lake. It perfectly captures the Gilded Age when wealthy Chicago families escaped the city for Lake Geneva's shores.

⏱ 90 min
2

Riviera Ballroom - This 1932 Art Deco building on the lakefront hosted the great swing-era orchestras. Today it serves as a performance venue and cruise departure point. The architecture is worth a stop even without a show.

⏱ 30 min
3

Geneva Lake Shore Path - This 21-mile trail circles the entire lake passing in front of Gilded Age estates. You don't have to do it all: a 3-5 mile section is enough to see the finest properties and enjoy the views.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Lake Geneva Cruise - Several cruise types: full lake tour (2h), mail boat cruise (the mailman jumps from the moving boat to deliver mail to lakefront property mailboxes - unique in the world), or sunset cruise.

Day 13

Horicon

America's largest freshwater marsh, stopover for thousands of migrating geese.

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Horicon Marsh National Wildlife Refuge📍 Horicon Marsh auto tour📍 Horicon Wisconsin birding
1

Horicon Marsh - The largest freshwater marsh in the United States: 32,000 acres of wetlands split between a national refuge and state area. In fall (September-November), tens of thousands of Canada geese stop here during migration - the spectacle is striking. In spring, it's egrets, herons, and sandhill cranes.

⏱ 60 min
2

Boardwalk - A wooden walkway extends over the marsh offering close-up views of water lilies, reeds, and birds. Binoculars essential. You'll probably see turtles and, with luck, a bald eagle.

⏱ 45 min
3

Auto Tour - A 3-mile driving loop crosses the refuge with pull-offs for observation. It's the easiest way to cover the marsh if you have limited time or young children.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Bird Watching - Over 300 bird species have been recorded at Horicon Marsh. The federal visitor center and state visitor center provide guides and maps of the best observation spots by season.

Day 14

Madison

Wisconsin's capital perched on an isthmus between two lakes, giant farmers' market.

★★★☆☆
📍 Olbrich Botanical Gardens Madison📍 Wisconsin State Capitol Madison📍 Dane County Farmers Market Madison
1

Olbrich Botanical Gardens - Sixteen acres of gardens on Lake Monona's shore. The Bolz Conservatory, under its glass pyramid, houses 750 tropical plants from 550 species and an indoor waterfall. Outside, don't miss the Thai garden with its sala pavilion - the only one outside Thailand. Free admission to outdoor gardens.

Wisconsin State Capitol - The white granite dome dominates downtown Madison, set on an isthmus between Lakes Mendota and Monona. Free guided tours. ⏱ 60 min
3

State Street and UW Campus - The pedestrian street connects the Capitol to the University of Wisconsin campus. Bookstores, restaurants, cafes, and student atmosphere. At the end, the Memorial Union Terrace on Lake Mendota is where locals drink beer in the evening facing the sunset.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

Biking Along the Lakes - Madison is one of America's most bike-friendly cities. The Capitol City Trail circles Lake Monona (12 miles) on separated paths. Bike-share rentals (B-cycle).

Day 15

Blue Mounds

Southern Wisconsin's high point and a 488-million-year-old limestone cave.

★★★☆☆
📍 Cave of the Mounds Wisconsin📍 Blue Mound State Park📍 Driftless Area Wisconsin
1

Cave of the Mounds - This limestone cave is 488 million years old. Discovered in 1939 during a quarry blasting operation, it remained intact and contains remarkable formations: stalactites, stalagmites, helictites (growing in all directions), and soda straws (hollow, translucent formations). The guided tour lasts about one hour and the underground temperature is constant at 50°F - bring a jacket.

⏱ 75 min
2

Surface Karst Trail - Above the cave, a trail through restored prairie explains the region's karst geology: sinkholes, fissures, and underground water circulation. Well-designed and educational.

⏱ 35 min
3

Blue Mound State Park - Southern Wisconsin's highest point (1,710 feet). Two observation towers offer views across the Driftless Area - this Wisconsin region never covered by glaciers that retained unique rolling topography.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Driftless Area Hiking - Blue Mound's trails cross wildflower prairies and oak forests. The contrast with the flat lands of the rest of the Midwest is striking.

Day 16

New Glarus

Midwest's Little Switzerland, birthplace of Spotted Cow sold nowhere else.

★★☆☆☆
📍 New Glarus Wisconsin Swiss village📍 New Glarus Brewing Company📍 Sugar River State Trail
1

New Glarus - Wisconsin's 'Little Switzerland.' Founded in 1845 by 108 immigrants from the Canton of Glarus, this town has taken its Swiss heritage all the way: flower-decked chalets, German signage, cheese factories, and bakeries. The historical museum recreates a nineteenth-century Swiss village.

⏱ 60 min
2

New Glarus Brewing Company - Wisconsin's most cult brewery. Their Spotted Cow (a creamy farmhouse ale) is sold only in Wisconsin - making it a prized contraband item for Illinois neighbors. Free tour of the exterior and beer garden. Tastings at the bar.

⏱ 50 min
3

Historical Museum - Several reconstructed buildings: chalet, cheese factory, forge, school. The story of Swiss immigration to Wisconsin is well told.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Sugar River State Trail - This former rail line converted to bike trail crosses 24 miles of rolling countryside between New Glarus and Brodhead. It passes through a railroad tunnel and several bridges. Bike rentals available in the village.

⏱ 120 min

Local Cheese Tasting - Wisconsin is America's top cheese producer. Around New Glarus, several artisan cheese factories offer tastings of gruyère, aged cheddar, and fresh cheese curds (the famous 'squeak-squeak' that squeaks under your teeth).

Day 17

Spring Green

Taliesin and Frank Lloyd Wright's last restaurant design in the world.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Taliesin Frank Lloyd Wright Spring Green📍 Hillside Home School Wright📍 Riverview Terrace Cafe Spring Green
1

Taliesin - Frank Lloyd Wright's residence, studio, and 800-acre estate in the Driftless Area hills. The name comes from Welsh meaning 'shining brow' - Wright built his house on the hillside, not the summit, because 'if you build on top of the hill, you lose the hill.' Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2019, it's Wisconsin's only publicly accessible UNESCO site. Budget 2-4 hours depending on the tour chosen.

⏱ 120 min
2

Hillside Home School (1903) - The school Wright designed for his aunts, with its enormous assembly hall and diamond-paned windows. Later became the Taliesin apprentice architects' workshop.

⏱ 45 min
3

Romeo and Juliet Windmill (1896) - One of Wright's early commissions. The structure of interlocking diamond and octagon was so bold his uncle bet it wouldn't stand. It's still there, 130 years later.

⏱ 25 min
4

Riverview Terrace Café - Wright's last restaurant design in the world. Panoramic windows overlooking the Wisconsin River. Local cuisine, relaxed atmosphere. Make it a lunch stop.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Taliesin Guided Tour - Four tour options, from 1h to 4h. The 'Highlights' tour (2h) offers the best time-to-content ratio: you visit the school, Wright's residence, and studio. The 'Estate' tour (4h) covers the entire grounds including Midway Barn and gardens. Booking essential.

⏱ 150 min

Day 18

Trempealeau

500-foot bluffs above the Mississippi and the mountain that soaks in the water.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Perrot State Park Trempealeau📍 Mississippi River bluffs Wisconsin📍 Trempealeau Mountain
1

Perrot State Park - Limestone bluffs rise 500 feet above the Mississippi. The name 'Trempealeau' comes from French 'la montagne qui trempe à l'eau' - the mountain that soaks in the water - an isolated peak rising from the floodplain like an island. From the bluff tops, the view across the Mississippi Valley is vast: meanders, wooded islands, and transport barges.

⏱ 60 min
2

Trempealeau Mountain - Climb to the summit of this 400-foot remnant butte for 360° views of the Trempealeau and Mississippi rivers' confluence. The trail is 2 miles round-trip.

⏱ 50 min
3

Trempealeau River Confluence - At the mountain's base, the river joins the Mississippi in a calm meander. Marshy area good for birding.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Kayaking Trempealeau Bay - Paddle through backwaters and channels of the Mississippi. The water is calm here, lined with willows and populated by herons and bald eagles. Outfitters rent kayaks at the water's edge.

⏱ 120 min

Bluff Hiking - Brady's Bluff Trail (1.5 miles round-trip, 500 feet elevation gain) is the most popular trail. The climb is steep but short, and the summit reward is worth it.

Day 19

Wisconsin Dells

Cambrian sandstone canyons and boat-access-only Witches Gulch.

★★★★☆
📍 Wisconsin Dells boat tour📍 Witches Gulch Wisconsin📍 Stand Rock Wisconsin Dells📍 Upper Dells sandstone canyon
1

Upper Dells Sandstone Gorges - The Wisconsin River carved these canyons through 500-million-year-old Cambrian sandstone. The walls rise 100-130 feet and take spectacular shapes: columns, arches, mushroom rocks. The only way to see them is by boat - the shores are private and protected.

⏱ 30 min
2

Witches Gulch - Accessible only from the cruise, this narrow gorge is carpeted with moss and ferns. A wooden walkway winds between sandstone walls so close together there's almost never direct sunlight. Photographer H.H. Bennett discovered and named it in the 1860s - his photos launched tourism at Wisconsin Dells.

⏱ 35 min
3

Devil's Lake State Park - Wisconsin's most-visited park, and for good reason: a glacial lake trapped between two 500-foot quartzite bluffs. These rocks, 1.6 billion years old, are among the continent's oldest. The contrast between turquoise water and pink cliffs is striking.

⏱ 45 min
2

East Bluff Trails - Climb the East Bluff Trail (1.5 miles) for plunging views over the lake and Baraboo Hills. The trail passes through 'talus fields' - piles of quartzite blocks stacked by erosion - a rare habitat for certain moss and lichen species.

⏱ 75 min
3

Circus World Museum - Baraboo was headquarters of the Ringling Brothers (America's greatest circus) from 1884 to 1918. The museum, housed in the original buildings, preserves decorated circus wagons, posters, and memorabilia. In summer, live circus performances are given under the big top.

⏱ 60 min

Activities
