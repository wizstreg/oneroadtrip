⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 8 and 12 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Peak Summer (July-August): Perfect weather for hiking and festivals, warmest temperatures, highest prices, most crowded attractions and alpine areas.

Shoulder Seasons (April-June, September-October): Excellent balance of good weather, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, ideal for sightseeing and cultural activities.

Winter (December-March): World-class skiing season, Christmas markets, cozy atmosphere, higher accommodation prices in ski resorts, magical snowy landscapes.

Spring (April-May): Blooming flowers, mild weather, perfect for Vienna and cultural sites, fewer tourists, excellent value for money.

🌍 What to discover in AT?

Mozart's birthplace offers imperial palaces, Alpine beauty, and world-class classical music scene. Vienna's coffeehouses and Salzburg's baroque architecture create cultural magic while ski resorts provide winter adventures. The Sound of Music landscapes and Danube River cruises enchant visitors. Austria perfectly combines high culture with natural splendor.

Stop 1

Vienna

Where imperial palaces waltz with coffeehouses and Klimt

★★★★★
📍 Stephansdom Wien📍 Hofburg Wien📍 Wiener Staatsoper📍 Schloss Schönbrunn Wien📍 Kunsthistorisches Museum Wien
1

St. Stephen's Cathedral (Stephansdom), a 12th-century Gothic masterpiece dominating Vienna's historic heart. Its glazed tile roof forming a double-headed eagle is instantly recognizable. Climb the Südturm tower—343 steps up—for sweeping panoramas over Vienna's rooftops and spires.

⏱ 75 min
2

Hofburg Palace, winter residence of the Habsburgs for six centuries, now an imperial museum complex. Sisi's apartments faithfully recreate the intimate life of the empress. The neighboring Baroque National Library shelters 200,000 rare volumes beneath a painted dome that seems to float.

⏱ 90 min
3

Ringstrasse and State Opera House: this monumental 5 km boulevard was laid out by Franz Joseph I between 1857 and 1865. The State Opera (Staatsoper), inaugurated in 1869, stages 300 performances per year—standing-room tickets from €3 let you attend dress rehearsals.

⏱ 60 min
4

Schönbrunn Palace (UNESCO), the Habsburgs' summer residence with 1,441 rooms and French-style gardens. The Imperial Apartments, the Hall of Mirrors where 6-year-old Mozart performed, and the Gloriette on the hilltop offer direct immersion in the Austro-Hungarian Empire.

⏱ 120 min
5

Kunsthistorisches Museum (Museum of Art History), facing the Natural History Museum on Maria-Theresien-Platz. One of Europe's greatest art collections featuring Vermeer, Rubens, Bruegel, and a world-class Egyptian collection.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Viennese coffeehouse at Café Central (Herrengasse 14), set in the former Ferstel Palace since 1876. Leon Trotsky played chess here before 1917. Order a Melange and a slice of Sachertorte for an authentic imperial pause.

⏱ 45 min

Stroll through the Naschmarkt, a 500-meter covered market along the Wienzeile. Over 120 stalls offer Austrian cheeses, cured meats, oriental spices, and Wachau wines. Open Monday through Saturday, liveliest in the morning.

⏱ 60 min

Visit the Upper Belvedere to see Klimt's The Kiss in its original setting, among the world's largest collection of Art Nouveau and Vienna Secession paintings. The terraced gardens with fountains are freely accessible.

⏱ 75 min

Stop 2

Durnstein

A cobalt-towered medieval gem floating above the Danube

★★★★☆
📍 Dürnstein Altstadt📍 Ruine Dürnstein📍 Stift Melk📍 Weingut Domäne Wachau📍 Wachau Donau
1

Medieval village of Dürnstein, a true jewel of the Wachau Valley. Its cobbled lanes lined with pastel Baroque houses, fountains, and wine cellars cluster around the Augustinian Abbey, whose blue tower is the valley's emblem—visible for kilometers along the Danube.

⏱ 60 min
2

Dürnstein Castle ruins perched 230 meters above the village. Here Richard the Lionheart was imprisoned in 1192 after the Third Crusade, held by Duke Leopold V of Austria. The ransom of 150,000 silver marks equaled twice England's annual revenue. 20-30 minute climb from the village, free access.

⏱ 75 min
3

Melk Abbey (UNESCO), a Benedictine fortress-monastery dominating the Danube from a rocky spur 60 meters high. Founded in the 11th century, redesigned in flamboyant Baroque in the 18th, it houses a library of 100,000 volumes, a gilded church, and a panoramic terrace over the Wachau. Benedictine monks still live and work here today.

⏱ 120 min
4

Terraced vineyards of the Wachau between Melk and Krems, 30 km of UNESCO-listed landscape since 2000. Three protected appellations: Steinfeder (light), Federspiel (medium), and Smaragd (age-worthy). The Grüner Veltliner and Riesling from these limestone slopes rank among Austria's finest white wines.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Short Danube cruise between Dürnstein and Spitz (30 min) with DDSG or Brandner. Unique views of vineyard cliffs, castle ruins, and villages tucked into hidden coves. Available May through October, hourly departures from Dürnstein pier.

⏱ 60 min

Grüner Veltliner tasting at a local winemaker's. Buschenschenken (vintner taverns marked by a pine branch) serve their own production alongside regional charcuterie and cheeses. Domäne Tegernseerhof and Weingut Knoll are among the most renowned.

⏱ 60 min
Bioweingut Schmidl Rosenstöckel10

Filzmoos

Where alpine meadows bow to the Bishop's Mitre peaks

★★★☆☆
📍 Filzmoos Dorfzentrum📍 Pfarrkirche Filzmoos📍 Bischofsmütze Filzmoos📍 Papageno Gondelbahn Filzmoos
1

Village of Filzmoos at 1,057 meters altitude, nestled between the twin peaks of the Bischofsmütze ('Bishop's Mitre') and the Dachstein massif. This authentic alpine hamlet in Salzburgerland has been certified as an 'Austrian Hiking Village' and offers 250 km of marked trails within a 10 km radius.

⏱ 45 min
2

Pilgrimage Parish Church of Filzmoos, a 15th-century Gothic edifice dedicated to Saints Peter and Paul. It houses the sculpture of the Filzmooser Kindl, a colorful Gothic Christ Child surrounded by a carved golden halo—one of the village's 17 'Power Spots.'

⏱ 30 min
3

Panorama of the Bischofsmütze (2,454 m) from the village, this twin-peaked summit dominating the entire Schladming-Dachstein region. Its silhouette recalls a bishop's mitre, hence the name. Part of the Gosaukamm massif in the Limestone Alps.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Arrival hike to the Hofpürglhütte (1,705 m), an alpine refuge at the foot of the Bischofsmütze. From the Unterhofalm parking lot, count on 2 hours uphill for 600 meters of elevation gain. Spectacular views of the Gosaukamm massif and Dachstein. Refuge open mid-May through late October.

⏱ 240 min

Papageno cable car from the village center to access high-altitude pastures and panoramic trails without effort. Starting point for numerous family hikes in summer, ski area in winter.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 4

Filzmoos

Where alpine meadows bow to the Bishop's Mitre peaks

★★★☆☆
📍 Dachstein Krippenstein Obertraun📍 5 Fingers Plattform Dachstein📍 Eisriesenwelt Dachstein📍 Hallstatt📍 Welterbeblick Hallstatt
1

Dachstein Krippenstein cable car from Obertraun, two successive sections climbing to 2,109 meters. From the summit, panoramic views over the Limestone Alps, Lake Hallstatt, and the Traun gorges. Bring warm clothing even in summer—temperatures drop 10 to 15°C.

⏱ 90 min
2

5 Fingers viewing platform at 2,109 meters, five steel walkways jutting into the void above a 400-meter cliff. Each has a different design—one features a transparent glass floor. From the cable car, a 20-minute walk on a marked trail.

⏱ 60 min
3

Dachstein Ice Cave (Rieseneishöhle), a 50-minute guided tour through ice galleries formed over millennia. King Arthur's Cathedral and the Ice Palace with its 9-meter ice cone are the highlights. Permanent temperature of -2°C—jacket required.

⏱ 75 min
4

Mammoth Cave (Mammuthöhle), one of Europe's largest caves with 70 km explored. Limestone galleries with spectacular formations carved by water over millennia. Guided tour independent of the ice cave—combination ticket recommended.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike on the Dachstein Rundwanderweg, a 3-day circuit looping around the massif. Even a half-day on the Krippenstein-Schönbergalm section offers magnificent glacier views. Well-marked trail, intermediate level.

⏱ 180 min

Hallstatt (20 min from Filzmoos): Austria's most photographed lakeside village, squeezed between lake and cliffs. 7,000-year-old salt mine (UNESCO), Beinhaus (ossuary) decorated with painted skulls, and views from the Welterbeblick platform 360 meters above the lake.

⏱ 180 min
meiZeit Lodge9.7

Stop 5

Zell Am See

Turquoise waters cradled between glacier and summit

★★★★☆
📍 Zeller See📍 Zell am See Altstadt📍 Schmittenhöhe Zell am See📍 Pfarrkirche St. Hippolyt Zell am See
1

Lake Zell (Zeller See), a 4.5 km by 1.5 km expanse of water cradled between the Kitzsteinhorn massif (3,203 m) and the Schmittenhöhe (1,965 m). The complete lakeside promenade covers 11.3 km and takes 2.5 hours on a flat, level trail. The turquoise waters reach 24°C in July-August.

⏱ 120 min
2

Town of Zell am See, a medieval borough founded in the 8th century by monks. The Romanesque tower of St. Hippolytus Church (12th century) dominates the old town. The lanes of the historic center with their shops and restaurants contrast with the alpine panoramas all around.

⏱ 60 min
3

Schmittenhöhe cable car (1,965 m), 15-minute ascent from the town center. At the summit, the Pinzgauer Spaziergang panoramic trail offers views of 30 peaks over 3,000 meters including the Grossglockner. Mountain restaurant with sunny terrace.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Grossglockner High Alpine Road (48 km, toll ~€36), a scenic route with 36 numbered hairpin turns crossing Hohe Tauern National Park. Start from Fusch an der Glocknerstraße. Must-stop viewpoints: Edelweißspitze (2,577 m, 360° panorama) and Kaiser-Franz-Josefs-Höhe (2,369 m) for views of the Pasterze Glacier.

⏱ 300 min

Swimming in Lake Zell from the public beach at Seespitz or the municipal campground beach. Pedal boats, sailboats, and paddleboards for rent on the west shore. Water temperature 22-24°C in July-August.

⏱ 90 min

Mayrhofen

Gateway to permanent glaciers and Zillertal wilderness

★★★☆☆
📍 Mayrhofen Hauptstraße📍 Penkenbahn Mayrhofen📍 Stilluptal Mayrhofen📍 Stillupstausee
1

Village of Mayrhofen at the head of the Zillertal valley, surrounded by the Penken (1,790 m) and Ahorn (2,000 m) massifs. This Tyrolean village with its flower-decked chalets is the gateway to four mountain areas. The main street Hauptstraße concentrates equipment shops, restaurants, and Heuriger (wine taverns).

⏱ 45 min
2

Penkenbahn, a modern 3S cable car (2015) linking the village to Ahorn station (2,000 m) in 10 minutes. Cabins hold 32 passengers, 1,000 meters of elevation, 3 km of cable. At the summit: panoramas over the Zillertal, the Hintertux glaciers, and peaks of the Zillertal Alps reaching 3,510 meters.

⏱ 120 min
3

Stilluptal, a wild valley starting from Mayrhofen via a toll road (€5). Protected nature reserve featuring the Stillupstausee reservoir (80 million m³), waterfalls, and forest trails. Perfect for a gentle forest hike with dam views.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Penken panoramic circuit (4.4 km) from the upper station, a trail suitable for strollers and families looping around alpine meadows with views over the four Zillertal valleys. Duration 1.5 hours, minimal elevation gain, several refuges along the route.

⏱ 120 min

Gold Mine of Zell am Ziller, 10 minutes by car—tour of underground galleries from a 16th-century gold mine. Journey by small mining train followed by an exhibition on extraction history. Open May-October.

⏱ 90 min
Apparthotel "Landhaus Veronika"9.7

Mayrhofen

Gateway to permanent glaciers and Zillertal wilderness

★★★☆☆
📍 Hintertuxer Gletscher📍 Gletschereishöhle Hintertux📍 Schlegeisspeicher📍 Erlebnisbad Mayrhofen
1

Excursion to Hintertux and its permanent glacier (3,250 m), Austria's only year-round skiable glacier. In summer, you can walk on the ice, explore marked crevasses, and enjoy panoramas over the Zillertal Alps from 3,250 meters. Cable car from the head of the Tuxertal valley.

⏱ 180 min
2

Hintertux Glacier Cave (Gletschereis- und Tropfsteinhöhle), accessible next to the cable car. Ice stalactites and stalagmites in a network of galleries carved into the glacier. Temperature -2°C, 45-minute guided tour, spectacular lighting.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike to Schlegeisspeicher (1,782 m), an alpine reservoir at the head of the Zillertal Grund accessible via a scenic road from Ginzling (20 km from Mayrhofen). Turquoise lake surrounded by peaks over 3,000 meters, easy trails around the lake. Parking €8.

⏱ 240 min

Erlebnisbad Mayrhofen, an aquatic complex with indoor pool, water slides, wellness area, and heated outdoor pool with mountain views. Ideal for uncertain weather or an evening of relaxation after hiking.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 8

Pertisau Achensee

Crystal waters where mountains dive into silence

★★★☆☆
📍 Achensee📍 Pertisau Dorf📍 Gaisalm Achensee📍 Achenseeschifffahrt
1

Lake Achensee, Tyrol's largest lake (9 km long, 1.5 km wide) at 930 meters altitude between the Karwendel and Rofan massifs. The exceptionally clear water (visibility up to 10 meters) and its turquoise hues shifting with the light make it one of the most beautiful lakes in the Austrian Alps.

⏱ 60 min
2

Village of Pertisau on the lake's west shore, preserved with its traditional alpine architecture. Lake center and starting point for all activities: boating, hikes toward the Karwendel, via ferratas. The 18th-century Baroque church is worth a look.

⏱ 45 min
3

Boat cruise on the Achensee with Achenseeschifffahrt company (130 years of history, 6 piers). The MS Stella Maris (1912) was Austria's first diesel-powered boat. Complete crossing Pertisau-Achenkirch in 40 minutes, possible stop at Gaisalm accessible only by boat or on foot.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Gaisalm hike from Pertisau (8.8 km, 2h) along the wild east shore with no vehicle road. Isolated Gaisalm refuge accessible only by this trail or by boat. Return by boat possible. Intermediate level, well-marked trail.

⏱ 240 min

Achensee Steam Railway (Achenseebahn, 1889), narrow-gauge track covering 6.79 km linking Jenbach to the Achensee with 440 meters of elevation gain. The original steam locomotive is Austria's oldest still in service. Departures from Jenbach (15 min by car from Pertisau) from April to October.

⏱ 90 min
Appartements Am Achensee9.6

Stop 9

Ehrwald

Standing in the shadow of Germany's highest throne

★★★☆☆
📍 Ehrwald Dorf📍 Zugspitze📍 Tiroler Zugspitzbahn📍 Seebensee Ehrwald📍 Coburger Hütte
1

Village of Ehrwald at 994 meters in the Außerfern valley, at the foot of the Wetterstein massif. Authentic Tyrolean town with its flower-balcony farmhouses, Baroque church, and direct views of the Zugspitze's north face (2,962 m). Austrian-side starting point for ascending Germany's highest summit.

⏱ 45 min
2

Zugspitze (2,962 m) via the Tiroler Zugspitzbahn, an Austrian cable car departing from Ehrwald and reaching the summit in 10 minutes in 100-person cabins. At the top: views across 4 countries (Austria, Germany, Switzerland, Italy), Faszination Zugspitze museum, panoramic restaurant, and the Schneeferner Glacier.

⏱ 180 min
3

Seebensee (1,657 m), a mountain lake in a rocky cirque at the foot of the Zugspitze. Accessible by hike from Ehrwald (2.5h, 650 m elevation gain) or via the Ehrwalder Almbahn. Icy green-reflecting waters, limestone boulders, alpine silence—one of the Wetterstein massif's most beautiful settings.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Hike to Coburger Hütte (1,917 m) from Seebensee—30 additional minutes from the lake to reach this refuge with views over the Drachensee. Intermediate level, well-marked trail, refuge open June to October.

⏱ 180 min

BAHNORAMA anno 1926 museum in the Zugspitzbahn cable car's lower station, tracing 100 years of alpine cable car history with models, archival documents, and period films. Entrance included with cable car ticket.

⏱ 45 min
Sonnblick9.5

Stop 10

Lech Am Arlberg

Europe's most beautiful village guards a heart-shaped lake

★★★★☆
📍 Lech am Arlberg Dorf📍 Formarinsee Lech📍 Rüfikopf Lech📍 Skyspace Lech
1

Village of Lech am Arlberg at 1,450 meters, founded in the 13th century by Walser shepherds from the Swiss Valais. Their traditional wooden houses with steep roofs still characterize the village, voted most beautiful village in Europe in 2004. The Lech torrent flows through the center under stone bridges.

⏱ 60 min
2

Formarinsee (1,789 m), a heart-shaped lake voted Austria's most beautiful place in 2015 by Heute magazine readers. Turquoise waters surrounded by alpine meadows and 2,500-meter peaks. Accessible by hike from Lech (2h, 350 m elevation gain) or by cable car to Rüfikopf then 45 min on foot.

⏱ 150 min
3

Rüfikopf cable car (2,362 m) offering 360° panoramas over the Arlberg massif, the Vorarlberg Alps, and, in clear weather, the Säntis massif in Switzerland. Mountain restaurant with terrace, starting point for numerous hikes into protected areas.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike on the Arlberg Trail (40 km linking Lech, Zürs, Stuben, St. Christoph, and St. Anton)—even a day on the Lech-Zürs section (8 km, 2h) offers exceptional panoramas over alpine pastures and glaciers. Red-white markings, intermediate level.

⏱ 240 min

Skyspace Lech, a James Turrell contemporary art installation integrated into the landscape. This contemplation space plays with natural light and sky perception through a zenithal opening. 45-minute guided tours in the evening for twilight effects.

⏱ 60 min
Naturhaus Loacker9.6

Stop 11

Brand Vorarlberg

Where limestone walls cradle the Pearl of the Alps

★★★☆☆
📍 Brand Vorarlberg📍 Lünersee📍 Lünerseebahn📍 Totalphütte📍 Bürser Schlucht
1

Brandnertal valley in Vorarlberg, enclosed between the Rätikon and Bregenzerwaldgebirge massifs. The village of Brand at 1,037 meters is the starting point for several cable cars and access to the Lünersee. Traditional Vorarlberg chalets, alpine cheese dairies, and open panoramas.

⏱ 45 min
2

Lünersee (1,970 m), a heart-shaped lake surrounded by limestone walls with direct views of the Schesaplana (2,965 m), the Rätikon's highest point. Classified as the 'Pearl of the Alps,' its turquoise waters contrast with the gray scree of surrounding cliffs. Complete lake loop: 6 km, 2h, easy.

⏱ 150 min
3

Lünerseebahn from Brand to the lake in 5 minutes, cable car open mid-May through late October. The views during the ascent over the valley and Rätikon peaks are already spectacular before even reaching the lake.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Hike toward Totalphütte (2,385 m) from Lünersee—1.5 hours additional ascent to reach this refuge facing the Schesaplana glaciers. Challenging level, mountain equipment required above 2,200 meters.

⏱ 180 min

Bürser Schlucht gorges (2h route), trail on walkways and suspension bridges through limestone gorges with waterfalls and rock formations. Start from Bürs (10 min from Brand). Spectacular and accessible for everyone.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 12

Innsbruck

Habsburg elegance launching skyward on Hadid steel

★★★★★
📍 Goldenes Dachl Innsbruck📍 Altstadt Innsbruck📍 Hofburg Innsbruck📍 Nordkettenbahn Innsbruck📍 Hofkirche Innsbruck
1

Golden Roof (Goldenes Dachl), a covered balcony with 2,657 fire-gilded copper tiles built in 1500 for Emperor Maximilian I. This imperial box above the old town allowed the emperor to watch tournaments from his palace. It's Innsbruck's absolute emblem.

⏱ 45 min
2

Innsbruck Old Town (Altstadt), a preserved medieval ensemble between the Golden Roof and the Nordkette. Cobbled lanes, colorful Baroque-fronted houses, Herzog-Friedrich Street with its Gothic arcades, and the exuberant 18th-century Baroque stucco Helbling House.

⏱ 60 min
3

Hofburg Palace Innsbruck, the Habsburgs' summer residence rebuilt by Maria Theresa in the 18th century in late Baroque style. The Giant Hall with life-size portraits of the empress's 22 children is particularly impressive. Audio guide included with admission.

⏱ 75 min
4

Nordkettenbahn, funicular and cable cars designed by Zaha Hadid linking Innsbruck's center to Seegrube (1,905 m) then Hafelekar (2,256 m) in 30 minutes from the old town. Complete aerial views over Innsbruck and the Inn Valley from the Karwendel peaks.

⏱ 150 min
5

Court Church (Hofkirche), Maximilian I's mausoleum with his cenotaph surrounded by 28 two-meter bronze statues nicknamed the 'black fellows.' These bronze giants represent ancestors, allies, and legendary figures of the Habsburg house.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Ascent to Bergisel, an Olympic ski jump designed by Zaha Hadid (2002) with a 50-meter panoramic tower. Breathtaking views over Innsbruck and the Alps from the glass tower. Summit restaurant accessible by elevator. Two Olympic Games were held here (1964 and 1976).

⏱ 60 min

Alpine Zoo Bergisel (2,000 alpine arc animals, Europe's highest zoo at 750 m), accessible from Bergisel. Wolves, lynx, brown bears, and golden eagles in enclosures open to alpine panoramas. Included with Innsbruck Card.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 13

Innsbruck

Habsburg elegance launching skyward on Hadid steel

★★★★★
📍 Schloss Ambras Innsbruck📍 Swarovski Kristallwelten Wattens📍 Mutteralmbahn📍 Nockspitze
1

Ambras Castle (4 km south of Innsbruck), Italian Renaissance built by Archduke Ferdinand II in the 1560s. Houses three museums: the armor collection (400 pieces, one of Europe's finest), the Wunderkammer (chamber of wonders), and the Habsburg portrait gallery. The Spanish Hall (43 m long) is one of Austria's most important Renaissance interiors.

⏱ 120 min
2

Swarovski Crystal Worlds (Kristallwelten) in Wattens, 20 km east of Innsbruck. Founded in 1895, Swarovski factory creates a cultural park around crystal with giant art installations, sculpture gardens, and museum boutique. Shuttle from Innsbruck in 30 min.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Hike on the Nockspitze (2,404 m) from the Mutteralmbahn station (1,620 m) above Mutters/Natters, accessible from Innsbruck by tram and bus. 800 meters additional elevation gain, unparalleled panorama over Innsbruck. Intermediate to challenging level.

⏱ 300 min

Innsbruck gastronomy in restaurants and Wirtshäuser (inns) in the historic center. Must-tries: Apfelstrudel at Strudelcafé Kröll (Innrain), Tiroler Gröstl (potato, bacon, and egg skillet), and Speckknödel (bacon dumplings) washed down with local Stiegl Brewery beer.

⏱ 90 min
Nala Individuellhotel9.2

Stop 14

Salzburg

Where Mozart's notes echo through Baroque golden halls

★★★★☆
📍 Festung Hohensalzburg📍 Altstadt Salzburg📍 Salzburger Dom📍 Schloss Mirabell Salzburg📍 Getreidegasse Salzburg
1

Hohensalzburg Fortress, one of Europe's best-preserved fortresses, dominating the city from a rocky outcrop 120 meters high. Built in 1077 by Archbishop Gebhard, expanded through the 16th century. The princely apartments, armory, and panoramas over the Salzburg Alps from the ramparts justify the funicular or foot ascent.

⏱ 120 min
2

Salzburg Old Town (Altstadt), a UNESCO-listed exceptional Baroque ensemble erected under the prince-archbishops of the 17th century. Getreidegasse with its wrought-iron signs, Residenzplatz with its Baroque fountain, and numerous art galleries and boutiques compose a unique urban scenography.

⏱ 90 min
3

Salzburg Cathedral (Dom), a North Italian Baroque masterpiece with three naves completed in 1628. The dome frescoes, monumental organs, and facade bronzes make it one of the Alps' most beautiful religious buildings. Mozart was baptized here on January 28, 1756.

Mirabell Gardens, a 17th-century French garden designed by Fischer von Erlach, with Hohensalzburg Fortress as backdrop. The geometric parterres, mythological statues, central basin, and fountains offer Salzburg's most famous photographic composition.

Salzburg

Where Mozart's notes echo through Baroque golden halls

★★★★☆

Mozart's Birthplace (Mozarts Geburtshaus), Getreidegasse 9—the third-floor apartment where Wolfgang Amadeus was born on January 27, 1756. The child's harpsichord, miniature violin, and original manuscript scores are among the most touching pieces in this biographical museum.

⏱ 60 min
2

Residenz of the Prince-Archbishops (Residenz), a Baroque palace of 180 rooms occupying an entire side of Residenzplatz. The Prince's Gallery houses a remarkable collection of Rembrandt, Rubens, and Flemish paintings. The grand reception hall with its illusionist frescoes is one of Austria's most impressive Baroque spaces.

⏱ 75 min
3

Hellbrunn, a 17th-century pleasure palace 4 km from the center with its famous Wasserspiele (mechanical water games). Archbishop Markus Sittikus installed 265 hidden water jets to surprise his guests during banquets. Still in working order, these hydraulic automata are unique in Europe.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Tour of the Salzburg lakes—excursion to the nearby Salzkammergut (St. Wolfgang, Mondsee, Wolfgangsee) 40-50 km from Salzburg. Mondsee with its Baroque church (setting for The Sound of Music) and St. Wolfgang with Pacher's altarpiece (1481) are accessible in a half-day.

⏱ 240 min

Salzburg market and gastronomy at the Grünmarkt (daily market) near the cathedral. Specialties: authentic Fürst Mozartkugeln (not the aluminum foil version), peasant bread, alpine cheeses, and Stiegl Goldbräu on tap in the Mönchsberg beer gardens.
