Off the Beaten Track Cambodia: Mondulkiri, Ratanakiri & Wild Coastlines

📅 13 days📍 12 stops🚗 1310 km⏱ 26h

Phnom Penh → Sen Monorom → Banlung → Kratie → Kampot → Koh Rong Samloem

This 12-day circuit takes you into the Cambodia most travelers never see. You leave Phnom Penh for the hills of Mondulkiri, nicknamed the Switzerland of Cambodia, where you walk with elephants through the jungle and visit Bunong villages. You continue to Ratanakiri and its 700,000-year-old volcanic lake Yeak Laom, surrounded by tribes living outside of time. After a stop on the Mekong at Kratie for the Irrawaddy dolphins, you descend to the pepper plantations of Kampot and finish on the preserved beaches of Koh Rong Samloem. A journey for those who want to see the other face of Cambodia.

✨ Highlights
  • Mondulkiri: elephants walk freely through the jungle.
  • Ratanakiri: the volcanic lake has held its crystal waters for 700,000 years.
  • Kratie: freshwater dolphins swim up the Mekong at twilight.
  • Kampot: pepper grows on hillsides facing the gulf.
  • Koh Rong Samloem: plankton lights up the sea on moonless nights.
📆 Best period

November, December, January, February, March

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 14 and 18 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Cool Dry Season (November-February): Perfect weather, comfortable temperatures, minimal rainfall, ideal touring, clear skies, peak tourist season, advance booking recommended.

Hot Season (March-May): Hot temperatures, dry conditions, fewer crowds, reasonable prices, early morning activities, challenging midday heat.

Rainy Season (June-October): Heavy rainfall, lush landscapes, muddy roads, lowest prices, fewer tourists, challenging travel, dramatic storms.

Best Temple Visiting (November-March): Comfortable temperatures, clear skies, optimal photography, manageable heat, ideal conditions, peak season.

🌍 What to discover in KH?

Ancient Khmer empire with Angkor Wat temples, rich history, and warm hospitality. Experience archaeological wonders, traditional crafts, and authentic Southeast Asian culture. The country offers cultural depth with natural beauty and historical significance. Cambodia provides transformative cultural experiences with archaeological treasures and cultural resilience.

📋 Important notes

The circuit ends at Koh Rong Samloem. For the return, ferry to Sihanoukville (45 min) then road or domestic flight to Phnom Penh (4h by road or 45 min by flight). Allow an extra half-day for the return journey.
