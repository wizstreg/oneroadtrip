Changsha

★★★☆☆
1

Juzizhou Island stretches 5 km down the middle of the Xiang River, and it was here that Mao wrote his 1925 poem *Changsha*. A colossal head of young Mao was planted there in 2009, something to see or skip depending on your appetite for state mythology. Go in late afternoon, sun low over the water, sparse crowds. Allow 1h30 including the walk from the north entrance.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Taiping Quarter is unabashedly kitsch: a pedestrian street rebuilt from scratch, red lanterns, frying steam clouds. It's also your best base for first contact with *xiang* cuisine, the spiciest of China's eight great cuisines. Ask for 微辣 (*wēi là*, mildly spicy) if you want to survive. Try the red-braised pork, Mao's favorite dish.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Dine early at a local *xiang cai* brasserie in the Taiping Quarter. On the menu: fried fermented tofu (stinky tofu), red-braised pork, spicy green beans. If the heat scares you, say 不要辣 (*bù yào là*, no spice). Jet lag will catch up around 9 p.m., don't linger after the meal. Rest is mandatory, two weeks of walking begin tomorrow.

⏱ 90 min

Changsha

★★★☆☆
1

A 2,200-year-old mummy with joints still flexible: that's Lady Dai, pulled from the Mawangdui tomb sealed in 168 BC and excavated in 1972. The centerpiece of the Hunan Museum, free with WeChat reservation, closed Mondays. Add the embroidered silks, silk manuscripts, black lacquerware from the same tomb. Allow a full half-day, no less.

⏱ 240 min
2

Pozi Street is a surviving market alley in the heart of old Changsha, miraculously spared by the city's massive construction campaigns. Narrow sidewalks, doorstep restaurants, baozi steam in the morning. Good photo of the calligraphic signboards. Allow 1h for a leisurely crossing, early morning ideal before the lunch crowd.

⏱ 60 min
3

Lines of fifty people in front of a Cha Yan Yue Se counter? This is Changsha, capital of modern Chinese *milk tea* and home to Hunan TV, the country's most-watched channel. Continental pop culture is manufactured here. Spot the young crowd in streetwear on Huangxing Road. A cup costs 15-25 yuan, 30-min wait, worth trying once for the local experience.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Book the Hunan Museum on WeChat 48h in advance (free but daily quota). Present your passport at the entrance. English audio guide available, 20 yuan. The Mawangdui section is in the basement - crowds concentrate there, rush in at 9 a.m. opening. Natural exit toward Lantern Festival Park.

⏱ 30 min

If the political angle interests you, allow a half-day for Mao's Memorial in Shaoshan, 1h30 drive from Changsha. Birth house, lotus pond, museum. Very ideologically charged, instructive on the construction of the cult. Otherwise, stay in town and walk Hexi toward the river at dusk, view of illuminated Juzizhou. For Shaoshan, full-day taxi 600 yuan.

⏱ 240 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 342 km · 1h50

Nanchang

★★★☆☆
1

Built in 653, destroyed and rebuilt 29 times, today's Tengwang Pavilion overlooking the Gan River dates from 1989, on a Song model. Poet Wang Bo composed his *Preface to the Pavilion of Prince Teng* there in 675, a text every Chinese schoolchild learns by heart. Ticket 50 yuan, climb to the top floor. River view, especially at dusk.

⏱ 90 min
2

Nanchang lacks the charm of southern old towns: it's a gray, functional metropolis, marked by militant communism. But Bayi Square (八一, eight-one) commemorates August 1, 1927, day of the uprising that marks the birth of the People's Liberation Army. To understand state mythology, it's the useful stop. Vast square, statue, vertical monument.

⏱ 45 min
3

Bayi, pronounced 'eight-one', thus commemorates the 1927 uprising; the adjoining museum retraces the episode (free, closed Mondays). One hour suffices to grasp the official angle. Explanations are in Chinese with partial English, period photos, weapons, letters. Exit toward Renmin Square side, nearby restaurants for lunch.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Go up to the pavilion at dusk: warm light on the curved roofs and river compensates for the modern reconstruction. Avoid weekends after 5 p.m., 45-min queue. On weekdays, allow 15 min. Audio guide in Chinese only, but panels give the essentials in English. Floating restaurants on the Gan River below for dinner.

⏱ 90 min

Sesame oil and rice wine form the base of *three-cup chicken* (三杯鸡, sanbei ji), Jiangxi's signature dish. Try it in the Honggutan district, chosen for your night. Gan cuisine is milder than Hunan but still spicy - stick with 微辣. Allow 60-90 yuan per person, decent local restaurant.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 270 km · 1h30

Wuyuan

★★★☆☆
1

White walls, black roofs shaped like bird wings (*matou qiang*, horse-head walls), carved beams: Huizhou architecture spans 35 villages scattered across Wuyuan County. These villages grew wealthy from the 14th to 19th centuries thanks to Huizhou merchants who controlled tea and salt trade on the Yangtze. Today, they tell the story of pre-industrial merchant China.

⏱ 60 min
2

Likeng dates back to 1010 under the Song: canals crossing the lanes, arched stone bridges, houses perched on water. First stop on the classic *Eastern Route*. More touristy than neighboring villages, but it's the ideal introduction to understand the architecture. Allow 1h30 on site. Avoid bus groups arriving around 10 a.m.

⏱ 90 min
3

Wangkou's Yu Xin Hall is a masterpiece of wood carving, beams and lintels elaborately worked. The village also preserves an ancient wharf, testament to Huizhou river trade. Quieter than Likeng, real houses still occupied by their owners. Allow 1h. In the morning, oblique light ideal for photographing interior courtyards.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Book a full-day driver through your hotel for the Eastern Route: Likeng, Wangkou, Jiangwan. Allow 300-400 yuan for the day, negotiable. Public transport exists but wastes 2h. Departure 8:30 a.m., return around 5 p.m. Driver waits during visits, simplifies access to more remote villages. Cash or WeChat Pay.

⏱ 60 min

Steamer baskets of *four steamed delicacies* (蒸菜四宝) stack fish, vegetables, ham and bamboo cooked by steam. Wuyuan specialty, simple and just right. Look for *minsu* offering countryside menu at 80-120 yuan. Rice grows locally, fish come from surrounding rivers. No alcohol required, local tea accompanies.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 40min

Xiaoqi

★★★☆☆
1

Xiaoqi is the most beautiful village in the county according to local consensus, divided into Upper and Lower Xiaoqi. Look for wood carvings in the ancestral halls, mixing pine flowers, qilin and scenes from Han mythology. Morning light falls perfectly on the central square. Allow 1h30, early departure mandatory to beat the groups.

⏱ 90 min
2

Huangling is only accessible by cable car: village suspended on a mountainside, famous for its *shaiqiu* (晒秋, autumn drying) in October-November - rooftops covered with round baskets filled with red peppers, yellow squash, corn. The most viral image of Wuyuan. Off-season, architecture alone justifies the ascent. Ticket 110 yuan cable car included.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

If you're traveling in April, replace Huangling with Jiangling: terraced rapeseed fields cascade in golden waves, the springtime photo of Wuyuan. The choice between the two depends strictly on season, see the When to Go to China blog post. Outside April and October-November, Xiaoqi alone suffices, save the half-day to rest your legs before Jingdezhen.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 25min

Jingdezhen

★★★☆☆
1

The English word *china* (porcelain) comes from here. Jingdezhen has been producing since the year 1004 and supplied the Ming and Qing imperial courts for six centuries. The *blue and white* you know as Delft, Meissen and other European copycats starts from this city. The technique of pure kaolin was invented on site. Every showcase in the world's great museums has a piece born in Jingdezhen.

⏱ 30 min
2

Opened in 2020, the Imperial Kiln Museum designed by Studio Zhu-Pei lines up brick vaults inspired by ancient kilns. Remarkable architecture, collection of fragments found on the very site. Free entry, WeChat reservation mandatory, closed Mondays. Allow 1h30. Outer courtyard overlooks the antiques market.

⏱ 90 min
3

At Taoyangli, you hear the potter's wheel from the entrance. The Ancient Kiln Folk Museum occupies a vast park where artisans work live. You can climb into a reconstructed Qing dragon kiln, buy a piece directly from the workshop. Ticket 53 yuan. Allow 3-4h. Demonstrations stop at 4 p.m., plan for early afternoon.

⏱ 210 min

Activities

Aim for the Taoxichuan market on Saturday or Sunday evening for contemporary ceramics, Tuesday and Friday for vintage pieces. Factory district converted to artist studios - the city's alternative quarter. If you want to bring back a real piece, it's here, not in airport shops. Allow 200-2000 yuan depending on the piece.

⏱ 120 min

Book Imperial Kiln Museum entry on WeChat the day before, daily quota (free but limited). Passport scanned at entrance. English audio guide available, 30 yuan. Museum bookstore sells bilingual editions on Chinese porcelain history, interesting as intellectual souvenirs. Avoid Monday (closed).

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 210 km · 1h40

Lushan

★★★★☆
1

A local proverb says: 'You don't know Mount Lu's true face, because you're inside it' (Su Shi, 11th c.). Mist rises so quickly at Lushan that contours disappear. The mountain, UNESCO-listed in 1996, is also a political place: altitude station developed by British missionaries in the 19th century, became summer residence for Chiang Kai-shek then Mao.

⏱ 60 min
2

Meilu Villa housed Chiang Kai-shek then Mao. Small, modest, heavy with history - it was here that the 1959 Lushan Conference took place, where Peng Dehuai dared criticize the Great Leap Forward and was purged. Period furniture is in place. Entry 10 yuan. Allow 45 min. Panels in Chinese, phone translation via Google Translate useful.

⏱ 45 min
3

Five rocky peaks lined up above Poyang Lake: the Wulaofeng, or Five Old Heads. Clear view on clear days, thick fog otherwise. Sandiequan Falls tumbles in three stages, celebrated by Li Bai in the 8th century. Allow 2h round trip on foot. Well-marked trails, sustained elevation gain, closed-toe shoes recommended.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

If you come in November, check high-altitude trail conditions before departure: frost can close Wulaofeng and Sandiequan. Guling tourist office (the summit village) posts info in Chinese; ask your hotel to verify. In case of closure, stick to Meilu Villa and the village, which are worth the detour alone.

⏱ 30 min

Allow two hours round trip to Sandiequan Falls from the main trail. Good shoes, water, altitude rises to 1474 m. Stay in Guling for sunrise if you want magical light on the sea of mist - Lushan Hotel guesthouse stays open year-round. Otherwise, sleeping down in Jiujiang is more comfortable.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 617 km · 4h30

Zhangjiajie

★★★★★
1

Assumed transport day, no mid-route visit. Morning, descent from Mount Lushan to Jiujiang station. High-speed rail Jiujiang-Changsha South (2h), 45-min connection, high-speed rail Changsha South-Zhangjiajie West (2h30). The high-speed line to Zhangjiajie opened late 2019 and radically changes access to the old Hunan hinterland. Arrival late afternoon.

⏱ 30 min
2

Wulingyuan, UNESCO-listed in 1992, opens five entrances onto 4,810 hectares. You'll sleep in Wulingyuan town, near the East Gate, more practical than Zhangjiajie city for the two hiking days that follow. Driver-guide picks you up at the West station, 40-min drive. Light Tujia dinner, rest. Trails begin tomorrow at 8 a.m.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Book the local driver-guide in advance via a local agency or hotel in Wulingyuan: 800-1200 yuan per day for two days in the park. English-speaking difficult to find, Mandarin guaranteed - use Google Translate via Pleco. Guide simplifies multiple tickets and anticipates less crowded cable car time slots. Cash or WeChat Pay.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 40min

Wulingyuan

★★★★★
1

Bailong Elevator swallows 326 m in 1 min 32 sec against the canyon wall, world's tallest outdoor elevator. 72 yuan for the ascent, alternative to the 2h trail. In high season (Golden Week, Chinese New Year, summer), allow 2-3h queue - arrive 7:30 a.m. or book through guide. Exit leads directly to Yuanjiajie plateau.

⏱ 60 min
2

Avatar Hallelujah Mountain pillar was officially renamed in 2010: Yuanjiajie inspired James Cameron for the film's Hallelujah Mountains, and China seized the marketing opportunity. 3h loop, First Bridge under Heaven platform spanning two pinnacles. Crowds concentrated midday, plan early or late.

⏱ 180 min
3

Tianzi Mountain cable car opens panoramic view over Suoxi Canyon from the upper plateau. 67 yuan one way. Allow 30 min queue off-season, 1h30 in high season. Summit plateau covers 2h on foot, flat trails, multiple observation platforms. Red sandstone pillars glow at sunset, exit 6 p.m.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Arrive at Bailong at 7:30 a.m. in high season, it's the only slot without a 2h queue. Local guide knows the park's internal buses that avoid saturated cable cars. Hat, water, snacks - interior park restaurants are expensive and mediocre. Bring meal from hotel or Wulingyuan town. Allow 8h on site.

⏱ 60 min

Tujia call *Sansheng pork* their signature dish: fatty belly cooked three times, melt-in-your-mouth, served with scallion and pepper. Try it in Wulingyuan town at dinner, accompanied by sweet potato noodles. Allow 80-120 yuan for two. Restaurants lined up on main street, little English. Point at menu photos.

⏱ 90 min

Wulingyuan

★★★★★
1

Huangshizhai offers the park's widest panoramic platform, accessible by cable car (72 yuan) or on foot in 2h30 ascent. The most widely distributed photo of Zhangjiajie is taken from the Six Wonders Pavilion viewpoint. If you climb on foot, plan 3L of water and walking sticks. Cable car is cost-effective in high season to save two hours.

⏱ 150 min
2

Golden Whip Stream winds 5.7 km along a torrent, pinnacles on both sides, flat trail. The park's most contemplative moment: dense forest, green water, little noise. Allow 3-4h round trip. Macaque monkeys at trailside, don't feed them, they steal. Golden light around 4 p.m. in the first half of the gorge.

⏱ 240 min
3

Six Wonders Pavilion overlooks the true postcard panorama. Platform built on stilts, packed midday. Early morning (8-9 a.m.), sparse crowds, residual mist softening the pinnacles. Allow 30 min on site. Bench, shelter in case of rain, starting point for several secondary trails.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb on foot to Huangshizhai if weather is clear and legs are good: 2h30 ascent, 700 m elevation gain, paved trail. Cable car is the simple option at 72 yuan. Both lead to the same viewpoint. If the day forecasts rain, do Golden Whip Stream in the morning (more protected cover) and Huangshizhai in the afternoon by cable car.

⏱ 150 min

If your legs hold, descend Golden Whip Stream in late afternoon, return around 6 p.m. Exit via South Gate, internal shuttle (free) to Wulingyuan. Sunset in the gorge remains a great moment. Late dinner in Wulingyuan town, early bed - tomorrow's Tianmen day is just as demanding.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 50min

Zhangjiajie

★★★★★
1

Tianmen Shan peaks at 1518 m, 8 km from downtown - distinct from Wulingyuan National Forest Park. The cable car measures 7,455 m, one of the world's longest, opened in 2005. Flies over Tongtian Avenue, the 99-bend road. Allow 30 min ascent. Buddhist temple at summit, glass walkway glued to the cliff.

⏱ 240 min
2

A natural arch 131.5 m high pierced through the cliff: Tianmen Cave, the Heaven's Gate. 999 steps climb to the arch. Russian fighter jets flew through it in 1999, event that launched local tourism. Today escalator in the cliff for those who don't climb. Vertiginous views, mineral and misty world.

⏱ 120 min
3

Glass Bridge measures 430 m long and 300 m high, world's longest glass bridge at its 2016 opening. 50 km east of the main park, in Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon. Strong sensation for those who handle heights; others stay below, equally spectacular panorama from canyon bottom. Massive Chinese crowds.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If you keep both sites in one day, it's exhausting: Tianmen morning (cable car 7:30 a.m.), express transfer to Grand Canyon early afternoon, Glass Bridge 4 p.m. 11-12h day, doable. Simpler: Tianmen alone, relaxed dinner in Zhangjiajie city. Glass Bridge then becomes optional extension.

⏱ 60 min

Choose Tianmen in the morning, base cable car departure at 7:30 a.m. to avoid 1h30 queues at noon. Combined tickets (entry + cable car + escalators) 261 yuan. If yesterday was heavy at Wulingyuan, keep Tianmen alone and rest up for Furong tomorrow. Light meal below, summit restaurant is expensive and average.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 30min

Furong

★★☆☆☆
1

A 60 m waterfall tumbles right through the village center: this is Furong Ancient Town, the thousand-year-old town suspended by the waterfall. 40 m wide, western Hunan's largest falls. Tujia village clings to the cliff above Youshui River, 2,000 years of claimed history. A walkway passes behind the water curtain - guaranteed sensation of sonic vertigo.

⏱ 90 min
2

Tusi Palace, entirely in wood, served as summer palace for Tusi Tujia kings. Structure on the cliff, stacked roofs, steep stairs. Allow 1h. Five-Mile Flagstone Street, the paved street crossing Furong, was the old Hunan-Sichuan trade route. Today Tujia handicraft shops, some authentic, many not.

⏱ 90 min
3

Xizhou Bronze Pillar dates from late Tang: erected by Ma Yin, King of Chu, to seal a pact with Tujia chiefs. The engraved document is still legible on the patinated bronze. Precious historical piece, displayed under glass shelter. Allow 30 min. Village served as set for Xie Jin's 1986 film *Hibiscus Town*, two awards at Beijing Festival.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take the walkway passing behind the waterfall in late afternoon, when golden light strikes the water curtain. In the evening, the village lights up and daytime crowds disappear. Allow 1h for crossing and photos. Jackets slightly damp at exit, spray reaches the walkway. Village entry ticket 108 yuan.

⏱ 60 min

Tujia *bacon* smoked six months over wood fire is Furong's other signature, along with *pickled mustard* made famous by the film. Hibiscus Town Restaurant on the main square offers both. Allow 100-150 yuan for two. View of illuminated waterfall from evening terrace. Reservation unnecessary on weekdays.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 75 km · 40min

Fenghuang

★★★★☆
1

Very touristy by day, Fenghuang truly awakens in the evening, when buses leave and lanterns light up along the Tuojiang. Wooden stilt houses, stone bridges, paved alleys: Phoenix town remains one of Hunan's best-preserved ancient villages. Tujia-Miao Autonomous Prefecture of Xiangxi. Allow 1h for the first crossing of both banks.

⏱ 90 min
2

Shen Congwen, one of 20th-century China's great writers, was born here. His house-museum is small, moving: photos, manuscripts, work table. *Border Town* (边城), his best-known novel, takes place between Fenghuang and the Tuojiang River. A short book to read before the trip. Allow 45 min on site. Entry 10 yuan.

⏱ 45 min
3

East Gate, Qing bricks on red sandstone base, guards the northeast entrance to the old town. 18th-century watchtower, paid climb (10 yuan) for rooftop view. Allow 20 min. Nearby, the contemporary Tujia painter Huang Yongyu's workshop-museum, Fenghuang native and still living, opens when the artist isn't in residence.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wake before 8 a.m. to see women beating laundry on Tuojiang rocks, daily life scene before tour buses arrive. Walk from the north bank, cross stone bridges, take bamboo walkways. Morning light is best for photographing stilt houses. Allow 1h30, riverside coffee afterwards.

⏱ 90 min

Xiangxi Miao still wear silver headdresses in surrounding villages. Half-day excursion to Miaowangzhai or Shanjiang (30-45 min drive), to see the crafts - silverwork, black-blue embroidery - outside the central tourist décor. Allow 250-350 yuan for half-day driver. Try the *sour fish soup* for lunch on site.

⏱ 240 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 175 km · 2h50

Fanjingshan

★★★★★
1

Two twin rocky pinnacles, each crowned by a temple, separated by a deep crevasse, linked by a narrow stone bridge: this is the Golden Summit of the Red Cloud (Hongyun Jinding) of Fanjingshan. Buddha Temple and Maitreya Temple seem to levitate when the sea of mist rises around 4 p.m. Guizhou's most widely distributed photo.

⏱ 180 min
2

Fanjingshan peaks at 2,572 m, UNESCO-listed in 2018 for exceptional biodiversity: endemic Guizhou golden monkey, Abies fanjingshanensis fir, giant salamander. Sacred Buddhist sanctuary, one of China's five Maitreya mountains. Still little visited by Westerners. East entrance via Jiangkou, ticket 110 yuan plus cable car 160 yuan.

⏱ 60 min
3

Ten meters high, a summit much wider than the base, balanced for millions of years: the Mushroom Rock (Mogu Shi) visibly defies gravity, improbable geological formation of Fanjingshan. 30 min walk from summit cable car, allow 1h round trip. Frequent mist, humid trail, caution. No railing, stay behind safety line.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Allow a long stairway after the cable car to reach the Golden Summit stone bridge, several thousand steps. The ordeal is real, especially in humid heat. Departure at 7 a.m., return 2-3 p.m. on the sea of mist. Good shoes, water, snacks. Cable car closed in case of strong wind or snow (rare). Opening 07:00-15:00 last ascent.

⏱ 360 min

If weather cuts bridge access at altitude, cable car remains operable to mid-height and lower botanical trail remains accessible. The trip's buffer day serves precisely for this case. Check weather the day before with local tourist office (limited English, ask hotel). Plan B: day in Tongren and its Tujia museum.

⏱ 60 min

Guiyang

★★☆☆☆
1

Perched on a rocky islet in the middle of Nanming River, Guiyang's Jiaxiu Pavilion lines up three floors of curved roofs since 1598, Ming dynasty. An arched stone bridge leads there. Allow 1h on site, photo of illuminated pavilion at night. Ideal passing between two trains. Free entry, riverside cafés.

⏱ 60 min
2

Miao, Dong, Buyi, Yi, Shui: Guizhou is one of China's most mixed provinces, and the provincial museum in Guiyang showcases this ethnic diversity. Costumes, instruments, modern scenography. Free entry, closed Mondays, WeChat reservation. Allow 1h30. Useful to prepare the gaze before Longji and the Red Yao villages that will follow.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Take advantage of the connection to visit Guiyang in a few hours: high-speed rail Tongren-Guiyang North (1h40), luggage storage at station, taxi to Jiaxiu Pavilion (15 min), then provincial museum. Leave late afternoon for Guilin West (3h). Total 5h train, 2-3h on site. If timing tight, skip museum and stick to pavilion plus lunch.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 413 km · 3h

Guilin

★★★☆☆
1

An elephant drinking in the river, that's the silhouette: Elephant Trunk Hill plunges into the Li forming a natural arch. Five minutes suffice for the glance, but the photo is obligatory, it's Guilin's graphic symbol. Avoid weekends after 2 p.m., lines of domestic tourists. The surrounding park deserves 30 min more. Entry 75 yuan.

⏱ 60 min
2

Unabashed kitsch: 240 m of limestone tunnel lit in multicolor LED since the 2000s, that's the modern experience of Reed Flute Cave. The site has been open to tourists since the Tang, ancient inscriptions on walls. Allow 1h with grouped guided visit (English on reservation). Ticket 120 yuan. Afternoon exit to avoid school groups.

⏱ 60 min
3

Rare outside Beijing: a Ming-Qing palatial complex so well preserved. Jingjiang Princes' Palace, in Guilin's very center, keeps ramparts, courtyards and ceremonial halls. Today shared with Guangxi Normal University. Allow 1h30. Ticket 100 yuan, Chinese guided tour by default, English audio guide possible.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Climb Diecai in 30 minutes for 360° view over Guilin and karst pinnacles. 70 yuan entry. Go at sunset - golden light strikes limestone rocks, Li River stands out in meanders. Summit pavilion, bench, classic Guilin photo. Descent in 25 min, dinner afterwards near Banyan Lake.

⏱ 90 min

If you insist on the evening Two Rivers Four Lakes boat ride, allow 210 yuan for 1h. Illuminated bridges, Sun and Moon Lake pagodas reflected in water. Touristy, but lighting transforms the city. Embarkment around 8 p.m. If you prefer calm, dinner on terrace of a Banyan Lake-side restaurant suffices.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 1h20

Yangshuo

★★★★☆
1

Pinnacles file past for 83 km between Guilin and Yangshuo, the Li River descent remains a Chinese classic. Karst peaks succeed one another, some drowned in mist, others reflected in flat water. Foreign boats with panoramic windows and lunch included, 380-450 yuan, rates revised each year. Embarkment at Mopanshan around 9:30 a.m., arrival Yangshuo 1:30 p.m.

⏱ 240 min
2

The Xingping-Yangdi section appears on the 20-yuan bill, around km 60 of the descent. When the boat approaches, pull out the bill and compare: it's exactly the same view. Ritual little game for all Chinese passengers on board, who pull out their wallets at the same moment. Mandatory deck platform for photo, limited space.

⏱ 30 min
3

West Street (Xijie) is super-touristy but live music bars and restaurants deserve a detour - legacy of 80s-90s backpackers, first Westerners to land here. Chinese and international cuisine, prices doubled but correct quality. Allow 1h30 for early evening stroll. Dense crowd after 8 p.m.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Embark at Mopanshan around 9:30 a.m. for Li River descent. Book 24-48h in advance via Trip.com or hotel. Foreign boats have panoramic windows, lunch served on board, multilingual microphone. Local boats cheaper from Yangti, different embarkment pier. Toilets on board, don't eat too much before - waters sometimes rough the first hour.

⏱ 60 min

Yulong bamboo raft is now in PVC painted to imitate bamboo, less authentic than before. But the landscape of rice paddies and pinnacles remains superb. Allow 200-300 yuan for two hours of descent. Prefer Yulong River (calmer) to Li River (already traveled in the morning by boat). Departure 3 p.m. after siesta, arrival 5 p.m.

⏱ 120 min

Yangshuo

★★★★☆
1

Moon Hill, hill pierced by a half-moon natural arch, 8 km south of Yangshuo, is a world-renowned climbing site - hundreds of sport routes on the walls. On-foot ascent in 30 min, paved trail. Panoramic view over Yulong valley from the arch. Entry 15 yuan. Go early, arch in full sun after 11 a.m.

⏱ 90 min
2

Yulong rice paddies file past by bike: rent an electric bike in Yangshuo (50-80 yuan per day) and ride along the river toward Jiuxian, Shuting, Aishanmen. Flat trail, pinnacles everywhere, inhabited traditional villages. Allow 4-5h round trip. Helmet advised, signs in Chinese only. GPS via Amap.

⏱ 240 min
3

Impression Liu Sanjie show is staged by Zhang Yimou, director of the 2008 Olympics - 600 actors on water, illuminated pinnacles in background, immense scenography. Very kitsch, very impressive. 240-680 yuan depending on seat. Performance at 8 p.m., duration 70 min. Mandatory reservation in high season via Klook or hotel.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Rent an electric scooter and lose yourself in the countryside beyond Moon Hill. 80-120 yuan per day, helmet included, Chinese license not required for capped models. Stop at a family's - *Farmer's restaurant* signs on secondary roads. Lunch 30-50 yuan, local chicken, garden vegetables, unlimited rice.

⏱ 240 min

Look for *Farmer's restaurant* signs on secondary roads between Aishanmen and Jiuxian. Best tables don't look like much: dirt courtyard, chickens passing, garden vegetables. Point at photo menu, or show 微辣 (mildly spicy) written on phone. Allow 40-80 yuan per person, generous meal.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 45min

Xingping

★★★★☆
1

Xingping Fisherman Cliff overlooks the exact landscape of the 20-yuan bill, 15 min walk from village center. The currency photo is taken from this precise viewpoint. Much less touristy than Yangshuo, old houses on Li bank, paved alleys. Allow 30 min on cliff, plus 1h walk in old Xingping. Entry 20 yuan.

⏱ 90 min
2

A few elders still work with cormorants in Xingping, at dawn, on the river. The trade is disappearing, transmitted less and less, but those who still practice accept spectators (early morning, around 6 a.m.). Photos possible for tip. Don't pay more than 20-30 yuan, local rate. Magnificent in reality, outside tourist décor.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Book the return driver: local buses Xingping-Guilin become scarce after 7 p.m., and you'll want to stay at Fisherman Cliff until sunset. 250-300 yuan for driver waiting plus return. Arrival Guilin around 9 p.m., late dinner at Banyan Lake. Confirmation by SMS the day before via hotel, payment in cash or WeChat Pay.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 80 km · 2h30

Pingan

★★★☆☆
1

Longji terraces are carved on mountainside slopes of the Yueliang Mountains since the Yuan (13th century) through the Qing by Zhuang and Red Yao peoples. Three zones: Ping'an (Zhuang, most accessible), Jinkeng/Dazhai (Yao, wildest), and old Longji. The spiral curve of rice terraces impresses whatever the season. Allow 30 min pause at first viewpoint.

⏱ 60 min
2

Seven Stars and Moon, first Ping'an viewpoint, opens onto the most photographed motif of the terraces: seven small water planes and a moon-shaped rounded terrace. 30 min walk from village. Morning, light from east, view without backlight. Allow 1h on site with photos. Bench, explanatory panel, light crowd on weekdays.

⏱ 60 min
3

Ping'an remains the most accessible of Longji's three villages: practicable road, restaurants, wooden Zhuang guesthouses. Village clings to ridge, narrow paved alleys, stairs between houses. Rice paddy colors vary radically by season (flooded May-June, green July-August, golden October, snow winter). See When to Go to China blog post.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Loop the three viewpoints in 4h30: Seven Stars and Moon (no.1), Nine Dragons and Five Tigers (no.2), Sunrise/Sunset (no.3). Paved trails, light elevation gain, water and hat. Driver-guide waits at main parking. If your legs tire, do first two only, the third is reached by internal shuttle (10 yuan).

⏱ 270 min

Bamboo chicken cooked in its trunk over wood fire, sticky black rice with pandan leaves, five colors of rice dyed with local plants: Zhuang cuisine accompanies any evening in a guesthouse. Communal meal at 6:30 p.m., 50-80 yuan per person. Local rice wine optional, ask before meal.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 30min

Dazhai

★★★☆☆
1

Red Yao never cut their hair: some women have 2 meters of it, rolled into buns under red headdress. Dazhai, Yao village at heart of Jinkeng zone, is more rural than Ping'an, fewer shops, more walking. Optional cable car to platform no.3, otherwise hike Dazhai-no.2-no.1 about 4h. Entry included in Longji ticket.

⏱ 60 min
2

Tiantouzhai stretches on the ridge in stone stairways, Yao hamlet perched above the terraces. Panoramic platforms over spiral rice paddies. Quieter, less touristy than lower Dazhai. Allow 1h going from Dazhai, steep but well-marked trail. Clear view on clear days, frequent mist after 3 p.m. in wet season.

⏱ 120 min
3

Golden Buddha Summit offers Jinkeng zone's highest platform, platform no.3. Accessible by cable car (135 yuan round trip) or 2h hike. 360° view over terraces in all directions, open summit when sky permits. Covered pavilion if mist rises. Allow 1h on site.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If your legs hold, cross via the ridge between Ping'an and Dazhai: 5-6h trek, one of southern China's great trails. Paved trail, sustained elevation gain, continuous view over rice paddies on both sides. Water, snacks, 8 a.m. start. Driver-guide picks up at Dazhai late afternoon, luggage transferred by car during walk.

⏱ 360 min

Attend Yao hair washing ceremony at Huangluo village, Guinness-listed as world's longest hair village. Folklorized for tourists but authentic costumes and techniques. Allow 1h. Entry 80-100 yuan. Afternoon show only, verify times by season. Photos allowed for tip.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 880 km · 8h30

Detian

★★★★☆
1

Detian stretches 200 m wide and 70 m high, transborder waterfall shared with Vietnam (Vietnamese side: Ban Gioc). Brown waters in rainy season, clear in winter. Very little known to Westerners, real surprise. Approached from viewpoint then lower basin, two complementary angles. Allow 2h on site. Entry 80 yuan.

⏱ 120 min
2

Imposing transborder waterfall, Detian lines up 200 m width over 70 m height. Flow varies with rains, spectacular in July-August, more modest in winter, but karst peak framing holds all year. View from Chinese bank wider than Vietnamese side.

⏱ 60 min
3

Marker 53 marks China-Vietnam border at waterfall's foot. You can approach and place one foot on each side - but don't cross the marker, sensitive border. Discreet but real surveillance. No games, no humorous attempts. Vietnamese merchant next door will sell coffee to passersby of both nationalities, that's all.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take a bamboo raft right under the waterfall: embarkment at main viewpoint foot, 60 yuan per person, 20 min ride. Rafts pass just under border, plunging view over both countries. Stay seated, precarious balance. Life jacket provided. Avoid rainy days, browner water and unstable rafts.

⏱ 60 min

If you arrive early, descend to lower basin first in early afternoon, frontal light on waterfall. Upper viewpoint late afternoon, soft backlight, rainbow possible. Lodging Detian Resort or Daxin town, equivalent distance. Zhuang restaurants at neighboring village, local cuisine 50-80 yuan per person.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 180 km · 3h

Nanning

★★☆☆☆
1

Nanning Museum presents Zhuang culture, China's first ethnic minority (~19 million people). Embroidered costumes, stringed instruments, mythology. Modern architecture opened in 2017, free with WeChat reservation, closed Mondays. Allow 1h30. Useful to close the trip: Zhuang seen at Longji, you find them in museum context here.

⏱ 90 min
2

Zhongshan Lu mixes Cantonese and Zhuang cuisine in a dense nighttime pedestrian street of street stalls: rice noodles, skewers, seafood. Allow 1h to cross, plus 1h to taste. Point at what you like, allow 30-60 yuan per person for complete meal. Open until 11 p.m., noisy atmosphere, neons everywhere.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take advantage of lunch on Zhongshan Lu before catching high-speed rail back to Guilin North (2h45). If timing tight, skip museum and stick to street stalls. Station luggage storage possible (10 yuan, 6h max). Return to Guilin in mid-afternoon to recover night in city, near airport for next day's departure.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 510 km · 2h45

Guilin

★★★☆☆
1

Zhengyang market concentrates Guilin and Guangxi handicrafts, paved pedestrian street in historic heart: Guilin Sanhua tea, Zhuang embroideries, silk paintings, modest jade sculptures. Allow 1h for final purchases. Negotiate 30-40% under posted price, WeChat Pay accepted everywhere. Merchants comfortable with basic commercial English.

⏱ 60 min
2

A foot massage, 30 to 80 yuan in main street salons, becomes last-day ritual after two weeks of walking. One hour of local reflexology, green tea served, calm atmosphere. Don't ask too loudly if Hunan spice murdered your legs, it's not reimbursed. Liangzi salon near Banyan Lake, reliable.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If weather cut a previous stage (Fanjingshan closed, Longji under mist, Lushan frosted), use this day as catch-up or recovery day. Otherwise, free morning, shopping, massage, then taxi to Liangjiang airport 30 min. Europe flight often evening, arrival next morning with 6-7h reverse time difference, plan recovery day.
