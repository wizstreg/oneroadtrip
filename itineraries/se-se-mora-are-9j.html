Mora

★★★☆☆
1

Lake Siljan spreads before you like an inland sea – 350 km² formed 377 million years ago by a meteorite impact, making it one of Europe's largest impact craters. Its shores are lined with wooden houses painted in dark red (the famous Falu rödfärg, a paint made from copper pigments extracted from the Falun mine) – the most quintessentially Swedish tableau imaginable.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Zornmuseet holds the world's largest collection of works by Anders Zorn (1860-1920), a Mora-born painter who created portraits of three American presidents – Grover Cleveland, William Taft and Theodore Roosevelt. Don't miss Zorngården, his house preserved exactly as it was when his wife Emma died in 1942: guided tours in English run at 3pm Tuesday through Sunday. Allow 1.5 hours for both.

⏱ 90 min
3

In Nusnäs, a few kilometres from Mora, you'll find the workshops where the famous Dalecarlian horses (Dalahäst) are hand-carved and painted – Sweden's unofficial symbol for generations. The craftspeople work before your eyes; it's far more engaging than any museum.

⏱ 60 min
4

On Mora's main square stands the Vasaloppet finish gate – the line that 15,000 cross-country skiers reach every first Sunday in March after 90 km from Sälen. A statue commemorates Gustav Vasa, whose ski escape in 1520 inspired the race. In 1521, the people of Mora had initially refused to join him, before changing their minds and catching up with him in Sälen to launch the uprising against the Danes.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Cycle around Lake Siljan (Siljansleden, roughly 310 km for the full circuit – take a section) or rent a kayak to paddle its calm waters. In summer, several beaches offer swimming: Siljansbadet in Rättvik, 30 km away, is one of the finest. Local outfitters provide rentals.

⏱ 180 min

If you're here in mid-June, don't miss Midsommar: Dalarna is the beating heart of Sweden's Midsummer celebration. In Leksand, on the lakeshore, over 30,000 people gather around the maypole each year – one of the country's largest celebrations.

⏱ 240 min

In the evening, stroll through central Mora – barely 10,000 inhabitants, yet packed with cafés, restaurants and craft shops. It's also the starting point of the Inlandsbanan, the legendary train that runs 1,300 km through Sweden all the way to Lapland.

⏱ 90 min

Salen

★★☆☆☆
1

At Berga By, a small, unassuming sign marks the official starting line of the Vasaloppet – the 90 km that have connected Sälen to Mora since 1922. The inaugural race on 22 March 1922 brought together 119 skiers starting from here; the course record now stands at 3h28. Setting foot on this starting line gives concrete scale to what the skiers are about to traverse.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Högfjällshotellet, built in 1937, is the oldest mountain hotel in the region – and one of Sweden's most beloved. Even if you're not staying here, the architecture and the view from its terrace over the snow-covered (or summer-green) slopes merit a stop.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

In summer, the Vasaloppsleden transforms into a hiking trail over the historic 90 km route between Sälen and Mora, marked by eight refuges. You don't have to do it all: choose a 10-20 km section from Sälen toward Smågan, the race's first checkpoint. The trail crosses pine forests, gentle hills and frozen lakes – the same landscape that 15,000 skiers traverse every March.

⏱ 240 min

Sälen is also Sweden's largest alpine ski domain, with several resorts clustered around the massif. In summer, the pistes convert to mountain bike trails and outfitters offer bike rentals and guided descents. The region is far less crowded than in winter – take advantage.

⏱ 180 min

Idre

★★☆☆☆
1

Idre is a small village set close to the Norwegian border in northwestern Dalarna. The surrounding region belongs to the fjäll – those bare high plateaus above the treeline characteristic of Scandinavia. Take time to drive slowly as you arrive: the landscape shifts gradually from dense forest to bare, windswept ridges.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Städjan-Nipfjället nature reserve deserves the detour. Nipfjället rises to 1192 m and the 16 km² reserve was created in 1974 (expanded in 2010) to protect unique Arctic flora and fauna: reindeer, foxes, numerous mountain raptors. Neighbouring Städjan (1131 m) is the southernmost summit of the Swedish mountain massif – its characteristic volcano-like profile stands out from afar.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The loop hike up Städjan (6 km, ~380 m elevation gain) from the Nipstugan car park is the region's flagship excursion. The 360° panorama from the summit embraces Norwegian fells to the west and Dalarna's forests to the east. Count on 2.5-3 hours. The trail is well marked but the terrain is rocky on the upper section – proper footwear essential.

⏱ 180 min

Shorter alternative: the ascent to Lillnipen (1100 m), starting from the upper Nipfjället car park, 1.5 km round trip. Very easy, perfect if you just want to feel the fjäll wind without exertion. The view over the Härjedalen fells to the east and Fulufjället to the west is well worth the minimal effort.

⏱ 60 min

Idre Fjäll, the ski resort a few kilometres north of the village, offers 25 marked mountain bike trails in summer including the famous Städjan DH (rated black). Outfitters rent equipment on-site. It's one of Sweden's best-known MTB parks.

⏱ 180 min

Grovelsjon

★★☆☆☆
1

The STF Grövelsjön Fjällstation stands at 816 m altitude on the flank of Långfjället, at the northern tip of Dalarna, right against the Norwegian border. It's one of the STF (Swedish Tourist Association) mountain stations – lodging, restaurant and shop included. Settle in: you're at the gateway to exceptional and uncrowded hiking territory.

⏱ 30 min
2

Lake Grövelsjön itself stretches like a banana for 15 km, straddling the Swedish-Norwegian border – its northern end lies in Norway. From Sjöstugan, 1.5 km from the fjällstation, a boat crosses to Sylen on the Norwegian shore (crossing about 40 min). You'll pass into Norway without noticing – the border runs right through the middle of the lake.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Linnaeus Trail (Linnéstigen) starts from Sjöstugan and climbs toward Norway following the exact route taken by botanist Carl von Linné in 1734 during his journey through Dalarna. On Långfjället, a sign marks the spot where Linnaeus observed falconers capturing wild falcons for hunting training – a remnant of a falconers' camp whose ruins still survive.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The hike to the summit of Storvätteshågna (1204 m) is the day's major excursion – the highest point in Svealand, with a dizzying panorama over Norwegian fells, Töfsingdalen and the Helags massif to the north. Count on 24 km round trip from the fjällstation, roughly 7-8 hours. If you leave at 8am, you have plenty of time. The final ascent from Fosksjöarna is the steepest section.

⏱ 480 min

For a more relaxed day, take the morning boat from Sjöstugan to Sylen (Norway), then walk back via the Linnaeus Trail (9 km return along the lake, easy terrain). You "enter" Norway without a passport, no customs, nothing – just a name change on the map. Silverfallet, a pretty waterfall 7 km from the fjällstation, is an even shorter alternative.

⏱ 300 min

Sveg

★★★☆☆
1

You'll arrive in Sveg through seemingly endless forests – Härjedalen is Sweden's least populated province, and you feel it. At the main crossroads, you'll run straight into it: the giant wooden bear standing 13 m tall, weighing 80 tonnes, built between 2003 and 2005 on 570,000 nails. It has become the symbol of the town and the entire province. The brown bear is Härjedalen's emblem animal – and the province holds one of Sweden's highest brown bear densities.

⏱ 30 min
2

Sveg's Gammelgården is an open-air museum run by the Härjedalen handicraft association, founded in 1913. It gathers historic buildings from the region and traces local culture. At Överhogdal, 50 km north of Sveg, lie Europe's oldest Viking tapestries – the Överhogdal tapestries, parts of which date back to the 9th century – visible at Jamtli in Östersund.

⏱ 75 min
3

Sveg's Inlandsbanan station is a stop on the legendary line that connects Kristinehamn to Gällivare over 1,300 km through central Sweden. The Inlandsbanan only runs in summer (June-August), one train daily in each direction. If you're passing in July, go watch the train depart.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

80 km north of Sveg, Sonfjället National Park merits an excursion. Its volcano-like silhouette is visible from afar. It holds one of Sweden's densest brown bear populations – guides offer bear safaris at sunrise and sunset. Tracks are frequent even without spotting the animal. The summit Lilltoppen (approx. 1000 m) is accessible without major difficulties from the north entrance.

⏱ 240 min

Alternatively, Björnberget, the local mountain 8 km from central Sveg, offers a pleasant half-day walk with views over Härjedalen's forests and hills. In the evening, Sveg is the sleepy provincial capital – 2,500 inhabitants – but the atmosphere is authentic, with no tourist artifice.

⏱ 120 min

Funasdalen

★★★☆☆
1

Funäsdalen is the life centre of mountainous Härjedalen: a mountain village at the foot of Funäsdalsberget, surrounded by over 30 peaks exceeding 1000 m in the area. The Härjedalens Fjällmuseum, inaugurated in 1999, is one of Sweden's most modern museums. It presents, in beautiful exhibitions, the region's history through mountain farmers, Sami people and craftspeople – collections among the country's oldest. It received a special mention at the 2001 European Museum of the Year Awards.

⏱ 90 min
2

Push on to Tännäs, about twenty kilometres east, to see the musk oxen at Myskoxcentrum. In 1971, five musk oxen spontaneously crossed the Norwegian border and settled in the mountains west of Tännäs – forming the basis of the current herd. The centre, created in 2010, offers guided visits at a respectful distance. These animals, covered in their thick woolly coats, look like something out of prehistory – because that's exactly what they are. Book ahead.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Funäsdalsberget (the relief just above the village) offers a half-day hike accessible to all. From the summit, the view carries to Sylarna in the north and Sonfjället in the south – a panorama embracing 30 peaks. In summer, the ski runs transform into marked trails.

⏱ 180 min

Nordic Ski Center (NSC) at Funäsfjällen claims one of the world's longest groomed cross-country ski networks, with 300 km of trails. In summer, these same trails become mountain bike and hiking paths. Outfitters offer mountain bike rentals on-site.

⏱ 180 min

Ljungdalen

★★☆☆☆
1

The Flatruet road stops you dead the moment you emerge from the forest: you're at 975 m altitude, on Sweden's highest road, inaugurated in 1938. Before you, a bare, windswept plateau without trees – and reindeer on the tarmac, indifferent to your car. Stop at the ridgeline car park: in clear weather, you'll spot Helagsfjället and its glacier to the north. The road is partly gravel, closed between October and May.

⏱ 45 min
2

Ljungdalen is a tiny village at the foot of Helagsfjället. It's the starting point for reaching STF Helags Fjällstation, 12 km hiking from the Kläppen car park (by car from Ljungdalen, 6 km). The mountain station is the base for the glacier ascent.

⏱ 30 min
3

Helagsfjället (1797 m) is Sweden's highest peak south of the Arctic Circle – and on its flanks lies Helags glacier, Sweden's southernmost glacier. The summit is visible from afar: it dominates the surrounding topography spectacularly. National Geographic ranked it among the world's three best "toppturer" (summit climbs).

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The ascent of Helagsfjället from the fjällstation is 8 km round trip (or 24 km from Kläppen car park). From the 1797 m summit, the view carries over Sylarna to the north, Norway to the west and the Härjedalen fells in all directions. On the climb, you skirt the southern edge of the glacier – a geological curiosity in the heart of Scandinavia. Count on 4-5 hours from the fjällstation. Bring warm clothing even in summer: weather changes very quickly here.

⏱ 300 min

If you don't want to climb to the summit, the hike from Kläppen to the fjällstation (12 km one-way, Kesudalen valley and birch forest) is already beautiful. You can sleep at the fjällstation and return the next day. Book ahead: limited spaces.

⏱ 240 min

Ostersund

★★★☆☆
1

Östersund is the only real city on the entire circuit – 60,000 inhabitants, capital of Jämtland, founded in 1786 by King Gustav III on the shores of Storsjön. It's a jolt of modernity after the fells: cafés, restaurants, street life. Take an hour to stroll along the lake before visiting.

⏱ 60 min
2

Jamtli is Jämtland's regional museum, 1 km north of the centre. It combines an indoor museum with an exceptional collection – including the Överhogdal tapestries, discovered 50 km north of Sveg, parts of which date back to the 9th century and constitute Europe's best-preserved Viking tapestries – and an open-air museum with historic 18th-19th century buildings where costumed actors have recreated peasant life every summer since 1986. The Storsjöodjuret exhibition notably presents the giant trap built in 1894 to capture the beast, baited with a live pig.

⏱ 150 min
3

The island of Frösön, linked to Östersund by a bridge, is Jämtland's historic heart, predating the city by centuries. It holds Sweden's northernmost runestone, erected between 1050 and 1080 – and the monster carved on the stone is supposedly the Storsjöodjuret. Legend says a wizard named Ketil Runske imprisoned the beast in the stone by spell. Frösön church (late 12th century) has a separate bell tower typical of Jämtland – the view over Östersund and the lake from the island is the region's finest.

⏱ 75 min
4

Along the shores of Storsjön, Storsjöodjuret statues punctuate a pleasant walk. The creature was first mentioned in writing in 1635. In 1986, for Östersund's bicentenary, it was even placed under legal protection – a law repealed in 2005. There are now 200 documented testimonies from people claiming to have seen it.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

In summer, Storsjön lends itself to kayaking and stand-up paddling – outfitters offer rentals and outings from Badhusparken. In calm weather, paddling on the lake while watching Östersund's silhouette reflected in the water has something quite surreal about it.

⏱ 120 min

Östersund has been a UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy since 2010 – remarkable for a city this size. In the evening, try the downtown restaurants showcasing Jämtland products: game, mountain cheeses, wild herbs. It's the circuit's only real "city night".

⏱ 120 min

Åre is Sweden's premier mountain resort – and one of Scandinavia's most important. In winter, it's the country's most popular alpine domain, host of the Alpine Ski World Championships (1954, 2007, 2019). In summer, the crowds vanish and the village returns to human scale: a few lively streets, restaurants, a central square where hikers and mountain bikers mingle.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Kabinbanan cable car climbs in 7 minutes from the central square (421 m) to the upper station at 1274 m – 853 m elevation gain in cabins accommodating 75 people. From the station, an additional 30-minute walk leads to Åreskutan summit (1420 m) and Toppstugan – Sweden's highest café, operating since 1892, where they serve waffles without running water or electricity (everything is carried up by hand). The panorama from up there over Jämtland's ranges is the perfect endpoint for this circuit.

⏱ 150 min
3

The village of Fröå Gruva, east of Åreskutan, marks the site of an old mine surrounded by hiking and cross-country ski trails. A bit off the main tourist flow, it's a quiet way to close out the day before catching the train.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Åre is renowned for downhill mountain biking – the lifts operate in summer to bring riders up. Outfitters offer rentals and downhill courses. If you're not a mountain biker, the marked hiking trails around the massif allow 5-15 km loops with beautiful views over Lake Åresjön below.

⏱ 240 min

Åre is connected by train to Östersund (1h) and Stockholm (6h30), as well as to Storlien and Norway to the west (30 min to the border). If you continue to Norway, the train journey through the Jämtland valley is one of the country's most beautiful – plan to sit window-side.
