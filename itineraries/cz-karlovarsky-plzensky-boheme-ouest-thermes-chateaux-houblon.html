Waldsassen

★★★☆☆
1

Ten life-size wooden atlantes support the gallery of the Stiftsbibliothek in Waldsassen. Carved by Karl Stilp between 1724 and 1726, they embody human failings: the ignorant, the hypocrite, the mocker. The Rococo hall ranks among the finest monastic libraries in Central Europe. Admission €3.50, guided tours every 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
2

The adjoining Stiftsbasilika dates from the 12th century and blends late Romanesque with Baroque. The ceiling frescoes by Karl Hofreiter trace the life of St. Bernard of Clairvaux. Free access outside service hours. Take a look at the carved choir stalls.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Wander through the monastery's botanical garden (Naturerlebnisgarten), open from May to mid-October. The Cistercian nuns cultivate medicinal plants and aromatic herbs across one hectare. Combined ticket with the library €6.

⏱ 40 min
$$$9.8Studio-Apartments Boulevard
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min

Cheb

★★★☆☆
1

Underrated, Cheb deserves more than a quick stop. Cheb Castle, the only Hohenstaufen imperial palatinate in Czechia, preserves a two-story Romanesque chapel and the Black Tower. This is where Albrecht von Wallenstein was assassinated in 1634. Full price 120 Kč.

⏱ 60 min
2

Touristy, the Špalíček? Yes, but these eleven 13th-century merchants' houses form a compact block without parallel in Bohemia. Slip through the Kramářská alley, barely 1.6 meters wide, that separates the two blocks. Free, five minutes is enough.

⏱ 20 min
3

The echo resonates under the Romanesque vault of St. Nicholas Church, whose twin towers dominate the main square. The Gothic interior has been remodeled in Baroque, but the original structure remains legible. Free entry.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Follow the Ohře River toward Krajinka Park for a quiet stroll away from the tour groups. The park offers sports activities in summer and views of the old bridge. Allow an hour round-trip.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.5Wellness Hotel Ida
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min

Frantiskovy Lazne

★★☆☆☆
1

Founded in 1793 by Emperor Francis I, Františkovy Lázně is the smallest of Bohemia's spa triangle. Its classicist center has been a UNESCO World Heritage site since July 2021 as part of the Great Spas of Europe. Twenty-one mineral springs gush across the town's territory.

⏱ 45 min
2

If weather permits, the Salt and Meadow Spring Colonnade is the perfect spot to sample the waters. Each spring has a distinct taste, from salt to iron. The surrounding parks are beautifully maintained, with benches and flowerbeds. Bring your porcelain cup.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Taste the water from František's Spring, the town's oldest, in its neoclassical pavilion. Touch the statue of little Františka, the town's mascot linked to fertility. The superstitious rub its toes.

⏱ 30 min
$$$8.9Rezidence Zámeček - Boutique Hotel
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 35min

Lazne Kynzvart

★★★☆☆
1

Metternich's cabinet of curiosities alone makes Kynžvart Castle worth the detour. The Austrian chancellor assembled personal objects from Marie Antoinette, a 3,500-year-old Egyptian mummy, and a cast of Alexandre Dumas's hand. The castle, built in 1690 and remodeled in Viennese classicism between 1820 and 1833, received the Europa Nostra prize in 2002.

⏱ 60 min
2

Stroll through the castle's English park, designed by the imperial gardener from Schönbrunn. Three-hundred-year-old oaks stand beside a pond with an islet, an obelisk, and Májová Hill. The park has free access and takes about 1 km to tour.

⏱ 40 min
$$$9.2Falkensteiner Spa Resort Marianske Lazne
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min

Kladska

★★★★☆
1

The spongy ground creaks beneath the wooden boardwalk of the Kladská nature trail, one of the oldest of its kind in Czechia (pioneering since 1977). The Kladské Peat Bogs National Reserve covers nearly 300 hectares of peatland above 800 meters altitude. Sundew and butterwort thrive here thanks to the ultra-acidic soil.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you arrive early, the little Kladská château and its pond offer a postcard-perfect setting. The Alpine-style buildings date from the second half of the 19th century, built by Prince Schönburg-Waldenburg. Trail of 2.4 km, twelve information panels.

⏱ 50 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 22min

Loket

★★★☆☆
1

Czechs call it the 'key to the Kingdom of Bohemia'. Loket Castle stands on a granite spur encircled by a meander of the Ohře River since the 13th century. The exhibition of torture instruments in the cellars is striking. The view from the gallery embraces the entire town.

⏱ 60 min
2

Goethe came here seven times and celebrated his 74th birthday here in 1823. His bronze statue stands on the square, a romantic scene he shared with young Ulrike von Lewetzov. The porcelain museum in the castle displays local productions. Combined castle + museum ticket available.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk down to the Ohře River to watch kayakers descend the rapids at the foot of the castle. In summer, a small rental center offers rowboats. The riverside path leads to Svatošské Rocks in 6 km.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.1Pension Masonic House
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 8min

Svatosske Skaly

★★★★☆
1

Granite pillars rise above the Ohře like a petrified procession. The Svatošské Rocks, a national natural monument since 1933, owe their name to the legend of Jan Svatoš: a groom turned to stone by the devil. The columns reach about fifty meters. Goethe sketched them in his notebooks.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Follow the trail from Loket along the Ohře (6 km, easy) or come by car via Doubí. A rustic restaurant with a terrace on the river offers a rest stop. The rocks are also used by climbers.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.6Na Nebi 187
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Horni Slavkov

★★☆☆☆
1

A mining camp since the 13th century, Horní Slavkov exported tin throughout Europe. The Museum, housed in a Renaissance building, presents the tin, silver, and uranium industry, plus a period bourgeois black kitchen. This is also where Bohemia's first porcelain manufactory was born in 1792.

⏱ 45 min
2

The fortified church of St. George deserves a detour for its massive silhouette, rare in the region. It ranks among the most powerful fortified churches in Bohemia. Exterior access is free; for the interior, inquire at the town hall.

⏱ 20 min
$$$9.4Hotel Villa Golf Cihelny
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 22min

Becov Nad Teplou

★★★☆☆
1

A ruined castle above, a Baroque château below, and between them, Czechia's second national treasure. Bečov nad Teplou Castle and Château hides the St. Maurus Reliquary in Pluhovský Palace, a 13th-century Romanesque goldsmith masterpiece. Found under a floor in 1985 by investigators, it rivals Prague's crown jewels.

⏱ 60 min
2

The gold, silver, and precious stones of the reliquary are visible in a climate-controlled treasury room. The casket houses relics of St. John the Baptist, St. Maurus, St. Timothy, and St. Apollinaris. It even contains a 'toad stone,' a fossil fish tooth from the Cretaceous. Circuit I without reservation for individuals.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Book Circuit II to discover the château's Baroque salons, the Beaufort library, and St. Peter's chapel. The view from the terraced gardens plunges into the Teplá valley. Allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.3Hotel Rezidence Golf
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 15min

Tepla

★★★☆☆
1

Founded in 1193 by the Blessed Hroznata, Teplá Monastery is one of the oldest Premonstratensian monasteries in Bohemia. The Romanesque Basilica of the Annunciation, consecrated in 1232 in the presence of King Wenceslas I, has spanned eight centuries. The monastery served as a barracks under the communist regime before being returned to the Premonstratensians in 1990.

⏱ 45 min
2

The monastery library impresses as much as Strahov in Prague, in a more intimate setting. The collection of portraits of all abbots since the 12th century is complete. The park along the Teplá River, with its Lourdes grotto and modern Stations of the Cross (2016), takes about half an hour to walk.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.5Apartmán Nežichov 2 U Polocka
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 18min

Marianske Lazne

★★★★☆
1

If you arrive on an odd hour, the Singing Fountain in Mariánské Lázně greets you with a ballet of water and music. The 18-meter diameter basin, inaugurated in 1986, plays Mozart, Dvořák, and Smetana. Light show at 9pm and 10pm from April to October.

⏱ 30 min
2

The ferruginous water of the Cross Spring leaves a persistent metallic taste. More than a hundred mineral springs gush around Mariánské Lázně, a UNESCO town since 2021. The main Colonnade, 180 meters long, is a unique cast-iron structure. Free entry, porcelain cups sold on-site.

⏱ 40 min
3

Climb to the Hamelika lookout tower for a panorama of the town and the Slavkov Forest. The 20-meter tower has free access, 120 steps. Open year-round.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Kitsch, the fountain? Maybe, but the evening show is worth it. Settle on a bench with a waffle from the nearby kiosk and watch the water dance under LEDs. The first composition was signed by Petr Hapka, a figure in Czech song.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.2Falkensteiner Spa Resort Marianske Lazne
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 40min

Tachov

★★★☆☆
1

The Bohemian Forest Museum in Tachov occupies a former Franciscan convent. The exhibitions cover the nature of the Bohemian Forest, frontier life, and villages that disappeared after 1945. The neighboring château and remains of the town walls can be visited in passing. Quiet stopover town, no crowds.

⏱ 40 min
2

Don't miss the convent refectory, where a life-size trompe-l'oeil supper and biblical frescoes cover the walls and ceiling. The building dates from 1686-1689, designed by architect Martin Allio. Access included in the museum ticket.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Climb the 150 steps of the Vysoká lookout tower on the hill above town. The panorama embraces the entire Bohemian Forest to the Bavarian border. Free access, allow 20 minutes round-trip from the center.

⏱ 30 min
$$$10Apartmán v Plané
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 15min

Primda

★★☆☆☆
1

Mentioned in 1121 in Cosmas's chronicle, Přimda Castle is the oldest known stone castle in Bohemia. Built illegally by Germans, it was immediately seized by Prince Vladislav I. The granite ashlar residential tower preserves a rare Romanesque garderobe. From the summit (848 m), clear view to Germany.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Allow thirty minutes' walk through the forest from the village parking lot. An educational trail of 7 km with 12 stations starts from Přimda church. Guided tours are offered by reservation via hrad-primda.cz.

⏱ 40 min
$$$8.8Penzion - Horský Hotel
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 20min

Bor U Tachova

★☆☆☆☆
1

If Bor Castle is open, climb the 117 steps of the medieval cylindrical tower. The gallery at 30 meters offers a view of the Bohemian Forest and Přimda Castle in the distance. The Švamberk Hall, with its honeycomb vaults, and St. Lawrence chapel preserve a pair of handprints in the Renaissance plaster, according to legend those of two separated lovers.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Cross the street to visit the Loreta of Bor, a Baroque chapel with a Black Madonna in its Santa Casa. The restored ambulatories display 18th-century ceiling paintings. Combined castle + Loreta tour: 70-80 minutes.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.5Ubytování Bor
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 20min

Stribro

★★☆☆☆
1

The old stone bridge spans the Mže at Stříbro, a former mining town whose name means 'silver'. The historic center preserves some Renaissance houses and a Dominican convent. Quiet town, ideal for a lunch stop.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The Amati factory, which produces musical instruments in Stříbro, is known to brass players worldwide. If the shop is open, have a look. The Saturday morning market on the main square offers local fruits and vegetables.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9Spa Hotel Garni Löwenstein
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 15min

Kladruby

★★☆☆☆
1

From the outside, Kladruby church seems Gothic. Push the door: it's Baroque. Jan Blažej Santini rebuilt this Romanesque basilica founded in 1115 between 1712 and 1726, inventing a unique hybrid style, 'Baroque Gothic'. The dome rises to 46 meters, crowned with a gilded Marian crown. Third largest temple in Bohemia after Prague Cathedral and Sedlec.

⏱ 60 min
2

Cross through Dientzenhofer's New Convent, the other genius of Czech Baroque architecture. The contrast between Santini's original triangular plan (never realized) and the building ultimately constructed by Dientzenhofer is explained in the permanent exhibition on St. John of Nepomuk, a martyr directly linked to the monastery (1393).

⏱ 40 min
$$$9.5Apartmány pod Klášterem
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 25min

Konstantinovy Lazne

★★☆☆☆
1

The smallest spa in West Bohemia has kept an old-world charm. Konstantinovy Lázně specializes in cardiac rehabilitation and the musculoskeletal system. The spa park is modest but soothing, with a sulfurous spring known since the 16th century.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Push on to the ruins of Gutštejn Castle, a few kilometers away. This romantic ruin, perched above the Hadovka valley, has free access. Beautiful forest walk of 30 minutes from the parking lot.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.5Apartmán Nežichov 2 U Polocka
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 45min

Plzen

★★★☆☆
1

The scent of hops floats over the entire Plzeňský Prazdroj (Pilsner Urquell) brewery quarter. This is where Bavarian brewer Josef Groll produced the world's first pale lager beer on October 5, 1842. The guided tour traverses the historic cellars beneath the city, where beer fermented in beech barrels. Book online.

⏱ 90 min
2

St. Bartholomew's Cathedral tower rises to 102 meters, a Bohemian record. It dominates Republic Square, the country's largest medieval square after Prague. Climb for a 360° panorama. Tower admission charged.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Great Synagogue surprises by its dimensions: third largest in the world after Budapest and New York, built in 1893. The façade blends Moorish and Romanesque styles, with two 45-meter towers. The interior hosts concerts and photography exhibitions.

⏱ 30 min
4

Descend into Plzeň's historic underground, a network of cellars dug since the 13th century beneath the center's streets. The guided tour lasts 50 minutes, cool temperature year-round. The Brewing Museum is housed in a neighboring Gothic malthouse.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

The unfiltered beer drawn from the oak barrel at the end of the brewery tour is the only way to taste a Pilsner Urquell before filtration and pasteurization. Nothing like the bottled version. Allow 2 hours for the complete tour.

⏱ 30 min

If you're in town on a Saturday, the farmers' market on Republic Square is worth a visit. Fresh trdelník, Šumava cheeses, local honey. The Patton Memorial Museum, dedicated to Plzeň's liberation by the American army in 1945, takes 30 minutes to visit.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.4Wellness luxury apartments
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 35min

Svihov

★★☆☆☆
1

Water surrounds Švihov Castle on all sides. This Gothic fortress, built from 1480 by Půta Švihovský in the Úhlava meadows, was protected by two moats and two enclosures. The Habsburgs ordered its demolition after the Thirty Years' War, but the work dragged on and the castle survived. It was then converted into a granary, which paradoxically preserved it from modifications.

⏱ 60 min
2

Czechs know it mainly as the setting for the film Tři oříšky pro Popelku (Three Wishes for Cinderella, 1973), a Christmas classic in Central Europe. A 'Cinderella' themed trail connects filming locations around the castle. The Gothic chapel and the ballroom with its painted Renaissance ceiling are the highlights of the interior visit.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Rent a rowboat to circle the water-filled moat. The ride lasts about fifteen minutes and offers a unique perspective on the bastions. On-site rental in season.

⏱ 20 min
$$$9.7Apartmán Stará cihelna
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min

Klatovy

★★★☆☆
1

Beneath the Jesuit Church of the Immaculate Conception, the frigid air of Klatovy's catacombs has preserved mummies since the 17th century. The bodies of Jesuits and notables rest in glass-topped coffins, a spectacle between fascination and unease. Guided tour only, about 30 minutes.

⏱ 30 min
2

Climb the steps of the Black Tower, 81 meters high, to overlook Klatovy and glimpse the Šumava in clear weather. The Renaissance tower is accessible in season for a few dozen crowns.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

The Baroque pharmacy At the White Unicorn, on the main square, is one of the best-preserved in Czechia. The original faience jars and furniture are intact. Quick but striking visit.

⏱ 20 min
$$$10Apartmán Nová Dragounská
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Kdyne

★★☆☆☆
1

Perched above Kdyně, the ruins of Rýzmberk Castle belonged to the powerful Švihovský of Rýzmberk family. Only wall fragments remain in the forest, but the ascent (20 minutes) rewards with a clear view of the valley and the ridges of the Bohemian Forest. Free access.

⏱ 40 min
2

The Borderland Museum in Kdyně, awarded in 2007 (Gloria musaealis), tells the story of frontier villages that disappeared after 1945. Among the pieces: 19th-century blue-print dies and embroidery from the Tšída factory. Small but well done.

⏱ 25 min

Activities

Make a detour to Kdyně's Water Fun Center if you're traveling with children. Pool, slides, and sauna in a human-scale complex, without the crowds of large water parks. Open in season.

⏱ 60 min
$$$8.9ROCH800 - industrial aparthotel Klatovy - free parking - self-checking
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Domazlice

★★★☆☆
1

The Chods, historic guardians of the Bohemian frontier, left their mark on Domažlice. The 13th-century Chod Castle houses the region's oldest museum, with costumes, bagpipes (dudy), and ceramics. The elongated arcaded square is one of the most photogenic in West Bohemia.

⏱ 45 min
2

The leaning tower of Chod Castle offers a vantage point over Domažlice's rooftops and the Bohemian Forest beyond. The Sladovna, a former malthouse converted into a gallery and microbrewery, deserves a beer and a look at the Hvozd gallery.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you pass in August, the Chod Festival animates the town with traditional music, embroidered costumes, and bagpipe competitions. Outside the festival, taste the Chod koláče, small pastries filled with poppy seed or curd cheese, in the center's bakeries.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9Selská chalupa Anetka
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min

Klenci Pod Cerchovem

★★☆☆☆
1

Climb to the summit of Čerchov (1,042 m), the highest peak of the Bohemian Forest, accessible in 1.5 hours' walk from Klenčí pod Čerchovem. The former military surveillance tower at the summit offers a frontier panorama over Bavaria. Typical Chodsko village, with painted half-timbered houses.

⏱ 120 min
$$$8.7Hotel Zlatá Včela
