✨ Highlights
  • Jalapão State Park covers 34,000 km² of preserved cerrado, one of the last great expanses of this Brazilian savanna
  • Fervedouros are artesian springs where water pressure makes you float effortlessly, a rare phenomenon worldwide
  • The Jalapão dunes formed through millennial erosion of Serra do Espírito Santo, the wind depositing sand in the same place
  • Capim dourado, a fine golden plant stem, is endemic to Jalapão and has been woven since the early 20th century by artisans of Quilombo Mumbuca
📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September

⏳ How many days

7 days of itinerary, designed to savour rather than tick off.

☀️ Weather

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 Discover

Carnival energy, Amazon rainforest, and pristine beaches create the ultimate South American adventure. Experience vibrant cities, incredible biodiversity, and passionate culture from Rio's beaches to Pantanal wetlands. Samba music, capoeira martial arts, and incredible football culture showcase national spirit. Brazil offers unparalleled natural wonders with infectious cultural energy.

📋 Notes

Remote region. 4x4 vehicle mandatory, local agency strongly recommended. Dry season May-September only - during rainy season tracks become impassable. Bring plenty of cash, little phone signal. Long distances on sand tracks.

Ponte Alta Do Tocantins

★★☆☆☆
1

You arrive in Palmas, capital of Tocantins, and the road heads east. Three hours later, Ponte Alta do Tocantins marks the true gateway to Jalapão: this is where asphalt surrenders to red dirt tracks. The town itself is modest, but its position is strategic, 187 km from Palmas via the TO-030 then the TO-130. Upon arrival, head to Sussuapara Canyon, 15 km away: a narrow fissure between red walls where light barely filters through. Count on 25 minutes of downhill walking.

⏱ 90 min
2

Sussuapara Canyon descends to 25 meters deep. At the bottom, trickles of water flow from overhanging roots and a small 5-meter jet falls into a natural pool where only one person fits at a time. The sandstone releases a reddish pigment on contact with water - you'll see it beneath your feet. The name comes from the sussuapara, a small deer from the region. Tip: descend around midday, it's the only time when sunlight truly penetrates to the bottom.

⏱ 75 min
3

Built in 1989 when Tocantins State was carved out from Goiás, Ponte Alta lives up to its name: the town overlooks the Tocantins River. Take a walk through the center, grab a coffee at the rodoviária, watch the pickups equipped for the trail departing in convoy at dawn. It's also your last real opportunity to withdraw cash before heading toward Mateiros - ATMs become scarce inside the park.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Briefing with the agency for the coming days. Bag adjustments, 4x4 checks, buying water in quantity (count on 3 liters per person per day minimum), some snacks and dried fruits. Most accommodations inside the park operate without air conditioning and with limited electricity - pack your gear accordingly.

⏱ 60 min

Taste Tocantins cuisine at dinner on the central praça: galinha caipira (slow-cooked free-range chicken), rice pilaf with pequi (a cerrado fruit with a powerful local flavor) and beiju, the tender manioc pancake. Restaurants close early, around 9 PM. It's also time to go to bed early - tomorrow, departure before 8 AM to tackle the 95 km dirt track to Mateiros.

⏱ 90 min

Mateiros

★★★☆☆
1

Behind the wheel early in the morning, head east on the TO-255 toward Mateiros, the tourist capital of Jalapão. The track is sandy, sometimes fine as flour - hence the 4x4. Before arriving, make the detour to Cachoeira da Velha, the park's largest waterfall: 100 meters wide and about 15 meters high on the Rio Novo. A well-maintained wooden walkway descends to several viewpoints facing the double curtain of water. Swimming is prohibited, the flow being too violent.

⏱ 90 min
2

Just downstream from Cachoeira da Velha, walk 1.5 km along a forest gallery trail to Prainha do Rio Novo. It's the waterfall's reverse side: calm waters, white sand, small fish visible to the naked eye. Here swimming is not only permitted but practically mandatory after the long track from Ponte Alta. The cachoeira is located on Fazenda Triagro, and a tourist legend widely repeated in the region claims that Pablo Escobar used the location in the 1980s - no official source confirms it, but local guides willingly tell the story along the way.

⏱ 90 min
3

Mateiros, about 3,000 inhabitants, lives from tourism and cattle ranching. The red-dust grid streets converge on a modest central square. Drop your bags at the pousada in late afternoon, take a walk through town, locate the restaurant where you'll dine (most close around 9 PM). Mateiros will be your base for the next three days - the position is ideal for exploring the dunes, fervedouros and cachoeiras.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

For thrill-seekers, level-4 rafting starts right at the foot of Cachoeira da Velha and descends the rapids of the Rio Novo to Prainha. It's the angle the walkway doesn't show you. Count on about 2 hours including briefing and suiting up. Advance booking essential with a licensed agency.

⏱ 120 min

Sample regional cuisine at dinner: galinha caipira (free-range chicken) with rice pilaf, paçoca de carne seca (dried beef pounded with manioc flour), beiju (manioc pancake). Many pousadas serve everything under an awning, no menu - you eat what's ready.

⏱ 90 min

Mateiros

★★★☆☆
1

Rise before 4 AM. The Serra do Espírito Santo trail begins in darkness: 500 to 800 meters of steep climb on sandstone, sometimes with ropes and benches to catch your breath. The equivalent of 80 floors of a building according to local guides. At the summit, the plateau opens onto a flat cerrado horizon, and just after 6 AM the sun emerges behind the chapadões. It's this serra that, through millennial erosion, provided the sand for the dunes you see below. Headlamp, water, windbreaker essential.

⏱ 240 min
2

The Jalapão dunes extend over about 38 km² at the foot of the serra. The highest peaks around 30 meters, and the vivid orange color comes from iron oxides in the sand. Park at the base, climb on foot (10 to 20 minutes depending on your pace) and settle in for late afternoon. The park closes at 5:30 PM, mandatory exit before 6:30 PM. Note: no drinks on the dunes, no camping, photos only.

⏱ 120 min
3

On the TO-255 road toward the dunes, Morro do Saca Trapo rises about 30 km from the center of Mateiros, adjacent to Serra do Espírito Santo. It's an isolated sandstone cone that juts from the flat - a classic photo on the car roof with the morro behind, and many visitors stop there. The nickname comes from a sack of old clothes ("saca de trapo") that old local hunters allegedly left at the foot of the morro. No trail to climb here, you stop at the roadside, look, photograph, and leave. A lanchonete offers drinks before the final stretch to the dunes.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Sunset on the dunes is Jalapão's postcard image. The sand gradually shifts from vivid orange to dark red then to purple. Sit on the main crest, stay silent - the winds are strangely calm there, you hear the buritis rustling in the distance. No music, no drones, that's the rule.

⏱ 60 min

Between the trail and the dunes, lunch in Mateiros and take a siesta at the pousada - the morning will have knocked you out. Jalapão's rhythm alternates physical efforts and long drives: save your energy when you can.

⏱ 120 min

Mateiros

★★★☆☆
1

Head to Cachoeira do Formiga, 35 km south of Mateiros on the TO-110. The pool measures about 8 meters in diameter and 2 meters deep, and the emerald-green water remains stable around 24°C year-round. The waterfall is only 2 meters high but its pressure is powerful: a true natural hydromassage. The bottom sand is white, visibility perfect. Bring a mask or snorkel, you'll see the small fish. Paid entry (about 20 R$), quilombola territory.

⏱ 180 min
2

Fervedouro do Ceiça, 30 km from Mateiros, is one of the most accessible in the park. The principle is unusual: a water table rises through an opening in the sand, and the water pressure is such that you float effortlessly. No need to know how to swim. The rule is strict: entry in small groups, limited duration to avoid disturbing the source, no sunscreen or repellent (it pollutes immediately).

⏱ 75 min
3

For comparison, stop by Fervedouro Buritizinho, smaller, surrounded by buriti palms. The sensation is the same but the setting is different - turquoise-blue water, visible roots at the bottom. A few minutes are enough to feel the upward push. These springs are fragile: walk on the walkways, not on the banks.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Between two fervedouros, picnic in the shade of the buritis. Many agencies provide a cold meal (sandwich, fruit, cashew juice) included in the passeio. Otherwise, bring your own - no restaurants between sites, just red track.

⏱ 60 min

If you still have time in late afternoon, Fervedouro Bela Vista offers a nighttime session with lighting, usually reserved for Pousada Bela Vista guests. Cinema atmosphere, water still at 24°C even at night. Ask your guide about the slot.

⏱ 60 min

Mumbuca

★★☆☆☆
1

The povoado of Mumbuca is located 35 km from Mateiros, on the TO-110 road between Mateiros and São Félix do Tocantins. The quilombola community descends from families who arrived from Bahia in the early 20th century - the parents of matriarch Dona Miúda (Guilhermina Pereira Matos, 1928-2010) came to the region around 1909. Today, about 165 inhabitants. The word "mumbuca" refers to a species of stingless bee, very common here, which gave its name to the place. Drop your bags at a local pousada or homestay accommodation.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Associação Capim Dourado, founded in 2000, is the village's economic heart. About fifty artisans work "capim dourado" there - which is not a grass but a plant called sempre-viva (Syngonanthus nitens), endemic to the cerrado. The fine golden stems are sewn with buriti fiber to make hats, baskets, mandalas, jewelry. According to Dona Miúda's memory, the technique was transmitted in the early 20th century by passing Xerente Indians, who taught the already settled inhabitants to "sew the capim." Buy here directly from the artisans, not from resellers.

⏱ 90 min
3

Fervedouro do Mumbuca, managed by the community, is one of the oldest to have been opened to tourism. Crystal-clear water, white sand bottom, palms all around. The upward pressure lifts your legs in seconds. The entry fee goes to the association - each visit contributes directly to the community.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Participate in a weaving workshop with an artisan: you're shown how to sort the stems, moisten them, sew the first stitches with seda de buriti. Count on about 2 hours to understand the technique. You leave with a small handmade object and the measure of what a hat representing several days of work means.

⏱ 120 min

If you're in Jalapão in late August or September, time your visit for the Festa da Colheita do Capim Dourado. Official harvest starts on September 20, preceded by a weekend of songs, dances and viola de buriti. It's the year's highlight for the community - don't photograph anything without asking.

⏱ 180 min

Ponte Alta Do Tocantins

★★☆☆☆
1

Return west on the same sandy track. Pedra Furada is located about 30 km from Ponte Alta - not the same one as in Mateiros: here too the sandstone was hollowed into a portal by the wind, and late afternoon light filters through the hole like a natural projector. You get out of the 4x4, two minutes' walk through a eucalyptus grove, and you're there. Free visit.

⏱ 60 min
2

In Pindorama do Tocantins, on the road, Lagoa do Japonês offers a refreshing stop: transparent waters with bluish hues, clear bottom, quieter atmosphere than the star sites. Many make it their last swim before returning to Palmas. Grass beach, a few tables under the trees.

⏱ 90 min
3

On the final section, heading south before Ponte Alta, you cross capim dourado fields in full vereda - those fine golden stems you saw woven in Mumbuca. Ask your guide to stop for a few minutes on the roadside: backlit, in late afternoon, the vereda becomes literally golden. It's the image you'll keep of Jalapão beyond the dunes.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Long 4x4 drive between Mumbuca and Ponte Alta - about 3 hours on track, sometimes more if the road condition has suffered from recent rains. Prepare a long playlist, keep water within reach and accept that half the day will be spent in the pickup. The landscape scrolls by: veredas of buritis, chapadões, sudden watercourses around a bend.

⏱ 180 min

Evening in Ponte Alta: choose a simple churrasco on the praça, with grilled picanha and farofa. It's also an opportunity to buy a last kilo of dried capim dourado or a small hat if you didn't have time in Mumbuca.

⏱ 90 min

Palmas

★★★★☆
1

Palmas is Brazil's last entirely planned state capital in the 20th century: foundation stone laid on May 20, 1989, seven months after Tocantins State was created. The avenues are straight, wide, numbered by quadras - a bit like Brasília, cerrado version. Take a tour of Praça dos Girassóis, one of the world's largest urban squares. At the center, the Araguaia Palace houses the state government headquarters.

⏱ 75 min
2

35 km from downtown Palmas, the Taquaruçu district hides several cachoeiras in the mata. The best known, Escorrega Macaco, descends about 50 meters in two levels and ends in a pool ideal for swimming. Easy trail of about 1.5 km from the parking lot. The name comes from a local climbing plant said to be "slippery as a monkey." If your flight leaves in the evening, it's a perfect last swim.

⏱ 120 min
3

Praia da Graciosa, on the lake formed by the Lajeado dam on the Tocantins River, is Palmas's urban beach: imported white sand, aligned kiosks, fresh and warm water. It's one of the capital's five official beaches, open year-round. Perfect for finishing the trip gently, after a week of tracks and dust. Local fish restaurants all along the shore, particularly grilled tucunaré and pirarucu.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If your flight leaves in the evening, lunch at Praia da Graciosa with a peixe na telha (fish cooked on clay tile, local specialty) accompanied by farofa and rice. It's the Palmas version of the return-from-travel meal.

⏱ 90 min

Stop by the Mercado Municipal for last purchases: pequi (cerrado fruit with powerful flavor), pamonha de Goiás, artisanal cachaça, and of course a few certified capim dourado pieces. Open early morning, quieter before 10 AM.

⏱ 60 min
