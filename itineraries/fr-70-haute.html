Vesoul

Where panoramic hilltops meet Comtois museum treasures

★★★☆☆
1

Climb the Motte hill to reach Notre-Dame Chapel, a pilgrimage site since the Middle Ages. The exceptional panorama sweeps across the Vosges, the Jura mountains, and on clear days, even the Alps.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Musée Georges-Garret occupies a former Ursuline convent built in 1680. Explore 1,200 m² across 14 rooms: the region's most important collection of Gallo-Roman funerary stelae, works by Prud'hon, Tissot, Boucher, Van Loo, and an Orientalist section. The museum pays tribute to Jean-Léon Gérôme (1824-1904), the celebrated painter born in Vesoul.

⏱ 90 min
3

Saint-Georges Church dominates Vesoul's historic center, an imposing religious edifice that bears witness to the local sacred architecture.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Enjoy Lake Vesoul-Vaivre below the Motte hill, a developed natural area perfect for a relaxing break with water activities and family walks.

⏱ 90 min

Wander through the streets of old Vesoul, discover its private mansions, and soak up the atmosphere of this prefecture of Haute-Saône, gateway to the Vosges Saônoises.

Jussey

A town where eighteen fountains whisper republican history

★★☆☆☆
1

Set off to discover the 18 fountains that have made this Cité de Caractère famous. The Marianne Fountain (Place de la Libération), listed as a Historic Monument, is the oldest public representation of the Republic in France. The Lions Fountain displays its elegant sculptures.

⏱ 60 min
2

Saint-Pierre Church, rebuilt between 1750 and 1760, houses a remarkable high altar and superb woodwork created by Jean Gerdolle, testament to the craftsmanship of 18th-century artisans.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Maison Cordienne features a main building and staircases listed as Historic Monuments, a characteristic example of Jussey's medieval civil architecture.

⏱ 30 min
4

Nearby, discover Château de Bougey, founded in the 14th century and transformed in the 16th-17th centuries. Its strategic defensive position on the heights bears witness to its past as a medieval stronghold.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

⏱ 90 min

Champlitte

Renaissance château sheltering centuries of rural memory

★★☆☆☆
1

Château de Champlitte, listed as a Historic Monument since 1909, displays its elegant Renaissance façade flanked by two 18th-century neoclassical wings. French formal gardens laid out in 2008 enhance the main courtyard.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Departmental Museum of Arts and Popular Traditions transports you into Haute-Saône's rural society at the end of the 19th century. Explore 40 rooms featuring immersive recreations of peasant interiors, artisan workshops (leather, hemp, iron, lace), and shops (grocery store, café, pharmacy). The collection founded by Albert and Félicie Demard preserves objects and furniture threatened by rural exodus.

⏱ 120 min
3

The château's salon evokes the wealth of the Toulongeon family, owners until 1825. Panoramic wallpapers from 1804 titled 'The Savages of the Pacific Ocean' still adorn the walls of the oval salon.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Experience an immersive escape game called 'The Countess's Jewels,' which transports you to 1751 in a period-furnished château room. Solve puzzles to recover the precious jewels of Countess Anne-Prospère Cordier de Launay.

⏱ 90 min

Explore the vineyards still cultivated in Champlitte. Follow the Dry Stone Path at Les Cabordes to discover how humans have shaped these limestone wine-growing landscapes while enjoying magnificent panoramas.

⏱ 75 min

Walk in pilgrims' footsteps along the Via Francigena, a Cultural Route of the Council of Europe linking Canterbury to Rome. Champlitte hosts the European Association's office and constitutes a major stage on this pilgrimage route.

⏱ 60 min

Gray

Baroque splendor rising above the silver Saône

★★☆☆☆
1

The Baron Martin Museum occupies an 18th-century château that hosted the Count of Provence (future Louis XVIII) from 1777 to 1783. Labeled as a Museum of France, it unfolds 14 fine arts salons with sumptuous decor: overdoor paintings, female mascaron heads, pier glasses, and stuccowork. The collections feature Prud'hon, Tissot, Boucher, Van Loo, Van Kessel, and Monticelli.

⏱ 90 min
2

The museum's medieval cellars house 600 archaeological pieces, including the famous pagan sarcophagus from Mantoche (1844) and Greek vases from the 4th-5th centuries BC.

⏱ 45 min
3

Look up at Notre-Dame Basilica, a 15th-16th century Gothic edifice dominating the upper town. Built after the 1477 fire, it has housed the heart of Saint Pierre Fourier since 1641 and since 1802 the miraculous statue of Our Lady of Gray, which earned it the title of minor basilica in 1948.

⏱ 75 min
4

Inside the basilica, admire the Tree of Jesse stained-glass window measuring 11.1 meters—the largest in Europe—created in the 16th century by the Flemish sculptors of Brou. Sacred art treasures include reliquaries, chalices, monstrances, and sculptures.

⏱ 45 min
5

Discover the Hôtel de Conflans built in the 16th century, with its remarkable Renaissance-style spiral staircase tower (viorbe) that impresses with its size.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll through the museum's French formal gardens, which offer a stunning view of the Saône valley from their terrace graced with two ceremonial cannons. The entrance is marked by the Paravis tower, a crenellated remnant of the medieval fortress.

⏱ 45 min

Climb the Saint-Pierre Fourier tower with its pivoting staircase unique in France, leading to a small Renaissance room where Pierre Fourier, a Lorraine priest fleeing Lorraine in 1636, hid. Once rotated, the staircase conceals access to this secret hideaway.

⏱ 60 min

Board the Audacieux, a solar-electric boat, for a narrated cruise on the Saône. Admire the river landscapes and discover the history of river trade that brought prosperity to Gray since the 12th century.

⏱ 90 min

Luxeuil Les Bains

Where Saint Columbanus's abbey meets millennial thermal waters

★★★☆☆
1

The Saint-Pierre Basilica, the fourth church erected on this site where Saint Columbanus founded one of Europe's first monasteries in 590, harmoniously blends Romanesque and Gothic styles. Built between 1215 and 1330, its pink Vosges sandstone walls lend it unique beauty. The bell tower contains Haute-Saône's largest bell at 3,563 kg.

⏱ 75 min
2

Look up at the basilica's crown jewel: its 17th-century organ case (1617, expanded in 1685), one of the oldest in Franche-Comté. Supported by a statue of Atlas, this instrument with 3,137 pipes and 44 stops evokes in its sculpted form the prow of a royal vessel.

⏱ 30 min
3

Stroll through the abbey cloister built in pink sandstone at the end of the 15th century, the last surviving medieval element of the Benedictine monastery. On the vaulted keystones, discover the coats of arms of the abbots who contributed to its construction.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Abbey of Saint-Pierre-et-Saint-Paul, founded in 590 by Saint Columbanus on the ruins of ancient Luxovium (ravaged by Attila in 451), was a leading religious and intellectual center. Its scriptorium gave birth in the 7th century to Luxeuil script, the first calligraphic minuscule writing.

⏱ 60 min
5

Discover the thermal baths, a classical pink Vosges sandstone building constructed in the 18th century under Louis XV, listed as a Historic Monument in 1862. Since Celtic and Roman times, the thermal waters gushing at over 63°C have been renowned for their curative properties. The Art Deco architecture with its refined mosaics makes this establishment a remarkable place.

⏱ 60 min
6

The François I House with its three arcades is the last vestige of the arcaded houses that once lined the main street. Also admire the Spanish House with its flamboyant Gothic façade, the Bailiff's House with its balcony and crenellated polygonal tower, and the Cardinal Jouffroy House adorned with France's oldest suspended stone balcony.

⏱ 60 min
7

Visit the 33-meter-high Tour des Échevins, built in the 15th century, which houses one of France's oldest museums. The collections trace Luxeuil's history through Gallo-Roman archaeological remains, and one floor is dedicated to the famous painter Jules Adler.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Follow the 2-km Heritage Trail marked by bronze suns embedded in the ground, punctuated by 14 stops with QR codes for audio explanations. It crosses the 17 historic monuments: basilica, cloister, thermal baths, Ecclesia archaeological site, Renaissance houses, and the Street of Balconies.

⏱ 90 min

Look up along Rue Carnot to admire the 52 wrought-iron and cast-iron balconies that testify to the golden age of Haute-Saône foundries in the 19th century. These foundries notably manufactured Monaco's war memorial and ornaments for Cuba's Parliament Palace.

⏱ 30 min

Explore the Ecclesia archaeological site presenting over 2,000 years of history with 150 sarcophagi, one of eastern France's principal sites for early Christian and Merovingian periods.

⏱ 60 min

Fougerolles

Cherry orchards distilling liquid gold since 1829

★★★☆☆
1

The Cherry Country Ecomuseum, housed in a former distillery from 1829 listed as a Historic Monument, traces 150 years of expertise around cherries and kirsch AOC. Tour the master's house, discover the stills, the cellar with its oak barrels, and the attic where eaux-de-vie age in their glass demijohns.

⏱ 120 min
2

The conservatory orchard brings together nearly 40 heritage cherry tree varieties, some threatened with extinction: Béchat, Caraude, Jean Blanc, Marie Jean Diaude, Tinette, Chapendu. In spring, the blossoms offer an enchanting spectacle evoking a Japanese atmosphere.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Follow the Chalots Route, a tourist circuit that lets you discover chalots—traditional grain lofts typical of Vosges architecture—while meeting local artisans who perpetuate the distillation craft.

⏱ 90 min

Meet the master distillers who have perpetuated the tradition of Fougerolles Kirsch AOC for generations. Today, about a hundred producers cultivate 10,000 cherry trees that produce 500 tons of cherries for 35,000 liters of eau-de-vie annually.

⏱ 75 min

Ronchamp

Le Corbusier's concrete prayer sculpted by light

★★★★☆
1

Le Corbusier's Notre-Dame-du-Haut Chapel, a masterpiece of modern architecture and Haute-Saône's only UNESCO site, revolutionized 20th-century religious architecture. Built between 1953 and 1955 on Bourlémont hill, it replaces a sanctuary destroyed by 1944 bombings. The architect drew inspiration from a crab shell for the reinforced concrete roof's shape.

⏱ 90 min
2

Cross the chapel's threshold to discover an intimate space where light sculpts volumes. The 27 windows piercing the 2.72-meter-thick south wall create wells of colored light. The 18th-century statue of the Virgin and Child, spared by war, is placed against the light in the east wall.

⏱ 45 min
3

Admire the steel campanile created in 1975 by Jean Prouvé, bearing three bells. This elegant structure harmoniously completes the architectural ensemble.

⏱ 20 min
4

Renzo Piano's contemporary buildings have completed the site since 2011: the Sainte-Claire monastery semi-buried in the hill and the reception portery. The landscape ensemble designed by Michel Corajoud harmonizes the spaces with planted roofs.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

From Bourlémont hill, admire the panorama that captivated Le Corbusier during his first visit. Views open onto the first Jura mountains to the south and the Vosges to the north, creating what the architect called 'the four horizons.'

⏱ 30 min

Throughout the year, the hill hosts concerts, exhibitions, conferences, and theatrical performances that make this pilgrimage site an essential cultural venue, visited by 65,000 people from 60 countries annually.

⏱ 90 min
