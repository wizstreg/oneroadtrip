✨ Highlights
  • Canudos State Park, created in 1986, preserving the sites of the 1896-1897 messianic war
  • Monte Santo, sacred Bahian sanctuary listed by IPHAN in 1983, military base of the fourth expedition against Canudos
  • Estação Ecológica do Raso da Catarina, roughly 105,000 hectares of preserved caatinga and primary habitat of Lear's macaw
  • São Francisco Canyon, 65 km of granite wall up to 50 m high between Paulo Afonso and the Xingó dam
📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 1 and 3 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 What to discover in BR?

Carnival energy, Amazon rainforest, and pristine beaches create the ultimate South American adventure. Experience vibrant cities, incredible biodiversity, and passionate culture from Rio's beaches to Pantanal wetlands. Samba music, capoeira martial arts, and incredible football culture showcase national spirit. Brazil offers unparalleled natural wonders with infectious cultural energy.

📋 Important notes

Semi-arid climate, temperatures reaching 40 °C during the day. Dry season from May to October strongly recommended. Visiting the interior of the Estação Ecológica do Raso da Catarina is forbidden to the public outside scientific research—observation of Lear's macaws takes place at the Estação Biológica de Canudos (Biodiversitas) and in accessible buffer zones with local guide. Car required. Arrival airport: Salvador (SSA). Possible return airport: Paulo Afonso (PAV) or Aracaju (AJU).

Bahia Sertão - 5 Days Tracing Canudos, the Raso da Catarina, and the Rio São Francisco

📅 5 days📍 5 stops🚗 671 km⏱ 10h50

Feira De Santana → Monte Santo → Canudos → Paulo Afonso

Feira De Santana → Monte Santo → Canudos → Paulo Afonso

Five days in the deep sertão of Bahia, tracing the War of Canudos (1896-1897) immortalised by Euclides da Cunha in 'Os Sertões.' The route leads from Salvador to Feira de Santana, Canudos, Monte Santo, and Paulo Afonso, to the canyons of the Rio São Francisco near the Xingó dam. Preserved caatinga, historic sites, sertaneja cuisine, and the river at the heart of the journey.

Stop 1

Feira De Santana

Where the sertão's cattle trails met before the long march to the capital

📍 Centro de Abastecimento Feira de Santana📍 Catedral Senhora Sant'Ana Feira de Santana📍 Museu Casa do Sertão Feira de Santana
1

You start in Salvador, pick up your rental car, then take the BR-324 northwest to Feira de Santana, 116 km away. Count on 1h30 on dual carriageway. Feira de Santana is Bahia's second city and the historic crossroads of the tropeiros who drove cattle from the sertões to the capital—hence its name, 'Sant'Ana's Fair.' This stop saves you from tackling the Canudos route in one push (another 280 km tomorrow).

⏱ 60 min
2

Drop your bags and head straight for the Centro de Abastecimento, the city's big covered market. You're standing in the commercial heart of the sertão: carne de sol, queijo coalho, rapadura, farinha, dried fruit, medicinal herbs. Spot the umbu and cajá stalls—typical fruits you'll encounter further along. It's the best sensory introduction to the sertão before you dive in tomorrow.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Museu Casa do Sertão, on the UEFS campus, is worth a detour. You'll find the material culture of the sertanejo: saddles, bridles, cangaceiro clothing, ex-votos, cordel instruments. Valuable context before Canudos. Open weekdays, closed weekends—check hours before you leave.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Dine at a local carniceria or carne de sol restaurant. The specialty here is sun-dried meat, grilled and served with aipim (cassava) and farofa. It's uncompromising, and it's exactly what you need to taste as you arrive in the sertão.

⏱ 90 min

Before heading to the hotel, spend 30 minutes filling the tank, checking the tyres, and buying water in quantity. Starting tomorrow, you enter the semi-arid zone: petrol stations grow scarcer and temperatures rise fast.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 281 km · 4h05
Stop 2

Monte Santo

Stone steps climbing to heaven, where pilgrims and soldiers once crossed paths

📍 Santuário Santa Cruz Monte Santo📍 Via Sacra Monte Santo📍 Capela Senhor dos Passos Monte Santo📍 Museu de Monte Santo
1

You leave Canudos on the BR-116 heading south, then branch off toward Monte Santo via Euclides da Cunha (formerly Cumbe, renamed after the author of 'Os Sertões'). Count on about 2h15 of driving. Monte Santo served as the main military base for the fourth expedition against Belo Monte in 1897—from here the troops marched on foot to Canudos, roughly 150 km away.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Via Sacra de Monte Santo climbs 1,969 metres along the Serra do Piquaraçá. Twenty-five chapels mark the Stations of the Cross, created in the late 18th century by the Italian Capuchin friar Apolônio de Todi—one of Brazil's principal 'sacromontes,' listed by IPHAN in 1983. Antônio Conselheiro passed through around 1892 with his thousands of followers and repaired part of the steps—five years before these same places became the base of the army that came to fight him. Steep ascent, allow 1h30 to 2h return.

⏱ 150 min
3

At the summit, the Santuário da Santa Cruz dominates the sertanejo plain. The main chapel preserves the images of Our Lady of Solitude, the Dead Christ, and Saint John the Evangelist. The 360° view sweeps across cerrado and caatinga: this is the landscape Antônio Conselheiro saw from the lookout, and it's in these expanses that he preached. The geographical effect hits you: you understand why the sertão was for him a land of election.

⏱ 60 min
4

Back down in town, the Museu de Monte Santo occupies the former house of Coronel Galdino Andrade, from the early 20th century. Small collection but well curated on the tradition of romarias, Conselheiro's passage, and the city's history. Allow 45 minutes.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you're here on a Sunday or during Semana Santa, you'll encounter romeiros climbing to the sanctuary barefoot, sometimes on their knees, with stones on their heads. This tradition isn't folkloric—it's alive, and the All Saints' feasts (31 October - 1 November) draw thousands of pilgrims. Stay discreet, photograph respectfully.

⏱ 60 min

Taste sertaneja cuisine in a restaurant on Rua Senhor dos Passos: bode guisado (kid stew), buchada de bode (stuffed tripe), galinha caipira, accompanied by a suco de umbu. These dishes are the staples of local cuisine, adapted to drought and the dominant goat farming.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 280 km · 4h30
Stop 3

Canudos

Where Antônio Conselheiro's dream drowned in cannon fire and silence

📍 Parque Estadual de Canudos📍 Memorial Antônio Conselheiro Canudos📍 Açude de Cocorobó📍 Igreja Matriz de Canudos
1

Long drive this morning: 281 km from Feira de Santana to Canudos via BR-116 then BR-235, count on about 4h of driving. The landscape gradually shifts, vegetation grows scrubby, mandacarus appear, distances stretch. You're entering the deep sertão. Make a coffee stop at Tucano or Euclides da Cunha to breathe and break the journey.

⏱ 30 min
2

You arrive in Canudos early afternoon. The present town is the third of its name—emancipated in 1985 under this name from the district of Cocorobó. The first Belo Monte founded by Antônio Conselheiro in 1893 was razed on 5 October 1897, the second founded around 1910 by survivors was drowned by the Cocorobó dam in the 1960s. During drought, the ruins of the first Canudos emerge from the reservoir.

⏱ 60 min
3

Head to the Parque Estadual de Canudos, created in 1986 and managed by the State of Bahia. You'll find the Alto do Mário, the Alto da Favela, and the Fazenda Velha—the sites of the fourth military expedition's battles led by General Artur Oscar with roughly 10,000 men. This is where the war ended on 5 October 1897, after twelve days of intense bombardment with Krupp cannon and the 105mm Whitworth 'matadeira.'

⏱ 120 min
4

The Instituto Popular Memorial de Canudos (IPMC), separate from the state park, preserves Antônio Conselheiro's Cruzeiro riddled with bullets during the war, a collection of popular art inspired by Belo Monte, and a dedicated library. It's the other major historical stop in town—more intimate than the park, and complementary.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

If it's the dry season and the reservoir level is low, go down to the edge of the Cocorobó dam. You can see the ruins of the first Canudos emerge from the water—foundations, wall sections, traces of Belo Monte's urban layout. Striking effect when you have Euclides da Cunha's 'Os Sertões' (1902) in mind.

⏱ 60 min

In the evening, settle into a local lanchonete and strike up conversation with the inhabitants. The memory of Canudos isn't just textbook material here—it's family history for several lineages. Many have ancestors among the survivors, and the event remains a strong identity reference for the Bahian sertão.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 110 km · 2h15
Stop 4

Paulo Afonso

Where the Old Frankie cuts through granite and cangaceiros once hid in caves

📍 Complexo Hidrelétrico Paulo Afonso📍 Estação Biológica de Canudos Biodiversitas📍 Raso da Catarina Paulo Afonso📍 Cânion Rio São Francisco Paulo Afonso
1

Long drive from Monte Santo to Paulo Afonso, roughly 280 km via BR-116 then BR-110, count on 4h30 with stops. To break the journey, stop at Jeremoabo, about 100 km from Paulo Afonso: it's one of the municipalities from where Lear's macaws are observed and the seat of the 'Programa de Conservação da Arara-Azul-de-Lear' created in 2001.

⏱ 30 min
2

If your schedule allows, the Estação Biológica de Canudos, managed by the Fundação Biodiversitas (130 hectares acquired in 1993, expanded to 1,500 hectares in 2007), is one of the rare sites where visitors can observe Lear's macaws nesting in the sandstone cliffs. The species, endemic to the caatinga of northeastern Bahia, was rediscovered in 1978 by ornithologist Helmut Sick—it had been considered lost since its description by Bonaparte in 1856. Wild population estimated at around 2,000 individuals. Booking required in advance.

⏱ 180 min
3

The Raso da Catarina protected area itself, instituted as an ecological reserve by decree 89.268 of 3 January 1984 then reclassified as Estação Ecológica in 2001, covers roughly 105,000 hectares of shrubby caatinga between the rios São Francisco and Vaza-Barris. Entry is forbidden to the public outside scientific research. What you can do: drive along the BR-110 between Paulo Afonso and Jeremoabo, observe the landscapes from the buffer zone, and—with a local guide—access certain viewpoints over the sandstone cliffs, refuge of cangaceiros in the early 20th century, including Lampião.

⏱ 90 min
4

You arrive in Paulo Afonso late afternoon. The town owes its name to Paulo Viveiros Afonso, sertanista who received a sesmaria in 1725 including the cachoeira. Before 1948, it was a dusty village—the creation of CHESF (Companhia Hidrelétrica do São Francisco) that year transformed everything. The historic cachoeira, which impressed Dom Pedro II in 1859, is now dry because of the dams, except during exceptional floods.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Dine facing the Rio São Francisco. The cuisine of the 'Velho Chico' (Old Frankie) revolves around freshwater fish: surubim, pintado, dourado. Pintado na telha, cooked on tile in its juice, is a classic. Also taste umbu juice, king fruit of the sertão, and local cachaça.

⏱ 90 min

Walk across the Ponte Metálica Dom Pedro II, 1950s ironwork on the BR-110 that links Paulo Afonso (Bahia) to Delmiro Gouveia (Alagoas). It marks the start of the São Francisco canyon and offers a plunging view over the first granite cliffs, up to roughly 50 metres above the water. It's also kilometre zero of the Xingó reservoir.

⏱ 45 min
Stop 5

Paulo Afonso

Where the Old Frankie cuts through granite and cangaceiros once hid in caves

📍 Cânion de Xingó Canindé do São Francisco📍 Usina Hidrelétrica de Xingó📍 Museu de Arqueologia de Xingó MAX📍 Porto Karrancas Canindé São Francisco📍 Piranhas Alagoas centro histórico
1

You cross the Ponte Metálica into Alagoas, then follow the south bank to Canindé do São Francisco (Sergipe), about 65 km from Paulo Afonso. This is where the catamarans embark for the Xingó canyon. The Xingó dam, closed on 10 June 1994, drowned the river's old corredeiras and created a 65 km reservoir, navigable to Paulo Afonso. Depth up to 180 metres between the granite cliffs.

⏱ 60 min
2

Board for the canyon cruise. Count on roughly 2h30 of navigation through what's billed as 'the world's 5th navigable canyon'—local marketing formula, but the scenery delivers: emerald waters, orange walls, sparse caatinga atop the cliffs. Chance to swim in certain coves, and close passage of the caves known as Furnas do Morcego, which sheltered Lampião and his bando in the 1930s.

⏱ 180 min
3

In Canindé, don't miss the Museu de Arqueologia de Xingó (MAX), managed by the Universidade Federal de Sergipe. Created in 2000, it preserves the archaeological material saved before the dam's flooding: ceramics, burials, lithic tools of prehistoric populations of the São Francisco valley. Scientific perspective on everything you've just seen disappear beneath the waters.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

To finish the trip, cross over to Piranhas in Alagoas, small listed colonial port on the opposite bank, historic base of Lampião's bando. The town preserves its 19th-century centre and offers 'Rota do Cangaço' tours that retrace the famous cangaceiro's end at Angicos in 1938. Count 1h30 on site before heading back to Paulo Afonso for the night, or pushing on to Aracaju (200 km) if your flight leaves from there.

⏱ 180 min

Final regional lunch at the port of Canindé: moqueca de surubim, sopa de pirarucu, or grilled pintado. The riverside restaurants serve fish caught that same morning. Close the loop with a coffee and a rapadura from the region.

⏱ 90 min
