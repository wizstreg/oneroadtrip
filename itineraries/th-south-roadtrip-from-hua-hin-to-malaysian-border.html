✨ Highlights
  • Cheow Lan Lake in Khao Sok: 165 km² surrounded by 100 limestone towers in southern Thailand's largest primary rainforest, estimated at over 160 million years old.
  • Railay Beach: peninsula accessible only by longtail, with Phra Nang Cave Beach and its cliffs towering directly above white sand.
  • Koh Lipe: Thailand's southernmost island, 30 km from Langkawi, waters with 20–25 m visibility in Tarutao marine park.
  • Phuket Old Town: Sino-Portuguese quarter with 19th-century shophouses, Baba-Nyonya heritage, and street art from the twinning with Penang.
📆 Best period

November, December, January, February, March, April

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 14 and 18 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Cool Season (November-February): Perfect weather (20-30°C) with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures, and peak tourist season. Highest prices but optimal conditions for all activities and sightseeing.

Hot Season (March-May): Very hot temperatures (30-40°C) with moderate crowds and prices. Challenging for extensive outdoor activities but good for beaches and air-conditioned attractions.

Rainy Season (June-October): Heavy afternoon rains, high humidity, but lowest prices and fewer crowds. Lush landscapes, some flooding, but authentic experiences and great value.

🌍 What to discover in TH?

Southeast Asian paradise with ancient temples, incredible cuisine, and vibrant culture. Experience Buddhist traditions, beautiful beaches, and authentic Thai hospitality. The country offers cultural immersion with natural beauty and culinary excellence. Thailand provides diverse experiences with cultural authenticity and natural preservation.

📋 Important notes

This itinerary runs entirely along the Andaman (western) coast. The provinces of Yala, Pattani and Narathiwat (Deep South) are excluded — not recommended for travel due to government advisories. The Similan Islands are accessible only between November and April. Koh Lipe is closed to visitors from May through October. Factor in ferries from Krabi to Koh Lanta and from Pak Bara to Koh Lipe.

📍 Thung Wua Laen Beach Chumphon📍 Khao Matsee Viewpoint Chumphon📍 Chumphon Night Market

Head down to Hat Thung Wua Laen, the finest beach along Chumphon's coastline. This 2.7-kilometer ribbon of white sand runs alongside a quiet road lined with bungalows and modest seafood restaurants. The name means 'beach where the buffalo runs' — a remnant from when herds grazed here. On weekdays, you'll practically have it to yourself.

Activities

Drive from Hua Hin or Bangkok to Chumphon (3.5 hours from Hua Hin, around 500 km from Bangkok). Chumphon is also a stop on the Bangkok–Surat Thani overnight train — arrival possible in the early morning.

In the evening, explore Chumphon's night market, set up in the town center. The stalls selling tropical fruit from local orchards are particularly abundant — mangosteens, durians, rambutan depending on the season.

Ranong

Hot springs at 65°C, Myanmar border crossing, and the country's densest rainforest.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Raksawarin Hot Springs Ranong📍 Saphan Pla Fishing Port Ranong📍 Ranong Night Market Kamlangsap Road
1

Soak your feet at Raksawarin Hot Springs, 2 km from Ranong's center. Water gushes out at 65°C year-round — three separate pools (the Father, the Mother, the Child) let you choose your intensity. King Rama V visited here in 1890 and named the access road 'Chon Ra-u,' meaning 'hot water.' The public pools are free; private enclosures are available for a small fee.

⏱ 90 min
2

Stroll along Saphan Pla fishing port, one of the busiest along the Andaman coast. Across the Kraburi River, you can see Kawthaung — Myanmar's southernmost continental point — just a few hundred meters away. Colorful longboats shuttle between the two countries.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Coastal drive from Chumphon to Ranong (around 2 hours) via Highway 4, which follows a mountainous, heavily forested shoreline. Ranong is Thailand's rainiest province — 8 months of monsoon — but this gives it vegetation of remarkable density.

⏱ 130 min

In the evening, take a turn through the night market on Kamlangsap Road, 200 m from Highway 4. Small but lively, it concentrates the Thai-Burmese specialties of the region — strong Hokkien influence in the snacks and soups.

Stop 3

Khao Sok

Rainforest 160 million years in the making, Cheow Lan Lake, and floating bungalows among karsts.

★★★☆☆
📍 Khao Sok National Park Village📍 Sok River Khao Sok📍 Khao Sok Night Walk Trail
1

Check into a riverside bungalow at the entrance to the national park. The forest surrounding you is estimated to be over 160 million years old — older and richer in biodiversity than the Amazon. As you make your way down to your lodging, listen closely: male gibbons unleash their territorial calls at dawn.

⏱ 60 min
2

Set off hiking the park trails late in the afternoon, when light filters through the canopy. The park is home to wild elephants, Malayan sun bears, tapirs, and clouded leopards. Between January and March, the rafflesia can bloom here — its flower reaches up to 80 cm in diameter, one of the largest in the world.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Drive from Ranong to Khao Sok (around 3 hours) via Highway 4 then Route 401. Along the way, you'll pass through rubber and palm oil plantations that blanket the southern hills. Arrive in the afternoon to enjoy the forest light.

⏱ 180 min

Night safari on the park trails with a local guide. The forest reveals itself most at night: slow lorises, palm civets, frogs of improbable size. Bring a headlamp and closed shoes.

Khao Sok

Rainforest 160 million years in the making, Cheow Lan Lake, and floating bungalows among karsts.

★★★☆☆
📍 Cheow Lan Lake Ratchaprapha Dam Khao Sok📍 Khao Sam Kloe Guilin Cheow Lan📍 Floating Bungalows Cheow Lan Lake
1

Board a longtail boat onto Cheow Lan Lake from the Ratchaprapha pier. The lake, created by the dam of the same name in 1986, spans 165 km² deep in the forest. The limestone karsts — some rising to 960 meters — plunge straight into the emerald water. First stop: Khao Sam Kloe, a trio of limestone towers surging from the water, which Thais nickname 'the Guilin of Cheow Lan.'

⏱ 60 min
2

Explore Phra Kay Petch cave, 100 meters long, carved into the Klong Ka tributary. Inside, colored stalactites and stalagmites drop into the darkness. Millions of bats nest on the ceiling.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Full-day longtail excursion on Cheow Lan Lake from Ratchaprapha pier. Book the day before through your accommodation. Leave early — morning mist shrouds the karsts and the light is perfect before 9 a.m.

⏱ 360 min

Kayak from the floating bungalows: paddle among the limestone islets in absolute calm. Most accommodations on the water provide kayaks to their guests. Early morning, watch for monkeys in the treetops along the shores.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 5

Khao Lak

Long, quiet beaches, 2004 tsunami memorial, and departure point for the Similan Islands.

★★★★★
📍 Bang Niang Beach Khao Lak📍 Tsunami Police Boat Memorial Khao Lak📍 Nang Thong Beach Khao Lak
1

Visit the tsunami memorial on Highway 4, north of Khao Lak. A police boat — number 813 — rests there, carried 2 km inland by the wave of December 26, 2004. This boat belonged to the Royal Thai Navy and was at sea that morning. Khao Lak recorded the highest run-up wave ever measured in Thailand that day.

⏱ 45 min
2

Walk along Nang Thong Beach and Bang Niang Beach, Khao Lak's two main beaches. A post-tsunami regulation bans high-rise construction along the shoreline — result: no towers, no concrete dominating the sea, just discreet resorts in the trees. Khao Lak Beach stretches 16 km in total.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Drive Khao Sok to Khao Lak (around 1.5 hours). Khao Lak is the departure point for excursions to the Similan Islands, accessible only from October to May from Thap Lamu pier. Off-season, inquire about snorkeling trips around the coastal islands.

⏱ 90 min

If you're here between November and April, book a snorkeling trip to the Similan Islands departing from Thap Lamu pier (60 km offshore). The Similans rank among Asia's top ten dive sites — multicolored corals, manta rays, white-tip reef sharks. The park closes from May to October.

⏱ 480 min

Stop 6

Phuket

Sino-Portuguese old town, Baba-Nyonya street art, and night markets among pastel shophouses.

★★★★★
📍 Soi Romanee Phuket Old Town📍 Thalang Road Phuket Old Town📍 Phuket Sunday Walking Street Market📍 Thai Hua Museum Phuket
1

Get lost in Phuket's old town, around Thalang Road and Soi Romanee. The Sino-Portuguese shophouses with pastel facades date from the early 20th-century mining boom, when Hokkien merchants and Portuguese engineers fused their styles. Soi Romanee was once Phuket's entertainment district — today, its pink houses shelter cafés and boutiques. It's the most photographed street on the island.

⏱ 120 min
2

Step into the Thai Hua Museum on Krabi Road, housed in a former Sino-Portuguese school from 1934. The exhibition traces the history of Phuket's Baba-Nyonya (Peranakan) community — born from the crossroads of Hokkien Chinese miners and local Thai women, with their own cuisine, language, and crafts.

⏱ 75 min
3

On Sundays from 4 p.m. to 10 p.m., Thalang Road transforms into a pedestrian market. Street food stalls, local musicians, and artisans take over the street: it's the best time to sample Phuket's Sino-Thai specialties in the open air.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Drive Khao Lak to Phuket (1.5 hours). Stay in the old town rather than Patong — you'll sleep in a heritage building, 10 minutes' walk from everything that matters.

⏱ 90 min

Go hunting for the street art scattered around the old town since 2014, when Phuket and Georgetown (Penang) became sister cities. Look for Alex Face's work on Phang Nga Road and Rukkij Kuanhavej's murals at the corner of Soi Romanee.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 7

Phuket

Sino-Portuguese old town, Baba-Nyonya street art, and night markets among pastel shophouses.

★★★★★
📍 James Bond Island Phang Nga Bay📍 Koh Panyee Floating Village Phang Nga📍 Ao Phang Nga National Park
1

Join a longtail tour of Phang Nga Bay from Tha Dan pier. First stop: Koh Tapu, that 20-meter rock jutting vertically from the sea — made famous as 'James Bond Island' after filming 'The Man with the Golden Gun' in 1974. The real name of the neighboring island, Khao Phing Kan, means 'mountains leaning against each other' — a massive split limestone block leans against its twin.

⏱ 40 min
2

Stop at Koh Panyee, a floating village founded by Muslim fishermen over 200 years ago. All the houses, the school, the mosque, and even a football pitch are perched on stilts against a vertical limestone tower. Walk the wooden walkways — away from the main tourist flow, a few alleys still show the village's daily life.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Full-day longtail excursion in Phang Nga Bay. Depart from Tha Dan Pier (Phang Nga Town) — the boat ride is shorter and you'll cross paths with fewer speedboats. Opt for a private longtail for more flexibility.

⏱ 480 min

Kayak through the 'hongs' — those marine caverns carved into limestone cliffs by erosion, accessible only at low tide by kayak. Inside, a miniature jungle and a secret lagoon await. Operators based in Phang Nga provide paddling guides.

Stop 8

Krabi

Railay cut off by road, Tiger Cave Temple's 1,260 steps, and 4-island boat tours.

★★★★☆
📍 Railay Beach Krabi📍 Phra Nang Cave Beach Railay📍 Ao Nang Krabi
1

Take a longtail from Ao Nang to Railay — 15 minutes across turquoise water between cliffs collapsing into the sea. Railay is a peninsula completely cut off from the road by its own karsts: you can only reach it by boat. Walk to Phra Nang Cave Beach, at the end of the coastal path. The cliffs lean over the white sand, creating an almost surreal scene.

⏱ 120 min
2

Visit Phra Nang Cave (Princess Cave) at the far end of the beach. Local fishermen have left carved wooden ex-votos here for decades — a tradition linked to the legend of a princess drowned at sea. The cave is a sacred place where the devotion of local sailors can be read in every offering.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Drive Phuket to Krabi (around 3 hours). Arrive early afternoon to catch the longtail from Ao Nang to Railay. Boats leave as soon as they're full — 8 passengers minimum. Otherwise share the cost with other travelers.

⏱ 180 min

Rock climbing on Railay West's limestone cliffs: one of Southeast Asia's premier limestone climbing sites. Operators offer sessions for beginners and advanced climbers alike, gear provided.

⏱ 180 min

Krabi

Railay cut off by road, Tiger Cave Temple's 1,260 steps, and 4-island boat tours.

★★★★☆
📍 Tiger Cave Temple Krabi Wat Tham Suea📍 Koh Poda Island Krabi📍 Chicken Island Krabi 4 Islands
1

Tackle Wat Tham Suea (Tiger Cave Temple) early in the morning, before the heat sets in. The temple was founded in 1975 by a monk meditating in the cave — tiger paw prints are still visible in the rock. The ascent counts 1,260 steps to the summit at 309 meters altitude. At the top: a golden Buddha, a chedi, and a panoramic view over Krabi, the Andaman Sea, and the 836 islands of the province. Allow 45 minutes to 1 hour to climb. Bring water.

⏱ 150 min

Activities

4-island longtail excursion from Ao Nang: Koh Poda, Tup Island (sandbar submerged at high tide), Chicken Island, and Koh Mor. Snorkeling stop between the islands. Lunch on the beach before returning late afternoon.

Koh Lanta

Stilt houses of Lanta Old Town, calm southern beaches, and snorkeling at Koh Rok.

★★★★☆
📍 Lanta Old Town Koh Lanta📍 Long Beach Koh Lanta📍 Mu Ko Lanta National Park Lighthouse
1

Arrive at Koh Lanta by ferry from Krabi. Get settled, then head down to Lanta Old Town, on the island's east coast. These are century-old teak houses on stilts, once a Chinese trading post on the maritime route between Phuket, Penang, and Singapore. The inhabitants: Thai-Chinese, Thai-Muslims, and Chao Lay (sea nomads). Lunch at one of the wooden restaurants whose tables overlook the sea.

⏱ 90 min
2

Drive down to the southern tip of the island to enter Mu Ko Lanta National Park. Short hike to the lighthouse — it stands at the end of a headland plunging into the Andaman Sea. Around you, dense jungle and reefs visible through the transparent water.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Ferry Krabi to Koh Lanta from Ao Nang or Krabi Town pier. The crossing takes around 2 hours depending on the boat type. Upon arrival, rent a scooter in Saladan — it's the best way to explore the island at your own pace.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset from Kantiang Bay, on the island's south. This long, lightly frequented bay offers Koh Lanta's most peaceful panorama — a few terraced resorts and a nearly deserted beach in low season.

⏱ 90 min

Koh Lanta

Stilt houses of Lanta Old Town, calm southern beaches, and snorkeling at Koh Rok.

★★★★☆
📍 Koh Rok Nok snorkeling Koh Lanta📍 Long Beach Koh Lanta sunset📍 Kantiang Bay Koh Lanta
1

Take a longtail to Koh Rok, 47 km south of Koh Lanta. This pair of uninhabited islands in the Mu Ko Lanta marine park is one of the region's finest snorkeling spots: water with 20–25 meter visibility, corals in excellent condition, sea turtles regularly sighted.

⏱ 300 min

Activities

Snorkeling around Koh Rok: gear up from the boat and explore the reefs in open water. Operators based on Koh Lanta organize small-group trips — departure 8:30 a.m., return around 4 p.m.

⏱ 360 min

In the evening, return to Long Beach or Klong Dao Beach for sunset. Koh Lanta faces due west — sunsets over the Andaman Sea are among the most beautiful on the Thai coast.

Stop 12

Trang

Sino-Thai town famed for dim sum, charcoal-roasted pork, and underground river caves.

★★★★★
📍 Tham Le Khao Kob Cave Trang📍 Trang Night Market Centrepoint📍 Trang train station
1

Detour to Tham Le Khao Kob cave, 35 km north of Trang. You visit it by rowboat on an underground river that runs through a limestone massif. The boatmen know every inch of the vault — at certain passages, you have to lie down in the boat to avoid hitting the ceiling. Locals call the narrowest section 'the dragon's backbone.'

⏱ 75 min
2

In the evening, explore Trang's night market, known as 'Centrepoint,' a few streets east of the train station. Trang is renowned for its dim sum and charcoal-roasted pork — a legacy of Hokkien immigrants who populated the town when the tin mines were running full tilt. Vendors offer 20 varieties of curry at once. Order by pointing.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Return ferry to the mainland from Koh Lanta (1.5–2 hours via Trang or Pak Meng pier). From the pier, drive to Trang (30–45 min). Trang is an ordinary Thai town with extraordinary gastronomy.

⏱ 150 min

Roti mataba for breakfast at one of the halal stalls near the station: pancake stuffed with spiced beef, onion, and egg, served with yellow curry for dipping. It's Trang's signature morning specialty that everyone orders standing up before heading out.

⏱ 45 min

Koh Lipe

Thailand's southernmost island: crystal reefs, Walking Street, and 30 km from Langkawi.

★★★★★
📍 Walking Street Koh Lipe📍 Sunrise Beach Koh Lipe📍 Pattaya Beach Koh Lipe pier
1

Disembark at Pattaya Beach from the speedboat arriving from Pak Bara. Koh Lipe is Thailand's southernmost island — 30 km from Langkawi, Malaysia. It measures barely 4 km² and can be explored entirely on foot. Cross the Walking Street, the pedestrian artery linking Pattaya Beach to Sunrise Beach in 15 minutes: swimsuit shops, beachfront restaurants, fruit vendors, masseurs. Laid-back vibe, no cars.

⏱ 60 min
2

Settle in on Sunrise Beach (Hat Chao Ley) late afternoon. It's the island regulars' favorite beach — finer sand, calmer water, views of the small islets of Koh Kra and Koh Usen. Opposite, the larger Koh Adang closes the horizon to the north.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Speedboat from Pak Bara Pier to Koh Lipe (1.5 hours). Pak Bara is 90 km south of Trang by road. Arrive at the pier early in the morning — boats generally depart between 9 a.m. and 11:30 a.m. From Koh Lipe, a ferry also connects Langkawi (Malaysia) directly.

⏱ 90 min

Snorkeling from Sunrise Beach: two islets (Koh Kra and Koh Usen) are reachable by kayak in 20 minutes. Corals, clownfish, and giant clams at just a few meters depth. Rent mask and fins directly on the beach.

Koh Lipe

Thailand's southernmost island: crystal reefs, Walking Street, and 30 km from Langkawi.

★★★★★
📍 Sunset Beach Koh Lipe📍 Koh Adang viewpoint Tarutao National Park📍 Koh Lipe Walking Street evening
1

Take the taxi-boat from Sunrise Beach to Koh Adang (10 minutes). The island is jungle-covered and nearly deserted. Three viewpoints succeed each other climbing the trail: the first is reached in 15 minutes, the last — Chado Hill — in 45 to 60 minutes. From the summit, Koh Lipe appears in its entirety, ringed by turquoise reefs. It's the journey's final panorama.

⏱ 150 min
2

Last swim at Sunset Beach, a 15-minute walk west of Walking Street. The beach faces the Malaysian horizon. A few rocks on the south side harbor corals accessible by swimming at high tide.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Kayak around the small islets north of Sunrise Beach: Koh Kra, Koh Usen, Koh Kla. Allow 3 hours to circle them completely. The water is so clear you'll paddle above the corals without having to dive. Rental operators are right on Sunrise Beach.

⏱ 180 min

For the return: speedboat to Pak Bara then road to Hat Yai (domestic airport) or Trang (train station). From Koh Lipe, it's also possible to reach Langkawi in Malaysia directly by ferry to continue the journey south.
