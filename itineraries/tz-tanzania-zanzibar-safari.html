✨ Highlights
  • The Serengeti's Great Migration is a permanent phenomenon—not a seasonal event. Wildebeest and zebras move year-round; the spectacular river crossings occur from mid-July to October.
  • Ngorongoro is the only place on Earth where all the Big Five can be seen in a single day's game drive within a closed 260 km² perimeter.
  • Serengeti → Zanzibar flights (Coastal Aviation, Auric Air, ZanAir) last 2h30 to 3h with stopovers. Baggage limited to 15 kg in soft-sided bags—mandatory.
  • Stone Town has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2000 for its unique fusion of African, Arab, Indian, and European cultures across more than a millennium of maritime trade.
  • Jambiani, on Zanzibar's southeast coast, harbors the island's largest lagoon and remains one of the least touristy beaches—women still harvest seaweed by hand, an activity supporting 15,000 islanders.
📆 Best period

July, August, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 8 and 12 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (June-October): Perfect safari weather with excellent wildlife viewing, minimal rainfall, and comfortable temperatures (15-25°C). Peak season with highest prices but optimal conditions for Serengeti and climbing Kilimanjaro.

Calving Season (January-March): Spectacular time for Serengeti wildlife with wildebeest births and predator action. Excellent photography opportunities, fewer crowds, moderate prices.

Wet Season (November-December, April-May): Heavy rains with challenging road conditions but lush landscapes and lower prices. Bird watching excellent, some lodges closed, authentic experiences.

🌍 What to discover in TZ?

East African paradise with Serengeti wildlife, Mount Kilimanjaro, and pristine beaches. Experience safari adventures, traditional cultures, and incredible natural beauty. The country offers wildlife experiences with cultural authenticity and natural preservation. Tanzania provides life-changing African experiences with natural wonders and cultural richness.

📋 Important notes

This circuit is one of the rare few to chain the four major northern circuit parks in the right geographic order—Tarangire, Manyara, Ngorongoro, Serengeti—without backtracking or zigzagging, before switching directly by air to Zanzibar. Each stop has a strong, irreplaceable identity: the elephants beneath Tarangire's baobabs have no equivalent elsewhere, Manyara's tree-climbing lions exist only here and at Ishasha in Uganda, Ngorongoro is the only place on Earth where all the Big Five fit within a 260 km² caldera, and the Serengeti harbors the planet's largest animal migration. The final flight to Zanzibar is the perfect pivot: from dusty savanna to coral reefs in three hours. Jambiani, less frequented than Nungwi or Kendwa, preserves authentic village life while offering the island's largest lagoon.

⚠️ SAFETY WARNING: Generally safe for tourists but exercise caution due to petty crime in cities, armed robbery in remote areas, and wildlife dangers. Avoid displaying valuables, use reputable safari operators, stay in tourist areas after dark, and be aware that some border regions may have security concerns.

Tarangire holds the highest elephant density in Tanzania—up to 300 individuals gather around the Tarangire River during the dry season (June–October). Lac Manyara

Lions that climb trees in a reserve tucked beneath the Rift escarpment

★★★★★
📍 Lake Manyara National Park Tanzania📍 Lake Manyara tree climbing lions📍 Lake Manyara flamingos📍 Mto wa Mbu village Tanzania📍 Lake Manyara treetop walkway
1

Lake Manyara occupies two-thirds of a 330 km² park nestled at the foot of the Rift escarpment. This is where lions—a behavior unique in East Africa—have developed the habit of climbing fig and acacia trees to escape insects and heat. Spotting them perched 10 meters up, tail dangling, feels like an apparition.

⏱ 240 min
2

The treetop walkway, a 370-meter bridge suspended through the mahogany forest, offers an aerial view into the canopy—blue monkeys, baboon troops, and forest birds parade at eye level.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit Mto wa Mbu village at the park's northern gate: 120 ethnic groups coexist here, a cultural density unique in Tanzania. Neighborhood tours organized by local guides redistribute 80% of proceeds to village families.

⏱ 90 min

Ngorongoro

The world's largest intact volcanic crater and its 25,000 resident animals

★★★★☆
📍 Ngorongoro Crater floor Tanzania📍 Ngorongoro black rhino📍 Ngorongoro crater rim viewpoint📍 Olduvai Gorge Tanzania📍 Ngorongoro hippo pool Magadi
1

Descend the walls of the world's largest intact volcanic crater: 260 km² of floor, 610 meters deep, 25,000 resident animals including all of the Big Five. Predator density—lions, leopards, spotted hyenas, cheetahs—is among the highest in Africa. The black rhinoceros here represents one of Tanzania's best-protected populations.

⏱ 360 min
2

Olduvai Gorge, northwest of the caldera, is the site where Mary Leakey discovered hominid fossils 1.75 million years old in 1959. The small on-site museum traces this story of human evolution in a few understated yet powerful rooms.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Sunrise game drive on the crater floor: lions are active, zebras and wildebeest begin their rounds near Lake Magadi. Arrive when the descent opens—the ascent is charged at fixed hours and vehicles pile up by midday.

Entering the Serengeti for the first time, you instantly grasp what "endless plains" means—the Maasai translation of the name. The Great Migration mobilizes more than 1.5 million wildebeest and 300,000 zebras in a perpetual cycle dictated by the rains.

Stone

★★★★★
📍 Stone Town Zanzibar UNESCO📍 House of Wonders Zanzibar📍 Old Slave Market Stone Town📍 Forodhani Gardens night market📍 Freddie Mercury birthplace Zanzibar
1

A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2000, Stone Town is a living city—not a stage set. Its one-meter-wide alleys wind between coral and mangrove houses dating from the 18th and 19th centuries, punctuated by carved wooden doors with Arab and Indian motifs. The Old Slave Market, with its underground cells, and the Anglican cathedral erected on the exact site of the whipping post are the two essential visits—5 USD, guide included.

⏱ 180 min
2

The House of Wonders (Beit-al-Ajaib), built in 1883 for Sultan Barghash, was the first building in Zanzibar equipped with electricity and an elevator. Part of it partially collapsed in 2020—Omani funds are financing its restoration. Its seafront facade remains imposing.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Forodhani Gardens in the evening: this small waterfront park transforms into a street market at sunset. The 'Zanzibar pizza' (filled crêpe of your choice), octopus skewers, and fresh fruit juices share space with locals. Entry is free, prices minimal.

Jambiani

A Swahili fishing village on Zanzibar's east coast's largest lagoon

★★★★☆
📍 Jambiani Beach Zanzibar📍 Jambiani village seaweed farmers📍 Blue Lagoon Dongwe snorkeling📍 Kuza Cave Jambiani📍 Jambiani dhow sailing
1

Jambiani is a fishing village on Zanzibar's southeast coast, an hour from Stone Town. Its 6 km of white sand beach runs along the island's largest lagoon—at high tide, the turquoise waters invite swimming; at low tide, women in kangas harvest seaweed as they've done for generations, 15,000 islanders depend on it for their livelihood.

⏱ 180 min
2

Kuza Cave, in the Jambiani jungle, is a limestone grotto sheltering an underground freshwater pool fed by a river. The visit includes a local guide, explanations of Swahili history, and the sanctuary's fauna (monkeys, hornbills, bushbabies). Entrance: 25,000 TZS (~10 USD).

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Traditional dhow sailing on the lagoon at sunset—triangular sail, no engine, tidal rhythm. Local fishermen offer 2-hour circuits from Jambiani beach.
