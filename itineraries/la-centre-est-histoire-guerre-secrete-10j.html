Secret War Trail in Laos — 10-Day Plain of Jars and Pathet Lao Caves Journey

📅 11 days📍 10 stops🚗 625 km⏱ 17h30

Immerse yourself in Laos' hidden history through a journey unveiling the secrets of the Secret War, the most heavily bombed conflict in history. From the mysterious Plain of Jars scattered with craters to Vieng Xai's underground caves where the Pathet Lao resisted for years, this historical itinerary combines UNESCO sites, moving testimonies and spectacular landscapes. From Phonsavan to Luang Prabang via Sam Neua and Nong Khiaw, cross remote regions still bearing scars of a tragic past.

✨ Highlights
  • Plain of Jars Sites 1, 2 and 3 with 250+ millennia-old megalithic vessels surrounded by bomb craters witnesses to the most bombed war
  • COPE and MAG centers in Phonsavan with moving exhibits on cluster bomb impact and ongoing clearance work
  • Vieng Xai caves, Pathet Lao underground city where 20,000 people lived from 1964 to 1973 with audio guide and survivor testimonies
  • Spectacular 8-hour Sam Neua-Nong Khiaw road through remote mountains and isolated Tai Dam and Khmu villages
  • UNESCO Luang Prabang to conclude with golden temples, Kuang Si waterfalls and colonial architecture of the ancient royal capital
📆 Best period

November, December, January, February, March

★★★★★
1

Site 1 of the Jars gathers over 250 stone jars arranged on a hill with panoramic views over the plain. The largest jars reach three meters high and several tons in weight.

⏱ 75 min

The origin of these megalithic vessels remains mysterious despite theories about the Iron Age and funerary practices. Archaeologists continue debating their dating between 500 BC and 500 AD.

Bomb craters dot the immediate surroundings of jar sites, testifying to the intensity of bombing. Some jars bear traces of shrapnel and projectiles.

⏱ 75 min

⏱ 75 min

Activities
