Vosges Abbey Trail: Monastic Heritage Unveiled

📅 16 days📍 15 stops🚗 398 km⏱ 7h58

Epinal → Saint Die Des Vosges → Autrey → Etival Clairefontaine → Moyenmoutier → Senones → Etanche → Domremy La Pucelle → Neufchateau → Relanges → Claudon → Les Thons → Val D Ajol → Remiremont

Follow the Monastic Cross of the Vosges, that unique ensemble of five abbeys founded in the 7th century that formed the spiritual heart of medieval Lorraine. From Épinal and its pink sandstone basilica to the canonical residences of Remiremont, passing through the baroque abbey of Moyenmoutier, the Benedictine foundation at Senones and the Romanesque gem at Étival, fifteen stages reveal unexpectedly rich monastic heritage nestled in the hollows of Vosges valleys.

✨ Highlights
  • The Monastic Cross of the Vosges: five abbeys founded in the 7th century (Étival, Moyenmoutier, Senones, Bonmoutier, Saint-Dié) forming a unique geographical cross in Lorraine
  • Senones Abbey and its library of 15,000 volumes, frequented by Voltaire who stayed there for three weeks in 1753
  • Étival Abbey and its first paper mill from 1512, direct ancestor of the Clairefontaine paper mills
  • The Charles Friry Museum in Remiremont, installed in an 18th-century canonical residence and housing a Georges de La Tour painting
📆 Best period

May, June, July, August, September

Day 1

Epinal

Where thousand-year stone meets the art of printed images

★★★☆☆
📍 Basilique Saint-Maurice Épinal📍 Musée de l'Image Épinal📍 Place des Vosges Épinal
1

You'll find the Basilica of Saint Maurice right in the heart of the old town, and it's going to surprise you. Built between the 11th and 13th centuries, it blends Rhenish Romanesque, Champagne Gothic and Burgundian Gothic within a single structure — a rare combination in Lorraine. Look for the Portail des Bourgeois on the north side: its apses are offset at 45° from the church's main axis, a layout so unusual in Romanesque art that only a handful of equivalents exist in France. Inside, the nave rises to 14 meters and the Virgin and Child on the trumeau dates from the 13th century. The basilica only received its title in 1933 — a thousand years of history for one ceremony.

⏱ 75 min
2

If you think images from Épinal are tacky, the Image Museum will change your mind. Over 110,000 printed pieces from the 17th century to today — the largest collection of its kind in Europe — are preserved in this glass building facing the old Pellerin workshops. A tablet is provided at the entrance to navigate through the rooms. In summer, don't miss the Chemin des Images: an urban art trail through the streets of Épinal that connects the museum to the rest of the city's heritage — a great way to end the day on foot.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Head up to Parc du Château in late afternoon — the former medieval fortress of the bishops of Metz towers over the rooftops of Épinal and offers a direct view of the Basilica of Saint Maurice and the Moselle below. Then walk back down into the historic quarter and spot the few remaining sections of 13th-century ramparts still standing near the Tour du Chapitre. The raking evening light on the pink sandstone is particularly beautiful.

Day 2

Epinal

Where thousand-year stone meets the art of printed images

★★★☆☆
📍 Abbaye de Chaumousey📍 Lac de Bouzey Sanchey📍 Étang de Bouzey Chaumousey
1

Prepare yourself for a rather unusual visit: at Chaumousey, 10 km southwest of Épinal, almost nothing remains of the abbey. Founded in 1090 by a priest named Séhère with the support of lord Thierry and his wife Hadevide, it was sold as national property in 1796 and dismantled stone by stone to serve as a quarry. What remains — a section of wall, two statues embedded in house facades, an open vault — is still enough to gauge what was once one of the most powerful Augustinian abbeys in Lorraine. What makes the place endearing: this is where Saint Peter Fourier celebrated his first Mass on June 24, 1589, before becoming the great reformer of the regular canons of Lorraine. And the baroque high altar from the abbey church, saved by parishioners during the Revolution, still stands in the church of Girancourt 5 km away — if you want to see it, make the detour.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Head to Lake Bouzey at Sanchey, a few minutes from Chaumousey. This artificial body of water created to supply the Eastern Canal has a dark history: on April 27, 1895, its dam suddenly gave way and the resulting flood killed about a hundred people in the valley. Panels along the banks tell the story of the disaster. The loop around the lake is about 5 km and can be walked without effort — ideal in late afternoon before returning to Épinal.

Day 3

Saint Die Des Vosges

Pink sandstone cathedral complex spanning seven centuries from Irish monk to Jean Bazaine

★★★★★
📍 Cathédrale Saint-Dié-des-Vosges📍 Cloître gothique Saint-Dié📍 Musée Pierre-Noël Saint-Dié📍 Église Notre-Dame de Galilée Saint-Dié
1

The cathedral complex at Saint-Dié is going to throw you off balance — in a good way. Three pink sandstone buildings connected together: the cathedral, the small church of Notre-Dame de Galilée, and their Gothic cloister. The cathedral dates back to the 7th century, founded by Saint Déodat, an Irish monk who gave his name to the town. Its current walls date from the 12th–13th centuries, but the 18th-century baroque facade (signed by Giovanni Betto) clashes with everything else. The surprising detail: the cathedral was dynamited by the Germans in November 1944, then rebuilt identically with new stained glass windows designed by Jean Bazaine. If you look carefully in the cloister, you'll find the gargoyle nicknamed the Iroquoise — because of its feathers, not because of any link to America. It had been torn off by the 2003 earthquake; it returned to its place in 2017.

⏱ 75 min
2

Don't walk past the cloister without entering — it's one of the largest in eastern France, rebuilt from 1444 thanks to indulgences granted by Pope Eugene IV (Charles VII and Duke René I had put their weight behind it). The galleries blend flamboyant Gothic and early Renaissance accents. On the cloister floor, look for the stylized map of the New World embedded in the pavement: this is where, in 1507, a small printing press published the first map to print the name America.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Pierre-Noël Museum is right next to the cathedral and worth 45 minutes: it preserves the documents and works of the master glaziers who worked on the reconstruction of the building after 1944. A good complement to understanding what you just saw in the cathedral.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

At the end of the day, climb up to the Roche Saint-Martin via the trail at the edge of town. Allow 1 hour round trip. The plunging view over the Meurthe valley and the pink sandstone spires of the cathedral is well worth the effort, especially in late afternoon when the light warms the stone.

Day 4

Autrey

Where Cistercian silence blooms into four thousand botanical species

★★★★☆
📍 Abbaye Notre-Dame Autrey📍 Jardin Remarquable Abbaye Autrey📍 Chapelle Saint-Hubert Autrey
1

Autrey Abbey is the only classified Vosges abbey still inhabited by a religious community — and you can feel it. Founded in 1149 by Cardinal Étienne de Bar, bishop of Metz, upon his return from the Second Crusade, it has weathered the centuries with a chaotic destiny: wire-drawing factory during the Revolution, old people's hospice, war hospital in 1914, total abandonment until 1982. Since then, the Beatitudes Community has undertaken spectacular restoration. If you only see one thing, it should be the Saint-Hubert chapel in the northeast of the transept: its carved coffered ceiling in 16th-century Renaissance style is described as unique in the Vosges. Plan to arrive before 5 pm for the visit.

⏱ 75 min
2

The abbey gardens are labeled Jardin Remarquable and extend over 4 hectares with 3,500 varieties of trees, shrubs, old roses, perennials and medicinal plants. Don't just skim through them — allow at least an hour. Open from April to November, generally from 2 pm to 5 pm except Mondays.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take the hiking trail that follows the Mortagne from Autrey towards Rambervillers (8 km north). Walk out and return by road if you don't want to double back. The alluvial vegetation along the river, the calm of this rarely frequented valley, contrasts pleasantly with the solemnity of the abbey. Prefer the second half of the day — the filtered light under the trees is more beautiful.

⏱ 75 min

Day 5

Etival Clairefontaine

The Premonstratensian abbey that gave birth to France's paper notebooks

★★★★☆
📍 Abbaye Saint-Pierre Étival-Clairefontaine📍 Pierre d'Appel Étival-Clairefontaine📍 Rocher de la Repy Étival-Clairefontaine
1

Saint-Pierre Abbey at Étival is the oldest in the Land of Abbeys — its first monastery dates back to the 7th century. Pass through the 18th-century classical facade (built in 1726 by architect Nicolas Pierson) and prepare for a surprise: behind it lies a Romanesque abbey church from around 1200, with its Romanesque capitals, its Gothic choir remodeled between 1510 and 1516, and its 18th-century choir stalls. It was the Premonstratensian monks installed here in 1147 who founded the first Vosges paper mill in 1512 — the direct ancestor of the Clairefontaine paper mills whose notebooks you used in school. The abbey is fortified and you can still see it in its thick walls. Free self-guided tour every day from 9 am to 6 pm; guided tours on Wednesdays in July-August at 10:30 am and the first Saturday of each month, by reservation at the Tourist Office.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Climb to the Pierre d'Appel, a rock at 492 meters that overlooks the valleys of the Meurthe, Rabodeau, Hure and Valdange. Excavations have revealed a Celtic presence in prehistoric times — the Gauls already used it as a surveillance and alert post. On clear days, you'll see the Vosges ballons on the horizon. Allow 1h30 round trip from the village, on a marked trail.

⏱ 90 min

Day 6

Moyenmoutier

Baroque splendor at the beating heart of the Monastic Cross

★★★★★
📍 Abbaye Saint-Hydulphe Moyenmoutier📍 Oratoire Saint-Grégoire Moyenmoutier📍 Jardins Abbaye Moyenmoutier
1

Welcome to the geographical center of the Monastic Cross of the Vosges. Founded in 671 by Saint Hydulphe, chorepiscopus of Trier who came to live as a hermit in this wooded valley, the abbey took the name Medianum Monasterium — the monastery in the middle — because it stands exactly at the crossroads of the four other abbeys founded at the same time. The current buildings are a baroque masterpiece built in ten years by the Benedictine architect Ambroise Pierson, inaugurated in 1776. Inside, the church has cathedral dimensions but total sobriety — the contrast with the furnishings is striking. Don't miss the choir stalls from 1698: 36 seats whose carved misericords show faces, and the backs are decorated with cloister perspectives painted in trompe-l'œil. The church is open from 10 am to 5 pm.

⏱ 75 min
2

At the old abbey cemetery, the Saint-Grégoire Oratory preserves the 8th-century Vosges pink sandstone sarcophagus believed to have been the first tomb of Saint Hydulphe — founder of the abbey who died in the early 8th century. From the hill, you have a view of the entire abbey complex and the Rabodeau. Allow 20 minutes, it's short but frames the day well.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll through the 4 hectares of gardens surrounding the abbey — for two centuries, industrial buildings had hidden the facade and no one could see them. Their recent clearance has changed everything. Then walk along the Rabodeau to the east: you'll see remnants of the abbey's enclosure wall, an old gate and a corner tower half collapsed. Guided tours are offered on the third Saturday of each month from April to October, by reservation at the Senones Tourist Office.

⏱ 60 min

Day 7

Senones

The tiny principality where Voltaire came seeking Benedictine wisdom

★★★★☆
📍 Abbaye Saint-Pierre Senones📍 Cloître Abbaye Senones📍 Palais abbatial Senones📍 Château Salm Senones
1

Senones Abbey is like an adventure novel. Founded around 640 by Gondelbert, bishop of Sens, it came under the protection of Charlemagne — who appointed his own archichaplain as abbot around 768 — then was slowly captured by the Salm family, which created in 1751 the Principality of Salm-Salm, a small independent state of which Senones became the capital until 1793. You can still see today the abbatial palace and its monumental spiral staircase, decorated with a wrought iron railing signed by Jean Lamour — the same craftsman who created the gates of Place Stanislas in Nancy. Abbot Dom Calmet (died 1757) had assembled here 15,000 volumes, a library so renowned that Voltaire came to stay there for three weeks in 1753, attending all the services and searching through the archives. Guided tours during school holidays and on the 2nd and 4th Saturdays of each month from April to October, by reservation at the Tourist Office.

⏱ 75 min
2

Continue to the two remaining wings of the pink sandstone cloister — they now house shops and the Tourist Office. The entire 18th-century building complex is well preserved: large library to the south, farm buildings, abbatial residence. Opposite the abbey, the castles and mansions of the former princely capital deserve a look — you can still sense the ambition of this tiny independent state.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Senones is labeled Petite Cité de Caractère — stroll through the narrow streets to spot the 18th-century pink sandstone residences quarried from a site at the town gates. A local craft brewery offers tours in season: check with the Tourist Office for time slots. If you like character beers, it's a good way to end the day.

⏱ 60 min

Day 8

Etanche

Cistercian solitude echoing through wooded valley silence

★★☆☆☆
📍 Abbaye Notre-Dame de l'Étanche Rollainville📍 Vomécourt-sur-Madon église Saint-Martin📍 Neufchâteau Vosges
1

The Cistercian abbey of Notre-Dame de l'Étanche is the most discreet foundation on this itinerary. In 1148, Duke of Lorraine Mathieu I had it erected at the request of his mother Adélaïde, widow of Duke Simon I, who wanted to end her days near her children. The monastery's possessions eventually spread across about forty parishes. Of the medieval buildings, nothing remains — the church was destroyed during the Thirty Years' War, the nuns had to flee to Neufchâteau in 1636 taking their deeds and silverware with them. A 1778 convent building with a Mansard roof survives, now private property. You can walk around the outskirts from the outside — the melancholic atmosphere of the wooded valley is in itself a form of visit.

⏱ 45 min
2

On the way to Neufchâteau, stop at Vomécourt-sur-Madon for the Romanesque church of Saint-Martin. Built by the Templars in the 12th century, it overlooks the Madon valley and is considered one of the four most beautiful Romanesque churches in Lorraine. Its portal depicts the holy women at the tomb — the sculptural details are remarkably precise for a rural building. Allow 20 minutes.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk around the Étanche site and in the surrounding forest — the wooded and isolated setting of this valley helps you understand why the Cistercians chose places away from the world to settle. Then head to Neufchâteau for the evening stop, about forty minutes by road.

Day 9

Domremy La Pucelle

The shepherd girl who became France's patron saint began here

★★☆☆☆
📍 Basilique du Bois-Chenu Domrémy-la-Pucelle📍 Maison natale Jeanne d'Arc Domrémy📍 Chapelle Notre-Dame de Bermont Greux
1

The Basilica of Bois-Chenu is not medieval — people often forget that. The first stone was laid on November 3, 1881 on the hillside where Joan, then a shepherdess, is said to have heard her voices. She was not yet canonized at the time, and the building was initially dedicated to Saint Michael. Designed by architect Paul Sédille then completed by the Demay family until 1926, it blends pink Vosges granite and white Euville stone in a striking polychrome. Inside, mosaics cover everything from floor to vault — a decorative program carried out from 1891 to 1934. In the crypt, look for the statue of Notre-Dame de Bermont, the Virgin whom Joan came to pray to every week 3 km from here. The building is illuminated every evening — if you pass at nightfall, it's striking from the valley.

⏱ 60 min
2

500 meters from the basilica, Joan of Arc's birthplace (born around 1412) is one of the most visited monuments in the Vosges. What's remarkable: it survived thanks to a tax exemption granted by the Duchy of Lorraine from the 15th century to preserve the dwelling. Joan spent her entire childhood here before leaving in 1429 to liberate Orléans. An adjoining museum traces the Maid's history with original documents and objects.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb to the Bois-Chenu hillside after visiting the basilica and walk on the heights overlooking the Meuse valley. If you have time, continue to the chapel of Notre-Dame de Bermont in Greux, 3 km north: this is the pilgrimage site that Joan frequented every Saturday from Domrémy, in all weathers. A quiet, almost forgotten place, which changes from the tourist sites of the village.

Day 10

Neufchateau

Where two-story churches and pendant vaults defy architectural convention

★★☆☆☆
📍 Église Saint-Nicolas Neufchâteau📍 Église Saint-Christophe Neufchâteau📍 Hôtel de Houdreville Neufchâteau📍 Place Jeanne d'Arc Neufchâteau
1

The church of Saint-Nicolas is a fascinating architectural puzzle — and that's what makes it so endearing. Founded at the end of the 11th century by Duke of Lorraine Thierry II, it has two superimposed levels: an 11th-century Romanesque crypt below, a 12th–13th-century Gothic upper church above, each with its own stacked chapels. You've never seen this anywhere else in Lorraine — even specialists say so. In the upper church, don't miss the 15th-century polychrome stone Entombment: nine life-size figures of striking expressiveness. Outside July-August, visits are by guided tour only — book at the Tourist Office.

⏱ 60 min
2

The church of Saint-Christophe, first mentioned in 1094, dates in its main structure from the 13th century with a rare Burgundian influence in Lorraine. Don't leave without seeing the Wiriot chapel (1505): it's the only pendant vault chapel in France to have a vault entirely without voûtains — a technical feat that intrigued architects for centuries.

⏱ 45 min
3

The historic center of Neufchâteau is the first Site Patrimonial Remarquable of the Vosges — labeled Petite Cité de Caractère and ranked among the 100 Plus Beaux Détours de France. The Hôtel de Houdreville hides a 16th-century Renaissance staircase and medieval cellars that can be visited. On Place Jeanne d'Arc, look for the Maison des Goncourt, which belonged to the grandfather of writers Jules and Edmond — an unexpected literary connection in this Vosges town.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Push open the door of the old Scala theater, installed in the outbuildings of an Augustinian Ladies convent sold as national property in 1791. Its interior decor in plasterwork and painting dates from renovations in 1891-1893. The Tourist Office offers guided tours of the town throughout the year by reservation — the VIP package includes access to normally closed sites.

Day 11

Relanges

Eleventh-century Cluniac whispers surviving in southern bocage

★★☆☆☆
📍 Église Notre-Dame Relanges📍 Château de Lichecourt Relanges📍 Saint-Baslemont prieuré ruines
1

At Relanges, the church of Notre-Dame is an extremely rare vestige of 11th-century Romanesque art in southern Lorraine. A Cluniac priory was founded here around 1030 by Ricuin de Darney and his wife Lancède — in the pure Cluniac tradition of intercession for the souls of the deceased. The priory disappeared without a trace, but the church's facade gable is one of the rare 11th-century remnants still standing in the region. The nave and side aisles were rebuilt in the 16th century. Don't miss the lavoir d'en-haut in the village, and the Château de Lichecourt if you like discreet ruins at the bottom of meadows.

⏱ 45 min
2

7 km north, the ruins of the priory of Bonneval at Saint-Baslemont deserve a stop for lovers of abandoned sites. Only walls and the outlines of a former church remain, but the chapel of Notre-Dame-des-Neiges (1861) still stands as guardian of the place. In Saint-Baslemont itself, the 16th-century church of Saint-Jean-Baptiste and the remains of the 11th-century Tour Séchelles complete the visit.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

This part of the southern Vosges — bocage, agricultural plateaus, hamlets in blond stone — is one of the least frequented in the entire department. Take the opportunity to simply walk from Relanges on the marked trails around the village. The light in late afternoon over these meadows has something special, especially in May-June. The calm is complete.

⏱ 60 min

Day 12

Claudon

Forest abbey reborn as contemporary art sanctuary

★★☆☆☆
📍 Abbaye Notre-Dame de Droiteval Claudon📍 Forêt de Darney Vosges📍 Châtillon-sur-Saône village Renaissance
1

Droiteval Abbey keeps a low profile deep in the Darney forest. Founded in 1130 by lord Aubert de Darney — who wanted, according to texts, to atone for his faults — it's a Cistercian abbey that became a priory in the 15th century, then national property at the Revolution. A royal forge was installed there, then a scythe factory. Restored in the 19th century, it became private property again in 1987 and has since the 2000s housed an Art and Culture Center. Check opening hours before coming — they vary according to seasons and current exhibitions.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Darney forest surrounds the abbey site and offers forest paths easily walkable on foot. If you have energy, continue to Châtillon-sur-Saône, about twenty kilometers south: it's an exceptionally preserved Renaissance village, with 16th and 17th-century mansions that have barely moved since. Little known, rarely crowded — go in the afternoon.

Day 13

Les Thons

France's finest Franciscan legacy preserved in twin hamlets

★★☆☆☆
📍 Couvent des Cordeliers Les Thons📍 Église Notre-Dame-des-Anges Les Thons📍 Cloître Cordeliers Les Thons
1

The Franciscan convent of Les Thons is a treasure that nobody knows about — and that's exactly what makes it so precious. Founded in 1452 by Guillaume de Saint-Loup and his wife Jeanne de Choiseul with the support of Duke Jean II of Lorraine, it is today the best-preserved Franciscan complex in all of northeastern France. The central part houses a country cuisine restaurant and a museum where you can see a reconstructed friar's cell, a prison arranged by order of King Louis XV, the cloister, the Saint-François fountain and the refectories. The convent church (consecrated in 1483), a masterpiece of late Gothic, is undergoing restoration thanks in part to the Loto du Patrimoine — work is progressing, check accessibility before arriving.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

The convent restaurant serves country cuisine in a large Louis XIII fireplace — if you arrive at lunchtime, it's a stop you won't regret. After the meal, stroll between Petit-Thon and Grand-Thon, the two hamlets that make up the commune. This bocage of the southwestern Vosges, silent and intact, has a rare charm.

Day 14

Val D Ajol

Twenty-five hamlets scattered across valleys where Augustinians once prayed

★★★★☆
📍 Prieuré d'Hérival Val-d'Ajol📍 Belvédère de Mérelle Val-d'Ajol📍 Fayetterie Val-d'Ajol
1

The priory of Hérival takes a bit of effort to reach. Founded in the 12th century by Augustinian canons from Chaumousey, it hides deep in a wooded valley a few kilometers from the town of Val-d'Ajol, accessible by a forest path that follows a stream. It's not a spectacular site — the partially surviving convent buildings are undergoing gradual and discreet rehabilitation — but the atmosphere of a place forgotten by the world is exactly what you're looking for on this itinerary. If access is open, walk along the walls and observe what remains of the 13th-century structures. Allow 45 minutes for the round trip from the road.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Val-d'Ajol is a commune of 25 hamlets scattered across a landscape of valleys and streams — you won't get lost, but you'll linger. Climb to the Belvédère de Mérelle (845 m) for a panoramic view of the Vosges ballons: the winding road from the town is worth the climb, especially in late afternoon when mist settles in the valleys. If textiles interest you, check with the Tourist Office for a possible visit to the Fayetterie, a former hydraulic spinning mill still in operation.

⏱ 90 min

Day 15

Remiremont

Where aristocratic canonesses built an elegant quarter around prayer and learning

★★★☆☆
📍 Abbaye Saint-Pierre Remiremont📍 Musée Charles Friry Remiremont📍 Quartier canonial Remiremont📍 Crypte abbatiale Remiremont
1

Remiremont has a particularity that few French towns can claim: its 18th-century canonical quarter has remained practically intact. The noble canonesses who populated this chapter — reserved for the aristocracy, it wasn't within everyone's reach — lived in these elegant residences around the abbey church of Saint-Pierre. The Romanesque crypt of the abbey is one of the last witnesses to the monastery founded in the 7th century on the neighboring Saint-Mont by the monks Amé and Romaric. Audio guides are available at the Tourist Office to explore the quarter at your own pace.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Charles Friry Museum is going to surprise you. Installed in a canonical residence built in 1750 for the last dean of the chapter, it preserves the collection assembled by Charles Friry, prosecutor to King Louis-Philippe who bought it in 1833 and for fifty years accumulated paintings and art objects there. The centerpiece: The Hurdy-Gurdy Player with a Bag by Georges de La Tour — one of forty known works by this 17th-century Lorraine painter, long obscure before being rediscovered in the 20th century. Added are prints by Rembrandt, Callot and Goya. The garden preserves two 18th-century rocaille fountains classified as Historic Monuments. Open Wednesday to Sunday.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stroll along Rue de la Xavée, lined with porticoed arcades, one of the most picturesque in Lorraine. End on the quays of the Moselle at the end of the day. For a panoramic view of the town and valley, climb to the chapel of Notre-Dame du Calvaire — the climb takes about twenty minutes from the center, and the view over the rooftops and wooded hills is well worth the effort to close this itinerary in style.

⏱ 60 min
