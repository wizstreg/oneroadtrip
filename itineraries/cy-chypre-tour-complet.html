Larnaca

★★★☆☆
1

The scent of incense hits you the moment you step into Agios Lazaros church. Built in the 9th century over the tomb of the saint resurrected by Christ, it preserves a gilded baroque iconostasis from 1773. Descend into the crypt: the atmosphere shifts instantly.

⏱ 45 min
2

Built in the 9th century by Leo VI, Larnaca's medieval castle houses Cyprus's medieval collection. Compact museum, €4.50 entry, decent rooftop views. Combined with the neighboring church, it makes a solid one-hour tandem.

⏱ 30 min
3

Larnaca Salt Lake is worth the detour between November and March: hundreds of flamingos land here. The rest of the year, the lake dries up and turns white with salt. Hala Sultan Tekke mosque on the western shore adds a photogenic frame.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take time to stroll the Finikoudes promenade, lined with palms and cafés. It's the heart of Larnaca, lively in the evenings. Try a Cypriot coffee at one of the seafront bars.

⏱ 30 min

If you dive, the Zenobia wreck rests at 42 meters depth, 1.5 km from the port. This 172-meter Swedish ferry sank in 1980 on its maiden voyage. Several dive centers offer trips for all levels.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 35min

Lefkara

★★★★☆
1

Touristy, yes, but the lacemakers of Pano Lefkara are real. They still embroider on their doorsteps, needle in hand. Lefkaritika lace has been UNESCO Intangible Heritage since 2009. Each tablecloth takes weeks of work.

⏱ 60 min
2

Visit the Embroidery and Silversmithing Museum, free entry, open daily. It traces the village's history from Venetian times. Legend has it Leonardo da Vinci bought a tablecloth here in 1481 for the Milan Duomo altar.

⏱ 30 min
3

Limestone alleyways creak underfoot between houses with blue and green shutters. The village sits at 600 meters altitude, the air stays cool even in summer. Climb to Timios Stavros church for valley views.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Lefkara silverwork deserves as much attention as the lace. Filigree workshops sell handmade jewelry at prices well below coastal boutiques. Ask to see the work in progress.

⏱ 30 min

Locals call their village 'the white one' because of the surrounding limestone. Lunch at House 1923 tavern for local chicken souvlaki, a reliable choice.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 15min

Choirokoitia

★★★★☆
1

Inhabited since 7000 BC, the Neolithic site of Choirokoitia has been UNESCO-listed since 1998. The round stone-and-mud huts have been identically reconstructed at the bottom of the site. The originals sit at the top of the hill, by the Maroni river. Allow 1h30, no shade.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Allow an hour for the marked archaeological trail. Inhabitants rarely lived beyond 35 and buried their dead beneath their house floors. Combined with the nearby Agios Minas monastery, it makes a full morning.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 40min

Limassol

★★★☆☆
1

Limassol Castle is compact but history-laden: Richard the Lionheart supposedly married Berengaria of Navarre here in 1191. The medieval museum inside displays weapons, armor, and coins. €4.50 entry, climb to the roof for the view.

⏱ 45 min
2

The old town smells of leather and spices in the morning when the municipal market opens. Alleyways around the castle mix trendy cafés and old shops. Spot remnants of ancient columns set right into the sidewalk.

⏱ 45 min
3

Climb to the castle roof for a 360° panorama over Limassol and the sea. The city is Cyprus's most dynamic, with its modern marina a five-minute walk away. Guaranteed contrast between medieval and contemporary.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

If you enjoy wine, taste Commandaria, the world's oldest named wine, produced without interruption since Crusader times. Several cellars offer tastings in the Kolossi region, 15 minutes away.

⏱ 60 min

The Molos promenade runs along the seafront for 1 km, dotted with contemporary sculptures and water features. Perfect late afternoon for a walk before dinner. Fish restaurants cluster around the old port.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 15min

Kolossi

★★★★☆
1

A squat keep amid sugarcane fields: that's Kolossi. This 1454 Knights Hospitaller castle served as both fortress and sugar production center. Louis de Magnac's coat of arms is carved on the façade. Three floors, clear views from the top.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Taste Commandaria at a neighboring wine estate. This amber dessert wine takes its name from the Hospitaller commandery established here. Richard the Lionheart supposedly called it 'the king of wines'. True or not, it's worth tasting.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 8min

Kourion

★★★★★
1

Kourion's Greco-Roman theatre overlooks the cliff and the Mediterranean. Built in the 2nd century BC and expanded under the Romans, it held 3,500 spectators. In summer, concerts still take place here. The acoustics are stunning.

⏱ 30 min
2

The mosaics of the House of Eustolios are worth the trip alone. Geometric and animal motifs cover the floors of this 5th-century villa converted into public baths. Explanatory panels are clear and well-placed.

⏱ 30 min
3

The House of the Gladiators takes its name from its mosaics depicting combats. Less visited than the theatre, it offers a quiet moment amid the site. An earthquake in 365 AD destroyed the entire city.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Arrive early morning: the site is in full sun, no shade, and tour groups arrive around 10 am. Allow two solid hours to cover theatre, villas, and agora. Kourion Beach below allows a swim break after the visit.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 8min

Sanctuary Apollo Hylates

★★★★☆
1

Cypriots called him Hylates, protector of the woods. This sanctuary of Apollo, active from the 8th century BC to the 4th century AD, retains its restored columns, palaestra, and Roman baths. €2.50 entry. The site is 2.5 km from Kourion, often combined.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If coming from Kourion, the drive takes 5 minutes. Allow 30 to 45 minutes on site. The circular monument at the center was dedicated to processions around sacred trees. Good complement to Kourion in the same half-day.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 25min

Petra Tou Romiou

★★★★☆
1

White foam crashing on Aphrodite's Rock: this is where, according to legend, the goddess was born from the sea. The name 'Petra tou Romiou' actually comes from Byzantine hero Digenis, who supposedly hurled this rock at Saracen pirates. Swimming discouraged, rough sea.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Park on the upper road and descend via the underpass. Sunset is the best time, but also the busiest. Early morning, you'll have the pebble beach almost to yourself.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 5min

Kouklia

★★★★☆
1

The Sanctuary of Aphrodite at Palaepaphos dates back to the 12th century BC, making it one of the oldest religious centers in the Mediterranean. UNESCO-listed in 1980. Only megalithic walls remain, but the aura of the place is palpable. The goddess was worshipped here as a conical stone, not a statue.

⏱ 45 min
2

The site museum preserves the famous black conical stone, the cult object described by ancient authors. Small museum, half an hour suffices, but the exhibited pieces are first-rate. The combined ticket with Paphos Archaeological Park is good value.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Combine the visit with Petra tou Romiou, 5 minutes by car. The two sites form the 'Aphrodite route': mythical birthplace and historical cult site. Allow a morning for both.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 20min

Paphos

★★★★★
1

The colors of the House of Dionysus mosaics leap out after two millennia. Over 500 m² of mythological floors: hunting scenes, grape harvests, the wine god. Discovered by chance in 1962 by a farmer plowing his field.

⏱ 45 min
2

The House of Dionysus covers over 2,000 m², including 556 m² of mosaics. Right next door, the House of Aion holds a 4th-century panel considered one of the finest in late Roman art. Five mythological scenes in a single floor.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Tombs of the Kings impress more through their architecture than their contents. Carved into rock in the 3rd century BC, these underground peristyle tombs imitate the houses of the living. No kings were buried here, despite the name.

⏱ 45 min
4

Paphos Castle, tiny though it is, offers the best view over the harbor. Built by the Byzantines, dismantled by the Venetians, rebuilt by the Ottomans. In summer, performances take place here. Quick entry, 10 minutes suffices inside.

⏱ 20 min
5

Push on to Chrysopolitissa basilica and St. Paul's pillar. According to tradition, Paul was flogged here before converting the Roman governor. The 4th-century basilica was the largest in Cyprus. Free entry.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

If visiting in summer, the mosaics are covered by protective structures that create some shade. Allow 2 to 3 hours for the complete archaeological park. €4.50 entry, or €8.50 for the all-sites day pass.

⏱ 150 min

Harbor fishermen recommend Hondros restaurant for grilled fish. Paphos harbor takes 20 minutes to walk, but you linger longer for the atmosphere. Colorful fishing boats set the scene.

⏱ 60 min

Sunset from Paphos fort is one of the island's finest. Arrive 30 minutes early to get a spot on the rocks. Golden light on the archaeological park columns in the background is photo-worthy.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 50min

Avakas Gorge

★★★★☆
1

Limestone walls narrow to two meters wide and rise over 30 meters high. Avakas Gorge, carved by the Avgas river on the Akamas peninsula, shelters centaurea akamatis, a plant endemic to Cyprus. Cool atmosphere even in midsummer.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Bring waterproof hiking shoes: the trail crosses the riverbed several times. The full loop is 7 km for 2h30 walking. Don't go after heavy rain, water levels rise fast. No phone signal in the gorge.

⏱ 150 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Polis Chrysochous

★★★☆☆
1

The Baths of Aphrodite disappoint slightly in size: a grotto shaded by a century-old fig tree, with a trickle of water. But the place is legend-laden. According to mythology, Aphrodite met Adonis here. Access is free, 5 minutes' walk from the parking.

⏱ 20 min
2

The Marion archaeological museum displays pottery, sculptures, and tools from the ancient city that preceded Polis. Small free museum, half an hour suffices. The village itself is quiet, far from coastal bustle.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Lunch at Latchi harbor, where tavernas serve fish caught that morning. The port is 5 minutes from Polis. Prices are reasonable and the atmosphere relaxed, far from southern tourist zones.

⏱ 60 min

If you hike, the Aphrodite trail starts from the Baths and makes a 7.5 km loop with panoramic views over Chrysochou Bay. Allow 3 hours, bring water and hat. The Adonis trail, shorter, offers similar views.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 1h15

Kykkos Monastery

★★★★☆
1

Cyprus's wealthiest monastery looks nothing like a place of asceticism. Golden mosaics on the outer walls, flowered inner courtyards, a museum brimming with reliquaries. Founded around 1100 by Emperor Alexios I Komnenos, it houses an icon of the Virgin attributed to St. Luke, never unveiled.

⏱ 60 min
2

Climb to Throni, the tomb of Archbishop Makarios III, first president of Cyprus. The site offers clear views over the Troodos mountains. The ascent takes 10 minutes by car from the monastery. Makarios began his ecclesiastical career here as a monk in 1926.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The pine-scented air at 1,318 meters altitude is a radical change from the coastal atmosphere. The monastery is open daily, free entry. The museum costs €5. Covered shoulders and knees required, but the rule is flexibly enforced.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 35min

Kakopetria

★★★☆☆
1

Agios Nikolaos tis Stegis church dates from the 11th century and is one of ten painted Troodos churches UNESCO-listed since 1985. Its double wooden roof hides a Byzantine dome, and its frescoes span seven centuries. Closed Mondays, photos forbidden inside.

⏱ 30 min
2

The old village of Kakopetria, with its stone houses and tile roofs, is one of the best-preserved in Troodos. Set at the confluence of two rivers at 667 meters altitude, it maintains pleasant coolness. The stone bridge at the center is the village landmark.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Cross the stone bridge toward the old part of the village for a half-hour stroll. Restored houses shelter guesthouses and discreet cafés. A good spot to sleep cool after coastal days.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h05

Omodos

★★★★☆
1

The scent of fermenting grapes floats through Omodos's alleyways in autumn. This Troodos wine village is built around Timios Stavros monastery, whose cobbled square serves as a gathering place. Shops sell zivania, the local firewater clocking in around 45°.

⏱ 30 min
2

Timios Stavros monastery houses a fragment of the True Cross and a rope attributed to Christ's flagellation, according to local tradition. The church is open and free. The restored wine press in the courtyard shows ancient techniques.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Taste local zivania in one of the village cellars. Less known than Commandaria, this marc spirit is drunk ice-cold as a digestif. Omodos winemakers also produce Maratheftiko, a red grape native to Cyprus.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 12 km · 15min

Platres

★★★☆☆
1

A seaside resort without the sea, Platres attracted colonial British for summer holidays. The village remains a convenient base for Troodos hiking. The altitude (1,100 m) guarantees cool nights and omnipresent pine forests.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you only have an hour, Caledonia waterfall is reached by a 3 km trail through the forest. Pleasant and shaded, even in summer. The waterfall stands about 12 meters high.

⏱ 90 min

Push on to Millomeris waterfall, Cyprus's highest at 15 meters. The trail is short (1 km) but steep in places. Water flows year-round, even in summer. Good shoes recommended.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 55min

Nicosia

★★★★☆
1

Nicosia is the only capital still divided in two in the world. The Green Line has crossed the old town since 1974, separating the Greek and Turkish quarters. This isn't Berlin: here, you cross on foot, passport in hand, in 30 seconds.

⏱ 30 min
2

Büyük Han smells of roasted Turkish coffee and worked leather. This 1572 Ottoman caravanserai is the best-preserved on the island. Two floors of arcades around an inner courtyard with a tiny mosque at the center. Today, artisan workshops and galleries.

⏱ 45 min
3

Selimiye Mosque was Gothic St. Sophia Cathedral before the 1570 Ottoman conquest. Two minarets flank a rose-windowed façade with flying buttresses. The interior, whitewashed, contrasts with the medieval exterior.

⏱ 30 min
4

Cross the Ledra Street checkpoint to pass to the north side. The transition is immediate: from trendy boutiques to Bandabulya covered bazaar stalls. The crossing is free and takes seconds. Keep your passport or ID card.

⏱ 30 min
5

Nicosians say you don't understand Cyprus without seeing both sides. On the south side, the Cyprus Museum displays the island's most important archaeological collection, including Bronze Age figurines. €4.50 entry.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you cross to the north side, prices in Turkish lira are much lower. A full meal (kebab, pide, baklava) costs half as much as in the south. The Arabahmet quarter, with its overhanging Ottoman houses, is the most photogenic.

⏱ 60 min

Walk along the eleven-pointed star-shaped Venetian ramparts. Built in 1567, they didn't prevent the Ottoman conquest three years later. Today, they delimit the old town and offer a 5 km walk.

⏱ 45 min

The Cyprus Museum, south side, deserves an hour despite its dated scenography. The masterpieces are the Agia Irini terracotta statues and the Aphrodite of Soloi statue. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Kyrenia

★★★★☆
1

Kyrenia's horseshoe harbor is Cyprus's most photogenic. Colorful fishing boats, terrace cafés, Byzantine castle at the east entrance. The whole thing fits in 200 meters of quay. The place is lively in the evening, calm in the morning.

⏱ 30 min
2

Kyrenia Castle houses a 4th-century BC merchant shipwreck recovered in 1969, one of the oldest in the world. The shipwreck museum is the centerpiece. The castle itself mixes Byzantine, Venetian, and Ottoman layers.

⏱ 60 min
3

The old harbor is Northern Cyprus's most charming, and it knows it. Terraces are slightly pricier than elsewhere, but the setting justifies a Turkish coffee facing the boats. Medieval alleys behind the harbor hide artisan shops.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Settle into a harbor café for Turkish coffee and a piece of baklava. The pace here is slow, embrace it. Prices are in Turkish lira, much gentler than in the south.

⏱ 30 min

If you dive, the coastline around Kyrenia offers spots accessible from shore. Local dive centers offer introductory dives at modest prices.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min

Bellapais

★★★☆☆
1

Bellapais Abbey's Gothic ogives frame the sea below. Built by Augustinian monks from 1198, it preserves an intact refectory with impressive vaults. Lawrence Durrell lived here and made it the setting of 'Bitter Lemons' in 1957.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Explore Bellapais village, where café terraces overlook the north coast. The atmosphere is calm, houses well-maintained. A good spot to lunch with a view before continuing to St. Hilarion.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 15min

Saint Hilarion Castle

★★★★☆
1

St. Hilarion Castle supposedly inspired Disney's Sleeping Beauty castle. Perched on a rocky pinnacle of the Kyrenia mountains, it unfolds across three levels: garrison, Lusignan royal quarters, and Prince John's tower at the summit. The view from the top covers the north coast and, on clear days, Turkey.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb all the way to Prince John's tower at the top. The ascent is steep and uneven, but the panoramic view rewards the effort. Allow 1h to 1h30 for the entire castle. Toilets are up an irregular staircase.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 75 km · 1h10

Salamis

★★★★★
1

The columns of Salamis gymnasium stand facing the sea, mismatched. The Byzantines recycled Greek and Roman shafts without concern for harmony after the 331 earthquake. The result is strangely beautiful. The site extends over 1 km², most still under sand.

⏱ 45 min
2

Salamis's ancient theatre, with its 50 rows and 15,000 seats, is Cyprus's largest. Built under Augustus, it has been restored and still serves for summer performances. The view over the mimosa forest and the sea is a bonus.

⏱ 30 min
3

Founded according to legend by Teucer after the Trojan War, Salamis was capital of Cyprus in the 11th century BC. The Roman baths, with their hot, warm, and cold rooms still identifiable, show the city's refinement at its peak.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Continue on foot to St. Barnabas monastery, 10 minutes by car. It houses an icon museum and Salamis archaeological remains displayed in a former press. The site is quiet and photogenic.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min

Famagusta

★★★★☆
1

Famagusta fascinates as much as it saddens: a dense medieval city, surrounded by massive Venetian ramparts, turning its back on Varosha, the ghost quarter abandoned since 1974. You can't visit Varosha, but you see it from an official viewpoint.

⏱ 30 min
2

Lala Mustafa Pasha Mosque was Gothic St. Nicholas Cathedral, coronation site of Jerusalem kings in the 14th century. The rose window and flying buttresses are intact, minarets added. The interior is stripped but the façade is striking.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Venetian walls, several meters thick, encircle the old town in a star shape. Designed by Sanmicheli, they resisted an 11-month Ottoman siege in 1570-71. You can walk certain sections to overlook the city.

⏱ 30 min
4

Othello's Tower is worth the detour for its Shakespeare connection more than its contents. This Crusader citadel guarded the harbor. Local legend links it to the Moor of Venice. Harbor view from the ramparts.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

From the official viewpoint, you glimpse Varosha's empty buildings, frozen since 1974. Hotels, shops, cars sometimes still parked. The place is heavy, even without entering. Respect photography bans in certain zones.

⏱ 30 min

If you have time, push on to Petek pastry shop in the old town. Baklavas are homemade. Noah's Bazaar covered market sells spices, textiles, and silverware under Ottoman vaults.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 85 km · 1h15

Cape Greco

★★★★☆
1

Cape Greco's turquoise water is surreally clear: 20 to 30 meters visibility underwater. This forest national park, integrated into the Natura 2000 network, occupies the island's southeastern tip. Limestone cliffs plunge into the sea, hollowed with caves accessible on foot or by kayak.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Love Bridge, a natural limestone arch above the sea, has become Ayia Napa's most sought-after photo spot. Less romantic in mid-afternoon with crowds, much better at sunrise. Free access, 5 minutes' walk from parking.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Descend to Konnos Bay after visiting the cape. This small cove protected by hills offers calm, turquoise water, much less crowded than Nissi Beach. Ideal for an end-of-road-trip swim.

⏱ 60 min

If you dive, visibility reaches 30 meters around the cape. Loggerhead turtles regularly spotted, especially early morning. Several dive centers in Ayia Napa offer trips to the sea caves.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 80 km · 1h05

Nicosia

★★★★☆
1

Take advantage of this return to Nicosia to explore the Chrysaliniotissa quarter, the city's oldest. Restored houses, small art galleries, and ceramics workshops nestle between the old town and the Green Line. Calm and authentic.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Cypriots always finish with meze. Settle into an old Nicosia tavern for an end-of-trip meal: grilled halloumi, kleftiko, honey loukoumades. Allow €15-20 per person for a full meze.

⏱ 60 min
