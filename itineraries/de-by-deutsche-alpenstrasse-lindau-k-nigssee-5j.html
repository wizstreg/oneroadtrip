German Alpine Road: Lake Constance to Königssee

📅 6 days📍 5 stops🚗 370 km⏱ 6h55

Fussen → Garmisch Partenkirchen → Prien Am Chiemsee → Schoenau Am Koenigssee

Medieval Lindau Island on the Bodensee, the 106 hairpin bends of Oberjochpass, Neuschwanstein perched on its rocky spur, the Zugspitze and its glaciers, Herrenchiemsee Palace - grander than Versailles - and the Königssee trapped between limestone walls. Five days on the Deutsche Alpenstraße, Germany's oldest scenic road.

✨ Highlights
  • Lindau Island on the Bodensee with its twin lighthouses
  • Neuschwanstein and Hohenschwangau - book tickets online several weeks in advance
  • The Zugspitze by cogwheel train and cable car from Garmisch
  • Electric boat cruise on the Königssee to St. Bartholomä and the Obersee
  • Herrenchiemsee Palace on Herreninsel Island, accessible only by boat

Day 1

Fussen

Gateway to Ludwig's fairy-tale castles at the foot of the Bavarian Alps

★★★★★
📍 Lindau Hafen Leuchtturm Bodensee📍 Mangturm Lindau📍 Maximilianstrasse Lindau📍 Altes Rathaus Lindau📍 Oberjochpass Bad Hindelang📍 Kanzel Aussichtspunkt Oberjoch
1

Begin your journey on Lindau Island, perched on Lake Constance (Bodensee). The old town occupies a 70-hectare island that's been linked to the mainland by a railway bridge since 1853. Two guardians mark the harbor entrance: the Mangturm, a 37-meter medieval lighthouse from the 13th century that guided boats across the lake since 1230, and the newer white lighthouse built between 1853 and 1856 - Bavaria's only lighthouse. Standing watch beside them is the 6-meter Bavarian lion carved in sandstone by sculptor Johann von Halbig and unveiled in 1856.

⏱ 75 min
2

Stroll down Maximilianstrasse, the old town's main artery lined with burgher houses spanning Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque styles - each distinct yet harmoniously aligned. On Reichsplatz stands the 15th-century Altes Rathaus with its painted fresco facade. Lindau was a Free Imperial City from 1275 to 1805, and this town hall hosted several Imperial Diets of the Holy Roman Empire.

⏱ 45 min
3

Take the Deutsche Alpenstraße east - Germany's oldest scenic road, inaugurated in 1933, linking Lindau to Berchtesgaden across 450 km along the Alps. Your first dramatic stop is Bad Hindelang and the Oberjochpass: 106 hairpin bends climbing 300 vertical meters over 5 km, including 9 dramatic switchbacks that spiral above the village. Built in 1895, the Jochstraße was already an engineering marvel - back in 1662, 15,850 barrels of salt crossed this pass annually on the backs of 300 horses.

⏱ 60 min
4

Halfway up the Oberjochpass, pull over at the Kanzel viewpoint for sweeping panoramas over the Ostrach valley, Bad Hindelang and Bad Oberdorf below, with the Nagelfluhkette ridge and the 2,224-meter Nebelhorn rising behind. It's one of the finest drive-to viewpoints in the Bavarian Alps.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take a ferry from Lindau harbor across the Bodensee toward Bregenz (Austria) or Friedrichshafen. From the water, the island town reveals its true character - colorful facades, twin lighthouses, and the full sweep of the harbor entrance framed by Alpine peaks in the distance.

⏱ 60 min

Walk freely at the Aussichtspunkt Kanzel on the Oberjochpass, where a short trail from the parking area reaches the viewpoint in 2 minutes. On clear days, the panorama extends to the Austrian mountain chains.

⏱ 30 min

Day 2

Garmisch Partenkirchen

Alpine resort beneath Germany's highest peak, the 2,962-meter Zugspitze

★★★★★
📍 Schloss Neuschwanstein Schwangau📍 Marienbrücke Neuschwanstein📍 Schloss Hohenschwangau📍 Alpsee Hohenschwangau📍 Lechfall Füssen📍 Hohes Schloss Füssen
1

Neuschwanstein Castle rises on a rocky outcrop 200 meters above Hohenschwangau village. Ludwig II of Bavaria laid its first stone on September 5, 1869 as a "worthy temple" dedicated to his friend Richard Wagner - the interior frescoes illustrate the composer's operas directly. The king spent only 172 nights here before being declared "insane" by the government in June 1886, then found dead in Lake Starnberg three days later. The castle opened to the public seven weeks after his death and now welcomes over a million visitors annually. Book your guided tour slot well in advance.

⏱ 120 min
2

Climb to Marienbrücke, a bridge spanning the Pöllat gorge, for the soaring bird's-eye view of Neuschwanstein - this is the angle that inspired Walt Disney's Sleeping Beauty Castle at Disneyland in 1955, and later the studio logo itself. The bridge closes in winter due to ice.

⏱ 30 min
3

Descend to Hohenschwangau Castle, Ludwig II's childhood residence, restored in neo-Gothic style by his father Maximilian II from 1833. The murals depict medieval legends of the Swan Knight and the Nibelungen - a backdrop that shaped the future king's imagination. Available as a combined ticket with Neuschwanstein.

⏱ 60 min
4

Late afternoon, head to Füssen to see the Hohes Schloss, the former summer residence of the prince-bishops of Augsburg dominating the old town. Its exterior facade is adorned with trompe-l'oeil paintings imitating Gothic windows and carved balconies - an exceptional illusionist display. The castle now houses a Bavarian State Collections gallery.

⏱ 45 min
5

Continue to the Lechfall, an artificial cascade built in the 18th century to prevent Füssen from flooding. The Lech River, with the turquoise waters characteristic of Alpine limestone torrents, plunges 12 meters in several tiers through a narrow gorge. From the König-Max-Steg bridge (1895), named after King Maximilian II, the view over the falls is striking.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk around the Alpsee, the lake directly below both castles. A flat trail follows the shore for about 4 km. Ludwig II himself walked here regularly. In summer, rowboats are available for rent.

⏱ 60 min

Day 3

Prien Am Chiemsee

Harbor town where ferries depart for Herrenchiemsee's island palace

★★★★★
📍 Partnachklamm Garmisch-Partenkirchen📍 Eibsee Grainau📍 Zugspitze Talstation Garmisch-Partenkirchen📍 Ludwigstrasse Partenkirchen📍 Alpspix Garmisch-Partenkirchen
1

Start your morning at the Partnachklamm, a 700-meter gorge carved up to 80 meters deep by the Partnach River through limestone cliffs south of Garmisch. A path hewn into the rock follows the glacial torrent - the water, flowing from Zugspitze glaciers, maintains an intense blue-green tint. Designated a natural monument in 1912, the gorge is a 25-minute walk from the Olympic stadium.

⏱ 90 min
2

Continue to Lake Eibsee, its turquoise waters framed by limestone rocks and pine forests at the foot of the Zugspitze. From its shores, the cable car inaugurated in 2017 launches toward the summit holding three world records: tallest pylon tower (127 m), greatest elevation gain (1,945 m), and longest unsupported span in the world.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Ascend the Zugspitze, Germany's highest peak at 2,962 meters on the Austrian border. The 90-year-old cogwheel train climbs from Garmisch to the glacier plateau at 2,600 meters, where a second cable car reaches the summit. On clear days, the panorama extends to the Dolomites and the Swiss Alps. Allow half a day and book ahead in season.

⏱ 180 min

Day 4

Schoenau Am Koenigssee

Where electric boats glide across emerald waters trapped between vertical cliffs

★★★★☆
📍 Schloss Herrenchiemsee Herreninsel📍 Kloster Frauenwörth Frauenchiemsee📍 Chiemsee Schifffahrt Prien Stock📍 Herreninsel Chiemsee📍 Fraueninsel Chiemsee
1

Board a ferry from Prien-Stock harbor to reach Herreninsel Island on Chiemsee, Bavaria's largest lake. The crossing takes about 20 minutes. In 1873, Ludwig II purchased the entire island for 350,000 florins from an industrialist who planned to clear-cut all its forests. Construction of the palace began in 1878 under architect Georg von Dollmann.

⏱ 30 min
2

Tour Herrenchiemsee Palace, Ludwig II's final and most lavish project - and where he spent the least time: just 16 days total. The king spent more money on this single construction than on Neuschwanstein and Linderhof combined. At his death in 1886, 50 rooms remained unfinished. The Great Hall of Mirrors, inspired by Versailles, measures 98 meters - 25 meters longer than its French model. To illuminate it, 70 servants had to light 33 candelabras and 52 chandeliers.

⏱ 90 min
3

After the palace, take the boat to Fraueninsel (Women's Island), where the Frauenwörth Benedictine convent has existed for 1,200 years - one of Germany's oldest monastic communities still in operation. Walk around the small island (one hour on foot) among fishermen's houses and monastery gardens. The nuns produce a renowned local brandy.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Late afternoon from Schönau am Königssee, stroll the Chiemsee shores (Priener Uferweg) or follow the lakeside paths from Prien. Sunset with the Alps as backdrop.

⏱ 60 min

Day 5

Schoenau Am Koenigssee

Where electric boats glide across emerald waters trapped between vertical cliffs

★★★★☆
📍 Königssee Schifffahrt Schönau📍 St. Bartholomä Königssee📍 Obersee Salet Königssee📍 Jennerbahn Schönau am Königssee📍 Malerwinkel Königssee
1

Board one of the electric boats on Königssee from Schönau - the only craft allowed on this protected lake since 1978 within Berchtesgaden National Park. The lake, 8 km long and 190 meters deep, lies trapped between vertical limestone walls. Midway across, the pilot plays trumpet against the rock face to demonstrate the extraordinary echo.

⏱ 60 min
2

Disembark at St. Bartholomä, a peninsula accessible only by boat. The baroque pilgrimage church, first built in 1134 and remodeled in the early 18th century, is recognizable from afar by its red onion domes. The ensemble of church and former royal hunting lodge, silhouetted against the 1,800-meter vertical east face of the Watzmann, is one of Germany's most photographed scenes.

⏱ 60 min
3

Continue by boat to Salet to walk to the Obersee - a flat 15-minute walk from the dock. This smaller lake, with no boats whatsoever, displays absolutely transparent water and perfect reflections of the surrounding rock walls. Since commercial fishing ended in 1978, the lake has returned to wilderness.

⏱ 60 min
4

Ride the Jennerbahn to reach the Jenner summit at 1,800 meters. The view embraces the entire Königssee, the east face of the Watzmann, and the Berchtesgaden Alps on the Austrian side. Salzburg is 30 km away - this is where the circuit naturally ends for those returning rental cars in Austria.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hike the Malerwinkel-Rundweg, a panoramic forest trail above the Königssee offering the classic elevated view of the lake. In 2015, this spot ranked as Germany's 12th most popular tourist attraction. Allow 1 to 2 hours depending on your pace.

⏱ 90 min
