Hradec Kralove

1

The Drawing Room of the Republic: that's what Czechs called Hradec Králové in the early 20th century, when Josef Gočár and Jan Kotěra transformed this medieval town into a laboratory of modernist architecture. The historic center, including the Great Square, is a protected national monument reserve. Arrive on a weekday to avoid the school groups that swarm the city on weekends.

⏱ 60 min
2

White and slender, the Bílá věž tower dominates the Great Square from its 72-meter height. Built in the 16th century, it hides an illuminated glass model at its base that changes color. The 226 steps lead to a panorama over the Elbe and the Bohemian hills—count on 20 minutes for the climb.

⏱ 45 min
3

Gothic from the 14th century and never baroquized, the Cathedral of the Holy Spirit is one of the few in Eastern Bohemia to have retained its medieval austerity. Inside, look for Petr Brandl's altarpiece and the pewter baptismal font. Free entry; a guided tour grants access to the bell tower.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Walk north along the Elbe from the Great Square and you'll stumble upon the Hučák power station, an Art Nouveau watermill converted into a renewable energy exhibition center. Open March to October, entry around 80 CZK.

⏱ 45 min

If you arrive on Saturday morning, the Great Square market sets up at 8am—cheeses, charcuterie, rye bread. The bike path along the Elbe heading south reaches Hradec Králové's urban forests in 45 minutes.

⏱ 60 min

Kuks

1

A baroque hospital on the banks of the Elbe, in the middle of Bohemian countryside: incongruous, until you understand that Count Sporck wanted to make Kuks his personal Versailles. Built between 1707 and 1715, the hospital houses a three-hundred-year-old baroque pharmacy—with drawers labeled wolf liver, dragon's blood, and hanged man's powder. Guided tours only; English is available by email reservation.

⏱ 90 min
2

On the hospital facade, twelve statues of Virtues and twelve of Vices, copies of the originals by Matthias Bernard Braun, known as the Czech Michelangelo. The originals are in the indoor lapidarium. In the forest 4 km away, the Braun Bethlehem: sculptures carved into living rock, of which only ten of the original twenty-six remain.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Set out on foot on the 7 km loop to the Braun Bethlehem: the trail follows the Elbe then climbs into the beech forest. Hiking boots essential—the path is slippery after rain. Count on 2h30 round trip from the hospital.

⏱ 150 min

Kuks

1

The Braun Bethlehem is a baroque nativity scene carved into sandstone rocks in the heart of the forest, commissioned by Count Sporck from Matthias Bernard Braun around 1726. The original program included twenty-six sculptures and reliefs; ten survive today, classified as a national cultural monument.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Adoration of the Shepherds and the Arrival of the Magi emerge from the rock as if the forest had always borne these faces. The effect comes from the setting as much as the sculptures: an ordinary undergrowth, no sign announcing what you're about to find, then suddenly the bas-reliefs in the stone.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

From the Kuks bridge, follow the green trail markers: the complete loop is 7 km, 2h30 of walking. Hiking boots required—the path is slippery after rain. Information panels in Czech on each sculpture.

⏱ 150 min

Dvur Kralove Nad Labem

1

The only place in the world where the northern white rhino has bred in captivity, Dvůr Králové Safari Park concentrates Europe's largest collection of African animals—buffalo, giraffes, zebras in semi-freedom—across 72 hectares. Its Lion Safari, where visitors drive among lions without fences, is the only one of its kind in Central Europe.

⏱ 180 min
2

A safari in Central Europe: the idea seems absurd until an elephant grazes your window. The Zdeněk Burian gallery, featuring the Czech prehistory illustrator, hides within the park grounds—unexpected and quite beautiful.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Choose the Africa Truck over the Safaribus: the open flatbed truck lasts 60 minutes versus 25, and enters the Lion Safari. The Safaribus doesn't have access. Reserve your ride at the ticket office upon entry—spots go quickly in high season.

⏱ 60 min

Vrchlabi

1

The air changes here. The Elbe is now just a torrent and the first peaks of the Krkonoše close the northern horizon. Vrchlabí presents itself as the gateway to the Giant Mountains: a quiet small town with a Great Square and a Renaissance château you can walk around in twenty minutes.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Krkonoše Museum, reopened in 2023 after a lengthy renovation, is the best reason to stop in Vrchlabí. Housed in a former Augustinian convent, it traces the geological and human history of the Krkonoše with polished multimedia installations. The ticket is valid for two days at all national park museums.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Spend twenty minutes in the park of Vrchlabí château, headquarters of the KRNAP national park administration: the courtyard is freely accessible, even though the interior isn't open for visits. The old linden trees and the parapet overlooking the river make it a pleasant stop.

⏱ 20 min

Spindleruv Mlyn

1

A white arched bridge over the nascent Elbe, chalets packed into a narrow valley, a torrent roaring below: Špindlerův Mlýn announces itself before you even arrive. The main ski resort of the Krkonoše is admittedly touristy, but the natural setting makes up for everything—and in summer, the ski lifts serve as a springboard for hikers.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Pančava Waterfall, with its 148 meters of drop in four tiers (36, 39, 23, and 20 m), is the highest in the Czech Republic. It falls into the Labský důl from the Pančava plateau. You can reach it from the Medvědín cable car upper station in about 45 minutes on foot.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Labský Waterfall once had a peculiarity that guides no longer mention: the former owner of the Labská bouda had built a small dam above the precipice. When tourists gathered, he would open the valve to make the spectacle more impressive. The dam has disappeared—for ecological reasons.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Take the Medvědín cable car up, then descend on foot through the Labský důl valley (11 km blue-marked trail, 2h30 easy walk): you'll pass the Pančava Waterfall seen from below, then the Labský Waterfall, before rejoining Špindlerův Mlýn along the river.

⏱ 180 min

If your legs can handle a full day, continue from Labská bouda to Pramen Labe, the symbolic source of the Elbe, 1 km further: a stone well, coats of arms of cities the river crosses to the North Sea, a wooden sculpture. The actual source area is closed to the public to protect the vegetation.

⏱ 30 min

Spindleruv Mlyn

1

The highest in the Czech Republic and yet almost invisible from below: the slope of the Labský důl hides the Pančava Waterfall until the last turn. You have to climb to the Medvědín station to see it plunge 148 meters into the valley.

⏱ 60 min
2

Four tiers—36, 39, 23, and 20 meters—make up the total drop from the Pančava meadow. The flow varies greatly by season: impressive during snowmelt, reduced in summer. Below, the Labský Waterfall (45 m) offers a second stop on the same descent route.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

From the Medvědín cable car upper station, follow the red-marked trail toward Pančavská louka: 45 minutes one way, well-visible markers. Return by the same path or continue to Labská bouda to join the Labský důl circuit.

⏱ 90 min

Pec Pod Snezkou

★★★☆☆
1

Pec pod Sněžkou is functionally a ski resort like any other—hotels, lifts, equipment shops. What sets it apart: it's the only starting point for the direct cable car to Sněžka, at 824 meters of elevation gain. The village itself can be toured in thirty minutes; all the interest is up there.

⏱ 30 min
2

If you go up by cable car, descend on foot via the red trail through Obří důl: the descent into this former glacial valley takes 2h15 and shows characteristic striated rocks. The cable car (lanovka) doesn't accept reservations—expect a queue on fine-weather days in high season.

⏱ 135 min

Activities

Bring a waterproof jacket and closed shoes before going up to Sněžka: the average annual temperature at the summit is 0.2°C and the wind can blow hard even in midsummer. If storms are forecast, the ascent is discouraged even if the sky looks clear from Pec.

⏱ 15 min

Pec Pod Snezkou

★★★☆☆
1

At 1,603 meters, you'd expect solitude. Instead: a post office, a bar, an observatory, and an international border running through the middle of the rock. Sněžka is the highest point in the Czech Republic and Poland's Lower Silesian Voivodeship—two countries literally share the same summit.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Czech building Anežka—its name comes from an architecture competition where candidates had to choose female first names—looks like a spaceship moored on the rock. Inside, the Czech Post office stamps every postcard with a Sněžka seal. On the Polish side, the circular observatory and St. Lawrence Chapel evoke mountain stations from another era.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Send a postcard from the post office in Anežka: it's the highest post office in the Czech Republic. The Sněžka stamp is distinctive enough to be worth the postal detour. The souvenir shop is small, but the bar serves hot drinks that are precious in strong winds.

⏱ 20 min

Trutnov

1

Krakonoš, the guardian giant of the Giant Mountains, watches over the square that bears his name in Trutnov—a large bronze fountain in the center of a pleasant colonnaded space to walk through. The city calls itself the 'gateway to the Giant Mountains,' and the formula fits: it's the last major town before the mountains.

⏱ 30 min
2

The cast-iron obelisk on Šibeník hill doesn't look like much from afar, but it carries historical weight: erected in 1868 to commemorate the Battle of Trutnov on June 27, 1866, Austria's only victory against Prussia during that war. Baron Ludwig von Gablenz himself had asked to be buried at the foot of this monument—done in 1874.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb Šibeník by following the marked 4 km trail from Krakonoš Square, called 'In the Footsteps of the 1866 War': a dozen panels in Czech, German, and Polish punctuate the battle's progression. Count on 1h30 round trip from downtown.

⏱ 90 min

Adrspach

1

Morning light filters between 50-meter sandstone pillars like in an open-air cathedral. The Adršpach Rocks are Central Europe's largest sandstone rock labyrinth: narrow streets between cliffs, wooden bridges over ravines, black and calm water at the bottom of the Great Lake. The effect is genuinely disorienting.

⏱ 150 min
2

A fire in 1824 burned the forest for several weeks. Until then, the rocks were masked by trees and the site little frequented. It was this forest accident that revealed the formations and triggered tourism: the first alpinists arrived from Saxony, and the first documented ascent of 'The King' dates to May 20, 1923.

⏱ 20 min
3

The formations have names worth the detour: the Mayor and Mayoress, the Lovers, the Gothic Gate, the Sugar Loaf. Spot the Gothic Gate at the circuit entrance—it's the most photographed and narrowest passage of the site. Locals know others, unofficial ones, that the signs ignore.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Reserve your tickets online before leaving: the ticket offices no longer sell tickets on-site since a few seasons ago, and agencies buy up entire batches. On weekend days, traffic in the gorges can be gridlocked—arrive at opening at 8am.

⏱ 15 min

The boat ride on the sandstone lake of the circuit lasts twenty minutes and is worth every crown—50 CZK per person. It's not spectacular in itself, but the angle of view on the cliffs from the water is unique. The boatmen propel with poles: gondola atmosphere in sandstone.

⏱ 20 min

Teplice Nad Metuji

1

Less famous than Adršpach, judged more spectacular by most hikers who do both. The blue loop through the Teplice Rocks crosses 6 km of taller gorges, with clear views from the ridges. The two sites are connected by the Vlk gorge (2.5 km)—can be combined in a long day.

⏱ 180 min
2

Fifteen minutes from the entrance, a metal staircase of 300 steps climbs to the ruined Strmen castle and a panoramic view of the entire massif. A portion of the trail is closed in April and May during peregrine falcon nesting—check before coming.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Start early on the Teplice circuit: by midday the parking overflows and the gorges fill up. Bring water and a snack—nothing is sold inside the site. Entry about 100 CZK, parking 100 CZK.

Broumov

1

The inner courtyard of Broumov Benedictine Monastery strikes with its baroque severity—gray stone, round arches, imposing proportions. Christoph Dientzenhofer rebuilt the complex in the late 17th century after the destructions of the Thirty Years' War. The monastery also preserves a copy of the Turin Shroud—this 1651 copy is now kept at Břevnov Abbey, but a replica is visible during the visit.

⏱ 90 min
2

In the cellars of Broumov Monastery, 34 mummies from the 17th and 18th centuries, including that of Magdalena Grambová, a Flemish baroness who founded the lace-making tradition in Eastern Bohemia. Above ground, the Benedictine brewery produces one of the country's oldest abbey beers—taste it in the cloister café.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Drive out to the eight baroque churches of the Broumov Group: built between 1709 and 1743 by Christoph and Kilian Ignaz Dientzenhofer, they emerge isolated in the countryside like little baroque UFOs. The most accessible is St. Mary Magdalene in Božanov, 6 km north.

⏱ 60 min

Mezimesti

1

The ridge of the Broumov Walls drops on one side in 100-meter cliffs over the Broumov valley, and descends gently on the other toward Poland. For 12 km, the trail crosses gorges, sandstone mushrooms, and narrow passages. Outside of Hvězda, you rarely encounter other hikers.

⏱ 120 min
2

At the center of the national reserve, the Chapel of Our Lady of the Snows on the Hvězda hillock was designed by Kilian Ignaz Dientzenhofer and completed in 1733—on a star-shaped plan, hence the name. Next to it, a mountain chalet built in 1856 in Swiss style serves as a restaurant and trail junction point.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

From Hvězda, take the red trail toward Kovářova rokle: a 2 km long gorge, bordered by 100-meter sandstone cliffs, and only 2 meters wide in places. The Mariánská Cave is at the bottom. Count on 2h for the loop from Hvězda. Paid parking.

⏱ 120 min

Police Nad Metuji

1

Police nad Metují is small, but its Masaryk Square—classified as a municipal monument reserve since 1990—is a pleasant surprise: Renaissance arcades, the Julinka fountain with water directly drinkable from the underground aquifer of the Polické křídové pánve. Thirty minutes is enough to walk around it.

⏱ 30 min
2

The tabletop mountain Ostaš, at 700 meters elevation and 3 km northwest of Police, hides a labyrinth of monolithic sandstone blocks on its plateau—more than 500 marked climbing routes. The view from the Krtičkova lookout embraces Adršpach, the Broumov Walls, and on clear days, the Krkonoše.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Loop the 3.5 km marked Ostaš circuit in 1h30—accessible to all levels. Paid parking at the Ostaš hamlet. Combine with the Merkur Museum in Police (construction toys, unique in the Czech Republic) if you arrive early—it closes at 5pm.

Nachod

1

Five courtyards, a cylindrical medieval tower, and a dominant position on the Polish border since the 13th century: Náchod Castle has passed through the Piccolomini, Courland, and Schaumburg-Lippe dynasties without losing its character. It successively belonged to the Italian Piccolomini, the Courlanders, and the Schaumburg-Lippe princes—each family left its layer in the interiors: Brussels tapestries, portraits from the Piccolomini gallery, aristocratic medals. Three separate tour circuits.

⏱ 90 min
2

Josef Škvorecký, born in Náchod in 1924, renamed his hometown 'Kostelec' in his novels. Exiled to Canada after 1968, he founded a publishing house there for censored Czech literature. A statue of him sitting on a bench has been waiting since 2014 on Masaryk Square—with an empty spot next to him.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Climb to the ochoz of the castle's cylindrical tower to see Náchod and the Polish plain—separate ticket to request at the ticket office. On the way down, stop by the Gallery of Fine Arts at the foot of the castle: 19th-century Russian painting and Czech art, fully accessible at ground level.

⏱ 60 min

Ratiborice

1

You don't come to Ratibořice to visit a castle. You come to inhabit a novel: Grandmother's Valley, Babiččino údolí, is the setting of 'Babička' by Božena Němcová (1855), one of the most read texts in Czech literature—and the author's face graces the 500 CZK banknote.

⏱ 60 min
2

A discreet château in a bucolic valley, and yet: in 1813, Metternich and Tsar Alexander I met here to seal the coalition against Napoleon. The Duchess of Zaháň, owner of the premises, had the habit of gathering European aristocracy in her classicist salons, rebuilt in 1825-1826 on Italian plans.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Follow on foot the Babiččino údolí trail from the château to the Staré Bělidlo (3 km one way): the Rudr Mill, the water mangle, the half-timbered house of the old bleachery, and Otto Gutfreund's sculpture group 'Grandmother and Grandchildren' punctuate the walk. Total duration: 1h30 round trip.

⏱ 90 min

Nove Mesto Nad Metuji

1

From the outside, everything suggests Renaissance: sgraffito, round tower, stone arcade. Inside, architects Dušan Jurkovič and Pavel Janák redid the salons in Art Nouveau, Art Deco, and even Cubist style for the Bartoň family in the early 20th century. Nové Město nad Metují Castle, founded in 1501, ranks among the most unexpected aristocratic interiors in Bohemia: central heating and telephone at the dawn of the 20th century.

⏱ 90 min
2

The castle's terraced gardens descend toward the Metuje in several tiers connected by a covered wooden bridge—English countryside atmosphere on a Czech river. In the corner of the upper garden, a few baroque statues from Braun's workshop. The park is freely accessible; the castle can be visited from May to September.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Climb the round tower for a view of Hus Square (50 CZK on top of the castle ticket), then descend into the old town: a few cafés and an artisan grocery hide there. Count on 2h for castle + gardens + old town.

⏱ 120 min
