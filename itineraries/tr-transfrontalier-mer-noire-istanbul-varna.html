Istanbul

Where Byzantine domes and Ottoman minarets trace empires in stone.
Hagia Sophia IstanbulTopkapi Palace IstanbulBasilica Cistern Istanbul
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car
1

Fifteen centuries of layered history – Ayasofya (Hagia Sophia) was a Byzantine cathedral in 537, then a mosque in 1453, a museum from 1934 to 2020, then an active mosque again. Tourist access is via the upper gallery (paid ticket), where Byzantine mosaics remain intact. Avoid Friday prayer hours.

⏱ 90 min
2

Water laps at columns in eerie green light inside the Basilica Cistern (Yerebatan Sarnıcı), carved in the 6th century to supply the imperial palace. The 336 columns support two inverted Medusa heads whose origin remains debated. Come on a weekday right at opening: reflections are clearest before tour groups arrive.

⏱ 60 min
3

Touristy and mobbed in summer, Topkapi Palace still warrants several hours. Main residence of Ottoman sultans for nearly four centuries, it closes on Tuesdays – check the date before planning. The Harem requires a separate ticket; the views over the Bosphorus from the fourth courtyard alone justify the visit.

⏱ 180 min
4

Take the T1 tram to Sultanahmet: the Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmet Camii) awaits with its six minarets, a theological scandal at its 1616 construction because only Mecca had as many. The interior of Iznik blue tiles is free to enter outside the five daily prayers.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you arrive before 9 a.m. at Hippodrome Square (Sultanahmet Meydanı), the Obelisk of Theodosius and Serpent Column are nearly deserted. The atmosphere shifts dramatically at 10 a.m. – the square is one of the few in Istanbul you can walk comfortably.

⏱ 30 min

Crowded, noisy, seemingly soulless, the Grand Bazaar (Kapalıçarşı) actually conceals several hammams and mosques tucked into its alleys. The real deals are on perpendicular streets – avoid the central corridors for prices.

⏱ 60 min

Vize Castle KırklareliLittle Hagia Sophia Vize
✈️ Book a flight🚗 Rent a car

According to a 2nd-century AD inscription preserved in Edirne museum, Vize Castle (Vize Kalesi) dates to 72–76 BCE. The fortress served as acropolis for the Thracian city of Bizye. The still-standing south face rises over 5 m high. The site is freely accessible but unsigned – ask locals for Kale Caddesi.

⏱ 40 min
2

Locals simply call it Küçük Ayasofya – the Little Hagia Sophia of Vize (Gazi Süleyman Paşa Camii). A former Byzantine church converted to a mosque in the 14th century, it retains its original proportions. The spare interior contrasts with its illustrious Istanbul namesake.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Walk up to the castle via Kale Caddesi from the town center – about 700 m. Vize is designated Cittaslow (slow city label); the old wooden houses in the castle quarter merit the stroll.

The Archaeological and Ethnographic Museum of Kırklareli displays discoveries from the Aşağı Pınar tumulus, one of Europe's earliest organized settlements dating to prehistory. The first-floor ethnographic section offers a glimpse into Eastern Thracian traditions. Closed Mondays.

Activities
