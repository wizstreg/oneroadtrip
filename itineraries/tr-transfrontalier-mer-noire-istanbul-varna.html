Istanbul

★★★★★
1

Fifteen centuries of layered history – Ayasofya (Hagia Sophia) was a Byzantine cathedral in 537, then a mosque in 1453, a museum from 1934 to 2020, then an active mosque again. Tourist access is via the upper gallery (paid ticket), where Byzantine mosaics remain intact. Avoid Friday prayer hours.

⏱ 90 min
2

Water laps at columns in eerie green light inside the Basilica Cistern (Yerebatan Sarnıcı), carved in the 6th century to supply the imperial palace. The 336 columns support two inverted Medusa heads whose origin remains debated. Come on a weekday right at opening: reflections are clearest before tour groups arrive.

⏱ 60 min
3

Touristy and mobbed in summer, Topkapi Palace still warrants several hours. Main residence of Ottoman sultans for nearly four centuries, it closes on Tuesdays – check the date before planning. The Harem requires a separate ticket; the views over the Bosphorus from the fourth courtyard alone justify the visit.

⏱ 180 min
4

Take the T1 tram to Sultanahmet: the Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmet Camii) awaits with its six minarets, a theological scandal at its 1616 construction because only Mecca had as many. The interior of Iznik blue tiles is free to enter outside the five daily prayers.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you arrive before 9 a.m. at Hippodrome Square (Sultanahmet Meydanı), the Obelisk of Theodosius and Serpent Column are nearly deserted. The atmosphere shifts dramatically at 10 a.m. – the square is one of the few in Istanbul you can walk comfortably.

⏱ 30 min

Crowded, noisy, seemingly soulless, the Grand Bazaar (Kapalıçarşı) actually conceals several hammams and mosques tucked into its alleys. The real deals are on perpendicular streets – avoid the central corridors for prices.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 130 km · 1h50

Vize

★★★☆☆
1

According to a 2nd-century AD inscription preserved in Edirne museum, Vize Castle (Vize Kalesi) dates to 72–76 BCE. The fortress served as acropolis for the Thracian city of Bizye. The still-standing south face rises over 5 m high. The site is freely accessible but unsigned – ask locals for Kale Caddesi.

⏱ 40 min
2

Locals simply call it Küçük Ayasofya – the Little Hagia Sophia of Vize (Gazi Süleyman Paşa Camii). A former Byzantine church converted to a mosque in the 14th century, it retains its original proportions. The spare interior contrasts with its illustrious Istanbul namesake.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Walk up to the castle via Kale Caddesi from the town center – about 700 m. Vize is designated Cittaslow (slow city label); the old wooden houses in the castle quarter merit the stroll.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 50min

Kiyikoy

★★☆☆☆
1

Morning mist clings to the wooden houses of Kıyıköy (ancient Medea) above the Black Sea. The village retains its medieval ramparts and a few sloped lanes not yet disfigured. Population around 2,500 lives on fishing and summer tourism.

⏱ 45 min
2

Discreet, almost ignored by guidebooks, the rock-cut Saint Nicholas Monastery (Aya Nikola) is carved into the hillside cliff, a Byzantine-era legacy. Accessible on foot from the village via a forest trail of about 20 min. Wear closed shoes – the path can be muddy.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Head to the harbor before dawn to watch the boats come in. The river emptying into the Black Sea just north of the port offers rowboat rentals in season. Peaceful atmosphere, cheap grilled fish on return.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 1h30

Igneada

★★★★☆
1

Declared a national park in 2007, İğneada Longoz Ormanları covers 3,155 ha of floodplain forests (longoz) – an ecosystem where trees grow with their feet in water part of the year. Rare in Turkey (only three longoz sites in the country), the park shelters seven forest lakes. Free entry, marked trails.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

If you come in May or September, the Via Pontica – the great bird migration route between Northern Europe and Africa – passes directly over İğneada. Binoculars are essential: white storks, Dalmatian pelicans, and white-tailed eagles have been observed in the reserve.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 4 km · 10min

Igneada

★★★★☆
1

Mert Lake (Mert Gölü) reflects the alders with mirror-like precision when the wind drops. It's the most visited lake in İğneada national park, accessible 4 km from the town center. Bring water – no supply points inside the park.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 65 km · 1h10

Kirklareli

★★★☆☆
1

The Archaeological and Ethnographic Museum of Kırklareli displays discoveries from the Aşağı Pınar tumulus, one of Europe's earliest organized settlements dating to prehistory. The first-floor ethnographic section offers a glimpse into Eastern Thracian traditions. Closed Mondays.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Hızırbey Complex (Hızırbey Külliyesi) – mosque-bazaar-hammam – dates from 1383 and was erected by Köse Mihalzade Hızırbey. The ensemble on the central square is one of the rare preserved examples of early Ottoman architecture in Turkish Thrace.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take Mustafa Kemal boulevard toward the covered market – Kırklareli is renowned for its hardaliye, a non-alcoholic fermented drink made from red grapes and crushed mustard seeds. Sample it in market groceries, take home in bottles.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 1h15

Malko Tarnovo

1

Founded in 1983 as the Strandja Museum Complex, the Malko Tarnovo History Museum is the only cultural institute at the heart of the Strandja massif. Five exhibitions spread across four Bulgarian Revival houses trace Thracian archaeology, local fauna, and the history of the 1903 Preobrazhenie Uprising.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The scent of wet oak floods the Strandzha Nature Park trails as soon as you leave the road. The park director based in Malko Tarnovo offers guides for trails to caves and springs along the Mladezhka – inquire at the information center on the main square.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Malko Tarnovo

1

Brashlyan's elders say their houses are the oldest still inhabited in Strandja. The village is a classified architectural reserve: 17th–19th-century wooden buildings, Saint Dimitar Church with its attached school. Free visit, no tickets.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 1h

Sinemorets

1

Fine sand, limpid water, not a concrete hotel in sight: Veleka Beach in Sinemorets remains one of the rare unbuilt stretches of Bulgaria's coast. Until the 1990s, the area was off-limits to civilians – communist border zone. A blessing for the shoreline: nothing was ever constructed.

⏱ 90 min
2

Where the Veleka empties into the Black Sea, the sand forms a tongue between two worlds: brackish lagoon waters behind, dark sea swell ahead. The Veleka is the longest river in Strandja. On the beach's north side, rock formations resemble beached boat hulls.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Rent a kayak beside the Veleka and paddle upriver 2–3 km under the Strandja canopy. The water is calm, wildlife dense – kingfishers, grey herons. Windsurfing and SUP school in season on the beach. Prefer the river to the sea for young children.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 12 km · 20min

Ahtopol

1

The southernmost Bulgarian town on the Black Sea, Ahtopol is ideal for those fleeing big resorts. The vibe is resolutely local – Bulgarian families, a few fishing boats, a seaside promenade without commercial bustle. Not made for partying: perfect for decompression.

⏱ 45 min
2

Sole survivor of the 1918 fire that ravaged nearly the entire town, the Church of the Ascension of Christ (Vaznesenie Hristovo) is now a historical monument. Next door, the old Greek school building, built in the early 20th century, recently became an artists' residence.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Walk the southern promenade to the rocks of Lardigo Bay – a 1.8-km beach enclosed in a verdant cove, out of sight from town. No infrastructure aside from a seasonal snack bar. The seabed is clean, ideal for snorkeling.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 12 km · 20min

Tsarevo

1

Tsarevo, a small port town, turns its back on mega-complexes to cultivate a history-arts-sea blend. The historic center on the hill offers a bay view. Tsarevo is the administrative center of the municipality that includes Sinemorets, Ahtopol, and Rezovo.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 15min

Primorsko

1

Beglik Tash is an open-air Thracian sanctuary: large granite blocks carved and arranged in ritual circles on a wooded plateau north of Primorsko. Site poorly signposted on roads – GPS or offline map advised. Free access.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min

Primorsko

1

Primorsko sprawls on a peninsula between two bays – the village keeps a human scale the northern mega-resorts have lost. The promenade between the two beaches takes twenty minutes; the fresh fish restaurants on the waterfront are cheap outside July–August.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min

Primorsko

1

The Ropotamo's dark water glides under water lilies in the nature reserve of the same name, a few kilometers north of Primorsko. The river empties into the Black Sea forming a dense, undisturbed estuary largely spared from mass tourism.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Sozopol

1

Apollonia Pontica, founded in the 7th century BCE as a Greek colony – over 2,600 years of continuity on this coastal site. The Sozopol Archaeological Museum preserves coins, pottery, and sculptures from Greek, Roman, and Thracian periods. Plan a minimum of 1h.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Sozopol Museum is small, discreet – two skeletons discovered in 2012 with iron stakes through their chests, medieval Bulgarian vampires now displayed under glass. Off the usual circuits, the collection surprises with its blend of archaeology and folklore.

⏱ 30 min
3

Rounded cobbles, wooden balconies overhanging the lane, courtyards hidden behind painted doors – Sozopol's old town is a classified architectural reserve. The houses with stone ground floors and wooden upper stories are the characteristic style of the Bulgarian Revival on the seafront.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wander toward the harbor at sunset for the smell of grilled fish and the bells of Saint Zosimus Church. The harbor promenade is the liveliest on the south coast: fishermen, craft galleries, small cafés without plastic chairs.

⏱ 60 min

Early September, the Apollonia festival transforms the old town into an open stage: concerts, exhibitions, performances by international artists in the lanes and on the harbor. If your visit coincides, book accommodation well in advance.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 40min

Burgas

1

If you arrive in the morning, Saints Cyril and Methodius Cathedral is nearly empty. The neo-Byzantine building in central Burgas conceals behind a severe façade stained glass of rare intensity – kaleidoscopic light in the nave as soon as the sun climbs.

⏱ 30 min
2

Neo-Byzantine stained glass explodes in color in the nave, but it's the Burgas Archaeological Museum that concentrates the important pieces: Thracian, Greek, and Roman artifacts tracing the coast's history from the Bronze Age. The ancient coin collection is especially dense.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Walk down Bogoridi to the Sea Garden (Morska Gradina), the large waterfront park stretching several kilometers. The main alley leads to the pier and central beach – good lunch at the port restaurants before heading toward Pomorie.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 30min

Pomorie

1

Bulgaria's only museum entirely devoted to solar-evaporation salt production, the Pomorie Salt Museum (Muzeya na Solya) has been open since 2002. The panels displayed in the warehouse trace exploitation since Thracian times – Pomorie was then called Anchialos. The 20 ha of active salt pans are visible from the terrace.

⏱ 60 min
2

Fishermen call the Pomorie Lake mud 'black gold' – and have exploited it for centuries to treat rheumatism and skin conditions. Lake Pomorie is a 760-ha hypersaline lagoon part of the Natura 2000 network, with nearly 270 bird species observed.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Dip your feet in the therapeutic mud of Lake Pomorie at the sanatorium entrance or on the public shore – strange and effective experience. The waters are warm in summer, the mud stays black and elastic. Shower immediately afterward essential.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 18 km · 25min

Nessebar

1

Thracian Mesambria, then Greek colony of Dorian Megara, then Byzantine, then Ottoman – Nessebar's old town accumulates over 3,000 years of history on a peninsula linked to the mainland by an artificial isthmus. Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.

⏱ 90 min
2

The windmill at the entrance creaks in the Black Sea wind – symbol of Nessebar, it marks the island's former limit before an isthmus linked it to the coast. Continue into the cobbled streets: ruins of medieval churches, craft workshops, houses with stone ground floors.

⏱ 45 min
3

Saint John the Baptist Church of Nessebar is better than its postcard reproductions: partially preserved frescoes, slender proportions, intact medieval Bulgarian architecture. Most of the old town's church ruins are freely accessible – about a dozen to cover on foot.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Come early in the morning or after 5 p.m.: Nessebar peninsula empties rapidly of Sunny Beach groups. In the evening, warm lights on old walls and boats in the harbor give the old town a photographic quality rare on Bulgaria's coast.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 15min

Sveti Vlas

1

Modern beach resort, 300-berth marina inaugurated in 2007 – Sveti Vlas is the contemporary face of Bulgaria's coast. The south-facing beaches, sheltered by Balkan foothills descending to the sea, enjoy some of the coast's best sunshine.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Sea at your back facing the Balkans, morning sun ahead: Sveti Vlas central beach stretches 1.5 km total. Full water sports in season – jet-ski, wakeboard, beach volleyball. Ideal transit stop between Nessebar and Obzor.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 35min

Obzor

1

Obzor stretches its ribbon of sand between Cape Emine to the north and pine forests to the south – one of the longest beaches on Bulgaria's central coast. The town keeps a more local character than neighboring mega-complexes, with markedly lower prices.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Enjoy a final swim at Obzor beach before tackling Cape Emine's curves northward. The road to Byala winds between cliffs and forests – the drive itself warrants the stop.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 35min

Byala

1

White limestone, turquoise water, no construction on the clifftop – Byala's cliffs plunge into the sea with photogenic brutality. The town is small, prices reasonable, the sea a bit rougher than farther south – good for windsurfing.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If the wind is head-on, climb the north hill for the cliff-sea panorama. The coastal hiking trail between Byala and Shkorpilovtsi (about 4 km) skirts the cliffs with open views of the shoreline.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 40min

Kamchia

1

Under the canopy of ash and alder that shuts out the sky, the Kamchia Reserve has protected since 1951 one of the Balkans' last alluvial forest massifs. The reserve covers 842 ha of dense woods, channels, and marshes. Marked trails, mandatory passage on indicated paths.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Kamchia River mouth forms a liman: sandbanks, reed fringes, and shallow lagoons create a mosaic of habitats at the Black Sea edge. The Kamchia River (244.5 km) rises in the Eastern Balkans.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Bring binoculars: the Via Pontica, the great migration route from Northern Europe to Africa, passes directly over the reserve. Dalmatian pelicans, white storks, white-tailed eagles, and several egret species have been recorded on site. Spring and autumn are the best seasons.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 40min

Pobiti Kamani

1

Desert in Bulgaria: surprising. Hollow columns reaching 7 m high emerge from fine sand 18 km west of Varna – the Pobiti Kamani (literally 'planted stones'). Remnants of a 50-million-year-old coral reef, their exact origin is still debated among geologists.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Fine sand squeaks underfoot, pillars rise house-high – exploring Pobiti Kamani takes 1h30 to 2h for those who want to explore the clusters of columns scattered over several hectares. Information panels on site, free access. No café or restaurant nearby.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min

Varna

★★★★☆
1

Seven thousand square meters of vaults and hypocausts: Varna's Thermae Odessos are among the largest preserved Roman baths in the Balkan Peninsula, built in the late 2nd century. The double-floor heating system is still visible. Open Tuesday to Sunday, 10 a.m.–5 p.m.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Varna Gold is the real reason to visit the Varna Archaeological Museum. Discovered in 1972 in a prehistoric necropolis west of the city, this worked gold dates back about 7,000 years – the oldest known in the world. The 19th-century neo-Renaissance building is itself a monument.

⏱ 90 min
3

Sea Garden (Morska Gradina): touristy, but the shaded alleys and sea views more than compensate. The park runs along the coast for several kilometers from central Varna. In the evening, the terraces and harbor promenade concentrate local life.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If the museum closes at 5 p.m., walk up Knyaz Boris I boulevard toward the central square and its terraces – Varna evening life concentrates here. The Dormition of the Mother of God Cathedral, a few steps away, is the city's largest church.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Varna

★★★★☆
1

Carved into the limestone cliffs of Golden Sands Nature Park (Zlatni Pyasatsi), Aladzha Monastery spreads over two levels – cells, church, crypt. Founded in the Middle Ages, it is one of Bulgaria's most imposing rock monasteries. 15 km north of Varna.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Varna beach – accessible from the Sea Garden in 10 minutes – is the city's main beach with fine sand and full facilities. For a wilder vibe, Perla beach (15 km north) or Gradina beach (20 km south) offer calmer waters.

⏱ 60 min

The Varna Chalcolithic Necropolis, 4 km from the center, is the archaeological site where the treasure was discovered. Excavations are still active. An on-site presentation panel lets you visualize the necropolis extent and locations of excavated tombs.

⏱ 60 min
