Klagenfurt

★★★★☆
1

Klagenfurt am Wörthersee, capital of Carinthia, revolves around the Alter Platz—its main square flanked by Italian-style Renaissance and Art Nouveau facades. The Dragon Fountain (Lindwurmbrunnen, 1590) commands the center: this mythical creature has been the city's emblem since medieval times, when locals believed a dragon inhabited the marshes where Klagenfurt was founded.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Landhaus, a 16th-century Renaissance masterpiece, houses the Hall of Coats of Arms with 665 hand-painted shields representing Carinthia's noble families. Its inner courtyard, with double-tiered arcaded galleries, ranks among Austria's most elegant Renaissance spaces.

⏱ 60 min
3

The MMKK (Museum Moderner Kunst Kärnten), housed in Klagenfurt Castle, presents one of Austria's most important contemporary art collections, spanning early modernism to cutting-edge installations.

⏱ 75 min
4

Minimundus, on the shores of Lake Wörth, displays over 160 miniature replicas at 1:25 scale of the world's most famous landmarks. The Eiffel Tower, Taj Mahal, Colosseum, and Sagrada Família stand alongside Austrian monuments in meticulously maintained gardens.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Evening lakeside stroll along the Wörthersee promenade from the city center. The walkway stretches for kilometers along the lake, with views of the Carinthian Alps rising behind Belle Époque villas built by the Austro-Hungarian aristocracy.

⏱ 60 min

Sankt Veit An Der Glan

★★☆☆☆
1

Hochosterwitz Castle ranks among Austria's most spectacular fortresses: a medieval stronghold perched atop a 160-meter limestone pinnacle dominating the Carinthian plain, visible for dozens of kilometers in every direction. Built from 860 AD and remodeled in the 16th century by the Khevenhüller family, it has remained virtually unchanged since. The 620-meter helical access road climbs through 14 successive fortified gates, each equipped with different defensive mechanisms—portcullises, machicolations, drawbridges—which, according to tradition, kept the castle impregnable for centuries.

⏱ 120 min
2

The interior rooms open to visitors display collections of armor, paintings, and prehistoric artifacts. The castle chapel, built in 1571, preserves its original frescoes. An elevator from the parking area allows visitors with mobility limitations direct access to the main entrance.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swimming at Lake Längsee, a few kilometers from the castle. This small, clean, uncrowded lake offers a refreshing pause after exploring the fortress. Its wooded shores and clear water make it an ideal family stop.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.3Apartment Simonhöhe

Friesach

★★★☆☆
1

Friesach is Carinthia's oldest town, documented since 860 AD, and southern Austria's best-preserved medieval city. Its historic center remains surrounded by virtually intact 12th-century ramparts stretching 1.8 kilometers—a rarity in the Alps. The Hauptplatz is lined with Gothic and Renaissance houses, many retaining their original arcades and medieval inner courtyards.

⏱ 75 min
2

Petersberg Castle, erected by the Bishops of Salzburg in the 12th century on the hill overlooking town, houses the municipal museum. Archaeological collections, sacred art, and medieval life reconstructions trace the history of this town that was, for centuries, Carinthia's most important.

⏱ 75 min
3

The 12th-century Romanesque parish church preserves its Gothic frescoes and carved wooden altar. The adjacent Dominican monastery, founded in 1217, is one of the oldest German-language monasteries still in operation.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Friesach hosts a unique experimental archaeology project: the reconstruction of a medieval castle using exclusively 13th-century techniques and tools. Visitors can watch craftsmen at work and understand how fortresses were built 800 years ago.

⏱ 60 min
$$$9.2Gasthof zur Sonne
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Gurk

★★☆☆☆
1

Gurk Cathedral, consecrated in 1200, is considered Austria's most important Romanesque religious building. Its facade with twin 44-meter towers and its interior covered with over 1,000 m² of Romanesque and Gothic frescoes make it an exceptional monument. The crypt, supported by 100 columns with all-different carved capitals, houses the sarcophagus of Blessed Emma—one of the purest examples of 11th-century Romanesque architecture in Central Europe.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Diocesan Museum, in the adjacent episcopal palace, displays one of Austria's richest collections of medieval sacred art: liturgical vestments, gold reliquaries, and the 12th-century Chalice of Gurk with cloisonné enamels. The library preserves illuminated manuscripts from the 13th-15th centuries.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Michael Hönel's baroque altar (1740) dominates the cathedral choir. The Gurk Treasury, displayed in an adjacent room, gathers seven centuries of sacred goldwork—a visit that perfectly complements the Romanesque cathedral.

⏱ 45 min
$$$9.9Ferienwohnung Familie Wieser
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h05

Klopeiner See

★★★☆☆
1

Klopeiner See is Central Europe's warmest lake: its waters reach 29°C in summer, thanks to its shallow depth (14 m maximum) and sun exposure. This 1.8-km-long lake with turquoise waters is surrounded by wooded hills and offers well-equipped beaches and facilities around its entire perimeter.

⏱ 90 min
2

The climb to the Kitzelberg summit, on the western shore, rewards you after a 45-minute rocky trail with a 360° panorama of the lake's turquoise waters and surrounding Carinthian mountains. It's the best viewpoint over the lake.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swimming, kayaking, paddleboarding, and windsurfing from Klopeiner See's developed beaches. Water sports rental companies operate on both main shores. Cycling trails around the lake allow you to circle it in two hours.

⏱ 120 min
$$$10Haus Sunshine
🚗Drive to next stop: 50 km · 1h

Worthersee

★★★☆☆
1

Wörthersee, 19.4 km long, is Carinthia's most famous lake and one of Austria's most exclusive bathing destinations. Its waters reach 28°C in summer thanks to its sheltered location. Since the Belle Époque, the Austro-Hungarian aristocracy built sumptuous Art Nouveau villas along its shores—Brahms composed his Second Symphony here in Pörtschach in 1877.

⏱ 60 min
2

Pyramidenkogel, inaugurated in 2013, is the world's tallest wooden observation tower at 100 meters high. An elevator reaches the panoramic platform in 30 seconds: 360° views of the entire Wörthersee, neighboring lakes, and the Carinthian Alps. The descent via the 120-meter spiral slide is an attraction in itself.

⏱ 75 min
3

Velden am Wörthersee, nicknamed the "Austrian Monte Carlo," boasts its historic casino and the Grand Hotel Schloss Velden (1590, converted into a luxury hotel). Its elegant lakeside promenade and terraces animate Carinthian evenings.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swimming at the beaches of Pörtschach or Maria Wörth. The Maria Wörth peninsula, with its 10th-century church reflected in the lake, is the iconic image of Wörthersee. Public beaches are free to access.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.2Fischerhaus
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 25min

Faaker See

★★★☆☆
1

Faaker See is one of Carinthia's most beautiful lakes: its deep turquoise waters reach 27°C in summer. This 2.5-km-long lake is characterized by its small central island, which remains one of the region's most photographed images. Its modest size guarantees rapid warming and ideal swimming conditions.

⏱ 75 min
2

The ascent to Hochhinauf-Taborhöhe offers the best plunging views of Faaker See and its island. Access via forest trails takes about 45 minutes from the lakeshore.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Water sports equipment rental at the developed beaches of Drobollach and Egg am Faaker See: pedal boats, kayaks, paddleboards. A walking trail completely encircles the lake in 1.5 hours.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.3Apart of me Faaker See
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Villach

★★★☆☆
1

Villach, Carinthia's second city with 65,000 inhabitants, is crossed by the Drau River. Its historic center concentrates medieval arcades, fountains, and baroque facades around the Hauptplatz. The Drau Cycle Path, which follows the river for hundreds of kilometers, makes Villach an ideal base for cycling excursions.

⏱ 60 min
2

Landskron Castle, perched in the hills north of Villach, hosts some of Austria's most impressive falconry displays. Free-flight demonstrations—golden eagles, buzzards, falcons, and owls—take place in a natural amphitheater with the Alps as backdrop.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Affenberg, a few kilometers from Villach, is one of the few places in Europe where over 150 Japanese macaques roam semi-freely in a natural setting. Guides explain these primates' social behavior and their conservation program.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Relaxation at the Warmbad Villach thermal baths (Kärnten Therme): natural thermal springs at 29°C, indoor and outdoor pools, saunas. The thermal complex is one of Austria's oldest, frequented since Roman times.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.5Panda Apartment in der Nähe von Therme und Atrio
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 15min

Ferlach

★★★☆☆
1

Tscheppaschlucht is one of Carinthia's most beautiful gorges. Water churns between steep rock walls, forming cascades and whirlpools. The well-equipped trail—metal walkways, ladders, stairs carved into rock—follows the river for about 2 km in 2 hours of exploration. The Teufelsbrücke (Devil's Bridge) section offers the most dramatic views of the waterfalls.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Bodental Valley, accessible from the gorge exit, shelters the Meerauge—a small lake with intense blue-green colors and exceptionally clear water, fed by underground springs. The Märchenwiese (Fairy Meadow), a 20-minute walk away, is an alpine meadow surrounded by wooded walls of remarkable serenity.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Combined gorge + Bodental Valley as a full-day excursion. Bring hiking shoes with non-slip soles—the Tscheppaschlucht trail is permanently wet. Picnicking possible on the Märchenwiese lawns.

⏱ 60 min
$$$10Berghaus auf 1200m Höhe
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 45min

Dreilandereck

★★☆☆☆
1

The Dreiländereck is the geographical meeting point of Austria, Italy, and Slovenia, accessible by chairlift from Arnoldstein in 15 minutes. From the upper station, the panorama extends simultaneously across three countries: the Carinthian Alps, the Friulian Dolomites, and Slovenian mountains.

⏱ 75 min
2

Several hiking trails depart from the upper station: easy ridge walks or full-day hikes for the more athletic. The refuge chalet terrace serves Jausen (alpine snacks) with views of peaks in three countries.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Dreiländereck chairlift is included in the Kärnten Card. Open from late May to late September. The Kärnten Card provides access to over 100 Carinthian attractions—cost-effective after 4-5 uses.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.4Hotel Marko
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 35min

Dobratsch

★★★☆☆
1

The Villacher Alpenstrasse climbs 16.5 km to 1,732 meters on the Dobratsch massif, with designated viewpoints over all the Carinthian lakes. The Alpine Botanical Garden, at mid-route, catalogs over 600 high-mountain plant species. The new Rote Wand viewpoint offers exceptional panoramic views over Villach and the Karawanken range.

⏱ 90 min
2

The hike from the terminal parking lot to the Dobratsch summit (2,166 m) takes 1.5 hours. The panoramic summit restaurant serves alpine cuisine and regional beers with all of Carinthia spread out before you in clear weather.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Evening descent to Warmbad Villach thermal baths (KärntenTherme) for a wellness session after the day at altitude. The natural thermal springs at 29°C and the panoramic outdoor pools are particularly appreciated at sunset.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.5Ferienhaus Tolazzi

Nassfeld

★★★★☆
1

Nassfeld-Pressegger See is a mountain region on the Austrian-Italian border, an outdoor sports paradise in all seasons. Pressegger See, a natural thermal lake at 560 m altitude, offers swimming in warmer waters than most alpine lakes. In winter, Nassfeld is one of Carinthia's largest ski resorts with 110 km of slopes.

⏱ 75 min
2

The ascent to Gartnerkofel (2,195 m) by cable car then on foot opens onto an exceptional border panorama: on one side Carinthia and its lakes, on the other the Italian Friulian Dolomites. In clear weather, the view extends to the Adriatic.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Hiking or mountain biking on Nassfeld massif's marked trails. Marked routes range from family-friendly valley-floor walks to demanding ridge hikes. The downhill mountain bike trails from Gartnerkofel are among Carinthia's most technical.

⏱ 120 min
$$$9.2Hapimag Ferienwohnungen Sonnleitn
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 1h10

Wolayersee

★★★☆☆
1

Wolayersee is one of Carinthia's most beautiful mountain lakes, perched at altitude on the Austrian-Italian border in the Carnic Alps. The lake is accessible after a 3-hour hike from Hubertuskapelle chapel (trailhead with parking). The crystal-clear blue-green water, alpine meadows, and surrounding peaks compose a tableau of striking beauty. A lakeside refuge offers food and accommodation.

⏱ 90 min
2

Hubertuskapelle chapel, at the trail's start, merits a stop before beginning the ascent. This small 18th-century alpine chapel is a traditional pilgrimage site for regional hunters and shepherds.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Round-trip hike Hubertuskapelle - Wolayersee: 3 hours up, 2.5 hours down, moderate elevation gain. Arrive early for parking (limited spaces). Picnic or meal at the lake refuge. Bring hiking boots and warm clothing—the lake sits above 1,800 m.

⏱ 360 min
$$$9.4Berggasthof - Pension Lahnerhof
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h05

Weissensee

★★★☆☆
1

Weissensee is Carinthia's highest (930 m) and most pristine natural lake, in the Carnic Alps. Stretching 11.6 km long, only one-third of its shores are accessible—the rest remains completely wild and protected. Water quality is exceptional, with underwater visibility reaching 10 meters in summer, making it one of Europe's clearest lakes.

⏱ 60 min
2

In winter, Weissensee transforms into one of Europe's largest natural skating rinks with 6.2 km² of frozen surface. The natural ice reaches 40 to 50 cm thick and hosts the annual Weissensee Skating Marathon, one of Europe's oldest and most popular.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Swimming, kayaking, and hiking on the wilder, quieter eastern shore. The western shore, around Techendorf village, gathers hotels and developed beaches. The lake circuit on foot or by bike is a half-day excursion revealing the contrasts between both shores.

⏱ 120 min

Hike to Granattor from Techendorf—marked 3-hour trail offering plunging views of the entire lake from the Carnic Alps ridges.

⏱ 180 min
$$$9.6Lottas Lodge am Weissensee - Exklusive Ferienwohnung mit privatem Seezugang
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Kreuzeck

★★★☆☆
1

The Kreuzeckbahn is one of Austria's last rack railways, departing from Spittal an der Drau to climb in minutes to the alpine pastures of the Kreuzeck massif. This historic railway offers a nostalgic journey through spectacular alpine landscapes, accessible to all without special equipment.

⏱ 60 min
2

Spittal an der Drau, at the cable car base, possesses one of Austria's most beautiful Renaissance palaces: Schloss Porcia (16th century), with its inner courtyard featuring three superimposed arcaded galleries. The district museum it houses traces Upper Carinthia's history.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Family-friendly hiking from Kreuzeckbahn's upper station on marked trails between alpine pastures and forests. Views extend to Lakes Millstätter See and Weissensee below. The Kreuzeckbahn is included in the Kärnten Card.

⏱ 120 min
$$$8.6Gasthof Post
🚗Drive to next stop: 25 km · 30min

Flattach

★★★☆☆
1

Raggaschlucht, in Hohe Tauern National Park near Flattach, is one of Carinthia's most spectacular gorges. Wooden walkways guide visitors along the river through successive rapids and waterfalls, on an 800-meter course ascending to the main waterfall. The confined walls and vegetation of ferns and mosses create a primeval forest atmosphere.

⏱ 75 min
2

Groppensteinschlucht, a few kilometers away, is a 2.5-km gorge with wooden trails alongside the wild river. The easy route suits families and takes 1.5 to 2 hours. In season, nighttime visits allow you to discover the beautifully illuminated gorge after dark.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Both gorges can be visited on the same day. Waterproof or hiking shoes essential—the walkways are constantly sprayed. Open from May to October.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.6Chalet am Bachl 2

Molltaler Gletscher

★★★★☆
1

Mölltal Glacier, at 3,122 m altitude on the Carinthia-Salzburg border, is accessible by a unique combination: an underground cable car (Glacier Express) carved through the mountain, followed by a second cable car. Around the glacier, intensely turquoise lakes contrast with gray rock walls and blue ice.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Guided glacier hike in summer on marked trails around the glacier. Views of the Hohe Tauern peaks from 3,000 m are among the most spectacular accessible without mountaineering. The cable car is included in the Kärnten Card.

⏱ 120 min
$$$9.6Chalet am Bachl 2
🚗Drive to next stop: 65 km · 1h15

Heiligenblut

★★★★☆
1

The Grossglockner High Alpine Road (Grossglockner Hochalpenstraße) is one of the world's most beautiful scenic routes. This 48-km itinerary winds through Hohe Tauern National Park with 36 designated viewpoints, reaching Edelweiss Pass at 2,571 m. Built between 1930 and 1935, it remains one of Austrian civil engineering's great masterpieces.

⏱ 120 min
2

Kaiser-Franz-Josefs-Höhe offers a direct view of the Pasterze Glacier (8.4 km long, the largest glacier in the Eastern Alps) and Grossglockner (3,798 m, Austria's highest peak). Wild marmots, ibex, and golden eagles inhabit the viewpoint surroundings. A funicular descends to the glacier front.

⏱ 90 min
3

Heiligenblut, at the road's foot on the Carinthian side, is a classically beautiful alpine village with its Gothic church and pointed steeple framed by glaciers. The Jungfernsprung waterfall (130 m), visible from the village, takes its name from a medieval legend.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The road is toll (car rate). Open from May to November depending on snowfall. Bring warm clothing even in summer—the temperature difference between valley and pass can exceed 15°C. The Mallnitz visitor center presents the National Park's fauna and flora.

⏱ 60 min
$$$10Chalet Petzeckblick
🚗Drive to next stop: 35 km · 45min

Malta Hochalmstrasse

★★★☆☆
1

The Malta Hochalmstrasse is one of Carinthia's most beautiful scenic roads over 14.4 km. The route follows a succession of hydroelectric dams wedged between vertical walls, with waterfalls cascading from upper glaciers. Traffic lights regulate traffic in the narrowest sections.

⏱ 75 min
2

At the road's terminus, Kölbrein Dam is Austria's highest dam (200 m tall). A glass-floored observation platform cantilevered over the void gives vertiginous views of the lower basin. The hike to Osnabrücker Hütte refuge (2.5 hours one-way) crosses a high hanging valley with waterfalls and scree.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

The road is toll (€6 per person, free with Kärnten Card). Open from May to late October. The ascent to the dam can be replaced by the parallel hiking trail for walking enthusiasts.

⏱ 30 min
$$$9.2Berghotel Malta
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 55min

Millstatter See

★★★☆☆
1

Millstätter See (11.5 km long, 141 m deep) is Carinthia's second-largest lake and one of its most pristine. Surrounded by mountains exceeding 2,000 m and forests of fir and beech reflecting in crystalline water, it supplies drinking water to local communities without treatment—an indicator of its exceptional purity.

⏱ 60 min
2

Millstatt Benedictine Abbey, founded in 1070, is one of Austria's most important Romanesque monastic complexes. The 12th-century cloister presents carved capitals depicting unique biblical and mythological scenes. The library preserves over 30,000 volumes including medieval illuminated manuscripts. Each summer, the abbey church hosts the Millstatt International Concerts.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Cable car ascent to Goldeck (2,142 m) for hikes with panoramic views of the lake from the ridges. The Goldeck Panoramastrasse offers a driving alternative to 1,900 m. Swimming at the lake beaches in late afternoon.

⏱ 120 min
$$$9.9Villa Bella Vita
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 50min

Bad Kleinkirchheim

★★★☆☆
1

Bad Kleinkirchheim is a thermal spa and ski resort at 1,100 m altitude in the Nockberge mountains. Its natural thermal springs emerge at 36°C—discovered in 1834 and used since for their healing properties. The Therme Römerbad and Therme St. Kathrein offer panoramic outdoor pools, alpine saunas, and wellness treatments with mountain views.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Nockalm-Straße traverses the UNESCO Biosphere Park Nockberge over 35 km with 52 bends and 8 designated viewpoints. The rounded, grassy landscapes, sculpted by Quaternary glaciers, harbor over 2,000 plant species—exceptional botanical diversity linked to the Nockberge limestone.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Thermal relaxation after the scenic drive. In winter, Bad Kleinkirchheim offers 103 km of slopes with 25 lifts and regularly hosts Alpine Ski World Cup rounds on the legendary Franz Klammer piste.

⏱ 90 min
$$$9.3Evident Hotel Prägant 4-Superior
