✨ Highlights
  • Suzhou: 9 UNESCO classical gardens listed in 1997, including China's largest (Humble Administrator's Garden, 5 hectares, built 1509)
  • Huangshan: 77 peaks exceeding 1,000m, UNESCO-listed in 1990, defining landscape of Shanshui painting school since Ming Dynasty
  • Hongcun & Xidi: Hui villages founded in 1131, UNESCO-listed in 2000, filming locations for Ang Lee's Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon
  • Nanjing: 35km of Ming ramparts, the world's best-preserved, and memorial to the December 13, 1937 massacre (6 million visitors annually)
📆 Best period

March, April, May, September, October, November

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 12 and 16 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming flowers, ideal weather, moderate crowds, excellent for most regions, comfortable travel.

Fall (September-October): Perfect weather, clear skies, comfortable temperatures, peak tourist season, higher prices, autumn colors beautiful.

Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, rainy season, crowded tourist sites, highest prices, but best for Tibet and western regions.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, fewer crowds, lower prices, perfect for southern regions, harsh in north, unique winter experiences.

Regional Variations: Vast climate diversity, southern subtropical, northern continental, western plateau, plan according to regions visited.

🌍 What to discover in CN?

Ancient civilization meets modern superpower with Great Wall, forbidden cities, and cutting-edge technology. Experience diverse landscapes, incredible cuisine, and 5000-year cultural heritage. The country offers bustling megacities, pristine nature, and traditional arts. China provides overwhelming cultural richness and geographical diversity in rapidly modernizing nation.

📋 Important notes

Avoid July-August: humid heat and peak crowds. An overnight at Huangshan summit is essential for sunrise - book the hotel several weeks in advance. Lotus Peak and West Sea Canyon sections sometimes closed in bad weather. Return flight from Tunxi (TXN airport). The Yangzhou → Wuhu → Huangshan route covers 260 km in two driving segments (100 km + 160 km) with lunch stop in Wuhu.

Shanghai

★★★★★
📍 The Bund Shanghai📍 Yu Garden Shanghai📍 French Concession Shanghai📍 Shanghai Museum📍 Longhua Temple Shanghai
1

The Bund delivers Shanghai's first visual punch: 52 colonial buildings from the 1920s-1930s lined up facing Pudong's forest of skyscrapers. It's the only place where you see Republican China and 21st-century China simultaneously. Come at golden hour or after 9pm when Pudong lights up - the photo is worth the jet lag.

⏱ 60 min
2

Yu Garden (豫园) was designed from 1559 onward for the Pan family during the Ming Dynasty. Its 2 hectares pack Taihu rocks, zigzag pavilions, and lotus ponds into an intricate composition. Arrive at opening time (8:30am) to have the paths nearly to yourself - crowds are dense all day. Don't miss the Exquisite Jade Rock pavilion perched in the middle of the pond.

⏱ 90 min
3

Shanghai Museum (上海博物馆) on People's Square houses one of China's finest collections of bronzes, ceramics, and paintings - over 140,000 pieces. The bronze and Ming furniture galleries alone deserve an hour. Free admission.

⏱ 120 min
4

The French Concession unfolds its century-old plane trees across a few kilometers of quiet streets. It shelters Sun Yat-sen's former residence and Zhou Enlai's home. Wander around Tianzifang - cobblestone lanes converted into galleries and boutiques - then head to Xintiandi to feel the contrast with the rest of the city.

⏱ 90 min
5

Longhua Temple is Shanghai's oldest Buddhist temple, founded according to tradition during the Wu Dynasty in 247 CE. The seven-story octagonal pagoda dates from the Song reconstruction. It's one of the few temples in the city where you still sense genuine religious life - monks, incense, worshippers. Far more authentic than Yu Garden.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Ascend the Shanghai Tower observatory (632m, world's 2nd tallest) or the Oriental Pearl Tower for a 360° view over the megacity. At night, Pudong transforms into a giant circuit board.

⏱ 90 min

Dine in the Xintiandi quarter or along Hongkou's lanes to taste Shanghainese cuisine: hongshao rou (braised pork in soy and sugar), drunken shrimp, hairy crab in season (October-November).

Shanghai

★★★★★

1

Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street stretches 1.2 kilometers of neon and packed crowds all the way to the Bund - tens of thousands of people in the evening, signs everywhere. No architectural interest, but essential for measuring Shanghai's raw energy. A 10-minute walk away, the Bund waterfront offers the perfect contrast.

Jing'an Temple (Temple of Peace and Tranquility) was rebuilt in 1882 on a Tang-era site from the 9th century. The contrast is striking: a golden bronze facade wedged between skyscrapers. Inside, four Burmese jade Buddha statues and a massive 3.6-ton jade piece.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Power Station of Art (PSA) is Shanghai's only public contemporary art museum, housed in a converted 1897 power plant reopened in 2012. Rotating exhibitions of Chinese contemporary artists and a permanent 20th-century collection. The original chimney is preserved within the building.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Take a one-hour Huangpu River cruise to see Shanghai from the water - colonial Bund on one side, Pudong towers on the other. Departs from Shiliupu pier. The 6:30pm cruise offers the day-to-night transition - the most photogenic.

⏱ 60 min

Book your Shanghai → Suzhou train tickets (high-speed rail, 25 min) for tomorrow morning via the 12306 app. Trains depart every 15 to 30 minutes between 6am and 10pm from Shanghai Hongqiao Station.

Suzhou

Garden capital of the Jiangnan where silk and stone have shaped beauty for a millennium

📍 Humble Administrator's Garden Suzhou📍 Suzhou Museum I.M. Pei📍 Lion Grove Garden Suzhou📍 Pingjiang Road Suzhou
1

The Humble Administrator's Garden (拙政园), built in 1509 during the Ming Dynasty by disgraced official Wang Xianchen, is Suzhou's largest classical garden - 5 hectares, two-thirds covered in water. Pavilions seem to float between ponds and lotus flowers. UNESCO-listed in 1997. Arrive at 7:30am to beat the crowds.

⏱ 120 min
2

The neighboring Suzhou Museum, designed by I.M. Pei and opened in 2006, is itself a masterpiece. Born in Suzhou, Pei reinterpreted garden architecture in refined modernism: white walls, dark angles, geometric roofs. Collections of bronzes, ceramics, and Ming-Qing paintings. Free admission.

⏱ 90 min
3

Lion Grove Garden (狮子林), built in 1342 as a retreat for Zen Buddhist monks, is famous for its labyrinth of Taihu rock formations - dozens of caves and passages that seem from another planet. Easy to get lost. The painter Ni Zan stayed here in the 14th century and created several scrolls depicting it.

⏱ 60 min
4

Pingjiang Road (平江路) is the old town's best-preserved lane - one kilometer along Pingjiang Canal, white facades and gray tiled roofs, arched bridges, silk shops and cafes in interior courtyards. Come in late afternoon when the low-angle light plays on the water.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit a silk workshop: Suzhou was for centuries China's leading silk-producing city. Several factories offer tours with weaving demonstrations and double-sided embroidery - a technique visible from both sides with no reverse, a national specialty.

⏱ 60 min

Dine on the lanes around Guanqian Street (观前街), Suzhou's gastronomic artery. Try: zhurou tongyuan (glutinous rice balls with pork), shumo (crab dumplings), eight-treasure rice soup.

⏱ 90 min
Stop 4

Suzhou

Garden capital of the Jiangnan where silk and stone have shaped beauty for a millennium

📍 Master of the Nets Garden Suzhou📍 Tiger Hill Suzhou leaning pagoda📍 Lingering Garden Suzhou📍 Shantang Canal Suzhou
1

The Master of the Nets Garden (网师园), founded in 1180 during the Southern Song Dynasty, is Suzhou's smallest classical garden - less than half a hectare - and probably the most refined. Its designer Shi Zhengzhi multiplied depth effects so each meter seems doubled. A design masterpiece to be savored slowly.

⏱ 75 min
2

Tiger Hill (虎丘) rises above the supposed tomb of King He Lü, founder of Suzhou in the 5th century BCE. At the 36-meter summit, Yunyan Pagoda (961 CE) leans 2.3 degrees - the Chinese call it "the Leaning Tower of the East." Come in the morning before the tour groups arrive.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Lingering Garden (留园, Liuyuan) is one of China's four greatest gardens. Its distinctive feature: a 700-meter covered gallery connected by over 200 openings cut in varied shapes (vase, leaf, fan) that frame the garden like moving paintings.

⏱ 75 min
4

Shantang Street (山塘街) runs 3.5 kilometers along the canal dredged by poet-governor Bai Juyi in the 9th century. Called "China's first ancient street" by local tradition, it lines up silk shops, shadow puppet theaters, and teahouses. In the evening, red lanterns on the water create the effect of an animated woodblock print.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Attend an evening kunqu opera performance in one of the gardens. Kunqu is China's oldest opera form, born in Suzhou in the 16th century - it preceded and influenced Beijing Opera. Intimate chamber performances in pavilions: intimate setting, natural acoustics.

⏱ 90 min

Take an evening boat ride on Suzhou's canals from Shantang pier. The navigation passes illuminated facades and goes under stone bridges. Duration about 1 hour.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 20 km · 30min
Stop 5

Tongli

Five islands threaded by water and crossed by scholars' bridges since the Song Dynasty

📍 Tuisi Garden Tongli UNESCO📍 Three Bridges Tongli📍 Water town canals Tongli
1

Tongli (同里) is the region's most elegant water town. Tuisi Garden (退思园), UNESCO-listed in 2000, was designed in 1885 by Ren Lansheng, a disgraced official - its name means "reflecting while retiring." Unique feature: the buildings descend eastward following a progression toward the water, as if the entire composition were sliding into the pond.

⏱ 75 min
2

Tongli's canals connect five islands - a configuration unique in the region. Three historic bridges (Taiping, Jili, Changqing) span the waters face-to-face: newlyweds would cross them in a precise order to ensure happiness, fortune, and longevity. The tradition lives on.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take a ride in a round-roofed boat through the canals - the navigation passes under bridges and alongside houses built directly on the water. The boatman paddles standing at the stern, traditional style.

⏱ 40 min

Zhouzhuang

Where twin bridges from the 1500s frame a canvas that made the village famous overnight

📍 Twin Bridges Zhouzhuang 16th century📍 Shen House Zhouzhuang📍 Zhouzhuang canals
1

Zhouzhuang (周庄) owes its fame to the Twin Bridges (双桥) - two adjacent 16th-century bridges forming together the motif of an ancient key. In 1984, painter Chen Yifei created a canvas of them that sold for $550,000 in New York, and Zhouzhuang became famous overnight. Arrive early in the morning before tour buses.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Shen House (沈厅), built in the early 18th century, is the most complete example of a Jiangnan merchant residence: seven successive courtyards, about 100 rooms, separate kitchen accessible from the river via a water gate - deliveries arrived directly by boat.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Explore the village's interior lanes, away from the main circuit. The most authentic houses are in the side alleys where residents bring out their chairs in late afternoon - ideal time for street photography.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 7

Hangzhou

The city Marco Polo called the world's most beautiful, wrapped around a legendary lake

📍 West Lake Hangzhou UNESCO📍 Leifeng Pagoda Hangzhou📍 Bai Causeway Hangzhou📍 Baochu Hill Hangzhou
1

West Lake (西湖) has been UNESCO-listed since 2011 - one of the rare designed landscapes to receive this recognition. It covers 6.39 km², punctuated by three artificial islands built during the Song Dynasty (960-1279). The Bai Causeway, 1.5 km long, bears the name of the Tang poet who had the lake dredged when he was magistrate of Hangzhou in the 9th century. Marco Polo described the city as "the most beautiful in the world."

⏱ 180 min
2

Leifeng Pagoda (雷峰塔) rises on the lake's south shore, rebuilt in 2002 as an identical replica of the Song original (977 CE) that collapsed in 1924. The new tower incorporates the ancient foundations, visible on the ground floor through a glass floor. From the top, the view over the lake and Su Causeway is Hangzhou's finest.

⏱ 60 min
3

Baochu Hill (宝叔岭) north of the lake offers the most classic climb - 45 minutes from the water's edge to Baochu Pagoda (renovated in 1933). From the summit, the entire lake spreads beneath your feet: the five causeways, the islands, and the hills on the opposite shore.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Rent a bike and do the full West Lake loop (15 km). The bike paths are flat, well-marked, follow the water almost everywhere. It's the best way to see the lake without getting stuck with walking groups.

⏱ 150 min

Dine on the lake shores in the Nanshan Road area. Try: Dongpo pork (pork braised in rice wine, recipe attributed to poet Su Dongpo, magistrate here in the 11th century), West Lake vinegar fish, and cooked lotus.

⏱ 90 min
Stop 8

Hangzhou

The city Marco Polo called the world's most beautiful, wrapped around a legendary lake

📍 Lingyin Temple Hangzhou📍 Feilai Peak rock carvings Hangzhou📍 Longjing tea plantations Hangzhou📍 Su Causeway Hangzhou
1

Lingyin Temple (灵隐寺), founded in 326 CE by Indian monk Huili, is one of China's ten largest Buddhist temples - reconstructed sixteen times. The adjacent Feilai Peak (飞来峰, Peak that Flew Here) is covered with 470 rock carvings made between the 10th and 14th centuries, including a particularly remarkable bas-relief Laughing Buddha.

⏱ 120 min
2

If you leave early this morning, the Longjing tea plantations (龙井) 4 km west of the lake await you. Longjing (Dragon Well) is China's most prized green tea - leaves hand-flattened in cast-iron woks. In April, it's Mingqian season, the first harvest before the Qingming Festival, the finest and most expensive.

⏱ 90 min
3

Su Causeway (苏堤), 2.8 km long, crosses the lake from shore to shore with six arched bridges and weeping willows. Built under the governance of poet Su Dongpo in the 11th century with silt from dredging the lake, it's inscribed within the UNESCO perimeter. The late afternoon walk is one of Hangzhou's most pleasant.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Longjing tea tasting at a producer's in Longjing or Meijiawu villages. Several teahouses set among the tea bushes offer guided tastings with views over the cultivation rows. Much cheaper than in the city.

⏱ 90 min

Take a boat ride on the lake to reach Three Pools Mirroring the Moon Island (三潭印月) - three small stone towers built in the water during the Song Dynasty. The crossing takes 20 minutes from Hubin pier.

⏱ 60 min

Wuzhen

A water town that comes alive after dark when lanterns turn canals to liquid gold

📍 Wuzhen Xizha West Quarter night lanterns📍 Wuzhen Dongzha East Quarter📍 Mao Dun's Former Residence Wuzhen📍 Indigo dyeing workshop Wuzhen
1

Wuzhen West (西栅, Xizha) is the most successful part of the water town: renovated in the 2000s, it has preserved rare architectural coherence - gray wooden houses, black tiles, covered galleries above the water. In the evening, red lanterns light up along the entire length of the main canal and the water surface becomes a mirror. This is the hour not to miss - ticket valid until 10:30pm.

⏱ 180 min
2

Writer Mao Dun's house (茅盾故居), in the East Quarter, is the birthplace of the revolutionary novelist (1896-1981), author of Midnight. Opened to the public in 1985, the rooms preserve period furniture - a window into early 20th-century bourgeois Jiangnan life.

⏱ 45 min
3

Wuzhen's indigo dyeing workshop is one of the region's oldest. Hundreds of indigo fabric squares dry hanging in garlands above the East Quarter lanes - an unmissable photographic image. The artisan demonstrates the tie-resist process before vat dyeing.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take a covered boat (乌篷船) on Wuzhen's canals in late afternoon to navigate between the houses before sunset. Depart from Xizha's central pier, less crowded than the main dock.

⏱ 45 min

Watch the shadow puppet show (皮影戏) and opera performance in the East Quarter - 20 to 30-minute shows, repeated several times throughout the day. Schedules are posted at the entrance.

⏱ 30 min

Stop 10

Nanjing

Where 600-year-old walls encircle a city that carries the weight of history

📍 Nanjing Massacre Memorial Jiangdongmen📍 Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum Nanjing📍 Ming Xiaoling Tomb Spirit Way Nanjing
1

The Nanjing Massacre Memorial (侵华日军南京大屠杀遇难同胞纪念馆) is a difficult but essential stop for understanding contemporary China. On December 13, 1937, the Japanese army took the city; for six weeks, official Chinese estimates count 300,000 dead. Built in 1985 on the site of the Jiangdongmen mass grave, the memorial displays 208 bones exhumed in situ and over 1,000 objects. Six million visitors per year.

⏱ 120 min
2

Ming Xiaoling Tomb (明孝陵), UNESCO-listed in 2003, is the burial site of Zhu Yuanzhang, founder of the Ming Dynasty (reign 1368-1398). The Spirit Way is one of China's most impressive: 800 meters lined with 24 monumental stone statues - elephants, lions, camels, tigers. These guardians have been watching for over 600 years.

⏱ 90 min
3

Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum (中山陵) imposes with its scale: 392 steps from the entrance arch to the tomb at the summit of Purple Mountain. Built between 1926 and 1929, it mixes Beaux-Arts architecture and nationalist symbolism. Sun Yat-sen (1866-1925), founder of the Republic of China, rests here.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Stroll through Zhongshan Park (紫金山) between the mausoleum and Ming tomb - forest of plane trees and cypresses, peaceful trails, artificial lake. One of China's finest urban walks, 5 km from downtown.

⏱ 60 min

Dine in the Xinjiekou district. Nanjing specialties: ya xue fen si (vermicelli with duck blood), pan mian (crab stir-fried noodles), salted duck - Nanjing's salted duck has been a national institution since the Ming Dynasty.

⏱ 90 min
Stop 11

Nanjing

Where 600-year-old walls encircle a city that carries the weight of history

📍 Ming City Wall Nanjing Zhonghua Gate📍 Confucius Temple Nanjing Fuzi Miao📍 Qinhuai River Nanjing night📍 Xuanwu Lake Nanjing
1

Nanjing's Ming city walls are the world's best-preserved: 35.3 km on the original layout, with 25 km still standing. Built between 1366 and 1393, their bricks bear the kiln's name and the foreman's - a unique quality control measure. The section around Zhonghua Gate is the most accessible for walking the ramparts.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Confucius Temple area (夫子庙, Fuzi Miao) runs along the Qinhuai River - the former imperial examination district where thousands of scholar candidates came to try their luck for centuries. The evening atmosphere with red lanterns and boat rides on the Qinhuai is genuinely pleasant.

⏱ 60 min
3

Xuanwu Lake (玄武湖), at the foot of the northern Ming walls, is a 4.73 km² park in the heart of the city. Its five islands connected by causeways served as training grounds for the Ming imperial fleet. Pleasant walk away from tourist bustle.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb onto the Ming wall ramparts in the evening from Zhonghua Gate - the night view over the city and illuminated defenses is striking. The section toward the Qinhuai River takes 45 minutes on foot.

⏱ 60 min

Take an evening boat on the Qinhuai River - the Confucius Temple quarter viewed from the water, with its lanterns and reflected pavilions, is Nanjing's nocturnal postcard. Departs from Fuzi Miao pier.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 12

Yangzhou

A city built on salt fortunes and morning rituals refined over dynasties

📍 Slender West Lake Yangzhou📍 He Garden Yangzhou📍 Ge Garden Yangzhou📍 Dongguan Street Yangzhou
1

Slender West Lake (瘦西湖) extends over 4.3 km of lakes and canals linked in a chain, landscaped during the Qing Dynasty. Its name comes from an 18th-century poet who saw it as a graceful variant of Hangzhou's West Lake. The Fishing Platform (钓鱼台) is the most recognizable image: its two circular openings frame the Five-Pavilion Pagoda like a double lens.

⏱ 120 min
2

He Garden (何园), built in 1883 during the Qing Dynasty, is the most refined of Yangzhou's private gardens. Its two-story covered gallery runs 1,500 meters and connects all pavilions while offering framed views at every angle - an invention unique in China.

⏱ 75 min
3

Ge Garden (个园), founded in 1818, is famous for its rocks organized by four seasons - each section represents a season's landscape with different stones. The "summer mountain" in Taihu rock pierced with a hundred holes symbolizes water and coolness.

⏱ 60 min
4

Dongguan Street (东关街) - 1.1 km of preserved Qing merchant houses, antique shops, and local specialties. Yangzhou was for centuries one of China's wealthiest cities thanks to the salt trade. The shaobing bakeries (sesame flatbreads) and gansi vendors (shredded tofu) are unmissable.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rise early for breakfast at one of Yangzhou's traditional teahouses (茶馆). The culture of elaborate breakfast has been an institution since the Tang era - the fabulously wealthy salt merchants of the 18th century spent fortunes on morning dim sum. Fu Chun Cha She is the city's oldest.

⏱ 60 min

Walk along the banks of the Grand Canal (大运河) that crosses Yangzhou - UNESCO-listed in 2014, 1,794 km long. This is where the canal met the maritime route south during the Sui and Tang dynasties.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 100 km · 1h20
Stop 13

Wuhu

A river city on the Yangtze where Jiangsu ends and the mountains of Anhui begin

📍 Guangji Temple Wuhu Tang Dynasty📍 Zheshan Park Wuhu Yangtze📍 Wuhu Yangtze Bridge
1

Wuhu (芜湖) marks the gateway to deep Anhui on the southern bank of the Yangtze. Guangji Temple (广济寺), founded in 894 during the Tang Dynasty, is nicknamed "Little Jiuhua" - its 88 steps lead to the halls of Maitreya, the Great Pagoda, and the Medicine Buddha. River view from the hill. A brief but solid stop between Jiangsu's China and Anhui's mountains.

⏱ 45 min
2

Zheshan Park (赭山公园) is Wuhu's emblematic hill, overlooking the Yangtze. The trails that circle it pass historical remnants and offer a panorama of the river and city. Pleasant for a 45-minute walk between drives.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Lunch on the Yangtze banks in the Jinghu district - fresh freshwater fish, a regional specialty. Wuhu has been nicknamed "the city of fish and rice" since antiquity.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 160 km · 2h10
Stop 14

Huangshan Montagne

Granite pinnacles rising through clouds that inspired centuries of ink painters

📍 Yungu Cable Car Huangshan📍 Bright Summit Guangmingding Huangshan📍 White Goose Ridge Huangshan📍 Famous Pines Huangshan📍 West Sea Hotel Huangshan summit
1

Lotus Peak (莲花峰) reaches 1,864 m - Huangshan's highest summit. The mountain was named by imperial decree in 747 CE; legend has it that Yellow Emperor Huangdi came here seeking the elixir of immortality 2,600 years BCE. Since the 16th century, it's been the favorite subject of the Shanshui painting school. UNESCO-listed in 1990.

⏱ 90 min
2

White Goose Ridge (白鹅岭) is directly accessible from the Yungu cable car on the east side. From there, a trail connects to Bright Summit in 2 hours via the ridges. It's along this route that you'll find Huangshan's most famous pines: Welcoming Guest Pine, Couple Pine, Umbrella Pine - each has a name, a silhouette from classical painting.

⏱ 120 min
3

Bright Summit (光明顶, 1,860 m) is the logistical hub for summit stays: this is where hotels, the weather center, and high-altitude restaurants concentrate. Book the West Sea Hotel several weeks in advance. The flat summit allows a clear 360° view even in light mist. Bring warm clothing even in May - 10°C at night is common.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take the Yungu cable car on the east side then hike to connect Bright Summit, Lotus Peak, and White Goose Ridge - count 5 to 6 hours for this classic circuit. The trails are in excellent condition, with steps carved into the rock on steep passages.

⏱ 360 min

Set yourself up at sunset on Bright Summit or Celestial Capital Peak. The late-day light tints the granite peaks from gold to orange - Huangshan's other magic hour, besides sunrise.

⏱ 60 min
Stop 15

Huangshan Montagne

Granite pinnacles rising through clouds that inspired centuries of ink painters

📍 Sunrise Huangshan sea of clouds📍 West Sea Grand Canyon Huangshan📍 Huangshan Hot Springs Tangkou📍 Celestial Capital Peak Huangshan
1

The West Sea Grand Canyon (西海大峡谷) is Huangshan's most spectacular hike - a 5-hour loop that descends into the gorges then ascends by monorail. The site's most dramatic granite formations concentrate here, with stone columns emerging from the clouds. The West Sea Pavilion (built in 1935) offers the widest view over the entire canyon.

⏱ 300 min
2

Huangshan Hot Springs (黄山温泉), at the foot of the mountain on the Yungu side, let you decompress after two days of hiking. The water emerges at 42°C and the outdoor pools face the forest.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Wake at 5am for sunrise from Bright Summit or Celestial Capital Peak. The sea of clouds below the ridges is the spectacle that defines Huangshan - it's why Ming painters would set up here for months. Best period: November to May, after rain.

⏱ 90 min

Descend the mountain by cable car after sunrise, retrieve your luggage at Tangkou, and drive toward Hongcun (25 km, 40 min). Lunch in Tangkou village before departure.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 16

Hongcun

An ox-shaped village frozen in Ming perfection, immortalized on screen and canvas

📍 Moon Pond Hongcun📍 Hongcun Village Yixian📍 Lexu Hall Hongcun📍 Hui Architecture Hongcun UNESCO
1

Hongcun (宏村), founded in 1131 during the Song Dynasty by the Wang family, is designed in the shape of an ox. The Moon Pond (月沼) at the center, dug in 1403, is the village's most photographed setting: white-walled, gray-roofed Hui houses reflect in the still water. This is where Ang Lee filmed several scenes of Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon (2000). UNESCO-listed the same year.

⏱ 90 min
2

Hongcun's 150 Ming-Qing residences are the region's finest collection of Hui architecture. Lexu Hall and Jingde Tang are open to visitors: carved bracket roof frames, light-well courtyards (天井), blue stone paving. Explore the side lanes for houses still inhabited.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Set yourself up by the Moon Pond before 7am with a sketchbook or camera. Chinese amateur painters are there before you - genuine open-air artistic culture at Hongcun. It's also the hour when residents go about their business before the influx of tour groups.

⏱ 60 min

Xidi

Quieter than its neighbor but just as beautiful - merchant halls in a valley of ancestors

📍 Xidi Village Yixian📍 Qingyi Memorial Arch 1578 Xidi📍 Hui Houses Xidi UNESCO
1

Xidi (西递), founded during the Song Dynasty and developed by the Hu family from the 14th to 18th centuries, is Anhui's best-preserved Hui village. The Qingyi Memorial Arch (1578), carved on three levels, celebrates a Hu official's success in the imperial examinations. Behind it, 124 Ming-Qing residences organize along lanes paved with blue flagstones. UNESCO-listed in 2000.

⏱ 75 min
2

Xidi is quieter and less crowded than Hongcun - tour groups favor the Moon Pond. Get lost in the side lanes behind the merchant houses: ochre rammed-earth walls pierced with geometric windows, dead-ends closed by raw wood doors. The atmosphere of a village still alive.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Look for a Chinese ink or calligraphy workshop in Xidi's lanes. Huizhou ink has been produced in the region for 2,000 years and remains the reference for traditional calligraphers nationwide.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 18

Tunxi

Where the last Ming street meets the modern world at the foot of the Yellow Mountains

📍 Old Street Laojie Tunxi Huangshan City📍 Huangshan Tunxi Airport TXN
1

Tunxi's Old Street (老街) stretches 1.3 km along the Xin'an River: continuous Ming-Qing facades, antique shops, tea and traditional painting merchants. This is where collectors from all over China come to find paintings from the Xin'an school and Huizhou inkstones. A final easy stroll before the return flight from TXN airport (1h to Shanghai).

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Final stop at an Old Street teahouse to taste Keemun tea (祁门红茶) - Anhui's most renowned black tea, produced 60 km away in Qimen County. Also a good moment to buy Chinese ink, traditional brushes, or Xin'an school paintings.
