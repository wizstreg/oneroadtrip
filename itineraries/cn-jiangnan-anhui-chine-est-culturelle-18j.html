The Bund delivers Shanghai's first visual punch: 52 colonial buildings from the 1920s-1930s lined up facing Pudong's forest of skyscrapers. 4

The French Concession unfolds its century-old plane trees across a few kilometers of quiet streets. It shelters Sun Yat-sen's former residence and Zhou Enlai's home. Wander around Tianzifang - cobblestone lanes converted into galleries and boutiques - then head to Xintiandi to feel the contrast with the rest of the city.

⏱ 90 min

Suzhou

Garden capital of the Jiangnan where silk and stone have shaped beauty for a millennium

★★★★★
1

The Humble Administrator's Garden (拙政园), built in 1509 during the Ming Dynasty by disgraced official Wang Xianchen, is Suzhou's largest classical garden - 5 hectares, two-thirds covered in water. Pavilions seem to float between ponds and lotus flowers. UNESCO-listed in 1997. Arrive at 7:30am to beat the crowds.

⏱ 120 min
2

The neighboring Suzhou Museum, designed by I.M. Pei and opened in 2006, is itself a masterpiece. Born in Suzhou, Pei reinterpreted garden architecture in refined modernism: white walls, dark angles, geometric roofs. Collections of bronzes, ceramics, and Ming-Qing paintings. Free admission.

⏱ 90 min
3

Lion Grove Garden (狮子林), built in 1342 as a retreat for Zen Buddhist monks, is famous for its labyrinth of Taihu rock formations - dozens of caves and passages that seem from another planet. Easy to get lost. The painter Ni Zan stayed here in the 14th century and created several scrolls depicting it.

⏱ 60 min
4

Pingjiang Road (平江路) is the old town's best-preserved lane - one kilometer along Pingjiang Canal, white facades and gray tiled roofs, arched bridges, silk shops and cafes in interior courtyards. Come in late afternoon when the low-angle light plays on the water.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit a silk workshop: Suzhou was for centuries China's leading silk-producing city. Several factories offer tours with weaving demonstrations and double-sided embroidery - a technique visible from both sides with no reverse, a national specialty.

⏱ 60 min

Dine on the lanes around Guanqian Street (观前街), Suzhou's gastronomic artery. Try: zhurou tongyuan (glutinous rice balls with pork), shumo (crab dumplings), eight-treasure rice soup.

Suzhou

Garden capital of the Jiangnan where silk and stone have shaped beauty for a millennium

★★★★★
1

The Master of the Nets Garden (网师园), founded in 1180 during the Southern Song Dynasty, is Suzhou's smallest classical garden - less than half a hectare - and probably the most refined. Its designer Shi Zhengzhi multiplied depth effects so each meter seems doubled. A design masterpiece to be savored slowly.

⏱ 75 min
2

Tiger Hill (虎丘) rises above the supposed tomb of King He Lü, founder of Suzhou in the 5th century BCE. At the 36-meter summit, Yunyan Pagoda (961 CE) leans 2.3 degrees - the Chinese call it "the Leaning Tower of the East." Come in the morning before the tour groups arrive.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Lingering Garden (留园, Liuyuan) is one of China's four greatest gardens. Its distinctive feature: a 700-meter covered gallery connected by over 200 openings cut in varied shapes (vase, leaf, fan) that frame the garden like moving paintings.

⏱ 75 min
4

Shantang Street (山塘街) runs 3.5 kilometers along the canal dredged by poet-governor Bai Juyi in the 9th century. Called "China's first ancient street" by local tradition, it lines up silk shops, shadow puppet theaters, and teahouses. In the evening, red lanterns on the water create the effect of an animated woodblock print.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Attend an evening kunqu opera performance in one of the gardens. Kunqu is China's oldest opera form, born in Suzhou in the 16th century - it preceded and influenced Beijing Opera. Intimate chamber performances in pavilions: intimate setting, natural acoustics.

⏱ 90 min

Take an evening boat ride on Suzhou's canals from Shantang pier. The navigation passes illuminated facades and goes under stone bridges. Duration about 1 hour.

Tongli

Five islands threaded by water and crossed by scholars' bridges since the Song Dynasty

★★★☆☆
1

Tongli (同里) is the region's most elegant water town. Tuisi Garden (退思园), UNESCO-listed in 2000, was designed in 1885 by Ren Lansheng, a disgraced official - its name means "reflecting while retiring." Unique feature: the buildings descend eastward following a progression toward the water, as if the entire composition were sliding into the pond.

⏱ 75 min
2

Tongli's canals connect five islands - a configuration unique in the region. Three historic bridges (Taiping, Jili, Changqing) span the waters face-to-face: newlyweds would cross them in a precise order to ensure happiness, fortune, and longevity. The tradition lives on.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take a ride in a round-roofed boat through the canals - the navigation passes under bridges and alongside houses built directly on the water. The boatman paddles standing at the stern, traditional style.

Zhouzhuang

Zhouzhuang (周庄) owes its fame to the Twin Bridges (双桥) - two adjacent 16th-century bridges forming together the motif of an ancient key. The Shen House (沈厅), built in the early 18th century, is the most complete example of a Jiangnan merchant residence: seven successive courtyards, about 100 rooms, separate kitchen accessible from the river via a water gate - deliveries arrived directly by boat.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Explore the village's interior lanes, away from the main circuit. The most authentic houses are in the side alleys where residents bring out their chairs in late afternoon - ideal time for street photography.

Hangzhou

The city Marco Polo called the world's most beautiful, wrapped around a legendary lake

★★★★☆
1

West Lake (西湖) has been UNESCO-listed since 2011 - one of the rare designed landscapes to receive this recognition. It covers 6.39 km², punctuated by three artificial islands built during the Song Dynasty (960-1279). The Bai Causeway, 1.5 km long, bears the name of the Tang poet who had the lake dredged when he was magistrate of Hangzhou in the 9th century. Marco Polo described the city as "the most beautiful in the world."

⏱ 180 min
2

Leifeng Pagoda (雷峰塔) rises on the lake's south shore, rebuilt in 2002 as an identical replica of the Song original (977 CE) that collapsed in 1924. The new tower incorporates the ancient foundations, visible on the ground floor through a glass floor. From the top, the view over the lake and Su Causeway is Hangzhou's finest.

⏱ 60 min
3

Baochu Hill (宝叔岭) north of the lake offers the most classic climb - 45 minutes from the water's edge to Baochu Pagoda (renovated in 1933). From the summit, the entire lake spreads beneath your feet: the five causeways, the islands, and the hills on the opposite shore.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Rent a bike and do the full West Lake loop (15 km). The bike paths are flat, well-marked, follow the water almost everywhere. It's the best way to see the lake without getting stuck with walking groups.

⏱ 150 min

Dine on the lake shores in the Nanshan Road area. Try: Dongpo pork (pork braised in rice wine, recipe attributed to poet Su Dongpo, magistrate here in the 11th century), West Lake vinegar fish, and cooked lotus.

1

The Nanjing Massacre Memorial (侵华日军南京大屠杀遇难同胞纪念馆) is a difficult but essential stop for understanding contemporary China. Built in 1985 on the site of the Jiangdongmen mass grave, the memorial displays 208 bones exhumed in situ and over 1,000 objects. Six million visitors per year.

⏱ 120 min
2

⏱ 90 min
3

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Stroll through Zhongshan Park (紫金山) between the mausoleum and Ming tomb - forest of plane trees and cypresses, peaceful trails, artificial lake. One of China's finest urban walks, 5 km from downtown.

⏱ 60 min

Dine in the Xinjiekou district. Nanjing specialties: ya xue fen si (vermicelli with duck blood), pan mian (crab stir-fried noodles), salted duck - Nanjing's salted duck has been a national institution since the Ming Dynasty.

Yangzhou

A city built on salt fortunes and morning rituals refined over dynasties

★★★★☆
1

Slender West Lake (瘦西湖) extends over 4.3 km of lakes and canals linked in a chain, landscaped during the Qing Dynasty. Its name comes from an 18th-century poet who saw it as a graceful variant of Hangzhou's West Lake. The Fishing Platform (钓鱼台) is the most recognizable image: its two circular openings frame the Five-Pavilion Pagoda like a double lens.

⏱ 120 min
2

He Garden (何园), built in 1883 during the Qing Dynasty, is the most refined of Yangzhou's private gardens. Its two-story covered gallery runs 1,500 meters and connects all pavilions while offering framed views at every angle - an invention unique in China.

⏱ 75 min
3

Ge Garden (个园), founded in 1818, is famous for its rocks organized by four seasons - each section represents a season's landscape with different stones. The "summer mountain" in Taihu rock pierced with a hundred holes symbolizes water and coolness.

⏱ 60 min
4

Dongguan Street (东关街) - 1.1 km of preserved Qing merchant houses, antique shops, and local specialties. Yangzhou was for centuries one of China's wealthiest cities thanks to the salt trade. The shaobing bakeries (sesame flatbreads) and gansi vendors (shredded tofu) are unmissable.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rise early for breakfast at one of Yangzhou's traditional teahouses (茶馆). The culture of elaborate breakfast has been an institution since the Tang era - the fabulously wealthy salt merchants of the 18th century spent fortunes on morning dim sum. Fu Chun Cha She is the city's oldest.

⏱ 60 min

Walk along the banks of the Grand Canal (大运河) that crosses Yangzhou - UNESCO-listed in 2014, 1,794 km long. This is where the canal met the maritime route south during the Sui and Tang dynasties.

⏱ 45 min

Lotus Peak (莲花峰) reaches 1,864 m - Huangshan's highest summit. The mountain was named by imperial decree in 747 CE; legend has it that Yellow Emperor Huangdi came here seeking the elixir of immortality 2,600 years BCE. Since the 16th century, it's been the favorite subject of the Shanshui painting school. UNESCO-listed in 1990.

⏱ 90 min
2

White Goose Ridge (白鹅岭) is directly accessible from the Yungu cable car on the east side. From there, a trail connects to Bright Summit in 2 hours via the ridges. It's along this route that you'll find Huangshan's most famous pines: Welcoming Guest Pine, Couple Pine, Umbrella Pine - each has a name, a silhouette from classical painting.

⏱ 120 min
3

Bright Summit (光明顶, 1,860 m) is the logistical hub for summit stays: this is where hotels, the weather center, and high-altitude restaurants concentrate. Book the West Sea Hotel several weeks in advance. The flat summit allows a clear 360° view even in light mist. Bring warm clothing even in May - 10°C at night is common.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take the Yungu cable car on the east side then hike to connect Bright Summit, Lotus Peak, and White Goose Ridge - count 5 to 6 hours for this classic circuit. The trails are in excellent condition, with steps carved into the rock on steep passages.

⏱ 360 min

Set yourself up at sunset on Bright Summit or Celestial Capital Peak. The late-day light tints the granite peaks from gold to orange - Huangshan's other magic hour, besides sunrise.

⏱ 60 min

1

The West Sea Grand Canyon (西海大峡谷) is Huangshan's most spectacular hike - a 5-hour loop that descends into the gorges then ascends by monorail. The site's most dramatic granite formations concentrate here, with stone columns emerging from the clouds. The West Sea Pavilion (built in 1935) offers the widest view over the entire canyon.

⏱ 300 min
2

Huangshan Hot Springs (黄山温泉), at the foot of the mountain on the Yungu side, let you decompress after two days of hiking. The water emerges at 42°C and the outdoor pools face the forest.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Wake at 5am for sunrise from Bright Summit or Celestial Capital Peak. The sea of clouds below the ridges is the spectacle that defines Huangshan - it's why Ming painters would set up here for months. Best period: November to May, after rain.

⏱ 90 min

Descend the mountain by cable car after sunrise, retrieve your luggage at Tangkou, and drive toward Hongcun (25 km, 40 min). Lunch in Tangkou village before departure.

⏱ 120 min

Hongcun

An ox-shaped village frozen in Ming perfection, immortalized on screen and canvas

★★★☆☆
1

Hongcun (宏村), founded in 1131 during the Song Dynasty by the Wang family, is designed in the shape of an ox. The Moon Pond (月沼) at the center, dug in 1403, is the village's most photographed setting: white-walled, gray-roofed Hui houses reflect in the still water. This is where Ang Lee filmed several scenes of Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon (2000). UNESCO-listed the same year.

⏱ 90 min
2

Hongcun's 150 Ming-Qing residences are the region's finest collection of Hui architecture. Lexu Hall and Jingde Tang are open to visitors: carved bracket roof frames, light-well courtyards (天井), blue stone paving. Explore the side lanes for houses still inhabited.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Set yourself up by the Moon Pond before 7am with a sketchbook or camera. Chinese amateur painters are there before you - genuine open-air artistic culture at Hongcun. It's also the hour when residents go about their business before the influx of tour groups.

⏱ 60 min

Xidi

Quieter than its neighbor but just as beautiful - merchant halls in a valley of ancestors

★★★☆☆
1

Xidi (西递), founded during the Song Dynasty and developed by the Hu family from the 14th to 18th centuries, is Anhui's best-preserved Hui village. The Qingyi Memorial Arch (1578), carved on three levels, celebrates a Hu official's success in the imperial examinations. Behind it, 124 Ming-Qing residences organize along lanes paved with blue flagstones. UNESCO-listed in 2000.

⏱ 75 min
2

Xidi is quieter and less crowded than Hongcun - tour groups favor the Moon Pond. Get lost in the side lanes behind the merchant houses: ochre rammed-earth walls pierced with geometric windows, dead-ends closed by raw wood doors. The atmosphere of a village still alive.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Look for a Chinese ink or calligraphy workshop in Xidi's lanes. Huizhou ink has been produced in the region for 2,000 years and remains the reference for traditional calligraphers nationwide.

Tunxi

Where the last Ming street meets the modern world at the foot of the Yellow Mountains

★★★★☆
1

Tunxi's Old Street (老街) stretches 1.3 km along the Xin'an River: continuous Ming-Qing facades, antique shops, tea and traditional painting merchants. ⏱ 75 min

Activities

Final stop at an Old Street teahouse to taste Keemun tea (祁门红茶) - Anhui's most renowned black tea, produced 60 km away in Qimen County. Also a good moment to buy Chinese ink, traditional brushes, or Xin'an school paintings.
