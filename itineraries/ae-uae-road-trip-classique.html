✨ Highlights
  • Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque is open 7 days a week—free entry, free guided tours at 10am, 11am, and 5pm. Modest dress required, provided at entrance.
  • Liwa desert is accessible in a standard sedan: the road to Tal Moreeb is fully paved. Fuel up in Mezairaa—no station on the following 25 km.
  • Al Ain Oasis (1,200 ha, 147,000 palms) is free and open daily 9am to 5pm. Bike rental at entrance.
  • Kayaking on Hatta Dam costs 60 AED/person for unlimited time, open from 7am. Swimming is prohibited.
  • Al Bidiyah Mosque (circa 1446) is an active place of worship: plan modest dress and remove shoes. No fixed hours—open continuously except during prayers.
📆 Best period

November, December, January, February, March

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 4 and 8 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Peak Season (November-March): Perfect weather with temperatures 20-30°C (68-86°F), ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Most expensive period with highest demand.

Shoulder Season (April-May, October): Still pleasant with warm but manageable temperatures. Good balance of weather and prices, fewer crowds.

Off-Season (June-September): Extremely hot and humid (40-50°C/104-122°F) but lowest prices and indoor attractions are well air-conditioned. Best for shopping and indoor experiences.

Ramadan Period: Varies yearly. Reduced restaurant hours during day, but special evening festivities and cultural experiences.

🌍 What to discover in AE?

Dubai and Abu Dhabi showcase futuristic architecture, luxury shopping, and desert adventures. Experience traditional souks alongside ultra-modern skyscrapers and pristine beaches. Desert safaris, camel racing, and world-class dining create unforgettable memories. The UAE perfectly blends ancient traditions with cutting-edge innovation.

Abu Dhabi

Where the white grace of the Grand Mosque meets the calm of mangrove kayaking

★★★★☆
📍 Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque Abu Dhabi📍 Eastern Mangrove Lagoon National Park kayak Abu Dhabi
1

Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque features 82 Carrara marble domes, four 106-meter minarets, and accommodates 40,000 worshippers. Inside: the world's largest hand-knotted carpet—5,625 m², crafted by 1,200 Iranian artisans over 18 months. Construction commissioned in 1996 by the UAE's founding father, inaugurated in 2007. Allow at least 2 hours. Free guided tours at 10am, 11am, and 5pm (booking at szgmc.gov.ae).

⏱ 120 min
2

Mangrove National Park covers 75% of the country's mangroves on Abu Dhabi island's eastern flank. Flamingos, herons, and dugongs inhabit these protected channels. The 2-km boardwalk has free entry. For a deeper experience, guided kayak tours last 2 hours (160 AED adult, departing from Eastern Mangroves Promenade). Best time: high tide between 1pm and 3pm.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Sunset kayaking through mangrove channels—operators also offer night tours with underwater LED lighting to observe marine life.

Al Ain

Where 147,000 date palms and a 3,000-year-old falaj flourish in UNESCO-listed oasis shade

★★★★☆
📍 Al Ain Oasis UNESCO falaj date palms📍 Al Jahili Fort Al Ain📍 Al Ain Camel Market
1

Al Ain Oasis spans 1,200 hectares—147,000 date palms of about a hundred varieties, irrigated by a 3,000-year-old falaj. UNESCO site since 2011, opened to the public in 2016. At the interactive eco-center, a 3D video explains how water flows through underground galleries. Rent a bike at the entrance: one hour suffices for the essentials.

⏱ 90 min
2

Al Jahili Fort (1891), one of the UAE's largest forts, houses a permanent exhibition on Wilfred Thesiger—the British explorer who crossed the Rub al Khali on foot from Liwa in 1946 and 1948. Built to defend the oasis under orders from Sheikh Zayed bin Khalifa.

⏱ 75 min
3

Al Ain Camel Market, on the city's outskirts, is one of the last of its kind in the UAE. Open 6am to 7pm, traders negotiate young racing camels. Authentic scene, rarely frequented by tourists.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Bike or walk through the shaded oasis pathways in late afternoon. Electric carts available for rent for less mobile visitors.

Al Ain

Where 147,000 date palms and a 3,000-year-old falaj flourish in UNESCO-listed oasis shade

★★★★☆
📍 Jebel Hafeet summit road Al Ain📍 Jebel Hafeet beehive tombs Bronze Age📍 Green Mubazzarah hot springs Al Ain
1

Jebel Hafeet reaches 1,249 m—the highest point in Abu Dhabi emirate, second-highest peak in the UAE. The 12-km scenic road to the summit has been ranked among the world's most beautiful by several automotive magazines. 360° view over the desert, Al Ain below, and the Omani border.

⏱ 90 min
2

At the foot of Jebel Hafeet, over 500 beehive tombs dating from 3,200 to 2,600 BC mark the beginning of the Bronze Age in the region. UNESCO-listed since 2011. Danish archaeologists uncovered Mesopotamian pottery and copper objects here in 1959—proof of ancient maritime trade networks between Arabia, Mesopotamia, and the Indus Valley.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Green Mubazzarah at Jebel Hafeet's base: park with natural hot springs, lawns, and picnic areas. Good recovery stop after the climb, especially for families.

Mleiha

Where 90-million-year-old fossils and Bronze Age tombs scatter across a 2025 UNESCO desert

★★★★☆
📍 Mleiha Archaeological Centre Sharjah📍 Fossil Rock Jebel Mleiha📍 Umm Al Nar tomb Bronze Age Mleiha
1

Mleiha Archaeological Centre sits at the heart of a desert landscape UNESCO-listed in 2025 (Faya Palaeolithic Landscape). Excavations have unearthed traces of Homo sapiens dating back 130,000 years—among the earliest evidence of human presence outside Africa in the region. The museum is built around an Umm Al Nar culture tomb (Bronze Age, ~2,300 BC) still in situ.

⏱ 90 min
2

Fossil Rock (Jebel Mleiha), a few kilometers from the centre, is a limestone formation riddled with 90-million-year-old marine fossils—the ancient floor of the Tethys Sea. Accessible by 4x4 or on foot from the parking area (45 min round trip). Panoramic view of the transition from beige desert to orange dunes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The site lends itself to stargazing: 65 km from Sharjah, light pollution is nearly zero. The centre organizes non-exclusive observation evenings (book on-site).

⏱ 60 min

Hatta Dam forms a turquoise lake wedged into the Hajar Mountains, 130 km from Al Ain via the E66. Built in the 1990s, its red rock shores plunging into calm water offer one of the UAE's most unexpected panoramas. Kayaks for rent on-site (60 AED/single kayak, unlimited time), open from 7am.

Al Bithnah Fort (1735) controlled the only natural pass through the Hajar Mountains from Fujairah's coast to the interior—Wadi Ham. Rectangular fort of stone, coral, and mud-brick, restored in 2012. The view from the upper terraces over the valley and palm groves warrants the stop. Free entry.

⏱ 45 min

Al Aqah

Where limestone cliffs plunge straight into Arabian Sea turquoise

★★★★★
📍 Al Bidyah Mosque oldest UAE Fujairah📍 Al Aqah beach Fujairah Indian Ocean📍 Dibba bay snorkeling
1

Al Bidiyah Mosque (circa 1446) is the oldest mosque still in service in the UAE. Built from earth bricks and gypsum over 53 m², it houses four unequal domes supported by a single central pillar—an engineering system without parallel in the region. Two watchtowers of the same era stand on the hill behind. Active mosque: modest dress required.

⏱ 45 min
2

Dibba Bay and the Al Aqah coast: here the Hajar Mountains plunge directly into the Arabian Sea. Offshore rocks shelter coral colonies and parrotfish visible while snorkeling from the beach.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Swimming and snorkeling on Al Aqah public beach—warm water, little current, sandy and rocky bottom. One of the most accessible spots to observe marine life on the east coast.

Ras Al Khaimah

The last hilltop fort standing in the Emirates and a ghost town where pearlers once thrived

★★★☆☆
📍 Dhayah Fort hilltop Ras Al Khaimah📍 Al Jazirah Al Hamra ghost town pearling village📍 Ras Al Khaimah mangroves waterfront
1

Dhayah Fort, the last hilltop fort still standing in the UAE, dominates the plain from its rocky spur after a 200-step climb. Built in the early 19th century, it was the last bastion during the 1819 British expedition. Panoramic view of mangroves, sea, and mountains.

⏱ 75 min
2

Al Jazirah Al Hamra, a pearling village abandoned in 1968, is the only preserved example of a pre-oil coastal village in the entire Gulf. Its houses of coral blocks and palm wood were mass-abandoned by the Al Zaabi tribe—who received Sheikh Zayed's offer to relocate to Abu Dhabi. The alleyways are open to visit; do not enter fragile buildings.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Walk along Ras Al Khaimah's coastal mangroves in late afternoon—the low light highlights the mangrove roots.

Dubai Creek has separated Deira from Bur Dubai since the 19th century. The Al Fahidi district (1787) is the city's oldest—its wind towers, coral alleyways, and museum in the fort tell the story of Dubai before oil. The abra crossing costs 1 AED and takes 5 minutes.
