☀️ Weather

Spring (April-May): Perfect weather across most regions (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, moderate crowds, and excellent conditions for national parks and cities. Ideal for diverse activities nationwide.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with spectacular fall foliage, harvest festivals, and comfortable weather. Perfect for outdoor activities, cultural events, fewer crowds than summer.

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warmest weather but highest crowds and prices. Excellent for northern regions, challenging in southwestern deserts, perfect for beaches and national parks.

Winter (December-February): Variable conditions from tropical Florida to snowy Colorado. Perfect for skiing, southern destinations, indoor attractions, lowest prices outside holiday periods.

📋 Notes

10-day loop starting and ending in Cedar Rapids (CID airport). The route briefly crosses into Illinois (Galena), South Dakota (Sioux Falls), and Nebraska (Omaha). Best time: May through October. The Covered Bridge Festival in Winterset takes place in October. Des Moines' Saturday morning Downtown Farmers Market (May-October) is one of the Midwest's largest.

Cedar Rapids Ia

Grant Wood's hometown with Czech Village and Brucemore mansion.

★★★★☆
📍 Brucemore Mansion Cedar Rapids📍 Czech Village Cedar Rapids📍 National Czech Slovak Museum Cedar Rapids📍 Cedar River Cedar Rapids
1

Welcome to Cedar Rapids, Iowa's second-largest city. Start at Brucemore, the state's only National Trust for Historic Preservation site. This 21-room Queen Anne mansion was built in 1886 by Caroline Sinclair, widow of a meatpacking magnate—her husband had fallen down an elevator shaft in his own plant. Don't miss the porch decorated by Grant Wood in 1925 (the painter of American Gothic lived in Cedar Rapids) and the 1930s Tahitian-style bar, installed by the Hall family in what was their man cave before the term existed.

⏱ 75 min
2

Head to Czech Village across the Cedar River. This neighborhood bears witness to the massive Czech and Slovak immigration that shaped Cedar Rapids in the 19th century—the city had the largest Czech community in the United States outside Chicago. The National Czech & Slovak Museum & Library, affiliated with the Smithsonian, traces this history through exhibits on immigration, life behind the Iron Curtain, and the 1989 Velvet Revolution.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Cross the pedestrian bridge over the Cedar River and stroll along the waterfront. In the evening, downtown's microbreweries are worth exploring—Cedar Rapids has a booming craft beer scene.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 2

Galena Il

Victorian hilltop town where President Ulysses Grant once lived.

★★★★☆
📍 Field of Dreams Dyersville Iowa📍 Galena Main Street Illinois📍 Ulysses Grant Home Galena📍 Grant Park Galena overlook
1

En route to Galena, make a mandatory stop in Dyersville—40 minutes from Cedar Rapids—at the Field of Dreams Movie Site. The baseball diamond from the 1989 Kevin Costner film is still there, nestled among the cornfields. Universal built it in 1988 on the Lansing family's farm, and when filming wrapped, the owners hesitated to tear it down. Good thing they didn't: visitors came, just as the film predicted. You can walk on the field, tour the white farmhouse, and on select summer days, the "Ghost Players" in vintage uniforms emerge from the cornfields to play. A permanent 8,000-seat stadium was built next door—MLB hosted games there in 2021 and 2022.

⏱ 60 min
2

Continue 1 hour 15 minutes to Galena, a small town perched in the Illinois hills just across the Mississippi. Main Street is a lineup of 19th-century brick facades, with over 100 shops, galleries, and restaurants. Galena was spared from modernization because the mining economy collapsed after the Civil War—result: 85% of the town is designated a historic district.

⏱ 60 min
3

Visit Ulysses S. Grant's home, given to the general by Galena's citizens upon his return from the Civil War in 1865. Grant lived here briefly before becoming the 18th President of the United States. The Italianate-style brick house is preserved with original furnishings.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Climb to Grant Park for the overlook—the view plunges over Galena's rooftops and the river below. Late in the day, the Galena River Trail offers a walk or bike ride along the water.

⏱ 50 min

Stop 3

Decorah Ia

Norwegian heritage, waterfalls, and trout in the Upper Iowa River valley.

★★★☆☆
📍 Effigy Mounds National Monument Iowa📍 Vesterheim Norwegian-American Museum Decorah📍 Dunnings Spring Park Decorah📍 Upper Iowa River Decorah
1

Head north along the Mississippi. Stop at Effigy Mounds National Monument, one of the Midwest's most ancient sites. Over 200 earthen mounds built by Native Americans between 750 and 1,400 years ago are scattered across the bluffs overlooking the Mississippi. Some are shaped like bears or birds—the Great Bear Mound measures 20 meters long. The trail to Fire Point (5 km round trip) offers a vertiginous view of the river from the cliffs.

⏱ 120 min
2

Continue to Decorah, a small town nestled in the Upper Iowa River valley. The Vesterheim Norwegian-American Museum is the largest museum of Norwegian heritage outside Norway—33,000 objects spread across 12 buildings. Norwegian immigration to this part of Iowa in the 19th century profoundly marked the region, and it still shows in the architecture and local cuisine (try lefse, a potato flatbread).

⏱ 60 min
3

End at Dunning's Spring Park, a 60-foot (18-meter) waterfall right in the heart of town. A staircase descends to the base of the falls. In summer, the water is cool and the setting shaded—perfect for catching your breath after the drive.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Decorah is renowned for its trout—if you enjoy fishing, the Upper Iowa River is one of the state's best spots. Otherwise, stroll along the river on the Trout Run Trail (18-km loop, also accessible by bike).

⏱ 60 min

Stop 4

Spirit Lake Ia

Iowa Great Lakes resort town on glacial blue Okoboji waters.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Arnolds Park Amusement Park Iowa📍 West Okoboji Lake Iowa📍 Iowa Great Lakes Maritime Museum
1

Long drive west across the Iowa prairies—3.5 hours of corn and soybean fields stretching to the horizon. You arrive at the Iowa Great Lakes, the state's resort area. West Okoboji Lake is renowned for its clarity—it's one of only three blue water lakes in the world formed by glaciers, along with Lake Geneva and a lake in Manitoba. Settle onto the beach at Arnolds Park in early afternoon.

⏱ 60 min
2

Arnolds Park Amusement Park, open since 1889, is Iowa's oldest operating amusement park. This isn't Disneyland—it's a retro lakeside carnival with wooden roller coasters, vintage rides, and a "1950s American summer" vibe. Next door, the Iowa Great Lakes Maritime Museum traces the region's nautical history.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Late in the day, drive around the lake or rent a pontoon to cruise the water. The sunset over West Okoboji is one of Iowa's most beautiful—locals compare it (with considerable enthusiasm) to those of Lake Como.

⏱ 60 min

Sioux Falls Sd

Pink quartzite cascades meet a revitalized downtown arts scene.

★★★★☆
📍 Falls Park Sioux Falls📍 SculptureWalk Sioux Falls📍 Big Sioux River Sioux Falls📍 Historic downtown Sioux Falls
1

Head to Sioux Falls, South Dakota's largest city. Falls Park is the heart of town: the Big Sioux River cascades over pink quartzite formations 1.7 billion years old. In season, 14,000 liters of water per second rush over the rocks. Climb the observation tower (5 stories) for an overview, then descend onto the walkways at water level. The ruins of an old 1881 stone mill add a photogenic touch to the scene.

⏱ 60 min
2

Downtown Sioux Falls has completely reinvented itself in recent years. SculptureWalk is an outdoor trail of over 60 contemporary sculptures installed along the sidewalks—they change annually. Phillips Avenue is the main street, with pink quartzite buildings (the same stone as the falls) converted into restaurants, galleries, and bars.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

In the evening, sample Sioux Falls' food scene—the city has exploded gastronomically with chefs highlighting Great Plains products: bison, sweet corn, wild berries. For local beer, downtown craft breweries abound.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 6

Omaha Ne

Old Market charm, world-class zoo, and a pedestrian bridge across the Missouri.

★★★★☆
📍 Old Market Omaha Nebraska📍 Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge Omaha📍 Henry Doorly Zoo Omaha📍 Lauritzen Gardens Omaha
1

Arrive in Omaha, Nebraska, across the Missouri River. If you're traveling with family or love zoos, the Henry Doorly Zoo & Aquarium is consistently ranked among the world's best. Its Desert Dome is the world's largest glazed geodesic dome (40 meters high), and the underwater tunnel lets you walk beneath a shark aquarium. Allow at least 3 hours.

⏱ 180 min
2

In the afternoon, explore the Old Market, Omaha's historic district. Cobblestone streets, brick warehouses converted into galleries, independent shops, and restaurants. The atmosphere recalls New York's Meatpacking District in Midwest form—much more laid-back.

⏱ 60 min
3

Cross the Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge at sunset—this 1-km footbridge spans the Missouri and lets you walk from Nebraska to Iowa. A circle in the middle of the bridge marks the exact state line. It's one of Omaha's most photographed spots.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Omaha is Warren Buffett's hometown—he still lives in the same house he bought in 1958. If you visit in May, the Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meeting draws 40,000 shareholders. The rest of the year, the atmosphere remains quietly prosperous, with an increasingly acclaimed restaurant scene.

⏱ 45 min

Des Moines Ia

Gold-domed capitol, outdoor sculptures, and the hip East Village quarter.

★★★☆☆
📍 Iowa State Capitol Des Moines📍 Pappajohn Sculpture Park Des Moines📍 East Village Des Moines📍 Des Moines Botanical Garden
1

Welcome to Des Moines, Iowa's capital and most dynamic city. Start at the Iowa State Capitol with its dome covered in 23-karat gold leaf—it's the only state capitol with five domes. The marble and carved wood interior is worth the visit. Entry and guided tours are free.

⏱ 50 min
2

Head to Pappajohn Sculpture Park, a 1.8-hectare outdoor sculpture park right downtown. Over 30 works by artists like Louise Bourgeois, Ai Weiwei, and Jaume Plensa—including Nomade, an 8-meter-tall human silhouette made of steel letters, which has become Des Moines' symbol. The park is free, open from sunrise to midnight.

⏱ 45 min
3

Explore the Historic East Village, tucked between the Capitol and the Des Moines River. It's the city's liveliest neighborhood: trendy cafes, galleries, independent boutiques (don't miss Raygun, which proclaims itself the "greatest store in the universe"), and farm-to-table restaurants. Saturday mornings from May through October, the Downtown Farmers Market takes over the streets with over 300 vendors.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

Late in the day, the Greater Des Moines Botanical Garden offers a tropical geodesic dome where you'll forget you're in Iowa. The outdoor gardens along the river are free. Finish with a stroll at Gray's Lake Park—the illuminated pedestrian bridge is spectacular at nightfall.

⏱ 75 min

Stop 8

Pella Ia

Dutch windmill soaring 37 meters high and May's tulip festival.

★★★☆☆
📍 Roseman Covered Bridge Winterset📍 John Wayne Birthplace Winterset📍 Holliwell Covered Bridge Madison County📍 Vermeer Mill Pella📍 Molengracht Canal Pella
1

A day of road-tripping through central Iowa countryside. First stop: Winterset (40 minutes southwest of Des Moines), John Wayne's birthplace and Iowa's covered bridge capital. Six bridges survive in Madison County, built between 1868 and 1884. Roseman Bridge is the most famous—it's the one from the 1995 film with Meryl Streep and Clint Eastwood. Robert James Waller's novel sold 50 million copies. You can walk across the bridge and read messages left by visitors on the interior walls.

⏱ 45 min
2

Holliwell Bridge, the longest of the six (33 meters), spans the Middle River amid rolling prairies. Count on 30 minutes of gravel road to reach it—that's part of the charm. If you have time, also visit the Cutler-Donahoe Bridge, accessible from Winterset's City Park without dirt roads.

⏱ 40 min
3

In downtown Winterset, visit the John Wayne Birthplace Museum—the modest 4-room house where Marion Robert Morrison (his real name) was born in 1907. The adjacent museum contains personal items, film costumes, and a photo collection.

⏱ 40 min
4

Drive 1 hour 15 minutes east to Pella, a Dutch town transplanted to Iowa. The Vermeer Mill, built in 2002 by craftsmen from the Netherlands, is North America's tallest working windmill—37 meters. It still grinds grain. The Molengracht Canal downtown, with its gabled houses and tulips (in May), will make you feel like you've landed in the Netherlands. The Tulip Time Festival in May attracts 150,000 visitors.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

End the day with a Dutch pastry at one of Pella's bakeries—the letterbanketen (almond paste puff pastry sticks) and stroopwafels are homemade.

⏱ 30 min

Stop 9

Iowa Ia

★★★★☆
📍 Amana Colonies Iowa📍 Old Capitol Iowa City📍 Pedestrian Mall Iowa City📍 University of Iowa campus
1

En route to Iowa City, stop at the Amana Colonies (30 minutes north). Seven villages founded in 1855 by German Pietists who lived in a self-sufficient community until 1932. The brick and sandstone buildings have remained intact. You'll find woodworking shops, a communal brewery, a bakery producing wood-fired bread, and a distillery. The Woolen Mill, operating since 1857, still produces wool blankets. It's a National Historic Landmark.

⏱ 90 min
2

Arrive in Iowa City, Iowa's first capital and a university town. The Old Capitol Building (1840) sits atop a hill at the center of the University of Iowa campus—this is where Iowa was admitted to the Union in 1846. The Greek Revival-style building is restored and open to the public (free). In 2008, UNESCO designated Iowa City a "City of Literature"—the first in the United States—thanks to the renowned Iowa Writers' Workshop, which has trained dozens of Pulitzer Prize winners.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

The downtown Pedestrian Mall is where students gather: independent bookstores (Prairie Lights is an institution), cafes, restaurants, and bars. The atmosphere is young, relaxed, and intellectual—a refreshing contrast after the small rural towns elsewhere on the circuit.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 10

Cedar Rapids Ia

Grant Wood's hometown with Czech Village and Brucemore mansion.

★★★★☆
📍 American Gothic House Eldon Iowa📍 Grant Wood Studio Cedar Rapids📍 NewBo Market Cedar Rapids
1

Final day. If you leave early, detour to Eldon (1.5 hours south of Iowa City) to see the American Gothic House—the small white house with the Gothic window that Grant Wood painted in his most famous 1930 painting. The visitor center next door lends aprons and pitchforks to recreate the pose in front of the house. The original painting is at the Art Institute of Chicago, but the house is real.

⏱ 40 min
2

Return to Cedar Rapids. If you didn't do it on day 1, visit the Grant Wood Studio—the loft above a former garage where Grant Wood painted American Gothic and his other major works in the 1930s. The studio is in the same building as Cedar Rapids' History Center.

⏱ 40 min
3

Finish at NewBo City Market, an indoor market in the old bohemian neighborhood south of the river. Local food vendors, artisans, coffee from local roasters. It's a good spot for a final meal before the airport (CID, 15 minutes away).

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you have time before your flight, stop by Indian Creek Nature Center, 210 hectares of prairies, forests, and wetlands northeast of the city. The trails are short and easy—perfect for one last breath of Great Plains air.

⏱ 45 min
