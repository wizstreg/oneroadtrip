Bangladesh Grand Tour: Ganges Delta, Sylhet Tea Gardens & Cox's Bazar

📅 7 days📍 7 stops🚗 1578 km⏱ 18h50

Dive into Dhaka's pulsing energy, navigate the Ganges Delta's winding waterways, explore Sylhet's emerald tea gardens, and unwind on the world's longest beach at Cox's Bazar. Bangladesh reveals itself one river at a time.

✨ Highlights
  • Jatiya Sangsad Bhaban: Louis Kahn masterpiece, 1989 Aga Khan Award—one of the 20th century's greatest buildings
  • Overnight launch Dhaka-Barishal: multi-deck river crossing through the Ganges Delta, VIP cabins available
  • Malnicherra Tea Estate: the subcontinent's oldest tea plantation, founded 1849—5 minutes from Sylhet airport
  • Shah Jalal Dargah: 14th-century Sufi pilgrimage site, sacred catfish ponds and unique devotional atmosphere
  • Cox's Bazar: 120 km of uninterrupted beach, Inani Beach with coral formations at low tide
📆 Best period

November, December, January, February, March

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 10 and 14 days.

Day 1

Dhaka

Where Mughal palaces rise above the chaos and the Buriganga pulses with river life

★★★★★
1

Kawran Bazar, one of South Asia's largest wholesale markets: founded in the 18th century by a Marwari merchant, it now supplies the entire capital with vegetables, fish and fruit. Over 2,000 shops across 1 km² handle 50 million taka worth of transactions daily. At night, hundreds of trucks unload their cargo while porters weave through the aisles balancing baskets on their heads—a loud, sensory immersion into Dhaka's grassroots economy.

⏱ 75 min
2

Jatiya Sangsad Bhaban, the National Parliament complex designed by American architect Louis Kahn between 1962 and 1982: nine raw concrete blocks encircle the central octagonal legislative chamber, the entire ensemble set on 200 acres beside an artificial lake. Winner of the 1989 Aga Khan Award for Architecture, it's universally regarded as one of the 20th century's masterpieces. Visit from the outer esplanade and lakeside paths—interior access is restricted.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Arrival at Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport (DAC) and visa-on-arrival formalities (available for most European nationalities, expect $50 USD).

⏱ 75 min

Purchase an eSIM or local Grameenphone SIM at the Arrivals hall—4G network covering the entire country, essential for maps and transportation.

⏱ 20 min

Prepaid taxi or Uber transfer to the city center (Paltan/Tejgaon)—expect 45 to 90 minutes depending on Dhaka's notorious traffic.

⏱ 75 min

Day 2

Dhaka

Where Mughal palaces rise above the chaos and the Buriganga pulses with river life

★★★★★
1

Baitul Mukarram, Bangladesh's National Mosque: a modernist white marble structure inspired by the Ka'aba in Mecca, built from 1960 onward in a cubic style. Its grand prayer hall can accommodate up to 40,000 worshippers. Non-Muslims may visit outside prayer times. The bustle of religious-goods markets surrounding the building is especially vibrant.

⏱ 60 min
2

Ahsan Manzil, the Pink Palace of the Nawabs of Dhaka on the banks of the Buriganga: built between 1859 and 1872 in an Indo-Saracenic style blending Mughal and European influences, this pink-domed palace now houses 23 galleries chronicling the opulent life of Bengali aristocracy under the British Raj—period furniture, portraits and personal belongings of the Nawabs. Managed by the National Museum. Closed Thursdays. Paid entry (500 Tk for foreigners).

⏱ 90 min
3

Lalbagh Fort, an unfinished 17th-century Mughal fortress in Old Dhaka: begun in 1678 by Prince Muhammad Azam, son of Aurangzeb, and abandoned after the death of Pari Bibi, daughter of Governor Shaista Khan. Spread over 18 acres, the complex unites Pari Bibi's marble and basalt mausoleum, the still-active three-domed mosque, and the Diwan-i-Aam (audience hall), all linked by Persian-style gardens. On-site museum of Mughal artifacts.

⏱ 90 min
4

Dhakeshwari Temple, Dhaka's principal Hindu tutelary deity: according to tradition, built in the 12th century under the Sena dynasty—it's Bangladesh's most important Hindu sanctuary, whose very name gave the city its own. Lively compound, fragrant with incense and steeped in devotion. Free access.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Travel by CNG (natural-gas tuk-tuk) or rickshaw between Old Dhaka sites—iconic mode of transport; negotiate the fare before boarding.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 320 km · 45min
Day 3

Sylhet

Where emerald tea gardens cascade down hillsides beneath monsoon clouds

★★★★☆
1

Shah Jalal Dargah, Bangladesh's largest pilgrimage site: the tomb of 14th-century Sufi saint Shah Jalal, who introduced Islam to the Sylhet region in 1303 after defeating Hindu chieftain Gour Govinda, draws thousands of faithful daily. The complex comprises four mosques, a mausoleum draped in brocade and illuminated by candles at dusk, and two ponds of sacred catfish—according to legend, the reincarnation of Govinda's defeated soldiers. Non-Muslims are admitted to the compound, barefoot from the East Dargah Gate, but not inside the main mosque.

⏱ 75 min
2

Malnicherra Tea Estate, the subcontinent's oldest tea plantation: founded in 1849 by Lord Hudson across 1,500 acres along the road to Sylhet airport, it's Bangladesh's first commercial tea operation. You can wander among the rows of pruned tea bushes, watch pickers at work, and stroll shaded avenues between the plants. Orange and rubber trees punctuate the estate. Permission from private managers recommended before entering.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Domestic flight from Dhaka (DAC) to Sylhet (ZYL)—approximately 45 minutes flight time. Multiple daily connections (Biman, US-Bangla, Novoair).

⏱ 120 min

Evening stroll through the Zindabazar shopping district—Sylhet's bustling artery, with its stalls of spices, paan (betel leaf) and local textiles.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 320 km · 45min

Day 5

Barisal

The Venice of Bangladesh, where a thousand canals lead deep into the Ganges Delta

★★★★☆
1

Baitul Aman Mosque (Guthia Mosque), 20 km from Barishal in Wazirpur upazila: inaugurated in 2006, this private 14-acre complex on the banks of a canal unites 20 domes and a 58-meter minaret—Bangladesh's tallest—surrounded by three lakes whose waters reflect the entire edifice. Architecture inspired by great Ottoman and Middle Eastern mosques, with Quranic inscriptions in calligraphy on imported marble. On-site garden and orphanage. Free entry.

⏱ 75 min
2

Pirogue excursion on the canals of Swarupkathi (35 km south of Barishal): this delta town is crisscrossed by a network of waterways that locals still navigate daily. Swarupkathi is also known for its woodworkers—cabinetmakers and sculptors working with jackfruit and local teak. In season (May–August), pirogues laden with watermelons descend the canals from upstream villages, an emblematic image of the Bengali delta.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Sunrise arrival of the launch on the Barishal delta—the brown-golden waters of the Kirtankhola come alive at dawn with fishermen's pirogues and boats loaded with vegetables.

⏱ 30 min

Barishal → Swarupkathi road trip (35 km, ~45 min) for the pirogue excursion, then return to Barishal for the night.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 350 km · 55min
Day 6

Cox Bazar

★★★☆☆
1

Laboni Point, Cox's Bazar's tourist epicenter: first access to the world's longest uninterrupted beach—120 km of golden sand along the Bay of Bengal. Shell sellers and grilled-seafood vendors, horsemen offering beach rides, souvenir markets at the entrance. The finest hour is sunset, when sampan fishing boats return to port, black silhouettes against the orange sea.

⏱ 90 min
2

Inani Beach, 25 km south of Cox's Bazar along the Marine Drive: a wilder, less-frequented stretch of beach, distinctive for its black and green coral rock formations surfacing at low tide across 18 km. The water is clearer than at Laboni and the setting more pristine. The sole coastal road between Cox's Bazar and Inani hugs the sea the entire distance.

⏱ 90 min
3

Burmese Market of Cox's Bazar: a market historically run by traders from neighboring Myanmar, where you'll find sarongs, spices, bamboo goods, natural cosmetics and local handicrafts. A vibrant cultural curiosity in the heart of Cox's Bazar bazaar.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Domestic flight from Sylhet (ZYL) to Cox's Bazar (CXB)—approximately 1 hour flight time. Several Bangladeshi carriers operate this route.

⏱ 150 min

Sunset walk on the beach: photos of sampan fishing boats moored in rows, raking light on the sea and silhouettes of nets drying in the wind.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 150 km · 3h
Day 7

Chattogram

Where tribal heritage meets colonial history on the shores of the Karnaphuli

★★★★★
1

Ethnological Museum of Agrabad: one of Bangladesh's few ethnographic museums, dedicated to the tribal peoples of the Chittagong Hill Tracts—Chakma, Marma, Tripura, Khyang and Bawm. Collections of textiles, jewelry, musical instruments and everyday objects presented by ethnic group, offering essential background before any visit to the hills.

⏱ 75 min
2

Chandanpura Masjid, an 18th-century mosque with facades adorned with blue-and-white polychrome glazed tiles—an exceedingly rare decorative scheme in Bangladeshi religious architecture, earning it renown among Islamic architecture enthusiasts. Located in Chittagong's old commercial quarter.

⏱ 60 min
3

Foy's Lake, an artificial lake created by the British in 1924 by excavating the hillside for construction of the Assam-Bengal Railway. Stroll along the wooded shores, rent rowboats, and enjoy views of the hills encircling Chittagong—a green lung in the country's second city.

⏱ 75 min
4

Patenga Sea Beach, at the mouth of the Karnaphuli, facing the open sea of the Bay of Bengal. View of cargo ships entering Chittagong port—Bangladesh's second export hub. Popular promenade vibe at sunset, frequented mainly by city residents.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Cox's Bazar → Chittagong road trip in the morning (150 km, ~2.5 hours on the Highway). Early departure recommended to make the most of the day in Chittagong.

⏱ 150 min

Chittagong → Dhaka return in the evening—domestic flight CGP→DAC (1h, most practical option) or bus (250 km, ~4-5h depending on traffic).

⏱ 180 min

Last-minute shopping at New Market or Bashundhara City Mall—jamdani fabrics (Bengali brocade inscribed on UNESCO intangible heritage list), spices, bamboo handicrafts and nakshi kantha (traditional embroideries). Transfer to Hazrat Shahjalal Airport (DAC) in the evening or next morning.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 250 km · 5h
