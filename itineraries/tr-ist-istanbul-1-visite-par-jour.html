⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 2 and 6 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Perfect weather (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, comfortable temperatures, and excellent conditions for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Ideal for Istanbul, Cappadocia, and coastal regions.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures after summer heat with stable weather and fewer crowds. Perfect for all regions, cultural exploration, and outdoor adventures.

Summer (June-August): Hot temperatures (25-40°C) with peak tourist season and crowded attractions. Excellent for Mediterranean and Aegean coasts, challenging for inland areas.

Winter (December-February): Cool temperatures (5-15°C) with occasional snow but lowest prices and authentic experiences. Good for Istanbul and covered attractions, limited beach activities.

Istanbul Sainte Sophie

★★★★★
📍 Hagia Sophia Istanbul📍 Ayasofya Mosque interior📍 Hagia Sophia mosaics gallery📍 Hagia Sophia dome Justinian📍 Imperial Gate Hagia Sophia📍 Hagia Sophia upper gallery
1

Push through the Imperial Gate of Hagia Sophia and look up: the dome, 31 metres in diameter, soars 56 metres above the floor, carried by four pendentives. Built between 532 and 537 under Justinian I by Anthemius of Tralles and Isidore of Miletus, this engineering masterpiece defined monumental architecture for a millennium.

⏱ 90 min
2

In the upper gallery, Byzantine mosaics from the 9th to 12th centuries stand alongside Ottoman calligraphic medallions 7.5 metres in diameter. The 13th-century Deësis, depicting Christ flanked by the Virgin and St John the Baptist, remains one of the peaks of late Byzantine art.

⏱ 45 min
3

Orthodox cathedral for 916 years, mosque for 482 years, museum from 1934 to 2020, then mosque again: the building encapsulates Istanbul's history in stone. Its 40 windows at the dome's base create a floating-light effect that impressed medieval visitors just as it does today.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Slide your thumb into the copper-ringed hole of the Weeping Column and rotate your finger a full circle without pulling it out—tradition promises your wish will be granted.

⏱ 10 min

On your way out, turn back to study the Ottoman ablutions fountain and the buttresses Sinan added in the 16th century to prevent the structure from collapsing.

⏱ 15 min

Settle down with a çay on a bench in Sultanahmet Park, facing the façade: at sunset, the stone takes on a golden hue that transforms the building's appearance.

Istanbul Mosquee Bleue

★★★★★
📍 Blue Mosque Sultanahmet📍 Sultan Ahmed Mosque six minarets📍 Blue Mosque Iznik tiles interior📍 Sultanahmet Camii Istanbul📍 Hippodrome obelisk Istanbul📍 German Fountain Sultanahmet
1

Six minarets pierce the sky above the Blue Mosque (Sultanahmet Camii), commissioned by Sultan Ahmed I in 1609 and completed in 1616. The count sparked controversy: only Mecca had as many, forcing the sultan to fund a seventh there to restore the hierarchy.

⏱ 60 min
2

Enter through the north gate and let your eyes adjust: over 20,000 blue-and-white Iznik tiles line the walls, patterned with tulips, carnations and cypresses. The 260 windows bathe the prayer hall in soft light that makes the shades of blue shimmer—hence the nickname.

⏱ 30 min
3

On the neighbouring Hippodrome, three ancient monuments still stand: the Obelisk of Theodosius (15th century BC, brought from Egypt in 390), the bronze Serpent Column (5th century BC, from Delphi) and the Walled Obelisk of Constantine VII. This 480-metre esplanade hosted chariot races before crowds of 100,000.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Pause at the Fountain of Wilhelm II, gifted in 1901 by the German emperor: octagonal, crowned with a neo-Byzantine dome and adorned with golden mosaics inside.

⏱ 15 min

Cross the square to enter the Museum of Turkish and Islamic Arts, housed in the former palace of Ibrahim Pasha (16th century). The collection of calligraphy and Anatolian carpets is one of the world's finest.

Istanbul Topkapi

★★★★★
📍 Topkapi Palace Istanbul📍 Topkapi Harem sultan📍 Topkapi Treasury Spoonmaker Diamond📍 Topkapi Palace courtyards📍 Gulhane Park Istanbul📍 Topkapi Palace Bosphorus view
1

Pass through the Imperial Gate (Bab-ı Hümayun) and cross four successive courtyards: Topkapı Palace served as the residence of Ottoman sultans from 1465 to 1856. This 70-hectare complex housed up to 4,000 people—pages, eunuchs and women of the Imperial Harem.

⏱ 60 min
2

In the Treasury, the 86-carat Spoonmaker's Diamond gleams behind its armoured case, beside the Topkapı Dagger encrusted with three massive emeralds and the golden throne of Murad III. The Sacred Relics chamber preserves the cloak and sword attributed to the Prophet Muhammad.

⏱ 60 min
3

Take the corridor to the Harem: over 400 rooms decorated with Iznik tiles in floral patterns. The apartments of the Valide Sultan (the sultan's mother) and the domed Imperial Hall illustrate the power women wielded in Ottoman politics.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Descend into Gülhane Park, the palace's former garden planted with centuries-old plane trees, for an unobstructed view over the Golden Horn and the Sea of Marmara.

⏱ 30 min

Head to the terrace of the fourth courtyard to photograph the confluence of the Bosphorus, the Golden Horn and the Sea of Marmara—all three visible in a single panorama.

Istanbul Citerne Basilique

★★★★★
📍 Basilica Cistern Istanbul📍 Yerebatan Sarnici columns📍 Medusa heads Basilica Cistern📍 Underground cistern Istanbul📍 336 columns Basilica Cistern📍 Justinian cistern Istanbul
1

Descend the 52 steps and let your eyes adjust to the gloom: 336 columns, nine metres tall, support this subterranean space of 9,800 m². The Basilica Cistern (Yerebatan Sarnıcı), built by Justinian in 532, supplied drinking water to the Great Imperial Palace.

⏱ 45 min
2

At the far end of the cistern, two columns rest on carved Medusa heads—one laid sideways, the other upside down. Their origin remains disputed. The cistern could hold 80,000 m³ of water piped from the Belgrade Forest via the Valens Aqueduct.

⏱ 30 min
3

On your way up, note the Weeping Column (peacock-eye column), carved with teardrop motifs. It recalls a style similar to Trajan's Forum in Rome and may have been salvaged from a Roman monument during construction.

⏱ 15 min

Activities

Walk the wooden platforms between the columns bathed in shifting lights and reflections in the residual water—an excellent moment for tripod photography.

⏱ 20 min

Back on the surface, walk up Divanyolu Caddesi, the ancient Roman triumphal route (Mese) linking the forum to the Great Palace. The Column of Constantine (Çemberlitaş), blackened by a 1779 fire, still stands midway, 35 metres tall.

⏱ 30 min

Istanbul Musee Archeologique

★★★★★
📍 Istanbul Archaeological Museums📍 Alexander Sarcophagus Istanbul📍 Tiled Kiosk Museum Cinili📍 Ancient Orient Museum Istanbul📍 Istanbul museum sarcophagi📍 Gulhane museums Istanbul
1

Founded in 1891 by Osman Hamdi Bey to halt the dispersion of Ottoman antiquities, the Istanbul Archaeological Museum holds over one million objects. The centrepiece: the Alexander Sarcophagus (4th century BC) discovered at Sidon, carved with battle and hunting scenes with traces of original polychromy.

⏱ 90 min
2

Cross the courtyard to enter the Tiled Kiosk (Çinili Köşk), built in 1472 by Mehmed II: it's the oldest surviving civilian Ottoman building in Istanbul. It exhibits Seljuk and Ottoman faience, including 16th-century floral panels from imperial mosques.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Museum of the Ancient Orient, in the third building of the complex, displays cuneiform tablets including the Treaty of Kadesh (1259 BC)—the oldest known peace treaty, signed between Ramesses II and the Hittites—alongside a copy of the Code of Hammurabi.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Step into the Museum of the History of Science and Technology in Islam, set in Gülhane Park: astronomical instruments, water clocks and mechanical devices from the 9th to 16th centuries are reconstructed at full scale.

⏱ 45 min

Take a break in the archaeological museum's inner courtyard: the sarcophagi and ancient columns arranged in the open air form a free, peaceful sculpture garden.

⏱ 20 min

Istanbul Grand Bazar

★★★★☆
📍 Grand Bazaar Istanbul Kapalicarsi📍 Grand Bazaar 4000 shops📍 Covered market Istanbul oldest📍 Grand Bazaar jewelry copper📍 Beyazit Mosque Istanbul📍 Sahaflar book market Istanbul
1

Get lost in the 66 covered streets of the Grand Bazaar (Kapalıçarşı), founded in 1461 under Mehmed II. Over 4,000 shops line painted vaults and stone domes across 30,000 m²: it's the world's oldest and one of the largest covered markets.

⏱ 90 min
2

Each street bears the name of its trade guild: Kalpakçılar for jewellers, Perdahçılar for coppersmiths, Halıcılar for carpet merchants. The Beyazıt and Nuruosmaniye gates are the main entrances to this labyrinth where haggling is a codified art.

⏱ 45 min
3

Just beyond the west exit, the Book Market of Sahaflar (Sahaflar Çarşısı) occupies an open courtyard used by papermakers since Byzantine times. You can still find antiquarian books, Ottoman calligraphy and Persian miniatures.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Step into Beyazıt Mosque (1506), the oldest imperial mosque still in use in Istanbul: its 20-column portico of granite and porphyry leads to a peaceful courtyard.

⏱ 30 min

Scout by theme without purchase pressure: hammered copper, Iznik ceramics, mosaic lamps, leather, spices. Bargaining always begins with a çay offered by the seller.

⏱ 30 min

Istanbul Suleymaniye

★★★☆☆
📍 Suleymaniye Mosque Istanbul Sinan📍 Suleymaniye complex tombs📍 Mimar Sinan masterpiece mosque📍 Golden Horn view Suleymaniye📍 Suleiman Magnificent tomb📍 Suleymaniye courtyard fountain
1

Completed in 1557 by Sinan for Suleiman the Magnificent, Süleymaniye Mosque crowns one of Istanbul's seven hills. Its four minarets with ten balconies encode the sultan's place in Ottoman history: fourth sultan of Istanbul, tenth of the dynasty.

⏱ 60 min
2

The complex (külliye) extends far beyond the mosque: tombs of Suleiman and his wife Roxelana (Hürrem Sultan), four madrasas, a hospital, a caravanserai, a hamam and a public kitchen (imaret). Sinan himself is buried in a modest tomb in the north-east corner of the garden.

⏱ 45 min
3

Approach Sinan's tomb (türbe), a small open-air stone enclosure, modest compared to the scale of his oeuvre: over 300 buildings across the Ottoman Empire, including 84 mosques. An inscription records that he lived almost a century (1489–1588).

⏱ 15 min

Activities

From the terrace of the funerary garden, the view plunges over the Golden Horn and the Galata shore—one of the city's best free viewpoints.

⏱ 20 min

In the street below, try the white beans (kuru fasulye) and rice pilavı at the traditional restaurants backed against the mosque wall. These canteens haven't changed their recipes in decades.

Istanbul Bazar Epices

★★★★★
📍 Spice Bazaar Istanbul Egyptian📍 Misir Carsisi Istanbul spices📍 New Mosque Yeni Cami Eminonu📍 Eminonu waterfront Istanbul📍 Rustem Pasha Mosque Iznik tiles📍 Galata Bridge fishermen
1

Built in 1660, the Spice Bazaar (Mısır Çarşısı) is Istanbul's second-largest covered market. Its L-shaped plan shelters 85 shops piled with spices, Turkish delight, teas, dried fruits and caviar. It owes its name (Egyptian Bazaar) to the taxes on Egyptian goods that funded the neighbouring mosque.

⏱ 60 min
2

Completed in 1665 after 66 years of construction interrupted by wars and unrest, the New Mosque (Yeni Cami) dominates Eminönü Square. Its silhouette—two minarets and a cascade of semi-domes—has become the defining image of Istanbul's waterfront.

⏱ 30 min
3

Climb the narrow staircase to the tiny Rüstem Pasha Mosque (1563), signed by Sinan. Every interior surface is covered with Istanbul's finest Iznik tiles: crimson tulips, carnations and geometric patterns on white-and-blue ground.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Order a balık ekmek (grilled fish sandwich) on the Eminönü quays, facing the Galata Bridge where fishermen cast their lines from morning to night.

⏱ 20 min

Cross the Galata Bridge on the lower level: fish restaurants with Golden Horn views occupy the entire length of the deck beneath the roadway.

Istanbul Fener Balat

★★★★★
📍 Fener Balat Istanbul colorful houses📍 Greek Orthodox Patriarchate Fener📍 Bulgarian Iron Church Istanbul📍 Balat Jewish quarter Istanbul📍 Fener Greek lyceum red building📍 Chora Museum Kariye mosaics
1

Fener was the heart of the Greek Orthodox community after 1453 and still houses the Ecumenical Patriarchate of Constantinople, spiritual seat of 300 million Orthodox worldwide. The small complex of modest buildings on the main street contrasts with the institution's importance.

⏱ 45 min
2

Balat, the old Jewish quarter where Sephardim expelled from Spain settled in 1492, is best explored by climbing its steep lanes lined with colourful wooden houses—pink, yellow, blue—now one of Istanbul's most photographed backdrops.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Bulgarian Church of St Stephen (Sveti Stefan), completed in 1898, is one of the rare churches built entirely from prefabricated cast iron in Vienna then assembled on site. Its iron components, totalling 500 tonnes, were transported via the Danube and the Black Sea.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb to the Greek Lyceum of Fener (Megali Scholi), an imposing red-brick building from 1881 dominating the Golden Horn—the view from the steps is worth the effort.

⏱ 20 min

Settle into one of Balat's café-galleries that have moved into renovated houses on Vodina Street. The neighbourhood has transformed in five years with the arrival of these creative studios.

Istanbul Murailles Eyup

★★★★★
📍 Eyup Sultan Mosque Istanbul📍 Pierre Loti Cafe Golden Horn📍 Theodosian Walls Istanbul📍 Chora Mosque Kariye mosaics📍 Eyup cemetery Golden Horn view📍 City walls Constantinople
1

Start with the Chora Mosque (Kariye Camii), a former 11th-century Byzantine church: its mosaics and frescoes from the 14th century rank among the finest achievements of late Byzantine art. The narrative cycles illustrate the lives of Christ and the Virgin with unprecedented realism for the period.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Walls of Theodosius II, raised between 413 and 439, stretch 6.5 kilometres from the Sea of Marmara to the Golden Horn. This triple fortification system—moat, outer wall, 12-metre inner wall punctuated by 96 towers—defended Constantinople for a millennium.

⏱ 45 min
3

Eyüp Sultan Mosque houses the tomb of Abu Ayyub al-Ansari, a companion of the Prophet who died during the first Arab siege of Constantinople in 674–678. It was here that Ottoman sultans girded the sword of Osman during their enthronement—the equivalent of European coronation.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Take the cable car up to the Pierre Loti Café, perched above the Eyüp Ottoman cemetery. The view over the Golden Horn between the cypresses is one of the city's most iconic.

⏱ 30 min

Walk down through the old Eyüp Ottoman cemetery, one of Istanbul's oldest. The turbaned headstones indicate the deceased's rank in the Ottoman hierarchy.

Istanbul Galata

★★★★★
📍 Galata Tower Istanbul panorama📍 Karakoy street art Istanbul📍 Galata Bridge Istanbul📍 Galata Mevlevi Lodge whirling📍 Karakoy waterfront cafes📍 SALT Galata art Istanbul
1

Built in 1348 by the Genoese as the Christea Turris, Galata Tower rises 67 metres. Its circular balcony offers a 360° panorama over Sultanahmet, the Golden Horn, the Bosphorus and the Asian shore. The Genoese quarter of Galata was an autonomous, prosperous merchant colony facing Constantinople.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Galata Mevlevi Museum (Galata Mevlevihanesi), a former whirling dervish lodge founded in 1491, is Istanbul's oldest Mevlevi tekke. Sema ceremonies (whirling dance) are performed in an octagonal hall adorned with calligraphy.

⏱ 45 min
3

Walk down the steep lanes towards Karaköy, the old quarter of bankers and merchants. The contemporary art space SALT Galata, housed in the former Ottoman Bank (1892), offers exhibitions and a research library on the Empire's history.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Browse the designer boutiques and speciality cafés of Karaköy, now the European shore's trendiest neighbourhood.

⏱ 30 min

Order a künefe (hot cheese-and-syrup dessert) at one of the Mumhane Street artisans—the cheese should stretch when you cut the first slice.

Istanbul Istiklal

★★★★★
📍 Istiklal Avenue Istanbul pedestrian📍 Taksim Square Republic Monument📍 Nostalgic tramway Istiklal📍 Cicek Pasaji flower passage📍 St Antoine Padua church Istanbul📍 Fish market Balik Pazari Istanbul
1

Running 1.4 kilometres between Taksim Square and Galata Tower, Istiklal Avenue (Istiklal Caddesi) is traversed by the red nostalgic tram. Three million people pass through daily, among 19th-century embassies, Art Nouveau arcades and theatres.

⏱ 60 min
2

Step into the Basilica of St Anthony of Padua (1912), Istanbul's largest Catholic church. Its neo-Gothic red-brick façade and vaulted interior contrast with the surrounding mosques, testifying to Beyoğlu's religious diversity.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Çiçek Pasajı (Flower Passage) occupies the former Cité de Péra (1876). Its glass-roofed taverns (meyhane) are the traditional venue for rakı and mezze. The adjacent Fish Market (Balık Pazarı) brims with seafood stalls and lively little restaurants.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Head up to Taksim Square to see the Republic Monument (1928), the work of Italian sculptor Pietro Canonica, commemorating the foundation of the Turkish Republic in 1923.

⏱ 20 min

Explore the Atlas Passage and the independent cinemas of Istiklal: Beyoğlu remains the heart of Istanbul's alternative cultural scene, with screenings, galleries and bookshops in every covered arcade.

Istanbul Dolmabahce

★★★★★
📍 Dolmabahce Palace Istanbul📍 Dolmabahce Harem Bosphorus📍 Crystal chandelier Dolmabahce📍 Dolmabahce clock tower📍 Ataturk room Dolmabahce📍 Dolmabahce Palace gardens
1

Completed in 1856 by architect Nikoğos Balyan, Dolmabahçe Palace stretches 600 metres along the Bosphorus. The last palace of the Ottoman sultans, it blends Baroque, Rococo and Neoclassical styles. Its Throne Room houses a 4.5-tonne Bohemian crystal chandelier gifted by Queen Victoria—the world's largest.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Dolmabahçe Harem, separate from the main palace, comprises the imperial family's apartments furnished with European furniture and Hereke carpets. Atatürk spent his final days here and died on 10 November 1938 at 9:05—the time at which all the palace clocks remain stopped.

⏱ 60 min
3

The neo-Baroque Clock Tower (27 metres), erected in 1895 facing the main entrance, served as a time marker for Bosphorus boatmen. Its four dials bear Roman and Arabic numerals, reflecting the dual European and Ottoman influences of the era.

⏱ 15 min

Activities

Walk the gardens between the palace and the Bosphorus to photograph the ornate grilles and the pleasure pavilions at the water's edge.

⏱ 20 min

Continue to the Beşiktaş quarter for a çay by the water, facing the ferries criss-crossing the strait between two continents.

Istanbul Ortakoy

📍 Ortakoy Mosque Bosphorus Bridge📍 Ortakoy waterfront Istanbul📍 Bosphorus Bridge 15 July Martyrs📍 Ortakoy kumpir potato boats📍 Ortakoy square cafes market📍 Mecidiye Mosque baroque Ortakoy
1

Ortaköy Mosque (Büyük Mecidiye Camii), built in 1856 by the same architect as Dolmabahçe, is a Baroque Ottoman jewel set beside the Bosphorus. Its white façade topped by two slender minarets stands out against the 15 July Martyrs Bridge (former Bosphorus Bridge), creating Istanbul's most reproduced postcard view.

⏱ 30 min
2

Ortaköy Square, buzzing with outdoor cafés and craft stalls, hosts a weekend market. The kumpir (giant stuffed baked potato with choice toppings) sold on the quays is a local speciality that has become a neighbourhood ritual.

⏱ 30 min
3

Walk up the coast towards Kuruçeşme and Arnavutköy to admire the painted wooden Ottoman yalıs at the water's edge. The village of Arnavutköy, with its fishermen's houses lined up facing the strait, has kept a village feel despite the proximity of downtown.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Try a kumpir on the Ortaköy quays: vendors line up dozens of toppings in trays—cheese, olives, sweetcorn, sausage, salad—and stuff the potato in front of you.

⏱ 20 min

In the evening, the waterfront bars and clubs of Ortaköy come alive with a direct view of the illuminated bridge, which changes colour every hour.

Istanbul Bosphore Croisiere

★★★★☆
📍 Bosphorus cruise Istanbul Eminonu📍 Bosphorus strait Europe Asia📍 Rumeli Fortress Bosphorus📍 Ottoman waterfront mansions yali📍 Bosphorus ferry Anadolu Kavagi📍 Bosphorus sunset cruise
1

Board the Şehir Hatları line at Eminönü and sail up the 32 kilometres of the Bosphorus to Anadolu Kavağı. The ferry passes the fortresses of Rumeli Hisarı (1452) and Anadolu Hisarı (1394), built by the Ottomans to choke Constantinople's supplies before the final assault.

⏱ 120 min
2

Along the shores, yalıs (Ottoman wooden mansions) alternate with imperial palaces. The Yalı of Zarif Mustafa Pasha (18th century), painted red, and the Yalı of Amcazade Hüseyin Pasha (1699), the oldest survivor, testify to the art of living beside the strait.

⏱ 30 min
3

At Anadolu Kavağı, the ferry's final stop, climb to the ruins of the Genoese Yoros Fortress for a view over the Bosphorus's outlet to the Black Sea. The fishing village at the foot of the hill serves grilled fish at prices well below downtown.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

On the return ferry, sit on the western side (left when facing forward) for the sunset behind the minarets of the European shore.

⏱ 20 min

Lunch at Anadolu Kavağı in one of the harbour fish restaurants—grilled octopus mezze and whole turbot are local specialities.

Istanbul Uskudar

★★★★★
📍 Uskudar waterfront Istanbul Asian📍 Mihrimah Sultan Mosque Uskudar Sinan📍 Maiden Tower Kiz Kulesi Bosphorus📍 Salacak viewpoint Istanbul📍 Cinili Mosque Uskudar tiles📍 Uskudar ferry terminal Istanbul
1

Set on a raised terrace facing the jetty, Mihrimah Sultan Mosque in Üsküdar (completed 1548) is Sinan's first work to use the concept of the floating dome. Commissioned by Princess Mihrimah, daughter of Suleiman and Roxelana, its central dome flanked by three semi-domes once rose directly above the water, before the coastal road was built.

⏱ 45 min
2

Take the boat from Salacak to reach the Maiden's Tower (Kız Kulesi), on its islet at the entrance to the Bosphorus. The first tower was erected in 408 BC by the Athenian general Alcibiades. Successively lighthouse, customs post, lazaretto and fortification, it now houses a museum and restaurant.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Çinili Mosque (1640), commissioned by Kösem Sultan, one of the most powerful women in Ottoman history, is lined with blue-and-turquoise Iznik tiles in floral patterns. The adjacent Çinili Hamam still operates as a traditional Turkish bath.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Stroll along the Salacak waterfront: the view over Sultanahmet, Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque from the Asian shore is Istanbul's most photographed.

⏱ 20 min

Walk up to the garden of Fethipaşa Korusu, a wooded park overlooking the Bosphorus where Üsküdar families come to drink çay under the trees.

⏱ 25 min

Explore Kuzguncuk, a former cosmopolitan village where synagogue, Armenian church, Orthodox church and mosque coexist on the same street—a microcosm of Istanbul's multicultural past.

Istanbul Kadikoy

★★★★★
📍 Kadikoy market fish Istanbul Asian📍 Moda waterfront promenade Istanbul📍 Ciya Sofrasi restaurant Kadikoy📍 Bahariye street Kadikoy tramway📍 Sureyya Opera House Art Deco📍 Kadikoy nostalgic tram Moda
1

Kadıköy is ancient Chalcedon, founded in 685 BC by the Megarians, seventeen years before Byzantium. It was nicknamed the 'city of the blind' because its founders supposedly failed to see the superior site of the Golden Horn opposite. Today it's the liveliest neighbourhood on the Asian shore.

⏱ 30 min
2

Dive into the Fish Market (Kadıköy Balıkçılar Pazarı), a maze of alleys lined with stalls selling fish, olives, cheese, spices and dried fruit. This is where Istanbulites do their shopping, not in the tourist quarters. Güneşli Bahçe Street is home to Çiya Sofrası, a regional Anatolian restaurant open since the 1980s.

⏱ 60 min
3

Built in 1927 by politician Süreyya İlmen Pasha in Art Deco style, the Süreyya Opera is the Asian shore's leading performance venue. Bahariye Street leading to it, traversed by the nostalgic tram, is the main shopping artery—often compared to Istiklal on the European side.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Head down to Moda and walk the coastal promenade to Moda Park: the view stretches over the Sea of Marmara and the silhouette of Sultanahmet opposite.

⏱ 40 min

Explore Kadife Sokak (Bar Street), the heart of Kadıköy's alternative nightlife, lined with live bars and independent clubs like Arkaoda, open since 1999.

⏱ 25 min

Order a Turkish coffee at Fazıl Bey, a neighbourhood institution for decades, in Kadıköy market.

⏱ 20 min

Istanbul Camlica

★★★★★
📍 Camlica Mosque Istanbul largest Turkey📍 Camlica Hill panorama Bosphorus📍 Buyuk Camlica Camii 2019📍 Beylerbeyi Palace Bosphorus📍 Camlica Hill tea garden Istanbul📍 Museum Islamic Civilisations Camlica
1

Inaugurated in 2019, the Grand Çamlıca Mosque (Büyük Çamlıca Camii) is Turkey's largest. Its main dome rises to 72 metres (symbolising Istanbul's 72 nations) with a diameter of 34 metres (the city's vehicle code). Designed by two female architects, Bahar Mızrak and Hayriye Gül Totu, it can accommodate 63,000 worshippers.

⏱ 60 min
2

Çamlıca Hill (Büyük Çamlıca Tepesi), Istanbul's highest point at 267 metres, offers a panorama over the Bosphorus, both shores and the Sea of Marmara islands. The pine-wooded park surrounding it hosts tea gardens frequented by Istanbul families for generations.

⏱ 30 min
3

Built in 1865 by Sarkis Balyan in Ottoman Neoclassical style, Beylerbeyi Palace served as the sultans' summer residence. Its interiors blend Bohemian chandeliers, Hereke carpets and European furniture. Empress Eugénie of France and the Duke of Windsor stayed here.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit the Museum of Islamic Civilisations, opened in 2022 in the Çamlıca Mosque complex. Allow at least two hours to walk through its thematic galleries.

⏱ 60 min

Settle into a Çamlıca tea garden at sunset: the Bosphorus turns gold between two continents, with the minarets of the European shore in silhouette.

⏱ 30 min

Istanbul Iles Princes

★★★★★
📍 Princes Islands Istanbul Buyukada📍 Buyukada bicycle monastery Aya Yorgi📍 Heybeliada seminary Halki📍 Princes Islands ferry Adalar📍 Buyukada Victorian mansions📍 Princes Islands car-free Istanbul
1

Büyükada (5.4 km²), the largest of the nine Princes' Islands (Adalar), is a car-free archipelago in the Sea of Marmara. The islands served as a place of exile for disgraced Byzantine princes and aristocrats. Trotsky lived here four years (1929–1933) in a 950 m² mansion now in ruins, where he wrote his History of the Russian Revolution.

⏱ 30 min
2

Climb to the Greek Orthodox monastery of Aya Yorgi (St George), perched atop Büyükada's southern hill at 188 metres altitude. An active pilgrimage site since 1751, the path is dotted with ribbons tied to trees by pilgrims making a wish on the way up.

⏱ 60 min
3

Heybeliada, the archipelago's second island, houses the Orthodox Halki Seminary (1844), closed since 1971 by Turkish authorities but still symbolically important to 300 million Orthodox Christians. Its pine forests cover the hills and run down to the coves on the southern coast.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Rent a bike at the Büyükada jetty and cycle round the island (about 1h30) via the beaches, Victorian wooden villas and pine groves.

⏱ 90 min

Lunch at a fish restaurant in Büyükada harbour, trying the stuffed mussels (midye dolma) and balık ekmek, island specialities.

⏱ 45 min

Visit the Princes' Islands Museum (Adalar Müzesi), housed in a former warehouse east of Büyükada, to understand the archipelago's multicultural history—Byzantine, Ottoman, Greek, Jewish, Armenian.

⏱ 30 min

Istanbul Besiktas

★★★★★
📍 Besiktas waterfront Istanbul📍 Ciragan Palace Kempinski Istanbul📍 Yildiz Park Palace Istanbul📍 Besiktas fish market Istanbul📍 Naval Museum Istanbul Besiktas📍 Barbaros Hayrettin statue Besiktas
1

Built in 1871 by Nikoğos Balyan for Sultan Abdülaziz, Çırağan Palace stretches 300 metres along the Bosphorus. Gutted by fire in 1910, it was restored and converted into a hotel (Kempinski). Its white marble façade and gardens are accessible for a coffee with a view over the strait.

⏱ 30 min
2

Walk up to Yıldız Park, Sultan Abdülhamid II's former private gardens, covering a wooded hill above the Bosphorus. The Sale Pavilion (Şale Köşkü), built to host Emperor Wilhelm II in 1889, contains the world's largest single-piece carpet (400 m²).

⏱ 60 min
3

The Beşiktaş Naval Museum displays Ottoman imperial caïques—40-metre gilded ceremonial barges rowed by 144 oarsmen—alongside galley models and the sea charts of Admiral Piri Reis (16th century), the Ottoman cartographer whose 1513 world map shows the American coasts.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Browse Beşiktaş Market, one of the rare authentic neighbourhood markets on the European shore, to sample cheeses, olives and Turkish pastries.

⏱ 30 min

Finish the circuit from the Beşiktaş quays, çay in hand: on one side Dolmabahçe Palace, on the other the Bosphorus Bridge, and between them the strait where tankers and ferries pass.

⏱ 20 min
