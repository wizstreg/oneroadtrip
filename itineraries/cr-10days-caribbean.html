Costa Rica Road Trip: Wild Caribbean & Southern Pacific Coast

📅 11 days📍 10 stops🚗 910 km⏱ 22h45

Tortuguero and its Amazonian canals, Puerto Viejo and its Afro-Caribbean vibe, Uvita's whale tail tombolo, and Manuel Antonio Park: this circuit explores both faces of coastal Costa Rica, from the Atlantic side to the southern Pacific in ten days.

✨ Highlights
  • Tortuguero: silent navigation through Amazonian canals at sunrise
  • Green sea turtles: nighttime nesting on Tortuguero beaches (July-October)
  • Cahuita reef: one of Costa Rica's last intact coral reefs
  • Uvita: whale's tail tombolo visible only at low tide
  • Humpback whales at Marino Ballena Park: two migration seasons per year
📆 Best period

February, March, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 7 and 11 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (December-April): Perfect weather, minimal rainfall, peak tourist season, highest prices, ideal for beaches and outdoor activities.

Green Season (May-November): More rainfall, lush landscapes, fewer crowds, lower prices, afternoon showers typical, excellent for wildlife viewing.

Shoulder Seasons (April-May, November-December): Good balance weather and prices, moderate crowds, transitional periods, flexible travelers benefit.

Regional Variations: Caribbean coast different pattern, Pacific drier, mountain areas cooler, microclimates vary significantly.

🌍 What to discover in CR?

"Pura Vida" philosophy with incredible biodiversity, adventure activities, and eco-tourism leadership. Experience cloud forests, pristine beaches, and exotic wildlife in stable Central American democracy. The country offers zip-lining, volcano hikes, and sustainable tourism practices. Costa Rica provides adventure and nature in environmentally conscious paradise.

Day 1

Tortuguero

Where jungle canals whisper with howler monkeys and sea turtles claim the night

★★☆☆☆
1

The journey from San José to Tortuguero unfolds in two stages: a roughly 3-hour drive to the La Pavona boat dock, then a 90-minute river cruise along the canals of the Rio La Suerte and Tortuguero River. Both boat segments are experiences in themselves—howler monkeys swinging through the treetops, herons stalking the muddy banks, vegetation closing overhead like a living tunnel.

⏱ 90 min
2

Tortuguero is a village of roughly 1,800 people sandwiched on a narrow strip of land between the Caribbean Sea and the national park canals. No cars allowed—people get around on foot, by bicycle, or by boat. The main walkway hosts a handful of restaurants, souvenir shops, and tour operators.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Arrive mid-afternoon. First evening in the village: stroll the main walkway, grab dinner at one of the small Caribbean eateries serving rice & beans with fresh-caught fish. No ATMs here—bring enough cash before you leave San José.

⏱ 120 min

Day 2

Tortuguero

Where jungle canals whisper with howler monkeys and sea turtles claim the night

★★☆☆☆
1

Tortuguero National Park spans 76,937 hectares, more than 50,000 of which are marine. Its network of canals snakes through humid tropical rainforest often called Costa Rica's mini-Amazon. The waterways—Caño Chiquero (2.3 km), Caño Harold, Caño Palma (5.1 km)—can be navigated by canoe or kayak without motors to preserve the silence.

⏱ 120 min
2

The El Jaguar land trail (2.5 km, running parallel to the beach) cuts through humid tropical forest from the village. Waterproof boots are available to rent at the entrance—essential during wet conditions.

⏱ 120 min
3

Cerro Tortuguero is a small 120-meter hill accessible by boat then on foot from the village. Its summit offers the only panoramic view in the region: Caribbean Sea, network of canals, and endless forest.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Head out on an early morning canoe or kayak excursion through the canals—ideally without a motor. The boat glides silently beneath the canopy: caimans sunning on the banks, spider monkeys and howlers in the branches, green and blue kingfishers perched in ambush. Wildlife is most active in the morning.

⏱ 120 min

During nesting season (July-October), a guided nighttime walk on the beach lets you watch green sea turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs in the black sand. An accredited guide is mandatory. No lights or flash photography permitted.

⏱ 150 min
Day 3

Puerto Viejo

Where reggae rhythms meet coral reefs and the Caribbean pulses Afro-Tico

★★★☆☆
1

The return from Tortuguero involves a boat ride back to La Pavona, then a drive toward the Caribbean coast past Limón. Puerto Viejo de Talamanca is about 5-6 hours total travel time. The road hugs the coast after Limón—your first glimpse of Costa Rica's Atlantic side, very different from the Pacific: greener water, jungle straight down to the sand.

⏱ 60 min
2

Puerto Viejo is a former fishing village that's become the epicenter of the southern Caribbean coast. Its Afro-Caribbean, Bribri, and Tico blend creates an atmosphere you won't find anywhere else in Costa Rica—reggae music, vivid colors, rondón (Caribbean coconut milk soup) served in roadside shacks.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

First evening in Puerto Viejo: explore the waterfront on foot or by bike. Bike rental is the local mode of transport—distances between beaches are short and the road is flat.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 220 km · 5h30
Day 4

Puerto Viejo

Where reggae rhythms meet coral reefs and the Caribbean pulses Afro-Tico

★★★☆☆
1

Cahuita National Park, 20 km from Puerto Viejo, protects one of Costa Rica's best-preserved coral reefs. The reef surrounds Punta Cahuita: 35 coral species including some colonies reaching 2 meters in diameter, 123 fish species, nurse sharks, and manta rays. A 1991 earthquake lifted the coast by one meter, damaging part of the coral—but the reef is regenerating.

⏱ 60 min
2

The park's coastal trail (8 km from Kelly Creek to Puerto Vargas) follows the beach, alternating between humid tropical forest and oceanfront. Sloths, howler monkeys, raccoons, iguanas, and countless bird species are regularly spotted. The park is community-managed and operates on a voluntary donation basis—entry is free.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Snorkeling trip with a certified guide on the Cahuita reef, accessible only by boat with an escort. Local guides offer 2-hour sessions from the beach with all equipment provided.

⏱ 120 min

Return to Puerto Viejo for a free afternoon at Playa Cocles, the area's most popular surf beach. Waves suitable for all levels—locals offer year-round lessons.

⏱ 90 min
Day 5

Puerto Viejo

Where reggae rhythms meet coral reefs and the Caribbean pulses Afro-Tico

★★★☆☆
1

The road south of Puerto Viejo passes a succession of beaches for 13 km to Manzanillo. Punta Uva is the most stunning in the area: turquoise water, golden sand, jungle right down to the shore. Playa Manzanillo, within the Refugio Nacional Gandoca-Manzanillo, marks the southern boundary of the protected zone.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Gandoca-Manzanillo Refuge protects 10 km of beach, 740 hectares of forest, a coral reef, and two marshes. It's also a nesting area for sea turtles, manatees, crocodiles, and dolphins. Marked trails run through the forest from Manzanillo village.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Full-day bike ride along the Puerto Viejo-Manzanillo coastal road. The 13 km stretch is flat, shaded, and punctuated with beaches. It's the best way to explore the coast at the local pace.

⏱ 180 min

Snorkeling at Punta Uva or Manzanillo. These are the two best snorkeling spots on the Caribbean coast after Cahuita: coral reefs, tropical fish, rays, and sometimes sea turtles.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 210 km · 4h30
Day 6

San Jose

Where coffee barons built an opera house and pre-Columbian gold still glitters underground

★★★★★
1

San José is the pivot point between the two coasts. The Puerto Viejo-San José drive (approx. 4h30) crosses Braulio Carrillo Park, recognizable by its hyper-dense vegetation and the Zurquí Tunnel—one of the few passages through the central mountain range.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Arrive late morning. Free afternoon in the center: Barrio Amón for its Victorian houses, Mercado Central for lunch, Plaza de la Cultura for a first urban taste before descending to the Pacific.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 175 km · 3h30
Day 7

Manuel Antonio

Where the Pacific catches its breath in turquoise coves guarded by capuchins

★★☆☆☆
1

Manuel Antonio National Park is Costa Rica's smallest (683 hectares of land) but one of its richest in biodiversity. The main trail, flat and well-maintained, follows an 18-hectare mangrove before reaching Playa Manuel Antonio, considered among the country's finest beaches.

⏱ 180 min
2

Punta Catedral is a former island connected to the mainland by a sand tombolo, forming a whale-tail-shaped peninsula. The trail circling it offers views of both beaches and the park's 12 islands, seabird sanctuaries.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hire a certified naturalist guide at the park entrance. Equipped with a spotting scope, they'll locate two-toed sloths, white-faced capuchin monkeys, and the squirrel monkeys endemic to Manuel Antonio—virtually impossible to find on your own in the dense vegetation.

⏱ 150 min

Swimming at Playa Manuel Antonio after the hike. Snorkeling possible on the sides of the beach to spot moray eels, angelfish, and starfish.

⏱ 90 min
Day 8

Manuel Antonio

Where the Pacific catches its breath in turquoise coves guarded by capuchins

★★☆☆☆
1

Biesanz Bay, a small protected cove minutes from the hillside hotels, is ideal for relaxed snorkeling: sandy bottom, clear water, rays and parrotfish frequently spotted.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Kayak excursion through the Isla Damas mangrove. The channels of the Río Damas estuary harbor crocodiles, monkeys, egrets, and terns. Local operators provide equipment and guides.

⏱ 180 min

Free afternoon in Manuel Antonio. The Quepos marina offers offshore sport fishing, coastal tours, and sea kayak rentals.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h15
Day 9

Uvita

Where the ocean sculpted a whale's tail in sand and humpbacks breach twice a year

★★☆☆☆
1

Marino Ballena National Park, Costa Rica's first marine park (1989), takes its name from the humpback whales that migrate annually through its waters. Its main attraction is the Punta Uvita tombolo: at low tide, a sand spit emerges from the water and takes on the exact shape of a whale's tail when viewed from above. Walking on this sandbar, ocean on both sides, is one of the country's most beautiful experiences.

⏱ 120 min
2

Nauyaca Falls, accessible from Dominical about 30 minutes from Uvita, ranks among Costa Rica's most spectacular waterfalls. The ~16 km round-trip hike can also be done on horseback. Swimming is permitted in the lower pool.

⏱ 300 min

Activities

Boat excursion for humpback whale watching. Two migration seasons: December-April (Northern Hemisphere whales) and July-October (Southern Hemisphere whales). Spotted dolphins and bottlenose dolphins are present year-round. Manta rays are also regularly spotted.

⏱ 180 min

Low-tide snorkeling near the tombolo. The coral reefs around Isla Ballena (2 km from the tombolo) harbor whitetip reef sharks, spiny lobsters, and scorpionfish.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 170 km · 3h30
Day 10

San Jose

Where coffee barons built an opera house and pre-Columbian gold still glitters underground

★★★★★
1

San José's National Theater, inaugurated in 1897, was financed by a coffee tax imposed on Central Valley producers. Its neoclassical facade opens onto an Italian-style hall decorated with imported marble and paintings commissioned from European artists.

⏱ 60 min
2

Beneath Plaza de la Cultura, the Pre-Columbian Gold Museum houses more than 1,600 pieces created by the peoples who lived in Costa Rica before the Spanish arrival: animal figurines, ritual jewelry, fine gold pendants. The underground architecture of the museum is itself remarkable.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Jade Museum, facing Plaza de la Democracia, holds the largest collection of pre-Columbian jade in the Americas. The jadeite pendants, masks, and figurines date from 500 BC to 800 AD.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Final morning in San José before your flight. Stroll along Avenida Central between Plaza de la Cultura and Parque Central. Stop at Mercado Central for a last Costa Rican coffee and end-of-trip shopping.

⏱ 120 min
