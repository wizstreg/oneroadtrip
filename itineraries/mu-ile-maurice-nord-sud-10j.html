May, June, July, August, September, October, November

⏳ How many days

To make the most of it, plan between 2 and 6 days.

☀️ Weather

Dry Season (May-October): Perfect weather with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures (20-26°C), and trade winds. Peak season for outdoor activities, hiking, and beach experiences with clear skies.

Shoulder Season (April, November): Excellent weather with brief showers, moderate hotel rates, and fewer crowds. Ideal balance of good conditions and value pricing for most activities.

Wet Season (November-April): Hot, humid weather (25-32°C) with cyclone risk, but lush landscapes and excellent diving conditions. Lower accommodation rates but weather monitoring essential.

Cyclone Season (January-March): Highest rainfall and storm risk requiring flexible planning and comprehensive travel insurance. However, many cyclones pass without major impact.

1

Cap Malheureux owes its name to the shipwrecks that littered this wind-battered headland. The Notre-Dame Auxiliatrice church - red roof, white walls - is one of the most photographed in Mauritius. Facing it, Coin de Mire rises on the horizon about 18 km offshore.

Pereybere is a pocket beach with a different vibe from Grand Baie - more local, less touristy. Vendors selling gâteaux piments walk along the waterfront late morning. ⏱ 60 min

Activities

Option A - if you haven't done the catamaran yet: afternoon swim at Pereybère or Bain Boeuf, local atmosphere, no resort in sight. Option B - if you prefer to dedicate this entire day to the islets: catamaran excursion to Coin de Mire, Île Plate, and Îlot Gabriel (about 6 hours, lunch on board, snorkeling around Îlot Gabriel). If going this route, book the day before and depart in the morning directly from Grand Baie harbour - Cap Malheureux and Pereybère can be done in 1 hour the evening before.

Grand Baie

★★★★☆
1

The Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam Botanical Garden in Pamplemousses is one of the oldest in the tropics - founded in 1770 under Pierre Poivre, administrator of the Îles de France. It's home to over 650 species including giant Victoria amazonica water lilies and talipot palms, which flower only once in 60 years before dying. Allow at least 1.5 hours.

⏱ 90 min
2

L'Aventure du Sucre in Beau Plan is a museum housed in a 19th-century sugar factory. It tells the story of sugarcane - and of the slavery that made it possible. The period machinery is still in place. Allow 1 to 1.5 hours.

⏱ 75 min
3

Château de Labourdonnais in Mapou is a colonial mansion built in 1859 - one of the few in the country still habitable. The estate includes an orchard with 70 fruit species and a craft rum distillery. The guided tour lasts about 45 minutes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Tasting of rum and artisanal jams at Château de Labourdonnais. The house-made flavoured rums use fruit from the orchard - it's a short but pleasant stop at the end of the tour.

⏱ 30 min

Trou D Eau Douce

A fishing village where painted boats set off for paradise lagoons

★★★☆☆
1

Île aux Cerfs is reached from Trou d'Eau Douce by boat - a 10-minute crossing. The island belongs to a hotel complex but beach access is public. Take time to walk along the eastern shore: the sand is white, the lagoon a vivid blue, and crowds concentrate mainly around the central snack bars - just walk 10 minutes to find space.

⏱ 180 min
2

The lagoon on the northwest side of Île aux Cerfs is the calmest and most photogenic - shallow waters, white sand bottom, corals visible to the naked eye. This is where glass-bottom boat tours operate. Operators offer short rotations of about 45 minutes.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Snorkeling in the Île aux Cerfs lagoon - visibility is excellent and coral beds are accessible without tanks. Operators also offer glass-bottom boat tours around the island.

⏱ 120 min

Return to Trou d'Eau Douce late afternoon. Take a walk along the waterfront - it's a quiet fishing village with painted boats moored along the wharf. A good hour before dinner.

⏱ 45 min

1

Belle Mare is one of the longest beaches in Mauritius - about 9 km of continuous white sand. Fewer resorts than on the west coast, more space. Go early morning to catch the low-angle light on the water.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Vallée de Ferney Nature Reserve protects one of the island's last endemic forests. You may spot the Mauritius kestrel - a falcon that nearly went extinct in the 1970s and whose population recovered thanks to a captive breeding programme. The main trail takes 2 hours return.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Flacq Market if you're there Wednesday or Sunday - it's one of the liveliest open-air markets on the island. Spices, vegetables, fabrics, fish: all of everyday Mauritian life converges here. Allow 1 hour to wander.

⏱ 60 min

If it's not a market day: drive along the coast between Pointe de Flacq and Roches Noires - near-deserted beaches, fishermen mending their nets, no tourists. This is the authentic east, 15 minutes from Belle Mare.

⏱ 60 min

Mahebourg

Where Creole verandas overlook the island's only classified marine park

★★☆☆☆
1

Mahébourg is the most authentic village in the southeast - a waterfront without hotels, Creole houses, old fishermen on the wharves. The National History Museum recounts the Battle of Grand Port in August 1810: Napoleon's only naval victory against the British, it's inscribed on the Arc de Triomphe in Paris. The visit takes about 45 minutes.

⏱ 60 min
2

Blue Bay has been classified as a marine park since 2000 - one of the best-preserved coral reefs in the western Indian Ocean. Operators offer glass-bottom boat tours that let you see the corals without getting wet. Allow 1 hour for the outing.

⏱ 60 min
3

Pointe d'Esny is a quiet beach 3 km from Blue Bay - less crowded, lined with casuarinas, with a clear, shallow lagoon. Perfect for a calm swim late afternoon.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Snorkeling in Blue Bay Marine Park - branching corals, surgeonfish, leopard rays. The protected zone starts right at the surface. If you're a beginner, stay near shore; the seabed is readable from 1 to 2 metres down.

⏱ 90 min

Mahébourg market on Monday morning - fruit, spices, fresh fish of the day. It's a short stop but it gives a good pulse of the village. If you arrive Sunday evening, plan your Day 6 accordingly.

⏱ 45 min

Chamarel

Where volcanic soil bleeds seven colours and waterfalls carve basalt gorges

★★★★☆
1

Gris-Gris is one of the few places in Mauritius without a coral barrier - the Indian Ocean strikes the basalt rock directly. Waves throw spray several metres high. Don't climb down onto wet rocks. La Roche qui Pleure is 5 minutes on foot: water permanently seeps down the cliff face, creating a weeping effect - a geological curiosity.

⏱ 45 min
2

Rochester Falls are located 4 km from Souillac, accessible by a dirt track. The waterfall cascades over basalt columns with perfectly straight angles - a rare volcanic formation that gives the impression of manmade architecture. Drop height: about 10 metres. Wear closed shoes.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Macondé viewpoint offers one of the most spectacular coastal views in Mauritius - the cliff plunges down to Baie du Cap, and on clear days you can make out Île aux Bénitiers in the distance. It's on the road between the south coast and Chamarel: don't miss it even if you're tired. Parking on the roadside, 2 minutes' walk.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Road trip along the southern coastal road - from Souillac to Baie du Cap, the road follows wind-battered cliffs, abandoned cane fields, and fishing villages without tourists. Take your time, stop at random. This is the most 'road' day of the circuit: savouring the movement is part of the programme.

⏱ 120 min

Climb toward Chamarel via the switchbacks - the road rises quickly from the coast to the island's highlands. The bends offer sea views at every turn. Arrive late afternoon: settle in, and eat local tonight.

⏱ 30 min

Le Morne Brabant

A UNESCO mountain that holds the memory of those who chose freedom

★★★★☆
1

The Seven Coloured Earths of Chamarel are a geological curiosity formed by the decomposition of volcanic basalt into clays of different colours - ochre, brick red, violet, khaki green. The dunes are about 100 metres in diameter. What's remarkable is that the colours don't mix even in the rain. The visit takes 30 minutes.

⏱ 30 min
2

Chamarel Falls plunge about a hundred metres into the gorge below. They're among the highest in Mauritius. The viewing platform is accessible from the Seven Coloured Earths entrance - both visits are done together.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Rhumerie de Chamarel produces the island's only agricultural rums - and the only arabica coffee grown in Mauritius. Visit the plantations, follow the distillation process, finish with a tasting. Allow 1 hour. If Mauritian coffee intrigues you, this is the only place.

⏱ 60 min
4

Ebony Forest is a private reserve protecting a forest of endemic black ebony - a critically endangered species. The trail to Point Sublime (about 1 km return) opens onto a 180° view of Le Morne and the west coast. Leave early morning for the best light.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Descent to Le Morne mid-afternoon - 15 minutes' drive from Chamarel. The beach at Le Morne opens onto a perfectly calm lagoon on the north side. Swimming, hammock, or simply watching Le Morne Brabant from the water.

⏱ 120 min

Le Morne Brabant

A UNESCO mountain that holds the memory of those who chose freedom

★★★★☆
1

Le Morne Brabant rises to 556 metres and has been UNESCO-listed since 2008 - not for its geology, but for what it represents: in the 18th and 19th centuries, fugitive slaves took refuge in its inaccessible caves. Legend has it that they threw themselves off the cliffs on the day of the abolition of slavery in 1835, believing the soldiers who came to announce it were there to arrest them. The guided ascent takes 3 to 4 hours return. A guide is mandatory to reach the summit - book the day before.

⏱ 240 min

Activities

Kitesurfing in the Le Morne lagoon - it's one of the world's most renowned spots. The southeast trade wind blows steadily from May to November, and the lagoon is shallow with no current. Operators offer beginner courses. If you don't kitesurf, watching the riders from the beach is already worth something.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset facing Île aux Bénitiers from Le Morne beach - the islet silhouettes to the west, the sky turns orange then red. This is the last proper evening of the circuit. Take it in.

⏱ 60 min

Where Creole verandas overlook the island's only classified marine park

★★☆☆☆

Tamarin Bay is one of the few places in the world where spinner and humpback dolphins live year-round. Departures leave around 6:30 am from the wharves - dolphins are active early morning. Activities

Drive to SSR airport via the interior - about 1 hour 15 minutes from Tamarin. No particular difficulty, the road is well signposted. Plan to arrive 2.5 hours before your flight.
