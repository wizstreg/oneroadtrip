Italy's Most Beautiful Villages in Abruzzo

📅 27 days📍 26 stops🚗 749 km⏱ 14h59

From the trabocchi fishing platforms on the Adriatic Coast to the alpine meadows of Gran Sasso, passing through the impregnable fortress of Civitella del Tronto and the stone alleyways of Santo Stefano di Sessanio, Abruzzo forms central Italy's richest region for authentic medieval villages. Scanno and its women photographed by Cartier-Bresson, Pescocostanzo and its bobbin lace, Guardiagrele and its goldsmiths, Castelli and its ceramics—each stop adds a chapter to a story spanning centuries.

✨ Highlights
  • Medieval villages among the best-preserved in central Italy
  • Two national parks: Gran Sasso-Monti della Laga and Majella
  • Marsican bear, wolf, and red deer in Parco Nazionale d'Abruzzo
  • Living crafts: ceramics in Castelli, goldsmithing in Guardiagrele, lace in Pescocostanzo
  • Mountain cuisine: arrosticini, transhumance pecorino, Navelli saffron, Sulmona confetti
📆 Best period

May, June, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 28 and 32 days.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather, peak tourist season, highest prices, crowded attractions, vibrant atmosphere, beach season, festival time.

Fall (September-October): Excellent weather, harvest season, wine tourism, comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, beautiful colors, culinary focus.

Winter (November-March): Mild southern weather, Christmas markets, lowest prices, minimal crowds, cultural focus, cozy atmosphere, indoor attractions.

Cultural masterpiece with incredible art, cuisine, and historical heritage from Rome to Venice. Experience Renaissance treasures, regional diversity, and authentic Italian lifestyle. Day 1

Vasto

Where Roman ruins crown an Adriatic promontory at 144 meters

★★★★★
1

Vasto - a medieval town perched on a 144-meter promontory overlooking the Adriatic Sea, founded by the Romans as Histonium. The historic center preserves its medieval street layout with Renaissance palaces and cobblestone lanes paved with river stones.

⏱ 60 min
2

Castello Caldoresco - a 15th-century fortress built by Giacomo Caldora, Lord of Abruzzo, on the ruins of a Norman tower. Four corner towers and a central keep dominate the town. Now houses the Museo Civico and its Roman archaeological collection.

⏱ 75 min
3

Loggia Amblingh - a 16th-century Renaissance gallery, the sole surviving remnant of the D'Avalos palace. This Spanish-origin family ruled Vasto for two centuries. The poet Gabriele D'Annunzio, a native of Abruzzo, used to stroll here as a child during visits to the town.

⏱ 45 min
4

Punta Penna and lighthouse - a rocky point at the southern tip of the promontory, offering views of the Trabocchi Coast to the north and the Tremiti Islands on clear days. The lighthouse dates from 1905.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stroll along the Lungomare di Vasto - the seafront promenade below the promontory, with Vasto Marina beach and its 8 km of fine sand.

⏱ 90 min

Explore the Costa dei Trabocchi heading north - this coastline, designated a nature reserve, is famous for the trabocchi, ancient fishing machines on stilts planted in the sea, some dating back to the 17th century.

⏱ 120 min

Day 2

Rocca San Giovanni

A fortified hamlet guarding the ancient Benedictine abbey below

★★★★☆
1

Rocca San Giovanni - a tiny hilltop village of 2,300 inhabitants perched at 155 meters, whose fortified center once protected the Benedictine abbey of San Giovanni in Venere, founded in the 7th century. The Piazza degli Eroi brings together the Palazzo Municipale and the medieval Torre Civica in a compact ensemble.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Panoramic stop on the ramparts with views over the Adriatic Sea and the trabocchi of the Costa dei Trabocchi.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 10min
Day 3

Lanciano

Where flesh and blood appeared in the world's first documented Eucharistic miracle

★★★★★
1

Lanciano - a town of 35,000 inhabitants whose medieval center is divided into four historic quarters: Civitanova, Lancianovecchia, Sacca, and Borgo. In the Middle Ages, the town was an important commercial crossroads hosting international fairs that attracted Venetian and Oriental merchants.

⏱ 60 min
2

Santuario del Miracolo Eucaristico - an 8th-century building housing what is considered the first documented Eucharistic miracle in Catholic history, dated 750 AD. A Basilian monk reportedly witnessed the host transform into flesh and wine into blood. The relics are preserved in gilded urns, scientifically examined in 1971: analyses identified human cardiac tissue of blood type AB.

⏱ 60 min
3

Torri Montanare - two 14th-century medieval towers connected by an arch, remnants of the town's ancient Angevin fortifications. They constitute Lanciano's visual emblem.

⏱ 30 min
4

Cattedrale della Madonna del Ponte - a baroque cathedral built between 1687 and 1791 on a 1st-century AD Roman bridge with five arches, still visible below. This juxtaposition of two eras is unique in the region.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk through the Lancianovecchia quarter, the oldest district, with its medieval alleyways, carved portals, and 15th-century palaces.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 14 km · 17min
Day 4

Crecchio

A four-towered ducal castle protecting Byzantine gold treasures

★★☆☆☆
1

Castello Ducale di Crecchio - a 14th-century fortress with four corner towers, dominating the village from a 265-meter hill. The castle houses the Museo dell'Abruzzo Bizantino ed Altomedievale, whose centerpiece is the Reggio treasure, a collection of 7th-century gold jewelry discovered in 1955. The last king of the Two Sicilies, Francesco II, took refuge here in 1861 after the fall of Gaeta.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Quick stop in the medieval center's narrow streets before continuing to Penne.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 59 km · 1h11
Day 5

Penne

Where every street glows orange with locally fired red brick

★★★★★
1

Penne - the city of red brick, built across four hills (Sacro, Romano, Castello, and Cappuccio) at 438 meters altitude. The entire historic center, ranked among Italy's most beautiful, is constructed from locally fired bricks that give the streets a characteristic orange hue, unique in Abruzzo.

⏱ 60 min
2

Cattedrale di San Massimo - a Romanesque cathedral rebuilt in the 12th-13th centuries on the foundations of a Roman temple. The red brick façade and 14th-century octagonal bell tower make it the most representative building of Penne's architecture.

⏱ 45 min
3

Chiesa dell'Annunziata - a 15th-century church with a late Gothic carved portal, central to Holy Week ceremonies with the Good Friday procession that has united the entire town since the 16th century.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Visit the Riserva Naturale Lago di Penne - a 146-hectare WWF reserve around an artificial lake created in 1959. Home to grey herons, redshanks, and otters. Visitor center with exhibitions on local fauna.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 21 km · 25min
Day 6

Citta Sant Angelo

A ridge-top village gazing at both sea and mountain peaks

★★★★★
1

Città Sant'Angelo - a village of 15,000 inhabitants on a ridge at 317 meters, with simultaneous views of the Adriatic Sea and the Gran Sasso peaks. The Chiesa di San Michele Arcangelo, rebuilt in the 14th century, features a 40-meter bell tower and a columned portico that forms the main entrance to the fortified town.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stop at Piazza Municipio for views of Gran Sasso and the coast before continuing on.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 69 km · 1h23
Day 7

Civitella Del Tronto

The kingdom's last defiant fortress, surrendering only in 1861

★★★★★
1

Civitella del Tronto - a village of 5,000 inhabitants perched on a travertine spur at 589 meters in the Val Vibrata hills. The medieval center stretches along a narrow ridge with alleyways 1-2 meters wide, lined with medieval houses with white limestone façades.

⏱ 60 min
2

Fortezza Borbonica - one of Europe's largest military fortresses, 500 meters long and 50 meters wide, covering 25,000 square meters. Built between the 14th and 17th centuries, it resisted all assaults until 1861, capitulating two weeks after the proclamation of the Kingdom of Italy—the last stronghold of the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies to surrender.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Excursion to the Gole del Salinello - 5-kilometer limestone gorges carved by the Salinello torrent, accessible from the village of Ripe (6 km away). Marked trail along waterfalls and vertical walls.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 10min
Day 8

Campli

Where pilgrims climb 28 sacred steps on their knees

★★★★★
1

Campli - a medieval town of 7,000 inhabitants at the foot of the Monti Gemelli, between the Siccagno and Fiumicino valleys. The Scala Santa is a replica of Pontius Pilate's staircase in Jerusalem, brought to Rome by Saint Helena in the 4th century. Campli's version, built in 1480 on commission from the Farnese family, has 28 steps that pilgrims climb on their knees.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Quick stop at the Palazzo Farnese, a 15th-century Renaissance building testifying to the powerful Roman family's patronage in the region.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 40 km · 48min
Day 9

Pietracamela

Stone houses clinging to vertical walls beneath the Apennines' highest peak

★★★★☆
1

Pietracamela - a village of 300 inhabitants at 1,005 meters altitude, perched on a rocky spur at the foot of Corno Piccolo (2,655 m) in the Gran Sasso. The medieval center preserves characteristic dry-stone houses built side by side and vaulted alleyways. Starting point for climbers ascending Corno Grande (2,912 m), the roof of the Apennines.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stop at Prati di Tivo (1,450 m), a ski station and alpine meadow 4 km from the village, with sweeping views of the Gran Sasso walls.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 22 km · 26min
Day 10

Castelli

Where 1,200 painted majolica tiles cover a church ceiling

★★★☆☆
1

Castelli - a village of 1,000 inhabitants at the foot of Monte Camicia (2,564 m), renowned since the 16th century for its majolica ceramics. The Grue, Gentili, and Cappelletti families produced pieces here now preserved in Europe's greatest museums, including London's Victoria & Albert Museum.

⏱ 60 min
2

Chiesa di San Donato - a small 16th-century church outside the center, whose ceiling is entirely covered with 1,200 majolica tiles painted by the village's master ceramists between 1615 and 1617. Each tile depicts a saint or floral motif, forming Italy's most extraordinary architectural majolica ensemble.

⏱ 60 min
3

Museo della Ceramica di Castelli - a museum housed in a modern building tracing five centuries of local ceramic production. The collection includes pieces from the 16th-18th centuries, the golden age of Castelli workshops.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit working artisan workshops in the village: about ten ceramic workshops continue traditional techniques of painting on unfired majolica and wood-fired kiln firing.

⏱ 60 min

Hike toward Monte Camicia from Castelli - starting from the village toward the Gran Sasso alpine pastures, with views over the Vomano valley.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 27 km · 32min
Day 11

Santo Stefano Di Sessanio

A Medici mountain stronghold where houses become ramparts

★★★★☆
1

Santo Stefano di Sessanio - a village of 115 inhabitants at 1,251 meters altitude, built in concentric circles of gray limestone house-ramparts. The medieval defensive structure remains virtually intact: the outer houses themselves form the village perimeter, without a separate wall.

⏱ 60 min
2

Torre Medicea - a 20-meter cylindrical tower, the village's symbol, erected in the 15th century under Medici rule from Florence, who controlled the town from 1579 to 1743. Collapsed during the 2009 earthquake, it was completely rebuilt and reopened to the public in 2014.

⏱ 45 min
3

Palazzo Mediceo del Capitano - a 16th-century palace, residence of the Medici representative in the village. Now partially restored, it testifies to two centuries of Florentine domination over this mountain territory.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Excursion to Campo Imperatore (2,130 m) - a 30-kilometer high-altitude plateau nicknamed the 'Little Tibet.' Here Mussolini was imprisoned in 1943 before being exfiltrated by German glider on September 12.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 11 km · 13min
Day 12

Castel Del Monte

Where shepherds began their 244-kilometer transhumance march south

★★★☆☆
1

Castel del Monte (AQ) - not to be confused with the fortress in Puglia - a medieval village at 1,345 meters at the foot of Gran Sasso, with 400 permanent inhabitants. The village is historically linked to transhumance: each autumn, shepherds drove their flocks down to Puglia via the Tratturo Magno, a 244-kilometer transhumance route inscribed in national heritage.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Tasting of Pecorino Canestrato di Castel del Monte - a PDO sheep's cheese aged in woven baskets (canestri), an emblematic product of local transhumance.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 22 km · 26min
Day 13

Navelli

Where saffron brought by medieval monks turns fields to gold

★★☆☆☆
1

Navelli - a medieval village of 400 inhabitants at 970 meters, on a spur between Gran Sasso and Valle dell'Aterno. The Piana di Navelli produces Zafferano dell'Aquila PDO, introduced to the region in the 14th century by a Dominican monk returning from Spain. This saffron, hand-harvested in October, is considered among the world's finest for its safranal content.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Purchase Navelli PDO saffron from local cooperatives - several producers sell directly from the village.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 69 km · 1h23
Day 14

Tagliacozzo

Where Dante's battlefield still echoes with medieval steel

★★★☆☆
1

Tagliacozzo - a medieval town of 7,000 inhabitants in the Marsica, near the border with Lazio. The Battle of Tagliacozzo in 1268 saw Charles of Anjou crush the forces of Conradin of Hohenstaufen, the last Swabian claimant to the throne of Sicily. Dante cites this confrontation in the Divine Comedy.

⏱ 60 min
2

Piazza dell'Obelisco - the main 15th-century square, bordered by Renaissance palaces and arcades. The central obelisk, erected in 1770, commemorates the 1268 Angevin victory. The square is considered one of the region's most harmonious.

⏱ 45 min
3

Chiesa e Chiostro di San Francesco - a 13th-century Franciscan complex with a double-gallery cloister and a church preserving 15th-century frescoes attributed to the school of Benozzo Gozzoli.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Excursion to Lago del Salto - an artificial lake created in 1940 by the dam on the Salto river, 12 km from the center. Offers water activities and forest walks along its shores.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 91 km · 1h49
Day 15

Opi

A stone promontory overlooking valleys where bears still roam

★★★★☆
1

Opi - a village of 400 inhabitants at 1,250 meters, perched on a promontory-shaped rocky spur in the heart of Parco Nazionale d'Abruzzo, Lazio e Molise, founded in 1923. The medieval town with narrow alleyways offers direct panoramic views of the Valle del Sangro and the park's beech forests.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Starting point for wildlife observation in the national park: Marsican bear (about 50 individuals in the park), Apennine wolf, and red deer are regularly spotted in nearby alpine pastures.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 6 km · 7min
Day 16

Pescasseroli

Where Italy's first national park begins at a philosopher's birthplace

★★★★★
1

Pescasseroli - the unofficial capital of Parco Nazionale d'Abruzzo, Lazio e Molise, with 2,200 inhabitants at 1,167 meters. Birthplace of Benedetto Croce (1866-1952), a major philosopher and historian of liberal Italy. Its historic center with 18th-century houses is the liveliest and best-preserved of the park's villages.

⏱ 60 min
2

Museo Naturalistico del Parco - the national park's museum presenting local fauna and flora, with taxidermied specimens of Marsican bear, wolf, and lynx. Ideal for understanding conservation issues before exploring the territory.

⏱ 60 min
3

Area Faunistica del Lupo - a 2-hectare enclosure on the village outskirts where injured or non-releasable Apennine wolves live in a semi-natural habitat. Open year-round.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hike on the B1 trail toward Valle di Rose - 3-hour round trip through beech forests with high probability of observing deer and roe deer. Marsican bears frequently visit this area in spring.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 21 km · 25min
Day 17

Scanno

Where Cartier-Bresson found timeless life beside a heart-shaped lake

★★★★★
1

Scanno - a village of 1,800 inhabitants at 1,050 meters, photographed since the 1950s by Henri Cartier-Bresson, Mario Giacomelli, and Josef Koudelka, who saw in it a peasant world of rare visual coherence. The historic center preserves 17th-century carved portals, noble palaces, and Renaissance fountains.

⏱ 90 min
2

Museo della Lana - a museum housed in an 18th-century palace, tracing two centuries of local textile industry. Scanno was a wool cloth production center until the early 20th century, exporting to Naples and Rome.

⏱ 45 min
3

Lago di Scanno - a heart-shaped natural lake as seen from the SS479 road belvedere, formed 12,000 years ago by a landslide that blocked the Tasso valley. At 1,000 meters altitude, its waters reach 32 meters deep.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb to the Scanno panoramic viewpoint on the SS479 - aerial view of the heart-shaped lake, 10 minutes on foot or by car from the village.

⏱ 45 min

Hike through the Gole del Sagittario - 3-kilometer limestone gorges between Scanno and Anversa degli Abruzzi, traversed by a narrow road along the torrent. Habitat of the griffon vulture reintroduced in 2000.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 19 km · 23min
Day 18

Anversa Degli Abruzzi

A Norman outcrop guarding the entrance to 200-meter gorges

★★☆☆☆
1

Anversa degli Abruzzi - a village of 400 inhabitants on a rocky spur at 660 meters at the entrance to the Gole del Sagittario. Vestiges of an 11th-century Norman castle dominate the village. The Chiesa di Santa Maria delle Grazie preserves a 12th-century Romanesque portal and 15th-century frescoes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

View of the Gole del Sagittario entrance from the village belvedere - the 200-meter-high limestone cliffs framing the torrent are visible from the historic center terraces.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 19 km · 23min
Day 19

Sulmona

Where Ovid was born and sugared almonds became an art form

★★★★☆
1

Sulmona - a town of 24,000 inhabitants in the Valle Peligna basin, birthplace of the Latin poet Ovid (43 BC - 17 AD), who opens his Tristia with 'Sulmo mihi patria est' ('Sulmona is my homeland'). The medieval historic center is built on Roman ruins and preserves a coherent urban fabric from the 13th to 17th centuries.

⏱ 60 min
2

Palazzo dell'Annunziata - a 14th-15th century Gothic-Renaissance building, considered the most representative monument of Abruzzese civil architecture. The façade combines a flamboyant Gothic portal, Renaissance windows, and bas-relief sculptures depicting the Annunciation.

⏱ 60 min
3

Acquedotto Medievale - a 13th-century aqueduct crossing Piazza Garibaldi on Gothic arches. Built by Charles I of Anjou to supply the town with water from the mountains, it is still partially functional.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Discover the confetti di Sulmona - sugar-coated almonds produced in Sulmona since the 15th century. The confectioners on Via Mazara are organized in a cooperative; they create floral compositions from silk threads and dragées, exported worldwide.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 12min
Day 20

Pacentro

Where Madonna's ancestors built in white Majella stone

★★★★☆
1

Pacentro - a village of 1,200 inhabitants built entirely from white Majella stone at 720 meters altitude. The Castello Caldoresco, with its three 14th-century cylindrical towers, dominates the village. Madonna Ciccone, whose ancestors are from Pacentro, visited the village in 1995 to trace her roots.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb to the castle towers for panoramic views of Valle Peligna and the Majella foothills.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 8 km · 10min
Day 21

Pettorano Sul Gizio

A 13th-century castle watching two rivers merge

★★★☆☆
1

Pettorano sul Gizio - a medieval village of 900 inhabitants at 720 meters overlooking the confluence of the Gizio and Sagittario rivers. The 13th-century castle, successively owned by the Cantelmo and Caracciolo families, preserves its square keep and curtain walls. At its foot, the Riserva Naturale Monte Genzana Alto Gizio protects ancient beech forests.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stop at Piazza del Municipio to view the castle-village ensemble from the central fountain before continuing on.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 26 km · 31min
Day 22

Caramanico Terme

Where Romans soaked in sulfurous springs beneath the Majella

★★★★★
1

Caramanico Terme - a thermal village of 1,900 inhabitants on a spur at 650 meters, at the confluence of the Orfento and Orta rivers in the heart of Parco Nazionale della Majella. The sulfurous springs have been exploited since Roman times; modern thermal baths use the waters for respiratory and rheumatological treatments.

⏱ 60 min
2

Chiesa di Santa Maria Maggiore - a 1476 Gothic building with a façade carved with plant and animal motifs. The interior preserves 15th-century frescoes, a 14th-century wooden crucifix, and a 16th-century Flemish tapestry depicting the Virgin and Child.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hike through the Gola dell'Orfento - a 6-kilometer gorge carved by the Orfento torrent into the Majella limestone, accessible from Caramanico. Habitat of peregrine falcon and otter.

⏱ 180 min

Thermal cure at Caramanico's establishments - sulfurous water treatments recommended for respiratory conditions, open from May to November.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 21 km · 25min
Day 23

Pretoro

A village stacked like a nativity scene on the mountainside

★★★★☆
1

Pretoro - a village of 900 inhabitants at 590 meters, clinging to the eastern slopes of Majella. Nicknamed 'il paese presepe' ('the nativity village') for its stone houses stacked on the hillside that form, from the valley below, a crèche-like silhouette. Home to the Museo del Lupo, one of Italy's rare museums entirely dedicated to the Apennine wolf.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stop at the belvedere overlooking the Aventino valley with views of the Majella's limestone walls before descending toward Guardiagrele.

⏱ 20 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 59 km · 1h11
Day 24

Pescocostanzo

Where baroque façades hide centuries of lace-making secrets

★★★☆☆
1

Pescocostanzo - a village of 1,000 inhabitants at 1,400 meters in the Altipiani Maggiori d'Abruzzo, considered one of Abruzzo's best-preserved towns. The historic center dates mainly from the 17th century, rebuilt after the 1706 earthquake in a sober baroque style with local stone houses and wrought-iron balconies.

⏱ 60 min
2

Collegiata di Santa Maria del Colle (La Collegiata) - an 11th-century basilica rebuilt between the 14th and 17th centuries. The interior features a 17th-century gilded coffered ceiling, carved wooden choir stalls, and wrought-iron grilles from local workshops considered masterpieces of Abruzzese ironwork.

⏱ 60 min
3

Museo del Tombolo - the bobbin lace museum, an art practiced in Pescocostanzo since the 17th century. The village's lacemakers produced commissions for the royal courts of Naples and Madrid. A few artisans still perpetuate the tradition.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk through the Bosco di Sant'Antonio - a monumental 1,200-hectare forest of black pines, beeches, and maples 6 km from the village, one of Italy's oldest managed forests, with some trees exceeding 500 years.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 19 km · 23min
Day 25

Palena

A phoenix village rising from 1943 ashes to shelter endangered bears

★★☆☆☆
1

Palena - a village of 1,500 inhabitants at 875 meters in the heart of Parco Nazionale della Majella, entirely rebuilt after its near-total destruction by German troops in 1943. The Castello Ducale, the only surviving medieval building, houses the Museo Geopaleontologico with Majella fossils.

⏱ 60 min
2

Area Faunistica dell'Orso Marsicano - a natural enclosure of several hectares where Marsican bears unfit for wild life are housed, visible from a marked discovery trail. The Marsican bear (Ursus arctos marsicanus) is an endemic subspecies of Abruzzo, with a total population estimated at 50-60 individuals.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike to the Eremo della Madonna dell'Altare - a 16th-century hermitage perched on a rocky spur at 1,100 meters, accessible by a marked 2-kilometer trail from the village.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 38 km · 46min
Day 26

Guardiagrele

Where 15th-century masters hammered silver into miracles

★★★★★
1

Guardiagrele - a town of 9,000 inhabitants at 576 meters, at the eastern foot of Majella, renowned since the Middle Ages for its goldsmiths and ironworkers. Nicola da Guardiagrele (c. 1385-1462), a goldsmith considered one of the greatest 15th-century Italian artists, was born here. His repoussé silver works are preserved in museums in Chieti, L'Aquila, and the Vatican.

⏱ 60 min
2

Collegiata di Santa Maria Maggiore - a 12th-century Romanesque cathedral with a 14th-century Gothic portal attributed to the school of Nicola da Guardiagrele. The 42-meter octagonal bell tower dominates the historic center. Preserves a silver polyptych by Nicola da Guardiagrele (1434).

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Discover the Presentosa in goldsmith workshops - this traditional gold or silver filigree jewel, circular and adorned with geometric motifs, is Abruzzo's emblematic jewelry piece, offered to women to ward off the evil eye.

⏱ 60 min

Tasting of Sise delle Monache - a three-humped pastry filled with ricotta cream and chocolate, a specialty invented by Clarissan nuns from the local convent in the 18th century.

⏱ 30 min
