Sao Raimundo Nonato

★★★☆☆
1

São Raimundo Nonato is the mandatory base for exploring the park. This town of roughly 35,000 inhabitants concentrates everything you won't find elsewhere: hotels, banks, supermarkets, restaurants. Since 2025, NSR airport no longer has regular commercial flights—access is via Petrolina (PE), 303 km away, about 4.5 hours along BR-235 then BR-324, now entirely paved. Drop your bags, locate the agency providing your guide, and save the afternoon for the Museu do Homem Americano, which lays the groundwork for everything you'll see next.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Museu do Homem Americano (FUMDHAM), opened in 1998 in São Raimundo Nonato, displays artifacts from over forty years of excavations: lithic tools, pottery fragments, human remains. This is where the skull from Toca dos Coqueiros is exhibited—it shows distinct characteristics from classic Amerindians and fuels the debate over early American settlement. Allow two hours to cover the entire collection. The foundation, created by Niède Guidon in 1986, manages the park in partnership with ICMBio and IPHAN.

⏱ 120 min
3

Make use of late afternoon with a visit to the Fábrica de Cerâmica Serra da Capivara in Coronel José Dias, 30 km from São Raimundo. The workshop was founded in 1995 by Niède Guidon as both a social and artistic project: it reproduces the park's rock art motifs on ceramics and provides employment for several dozen local artisans. It's also the only place to buy serious reproductions, made on-site—far superior to market copies. Open Monday through Saturday, reasonable prices.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Briefing with your guide (mandatory for park entry) on the next four days' program: choosing circuits according to ability level, packing your bag, water (3 liters minimum per person), closed-toe shoes, hat, flashlight for dark shelters. Ask your guide how to book evening access to Boqueirão da Pedra Furada when the site is lit up.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner on the central praça: carne de sol (dried and grilled meat), baião de dois (rice and beans melded together), grilled queijo coalho, all washed down with cajá or umbu juice—caatinga fruits you won't find anywhere else. Restaurants close around 10 p.m.

⏱ 90 min

Sao Raimundo Nonato

★★★☆☆
1

The Desfiladeiro da Capivara, roughly 40 km from São Raimundo, was the first circuit Niède Guidon explored in the 1970s and remains one of the most visited. First stop: Toca da Entrada do Pajaú, a small rock shelter whose paintings date to around 12,000 years BP. You'll see hunting scenes, dances, daily life, painted in red from iron oxides. The circuit is of medium difficulty—stone paths, stairs, moderate elevation changes.

⏱ 90 min
2

Toca do Inferno sits in a deep canyon, lacking paintings but boasting a rock cathedral with walls over 50 meters high in sandstone. Local legend claims a mysterious chant rises from the depths at noon—guides enjoy putting you to the test. Further along, Toca da Entrada do Baixão da Vaca offers 749 rock paintings spread across 100 meters of wall, one of the densest panels in the park. Watch out for snakes and bees in the shaded recesses.

⏱ 120 min
3

To finish the circuit, Toca do Paraguaio was the first site officially catalogued by researchers. Two skeletons were recovered here, dated respectively to 7,000 and 8,670 years BP. The paintings, from the Nordeste tradition, sometimes exceed 1.5 meters in height—rare in the park. Tip: get a guide who'll point out the details (running hunter, copulation scene, animal figures) because some panels are hard to read with the naked eye.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Lunch in the povoado of Barreirinho, near the park: local restaurant, comida caseira, galinha caipira, paçoca, mandacaru salad. Many agencies include this in the package. It's also a good time to chat with your guide about the park's oral history—many locals saw Niède Guidon arrive in the 1970s, and their families contributed to the excavations.

⏱ 75 min

On the way back, detour through Baixão das Andorinhas in late afternoon to watch the swallows return to the canyon. The spectacle lasts about 30 minutes: thousands of birds pour into the cliff at dusk to roost. Viewing platform provided, easy car access.

⏱ 60 min

Sao Raimundo Nonato

★★★☆☆
1

Serra Branca is the latest major circuit opened in the park, about 50 km from São Raimundo. Low difficulty, a nice day to rest your legs after yesterday. The olho d'água da Serra Branca, a natural spring, was the only water source for inhabitants before the park's creation in 1979. Several families were relocated to the povoado Novo Zabelê in compensation. The circuit includes sites Toca do Vento, Toca Extrema II, Toca do Veado, and Toca do João Arsena.

⏱ 90 min
2

Along this circuit, you'll also find remnants of the 'maniçobeiros,' families who extracted latex from the maniçoba (Manihot glaziovii) in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, during the rubber boom period. The local rock paintings, later than those at Desfiladeiro, depict hunting, warfare, and sexuality scenes with remarkable narrative precision. The stylistic tradition called 'Nordeste' has been studied since the 1980s by Anne-Marie Pessis, Guidon's longtime collaborator.

⏱ 90 min
3

Sítio João Pimenta, 20 km from São Raimundo in the Serra Vermelha region, is a private reserve (RPPN) managed by the descendants of João Pimenta, a former traditional occupant of the area. The trail is 1.5 km round-trip with sandstone formations that have been compared to Turkey's Cappadocia—exaggerated, but the atmosphere is superb. A few rock art sites and, by reservation, rappelling is possible. Visits in small groups only.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Visit the povoado of Novo Zabelê, created to resettle families displaced when the park was established in 1979. About a hundred inhabitants now live from beekeeping, cerrado fruit preserves, honey, and handicrafts. The project is partly supported by FUMDHAM. It's a chance to talk with the elders, who still remember the caves their parents frequented long before they became archaeological sites.

⏱ 90 min

For the more adventurous, the Caldeirão dos Rodrigues circuit offers a scalaminhada (half-climbing, half-hiking) on the plateau, passing through Toca do Caldeirão dos Rodrigues—one of the park's finest rock art compositions, dated to around 18,000 years BP according to excavations. Panoramic view of Grotão da Esperança canyon from the summit. Difficult level, plan 4 to 5 hours, bring plenty of water.

⏱ 240 min

Coronel Jose Dias

★★☆☆☆
1

The Museu da Natureza, opened December 18, 2018 in the municipality of Coronel José Dias, is the park's other major scientific institution. Scenography by Marcello Dantas (creator of the Museu da Língua Portuguesa in São Paulo), 1,700 m² of exhibitions across 12 rooms: birth of the universe, formation of the Earth, cerrado megafauna (giant sloths, mastodons), arrival of humans in the region. The final film, screened in the last room, is narrated by Maria Bethânia. Open Wednesday through Monday from 1 p.m. to 7 p.m., admission around 30 R$.

⏱ 150 min
2

At the museum exit, the mirante overlooks the park's sandstone cuestas—a breathtaking view, especially in late afternoon when the rock turns orange. A life-size replica of a giant sloth (up to 8 meters tall according to paleontologists) greets visitors on the way out. The project cost 13.7 million reais, financed by BNDES.

⏱ 30 min
3

Head to Toca da Pedra Furada around midday. No confusion with its namesake in Tocantins: here we're talking about a sandstone formation sculpted by wind into a natural arch, dominating the valley of the same name. The site has essentially lent its name to the park—it's become the iconic image of Serra da Capivara. Short access trail, little elevation gain.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In mid-afternoon, set out to visit Boqueirão da Pedra Furada itself, the great rock shelter that made the park known in the scientific world. The cavity measures 70 meters wide by 12 meters maximum depth and dominates the valley by about 20 meters. This is where excavations directed by Niède Guidon between 1978 and 1985, then by Fabio Parenti, uncovered traces of hearths and lithic tools studied in Nature in 1986. The stratigraphic sequence runs 5 meters deep, and the bottom contains dates exceeding 40,000 years BP—still debated in the scientific community.

⏱ 90 min

Dinner and overnight in a pousada in Coronel José Dias or return to São Raimundo depending on your program. Coronel José Dias is tiny (under 5,000 inhabitants) and very quiet—a good option for those who want a night in a more rural setting, near the park, after four intense days.

⏱ 90 min

Sao Raimundo Nonato

★★★☆☆
1

The Trilha Hombu—the word means 'come see, come with me' in a local indigenous language according to guides—is one of the most comprehensive circuits to wrap up your visit. It blends prehistoric sites (Toca da Invenção, Toca da Pedra Caída, Tocas da Ema do Sítio do Brás) with recent history: the Casa do Neco Coelho, one of the first families settled in the area before the park's creation, and the small Museu do Neco Coelho which traces the lives of 20th-century residents.

⏱ 120 min
2

Along this circuit, the guide is often a descendant of the region's historic families—sometimes a son or grandson of the former mateiro guides who accompanied Niède Guidon from the earliest expeditions. The narration thus weaves together scientific archaeology and family memory, lending the route a special character. The circuit includes an option to climb, via a ladder planted in the rock, to the top of the chapadão for a panoramic view.

⏱ 90 min
3

Before heading back to Petrolina, stop by downtown São Raimundo Nonato for last-minute shopping at the Fábrica de Cerâmica if you didn't make it on D1, or to buy copies of rock paintings directly from the artisans. The town lives largely off the park and the jobs it generates—every purchase counts.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Depending on your flight, drive to Petrolina in the afternoon: 303 km, about 4.5 hours on BR-324 then BR-235. The road is now entirely paved, passes through Dirceu Arcoverde, Remanso (BA), Casa Nova, and runs alongside the Rio São Francisco toward the end. Plan to leave no later than 2 p.m. for an evening flight from Petrolina.

⏱ 270 min

If you arrive in Petrolina early, detour to the banks of the Rio São Francisco at sunset. The city is twinned with Juazeiro (BA) on the opposite bank and this is one of South America's greatest rivers. A few floating restaurants serve grilled surubim, the river's king fish.

⏱ 90 min
