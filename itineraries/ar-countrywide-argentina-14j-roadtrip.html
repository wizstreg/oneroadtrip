Buenos Aires

★★★★☆
1

Plaza de Mayo and Casa Rosada - the square where Argentina's entire political history was written. The pink facade of the presidential palace, built in the 19th century, sets the tone for the city.

⏱ 60 min
2

Walk through San Telmo neighborhood via Defensa Street. The cobblestones, preserved colonial houses, and antique dealers operating from 19th-century galleries make this Buenos Aires' oldest quarter.

⏱ 75 min
3

Caminito in La Boca - alleyways lined with brightly painted corrugated metal facades created by Italian immigrants in the early 20th century. Tango dancers animate the terraces.

⏱ 60 min
4

La Bombonera - the exterior of Boca Juniors' legendary stadium, whose compressed U-shape is unique in the world. In Argentina, football structures neighborhood identities.

⏱ 45 min
5

Recoleta Cemetery - a true city of the dead, its neoclassical mausoleums and paved alleys house the remains of Argentina's elite, including Eva Perón. Allow at least 90 minutes.

⏱ 90 min
6

El Ateneo Grand Splendid, Recoleta - an early 20th-century theater converted into a bookstore. The boxes and painted ceiling have been preserved. Regularly ranked among the world's most beautiful bookstores.

⏱ 45 min
7

Teatro Colón - the exterior and foyer of this opera house inaugurated in 1908 are enough to measure the cultural ambition of Belle Époque Buenos Aires. Its auditorium has one of the world's finest acoustics.

⏱ 60 min
8

Bosques de Palermo - the city's grand park with its lakes and rose garden. Porteños come here to drink maté at all hours.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

On Sundays, the San Telmo Fair takes over Defensa Street and Plaza Dorrego. More than 300 antique dealers, second-hand vendors, artisans, and musicians transform the neighborhood into a massive market.

⏱ 120 min

Evening on Avenida Corrientes - theaters light up the night and bookstores stay open until midnight. This is Buenos Aires' Broadway.

⏱ 90 min

Domestic flight from Buenos Aires to Salta for the next leg of the itinerary.

⏱ 60 min

Salta

★★★★☆
1

Plaza 9 de Julio - Salta's colonial heart, shaded by more than 250 trees including jacarandas and ceibos. The surrounding buildings showcase the city's preserved architecture: a pink and cream cathedral, white cabildo.

⏱ 45 min
2

Catedral Basílica de Salta - built in the 19th century on the site of the first church, its pink and cream neocolonial facade is the city's emblematic image. It houses the remains of General Güemes, hero of independence.

⏱ 45 min
3

Iglesia San Francisco - its red and gold baroque facade and 53-meter bell tower make it Salta's most imposing building. Built in the 18th century, declared a national historic monument in 1941.

⏱ 40 min
4

Cabildo and Museo Histórico del Norte - one of Argentina's best-preserved colonial cabildos. The museum brings together 2,000 pieces: archaeology, colonial furniture, sacred art, even vintage cars in the courtyard.

⏱ 75 min
5

MAAM - Museo de Arqueología de Alta Montaña. Created to preserve the Inca mummies discovered in 1999 on Llullaillaco volcano (6,739 m), this museum presents three children sacrificed 500 years ago during the Capacocha ritual. The rooms are kept cold. Not to be missed.

⏱ 90 min
6

Cerro San Bernardo - the hill rises 269 m above Salta. From the summit, the view embraces the entire city and surrounding mountains. A Christ the Redeemer sculpture marks the highest point.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Cable car ascent to Cerro San Bernardo from San Martín Park - 8 minutes gliding above Salta's rooftops. The descent on foot via the 1,000-step staircase is possible for the brave.

⏱ 60 min

San Miguel Market - Andean crafts in an 18th-century colonial house: basketry, pottery, wrought iron, textiles. Argentina's first artisan market (1968).

⏱ 60 min

Evening peña folklorique in the Balcarce neighborhood - Andean music, dance, and Salta cuisine (empanadas, locro, tamales). Peñas come alive after 9pm.

⏱ 120 min

Purmamarca

★★★☆☆
1

Cerro de los Siete Colores - from the village's main square, the mountain displays its seven geological layers: red, purple, orange, green, ochre, white, blue. The result of millions of years of mineral deposits. Morning light is ideal.

⏱ 60 min
2

Iglesia Santa Rosa de Lima - small 17th-century colonial adobe church, designated a national historic monument. Its earthen walls and mud-brick bell tower blend perfectly with the mineral landscape.

⏱ 30 min
3

Village of Purmamarca - unpaved alleyways bordered by adobe houses, a few artisans still weaving. The village, designated a historic site since 1975, has successfully limited tourist growth.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Paseo de los Colorados - 3 km circular hike entirely pedestrian since 2023, circling the village at the foot of the mountains. Red rock formations, cacti, views over the desert valley.

⏱ 120 min

Plaza Mayor artisan market - the largest in the Quebrada. Vicuña wool textiles, ceramics, jewelry, cactus wood objects. A chance to chat with Coya artisans.

⏱ 60 min

Humahuaca

★★★★☆
1

Pucará de Tilcara - pre-Hispanic fortress perched on a hilltop, excavated from the early 20th century and partially restored. The stone walls, tombs, and corrals recreate the life of a people who preceded the Incas. Cactus garden at the entrance.

⏱ 75 min
2

Paleta del Pintor at Maimará - a stop along the way to see this multicolored wall: large slabs of rock with ochre, red, and green reflections composing a striking natural tableau. The small cemetery on the opposite hill completes the landscape.

⏱ 30 min
3

Historic center of Humahuaca - cobbled streets and white colonial houses at 2,939 m altitude. It's the most populated village in the Quebrada, with 11,500 inhabitants.

⏱ 45 min
4

Catedral Nuestra Señora de la Candelaria - on the main square. The interior preserves paintings from the 17th-century Cuzco School and the cactus-wood sculpture of the Virgin, protector of the village.

⏱ 40 min
5

Monument to the Heroes of Independence - erected in 1950, it dominates the village from a stone staircase. Its central figure represents the Cry of Freedom. Every day at noon, a figure of San Francisco Solano emerges from the cabildo to give the blessing.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Scenic drive through Quebrada de Humahuaca - inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2003, this 155 km valley follows the Río Grande between multicolored rock walls. National Route 9 offers photo stops at every turn.

⏱ 90 min

Excursion to Hornocal Viewpoint - 27 km track from Humahuaca climbing to 4,350 m altitude to discover the Serranía de Hornocal, a mountain range with V-inverted folded strata and extraordinary colors.

⏱ 180 min

Purmamarca

★★★☆☆
1

Salinas Grandes - at 3,450 m altitude, 525 km² of blinding white salt crust. Natural hexagons formed by evaporation and active extraction basins create a surreal landscape.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Cuesta del Lipán route - spectacular ascent from Purmamarca to the salt flat. In 35 km, the road climbs from 2,165 m to 4,170 m altitude in tight switchbacks, with plunging views over the Quebrada.

⏱ 90 min

Free walk on the salt flat - perspective games on the white salt allow for classic trompe-l'oeil photos. Local artisans set up stands at the salt flat entrance.

⏱ 60 min

Sunset from Cuesta del Lipán - descending back to Purmamarca, stop at the cuesta viewpoints to watch evening colors illuminate the Quebrada below.

⏱ 45 min

Cachi

★★★☆☆
1

Iglesia San José de Cachi - on the central square, this 17th-century church (renovated in 1947) has a ceiling and framework entirely carved from cardon cactus wood. The sky-blue altar contrasts with the white walls.

⏱ 40 min
2

Museo Arqueológico Pío Pablo Díaz - on the square, this well-presented museum traces 10,000 years of human occupation in the Calchaquí Valleys. More than 5,000 pieces: ceramics, tools, everyday objects from the Calchaquí people.

⏱ 60 min
3

Plaza 9 de Julio and Cachi center - village at 2,280 m altitude, cobbled streets, white houses with cactus-wood frameworks. Several signposts in the village are themselves carved from cardon wood.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Crossing Los Cardones National Park from Purmamarca - Route 33 climbs via Cuesta del Obispo (pass at 3,450 m) before descending into the park. 65,000 ha of candelabra cacti reaching up to 10 m high and living 300 years.

⏱ 180 min

Recta del Tin Tin - long 15 km straight line traced by the Incas through the park. The arid and windy plateau, bristling with thousands of cardons, creates a striking perspective effect. Stop at Valle Encantado (4 km track).

⏱ 45 min

⚠️ IRUYA OPTION (variant): from Humahuaca, a 70 km detour via a spectacular track leads to the perched village of Iruya (2,780 m), isolated in a mountain cirque. Route for 4x4 only, about 2 hours from Humahuaca. Plan the day before if chosen.

⏱ 240 min

Cafayate

★★★★☆
1

Museo de la Vid y el Vino - interactive museum opened in 2001 in a contemporary building designed to stimulate all the senses. It traces the wine history of the Calchaquí Valleys from Jesuit pioneers to current techniques.

⏱ 75 min
2

Plaza 20 de Febrero - Cafayate's central square, shaded and surrounded by bars and restaurants. The Catedral Nuestra Señora del Rosario (late 19th century) is one of the rare cathedrals with five naves.

⏱ 45 min
3

Quebrada de las Conchas - El Anfiteatro: semi-enclosed formation in red rock with almost perfect acoustics. Musicians often play here. Craft stands line the access path.

⏱ 60 min
4

Quebrada de las Conchas - Garganta del Diablo: deep funnel-shaped gorge, vertical sandstone walls. From the bottom, your gaze rises toward a corridor of blue sky.

⏱ 60 min
5

Cafayate artisan market - textiles, leather and wood objects, ceramics. Local products include torrontés ice cream, a specialty of the town.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Scenic RN68 route through Quebrada de las Conchas - 50 km of multicolored canyon between Cafayate and Alemania. Geological formations succeed one another with evocative names: Los Castillos, El Obelisco, El Sapo, Los Colorados. Morning light intensifies the reds.

⏱ 180 min

Bodega visit with tasting - Cafayate is one of Argentina's two major wine regions. At 1,600 m altitude, the vineyards produce torrontés, an aromatic white grape unique to this region, and high-altitude malbec with distinctive character.

⏱ 90 min

Salta

★★★★☆
1

Convento San Bernardo - built in the late 16th century, it cannot be visited inside but the baroque door carved from carob wood is one of the masterpieces of Argentine colonial art.

⏱ 30 min
2

Museo de Arte Étnico Americano Pajcha - collections dedicated to the indigenous peoples of Latin America from pre-Columbian times: macaw feather headdresses, Kallawaya healer instruments, Mapuche jewelry. A museum founded by an anthropologist.

⏱ 75 min
3

Quebrada de San Lorenzo - 15 km from downtown Salta, this wooded canyon reveals the yungas, a high-altitude subtropical forest contrasting with the puna's aridity. The main trail follows the river.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Hiking in Quebrada de San Lorenzo - access from the village of San Lorenzo (15 min from Salta). Several marked trails cross the yungas forest, populated with orchids, giant ferns, and endemic birds.

⏱ 150 min

Balcarce neighborhood at night - Balcarce Street concentrates Salta's peñas, bars, and restaurants. After 9pm, folk music spills from open doorways. This is where the city's nightlife happens.

⏱ 120 min

Tasting Salta empanadas - smaller and spicier than those from Buenos Aires, filled with bell pepper and cumin. Salta claims the best empanadas in Argentina.

⏱ 60 min
