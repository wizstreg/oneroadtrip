1

Since 1147, the Stephansdom has towered over Vienna at 136 meters - but it's in the catacombs that the visit gains real depth. 2

Falkenstein

★★★☆☆
1

Locals call it their Acropolis: the Burgruine Falkenstein on its limestone spur has dominated the Weinviertel vineyards since the mid-11th century. The fortress was built under Emperor Heinrich III as a bulwark against Bohemia and Hungary. The natural cave in the limestone and the museum of the Täuferbewegung - the Anabaptists once imprisoned here - justify the climb. Open April to October, guided tours on request.

⏱ 60 min
2

A wooden galley in an Austrian fortress: in the first courtyard of Burgruine Falkenstein, a full-size replica of a Venetian galley recalls the fate of Anabaptists condemned to the galleys of Trieste in the 16th century. The model was built by HTL-Mödling in 2010-2011. A detail that few audioguides mention, and one that children remember effortlessly.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb early: the view across the vineyards of Bohemia stretches to the Czech horizon in clear weather. The path from the village takes about twenty minutes on foot, well signposted. On Sundays at 3pm, a guided tour starts automatically with 8 or more people - no booking needed if you're in a large enough group.

Geras

1

Founded in 1153 by Count Ulrich von Pernegg, Stift Geras houses in its Marmorsaal the only Paul Troger ceiling fresco that has never been restored - an absolute rarity in the region. ⏱ 75 min
2

In the Naturpark Geras adjacent to the abbey, reconstituted aurochs graze freely on 132 hectares of forest and meadow - an extinct species whose genetic recreation programme produces animals surprisingly close to their ancestors. An observation platform allows you to see lynx, deer and heron without a guide. Open from Palm Sunday to 31 October.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The garden is freely accessible from May to October. The shop sells in-house preparations: Waldviertel herbal teas are among the best local labels to take home.

Two monasteries for the price of one: Stift Altenburg, founded in 1144, conceals beneath its sumptuous baroque building the entirety of a medieval convent, rediscovered during excavations in 1994. ★★★☆☆
1

Hard not to be intimidated entering the Haydnsaal of Schloss Esterházy in Eisenstadt: the acoustics have been praised since the 17th century, and the ceiling fresco of Cupid and Psyche forgives no inattention. Austria's largest wine museum is in the cellar, beneath Schloss Esterházy: the Vinothek Burgenland in the basement offers more than 850 regional labels by the glass or bottle, accompanied by local tapas. Ideal for an introduction to Pannonian reds before the Rust stop.

3

The Bergkirche of Eisenstadt: Haydn has been buried there since 1820, after a posthumous journey that surpasses fiction. The Haydngruft in the crypt of the Bergkirche is open year-round.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Stroll through the old town to Dom St. Martin: the cathedral is Burgenland's most important religious building, with its Gothic stained glass and cloister. ⏱ 60 min

If it's September, the Herbstgold festival occupies the Haydnsaal and the castle park with a programme mixing jazz, Balkan music and classical - it's the successor to the old Haydn Festspiele since 2017. Booking essential, tickets on esterhazy.at. Outside the festival, chamber concerts take place almost every week.
