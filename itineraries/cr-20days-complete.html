✨ Highlights
  • Tortuguero: silent navigation through Amazonian canals at sunrise
  • Green sea turtles: nighttime nesting on Tortuguero beaches (July-October)
  • Cahuita reef: one of Costa Rica's last intact coral reefs
  • Arenal 1968: walking the 1968 lava flows with direct views of the volcano
  • Resplendent Quetzal: observable in Monteverde and San Gerardo de Dota
  • Manuel Antonio: endemic squirrel monkeys and turquoise Pacific beaches
  • Uvita: whale tail tombolo visible only at low tide
  • Corcovado: among the most biodiverse primary forests on the planet
📆 Best period

December, January, February, March

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 14 and 18 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (December-April): Perfect weather, minimal rainfall, peak tourist season, highest prices, ideal for beaches and outdoor activities.

Green Season (May-November): More rainfall, lush landscapes, fewer crowds, lower prices, afternoon showers typical, excellent for wildlife viewing.

Shoulder Seasons (April-May, November-December): Good balance weather and prices, moderate crowds, transitional periods, flexible travelers benefit.

Regional Variations: Caribbean coast different pattern, Pacific drier, mountain areas cooler, microclimates vary significantly.

🌍 What to discover in CR?

"Pura Vida" philosophy with incredible biodiversity, adventure activities, and eco-tourism leadership. Experience cloud forests, pristine beaches, and exotic wildlife in stable Central American democracy. The country offers zip-lining, volcano hikes, and sustainable tourism practices. Costa Rica provides adventure and nature in environmentally conscious paradise.

The journey from San José to Tortuguero unfolds in two stages: drive to the La Pavona dock (~3h), then glide by boat through the Rio La Suerte and Tortuguero River canals (~1h30). Both boat segments feel less like transportation and more like your first wildlife safari—howler monkeys roar from overhead branches, herons stalk the muddy banks, and the jungle canopy closes in above the water like a green cathedral.

⏱ 90 min
2

Tortuguero village sits on a narrow strip of land pinched between the Caribbean Sea and the national park canals—home to roughly 1,800 people and zero cars. You navigate by foot, bicycle, or boat. The main walkway runs through the heart of town, lined with Caribbean restaurants, souvenir shops, and tour operators offering everything from night turtle walks to dawn kayak expeditions.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Arrive mid-afternoon. First evening in the village: stroll the main walkway, grab dinner at one of the small Caribbean eateries serving rice & beans with fresh-caught fish. No ATMs on-site—bring cash before you leave the mainland.

Tortuguero

Where jungle canals wind through the rainforest and sea turtles nest on midnight shores

★★☆☆☆
📍 Canaux parc national Tortuguero bateau📍 Trail El Jaguar Tortuguero📍 Cerro Tortuguero vue panoramique📍 Sea turtle beach night Tortuguero📍 Caño Harold Tortuguero kayak
1

Tortuguero National Park sprawls across 76,937 hectares—more than 50,000 of them submerged. Its labyrinth of waterways snakes through rainforest so dense it's been dubbed Costa Rica's Little Amazon. The aquatic trails—Caño Chiquero (2.3 km), Caño Harold, Caño Palma (5.1 km)—are navigable by canoe or motorless kayak, where silence is your passport to wildlife encounters.

⏱ 120 min
2

The El Jaguar trail (2.5 km) cuts through primary rainforest from the village edge. Waterproof rubber boots are available for rent at the entrance—essential when the trail turns to boot-sucking mud after rain.

⏱ 120 min
3

Cerro Tortuguero rises 120 meters as the only viewpoint in an otherwise pancake-flat region. Accessible by boat then on foot from the village, the summit delivers the region's sole panoramic vista: Caribbean Sea stretching east, the canal network threading west, and unbroken jungle canopy rolling to the horizon.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Early morning canoe or kayak excursion through the canals—motorless if possible. Your vessel glides silently beneath the canopy: caimans sunning on muddy banks, spider monkeys and howlers swinging through branches overhead, kingfishers perched in ambush. Wildlife activity peaks before 9am.

⏱ 120 min

During nesting season (July-October), join a guided night walk to watch green sea turtles haul themselves onto the black sand to lay eggs. Accredited guide required—no lights or camera flashes permitted.

⏱ 150 min

Leaving Tortuguero reverses your arrival: boat back to La Pavona, then drive south along the Caribbean coast past Limón (~5h30 total). Puerto Viejo de Talamanca began as a fishing village and evolved into the beating heart of Costa Rica's southern Caribbean—Afro-Caribbean soul, reggae rhythms drifting from beach bars, coconut milk in everything worth eating.

Puerto Viejo

A sun-drenched slice of the Caribbean where reggae rhythms meet turquoise reefs

★★★☆☆
📍 Cahuita National Park coastal trail📍 Récif corallien Punta Cahuita📍 Playa Blanca Cahuita📍 Playa Vargas Puerto Vargas Cahuita📍 Snorkeling récif Cahuita guide certifié
1

Cahuita National Park protects one of Costa Rica's healthiest coral reefs: 35 coral species, 123 fish species, nurse sharks cruising the shallows, and manta rays gliding past on invisible currents. The coastal trail (8 km from Kelly Creek to Puerto Vargas) alternates between beach walking and rainforest shade. Sloths, howler monkeys, raccoons, and iguanas make regular cameos.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Snorkeling excursion with a certified guide to Cahuita's reef, accessible only by boat. Two-hour sessions with all equipment provided.

⏱ 120 min

Return to Puerto Viejo for late afternoon at Playa Cocles, a renowned surf beach where the Salsa Brava wave breaks over shallow reef—a magnet for experienced surfers from December through April.

⏱ 90 min

The road south of Puerto Viejo traces a necklace of beaches for 13 km to Manzanillo. Punta Uva stands out as the jewel: turquoise water lapping golden sand, jungle pressing right to the tideline. The Gandoca-Manzanillo Wildlife Refuge safeguards 10 km of coastline, 740 hectares of forest, a coral reef, two wetlands, and nesting grounds for sea turtles, manatees, and dolphins.

⏱ 180 min

Bike day from Puerto Viejo to Manzanillo (13 km flat and shaded) with beach-hopping stops. Snorkeling at Punta Uva or Manzanillo—among the best mask-and-fin spots on the Caribbean coast after Cahuita.

La Fortuna

Where volcanic hot springs bubble beneath a perfectly conical peak that shaped the land in fire

★★★★☆
📍 La Fortuna volcan Arenal Costa Rica📍 La Fortuna Waterfall📍 Sources chaudes La Fortuna📍 Lake Arenal La Fortuna📍 Arenal 1968 lava trail
1

La Fortuna sits at the base of Volcán Arenal as the staging ground for exploring the volcano zone. Arenal (1,657 m) erupted continuously from 1968 to 2010—it's now in a resting phase but remains active. Solidified lava flows are visible from the Arenal 1968 trail.

⏱ 60 min
2

La Fortuna Waterfall, fed by runoff from the volcano's northern slopes, plunges 70 meters into a turquoise pool reached by descending 500-530 steps. Going down takes 15-20 minutes; coming back up demands proper footwear and functioning lungs.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Evening at the natural hot springs heated by volcanic activity. Multiple options at different price points: the public Tabacón springs are the most famous, while the free springs along Rio Chollin downstream can be accessed independently.

⏱ 120 min

La Fortuna

📍 Arenal 1968 trail lava flows📍 Arenal National Park volcano trail📍 Lake Arenal kayak windsurf📍 Canopy tyrolienne La Fortuna📍 Mistico hanging bridge Arenal

The Arenal 1968 trail (4 to 4.7 km, 1h30 to 3h) crosses the solidified lava flows from the founding eruption. Two route options: Sendero Bosque (regenerating forest) and Sendero Colada (open lava field). Direct views of the volcano's cone when skies cooperate.

⏱ 180 min
2

The Mistico Hanging Bridges (3 km, 16 bridges including 6 suspended) traverse the rainforest canopy. Walking height ranges from 40 to 80 meters above ground—prime territory for spotting birds and monkeys from the treetops.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Kayaking or windsurfing on Lake Arenal. The artificial lake (85 km²) is framed by the volcano to the east and green hills to the west—prevailing winds make it one of Central America's premier windsurfing destinations.

Monteverde

📍 Réserve biologique Monteverde nuages📍 Santa Elena Monteverde village📍 Canopy tyrolienne Monteverde📍 Hanging bridges cloud forest Monteverde📍 Quetzal observation Monteverde

The Monteverde Cloud Forest Biological Reserve (10,500 hectares, elevation ~1,400 m) ranks among the world's most celebrated cloud forests. Perched atop the Tilarán Range, it shelters more than 400 bird species including the Resplendent Quetzal, 100 mammal species, and 500 orchid varieties.

⏱ 240 min

Activities

Early morning guided hike in search of the Resplendent Quetzal—local guides spot them where solo visitors walk right past. The morning mist wraps the forest in an otherworldly atmosphere.

Pivot stop in San José between the Highlands and Pacific coast. The Jade Museum, facing Plaza de la Democracia, houses the Americas' largest collection of pre-Columbian jade: jadeite figurines, pendants, and ritual masks dating from 500 BC to 800 AD.

⏱ 90 min

The Pre-Columbian Gold Museum, beneath Plaza de la Cultura, displays over 1,600 fine gold pieces created by Costa Rica's pre-Columbian peoples: animal figurines, ritual jewelry, pendants crafted between 700 and 1500 AD.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Arrive mid-morning from Monteverde (~3h30). Afternoon museums or stroll through Barrio Amón before descending to Manuel Antonio.

Manuel Antonio

Where howler monkeys wake you at dawn and the jungle spills onto pristine Pacific sand

★★☆☆☆
📍 Parc national Manuel Antonio entrée📍 Playa Manuel Antonio parc📍 Quepos front de mer📍 Playa Espadilla Norte Manuel Antonio📍 Punta Catedral tombolo Manuel Antonio
1

Manuel Antonio National Park is Costa Rica's smallest (683 terrestrial hectares) but one of its biodiversity heavyweights. The main trail skirts an 18-hectare mangrove before reaching Playa Manuel Antonio, widely considered among the country's most stunning beaches.

⏱ 180 min
2

Punta Catedral is a former island now tethered to the mainland by a sand tombolo forming a peninsula. The trail circling it offers views of both flanking beaches and the park's 12 offshore islands—sanctuaries for seabirds.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Certified naturalist guide right at park entrance: armed with a spotting scope, they locate two-toed sloths, white-headed capuchin monkeys, and the endemic Central American squirrel monkey found only in Manuel Antonio.

⏱ 150 min

Swimming at Playa Manuel Antonio. Snorkeling possible on the beach's flanks for moray eels, angelfish, and starfish.

Uvita

Where the ocean sculpts a whale's tail in sand and humpbacks breach just offshore

★★★☆☆
📍 Parc national Marino Ballena Uvita📍 Queue de baleine tombolo Uvita marée basse📍 Observation baleines à bosse Uvita📍 Nauyaca Waterfall Dominical📍 Playa Uvita Pacifique sud
1

Marino Ballena National Park, Costa Rica's first marine park (1989), is famous for its Punta Uvita tombolo: at low tide, a tongue of sand emerges from the water taking the shape—from above—of a whale's tail. Walking this sandbar with ocean on both sides is one of the country's most striking experiences.

⏱ 120 min
2

Nauyaca Waterfalls, accessible from Dominical (~30 min north of Uvita), rank among Costa Rica's most spectacular cascades. The hike (~16 km round-trip) can also be done on horseback. Swimming permitted in the lower pool.

⏱ 300 min

Activities

Boat excursion for humpback whale watching (two seasons: December-April and July-October). Spotted dolphins and manta rays present year-round.

★★☆☆☆
📍 Corcovado National Park Los Patos trail📍 Isla del Caño réserve biologique snorkeling📍 Dauphins baleines Drake Bay📍 Tropical rainforest Osa biodiversity📍 Scarlet Macaw ara rouge Corcovado
1

Corcovado National Park, described by National Geographic as the most biologically intense place on Earth, spans 41,788 hectares of primary rainforest. It contains 2.5% of the world's biodiversity on 0.001% of Earth's surface. Jaguars, Baird's tapirs, pumas, scarlet macaws, and four monkey species coexist in this lowland forest.

⏱ 360 min
2

Isla del Caño, a biological reserve 20 km offshore from Drake Bay, is one of Costa Rica's premier diving and snorkeling sites. Whitetip reef sharks, sea turtles, manta rays, dolphins. The pre-Columbian stone spheres scattered across the island remain an archaeological enigma.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Corcovado or Isla del Caño—both are mutually exclusive for one day. Corcovado for hiking enthusiasts and terrestrial wildlife (jaguars, tapirs, scarlet macaws). Isla del Caño for divers and marine life lovers.
