✨ Highlights
  • Khao Yai: wild elephants, 4 hornbill species and the waterfall from The Beach movie
  • Phimai: Thailand's largest Khmer temple, built before Angkor Wat in the 11th century
  • Ban Chiang: red spiral prehistoric pottery, UNESCO-listed since 1992
  • Koh Chang: 70% virgin forest, calm beaches and snorkeling excursions to islets
📆 Best period

November, December, January, February

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 14 and 18 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Cool Season (November-February): Perfect weather (20-30°C) with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures, and peak tourist season. Highest prices but optimal conditions for all activities and sightseeing.

Hot Season (March-May): Very hot temperatures (30-40°C) with moderate crowds and prices. Challenging for extensive outdoor activities but good for beaches and air-conditioned attractions.

Rainy Season (June-October): Heavy afternoon rains, high humidity, but lowest prices and fewer crowds. Lush landscapes, some flooding, but authentic experiences and great value.

🌍 What to discover in TH?

Southeast Asian paradise with ancient temples, incredible cuisine, and vibrant culture. Experience Buddhist traditions, beautiful beaches, and authentic Thai hospitality. The country offers cultural immersion with natural beauty and culinary excellence. Thailand provides diverse experiences with cultural authenticity and natural preservation.

📋 Important notes

This itinerary skips Chiang Mai, Phuket and Krabi entirely. The Red Lotus Sea (Nong Han Lake, Udon Thani) is only accessible from mid-December through late February - if visiting outside this window, replace with a trip to Phu Phra Bat Historical Park or Udon Thani's local market. The Laem Ngop → Koh Chang ferry (45 min) is mandatory for D12; plan to leave your car in Laem Ngop or return by taxi. For the Phu Kradueng trek (D8), the park sometimes closes during the rainy season (June-August): check before departure.

Bangkok

Where century-old Chinatown alleys wear giant murals like tattoos

★★★★★
📍 Talad Noi Wall Art Bangkok📍 Trok San Chao Rong Kueak Bangkok📍 Chow Sue Kong Shrine Bangkok📍 Wat Traimit Bangkok📍 Bang Rak Bangkok📍 Nonthaburi Market Pier
1

Get lost in the narrow lanes of Talad Noi, one of Bangkok's oldest Chinatown districts. Take the ferry from Sathorn to the Marine Department pier - the journey itself is an immersion. In Trok San Chao Rong Kueak alley, 4-meter-high murals cover the walls of active mechanical workshops. A rusted Fiat 500, abandoned here for decades, has become one of Bangkok's most photographed street art spots.

⏱ 90 min
2

Step through the doors of Chow Sue Kong Shrine, tucked at the end of the alley. This 18th-century Chinese temple opens onto a courtyard facing directly onto the Chao Phraya. The interior bas-reliefs tell the story of the Teochew community that founded this neighborhood.

⏱ 30 min
3

Walk through Bang Rak, the district of embassies and 19th-century Portuguese trading posts. Holy Rosary Church, founded in 1787, is Bangkok's oldest. Its cream-colored facade and baroque bell tower reflect in the river late in the morning.

⏱ 40 min
4

Take the orange express boat upriver to Nonthaburi terminal. The journey lasts 50 minutes from the center - one of the best vantage points for watching daily life on the river. Nonthaburi Market spreads right behind the pier: live fish, whole durians, satay vendors. Few foreign tourists make it here.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Order a coffee in one of the small design cafés that have taken over Talad Noi's old shophouses. Several serve Thai mountain arabica. The terrace at River View Guesthouse (8th floor) offers direct views over the bend in the Chao Phraya.

⏱ 45 min

In the evening, walk north along Charoen Krung Road toward Chinatown (Yaowarat). The main street ignites around 6pm: lacquered ducks hanging in front of stalls, steam rising from woks, fish grilling over charcoal. Order khao man gai (steamed chicken on broth-flavored rice) or pad see ew at one of the stalls without English signage.

Stop 2

Khao Yai

Where elephants roam beneath waterfalls and hornbills rule the canopy

★★★★★
📍 Khao Yai National Park Visitor Centre📍 Haew Narok Waterfall Khao Yai📍 Nong Phak Chi Grassland Khao Yai📍 Lam Ta Khong Campsite Khao Yai
1

Pass through the gate of Khao Yai National Park - 2,168 km² of tropical rainforest, UNESCO-listed in 2005, Thailand's first national park established in 1962. Start at the visitor center to pick up trail maps and arrange your guide. Seven official trails wind through the park; most require an escort.

⏱ 30 min
2

Descend the 150 meters to Haew Narok waterfall, the park's highest, which plunges in three tiers through the jungle. The path from the parking area runs 600 meters. On the surrounding cliffs, red orchids (Renanthera) give the falls their name - "Haew Narok" means "ravine of hell." Wild elephants frequent the area - stay on the trail.

⏱ 90 min
3

At dusk, head to Nong Phak Chi grassland. This is where great hornbills emerge from the trees to feed. Khao Yai shelters four hornbill species and one of the region's largest populations of white-handed gibbons. Settle in and wait - sambar deer and pig-tailed macaques often appear before dark.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Join a guided trek on one of the marked forest trails (allow 3 to 5 hours depending on the route). Trail #8 runs one-way from Haew Suwat to the visitor center over 8 km. Guides, available through operators in Pak Chong, can read elephant tracks and locate gibbons by sound.

⏱ 240 min

Optional evening activity: night safari by 4x4 with guide. Operators in Pak Chong offer 2 to 3-hour outings after 8pm. With a flashlight and patience, you can spot civets, hares, barking deer and occasionally a sun bear in the vegetation.

⏱ 150 min

Khao Yai

Where elephants roam beneath waterfalls and hornbills rule the canopy

★★★★★
📍 Haew Suwat Waterfall Khao Yai📍 Pha Kluai Mai Waterfall Khao Yai📍 GranMonte Winery Khao Yai📍 PB Valley Winery Khao Yai
1

Head down to Haew Suwat waterfall, a 20-meter drop into a sandstone pool encircled by jungle. This is where Leonardo DiCaprio and his companions jumped in the film "The Beach" (2000). The scene of the leap into the lagoon was actually filmed at Koh Phi Phi - but the waterfall itself is real. Take the short trail (100 meters from the road) and descend the steps to water level.

⏱ 75 min
2

Continue 700 meters north to Pha Kluai Mai waterfall. More discreet and far less crowded, it borders the campsite of the same name. A rope bridge crosses the Lam Ta Khong River. The atmosphere is quiet, especially midweek.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the afternoon, leave the park to explore the vineyards that have developed on the heights around Pak Chong since the 1990s. PB Valley Khao Yai Winery and GranMonte are the two best-known estates. The climatic conditions at altitude (600-800 m) allow for growing varieties like Shiraz and Chenin Blanc in a tropical climate - a rare viticultural curiosity in Asia.

⏱ 120 min

Wake up early (6am) and walk from your accommodation for sunrise at the Lam Ta Khong reservoir. Rhesus macaques descend from the trees, water birds take off en masse, and if you're lucky, a herd of elephants comes to drink before dawn.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 4

Phimai

A thousand-year temple that whispered blueprints to Angkor before dawn

★★★★★
📍 Prasat Hin Phimai Historical Park📍 Phimai National Museum📍 Sai Ngam Banyan Tree Phimai📍 Phimai old city gate Pratu Chai
1

Enter the Prasat Hin Phimai complex by crossing the Naga bridge, whose railings carved in the shape of seven-headed serpents symbolically carry you from the human world to the realm of the gods. This 11th-century Khmer temple was built BEFORE Angkor Wat - some historians believe the central white sandstone prang directly inspired Angkor's architects. Its particularity: unlike most Hindu Khmer temples, Phimai has always been Buddhist. The rectangular enclosure measures 1,020 by 580 meters - comparable to Angkor Wat.

⏱ 90 min
2

750 meters north of the ruins, Phimai National Museum displays a statue of Jayavarman VII, the great 12th-century Khmer king, and numerous carved lintels from the excavations. One of the finest Khmer museums outside Cambodia.

⏱ 60 min
3

Walk around Sai Ngam, Thailand's largest banyan tree. Its aerial roots have colonized a surface of about 2,500 m² along the Mun River. The mass of intertwined trunks creates a dense forest on its own. Thais attribute spiritual powers to it and hang fortune offerings from its branches.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Stroll through the streets of Phimai's old town on foot. Pratu Chai gate, a few hundred meters south of the temple, is one of the vestiges of the ancient fortified city - it once guarded the royal road to Angkor, 225 km southeast. Phimai sees few foreign tourists: enjoy the tranquil atmosphere of the evening market to sample Isan specialties - gai yang (chicken grilled with herbs), sticky rice, and som tam pounded in a mortar to order.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 5

Khon Kaen

Where a nine-story chedi watches over a lake and sticky rice reigns

★★★★★
📍 Khon Kaen National Museum📍 Wat Phra That Kaen Nakhon Khon Kaen📍 Ton Tann Night Market Khon Kaen📍 Bueng Kaen Nakhon Lake Khon Kaen
1

Visit Khon Kaen National Museum, one of Isan's richest. It presents regional excavations: Bronze Age pottery, iron tools, copper jewelry from sites near Ban Chiang and surroundings. The archaeological context of the Khorat Plateau is well explained here.

⏱ 75 min
2

Climb the nine stories of Wat Phra That Kaen Nakhon on the shores of Bueng Kaen Nakhon Lake. Each level of the chedi is decorated with carved panels illustrating episodes from the Buddha's life. The view from the top takes in the lake, parks and the entire city.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the evening, head to Ton Tann night market, the largest in Khon Kaen. Try larb moo - minced pork mixed with fresh mint, lime juice and roasted ground rice, served at room temperature. It's the emblematic dish of northeastern Thailand, spicy and bright, worlds away from central Thai cuisine.

⏱ 90 min

Cycle or walk around Bueng Kaen Nakhon Lake. The route runs about 4 km and passes public gardens, temples and waterfront cafés. It's where locals stroll in late afternoon.

Stop 6

Udon Thani

Where a Harvard student's stumble unearthed civilizations lost for millennia

★★★★★
📍 Ban Chiang National Museum Udon Thani📍 Wat Pho Si Nai Ban Chiang excavation site📍 Red Lotus Sea Nong Han Lake Udon Thani📍 Udon Thani walking street
1

Drive 50 km east of Udon Thani to Ban Chiang, a UNESCO archaeological site since 1992. An American Harvard student tripped over a root here in 1966 - and discovered pottery dating to 1,500 BC. Excavations have uncovered hundreds of burials, bronze tools, copper jewelry and the red spiral pottery on an orange background that has become Udon Thani's symbol.

⏱ 90 min
2

At Wat Pho Si Nai temple, 500 meters from the museum, an active excavation remains open to the public. You can see pottery and bones still in place in the earth, exactly as they were uncovered. It's one of the few places in the world where you can observe an active archaeological dig as a casual visitor.

⏱ 45 min
3

If you're here between mid-December and late February: visit the Red Lotus Sea (Talay Bua Daeng) on Nong Han Lake, 45 minutes from Udon Thani. Thousands of pink lotuses cover several hectares of the lake at dawn. Take a boat early in the morning - the flowers close after 10am. Off-season: replace with a visit to Phu Phra Bat Historical Park (Dvaravati-era carved rocks, 65 km away).

⏱ 120 min

Activities

In the evening, wander Udon Thani's walking street in the city center. Isan cuisine expresses itself here in its most direct form: som tam Isan (shredded papaya, cherry tomatoes, chili and fermented crab paste - sharper and saltier than the Bangkok version), charcoal-grilled gai yang, sticky rice compressed in a small bamboo basket.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 7

Nong Khai

Where a mystic's exile birthed giants frozen in prayer along the Mekong

★★★★☆
📍 Sala Kaew Ku sculpture park Nong Khai📍 Nong Khai Mekong Riverside promenade📍 Wat Pho Chai Nong Khai📍 Tha Sadet Market Nong Khai
1

Head to Sala Kaew Ku, 6 km east of town. This park of raw concrete sculptures is the work of one man: Bunleua Sulilat, a Lao mystic exiled in 1975 during the communist revolution. He bought this land in 1978 and spent 20 years erecting over 100 giant statues, some 25 meters tall, funded solely by concrete donations from his followers. The Buddha seated on a seven-headed naga, the Wheel of Life you enter through a giant's mouth, the eight-armed goddesses - none were built by professionals. Sulilat died in 1996; his mummified body is visible under a glass bell on the third floor of the pavilion.

⏱ 90 min
2

Visit Wat Pho Chai in central Nong Khai. It houses Luang Pho Phra Sai, a Lan Xang-style Buddha covered in gold, bronze and precious stones. According to legend, three similar Buddhas were crossing the Mekong on a raft: two sank, this one alone reached Nong Khai.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk along the Mekong promenade in late afternoon. From the Thai side, you look directly at the hills of Laos 400 meters away. Fishermen cast their nets from small pirogues. The Thai-Lao Friendship Bridge, built in 1994, is visible to the east.

⏱ 60 min

Browse Tha Sadet Market along the river, known for Lao products, medicinal herbs and indigo textiles. It's one of the few markets in the region where Thai and Lao traders still directly exchange their goods.

Chiang Khan

Where wooden houses breathe dawn prayers over the Mekong's embrace

★★★★★
📍 Chai Khong Road Chiang Khan walking street📍 Kaeng Khut Khu rapids Chiang Khan📍 Wat Si Khun Muang Chiang Khan📍 Chiang Khan Skywalk viewpoint
1

Wake before 6am. On Chai Khong Road, monks parade in silence at dawn for alms collection - a ritual borrowed from Lao rather than Thai tradition, a vestige of ancient ties to the Lan Xang kingdom across the river. Locals lay out baskets of sticky rice and fruit on the sidewalk before the sun touches the Mekong.

⏱ 60 min
2

Then stroll Chai Khong Road in the morning mist. This street of two-story wooden houses runs along the Mekong for 1 km. Each building houses a guesthouse, café or craft shop. Riverside, the Lao bank lies 300 meters away. It's one of the region's best-preserved wooden streets.

⏱ 60 min
3

3 km east of the center, Kaeng Khut Khu rapids form a succession of rocks and swift currents on the Mekong. A walkway runs along the shore. In the dry season, rocks emerge and form islands. Boats offer 30 to 60-minute outings to see the rapids from the water.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

In the evening, Chai Khong Road becomes a walking street from 5pm. Stalls offer local specialties you won't find anywhere else: kai yang Chiang Khan (chicken marinated with Loei mountain herbs, grilled over charcoal), khanom jeen (fermented rice vermicelli with curry), and sweets made from sesame and coconut. The crowd is almost exclusively Thai.

⏱ 90 min

Optional: climb up to Chiang Khan Skywalk, a horseshoe-shaped glass walkway built in 2020, 80 meters above the confluence of the Mekong and Hueang rivers. Best done early morning for mist blankets over the Lao mountains.

⏱ 60 min

Loei

Where mist-cloaked mountains harbor pines and wildflowers under freezing skies

★★★★★
📍 Phu Kradueng National Park Loei📍 Phu Ruea National Park Loei📍 Loei city centre market📍 Wang Saphung Loei
1

Loei province is Isan's most mountainous. If you have the energy, leave early for Phu Kradueng, a basalt plateau rising to 1,325 meters. The ascent takes 5 to 9 km depending on the entry point. At the top, a 60 km² plateau planted with Scots pines, wildflowers and crossed by waterfalls. In the cool season (November-January), temperatures drop to 0°C at night.

⏱ 240 min
2

Less strenuous alternative: Phu Ruea, 50 km from Loei. This mid-mountain national park (1,365 m at the summit) offers short trails of 1 to 3 km with views over misty valleys. The sunset viewpoint is accessible by car.

⏱ 150 min

Activities

If you stay in the lowlands, explore Loei's central market in the morning. The province produces cotton and indigo: you'll find naturally dyed fabrics, bamboo baskets and fresh herbs you won't see in Bangkok markets. Vendors often come from surrounding villages starting at 5am.

⏱ 90 min

Stop 10

Nakhon Ratchasima

Where a warrior woman's statue guards the threshold to Isan's heart

★★★★☆
📍 Thao Suranari Monument Korat📍 Manat Night Market Korat📍 Korat old city moat📍 Maha Viravong National Museum Korat📍 Wat Sala Loi Korat
1

Arrive in Nakhon Ratchasima, known as Korat, after a 4-hour drive from Chiang Khan. The statue of Thao Suranari has stood at the center of the old city since 1934. It represents Mo, the wife of a local governor who repelled the Vientiane army in 1826 by arming women and prisoners. Thais come on pilgrimage to bring her jasmine garlands and silk robes - a scene you won't witness anywhere else.

⏱ 45 min
2

Cross the moats that still mark the perimeter of the old fortified city. Several bastions have been restored. Maha Viravong National Museum, within a temple complex, displays Khmer sculptures from the region and prehistoric pottery from Isan excavations.

⏱ 60 min
3

Step inside Wat Sala Loi, a modern temple whose ubosot is shaped like a Chinese junk resting on a pond. Built in 1973, it's unique in Thailand: the ship's bow and stern are carved with nagas, the windows shaped like portholes. A striking contrast with the Khmer temples visited in recent days.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

In the evening, head to Manat night market, steps from the Thao Suranari statue. It's one of the city's liveliest markets - pad kra pao (Thai basil stir-fry) wokked before your eyes, pork skewers marinated in turmeric, coconut milk desserts. A good stop for dinner before tomorrow's long drive to Chanthaburi.

⏱ 90 min

Chanthaburi

Where Gothic spires rise above gemstone streets forged by three empires

★★★☆☆
📍 Cathedral of Immaculate Conception Chanthaburi📍 Chanthaboon Waterfront Community Chanthaburi📍 Gem Market Si Chan Road Chanthaburi📍 Namtok Phlio National Park Chanthaburi
1

Stop at the Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception - Thailand's largest Catholic cathedral. Its 59-meter Gothic spire rises above the Chanthaburi River. Built in the 18th century, reconstructed four times, it took its current form in 1909. The French briefly occupied Chanthaburi in the late 19th century to pressure Siam - the architecture preserves that trace. Inside houses a statue of the Virgin Mary inlaid with local gemstones.

⏱ 45 min
2

Walk along the riverfront of the historic Chanthaboon district. This 300-year-old community blends Thai wooden houses, Chinese facades and Vietnamese influences - descendants of Catholic immigrants from Vietnam still live here. The bilingual Thai-Chinese signs hanging from awnings and old teak counters give the feeling of a neighborhood frozen in the 19th century.

⏱ 60 min
3

If you're here on a Friday, Saturday or Sunday, don't miss gem street (Si Chan Road). Over 120 shops dealing in rubies, sapphires and semi-precious stones concentrate in 300 meters. Chanthaburi has been the global center of ruby and sapphire trade since the 15th century - stones today come mainly from Cambodia and Burma. Traders arrive from around the world.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Take a trip to Namtok Phlio National Park, 14 km from town. The main waterfall tumbles into a pool where 60cm fish come to feed directly underwater. The park is crossed by a forest trail that passes the steles of several Rama kings who vacationed here.

⏱ 120 min

Koh Chang

Where jungle swallows the island whole and coral reefs guard secret waters

★★★★☆
📍 Bang Bao fishing village Koh Chang📍 Lonely Beach Koh Chang📍 Klong Plu Waterfall Koh Chang📍 Klong Prao Beach Koh Chang
1

Take the ferry from Laem Ngop pier (45-minute crossing). On Koh Chang, start with Bang Bao village at the southern tip - a fishing village built entirely on stilts over the bay. Wooden houses are connected by walkways. Restaurants and dive operators have set up shop, but the village's original character remains intact.

⏱ 75 min
2

Head up the west coast to Lonely Beach, the island's most preserved strand. A kilometer of sand between jungle-covered hills. The road overlooks it - you descend on foot. Accommodation here is simple and budget-friendly.

⏱ 60 min
3

Make your way to Klong Plu waterfall in the national forest, accessible from Klong Prao beach. A well-marked 600-meter trail leads to a 30-meter fall into a swimming hole carved in sandstone. Fish paddle in the clear waters. Arrive before 9am to avoid organized groups.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

From Bang Bao, take a boat tour around the south of the island. Pier operators offer excursions to the islets of Koh Rang and Koh Kluay, with snorkeling on still-undisturbed reefs. Visibility can reach 15 to 20 meters in the dry season.

⏱ 180 min

Koh Chang

Where jungle swallows the island whole and coral reefs guard secret waters

★★★★☆
📍 Koh Wai snorkeling Koh Chang📍 Koh Mak island excursion📍 Salak Phet Bay mangroves kayak Koh Chang📍 Koh Kood day trip
1

Take a day trip to Koh Wai - 45 minutes by boat from Bang Bao. This small 3 km² island has no roads or mains electricity. The coral is in good condition, especially on the northeast side. You can snorkel directly from the sand without needing another boat.

⏱ 180 min
2

Alternative: Koh Mak, a 16 km² island, is 45 minutes by ferry from Ban Laem. Calm, sparsely populated, no jet skis - agencies on Koh Chang organize day trips combining beach, snorkeling and visits to the small fishing village.

⏱ 240 min

Activities

If you stay on Koh Chang: rent a kayak in Salak Phet Bay on the east side of the island. This enclosed bay is bordered by mangroves 10 to 15 meters high. You paddle between aerial roots, spotting fiddler crabs and grey herons. Operators also offer guided sunset outings.
