Fall (September-November): Autumn colors, comfortable weather, clear skies, beautiful foliage, excellent touring, fewer crowds, ideal photography, pleasant conditions.

Summer (June-August): Hot humid weather, rainy season, festival season, vibrant atmosphere, mountain hiking, beach activities, air conditioning essential.

Winter (December-February): Cold weather, snow sports, illuminations, onsen season, fewer crowds, winter festivals, cozy atmosphere, skiing opportunities.

Stop 1

Naha

Where Makishi Market and Kokusai-dori pulse with island energy

★★★★★
📍 Kokusai Street Naha📍 Makishi Public Market Naha📍 Tsuboya Yachimun Street Naha📍 Naminoue Shrine Naha📍 Heiwa Dori Naha
1

Kokusai-dori, Naha's main artery, stretches 1.6 km between the bus terminal and Makishi Station. Dubbed the "Miracle Mile" because it rose from wartime ashes within just a few years, it owes its name to the Ernie Pyle International Theater, a cinema built for American soldiers after 1945. Around 600 shops line the street—souvenir stores, soba restaurants, awamori bars. On Sundays from noon to 6 PM, the street closes to traffic and street performers take over. Go in the evening when the atmosphere really comes alive.

⏱ 60 min
2

Makishi Public Market, nicknamed the "Kitchen of Okinawa," has existed since 1950—born as a black market during the American occupation. After four years of renovation, it reopened in March 2023 in a brand-new three-story building. On the ground floor, you'll find tropical fish in improbable colors, Agu pork, and sea grapes. Buy your ingredients downstairs and have them cooked on the second floor—this is the famous "mochiage" system. If the vendor throws in a little something extra when you pay, that's the "shibun," a gesture of generosity typical of Okinawa.

⏱ 75 min
3

Step into the Tsuboya district through the Heiwa-dori arcade: a 400-meter street paved with Ryukyu limestone awaits, lined with some twenty pottery workshops. The district was born in 1682, when the royal government consolidated all the scattered pottery kilns across the island into one location. For over 340 years, artisans have been producing yachimun—glazed pottery fired at 1,200°C. Don't miss the Tsuboya Pottery Museum at the entrance, which houses pieces dating back 6,700 years.

⏱ 60 min
4

Naminoue Shrine perches on a coral cliff above Naha's only beach. Its name means "above the waves." Before becoming a Shinto shrine, the site was an utaki dedicated to Nirai Kanai, the mythical paradise beyond the sea. The king himself came here to pray every New Year. Destroyed in 1945, the main hall was rebuilt in 1953. A highway bridge somewhat spoils the view from the beach, but walk onto that bridge for the best vantage point of the vermillion shrine perched on the cliff.

⏱ 45 min
5

The covered shopping arcades of Heiwa-dori, Mutsumi-dori, and Ichiba Hondori form a labyrinth of alleys perpendicular to Kokusai-dori. You'll find shisa statues, colorful kariyushi shirts, artisanal awamori, and beni-imo tarts. Prices are often negotiable—a smile might earn you a little gift on top.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Taste awamori, Okinawa's traditional alcohol distilled from rice with black kōji yeast—it ranges from 25 to 60% ABV. The bravest will try habushu, awamori infused with a habu viper. Several restaurants on Kokusai-dori offer tastings.

⏱ 45 min

Catch Saturday evening street performances on Kokusai-dori: traditional dancers, sanshin musicians, fire breathers. Most shops stay open until 11 PM—the atmosphere is far livelier in the evening than during the day.

⏱ 60 min

Join a yachimun pottery workshop in Tsuboya—allow 60 to 90 minutes to mold your own shisa, Okinawa's guardian lion-dog. Shisa come in pairs: mouth open to chase away evil spirits, mouth closed to keep in the good. Your creation will be mailed to you after firing. Book online in advance.

⏱ 75 min

Stop 2

Naha

Where Makishi Market and Kokusai-dori pulse with island energy

★★★★★
📍 Shuri Castle Naha📍 Tamaudun Mausoleum Naha📍 Kinjo Stone Pavement Shuri Naha📍 Shureimon Gate Naha📍 Sonohyan Utaki Ishimon Naha
1

Shuri Castle served as the palace of the Ryukyu Kingdom for 450 years, from 1429 to 1879. A unique blend of Chinese and Japanese architecture, it has been destroyed five times—in 1453, 1660, 1709, during the Battle of Okinawa in 1945, and by fire in October 2019 that consumed seven buildings in 11 hours. Reconstruction of the main hall (Seiden) is underway and should be completed by autumn 2026—you can watch the construction from an observation deck and see artisans laying the 60,000 roof tiles.

⏱ 90 min
2

Shureimon Gate, printed on the 2,000-yen bill, stands at the castle entrance. Built in the 16th century by King Shō Sei, it bears the inscription "Land of Propriety" in classical Chinese. Destroyed during the war, rebuilt in 1958, it survived the 2019 fire.

⏱ 20 min
3

Tamaudun Royal Mausoleum, a five-minute walk away, is the tomb of the second Shō dynasty built in 1501 by King Shō Shin. It houses the remains of 18 kings in a massive stone structure divided into three chambers. Designated a National Treasure in 2018 and a UNESCO site since 2000.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Kinjo-chō stone pavement descends 300 meters through a quiet residential neighborhood lined with large akagi trees over 200 years old. This was the main road to the castle in the 16th century. The atmosphere is completely different—here, you're in residential Shuri.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Visit the underground museum at Tamaudun (included in the ticket) to understand the Ryukyuan senkotsu funeral ritual—bones were washed and placed in urns several years after death.

⏱ 30 min

Watch artisans at work on the Seiden reconstruction site from the observation platform. The castle's bright red lacquering requires dozens of successive coats.

⏱ 30 min

Discover bingata dyeing at Shuri Ryusen workshop, just minutes from the castle. This 500-year-old textile art uses vivid colors inspired by tropical nature—you can dye your own handkerchief. Allow about an hour; reservation recommended.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 3

Itoman

Where Himeyuri Memorial watches over southern Okinawa's cliffs

★★★★☆
📍 Umikaji Terrace Senaga Island📍 Himeyuri Peace Museum Itoman📍 Ryukyu Glass Village Itoman📍 Senaga Island Okinawa
1

Umikaji Terrace, on Senaga Island (1.8 km circumference), is a complex opened in 2015 whose white terraced buildings cascade toward the sea—you'd think you were in Santorini. Over 40 shops and restaurants. The island is 15 minutes from the airport. Go in late afternoon: the sunset over the Kerama Islands is spectacular, and planes land in silhouette.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Himeyuri Memorial tells the heartbreaking story of 222 high school girls and 18 teachers mobilized as auxiliary nurses during the Battle of Okinawa in 1945. Abandoned on the battlefield on June 18, 136 of them perished. The museum, built in 1989 by survivors and renovated in 2021, features video testimonies and a recreation of an underground surgical shelter. Plan at least an hour.

⏱ 75 min
3

Ryukyu Glass Village in Itoman showcases the art of blown glass that emerged after the war, when artisans recycled bottles left behind by American soldiers. The vivid colors—ocean blue, emerald green—are the signature of this craft. You can watch glassblowers at their 1,300°C furnaces.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Join a glassblowing workshop—in 15 minutes, you'll create your own glass piece under an artisan's guidance. The piece will be shipped to you after cooling.

⏱ 30 min

Enjoy the sunset from Umikaji Terrace. In the evening, a projection mapping show plays every 30 minutes starting at 7:30 PM.

⏱ 45 min

Relax at Senaga Island's natural hot springs with ocean views—rare in Okinawa. Perfect after the emotional visit to the Himeyuri Memorial.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 4

Nanjo

Sacred groves of Sefa Utaki and limestone caves shaped by millennia

★★★★☆
📍 Sefa Utaki Nanjo📍 Valley of Gangala Nanjo📍 Okinawa World Gyokusendo Cave Nanjo📍 Nirai Kanai Bridge Nanjo
1

Sefa Utaki is the most sacred site of the Ryukyu Kingdom, a UNESCO site since 2000. There are no buildings here—it's the rocks and forest that are venerated. When you reach the Sangui, a triangular tunnel formed by two massive rocks, you'll understand why priestesses came here to glimpse the sacred island of Kudaka. This is where the kingdom's highest priestess was enthroned. Buy tickets at the Nanjo local products center, 500 meters from the entrance.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Valley of Gangala formed when limestone caves collapsed, creating a 48,000-square-meter chasm covered in subtropical forest. The Ufushu Gajumaru, a giant banyan tree 20 meters tall estimated at 150 years old, is the highlight. The world's oldest fishhook was found here—23,000 years old. The 80-minute guided tour is the only way to enter—book online the day before, with four daily departures (10 AM, noon, 2 PM, 4 PM).

⏱ 90 min
3

Okinawa World houses Gyokusendo Cave—Okinawa's largest, 5 km long with 890 meters open to the public. Formed 300,000 years ago, it contains over a million stalactites that grow 1 mm every three years thanks to the subtropical climate. Constant temperature of 21°C—a refuge on hot days.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Have coffee in Sakitari Cave at the entrance to the Valley of Gangala—the bar is set up under stalactites in what was once a prehistoric dwelling. Beverage included in the tour price.

⏱ 20 min

At Okinawa World's Ryukyu Kingdom Village, watch an Eisa performance—traditional Okinawan drum and dance. Multiple shows daily.

⏱ 45 min

Make a detour to the Nirai Kanai Bridge overlook—the view plunges down to the coast and Pacific, one of southern Okinawa's finest panoramas.

⏱ 20 min

Stop 5

Chatan

Where American Village's neon glow meets Pacific sunsets

★★★★☆
📍 American Village Chatan Okinawa📍 Sunset Beach Chatan📍 Cape Zanpa Lighthouse Yomitan📍 Yachimun no Sato Yomitan
1

Chatan's American Village is a complex built on the former Hamby military airfield, returned in 1981. Opening from 1998 onward, it blends American pop architecture with Okinawan culture. It's a real neighborhood where American families from nearby Kadena Air Base mingle with tourists. In the evening, the buildings light up—rooftop bars, terraces facing the sea.

⏱ 75 min
2

Sunset Beach, at the foot of American Village, lives up to its name: facing due west, it's one of Okinawa's best sunset spots. About 600 meters of sand with showers and barbecue facilities.

⏱ 45 min
3

Cape Zanpa in Yomitan (20-minute drive): 30-meter coral cliffs with a 31-meter white lighthouse—one of the few climbable lighthouses in Japan. 360-degree views of Aguni and Kume Islands. At the entrance, the Zanpa Dai-Shisa measures 8.75 meters—Japan's largest shisa statue.

⏱ 60 min
4

Yachimun no Sato in Yomitan emerged when wood-fired kilns were banned in Naha in the 1970s. Over 60 workshops produce traditional yachimun—you can freely enter and buy directly from artisans.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Settle onto a rooftop bar at Depot Island to watch the sun sink into the sea. The vibe blends awamori cocktails with taco rice—Okinawa's star fusion dish.

⏱ 60 min

Climb Cape Zanpa's lighthouse via the spiral staircase—the view is worth the effort. Below, Zanpa Beach is perfect for a quick swim.

⏱ 30 min

Stop 6

Uruma

Where Kaichu Road glides over emerald ocean toward four tranquil islets

★★☆☆☆
📍 Katsuren Castle Ruins Uruma📍 Kaichu Road Uruma📍 Nakagusuku Castle Ruins📍 Hamahiga Island Uruma📍 Bios no Oka Uruma
1

The ruins of Katsuren Castle, a UNESCO site, crown the Katsuren Peninsula from a hill 100 meters high. In the 15th century, Lord Amawari made it a commercial center rivaling Shuri through maritime trade with China and Southeast Asia. Too ambitious, he attempted to overthrow King Shō Taikyu and was defeated in 1458. Roman coins were found here—a first in Japan. From the summit, the 360-degree view across the Pacific and East China Sea is among Okinawa's finest.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Kaichu Road (Kaichu-doro) is a scenic 5-km road built over the sea, connecting the Katsuren Peninsula to the islands of Henza, Miyagi, Hamahiga, and Ikei. Free to cross, it offers the exhilarating sensation of driving over the emerald ocean. Midway, the Ayahashi Station (shaped like a ship) has local products and a small maritime museum.

⏱ 60 min
3

The ruins of Nakagusuku Castle, also a UNESCO site, possess the best-preserved stone walls of all Okinawan gusuku. Built with perfectly fitted masonry technique, the walls follow the natural terrain with elegant curves. The view embraces both the Pacific and East China Sea.

⏱ 60 min
4

Bios no Oka is a 75,000-square-meter subtropical park where you can take a 25-minute boat cruise on Lake Nuchi-unai through a forest of tree ferns and vines. Kids love the water buffalo and canoes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Drive across the islands via Kaichu Road: Henza for its small fishing port, Hamahiga for the sacred Shirumichu shrine linked to the goddess Amamikiyo, and Ikei for its deserted beaches. Allow half a day for all four islands.

⏱ 120 min

Visit the Amawari Park Museum at the base of Katsuren Castle—opened in 2021, it displays excavated objects, including the famous Roman coins, and animations about Lord Amawari's history.

⏱ 30 min

Onna

Where the Blue Cave glows beneath Maeda Point's towering cliffs

★★★☆☆
📍 Cape Manzamo Onna Okinawa📍 Blue Cave Maeda Point Onna📍 Ryukyu Mura Onna📍 Onna Village Beach Okinawa
1

Cape Manzamo is a 20-meter coral cliff whose profile evokes an elephant's trunk—it's Okinawa's most famous postcard image. Its name means "grassland where ten thousand people can sit," bestowed by King Shō Kei in the 18th century. Come early morning or late afternoon to avoid tour buses.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Blue Cave (Aoi Dōkutsu) at Maeda Point is a sea cavern where sunlight penetrates from below and tints the water electric blue. It's Okinawa's most popular diving and snorkeling site.

⏱ 30 min
3

Ryukyu Mura is a reconstructed village with traditional Okinawan houses—some over 100 years old—dismantled and reassembled in a subtropical garden. You'll see eisa dance performances, sanshin folk songs, and demonstrations of old-fashioned sugarcane processing.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Go snorkeling or diving in the Blue Cave—operators offer roughly 2-hour outings, transport and equipment included. Book the day before, especially in summer. Conditions are best in the morning when light enters the cave.

⏱ 120 min

Paint your own ceramic shisa at Ryukyu Mura and try on traditional Ryukyu costume for a souvenir photo. Kids love the water buffalo trail.

⏱ 45 min

Catch the folk song and sanshin performance at Ryukyu Mura—musicians play in the traditional houses, creating an intimate atmosphere.

⏱ 30 min

Nago

Where Orion Brewery and cherry blossom hills overlook northern bays

★★★☆☆
📍 Nago Pineapple Park Okinawa📍 Orion Beer Factory Nago📍 Nago Castle Ruins Cherry Blossoms📍 Okinawa Fruits Land Nago
1

Nago Pineapple Park takes you on an automated cart through pineapple fields, tropical greenhouses, and a garden with over 200 varieties of subtropical plants. Okinawan pineapples are smaller but sweeter than what you're used to—try the pineapple wine and ice cream on the way out.

⏱ 60 min
2

OKINAWA Fruits Land, a few minutes' drive away, is a tropical fruit-themed park with an interactive game trail (the Tropical Kingdom Story) and a café where you can taste seasonal fruit parfaits—mango, dragon fruit, shikuwasa.

⏱ 60 min
3

The ruins of Nago Castle, on a hill above town, offer panoramic views of Nago Bay and the Motobu Peninsula. In January–February, Kanhizakura cherry blossoms—the first to bloom in Japan—blanket the hill in vivid pink. Even off-season, the climb is worth it for the view.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Tour the Orion Brewery in Nago—Orion is THE Okinawan beer, brewed here since 1957. The free guided tour (about 40 minutes) concludes with tasting fresh beers. Book online.

⏱ 60 min

Ride the automated cart through Pineapple Park's plantation—the driverless vehicle cruises through rows of outdoor pineapple plants.

⏱ 30 min

Stroll Nago's streets in the evening—the town is much quieter than Naha and retains an authentic local atmosphere. The small soba restaurants and izakaya are worth seeking out.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 9

Motobu

Where Churaumi Aquarium's record-holding whale shark has reigned since 1995

★★★☆☆
📍 Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium Motobu📍 Bise Fukugi Tree Road Motobu📍 Nakijin Castle Ruins Okinawa📍 Ocean Expo Park Motobu
1

Churaumi Aquarium, opened in 2002 on the site of the 1975 Ocean Expo, is one of the world's largest. Its star is the 7,500-cubic-meter Kuroshio Tank—behind a 22.5-meter-wide by 8.2-meter-tall acrylic panel, an 8.8-meter whale shark has swum here since March 1995, a world record for captivity longevity. The aquarium was also the first in the world to successfully breed manta rays in captivity, in 2007. Whale shark feedings happen at 9:30 AM, 3 PM, and 5 PM.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Bise Fukugi Tree Road is a shaded one-kilometer path lined with thousands of fukugi trees 10 to 20 meters tall, planted centuries ago as a natural windbreak to protect the village. These dense-leaved trees create a green tunnel leading to a quiet beach facing Ie Island.

⏱ 45 min
3

The ruins of Nakijin Castle, a UNESCO site, stretch across 1.5 km of walls reaching 8 meters high—the longest of all Okinawan gusuku. This 13th-century castle was the residence of the King of the North during the Sanzan period, before kingdom unification. In January–February, cherry blossoms along the walls make it one of Japan's first hanami spots.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Watch the free dolphin show at Okichan Theater in Ocean Expo Park—five performances daily.

⏱ 30 min

Walk or ride a buffalo-drawn cart through the Bise Fukugi Tree Road—the light filtering through the trees creates a peaceful atmosphere. At the path's end, the beach offers views of Ie Island.

⏱ 45 min

Explore Nakijin's 1.5 km of walls on foot—the promenade atop the walls offers views of the East China Sea on one side and forested hills on the other.

⏱ 45 min

Stop 10

Naha

Where Makishi Market and Kokusai-dori pulse with island energy

★★★★★
📍 Kouri Bridge Okinawa📍 Kouri Island Heart Rock📍 Cape Hedo Okinawa📍 Yanbaru National Park Okinawa📍 Daisekirinzan Kunigami
1

Kouri Bridge soars 1,960 meters over turquoise waters to tiny Kouri Island (8 km circumference). The water below is so intensely blue that locals call it "Kouri Blue." On the island, seek out Heart Rock—two heart-shaped rocks on Tinu Beach, now a famous romantic spot.

⏱ 60 min
2

Cape Hedo marks the northernmost point of Okinawa's main island. Steep coral cliffs plunge into rough ocean where the Pacific meets the East China Sea. On clear days, you'll glimpse Yoron and Izena Islands offshore. The site is windswept and wild—a striking contrast with the southern beaches.

⏱ 45 min
3

Yanbaru Forest, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage list in 2021, covers northern Okinawa's densely wooded hills. It's the territory of the Okinawa rail (yanbaru kuina), a flightless bird threatened with extinction. Daisekirinzan Park offers 30- to 60-minute trails through impressive karst formations.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Drive across Kouri Bridge—the view of the turquoise water below is breathtaking. On the other side, explore the island's sugarcane fields and small deserted beaches.

⏱ 45 min

After visiting the north, drive back to Naha (about 2 hours via Okinawa Expressway) to spend the night before tomorrow's flight to Ishigaki. If departing from Cape Hedo, allow at least 2.5 hours to the airport.

⏱ 150 min

Stop 11

Ishigaki

Where Kabira Bay shimmers in turquoise depths known as Kabira Blue

★★★☆☆
📍 Kabira Bay Ishigaki📍 Banna Park Ishigaki📍 Ishigaki Limestone Cave📍 Ishigaki Island Okinawa
1

Kabira Bay is ranked among Japan's 100 Most Beautiful Views—the water's intense turquoise, dubbed "Kabira Blue," surrounds small islets covered in tropical vegetation. Swimming is prohibited due to currents, but glass-bottom boats let you admire corals and tropical fish without getting wet. This is also where Okinawa's black pearls are cultivated.

⏱ 75 min
2

Banna Forest Park covers a central Ishigaki hill with several observation platforms. The Emerald Sea Observatory offers the best view of the sea and Yaeyama archipelago islands. Trails wind through subtropical forest where butterflies unique to the region flutter.

⏱ 60 min
3

Ishigaki Limestone Cave, 200,000 years old, is Japan's southernmost tourist cave. The 660-meter route passes through illuminated halls with stalactites—one resembles the character Totoro.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Morning flight from Naha to Ishigaki—about 1 hour. Arrive at Naha Airport at least 1 hour before departure. From Ishigaki Airport, rent a car: the island is compact, 30 minutes is enough to reach Kabira Bay.

⏱ 120 min

Board a glass-bottom boat in Kabira Bay to observe corals and tropical fish—departures are frequent, allow about 30 minutes.

⏱ 30 min

Taste Ishigaki beef, considered one of Japan's finest wagyu. Downtown restaurants serve it as yakiniku or sushi.

⏱ 60 min

Stop 12

Miyakojima

Where seven kilometers of white sand embrace an impossibly blue lagoon

★★☆☆☆
📍 Yonaha Maehama Beach Miyakojima📍 Miyako Island Marine Park📍 Higashi Hennazaki Cape Miyakojima📍 Irabu Bridge Miyakojima
1

Yonaha Maehama Beach stretches 7 km of white sand facing emerald-green water—regularly ranked the most beautiful beach in the Orient. Just 15 minutes from Miyako Airport, you can be there right after landing. The seabed is sandy with a gentle slope—perfect for swimming. Arrive early morning to have the beach almost to yourself.

⏱ 75 min
2

Miyako Marine Park has an underwater observatory 4 meters deep—you descend into a tower and observe tropical fish and sea turtles through portholes, keeping your feet dry. It's a perfect alternative for those who don't want to dive.

⏱ 45 min
3

Cape Higashi Hennazaki is a narrow 2-km peninsula jutting into the ocean, ranked among Japan's 100 Most Beautiful Parks. The lighthouse at the tip offers 360-degree views of the Pacific on one side and the China Sea on the other. The road there runs along wave-battered cliffs—spectacular in fine weather.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Morning flight from Ishigaki to Miyakojima—about 30 minutes. Rent a car at the airport to explore the island. For the return, direct flights connect Miyakojima to Naha (50 minutes) and Tokyo-Haneda (3 hours).

⏱ 60 min

Cross Irabu Bridge (3,540 meters, Japan's longest free bridge) to Irabu Island and continue to Shimoji Beach—surreally blue water, often deserted. The road itself is a spectacle with turquoise water on either side.

⏱ 60 min

Go snorkeling on one of Miyakojima's reefs—the island is ringed by coral and the water clarity is exceptional. Operators offer roughly 1.5-hour outings from Yoshino or Aragusuku Beach. Book the day before in high season.

⏱ 90 min
