☀️ Weather

Autumn (March-May): Perfect weather with comfortable temperatures (18-26°C), minimal rainfall, and ideal conditions for safari and outdoor activities. Excellent wine harvest season, beautiful landscapes, moderate tourist numbers, reasonable accommodation rates.

Spring (September-November): Excellent weather with warming temperatures (16-24°C), blooming wildflowers, and perfect conditions for wildlife viewing. Whale watching season, jacaranda blooms, moderate prices, excellent photography opportunities.

Summer (December-February): Warm temperatures (20-30°C) with occasional rainfall, peak holiday season, and vibrant outdoor atmosphere. Beach season, highest accommodation rates, school holidays, excellent for coastal activities.

Winter (June-August): Cool, dry weather (8-20°C) perfect for safari with excellent wildlife viewing and clear skies. Peak safari season, whale watching, cozy wine estates, moderate accommodation rates.

Storms River

mouth trail suspension bridges kayaking forest

★★★☆☆
1

Experience the world-famous Bloukrans Bungy, recognized as the highest commercial bungy jump from a bridge on Earth. Plunge more than 200 metres towards the magnificent Bloukrans River valley using state-of-the-art pendulum bungy technology that ensures the smoothest jump possible. The breathtaking views from Africa's highest bridge capture all the majesty of this pristine wilderness area.

⏱ 75 min
2

Embark on an exhilarating Tree Top Canopy Tour, ziplining between platforms along steel cables set amongst the magnificent Tsitsikamma forest surroundings. This unforgettable adventure is suitable for ages 7 to 70, making it perfect for families, nature lovers, and thrill-seekers alike. Glide through the ancient indigenous forest canopy for a unique perspective on this protected ecosystem.

⏱ 75 min
3

Cross the iconic Storms River Suspension Bridge within Tsitsikamma National Park, where over 80 kilometres of protected coastline meet diverse ecosystems including fynbos, forests, and abundant marine life. The park, established in 1964, offers stunning scenery, hiking trails, and opportunities to observe the unique flora and fauna of this pristine coastal wilderness.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Join a Kayak and Lilo Experience, paddling across the ocean and beneath the Storms River suspension bridge before venturing deeper into the dramatic Storms River Gorge. This family-friendly adventure allows you to leap from cliff overhangs into deep pools and explore the spectacular gorge by inflatable lilo. The experience is consistently recommended as a highlight by visitors from around the world.

⏱ 60 min

Discover Storms River Village and the surrounding Tsitsikamma Forest on a guided Segway tour. Glide through cool pine forests and indigenous woodland, crossing the historical Witteklip Bridge and visiting the magnificent 1,000-year-old Tsitsikamma Big Tree. This unique mode of exploration offers an unforgettable way to experience the area's natural wonders.

⏱ 60 min

Jeffreys Bay

supertubes surf mecca shell museum dolphins

★★★★☆
1

Ride the legendary Super Tubes at Jeffreys Bay, consistently ranked among the best surfing spots in the world. When conditions align with a 4-8ft swell and southwest winds, this world-class wave can break from the point all the way past the car park. The warm local hospitality and laid-back coastal atmosphere make this surfer's paradise an unmissable destination on the Eastern Cape coast.

⏱ 75 min
2

Experience the ultimate adrenaline rush with a tandem skydive over Jeffreys Bay. A scenic 20-minute flight takes you to over 2,700 metres before a 30-second freefall at 200 kilometres per hour, followed by a peaceful 5-minute parachute descent with panoramic coastal views. All instructors have completed a minimum of 1,000 jumps, ensuring the highest safety standards.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Explore the pristine beaches surrounding Jeffreys Bay, including the family-friendly shores of Aston Bay, Kabeljous, and Paradise Beach. The town offers excellent accommodation options, diverse restaurants, and vibrant evening entertainment with live music and beach bonfires. Local markets provide opportunities to shop for beachwear, surf gear, and unique souvenirs.

Addo

big five elephants sunday river citrus

★★★★☆
1

Explore Addo Elephant National Park, South Africa's third-largest national park and the only Big 7 game reserve in the world. Witness the largest density of African elephants on the planet alongside Cape buffalo, leopard, black rhino, and lion, plus great white sharks and seasonal Southern right whales in the marine section. The park's diverse ecosystems encompass 5 of South Africa's 7 biomes.

⏱ 75 min
2

Soar through the air on the highest, longest, and fastest double zipline in South Africa, the only adventure of its kind in the country. The experience includes a moderate 500-metre hike to the launch platform, rewarding participants with spectacular views of the Sundays River Valley. A unique giant swing adds additional thrills for adventure seekers.

⏱ 75 min
3

Visit the Daniell Cheetah Project for an educational Predator Tour led by trained guides. This one-hour experience provides insights into cheetah conservation and the facility's rehabilitation work. Guests have the unique opportunity to enter a cheetah enclosure under expert supervision, learning about these magnificent cats and their critical conservation status.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Cruise the Sundays River and experience the thrill of sand sledding on towering dunes just 5 kilometres from Addo Elephant National Park. This family-friendly adventure combines peaceful river exploration with the excitement of sliding down golden sand dunes. The activities are suitable for all ages and offer a refreshing contrast to traditional game viewing.

⏱ 60 min

Join an Addo Wildlife Eco Tour on the banks of the Sundays River, where expert guides share knowledge about unique Eastern Cape plants and trees. Observe the reserve's prolific wildlife including the locally endemic flightless dung beetle, a tiny creature vital to the ecosystem. The experience concludes with refreshments at a riverside boma.

⏱ 60 min

Makhanda

arts festival settler history national monument

★★★★☆
1

Discover Makhanda, host city of the National Arts Festival, the largest annual celebration of arts on the African continent held each July. This historic town offers a rich programme of drama, dance, theatre, comedy, opera, music, jazz, visual art, and film, transforming into a vibrant cultural hub that attracts artists and audiences from around the world.

⏱ 75 min
2

Explore the Amazwi Museum of South African Literature, one of the country's literary treasures tucked away in Makhanda. The museum collects creative writing by southern African authors across all genres including novels, plays, poetry, and memoirs. Critical writings, manuscripts, and audio-visual materials document the rich literary heritage of the region.

⏱ 75 min
3

Visit the Albany Museum, established in 1855 as South Africa's second oldest museum, and its Observatory Museum housing one of only two Victorian Cameras Obscura in the world. This magical mirror device reflects the town onto a large circular table, offering a unique historical perspective. The companion piece exists only in Bath, England.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stop at the historic Pig and Whistle Inn in nearby Bathurst, providing hospitality for over 180 years and home to the oldest continuously licensed pub in South Africa. Nearby stands the Big Pineapple, a three-storey landmark constructed in the 1980s containing a museum about pineapple cultivation and an observation platform with panoramic views.

⏱ 60 min

Experience world-class Big 5 game viewing at one of Frontier Country's prestigious reserves including Shamwari, Kwandwe, Lalibela, Pumba, and Amakhala. These private reserves offer exceptional wildlife encounters in landscapes where five great cultures-San, Khoi-Khoi, Dutch, Xhosa, and British-once met during nine frontier wars.

⏱ 60 min
