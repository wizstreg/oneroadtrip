★★★☆☆
1

Phou Fa, the sky mountain, rises a few kilometers from town and offers the region's most spectacular panorama. The 30-minute ascent remains accessible to most hikers.

2

⏱ 75 min
3

⏱ 75 min
4

Phongsali's ethnic museum ranks among Laos's best with its detailed information on groups populating the province. The 2013 exhibition presents clothing, music, agriculture, dwellings, and rituals.

5

Activities

1

Ban Komaen village nestles across the valley facing Phongsali and houses the century-old tea plantations making the region's fame. Some tea trees exceed 400 years old.

⏱ 75 min
4

⏱ 75 min
5

⏱ 75 min

Activities

The morning at the plantations allows tasting and buying tea directly from producers. Traditional bamboo packaging preserves the aromas.

⏱ 60 min

Early afternoon trek departure marks the beginning of the southward adventure. The local guide carries part of the provisions and facilitates village contacts.

⏱ 60 min

The walk crosses terraced rice fields and forest sections to Ban Hatsa village. The tranquil pace allows appreciating changing landscapes.

Ban Yo

★★☆☆☆
1

The second trek day continues the southward descent through several isolated hamlets. Villages succeed each other in a landscape of cultivated hills and forests.

⏱ 75 min
3

4

⏱ 75 min
5

Ban Yo Akha village hosts the second night in a different atmosphere from the previous evening. Each community possesses its own traditions of hospitality and sharing.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

The walk covers about fifteen kilometers through terrain that gradually softens. Climbs become rarer while the southern route reveals itself.

⏱ 60 min

Women weavers work at their looms in front of houses and sometimes share their techniques with visitors. Indigo dyeing colors their hands deep blue.

⏱ 60 min

Lao Lao, locally distilled rice alcohol, accompanies the welcoming of visitors in villages. Exchanges pass through gestures and smiles when the guide translates.

⏱ 60 min

Respecting customs is essential: covering clothing, asking permission before photos, attention to ritual barriers during ceremonies.

Luang Prabang

Ancient royal UNESCO capital: refined conclusion to the journey among golden temples and colonial avenues

★★★★★
1

Pak Ou caves open in a limestone cliff at the Mekong and Nam Ou confluence. Thousands of Buddha figurines deposited over centuries line the walls.

⏱ 75 min
2

The darker upper cave reveals hundreds of statues piled in corners. A flashlight helps pierce the darkness.

⏱ 75 min
3

Kuang Si falls descend 50 meters of limestone tiers in the jungle's heart. Turquoise pools invite swimming in covering attire.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Moon Bear sanctuary shelters Asian black bears rescued from bile trafficking at the park entrance.

⏱ 75 min
5

TAEC gathers costumes and textiles from Laos's 49 ethnic groups. This museum complements the encounters lived in Phongsali villages.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Morning navigation to Pak Ou parades karst cliffs and fishing villages. The journey on the Nam Ou recalls the descent from Muang Khua a few days earlier.

⏱ 60 min

The afternoon at Kuang Si falls rewards with a cool swim. The turquoise water contrasts with the jungle's deep green.

⏱ 60 min

The next morning's tak bat can close the stay if the traveler remains an extra night. Monks file silently from 5:45am to receive sticky rice from the faithful.

⏱ 60 min

The journey from the far north ends in the gentleness of the former royal capital, rich with encounters and landscapes crossed from Phongsali.
