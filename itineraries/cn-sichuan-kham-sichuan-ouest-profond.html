☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming flowers, ideal weather, moderate crowds, excellent for most regions, comfortable travel.

Fall (September-October): Perfect weather, clear skies, comfortable temperatures, peak tourist season, higher prices, autumn colors beautiful.

Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, rainy season, crowded tourist sites, highest prices, but best for Tibet and western regions.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, fewer crowds, lower prices, perfect for southern regions, harsh in north, unique winter experiences.

Regional Variations: Vast climate diversity, southern subtropical, northern continental, western plateau, plan according to regions visited.

Chengdu

Sichuan's capital, panda base and gateway to Tibetan Kham

★★★★☆
📍 Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding📍 Jinli Ancient Street Chengdu📍 Wenshu Monastery Chengdu📍 People's Park Chengdu
1

Start with the Chengdu Research Base, founded in 1987 with six rescued pandas. Today, over 200 pandas live here. Arrive right at opening, 7:30am—pandas are active early, by 9:30am they're already dozing. Plan three hours to explore the enclosures, nursery, and museum. Skip weekends if you can; the crowds are crushing.

⏱ 180 min
2

Step through the gate of Wenshu Monastery, founded during the Tang dynasty in the 7th century—you're entering Chengdu's largest active Buddhist temple. Pass through the Hall of a Thousand Buddhas, then settle in for tea in the inner courtyard: monks serve jasmine for a few yuan. The incense smoke, the clatter of mahjong tiles from the neighboring teahouse—this is authentic Chengdu.

⏱ 75 min
3

Head to Jinli Street, a reconstructed Qing-dynasty commercial alley. Yes, it's touristy, but at night when the red lanterns glow and street vendors roll out their carts of dan dan mian, you're swept up in it. Sample san da pao (glutinous rice balls), spicy tofu skewers, and herbal tea. Budget 1-1.5 hours for a proper food crawl.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Dive into a proper teahouse in People's Park. Sit down, order a bowl of jasmine tea (15 yuan), keep your ears open: an ear cleaner will pass by, chiming his metal tools. It's a Sichuanese ritual thousands of years old, surprisingly relaxing for 50 yuan. You'll also see water calligraphy on the flagstones, mahjong games, and sometimes live Sichuan opera performances outdoors on weekends.

⏱ 90 min

Taste a real Sichuanese hotpot for dinner in the Yulin district. The ma la broth—Sichuan peppercorn plus chili—numbs your lips from the first bite. Order tripe, beef slices, enoki mushrooms, and sweet potato noodles. Wash it down with iced barley tea to cool the fire. Budget 100-150 yuan per person—it's the essential experience before you head into the mountains.

Wolong

200,000-hectare UNESCO reserve with roughly 150 wild pandas

📍 Yingxiu Earthquake Memorial📍 Xuankou Middle School Earthquake Ruins Yingxiu📍 Shenshuping Panda Base Wolong📍 Wolong Nature Reserve
1

Make your first stop at Yingxiu, epicenter of the May 12, 2008 earthquake—magnitude 8.0. You'll walk through the ruins of Xuankou Middle School, which collapsed at precisely 2:28pm; the frozen clocks bear witness. More than 87,000 dead or missing across the region. The visit is hard, silent, necessary. Plan 1-1.5 hours. Free entry, open 8am-5pm. Don't photograph local visitors who come to pay their respects.

⏱ 90 min
2

Head next to Shenshuping in Gengda—you'll find over 90 pandas in 59 enclosures spread across 150 hectares of forest. Far more authentic than Chengdu: the base, opened in 2016, replaced Hetaoping, which was destroyed in the earthquake. Don't miss feeding times: 9:30-10:30am and 2-4pm, when pandas climb trees or roll in the grass.

⏱ 180 min
3

Finish with the Wolong Panda Museum, 24 km from Shenshuping. You'll trace the species' history over three million years and the impact of the 2008 earthquake. One hour is enough, but the exhibit on captive breeding (between 1986 and 2000, 46 cubs were born here) is fascinating. The section on post-quake reconstruction puts everything you've just seen into perspective.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If you've got energy left, lace up your hiking boots for the Five Pavilions Trail (Wuyipeng), at 2,400m elevation. This is where the first WWF research station was installed in 1978. The trail cuts through bamboo forest where wild pandas actually roam—you probably won't see one, but tracks and droppings are common. Two hours round-trip from the road.

Siguniangshan

Four peaks nicknamed the Alps of Sichuan, Yaomei reaching 6,250m

📍 Mount Siguniang📍 Shuangqiao Valley Siguniangshan📍 Rilong Town Siguniangshan📍 Siguniangshan Scenic Area
1

Welcome to the "Alps of Sichuan." You arrive facing four peaks standing in a row, including Yaomei Feng at 6,250m, nicknamed the Queen of Sichuan Mountains. Legend tells of four sisters transformed into mountains to fight a demon—one for each peak. You'll stay in Rilong (3,200m), the base village. At sunset, look up: the range glows pink, then purple.

⏱ 60 min
2

Tackle Shuangqiao (Double Bridge) Valley, the most accessible of the park's three valleys. You'll ride an electric shuttle bus along 30 km with fifteen stops punctuating the route: Mermaid Bridge, Yak Meadow, Five-Color Lake. Budget 4-5 hours to hit the main stops. At 3,800m max elevation, breath comes short—move slowly.

⏱ 270 min

Activities

Try horseback riding with the Jiarong Tibetan herders of Rilong. For 200-300 yuan per person, you'll ride with a local guide (often a village teenager) into the high pastures up to 4,000m. Sichuan horses are small, sturdy, reliable—even for beginners. Count on 3-4 hours round-trip. Bring warm clothes; the wind blows hard.

⏱ 240 min

If you're there on a festival night, Rilong village hosts guozhuang evenings—Tibetan circle dances around a bonfire, accompanied by songs. Locals will pull you by the hand to join the circle. It's free, usually from June to September. Accept a bowl of warm barley beer (qingke)—it warms you right up.

Hike Changping (Long Bridge) Valley, the hikers' playground. You'll follow 18 km of wooden boardwalk tracing the river to Lama Monastery, hidden among centuries-old pines. Activities

Taste Jiarong cuisine with a family in Rilong. Hosts eat with you, translation by gestures. Prepare yourself for rancid butter—that's the authentic taste.

Danba

Jiarong Tibetan valley with 500 thousand-year-old stone towers

📍 Jiaju Tibetan Village Danba📍 Suopo Watchtowers Danba📍 Dadu River Danba📍 Zhonglu Tibetan Village Danba
1

After a pass at 4,500m, you plunge toward Danba—a 1,800m descent into the Dadu River basin. The town, stretched along the river at 1,800m, is your gateway to the Jiarong villages. Drop your bags, grab a coffee, climb to the Suopo viewpoint across the valley. The density of stone towers is breathtaking: over 80 towers in Suopo alone, the tallest reaching 67m.

⏱ 90 min
2

Climb next to Jiaju village, terraced up the slope—white and red houses with wooden roofs, barley terraces, prayer flags. National Geographic named it "China's most beautiful village" in 2005. Take the stone paths, ask permission before entering courtyards, buy wild honey from residents. Budget 2-3 hours.

⏱ 150 min

Activities

Climb inside a real Jiarong tower. You'll ascend interior ladders across 4 or 5 levels to the top at 30-40m—several families in Suopo rent access to their tower for 50 yuan. Built between 800 and 1,200 years ago from stone and dried mud, without any modern pillars—they've survived multiple earthquakes. Plunging view over the Dadu.

⏱ 60 min

If you arrive in October, Danba celebrates the Jiarong Harvest Festival. Women bring out traditional costumes—embroidered robes, silver belts, headdresses adorned with coral. Songs, dances, horse races in the meadows. The villages of Jiaju and Zhonglu are the most active. Ask your guesthouse for exact dates—they shift according to the Tibetan lunar calendar.

Danba

Jiarong Tibetan valley with 500 thousand-year-old stone towers

📍 Zhonglu Tibetan Village Danba📍 Suopo Tibetan Village Danba📍 Moerduo Sacred Mountain Danba
1

Head to Zhonglu, quieter than Jiaju, where you'll discover 81 watchtowers and 149 Tibetan families scattered across 5 km². Stay overnight if you can: several tower-houses rent rooms to travelers (200-300 yuan per night, meals included). Dawn here is magical—mist rises from the Dadu, towers emerge one by one.

⏱ 180 min
2

Dive into Suopo, which holds the world's densest tower forest—175 towers in 13 hamlets. You'll find rare 13-angled structures, unique in the world. Built by the Jiarong during the ancient Kingdom of Women, they served for defense, storing wealth, sometimes also for worship. Many lean dangerously, like a local Leaning Tower of Pisa.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Try the hike toward sacred Mount Moerduo (5,105m), the Jiarong's guardian mountain. You can do the full circuit in 3 days, or a half-day loop from Geshizha—count on 4-5 hours, 800m elevation gain. You'll cross paths with pilgrims on koras (circumambulation) and cairns with prayer flags. Stay on the trails—this is sacred ground.

⏱ 300 min

Evening embroidery workshop with a local family. Danba embroidery is inscribed on China's intangible heritage list, and several village women offer introductory workshops—one hour for 100 yuan, you leave with a small embroidered motif on fabric. Floral motifs symbolize Gasang, sacred flowers of Jiarong culture.

Activities

Take a gallop across Tagong grassland. Khampa nomads rent you horses for 100-150 yuan per hour. These stocky, thick-maned horses carry you at a gallop through the tall grass—demanding on your backside, unforgettable for your eyes. Snow-capped Mount Yala dominates the horizon. Welcome to Xinduqiao, nicknamed "photographers' paradise." At 3,300m, you cross a wide basin where Tibetan hamlets of wood and adobe houses dot meadows cut by streams. Step through the gate of Litang Monastery, founded in 1580 by the 3rd Dalai Lama—you enter one of Kham's largest Gelugpa sanctuaries. You'll encounter 1,500 monks who still live here. Walk Litang's old town, under restoration, where you'll discover a few authentic alleys south of the monastery. Taste salted butter tea in a real nomad's tent on the grassland. Five km from town, herders welcome you in season. 30 yuan for tea and a bit of tsampa, more if you want fresh yak yogurt. You fall for Xiangcheng the moment you arrive. Daocheng (3,750m) is your gateway to Yading. The town itself remains modest—a few streets, a monastery, hotels springing up fast. Stock up on water, chocolate, and altitude pills (ibuprofen + Diamox). You'll stay tonight at Riwa (Shangri-La in Chinese), 30 km further, at the reserve entrance.

Rise early to see first light hit Chenrezig. You'll watch the summit turn gold for five minutes, then fade back to white. Photographers: position at Chonggu Si by 6:45am in September. Pilgrims arrive on koras around the monastery at the same hour—worth the trip alone.

Head back north—Daocheng to Litang, 200 km, 4 hours. Moxi

Historic town at the foot of Hailuogou Glacier and Gongga

1

Welcome to Hailuogou ("Conch Gully"), at the foot of Mount Gongga (7,556m), Sichuan's highest. You'll discover Hailuogou Glacier, 14.7 km long, descending to 2,850m—the lowest modern glacier in East Asia. Unique phenomenon: it plunges into primary subtropical forest. 2

Enter the park at 8am, take the mandatory shuttle (70 yuan) to Camp 3. You'll follow a 30-min trail to the great icefall: 1,080m high, 1,100m wide—China's highest icefall. The thunder of avalanches echoes several times daily. At 2,960m, you see 45 peaks over 6,000m surrounding you. Stay cautious near edges; local guide mandatory to venture onto the ice.

⏱ 240 min
3

Ride the cable car (3,500m long) to Camp 4 at 3,600m, above the glacier. If weather cooperates, you see the golden summit of Gongga through the cabin's glass windows. Panoramic observation platform, red rock bank (caused by algae trentepohlia jolithus), cable car return. Count on 2 hours on-site. Freezing cold, bring fleece jacket.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Soak in Hailuogou's hot springs that evening, right facing Mount Gongga. A world-unique phenomenon: springs up to 80°C gushing at the glacier's foot. Several hotels exploit these waters (Gongga Shentang Hot Spring Hotel, 5-star, 15 min from entrance). 100-200 yuan per session. Soaking in hot water with glacier views—surreal moment.

⏱ 120 min
Stop 16

Chengdu

Sichuan's capital, panda base and gateway to Tibetan Kham

★★★★☆
📍 Moxi Catholic Church📍 Bifengxia Panda Base Ya'an📍 Mengshan Tea Mountain Ya'an
1

Before leaving Moxi, walk the cobbled street of the old town—you'll trace Mao Zedong's footsteps, who slept here the night of May 30, 1935, during the Long March. The relay house still exists, transformed into a museum. But the highlight is the stone Catholic church built by a French missionary, astonishing at the foot of the Himalayas. Mao reportedly held a strategic council here.

⏱ 90 min
2

Detour through Bifengxia (Ya'an), an alternative panda base to Chengdu, far less crowded. This is where Wolong's pandas were relocated after the 2008 earthquake. You'll find over 60 pandas in 1,067 hectares of forest—real forest trails with real waterfalls. Three hours on-site. Open 8:30am-5pm, 100 yuan.

⏱ 180 min
3

Make a final stop at Mengshan, 15 km from Ya'an, considered the birthplace of Chinese tea. You'll see the oldest recorded tea tree, planted according to tradition by Wu Lizhen under the Western Han (around 53 BC). Seven sacred trees (the "Seven Mothers") are preserved in a sanctuary. Visit the Han Tea Museum, buy Mengding green tea—famed as imperial tribute during the Tang.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Taste the seven teas of Mengshan in a proper temple teahouse. Traditional 30-minute ceremony, where the master presents the Ganlu, Huangya, Shifang varieties, etc.—each with its brewing technique. 80-150 yuan. You leave with a box of local tea and a deep understanding of why Sichuan is the birthplace of Chinese tea.

⏱ 60 min

Arrival dinner in Chengdu: return to a hotpot district, but this time try shuizhu yu (fish in chili broth) or fuqi feipian (Sichuan pepper-glazed beef slices). After 16 days of Tibetan cuisine and butter tea, your palate rediscovers chili—and loves it. Yulin, Jinli, or Lujiang Street: three solid neighborhoods.
