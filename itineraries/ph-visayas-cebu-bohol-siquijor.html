📆 Best period

December, January, February, March, April, May

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 5 and 9 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (December-May): Peak tourist season with minimal rainfall, comfortable temperatures (26-32°C), and excellent conditions for island hopping and beach activities. Highest prices and crowds.

Cool Dry Season (December-February): Most comfortable temperatures and lowest humidity. Perfect weather for all activities, though expect highest accommodation rates and tourist numbers.

Hot Dry Season (March-May): Hottest temperatures but still dry conditions. Good for beach activities, though midday heat can be intense. Shoulder season pricing.

Rainy Season (June-November): Monsoon season with typhoon risk, but lower prices and fewer tourists. Some islands remain accessible, afternoon rains common.

🌍 What to discover in PH?

Archipelago paradise with pristine beaches, vibrant culture, and incredible diversity. Experience traditional festivals, beautiful islands, and warm Filipino hospitality. The country offers cultural authenticity with natural beauty and adventure activities. Philippines provides diverse experiences with cultural richness and natural wonders.

📋 Important notes

Loop circuit starting from Cebu. Ferries between islands (2-4h per crossing). Bring cash - ATMs are scarce outside Cebu City.

⚠️ SAFETY NOTE: The Philippines is generally safe in main tourist areas, though avoid southern Mindanao and Sulu archipelago due to terrorism and kidnapping risks. Stick to established tourist destinations like Luzon, Visayas, and northern Mindanao. Check current government advisories and use reputable transportation and accommodation.

Cebu

The Philippines' oldest city, where Spanish forts, sacred basilicas and crispy lechon meet

★★★★☆
📍 Fort San Pedro Cebu📍 Basilica Santo Niño Cebu📍 Magellan's Cross Cebu📍 Colon Street Cebu📍 Carbon Market Cebu
1

Cebu City is the Philippines' oldest city - this is where Magellan landed in 1521, and where he died just weeks later, killed by Chief Lapu-Lapu on Mactan Island across the strait. The city preserves traces of this turbulent history.

⏱ 30 min
2

Fort San Pedro, built by the Spanish in 1565, is the country's oldest fort. It's a modest stone triangle heavy with history - it served as a Spanish garrison, a Japanese prison camp, and even a zoo before being restored. The interior gardens are peaceful and shaded.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Basilica of Santo Niño houses the Philippines' oldest Christian relic - a statue of the Child Jesus given by Magellan in 1521. The church is always packed with devotees lighting candles and praying aloud. The atmosphere is intense and sincere.

⏱ 40 min
4

Right next door, Magellan's Cross - or rather its shrine. The original cross planted by Magellan in 1521 is said to be inside a cross made of tindalo wood, itself protected by an octagonal pavilion with a painted ceiling. Historians debate its authenticity, but devotees don't care and come to pray.

⏱ 25 min

Activities

Stroll through Carbon Market, Cebu's largest and oldest market. It's loud, dense, chaotic - and fascinating. Mountains of mangoes, hundreds of dried fish, medicinal herbs, hardware goods. Not a tourist market, a real city market.

⏱ 60 min

Lechon dinner in Cebu - the city considers itself the roast suckling pig capital of the Philippines. The skin crackles when you bite it, the meat is infused with herbs and lemongrass. Ask for a local restaurant rather than a chain.

Cebu

The Philippines' oldest city, where Spanish forts, sacred basilicas and crispy lechon meet

★★★★☆
📍 Temple of Leah Cebu📍 Tops Lookout Cebu📍 IT Park Cebu📍 Sirao Flower Garden Cebu
1

The Temple of Leah, in the hills above Cebu, is a neo-Roman mausoleum built by a businessman for his deceased wife. Corinthian columns, golden statues, monumental staircases - it's kitsch, grandiose and touching all at once. The view over the city from the terrace is worth the trip.

⏱ 45 min
2

Continue climbing to Tops Lookout, the highest viewpoint accessible by vehicle above Cebu City. From the platform, the 360° panorama sweeps across the city, Cebu Strait, Mactan Island and the interior mountains. Best in late afternoon when the heat haze lifts.

⏱ 35 min
3

The Taoist Temple, in the Beverly Hills residential district (yes, like Los Angeles), is a colorful Chinese temple open to visitors. Red and green pagodas, carved dragons, and a clear view over the city. Cebu's Chinese community is old and influential - this temple is its symbol.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Evening at IT Park - Cebu's business district that comes alive at night. The call centers work on American hours, so restaurants and food courts stay open until 3am. Young, modern atmosphere, very different from the historic center.

⏱ 60 min

If you like live music, Filipinos are formidable singers. Cebu's karaoke bars aren't tourist traps - it's genuine national culture. Any tricycle driver knows a good bar.

Moalboal

A divers' village where millions of sardines swim just a few strokes from shore

★★★★☆
📍 Panagsama Beach Moalboal📍 Sardine Run Moalboal📍 White Beach Moalboal
1

Drive from Cebu City to Moalboal on the southwest coast - 3 hours by bus or van through the mountains. The landscape shifts from dense city to jungle, then sugarcane fields and coastal villages.

⏱ 25 min
2

Moalboal is a divers' village. No fine sandy beach here - the Panagsama shore is rocky and lined with bars and dive centers. What draws people is what's happening underwater: 5 meters from shore, the reef drops vertically into deep blue.

⏱ 35 min
3

White Beach, 10 minutes by scooter from Panagsama, is Moalboal's real beach - white sand, coconut palms, calm water. Much less crowded than northern Cebu's beaches, it offers a simple and pleasant spot to relax.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

First snorkeling at Panagsama: enter the water from shore and swim 20 meters - you hit the Sardine Run. A school of several million sardines forms a shifting silver wall right at the drop-off edge. The sardines constantly change shape like a single organism. It's free, accessible to everyone, and stunning.

⏱ 60 min

Dinner at one of Panagsama Beach's restaurants. The vibe is laid-back - divers comparing their days, solo travelers reading, reggae music in the background. The grilled fish is fresh and cheap.

Moalboal

A divers' village where millions of sardines swim just a few strokes from shore

★★★★☆
📍 Kawasan Falls Badian📍 Canyoneering Kawasan📍 Turtle Point Moalboal📍 Pescador Island Moalboal
1

Pescador Island, 20 minutes by bangka from Moalboal, is a vegetation-covered rock sitting in the middle of the strait. Underwater, it's a cathedral: the reef descends in vertical walls carpeted with soft corals, gorgonians and sponges. Green turtles reside around the island year-round - you'll almost certainly encounter one.

⏱ 50 min
2

Kawasan Falls, 30 minutes' drive south of Moalboal (Badian village), are among the Philippines' most beautiful. The turquoise water tumbles in two tiers into a jungle-surrounded pool. The water's almost surreal blue comes from the limestone it flows through.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Canyoneering to Kawasan Falls - the region's star activity. You work your way up the canyon for 3-4 hours, jumping from rock to rock, swimming through natural pools and jumping off cliffs (3 to 15 meters) into turquoise water. Intense, wet, and memorable.

⏱ 240 min

Return to Moalboal exhausted but happy. One last snorkel at sunset with the sardines - the school moves differently depending on the time and light. At night, the sardines rise toward the surface and shimmer under the lights.

Tagbilaran

Gateway to Bohol, island of Chocolate Hills, tarsiers and the Loboc River

★★★★☆
📍 Tagbilaran Bohol📍 Loboc River Bohol📍 Baclayon Church Bohol
1

Ferry from Cebu (Liloan port or Cebu City) to Tagbilaran, Bohol's capital. The crossing takes 2-4 hours depending on the ferry. Tagbilaran is a small provincial city with no major attractions, but it's the gateway to Bohol.

⏱ 25 min
2

Baclayon Church, 6 km from Tagbilaran, is one of the Philippines' oldest - built of coral stone by Jesuits in 1596. The 2013 earthquake severely damaged it, but restoration is progressing. The adjacent museum displays religious artifacts from the colonial era.

⏱ 40 min
3

The Loboc River winds through thick jungle in Bohol's interior. The banks are lined with coconut palms and stilt villages. It's one of the Philippines' greenest and most peaceful landscapes.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

Lunch cruise on the Loboc River. A floating restaurant boat travels up the river for an hour, Filipino buffet on board, accompanied by local musicians. It's touristy but enjoyable - the river is beautiful and the food decent.

⏱ 75 min

Alternative option: stand-up paddle or kayak on the Loboc River, more authentic than the cruise. You paddle beneath the canopy, encountering kingfishers and monitor lizards.

Tagbilaran

Gateway to Bohol, island of Chocolate Hills, tarsiers and the Loboc River

★★★★☆
📍 Chocolate Hills Bohol📍 Tarsier Sanctuary Corella Bohol📍 Man-made Forest Bohol📍 Hanging Bridge Sevilla Bohol
1

The Chocolate Hills are Bohol's natural landmark - 1,268 grass-covered conical hills that turn chocolate brown in the dry season (hence the name). From the Carmen viewpoint, the view is surreal: hundreds of identical domes stretch to the horizon like a giant model.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Corella Tarsier Sanctuary is the only ethical place to observe these tiny primates (the world's smallest - they fit in your hand). Tarsiers are nocturnal and extremely sensitive to noise and light. The sanctuary enforces silence, prohibits flash photography, and limits visitor numbers. Respect the rules - stress can literally kill a tarsier.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Man-Made Forest, a cathedral of mahogany trees planted in the 1950s, forms a green tunnel over 2 km of road between Loboc and Bilar. Light filters through the canopy - it's cool, quiet, and photogenic.

⏱ 25 min
4

The bamboo hanging bridge at Sevilla spans the Loboc River 25 meters above the water. It sways when you walk across - fun or terrifying depending on your relationship with heights. The view of the river below is worth the vertigo.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Scooter tour through Bohol's interior. The roads are decent, traffic almost non-existent outside towns. You pass rice paddies, village churches, coconut groves, and children shouting "Hey Joe!" (the nickname for foreigners since the war).

⏱ 120 min

Dinner in Tagbilaran - try kalamay, a local confection made from glutinous rice and coconut sugar, sold in coconut shells. It's Bohol's edible souvenir.

⏱ 45 min

Tagbilaran

Gateway to Bohol, island of Chocolate Hills, tarsiers and the Loboc River

★★★★☆
📍 Alona Beach Panglao Bohol📍 Balicasag Island Bohol📍 Hinagdanan Cave Bohol
1

Panglao, a small island connected to Bohol by two bridges, concentrates the beaches and resorts. Alona Beach, the most famous, is a curve of white sand bordered by restaurants and dive centers. It's Bohol's most developed beach - lively but not suffocating.

⏱ 50 min
2

Hinagdanan Cave, in Panglao's interior, hides a freshwater underground lake beneath a stalactite ceiling. You descend by staircase into a cavity lit by natural light shafts. Swimming is possible - the water is cool and green.

⏱ 40 min
3

Balicasag Island, 45 minutes by bangka from Panglao, is a marine sanctuary. The coral drop-off surrounding the island is one of the Visayas' most spectacular - the reef plunges vertically into inky blue 3 meters from shore.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

Diving or snorkeling at Balicasag. Green turtles are everywhere, corals in excellent condition, and schools of surgeonfish form silver clouds at the drop-off edge. For certified divers, the descending wall is spectacular.

⏱ 120 min

Dolphin watching off Panglao. Bangkas leave at dawn (5:30am) and regularly encounter groups of spinner dolphins. Not guaranteed, but frequent in dry season.

Siquijor

The Visayas' mystical island, famous for its healers, waterfalls and secret beaches

★★☆☆☆
📍 Siquijor Town Siquijor📍 Saint Francis Church Siquijor📍 Capilay Spring Park Siquijor
1

Ferry from Tagbilaran to Siquijor - 2 hour crossing. Siquijor is the most mysterious of the Visayas islands. Filipinos call it "the island of fire": early Spanish navigators saw glows in the night (probably fireflies) and thought the island was haunted. Even today, Siquijor is renowned for its traditional healers and potions.

⏱ 30 min
2

The town of Siquijor, around the port, is tiny and peaceful. The white and clean Saint Francis of Assisi Church dates from the 19th century. Next door, a converted convent museum displays artifacts from the colonial era.

⏱ 35 min
3

Capilay Spring Park, 10 minutes from the center, is a freshwater pool fed by a natural spring. Locals come to bathe with family on weekends. The water is cool, clear, and the park is shaded - a simple and pleasant stop.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Scooter tour to get a feel for the island. Siquijor is 20 km in diameter - the complete loop takes 2 hours without stopping. The coastal road passes deserted beaches, village churches, giant trees (the centuries-old balete tree) and views of Bohol and Negros.

⏱ 90 min

Sunset from Paliton Beach on the west coast. A row of coconut palms lean over a narrow white sand beach - the island's most photogenic natural frame.

Siquijor

The Visayas' mystical island, famous for its healers, waterfalls and secret beaches

★★☆☆☆
📍 Cambugahay Falls Siquijor📍 Salagdoong Beach Siquijor📍 Balete Tree Siquijor📍 Lazi Church Siquijor
1

Cambugahay Falls, in the island's interior, cascade in three tiers into turquoise pools. A rope swing system lets you fling yourself into the water from the bank - fun and refreshing. Access is easy, 10 minutes walking downhill.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Balete Tree, a centuries-old banyan fig with tentacle-like aerial roots, shelters a natural pool at its base where small fish nibble dead skin off your feet (natural fish spa). The tree is impressive - its roots form a network of organic columns.

⏱ 35 min
3

Lazi Church and Convent, built in 1857 from coral stone and hardwood, form one of the Visayas' finest colonial ensembles. The convent, enormous for such a small island, testifies to the ambition of the Augustinian Recollects who administered the island.

⏱ 35 min
4

Salagdoong Beach, on the east coast, is famous for its jumping platforms - 5 and 10 meters above deep, clear water. Even if you don't jump, the beach is beautiful and the water perfect for snorkeling.

⏱ 50 min

Activities

If you're curious, ask to meet a traditional healer (mananambal). These practitioners use plants, prayers and rituals to treat physical and spiritual ailments. The healers' festival is held every Holy Week in San Antonio. Outside this period, encounters are possible but discreet - ask your accommodation.

⏱ 60 min

Swimming and relaxing at Cambugahay Falls. In the afternoon, crowds thin out and you'll have the pools almost to yourself. Bring a picnic.

Dumaguete

A gentle university city by the strait, jumping-off point for Apo Island and the Twin Lakes

★★★★☆
📍 Dumaguete Boulevard📍 Apo Island Dumaguete📍 Silliman University Dumaguete📍 Rizal Boulevard Dumaguete
1

Ferry from Siquijor to Dumaguete - 1 hour crossing. Dumaguete is nicknamed "City of Gentle People." It's a lively university town but never stressful, with a pleasant waterfront boulevard and a restaurant and café scene driven by the student community.

⏱ 30 min
2

Rizal Boulevard, the seafront promenade, is the heart of Dumaguete. Locals gather here in the evening to walk, eat and watch the sunset over Siquijor Island across the water. Food stalls serve cheap barbecue and vegetable tempura.

⏱ 40 min
3

Silliman University, founded in 1901 by American Presbyterian missionaries, is Asia's oldest Protestant campus. The white period buildings, lawns and coconut wood church are worth a stroll - it's calm and shaded.

⏱ 35 min

Activities

Excursion to Apo Island, 45 minutes by bangka from Malatapay (30 min drive from Dumaguete). It's a 72-hectare marine sanctuary where green turtles are so numerous you sometimes see 10 in a single dive. Snorkeling from the beach is already excellent.

⏱ 180 min

Dinner on Rizal Boulevard. The waterfront terrace restaurants are affordable and the food is good - try kinilaw (Filipino ceviche with coconut vinegar).

Dumaguete

A gentle university city by the strait, jumping-off point for Apo Island and the Twin Lakes

★★★★☆
📍 Twin Lakes Balinsasayao Negros📍 Casaroro Falls Valencia Negros📍 Malatapay Market Negros
1

The Twin Lakes (Balinsasayao and Danao), 45 minutes into the mountains from Dumaguete, are two crater lakes nestled in dense rainforest at 900 meters elevation. The water is black and still, vegetation drips from the banks, the silence is total. You'd think you were in a different country.

⏱ 60 min
2

Casaroro Falls, near Valencia village (20 min from Dumaguete), tumbles 30 meters into a narrow fern-lined canyon. Access is via a 300-step staircase - the descent is easy, the climb back up less so. The pool water is cold and the current strong - be careful.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Kayaking on Lake Balinsasayao. Kayaks are available for rent on site. You paddle on mirror-black water surrounded by tree ferns and bird songs. The second lake, Danao, is accessible via a 20-minute forest trail.

⏱ 90 min

If it's a Wednesday, stop by Malatapay market on the way to the lakes. It's a noisy and colorful livestock and fish market - pigs, goats, dried fish, tropical fruits. Authentic and impressive.

Cebu

The Philippines' oldest city, where Spanish forts, sacred basilicas and crispy lechon meet

★★★★☆
📍 Oslob Cebu📍 Sumilon Island Cebu📍 Simala Church Cebu
1

Ferry from Dumaguete to Liloan (south coast of Cebu), then drive north up the coast. Total journey to Cebu City takes 5-6 hours with stops.

⏱ 25 min
2

Possible stop in Oslob. This is where whale sharks are fed by fishermen to allow tourists to swim with them. The site is controversial - feeding alters these wild animals' behavior and attracts massive crowds. If you go, know that the practice is criticized by marine conservation NGOs.

⏱ 40 min
3

Alternative to Oslob: Sumilon Island, right across the water, offers a white sandbar surrounded by turquoise water and a preserved reef for snorkeling - without ethical controversy.

⏱ 50 min
4

Heading back up to Cebu City, stop at the Simala Sanctuary (Monastery of the Holy Eucharist). This neo-Gothic church-fortress, perched in the hills, is a major pilgrimage site for Filipinos. The architecture is extravagant - turrets, stained glass, monumental staircases. A place of intense faith.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Scenic drive along Cebu's southeast coast. The road winds between sea and mountains, passing fishing villages and viewpoints over the Bohol Strait.

⏱ 60 min

Reunion dinner in Cebu City - the loop is complete. Return to lechon or discover satti (chicken and beef skewers in coconut sauce, a Mindanao specialty adopted by Cebu).

Bantayan

A powdery sand island off Cebu, a peaceful haven far from the tourist crowds

★☆☆☆☆
📍 Sugar Beach Bantayan📍 Santa Fe Beach Bantayan📍 Kota Beach Bantayan📍 Bantayan Town Cebu
1

Bus then ferry from Cebu City to Bantayan Island, northwest of Cebu - count on 5-6 hours total. Bantayan is the island where Filipinos themselves go when they want beaches without crowds. The sand is white and powdery, the water calm and warm, accommodations simple and cheap.

⏱ 30 min
2

Sugar Beach in Santa Fe is the main beach - a kilometer of fine sand bordered by a few discreet resorts. The water is so shallow you walk 100 meters before it reaches waist-deep. No jet-skis, no banana boats - just the sea.

⏱ 60 min
3

Bantayan Town village, west of the island, has a 17th-century coral church (Saints Peter and Paul Church) and a lively fish market. The streets are quiet, tricycles rare, dogs numerous.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

Island scooter tour. Bantayan is 15 km long - in two hours you've seen everything: deserted beaches on the north side, mangroves on the east, fishing villages. Stop at Ogtong Cave, a natural freshwater turquoise cave-pool.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset at Kota Beach, west coast. The beach is narrow but the view is clear and the sky colors are spectacular.

Return to Cebu from Bantayan - same ferry + bus route, 5-6 hours. If your flight is in the evening, you have time for one last stop on Mactan Island, across from Cebu City.

The Lapu-Lapu Shrine, on Mactan beach, marks the spot where the warrior chief killed Magellan on April 27, 1521. Mactan's guitar factories: the island is the Philippines' main producer of handcrafted guitars. The workshops (Alegre, Lapu-Lapu Guitar) shape ukuleles and guitars from local wood - you can visit and buy directly.

Activities

Last-minute shopping: dried mangoes from Cebu (the country's best), otap (flaky biscuits), and rosquillos (dry donuts). Mactan-Cebu Airport is international - direct flights to Manila, Palawan, and several Asian cities.
