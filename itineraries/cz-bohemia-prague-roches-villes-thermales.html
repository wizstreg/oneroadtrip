Bohemia: Prague, sandstone cities & thermal spas in 14 days

Prague

★★★★★
1

570 meters long, covering roughly 70,000 m² of ground—Prague Castle (Pražský hrad) holds the Guinness record for the world's largest castle complex and doesn't need invented superlatives. Founded around 880 AD, seat of Bohemian kings and the Czech president, it encloses cathedral, royal palace and narrow lanes within its walls. Set aside half a day; the combination ticket includes St. Vitus Cathedral and the Royal Palace.

⏱ 180 min
2

St. Vitus Cathedral deserves an hour just for its Art Nouveau stained glass by Alfons Mucha. Construction began in 1344, finished in 1929—nearly six centuries of patience. Inside, the gap between Gothic bays and baroque choir tells the whole story. Closed Sunday mornings during services; enter through the crypt then.

⏱ 60 min
3

The smell of the past clings to the Golden Lane (Zlatá ulička) inside Prague Castle: 16th-century guard houses shrunk to two meters wide, painted candy colors. Franz Kafka rented house number 22 for a few months in 1916. Free after 5 PM; before that, count on the castle combination ticket.

⏱ 45 min
4

Cross Charles Bridge (Karlův most) early morning, before 8 AM, to have it almost to yourself. 516 meters long, lined with 30 baroque statues, built from 1357 under Charles IV. Evening, same bridge, castle lights behind—entirely different mood. Street musicians set up shop.

⏱ 45 min
5

Locals just call it 'the clock,' but the Orloj on Old Town Square (Staroměstské náměstí) dates from 1410 and combines calendar, zodiac and apostle procession in a single mechanism. Watch it chime the hour from ground level, not from the crowd packed on the balcony. The Church of Our Lady before Týn across the square deserves five extra minutes.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you only have time for one walk in Malá Strana, follow the river on the left bank down to Kampa Island, then climb back toward the baroque St. Nicholas Church. The quarter stays quiet in the morning and fills up at noon. Figure 90 minutes on foot without rushing.

⏱ 90 min

Take tram 22 to Pohořelec to reach Strahov Monastery from the top of the castle district. The baroque library can be visited (separate ticket, ~150 CZK) and the view over the rooftops from the esplanade in front of the monastery ranks among Prague's best.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 1h

Kutna Hora

★★★★☆
1

Kitsch at first glance, the chandelier in Sedlec Ossuary (Kostnice Sedlec) at Kutná Hora is actually made from every bone in the human body—and the Schwarzenberg coat of arms includes a Turk's eye being pecked by a raven. Some 40,000 skeletal remains decorate the chapel since wood sculptor František Rint arranged them in 1870. Open year-round except December 24.

⏱ 45 min
2

The vaults soar like clasped hands: St. Barbara's Cathedral (Chrám svaté Barbory) in Kutná Hora, dedicated to the patron saint of miners, began construction in 1388. Flamboyant Gothic style, medieval frescoes, baroque altars—Bohemia's wealthiest silver town spared no expense. UNESCO-listed along with the historic center.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Italian Court (Vlašský dvůr) gets underestimated by visitors rushing to the ossuary. Yet this is where goldsmiths struck the famous Bohemian groschen that circulated across medieval Europe. The building contains the royal chapel and throne room. Combination ticket recommended with the Silver Museum.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Descend into the Hrádek museum to visit a real medieval silver mine: you put on the jacket and helmet, then head into 14th-century galleries beneath the town. The guided tour lasts about 50 minutes. Booking recommended in July-August.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 85 km · 1h20

Melnik

★★★☆☆
1

Twenty-three Czech queens lived at Mělník Castle (Zámek Mělník), property of the Lobkowicz family since 1739. From the terrace overlooking the confluence of the Vltava and the Elbe, the panorama across vineyards, Říp Hill and the central highlands ranks among central Bohemia's finest. Inside, a rare collection of 17th-century European maps and Czech baroque paintings signed by Škréta and Brandl.

⏱ 60 min
2

The crypt of St. Peter and Paul Church smells of cold stone and holds one of the Czech Republic's largest ossuaries, with remains of some 15,000 people. The church itself, mainly Gothic, towers over the town and stands out from afar with its two asymmetrical towers. Accessible at the same time as the castle.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb down into the 14th-century cellars if you haven't yet tasted Ludmila wine, the local red aged on the hillside slopes since the time of Princess Ludmila. The tasting takes place in the castle cellars, constant temperature at 10°C. Short but memorable.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 70 km · 1h10

Turnov

★★★☆☆
1

Hrubá Skála: a castle perched on sandstone, as if placed there by mistake. The 14th-century fortress now serves as a hotel, but the courtyard and tower can be visited. From the lookout, the twin horns of ruined Trosky Castle dominate the horizon—the emblematic image of Bohemian Paradise (Český ráj). Bring hiking shoes.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Set off on foot into the Podtrosecká údolí, the valleys below Trosky, to follow eight ponds surrounded by sandstone rocks. The nature reserve created in 1999 is crossed by marked trails suitable for all levels. Count on 2 hours for a complete circuit.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 6 km · 10min

Jicin

★★☆☆☆
1

Among the towers of Prachov Rocks (Prachovské skály), trails weave through Mesozoic sandstone formations classified as a nature reserve since 1933. The short circuit lasts 45 minutes and passes through a rock passage 35 cm wide—tricky with a large backpack. Seven lookouts dot the long circuit.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

After the rocks, Jičín deserves 30 minutes: Valdštejnovo náměstí square is surrounded by arcades and dominated by Valdice Tower, which you can climb for a view of the surroundings. The town was the capital of Albrecht von Wallenstein's domain in the 17th century.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Troskovice

★★★★☆
1

Czechs call them 'the lady and the knight' for their two isolated towers: the ruins of Trosky Castle (Hrad Trosky), founded in the early 15th century, offer the widest panorama across Bohemian Paradise. The climb takes 20 minutes from the parking lot. Check seasonal opening times before you go.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

The Podtrosecká údolí (valleys below Trosky) are just a stone's throw away: trails between ponds and sandstone rocks, accessible on foot from the castle parking lot. A 30-minute loop is enough to taste the forested atmosphere of Český ráj between the day's two visits.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 65 km · 1h15

Adrspach

★★★★☆
1

The sandstone towers of Adršpach (Adršpašské skály) reach up to 100 meters and form a 'stone city' carved by erosion since the Mesozoic era. The gorges sometimes narrow to barely 20 cm. Timed entry ticket in high season—book online the day before so you don't end up waiting at the gate.

⏱ 120 min
2

Pískovna Lake was born from a flooded quarry, not from nature—which makes its improbably green waters even more beautiful. A marked trail circles it with several lookouts. Swimming forbidden, scenery compensates.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Book your entry slot the day before in high season: parking costs 100 CZK for 4 hours and tickets are timed, no queue if you arrive before 9 AM. No food service inside the park—bring water and a picnic.

⏱ 15 min

The rowboat through the gorges is worth the 200 CZK asked: the boatman points out rock formations, including the famous 'Lovers' twin rocks,' and releases a mini-waterfall on request. Circuit of about 20 minutes from the embarkation point at the back of Adršpach rocks.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 60 km · 1h15

Spindleruv Mlyn

★★☆☆☆
1

At 1,603 meters, Sněžka touches Poland on one side and offers the only 360° panorama across the Giant Mountains (Krkonoše). The Czech Republic's highest summit, accessible from Špindlerův Mlýn by several marked trails including the ascent via Labský důl valley. The cable car from Pec pod Sněžkou remains the fastest option.

⏱ 240 min
2

The Medvědín cable car lifts you mid-slope in minutes from Špindlerův Mlýn, ideal starting point for hikes along the main ridge. View over the Elbe source from the plateau, and access to trails toward Sněžka if you're aiming for the summit. In winter, slopes are groomed into ski runs.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Strap on crampons in autumn: the ridge trail can ice over as early as October. In summer, the hike from Špindlerův Mlýn to Luční bouda takes about 3 hours round trip and follows the Bílé Labe river with several waterfalls. Bring a windbreaker even in July.

⏱ 180 min

The Beer Route connects five breweries over 30 km through the Krkonoše, including Friesovy boudy at 1,200 m altitude. Each stop: a pint, a stamp on the map. Itinerary available from Špindlerův Mlýn tourist office.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 13 km · 20min

Pec Pod Snezkou

★★★☆☆
1

From Pec pod Sněžkou, the cable car climbs straight to the Sněžka summit without effort—but the hike on foot via Obří důl valley, a former iron mine, takes 2.5 hours and offers the most dramatic views. At the 1,603 m summit, the Czech-Polish border runs underfoot and wind can blow hard even in summer. Polish chapel on one side, Czech chapel on the other—the summit is shared.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

If weather turns, the fallback option is Modrý důl valley: marked forest trail starting from Pec pod Sněžkou, flat and sheltered, ideal for observing the subalpine flora of the Krkonoše. Count on 1 hour round trip to the valley hut.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 57 km · 1h05

Liberec

★★★★★
1

You'd think it a space capsule stranded on a mountain: Ještěd Tower (1,012 m) near Liberec is actually a 94-meter reinforced concrete hyperboloid designed by Karel Hubáček between 1966 and 1973, which earned its creator the Prix Auguste Perret for architecture. Restaurant, hotel and panoramic lookout over Bohemia, Poland and Germany. Funicular from Horní Hanychov.

⏱ 90 min
2

Liberec Town Hall, completed in late 19th century neo-Renaissance style often compared to its Viennese counterpart, dominates the main square. Nearby, the 17th-century half-timbered Wallenstein houses form a striking contrast with the Radnice. Free exterior visit, museum inside.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Take tram 3 to Horní Hanychov, then board the funicular up to Ještěd: 4 minutes of ascent, 400 m of elevation spared. In summer, several hiking trails descend toward the Jizerské hory Mountains from the summit. Round trip funicular: about 149 CZK.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 1h30

Hrensko

★★★☆☆
1

Hřensko, at the bottom of the gorge, is better than its reputation as a tourist village: it's the Czech Republic's lowest village, at the confluence of the Kamenice and Elbe rivers, ringed by sandstone cliffs and pine forests. Ideal starting point for all excursions in Bohemian Switzerland National Park (České Švýcarsko).

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The boatman speaks, the sandstone walls echo back: the Edmund Gorge in Kamenice canyon is crossed in a guided rowboat. The narrowest sections barely let the boat through, mosses and ferns brush your shoulders. Figure around 80 CZK. Departure from Mezní Louka.

⏱ 60 min

Set off on the Gabriel trail from Mezní Louka to reach Pravčická brána via the heights: 2.5 km marked through the forest, with openings onto the sandstone landscape. Loop circuit possible via the red path on return, total about 7 km.

⏱ 150 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 4 km · 8min

Hrensko

★★★☆☆
1

26.5 meters span, 16 meters high: Pravčická brána is Europe's largest natural sandstone arch, classified as a national natural monument. Narnia scenes were filmed here. Climbing onto it is forbidden—erosion is already at work. From Hřensko, count on 4 km on foot via the red trail or drive to Mezní Louka.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Hike from Hřensko to Pravčická brána via the red-marked trail (about 3 km, 90 min ascent). Then descend into the Kamenice gorges for the crossing by flat-bottomed boat, guided by a boatman with a pole on translucent waters between 150-meter walls.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 16 km · 22min

Decin

★★☆☆☆
1

Chopin composed his Waltz of Děčín at Děčín Castle (Děčínský zámek) in 1835, during a stay with the Thun-Hohenstein family who owned the place from 1628 to 1932. Perched on a 51-meter rocky spur at the confluence of the Elbe and Ploučnice, the castle served as military barracks throughout the 20th century before complete restoration from 1991. Guided tour, exhibitions and baroque rose garden from May to October.

⏱ 75 min
2

The baroque rose garden on the north terrace of Děčín castle is an unexpected pleasure: gloriette, sala terrena and plunging view over the town and the Elbe. The only chain bridge preserved in place in the Czech Republic sits in the castle park, a technical detail few visitors notice.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If the Elbe level is low, look for the Hladový kámen ('Hunger Stone') on the bank: a rock carved with dates since the 15th century, hydrological marker of central Europe's historic droughts. It only appears when waters run low. The riverside walk from the castle takes 30 minutes.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 45 km · 45min

Litomerice

★★★☆☆
1

Mírové náměstí square in Litoměřice is one of northern Bohemia's finest: arcaded medieval houses painted in light colors, baroque town hall, St. Wenceslas Church on the corner. The entire historic center is classified as a historic monument and includes 44 streets. Twenty minutes on foot is enough to grasp its architectural coherence.

⏱ 30 min
2

The underground galleries of Litoměřice run for 366 meters beneath the old town, among Bohemia's most extensive. The route passes through a lapidary museum and ethnographic museum dug into Gothic cellars. The tasting in the town hall cellar extends the charm.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb up to Litoměřice castle for a wine tasting on traditional Czech grape varieties: the 'Journey through Wine' exhibition mixes wine history and tasting in 14th-century vaulted cellars. One of the rare wine regions of northern Bohemia.

⏱ 45 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min

Terezin

★★★★☆
1

More than 150,000 people passed through Terezín, including 15,000 children; about 33,000 died on site, the rest deported to extermination camps. The Small Fortress (Malá pevnost), Gestapo prison from 1940, can be visited on a guided tour of about 75 minutes: cells, execution courtyard, tunnels. Adult ticket ~310 CZK, including all sites.

⏱ 90 min
2

Survivors themselves helped build the exhibitions at the Ghetto Museum (Muzeum ghetta) in Terezín. Children's drawings are displayed there—some 4,500 works produced between 1942 and 1944 under the direction of painter Friedl Dicker-Brandeis, found in two suitcases after the war.

⏱ 60 min
3

If you still have time, the crematorium can be reserved by email from the Memorial (manager@pamatnik-terezin.cz)—it's 7 km from the main site, Litoměřice side, and closed on Saturdays. The national cemetery and memorial on the banks of the Ohře are freely accessible.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Allow at least half a day: combination tickets include the Small Fortress, the Ghetto Museum and the cemetery. A guided tour (available from Prague, about 5 hours total) adds valuable context. The memorial is open every day except December 24, 25 and 26.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 100 km · 1h30

Karlovy Vary

★★★★☆
1

72°C at the outlet, 12 meters high: what gushes beneath the Hot Spring Colonnade (Vřídelní kolonáda) in Karlovy Vary isn't a decorative fountain but the Vřídlo thermal spring, the only one among the city's twelve main springs used for baths. The glass and concrete building dates from 1975. Water comes out at five different temperatures from tasting fountains inside.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Mill Colonnade (Mlýnská kolonáda), with its 124 Corinthian columns, is the most imposing of Karlovy Vary's five colonnades. Designed by Josef Zítek, architect of Prague's National Theatre, it houses several springs. Stroll along the Teplá river between pastel facades of Belle Époque hotels.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Market Colonnade (Tržní kolonáda) smells of warm wood and minerals: this carved wood structure in Swiss style houses three springs including the one named after Charles IV. Designed by Viennese architects Fellner and Helmer in the late 19th century, it contrasts with the brutalist glass of the Vřídelní.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Buy a small Bohemian porcelain cup and taste the springs in order: rising temperature recommended, from Karla IV (lukewarm) to Vřídlo (scalding). Each spring supposedly cures a different organ—the digestive system appreciates, taste buds less so. Souvenir teapot-cups are sold everywhere in town.

⏱ 60 min

The Diana cable car climbs to the spa forests (Lázeňské lesy) above the town. From the observation tower at the summit, view over the entire Teplá valley and colonnades below. Downhill, Becherovka reveals itself at the herbal liqueur museum whose recipe remains secret.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 15 km · 20min

Loket

★★★☆☆
1

A prison until 1949, Loket Castle (Hrad Loket) has managed to make people forget its cells behind its porcelain collections, medieval frescoes and weapons. Founded in the late 12th century for the Přemyslid kings, this Romanesque castle—square tower, rotunda and bastions—overlooks a meander of the Ohře that surrounds the rock on three sides. Charles IV was imprisoned here as a child by his own father.

⏱ 75 min
2

Loket's main square is surrounded by historic burgher houses with a baroque Holy Trinity column at its center. The village itself is classified as an urban reserve. 'Loket' means 'elbow' in Czech: look at the Ohře meander from the castle to immediately understand the name.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

From the lookout at Loket castle, the Ohře serpentines below in an almost circular meander, colored houses tier up on the bank. A short descent to the medieval bridge and a walk around the rock takes 30 minutes—and the castle silhouette above is worth as much as the interior.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 30 km · 30min

Marianske Lazne

★★★★☆
1

Goethe came for the cure, Chopin played piano, Edward VII took the waters—the main colonnade of Mariánské Lázně (119 m long, in neo-baroque cast iron from 1889) has received distinguished guests. The ceiling is decorated with a fresco cycle by Josef Vyleťal on the theme of mankind's desire to fly, added during the 1973-1981 restoration. Three springs inside, gently sloping garden behind.

⏱ 45 min
2

The Singing Fountain (Zpívající fontána) of Mariánské Lázně, inaugurated in 1986, plays Mozart or Morricone every odd hour from May to October and lights up in the evening. The program is posted on a sign nearby. No ticket, no reservation—just come on the hour.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Fill your glass at the Cross Spring (Křížový pramen), the resort's first spring, in its own whitewashed pavilion steps from the colonnade. It's open 6 AM to 6 PM. Bring a bottle or buy a souvenir mug on site—springs are free.

⏱ 20 min

The Slavkov Forest (Slavkovský les), ten minutes from the center, changes registers completely: educational trails between pines and birches, geological park with rocks displayed along the path. An hour's walk is enough to escape the spa atmosphere and breathe.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 165 km · 2h10

Prague

★★★★★
1

Climb Vyšehrad hill for a final unobstructed view over Prague and the Vltava below. The medieval fortress less known than Hradčany houses a national cemetery where Smetana and Dvořák rest. Quiet, often empty of tourists, free except the basilica.

⏱ 60 min
2

Old Town Square (Staroměstské náměstí) on your return is the same as on departure—and yet it feels different. Grab a coffee on a terrace and watch the clock chime one last hour. This city deserves to be earned twice.

⏱ 30 min
