Sao Paulo

★★★★☆
1

MASP (São Paulo Museum of Art) hovers above Avenida Paulista on four bold red pillars. Lina Bo Bardi designed it in 1968 so the space beneath the building would remain open to the public. Inside: Van Gogh, Rembrandt, Picasso, Monet and a strong Brazilian art collection.

⏱ 120 min
2

Ibirapuera Park, designed by Oscar Niemeyer and Roberto Burle Marx for São Paulo's 400th anniversary in 1954, stretches across 1.6 km². Lakes, cycling paths, the Ibirapuera Auditorium, the Afro Brasil Museum and the Museum of Modern Art all share this green oasis.

⏱ 180 min
3

The Mercado Municipal (Mercadão), a covered market hall from 1933, features stunning mosaic stained-glass windows. Spices, tropical fruits, smoked meats, cheeses and the legendary mortadella sandwich — the city's signature street food — all under one roof.

⏱ 90 min
4

Liberdade is São Paulo's Japanese quarter, home to the largest Japanese community outside Japan. Red lanterns line the streets, specialty grocery stores stock every ingredient imaginable, and the Sunday market fills the neighborhood with the scent of yakisoba and gyoza.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

On Sundays, Avenida Paulista closes to traffic and becomes a giant open-air festival. Under MASP, an antiques market sets up; across the road, the Bixiga artisan market offers street food and live music all afternoon.

⏱ 180 min

Street art walk in Vila Madalena: Beco do Batman is a narrow alley covered floor-to-ceiling with spectacular murals. Independent galleries, designer shops and trendy bars fill the surrounding blocks.

⏱ 150 min

Dinner at a neighborhood churrascaria: all-you-can-eat grilled meats served rodízio-style, caipirinhas and live Brazilian music. The Jardins and Itaim districts have the best picks.

⏱ 120 min

Head up to Terraço Itália on the 41st floor of Edifício Itália to watch São Paulo stretch endlessly into the horizon at sunset.

⏱ 60 min

Curitiba

★★★★☆
1

Curitiba's Botanical Garden, opened in 1991, fans out in geometric French-style beds in front of an Art Nouveau greenhouse of glass and steel inspired by London's Crystal Palace. The scientific herbarium holds over 330,000 specimens.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Oscar Niemeyer Museum, nicknamed 'The Eye' for its giant eye-shaped tower, houses rotating exhibitions of Brazilian and international contemporary art. The sprawling sculpture garden surrounding it is a stroll in itself.

⏱ 120 min
3

Largo da Ordem, Curitiba's colonial heart dating back to the 17th century, lines up colorful townhouses, the Igreja da Ordem Terceira de São Francisco (1737) and the Curitiba Memorial. On Sundays, the Feira de Artesanato fills the cobblestones with hundreds of artisan stalls.

⏱ 90 min
4

Praça Tiradentes, birthplace of the city founded in 1693, is dominated by the Catedral Basílica de Nossa Senhora da Luz dos Pinhais, built in 1893. A stone obelisk on the square commemorates the city's founding under the Portuguese Crown.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Parque Tanguá, a former quarry converted in 1996, features a 65-meter artificial waterfall cascading down a sandstone cliff. Climb to the viewpoint for Curitiba's finest sunset: the city spreads out below all the way to the Serra do Mar.

⏱ 90 min

The Ópera de Arame, a theater with a tubular steel frame and transparent polycarbonate roof, floats above a lake in a former quarry. A walkway over the mirror-like water leads to the entrance. Jazz and MPB concerts echo from the floating stage.

⏱ 60 min

Try barreado, the signature dish of the Paraná coast: meat slow-cooked for hours in a sealed clay pot, served with manioc flour and banana. Restaurants on Largo da Ordem also serve Italian and Polish specialties inherited from waves of immigration.

⏱ 75 min

Florianopolis

★★★★☆
1

The Mercado Público of Florianópolis, a market hall from 1899 in the heart of the historic center, overflows with fresh fish, seafood, spices and artisanal products. The counters serve locally farmed oysters with a squeeze of lime.

⏱ 75 min
2

Lagoa da Conceição, a 20 km² lagoon ringed by lush green hills, is the bohemian soul of the island. Settled by Azorean colonists in 1750, it still has the Igreja de Nossa Senhora da Conceição and a small center of lanes lined with bars and galleries.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Joaquina Dunes, a vast sweep of sand between the lagoon and the ocean, are the birthplace of Brazilian sandboarding. From the top, you can see the surf of Joaquina on one side and the calm waters of the Lagoa on the other.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Ponte Hercílio Luz, a 340-meter suspension bridge from 1926 — the longest in Brazil — offers sweeping views of the bay and the city center. Long closed to traffic, it has been restored as a pedestrian landmark and symbol of Florianópolis.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Swimming and surfing at Praia de Joaquina, one of southern Brazil's most famous breaks. Powerful waves draw international competitions. For calmer water, Praia de Campeche offers white sand and natural tide pools at low tide.

⏱ 120 min

Hike to Lagoinha do Leste, a secluded beach only accessible on foot from Praia do Matadeiro. The 3 km trail winds through Atlantic Forest and opens onto a crescent of sand framed by cliffs — one of the wildest landscapes on the island.

⏱ 180 min

In the evening, grab a table at a terrace restaurant in Barra da Lagoa for a sequência de camarão — a succession of shrimp dishes: grilled, breaded, in coconut sauce and moqueca. Fishing boats pulled up on the sand complete the scene.

⏱ 90 min

Foz Do Iguacu

★★★★☆
1

The Itaipu Dam, built from 1975 to 1984 on the Paraná River between Brazil and Paraguay, is one of the largest hydroelectric plants in the world. Its 20 turbines generate 15% of Brazil's electricity and 90% of Paraguay's. The panoramic tour reveals the colossal scale of the structure.

⏱ 90 min
2

Iguaçu Falls from the Brazilian side: 275 cascades spread across 2.7 km. A 1,200-meter walkway of catwalks delivers a sweeping panoramic view of the falls — 80% of which are on Argentine territory. UNESCO World Heritage since 1986.

⏱ 180 min
3

Parque das Aves, just 100 meters from the national park entrance, is home to 1,300 birds of 130 species across 16 hectares of Atlantic Forest: toucans, parrots, cassowaries and macaws. Walk-through aviaries bring you remarkably close.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Arrive at the national park on the Brazilian side at dawn, right when the gates open at 7am, before the tour groups flood in. The first rays of sunlight strike the cascades and paint rainbows in the spray.

⏱ 120 min

At the Marco Três Fronteiras, where Argentina, Brazil and Paraguay meet at the confluence of the rivers, three obelisks in national colors face each other across the Paraná. The best spot for sunset.

⏱ 60 min

Bonito

★★★★☆
1

The Rio Sucuri winds between dense forest banks. Its spring-fed water is so transparent you can see every detail on the riverbed: emerald-green algae, giant snails and piraputanga fish with their bright red fins. The snorkeling float takes about 40 minutes.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Gruta do Lago Azul, a natural heritage site, descends 90 meters underground via a stairway carved into the rock. At the bottom, an intensely blue lake fills the main chamber. In December and January, a shaft of sunlight enters the cave mouth and lights up the water with an otherworldly glow.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Aquário Natural starts with a trail through cerrado forest before opening onto a spring-fed pool where hundreds of fish glide among aquatic plants and submerged tree trunks. The crystal-clear water earns this place its name.

⏱ 120 min
4

The Abismo Anhumas is a 72-meter-deep sinkhole accessible only by rappelling down. At the bottom, a royal-blue underground lake shelters giant stalagmites up to 19 meters tall. Scuba diving or snorkeling inside this limestone cathedral.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Hiking and waterfalls at Parque das Cachoeiras or Boca da Onça: marked trails follow the Salobra River through the forest, linking several waterfalls — some plunging 150 meters. Swim in the natural pools between each cascade.

⏱ 240 min

Wind down with a swim at the Balneário Municipal de Bonito, a natural pool fed by the Rio Formoso. Local families gather here for weekend barbecues.

⏱ 60 min

1

Pirenópolis, founded in 1727 by gold prospectors, is one of the oldest towns in Goiás. 3

Activities

⏱ 180 min

Stroll along Rua do Lazer, the pedestrian street that comes alive in the evening. Restaurants serve galinhada goiana (chicken and rice with pequi), pamonha (corn paste steamed in its husk) and empada goiana, washed down with artisanal cachaça from the area.

⏱ 90 min

Cool off at Cachoeira do Coqueiro or the Vargem Grande reserve, where the Santa Maria and Lázaro waterfalls tumble through regenerating cerrado. The cold natural pools offer welcome relief from the plateau heat.

Ouro Preto

★★★★☆
1

Praça Tiradentes, the heart of Ouro Preto, is dominated by the Museu da Inconfidência, housed in the former Town Hall and Prison of 1784. It tells the story of the 1789 independence movement led by Tiradentes and holds the remains of the conspirators in its underground Pantheon.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Igreja de São Francisco de Assis, the absolute masterpiece of Aleijadinho — the mixed-race sculptor afflicted by leprosy — dazzles with its curving rococo facade, soapstone reliefs and a ceiling painted by Mestre Ataíde showing a mulatto Virgin surrounded by angel musicians.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora do Pilar holds the most opulent interior in colonial Brazil: over 400 kilos of gold leaf cover the altars, columns and balconies. Red velvet curtains on the tribunes create a striking theatrical effect.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Mina da Passagem, between Ouro Preto and Mariana, is the largest gold mine open to visitors in Brazil. A trolley descends 120 meters underground into 18th-century galleries. At the bottom, a translucent underground lake even allows swimming.

⏱ 120 min
5

The Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário dos Pretos, built by and for enslaved Africans, stands out with its unique rounded shape and curves that break from conventional baroque.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Lose yourself in the steep cobblestone lanes of Ouro Preto. Every turn reveals a new perspective: twin bell towers framed by hills, colonial facades with wrought-iron balconies, panoramas of red-tile rooftops.

⏱ 120 min

Dinner of comida mineira in a downtown restaurant: frango com quiabo (chicken with okra), feijão tropeiro (beans with manioc flour), tutu de feijão and pão de queijo, all paired with artisanal cachaça from Minas.

⏱ 90 min

Rio's Jardim Botânico, founded in 1808 by Dom João VI, spreads across 54 hectares at the foot of Corcovado. Its imperial avenue of royal palms, planted in 1842, is one of the most famous in Brazil. Orchids, bromeliads, Victoria Amazonica water lilies and a butterfly greenhouse.

⏱ 90 min
2

Ipanema and Leblon, Rio's two chicest beaches, stretch 4 km along the Atlantic. Numbered kiosks (postos) structure beach life: each posto has its own vibe, its crowd, and its vendors selling mate and açaí.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Museu Nacional de Belas Artes, founded in 1937 in a neoclassical palace downtown, holds the largest collection of 19th-century Brazilian painting as well as European works brought over by the royal court in 1808.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Saturday feijoada lunch in Santa Teresa or the Lapa district: a slow-cooked stew of black beans and smoked meats, caipirinhas and live samba. The quintessential carioca weekend ritual.

⏱ 120 min

Ipanema Hippie Fair (Praça General Osório) on Sunday morning: crafts, jewelry, paintings and semi-precious stones. The largest street market in Rio.

⏱ 120 min

Hike Dois Irmãos from the Vidigal favela: 45 minutes up to the twin peaks that tower over Ipanema. A 360° view of the coastline, the lagoons, Christ the Redeemer and the bay. The trailhead is at the top of Vidigal, reached by communal moto-taxi.

⏱ 120 min
