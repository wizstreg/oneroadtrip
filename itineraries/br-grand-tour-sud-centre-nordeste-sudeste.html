⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 37 and 41 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 What to discover in BR?

Carnival energy, Amazon rainforest, and pristine beaches create the ultimate South American adventure. Experience vibrant cities, incredible biodiversity, and passionate culture from Rio's beaches to Pantanal wetlands. Samba music, capoeira martial arts, and incredible football culture showcase national spirit. Brazil offers unparalleled natural wonders with infectious cultural energy.

Stop 1

Sao Paulo

Where 12 million stories collide in a feast of food, art and concrete

★★★★☆
📍 MASP Museum Art Avenida Paulista São Paulo📍 Ibirapuera Park São Paulo📍 Mercado Municipal Mercadão São Paulo📍 Liberdade Japanese quarter São Paulo📍 Vila Madalena street art São Paulo📍 Historic center Praça da Sé São Paulo📍 Edifício Itália Terraço Itália São Paulo
1

MASP (São Paulo Museum of Art) hovers above Avenida Paulista on four bold red pillars. Lina Bo Bardi designed it in 1968 so the space beneath the building would remain open to the public. Inside: Van Gogh, Rembrandt, Picasso, Monet and a strong Brazilian art collection.

⏱ 120 min
2

Ibirapuera Park, designed by Oscar Niemeyer and Roberto Burle Marx for São Paulo's 400th anniversary in 1954, stretches across 1.6 km². Lakes, cycling paths, the Ibirapuera Auditorium, the Afro Brasil Museum and the Museum of Modern Art all share this green oasis.

⏱ 180 min
3

The Mercado Municipal (Mercadão), a covered market hall from 1933, features stunning mosaic stained-glass windows. Spices, tropical fruits, smoked meats, cheeses and the legendary mortadella sandwich — the city's signature street food — all under one roof.

⏱ 90 min
4

Liberdade is São Paulo's Japanese quarter, home to the largest Japanese community outside Japan. Red lanterns line the streets, specialty grocery stores stock every ingredient imaginable, and the Sunday market fills the neighborhood with the scent of yakisoba and gyoza.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

On Sundays, Avenida Paulista closes to traffic and becomes a giant open-air festival. Under MASP, an antiques market sets up; across the road, the Bixiga artisan market offers street food and live music all afternoon.

⏱ 180 min

Street art walk in Vila Madalena: Beco do Batman is a narrow alley covered floor-to-ceiling with spectacular murals. Independent galleries, designer shops and trendy bars fill the surrounding blocks.

⏱ 150 min

Dinner at a neighborhood churrascaria: all-you-can-eat grilled meats served rodízio-style, caipirinhas and live Brazilian music. The Jardins and Itaim districts have the best picks.

⏱ 120 min

Head up to Terraço Itália on the 41st floor of Edifício Itália to watch São Paulo stretch endlessly into the horizon at sunset.

⏱ 60 min

Curitiba

A city that turned quarries into opera houses and gardens into masterpieces

★★★★☆
📍 Botanical Garden greenhouse Curitiba📍 Oscar Niemeyer Museum MON Curitiba📍 Largo da Ordem historic center Curitiba📍 Parque Tanguá waterfall viewpoint Curitiba📍 Ópera de Arame Curitiba📍 Praça Tiradentes cathedral Curitiba📍 Rua XV Novembro pedestrian Curitiba
1

Curitiba's Botanical Garden, opened in 1991, fans out in geometric French-style beds in front of an Art Nouveau greenhouse of glass and steel inspired by London's Crystal Palace. The scientific herbarium holds over 330,000 specimens.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Oscar Niemeyer Museum, nicknamed 'The Eye' for its giant eye-shaped tower, houses rotating exhibitions of Brazilian and international contemporary art. The sprawling sculpture garden surrounding it is a stroll in itself.

⏱ 120 min
3

Largo da Ordem, Curitiba's colonial heart dating back to the 17th century, lines up colorful townhouses, the Igreja da Ordem Terceira de São Francisco (1737) and the Curitiba Memorial. On Sundays, the Feira de Artesanato fills the cobblestones with hundreds of artisan stalls.

⏱ 90 min
4

Praça Tiradentes, birthplace of the city founded in 1693, is dominated by the Catedral Basílica de Nossa Senhora da Luz dos Pinhais, built in 1893. A stone obelisk on the square commemorates the city's founding under the Portuguese Crown.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Parque Tanguá, a former quarry converted in 1996, features a 65-meter artificial waterfall cascading down a sandstone cliff. Climb to the viewpoint for Curitiba's finest sunset: the city spreads out below all the way to the Serra do Mar.

⏱ 90 min

The Ópera de Arame, a theater with a tubular steel frame and transparent polycarbonate roof, floats above a lake in a former quarry. A walkway over the mirror-like water leads to the entrance. Jazz and MPB concerts echo from the floating stage.

⏱ 60 min

Try barreado, the signature dish of the Paraná coast: meat slow-cooked for hours in a sealed clay pot, served with manioc flour and banana. Restaurants on Largo da Ordem also serve Italian and Polish specialties inherited from waves of immigration.

⏱ 75 min

Stop 3

Florianopolis

An island of 42 beaches strung between Atlantic surf and tranquil lagoons

★★★★☆
📍 Lagoa da Conceição Florianópolis📍 Joaquina Dunes sandboard Florianópolis📍 Mercado Público center Florianópolis📍 Ponte Hercílio Luz Florianópolis📍 Praia Joaquina surf Florianópolis📍 Praia Campeche island Florianópolis📍 Barra da Lagoa Florianópolis
1

The Mercado Público of Florianópolis, a market hall from 1899 in the heart of the historic center, overflows with fresh fish, seafood, spices and artisanal products. The counters serve locally farmed oysters with a squeeze of lime.

⏱ 75 min
2

Lagoa da Conceição, a 20 km² lagoon ringed by lush green hills, is the bohemian soul of the island. Settled by Azorean colonists in 1750, it still has the Igreja de Nossa Senhora da Conceição and a small center of lanes lined with bars and galleries.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Joaquina Dunes, a vast sweep of sand between the lagoon and the ocean, are the birthplace of Brazilian sandboarding. From the top, you can see the surf of Joaquina on one side and the calm waters of the Lagoa on the other.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Ponte Hercílio Luz, a 340-meter suspension bridge from 1926 — the longest in Brazil — offers sweeping views of the bay and the city center. Long closed to traffic, it has been restored as a pedestrian landmark and symbol of Florianópolis.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Swimming and surfing at Praia de Joaquina, one of southern Brazil's most famous breaks. Powerful waves draw international competitions. For calmer water, Praia de Campeche offers white sand and natural tide pools at low tide.

⏱ 120 min

Hike to Lagoinha do Leste, a secluded beach only accessible on foot from Praia do Matadeiro. The 3 km trail winds through Atlantic Forest and opens onto a crescent of sand framed by cliffs — one of the wildest landscapes on the island.

⏱ 180 min

In the evening, grab a table at a terrace restaurant in Barra da Lagoa for a sequência de camarão — a succession of shrimp dishes: grilled, breaded, in coconut sauce and moqueca. Fishing boats pulled up on the sand complete the scene.

⏱ 90 min

Foz Do Iguacu

Where 275 waterfalls crash into a single roaring curtain of mist

★★★★☆
📍 Iguazu Falls Brazilian side walkways📍 Garganta del Diablo Iguazu Argentine side📍 Parque das Aves Foz do Iguaçu📍 Itaipu Dam Foz do Iguaçu📍 Iguaçu National Park Cataratas trail📍 Triple border Puerto Iguazu📍 Macuco Safari boat falls
1

The Itaipu Dam, built from 1975 to 1984 on the Paraná River between Brazil and Paraguay, is one of the largest hydroelectric plants in the world. Its 20 turbines generate 15% of Brazil's electricity and 90% of Paraguay's. The panoramic tour reveals the colossal scale of the structure.

⏱ 90 min
2

Iguaçu Falls from the Brazilian side: 275 cascades spread across 2.7 km. A 1,200-meter walkway of catwalks delivers a sweeping panoramic view of the falls — 80% of which are on Argentine territory. UNESCO World Heritage since 1986.

⏱ 180 min
3

Parque das Aves, just 100 meters from the national park entrance, is home to 1,300 birds of 130 species across 16 hectares of Atlantic Forest: toucans, parrots, cassowaries and macaws. Walk-through aviaries bring you remarkably close.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Arrive at the national park on the Brazilian side at dawn, right when the gates open at 7am, before the tour groups flood in. The first rays of sunlight strike the cascades and paint rainbows in the spray.

⏱ 120 min

At the Marco Três Fronteiras, where Argentina, Brazil and Paraguay meet at the confluence of the rivers, three obelisks in national colors face each other across the Paraná. The best spot for sunset.

⏱ 60 min

On the Argentine side, the upper and lower circuits of Iguazú National Park let you approach the falls both from above and from their base. Curtains of water frame the catwalks on every side.

The Garganta del Diablo (Devil's Throat) is reached via a catwalk above the river. A permanent cloud of mist engulfs the 80-meter-deep chasm — the most intense experience at Iguazú Falls.

Activities

Bonito

Rivers so clear you float above schools of fish as if gravity forgot you

★★★★☆
📍 Rio Sucuri snorkeling Bonito📍 Aquário Natural Bonito📍 Gruta do Lago Azul Bonito📍 Rio da Prata floating Bonito📍 Boca da Onça waterfalls Bonito📍 Abismo Anhumas rappelling Bonito📍 Balneário Municipal Bonito
1

The Rio Sucuri winds between dense forest banks. Its spring-fed water is so transparent you can see every detail on the riverbed: emerald-green algae, giant snails and piraputanga fish with their bright red fins. The snorkeling float takes about 40 minutes.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Gruta do Lago Azul, a natural heritage site, descends 90 meters underground via a stairway carved into the rock. At the bottom, an intensely blue lake fills the main chamber. In December and January, a shaft of sunlight enters the cave mouth and lights up the water with an otherworldly glow.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Aquário Natural starts with a trail through cerrado forest before opening onto a spring-fed pool where hundreds of fish glide among aquatic plants and submerged tree trunks. The crystal-clear water earns this place its name.

⏱ 120 min
4

The Abismo Anhumas is a 72-meter-deep sinkhole accessible only by rappelling down. At the bottom, a royal-blue underground lake shelters giant stalagmites up to 19 meters tall. Scuba diving or snorkeling inside this limestone cathedral.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Hiking and waterfalls at Parque das Cachoeiras or Boca da Onça: marked trails follow the Salobra River through the forest, linking several waterfalls — some plunging 150 meters. Swim in the natural pools between each cascade.

⏱ 240 min

Wind down with a swim at the Balneário Municipal de Bonito, a natural pool fed by the Rio Formoso. Local families gather here for weekend barbecues.

⏱ 60 min

Campo Grande

Gateway to the Pantanal and the rich flavors of central-western Brazil

★★★☆☆
📍 Parque das Nações Indígenas Campo Grande📍 Mercadão Municipal Campo Grande📍 Museu das Culturas Dom Bosco Campo Grande📍 Street 14 de Julho center Campo Grande📍 Parque Estadual Matas do Segredo Campo Grande
1

The Parque das Nações Indígenas, a sprawling 119-hectare green space in the city center, holds a lake, shaded trails and the Museu das Culturas Dom Bosco. Capybaras, herons and toucans populate the banks. The museum houses an outstanding collection of artifacts from the indigenous peoples of Mato Grosso do Sul.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Mercadão Municipal of Campo Grande gathers the flavors of central-western Brazil: Japanese sobá noodles brought by immigrant communities, dried meats from the Pantanal, artisanal cheeses, doce de leite and cerrado fruits.

⏱ 75 min
3

Rua 14 de Julho, the pedestrian artery downtown, is lined with shops, cafés and Art Deco buildings dating from the city's economic boom of the 1930s–1950s. The Morada dos Baís, a 1913 mansion, now hosts exhibitions and concerts.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Museu José Antônio Pereira tells the story of Campo Grande's founding in 1899. The reconstructed wooden house, surrounded by cerrado gardens, recalls the life of the first settlers who came from Minas Gerais.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Detour to the southern Pantanal: from Campo Grande, road MS-262 dips into the wetlands in under an hour. Lookout points along the Estrada Parque let you spot caimans, capybaras, jabiru storks and macaws from the car.

⏱ 180 min

Cuiaba

Red-rock canyons and waterfalls hiding inside the ancient Chapada dos Guimarães

★★★★☆
📍 Chapada dos Guimarães Véu de Noiva Cuiabá📍 Cidade de Pedra Chapada Guimarães📍 Praça Alencastro center Cuiabá📍 Cathedral Senhor Bom Jesus Cuiabá📍 Mirante Geodésico Chapada Guimarães📍 Chapada Guimarães National Park
1

The Cachoeira Véu de Noiva, postcard image of Chapada dos Guimarães, plunges 86 meters from the rim of a red-orange sandstone amphitheater. Scarlet macaws nest in the cracks of the cliff face. The viewpoint, a gentle 550-meter walk from the parking lot, gives a bird's-eye view of the falls.

⏱ 90 min
2

Chapada dos Guimarães National Park protects 33,000 hectares of highland cerrado. The Circuito das Cachoeiras strings together seven waterfalls over 6 km: Sorrisal, Pulo, Prainha and the spectacular Andorinhas, where swallows dive through the curtain of water at dusk.

⏱ 180 min
3

The Mirante Geodésico, the highest point of the Chapada at 845 meters, marks the geodetic center of South America. The view sweeps across sandstone escarpments, cerrado forest and, on a clear day, the plains of Cuiabá on the horizon.

⏱ 45 min
4

The town center of Chapada dos Guimarães revolves around a tree-shaded square and the Igreja de Sant'Ana, founded in 1811 and listed by IPHAN. Shops sell semi-precious stones and cerrado-wood crafts.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Swim in the natural rock pools of Cachoeirinha and Cachoeira dos Namorados, both a short walk from the Véu de Noiva car park. The cool water is a welcome contrast to the cerrado heat.

⏱ 60 min

Catch the sunset from Mirante Alto do Céu, 8 km from the center of the Chapada. The cliffs shift from rust to gold as the sun sinks behind the plateau.

⏱ 45 min

Barra Do Garcas

River beaches of white sand and warm springs at the edge of the cerrado

★★★☆☆
📍 Cristo Rei Araguaia Barra do Garças📍 River beaches Rio Araguaia Barra do Garças📍 Serra Azul viewpoint Barra do Garças📍 Thermas dos Guimarães hot springs📍 Parque Estadual Serra Azul Barra
1

The Mirante do Cristo Rei overlooks Barra do Garças from atop the Serra do Roncador. The Christ statue — a modest cousin of Rio's — commands a 360° panorama of the confluence of the Rio Araguaia and the Rio Garças, the plains and the town below.

2

The Rio Araguaia, one of the great rivers of the cerrado, reveals white sand beaches (praias fluviais) from June to September when the water level drops. These sandbars in the middle of the river become swimming and camping spots.

3

The Parque Estadual da Serra Azul protects a patch of highland cerrado with natural hot springs, hiking trails and natural pools at a constant 38°C.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Swimming and lounging on the river beaches of the Rio Araguaia. During the dry season, local boats shuttle between the sandbars that emerge mid-stream. Warm, gentle freshwater with no strong current.

⏱ 120 min

Sunset on the banks of the Araguaia: the central-western Brazilian sky blazes with color above the river, with silhouettes of buriti palms in the foreground.

Pirenopolis

Colonial cobblestones surrounded by seventy waterfalls in the Goiás cerrado

★★★★☆
📍 Igreja Nossa Senhora Rosário Pirenópolis📍 Cachoeira do Abade Pirenópolis📍 Rua do Lazer Pirenópolis📍 Cavalhadas Museum Pirenópolis📍 Colonial historic center Pirenópolis📍 Parque Estadual Pireneus Pirenópolis📍 Rio das Almas Pirenópolis
1

Pirenópolis, founded in 1727 by gold prospectors, is one of the oldest towns in Goiás. Its cobblestone streets, low colonial houses and three 18th-century churches form a historic center listed as a national heritage site. The Rio das Almas runs through town, its banks lined with restaurants and bars in restored buildings.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário, built in 1732, is the oldest church in Pirenópolis. Destroyed by fire in 2002, it was faithfully reconstructed and now houses the Museu da Matriz, which traces the restoration process and the town's sacred heritage.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Museu das Cavalhadas displays the spectacular masks and embroidered costumes worn during the Cavalhadas de Pirenópolis, a horseback pageant reenacting the battle between Moors and Christians. Held every year at Pentecost since 1826, it is one of Brazil's grandest folk festivals.

⏱ 45 min
4

The Cachoeira do Abade, 16 km from the center along a partly unpaved road, tumbles down a sandstone amphitheater deep in the cerrado forest. A hanging bridge and a panoramic trail let you approach the main cascade and its crystal-clear pools.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Hike in the Parque Estadual dos Pireneus: the trail climbs to Pico dos Pireneus at 1,385 m, the highest point in the region. The view stretches across endless cerrado, reddish hills echoing the mining past and, on a clear day, the plains of Goiânia on the horizon.

⏱ 180 min

Stroll along Rua do Lazer, the pedestrian street that comes alive in the evening. Restaurants serve galinhada goiana (chicken and rice with pequi), pamonha (corn paste steamed in its husk) and empada goiana, washed down with artisanal cachaça from the area.

⏱ 90 min

Cool off at Cachoeira do Coqueiro or the Vargem Grande reserve, where the Santa Maria and Lázaro waterfalls tumble through regenerating cerrado. The cold natural pools offer welcome relief from the plateau heat.

⏱ 120 min

Stop 11

Brasilia

Niemeyer's bold modernist capital, drawn from nothing and crowned by UNESCO

★★★★☆
📍 Esplanade of the Ministries Brasília📍 Metropolitan Cathedral Niemeyer Brasília📍 National Congress Brasília domes📍 Praça dos Três Poderes Brasília📍 Palácio da Alvorada Brasília📍 JK Bridge Brasília Lake Paranoá📍 Museu Nacional Brasília
1

The Esplanade of the Ministries lines up 17 identical buildings designed by Oscar Niemeyer on either side of a 2 km monumental axis. At the far end, the National Congress with its two domes — one inverted, one upright — has become the icon of Brasília.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Metropolitan Cathedral, designed by Niemeyer in 1958, is a concrete hyperboloid whose 16 curved pillars support a ceiling of blue and green stained glass. You enter through a dark underground tunnel before emerging into a flood of light.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Praça dos Três Poderes unites the Palácio do Planalto (presidency), the Supreme Court and the Congress around an austere esplanade. The Panteão da Pátria and the Museu da Cidade complete this ensemble listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987.

⏱ 75 min
4

The Museu Nacional, a white dome 40 meters across planted like a saucer on the esplanade, was Niemeyer's last major work, inaugurated for his 99th birthday. Temporary exhibitions on art and architecture.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

UNESCO architecture loop by car or bicycle: link the Palácio da Alvorada (presidential residence with marble columns), the Dom Bosco Chapel (interior bathed in blue light from 12 tons of stained glass), and the JK Memorial, tomb of the man who built Brasília.

⏱ 180 min

Sunset from the Ponte JK, whose three steel arches span Lake Paranoá. Golden reflections on the water and the silhouette of government buildings make for a spectacular panorama.

Alto Paraiso De Goias

Gateway to a land of cascades, quartzite canyons and billion-year-old rock

★★★★☆
📍 Chapada dos Veadeiros National Park Alto Paraíso📍 Vale da Lua São Jorge Chapada Veadeiros📍 Almécegas Waterfalls Chapada Veadeiros📍 Catarata dos Couros Chapada Veadeiros📍 Vila São Jorge Chapada Veadeiros📍 Mirante da Janela Chapada Veadeiros📍 Saltos Rio Preto 80m 120m Chapada
1

Chapada dos Veadeiros National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2001, protects 240,000 hectares of highland cerrado on a plateau 1.8 billion years old. Quartzite formations, the canyons of the Rio Preto and waterfalls of 80 and 120 meters create a landscape that feels outside of time.

⏱ 300 min
2

The Vale da Lua (Valley of the Moon), 9 km from São Jorge, is a chaos of rocks sculpted by the Rio São Miguel into a lunar landscape. Craters, natural pools and rounded grey quartz formations bear witness to millions of years of erosion.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Almécegas Waterfalls, on Fazenda São Bento near Alto Paraíso, offer two distinct experiences: Almécegas I, a 50-meter cascade with a dizzying viewpoint and a permanent rainbow; Almécegas II, a shorter drop with a deep pool perfect for swimming.

⏱ 120 min
4

The Vila de São Jorge, gateway to the national park, is a village of 800 people with dirt streets, colorful houses, restaurants serving Goiás cuisine (arroz com pequi, empadão goiano) and crafts made from cerrado crystal stones.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike to the Mirante da Janela, a natural rock window framing the view of the Rio Preto waterfalls. The 8 km round-trip trail crosses rugged cerrado with sections of rock scrambling over quartzite slabs.

⏱ 300 min

Swim in the crystal waters of the Catarata dos Couros, a waterfall complex cascading down a canyon with over 300 meters of elevation change. The most spectacular fall plunges 100 meters.

⏱ 180 min

Stargazing from Alto Paraíso. The low light pollution of the Chapada makes it one of Brazil's best spots for astronomy. The quartz-rich soil has given the area a mystical reputation.

⏱ 60 min

Sao Raimundo Nonato

Serra da Capivara National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1991, holds the largest concentration of prehistoric sites in the Americas. Over 1,300 archaeological sites have been recorded, 173 of them open to visitors, preserving 30,000 rock paintings and engravings dating from 6,000 to 25,000 years ago.

⏱ 300 min
2

The Boqueirão da Pedra Furada, often called the 'Sistine Chapel' of Serra da Capivara, is a 60-meter sandstone wall pierced by a natural arch. Its surface is covered in paintings depicting hunting scenes, dances and rituals more than 10,000 years old.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Museum of the American Man in São Raimundo Nonato displays the excavation finds: 10,000-year-old human fossils (including the skeleton known as 'Zuzu'), funerary urns, stone tools and replicas of the rock paintings. Two floors sum up thirty years of research.

⏱ 120 min
4

The Museu da Natureza, a spiral-shaped building nestled in the caatinga landscape, traces the biological history of South America: megafauna fossils (glyptodonts, saber-toothed tigers), reconstructions of vanished ecosystems and interactive panels.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Guided hike into the Baixão das Andorinhas, a 125-meter-deep canyon where thousands of swallows nosedive at dusk. Watching their synchronized flight into the gorge is one of the park's most unforgettable moments.

⏱ 120 min

Visit the sites of Toca do Caldeirão dos Rodrigues and Sítio do Meio, perched above the valley. The paintings show scenes of prehistoric daily life: dancers with arms raised, couples, animals (deer, armadillos, capybaras). Boardwalks let you examine the details up close.

Oeiras, founded in 1712 as Vila da Mocha, served as the first capital of Piauí from 1759 to 1851. Its historic center, listed as a national monument, lines up colonial mansions with pastel facades, the Casa das Sete Janelas (House of Seven Windows) and the former governor's palace built in the 19th century.

The Museu de Arte Sacra of Oeiras preserves over 500 liturgical pieces from the town's three main churches: polychrome statues, silverwork, ex-votos and ornaments from the 18th and 19th centuries. Housed in the former town hall and jail, the building itself is worth the visit.

1

Praça Tiradentes, the heart of Ouro Preto, is dominated by the Museu da Inconfidência, housed in the former Town Hall and Prison of 1784. It tells the story of the 1789 independence movement led by Tiradentes and holds the remains of the conspirators in its underground Pantheon.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Igreja de São Francisco de Assis, the absolute masterpiece of Aleijadinho — the mixed-race sculptor afflicted by leprosy — dazzles with its curving rococo facade, soapstone reliefs and a ceiling painted by Mestre Ataíde showing a mulatto Virgin surrounded by angel musicians.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora do Pilar holds the most opulent interior in colonial Brazil: over 400 kilos of gold leaf cover the altars, columns and balconies. Red velvet curtains on the tribunes create a striking theatrical effect.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Mina da Passagem, between Ouro Preto and Mariana, is the largest gold mine open to visitors in Brazil. A trolley descends 120 meters underground into 18th-century galleries. At the bottom, a translucent underground lake even allows swimming.

⏱ 120 min
5

The Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário dos Pretos, built by and for enslaved Africans, stands out with its unique rounded shape and curves that break from conventional baroque.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Lose yourself in the steep cobblestone lanes of Ouro Preto. Every turn reveals a new perspective: twin bell towers framed by hills, colonial facades with wrought-iron balconies, panoramas of red-tile rooftops.

⏱ 120 min

Dinner of comida mineira in a downtown restaurant: frango com quiabo (chicken with okra), feijão tropeiro (beans with manioc flour), tutu de feijão and pão de queijo, all paired with artisanal cachaça from Minas.

⏱ 90 min

Rio De Janeiro Leme

📍 Christ the Redeemer Corcovado Rio de Janeiro📍 Sugarloaf cable car Rio de Janeiro📍 Copacabana Beach Rio de Janeiro📍 Ipanema Beach Rio de Janeiro📍 Santa Teresa neighborhood Rio de Janeiro📍 Escadaria Selarón Lapa Rio de Janeiro📍 Parque Lage Tijuca Forest Rio de Janeiro
1

Corcovado peaks at 710 m. Christ the Redeemer, unveiled in 1931, stands 28 m tall with arms outstretched. From the summit platform: Copacabana, Ipanema, Guanabara Bay, Sugarloaf and Tijuca Forest spread out in a full 360° sweep.

⏱ 90 min
2

Sugarloaf (396 m) is reached by two cable cars from Praia Vermelha. The view takes in the entire city, and is especially stunning in the late afternoon when golden light floods the bay.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Escadaria Selarón is a stairway of 215 steps covered with over 2,000 ceramic tiles collected from around the world. It links the Lapa neighborhood to Santa Teresa.

⏱ 30 min
4

Santa Teresa is Rio's Montmartre: steep cobblestone lanes, colorful colonial houses and artists' studios.

⏱ 90 min
5

Tijuca Forest covers 33 km² — the largest urban tropical forest in the world. Ocelots, monkeys and 300 bird species. Parque Lage at its entrance combines gardens, waterfalls, a 19th-century mansion and a contemporary art gallery.

⏱ 120 min
6

The Cathedral of São Sebastião, built between 1964 and 1979, is a 96-meter-tall concrete cone 104 meters in diameter. Four giant stained-glass panels run from floor to ceiling.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Early-morning walk along the Copacabana mosaic promenade (4.5 km) to Ipanema: jogging, beach volleyball, surfing. Leave valuables at the hotel.

⏱ 90 min

Hike from Parque Lage to Corcovado: 3 hours through Tijuca's tropical jungle, +700 m elevation gain.

⏱ 180 min

Sunset cruise on Guanabara Bay: Sugarloaf and Corcovado seen from the water.

⏱ 120 min

Head to Arpoador, between Copacabana and Ipanema, 30 minutes before sunset. The cariocas applaud as the sun disappears.

Rio De Janeiro Leme

★★★★☆
📍 Copacabana Beach Rio de Janeiro📍 Ipanema Beach Arpoador Rio de Janeiro📍 Tijuca Forest Parque Lage Rio de Janeiro📍 Museu do Amanhã Praça Mauá waterfront Rio de Janeiro📍 Praça XV Paço Imperial historic center Rio📍 MAC Niterói Oscar Niemeyer Rio de Janeiro📍 São Sebastião Cathedral Rio de Janeiro
1

The Tijuca National Park shelters 33 km² of dense tropical rainforest within the city limits — the world's largest urban jungle. Trails lead to the Mesa do Imperador lookout and the Cascatinha Taunay waterfall, a 30-meter cascade tucked into the canopy. At the park's entrance, Parque Lage pairs a stately 19th-century mansion with lush gardens and a contemporary art school.

⏱ 120 min
2

São Sebastião Cathedral rises in the city center like a massive concrete cone, 96 meters tall and 104 meters across. Inside, four floor-to-ceiling stained-glass panels pour colored light into the cavernous nave, creating an almost otherworldly atmosphere.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Museu do Amanhã (Museum of Tomorrow), opened in 2015 on the revamped Praça Mauá waterfront, is an architectural feat by Santiago Calatrava. Its interactive exhibitions explore humanity's major environmental and climate challenges.

⏱ 90 min
4

Praça XV and its surroundings form the colonial heart of Rio. The Paço Imperial (1743), the former royal residence, the Igreja Nossa Senhora do Carmo and the lanes of the old Portuguese town tell the story of Rio before the beaches.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Early-morning walk along the Copacabana mosaic promenade (4.5 km) to Ipanema: jogging, beach volleyball, surfing. Leave valuables at the hotel.

⏱ 90 min

Head to Arpoador, between Copacabana and Ipanema, 30 minutes before sunset. The cariocas applaud as the sun disappears.

⏱ 30 min

Visit the Niterói Museum of Contemporary Art (MAC) across the bay: Oscar Niemeyer's white flying-saucer perched on a cliff, with a panoramic view back to Rio. Reach it by ferry from downtown.

⏱ 180 min
Avenida Atlântica right in front of the beach10
Stop 30

Rio De Janeiro Leme

★★★★☆
📍 Jardim Botânico Rio de Janeiro📍 Ipanema Leblon Beach Rio de Janeiro📍 Dois Irmãos hike Vidigal Rio de Janeiro📍 Hippie Fair Praça General Osório Ipanema📍 Feijoada Santa Teresa Lapa Rio de Janeiro📍 Favela Vidigal viewpoint Rio de Janeiro📍 Museu Nacional Belas Artes Rio de Janeiro
1

Rio's Jardim Botânico, founded in 1808 by Dom João VI, spreads across 54 hectares at the foot of Corcovado. Its imperial avenue of royal palms, planted in 1842, is one of the most famous in Brazil. Orchids, bromeliads, Victoria Amazonica water lilies and a butterfly greenhouse.

⏱ 90 min
2

Ipanema and Leblon, Rio's two chicest beaches, stretch 4 km along the Atlantic. Numbered kiosks (postos) structure beach life: each posto has its own vibe, its crowd, and its vendors selling mate and açaí.

⏱ 120 min
3

The Museu Nacional de Belas Artes, founded in 1937 in a neoclassical palace downtown, holds the largest collection of 19th-century Brazilian painting as well as European works brought over by the royal court in 1808.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Saturday feijoada lunch in Santa Teresa or the Lapa district: a slow-cooked stew of black beans and smoked meats, caipirinhas and live samba. The quintessential carioca weekend ritual.

⏱ 120 min

Ipanema Hippie Fair (Praça General Osório) on Sunday morning: crafts, jewelry, paintings and semi-precious stones. The largest street market in Rio.

⏱ 120 min

Hike Dois Irmãos from the Vidigal favela: 45 minutes up to the twin peaks that tower over Ipanema. A 360° view of the coastline, the lagoons, Christ the Redeemer and the bay. The trailhead is at the top of Vidigal, reached by communal moto-taxi.

⏱ 120 min
Avenida Atlântica right in front of the beach10
