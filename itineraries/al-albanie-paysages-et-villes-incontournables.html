⏳ How many days

We spread this itinerary over 16 days rather than the 11-day minimum, so you're not rushing from one stop to the next.

☀️ Weather

Peak Season (June-August): Perfect beach weather on the coast, warmest temperatures, but most crowded and expensive. Ideal for Albanian Riviera and coastal activities.

Shoulder Seasons (April-May, September-October): Excellent balance of pleasant weather, moderate crowds, and reasonable prices. Perfect for hiking and cultural exploration.

Winter (November-March): Mild coastal temperatures but cooler in mountains. Fewer crowds, lowest prices, some mountain areas may be inaccessible due to snow.

Spring (March-May): Wildflowers bloom, perfect hiking weather, fewer tourists, and cultural sites are more accessible.

Tirana

★★★★★
1

Skanderbeg Square sprawls across 40,000 m², the largest pedestrian plaza in the Balkans. The dome of the National History Museum dominates the northern edge, crowned by the giant mosaic 'The Albanians' created in 1981.

⏱ 60 min
2

Bunk'Art 1 hides inside Mount Dajti: 106 rooms across five underground levels, built in the 1970s as a nuclear bunker for dictator Enver Hoxha. The galleries chronicle life under communist dictatorship through chilling exhibits and haunting photographs.

⏱ 90 min
3

Descend into Bunk'Art 2, a 1,000 m² bunker beneath the Interior Ministry. The Sigurimi (secret police) cells bear witness to political terror. Historical photographs document systematic persecution under the communist regime.

⏱ 75 min
4

Built between 1791 and 1821, Et'hem Bey Mosque stands out for its rare frescoes: trees, waterfalls and bridges adorn the portico. On January 18, 1991, 10,000 people prayed here despite the ban, marking the end of state-imposed atheism.

⏱ 45 min
5

The Orthodox Resurrection Cathedral was inaugurated in 2012. Its dome rises 32 meters, the bell tower reaches 46 meters. The 586 m² mosaics depict Christ Pantocrator in glowing gold.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk up the Boulevard of National Martyrs from Skanderbeg Square to the Grand Park. The colorful buildings and urban energy illustrate Tirana's rapid transformation from gray communist capital to vibrant Balkan hub.

Kruja

★★☆☆☆
1

Krujë Castle withstood three Ottoman sieges (1450, 1466, 1467) under Skanderbeg. The 5th-century ramparts overlook the confluence of rivers below. Mehmed II the Conqueror only captured the fortress in 1478, ten years after the national hero's death.

⏱ 75 min
2

The Skanderbeg Museum, inaugurated in 1982, occupies a monumental building inspired by Albanian stone towers. Architects Pranvera Hoxha and Pirro Vaso designed this tribute to the hero who defied the Ottoman Empire for 25 years.

⏱ 90 min
3

The old Ottoman bazaar winds along cobbled lanes. Artisans sell traditional embroidery, kilim rugs and copper objects. The wooden shops date from the Ottoman era and retain their original character.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb to the castle ramparts for the panoramic view. The Tirana plain stretches to the Adriatic Sea on clear days. Three rivers converge below in a green valley carved by centuries of flow.

⏱ 45 min

Shkoder

★★★★☆
1

Head to pedestrian Rruga Kole Idromeno, the beating heart of Shkodër. Colorful facades house cafés and bistros. Street art animates the walls. In the evening, locals gather on terraces to chat over raki and espresso.

⏱ 60 min
2

Rozafa Castle stands on a hill at the confluence of the Buna and Drin rivers. Founded by the Illyrians, rebuilt by the Venetians then the Ottomans, it preserves its 14th-century walls. Legend tells that Rozafa was buried alive in the walls to ensure the foundations' strength.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Marubi National Photography Museum preserves 150,000 historical negatives. The collection begins with the first photograph taken in Albania in 1858 by Pjetër Marubi. Three generations of Marubi photographers documented Albanian life through wars, occupations and revolutions.

⏱ 75 min
4

Lake Shkodra, the largest in the Balkans, straddles the border with Montenegro. The wetlands shelter 270 bird species, including pelicans. The village of Shiroka offers the closest access from the city.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Rent a bike to reach Shiroka (30 minutes). The wooden bridge crosses the Buna, the trail follows the green peninsula to the lake. Swimming possible in summer in the clear waters.

Valbone

★★★☆☆
1

The Koman ferry navigates the lake-fjord carved through the Albanian Alps. Rock walls plunge vertically into emerald waters. Isolated villages cling to cliffs, accessible only by boat. One of the most spectacular river journeys in Europe.

⏱ 150 min
2

The Valbona Valley stretches 25 km between the peaks of Prokletije (Accursed Mountains). The national park created in 1966 protects beech forests and alpine meadows. Jezerca peak reaches 2,694 meters, Albania's highest point.

⏱ 90 min
3

The viewpoints over the White Circle massif (Maja e Ropsit) offer high mountain panoramas. The white karst walls contrast with the green of the meadows. Best light in late afternoon when shadows define the ridges.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Early morning boarding at Koman (departure around 9am). The cruise to Fierza lasts 2.5-3 hours through dramatic gorges. Minibus from Fierza to Valbona (30 minutes). Arrival in early afternoon to enjoy the valley.

⏱ 180 min

Acclimatization hike in Valbona Valley in late afternoon. Marked trails follow the turquoise river through meadows and forests. Dinner at the guesthouse with local produce—fresh cheese, hearty stews, homemade raki.

⏱ 90 min

Theth

★★★☆☆
1

Theth National Park, created in 1966, protects 2,630 hectares of karst mountains. Traditional stone houses scatter across the enclosed valley. Isolation has preserved the mountain way of life for centuries.

⏱ 60 min
2

The Kulla Tower (Kulla e Ngujimit) served as refuge during blood feuds. According to the Kanun, Albania's traditional code, men threatened by a blood vendetta could hide here until family reconciliation. The restored tower now functions as a museum.

⏱ 45 min
3

Grunas Waterfall plunges 30 meters into a turquoise basin. The walk from the village center takes 45 minutes through meadows and beech forests. A rainbow forms in front of the falls on sunny afternoons.

⏱ 90 min
4

The Church of Theth, built in 1892, stands out for its simplicity: local stone, wooden roof, modest bell tower. Surrounded by meadows with views of the peaks, it's one of Albania's most photographed monuments.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

The Valbona-Theth trek via Valbona Pass (1,792 m) is the iconic hike of the Albanian Alps. Depart at dawn, gradual ascent to the pass, descent to Theth. Panoramic views over both valleys. Arrival in Theth in early afternoon.

⏱ 360 min

Pogradec

★★☆☆☆
1

Lake Ohrid, one of Europe's oldest lakes, straddles the border with North Macedonia. At 300 meters deep, UNESCO-listed, it shelters over 200 endemic species including the Koran trout, Pogradec's emblem.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Lin Peninsula juts into the lake: recent excavations revealed Europe's oldest lakeside settlement, 8,500 years old. The 4th-century early Christian basilica preserves mosaics. A bunker transformed into a chapel marks the tip.

⏱ 75 min
3

Drilon National Park protects a zone of karst springs feeding the lake. Crystal-clear waters wind between centuries-old oaks and plane trees. Trails border the natural channels through dappled shade.

⏱ 60 min
4

The village of Tushemisht preserves traditional houses with red tile roofs. Multiple springs create small channels and ponds. Restaurants specialize in the lake's freshwater fish—grilled trout, carp, eel.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Walk along Pogradec's promenade in late afternoon for the xhiro, the Albanian tradition of evening stroll. Families gather, cafés buzz, views stretch across the lake to the Macedonian mountains.

⏱ 60 min

Swim in the clear waters of Lake Ohrid from the beaches of Pogradec or Tushemisht. Pleasant temperature from July to September. Pedalo and kayak rental available in summer.

⏱ 120 min

Berat

★★★★☆
1

The Mangalem Quarter climbs the slope with its white Ottoman houses. Multiple windows (hence the nickname 'city of a thousand windows') capture light on wooden balconies. Cobbled alleys, 18th-century mosques, the Halveti order tekke from 1782.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Gorica Bridge crosses the Osum in seven stone arches. Built in wood in 1780, rebuilt in stone in the 1920s, it links Mangalem to the Orthodox Christian quarter of Gorica.

⏱ 30 min
3

The Gorica Quarter, traditionally Orthodox Christian, offers a calmer atmosphere. Saint Spyridon Church from the 19th century, Saint Thomas Church on the heights. Stone houses, narrow alleys, views over Mangalem from the hill.

⏱ 75 min
4

Berat Castle (Kalaja) has dominated the city since the 4th century BC. Rebuilt in the 13th century, it remains inhabited: families still live within the walls. Byzantine churches, Ottoman mosque, ramparts spanning 2,400 years of continuous occupation.

⏱ 120 min
5

The Onufri Museum occupies the Dormition Cathedral inside the castle. Collection of Byzantine and post-Byzantine icons, works by painter Onufri (16th century) famous for his unique vermillion red. Carved woodwork, gilded iconostasis.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Climb to the Gorica ruins on the hill opposite the castle. Panoramic view over both quarters, the bridge, the Osum and the mountains. Forest trail, 45 minutes round trip.

⏱ 90 min

Gjirokaster

★★★☆☆
1

Gjirokastër Castle, one of the largest in the Balkans, was built in the 12th century then rebuilt by Ali Pasha in 1811-1812. Clock tower visible from across the city, weapons museum, 800-meter communist-era underground bunker.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Old Bazaar winds along alleys paved with black and white stone, geometric patterns laid by a single master mason. Built in the 17th century, rebuilt after a fire 200 years later. Olive wood artisans, copper objects, rugs.

⏱ 75 min
3

Skenduli House, an 18th-century Ottoman mansion, preserves its original carved woodwork. Guided tour by the owning family: winter and summer rooms, private hammam, monumental fireplaces.

⏱ 60 min
4

Zekate House rises on a hill overlooking the city. Built in 1812, its 12 chimneys testify to the owner's wealth. Corner towers, reception rooms, panoramic view over the stone roofs.

⏱ 60 min
5

The Bazaar Mosque, sole survivor of the fifteen mosques destroyed under the communist regime. 18th-century Ottoman architecture, slender minaret. Still in use, free entry outside prayer times.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Hike to Ali Pasha's Bridge, an old Ottoman aqueduct leading to the castle. Shepherd's trail (45 minutes), view over the Drino valley. Flocks still cross the bridge around 5pm.

⏱ 90 min

Sarande

★★☆☆☆
1

Lekurësi Castle, an Ottoman fortress on the strategic hill, offers panoramic views over Sarandë, the Ksamil islands and Corfu (Greece) in the distance. Restaurant on site, ideal at sunset.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stroll along Sarandë's palm-lined boulevard. Cafés, fish restaurants on pontoons above the water, entertainment down to the port. Views over the Ionian bay and Corfu 10 km away.

Settle in and relax after the drive. Swimming possible from Sarandë's urban beaches. Seafood dinner on the waterfront as the sun sets over the Ionian.

Ksamil

★★★★☆
1

Butrint, UNESCO archaeological site, layers Greek, Roman and Byzantine civilizations. 4th-century BC Greek theatre remodeled by the Romans, early Christian baptistery with mosaics, Byzantine basilica. Forests and lagoons surround the ruins.

⏱ 150 min
2

The beaches of Ksamil spread white sand and turquoise waters facing four islets. Compared to the Maldives of Europe, they attract crowds in July-August. Seafood restaurants, pedalo rental to the islands.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Rent a pedalo or kayak to reach the Ksamil islets. Explore the four islands, snorkeling in the crystalline waters, isolated coves. Return via the main beaches.

Dhermi

★★☆☆☆
1

The Blue Eye (Syri i Kaltër) springs from a karst source over 50 meters deep. Electric blue water bubbles to the surface, creating the illusion of an eye with dark pupil and turquoise iris. Nature reserve, centuries-old oaks and plane trees.

⏱ 75 min
2

The village of Dhërmi clings to the mountainside at 200 meters altitude. Traditional stone houses, Greek Orthodox churches, cobbled alleys. The population speaks the Greek Himariote dialect and Albanian Tosk.

⏱ 60 min
3

Drymades Beach mixes white pebbles in the north and golden sand in the south over 1 km. Turquoise waters, natural rock arch on the shore. Beach clubs, restaurants, relaxed atmosphere. Green Coast Resort hosts Albania's only Blue Flag beach.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Morning stop at the Blue Eye on the road from Ksamil. Swim in the glacial spring (water at 12-14°C year-round). Refreshing break before reaching the coast.

⏱ 90 min

Late afternoon relaxation on Drymades beach. Sunbed, swimming, sunset from a beach bar. Chill vibes of the Albanian Riviera.

⏱ 180 min

Dhermi

★★☆☆☆
1

Gjipe Canyon carves through the mountain to the sea between Vuno and Dhërmi. Marked trail (1-2h), vertiginous viewpoints over red walls and green vegetation. Leads to the isolated beach of Gjipe, accessible only on foot or by boat.

⏱ 90 min
2

Gjipe Beach, at the canyon's end, spreads pebbles and sand between vertical cliffs. Limpid waters, snorkeling, sunbeds for rent. Modest bar-restaurant. One of the most spectacular beaches on the Albanian Riviera.

⏱ 180 min
3

The old village of Himarë climbs the slopes of Mount Çika at 200 meters altitude. Cobbled alleys, stone houses, Orthodox churches. The ruins of the Venetian castle dominate the bay. Traditionally Greek Himariote population.

⏱ 60 min
4

Potami Beach, 3 km south of Himarë, nestles in an isolated cove. Accessible by trail or water taxi. Fine pebbles, crystal water, rustic bar-restaurant. Less crowded than neighboring beaches.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Morning: hike into Gjipe Canyon and swim on the beach at the canyon's end. Afternoon: excursion to Himarë (30 min by car), climb to the Venetian castle for the panorama over the Riviera, return to Dhërmi for sunset.

⏱ 300 min

Vlore

★★★☆☆
1

Llogara National Park covers 1,020 hectares of black pine and oak forests on the Ceraunian Mountains. The Llogara Pass at 1,027 meters marks the divide between the Riviera and Vlorë Bay. Viewpoint with plunging views over turquoise beaches.

⏱ 90 min
2

Kaninë Castle overlooks Vlorë since the 3rd century BC. Founded by the Illyrians, reinforced under Byzantium then the Ottomans. The walls offer panoramic views over the bay and the Adriatic.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Muradie Mosque (Lead Mosque), designed by architect Mimar Sinan in 1537, is one of the Ottoman jewels of Albania. Octagonal dome, 18-meter minaret, alternating two-color bricks.

⏱ 60 min
4

The Independence Museum occupies the yellow house where Ismail Qemali proclaimed Albanian independence on November 28, 1912. Busts of early prime ministers, period furniture, historical flags.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Stop at Llogara Pass for the panoramic view over the Albanian Riviera. Forest trails in the national park (30-60 min). Stroll along Vlorë's Lungomare in late afternoon, views over the Karaburun peninsula.

⏱ 120 min
