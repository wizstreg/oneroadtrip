Bremen

★★★★☆
1

The scent of roasted coffee drifts through Böttcherstraße before you even step inside. This 100-meter expressionist brick alley was conceived in the 1920s by patron Ludwig Roselius. The carillon chimes every hour between noon and 6 PM: stand beneath the Haus des Glockenspiels to hear it ring.

⏱ 45 min
2

Touristy, yes, but Schnoorviertel delivers on its promises. This tangle of 13th-century lanes, where ship ropes were once manufactured, lines up galleries, workshops and tiny cafés. The New York Post ranked it among the world's most picturesque streets. Arrive early in the morning to dodge the crowds.

⏱ 60 min
3

Take time to look up at Bremen's Rathaus, a UNESCO World Heritage site along with the Roland statue since 2004. The Friedenssaal and the wine cellar of the Ratskeller, active since the 15th century, are open to visitors. Free admission to the hall.

⏱ 45 min
4

A hundred meters of expressionist brick may be short, but Bremen Cathedral deserves a separate stop. The Romanesque crypt and the Bleikeller with its natural mummies leave a lasting impression. Free entry to the cathedral, small supplement for the Bleikeller.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you arrive on a Saturday morning, the market at Domshof is in full swing: cheeses from the Lüneburger Heide, dark rye bread and smoked fish from the North Sea. Relaxed atmosphere, fair prices.

⏱ 45 min

Walk along the Weser via the Schlachte promenade at the end of the day. The old port warehouses now house breweries and terraces. Beck's beer is brewed here: try it on tap, a stone's throw from its factory.

⏱ 60 min

Worpswede

1

A farming village turned artists' colony in 1889: Worpswede owes everything to Fritz Mackensen, captivated by the light of the Teufelsmoor. In 2026, the four museums celebrate Paula Modersohn-Becker's 150th birthday with interconnected exhibitions. Plan on spending the morning.

⏱ 90 min
2

The low light of the Teufelsmoor filters through the windows of the Barkenhoff, a former farmhouse transformed into a Gesamtkunstwerk by Heinrich Vogeler as early as 1895. The Art Nouveau garden is the most photographed spot in the village. The Heinrich Vogeler Museum occupies the interior.

⏱ 60 min
3

Hoetger's Niedersachsenstein divides visitors: an expressionist brick sculpture perched on the Weyerberg, some find it brilliant, others hideous. Climb up for the view over the peat bogs. Free access, five minutes' walk from the center.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Rent a bike and pedal through the Teufelsmoor on dirt paths between drainage canals. The flat, misty landscape explains why the painters stayed. Count on 90 minutes for the classic loop.

⏱ 90 min

Wildeshausen

1

More than 530 burial mounds across 30 hectares of heathland: the Pestruper Gräberfeld is the largest Bronze Age necropolis in north-central Europe. The funerary urns date from approximately 800 to 200 BC. The site has free access, parking on the K248.

⏱ 60 min
2

Continue to the Alexanderkirche in Wildeshausen's historic center, the only basilica in the Oldenburger Land. The medieval Stadtwall that surrounds it can be walked in twenty minutes. Have a look at the Gothic-gabled Rathaus while you're passing by.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you come between mid-August and early September, the flowering heather transforms the Gräberfeld into a purple carpet. In the morning, fog clings to dew-covered cobwebs: guaranteed spectacle for photographers.

⏱ 60 min

Oldenburg

1

You don't expect such a castle right in the center of Oldenburg. The Schloss, residence of counts then grand dukes since the 12th century, houses the Landesmuseum für Kunst und Kulturgeschichte. The state rooms and chapel are worth the admission.

⏱ 60 min
2

The rhododendrons of the Schlossgarten bloom fragrant from May onward. This 16-hectare English park, laid out by Duke Peter Friedrich Ludwig, preserves specimens planted in 1828 among the oldest in Germany. Open daily from 8 AM.

⏱ 45 min
3

The Lappan, a 15th-century bell tower isolated from its church, stands as the city's symbol. Three minutes' walk from the Schloss, it marks the entrance to the pedestrian shopping zone.

⏱ 15 min

Activities

Make a detour to the Schlossgarten's Tropenhaus to see tropical plants in the middle of Lower Saxony. Free, open in the afternoon. In the evening, bats take over the park.

⏱ 30 min

Cloppenburg

1

An open-air museum in the countryside: the Museumsdorf Cloppenburg assembles some sixty rural buildings from the Middle Ages to the 1980s. Founded in 1934, it's one of Germany's oldest Freilichtmuseen. Count on a good two hours.

⏱ 120 min
2

The creak of wood beneath the hooves of Heidschnucken, the black-headed sheep grazing between the windmills: the Museumsdorf isn't a frozen museum. Three windmills, a church, a forge and even a rural 1980s disco. Adult admission: around 12 euros.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

If the Dorfkirmes happens during your visit (autumn), the museum village comes alive with historical fair stalls and wood-fired bread. Check dates on museumsdorf.de.

⏱ 60 min

Haseluenne

1

Locals call it the Kornstadt: Haselünne, the oldest town in Emsland, still counts three Korn distilleries (Berentzen, Heydt, Rosche). In the 19th century, there were 26. The Berentzen Hof offers tours with tasting.

⏱ 60 min
2

Stop by the Wacholderhain at the town's edge, a nature reserve of junipers across more than 30 hectares. Semi-wild Tarpan horses graze there. Marked trails, free access.

⏱ 40 min

Activities

The Korndiplom at Rosche is kitsch but well done: you take an exam on historic school benches at the Korn-Academie, then taste the result. Reservation required.

⏱ 60 min

Meppen

1

Built between 1824 and 1828, Meppen's Koppelschleuse is a double-chamber work of engineering, designed to overcome more than four meters of elevation between the Ems-Hase Canal and the river. It can be visited in its original state, open-air. Free access.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Renaissance gables of the old Rathaus, built in 1408 from glacial erratics, deserve a look. The baroque red-and-yellow Gymnasialkirche, a stone's throw away, contrasts with the northern Protestant sobriety.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Climb up to the Höltingmühle on the island between the Dortmund-Ems Canal and the Hase. The windmill is photogenic, and the Mühle café serves good homemade cakes.

⏱ 45 min

Papenburg

1

Cruise ships built 30 km from the sea, in a covered hangar perched on a river: Meyer Werft in Papenburg defies logic. The visitor center exhibits full-size cabins and a six-meter-diameter propeller. 90-minute guided tour, reservation required.

⏱ 90 min
2

Stroll along the Hauptkanal, the most unusual pedestrian promenade in the north. Replicas of historic sailing ships are moored right in the shopping district. No parking meters anywhere in town, free parking everywhere.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

If you're lucky, an Überführung of a cruise ship on the Ems happens during your stay: a 300-meter giant glides a few meters from the banks, escorted by tugboats. Dates depend on weather and tides, announced at the last minute.

⏱ 60 min

Soegel

1

Seen from the air, Schloss Clemenswerth resembles a star set in the forest. Eight pavilions arranged in an octagon around the central pavilion: it's the last preserved Parforce-Sternanlage in Europe. Designed by Johann Conrad Schlaun, built between 1737 and 1747 for Prince-Bishop Clemens August.

⏱ 90 min
2

The silence of the Klostergarten, broken only by birdsong, contrasts with the baroque pomp of the Festsaal. The painted chapels and the hunting pavilion with its trophies are worth the visit. Count on 75 minutes with the guided tour.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Eight pavilions in a star around a central castle, eight lime-tree avenues, a pond with five islands: walk through the park to grasp the baroque geometry at human height. The Marstall, at the west end, houses an exhibition on court life.

⏱ 60 min

Duemmer See

1

Locals call it 'das kleine Meer': the Dümmer See, Lower Saxony's second-largest lake at 12.5 km², is so shallow that the average depth is 1.2 meters. Common cranes and greylag geese stop here by the thousands in autumn. Bike loop around the lake: 27 km.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Rent a bike and circle the lake on the marked cycle path. The east side of the Dümmer offers free-access sandy beaches, ideal for a swimming break. About 90 minutes of leisurely pedaling.

⏱ 90 min

If you come in autumn, bring binoculars: the Dümmer is a wetland of international importance. Observation towers are scattered around the lake. The Naturschutzstation on the west side organizes guided tours.

⏱ 60 min

Kalkriese

1

In AD 9, three Roman legions were annihilated here by a Germanic coalition led by Arminius. The site was only identified in 1987, when a British amateur archaeologist officer found Roman coins in a field. The museum exhibits the famous iron Reitermaske and eight Roman gold coins.

⏱ 90 min
2

From the top of the 40-meter tower, your gaze sweeps the Kalkrieser Berg and the Großes Moor. Below, the Germanic rampart has been reconstructed on the probable route. The 20-hectare park features themed pavilions and a treetop adventure course.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Grab the audio guide and walk through the archaeological park following the battle route. The reconstructions of the Germanic palisade and the swamp give a concrete idea of the trap set for the legions. Admission: €9.50 adult.

⏱ 60 min

Osnabrueck

★★★☆☆
1

Osnabrück and Münster vie for the title of 'City of Peace.' It was here, in the Friedenssaal of the Rathaus, that the Treaty of Westphalia was signed in 1648. The 42 portraits of sovereigns and ambassadors still hang on the walls. The 1512 building has carried the European Heritage Label since 2015.

⏱ 60 min
2

The broken angles of Daniel Libeskind's Felix-Nussbaum-Haus (1998) unsettle you before you even enter. The museum preserves more than 200 works by the painter born in Osnabrück in 1904, murdered at Auschwitz in 1944. Architecture and content speak to each other.

⏱ 60 min
3

Push open the door of the Remarque-Friedenszentrum to discover the life of Erich Maria Remarque, author of All Quiet on the Western Front, born here in 1898. The permanent exhibition is compact but well done. Free admission.

⏱ 30 min
4

The Dom Sankt Peter blends Romanesque and Gothic across 109 meters. The cloister and treasury are accessible. Cathedral Square hosts a market on Wednesdays and Saturdays.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you're there on a Saturday, the Wochenmarkt on Domplatz is one of the region's best: smoked sausages, rye bread, cheeses from the Osnabrücker Land. Cheerful atmosphere, well-stocked stalls.

⏱ 45 min

Head down to the Altstadt to try Grünkohl (kale cooked long) in season, or a Pinkus Müller, Osnabrück's beer brewed since 1816. The pedestrian district around Heger-Tor-Straße is packed with small restaurants.

⏱ 60 min

Bad Rothenfelde

1

Designed to make salt, Bad Rothenfelde's Gradierwerke now serve as an open-air thermal cure. Salt water trickles down 412 meters of blackthorn walls, creating an iodine-rich aerosol. You'd think you were on the North Sea coast, 200 km from the shore.

⏱ 30 min
2

In the Inhalationskammer, a salt fog so dense you can't see your hands: slip on the plastic cape sold at the entrance (€1) and breathe for ten to twenty minutes. Effective against allergies, according to regulars.

⏱ 20 min

Activities

Climb the 55 steps of the Besucherplattform on the New Gradierwerk to see the reconstructed Windkunst, the wind pump that lifted the brine. The view of the Kurpark and the rosarium of 210 varieties is a bonus. Guided tours from Easter to September.

⏱ 45 min

Bad Iburg

1

The Rittersaal of Schloss Iburg is one of the first baroque halls in northwestern Germany, with trompe-l'oeil frescoes considered the last of their kind north of the Alps. Sophie Charlotte, first Queen of Prussia and grandmother of Frederick the Great, was born here in 1668.

⏱ 45 min
2

Descend to the Charlottensee for a quiet break. This lake at the castle's foot bears the princess's name. You can rent rowboats in season. The adjacent Kneipp-Erlebnispark offers water-walking basins.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If the Baumwipfelpfad is open, the Teutoburger Wald canopy reveals itself at treetop height. Otherwise, the marked Hermannsweg trail crosses Bad Iburg with easy sections. Count on an hour for a short loop.

⏱ 60 min

Tecklenburg

★★★☆☆
1

Westphalians call it 'the balcony of Münsterland': perched on the Teutoburger Wald, Tecklenburg offers a clear panorama over the plain. The medieval town is compact, all cobbled lanes, 16th-18th century half-timbered houses and stiegen (staircases) carved into the rock.

⏱ 45 min
2

Nestled in the ruins of Burg Tecklenburg, founded in the 12th century, sits Germany's largest Freilichtbühne: more than 2,300 seats for summer musicals. The acoustics of the old ivy-covered walls are remarkable.

⏱ 30 min
3

The uneven cobblestones and half-timbered facades of the Altstadt lead to the Puppenmuseum and the Wasserschloss Haus Marck, a moated manor hidden in the woods below. This is where the first negotiations of the Peace of Westphalia took place in 1643.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Book a seat for a musical at the Freilichtbühne if you're there in summer (June-September). Performances are in the evening, 1,800 seats are covered. Tickets at freilichtspiele-tecklenburg.de.

⏱ 150 min

Muenster

★★★☆☆
1

The Prinzipalmarkt deserves its reputation as Münster's 'Gute Stube.' The stepped gables and 13th-century arcades line a bustling shopping avenue. The Friedenssaal of the Rathaus, where the 1648 treaty was also signed, can be visited free of charge.

⏱ 45 min
2

Schloss Münster, designed by Johann Conrad Schlaun in 1767, now serves as the university's main building. Behind it, the Botanischer Garten is a free haven of peace. The tropical greenhouses and medicinal garden are open to the public.

⏱ 45 min
3

Enter the Friedenssaal of the Rathaus to see the portraits of the 1648 plenipotentiaries, then look up at the neighboring Lambertikirche. The Türmerstube, at the top of the bell tower, is still occupied every evening by a night watchman who sounds the horn.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

If you finish on a Wednesday or Saturday, the Wochenmarkt at Domplatz is the meeting point for Münsteraner: organic vegetables from Münsterland, fresh Pumpernickel, smoked Fleischkäse. A fine way to wrap up the road trip.

⏱ 45 min
