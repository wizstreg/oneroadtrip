Blue Bay

★★★☆☆
1

First encounter with the island: Blue Bay beach and its marine park, protected since 2004. This is Mauritius's only lagoon with Marine Park status, with electric-blue water that gives the place its name. The coral reef starts practically at swimming distance from the public beach: pack fins, mask and snorkel and the kids can watch parrotfish and surgeonfish in 1 meter of water. Perfect for a first afternoon without driving too far from the airport. Avoid the area on Saturday-Sunday when it's packed with Mauritian family picnics - charming but noisy.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Mahébourg waterfront for a gentle arrival. Founded in 1804 under Decaen as a former capital, the town has retained a provincial character that contrasts with Port Louis. Stroll along the waterfront, vendors selling chili cakes and samosas for 5 rupees each. Kids quickly connect with locals playing dominoes on the benches. If you arrive on a Monday morning, the big colorful bazaar is worth the detour - otherwise Saturday's market is even better stocked.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Peaceful swimming at Pointe d'Esny, right next to Blue Bay but far less crowded. White sand beach, shallow lagoon extending 200 meters offshore - ideal for the 6-year-old who can splash around without worry. A few casuarina trees provide shade in the afternoon. No street vendors here, you feel more at home than on a postcard beach.

⏱ 90 min

Blue Bay

★★★☆☆
1

Île aux Aigrettes, 850 meters offshore from Mahébourg: probably the most striking nature visit on the island for children. 26 hectares of coral limestone (so not volcanic like the rest of Mauritius) managed since 1986 by the Mauritius Wildlife Foundation. You walk among giant Aldabra tortoises weighing over 200 kg, some centenarians, reintroduced to replace the Mauritian tortoises extinct around 1735. Guided tour mandatory, 1h30 on the Dodo Trail. Book 2-3 days ahead at 631 2396, the 9:30am slots fill up fast. Price: around 1000 MUR adult, 500 MUR children 4-11 years.

⏱ 150 min
2

The Mahébourg National History Museum, housed in the Château Gheude, an 18th-century French colonial mansion. Room dedicated to the naval Battle of Grand Port (August 1810), the only French naval victory inscribed on the Arc de Triomphe in Paris. Teenagers often engage thanks to the ship models and cannons. Small but well-done museum, free, perfect mid-day if the heat's beating down. Closed Tuesdays.

⏱ 60 min
3

The Dutch ruins of Vieux Grand Port, 10 minutes north of Mahébourg. This is where the Dutch first set foot in 1598 - the birth certificate of the island's modern history. Modest ruins (foundations, tombs), but the panorama over the bay is superb and the site is deserted. A chance to explain to the kids that the dodo, exterminated here in less than a century, lived its final days in these forests.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Glass-bottom boat in Blue Bay Marine Park. 30 minutes above the coral gardens: table coral, brain coral, anemones, and with luck some green turtles. Perfect format for the 6-year-old who won't snorkel for long. Count on 800 MUR per adult, half-price for children. The boats leave from Blue Bay public beach, you can negotiate on site without trouble.

⏱ 45 min

Mahébourg market on Saturday morning, or Curepipe central market as an alternative. All the fruits of the austral winter are there: incredibly sweet lychees, José mangoes, Victoria pineapples. Taste the chili-tamarind fruit salad, strange but addictive. The kids choose their fruits, vendors often offer a piece to taste - it's the custom.

⏱ 60 min

Blue Bay

★★★☆☆
1

La Vanille Park in Rivière des Anguilles, the almost universal favorite for families with young children. 1,000 giant Aldabra tortoises in semi-free roaming, 2,000 Nile crocodiles bred on site, and an insectarium that gives teenagers the shivers. The big show: crocodile feeding, daily except Monday and Friday at 11:30am. Children can climb on baby tortoises' backs in the educational enclosure. Allow 2 to 3 hours on site. Decent restaurant on site if you want to continue.

⏱ 150 min
2

Rochester Falls at Souillac, an unusual waterfall formed by rectangular basalt prisms sculpted by erosion. Modest height, 10 meters, but the geological effect is unique on the island. Access via a rutted track through sugarcane fields - better to have a high-clearance vehicle. Swimming possible in the pool, provided rains haven't fallen in recent days (current). Avoid after 4pm, the surroundings empty out quickly.

⏱ 45 min
3

Gris-Gris, cliff battered by the wild southern swells. No coral reef here, so the ocean hits the rock directly: waves explode 10 meters high on the 'Weeping Rock', a limestone formation carved by salt. This is the only place in Mauritius that resembles the Irish coast, which always surprises. Swimming strictly forbidden, violent currents. Five minutes' walk from parking, accessible to young children.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Visit to Saint Aubin at La Flora to wrap up the southern day. 1819 colonial house, former sugar mill converted: they still produce agricultural rum and vanilla. Guided distillery tour (1h), tasting for adults, freshly pressed sugarcane juice for kids. The veranda restaurant serves authentic Creole cuisine - the octopus vindaye is worth it if you're not put off by octopus.

⏱ 90 min

Snack in Souillac: the famous chili cakes and samosas at Gardénia, a local institution since the 1970s. It's touristy, but well done - and far more instructive about Mauritian street food than hotel demonstrations. Count on 80 MUR for a snack for four.

⏱ 30 min

Trou D Eau Douce

★★★☆☆
1

Île aux Cerfs from Trou d'Eau Douce, 10 minutes by water taxi. 87 hectares of white sand, swimming-pool-colored lagoon - if you go, do it early. Touristy, definitely really touristy, but genuinely beautiful: before 9am, the southeastern beaches are almost empty and it's sublime. After 11am, it's a parade of catamarans and the beach transforms into Marbella beach. Private water taxis negotiate at 350-400 MUR round trip without guide or barbecue, much cheaper than group excursions.

⏱ 240 min
2

The Grande Rivière Sud-Est waterfall, accessible as an option from Île aux Cerfs or from Trou d'Eau Douce. 30 minutes by pirogue through the mangroves to the two falls about 30 meters high. The pirogue operators approach to within 5 meters of the water curtain, shower guaranteed. It's basic, but well placed in the program.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Snorkeling in the Île aux Cerfs lagoon on the east side, where the reef is most alive. Lots of butterflyfish, sergeant majors, sometimes stingrays in the sandy areas. The water is warm (24°C in July), shallow for 100 meters - suitable for the 6-year-old with a float vest. Water taxis provide fins-mask-snorkel on request, 100 MUR rental.

⏱ 90 min

Beach barbecue lunch 'feet in the sand' on the island, either by booking via a water taxi (grilled fish + salad formula for 600 MUR/person), or bringing your own picnic. More authentic than catamaran buffets, which feel industrial and end up at 80 people per table.

⏱ 60 min

Trou D Eau Douce

★★★☆☆
1

Domaine de l'Étoile, 1,200 hectares of private reserve 30 minutes from Trou d'Eau Douce. This is where you schedule the family buggy: minimum age 16 to drive, so dad or mom at the wheel and teen + little one as passengers in the back of a 4-seater buggy. 2-hour course through valleys, rivers and indigenous forests, stop in a river halfway to cool off. Probably the finest buggy spot on the island, and far less industrial than Casela. Count on 4,500 MUR for a 4-seater buggy.

⏱ 150 min
2

Bras d'Eau National Park, a free and little-frequented alternative for a family forest walk. Marked trail of about 3 km around an old colonial reservoir, doable in 1h with children. Mauritius fodys (emblematic scarlet red bird) and kingfishers easily spotted. Information centers at the entrance lend maps and give daily tips.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Archery, horseback riding or zip-lining at Domaine de l'Étoile: the full range of outdoor activities on the island is concentrated here. Children from 6 years old can ride double with a guide on horseback (30-min pony ride, 800 MUR). The domain's advantage: you combine several activities on the same day with a single entrance ticket at 300 MUR/person, more economical than doing them separately elsewhere.

⏱ 90 min

Sunset on Belle Mare beach, 15 minutes north. 8 km white sand beach, one of the island's longest, little frequented late in the day as hotels pull in their towels. Peaceful swimming, wind a bit stronger than at Trou d'Eau Douce. Small cocktail at the fishing village beach bar around 5:30pm.

Trou D Eau Douce

★★★☆☆
1

Port Louis, the capital, 1h drive. Start with the Aapravasi Ghat, UNESCO-listed in 2006: this is where between 1834 and 1920 more than 450,000 Indian indentured laborers landed, the first great contract migration in history. Original staircases and buildings remain, free museum. Five minutes away, the central market explodes with colors and smells - go through the spice wing, Mauritian curry is half the price of Curepipe.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam Botanical Garden at Pamplemousses, the oldest tropical garden in the southern hemisphere (1735). 37 hectares, more than 600 species including the famous giant Victoria amazonica water lilies 2 meters in diameter, strong enough to support a child - for the photo only, don't try. Avenue of talipot palms that only flower once in 60 years before dying. Guided tour recommended, 200 MUR for two hours with a French-speaking guide.

⏱ 120 min

Albion

★★★★☆
1

Black River Gorges National Park, 6,754 hectares created in 1994, 2% of Mauritian territory. For a family with a 6-year-old, two simple options: the Macchabée Forest Walk (2.5 km flat in shade, 1h30, departure from Pétrin center) or the combination Gorges Viewpoint by car + short trail (15 min walk, spectacular view over the valley). Avoid the climb to Piton de la Petite Rivière Noire (828m, highest point) with a young child, it's too much. 9 endemic bird species observable: Mauritius fody, pink pigeon, Mauritius kestrel - patience and binoculars.

⏱ 180 min
2

Grand Bassin (Ganga Talao), 3 minutes from Pétrin, crater lake sacred to the Mauritian Hindu community (52% of the population). 33-meter Shiva statue erected on the lake shore, one of the tallest in the world. Massive pilgrimage late February-early March (Maha Shivaratri, up to 500,000 faithful). In July, calm and majestic atmosphere. Covering clothing requested (shoulders and knees).

⏱ 60 min
3

Chamarel and the Seven-Colored Earth, 30 minutes out of the park. Unique geological phenomenon: volcanic ash dunes in 7 shades (red, brown, purple, green, blue, crimson, yellow). Small, but clearly worth seeing once. On the same site, view of Chamarel waterfall (100m drop) and an Aldabra tortoise park. Combined ticket 350 MUR/adult, free under 3.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Tasting break at Rhumerie de Chamarel for adults (distillery tour 1h, tasting of 5 agricultural rums at 350 MUR/person). L'Alchimiste restaurant on site, revisited Creole cuisine, colonial veranda atmosphere. Meal 1,200 MUR/adult, children's menu 450 MUR. Kids receive freshly pressed sugarcane juice during the tasting.

⏱ 120 min

Picnic at Pétrin Information Centre before the walk, or buy chili cakes at Plaine Champagne food trucks. These street vendors are the island's best for local fried snacks - locals stop here coming down from pilgrimages.

Domaine de Bel Ombre, 2,500 hectares preserved on the wild south coast. Far from the crowds, this is as close as it gets to pre-tourism Mauritius. 2

⏱ 150 min

Activities

Discovery dive at Flic en Flac for the teen (from 8 years with PADI certified instructor). Several centers: Sun Divers, Exploration Sous-Marine. ⏱ 180 min

Farewell dinner at La Pirogue or at Le Bistrot du Boucanier in Flic en Flac, last night before the all-inclusive at Le Morne. Creole-French fusion cuisine, terrace facing the sunset. Count on 1,500 MUR/adult. Good address for Mauritian seafood (prawns, lobsters, crabs).

Le Morne Brabant

★★★★☆
1

Arrival at Le Morne and check-in at the all-inclusive hotel. Before switching to relaxation mode: small discovery tour of the peninsula. Le Morne public beach, 7 km of white sand facing one of the island's most beautiful lagoons. Crystal Rock, an emerging coral formation in the middle of the turquoise lagoon, is visible 1 km offshore from the beach. Constant afternoon wind (June-September trade winds): it's the world Mecca of kitesurfing and surfing, ranked among the planet's 10 best spots.

⏱ 150 min
2

Le Morne Brabant, 556-meter mountain UNESCO World Heritage listed in 2008. Painful symbol: this is where in the early 19th century, maroon slaves had taken refuge. When soldiers came in 1835 to announce the abolition of slavery, they thought it was a punitive expedition and threw themselves from the summit. Slave Route Memorial at the foot of the mountain, free access. Hike to the summit (3h round trip, moderate level) not recommended with a 6-year-old - the teen can do it with a parent.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Late afternoon swim on Le Morne beach and sunset behind the sacred mountain. Golden light on the peak, turquoise lagoon, kitesurf sails in the background: one of the most beautiful day endings in the entire Indian Ocean. From here, time for all-inclusive and well-deserved rest.

⏱ 90 min
