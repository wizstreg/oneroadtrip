  • Jericoacoara's Pedra Furada, a wave-carved stone arch within the national park
  • Lagoa do Paraíso, a crystalline freshwater lagoon surrounded by towering dunes
  • Canoa Quebrada's reddish cliffs and the iconic moon-and-star symbol
  • Galinhos, a peninsula village accessible only by boat, ringed by salt flats and mangroves
  • Tourinhos petrified dunes at São Miguel do Gostoso, a world-class kitesurfing destination

📆 Best period

July, August, September, October, November, December

⏳ How many days

Announced duration: 8 days. Doable in 7 to 9 days depending on your available time.

☀️ Weather

Summer (December-March): Peak tourist season, hottest weather, Carnival season, highest prices, crowded beaches, vibrant atmosphere.

Fall (April-June): Excellent weather, fewer crowds, good prices, ideal for most regions, comfortable temperatures.

Winter (July-September): Dry season in most regions, perfect for Pantanal wildlife, cooler temperatures, great for cultural activities.

Spring (October-November): Good weather returning, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for outdoor activities.

Regional Variations: Amazon best May-September (dry season), Northeast beaches year-round, South cooler June-August.

🌍 Discover

Carnival energy, Amazon rainforest, and pristine beaches create the ultimate South American adventure. Experience vibrant cities, incredible biodiversity, and passionate culture from Rio's beaches to Pantanal wetlands. Samba music, capoeira martial arts, and incredible football culture showcase national spirit. Brazil offers unparalleled natural wonders with infectious cultural energy.

Jijoca De Jericoacoara

★★★★☆
1

The 4×4 transfer from Fortaleza first crosses the inland plains of Ceará before threading through coastal dunes. The final hour plays out entirely on sand, weaving between mangroves and lagoons—the adventure begins long before arrival.

⏱ 60 min
2

The village of Jericoacoara, a former fishing hamlet discovered in the 1980s, preserves its car-free sandy streets. The Washington Post once ranked it among the world's ten most beautiful beaches. Going barefoot here isn't optional—it's the only way to move through town.

⏱ 60 min
3

Climb the Duna do Pôr do Sol, a massive golden dune just west of the village. From its crest, the Atlantic stretches endlessly while the sun ignites the horizon—this is Jericoacoara's nightly ritual, and everyone gathers here to witness it.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Sunset from the summit of Duna do Pôr do Sol. Locals and travelers alike applaud each evening as the sun drops into the ocean—a ritual that sets the tone for your entire stay.

⏱ 60 min

First dinner in the village: grilled seafood, caju caipirinha, and live forró in the sandy bars. The lanes come alive beneath the stars, barefoot on warm sand.

⏱ 90 min

Jijoca De Jericoacoara

★★★★☆
1

Pedra Furada rises 1.7 km from the village, a stone arch carved by waves at the foot of Serrote hill. In July, the sun sets precisely through the arch's opening—a phenomenon locals applaud every evening.

⏱ 75 min
2

Lagoa do Paraíso unfolds across 15 km² of transparent freshwater surrounded by towering dunes. Hammocks suspended above the water invite contemplation. The contrast between turquoise lagoon and white dunes is breathtaking.

⏱ 90 min
3

Ascend the Serrote, a small 95-meter serra that dominates the village. The lighthouse (farol) occupies its summit. The 360° view reveals the dune field, rocky coastline, and the ocean's immensity.

⏱ 60 min
4

Lagoa Azul, more intimate than its larger sibling, nestles its blue-tinted waters between wind-sculpted dunes. Beach clubs set up hammocks and tables at the water's edge—lunch with your feet in a lagoon, a simple yet unforgettable luxury.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Launch into the dunes by buggy: Lagoa Azul, Lagoa do Paraíso, Tatajuba dunes, and river crossings. Wind whips the sand, the buggy plummets down steep slopes—adrenaline guaranteed.

⏱ 240 min

Strap on harness and wing for a kitesurfing session at Praia do Preá, 17 km from the village. Constant trade winds and steady waves make this one of Brazil's premier spots.

⏱ 120 min

Glide down the sand on a sandboard from the summit of Duna do Pôr do Sol. Rent a board from local vendors and ride the slope facing the ocean—pure freedom.

⏱ 60 min

Horseback ride along the deserted beach at sunset. Riders trace the dunes and Rio Guriú mangroves, where seahorses sometimes appear in the shallow waters.

⏱ 90 min

Canoa Quebrada

★★★★☆
1

Canoa Quebrada's cliffs rise 30 meters of reddish walls above an emerald ocean. The moon-and-star symbol, carved into the sandstone by a local artist in the 1970s, marks the site's entrance from atop the dunes.

⏱ 75 min
2

Aracati's historic center, 13 km inland, preserves 275 buildings classified by IPHAN. Portuguese-tiled sobrados from the 18th century line Rua Grande, remnants of when the river port exported charque (dried beef) across Brazil.

⏱ 75 min
3

The Museu Jaguaribano, housed in the Solar do Barão de Aracati, chronicles the charque cycle and colonial prosperity. The Igreja Matriz de Nossa Senhora do Rosário, an 18th-century temple, dominates the main square.

⏱ 60 min
4

In nearby Majorlândia village, artisans create garrafinhas de areia colorida: miniature landscapes composed of differently colored sands poured into glass bottles. A unique craft born on this coast.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Buggy tour along the cliffs and dunes, passing shrimp farms and lagoons. The zipline descent from the cliff summit offers an aerial view of red rock meeting turquoise water.

⏱ 150 min

Sunset from Canoa Quebrada's Duna do Pôr do Sol. The ritual mirrors Jeri: climb up, settle in, and watch the sun set the red cliffs ablaze before plunging into the Atlantic.

⏱ 60 min

Evening on Rua Broadway (officially Rua Dragão do Mar), Canoa's nightlife heart. Reggae, forró, and electronic music blend in bars and restaurants that awaken at sundown.

⏱ 120 min

Sail out with local fishermen in a jangada beyond the cliffs. These traditional flat-bottomed sailboats glide across turquoise waters—an ancestral navigation facing a postcard landscape.

⏱ 90 min

Galinhos

★★★★☆
1

Galinhos stretches across a sand peninsula 500 meters wide, squeezed between the ocean on one side and an estuary on the other. With 1,200 inhabitants, the village is accessible only by boat or 4×4 along the beach at low tide.

⏱ 60 min
2

Galinhos salt flats raise enormous white mountains amid the mangrove. Salt is extracted directly from seawater—Rio Grande do Norte is Brazil's leading salt producer.

⏱ 75 min
3

The village of Galos, even smaller than Galinhos, is reached on foot or by donkey cart along the estuary. Its sandy lanes lead to a beach where you pass from river to ocean in a few steps.

⏱ 60 min
4

At the peninsula's tip, the Galinhos lighthouse dominates a circular beach battered by waves. Wind farm turbines turn slowly in the background, sculpting a futuristic silhouette against ancestral sand.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Village discovery by donkey-drawn cart, the only local transport besides buggies. Sandy lanes run past colorful houses—welcome to a Brazil beyond time.

⏱ 60 min

Observe the salt flats and mangroves from the estuary. The vegetation shelters crabs, oysters, white and blue herons—and sometimes seahorses in shallow waters.

⏱ 60 min

Sunset from the peninsula's natural pools. Warm water rises to your knees, the sky blazes—a moment of absolute serenity.

⏱ 60 min

1

Duna do Capim emerges amid lagoons like a miniature Lençóis Maranhenses landscape. Freshwater lagoons form between dunes after rains—their hues oscillate from emerald green to deep blue.

2

3

4

Activities

Half-day boat excursion: departure from the village, mangrove crossing, stop at the salt flats, swimming in Duna do Capim lagoons, and grilled fish lunch at Galos.

⏱ 240 min

Buggy ride across the dunes to the deserted beaches north of the peninsula. Sand crunches beneath the wheels, wind lifts white swirls—a desert bordered by the Atlantic.

⏱ 120 min

Meet local fishermen returning in their boats. Taste fresh oysters served waterside with a squeeze of lime—Galinhos gastronomy is experienced barefoot in the sand.

Sao Miguel Do Gostoso

★★★★☆
1

São Miguel do Gostoso occupies Ponta do Santo Cristo, one of South America's easternmost points. Trade winds blow here with rare consistency—the region is among the windiest places on the planet.

⏱ 60 min
2

Praia de Tourinhos, semi-deserted, harbors a petrified dune 8 meters high—a unique geological formation sculpted by wind over millennia. Reefs form natural pools at low tide.

⏱ 75 min
3

Four urban beaches string along the village: Ponta do Santo Cristo for kitesurfing, Cardeiro for natural pools, Xepa for atmosphere, and Maceió for fresh seafood facing lush vegetation.

⏱ 60 min
4

Igreja São Miguel Arcanjo, in the village center, bears the patron saint's name that gave the town its identity. Around it, dirt streets preserve the atmosphere of an authentic Nordeste fishing village.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Kitesurfing or windsurfing session at Ponta do Santo Cristo. Winds of 20-25 knots blow nearly every day in season—world-class conditions with 29°C water.

⏱ 180 min

Sunset at Praia de Tourinhos: grazing light sets the petrified dune and brown cliffs ablaze. Dried fish and ice-cold beer on the beach—Gostoso's evening ritual.

⏱ 90 min

Buggy ride along the coast to Praia do Marco, where the first colonial marker was planted in Brazil in 1501. Thirty kilometers of virgin beaches, dunes, and coconut palms roll beneath the wheels.

⏱ 120 min

Genipabu dunes, 25 km north of Natal, form a massive shifting sand field in perpetual motion. Constant wind sculpts crests and valleys that change shape weekly—a miniature Sahara facing the Atlantic.

⏱ 75 min

Pipa

★★★★☆
1

The Chapadão dominates the coast from atop reddish cliffs eroded by wind and waves. This promontory offers panoramic views of the Atlantic, Praia do Amor below, and the rainforest carpeting the ravines.

⏱ 60 min
2

Descend to Praia do Amor, whose bay forms a heart shape from the sky at low tide. Powerful waves make this Pipa's historic surf spot. Cliffs frame the beach like walls of a natural amphitheater.

⏱ 75 min
3

Baía dos Golfinhos (Dolphin Bay) hides at the foot of vegetation-covered cliffs. Accessible at low tide by beach or by boat, it shelters a permanent dolphin colony that leaps just meters from shore.

⏱ 75 min
4

Santuário Ecológico de Pipa protects a fragment of semi-arid Atlantic forest. Its 16 trails traverse cliffs, dunes, and viewpoints where you'll spot marmosets, exotic birds, and sometimes sea turtles during nesting season.

⏱ 90 min
5

Praia do Madeiro unfolds its golden sand at the foot of cliffs draped in tropical forest. A sea turtle preservation zone, this beach has been ranked among the world's most beautiful by international travelers.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Morning surf at Praia do Amor: consistent waves and Nordeste swell provide steady conditions. Rent a board on site and glide facing the cliffs—a setting that exists nowhere else.

⏱ 120 min

Speedboat excursion to Baía dos Golfinhos and Guaraíras lagoon. Dolphins accompany the boat; the lagoon opens onto mangrove landscapes gilded by evening light.

⏱ 150 min

Hike through the ecological sanctuary at sunset. Trails emerge onto vertiginous viewpoints above the ocean—with luck, a pod of dolphins plays in the waves below.

⏱ 90 min

Evening stroll through Pipa's lively center. Cobbled streets overflow with seafood restaurants, cocktail bars, and live music—the cosmopolitan vibe of the Nordeste's most festive village.

⏱ 90 min
