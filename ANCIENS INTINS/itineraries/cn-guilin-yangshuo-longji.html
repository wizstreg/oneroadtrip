Karst Heartlands: Guilin, Yangshuo & Longji Rice Terraces

📅 9 days📍 9 stops🚗 353 km⏱ 10h45

Limestone peaks rising like giant brushes, the Li River winding between summits, bamboo rafts gliding in silence, rice terraces sculpted over seven centuries by the Zhuang and Yao. Guangxi unfolds the landscapes that inspired traditional Chinese painting.

✨ Highlights
  • 83 km cruise down the Li River through hundreds of karst pinnacles
  • Motor-free bamboo raft drift on the Yulong River
  • Longji rice terraces over 650 years old
  • Encounters with Zhuang and Red Yao minorities in their villages
📆 Best period

April, May, September, October

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 3 and 7 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming flowers, ideal weather, moderate crowds, excellent for most regions, comfortable travel.

Fall (September-October): Perfect weather, clear skies, comfortable temperatures, peak tourist season, higher prices, autumn colors beautiful.

Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, rainy season, crowded tourist sites, highest prices, but best for Tibet and western regions.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, fewer crowds, lower prices, perfect for southern regions, harsh in north, unique winter experiences.

Regional Variations: Vast climate diversity, southern subtropical, northern continental, western plateau, plan according to regions visited.

Day 1

Guilin

Where limestone towers rise straight from the Li River's flow

★★★★☆
1

Reed Flute Cave (Lúdíyán), 5 km northwest of downtown, takes its name from the reeds that once grew at its entrance—perfect for making bamboo flutes. Carved by karst erosion roughly 700,000 years ago, the cave extends 240 meters deep with 500 meters of galleries open to visitors. Seventy-seven ink inscriptions on the walls date back to the Tang dynasty (618–907), making this one of China's oldest documented tourist sites. The central hall, the Crystal Palace of the Dragon King, measures 93 meters wide and 18 meters high. During World War II, over a thousand Guilin residents sheltered here during Japanese bombing raids.

⏱ 90 min
2

Elephant Trunk Hill (Xiàngbíshān), where the Taohua River meets the Li River, is Guilin's emblem. This 55-meter limestone pinnacle, formed 360 million years ago on an ancient seabed, depicts an elephant dipping its trunk into the water. Between trunk and body opens the Shuiyue Cave (Moon in the Water), 12 meters high and 9.5 meters wide—at night, its arch and reflection form a perfect circle on the river. At the summit stands Puxian Pagoda, built during the Ming dynasty (1368–1644), rising about 14 meters. Legend tells of a celestial elephant sent by the Jade Emperor who fell in love with Guilin and refused to return; enraged, the Emperor pierced it with a sword, transforming it into stone.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Evening stroll along the Two Rivers and Four Lakes circuit (Liǎng Jiāng Sì Hú). The Sun and Moon Pagodas, rising from Shanhu Lake, light up after dark—the Sun Pagoda, cast in bronze, stands 41 meters tall across 9 stories. The route follows willow-lined and banyan-shaded banks, offering views of illuminated karst peaks in the heart of the city.

⏱ 75 min
Day 2

Guilin

Where limestone towers rise straight from the Li River's flow

★★★★☆
1

Solitary Beauty Peak (Dúxiù Fēng) and the Jingjiang Princes' Palace occupy the exact center of Guilin. The palace was built between 1372 and 1392 for Zhu Shouqian, great-nephew of the Ming dynasty founder—its construction predates Beijing's Forbidden City. Fourteen princes across twelve generations lived here for 257 years. Under the Qing, the palace became an imperial examination center where over 590 scholars trained. The peak itself—a 66-meter limestone pillar formed 350 million years ago—bears 136 rock inscriptions on its flanks, the most famous carved over 800 years ago: "Guilin's scenery is the finest under heaven." Climb 306 steps to the summit for a 360° view over the city and Li River.

⏱ 90 min
2

Seven Star Park (Qīxīng Gōngyuán), Guilin's largest at 120 hectares, takes its name from seven karst peaks arranged like the Big Dipper. A destination since the Sui and Tang dynasties (6th–10th century), it shelters the Seven Star Cave, whose galleries stretch over 1.5 km through Putuo Mountain—an ancient underground river roughly one million years old. Entry is through the Flower Bridge (Huā Qiáo), Guilin's oldest bridge, built during the Song dynasty (960–1279). At the foot of Crescent Mountain, the Guihai Forest of Steles assembles 218 engraved tablets from the Tang through Qing dynasties, considered China's second-largest stele collection after Xi'an.

⏱ 120 min

Activities

Tasting Guilin rice noodles (Guìlín mǐfěn) at a downtown shop. The local recipe uses a pork and beef broth slowly simmered with spices, served over fresh rice noodles with braised meat and pickled vegetables. Every neighborhood has its variant—try the smoked horse version (mǎròu mǐfěn), a local specialty since the Qin dynasty.

⏱ 45 min

Day 3

Yangshuo

Karst dreamscape explored by bamboo raft and bicycle

★★★☆☆
1

Li River cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo—83 km in 4 to 5 hours of navigation through hundreds of karst pinnacles sculpted by millions of years of erosion. Departure is from Zhujiang pier, 30 minutes from downtown Guilin. The most spectacular section stretches between Yangdi and Xingping, where peaks close in on both sides of the waterway. Nine Horses Fresco Hill (Jiǔmǎ Huàshān) unfolds a cliff over 400 meters high and 200 meters wide, whose natural reliefs trace nine horses in different poses. Shortly after, the Yellow Cloth Shoal (Huángbù Tān) offers the panorama printed on the back of the 20-yuan note: seven peaks reflected in the calm water like a mirror. Arrival in Yangshuo by early afternoon.

⏱ 270 min

Activities

Wandering through West Street (Xī Jiē) in Yangshuo. This 517-meter pedestrian street, paved with marble slabs, is lined with Ming and Qing architecture converted into shops, cafés, and workshops. After dark, the atmosphere shifts with red lanterns and little street restaurants where you can taste beer fish (píjiǔ yú), Yangshuo's signature dish: Li River fish cooked in local beer sauce.

⏱ 90 min
Day 4

Yangshuo

Karst dreamscape explored by bamboo raft and bicycle

★★★☆☆
1

Gongnong Bridge, at the confluence of the Yulong and Jinbao rivers, offers one of the region's finest views: bamboo rafts gliding between karst peaks whose reflections ripple on the water. It's the ideal starting point for observing the countryside at dawn or sunset.

⏱ 30 min
2

The Big Banyan Tree (Dà Róngshù), a few kilometers south of Yangshuo on the Ten-Mile Gallery, is a thousand-year-old tree standing 17 meters tall with a trunk 7 meters around. Its age is estimated at over 1,400 years, making it contemporary with the founding of Yangshuo county. Its cascading branches cover approximately 1,300 m². Scenes from the film Liu Sanjie (1961), a Chinese cinema classic, were shot nearby.

⏱ 30 min

Activities

Bamboo raft drift down the Yulong River, a tributary of the Li. The Jima–Gongnong Bridge section (about 6 km, 90 minutes) traverses the heart of karst scenery in silence: rafts are poled by the boatman with no motor. You pass under centuries-old stone bridges, between rice paddies and water buffalo, with peaks as backdrop.

⏱ 90 min

Cycling the Ten-Mile Gallery (Shílǐ Huàláng), a 10 km scenic corridor linking Yangshuo to Moon Hill. The road, closed to cars during the day, winds between rice paddies, villages, and karst peaks. You pass Butterfly Spring park, the Totem Path, and several stone bridges. Bike or e-scooter rentals are easy to arrange from Yangshuo.

⏱ 120 min
Day 5

Yangshuo

Karst dreamscape explored by bamboo raft and bicycle

★★★☆☆
1

Moon Hill (Yuèliàng Shān) is a 380-meter limestone peak pierced by a natural arch roughly 50 meters high and equally wide—the remnant of an ancient cave carved by karst erosion. From the road, the opening shifts shape with your viewing angle: crescent, half-moon, full moon. A trail of 800 marble steps leads to the arch in 20 to 30 minutes. At the summit, the panoramic view embraces all of Yangshuo county and the Yulong River. Moon Hill is also a climbing mecca: American climber Todd Skinner equipped the first routes here in the early 1990s, launching sport climbing in the region.

⏱ 90 min
2

Fuli, a thousand-year-old town 8 km east of Yangshuo on the Li River's banks, is nicknamed "the town of painted fans." A large portion of its inhabitants practice the craft of folding fans, made from local mao bamboo and rice paper, decorated with Guangxi landscapes. The main street is lined with workshops where you can paint your own fan. The town also preserves Nuo culture—ritual music and dances inherited from primitive tribes—and a temple dedicated to the goddess Mazu, brought by immigrants from Fujian and Guangdong who make up much of the population.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Countryside hike between Fuli and Liugong village along dirt paths following the Li River. The trail crosses hamlets where traditional houses nestle beside rice paddies, pomelo orchards, and resting water buffalo. Allow about 2 hours for a relaxed walk.

⏱ 120 min
Day 6

Yangshuo

Karst dreamscape explored by bamboo raft and bicycle

★★★☆☆
1

Xingping, a town founded in 265 CE, is one of the oldest settlements along the Li River, 25 km northeast of Yangshuo. Its lanes paved with blue-gray slabs are lined with white-walled houses. Precisely here, at the bend in the Li River, you'll find the panorama printed on the 20-yuan note. To reach the exact viewpoint from the bill, walk or cycle about 2 km to Yuanbao Hill.

⏱ 60 min
2

Climb Laozhai Mountain (老寨山) from Xingping: a steep trail of about 30 minutes leads to the summit where you overlook the Li River's meander at 180°, with karst peaks aligned as backdrop. This is the region's most celebrated photographic viewpoint—the same panorama that generations of traditional Chinese painters tried to capture in ink.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Cycling or walking in the countryside around Xingping. A dirt path follows the Li River's bank south to Yucun village (about 2 km), where you observe the 20-yuan panorama from a different angle, from the riverbank. Continuing inland, trails cross orange groves and rice paddies framed by peaks, far from tourist circuits. Allow half a day to explore at your own pace.

⏱ 120 min

Day 7

Longji

Seven centuries of terraces carved by Zhuang and Red Yao hands

★★★★★
1

The Ping'an rice terraces in the Longji massif (Dragon's Backbone), about 80 km from Guilin, spread thousands of stepped plots between 600 and 800 meters altitude. Construction of these terraces began under the Yuan dynasty (1271–1368) and concluded in the early Qing dynasty (1644–1911)—over 400 years of work by Zhuang communities. The "Nine Dragons and Five Tigers" viewpoint shows nine ridges undulating like dragons bending to drink from the Jinsha River, while five tiger-shaped rocks frame them. The second panorama, "Seven Stars Surrounding the Moon," shows seven small hills encircling a crescent-shaped rice paddy.

⏱ 150 min
2

Ping'an Zhuang village was the first Longji terraces site opened to visitors. Its traditional wooden stilt houses, the oldest dating from the Qing dynasty, cling to the slope between terraces. The Zhuang, one of China's 55 ethnic minority groups, perpetuate their architecture, textile crafts, and mountain cuisine—taste rice cooked in bamboo tubes (zhútǒng fàn), fragrant and smoky.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike from Ping'an to the old Zhuang village of Longji (Gǔzhuāng) via a flagstone trail descending through the terraces. Allow 40 minutes to 1h30 depending on pace. The ancient village preserves the region's best-kept stilt houses, some over 150 years old. The return can be made on the same path or by shuttle.

⏱ 120 min
Day 8

Longji

Seven centuries of terraces carved by Zhuang and Red Yao hands

★★★★★
1

The Jinkeng Dazhai terraces, Longji's most extensive, cover 40 to 50 km² between 300 and 1,100 meters altitude. Dazhai village is inhabited by Red Yao people, recognizable by their spectacular hairstyles—women cut their hair only once in a lifetime and coil it into buns that can exceed a meter long. Three viewpoints mark the ascent: viewpoint #2, "Thousand Layers to Heaven" (Qiāncéng Tiāntī), closest to the village, shows terraces stacked like a giant stairway; #1, "Music of West Mountain" (Xīshān Sháolè), at 1,180 meters, is the highest and offers the best sunrise view; #3, "Golden Buddha Peak" (Jīnfó Dǐng), is accessible by cable car from Dazhai's parking area.

⏱ 180 min
2

Huangluo Yao village, below the Dazhai terraces, is listed in the Guinness Book of Records for the highest concentration of long-haired women. The Red Yao perpetuate the tradition of the "Clothes Drying Festival" (Shài Yījiē), an annual celebration where brightly embroidered costumes are displayed on wooden balconies. You'll discover embroidery techniques, indigo dyeing, and local hot pepper sauce making.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike from Dazhai to Ping'an crossing through Zhongliu village—a 4 to 5 hour route through the most remote and least-visited terraces. The trail alternates between ridges and valleys, with changing views over the rice fields at each pass. Bring good shoes—the path is narrow and slippery in wet weather.

⏱ 270 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 100 km · 2h
Day 9

Guilin

Where limestone towers rise straight from the Li River's flow

★★★★☆
1

Daxu old town (Dàxū), 15 km southeast of Guilin on the Li River's banks, is one of Guangxi's four best-preserved ancient towns. Founded in 200 BCE, it was an important trading post on the Li River. Its lanes paved with river pebbles are lined with wooden and stone houses from the Ming and Qing dynasties. The covered market, still active, sells fermented tofu, medicinal herbs, and local basketwork.

⏱ 90 min

Activities

Final shopping and tasting at Guilin's central market: Guangxi tea, Flower Bridge hot pepper sauce, osmanthus cakes (guìhuā gāo)—the osmanthus flower is Guilin's emblem, whose name literally means "osmanthus forest."

⏱ 60 min
