Hidden China: Tibetan Gansu, Qinghai & Jiuzhaigou

📅 15 days📍 15 stops🚗 1547 km⏱ 26h10

From the monastery corridors of Labrang to the turquoise pools of Jiuzhaigou, via Rebkong's thangka ateliers, the sky-mirror lake of Chaka, and the borderland Tibetan villages of Langmusi. An adventure itinerary through accessible Chinese Tibet—no permit required.

✨ Highlights
  • Labrang Monastery - 1,600 monks, 3.5 km kora of prayer wheels
  • Rebkong's thangka art in Tongren - UNESCO workshops in every house
  • Qinghai Lake - 100,000 migratory birds from May through July
  • Chaka Salt Lake - mirror reflection of sky and mountains
  • Langmusi - two monasteries facing each other, Tibetan and Muslim culture
  • Jiuzhaigou - 114 turquoise lakes in a cradle of forest and waterfalls
📆 Best period

June, July, August, September

⏳ How many days should I plan?

To make the most of it, plan between 8 and 12 days.

☀️ What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming flowers, ideal weather, moderate crowds, excellent for most regions, comfortable travel.

Fall (September-October): Perfect weather, clear skies, comfortable temperatures, peak tourist season, higher prices, autumn colors beautiful.

Summer (June-August): Hot and humid, rainy season, crowded tourist sites, highest prices, but best for Tibet and western regions.

Winter (November-March): Cold temperatures, fewer crowds, lower prices, perfect for southern regions, harsh in north, unique winter experiences.

Regional Variations: Vast climate diversity, southern subtropical, northern continental, western plateau, plan according to regions visited.

🌍 What to discover in CN?

Ancient civilization meets modern superpower with Great Wall, forbidden cities, and cutting-edge technology. Experience diverse landscapes, incredible cuisine, and 5000-year cultural heritage. The country offers bustling megacities, pristine nature, and traditional arts. China provides overwhelming cultural richness and geographical diversity in rapidly modernizing nation.

📋 Important notes

High-altitude adventure itinerary (2,600 to 3,700 m) through the Tibetan regions of Gansu, Qinghai, and northern Sichuan. Acclimatization necessary—allow 1 to 2 days before any exertion. Roads sometimes unpaved between Langmusi and Songpan. Best season: June through September (green grasslands, Tibetan festivals, migratory birds at Qinghai Lake). Avoid December-February (snowy roads, extreme cold).

Day 1

Xiahe

Where the world's longest prayer wheel corridor encircles sacred halls

★★★★★
1

Labrang Monastery, established in 1709, ranks among the six great monasteries of the Gelugpa school (Yellow Hats) of Tibetan Buddhism and stands as the largest outside the Tibet Autonomous Region. Around 1,600 monks study here across six colleges covering philosophy, medicine, astronomy, and tantra. The Grand Prayer Hall, supported by 140 massive pillars, accommodates up to 3,000 monks for collective chanting. At its early 20th-century peak, Labrang housed nearly 4,000 monks.

⏱ 120 min
2

The Labrang kora—the circumambulation circuit—stretches 3.5 km around the monastery, lined with over 1,700 hexagonal painted prayer wheels. This is the world's longest prayer wheel corridor. Tibetan pilgrims walk it clockwise from dawn, spinning each wheel as they pass. A complete circuit takes about an hour.

⏱ 75 min
3

The golden Gongtang Stupa, within the monastery complex, houses relics of a revered lama. Its gilded roof gleams in the sunlight above the monastery's whitewashed roofs. From the hill behind, the panorama embraces the entire monastery, the Daxia River, and the surrounding grasslands.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Hike up the hill south of the monastery for a sweeping view over Labrang, the golden rooftops, and the Daxia River valley. The trail starts behind the white stupa to the left of the complex and takes roughly 45 minutes uphill.

⏱ 75 min

Day 2

Xiahe

Where the world's longest prayer wheel corridor encircles sacred halls

★★★★★
1

Sangke Prairie, 14 km from Xiahe, unfolds at the foot of rolling hills at 3,000 meters elevation. Tibetan nomadic families graze yaks and sheep here. In summer, wildflowers paint the steppe in color and yurt camps welcome overnight visitors.

⏱ 75 min
2

Ganjia Prairie, quieter than Sangke, preserves the ruins of the octagonal Bajiao fortress, a rammed-earth citadel visible from the road. Nomads here live in black yak-hair tents, raising horses and sheep as their ancestors did.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

If your visit coincides with a monastic debate day at Labrang (typically afternoons), monks square off in the courtyard, clapping their hands to punctuate philosophical arguments—a striking auditory and visual spectacle.

⏱ 60 min

Sample yak yogurt at the shops along Xiahe's main street. The local yogurt, thick as custard and slightly tangy, is served in ceramic bowls with sugar or honey.

⏱ 30 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 110 km · 2h30
Day 3

Tongren Rebkong

Every household is an atelier for UNESCO-listed thangka art

★★★★☆
1

Longwu (Rongwo) Monastery, founded in 1301, is the largest monastery in Rebkong and the third most important in the Amdo region. Originally of the Sakya tradition, it was later converted to the Gelugpa school. Around 400 monks reside here in a labyrinth of prayer halls with walls covered in murals and gilded statues.

⏱ 75 min
2

The upper and lower Wutun villages, 10 km from Tongren, are the birthplace of Regong art—thangka painting, sculpture, embroidery, and architectural decoration inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list since 2009. Nearly every household is a workshop. Artists use mineral pigments (gold, silver, turquoise, malachite, saffron, indigo) that keep colors from fading for centuries.

⏱ 90 min
3

The Regong Art Museum in Tongren (free admission) displays over 2,000 pieces from the 11th century to the present: thangkas, sculptures, embroideries. The collection traces the evolution of this art form, which blends Indian, Nepalese, and Chinese influences into a style unique to Amdo.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit a thangka workshop in Wutun village. Master painters work with a two-hair brush for the finest details, following the strict proportions of the Buddhist "Lection of Measurement." A small thangka requires at least a month; large pieces, several years.

⏱ 75 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 180 km · 2h30
Day 4

Qinghai Lake

Where 100,000 migratory birds meet the endless blue

★★★★☆
1

Qinghai Lake (Kokonor in Mongolian, meaning "Blue Sea") is China's largest lake: 4,500 km² of saltwater at roughly 3,200 meters elevation, framed by four mountain ranges peaking between 3,600 and 5,200 meters. Elliptical in shape, it freezes solid in winter. Tibetan Buddhist pilgrims circumambulate the lake—360 km that takes 18 days on horseback or 23 days on foot.

⏱ 90 min
2

The Erlangjian scenic area on the south shore offers the best lake access. A jetty extends into the turquoise water with views of the snow-capped Datong Mountains to the north. On clear days, you can make out the sand dunes of Shadao Island on the far shore. In July, fields of flowering rapeseed encircle the lake in a brilliant yellow belt.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Bike tour along Qinghai Lake's south shore. The Tour of Qinghai Lake, a professional cycling race held each July alongside the Tour de France, popularized this route. At 3,200 meters, the effort is real, but the scenery—turquoise lake, grasslands, yaks, snowy peaks—more than compensates.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 10 km · 15min
Day 5

Qinghai Lake

Where 100,000 migratory birds meet the endless blue

★★★★☆
1

Bird Island (Niaodao), on the lake's northwest shore, welcomes over 100,000 migratory birds each spring from Southeast Asia—bar-headed geese, whooper swans, cormorants, black-necked cranes. The island itself has been closed to visitors since 2017 to protect habitat, but shoreline observatories allow viewing of the colonies from May through July.

⏱ 75 min
2

Sand Island (Shadao), on the northeast shore, juxtaposes sand dunes and lake in a striking contrast. The area spans 120 km² with dunes rising several dozen meters high against the turquoise water.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Visit a Tibetan nomadic camp on the grasslands bordering the lake. Families offer yak butter tea, tsampa (roasted barley flour), and share daily pastoral life—milking yaks, making dried cheese.

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 150 km · 2h30
Day 6

Chaka

The salt lake where heaven and earth become one

★★★★☆
1

Chaka Salt Lake ("salt lake" in Tibetan), at 3,059 meters elevation, covers 105 km²—ten times the area of Hangzhou's West Lake. Its salt layer reaches 5 meters deep in places. In calm weather, the lake's surface reflects the sky and snowy Qilian Mountains like a perfect mirror, earning it the nickname "Mirror of the Sky." National Tourism Geographic Magazine ranked it among the "55 places to see in a lifetime."

⏱ 120 min
2

The old salt railway tracks, built in the 1950s to transport production, extend into the lake. Now abandoned, they serve as a photographic promenade. A tourist train runs between different areas of the site. Salt exploitation in the region dates back over 3,000 years, to the era of the Qiang people.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Photography at sunrise or sunset on Chaka Lake—the best times for the mirror effect are before 9am and after 5pm, when the wind is low and light rakes across the surface. Wear brightly colored clothing (red, white, yellow) for reflections. Don't walk barefoot on the salt crust (cuts).

⏱ 90 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 300 km · 4h
Day 7

Xining

Gateway to the plateau, where butter sculptures bloom in winter

★★☆☆☆
1

Kumbum Monastery (Ta'er Si), 25 km from Xining, was founded in 1583 on the birthplace of Tsongkhapa, reformer of Tibetan Buddhism and founder of the Gelugpa school. Around 600 monks reside here. The site is famous for its yak butter sculptures (created in winter when hands don't melt the butter) and its eight white stupas aligned before the main entrance.

⏱ 120 min
2

Kumbum's main hall blends Tibetan and Chinese architecture—golden roofs, white walls, red pillars. The Golden Tree Sanctuary houses a sandalwood tree that legend says grew where Tsongkhapa was conceived, bearing an image of Buddha on each of its 100,000 leaves (hence "Kumbum"—"hundred thousand images").

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Stroll through Xining's Tibetan market: turquoise and coral jewelry, Tibetan knives, incense, tsampa, and brick-compressed tea. Xining, at 2,275 meters, serves as a supply and acclimatization city before the higher-altitude stages.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 280 km · 4h
Day 8

Langmusi

A village where two provinces meet and prayer flags fly above red gorges

★★☆☆☆
1

The village of Langmusi ("Tiger Temple" in Tibetan—a tiger cave once stood at the monastery site) is split in two by a river marking the border between Gansu and Sichuan. Each province has its own monastery, school, police station, and hospital. Tibetan Buddhists and Hui Muslims coexist in this high-altitude hamlet (3,300 m) with an alpine-village feel.

⏱ 60 min
2

Kirti Monastery, on the Sichuan side, is the larger of the two: founded in 1413, expanded in 1748, it houses around 700 monks. Its particularity is preserving the intact body of the 5th Kirti Lama, displayed every 3 to 5 days. Behind the monastery, Namo Gorge marks the source of the Bailong (White Dragon) River.

⏱ 75 min
3

Sertri Monastery, on the Gansu side, dates from 1748. Smaller but recognizable by its golden roofs, it overlooks the town from a hill. Above, a sky burial (jhator) site is still in use—bodies of the deceased are offered to vultures according to Tibetan Buddhist tradition. The place is sacred and visitors must not attend ceremonies.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Hike through the red gorges behind Sertri Monastery. The trail climbs through wildflower meadows toward Red Mountain (Chongpoma), a red sandstone formation visible from the entire village. At the summit, prayer flags flutter facing snowy peaks. Allow 2 hours round trip.

⏱ 120 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 5 km · 10min
Day 9

Langmusi

A village where two provinces meet and prayer flags fly above red gorges

★★☆☆☆
1

Namo Gorge, at the back of Kirti Valley, shelters the source of the Bailong River in a cave lined with stalactites. The trail follows the river between moss-covered limestone walls in a primeval forest atmosphere.

⏱ 75 min

Activities

Horseback riding through the grasslands around Langmusi. Local agencies offer outings from half a day to three days through alpine pastures, nomadic camps, and remote valleys. Tibetan horses, small and sure-footed, are accustomed to steep trails.

⏱ 180 min

Meal at a Hui (Muslim) restaurant in Langmusi: hand-pulled noodles, grilled lamb skewers, flatbread. The Tibetan-Muslim coexistence gives the village a dual cuisine found nowhere else.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 2h
Day 10

Zoige

Where the Yellow River writes its first golden curve across the grassland

★★★★★
1

Flower Lake (Huahu), in the Zoige wetlands reserve, is a shallow lake surrounded by marshy grasslands at 3,400 meters elevation. From June through August, carpets of wildflowers surround its waters. Wooden boardwalks cross the marshes without damaging them. The reserve protects one of the world's largest high-altitude wetlands.

⏱ 75 min
2

The First Bend of the Yellow River at Tangke: the river, coming from the Tibetan Plateau, traces a monumental loop through the grassland here before turning north again. At sunset, from the viewing platform, the river gleams like a golden ribbon across the steppe. It's one of the most photographed landscapes of the eastern Tibetan Plateau.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Wildlife watching on the Zoige grasslands: black-necked cranes (protected species), Himalayan marmots, Tibetan foxes, and herds of domestic and wild yaks. Binoculars recommended.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 160 km · 3h
Day 11

Songpan

Ming ramparts guarding the ancient tea-and-horse caravan route

★★★★☆
1

Songpan, an ancient Ming-dynasty fortified town at 2,850 meters elevation, preserves three monumental gates and stone ramparts that straddle the Min River. The town was a strategic trading post on the tea route between Han China and Tibet—merchants exchanged tea, salt, horses, and furs here. The streets retain a Han, Tibetan, Qiang, and Hui mix.

⏱ 75 min
2

The covered bridge at the North Gate spans the Min River between two watchtowers. The encircling walls, 6.2 km long, were built under Emperor Hongwu's reign (14th century) to protect this key position at the empire's edge.

⏱ 45 min

Activities

Browse Songpan's market: Tibetan medicinal herbs (including the famous caterpillar fungus—yartsa gunbu), wild honey, dried yak cheese, and Qiang products. The town serves as a base for treks to Huanglong and Jiuzhaigou.

⏱ 60 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 55 km · 1h15
Day 12

Jiuzhaigou

Where crystal lakes hold forests and sky in perfect stillness

★★★★★
1

Huanglong ("Yellow Dragon") is a 700 km² UNESCO site in the Min Mountains. Its main attraction: 3.6 km of yellow travertine terraces formed by calcium deposits from a hot spring, creating hundreds of tiered pools filled with turquoise, blue, and green water. The Five-Color Pool, at the summit at 3,576 meters, is the trail's high point—its waters shift hue with the angle of light.

⏱ 180 min

Activities

Ascend on foot or by cable car to the summit of Huanglong (3,576 m), then descend on foot along the travertine terraces (3.6 km, roughly 3 hours). Oxygen bottles are sold at the entrance for visitors sensitive to altitude. The trail crosses conifer forest, waterfalls, and Taoist temples before reaching the pools.

⏱ 180 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 100 km · 2h
Day 13

Jiuzhaigou

Where crystal lakes hold forests and sky in perfect stillness

★★★★★
1

Jiuzhaigou Valley ("Valley of Nine Villages") covers 720 km² in the Min Mountains, between 2,000 and 4,764 meters elevation. Inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1992, it takes its name from nine Tibetan villages. Its 114 lakes in turquoise, blue, green, and violet waters owe their colors to high calcium carbonate concentration and algae. Lake bottoms are visible at depths of several meters.

⏱ 120 min
2

Five-Flower Lake, in the Rize Valley, is considered the most beautiful: its translucent water reveals a bottom of submerged logs between travertine beaches, in gradients of turquoise and emerald. Further up, Panda Lake owes its name to the giant pandas that once came to drink here—around 20 pandas still live within the park boundaries.

⏱ 90 min
3

Nuorilang Waterfall, 270 meters wide, is China's widest travertine waterfall. Partially damaged by the magnitude 7 earthquake of August 2017, it was restored and the park reopened in 2019.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Shuttle and walking route through Rize Valley (right branch of the Y): the bus climbs to the primeval forest at the valley's end, then the descent is on foot along wooden boardwalks that skirt the lakes. Allow 4 to 5 hours for the complete valley.

⏱ 240 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 3 km · 10min
Day 14

Jiuzhaigou

Where crystal lakes hold forests and sky in perfect stillness

★★★★★
1

Long Lake (Chang Hai), in Zechawa Valley (left branch of the Y), is the largest, deepest (103 meters), and highest lake in Jiuzhaigou, at 3,150 meters. Stretching 7.5 km, surrounded by forested mountains, it has no outflow stream—water seeps through the karst bottom. Local legend speaks of a monster in its depths.

⏱ 60 min
2

Five-Color Pond (Wucai Chi), despite its modest size, offers Jiuzhaigou's most colorful waters—blue, green, yellow, turquoise, and violet mingle according to depth, minerals, and algae. It's one of the smallest lakes but the most intense in color.

⏱ 45 min
3

Mirror Lake (Jing Hai), in Shuzheng Valley, perfectly reflects the surrounding mountains and forests in its motionless waters. The Tibetan village of Shuzheng, one of the original nine villages, preserves traditional houses and prayer wheels.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Route through Zechawa Valley and return via Shuzheng Valley (main trunk of the Y): Pearl Shoal's cascading waterfalls, Shuzheng Tibetan village, Rhinoceros Lake. Wooden boardwalks cross conifer forests and bamboo groves—habitat of the giant panda and Sichuan golden monkey.

⏱ 240 min
🚗Drive to next stop: 90 km · 1h30
Day 15

Jiuzhaigou

Where crystal lakes hold forests and sky in perfect stillness

★★★★★
1

Free morning in the town of Zhangzha, at the exit of Jiuzhaigou Valley. Tibetan shops offer turquoise jewelry, incense, thangkas, and ornate knives. The food market features plateau products: matsutake mushrooms, goji berries, wild honey, and dried yak cheese.

⏱ 60 min

Activities

Transfer to Jiuzhai-Huanglong Airport (1h30 drive, altitude 3,448 m) for a flight to Chengdu (45 min), Xi'an, or Beijing. The airport, perched on a mountain, offers a final panorama over the snow-capped peaks of the Min Mountains.

⏱ 90 min
