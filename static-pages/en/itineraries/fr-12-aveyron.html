Aveyron Road Trip: Causses, Tarn Gorges and Villages

Gorges carve through the causses, stone villages clinging to hillsides tell eight centuries of history, and the Roquefort caves burrow deep into the mountain. Aveyron blends Romanesque heritage with breathtaking landscapes.

📅 Best period May, June, September, October

Visit the Rodez Cathedral and the Soulages Museum, the villages of Belcastel, Conques, and Estaing — all listed among France's Most Beautiful Villages — admire the Millau Viaduct, a world record holder, explore the Roquefort caves, France's very first AOC, and the Laguiole knives.

  • Conques: Romanesque abbey church and Soulages stained glass windows
  • Roquefort: France's first AOC since 1925
  • Belcastel: Most Beautiful Village restored by Fernand Pouillon
  • Millau Viaduct: 343 metres high, a world record

Discover

1
The Soulages Museum opened in 2014 with nearly 500 works by the Rodez-born painter. The architecture by Catalan firm RCR Arquitectes features Corten steel that develops a patina over time. The galleries alternate between varying heights and half-light to showcase Soulages' creative periods.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Notre-Dame Cathedral required 300 years of construction, from the 13th to the 16th century. Its bell tower reaches 87 metres and offers a 360° view after climbing 400 steps. The interior houses ancient and contemporary stained glass windows, a monumental organ, and richly carved choir stalls.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Fenaille Museum holds the largest collection of statue-menhirs in Europe. These sculptures from the 3rd millennium BC remain enigmatic. The building occupies the Hôtel de Jouéry, saved from demolition in the early 20th century by Maurice Fenaille.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Follow the historic centre trail available from the tourist office on Place de la Cité. The numbered route takes you past the Maison d'Armagnac, the bishop's palace, and Renaissance façades.
⏱️ 90 min
→
The market takes place on Wednesday and Saturday mornings. You'll find Aveyron specialities there: farçous, aligot, and Aubrac beef. The brasseries in the centre serve these dishes throughout the week.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Belcastel Castle dates from the 11th century with its square keep and corner towers. Fernand Pouillon restored it from 1975 to 1982, involving numerous local craftsmen. Visit the water-filled moat, medieval chapels, and art galleries exhibiting contemporary works.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Alzias de Saunhac had the bridge built in the 15th century. It connects the Sainte-Madeleine Church to the village on the other bank of the Aveyron river. Passers-by once paid for their crossing with prayers and offerings at the altar stone.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The 15th-century Sainte-Madeleine Church houses the tomb of Alzias de Saunhac with its recumbent effigy. You will also see the Saunhac family coat of arms and four statues listed as historic monuments. Casimir Ferrer created an extraordinary Stations of the Cross for the church.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Maison de la Forge is over 200 years old and displays the tools of the blacksmith, farrier, clog-maker, and fisherman. Guides explain the use of the twitch to restrain horses and the tail docker.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Climb to the Roc d'Anglars located 500 m upstream from the village. This 5th-century fort occupies the heights above the Lourdou cave. The 3 km hike offers views of the village and the Aveyron river.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
The Sainte-Foy Abbey Church was built from 1041 by Abbot Odolric. The Last Judgement tympanum features 124 sculpted figures on 24 blocks of yellow limestone. The interior contains 250 Romanesque capitals spread over two levels.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Treasury houses the reliquary statue of Saint Foy, a unique piece of Carolingian goldsmith work set with gold and precious stones. This collection bears witness to nine centuries of pilgrimage on the Way of Saint James.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Pierre Soulages worked from 1987 to 1994 to create 104 stained glass windows for the abbey church. He carried out nearly 700 trials with master glassmaker Jean-Dominique Fleury to achieve a unique translucent glass. The light changes throughout the day thanks to variations in grain size.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Conques Nocturnes take place from May to October to illuminate the polychrome tympanum. The reimagined polychromy reveals the restored medieval colours.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Visit the abbey church galleries to get up close to the Soulages stained glass windows. The guided tour lasts 45 minutes and explores the painter's creative approach. Booking recommended, open to visitors aged 12 and above.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
Estaing Castle dates back to the 13th century with its octagonal keep surrounded by turrets. The d'Estaing family owned it for eight centuries before it was acquired by Valéry Giscard d'Estaing in 2005. The exhibition retraces the presidential term and the advances of his mandate.
⏱️ 75 min
2
François d'Estaing had the Gothic bridge built in the early 16th century. This UNESCO-listed structure is crossed each year by pilgrims heading to Santiago de Compostela. The wrought-iron cross in the middle is a symbol of Aveyron craftsmanship.
⏱️ 20 min
3
The 15th-century Church of Saint-Fleuret holds the relics of the Bishop of Clermont who died here in the 5th century. The saint is known for his miraculous healings. Grey-blue schist houses with lauze stone roofs line the narrow streets.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Maison de la Vigne, du Vin et des Paysages tells the story of the vineyard. The Vignerons d'Olt cultivate 20 hectares that earned the AOC Estaing designation in 2011. The wine was once shipped throughout Europe via the Lot river.
⏱️ 60 min
→
The Saint-Fleuret procession takes place on the first Sunday of July. Locals wear costumes representing saints, angels, and members of the d'Estaing family. This historical and religious re-enactment brings the whole village to life.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Entraygues comes from the Occitan entraigas meaning between the waters. The Lot and the Truyère rivers meet here at an altitude of 219 metres. Two 13th-century Gothic bridges connect the banks near the medieval castle.
⏱️ 45 min
2
The historic centre retains half-timbered houses from the 15th and 16th centuries. The cantous are small covered passageways running beneath the houses. Some façades still shelter craftsmen's workshops.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Entraygues-Le Fel vineyard covers 20 hectares of terraces between schist and granite. Five winemakers carry on a winemaking tradition dating back to the Roman occupation. The AOC was granted in 2011, following the VDQS classification of 1965.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Espace EDF Truyère occupies the former control room of the Couesques dam power station. This 600 m² educational trail explains how hydroelectricity works and the industrial adventure built around water.
⏱️ 75 min
→
The Calvaire viewpoint offers a panorama of the confluence of the Lot and the Truyère. Head towards Aurillac, cross the Cambeyrac dam and stop after a hairpin bend. A 5-minute path leads to the viewpoint marked by a large cross.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
Visit the cutlery workshops in the heart of Laguiole village. The cutlers handcraft each piece with the forged bee, chiselled spring, and guillochage patterns. Several manufacturers offer tours and assembly demonstrations.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The Forge de Laguiole lets you watch the complete making of a knife. The artisans hot-forge the blades, sculpt the bee with a file and punch, then assemble the different parts. Handles are made from precious wood, Aubrac horn, or stag antler.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Laguiole village is also the birthplace of the Aubrac cattle breed and the Laguiole AOC cheese. Burons dot the plateau where the cows graze. Aligot is the local speciality made from fresh tome cheese and potatoes.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the Aubrac plateau between 1,000 and 1,400 metres altitude. The landscapes alternate between summer pastures and peatlands. The traditional burons were used to make cheese during the transhumance.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Taste the aligot in the village restaurants. This mashed potato dish mixed with fresh tome cheese is beaten at length until it forms long ribbons. Each inn has its own recipe and technique.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
The Millau Viaduct reaches 343 metres in height, taller than the Eiffel Tower. Spanning 2,460 metres, it links the Causse Rouge to the Causse du Larzac. The visitor area features a 220 m² interactive exhibition and a viewing platform with free 24/7 access.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The glove-making workshops have been Millau's claim to fame since the 19th century. Maison Fabre has been making gloves in the same family since 1924. Visit the workshops to see the artisanal crafting of a pair of Made in Millau gloves.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The La Graufesenque site was one of the most important pottery centres of the Roman world. The Millau museum displays pieces that were exported across all Roman lands. The Belfry and the Baltard-style metal hall complete the town's heritage.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Explorers' Trail takes you under the viaduct deck with an Eiffage guide. The tour reveals the construction secrets of this steel giant. Booking required, the trail is suitable for all ages.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Millau is the capital of outdoor sports. Paragliding, rock climbing, via ferrata, canyoning, and mountain biking are all available on the surrounding causses and gorges. Certified instructors offer introductory sessions and courses.
⏱️ 180 min

Roquefort Sur Soulzon

6 photos
Day 8
Top hotels

9.5 Gite Aux Tourterelles
9.5 Gite Gabrielle
10 Gîte du Volcan des Causses

🔍

Discover

1
The Roquefort Société caves welcome over 100,000 visitors a year. The one-hour guided tour takes you through natural caves carved beneath the Combalou rock. The fleurines naturally ventilate the caves at 10°C with 80% humidity.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Penicillium roqueforti develops spontaneously in the caves. This mould gives the cheese its characteristic blue-green marbling and flavours. The master refiners carefully select and cultivate their own strains.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Roquefort was the very first AOC in history, granted in 1925. It celebrates its centenary in 2025. The AOP requires raw Lacaune ewe's milk, daily pasturing within the Roquefort area, and ageing in natural caves.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
A tasting of the three Roquefort AOP cheeses concludes the visit. The caves also offer workshops pairing wine and cheese. The standard tour costs €7.50, or €15 for the tour with a guided tasting.
⏱️ 45 min
→
The village is home to seven Roquefort producers. Maison Gabriel Coulet offers a free self-guided tour with a presentation film. The Papillon caves also provide discovery tours.
⏱️ 60 min

Saint Affrique

Top hotels

9.2 Gîte dans le sud Aveyron

Discover

1
Saint-Affrique stretches along the banks of the Sorgues in the heart of the Grands Causses Regional Natural Park. The Pont-Vieux, listed as a Historic Monument, connects the two banks. The centre retains stone houses, small squares, and vaulted passageways.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The Rocher de Caylus offers a panoramic view over the town and surrounding hills. A cross tops this landmark site. The Notre-Dame de la Miséricorde Church features a tall Gothic spire visible from afar.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Maison de la Mémoire du Pays Saint-Affricain displays objects, stories, and photographs of daily life in times past. The Tribunal des Arts occupies the former courthouse and exhibits works by local artists.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Saturday morning market brings the town centre to life. Producers sell local cheeses (Pérail, Roquefort, Recuite), charcuterie, and regional specialities. The atmosphere is friendly and Aveyron delicacies are plentiful.
⏱️ 90 min
→
The landscapes around Saint-Affrique range from blazing red rougiers to wild causses and green valleys. The Rougier de Camarès to the south offers spectacular red-earth terrain. Hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing are all available in the area.
⏱️ 120 min
