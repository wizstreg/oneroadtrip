Lake Allatoona ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Lake Allatoona, a sprawling 12,000-acre reservoir surrounded by forested hills and scenic shoreline offering excellent opportunities for swimming, boating, and lakeside relaxation. The clear waters and numerous sandy beaches make this destination perfect for families seeking outdoor recreation combined with natural beauty. Multiple marinas provide boat rentals for exploring hidden coves and peaceful inlets.
2
Explore the historic Allatoona Pass Battlefield, where dramatic red clay cliffs and a remarkable 175-foot-deep railroad cut tell the story of fierce Civil War combat. The strategic gorge and sweeping views over the lake create a striking landscape where history and natural beauty converge. Walking trails connect key battlefield sites with panoramic overlooks.
🎯

Activities

→
Hike the Pine Mountain Trail Loop for elevated perspectives over the shimmering lake waters, then camp under starlit skies at Red Top Mountain State Park. The area offers diverse recreational options from fishing and paddleboarding to scenic picnic areas along the forested shoreline.

Lookout Mountain ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Stand atop Lookout Mountain at Rock City Gardens, a remarkable natural park where ancient rock formations, lush gardens, and whimsical stone pathways lead to Lover's Leap overlook with views extending across seven states. The 180-foot suspension bridge provides exhilarating passage above forested ravines while cave grottos reveal fairytale scenes tucked into the mountain's mysterious recesses. Towering sandstone cliffs and carefully cultivated gardens create unforgettable vistas.
2
Descend into the mountain at Ruby Falls, where an underground pathway leads to a spectacular 145-foot waterfall cascading within an ancient limestone cavern. The illuminated cave formations and thundering waters create a subterranean spectacle unlike anything above ground. Guided tours reveal the geological history of this remarkable natural wonder.
🎯

Activities

→
Visit the dramatically positioned Covenant College, whose Gothic Revival stone buildings rise castle-like from the clifftops with commanding views across Georgia, Tennessee, and Alabama. Sunset Rock offers photographers ideal conditions for capturing the Blue Ridge Mountains bathed in golden evening light.

Brasstown Bald ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
Ascend Brasstown Bald, Georgia's highest peak at 4,784 feet, where an observation tower provides 360-degree panoramas across four states on clear days. The summit trail climbs through misty forests draped in wildflowers during spring and brilliant foliage in autumn, rewarding hikers with some of the most spectacular mountain views in the American Southeast. Interpretive exhibits at the visitor center explain the region's ecology and Cherokee heritage.
2
Explore the broader Northeast Georgia Mountains region, a landscape of forested ridges, cascading waterfalls, and crystal-clear streams that has attracted nature lovers for generations. Scenic byways wind through quaint mountain communities where local artisans create traditional crafts and family-owned vineyards produce award-winning wines. The area offers world-class hiking, fishing, and wildlife observation.
🎯

Activities

→
Visit Château Élan winery for tastings of Georgia wines accompanied by vineyard tours explaining traditional winemaking techniques. The rolling hills, elegant tasting rooms, and relaxing atmosphere make this an ideal introduction to the state's emerging wine region.

Tallulah Falls ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Marvel at Tallulah Gorge, a dramatic 2-mile-long chasm plunging nearly 1,000 feet deep where five magnificent waterfalls cascade over ancient rock formations. A suspension bridge spans the gorge 80 feet above the rushing Tallulah River, providing heart-stopping views of the canyon walls and roaring cascades. Free permits allow adventurous hikers to descend to the gorge floor for an intimate encounter with this geological wonder.
2
Explore over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails radiating from the gorge rim, each offering different perspectives of the dramatic landscape carved over millennia by rushing waters. The park's historic dam and interpretive center document the area's role in early hydroelectric development. Overlooks along the rim trail provide photographers with stunning compositions of waterfalls and canyon walls.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience the exhilarating natural waterslide at Sliding Rock, where smooth granite channels visitors into refreshing plunge pools surrounded by moss-covered boulders and echoing canyon walls. The nearby lake beach offers calmer swimming for those seeking relaxation after gorge adventures.

Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
Step into the charming Bavarian-themed village of Helen, where colorful Alpine architecture, flower-draped balconies, and cobblestone walkways create an unexpected European atmosphere nestled in the Blue Ridge Mountains. Over 200 specialty shops, authentic German bakeries, and traditional restaurants line the pedestrian-friendly streets beneath forested peaks. The town hosts the longest Oktoberfest celebration in America, spanning two full months of festive activities.
2
Hike to Anna Ruby Falls, a spectacular dual waterfall where two creeks plunge side by side into a misty pool surrounded by lush mountain forest. The accessible trail winds through hardwood groves and rhododendron thickets, offering a refreshing escape into pristine wilderness just minutes from town. Interpretive displays explain the area's unique ecology.

Activities

→
Sample wines at local vineyards producing distinctive Georgia varietals, then browse the Helen Arts & Heritage Center documenting the town's transformation from declining logging community to beloved tourist destination. The Chattahoochee River flows directly through town, perfect for tubing adventures.

Springer Mountain ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 7
🔍

Discover

1
Begin your Appalachian Trail experience at Springer Mountain, the legendary southern terminus of the 2,190-mile footpath stretching to Maine. Georgia's 79-mile section winds through misty forests, wildflower meadows, rhododendron tunnels, and offers spectacular panoramic overlooks. Standing at the bronze plaque marking Mile Zero creates a profound connection to one of America's greatest hiking traditions.
2
Trek to Blood Mountain, the highest point on Georgia's Appalachian Trail at 4,458 feet, where ancient stone shelter ruins and sweeping ridge views reward determined hikers. The challenging terrain passes through diverse ecosystems supporting black bears, deer, salamanders, and countless songbirds. Backcountry camping sites offer stargazing far from light pollution.
🎯

Activities

→
Visit the legendary Mountain Crossings outfitter at Neel Gap, the only building the Appalachian Trail passes directly through. The famous 'shoe tree' outside displays worn-out hiking boots left by through-hikers, many containing handwritten notes and memories. Detour to misty Long Creek Falls for a serene waterfall tucked into the forest.
🚗

Amicalola Falls ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 8
🔍

Discover

1
Stand before Amicalola Falls, Georgia's tallest waterfall tumbling 729 feet over a series of rock ledges into a lush forested gorge. The Cherokee name meaning 'tumbling waters' perfectly describes this spectacular cascade, one of the tallest waterfalls east of the Mississippi River. Trails to both the base and rim provide dramatically different perspectives of the thundering waters.
2
Hike through the surrounding state park where hardwood forests, mountain streams, and abundant wildlife create ideal conditions for nature photography and peaceful contemplation. The park serves as the southern approach to the Appalachian Trail, attracting hikers from around the world preparing for long-distance adventures. On-site naturalist programs offer archery, animal encounters, and guided wilderness exploration.
🎯

Activities

→
Follow the 8-mile Approach Trail connecting Amicalola Falls to the official southern terminus of the Appalachian Trail at Springer Mountain. Even short sections of this legendary pathway provide unforgettable hiking through pristine mountain wilderness frequented by deer, wild turkeys, and occasionally black bears.

Madison ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
3 photos
Day 9
🔍

Discover

1
Stroll through Madison's remarkably preserved antebellum district, where grand 19th-century mansions line oak-shaded streets virtually unchanged since the Civil War. Sherman's army famously spared this elegant town during the March to the Sea, preserving architectural treasures including Heritage Hall and the historic Rogers House. Manicured gardens, decorative fountains, and refined Southern storefronts create timeless elegance.
2
Visit Rose Cottage, built by Adeline Rose after she gained her freedom, offering moving insights into African American history in the antebellum South. The Steffen Thomas Museum showcases bold folk art in striking contrast to the town's refined classical architecture. Antique shops throughout the walkable historic district contain fascinating treasures from centuries past.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience Madison's genteel Southern hospitality while exploring the town's carefully preserved heritage spanning two centuries of American history. The intimate scale and exceptional preservation make this one of Georgia's most photographically rewarding destinations for lovers of historic architecture.

Macon ★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
2 photos
Day 10
🔍

Discover

1
Explore Ocmulgee Mounds National Historical Park, where massive earthen ceremonial mounds constructed over 1,000 years ago preserve evidence of sophisticated Native American civilization in the American Southeast. The reconstructed earth lodge contains an original clay floor where ancient councils gathered around a central fire pit. Museum exhibits display artifacts spanning 17,000 years of human habitation along the Ocmulgee River.
2
Discover Macon's rich musical heritage at the Tubman Museum, one of the largest museums in America dedicated to African American art, history, and culture. The city served as birthplace to music legends Little Richard and Otis Redding, whose influences still resonate through local venues and festivals. The Allman Brothers Band Museum preserves memorabilia from Southern rock's most influential group.
🎯

Activities

→
Tour the opulent Hay House, a stunning Italian Renaissance Revival mansion featuring original 19th-century furnishings and technological innovations ahead of their time. The Grand Opera House, with its plush red velvet interior, hosts performances in a magnificently restored 1884 theater. Window-shop along Cherry Street's indie boutiques in the revitalized downtown district.

4 photos
Day 11
🔍

Discover

1
Marvel at Providence Canyon, Georgia's 'Little Grand Canyon' where vivid walls of red, orange, pink, and white sandstone rise dramatically above wildflower-lined trails and pine forests. Poor farming practices in the 1800s triggered erosion that carved these spectacular formations, transforming agricultural failure into geological wonder. The Canyon Loop Trail winds through the colorful gorges offering photographers extraordinary compositions.
2
Discover rusted vintage automobiles from the 1950s along the quirky White Blaze Trail, abandoned relics slowly being reclaimed by the surrounding forest. Backcountry camping sites allow overnight visitors to experience canyon walls glowing golden at sunrise and sunset. The unexpected southwestern landscape feels transported from Arizona to rural Georgia.
🎯

Activities

→
Hike through pine-scented woods to remote backcountry campsites where crackling campfires beneath starry skies create unforgettable wilderness experiences. The park's dramatic terrain changes character throughout the day as shifting sunlight paints the multicolored canyon walls in ever-changing hues.

Okefenokee ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
2 photos
Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Enter the Okefenokee Swamp, a 438,000-acre wilderness where glassy blackwater channels wind through ancient cypress and tupelo forests draped in Spanish moss. This vast wetland, one of the largest intact freshwater ecosystems in North America, supports over 600 species including American alligators, river otters, and countless wading birds. The mysterious, primordial atmosphere creates a hauntingly beautiful landscape unlike anywhere else in the country.
2
Walk the elevated Chesser Island Boardwalk to an observation tower providing panoramic views across endless prairies of floating peat islands and wildlife-rich marshlands. The preserved homestead reveals how hardy pioneers once scratched out existence in this watery wilderness. The visitor center explains the swamp's unique ecology and cultural history.
🎯

Activities

→
Glide through mirror-calm waters on guided boat tours where experienced naturalists point out alligators basking on logs, herons stalking fish, and turtles sunning on cypress knees. The earthy, mossy scent of the wetlands and calls of countless birds create a sensory immersion in one of America's last great wild places.

Cumberland Island ★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
3 photos
Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Cumberland Island, Georgia's largest and wildest barrier island where 17 miles of pristine beaches stretch beneath towering dunes and maritime forests of moss-draped oaks. Wild horses descended from colonial-era stock roam freely across 36,000 acres of protected wilderness, creating unforgettable encounters along empty shorelines. The island remains deliberately undeveloped, accessible only by ferry from St. Marys.
2
Explore the haunting Dungeness Ruins, crumbling remains of Thomas Carnegie's Gilded Age mansion slowly being reclaimed by resurrection ferns and climbing vines. The grand Plum Orchard Mansion showcases meticulously preserved Carnegie-era architecture and furnishings. The historic First African Baptist Church and Stafford Plantation cemetery document the island's complex history of enslaved people and freed African Americans.
🎯

Activities

→
Hike or bike over 50 miles of trails winding through moss-draped maritime forest, past salt marshes teeming with wildlife, and along windswept beaches where loggerhead sea turtles nest each summer. Limited ferry capacity and minimal facilities preserve the island's remote, mysterious character for those willing to plan ahead.

Brunswick ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 14
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Brunswick, the historic mainland gateway to the Golden Isles, where Victorian architecture, tree-lined streets, and scenic tidal rivers create understated coastal charm. The centuries-old Lover's Oak, a massive live oak beneath which couples have traditionally pledged their devotion, spreads its ancient branches over a peaceful park. The Old Town district preserves authentic small-town Georgia character.
2
Tour Hofwyl-Broadfield Plantation, a remarkably intact antebellum rice plantation documenting the complex history of agriculture, slavery, and Southern society along Georgia's tidal rivers. The quiet marina and working waterfront reflect Brunswick's enduring connection to the sea. Cross the stunning Sidney Lanier Bridge for spectacular views of the coastal landscape.
🎯

Activities

→
Sample locally crafted spirits at Georgia's only rum distillery, where small-batch production creates distinctive rums with notes of dark cherry and caramel. The mainland location provides convenient access to all the Golden Isles while offering more affordable accommodations than the islands themselves.

Savannah ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
3 photos
Day 15
🔍

Discover

1
Fall under the spell of Savannah, one of America's most enchanting cities, where cobblestone streets wind past 22 oak-shaded squares draped in Spanish moss beneath grand antebellum mansions. Founded in 1733, the city's original grid plan created the intimate green spaces that define its romantic character today. The blend of architectural styles spanning three centuries creates streetscapes of extraordinary beauty and historical significance.
2
Explore the hauntingly beautiful Bonaventure Cemetery, where ornate Victorian monuments and moss-draped oaks create an atmosphere of melancholic grandeur overlooking the Wilmington River. The oak-canopied avenue through Wormsloe Historic Site, lined with 400 live oaks planted in the 1890s, ranks among the most photographed roads in the American South. The elegant Mercer-Williams House offers tours through Savannah's storied past.
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll along River Street where historic cotton warehouses now house eclectic shops, restaurants, and lively entertainment venues overlooking the Savannah River. Sunset paddlewheel cruises offer views of the brick waterfront accompanied by live jazz and Southern cuisine. Ghost tours after dark reveal the city's supernatural reputation as one of America's most haunted destinations.
→
Wander through the lush landscapes of Forsyth Park, anchored by an iconic 1858 fountain modeled after fountains in Paris and Cusco. The 30-acre park provides a green oasis for picnics, concerts, and people-watching beneath centuries-old trees. The surrounding Victorian district features some of Savannah's finest residential architecture.

Tybee Island ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
3 photos
Day 16
🔍

Discover

1
Escape to Tybee Island, Savannah's laid-back beach retreat where wide sandy shores, rolling dunes, and tranquil marshlands create a carefree coastal atmosphere. The historic lighthouse, Georgia's oldest and tallest, has guided mariners since 1736 and offers panoramic views from its observation deck after climbing 178 steps. The island maintains nostalgic beach-town charm with colorful cottages and relaxed local culture.
2
Explore the imposing Fort Pulaski National Monument, a masterpiece of 19th-century military engineering whose massive brick walls were breached by rifled cannon during the Civil War, forever changing the course of warfare. The moated fortification and surrounding marshlands offer walking trails rich with birdlife and historical significance. Interpretive programs bring the fort's dramatic history to life.
🎯

Activities

→
Swim and sunbathe at lively South Beach, spot dolphins offshore, and watch pelicans diving for fish as the sun sets over the Atlantic. The Tybee Pier and Pavilion area buzzes with activity while quieter stretches of shore provide peaceful escapes. Over 200 bird species make this island a rewarding destination for wildlife observation throughout the year.

Jekyll Island ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 17
🔍

Discover

1
Walk along Jekyll Island's ethereal Driftwood Beach, a surreal landscape where sun-bleached trees twisted into sculptural forms rise from golden sands. The entire island operates as a state park, ensuring conservation and wildlife protection across 5,700 acres of beaches, marshes, and maritime forests. This otherworldly scenery has attracted filmmakers, featuring in major productions including X-Men and The Walking Dead.
2
Explore the historic Millionaires' Village where Vanderbilts, Rockefellers, and Pulitzers once gathered in elegant 'cottages' that defined Gilded Age opulence. The Georgia Sea Turtle Center rescues and rehabilitates injured sea turtles, offering close encounters with these ancient mariners and their inspiring recovery stories. Passionate guides explain each turtle's journey from injury through rehabilitation.
🎯

Activities

→
Cycle the scenic island loop past serene marshes, under canopies of moss-draped oaks, and along beaches where dolphins frequently surface offshore. Picnic on windswept dunes while spotting wildlife including deer, alligators, and countless shorebirds in this carefully preserved coastal sanctuary.

St Simons Island ★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
2 photos
Day 18
🔍

Discover

1
Explore St. Simons Island, the largest of Georgia's Golden Isles, where wide golden beaches, moss-draped oaks, shimmering salt marshes, and charming village atmosphere create quintessential coastal Georgia scenery. The historic lighthouse dating to 1872 offers panoramic views from its tower after climbing 129 steps. Over 30 miles of bike paths wind past tree spirits carved into ancient oaks by local artists.
2
Visit Fort Frederica National Monument, the substantial ruins of a colonial-era British fortification established in 1736 to defend against Spanish incursion from Florida. The moss-covered walls and archaeological excavations reveal 18th-century military life on the frontier. Cannon's Point Preserve offers hiking through wild maritime forest where salty marsh aromas drift on ocean breezes.
🎯

Activities

→
Relax on sunlit East Beach, browse boutiques and galleries in the charming Pier Village, and watch spectacular sunsets paint the marshlands in brilliant colors. The island's laid-back atmosphere invites visitors to slow down and embrace the gentle rhythms of Georgia's coast.

  • Visit Château Élan winery for tastings of Georgia wines accompanied by vineyard tours explaining traditional winemaking techniques. The rolling hills, elegant tasting rooms, and relaxing atmosphere make this an ideal introduction to the state's emerging wine region.

Tallulah Falls ★★

Discover
  • Marvel at Tallulah Gorge, a dramatic 2-mile-long chasm plunging nearly 1,000 feet deep where five magnificent waterfalls cascade over ancient rock formations. A suspension bridge spans the gorge 80 feet above the rushing Tallulah River, providing heart-stopping views of the canyon walls and roaring cascades. Free permits allow adventurous hikers to descend to the gorge floor for an intimate encounter with this geological wonder.
  • Explore over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails radiating from the gorge rim, each offering different perspectives of the dramatic landscape carved over millennia by rushing waters. The park's historic dam and interpretive center document the area's role in early hydroelectric development. Overlooks along the rim trail provide photographers with stunning compositions of waterfalls and canyon walls.
Activities
  • Experience the exhilarating natural waterslide at Sliding Rock, where smooth granite channels visitors into refreshing plunge pools surrounded by moss-covered boulders and echoing canyon walls. The nearby lake beach offers calmer swimming for those seeking relaxation after gorge adventures.

Helen ★★

Discover
  • Step into the charming Bavarian-themed village of Helen, where colorful Alpine architecture, flower-draped balconies, and cobblestone walkways create an unexpected European atmosphere nestled in the Blue Ridge Mountains. Over 200 specialty shops, authentic German bakeries, and traditional restaurants line the pedestrian-friendly streets beneath forested peaks. The town hosts the longest Oktoberfest celebration in America, spanning two full months of festive activities.

The accessible trail winds through hardwood groves and rhododendron thickets, offering a refreshing escape into pristine wilderness just minutes from town.   • Sample wines at local vineyards producing distinctive Georgia varietals, then browse the Helen Arts & Heritage Center documenting the town's transformation from declining logging community to beloved tourist destination. Day 7

Springer Mountain ★★

Discover
  • Begin your Appalachian Trail experience at Springer Mountain, the legendary southern terminus of the 2,190-mile footpath stretching to Maine. Georgia's 79-mile section winds through misty forests, wildflower meadows, rhododendron tunnels, and offers spectacular panoramic overlooks. Standing at the bronze plaque marking Mile Zero creates a profound connection to one of America's greatest hiking traditions.
  • Trek to Blood Mountain, the highest point on Georgia's Appalachian Trail at 4,458 feet, where ancient stone shelter ruins and sweeping ridge views reward determined hikers. The challenging terrain passes through diverse ecosystems supporting black bears, deer, salamanders, and countless songbirds. Backcountry camping sites offer stargazing far from light pollution.
Activities
  • Visit the legendary Mountain Crossings outfitter at Neel Gap, the only building the Appalachian Trail passes directly through. The famous 'shoe tree' outside displays worn-out hiking boots left by through-hikers, many containing handwritten notes and memories. Detour to misty Long Creek Falls for a serene waterfall tucked into the forest.

Day 8

Amicalola Falls ★★

Discover
  • Stand before Amicalola Falls, Georgia's tallest waterfall tumbling 729 feet over a series of rock ledges into a lush forested gorge. The Cherokee name meaning 'tumbling waters' perfectly describes this spectacular cascade, one of the tallest waterfalls east of the Mississippi River. Trails to both the base and rim provide dramatically different perspectives of the thundering waters.
  • Hike through the surrounding state park where hardwood forests, mountain streams, and abundant wildlife create ideal conditions for nature photography and peaceful contemplation. The park serves as the southern approach to the Appalachian Trail, attracting hikers from around the world preparing for long-distance adventures. On-site naturalist programs offer archery, animal encounters, and guided wilderness exploration.
Activities
  • Follow the 8-mile Approach Trail connecting Amicalola Falls to the official southern terminus of the Appalachian Trail at Springer Mountain. Even short sections of this legendary pathway provide unforgettable hiking through pristine mountain wilderness frequented by deer, wild turkeys, and occasionally black bears.

Day 9

Madison ★★

Discover
  • Stroll through Madison's remarkably preserved antebellum district, where grand 19th-century mansions line oak-shaded streets virtually unchanged since the Civil War. Sherman's army famously spared this elegant town during the March to the Sea, preserving architectural treasures including Heritage Hall and the historic Rogers House. Manicured gardens, decorative fountains, and refined Southern storefronts create timeless elegance.
  • Visit Rose Cottage, built by Adeline Rose after she gained her freedom, offering moving insights into African American history in the antebellum South. The Steffen Thomas Museum showcases bold folk art in striking contrast to the town's refined classical architecture. Antique shops throughout the walkable historic district contain fascinating treasures from centuries past.
Activities
  • Experience Madison's genteel Southern hospitality while exploring the town's carefully preserved heritage spanning two centuries of American history. The intimate scale and exceptional preservation make this one of Georgia's most photographically rewarding destinations for lovers of historic architecture.

Day 10

Macon ★

Discover
  • Explore Ocmulgee Mounds National Historical Park, where massive earthen ceremonial mounds constructed over 1,000 years ago preserve evidence of sophisticated Native American civilization in the American Southeast. The reconstructed earth lodge contains an original clay floor where ancient councils gathered around a central fire pit. Museum exhibits display artifacts spanning 17,000 years of human habitation along the Ocmulgee River.
  • Discover Macon's rich musical heritage at the Tubman Museum, one of the largest museums in America dedicated to African American art, history, and culture. The city served as birthplace to music legends Little Richard and Otis Redding, whose influences still resonate through local venues and festivals. The Allman Brothers Band Museum preserves memorabilia from Southern rock's most influential group.
Activities
  • Tour the opulent Hay House, a stunning Italian Renaissance Revival mansion featuring original 19th-century furnishings and technological innovations ahead of their time. The Grand Opera House, with its plush red velvet interior, hosts performances in a magnificently restored 1884 theater. Window-shop along Cherry Street's indie boutiques in the revitalized downtown district.

Day 11

Providence Canyon ★

Discover

Backcountry camping sites allow overnight visitors to experience canyon walls glowing golden at sunrise and sunset. Day 12

Okefenokee ★★

Discover
  • Enter the Okefenokee Swamp, a 438,000-acre wilderness where glassy blackwater channels wind through ancient cypress and tupelo forests draped in Spanish moss. This vast wetland, one of the largest intact freshwater ecosystems in North America, supports over 600 species including American alligators, river otters, and countless wading birds. The mysterious, primordial atmosphere creates a hauntingly beautiful landscape unlike anywhere else in the country.
  • Walk the elevated Chesser Island Boardwalk to an observation tower providing panoramic views across endless prairies of floating peat islands and wildlife-rich marshlands. The preserved homestead reveals how hardy pioneers once scratched out existence in this watery wilderness. The visitor center explains the swamp's unique ecology and cultural history.
Activities
  • Glide through mirror-calm waters on guided boat tours where experienced naturalists point out alligators basking on logs, herons stalking fish, and turtles sunning on cypress knees. The earthy, mossy scent of the wetlands and calls of countless birds create a sensory immersion in one of America's last great wild places.

Day 13

Cumberland Island ★

Discover
  • Discover Cumberland Island, Georgia's largest and wildest barrier island where 17 miles of pristine beaches stretch beneath towering dunes and maritime forests of moss-draped oaks. Wild horses descended from colonial-era stock roam freely across 36,000 acres of protected wilderness, creating unforgettable encounters along empty shorelines. The island remains deliberately undeveloped, accessible only by ferry from St. Marys.
  • Explore the haunting Dungeness Ruins, crumbling remains of Thomas Carnegie's Gilded Age mansion slowly being reclaimed by resurrection ferns and climbing vines. The grand Plum Orchard Mansion showcases meticulously preserved Carnegie-era architecture and furnishings. The historic First African Baptist Church and Stafford Plantation cemetery document the island's complex history of enslaved people and freed African Americans.
Activities
  • Hike or bike over 50 miles of trails winding through moss-draped maritime forest, past salt marshes teeming with wildlife, and along windswept beaches where loggerhead sea turtles nest each summer. Brunswick ★★

Discover

Day 15

Savannah ★★

Discover
  • Fall under the spell of Savannah, one of America's most enchanting cities, where cobblestone streets wind past 22 oak-shaded squares draped in Spanish moss beneath grand antebellum mansions. Founded in 1733, the city's original grid plan created the intimate green spaces that define its romantic character today. The blend of architectural styles spanning three centuries creates streetscapes of extraordinary beauty and historical significance.
  • Explore the hauntingly beautiful Bonaventure Cemetery, where ornate Victorian monuments and moss-draped oaks create an atmosphere of melancholic grandeur overlooking the Wilmington River. The oak-canopied avenue through Wormsloe Historic Site, lined with 400 live oaks planted in the 1890s, ranks among the most photographed roads in the American South. The elegant Mercer-Williams House offers tours through Savannah's storied past.
Activities
  • Stroll along River Street where historic cotton warehouses now house eclectic shops, restaurants, and lively entertainment venues overlooking the Savannah River. Sunset paddlewheel cruises offer views of the brick waterfront accompanied by live jazz and Southern cuisine. Ghost tours after dark reveal the city's supernatural reputation as one of America's most haunted destinations.
  • Wander through the lush landscapes of Forsyth Park, anchored by an iconic 1858 fountain modeled after fountains in Paris and Cusco. The 30-acre park provides a green oasis for picnics, concerts, and people-watching beneath centuries-old trees. The surrounding Victorian district features some of Savannah's finest residential architecture.

Tybee Island ★★

St Simons Island ★

Discover
