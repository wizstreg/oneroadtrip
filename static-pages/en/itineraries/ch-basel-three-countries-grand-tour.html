Discover Dijon, the historic capital of Burgundy located in the heart of France's most celebrated wine region. The city boasts a rich heritage as the seat of the powerful Dukes of Burgundy, with the Ducal Palace now housing the exceptional Musée des Beaux-Arts. Medieval and Renaissance buildings line the pedestrianized streets of the remarkably preserved center.

Follow the Owl Trail (Parcours de la Chouette), a walking route marked by bronze owl plaques embedded in the pavement that leads past 22 historic landmarks. This self-guided tour reveals the city's architectural treasures, from Gothic churches to elegant hôtels particuliers.

Explore the gastronomic heritage for which Dijon is famous worldwide, including its renowned mustard and exceptional Burgundy wines. The city is the birthplace of fine Burgundy cuisine, where traditional recipes have been perfected over centuries.

→

Sample the celebrated wines of Burgundy, from the grand crus of the Côte de Nuits to the village appellations that surround the city. The vineyards of this region produce some of the world's most sought-after Pinot Noir and Chardonnay wines.

Visit the Notre-Dame du Haut chapel in Ronchamp, designed by legendary architect Le Corbusier and declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This revolutionary masterpiece of modern architecture, completed in 1955, breaks all conventions with its sculptural white walls, mushroom-shaped roof, and seemingly random window openings that create dramatic interior lighting.

Experience the spiritual atmosphere created by Le Corbusier's masterful manipulation of light and space. The chapel's organic forms and pilgrimage setting combine modernist architecture with ancient religious traditions, creating a contemplative environment that continues to inspire architects and visitors alike.

→

Explore the surrounding grounds designed by Renzo Piano, including the Monastery of Saint Clare and the gatehouse. The peaceful hilltop setting offers views of the Vosges Mountains and the charming landscapes of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region.

Sample local wines including Riesling, Gewürztraminer, Pinot Gris, and Muscat - the noble grape varieties that have made Alsace famous worldwide. The roadside cellars and tasting rooms offer opportunities to discover these exceptional wines directly from the producers.

Explore Strasbourg, the capital of Alsace and seat of the European Parliament, featuring a stunning Gothic cathedral and well-preserved Old Town designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The cathedral's 142-meter spire was the world's tallest building for over two centuries, while its intricate facade and astronomical clock continue to amaze visitors.

Wander through La Petite France, the most picturesque quarter of Strasbourg, where half-timbered houses from the 16th and 17th centuries line the canals of the Ill River. Former tanners' and fishermen's homes now house restaurants and shops, their flower-bedecked balconies reflected in the still waters below.

Visit the European Parliament building, where the European Union's directly elected assembly meets. Guided tours reveal the workings of this important democratic institution and the stunning hemicycle chamber where 705 members from 27 nations deliberate.

→

Discover Strasbourg's rich culinary heritage, from traditional winstubs to Michelin-starred restaurants. The city's cuisine reflects its unique position at the crossroads of French and German culture, producing distinctive dishes found nowhere else.

Explore Colmar, renowned for its beautifully preserved Old Town featuring half-timbered houses and idyllic canals in the Alsace region of France. Wander through La Petite Venise (Little Venice), where traditional half-timbered houses line the canals of the Lauch River. This picturesque district, best explored by boat or on foot, captures the essence of Alsatian charm with its pastel-colored buildings reflected in the still waters.

Explore Schaffhausen, a historic city featuring a well-preserved Old Town and the impressive Munot fortress dating to the 16th century. This unique circular fortification offers panoramic views of the city, the Rhine, and the surrounding vine-covered hills. The Old Town's ornate bay windows (Erker) and frescoed facades create a distinctive architectural heritage.

Marvel at the Rhine Falls, Europe's biggest waterfall at 150 meters wide and 23 meters high. This impressive natural spectacle sees 600,000 liters of water per second cascade over the rocks with tremendous force. The fish cannot climb upstream due to the falls' power, though eels are known to make the journey.

→

Experience Mount Rigi, known as the 'Queen of the Mountains' and Europe's first tourist mountain. This massif rises between three lakes - Lucerne, Zug, and Lauerz - creating stunning views in every direction. The panorama encompasses the Swiss Alps, the Jura Mountains, and the vast Swiss Plateau stretching to the horizon.

Reach the summit via the historic Rigi Railway, one of Europe's first mountain railways dating to 1871. Alternatively, take the scenic cable car from Weggis, ascending through pastoral landscapes dotted with traditional chalets and grazing cattle.

Relax at Rigi Kaltbad Mineral Baths and Spa, designed by renowned architect Mario Botta. The modern spa complex harmonizes with the Alpine setting, offering indoor and outdoor pools fed by natural mineral springs, with uninterrupted mountain views.

→

In summer, explore hiking trails ranging from gentle paths to challenging routes. In winter, the Rigi offers skiing, sledging, and snowshoe trails. The cultural significance of the mountain in Swiss history makes it a must-visit destination.

Discover Lucerne, a major tourist destination lying on the north end of Lake Lucerne where the River Reuss flows out. The city is famous for its Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke), the oldest standing truss bridge in the world, adorned with 17th-century paintings depicting local history and the lives of saints.

Admire the iconic Water Tower (Wasserturm), an octagonal structure that has served as prison, torture chamber, treasury, and archive throughout its 700-year history. Together with the Chapel Bridge, it forms the most photographed monument in Switzerland.

Visit the Swiss Museum of Transport, Switzerland's most visited museum, showcasing the country's pioneering role in Alpine transportation. From vintage locomotives to space exploration, the interactive exhibits trace the history of mobility in the mountains.

→

Wander through the Old Town's well-preserved historic buildings, painted with elaborate frescoes depicting historical scenes and legends. The car-free streets reveal medieval squares, Renaissance fountains, and traditional guild houses.

→

If visiting toward the end of winter, experience the famous Lucerne Carnival (Fasnacht), Switzerland's largest and most colorful celebration featuring elaborate costumes, satirical floats, and raucous music.

Ascend Mount Pilatus via the world's steepest cogwheel railway, with gradients reaching 48%. This engineering marvel, inaugurated in 1889, climbs 1,629 meters through dramatic Alpine scenery to the summit at 2,132 meters. The panoramic views encompass Lake Lucerne, the Swiss Plateau, and countless Alpine peaks.

Explore the summit complex featuring viewing terraces, restaurants, and the Hotel Pilatus-Kulm. On clear days, the 360-degree panorama extends from the Black Forest to the distant peaks of the Swiss Alps, with 73 Alpine peaks visible from this legendary vantage point.

→

Experience the Golden Round Trip: boat across Lake Lucerne, cogwheel railway up the mountain, aerial cable car descent to Kriens. This circular journey combines Switzerland's most scenic transport modes for an unforgettable day. In winter, enjoy skiing and toboggan runs on the mountain's slopes.

Ascend Mount Titlis near Engelberg, a peak offering snow sports activities year-round thanks to its permanent glacier. The Rotair cable car - the world's first revolving gondola - provides 360-degree views during the ascent to 3,238 meters. The rotating cabin ensures every passenger enjoys the spectacular panorama.

Cross the Titlis Cliff Walk, a spectacular suspension bridge at 3,041 meters that spans 100 meters across a dramatic Alpine chasm. Europe's highest suspension bridge offers vertigo-inducing views of crevassed glaciers and snow-covered peaks stretching to the horizon.

Explore the Glacier Grotto, carved into the eternal ice of the Titlis glacier. Descend into the crystalline ice cave to experience the heart of a living glacier, with frozen chambers revealing the stunning blue of compressed ancient ice.

→

In summer, enjoy hiking and mountain biking on trails that wind through Alpine meadows and past traditional chalets. The Engelberg valley offers activities for all abilities, from gentle walks to challenging peaks.

Explore Brig, officially known as Brig-Glis, a historic town of great national significance in the Valais canton. The entire Old Town is part of the official Swiss heritage sites, with the magnificent Stockalper Palace standing as the largest private building ever constructed in Switzerland. This 17th-century baroque masterpiece features distinctive onion-domed towers.

Visit the Church of Maria Himmelfahrt (Assumption of Mary), designated a heritage site of National Significance. The baroque interior features stunning frescoes, intricate woodwork, and gilded altars that reflect the prosperity brought by the Simplon trade route passing through the town.

→

Discover the strategic importance of Brig as the northern gateway to the Simplon Pass, connecting Switzerland to Italy since Roman times. The town's architecture and monuments reveal centuries of history as a vital Alpine crossing point.

Discover Geneva, the city of peace and international diplomacy, home to the European headquarters of the United Nations and the International Red Cross. Discover Grindelwald in the Bernese Highlands, a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts dominated by the legendary north face of the Eiger. The village's traditional chalets cluster beneath these imposing peaks.

Take the Firstbahn cableway to exciting attractions including the First Cliff Walk, a dramatic walkway suspended along the cliff face at 2,168 meters. The engineering marvel extends into the void, providing heart-pounding views of the valley far below and the surrounding peaks.

Hike to Bachalpsee, an idyllic Alpine lake at 2,265 meters that perfectly mirrors the Schreckhorn and Wetterhorn peaks. This relatively easy trail from First station rewards hikers with one of Switzerland's most photographed mountain panoramas.

→

Arrive in Interlaken, spectacularly situated between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz - its name literally meaning 'between lakes'. Explore Bern, the capital of Switzerland, renowned for its well-preserved medieval Old Town designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Federal Palace, seat of the Swiss government, dominates the cityscape alongside the iconic Zytglogge clock tower with its famous astronomical clock and mechanical figures that perform hourly.
