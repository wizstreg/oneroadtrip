Szczecin

3 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Explore the magnificent Szczecin Castle, the Renaissance-style former residence of the Dukes of Pomerania that dominates the city's skyline. This impressive fortress complex houses museums, concert halls and courtyards that tell the story of the region's noble heritage. The castle's towers offer panoramic views over the Oder River and the surrounding historic quarter.
2
Marvel at St. Jacob's Cathedral, one of the largest brick Gothic churches in the entire region, whose soaring spire was only recently restored to its former glory. The cathedral's imposing interior features magnificent vaulted ceilings and medieval artwork that showcase the architectural prowess of Hanseatic craftsmen. This sacred space has witnessed centuries of prayer and devotion since the Middle Ages.
3
Admire the award-winning Philharmonic Hall, a stunning contemporary building that opened in 2015 and has garnered international acclaim for both its architecture and exceptional acoustics. The building's angular white facade creates a striking contrast with the city's historic structures and represents Poland's modern cultural renaissance. Concert-goers from across Europe come to experience performances in this acoustic masterpiece.
4
Stroll along the Hakenterrasse (Wały Chrobrego), a magnificent ensemble of early 20th-century buildings perched above the Oder River. This elegant promenade offers spectacular views of the waterfront, the port and the ships passing by below. The terraced gardens and monumental architecture create one of the most photographed panoramas in all of Pomerania.
🎯

Activities

→
Challenge yourself at Tarzania Rope Park, where various courses with different difficulty levels allow visitors of all ages to test their agility among the treetops
→
Visit the Museum of Technology and Communication to discover vintage cars, motorcycles, trams and historic computing equipment with hands-on interactive exhibitions

Discover the Świdwie Nature Reserve, a paradise for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts located in pristine wetlands near Szczecin. This protected area is home to remarkable bird species including cranes, storks and majestic white-tailed eagles that nest in the marshlands. Educational trails and observation towers provide comfortable viewing platforms to witness these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.

Activities

Swinoujscie

3 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Ascend the Świnoujście Lighthouse, the tallest lighthouse on the entire Polish coast and one of the highest in the world. The climb to the top presents a rewarding challenge, with breathtaking panoramic views of the city, the bustling port and the endless Baltic Sea as your prize. This iconic maritime beacon has guided ships safely into harbor for over 160 years.
2
Step back in time at Fort Gerhard, where interactive tours led by guides in authentic 19th-century soldier costumes bring military history vividly to life. This well-preserved fortress offers a fascinating glimpse into the strategic importance of this coastal location and the soldiers who once defended it. The tunnels, ramparts and gun emplacements create an adventurous atmosphere perfect for young explorers.
3
Visit Lake Turquoise in nearby Wapnica, a stunning reservoir with intensely turquoise-colored water created on the site of a former chalk mine. This picturesque lake offers walking paths through extraordinary landscapes where the vibrant water colors create an almost surreal natural spectacle. Viewpoints and picnic areas provide perfect spots to absorb the unique beauty of this geological wonder.
🎯

Activities

→
Walk along the scenic trails surrounding Lake Turquoise to capture unique photographs of its remarkable turquoise waters

Miedzyzdroje

3 photos
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
Stroll along Międzyzdroje's famous Promenade of Stars, where handprints of beloved Polish movie stars are embedded in the pavement like a Baltic Hollywood Walk of Fame. This elegant seaside resort boasts one of the most beautiful beaches on the Polish coast, with fine sand stretching beneath dramatic coastal cliffs. The historic pier extends far into the Baltic Sea, offering romantic sunset views and fresh sea breezes.
2
Explore the Baltic Miniature Park where meticulously crafted 1:25 scale models showcase the most important monuments of Baltic countries. Each miniature faithfully reproduces architectural details of famous landmarks, creating a fascinating geography and history lesson in one enchanting location. The park also features playgrounds, a miniature train ride and family-friendly amenities.
3
Journey back to the Middle Ages at the Slavs and Vikings Centre in Wolin, an archaeological open-air museum recreating life over a thousand years ago. Visitors can explore reconstructed huts, workshops and authentic equipment used by the Slavic and Viking peoples who once inhabited this region. Craft demonstrations, warrior combat shows and educational workshops bring ancient history dramatically to life.
4
Visit Wolin National Park to encounter European bison at the renowned Bison Show Farm, where these majestic animals can be observed up close along with deer and wild boars. The park features numerous hiking trails, scenic viewpoints and educational paths that wind through pristine coastal forests. The Natural History Museum presents the region's diverse fauna and flora through engaging dioramas and interactive exhibitions.
🎯

Activities

→
Walk the educational trails of Wolin National Park to discover the unique ecosystems of this protected coastal wilderness

Discover

1
Marvel at the moving sand dunes of Słowiński National Park, where vast expanses of shifting sands create an almost desert-like landscape on the Baltic coast. These spectacular dunes, constantly reshaped by wind and waves, are among the most impressive natural phenomena in northern Europe. Walking trails lead through this unique ecosystem where land and sea meet in dramatic fashion.

The park combines entertainment with education through additional attractions including a rope park, mini zoo, 5D cinema and exciting playgrounds. This immersive experience stimulates young imaginations while teaching about Earth's fascinating prehistoric past.

Hel

9 photos
Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Discover the Hel Peninsula, a remarkable 35-kilometer sand spit extending into the Baltic Sea and separating the Bay of Puck from the open waters. This unique geographical formation offers pristine beaches on both sides, with calm bay waters on one shore and open sea waves on the other. The charming fishing town of Hel at the peninsula's tip retains its authentic maritime character with colorful cottages and traditional boats.
2
Visit the Fokarium seal sanctuary in Hel, where visitors can observe grey seals being rehabilitated before release into the wild. The research station plays a crucial role in Baltic marine conservation and offers educational programs about these fascinating marine mammals. Watching the seals swim, play and interact provides memorable moments for the entire family.
🎯

Activities

→
Cycle along the peninsula's scenic bike paths or enjoy the unique experience of beaches facing two different bodies of water

Sopot

10 photos
Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Explore Gdynia, the youngest member of the Tricity metropolitan area, built almost entirely in the 20th century as Poland's gateway to the sea. The modernist architecture and wide boulevards reflect the optimism of interwar Poland, while the bustling port remains vital to the nation's maritime commerce. Museums ships and the aquarium offer maritime attractions for curious visitors.
2
Stroll along Sopot's legendary wooden pier, the longest of its kind in Europe, extending over 500 meters into the Baltic Sea. This elegant seaside resort has attracted the rich and famous for over a century with its beautiful beaches, grand hotels and sophisticated atmosphere. The pier offers romantic sunset views and the refreshing experience of walking far out over the shimmering Baltic waters.
🎯

Activities

Gdansk

10 photos
Day 14
🔍

Discover

1
Immerse yourself in the magnificent Old Town of Gdańsk, once the pride of the Hanseatic League and today one of Europe's most beautifully restored historic centers. The colorful merchant houses lining Long Market and the ornate facades of Neptune Fountain Square transport visitors to the city's golden age of maritime commerce. Gothic churches, Renaissance gates and baroque palaces create an architectural treasure trove at every turn.
2
Visit Westerplatte, the peninsula that became a powerful symbol of Polish resistance when a small garrison held out against overwhelming German forces for seven days at the start of World War II in September 1939. The monument and museum commemorate this heroic defense and mark the spot where the deadliest conflict in human history began. This moving site offers profound lessons about courage, sacrifice and the costs of war.
3
Discover the European Solidarity Centre, a world-class museum documenting the rise of the Solidarity trade union movement that challenged communist rule and ultimately helped end the Cold War. The striking rust-colored building stands at the historic Gdańsk Shipyard where Lech Wałęsa led workers in their historic struggle for freedom. Interactive exhibitions trace this inspiring story of peaceful resistance that changed the world.
4
Explore the medieval Crane Gate (Żuraw), the largest working port crane of medieval Europe and Gdańsk's most recognizable landmark. This remarkable structure once loaded cargo onto ships and now houses fascinating maritime museum exhibits. The waterfront along the Motława River offers picturesque views and boat cruises through the heart of the historic port district.
🎯

Activities

→
Take a boat cruise along the Motława River to see the historic port and shipyards from the water

Malbork

10 photos
Day 15
🔍

Discover

1
Stand in awe before Malbork Castle, the largest castle in the world by surface area and a UNESCO World Heritage masterpiece of medieval brick Gothic architecture. This colossal fortress served as the headquarters of the Teutonic Knights who ruled vast territories of Northern Europe from these imposing walls. The castle complex includes the High Castle, Middle Castle and Lower Castle, each revealing layers of medieval political power and religious devotion.
2
Explore the castle's magnificent interior chambers, including the Grand Master's Palace with its stunning vaulted ceilings and the atmospheric cellars and passages that wind beneath the fortress. The castle museum displays medieval armor, weapons and artifacts that bring the Teutonic era vividly to life. Evening sound and light shows illuminate the castle walls, creating an unforgettable spectacle against the night sky.
🎯

Activities

→
Walk the castle's extensive defensive walls and towers for panoramic views over the Nogat River and surrounding countryside

Bydgoszcz

3 photos
Day 16
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Bydgoszcz (formerly Bromberg), a charming city where waterways weave through the historic center creating a unique urban landscape often called Little Berlin. The picturesque Mill Island (Wyspa Młyńska) features restored granaries, elegant bridges and peaceful canals that invite leisurely exploration. This former Prussian city blends German and Polish architectural influences in its well-preserved historic quarter.
2
Stroll along the Brda River waterfront, where historic red-brick granaries reflect in the calm waters and colorful facades line the embankments. The city's musical heritage is celebrated through numerous monuments and the renowned Opera Nova concert hall. Bydgoszcz offers a quieter, authentic Polish city experience away from the crowds of more famous destinations.
🎯

Activities

→
Take a boat trip through the city's canals to appreciate its unique waterway network and historic warehouse district

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather with mild temperatures (15-23°C), blooming landscapes, and longer days. Ideal for city exploration and outdoor activities without summer crowds.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather (18-25°C) with peak tourist season, highest prices, but best conditions for festivals, hiking, and outdoor experiences. Long daylight hours perfect for sightseeing.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent weather (12-20°C) with beautiful fall colors, fewer crowds, and moderate prices. Great for cultural tourism and photography.

Winter (November-March): Cold weather (-2 to 5°C) with potential snow, Christmas markets, and cozy indoor culture. Lowest prices, fewer tourists, magical winter atmosphere.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 14 and 20 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Poland?

Central European gem with rich history, beautiful cities, and authentic culture. Experience medieval towns, traditional cuisine, and genuine Polish hospitality. The country offers cultural depth with natural beauty and historical significance. Poland provides authentic European experiences with cultural richness and historical importance.
