❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Dry Season (May-October): Best weather overall, minimal rainfall, clearer skies, ideal for Salar de Uyuni, peak tourist season.

Wet Season (November-April): Heavy rains, challenging road conditions, Salar de Uyuni becomes mirror-like, lush landscapes, fewer tourists.

Shoulder Seasons (April-May, October-November): Good weather transitions, moderate crowds, reasonable prices, excellent for most activities.

High Altitude Considerations: La Paz and Altiplano best visited during dry season, less altitude sickness risk with clear weather.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 14 and 22 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Bolivia?

World's highest capital and salt flats create otherworldly landscapes alongside ancient Incan heritage. Experience authentic indigenous culture in dramatic Andean settings with incredible value for money. Lake Titicaca's floating islands and death road adventures thrill visitors. Bolivia offers authentic South American experiences with stunning natural phenomena.
