Portugal's Trás-os-Montes: Bragança, Douro Valley & Montesinho Park

An initiatory journey through secret Portugal, among Iberian wolves, terraced vineyards, and villages frozen in time.

Set off to discover authentic Portugal in the preserved regions of Trás-os-Montes and Douro. From Bragança's medieval citadels to UNESCO-listed vineyard terraces, this 10-day itinerary immerses you in a timeless Portugal where ancestral traditions, millennial thermal baths, and grandiose landscapes come together.

  • Bragança and Montesinho Natural Park, UNESCO Biosphere Reserve
  • The Roman Bridge of Trajan in Chaves, 2,000 years of history
  • Mateus Palace, a jewel of Portuguese baroque
  • Lamego and the sanctuary with 686 steps
  • The Cistercian monasteries of Tarouca
  • The historic thermal baths of São Pedro do Sul

This fascinating museum presents the ancestral traditions of Trás-os-Montes Carnival festivals with their impressive ritual masks. Discover how these pagan ceremonies have survived the centuries in this remote region of Portugal.

Explore Montesinho Natural Park, one of Portugal's best-kept secrets. This UNESCO Biosphere Reserve is home to exceptional biodiversity including Iberian wolves, golden eagles, and preserved flora. The landscapes of mountains, oak forests, and traditional schist villages transport you to a forgotten Portugal.

Visit the authentic village of Rio de Onor, a unique geographical curiosity where the Spanish-Portuguese border runs through the houses. The inhabitants still live according to ancestral communal traditions, sharing lands and herds. It's a living testimony to a rural way of life that has virtually disappeared elsewhere in Europe.
⏱️ 75 min

This picturesque hamlet is the ideal starting point for hikes in the natural park and offers total immersion in Trás-os-Montes rural life with its traditional houses and peaceful atmosphere.

Discover

1
Discover the famous Chaves Thermal Baths, whose thermal waters spring forth at over 70°C. These millennial baths, already appreciated by the Romans, are renowned for their curative properties against muscular and digestive ailments. Taste the water at Fonte do Povo, a unique sensory experience even if the taste may surprise you!
⏱️ 75 min
2
Cross the remarkable Roman Bridge of Trajan, an impressive 140-meter monument that has spanned the Tâmega River for nearly 2,000 years. This 12-arch bridge, one of the best preserved on the Iberian Peninsula, bears witness to the genius of Roman engineers and the strategic importance of Chaves on the road between Braga and Astorga.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll through the historic center of Chaves, admiring the Keep Tower of the medieval castle and the Fort of São Francisco transformed into a charming hotel. Taste the famous pastéis de Chaves, meat-filled pastries that have made the gastronomic reputation of this welcoming spa town.

Marvel at the sumptuous Mateus Palace, a jewel of Portuguese baroque whose silhouette adorns the bottles of the famous rosé wine. This 18th-century aristocratic manor, attributed to Nicolau Nasoni, features French-style gardens, a romantic lake, and refined interiors with period furniture and precious art collections.

2
Explore the mysterious Sanctuary of Panóias, a unique Roman archaeological site dedicated to oriental cults. These granite rocks sculpted with basins and Latin inscriptions were used for ritual sacrifices nearly two thousand years ago. An enigmatic place that reveals the religious diversity of the Roman Empire in its most remote provinces.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Admire the numerous heraldic stones that ennoble the facades, testimony to the nobility that settled here alongside the Marquises.

Discover

1
Climb the 686 steps of the baroque stairway at the Sanctuary of Nossa Senhora dos Remédios, Lamego's emblem and one of the most impressive monumental ensembles in Portugal. Azulejo panels, allegorical fountains like the Fountain of the Giants in the Patio of Kings, and breathtaking panoramic views punctuate this ascent culminating at the sanctuary dedicated to the city's patron saint.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit Lamego Cathedral, which dates back to the 12th century and combines original Romanesque elements with Gothic and Renaissance additions. The Manueline triple portal, the most significant work of this region, stands out, and inside, the baroque main altar and the vaults of the three naves covered with colorful Old Testament frescoes.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Discover the Lamego Museum, housed in the former Episcopal Palace of baroque style. It possesses one of the finest religious art collections in Portugal, including works by Grão Vasco and Flemish tapestries. Prepare for total amazement when seeing the gilded wood altars entirely transferred from the Chagas Monastery.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Explore Lamego Castle and its fascinating Cistern, one of the best-preserved castle cisterns in Portugal. Climb the 12th-century Keep Tower with its interactive and playful explanations. Don't miss the Gate of the Fig Trees with its two turrets and the Archaeological Center with its treasures from Roman times.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover the Monastery of São João de Tarouca, the first Cistercian monastery in Portugal founded in 1144. This enigmatic and very ancient monument preserves the church with paintings by Gaspar Vaz and the tomb of the Count of Barcelos. A place of spirituality and history where the Cistercian order began its transformative presence in the country.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit the Ucanha Toll Tower, a monument unique in Portugal that controlled passage over the medieval bridge spanning the Varosa River. This 12th-century defensive tower, with its fortified bridge, is an exceptional testimony to medieval territorial organization and offers an unforgettable photogenic landscape.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the Monastery of Santa Maria de Salzedas, an imposing Cistercian monastic complex that was one of the richest in Portugal. Although partially in ruins, the grandeur of its church and cloister bears witness to the power and wealth that Cistercian monks accumulated in this wine-growing region over centuries.

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Late Spring (April-June): Perfect weather conditions (18-25°C) with mild temperatures, moderate rainfall, and blooming landscapes. Ideal for exploring cities, coastal areas, and countryside with comfortable conditions and fewer crowds than summer.

Early Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures after summer heat, warm ocean waters, and stable weather patterns. Best time for beach activities combined with cultural exploration, moderate prices and pleasant conditions.

Winter (November-March): Mild temperatures (10-18°C) with increased rainfall but lowest prices and authentic experiences. Good for cultural immersion, indoor attractions, and experiencing Portuguese daily life without tourist crowds.

Summer (July-August): Peak season with hottest weather (25-35°C), highest prices, and largest crowds. Perfect for beach holidays but challenging for extensive sightseeing in inland areas due to heat intensity.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 9 and 13 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Portugal?

Atlantic charm with stunning coastlines, rich history, and authentic culture. Experience traditional fado music, beautiful cities, and genuine Portuguese hospitality. The country offers cultural depth with natural beauty and culinary excellence. Portugal provides authentic European experiences with natural wonders and cultural richness.
