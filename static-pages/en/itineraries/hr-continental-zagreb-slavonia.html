Continental Croatia Road Trip: Zagreb, Slavonia and Baroque Castles

16 days through Croatia's hidden interior — from Zagreb's museums and Zagorje castles to Slavonia's baroque fortresses, Vukovar's memorials and Ilok's Danube vineyards.

📅 Best period April, May, June, September, October

Discover a side of Croatia few travellers ever see — the continental heartland stretching from Zagreb's café-lined squares to Slavonia's golden plains and Danube vineyards. Over 16 days, this road trip winds through Neanderthal caves, star-shaped fortresses, baroque citadels and war memorials, revealing a country of extraordinary depth far beyond its famous coastline.

  • Zagreb, a vibrant capital between medieval Upper Town and Viennese café culture
  • Varaždin, Croatia's baroque gem and the Špancirfest street festival
  • Trakošćan Castle, a fairy-tale fortress above its own lake
  • The Tvrđa citadel of Osijek and Kopački Rit wetlands
  • Vukovar's wartime memorials and the 5,000-year-old Vučedol culture
  • Ilok's princely wine cellars overlooking the Danube

Ban Jelačić Square is the beating heart of Zagreb. Trams and pedestrians converge beneath the equestrian statue of the national hero, making it the ideal starting point for exploring the Upper and Lower Towns.

The cathedral raises its neo-Gothic spires to a height of 105 metres. Rebuilt by architect Hermann Bollé after the 1880 earthquake, it houses the tomb of Cardinal Stepinac and a medieval treasury dating back to the 11th century.

The Stone Gate is the only surviving medieval passage. Candles burn before the miraculous icon of the Virgin, the sole survivor of the 1731 fire. Zagreb residents still stop here to pray before an exam or a journey.

St. Mark's Church displays its famous polychrome-tiled roof depicting the coats of arms of Zagreb and the Croatian-Slavonian-Dalmatian Kingdom. In front of it, the Parliament and the Ban's Palace frame the seat of power.
