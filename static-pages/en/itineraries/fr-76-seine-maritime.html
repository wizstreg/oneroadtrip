Seine-Maritime Road Trip: Cliffs of Étretat, Rouen and Jumièges Abbey

Chalk cliffs pierce the Norman sky, abbey ruins whisper monastic chants, Étretat arches defy the ocean. Seine-Maritime reveals its mysteries.

From the medieval streets of Rouen to the vertiginous arches of Étretat, this 9-day circuit reveals the treasures of Seine-Maritime. Chalk cliffs sculpted by the elements, thousand-year-old abbeys, authentic fishing ports and Gothic heritage compose a journey between land and sea on the Alabaster Coast.

  • Étretat: Hollow Needle and 80m arches
  • Jumièges: France's most beautiful ruin
  • Rouen: Gros-Horloge from 1389
  • Fécamp: Neo-Gothic Bénédictine Palace

Discover

1
Gros-Horloge — This 14th-century Gothic belfry houses one of Europe's oldest astronomical clocks, with a mechanism dating from 1389. The Renaissance arch built between 1527 and 1529 spans the pedestrian street with its 2.50-meter golden dial depicting a 24-ray sun on a star-studded blue background. A single hand points to the hour while a globe indicates the phases of the moon.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Place du Vieux-Marché — It was in this square that Joan of Arc was burned on May 30, 1431. The Sainte-Jeanne-d'Arc church, inaugurated in 1979, features bold architecture shaped like an inverted flame and houses the Renaissance stained glass windows from the former Saint-Vincent church destroyed during the war.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Saint-Ouen Abbey Church — A masterpiece of Rayonnant Gothic built between the 14th and 16th centuries, this former Benedictine abbey impresses with its 137 meters length and its 14th-century stained glass windows among the most complete in France. Its grand Cavaillé-Coll organ from 1890 has 64 stops.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Musée Le Secq des Tournelles — Housed in the former 15th-century Saint-Laurent church, this museum unique in the world presents 12,000 pieces of ornamental ironwork from the 3rd to 19th century: signs, locks, grilles, everyday objects testifying to the craftsmanship of master blacksmiths.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Historial Jeanne d'Arc — Housed in the archbishop's palace where Joan was condemned then rehabilitated, this immersive scenographic journey retraces the destiny of the Maid through testimonies, projections and reconstructions of the 1431 trial.
⏱️ 75 min
→
Rue Eau-de-Robec — This picturesque cobbled street runs along a canal where dyers once washed their fabrics. Its half-timbered houses from the 15th and 16th centuries, restored after the 1944 bombings, plunge the stroller into medieval Rouen.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Jardin des Plantes de Rouen — This 8.5-hectare botanical garden created in 1840 houses more than 5,600 plant species, tropical greenhouses and a rose garden. A green lung in the heart of the city, conducive to strolling among botanical collections and century-old trees.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
Panorama Côte Sainte-Catherine — From this 150-meter hill overlooking the left bank, embrace the entire view of Rouen: the cathedral painted by Monet, the spires of Gothic churches, the meanders of the Seine. The viewpoint immortalized by the Impressionists in the 19th century.
⏱️ 30 min
2
Rouen Cathedral — Monet painted this Gothic masterpiece more than 30 times to capture its changing light. Built between the 12th and 16th centuries, its 151-meter cast-iron spire makes it France's tallest. The Lady Chapel and the tombs of Norman dukes bear witness to eight centuries of history.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Palais de Justice de Rouen — This flamboyant Gothic building from the late 15th century was the seat of the Exchequer of Normandy. Its western facade, ornate with sculpted gables and pinnacles, survived the 1944 bombings. The Salle des Pas Perdus reveals medieval vaults.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Musée des Beaux-Arts de Rouen — This museum founded in 1801 houses a major collection of paintings from the 15th to 21st century. Works by Caravaggio, Velázquez, Monet, Sisley and a room dedicated to Géricault born in Rouen illustrate five centuries of European art.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Aître Saint-Maclou — This unique ossuary from the 16th century is a rare testimony to the plague epidemics. Its half-timbered galleries surrounding a courtyard display macabre sculptures: skulls, bones, gravedigger's tools carved into the beams.
⏱️ 30 min
→
Panorama XXL — This 32-meter-high rotunda hosts giant 360° panoramas. Past exhibitions have transported visitors to ancient Rome, Monet's Rouen or the Amazon. A unique immersive artistic experience in France.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Seine Riverbanks — The redeveloped left bank offers 3 kilometers of walks from the Pierre-Corneille bridge to the Île Lacroix. Guinguettes, floating gardens and barges transformed into cultural venues bring life to the quays.
⏱️ 45 min

Jumieges

5 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Jumièges Abbey — Victor Hugo called these ruins "the most beautiful in France." Founded in 654, this Benedictine abbey preserves its twin Romanesque towers 46 meters high dating from 1067. The roofless nave open to the sky and the cloister evoke the grandeur of medieval monasticism.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Saint-Pierre Church of Jumièges — This 10th-century Carolingian church next to the abbey displays pre-Romanesque architecture with its irregular pillars and rough stone walls. One of the rare testimonies to Norman architecture before the year 1000.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Seine Viewpoint from Jumièges — The abbey's elevated position offers panoramic views of the Seine loop and surrounding forests. The sunset illuminating the ruins creates a romantic atmosphere that inspired 19th-century writers.
⏱️ 20 min
🎯

Activities

→
Abbey Gardens — These 15-hectare landscaped gardens surrounding the ruins include century-old yew trees, rose gardens and medicinal plant beds evoking monastic gardens. The paths offer constantly changing perspectives on the ruins.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Fruit Route — The Seine loop between Jumièges and Duclair crosses orchards producing Norman apples. Cider producers welcome visitors for tastings. The blossoming in April transforms the landscape into a white and pink sea.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Seine Loop Hike — A 15-kilometer circuit around the Jumièges peninsula offers panoramas of the abbey, forests and the river. The GR2 follows the banks through orchards and Norman villages.
⏱️ 180 min

Discover

1
Saint-Martin-de-Boscherville Abbey — This 12th-century Romanesque abbey, remarkably preserved, displays a purity of lines characteristic of Norman architecture. Its chapter house and 17th-century French-style gardens with their yew topiaries create a meditative setting.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Ruins of Château Robert le Diable — This fortress perched on a rocky outcrop overlooking the Seine dates from the 12th century. Legend attributes its construction to Robert the Magnificent, father of William the Conqueror. The panorama spans the valley.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Brotonne Forest — This 7,000-hectare state forest covering the Seine loop offers 250 kilometers of hiking and mountain biking trails. Centuries-old beeches and oaks create a canopy sheltering fallow deer and roe deer.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Brotonne Bridge — This cable-stayed bridge inaugurated in 1977 spans the Seine with a central span of 320 meters. Its height allows the passage of cargo ships sailing upriver to Rouen. An emblematic structure of Norman engineering.
⏱️ 15 min
→
Vieux-Port Viewpoint — This high point in the Brotonne Forest offers a panorama of the Seine meandering through forests and meadows. Picnic tables allow you to appreciate the peacefulness of the river valley.
⏱️ 30 min

Discover

1
Aval Cliff — This 80-meter-high chalk cliff carved by erosion forms a natural arch nicknamed "Elephant" for the shape of its pillar dipping into the sea. The Needle rising 70 meters inspired Arsène Lupin's hidden treasure in Maurice Leblanc's adventure novel.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Amont Cliff — This arch east of the beach is topped by the Notre-Dame-de-la-Garde chapel and a monument to aviators Nungesser and Coli who disappeared in 1927 attempting the first transatlantic crossing. The path offers dizzying views of the Manneporte.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Manneporte — This third monumental arch 30 meters high pierces the cliff west of the Aval. Accessible only at low tide via the beach, it reveals caves carved by the sea. Monet painted it several times between 1883 and 1886.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Étretat Gardens — These contemporary gardens perched on the cliff combine contemporary sculptures and plants from five continents. The walking path leads to a belvedere offering a 180° panorama from Le Havre to Fécamp.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Étretat Beach — This pebble beach framed by cliffs attracted Parisian high society in the 19th century. Beach cabins and Belle Époque villas lining the seafront perpetuate this bathing heritage. Swimming depends on tides and currents.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Clos Arsène Lupin — This villa where Maurice Leblanc lived from 1918 to 1939 has been transformed into a museum dedicated to the gentleman burglar. Period rooms, secret passages and scenographic installations bring the adventures to life.
⏱️ 60 min
→
GR21 Hiking Trail — This coastal path connecting Le Havre to Le Tréport runs along the cliff tops. The Étretat section offers spectacular views of the arches and the English Channel. Count 3 hours to reach Yport.
⏱️ 180 min

Discover

1
Yport — This authentic fishing village nestled in a cliff valley preserves its traditional character with its thatched-roof fishermen's huts called "calets." The beach offers a view of Étretat's arches 4 kilometers away.
⏱️ 45 min
2
Fécamp Abbey Church — This 12th-century Benedictine abbey houses a relic of the Holy Blood brought back from the Crusades. Its 127-meter length makes it one of the longest churches in France. The Lady Chapel preserves Renaissance sculptures.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Bénédictine Palace — This neo-Gothic palace built in 1888 houses the distillery of the famous liqueur created by a Fécamp monk in 1510. The museum displays a collection of liturgical art and the recipe room reveals some of the 27 plants used.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Cap Fagnet — This cliff rising 110 meters north of Fécamp offers a panorama of the Alabaster Coast. The sailors' chapel and the orientation table help identify Étretat, Saint-Valéry and the Normandy Bridge on clear days.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Fisheries Museum — Housed in a former cod-drying plant, this museum retraces Fécamp's great era when 80 trawlers sailed to Newfoundland banks. Models, tools and testimonies evoke the harsh life of cod fishermen.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Port of Fécamp — This fishing port, France's 4th largest, welcomes trawlers daily. The fish market in the early morning and the restaurants of the quays offer the day's catch: scallops, sole, turbot according to the season.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
Dieppe Castle — This 15th-century fortress perched on a cliff houses a museum exhibiting one of France's finest collections of carved ivories. Dieppe was Europe's main ivory working center in the 17th and 18th centuries.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Saint-Jacques Church — This 13th-16th century church mixes Gothic and Renaissance styles. Its sculpted frieze representing a procession of Brazilian Indians commemorates the 1520 expedition that brought exotic wood from Brazil to Dieppe.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Puits Salé Square — This central square lined with cafes hosts Saturday morning's famous fish market where fishermen sell their catch directly. An authentic and lively place appreciated by Dieppe residents and visitors.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Dieppe Beach — This kilometer-long pebble beach is France's oldest seaside resort. The 1930s seafront promenade, Belle Époque villas and beach cabins bear witness to 400 years of bathing history. Kite flying is a local tradition.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Pollet District — This former fishermen's district clinging to the cliff preserves narrow cobbled alleys and sailors' houses. The stairs climbing to the castle offer changing perspectives on the port and the English Channel.
⏱️ 45 min
→
August 19, 1942 Memorial — This museum commemorates the Dieppe Raid when 6,000 Allied soldiers, mainly Canadians, attempted a landing. Photographs, testimonies and objects evoke this tragic episode that cost 3,600 lives.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Eu Castle — This 16th-century brick and stone castle was the summer residence of King Louis-Philippe from 1830 to 1848. Its apartments preserve Second Empire furniture and decor. The park of 22 hectares includes a French garden and an English garden.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Saint-Laurent Collegiate Church of Eu — This 12th-16th century Gothic church shelters the crypt where the counts of Eu rest, including the Guise princes. Its flamboyant portal and Renaissance statues testify to four centuries of construction.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Le Tréport Funicular — This 1908 funicular climbs the 100-meter cliff in 3 minutes. The upper station offers a breathtaking panorama of the Bresle estuary, Mers-les-Bains and its Belle Époque villas. An emblematic experience of the resort.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Port of Le Tréport — This fishing port welcomes a fleet of about thirty trawlers. The daily fish auction and the port restaurants offer freshly caught scallops, sole and herring. The quays provide a close view of fishing boats.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Mers-les-Bains — Neighboring seaside resort on the Picardy coast, Mers-les-Bains displays 300 Belle Époque villas with polychrome facades classified as historical monuments. Their carved wooden balconies, bay windows and ceramics form a unique architectural ensemble.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Le Tréport Beach — This pebble beach bordered by cliffs has welcomed Parisians since the arrival of the train in 1873. Colorful beach cabins and seafood restaurants perpetuate the bathing tradition.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Saint-Jacques Church of Le Tréport — This 15th-16th century flamboyant Gothic church dominates the port with its massive silhouette. Its pendant keystones and Renaissance porch testify to the port's prosperity in the Middle Ages.
⏱️ 30 min

Discover

1
Notre-Dame Church of Caudebec — Described by Henry IV as "the most beautiful chapel in the kingdom," this 15th-16th century flamboyant Gothic church displays a lace stone facade with 333 statues. Its Renaissance organ and stained glass windows are among the treasures of Norman art.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Maison des Templiers — This 13th-century half-timbered medieval house, one of the oldest in Normandy, houses a local history museum. Its carved beams and low rooms testify to medieval civil architecture.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
MuséoSeine — This modern museum inaugurated in 2015 retraces the history of the river, navigation and the tidal bore that swept over Caudebec until 1963. Models of Viking ships, barges and river boats evoke 2,000 years of river history.
⏱️ 75 min
→
Caudebec-Villequier Seine Ferry — This free crossing allows you to cross the Seine in a few minutes to reach the north bank. An authentic river experience offering views of the cliffs and meanders of the river.
⏱️ 20 min
→
Boucles de la Seine Normande Regional Nature Park — This 89,000-hectare park protects the Seine meanders, the Brotonne and Roumare forests, marshes and orchards. Hiking, bird watching and discovery of Norman rural heritage.
⏱️ 60 min
