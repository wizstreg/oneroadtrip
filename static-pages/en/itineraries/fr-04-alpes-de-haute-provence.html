Alpes-De-Haute-Provence Circuit: Lavender and Perched Villages

Limestone cliffs plunge 700 meters into the emerald waters of the Verdon, lavender fields undulate endlessly under the Provençal sun, perched villages crown their rocky spurs. Haute-Provence unfolds its legendary landscapes.

🔄 Route type Circuit Digne-les-Bains ↔ Sisteron
📏 Daily average 32 km

From the vertiginous Verdon gorges to the lavender fields of the Valensole plateau, this circuit reveals the treasures of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence. Classified perched villages, medieval citadels and iconic landscapes compose a journey through authentic Provence, between spectacular canyon and fragrant plateaus.

  • Moustiers-Sainte-Marie: earthenware village suspended from cliffs
  • Verdon Gorges: Europe's largest canyon and the Martel trail
  • Valensole Plateau: ocean of blooming lavender from June to July
  • Sisteron Citadel: vertiginous fortress dominating the Durance

Discover

1
The Geological Reserve of Haute-Provence reveals its fossil treasures. Marine imprints emerge from limestone rock, witnesses of a vanished ocean 300 million years ago. The geological trail leads you through ammonite slabs and fossilized ichthyosaurs.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Gassendi Museum combines contemporary art and natural sciences in an unexpected dialogue. Naturalist collections sit alongside installations by resident artists. Zenith light floods the main gallery where Andy Goldsworthy's works and geological specimens interact.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Discover the limestone rock that rises above Digne-les-Bains like a stone ship. Steep trails lead to the summit where views embrace the spa town and surrounding valleys. The perched chapel has watched over the city since the Middle Ages.
⏱️ 75 min
4
The Digne Thermes have been exploiting sulfurous waters gushing at 42°C since 1930. The spa offers treatments and balneotherapy in a preserved Art Deco setting. The thermal gardens provide a relaxing haven in the shade of century-old plane trees.
⏱️ 60 min

Digne-les-Bains is the ideal starting point to explore the Verdon gorges. Scenic roads lead toward Castellane and the canyon entrances. Plan a full day for this memorable excursion.

The Route des Crêtes winds along the edge of the void. Viewpoints follow one another, offering vertiginous plunges into the canyon. Each bend reveals a new perspective on the turquoise meanders of the Verdon and sheer walls.

The old village of Castellane preserves its medieval gates and 14th-century ramparts. Walk through cobbled streets where Provençal houses line up. Place Marcel-Sauvaire brings the town center to life with its market and shaded terraces.

Discover

1
Climb up to Notre-Dame-de-Beauvoir chapel watching over the village. The 262 steps wind through oratories adorned with earthenware tiles. The 12th-century Romanesque sanctuary dominates Moustiers rooftops and offers a plunging view over Lac de Sainte-Croix.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The golden star suspended between two cliffs has intrigued since the 10th century. The 135-meter chain carries this mystical symbol above the village. According to legend, knight de Blacas had it suspended in gratitude to the Virgin Mary after his release from the crusades.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Earthenware Museum traces the history of this craft renowned throughout Europe. Over 300 exceptional pieces from the 17th and 18th centuries reveal the evolution of designs and techniques. The pharmacy goat signed by Clérissy in 1687 is the collection's jewel.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Earthenware workshops perpetuate centuries-old know-how. Seven workshops open their doors to visitors. Throwing, glazing and painting traditional bird motifs unfold before your eyes respecting ancestral methods.
⏱️ 60 min

⏱️ 120 min
→
Visit a traditional distillery where lavender essence is extracted. Copper stills transform freshly cut flowers into essential oil. The aroma fills stone buildings where bouquets once dried. Discover derivative products and natural cosmetics.
⏱️ 75 min
→
The Friday morning Provençal market brings Place Montelupo to life. Local producers offer goat cheeses, lavender honey, olives and olive oil. The colorful and fragrant atmosphere evokes authentic Provençal terroir.

Discover

1
The Sisteron citadel crowns the rocky spur blocking the Durance valley. The imposing fortress displays its bastioned enclosures and keep over eight centuries of military architecture. The underground staircase of 258 steps carved into rock in the 19th century links the town to the citadel in a mysterious ascent.
⏱️ 120 min
2
Notre-Dame-des-Pommiers cathedral blends Provençal Romanesque and Lombard influences. The sober 12th-century building displays a harmoniously proportioned nave. Side chapels house Baroque works and the 19th-century great organ resounds during summer concerts.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Walk through old town streets tiered between Durance and citadel. 14th-century medieval towers punctuate the former ramparts' layout. Renaissance fountains and fortified gates mark this labyrinth where Provençal town atmosphere reigns.
⏱️ 90 min
4
The Baume rock rises facing the citadel on the other side of the Durance. Horizontal limestone strata give the impression of a stack of slices. This remarkable geological site dominates the cluse where the valley narrows.
⏱️ 45 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
The Sisteron via ferrata winds on the Baume rock walls. Cables, ladders and aerial footbridges allow a vertiginous ascent. The equipped route offers thrills safely with plunging views over the Durance and citadel.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Paragliding takes off from surrounding heights. The Durance valley's powerful thermals carry wings in an aerial ballet. The panorama embraces citadel, Durance and Alpine valleys in an unforgettable flight.
⏱️ 90 min
→
The Route Napoléon crosses Sisteron on the Emperor's return historic route. On March 5, 1815, Napoleon crossed the town without resistance during his march to Grenoble. A museum in the citadel traces this epic episode of French history.
