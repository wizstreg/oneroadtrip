Cape Town

table mountain waterfront robben island iconic

10 photos
Days 1-4
🔍

Discover

1
Ascend Table Mountain, the iconic flat-topped landmark that dominates Cape Town's skyline and forms part of a spectacular 60km mountain chain stretching to Cape Point. Whether you choose the scenic cable car ride or the invigorating hike to the summit, you'll be rewarded with panoramic views considered among the finest in the world. From this UNESCO-recognized natural wonder, marvel at two World Heritage Sites simultaneously: the mountain itself and historic Robben Island in the distance.
2
Explore Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden, a world-renowned UNESCO World Heritage Site nestled against Table Mountain's eastern slopes. Wander through indigenous forests, relax on manicured lawns, and be amazed by ancient cycads and the remarkable Peninsula Garden featuring over 2,500 plant species found nowhere else on Earth. Don't miss the Tree Canopy Walkway, a suspended bridge offering a unique perspective through the forest canopy.
🎯

Activities

→
Discover the Cape Floral Kingdom, one of six floral kingdoms on Earth and a UNESCO World Heritage Site containing 3% of the world's plant species. This extraordinary ecosystem stretches from the Cape Peninsula to the Eastern Cape, with an astounding 69% of its flora found nowhere else on the planet. The diversity and uniqueness of this botanical treasure make it an essential experience for nature enthusiasts.

Simons Town

african penguins boulders beach naval heritage

2 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Boulders Beach in the charming naval town of Simon's Town, home to a thriving colony of approximately 3,000 African penguins. Protected by massive granite boulders that create sheltered coves, this remarkable beach offers an unforgettable opportunity to observe these charismatic seabirds in their natural habitat. Wooden boardwalks allow close encounters while protecting the sensitive penguin nesting areas.
2
Journey to Cape Point, considered one of the most beautiful places in South Africa, where two oceans dramatically meet at the continent's southwestern tip. Part of the Cape Floral Kingdom, this windswept headland is covered in indigenous fynbos vegetation and provides critical habitat for pelagic seabirds and a resident baboon population. The historic lighthouse offers commanding views over the turbulent waters where the Atlantic and Indian Oceans converge.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience whale watching along the Western Cape coast during migration season from June to October. These magnificent marine mammals journey past the coastline, with Hermanus hosting the renowned annual Whale Festival in September. The region's calm bays and nutrient-rich waters make it one of the world's premier land-based whale watching destinations.

Blaauwberg

4 photos
Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
Explore Blaauwberg Nature Reserve, located just 30km from Cape Town, offering spectacular views down fynbos-covered slopes across the cityscape and over 7km of pristine rocky and sandy coastline. This unique vantage point is one of few places on Earth where you can simultaneously view two UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Table Mountain and Robben Island. The reserve also preserves the historic site of the 1806 Battle of Blaauwberg.
🎯

Activities

→
Relax at Melkbosstrand, a quintessential seaside village popular with locals and water sports enthusiasts. The long main beach backed by green lawns is perfect for picnics, while the area holds historical significance as the landing site of British troops during the 1806 invasion.

Atlantis

coastal dunes west coast gateway industrial

Day 7
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Koeberg Private Nature Reserve, offering educational information about the nearby power station along with two spectacular walking trails showcasing diverse fauna and flora including grysbok, steenbok, and springbok. Explore the reserve by bicycle along several scenic routes, and visit the bird hide for peaceful picnicking and exceptional birdwatching opportunities.
2
Unwind at Silwerstroomstrand, a Blue Flag beach renowned for its excellent water quality and expansive stretch of coastline in a wonderfully secluded setting. A small rocky point provides natural protection from swells for safe ocean swimming, while a tidal pool offers warmer, calmer waters. Picnic areas, barbeque facilities, and accommodation options make this an ideal coastal retreat.
🎯

Activities

→
Adventure through the Witzand Aquifer Nature Reserve, home to the world-famous Atlantis Dunefields. This unique conservation area combines sandy beaches, rocky shorelines, and vast dunes offering spectacular views of Table Mountain. The dunes are perfect for 4x4 adventures and sandboarding, creating an unforgettable desert-meets-ocean experience.

Darling

wildflower season evita cabaret quirky village

Day 8
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the historic Mamre Mission Station, a hidden gem established in 1808 by Moravian missionaries. The original church and parsonage, now protected as national monuments, retain their authentic charm and continue to serve the community with weekly services. A beautifully restored watermill now operates as a fascinating museum showcasing the town's rich heritage.
2
Tour Groote Post Wine Cellar, a historic 18th-century estate where the unique terroir and cool climate of the Darling Hills produce exceptional wines. Part of the famous West Coast Flower Route, the property offers spectacular nature walks and game drives through indigenous fynbos landscapes. Enjoy wine tastings paired with delicious cuisine in this picturesque setting.
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the quaint town of Darling, situated in the heart of the West Coast Flower Region where spring transforms the surrounding fields into stunning tapestries of wildflowers. Visit during September for the celebrated Darling Wildflower Show held every third weekend. The town's charming streets also house the famous Evita se Perron theatre complex.

Riebeek Kasteel

olive groves wine tasting mountain backdrop

Day 9
🔍

Discover

1
Discover the Riebeek Valley, encompassing the picturesque towns of Riebeek West and Riebeek Kasteel nestled among rolling vineyards and silvery olive groves. This area is renowned for its vibrant markets and festivals, including the popular annual Olive Festival celebrating the region's Mediterranean-style produce. Kasteelberg mountain offers rewarding hikes with opportunities to spot falcons, eagles, and owls.
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the !Khwa ttu San Cultural and Educational Centre, offering profound insights into the culture, heritage, and knowledge of the original inhabitants of the West Coast. Qualified San guides share their unique skills and ancient wisdom during immersive tours. The centre also features spectacular mountain bike trails and the original farmhouse transformed into a welcoming restaurant.

Langebaan

lagoon kayaking west coast national park

Day 10
🔍

Discover

1
Explore the West Coast National Park, a pristine nature reserve stretching from Yzerfontein to Langebaan, offering diverse activities including exceptional birdwatching with over 200 species of land and sea birds. Wildlife enthusiasts can spot springbok, kudu, gemsbok, and the rare mountain zebra roaming the protected landscapes. Cycling trails and whale watching opportunities complement this remarkable coastal sanctuary.
2
Discover the charming seaside village of Yzerfontein, a haven for birdlife, wildlife, sea life, and indigenous flora. The area boasts South Africa's longest stretch of beach, extending 16 miles northward to the West Coast National Park. Popular with water sports enthusiasts, the village also offers excellent whale watching, fishing, and hiking opportunities.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience Langebaan, known as the Jewel of the West Coast, blessed with almost year-round sunshine, azure lagoon waters, and unsurpassed natural beauty. This internationally acclaimed Ramsar Site supports myriad bird species as a critical wetland habitat. The mild climate and protected waters make it the perfect destination for kitesurfing, sailing, and various water sports.

Saldanha

bay oysters steel industry working harbor

Day 11
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Saldanha Bay, situated in South Africa's largest natural bay, renowned for its striking contrasts and natural beauty. As the hub of the regional fishing industry and a popular holiday destination for water sports enthusiasts, the bay offers diverse experiences. The South African Military Academy campus includes a pristine nature reserve that transforms into a floral wonderland in spring and provides excellent vantage points for Southern Right Whale watching during calving season.
2
Explore the beautiful, rugged coastline of Jacobsbaai, boasting a spectacular 2km sea frontage encompassing seven distinct bays. A 17km hiking trail between Swartrietbaai and Tietiesbaai traverses coastal fynbos supporting diverse wildlife. Often called 'Namaqualand by the sea,' the area's wildflower displays are breathtakingly beautiful during spring.
🎯

Activities

→
Discover Hopefield, a historic town dating back to 1851, surrounded by delicate fynbos and waving green wheat fields. Visit the beautiful Dutch Reformed Church built in 1879, where the original organ still plays at weddings and during the Hopefield Fynbos Show. The Fossil Museum displays a replica of the famous Saldanha Man hominid skull, and the Simply Bee Observation Centre offers fascinating insights into honey production.

Paternoster

whitewashed cottages crayfish west coast charm

Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Experience Paternoster, one of the oldest and most authentic fishing villages on the West Coast, where life remains intimately connected to the sea. Fishermen still venture out daily in colourful traditional wooden boats, and their fresh catch can be purchased at the local market or savoured in quaint seaside restaurants. The pristine white-washed cottages and turquoise waters create a postcard-perfect setting.
2
Visit Shelley Point, a tiny unspoiled peninsula nestled in the calm waters of Shelley Bay. Protected from ocean swells, the sheltered waters are ideal for sailing, fishing, and safe swimming for families. This tranquil retreat offers the perfect escape from modern life, with a 9-hole golf course and wellness facilities complementing the natural serenity.
🎯

Activities

→
Explore St Helena Bay, an unspoilt coastline traversing 18 bays and fed by the nutrient-rich Benguela Current, making it one of the world's prime fishing areas. Dolphins and Southern Right Whales frequent the calm waters, while the Cape St Martin Private Nature Reserve offers the unique opportunity to watch the sunrise over the sea-the only such location on the West Coast.

Velddrif

Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Bokkomlaan in Velddrif, the heart of the West Coast fishing industry and an undiscovered gem recently granted heritage status. Cross the Carinus Bridge and stop at the viewpoint beneath tall palm trees to observe waders in the waterway. Time seems to stand still along this historic street as fishing boats bob beside numerous jetties and fishermen prepare their catch using traditional methods passed down through generations.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience Thali Thali Game Lodge, a pristine privately owned game and fynbos reserve abundant with wildlife and bird species. Home to various antelope and many other species in a predator-free environment, guests can safely explore on foot. Daily game drives, archery, and a 3D bow hunting track provide diverse activities amidst the natural beauty of the West Coast.

Oudtshoorn

ostrich farms cango caves karoo gateway

Day 14
🔍

Discover

1
Descend into the Cango Caves, a spectacular underground wonderland that has been forming its stalactite masterpiece for 4.5 billion years. Marvel at strange and wondrous formations including the Madonna and Child, the towering 9-metre Cleopatra's Needle, the Dried Tobacco Leaves cluster, the Cathedral's Pulpit, and even a Leaning Tower of Pisa. The adventure tunnel and ladder system offers an exciting challenge for the brave-hearted.
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the Karoo National Park, a remarkable desert ecosystem teeming with life including lions, rhinos, and buffalo. From quirky small towns to working farms, you could travel for months experiencing unique adventures in each location. The Karoo contains South Africa's largest ecosystem, boasting endemic plants and creatures found nowhere else in the world.

Storms River

tsitsikamma bridges bloukrans bungee forest trails

Day 15
🔍

Discover

1
Drive the legendary Garden Route along the N2 from the Southern Cape to Tsitsikamma Forest, flanked by massive mountain ranges and an ever-changing ocean panorama. One moment you'll be climbing mountain passes overlooking beaches dotted with dolphins, the next you're immersed in ancient, mysterious indigenous forest. This scenic drive is consistently ranked among the world's most beautiful road journeys.
2
Experience the dramatic Storms River Mouth, a spectacular gorge where the river thunders into the Indian Ocean. This top-rated National Park destination offers white-water rafting, swimming in cola-coloured pools, and kayaking through steep kloofs. Hike across suspension bridges, swim under waterfalls, and snorkel or dive in the protected marine reserve to understand why this remains one of South Africa's most beloved natural attractions.
🎯

Activities

→
Wander through the ancient Tsitsikamma Forest, part of the Garden Route National Park, where towering yellowwood trees create a mystical canopy over fern-covered forest floors. Walking trails wind through this pristine indigenous forest, home to diverse bird species and the elusive Knysna elephant population. The combination of forest, fynbos, and coastline creates an unparalleled natural sanctuary.

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Autumn (March-May): Perfect weather with comfortable temperatures (18-26°C), minimal rainfall, and ideal conditions for safari and outdoor activities. Excellent wine harvest season, beautiful landscapes, moderate tourist numbers, reasonable accommodation rates.

Spring (September-November): Excellent weather with warming temperatures (16-24°C), blooming wildflowers, and perfect conditions for wildlife viewing. Whale watching season, jacaranda blooms, moderate prices, excellent photography opportunities.

Summer (December-February): Warm temperatures (20-30°C) with occasional rainfall, peak holiday season, and vibrant outdoor atmosphere. Beach season, highest accommodation rates, school holidays, excellent for coastal activities.

Winter (June-August): Cool, dry weather (8-20°C) perfect for safari with excellent wildlife viewing and clear skies. Peak safari season, whale watching, cozy wine estates, moderate accommodation rates.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 12 and 18 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.
