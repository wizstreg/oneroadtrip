Discover

1
Discover Sofia, Bulgaria's capital where you can witness evidence of Roman, Medieval, Ottoman and Soviet past all woven together. This city has a real east-meets-west feel with medieval churches, onion-domed cathedrals and Ottoman mosques standing side by side in fascinating harmony.
⏱️ 75 min

Visit Boyana Church on Sofia's outskirts, a UNESCO World Heritage Site consisting of three buildings spanning the 10th to 19th centuries. ⏱️ 75 min

Hike the Seven Rila Lakes, a series of glacial lakes staggered between 2,000 and 2,500 meters elevation, one of the most picturesque places to visit in Bulgaria. These interconnected lakes linked by streams and waterfalls rank among Bulgaria's most famous tourist attractions.

⏱️ 75 min
🎯

1
Visit Rila Monastery (Sveti Ivan Rilski), a UNESCO World Heritage Site founded in 927 AD by the hermit Ivan of Rila who lived in a cave nearby. This is one of the best places to visit in Bulgaria thanks to its tranquil location in the mountainous national park.

Explore the monastery complex including the church, residential areas for the 60 monks who still live here, and the museum. The building as seen today dates back to the 15th century, with some parts older still, rebuilt between 1834 and 1862 after a devastating fire.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Admire this characteristic example of the Bulgarian Renaissance (18th-19th centuries) which symbolizes the awareness of Slavic cultural identity following centuries of occupation. The monastery played an important role in the spiritual and social life of medieval Bulgaria.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk through the surrounding Rila Monastery Nature Park, one of the largest national parks in Bulgaria. The tranquil mountain setting provides perfect conditions for reflection and photography.

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Summer (June-August): Perfect for Black Sea beaches and mountain hiking, warmest weather, peak tourist season, highest prices.

Spring (April-May): Excellent weather, blooming landscapes, ideal for sightseeing and cultural sites, moderate crowds, good value.

Fall (September-October): Pleasant temperatures, fewer crowds, harvest season, excellent for wine regions and cultural exploration.

Winter (December-March): Ski season in mountains, Christmas markets, lower prices except ski resorts, cultural indoor activities.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 19 and 29 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Bulgaria?

Ancient history meets mountain adventures with Black Sea beaches and thermal springs. Rose Valley produces world-famous perfumes while Plovdiv showcases Roman heritage. The country offers authentic Eastern European culture with excellent value and diverse landscapes. Bulgaria combines historical treasures with natural beauty and warm hospitality.
