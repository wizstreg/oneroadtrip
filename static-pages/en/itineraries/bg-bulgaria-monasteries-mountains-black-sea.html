Sofia ★★★★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Sofia, Bulgaria's capital where you can witness evidence of Roman, Medieval, Ottoman and Soviet past all woven together. This city has a real east-meets-west feel with medieval churches, onion-domed cathedrals and Ottoman mosques standing side by side in fascinating harmony.
2
Marvel at the Aleksander Nevsky Cathedral, one of Bulgaria's most sacred symbols with its beautiful blue and gold onion domes. This magnificent Orthodox cathedral ranks among the largest Eastern Orthodox churches in the world and serves as the seat of the Bulgarian Patriarch.
3
Visit Boyana Church on Sofia's outskirts, a UNESCO World Heritage Site consisting of three buildings spanning the 10th to 19th centuries. The frescoes painted in 1259 in the second church make it one of the most important collections of medieval paintings, among the most complete monuments of Eastern European medieval art.
4
Explore the National Museum of Military History for a journey through Bulgaria's ancient history through to its Soviet days. The museum's comprehensive collections document the country's military heritage across millennia.
🎯

Activities

→
Climb 2,292-meter Mount Vitosha for incredible panoramic views across Sofia's rooftops. The mountain makes an excellent day trip from the city, offering hiking in summer and skiing in winter within easy reach of the capital.

Seven Rila Lakes ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
3 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
Hike the Seven Rila Lakes, a series of glacial lakes staggered between 2,000 and 2,500 meters elevation, one of the most picturesque places to visit in Bulgaria. These interconnected lakes linked by streams and waterfalls rank among Bulgaria's most famous tourist attractions.
2
Discover each lake named for its unique characteristics: 'The Tear' for its clear waters, 'The Eye' as the deepest at 37.5 meters, 'The Kidney' for its shape, plus The Twins, The Fish Lake, The Trefoil, and The Lower Lake. The highest sits at about 2,500m, the lowest at 2,100m.
🎯

Activities

→
Take the chairlift from Panichishte resort to conserve energy for the 4-5 hour hike around all lakes. Return by 16:30 if planning to take the chairlift back down.
→
For experienced mountaineers, consider climbing Musala Peak (2,925m), the highest point in the Balkans, accessible from this region.
🚗

Rila Monastery ★★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
3 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Rila Monastery (Sveti Ivan Rilski), a UNESCO World Heritage Site founded in 927 AD by the hermit Ivan of Rila who lived in a cave nearby. This is one of the best places to visit in Bulgaria thanks to its tranquil location in the mountainous national park.
2
Explore the monastery complex including the church, residential areas for the 60 monks who still live here, and the museum. The building as seen today dates back to the 15th century, with some parts older still, rebuilt between 1834 and 1862 after a devastating fire.
3
Admire this characteristic example of the Bulgarian Renaissance (18th-19th centuries) which symbolizes the awareness of Slavic cultural identity following centuries of occupation. The monastery played an important role in the spiritual and social life of medieval Bulgaria.
🎯

Activities

→
Walk through the surrounding Rila Monastery Nature Park, one of the largest national parks in Bulgaria. The tranquil mountain setting provides perfect conditions for reflection and photography.

Discover

1
Discover Melnik at the heart of Bulgaria's wine region covering over 150,000 acres - the country was once the fourth-largest wine producer in the world. This charming city makes a perfect base for exploring nearby vineyards and wineries.

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
6 photos
Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
Experience Velingrad, another Rhodope Mountains town famous for its healing waters. Founded in 1948 after unifying several villages, the town now hosts over 90 mineral water springs including the renowned Ladzhene, Kamenitsa, Chepino, Rakitovo and Kostandovo.
2
Choose from 22 different mineral baths and pools varying in temperature and mineral content. Due to the town's popularity there are three sanitariums and over 60 hotels dedicated to wellness treatments.
3
Visit Lake Kleptuza, one of the largest karst springs in Bulgaria, where crystal-clear water emerges from underground caverns creating a picturesque lake setting.
🎯

Activities

→
The mineral waters are used to treat and prevent many diseases, drawing visitors from across the country. This is one of Bulgaria's premier wellness destinations.

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 10
🔍

Discover

1
Explore Perperikon, one of the most ancient monumental megalithic structures entirely carved into the rocks. Located in the East Rhodope Mountain 20km northeast of Kardzhali, this medieval archaeological complex stands on a clifftop at 470 meters elevation.
2
Discover the rock city that has been inhabited since ancient times, with the comfortable river valley creating conditions of life for millennia. The site is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Bulgaria, revealing layers of civilization carved directly into the stone.
🎯

Activities

→
The surrounding valley is strewn with dozens of archaeological sites from various ages, with Perperikon as the center. The dramatic setting overlooking the Perpereshka River valley creates unforgettable views.

Discover

1
Discover Plovdiv, Bulgaria's second largest city and often touted as the oldest continuously inhabited place in Europe. The city sits on the banks of the Maritsa River surrounded by seven hills, like Rome, with history dating back thousands of years.
2
Visit the incredible Theater of Philippopolis, constructed in the 90s of the 1st century AD with 28 concentric rows of marble seats surrounding the orchestra. This Roman theater with capacity for 6,000 spectators is still used for events today.
3
Explore Plovdiv Old Town with its charming cobbled streets and Renaissance Revival architecture. As European Capital of Culture in 2019, the city is considered Bulgaria's cultural capital.
4
Climb Nebet Tepe or Bunardzhik Tepe hills for enchanting views over the city and its architectural treasures spanning millennia.
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the Kapana suburb, the creative quarter filled with art galleries, cafes, and boutiques. The ancient and modern blend seamlessly in this vibrant cultural hub.

Shipka Pass ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Cross the Shipka Pass, a 1,150-meter pass through the Balkan Mountain Range famous for the Battle of Shipka Pass during the Russo-Turkish War of 1877-1878. The Russians with Bulgarian help fought the Ottoman Empire in this strategic location.
2
Visit the Shipka Monument, a 31.5-meter stone tower erected in 1934 to commemorate the battle. The memorial commands stunning views over the countryside and is a significant site in Bulgarian national history.
3
Discover the Thracian Tomb of Kazanlak nearby, a UNESCO World Heritage Site dating from the end of the 4th century BC. The tomb's murals representing Thracian burial rituals are Bulgaria's best-preserved artistic masterpieces from the Hellenistic period.
🎯

Activities

→
Hike the Shipka Pass area for spectacular mountain views. See the historic cannons that played a role in the famous battle.

Etar ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
3 photos
Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Architectural Ethnographic Complex Etar, the first of its type in Bulgaria founded in 1964 and located 8km south of Gabrovo. The museum features the only and unique collection of old-time water-driven machinery in Bulgaria.
2
Explore 10 water-driven objects forming one of the richest active technical collections among European open-air museums. All objects remain operational as they were in the past - hence the water wheel became Etar's emblem.
3
Wander the craftsman trade street with 16 models of Balkan architecture showing the original talent of Revival builders. Artisans demonstrate traditional crafts in authentic workshops.
🎯

Activities

→
This living museum offers a unique window into Bulgarian traditional life and craftsmanship, with demonstrations of blacksmithing, woodworking, and other traditional trades.

Descend into Saeva Dupka Cave, with over 400 meters of natural corridors and halls so impressive it's even used as a venue for concerts. Named after brothers Seyu and Sae who hid inside during the Ottoman invasion.

Witness an impressive array of stalactites and stalagmites formed over millennia. Five main galleries each feature unique geological formations. The caves have been inhabited since at least Roman times.

Discover

1
Explore Prohodna Cave, one of Bulgaria's most famous and easily accessible caves located near Karlukovo village. Look up to see 'The Eyes of God' - two eye-shaped holes in the cave ceiling that have become an iconic Bulgarian landmark and popular photography subject.

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 16
🔍

Discover

1
Marvel at the Belogradchik Rocks, natural phenomena that competed for the title of 8th wonder of the world. These red-colored rock formations start before you reach the town and are best viewed from behind the fortress, where you can see formations resembling a couple embracing.
2
Explore the impressive Belogradchik Fortress, a large rock-walled stronghold in the Balkan Mountains dating back to Roman times. The large rocky outcrops served as natural defensive walls, making it one of the best-preserved fortresses in Bulgaria.
3
Discover the town of Belogradchik itself, a popular tourist destination nestled among these extraordinary geological formations. The combination of natural wonders and historical fortification creates a unique landscape.
🎯

Activities

→
Hiking among the rock formations reveals ever-changing perspectives on these ancient sandstone sculptures. The wind is ever-present as you explore these remarkable natural monuments.

Ivanovo ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
3 photos
Day 17
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Ivanovo Rock Churches, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the valley of the Roussenski Lom River in northeast Bulgaria. This complex of rock-hewn churches, chapels, monasteries and cells developed where the first hermits dug their dwellings during the 12th century.
2
Admire the 14th-century murals testifying to the exceptional skill of artists belonging to the Tarnovo School of painting. These frescoes rank among the finest examples of medieval Bulgarian art.
3
Nearby, visit the Sveshtarska Tomb, a 3rd-century BC Thracian tomb discovered in 1982 with unique architectural decor including polychrome half-human, half-plant caryatids. The 10 female figures carved in high relief are the only examples found in Thracian lands.
🎯

Activities

→
The atmospheric rock-hewn complex reveals how monks lived in harmony with the dramatic landscape, carving their spiritual homes directly into the cliff faces.

Madara ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 18
🔍

Discover

1
Marvel at the Madara Rider, a UNESCO World Heritage Site carved into a 100-meter-high cliff near Madara village. This figure of a knight triumphing over a lion was carved when Madara was the principal sacred place of the First Bulgarian Empire before Bulgaria's conversion to Christianity.
2
Read the inscriptions beside the sculpture telling of events between AD 705 and 801. This unique rock relief is the only such monument from the early medieval period in Europe.
3
Visit Ovech Fortress near Provadiya, standing on a ship-shaped plateau. Reached via the picturesque Ovech eco-trail with steps cut into the rock, this fortress offers local legends from museum guides, plus archery and spear-throwing activities.
🎯

Activities

→
The Madara archaeological reserve includes other Thracian, Roman, and medieval remains around this sacred site that served multiple civilizations.

Discover

1
Visit the Palace and Botanical Garden in Balchik, a piece of heaven on earth and a must-see tourist attraction on the Bulgarian Black Sea coast. This architectural and park complex was created as a summer residence for Romanian Queen Maria starting in 1924.
2
Explore the villa 'Tenha Yuvah' ('lonely nest') and subsequent buildings including an Orthodox chapel, nymphaeum, Moorish-styled patios, a traditional Revival Period house, and fountains decorated with fine marble medallions depicting mythological characters.

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Summer (June-August): Perfect for Black Sea beaches and mountain hiking, warmest weather, peak tourist season, highest prices.

Spring (April-May): Excellent weather, blooming landscapes, ideal for sightseeing and cultural sites, moderate crowds, good value.

Fall (September-October): Pleasant temperatures, fewer crowds, harvest season, excellent for wine regions and cultural exploration.

Winter (December-March): Ski season in mountains, Christmas markets, lower prices except ski resorts, cultural indoor activities.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 18 and 26 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Bulgaria?

Ancient history meets mountain adventures with Black Sea beaches and thermal springs. Rose Valley produces world-famous perfumes while Plovdiv showcases Roman heritage. The country offers authentic Eastern European culture with excellent value and diverse landscapes. Bulgaria combines historical treasures with natural beauty and warm hospitality.

  • Discover Melnik at the heart of Bulgaria's wine region covering over 150,000 acres - the country was once the fourth-largest wine producer in the world. This charming city makes a perfect base for exploring nearby vineyards and wineries.
  • Marvel at the Melnik Earth Pyramids, sandstone pyramids and obelisks rising out of the foothills and towering 100 meters above the forest. These unique geological formations create a surreal landscape surrounding the town.
  • Explore the old town with its interesting churches, monastery, and distinctive architecture. The combination of wine culture and natural wonders makes Melnik one of Bulgaria's hidden treasures.

Day 6

Velingrad ★★★

Discover
  • Experience Velingrad, another Rhodope Mountains town famous for its healing waters. Founded in 1948 after unifying several villages, the town now hosts over 90 mineral water springs including the renowned Ladzhene, Kamenitsa, Chepino, Rakitovo and Kostandovo.
  • Choose from 22 different mineral baths and pools varying in temperature and mineral content. Due to the town's popularity there are three sanitariums and over 60 hotels dedicated to wellness treatments.
  • Visit Lake Kleptuza, one of the largest karst springs in Bulgaria, where crystal-clear water emerges from underground caverns creating a picturesque lake setting.
Activities
  • The mineral waters are used to treat and prevent many diseases, drawing visitors from across the country. This is one of Bulgaria's premier wellness destinations.

Batak Reservoir ★★

Discover

Day 8

Devin ★★

Discover
  • Relax in Devin, one of Bulgaria's most popular spa towns in the Vacha River valley. The hot springs, thermal spas and resorts here have been known for their healing properties since antiquity, treating ailments especially related to the skin.
  • Explore nearby Trigrad Gorge with its dramatic cliffs, the mysterious Devil's Throat Cave, and the impressive Yagodinska Cave. Devin's location nestled in the mountains makes it one of the most scenic parts of Bulgaria.

Day 9

Devils Bridge ★

Discover
  • Discover the Devils Bridge (Dyavolski Most), a beautiful three-arched stone bridge across the Arda River gorge in the Rhodope Mountains near Ardino. Built between 1515 and 1518 by Bulgarian builder Dimitar, it connected Thrace with the Aegean Sea.

Day 10

Perperikon ★★

Discover
  • Explore Perperikon, one of the most ancient monumental megalithic structures entirely carved into the rocks. Located in the East Rhodope Mountain 20km northeast of Kardzhali, this medieval archaeological complex stands on a clifftop at 470 meters elevation.
  • Discover the rock city that has been inhabited since ancient times, with the comfortable river valley creating conditions of life for millennia. The site is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Bulgaria, revealing layers of civilization carved directly into the stone.
Activities
  • The surrounding valley is strewn with dozens of archaeological sites from various ages, with Perperikon as the center. The dramatic setting overlooking the Perpereshka River valley creates unforgettable views.

Day 11

Plovdiv ★★★★

Discover
  • Discover Plovdiv, Bulgaria's second largest city and often touted as the oldest continuously inhabited place in Europe. The city sits on the banks of the Maritsa River surrounded by seven hills, like Rome, with history dating back thousands of years.
  • Visit the incredible Theater of Philippopolis, constructed in the 90s of the 1st century AD with 28 concentric rows of marble seats surrounding the orchestra. This Roman theater with capacity for 6,000 spectators is still used for events today.
  • Explore Plovdiv Old Town with its charming cobbled streets and Renaissance Revival architecture. As European Capital of Culture in 2019, the city is considered Bulgaria's cultural capital.
  • Climb Nebet Tepe or Bunardzhik Tepe hills for enchanting views over the city and its architectural treasures spanning millennia.
Activities
  • Explore the Kapana suburb, the creative quarter filled with art galleries, cafes, and boutiques. The ancient and modern blend seamlessly in this vibrant cultural hub.

Day 12

Shipka Pass ★★

Discover
  • Cross the Shipka Pass, a 1,150-meter pass through the Balkan Mountain Range famous for the Battle of Shipka Pass during the Russo-Turkish War of 1877-1878. The Russians with Bulgarian help fought the Ottoman Empire in this strategic location.
  • Visit the Shipka Monument, a 31.5-meter stone tower erected in 1934 to commemorate the battle. The memorial commands stunning views over the countryside and is a significant site in Bulgarian national history.
  • Discover the Thracian Tomb of Kazanlak nearby, a UNESCO World Heritage Site dating from the end of the 4th century BC. The tomb's murals representing Thracian burial rituals are Bulgaria's best-preserved artistic masterpieces from the Hellenistic period.
Activities
  • Hike the Shipka Pass area for spectacular mountain views. See the historic cannons that played a role in the famous battle.

Day 13

Etar ★★

Discover
  • Visit the Architectural Ethnographic Complex Etar, the first of its type in Bulgaria founded in 1964 and located 8km south of Gabrovo. The museum features the only and unique collection of old-time water-driven machinery in Bulgaria.
  • Explore 10 water-driven objects forming one of the richest active technical collections among European open-air museums. All objects remain operational as they were in the past - hence the water wheel became Etar's emblem.
  • Wander the craftsman trade street with 16 models of Balkan architecture showing the original talent of Revival builders. Artisans demonstrate traditional crafts in authentic workshops.
Activities
  • This living museum offers a unique window into Bulgarian traditional life and craftsmanship, with demonstrations of blacksmithing, woodworking, and other traditional trades.

Saeva Dupka ★

Discover

Day 15

Prohodna Cave ★

Discover
  • Explore Prohodna Cave, one of Bulgaria's most famous and easily accessible caves located near Karlukovo village. This natural rock bridge stretches 262 meters with two entrances - at the small entrance the height reaches 35 meters, while the highest cave vault in Bulgaria at 45 meters awaits at the big entrance.
  • Look up to see 'The Eyes of God' - two eye-shaped holes in the cave ceiling that have become an iconic Bulgarian landmark and popular photography subject.
Activities
  • The trail passes through the entire cave, offering a unique experience of walking through this natural geological wonder.

Day 16

Belogradchik ★★

Discover
  • Marvel at the Belogradchik Rocks, natural phenomena that competed for the title of 8th wonder of the world. These red-colored rock formations start before you reach the town and are best viewed from behind the fortress, where you can see formations resembling a couple embracing.
  • Explore the impressive Belogradchik Fortress, a large rock-walled stronghold in the Balkan Mountains dating back to Roman times. The large rocky outcrops served as natural defensive walls, making it one of the best-preserved fortresses in Bulgaria.
  • Discover the town of Belogradchik itself, a popular tourist destination nestled among these extraordinary geological formations. The combination of natural wonders and historical fortification creates a unique landscape.
Activities
  • Hiking among the rock formations reveals ever-changing perspectives on these ancient sandstone sculptures. The wind is ever-present as you explore these remarkable natural monuments.

Day 17

Ivanovo ★★

Discover
  • Visit the Ivanovo Rock Churches, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the valley of the Roussenski Lom River in northeast Bulgaria. This complex of rock-hewn churches, chapels, monasteries and cells developed where the first hermits dug their dwellings during the 12th century.
  • Admire the 14th-century murals testifying to the exceptional skill of artists belonging to the Tarnovo School of painting. These frescoes rank among the finest examples of medieval Bulgarian art.
  • Nearby, visit the Sveshtarska Tomb, a 3rd-century BC Thracian tomb discovered in 1982 with unique architectural decor including polychrome half-human, half-plant caryatids. The 10 female figures carved in high relief are the only examples found in Thracian lands.
Activities
  • The atmospheric rock-hewn complex reveals how monks lived in harmony with the dramatic landscape, carving their spiritual homes directly into the cliff faces.

Day 18

Madara ★★

Discover
  • Marvel at the Madara Rider, a UNESCO World Heritage Site carved into a 100-meter-high cliff near Madara village. This figure of a knight triumphing over a lion was carved when Madara was the principal sacred place of the First Bulgarian Empire before Bulgaria's conversion to Christianity.
  • Read the inscriptions beside the sculpture telling of events between AD 705 and 801. This unique rock relief is the only such monument from the early medieval period in Europe.
  • Visit Ovech Fortress near Provadiya, standing on a ship-shaped plateau. Reached via the picturesque Ovech eco-trail with steps cut into the rock, this fortress offers local legends from museum guides, plus archery and spear-throwing activities.
Activities
  • The Madara archaeological reserve includes other Thracian, Roman, and medieval remains around this sacred site that served multiple civilizations.

Varna ★

Discover

Kaliakra ★

Discover

Day 21

Balchik ★★

Discover
  • Visit the Palace and Botanical Garden in Balchik, a piece of heaven on earth and a must-see tourist attraction on the Bulgarian Black Sea coast. This architectural and park complex was created as a summer residence for Romanian Queen Maria starting in 1924.
  • Explore the villa 'Tenha Yuvah' ('lonely nest') and subsequent buildings including an Orthodox chapel, nymphaeum, Moorish-styled patios, a traditional Revival Period house, and fountains decorated with fine marble medallions depicting mythological characters.
  • Wander through the botanical garden with its remarkable collection of plants from around the world, including one of Europe's largest collections of cacti and succulents.
Activities
  • The romantic palace designed by Italian architects combines with the exotic gardens to create one of the most beautiful settings on the Black Sea coast.
