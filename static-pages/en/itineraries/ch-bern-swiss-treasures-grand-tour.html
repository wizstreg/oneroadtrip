Bern

Top hotels
9 Hotel Schweizerhof Bern & Spa
9.4 Senevita Residenz & Apartments Muri bei Bern
9.9 SONNEN Loft Bern - Bed & Breakfast

Discover Bern, the federal capital of Switzerland and a UNESCO World Heritage treasure. Stroll through the perfectly preserved medieval old town featuring over 6 kilometers of covered arcades, Renaissance fountains adorned with colorful statues, and vaulted cellars housing boutiques and eateries. This captivating city founded in 1191 transports you through centuries of history.

Visit the Zentrum Paul Klee, an architectural masterpiece designed by Renzo Piano housing the world's largest collection of Paul Klee's works. This undulating museum, harmoniously integrated into the rolling landscape, displays over 4,000 paintings, watercolors, and drawings by the Swiss-German artist, offering a fascinating journey through his revolutionary artistic evolution.

→

Obtain the Bern Museum Card for access to all city museums, including the Swiss Alpine Museum, the History Museum, and the Museum of Communication. This card allows you to explore the capital's rich cultural and historical collections at your own pace.

Visit the Emmentaler Schaukäserei (Show Dairy) in Affoltern, the heart of Switzerland's famous cheese region. Watch master cheesemakers craft authentic Emmentaler using centuries-old techniques. The distinctive large holes and nutty flavor of this iconic cheese result from a precise aging process you can observe firsthand.

Try your hand at making your own wheel of Emmentaler cheese under expert guidance. This hands-on experience teaches the traditional methods that have made Swiss cheese renowned worldwide. Your personally crafted cheese can be shipped to your home after proper aging.

→

Explore the rolling hills of the Emmental region, characterized by its traditional farmhouses with distinctive broad roofs. This pastoral landscape of green meadows and wooded hills epitomizes the idyllic Swiss countryside.

Explore Olten, a charming town on the banks of the Aare River, often overlooked by travelers. Visit the Naturmuseum Olten to discover a fascinating collection dedicated to the region's fauna, flora, and fossils. This intimate museum presents Central Switzerland's natural history in an accessible and engaging manner, perfect for an enriching cultural stop.

Discover Basel, Switzerland's third-largest city and cultural capital with over 40 museums. Explore the medieval old town dominated by the majestic red sandstone Münster cathedral, whose panoramic terrace offers spectacular views of the Rhine and city rooftops. The cobblestone alleys reveal architectural treasures at every corner.

Visit the Marktplatz, the vibrant heart of the city, dominated by the impressive Rathaus with its vivid red painted facades. This 16th-century town hall, adorned with remarkable frescoes and an elegant tower, testifies to the historic prosperity of this merchant city at the crossroads of three countries.

→

Cross the Rhine aboard one of the traditional ferries powered solely by the river's current. These unique vessels, connected to a cable, use the water's force to glide from one bank to the other, offering an authentic and ecological experience.

→

Head to the Dreiländereck, the meeting point of Swiss, German, and French borders. This symbolic monument on the Rhine marks the only place where you can contemplate three nations simultaneously.

Explore Schaffhausen, a historic town whose medieval center houses magnificent buildings adorned with frescoes and sculpted oriels. Climb to the circular Munot fortress, a unique 16th-century defensive structure, to enjoy panoramic views over the town, surrounding vineyards, and the Rhine winding through the valley.

Marvel at the Rhine Falls, Europe's largest waterfalls at 150 meters wide and 23 meters high. This grand natural spectacle sees 600,000 liters of water per second plunging with a deafening roar. Boats take you to the base of the falls and to the central rock for an unforgettable immersive experience.

→

Explore the terraced vineyards overlooking the Rhine during a local wine tasting. The Schaffhausen region produces excellent Pinot Noir and Riesling-Sylvaner wines, witnesses to a centuries-old viticultural tradition favored by the valley's mild microclimate.

Discover Zurich, Switzerland's largest city and economic center. Stroll along Bahnhofstrasse, one of the world's most prestigious shopping avenues, lined with luxury boutiques and major brands. This elegant 1.4-kilometer artery connects the main station to the lake, crossing the historic heart of the metropolis.

Explore the Altstadt, the old town with winding medieval lanes on both sides of the Limmat River. Admire the Grossmünster, the iconic Romanesque cathedral with its characteristic twin towers, whose construction began in the 12th century. Legend has it that Charlemagne himself discovered the tombs of Zurich's patron saints at this location.

→

Climb to Lindenhof, a linden tree-shaded hill offering panoramic views of the Limmat River and old town rooftops. This historic site, a former Roman fort, is now a peaceful haven in the heart of urban bustle, ideal for contemplating the Zurich landscape.

Walk along Lake Zurich and enjoy splendid views of the snow-capped Alps in the background. The landscaped quays invite leisurely strolling, with green spaces, sculptures, and terraces overlooking the crystal-clear waters.

→

Visit the Kunsthaus Zurich, one of Switzerland's most important art museums, housing an exceptional collection spanning medieval art to contemporary works, with masterpieces by Monet, Picasso, Giacometti, and numerous Swiss artists.

Lucerne

Discover Lucerne, the pearl of Central Switzerland nestled on the shores of its namesake lake. Cross the Kapellbrücke, Europe's oldest covered wooden bridge dating from 1333, adorned with 111 triangular 17th-century paintings illustrating the city's history and saints' lives. The accompanying octagonal Water Tower completes this iconic picturesque tableau.

Admire the Lion Monument, a poignant sculpture carved into natural rock commemorating the Swiss Guards who fell during the French Revolution. Mark Twain called this 1821 work, depicting a dying lion, 'the saddest and most moving piece of stone in the world'.

→

Wander through the cobblestone alleys of the old town, admiring facades painted with colorful frescoes and lively squares. The historic buildings with their carved oriels tell centuries of commercial and cultural history of this prosperous city.

→

Don't miss the Lilu Light Festival if visiting in winter, when the city illuminates with spectacular artistic projections transforming historic monuments into magical luminous canvases.

Pilatus Kulm

Top hotels
9 Gasthaus Engel Hasle
9.4 Bürgenstock Hotel & Alpine Spa

Ascend Mount Pilatus, the legendary mountain rising to 2,132 meters, accessible by the world's steepest cogwheel railway with a 48% gradient. This engineering marvel inaugurated in 1889 hoists you through vertiginous Alpine landscapes to the summit offering 360° panoramas over the Alps and Swiss Plateau.

Take the Drachenweg, the dragon path carved through the rock according to legends of dragons that once inhabited these caves. This mystical trail winds between rock walls and crevices, evoking the fantastical medieval tales associated with this mythical mountain.

→

Enjoy panoramic views from the summit's various viewpoints, embracing Lake Lucerne, the cantons of Nidwalden and Obwalden, and surrounding Alpine peaks. On clear days, views extend to the Jura Mountains and Black Forest.

→

Descend by red gondolas and cable car to Kriens or by boat from Alpnachstad for a complete 'Golden Round Trip' experience, combining different panoramic transport modes.

Explore Schwyz, capital of the eponymous canton that gave Switzerland its name. Visit the Forum of Swiss History, one of the most important cultural and historical museums in the Alpine region, presenting the origins of the Swiss Confederation and the nation's founding documents, including the Federal Charter of 1291.

Admire the imposing Baroque Town Hall and St. Martin's parish church, witnesses to the historic prosperity of this heritage-rich town. The painted facades of patrician mansions tell the history of families who shaped the region's destiny.

→

Take the Stoosbahn, the world's steepest funicular with gradients up to 110%, ascending to the car-free village of Stoos. This remarkable engineering feat offers breathtaking views during the climb.

→

Hike to the peaks of Rotenflue or Grosser Mythen for stunning panoramic views of the region. These iconic mountains offer trails for various skill levels with rewarding vistas of Central Switzerland.

Discover Brienz, a picturesque village on the shores of turquoise Lake Brienz in the Bernese Oberland. This charming settlement is renowned for its traditional wooden houses dating back to the 18th century and its centuries-old woodcarving heritage. The village still operates a wood carving school and a violin making school, keeping these artisanal traditions alive.

Board the Brienzer Rothorn steam railway, a historic cogwheel train climbing to 2,350 meters above sea level. At the summit, enjoy breathtaking 360° panoramic views including the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau peaks. The nostalgic journey on this century-old steam train is an experience in itself.

→

Take a scenic boat trip across Lake Brienz, renowned for its striking turquoise waters fed by glacial streams. The boat connects lakeside villages and offers stunning views of waterfalls cascading down steep mountainsides.

Interlaken

Arrive in Interlaken, spectacularly situated between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz in the heart of the Bernese Oberland. This charming resort town serves as the gateway to the Jungfrau region and offers stunning views of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau peaks. The town's unique position between two glacial lakes creates an unforgettable Alpine panorama.

Ascend to Harder Kulm by funicular to the peak at 1,322 meters above the town. This legendary viewpoint offers breathtaking 360° views down to Interlaken below and across to the majestic trio of Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau mountains. The viewing platform extends dramatically over the cliff edge for unobstructed Alpine vistas.

→

Explore the St. Beatus Caves, a fascinating underground world of stalactites, stalagmites, and subterranean lakes. According to legend, the Irish monk Beatus drove a dragon from these caves in the 6th century. The illuminated walkways lead through chambers carved by water over millennia.

→

Experience adventure activities that Interlaken is famous for, from paragliding over the lakes to kayaking on the crystal-clear waters. The town is Switzerland's adventure capital, offering unique experiences amidst spectacular scenery.

Explore Grindelwald, a stunning Alpine village nestled in a valley surrounded by the towering peaks of Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau. This year-round destination captivates with lush green meadows in summer and pristine snow-covered slopes in winter. The village offers spectacular mountain scenery with traditional chalets against a backdrop of dramatic rock faces.

Walk the First Cliff Walk, a thrilling walkway suspended along the cliff face at 2,168 meters altitude. This engineering marvel offers heart-pounding views of the Grindelwald valley below and the surrounding peaks. The platform extends into the void, providing an unobstructed Alpine panorama.

Visit the Glacier Canyon, a dramatic gorge carved by glacial waters over millennia. Walkways and bridges lead through this natural wonder, where you can observe the powerful forces that shaped the Alpine landscape and feel the cool mist from rushing waters.

→

Experience mountain karting, an exhilarating descent on three-wheeled carts down the Alpine slopes. This unique activity combines stunning scenery with adrenaline as you navigate the mountain trails with the peaks as your backdrop.

→

In winter, enjoy world-class skiing on slopes facing the iconic North Face of the Eiger. The Grindelwald ski area connects to the vast Jungfrau ski region, offering runs for all abilities amidst spectacular high-Alpine scenery.

Journey to Jungfraujoch - Top of Europe, home to Europe's highest railway station at 3,454 meters. This remarkable destination offers an otherworldly experience among eternal ice and snow. The Ice Palace, carved within the glacier, displays stunning ice sculptures in frozen chambers deep beneath the surface.

Step onto the Sphinx Observation Terrace for unparalleled 360° views of the Aletsch Glacier, the longest glacier in the Alps stretching 23 kilometers. On clear days, vistas extend to the peaks of four countries. The thin Alpine air and brilliant white landscape create an unforgettable high-altitude experience.

Explore the Schilthorn - Piz Gloria, famous as the filming location for the James Bond film 'On Her Majesty's Secret Service'. The revolving restaurant at 2,970 meters offers 360° views while you dine. The Flower Trail showcases diverse Alpine flora during summer months.

→

Walk across the snow plateau for a chance to experience Alpine conditions year-round. The crisp mountain air and brilliant white surroundings offer a unique perspective on the power of glacial landscapes.

Arrive in Saas-Fee, the 'Pearl of the Alps' and one of Switzerland's most spectacular car-free resort villages. Surrounded by 13 peaks over 4,000 meters, this glacier village sits at 1,800 meters in a dramatic mountain amphitheater. Electric vehicles and horse-drawn carriages maintain the peaceful atmosphere of this traffic-free paradise.

Dine at the Drehrestaurant Allalin, the world's highest revolving restaurant at 3,500 meters. Complete a full 360° rotation during your meal while enjoying uninterrupted views of 18 four-thousand-meter peaks. The journey to the restaurant via the Metro Alpin, the world's highest underground funicular, is an experience in itself.

→

Experience year-round skiing and snowboarding on the Fee Glacier. The resort's high altitude guarantees excellent snow conditions even in summer, making it a training destination for professional athletes and a unique opportunity for visitors to ski in any season.

→

Relax at one of the village's spa facilities offering mountain-inspired treatments. The combination of fresh Alpine air, stunning scenery, and wellness traditions creates a rejuvenating retreat in this romantic mountain setting.

Experience Zermatt, the iconic car-free village at the foot of the legendary Matterhorn. This world-renowned resort combines traditional Alpine charm with modern amenities, its cobblestone streets lined with chalets and boutiques beneath the dramatic silhouette of Switzerland's most famous peak. The Matterhorn's distinctive pyramid shape dominates the landscape from every vantage point.

Ascend to Gornergrat by the historic cogwheel railway, climbing to 3,089 meters for one of Switzerland's most spectacular panoramas. The viewing platform offers unobstructed views of 29 peaks over 4,000 meters, including Monte Rosa, the Matterhorn, and the Gorner Glacier stretching into the valley below.

Visit the Matterhorn Glacier Paradise, Europe's highest cable car station at 3,883 meters. The Glacier Palace, carved 15 meters below the ice surface, features stunning ice sculptures and crystalline formations. On clear days, views extend to the peaks of Italy, France, and Germany.

→

Explore the charming village on foot, discovering traditional Valais architecture and the Mountaineers' Cemetery honoring those who lost their lives on the surrounding peaks. The car-free environment creates a peaceful atmosphere perfect for evening strolls.

→

In winter, enjoy skiing across the vast ski area connecting to Cervinia in Italy, offering over 360 kilometers of slopes. The high altitude ensures excellent snow conditions and the unique experience of skiing with the Matterhorn as your constant companion.

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Summer (June-August): Perfect weather for hiking, warmest temperatures, peak tourist season, highest prices, long daylight hours, festival season.

Winter (December-March): World-class skiing, Christmas markets, winter sports paradise, expensive ski resorts, shorter daylight, magical snowy landscapes.

Spring (April-May): Mild weather, fewer crowds, moderate prices, wildflower blooms, excellent for cultural sites, unpredictable mountain weather.

Fall (September-October): Pleasant temperatures, autumn colors, harvest season, good weather, fewer tourists, excellent value before ski season.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 19 and 29 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Switzerland?

Alpine perfection with pristine mountains, luxury watches, and world-famous chocolate culture. Experience efficient trains, medieval towns, and outdoor adventures in Europe's playground. The country offers incredible hiking, skiing, and luxury experiences with stunning mountain scenery. Switzerland provides reliability, beauty, and luxury in compact Alpine paradise.
