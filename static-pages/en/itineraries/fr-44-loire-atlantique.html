Loire-Atlantique Road Trip: Nantes and the Jade Coast

A mechanical elephant strides along the quay, salt marshes shimmer under summer sun, Muscadet ripens on granite hillsides. Loire-Atlantique—creative, delicious, and unforgettable.

📅 Best period May, June, September, October

Discover where urban creativity meets Atlantic drama in France's most reinvented region. Explore Nantes' mechanical marvels at Les Machines de l'Île and witness the city's bold cultural renaissance. Journey through Clisson's vineyards where Muscadet wine ages on granite slopes. Walk coastal paths in Pornic where Belle Époque villas overlook secret coves. Unwind on La Baule's legendary nine-kilometer beach. Step back centuries in medieval Guérande and watch paludiers harvest salt by hand from ancient marshes. Dive into Saint-Nazaire's maritime heritage where the world's largest ships take shape.

  • Nantes: reimagined creative cultural capital
  • Guérande: medieval town of hand-harvested salt
  • La Baule: nine kilometers of golden sand
  • Clisson: romantic Tuscan-style wine valley
  • Saint-Nazaire: where industrial heritage meets the sea

Discover

1
The Château des ducs de Bretagne houses 32 museum rooms chronicling the city's layered past. Walk the 15th-century granite ramparts, then step into the inner courtyard where white tuffeau stone facades glow against the weathered battlements.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The Grand Elephant towers 12 meters high, a mechanical giant that trumpets and sprays water from its articulated trunk. Climb aboard this walking marvel at Les Machines de l'Île and feel the deck sway as it lumbers along the quays.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Inside the Galerie des Machines, machinists bring giant mechanical spiders and caterpillars to life. Ride the Carrousel des Mondes Marins on mechanical jellyfish and octopuses that rise and dive through three spinning levels.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Built in 1843, the Passage Pommeraye cascades down three levels across a 9-meter drop. Monumental stairways, statue-candelabras, and sculpted glass canopies make this covered arcade a neoclassical masterpiece.
⏱️ 45 min

The Château de Clisson has guarded the Sèvre Nantaise since the 12th century. Sprawling across ten acres, this frontier fortress of the Duchy of Brittany bristles with arrow slits and cannon ports carved into weathered stone.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Church of Notre-Dame crowns its bell tower with a Tuscan campanile—a surprising touch added in the 1800s. After the Vendée Wars razed the town, Clisson was rebuilt in an Italianate style inspired by Tuscany's sun-washed villages.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The medieval wooden market halls rank among France's oldest. Their massive oak beams once sheltered bustling trade, a window into Clisson's prosperous medieval past.
⏱️ 30 min
4
Cross the ancient stone bridge and wander cobbled lanes lined with ochre houses and terracotta roofs. Clisson weaves together medieval bones and Italian soul in an unlikely but enchanting mix.
⏱️ 60 min

Follow the Sèvre Nantaise on foot or by canoe. The river carves through vineyards, slipping between granite cliffs and past watermills that once powered the valley's industries.

The Chantiers de l'Atlantique shipyards sprawl across 250 acres. A giant gantry crane lifts 1,400-ton blocks to assemble the world's largest cruise ships in a modern marvel of engineering.

Climb to the rooftop terrace of the fortified lock. The 360-degree view sweeps across the harbor, the Loire estuary, and the elegant bridge that spans the wide river.
