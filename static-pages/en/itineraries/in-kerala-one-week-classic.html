Kerala, India Travel Guide: Backwaters, Tea Plantations and Coastal Beaches

Tea gardens rolling through mist, backwaters floating in absolute calm, beaches nestled at cliff bases. Kerala, pure serenity.

Explore Kochi and its spice port, cross Munnar's misty hills, navigate Alleppey's canals and reach Varkala perched above the sea. Kerala blends maritime heritage, tropical gentleness and spirituality, carried by the slow soothing rhythm of its backwaters.

  • Kochi: spice harbor heritage gateway
  • Munnar: tea plantations emerald escape
  • Alleppey: backwater canals floating soul
  • Varkala: cliff-side serenity spiritual crown

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Winter (October-March): Perfect weather, cool temperatures, dry conditions, peak tourist season, ideal touring, clear skies, advance booking essential.

Shoulder Season (April & September): Good weather, fewer crowds, moderate temperatures, reasonable prices, transitional seasons, excellent value.

Summer (April-June): Hot weather, challenging conditions, hill stations popular, lowest prices, fewer tourists, air conditioning essential.

Monsoon (June-September): Heavy rainfall, lush landscapes, romantic atmosphere, lowest prices, challenging travel, cultural experiences, spiritual season.

Regional Variations: Diverse climates, altitude differences, seasonal patterns, weather monitoring, regional planning, flexible scheduling essential.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 10 and 14 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.
