Hilton Head Island ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Explore Hilton Head Island's pristine beaches, featuring miles of soft, pale sand perfect for cycling, dolphin watching, and spectacular sunrise viewing. The island's coastline transforms into brilliant shades of pink and gold at dawn, creating an atmosphere of tranquility that defines this barrier island paradise. With gentle waves and wide-open stretches, this destination offers quintessential Lowcountry relaxation.
2
Discover Harbour Town, the island's iconic waterfront village crowned by the distinctive red-and-white striped lighthouse. Climb the lighthouse for panoramic coastal views stretching across the marshlands and harbor. The village combines maritime charm with boutique shopping and waterfront dining in a picturesque setting.
🎯

Activities

→
Cycle through more than 60 miles of scenic trails shaded by magnificent live oaks draped in Spanish moss. These pathways wind through maritime forests, past lagoons teeming with wildlife, and along beaches where the sand is firm enough for extended rides. The island's extensive trail network makes it one of the most bike-friendly destinations in the Southeast.
→
Paddle through the calm salt marshes by kayak, gliding past herons, dolphins, and jumping fish in some of the most pristine waterways in the Lowcountry. The Art League Gallery and the Gullah Museum of Hilton Head Island offer cultural enrichment, showcasing regional artistry and the rich heritage of the Gullah people.

Discover

1
Wander through Beaufort's antebellum historic district on Port Royal Island, where centuries-old oak trees draped in Spanish moss create an enchanting canopy over graceful streets. The waterfront park along the Beaufort River provides serene views of boats gliding across calm waters, while local art galleries showcase a surprisingly dynamic creative scene.
🎯

Activities

→
Drive the Avenue of Oaks on nearby St. Helena Island, a breathtaking canopied road that epitomizes Lowcountry romance. The ancient live oaks form a natural tunnel of greenery and Spanish moss that transports visitors to another era. This scenic route captures the timeless beauty of the Sea Islands.
→
Venture to Hunting Island State Park, where wide stretches of untouched beach meet maritime forests and salt marshes. Climb the historic lighthouse for panoramic coastal views, walk the Marsh Boardwalk at sunset, and spot dolphins swimming in the waves. The driftwood-strewn shores and wild palmetto groves create landscapes straight from a nature documentary.

Charleston Sc ★★

⏱️ Recommended stay : 1 day
4 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Stroll through Charleston's Historic District, where cobblestone streets wind past pastel-colored antebellum homes adorned with flower-filled window boxes. Rainbow Row's iconic facades and the Battery's waterfront promenade showcase architectural treasures spanning centuries. Every corner reveals ornate iron gates, hidden courtyards, and perfectly preserved Southern elegance.
2
Take a harbor boat tour to Fort Sumter National Monument, where the first shots of the Civil War were fired in 1861. The journey across Charleston Harbor offers stunning views of the city skyline and the Ravenel Bridge, with frequent dolphin sightings along the way. Explore the massive brick fortifications, examine historic cannons, and climb the ramparts for sweeping coastal panoramas.
3
Explore Magnolia Plantation and Gardens, home to some of the oldest public gardens in America where ancient live oaks frame romantic bridges, winding pathways, and reflective ponds. The Audubon Swamp Garden features haunting cypress trees, boardwalks over still green waters, and abundant wildlife including alligators and countless bird species. The historic Garden Train offers a relaxed way to tour these enchanting grounds.
4
Discover Middleton Place, featuring the oldest landscaped gardens in America with terraced lawns sweeping toward the Ashley River beneath massive oaks trailing Spanish moss. The perfectly sculpted hedges, secret pathways, and seasonal blooms create living works of art unchanged for centuries. The Stableyards present heritage animal breeds and demonstrations of 18th-century plantation life.
🎯

Activities

→
Visit Boone Hall Plantation, entering through the legendary Avenue of Oaks where dozens of massive live oaks draped in Spanish moss create an unforgettable natural cathedral. The preserved brick slave cabins along Slave Street contain powerful exhibits presenting the history of enslaved people. The plantation home, gardens of climbing roses, and working orchards complete this profound historical experience.
→
Stand in awe beneath the Angel Oak Tree, a natural monument estimated to be centuries old with limbs stretching so far they touch the ground before curving skyward again. This massive specimen creates its own cathedral of leaves and branches on Johns Island. The surrounding park provides a peaceful setting for contemplating one of nature's most impressive creations.
→
Tour the Middleton Place House Museum, filled with original furniture, portraits, and artifacts chronicling the prominent Middleton family and the people who lived and worked on the plantation. The serene riverside setting and meticulously maintained gardens offer hours of peaceful exploration through one of the most meaningful historic sites in the South.

Discover

1
Stroll along Georgetown's Front Street, the main corridor of this remarkably preserved historic district running parallel to the Sampit River. The Belltower overlooking the waterfront has become an iconic symbol of the town.

Activities

→
Walk the Harborwalk Marina at sunset, where open waterfront paths provide views of the Sampit River and surrounding marshlands. The measured, unhurried atmosphere invites lingering strolls past historic buildings and working boats. Georgetown's authentic character offers an alternative to busier coastal destinations while maintaining easy access to surrounding beaches and natural areas.

🔍

Discover

1
Stand at the Caesars Head overlook for endless views of Blue Ridge Mountain ridgelines and valleys stretching to the horizon. This dramatic granite outcrop provides some of the most spectacular mountain scenery in the entire state. The crisp mountain air and panoramic vistas make this a must-visit destination for nature lovers seeking awe-inspiring landscapes.
2
Hike to Raven Cliff Falls, the tallest waterfall in South Carolina, cascading dramatically over a 420-foot cliff. The approximately five-mile round-trip trail rewards hikers with one of the most impressive natural sights in the region. A suspension bridge on the Dismal Trail loop provides close-up views of the falls that few visitors forget.
🎯

Activities

→
Squeeze through Devil's Kitchen, a quirky narrow rocky passageway leading toward the overlook that adds adventure to the mountain experience. The rugged trails, peaceful forests, and dramatic rock formations of Caesars Head State Park showcase the Blue Ridge at its finest. This mountain destination reminds visitors why escaping to the highlands feels so rejuvenating.
→
Explore Landrum's charming downtown, where rolling foothills, sweeping mountain views, and country roads lined with farms create an idyllic setting. Visit Pearson's Falls, a beautiful 90-foot waterfall tucked into a lush nature preserve, and hike the Blue Wall Preserve for stunning mountain panoramas. The area's equestrian culture adds distinctive character to this hidden gem.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with spectacular fall foliage, harvest festivals, and comfortable weather. Perfect for outdoor activities, cultural events, fewer crowds than summer.

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warmest weather but highest crowds and prices. Excellent for northern regions, challenging in southwestern deserts, perfect for beaches and national parks.

  • Explore Brookgreen Gardens, where lush pathways wind past one of the finest outdoor sculpture collections in America displayed beneath towering oaks draped in Spanish moss. The gardens blend nature and artistry seamlessly, with hundreds of works ranging from classical to contemporary pieces positioned among reflective pools and manicured lawns. This former rice plantation now serves as a sanctuary of beauty and culture.
  • Wander through the extensive botanical gardens featuring seasonal blooms, themed garden rooms, and hidden corners perfect for quiet contemplation. The Lowcountry Zoo showcases native wildlife in naturalistic settings, providing insight into regional ecosystems. Brookgreen's combination of art, nature, and history creates hours of peaceful, inspiring exploration.
Activities
  • Discover the sculpture garden's shaded paths where art and nature interweave in constantly surprising ways. Each season brings different blooms and shifting light that transform the artistic experience. The gardens' commitment to preserving Lowcountry culture and environment makes this destination both beautiful and meaningful.

Day 9

Myrtle Beach Sc ★

Discover
  • Experience the vibrant energy of Myrtle Beach, where golden sands stretch for miles along the Atlantic and the famous boardwalk pulses with vacation excitement. The iconic SkyWheel observation wheel provides panoramic views of the coastline, while sunrise paints the sky in cotton-candy hues above the rolling waves. This classic American beach destination delivers sun-soaked fun for visitors of all ages.
  • Explore Broadway at the Beach, a sprawling entertainment district featuring waterfront attractions, live entertainment venues, and diverse dining options. The complex includes family-friendly activities ranging from aquarium visits to interactive experiences. Evening brings the boardwalk to life with arcades, shops, and the glow of neon reflecting off the ocean.
Activities
  • Visit North Myrtle Beach, the birthplace of Shag dancing and a community maintaining its connection to this beloved regional tradition. The Beach Shagger's National Hall of Fame celebrates the dance's history, while venues around Ocean Drive keep the tradition alive with regular gatherings. Barefoot Landing offers waterfront shopping and dining in a more relaxed setting than the main beach strip.

Aiken Sc ★

Day 12

Greenville Sc ★★

Discover
  • Marvel at Falls Park on the Reedy, an urban oasis where the Reedy River tumbles over a dramatic waterfall surrounded by gardens, stone pathways, and shady trees in the heart of downtown Greenville. The curved Liberty Bridge suspension walkway floats above the falls, offering sweeping views that never fail to impress. This transformed space represents one of the finest urban parks in the American South.
  • Stroll along the Swamp Rabbit Trail, miles of scenic pathway following the Reedy River through beautiful natural areas and charming neighborhoods. The trail connects downtown attractions to outlying communities, providing opportunities for walking, running, and cycling. Greenville's investment in green spaces and pedestrian infrastructure has created a remarkably livable city.
Activities
  • Explore downtown Greenville's wide, walkable streets lined with boutique shops, art galleries, and diverse dining options. The city's vibrant arts scene includes world-class performances and exhibitions throughout the year. Flowering gardens, hidden nooks, and benches overlooking the river invite visitors to slow down and appreciate this mountain-ringed cultural destination.

Day 13

Landrum Sc ★★

Discover
  • Stand at the Caesars Head overlook for endless views of Blue Ridge Mountain ridgelines and valleys stretching to the horizon. This dramatic granite outcrop provides some of the most spectacular mountain scenery in the entire state. The crisp mountain air and panoramic vistas make this a must-visit destination for nature lovers seeking awe-inspiring landscapes.
  • Hike to Raven Cliff Falls, the tallest waterfall in South Carolina, cascading dramatically over a 420-foot cliff. The approximately five-mile round-trip trail rewards hikers with one of the most impressive natural sights in the region. A suspension bridge on the Dismal Trail loop provides close-up views of the falls that few visitors forget.
Activities
  • Squeeze through Devil's Kitchen, a quirky narrow rocky passageway leading toward the overlook that adds adventure to the mountain experience. The rugged trails, peaceful forests, and dramatic rock formations of Caesars Head State Park showcase the Blue Ridge at its finest. This mountain destination reminds visitors why escaping to the highlands feels so rejuvenating.
  • Explore Landrum's charming downtown, where rolling foothills, sweeping mountain views, and country roads lined with farms create an idyllic setting. Visit Pearson's Falls, a beautiful 90-foot waterfall tucked into a lush nature preserve, and hike the Blue Wall Preserve for stunning mountain panoramas. The area's equestrian culture adds distinctive character to this hidden gem.

Day 15

York Sc ★★

Discover
  • Explore York's traditional Southern town center, where the historic York County Courthouse and prominent commercial buildings line streets that retain their 19th-century character. As part of South Carolina's Olde English District, the town connects to important Revolutionary War history. The McCelvey Center, housed in a handsome brick building, serves as a Southern Revolutionary War Institute with exhibits and programs.
Activities
  • Enjoy the outdoors at Anne Springs Close Greenway, an expansive natural area well-suited for quiet afternoon hikes, horseback riding, and nature observation. The greenway's trails wind through diverse habitats providing peaceful escapes from busier destinations. York's combination of history and natural beauty offers a fitting conclusion to a South Carolina journey.
