Aube Circuit: Champagne, Medieval Heritage and Orient Forest Lakes

Multicolored half-timbered facades reflected in the Seine, golden vineyards of Côte des Bar, Cistercian abbey nestled in its bright valley, blue lakes bordered by deep forests. Aube blends medieval heritage with exceptional champagne.

Explore Aube between Gothic cathedrals and champagne vineyards. Troyes reveals its 456 colorful half-timbered houses and 1,500 m² of exceptional stained glass, Essoyes unveils the Renoir family's intimacy, Les Riceys cultivates the rare Rosé des Riceys AOC since 1947. Clairvaux Abbey tells 900 years of monastic and prison history. The three major Orient Forest lakes offer 5,000 hectares of preserved nature sheltering 265 bird species.

  • Troyes: Bouchon de Champagne, 456 half-timbered houses 16th century, 1,500 m² stained glass, 10 churches
  • Clairvaux Abbey: 900 years of confinement, founded 1115 by Saint Bernard, prison 1808-2023
  • Les Riceys: only French commune holding 3 wine AOCs (Champagne, Coteaux, Rosé)
  • Essoyes: Renoir village, family home 1896, studio 1906, cultural center
  • Orient Forest Lakes: 5,000 ha water, 265 bird species, sailing, birdwatching

Discover

1
Troyes reveals its medieval history through nearly 456 half-timbered houses with multicolored facades in the district called Bouchon de Champagne (Champagne Cork). The historic quarter was rebuilt after the devastating fire of May 24, 1524 that destroyed a third of the city, showcasing Renaissance builders' ingenuity. Ruelle des Chats (Cat Alley) winds between facades so close the roofs nearly touch — a reminder of when taxes were calculated by ground surface, pushing houses to grow vertically. Carved beams, bold overhangs and cobbled courtyards at Maison du Dauphin and Hôtel du Lion Noir show the merchant city's wealth.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The cathedral of Saint-Pierre-et-Saint-Paul dominates Aube with impressive dimensions: 114 meters long, 28 meters high under the vaults. But its luminous display truly captivates: 1,500 m² of stained glass windows — equivalent to two handball courts — transform the building into a glass lantern. Built from 1200 to the 17th century and never fully completed (the South tower was never built), it showcases the excellence of the Champagne School of stained glass. Nearby, the church of Sainte-Madeleine, oldest of Troyes' sanctuaries, preserves a 16th-century carved rood screen unique in France along with windows illustrating the Creation and the Tree of Jesse.
⏱️ 75 min
3
In the impressive 18th-century Hôtel-Dieu-le-Comte, the Cité du Vitrail celebrates an extraordinary heritage: Aube holds 40% of the world's stained glass reserves. Permanent and temporary exhibitions reveal the art of colored light from the Middle Ages to today. Nearby, the Maison de l'Outil et de la Pensée Ouvrière occupies the elegant 16th-century Hôtel de Mauroy, lovingly restored by the Compagnons du Devoir. A contemporary display showcases 12,000 ancestral tools from the 17th-19th centuries — rasps, planes, chisels — witnesses to the trades that shaped France.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Historic hosiery capital since the Middle Ages, Troyes continues its textile legacy through four outlet centers totaling 200 shops. Marques City, McArthurGlen in Pont-Sainte-Marie, Villages de Marque and Marques Avenue in Saint-Julien-les-Villas offer major brands with discounted items and end-of-series products. The heritage of Troyes' textile industry lives on between smart shopping and economic history.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Four mornings a week (Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday), Troyes comes alive with traditional markets. Les Halles welcome local producers offering Troyes andouillette (IGP certified sausage), creamy chaource AOC cheese, Côte des Bar champagne and ratafia. Facing the cathedral, Cellier Saint-Pierre has been distilling Prunelle de Troyes since 1840 — an artisanal liqueur made from wild sloe berries macerated in alcohol. Secret recipe, ancestral method, exceptional bottle.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
30 km south of Troyes, Chaource sits in the bocage landscape with limestone houses and damp meadows typical of the Champagne countryside. The town gave its name to the famous soft cheese with bloomy rind, created in the Middle Ages by Cistercian monks from Pontigny. The Cheese Museum tells this dairy story through period objects, documentary film and final tasting that showcase this gastronomic heritage recognized as AOC in 1970.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The church of Saint-Jean-Baptiste is worth the visit alone. This Gothic building constructed from the 12th to 17th century houses a dazzling Renaissance sculpture collection. In the crypt rests the Entombment of 1515, created by the mysterious Master of Chaource — a polychrome sculpted group that attracts art pilgrims from around the world. 16th-century gilded wood nativity scene, majestic organ, sculptures blending Champagne art with Italian touches form an exceptional museum collection housed within a parish church.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Cheese artisans perpetuate ancestral methods: 2 to 4 liters of whole milk from local cows for a single chaource. Slow coagulation of minimum 12 hours, delicate hand-ladling (never mechanized), dry salt curing then minimum 14-day aging in cool humid cellars produce this creamy cylinder with flowing heart. On the palate: notes of fresh hazelnut and double cream. Chaource delighted royal courts even at François I's table. Visits possible with reservation.
⏱️ 60 min
→
The Pays d'Othe region around Chaource offers a network of marked trails through state forest, preserved bocage and secret valleys. Family circuits or experienced hikers explore this territory alternating deciduous woods, grazed meadows and viewpoints over the Champagne countryside. GPS tracks and paper maps available at the Othe Armance Tourist Office to independently explore these rolling landscapes between Champagne and Burgundy.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Nestled in the Côte des Bar vineyards, Essoyes lives in the memory of Pierre-Auguste Renoir. The Centre Culturel Du Côté des Renoir — housed in the former castle stables overlooking the Ource river — begins the story: 20-minute film, permanent exhibition on the family saga (Pierre the actor, Jean the filmmaker, Claude the ceramist), temporary exhibition hall. For thirty summers (1888-1919), the Impressionist master found here incomparable light, generous models and peaceful life far from Parisian turmoil.
⏱️ 60 min
2
The family home acquired in 1896 reveals the Renoirs' intimacy: period furniture, wallpaper restored to match the originals, everyday objects frozen as if the family just stepped out. The garden path leads to the 1906 Studio — built at the back of the property to not disturb the children's games. Under the exposed beams bathed in skylight: easel, brushes, wheelchair (Renoir painted despite hands deformed by gout), transport trunk for Paris trips. Labeled Maison des Illustres, the house still breathes the painter's soul.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Renoir Walking Trail connects the cultural center to the family cemetery passing by key locations. Aline Charigot's parents' house (who became Madame Renoir), the washhouse immortalized in Les Laveuses (The Washerwomen), the forge, the church where Renoir was married and buried, the Renoirs' graves in the municipal cemetery. Interpretation panels with reproductions guide visitors through the landscapes that inspired the master. Allow 1h30 for this immersive walk in the Renoir family's footsteps.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Essoyes holds its Renoir Festival each year in early August: art exhibitions, classical music concerts, painting workshops, guided tours, literary meetings around the impressionist's work. The whole village becomes an open-air museum. Book accommodation early as hotels fill quickly. Special atmosphere blending art, local traditions and Champagne conviviality.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Champagne houses in Essoyes open their cellars to visitors: guided tour of vaults, explanation of winemaking methods, tasting of Côte des Bar champagnes. This southern Champagne region produces rounder, more full-bodied champagnes than those from Reims or Épernay. More Pinot Noir, less acidity, longer aging. Discovery appointment and wine purchase possible.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Labeled Petite Cité de Caractère, Bar-sur-Seine sits on the Seine's left bank. The Saint-Étienne church stands out with 430 m² of stained glass windows from the 16th century, earning it the nickname "glass lantern." Remarkable Tree of Jesse Window, sculptures and altarpieces form a museum-quality ensemble. The old market hall, half-timbered houses, cobbled lanes compose an authentic medieval center. Walk the ramparts for views of the valley and vineyards.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Champagne houses line Bar-sur-Seine's streets. Drappier, historic house founded in 1808, opens its vaults carved into chalk for guided tours: traditional method explanation, aging secrets, Grand Cru tasting. The cellars extend under the town in a labyrinth of galleries maintaining 12°C year-round. Reserve in advance for tours — limited spots.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Romantic Cadoles Trail departs from Bar-sur-Seine for a 6 km loop through vineyards. Cadoles are small dry-stone shelters where winegrowers took refuge from bad weather. These typical Côte des Bar constructions dot the slopes. The marked path offers panoramas of the Seine valley and wine hills. Moderate difficulty with some elevation gain. Allow 2h walking time.
⏱️ 120 min
🎯

Activities

→
Every Saturday morning, Bar-sur-Seine holds its traditional market under the old hall: local producers offer vegetables, cheeses, charcuterie, honey. Friendly atmosphere, direct producer-consumer contact. Then stroll the antique shops and art galleries scattered through the old town. Discover artisan workshops perpetuating traditional crafts.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Rent bikes at Bar-sur-Seine tourist office to ride the Seine à Vélo route: flat paved path following the river through vineyards and fields. Circuit toward Essoyes or Mussy-sur-Seine, peaceful landscape, picnic spots by the water. Family-friendly easy route. Electric bikes available for less athletic riders.

Discover

1
Les Riceys holds the unique distinction in France of having three wine AOCs: Champagne, Coteaux Champenois and the legendary Rosé des Riceys. This pink wine, recognized AOC since 1947, was King Louis XIV's favorite. Produced only in exceptional years from 100% Pinot Noir, it develops aromas of red fruits and almonds. Only 50 to 100,000 bottles produced per year. Several estates offer tastings — book ahead as production is limited.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Largest wine commune in Champagne with 866 hectares of vines, Les Riceys actually groups three villages: Ricey-Bas, Ricey-Haut and Ricey-Haute-Rive, each with its own Renaissance church. The Saint-Pierre-ès-Liens churches in Ricey-Bas and Ricey-Haute-Rive showcase remarkable 16th-century stained glass. Walk through the three hamlets discovering half-timbered winegrowers' houses, wine cellars, communal washhouses. Authentic wine village atmosphere.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Riceys vineyard trail: 8 km marked circuit through vines. Interpretation panels explain viticulture, terroir, Rosé des Riceys specificity. Panoramic viewpoints over the three villages and surrounding hills. Moderate difficulty with elevation changes. Allow 2h30 walking. Download the map from the tourist office before departure.
⏱️ 150 min
→
Visit champagne estates: many Riceys producers welcome visitors to their cellars. Family estates explain their work from vine to bottle. Tasting of their three AOCs: champagne, Coteaux Champenois red and the famous rosé. Meet passionate winegrowers attached to their terroir. Direct purchase possible at estate prices.

Discover

1
Clairvaux Abbey, founded June 25, 1115 by Saint Bernard, embodied Cistercian spiritual power for centuries. The mother abbey spawned 80 daughter abbeys across Europe. Transformed into prison in 1808, it held up to 2,000 inmates and closed definitively in 2023. Guided tours reveal this dual history: 12th-century Lay Brothers' Building (74m long, exceptional), 18th-century refectory-chapel, 19th-century disciplinary quarter. Heavy atmosphere marked by centuries of confinement.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The Clairvaux Historical Circuit extends around the abbey: Bernard of Clairvaux Museum in Ville-sous-la-Ferté (childhood house), medieval hospice, Val des Choues where Bernard experienced his mystical visions. Interpretation trail connecting key sites of Cistercian history. Allow a full day to complete the circuit including abbey visit.
⏱️ 180 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Clairvaux forest surrounds the abbey with marked hiking trails. Peaceful paths through oak and beech woods, clearings, streams. Easy to moderate circuits from 5 to 15 km. Maps available at Clairvaux tourist office. Meditative walking in the footsteps of Cistercian monks who found solitude and prayer here.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Clairvaux celebrates its Cistercian heritage through cultural events: medieval market in summer, classical music concerts in the abbey, temporary exhibitions on monastic history. Check the calendar before your visit. Special atmosphere in these centuries-old walls. Book tickets in advance for concerts — limited seating.

Discover

1
Brienne-le-Château earned its fame as Napoleon Bonaparte's military school city. From 1779 to 1784, the young Corsican studied at the Royal Military School, now the Museum of Napoleon and the Revolution. Period collections, imperial souvenirs, weapons, documents retrace the emperor's youth. The castle, rebuilt after WWI bombings, houses temporary exhibitions. Allow 1h30 for the complete visit.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Saint-Pierre-et-Saint-Paul church houses an exceptional treasure: the Girardon Altarpiece, 17th-century sculpted masterpiece by François Girardon, Louis XIV's official sculptor. White marble composition depicting the Assumption, remarkable for its movement and expressiveness. One of France's most beautiful baroque altarpieces. Free admission but irregular opening hours — check before visiting.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk around Lac de la Forêt d'Orient: this artificial lake created in 1966 extends over 2,500 hectares. Marked trails allow walking or cycling the shores. Water activities: swimming from supervised beaches, sailing, kayaking, stand-up paddle. Nautical base rents equipment. Popular in summer, bring picnic and swimwear.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Every Wednesday and Saturday, Brienne-le-Château holds its market under the covered hall. Local products: vegetables, cheeses, charcuterie, honey. Then explore the town's shops and cafés. Lunch at a restaurant offering Champagne regional cuisine: andouillette, pig's trotters, chaource, Champagne-braised meat dishes. Authentic local flavors.

Discover

1
Lusigny-sur-Barse sits at the gates of Orient Forest Regional Natural Park. This protected area covers 70,000 hectares around three major lakes: Orient, Temple and Amance. The park headquarters houses an information center: interactive exhibitions on forest ecosystems, wetlands, migratory birds. Maps and brochures for planning your nature explorations. Free admission.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Lusigny beaches hold Blue Flag certification: fine sand, clear water, supervised swimming. Water sports base offers sailing lessons, catamaran rentals, windsurfing, stand-up paddle. Shallow calm waters perfect for families with young children. Picnic areas, children's playgrounds, snack bars. Lively atmosphere in summer, peaceful in low season.
⏱️ 120 min
🎯

Activities

→
Discover the Voie Verte trail: 42 km of paved path around the Orient Forest lakes reserved for cyclists and pedestrians. Flat easy route suitable for all. Rent bikes in Lusigny or Mesnil-Saint-Père. Stops at observation points, picnic areas, beaches. Allow a full day for the complete loop with breaks. Electric bikes available for less athletic riders.
⏱️ 240 min
→
Bird observation in Orient Forest: 265 species recorded including cranes, herons, osprey, harriers. Spring and fall migrations bring thousands of birds. The park offers guided walks with ornithologists. Bring binoculars. Observation hides placed along shores. Respectful quiet observation of nature. Magical moments guaranteed for bird lovers.

On the banks of the young Seine, Villenauxe-la-Grande represents authentic rural Champagne. This peaceful agricultural town has preserved its parish church, stone washhouses and traditional houses that witness a life paced by seasons and field work. The modest but informative Heritage House traces local history and Champagne traditions — viticulture, livestock farming, crafts. Restful stop in preserved countryside.
⏱️ 60 min

→
Seine riverbanks arranged as shaded promenade: flat paths following river meanders through wet meadows, willow groves and rustling reeds. Anglers find quiet spots to catch pike, zander and carp in fish-rich waters. Ideal family route for picnics, wildlife watching (grey herons, moorhens, kingfishers) and waterside flora. Absolute rural calm, far from tourist bustle.
