Zagreb

6 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Marvel at Zagreb Cathedral, Croatia's tallest church soaring 108 meters into the sky. Dating back to the 11th century with stunning 19th-century neo-Gothic renovations, this magnificent landmark dominates the Upper Town skyline and represents the spiritual heart of the nation.
2
Discover the enchanting Church of St. Mark with its iconic polychrome tiled roof depicting the coats of arms of Zagreb and the Kingdom of Croatia, Slavonia, and Dalmatia. Built in the 13th century and renovated multiple times, this beloved landmark from 1880 stands as one of Zagreb's most photographed monuments.
3
Explore the fascinating Nikola Tesla Technical Museum, dedicated to the genius inventor born in modern-day Croatia who revolutionized our understanding of electricity. The museum showcases technological innovations from the 19th and 20th centuries, including numerous vehicles, machines, and interactive exhibits.
4
Visit the world-renowned Museum of Broken Relationships, Zagreb's most unique and emotionally moving museum. This award-winning collection displays personal objects donated by heartbroken individuals from around the world, each accompanied by touching stories of love and loss.
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Climb the medieval Lotrščak Tower in the Upper Town and witness the daily cannon firing tradition at noon, a Zagreb custom since 1877. For just 3 euros, ascend to the top for one of the most spectacular panoramic views of the city and surrounding hills.
→
Ride the charming Zagreb Funicular, the world's shortest public transport funicular at just 66 meters. Operating since 1890, this beloved symbol of Zagreb connects the Upper and Lower Towns in less than a minute for only 60 cents.
→
Walk through the mysterious Grič Tunnel, a 350-meter underground passage built in 1943 as an air-raid shelter. Reopened to the public in 2016, this atmospheric tunnel now hosts exhibitions and events while serving as a unique shortcut through the historic center.

Samobor

6 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
Explore the picturesque King Tomislav Square, the beating heart of Samobor where elegant pastel baroque buildings house traditional cafes. Don't miss the charming fountain in the center, which according to local legend ensures that anyone who drinks from it will return to Samobor.
2
Discover the romantic ruins of Samobor Castle perched atop Tepec Hill, built in the 13th century by Czech King Otokar. This protected cultural monument offers breathtaking panoramic views over the town and Samobor Hills, with much of its original floor plan still visible among the atmospheric stone walls.
3
Visit the elegant Livadić Castle and Samobor City Museum, an L-shaped 18th-century manor set within beautiful parkland. The museum, housed here since 1948, showcases the rich history of Samobor through old documents, artworks, geological artifacts, and a detailed model of the medieval castle.
4
Admire the striking Church of St. Anastasia, Samobor's tallest structure with its beautiful bright yellow facade. This beloved parish church holds special significance for locals as the traditional venue for weddings, baptisms, and other important life celebrations.
🎯

Activities

→
Wander through the peaceful Anindol promenade on Tepec Hill, a wooded park featuring the baroque Chapel of St. Anne, patron saint of Samobor. Continue along the forest trails to discover the Chapel of St. George and enjoy the tranquil natural setting just steps from the main square.
→
Venture to Grgos Cave, located about 5 kilometers from Samobor and discovered by Josip Grgos in 1973. This fascinating underground world features two main halls with a 19-meter height difference, electric lighting, and walking paths through spectacular limestone formations.
→
Experience the legendary Samoborski Fašnik carnival if visiting in February, one of Croatia's most famous traditional festivals. This beloved celebration transforms the town with elaborate costumes, street parades, music, dancing, and the irresistible aroma of traditional carnival doughnuts.

Kumrovec

3 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Step back in time at the Staro Selo Open-Air Museum, a beautifully preserved ethnographic village showcasing traditional Zagorje life from the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Wander through authentic thatched-roof houses displaying original furniture, traditional costumes, farming tools, and household items that bring rural Croatian heritage to life.
2
Visit the birthplace of Josip Broz Tito, Yugoslavia's legendary leader who was born in this humble village house in 1892. Now a memorial museum, the restored building displays personal artifacts, photographs, and exhibits documenting the life and times of one of the 20th century's most influential political figures.
🎯

Activities

→
Photograph the Monument to the Croatian National Anthem, erected in 1935 at the entrance to the municipality. This unique memorial celebrates the patriotic song that became Croatia's official anthem, adding cultural significance to this charming Zagorje village.

Trakoscan

3 photos
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
Discover the fairytale Trakošćan Castle, one of Croatia's most romantic and picturesque fortresses dating back to the late 13th century. Originally built as an observation fortress, it was transformed into an elegant neo-Gothic residence in the 19th century by the Drašković family, who owned it from 1584 until 1944.
2
Explore the castle's permanent museum established in 1954, featuring original furniture, weapons, family portraits, and period artifacts across multiple floors. The beautifully preserved interiors transport visitors through centuries of noble Croatian life and artistic heritage.
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll through the romantic park surrounding the castle, landscaped in the 19th century with winding paths through lush forest. The idyllic artificial lake reflects the white castle towers, creating one of the most photographed scenes in inland Croatia.
→
Rent a pedal boat and glide across the serene lake, enjoying unique perspectives of the castle rising majestically from the forested hillside. The tranquil waters and surrounding nature make this an unforgettable escape from modern life.
→
Learn about the legend of Baba Jaga, who according to local folklore saved the fortress from Turkish invaders by magically conjuring hornets from straw that drove away the besieging army.

Varazdin

2 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Explore the magnificent Varaždin Old Town, a remarkably preserved medieval fortress with walls dating back to the 12th century and a 16th-century drawbridge and moat. The stunning baroque castle houses the Varaždin City Museum with its 20 rooms of fascinating artifacts including glass, clocks, ceramics, and historical weapons.
2
Stroll through elegant King Tomislav Square, the vibrant heart of Varaždin's historic center where locals gather for coffee amid beautiful baroque architecture. This charming square is lined with cafes, restaurants, and shops, offering the perfect spot to absorb the city's refined atmosphere.
3
Marvel at the exquisite Sermage Palace, a stunning 17th-century rococo masterpiece just a short walk from the castle. The palace's breathtaking architecture houses an impressive art museum, showcasing the cultural richness that earned Varaždin the nickname 'Little Vienna.'
4
Discover the World of Insects collection at Hercer Palace, an educational natural history exhibition featuring fascinating specimens from around the globe. This family-friendly museum offers an engaging hour of discovery into nature's most diverse creatures.
+2 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Wander through the hauntingly beautiful Varaždin Cemetery, one of Europe's most stunning burial grounds dating back to the 18th century. Designed as a romantic park with sculpted hedges and ancient trees, this peaceful oasis lies about 15 minutes from the Old Town.
→
Rub the toe of the Grgur Ninski statue for good luck, a beloved local tradition. The shiny golden toe testifies to the thousands of visitors who have sought fortune from this bronze monument to the medieval Croatian bishop.
→
Visit the Town Hall, one of Europe's oldest continuously operating municipal buildings dating from 1523. The baroque architecture features Varaždin's coat of arms and provides an excellent backdrop for photographs.

Cakovec

4 photos
Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
Explore Čakovec Castle, the 7-century-old fortress that symbolizes the rich history of Međimurje region. Originally a wooden fort built by Count Csaki in the 14th century, it was later expanded by the legendary Zrinski family in the 16th century, making Čakovec an important political and cultural center.
2
Discover the treasures of the Međimurje Museum housed within the castle walls, featuring approximately 17,000 items that tell the fascinating story of Croatia's northernmost region. The collection spans archaeology, ethnography, and art, revealing the diverse cultural heritage of this corner between the Mura and Drava rivers.
🎯

Activities

→
Relax in the beautiful Zrinski Park surrounding the castle, a green oasis perfect for peaceful walks among centuries-old trees and manicured gardens. This tranquil space honors the memory of the noble Zrinski family who shaped the region's destiny.
→
Experience the vibrant Porcijunkulovo Festival if visiting in late July or early August, when Čakovec transforms with colorful umbrellas, local food producers, handmade crafts, and excellent music that draws visitors from across Croatia.
→
Take a trip to the nearby Mura River, the natural boundary that defines Međimurje. This scenic waterway offers opportunities for peaceful walks and connects to the broader Pannonian landscape that shaped the region's character.

Koprivnica

3 photos
Day 7
🔍

Discover

1
Explore the historic Old Town of Koprivnica, built within ancient fortified walls that protected this strategic Renaissance-era settlement on the border with Slavonia. Walk through centuries of Croatian history as you discover the well-preserved medieval center that once housed important military and scientific institutions.
2
Visit the impressive Church of St. Nicholas, one of three historic churches that demonstrate Koprivnica's religious importance through the centuries. Together with the Church of St. Anthony of Padua and the Franciscan Monastery of the Virgin Mary, these sacred buildings reveal the town's rich spiritual heritage.
3
Discover the unique Museum of Food, celebrating Koprivnica's status as Croatia's gastronomic capital and home to Podravka, the company that invented the world-famous Vegeta seasoning in 1959. This fascinating museum explores the region's culinary traditions and food industry heritage.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience the annual 'Motifs of Podravina' event, when Koprivnica transforms into an open-air gallery showcasing the region's famous naive art tradition. This celebration honors the artistic legacy begun by Ivan Generalić and the renowned Hlebine school painters.
→
Enjoy cycling along the scenic routes near the Drava-Mura rivers, where well-maintained paths wind through the picturesque Podravina countryside dotted with traditional wooden houses. The gentle terrain makes this area perfect for leisurely exploration on two wheels.
→
Attend the Renaissance Festival if visiting in summer, when the medieval town comes alive with knights, craftsmen, and period entertainment that transport visitors back to Koprivnica's golden age as a strategic military outpost.

Durdevac

3 photos
Day 8
🔍

Discover

1
Discover the legendary Old Town Đurđevac fortress, one of the best-preserved lowland fortifications in Croatia. Built in the 1480s by Bishop Sigismund Ernušt to defend against Ottoman threats, this remarkable stronghold with its 9-meter tower never fell to invaders throughout its turbulent history.
2
Explore the Picokijada Interpretation Centre inside the fortress, where immersive exhibits tell the legendary tale of how brave townsfolk saved their city by firing a rooster at the besieging Ottoman army. This beloved story earned locals the proud nickname 'Picoki' that they cherish to this day.
3
Visit the Đurđevac City Museum featuring one of the region's most important art collections, including many works donated by celebrated local artist Ivan Lacković. The museum's paintings, statues, and artistic posters provide insight into Podravina's rich cultural heritage.
4
Experience the remarkable Đurđevac Sands Visitors Centre, among the most modern multimedia museums in southeastern Europe. Interactive exhibits, augmented reality displays, and engaging presentations reveal the secrets of Croatia's only desert and the unique Drava river ecosystem.
🎯

Activities

→
Wander through the extraordinary Đurđevački pijesci, the Croatian Sahara and the only desert in Croatia. This 20-hectare special geological and botanical reserve, protected since 1963, features spectacular dunes, endemic plant species, and over 30 distinct butterfly species.
→
Meet the camels, goats, donkeys, and llamas at Hrvatska Sahara Zoo, a charming animal park named after the nearby desert. This family-friendly attraction brings a touch of North African wildlife to the heart of the Pannonian Plain.
→
Experience the famous Picokijada Festival if visiting in late June, a spectacular three-day re-enactment protected as Croatian intangible cultural heritage since 2006. Thousands of participants and visitors gather for theatrical performances, sports, parades, and the popular cake competition.

Hlebine

4 photos
Day 9
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the award-winning Gallery of Naïve Art in Hlebine, the birthplace of Croatian naive art built in 1968 to house the renowned Hlebine school collection. The gallery features an extensive opus donated by Ivan Generalić in 1982, showcasing his evolution from village scenes to mystical works including the famous 'Cows under the Eiffel Tower.'
2
Explore the atmospheric Josip Generalić Gallery, a complex of houses where both Ivan and his son Josip lived and created their masterpieces. Guided tours reveal Josip's unfinished painting 'The White Frogs,' the playful red pantry door, and the touching wooden deer sculptures commemorating Ivan's famous 'Deer Wedding' painting.
3
Discover the Ivan Večenaj Gallery across the Drava River in Gola, dedicated to this second-generation master known for his vibrant colors and elongated figures reminiscent of Giacometti. His painting 'Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse' appears in the prestigious Bible Book of the 20th century alongside Picasso, Chagall, and Dalí.
🎯

Activities

→
Visit the atelier of Zlatko Kolarek, president of the Association of Naïve Painters and Sculptors from Hlebine. Admire his intricate miniatures of the 14 Stations of the Cross, each measuring just 29-42mm, created using the traditional reverse oil painting on glass technique.
→
Learn the secrets of reverse glass painting, the distinctive technique brought from Bavaria that became the hallmark of Croatian naive art. Local artists demonstrate how pigments are applied in reverse order on the back of glass to create these luminous, detailed works.
→
Experience the Stari Zanati (Old Crafts) estate run by Tihomir Želimorski, where traditional skills like blacksmithing, pottery, and weaving come alive through hands-on workshops. During the annual Colt City festival in July, this charming venue transforms into a Wild West town complete with cowboys, sheriff duels, and artisan demonstrations.

Osijek

8 photos
Day 10
🔍

Discover

1
Step into the magnificent Tvrđa, one of Europe's best-preserved 18th-century baroque military fortresses. Built by the Habsburgs in 1687 after defeating the Ottoman forces, this stunning complex of cobblestone streets, elegant baroque buildings, and atmospheric cafes represents the administrative, educational, and cultural heart of Slavonia.
2
Marvel at the neo-Gothic Co-Cathedral of Saints Peter and Paul, dominating Osijek's skyline with its soaring 94-meter spire. Beautifully restored after severe damage during the 1991 Croatian War of Independence, the cathedral's stunning stained glass windows and elegant interior make it one of Croatia's most impressive religious monuments.
3
Stroll through the elegant Trg Ante Starčevića, Osijek's main square featuring the historic Hotel Central and the baroque Prandua-Normann Palace. This vibrant heart of the city offers excellent shopping, cafes, and a glimpse into the refined urban culture of Slavonia's largest city.
4
Explore the wonders of Kopački Rit Nature Park, one of Europe's largest and best-preserved wetlands just outside Osijek. This remarkable zoological reserve protects 260 bird species, 40 fish species, and over 140 plant varieties across pristine marshlands accessible by boat, bike, horseback, or on foot.
🎯

Activities

→
Walk across the iconic Pjesački Most, the pedestrian bridge built in 1981 that has become a beloved symbol of Osijek. This graceful structure connects the city center to the popular Zoo and Aquarium, Croatia's largest zoological garden featuring giraffes, camels, and even panthers.
→
Spend a summer afternoon at Copacabana River Beach on the Drava River, where locals swim, sunbathe, and enjoy public pools in a lively atmosphere. The riverside promenade stretching along both banks offers perfect paths for walking, jogging, or cycling.
→
Sample Osječko beer, Croatia's first beer brand brewing with the same original recipe since 1697. Pair it with authentic Slavonian cuisine including fiš paprikaš (spicy fish stew), kulen sausage, and čvarci (crispy pork cracklings) at riverside restaurants.

Vukovar

7 photos
Day 11
🔍

Discover

1
Ascend the restored Vukovar Water Tower, a powerful symbol of Croatian resilience that survived over 600 direct hits during the devastating 1991 siege. Modern accessible walkways lead to an observation deck with breathtaking Danube views, while interactive exhibits and wartime testimonies share the deeply moving stories of those who endured.
2
Explore the elegant Eltz Manor, an 18th-century baroque castle built by the noble Eltz family overlooking the Danube. Now home to the Vukovar City Museum, the fully restored castle showcases prehistoric Vučedol ceramics, Roman artifacts, folk costumes, and exhibits documenting the town's multi-ethnic heritage and recent history.
3
Discover the fascinating Vučedol Culture Museum on a hill above the Danube, celebrating one of Europe's earliest civilizations that flourished here 5,000 years ago. Interactive exhibits and archaeological finds reveal daily life along the Danube in prehistoric times, including the famous Vučedol Dove ceramic vessel.
4
Visit the neo-Gothic Church of Saints Philip and James, rebuilt after wartime destruction as a symbol of faith and endurance. The soaring columns, beautiful stained glass, and memorial plaque commemorating Pope John Paul II's 2003 visit create a space for quiet reflection.
🎯

Activities

→
Pay respects at the Vukovar Memorial Cemetery, where thousands of white crosses honor victims of the 1991 siege. The nearby Vukovar Hospital Memorial reveals the extraordinary courage and suffering of medical staff and patients through emotional displays and preserved spaces.
→
Stroll along the scenic Danube Promenade, where tree-lined paths, parks, and riverside cafes invite peaceful contemplation. Summer evenings bring locals gathering for golden sunsets, concerts, and the simple pleasure of watching boats glide by on Europe's second-longest river.
→
Relax in the beautiful parks of Vukovar, including Adica Park with its forest trails, playground, and small lake perfect for family outings. The manicured gardens of Eltz Manor Park offer benches beneath ancient trees and occasional art installations.

Ilok

6 photos
Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Tour the legendary Ilok Wine Cellars, among the largest and oldest in Europe, where award-winning Traminac white wine has been crafted for centuries. Descend into the atmospheric underground vaults that once supplied royal courts across Europe and sample the distinctive wines that put this hilltop town on the world enological map.
2
Explore the imposing Ilok Castle, a magnificent 15th-century fortress perched dramatically above the Danube at Croatia's easternmost point. Walk through grand halls, medieval walls, and charming courtyards while enjoying panoramic views stretching across the river to Serbia.
3
Visit the Odescalchi Castle complex housing a museum with archaeological treasures and historical collections tracing Ilok's strategic importance from Roman times through the Ottoman period to the present day.
🎯

Activities

→
Wander through the surrounding vineyards that carpet the sunny hillsides, benefiting from the favorable microclimate and fertile soil that have produced exceptional wines for over two millennia. The peaceful landscape offers a slower pace of life worlds away from coastal Croatia.
→
Stroll along the Danube riverfront and enjoy the breathtaking views of this mighty waterway forming the natural border between Croatia and Serbia. The scenic promenade provides perfect vantage points for photography and quiet contemplation.

Dakovo

10 photos
Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Marvel at the monumental Cathedral of St. Peter, described by Pope John XXIII as 'the most beautiful cathedral between Venice and Istanbul.' Built between 1866-1882 in a harmonious blend of Romanesque and Gothic styles, this magnificent brick church rises as a beacon of faith over the Slavonian plains, visible from miles around.
2
Visit the historic State Stud Farm, one of Europe's oldest Lipizzaner breeding facilities with an unbroken tradition dating back five centuries. These majestic white horses, inspiration for unicorn legends, perform elegant displays in one of the continent's largest riding halls.
3
Explore the Archbishop's Palace and Seminary, testaments to Đakovo's importance as a religious center shaped by the visionary Bishop Josip Juraj Strossmayer, founder of the Croatian Academy of Sciences and Arts.
4
Discover the medieval walls and historic churches including the Church of All Saints, remnants of Đakovo's strategic importance through turbulent centuries of Croatian history.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience the spectacular Đakovački vezovi Festival held annually in July since 1967, Croatia's largest and most important folklore pageant. Tens of thousands gather to celebrate the original costumes, music, and traditions of Slavonia, Baranja, and Srijem alongside international participants and thrilling equestrian shows.
→
Witness the UNESCO-protected Spring Procession of Ljelje (Queens) in nearby Gorjani every Whitsunday. A dozen women in rich traditional folk costumes perform the ancient kolo circle dance, visiting homes throughout the village in a unique ritual passed down through generations.
→
Sample the renowned wines of the Đakovo region, cultivated in these fertile lands for over two thousand years. The vineyards of the Archdiocese Đakovo-Osijek continue a winemaking tradition stretching back to pre-Ottoman times.

Slavonski Brod

3 photos
Day 14
🔍

Discover

1
Explore the magnificent Brod Fortress, one of 18th-century Europe's largest military fortifications capable of housing 4,000 soldiers. This remarkably preserved star-shaped stronghold built by the Habsburgs now serves as a vibrant cultural hub hosting festivals, concerts, and events within its impressive 250-year-old walls.
2
Visit the Franciscan Monastery and Church of the Holy Trinity, the largest baroque religious complex in all of Slavonia. Step inside to admire the architectural grandeur and centuries of sacred art preserved within these hallowed walls.
3
Discover the Brod Regional Museum featuring an extraordinary collection spanning millennia, from Ice Age mammoth skulls and Roman artifacts to medieval treasures. The recently renovated galleries bring Slavonia's fascinating history vividly to life.
4
Pay homage at the Dragutin Tadijanović Memorial Home, dedicated to Croatia's beloved poet who lived to be 102 years old. The museum recreates his Zagreb apartment containing over 4,000 books that he personally read throughout his remarkable life.
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Walk along the Sava River, the natural border between Croatia and Bosnia-Herzegovina that flows through the heart of Slavonski Brod. Locals swim in its waters during summer months, making it Croatia's largest river swimming destination.
→
Escape to Lake Petnja, a peaceful artificial lake created in 1968 that has blended naturally into the forested landscape. Forest walking trails, fishing spots, and fresh air make this beloved local retreat perfect for unwinding.
→
Experience the adventure offerings at Ranch Ramarin outside the city, where horseback riding, tennis, mini golf, and even small plane flights await. The property's adopted dogs and farm animals add to the welcoming atmosphere.
