Yonne, Burgundy: Wine Villages, Vineyards and Medieval Basilicas

Vines climb the terraces. Cathedral vaults echo with bells. Stone sculpture whispers prayer. Burgundy's soul reveals itself, fully customizable.

Auxerre impresses with its cathedral and medieval architecture. Chablis reveals an iconic vineyard and renowned winemaking expertise. Noyers-sur-Serein charms with its village perched by the water. Vézelay marks the history of great pilgrimages. Between wine and spirituality, Yonne combines heritage and character.

  • Auxerre: cathedral wine gateway
  • Chablis: vineyard terraced crisp
  • Noyers: riverside village stone
  • Vézelay: basilica pilgrimage crown
  • Tonnerre: river town rampart

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather, blooming countryside, ideal for all regions, moderate crowds, excellent value, romantic atmosphere.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather, peak tourist season, highest prices, crowded attractions, perfect for Mediterranean coast, festival season.

Fall (September-October): Harvest season, comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, wine regions spectacular, excellent photography, good value.

Winter (November-March): Christmas markets, ski season Alps, fewer crowds, lowest prices, cozy indoor attractions, cultural experiences.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 9 and 13 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.
