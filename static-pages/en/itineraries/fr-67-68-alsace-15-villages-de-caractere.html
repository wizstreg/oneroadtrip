The Fairytale Alsace Route: A Driving Tour of the 15 Most Beautiful Villages

Half-timbered houses leaning over lanes, balconies overflowing with geraniums, wine traditions passed from father to son. Alsace, picturesque and warm.

Journey through Alsace and its half-timbered villages. Explore Wissembourg in the north, wind through Riquewihr and Kaysersberg at the vineyard's heart, discover Eguisheim and its circular layout, then marvel at Neuf-Brisach and its perfect military geometry.

  • Riquewihr: wine route fairytale
  • Kaysersberg: winemaker heritage charm
  • Eguisheim: circular village perfection
  • Wissembourg: border medieval treasure

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-June): Perfect weather, blooming countryside, ideal for all regions, moderate crowds, excellent value, romantic atmosphere.

Summer (July-August): Warmest weather, peak tourist season, highest prices, crowded attractions, perfect for Mediterranean coast, festival season.

Fall (September-October): Harvest season, comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, wine regions spectacular, excellent photography, good value.

Winter (November-March): Christmas markets, ski season Alps, fewer crowds, lowest prices, cozy indoor attractions, cultural experiences.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 13 and 19 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in France?
