Albuquerque

10 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Ascend the Sandia Peak Tramway, one of the world's longest aerial tramways, climbing 2.7 miles to the crest of the Sandia Mountains at over 10,000 feet elevation. The location between the Rio Grande and the mountain range creates ideal conditions for hot-air ballooning, a beloved activity that can be enjoyed throughout the year. Late afternoon rides offer spectacular views of world-famous New Mexico sunsets across the vast landscape below.
2
Wander through Albuquerque's historic Old Town, where adobe architecture and tree-shaded plazas preserve over 300 years of Spanish colonial heritage. The district features locally-owned businesses, quality vintage shops, and mid-century modern stores reflecting the city's eclectic character. The Los Ranchos and Nob Hill neighborhoods extend the shopping and cultural exploration opportunities.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience the magic of hot air ballooning in the city renowned worldwide for hosting the annual International Balloon Fiesta each October. Albuquerque's unique geographic position creates the famous 'Albuquerque Box' wind pattern that allows pilots to launch and land in nearly the same location. Balloon rides operate year-round, offering visitors a chance to float peacefully above the Rio Grande Valley.
🚗

Madrid

4 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
Drive the scenic Turquoise Trail, a 50-mile National Scenic Byway connecting Cedar Crest to Cerrillos through dramatic high desert landscapes dotted with old mining towns turned artist colonies. The route passes through terrain rich in turquoise deposits that have been mined for centuries by indigenous peoples and later settlers. Each small community along the highway offers unique galleries, studios, and roadside attractions.
2
Explore Madrid, a former coal mining town transformed into a vibrant art village where creative spirits have established galleries, studios, and unique shops in restored Victorian-era buildings. The town maintains a deliberately off-grid character with limited cell service, encouraging visitors to disconnect and appreciate the artistic atmosphere. Horseback riding excursions through the surrounding high desert landscape provide adventure beyond the galleries.

Santa Fe

4 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Santa Fe, the state capital and one of America's oldest cities, where 400 years of history blend Spanish colonial, Pueblo, and contemporary influences into a vibrant cultural tapestry. The historic Plaza serves as the heart of the city, surrounded by adobe architecture housing world-class galleries, museums, and artisan shops. Among the oldest churches and houses in the United States stand alongside progressive contemporary spaces.
2
Experience Meow Wolf, Santa Fe's groundbreaking immersive art installation that has redefined interactive entertainment and artistic expression. This vast, otherworldly environment invites visitors to explore fantastical rooms, hidden passages, and mysterious narratives created by a collective of local artists. The experience represents the counterculture spirit that has attracted creative minds to Santa Fe since the 1960s.
🎯

Activities

→
Sample Santa Fe's celebrated cuisine, which has earned recognition as some of the finest in New Mexico, featuring distinctive chile-forward flavors and innovative interpretations of traditional Southwestern dishes. The city's culinary scene reflects its multicultural heritage, blending Native American, Spanish, Mexican, and contemporary American influences. Farm-to-table dining and historic chile-roasting traditions create unforgettable gastronomic experiences.
🚗

Los Alamos

7 photos
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
Visit the Los Alamos History Museum, which chronicles the town's remarkable transformation from a remote homesteading outpost to the epicenter of scientific research during World War II and the Cold War. The exhibits document how this isolated mesa became home to the Manhattan Project and changed the course of world history. Personal stories and artifacts illuminate the human dimension of this extraordinary chapter.
2
Explore the Bradbury Science Museum at Los Alamos National Laboratory, offering deeper insights into the development of atomic science and the Laboratory's ongoing research programs. Interactive exhibits explain complex scientific concepts while presenting the historical context of nuclear research. The museum provides a thoughtful examination of both the achievements and ethical considerations surrounding atomic energy.
🎯

Activities

→
Discover Bandelier National Monument, protecting over 33,000 acres of ancestral Puebloan cliff dwellings, petroglyphs, and pristine mountain wilderness where the Rio Grande flows through dramatic canyons. The archaeological sites date back nearly 11,000 years, revealing how ancient peoples adapted to this challenging environment. Hiking trails lead to remarkable cave dwellings that visitors can climb into and explore.
→
Experience Valles Caldera National Preserve, an 89,000-acre protected area centered on a massive volcanic caldera formed approximately 1.25 million years ago. The vast grassland valley surrounded by forested mountain slopes creates landscapes of extraordinary beauty. Elk herds graze across the caldera floor, visible from scenic overlooks along the preserve's rim.
🚗

Abiquiu

3 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Explore Ghost Ranch, the 21,000-acre retreat in red-rock country where painter Georgia O'Keeffe lived and created some of her most iconic works beginning in 1934. The dramatic landscapes of colorful cliffs, mesas, and desert formations that inspired masterpieces continue to draw artists, writers, and spiritual seekers. Workshops, retreats, hiking trails, and museums now occupy this legendary creative sanctuary.
2
Visit the Ruth Hall Museum of Paleontology and the Florence Hawley Ellis Museum of Anthropology at Ghost Ranch, housing significant collections documenting the region's natural and human history spanning millions of years. The paleontology museum features important dinosaur discoveries made in the surrounding formations. Walking the same trails O'Keeffe painted reveals why northern New Mexico has attracted artists for generations.
🎯

Activities

→
Journey to Plaza Blanca, the striking white rock formation that Georgia O'Keeffe treasured as her 'White Place' and immortalized in numerous paintings. The towering white cliffs and spires of volcanic ash create an otherworldly landscape accessible via the Dar al Islam community. Reservations are required to visit this geological treasure that captivated one of America's greatest artists.
🚗

Discover

1
Discover the mountain village of Chama, nestled high in the Southern Rockies of northern New Mexico where outdoor adventures await in every season. The Edward Sargent Wildlife Management Area offers excellent opportunities for hunting, Nordic skiing, and snowshoeing through pristine mountain terrain. The surrounding wilderness provides a genuine escape into high-altitude landscapes far from urban development.

Explore Heron Lake State Park, where spectacular mountain scenery surrounds a quiet reservoir perfect for hiking, cross-country skiing, and ice fishing during winter months. The lake's no-wake policy ensures peaceful conditions for kayaking and sailing in warmer seasons. Nearby El Vado Lake State Park provides prime winter birdwatching opportunities, particularly for observing bald eagles and other migratory species.

Activities

→
Experience the authentic mountain town atmosphere of downtown Chama, where locally-owned businesses serve visitors seeking genuine New Mexico character away from tourist crowds. The village serves as a gateway to vast wilderness areas spanning the Colorado border, offering access to some of the most remote landscapes in the American Southwest.

Experience Red River Ski & Summer Area, a family-friendly resort featuring over 60 trails across more than 200 acres of skiable terrain in the heart of the Sangre de Cristo Mountains. Activities

Explore the vibrant mountain town of Red River, where year-round outdoor recreation combines with authentic New Mexico hospitality. The compact downtown offers convenient access to equipment rentals and all necessary supplies for mountain adventures.

Taos

6 photos
Day 8
🔍

Discover

1
Visit Taos Pueblo, the UNESCO World Heritage Site that stands as one of the oldest continuously inhabited communities in the world, where Tiwa Puebloan traditions have been preserved for over 1,000 years. The remarkable multi-story adobe structures, some reaching five stories, have housed generations of the Pueblo people since before European contact. Guided tours provide respectful access to this living community and its sacred traditions.
2
Explore the Millicent Rogers Museum, showcasing over 7,000 artifacts spanning centuries of Native American and Hispanic artistic traditions including jewelry, pottery, textiles, and paintings. The Harwood Museum of Art houses another 6,500 works representing local and regional artists from the 18th century to the present. Together, these institutions reveal why Taos has attracted artists and collectors for generations.
🎯

Activities

→
Stand at the Rio Grande Gorge Bridge, where the dramatic chasm plunges 650 feet below the roadway, creating one of the most photographed scenic viewpoints in New Mexico. The gorge provides spectacular photo opportunities, particularly at sunrise and sunset when shadows emphasize the depth and texture of the volcanic rock walls. Trails descend into the gorge for closer exploration of this geological wonder.
→
Take a tour of the Earthships community near Taos Mesa, a collection of self-sustaining, off-grid dwellings constructed from recycled materials that have attracted international attention for sustainable living innovation. These distinctive structures generate their own electricity, collect water, and maintain comfortable temperatures year-round without conventional utilities. The community demonstrates practical applications of radical environmental design.

Kasha Katuwe

Day 9
🔍

Discover

1
Marvel at Kasha-Katuwe Tent Rocks National Monument, where remarkable cone-shaped formations created by volcanic eruptions six to seven million years ago rise dramatically from the canyon floor. The volcanic activity left pumice, ash, and tuff deposits exceeding 1,000 feet thick that have eroded into these extraordinary pointed spires. Located within Cochiti Pueblo lands, the monument protects both geological wonders and cultural heritage.
2
Hike the slot canyon trail winding between towering tent rock formations, ascending to panoramic viewpoints overlooking the entire monument and surrounding mesas. The narrow passages and dramatic rock walls create an otherworldly hiking experience unlike anywhere else in New Mexico. The varied layers of volcanic deposits reveal millions of years of geological history in their colorful striations.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience the Rio Grande between Taos and Española, where excellent white-water rafting opportunities attract adventure seekers to challenging rapids cutting through volcanic gorges. Multiple outfitters along the river offer guided trips suitable for various skill levels and thrill preferences. The section south of Albuquerque toward Socorro provides calmer waters ideal for peaceful floating and kayaking.

Gallup

4 photos
Day 10
🔍

Discover

1
Explore the George Galanis Multicultural Center (Gallup Cultural Center), which provides comprehensive introduction to the rich Indigenous cultures of the surrounding region including Navajo, Hopi, and Zuni tribal communities. The center documents centuries of artistic traditions, spiritual practices, and daily life through exhibits and cultural programs. Gallup's location at the intersection of multiple Native nations makes it an ideal base for cultural exploration.
2
Discover Red Rock Park, where massive sandstone cliffs formed during the Mesozoic Era approximately 200 million years ago create a dramatic natural amphitheater. The on-site museum showcases exceptional items crafted by Navajo, Hopi, and Zuni artisans, representing living artistic traditions that continue today. The park hosts major cultural events including the annual Inter-tribal Indian Ceremonial gathering.
🎯

Activities

→
Browse Gallup's distinctive local boutiques specializing in Native American crafts, silver jewelry, turquoise work, and leather goods created by regional artisans. The downtown district maintains the authentic character of a trading post town that has served as a commercial hub for surrounding tribal communities for over a century. Quality craftsmanship and fair-trade practices distinguish the established merchants.

Acoma Pueblo

4 photos
Day 11
🔍

Discover

1
Ascend to Acoma Pueblo, known as Sky City for its dramatic position atop a 367-foot sandstone bluff that has been continuously inhabited for over 900 years. The pueblo represents one of the oldest continuously occupied settlements in North America, where traditional Keresan-speaking people maintain ancient ways of life. Guided tours provide respectful access to this remarkable community perched between earth and sky.
2
Visit the Acoma Sky City Cultural Center and Haak'u Museum, which document the rich heritage, artistry, and spiritual traditions of the Acoma people through exhibits, demonstrations, and cultural programs. The famous Acoma pottery, distinguished by its thin walls and intricate geometric designs, represents one of the finest ceramic traditions in the Americas. The museum provides essential context for understanding this ancient community.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience the dramatic approach to Sky City, where the sheer mesa walls rise abruptly from the surrounding desert plain, creating a natural fortress that has protected the community for centuries. The views from atop the mesa extend across vast distances of high desert landscape virtually unchanged since ancient times. Proper cultural protocols and respectful behavior enhance the experience of visiting this sacred place.

Truth Or Consequences

4 photos
Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Soak in the therapeutic hot springs of Truth or Consequences, a town whose unusual name dates to a 1950 radio quiz show promotion but whose healing waters have attracted visitors for centuries. The mineral-rich thermal pools scattered throughout the downtown area offer the least crowded and most affordable hot spring experiences in New Mexico. Multiple facilities provide options ranging from rustic to luxurious soaking environments.
2
Explore Elephant Butte Lake State Park north of town, New Mexico's largest reservoir offering excellent opportunities for hiking, biking, boating, and water sports. The lake's vast expanse and surrounding desert mountains create dramatic landscapes for outdoor recreation. The area serves as a convenient base for exploring the wild terrain of nearby Apache Kid Wilderness.
🎯

Activities

→
Experience the Rio Grande riverside hot springs where thermal waters emerge directly beside the river, creating unique soaking opportunities that combine natural settings with healing mineral content. The town's relaxed atmosphere encourages extended stays focused on rest, relaxation, and rejuvenation. Local wellness practitioners complement the natural springs with massage and therapeutic services.

Silver

4 photos
Day 13
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Silver City, a historic mining town nestled at the foot of the Mogollon Mountains where Victorian-era architecture houses a thriving arts community. The Western New Mexico University Museum contains one of the world's largest collections of medieval-era Mimbres pottery and other pre-colonization Indigenous artifacts. The Grant County Art Guild Gallery showcases contemporary works by 35 local artists working in diverse media.
2
Venture into Gila National Forest, the vast mountain wilderness surrounding Silver City where experienced hikers can tackle challenging trails ascending Bear Mountain or connecting to the Continental Divide Trail via Black Peak. The forest encompasses over 3.3 million acres of rugged terrain ranging from desert lowlands to forested peaks exceeding 10,000 feet. Wildlife including elk, black bears, and mountain lions inhabit these remote ranges.
🎯

Activities

→
Explore downtown Silver City's Bullard Street district, where locally-owned galleries, shops, and studios occupy restored Victorian buildings dating to the town's silver mining heyday. The creative community has transformed this former frontier outpost into a vibrant arts destination while preserving its authentic Western character. Annual arts festivals and studio tours celebrate the town's artistic renaissance.

White Sands

3 photos
Day 14
🔍

Discover

1
Experience White Sands National Park, where 275 square miles of brilliant white gypsum dunes create one of the most extraordinary landscapes on Earth. The crystalline sand formations shift constantly with the wind, creating an ever-changing terrain of pristine white waves rolling across the Tularosa Basin. This rare ecosystem supports uniquely adapted plants and animals found nowhere else, including species that have evolved white coloration to match their surroundings.
2
Hike the Interdune Boardwalk and Dune Life Nature Trail to discover how life persists in this seemingly barren landscape of pure white gypsum crystals. The trails reveal the surprising biodiversity hidden within the dune fields and explain the geological processes that created this natural wonder. The stark white landscape against deep blue desert skies creates unforgettable visual experiences.
🎯

Activities

→
Sled down the pristine dunes using specially designed discs available at the visitor center, creating exhilarating descents through powdery white sand. Sunset transforms the white sands into a canvas of pink, orange, and purple hues, offering photographers extraordinary opportunities for capturing desert magic. Camping permits allow overnight stays for experiencing full moons and stargazing in one of America's darkest locations.
🚗

Day 15

Discover

1
Discover Ruidoso, a charming mountain village in the Sierra Blanca range where the snow-capped peak dominates the horizon and outdoor recreation opportunities abound in every season. Ski Apache operates as the southernmost ski resort in the United States, featuring 55 trails across 750 skiable acres with stunning views of the surrounding mountain wilderness. The resort's location on Mescalero Apache tribal lands adds cultural significance to the alpine experience.
2
Experience Ruidoso Winter Park, offering family-friendly snow activities including a mountain coaster and tubing course for those seeking gentler thrills than the ski slopes provide. The accessible location and varied attractions make Ruidoso popular with families and visitors seeking mountain ambiance without extreme sports. Scenic chairlift rides operate during summer months, providing easy access to high-altitude views.

Activities

→
Sample New Mexico wines at local tasting rooms and enjoy craft beverages that reflect the state's growing reputation for quality viticulture in high-altitude conditions. The village atmosphere encourages leisurely exploration of shops, galleries, and casual dining establishments. The Inn of the Mountain Gods, an Indigenous-owned resort on Mescalero Apache land, offers gaming entertainment and full-service spa facilities.

Roswell

4 photos
Day 16
🔍

Discover

1
Embrace the extraterrestrial legacy of Roswell, the small town that captured global attention following a mysterious 1947 incident that has fueled decades of speculation about unidentified flying objects. The International UFO Museum & Research Center presents exhibits examining the original incident, government investigations, and broader questions about extraterrestrial life that continue to fascinate visitors from around the world.
2
Explore downtown Roswell's UFO-themed attractions, where the community has enthusiastically embraced its otherworldly reputation through alien-themed street lights, murals, and memorabilia scattered throughout the commercial district. While the town maintains a playful approach to its fame, the museums offer serious examination of the incident's historical documentation and its cultural impact. The annual UFO Festival draws enthusiasts from across the globe.
🎯

Activities

→
Discover Roswell's authentic history as a ranching and agricultural center beyond its extraterrestrial associations, including museums documenting the region's development from frontier territory to modern community. The surrounding high desert landscape provides context for understanding how an isolated town became internationally famous for events that occurred on a remote ranch decades ago.

Carlsbad Caverns

4 photos
Day 17
🔍

Discover

1
Descend into Carlsbad Caverns National Park, where over 100 limestone caves beneath the Chihuahuan Desert reveal spectacular underground formations created over millions of years. The park's namesake Carlsbad Cavern features the massive Big Room, one of the largest cave chambers in North America, where towering stalactites and stalagmites create an underground cathedral 750 feet below the surface.
2
Explore additional cave systems within the park, including guided tours of King's Palace and other decorated chambers revealing the extraordinary variety of formations found throughout the cave network. The Natural Entrance route allows visitors to walk the original historic path descending into the cave, following trails that earliest explorers used to discover this subterranean wonderland.
🎯

Activities

→
Witness the spectacular evening bat flight from May through October, when hundreds of thousands of Brazilian free-tailed bats spiral from the cave entrance at dusk to hunt insects across the desert. Ranger programs at the outdoor amphitheater explain the ecology and behavior of these remarkable creatures before the evening emergence begins. The sight of the swirling column of bats against the sunset sky creates unforgettable memories.

What weather should you expect?

Spring (April-May): Perfect weather across most regions (15-25°C) with blooming landscapes, moderate crowds, and excellent conditions for national parks and cities. Ideal for diverse activities nationwide.

Autumn (September-October): Excellent temperatures with spectacular fall foliage, harvest festivals, and comfortable weather. Perfect for outdoor activities, cultural events, fewer crowds than summer.

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warmest weather but highest crowds and prices. Excellent for northern regions, challenging in southwestern deserts, perfect for beaches and national parks.

Winter (December-February): Variable conditions from tropical Florida to snowy Colorado. Perfect for skiing, southern destinations, indoor attractions, lowest prices outside holiday periods.
