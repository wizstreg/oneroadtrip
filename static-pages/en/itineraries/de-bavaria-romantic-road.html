Wurzburg

10 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
Begin your Romantic Road journey at the magnificent Würzburg Residence, a baroque masterwork that ranks among Europe's finest palaces and marks the northern gateway to this legendary route. The UNESCO World Heritage palace features the largest ceiling fresco ever painted, a breathtaking work by Tiepolo depicting the four continents in vivid color. The grand staircase, ornate state rooms and meticulously restored Court Garden showcase the extravagant tastes of the prince-bishops who commissioned this architectural wonder.
2
Explore the charming wine town of Würzburg nestled in the hills of Franconian wine country along the Main River. The Old Main Bridge, lined with baroque statues of saints, offers stunning views of the Marienberg Fortress perched on the hillside above. Local wine taverns serve the region's distinctive dry white wines in the traditional Bocksbeutel bottles unique to Franconia.

Rothenburg Ob Der Tauber

8 photos
Day 2
🔍

Discover

1
Step into the quintessential medieval Bavarian town at Rothenburg ob der Tauber, where cobblestone streets wind between perfectly preserved half-timbered houses that have barely changed since the Middle Ages. Walk the ancient walls that protected the city for centuries, offering elevated views over red-tiled rooftops and church spires. This fairy-tale town has enchanted visitors long before becoming a popular destination, and overnight stays reveal its magic after the day-trippers depart.
2
Photograph the legendary Plönlein, where every medieval feature condenses into one endlessly photogenic corner that has become an icon of German romanticism. This picturesque junction where two streets meet at different levels, framed by a half-timbered house and medieval tower gate, epitomizes the town's storybook charm. Early morning or evening light transforms this scene into something truly magical.
3
Discover the fascinating contrasts of Rothenburg's offbeat museums that reveal different facets of medieval life. The Medieval Criminal Museum displays ingeniously gruesome torture devices and instruments of justice that illuminate the darker side of the Middle Ages. In stark contrast, the German Christmas Museum celebrates the infinitely more cheerful traditions of the festive season year-round, showcasing centuries of holiday decorations and customs.

Augsbourg

3 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Discover Augsburg, one of Germany's oldest cities and a compelling destination that offers a rich alternative to Munich for those seeking authentic Bavarian culture. Founded by the Romans over 2,000 years ago, this historic city rose to prominence as a major trading center where the powerful Fugger banking family financed emperors and shaped European history. The Renaissance architecture and grand public squares reflect centuries of wealth and influence.
2
Explore the remarkable Fuggerei, the world's oldest social housing complex still in operation since 1521, where residents pay the same annual rent of one Rhenish guilder that was established five centuries ago. The peaceful tree-lined streets and uniform yellow facades create an atmosphere of timeless tranquility within the bustling city. This pioneering example of philanthropy demonstrates the social consciousness that accompanied Augsburg's commercial success.
3
Admire the magnificent Renaissance Town Hall with its legendary Golden Hall, one of the most important secular Renaissance buildings north of the Alps. The three monumental fountains along Maximilianstraße, depicting Roman gods Augustus, Mercury and Hercules, are UNESCO-listed masterpieces of bronze sculpture. Historic churches, the ancient cathedral and elegant patrician houses complete Augsburg's impressive architectural heritage.

Fussen

10 photos
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
Complete your Romantic Road journey at the iconic Neuschwanstein Castle, the fairy-tale fortress that inspired Walt Disney and has become the very symbol of romantic Germany. Rising dramatically from forested Alpine slopes near Füssen, this 19th-century masterpiece was built by King Ludwig II as a retreat into his world of medieval fantasy and Wagnerian opera. The theatrical interiors, featuring a grotto, throne room and singer's hall, reveal the visionary imagination of Bavaria's beloved 'Mad King.'
2
Marvel at the castle's incomparable setting, perched on a rugged hilltop with the Alps rising behind and crystalline lakes shimmering in the valley below. The Marienbrücke bridge spanning a dramatic gorge offers the classic photograph of Neuschwanstein that has graced countless postcards and travel dreams. This breathtaking viewpoint rewards visitors with the full majesty of Ludwig's romantic vision realized in stone and spire.
3
Explore the charming town of Füssen, the southern terminus of the Romantic Road, where colorful baroque facades line pedestrianized streets at the foot of the Alps. The High Castle (Hohes Schloss) with its remarkable trompe-l'oeil painted facades overlooks the medieval old town and the rushing Lech River. This gateway to the Bavarian Alps has welcomed travelers for centuries as both a trading post and pilgrimage stop.
🎯
