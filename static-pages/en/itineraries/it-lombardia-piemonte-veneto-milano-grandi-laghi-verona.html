Grand Tour of the Italian Lakes: Milan to Verona in 17 Days

17 dream days from Milan through the great lakes to Verona: the ultimate northern Italy road trip.

📅 Best period April, May, June, September, October

Embark on an unforgettable journey through northern Italy, from vibrant Milan to romantic Verona. This 17-day itinerary takes you along Lakes Como, Maggiore and Garda, past sumptuous villas, extraordinary botanical gardens and art cities steeped in history. A trip that blends magnificent nature, UNESCO heritage and the Italian art of living.

  • Milan: the Duomo, Leonardo's Last Supper and the Navigli canals
  • Lake Como: Bellagio, Varenna and their legendary villas
  • Lake Maggiore: the Borromean Islands and Villa Taranto gardens
  • Orta San Giulio: the enchanted island and UNESCO Sacro Monte
  • Bergamo and Brescia: two gems of art and history
  • Lake Garda: Sirmione, Desenzano and Peschiera del Garda
  • Verona: the Roman Arena, Castelvecchio and Romeo and Juliet

Discover

1
Let yourself be enchanted by the majestic Milan Cathedral, a masterpiece of Gothic architecture that dominates the square of the same name. With its 3,500 statues, soaring spires reaching towards the sky and the famous gilded Madonnina standing 4 metres tall, this cathedral dedicated to Santa Maria Nascente is the beating heart of the city. Climb to the terraces for a spectacular view over the metropolis and the Alps on clear days.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Walk through the splendid Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, Milan's refined drawing room inaugurated in 1867. This elegant covered passage with its majestic iron and glass dome connects Piazza Duomo to Piazza della Scala, offering a unique experience among prestigious boutiques, historic cafés and exquisite mosaics. The architecture designed by Giuseppe Mengoni is a symbol of nineteenth-century Milanese ambition.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Explore the Museo del Novecento in the Palazzo dell'Arengario, a treasure trove of over 400 masterpieces of twentieth-century Italian art. From the celebrated Quarto Stato by Pellizza da Volpedo to works by Boccioni, Modigliani and Fontana, the chronological route guides you through Futurism, Metaphysical Art and Conceptual Art. The hall dedicated to Lucio Fontana offers a breathtaking view of the Duomo through the large windows.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Admire Leonardo da Vinci's Last Supper, one of the most famous paintings in the world housed in the refectory of Santa Maria delle Grazie. Created between 1494 and 1498 using an innovative technique, this depiction of the Last Supper reveals Leonardo's genius in its dramatic composition and the psychological depth of the figures. A deeply moving experience that requires advance booking.
⏱️ 75 min

Stroll along the romantic Navigli, the ancient canal system designed in part by Leonardo da Vinci to connect Milan to the lakes and the sea. The recently renovated Darsena offers picturesque views, artisan workshops and a uniquely bohemian atmosphere in the heart of the city.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Admire the Como Cathedral, a splendid church blending Gothic and Renaissance elements in the heart of the city. Built from 1396 and completed in the eighteenth century with the majestic dome designed by Filippo Juvarra, it is one of Lombardy's most important religious monuments. The richly decorated façade and solemn interiors bear witness to centuries of devotion and art.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit the Tempio Voltiano, an elegant Neoclassical museum dedicated to Alessandro Volta, Como's illustrious citizen and inventor of the electric battery. Set on the lakefront in a panoramic position, the museum houses original scientific instruments and documents tracing the revolutionary discoveries of the scientist who changed the course of history.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Ride the funicular up to Brunate, the balcony of the Alps offering breathtaking panoramas of the lake and the Alpine chain. This charming village at 720 metres altitude is reachable in just 7 minutes of scenic travel and rewards visitors with unforgettable views, particularly captivating at sunset.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Stroll along the Como lakefront, an elegant promenade lined with historic villas, lush gardens and panoramic cafés. The crystal-clear waters of the Lario and the mountain profiles create a setting of incomparable beauty that has inspired poets and artists through the ages.

Discover

1
Explore Punta Spartivento, the panoramic point where Lake Como divides into two branches creating its characteristic Y shape. From this legendary promontory you can admire a breathtaking view immortalised by Alessandro Manzoni in The Betrothed, with the mountains reflected in the crystal-clear waters.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Visit the Church of San Giacomo, a gem of Romanesque-Lombard art built in the twelfth century using the characteristic grey stone of Moltrasio. Inside you can admire precious frescoes and sculptures from different historical periods, while the bell tower bears witness to the village's ancient defensive system.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Wander through the grounds of Villa Serbelloni, a magnificent garden of nearly 21 hectares stretching across a steep promontory. This historic residence, now owned by the Rockefeller Foundation, boasts 18 km of paths among rare plants and panoramic vistas that have enchanted distinguished guests over the centuries, including Leonardo da Vinci and Alessandro Manzoni.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Discover the Gardens of Villa Melzi d'Eril, a botanical paradise on the western lakefront created in the early nineteenth century by the noble Milanese family. Among exotic plants, ancient trees, antique statues and archaeological finds, you can admire the famous statue of Dante and Beatrice that inspired Franz Liszt to compose his Dante Sonata.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Once known as Il Fossato for its medieval defensive role, it now features a commemorative plaque to the composer Franz Liszt, who stayed at length in this enchanting village.

Discover

1
Admire the Church of San Giorgio, a splendid example of thirteenth-century medieval Lombard architecture built on the foundations of an ancient Roman temple. The grey stone façade is dominated by a majestic bell tower, while inside the 1822 Baroque high altar and a precious cycle of frescoes stand out. The floor of fine black marble completes the elegance of this sacred place.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Explore Villa Monastero, an evocative sixteenth-century architectural complex that was once a Cistercian convent. Today it is one of the lake's main attractions with its house museum and extraordinary botanical gardens hosting hundreds of plant species from around the world, along a path winding beside the lakeshore.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit Villa Cipressi, an elegant late-Renaissance gem with terraces cascading down towards the lake. This historic complex offers romantic views and manicured gardens delivering unforgettable panoramas over the waters of the Lario and the surrounding mountains.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk the Passeggiata degli Innamorati (Lovers' Walk), a romantic walkway of about 500 metres suspended on the cliff face above the lake. This arched structure, draped in climbing plants that burst into a riot of colour and fragrance in the warmer months, connects the landing stage to the colourful houses of the old town in a route of incomparable beauty.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Climb to the Castello di Vezio, an ancient fortress overlooking Varenna from above with spectacular views of the lake and surrounding villages. You can explore the gardens, the atmospheric underground passages and the square watchtower housing a permanent art exhibition.

Explore the Grotte di Rescia, a fascinating system of natural cavities rich in stalactites and stalagmites bearing witness to millennia of geological activity. Discover the Oratory of San Maurizio Martire, a small church set just outside the centre that still preserves its original layout. This gem of religious architecture is a precious testimony to local devotion through the centuries.

Climb the Salita della Motta, a picturesque cobbled lane lined with frescoed houses leading to the Church of Santa Maria Assunta. From this elevated point you can admire one of the lake's most evocative panoramas, with the Island of San Giulio seemingly floating on the crystal-clear waters.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the small Church of San Rocco on the main street, a hidden gem that wins you over with its welcoming and spiritual atmosphere. Beside it stands the Palazzo Comunale with its wonderful flower garden overlooking the lake, a corner of paradise in the heart of the village.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Explore the Sacro Monte di Orta, a UNESCO World Heritage Site rising on the hill above the village. This sixteenth- to seventeenth-century devotional complex comprises twenty frescoed chapels dedicated to the life of Saint Francis of Assisi, set in an ancient wood with spectacular views of the lake and island.

Explore Isola Bella, the jewel of the Borromean Islands housing the splendid Palazzo Borromeo, a perfectly preserved noble residence with precious paintings, tapestries and antique furnishings. The magnificent Borromean Gardens, created in the seventeenth century in pure Italian Baroque style, cascade over ten terraces towards the lake with an extraordinary variety of floral species and monumental statues.

2
Visit Isola Madre, the largest and most evocative of the Borromean Islands, wrapped in a quiet, suspended atmosphere. Admire Villa Pallavicino, an elegant nineteenth-century residence surrounded by a vast English-style garden hosting a fascinating zoological park. 4
Discover the Church of Sant'Ambrogio in the heart of Stresa, an elegant example of Neoclassical architecture with a Greek-cross plan completed in 1790. Inside lie the relics of San Vitaliano, a young Christian martyr venerated as protector of children, whose cult was introduced in 1835 following the transfer of his remains from the Roman catacombs.

Discover

1
Lose yourself in the magnificent Botanical Gardens of Villa Taranto, twenty hectares of pure wonder on the shores of Lake Maggiore. Created by Scottish Captain McEacharn from 1931, these enchanting English-style gardens feature labyrinths of dahlias, water lily pools, valleys of rhododendrons, waterfalls and fountains in a place where care and love for nature are palpable at every turn.

Visit Villa San Remigio on the Colle della Castagnola, built in the early twentieth century by the poet and musician Marquis Silvio della Valle di Casanova and the painter Sofia Browne. Their dream garden is a true hymn to love, composed of several themed gardens overlooking the lake in a romantic atmosphere suspended in time.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Discover Villa Giulia and its park in the heart of Pallanza, built in the mid-nineteenth century by the Branca family, inventors of the famous Fernet. Now municipal property, it hosts temporary exhibitions while the park, always open and free, offers a privileged view of the lake and the picturesque village of Pallanza.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Walk the lakeside promenade from Verbania to Pallanza, a pedestrian and cycle path of about 2 km offering breathtaking panoramas, ancient villas and dream palaces. An itinerary for daydreaming of a less hectic life on the lakefront, imagining yourself in an age of noble titles and grand balls.

→
Venture along the path to the Church of Buon Rimedio, an ancient mule track through jasmine-scented woods to a small eighteenth-century church. From this natural balcony overlooking Lake Maggiore you can enjoy breathtaking panoramic views and picnic at the equipped area with lakeside tables.

→
Explore the historic village of Pallanza with the Museo del Paesaggio housed in the Palazzo Viani Dugnani, rich in paintings, sculptures and archaeological finds. Then treat yourself to a stop at one of the lakeside terraces on the main square to savour the relaxed atmosphere of this enchanting corner of Piedmont.

Bergamo

3 photos
Day 12
🔍

Discover

1
Ride the funicular up to Città Alta, one of Italy's best-preserved medieval villages enclosed within the majestic Venetian Walls, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Wandering through the cobbled streets you'll discover centuries of history and Renaissance art in a timeless atmosphere that has captivated travellers of every era.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Admire Piazza Vecchia, the true civic drawing room overlooked by the Palazzo della Ragione, the Civic Tower (Campanone) and the Angelo Mai Library. Every evening the Campanone bell tolls one hundred times, just as in Venetian times when it signalled the closing of the city gates, evoking an atmosphere steeped in historical charm.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Visit the Basilica of Santa Maria Maggiore with its richly Baroque interiors adorned with tapestries and gilded stuccowork, and the adjacent Cappella Colleoni, a Renaissance masterpiece built as a mausoleum for the condottiere Bartolomeo Colleoni. The chapel's polychrome marble façades rank among the finest in Italy.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Explore the Accademia Carrara, one of Italy's most prestigious art galleries with over 1,800 paintings by artists such as Botticelli, Raphael, Mantegna, Titian and Canaletto. Directly opposite stands the GAMeC, the Gallery of Modern and Contemporary Art, creating a unique dialogue between tradition and modernity.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

Ride even higher on the San Vigilio Funicular to the Castle, crossing breathtaking landscapes with spectacular views of the city rooftops, medieval towers and the Lombard plain. From the castle ramparts you can enjoy one of the most beautiful sunsets in Lombardy.

→
Lose yourself in the Lorenzo Rota Botanical Garden, a small urban Eden of 2,400 sq m on the panoramic terraces of Città Alta. From here you can admire a spectacular view of the city and surrounding mountains, with the Venetian Walls seeming to merge with the landscape in perfect harmony.

Explore the Museo di Santa Giulia, an extraordinary monastic complex and the city's main museum housing treasures of exceptional historical and artistic importance. From the ninth-century Cross of Desiderius to the incredible frescoes of the Nuns' Choir, from the remains of ancient Roman Domus houses to the marvellous interiors of the Basilica of San Salvatore, it is part of the UNESCO site in the heart of Brescia.

Admire the Roman Archaeological Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site comprising Piazza del Foro, the Capitolium with the hall dedicated to the Winged Victory, the Republican Sanctuary, the ancient theatre and the Roman Basilica. This extraordinary complex bears witness to the greatness of Brixia, one of northern Italy's most important cities in the Roman era.

3
Visit Piazza Loggia, the beating heart of Brescia characterised by a splendid blend of styles with a clear Venetian imprint. The Palazzo della Loggia, built between 1492 and 1570 with contributions from Palladio and Sansovino, dominates the square alongside the Clock Tower with its ancient astronomical clock.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Discover the Pinacoteca Tosio Martinengo, one of northern Italy's most important galleries with works by Raphael, Foppa, Moretto, Romanino, Lotto, Ceruti and Hayez. Admire the Scaliger Castle, a majestic medieval fortress built after the mid-fourteenth century standing at the entrance to the old town. ⏱️ 75 min

Discover the Palazzo Callas Exhibitions, dedicated to the memory of the Divine Maria Callas who chose Sirmione as her Italian refuge. Activities

Relax at Spiaggia Giamaica, an exotic-feeling spot at the tip of Sirmione below the Grotte di Catullo. Formed of smooth white rocks polished by the waves, surrounded by crystal-clear waters and wild vegetation, it offers spectacular views towards the Rocca di Manerba and the northern part of the lake.

Let yourself be enchanted by the Arena di Verona, a majestic Roman amphitheatre built in 30 AD and splendidly preserved. Originally capable of seating up to 30,000 spectators, today it is one of the world's most prestigious venues for open-air opera and concerts, where the perfect acoustics and magical atmosphere make every performance unforgettable.

Visit Juliet's House on Via Cappello, the iconic symbol of Shakespearean romance with its famous balcony and courtyard that have inspired the timeless love story. In the courtyard you can admire the bronze statue of Juliet by Nereo Costantini, which tradition says brings luck in love to those who touch it.
⏱️ 75 min
3
Explore Castelvecchio and the Ponte Scaligero, an imposing medieval fortress with a magnificent fortified bridge offering breathtaking views over the River Adige. The museum inside houses extraordinary collections of medieval and Renaissance art, sculptures, paintings and the city's ancient bells displayed across 29 unique rooms.
⏱️ 75 min

Admire the Basilica of San Zeno Maggiore, a masterpiece of Romanesque architecture built between 967 and 1398 AD. The intricate bronze doors, extraordinary frescoes and imposing altarpiece by Mantegna will take your breath away. According to tradition, the crypt is the place where Romeo and Juliet celebrated their secret marriage.
