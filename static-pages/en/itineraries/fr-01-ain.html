Discover

1
The Royal Monastery of Brou stands like a Bressan Taj Mahal, built out of love between 1506 and 1512. A masterpiece of Flamboyant Gothic architecture, it captivates with its stone lacework, three princely tombs, and poignant history: Margaret of Austria had this mausoleum built for her husband Philibert the Fair, who died too soon. The three cloisters and the Fine Arts Museum complete the visit to this listed monument.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Notre-Dame Church reveals Flamboyant Gothic architecture typical of the Savoy Bresse region. Built with carrons, those characteristic Savoy bricks, it bears witness to the town's medieval past. Its bell tower has watched over the city center for centuries, offering a visible landmark from the surrounding cobblestone streets.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Stroll through the historic center's narrow streets where half-timbered houses tell the story of this former capital of Bresse. Place Carriat and its terraces invite you to observe the daily ballet of the Burgiens. Colorful facades and arcades shelter artisan shops that perpetuate local craftsmanship.
⏱️ 60 min
4
The Ferme du Sougey, a listed historic monument, is one of the oldest half-timbered farms in Bresse, dating from the 15th century. Transformed into a local heritage museum, it showcases traditional Bressan habitat and houses a breeding farm for AOC Bresse poultry. The surrounding 35 hectares of bocage landscape bear witness to ancestral local agriculture.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Discover Bresse poultry AOC, the only poultry in the world to benefit from a protected designation of origin since 1957. In local farms and restaurants, taste this fine, marbled meat from chickens raised free-range in the Bressan bocage. Local gastronomy highlights this blue-legged chicken, the region's crown jewel.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Get lost in the cobblestone streets of the Brou district. Old houses, secret passages, and small interior courtyards create a preserved medieval atmosphere. Every facade tells a story, every stone bears witness to the passage of centuries in this city of weavers and merchants.
⏱️ 60 min
→
The market halls buzz every Saturday morning with the activity of local producers. Bresse poultry, mountain cheeses, sausages, thick fresh cream: the stalls overflow with local products. The friendly atmosphere and discussions with producers keep the Bressan agricultural tradition alive.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Pérouges, the only Most Beautiful Village of France in Ain, unfolds its cobblestone streets like an intact movie set. The fortified medieval town, perched on its tuff outcrop, has crossed the centuries without losing its soul as a 15th-century weaver village. Eighty historic monuments bear witness to this exceptional heritage, where the powerful Dukes of Savoy once lived.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The Place du Tilleul forms the beating heart of the village, organized around a centuries-old linden tree that has witnessed the daily life of residents for generations. Surrounded by half-timbered houses with overhanging upper floors and carved corbels, the square embodies the charm of medieval architecture. The old well, still visible in the center, recalls the times when water collection organized community life.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Church of Sainte-Marie-Madeleine overlooks the village with its octagonal bell tower, an unusual architectural feature. Built in the 15th century, it houses remarkable religious furniture and stained glass windows that filter colored light onto the stone walls. The fortified porch served as both a place of worship and defense during troubled times.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Walk along the covered walkway that runs along the ramparts, offering panoramic views of the Dombes plain. The medieval fortifications, partially restored, testify to the strategic role of Pérouges in controlling the Ain valley. The defensive towers and gates, still visible, recall the turbulent era when the village had to protect itself from invasions.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Taste the famous galette de Pérouges, a sweet flat cake with butter and sugar that has become the village's signature dish. The traditional recipe, jealously guarded by local artisans, dates back several centuries. Each bakery offers its version of this golden pastry, crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, best enjoyed warm with a cup of coffee.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Explore the artisan workshops that perpetuate ancestral know-how: weaving, pottery, woodworking. Craftsmen open their doors to explain traditional techniques that made the fortune of this merchant town. Demonstrations of period looms fascinate visitors with the complexity of gestures and the beauty of fabrics produced.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Wander the covered walkways and hidden passages that connect the streets. These shaded spaces, sheltered from bad weather, were used by weavers to protect precious fabrics from moisture. Stone arches and sculpted corbels reveal unsuspected architectural details with each step.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
Châtillon-sur-Chalaronne charms with its flowered canals and Burgundian-style half-timbered houses reflected in the clear waters of the Chalaronne. The medieval town, former border of the Duchy of Savoy, has preserved its defensive character with fortified gates and watchtowers. The eleven bridges that span the river create a romantic landscape worthy of the most beautiful Venetian canals.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The covered market, built on wooden pillars in 1440, stands as one of the finest examples of Bresse civil architecture. Under its impressive oak framework, merchants have set up stalls for six centuries. Local producers still offer Bresse specialties there every Saturday: poultry, cheeses, vegetables from the market gardens.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The former Hôtel-Dieu hospital, founded in 1442, houses a remarkable apothecary with 120 ceramic jars from Meillonnas. The painted woodwork, period medical instruments, and herb drying room plunge visitors into 18th-century medicine. The nuns' chapel and patients' rooms complete this fascinating journey through time.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Follow the Heritage Trail that winds through narrow streets and along canals. Informative panels reveal the history of brick houses, stone bridges, and wash houses where Bressan women came to do their laundry. The Saint-André gate and the Clock Tower punctuate this walk through medieval history.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Rent a canoe to explore the Chalaronne from the water and discover hidden perspectives of the town. The calm current makes navigation accessible to everyone, even beginners. Passing under arched bridges and along flowered gardens offers a bucolic experience. Swans and ducks accompany paddlers along this aquatic walk.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Stroll through the exceptional flower gardens that earned Châtillon the title of 4-flower town. Terraced gardens descend to the water, geraniums cascade from windows, and roses climb ancient facades. The municipal park and its century-old trees invite contemplation and rest.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit the Dream Train Museum, unique in Europe, which brings together an exceptional collection of old toy trains. Miniature landscapes recreate various French and European regions. Animated dioramas fascinate young and old with their precision and realism. Rare pieces and collector trains complete this nostalgic journey.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Juliénas, the northernmost cru of Beaujolais, unfolds its vineyards on granite hillsides that give character to its powerful and structured wines. Legend attributes the village's name to Julius Caesar, who supposedly planted the first vines here. The Château du Bois de la Salle, a former commandery, dominates the appellation and houses a renowned tasting cellar.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Church of Saint-Just-et-Saint-Pasteur stands atop the hill, offering a panoramic view of the vineyards stretching to the horizon. Its Romanesque bell tower watches over the vines that have made the village's reputation for centuries. The stained glass windows depict wine-related scenes, celebrating the inseparable bond between faith and viticulture.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The former church of Juliénas, now a wine cooperative, represents a unique architectural conversion. The sacred building, deconsecrated in the 1950s, was transformed into a cathedral dedicated to wine. Frescoes by artists Victor Charreton and Paul Borel adorn the walls, celebrating wine and work in the vineyards.
⏱️ 60 min
4
Walk through the old village where golden stone houses bear witness to the prosperity brought by wine. The vaulted cellars, dug directly into granite, maintain constant temperature ideal for wine aging. Some cellars open their doors to reveal bottles dating back several decades.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Taste Juliénas wines in one of the many estate cellars. This cru, produced on only 600 hectares, reveals rich aromas of red fruits and spices. Independent winegrowers passionately explain the influence of granite terroir on character. The convivial tasting includes several vintages to understand the evolution over the years.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Hike the Cru Trail that winds through vineyards and offers spectacular views of Mont Blanc on clear days. The marked path passes through the different climats that make up the appellation: Juliénas, Les Mouilles, Les Capitans. Information panels explain viticultural practices and the influence of terroir.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Visit the Cellier de la Vieille Église, a former cooperative transformed into a tasting temple. The frescoes depicting vineyard work and winemaking techniques create a unique atmosphere. Tastings are accompanied by explanations of specific characteristics of Juliénas wines compared to other Beaujolais crus.
⏱️ 75 min

Discover

1
Chiroubles, the highest cru of Beaujolais perched at 400 meters altitude, produces elegant and delicate wines nicknamed the 'flowers of Beaujolais.' The village clings to granite hillsides overlooking valleys dotted with vineyards and forests. This elevated position creates a unique microclimate that gives wines their distinctive finesse and floral character.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Terrasse de Chiroubles offers a breathtaking 360-degree panorama over the entire Beaujolais region. On clear days, the view extends to Mont Blanc and the Alps in the east, the Monts du Forez in the west. Information panels identify the different Beaujolais crus visible from this exceptional belvedere.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Church of Saint-Étienne, with its Romanesque bell tower, watches over the village from its elevated position. The interior houses a remarkable collection of religious furniture and ex-votos testifying to popular devotion. The stained glass windows filtered by the sun create a mystical atmosphere conducive to meditation.
⏱️ 45 min
4
The Victor Pulliat Museum, housed in a former wine storehouse, pays tribute to this ampelographer who revolutionized viticulture in the 19th century. Pulliat studied grafting to save French vines from phylloxera. Tools, documents, and explanatory panels retrace his essential work for world viticulture.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Discover Chiroubles wines through tastings at independent winegrowers who cultivate steep hillsides. This lightest and most aromatic cru of Beaujolais reveals notes of violet, peony, and red fruits. Producers explain the influence of altitude on ripeness and the delicate balance they seek. Small family estates, often spanning 5-10 hectares, welcome visitors with legendary Beaujolais conviviality.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Hike the Sentier des Crus trail that crosses vineyards while offering spectacular views of the Alps. The 8 km marked loop passes through different climats and explains the geological particularities of granite terroir. Resting areas allow you to contemplate the landscape while enjoying a snack. The gentle slopes make this walk accessible to families.
⏱️ 150 min
→
Visit during the Fête des Crus in April, when the entire village celebrates the new vintage. Producers open their cellars for free tastings, street entertainment animates the streets, and regional specialties are served on every corner. The festive and authentic atmosphere showcases Beaujolais conviviality.
⏱️ 180 min

Discover

1
Fleurie, the 'Queen of Beaujolais,' produces arguably the most elegant and refined wines of the region. The 880 hectares of vines unfold on pink granite hillsides that give the wine its characteristic finesse. The village itself charms with its golden stone houses and Romanesque chapel overlooking the vineyards. Fleurie embodies the perfect balance between viticultural excellence and preserved architectural heritage.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Madonna Chapel, perched on the hill overlooking Fleurie, offers a breathtaking panorama of the vineyards stretching to the horizon. Built in the 19th century, it became a place of pilgrimage for winegrowers seeking protection for their harvests. The stained glass windows depict wine-related scenes, celebrating the sacred bond between faith and viticulture. The walk to reach it crosses the most beautiful parcels of the appellation.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The village center preserves remarkable wine architecture with vaulted cellars, sculpted doorways, and period wineries. Golden stone facades reflect warm light at dusk, creating a romantic atmosphere. The central square and its century-old plane trees provide a shaded meeting place where producers and visitors exchange impressions on the latest vintage.
⏱️ 75 min
4
The Caveau des Producteurs du Cru Fleurie groups together sixty independent winegrowers under one roof. This cooperative tasting room allows discovery of wine diversity despite the common appellation. Each parcel, each climat brings subtle nuances that passionate producers decipher. Modern architecture contrasts with ancient cellars while respecting the site.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Taste Fleurie wines at renowned estates like Clos de la Roilette or Domaine de la Grand'Cour. These elegant wines, with silky tannins and aromas of iris and violet, represent the quintessence of Beaujolais. Producers explain the influence of pink granite on finesse and the winemaking techniques that preserve aromatic delicacy. Some estates offer vertical tastings of several vintages to understand evolution over years.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Walk the Wine Trail that crosses the most beautiful climats of the appellation: La Roilette, Grille-Midi, Les Moriers. Information panels explain the influence of exposure, slope, and soil composition on wine character. The 5 km loop offers spectacular views of Mont Blanc on clear days and passes through century-old vines.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Attend a wine and cheese pairing workshop organized by local sommeliers. Young Beaujolais cheeses, goat cheeses from nearby mountains, and aged Comté accompany different cuvées of Fleurie. Professionals explain harmony principles between wine tannins and cheese textures. The convivial format encourages exchanges between participants.
⏱️ 105 min

Discover

1
Beaujeu, the historic capital that gave its name to the entire Beaujolais region, preserves exceptional medieval heritage. Founded in the 9th century by the Lords of Beaujeu, the town was the seat of a powerful feudal domain controlling the region for four centuries. The vestiges of ramparts, fortified gates, and the seigneurial castle recall this glorious past. The narrow streets and half-timbered houses create an authentic medieval atmosphere.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Hospices de Beaujeu, founded in 1235, rank among the oldest hospitals in France still in operation. The Gothic facade, chapel with remarkable frescoes, and apothecary with period ceramic jars bear witness to eight centuries of charity. The courtyard garden, maintained according to medieval principles, cultivates medicinal plants once used in care. Guided tours reveal surprising secrets about ancient medicine.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Temple de Bacchus, housed in a beautiful Renaissance building, serves as the main tasting cellar for Beaujolais wines. The vaulted cellars, dug into rock, maintain ideal temperature and humidity for aging. The sommeliers offer thematic tastings to understand the differences between ten crus and villages Beaujolais. An oenology museum completes the visit with tools and objects related to wine work.
⏱️ 90 min
4
The Sources du Beaujolais visitor center explains the formation of this viticultural region through interactive exhibits. Geological models show how granite and schist give specific characteristics to wines. Videos retrace the history of Beaujolais from Celtic times to today. A terrace offers panoramic views of surrounding vineyards.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Visit during the famous Sarmentelles festival in December, marking the official release of Beaujolais Nouveau. The entire town lights up with thousands of vine prunings (sarments) that warm the winter air. Wine flows freely in the streets, producers offer tastings of their latest vintage, and concerts animate every corner. This unique tradition attracts thousands of visitors who share a friendly glass around bonfires.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Explore the Monday market that has taken place on Place de l'Hôtel de Ville for centuries. Stalls overflow with local products: Beaujolais wines of course, but also regional charcuterie, mountain cheeses, and seasonal vegetables. Producers sell directly to consumers, preserving personal relationship and advice. The market atmosphere, both authentic and lively, embodies Beaujolais conviviality.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Hike the Chemins du Beaujolais circuit that starts from the town center. The marked trail climbs to surrounding heights offering spectacular views of vineyard-covered valleys. The route passes through forests, vineyards, and small hamlets where time seems suspended. Information panels explain the landscape's geological and viticultural history.
⏱️ 150 min

Discover

1
Vaux-en-Beaujolais, the real Clochemerle made famous by Gabriel Chevallier's satirical novel, has kept the soul of a Beaujolais wine village where life flows peacefully. The novel, published in 1934, depicted the colorful rivalries and picturesque characters of a wine village. The town embraced this literary identity by creating a circuit retracing locations described in the book. Golden stone houses and flower-filled alleys preserve authentic charm untouched by time.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The famous public urinal, at the heart of literary controversy, stands on the church square as a symbol of local humor. The monument, erected as homage to the novel, became a pilgrimage destination for literature lovers. A nearby panel recounts the story of this scandalous facility that divided the fictional village. The Church of Saint-Pierre overlooks this unusual square with its Romanesque bell tower.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Musée Clochemerle, housed in the old town hall, immerses visitors in the universe of Gabriel Chevallier's novel. Period objects, photographs, and excerpts recreate the atmosphere of the 1930s and the story's colorful characters. The collection includes original editions, translations into dozens of languages, and film adaptations. A wine tasting room completes the visit with local Beaujolais.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Walk the Heritage Trail that winds through vineyards and hamlets. Information panels reveal the history of golden stone houses, vaulted wine cellars, and bread ovens. The circuit passes by the wash house where village women gathered, crosses vineyards stretching to the horizon, and climbs to belvederes offering views of surrounding valleys.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Taste Beaujolais wines at family estates that have cultivated vines for generations. Independent winegrowers open their cellars to explain their work and passion. Tastings include different cuvées to understand terroir diversity. The warm welcome and informality typical of Beaujolais make each visit a moment of authentic sharing. Some estates offer picnics in the vineyards with a view.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Attend a reading of excerpts from Clochemerle at the village café, organized regularly by a local theater troupe. Actors bring the novel's colorful characters to life with humor and authentic local accent. The informal atmosphere encourages exchanges between spectators who share their memories of reading the book. A Beaujolais tasting follows the performance.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Cycle through surrounding vineyards on small roads where cars rarely pass. The gentle terrain makes cycling accessible to everyone. The route connects several wine hamlets, passes through century-old vines, and offers spectacular views of Mont Blanc on clear days. Stops at independent winegrowers allow discovery of production diversity.
⏱️ 150 min

Discover

1
Oingt, classified among the Most Beautiful Villages of France, unfolds its golden stone houses on a rocky outcrop overlooking the Azergues valley. The medieval village, fortified in the 13th century, has preserved its authentic character with ramparts, fortified gates, and cobbled streets called 'calades.' The golden color of buildings, due to local limestone, creates a unique luminosity particularly magical at sunset. Oingt embodies the quintessence of Golden Stone Country.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The Church of Saint-Mathieu, perched at the village's highest point, offers a panoramic view extending from Mont Blanc to the Pilat massif on clear days. Built in the 12th century in Romanesque style, it houses remarkable religious furniture and frescoes recently restored. The square bell tower serves as a landmark visible from the entire valley. The interior, soberly decorated, invites contemplation.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The medieval castle, though partially ruined, testifies to Oingt's strategic importance during the Middle Ages. The square tower, still standing, dominates the village and offers exceptional views of surrounding landscapes. Guided tours reveal the secrets of medieval defense architecture and daily life of lords. The castle gardens, redesigned according to period principles, showcase plants cultivated in the Middle Ages.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Walk the covered walkways and narrow calades that climb between golden stone houses. Each corner reveals new perspectives on surrounding landscapes: vineyards, forests, and hills unfolding to the horizon. Artisan workshops occupy vaulted cellars: pottery, painting, sculpture. The charming village has attracted many artists seeking authentic beauty.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Discover local wines at winegrowers cultivating hillsides around the village. The terroir of Golden Stones produces fruity and elegant wines, less known than great crus but of remarkable quality. Independent producers welcome visitors to explain their work and passion. Tastings take place in vaulted cellars dug into rock, creating an intimate and authentic atmosphere.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Visit artisan workshops perpetuating traditional know-how: pottery, weaving, painting on stone. Artists draw inspiration from surrounding landscapes and special light of Golden Stones. Some open their workshops for demonstrations and explanations of techniques used. Unique creations reflect the village's soul and beauty of local materials.
⏱️ 75 min
→
Hike the Sentier des Pierres Dorées circuit that crosses vineyards and hamlets. The 8 km marked trail offers spectacular views of the Azergues valley and Mont Blanc in the distance. The route passes through several golden stone villages, each with its own character. Information panels explain geological particularity of local limestone and its influence on architecture.
⏱️ 150 min

1
Villefranche-sur-Saône, economic capital of Beaujolais, charms with its Rue Nationale lined with Renaissance facades and inner courtyards. Founded in the 12th century by the Lords of Beaujeu, the town prospered through trade and viticulture. The arcaded houses, sculpted corbels, and hidden courtyards testify to past wealth. The 500-meter-long main street concentrates architectural treasures accumulated over centuries.
⏱️ 90 min
2
Notre-Dame des Marais Collegiate Church stands out with its impressive facade and 55-meter-high bell tower dominating the city. Built from the 13th to 15th centuries, it showcases the evolution of Gothic style. The ribbed vaults, stained glass windows, and carved capitals create a majestic interior space. The recently restored organ ranks among the finest in the region.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Municipal Museum Paul Dini houses an exceptional collection of modern and contemporary art. Works by Renoir, Dufy, Picasso, and local artists illustrate artistic movements of the 20th century. Temporary exhibitions showcase emerging artists and current trends. The building itself, a beautifully restored 18th-century mansion, is worth the visit.
⏱️ 75 min
4
The Covered Market, built in the 19th century, buzzes with activity every morning except Monday. The metal and glass structure shelters stalls overflowing with local products: Beaujolais wines, regional charcuterie, mountain cheeses, seasonal vegetables. Exchanges between producers and customers perpetuate the tradition of direct sales. The festive atmosphere and authentic smells create a total immersion in Beaujolais gastronomy.
⏱️ 90 min
🎯

Activities

→
Explore the inner courtyards hidden behind Renaissance facades of Rue Nationale. These secret spaces, once economic heart of the city, reveal spiral staircases, sculpted galleries, and ancient wells. Some courtyards have been magnificently restored and now house artisan shops and artist studios. Guided tours reveal the history of merchant families who built their fortunes here.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Stroll along the Saône on the newly developed waterfront. The shaded promenade offers views of the river and surrounding hills. Play areas, picnic areas, and flowered gardens invite rest. Swans and ducks enliven the peaceful waters. The path connects to walking trails extending along the riverbanks.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Visit during the Beaujolais Nouveau festival in November, when the city becomes the epicenter of worldwide celebrations. Producers set up stands offering tastings of the new vintage. Concerts, wine-related games, and fireworks animate the streets. The festive atmosphere attracts thousands of visitors who share a glass around street braziers.
⏱️ 180 min

Discover

1
Trévoux, former capital of the Principality of Dombes, unfolds its medieval heritage along the right bank of the Saône. The town knew its golden age under the Princes of Dombes who maintained an independent court and their own parliament from the 15th to 18th centuries. The castle perched on the rock, rampart vestiges, and narrow streets climbing the hillside testify to this prestigious past.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Château de Trévoux, partially restored, dominates the city from its rocky outcrop. The fortified enclosure, round towers, and keep evoke medieval architecture. The site houses a museum retracing the history of the Principality of Dombes through objects, documents, and reconstructions. The terrace offers panoramic views of the Saône, surrounding plains, and distant Alps.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The former Parliament of Dombes, built in the 17th century, served as the seat of sovereignty for this autonomous principality. The building, with its classical facade and period interior layout, hosts exhibitions on Dombes' judicial and political history. The wood-paneled council chambers and ancient courtrooms transport visitors into the 18th century. Guided tours reveal surprising anecdotes about this unique institution in France.
⏱️ 60 min
4
The Church of Saint-Symphorien, rebuilt in the 19th century in neo-Gothic style, houses an exceptional collection of religious furniture from demolished Dombes churches. Carved altarpieces, period statues, and golden reliquaries form a remarkable ensemble. The recently restored stained glass windows depict scenes from local religious history.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Walk the Heritage Trail that winds through old town. The marked route passes by Renaissance houses, ancient mansions, and medieval vestiges. Information panels reveal the history of the Principality of Dombes and daily life of its inhabitants. The climb to the castle offers breathtaking views of surrounding landscapes at each turn.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Cruise the Saône aboard a traditional gabarre, these flat-bottomed boats that once transported goods. The cruise offers a different perspective on the city and river landscapes. Skippers, passionate about local history, recount river anecdotes and evolution of river transport. The calm waters and preserved nature create a relaxing atmosphere.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Visit the Museum of Old Trévoux, housed in a beautiful 18th-century mansion. The collections illustrate daily life in the principality: period furniture, work tools, traditional costumes. A room dedicated to the famous Trévoux dictionary, published in the 18th century, explains the importance of this monumental work in French language history.
⏱️ 75 min

Discover

1
Old Lyon unfolds between the Saône and Fourvière hill, forming the largest Renaissance quarter in Europe after Venice. The traboules, these covered passages crossing buildings, allowed silk workers to transport fabrics sheltered from bad weather. The Renaissance facades, inner courtyards, and spiral staircases create a unique architectural ensemble listed as UNESCO World Heritage. Rue Saint-Jean and its shops, Place du Change and its old bank, testify to past commercial prosperity.
⏱️ 150 min
2
Fourvière Basilica, perched on its hill, dominates Lyon like a wedding cake. Built in the late 19th century in Romano-Byzantine style, it fascinates with golden mosaics, marble columns, and exceptional stained glass windows. The crypt, as richly decorated as the upper church, houses remarkable ex-votos. The esplanade offers breathtaking panoramic views of the entire city, from Croix-Rousse to Confluence.
⏱️ 90 min
3
The Gallo-Roman theaters of Fourvière, built in the 1st century BC, rank among the oldest in France. The great theater could accommodate 10,000 spectators, while the odeon served more intimate performances. Excavations revealed sophisticated stage sets and audience organization. Summer evening performances recreate ancient magic. The attached Lugdunum Museum presents exceptional collections of objects found on site.
⏱️ 120 min
4
The Croix-Rousse district, former stronghold of canuts (silk workers), climbs steep slopes north of the city center. Tall buildings with huge windows once housed weaving looms. Traboules allowed workers to go from workshop to market without climbing stairs. Today, the neighborhood vibrates with artists, craftsmen, and designers who perpetuate creative spirit. The Gros Caillou belvedere offers spectacular views of the city.
⏱️ 105 min
🎯

Activities

→
Discover Lyon's famous bouchons, these traditional restaurants serving Lyonnais gastronomy. Quenelles, pike perch, sausage with pistachios, tablier de sapeur accompany Beaujolais and Côtes du Rhône wines. Authentic bouchons, recognizable by their checkered tablecloths and regional dishes, perpetuate ancestral recipes. The warm atmosphere and hearty portions embody Lyonnais conviviality.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Visit Les Halles de Lyon Paul Bocuse, temple of local gastronomy where the greatest chefs source supplies. Fifty artisan vendors offer exceptional products: Bresse chickens, mountain cheeses, regional charcuterie, artisanal chocolates. The festive atmosphere and quality products make this visit an immersion into French culinary art. Tastings allow discovery of unique flavors.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Cruise the Rhône or Saône to discover Lyon from the water. The perspective on quays, bridges, and monuments completely changes the city's perception. Commentary reveals river history and evolution of neighborhoods. Lunch or dinner cruises offer a romantic experience combining gastronomy and sightseeing. Night cruises showcase illuminated buildings.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Ambérieu-en-Bugey marks the gateway to Bugey, the transition between Dombes plains and Jura mountains. The town developed around its strategic position at the crossroads of major roads and its railway node. The Saint-Germain church, with its Romanesque bell tower, watches over the old center. The nearby Château des Allymes, perched on a rocky outcrop, testifies to medieval military architecture with its square keep and defensive walls.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The Château des Allymes, built in the 14th century by the Dukes of Savoy, dominates the plain from its 820-meter perch. The fortified ensemble, remarkably preserved, illustrates defensive architecture of the late Middle Ages. The keep, ramparts, and towers allowed control of roads leading to Geneva and Lyon. Guided tours reveal daily life of garrison soldiers. The panoramic view extends from the Alps to Mont Blanc.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Walk through the old town where 19th-century houses bear witness to industrial prosperity brought by the railway. The railway station, built in 1856, marked a decisive turning point in urban development. The adjacent railway museum recounts this epic through period locomotives, tools, and documents. Model trains recreate the network's different eras.
⏱️ 60 min
4
The Albarine gorges, a few kilometers from town, offer spectacular landscapes where the river has carved its bed through limestone rock. Steep cliffs, caves, and natural cavities dot the course of this tumultuous torrent. Marked trails allow safe exploration while admiring geological formations. The site attracts climbers who train on vertical walls.
⏱️ 90 min

Taste Bugey wines at local producers cultivating terraced hillsides. This little-known appellation produces remarkable white, red, and rosé wines. Tastings reveal unexpected diversity of this mountain terroir.

Hike the Sentier des Crêtes which climbs to heights dominating the plain. The marked trail offers spectacular views of the Alps, Mont Blanc, and surrounding valleys. Visit the Tuesday morning market that animates the town center. Stalls overflow with local products: mountain cheeses, Bugey charcuterie, vegetables from market gardens, regional wines. Producers sell directly to customers, perpetuating personal relationship. The friendly atmosphere makes shopping a pleasant moment of exchange.

Discover

1
Hauteville-Lompnes unfolds on a plateau at 800 meters altitude, benefiting from an exceptional climate that earned it the nickname 'Little Switzerland.' The town developed in the 19th century thanks to tuberculosis sanatoria that attracted thousands of patients. Period architecture, large parks, and tree-lined avenues testify to this past. Today, medical institutions have been converted while preserving their heritage character.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The Church of Saint-Pierre, rebuilt in the 19th century, stands out with its imposing neo-Gothic facade. The interior houses remarkable stained glass windows and period religious furniture. The bell tower serves as a landmark visible from the entire plateau. The peaceful square invites contemplation while enjoying pure mountain air.
⏱️ 45 min
3
Walk through the Vallon park, a green oasis in the heart of town. Century-old trees, flowerbeds, and winding paths create a romantic atmosphere inherited from the sanatorium era. The arboretum presents rare species adapted to mountain climate. Children's play areas and picnic tables attract families. The park hosts outdoor concerts in summer.
⏱️ 60 min
4
The Plateau de Retord, accessible by small mountain roads, offers spectacular wild landscapes. Vast expanses of meadows alternate with dense forests. The plateau, at 1200 meters altitude, serves as a Nordic skiing area in winter. In summer, hikers discover intact nature and exceptional panoramas extending to Mont Blanc. Marmots and chamois can be observed with binoculars.
⏱️ 120 min
🎯

Activities

Cross-country ski on the Plateau de Retord, one of the finest Nordic domains in the region. Fifty kilometers of groomed trails wind through forests and clearings. All difficulty levels are represented, from gentle beginner loops to demanding courses for experts. Equipment rental and ski lessons are offered on site. The snowy landscapes and pure air create a magical atmosphere.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Hike the marked trails crossing Bugey mountains. The Crêt du Nu trail climbs to a belvedere offering 360-degree views of the Alps and Jura. The route passes through spruce forests, mountain pastures, and rocky passages. Diverse flora includes gentians, edelweiss, and rare orchids. The approximately 3-hour round trip is accessible to regular hikers.
⏱️ 150 min
→
Discover local gastronomy featuring mountain products. Restaurants serve comté cheese, smoked meats, blueberry tart, and Bugey wines. Some establishments perpetuate recipes from the sanatorium era when refined cuisine aided patient recovery. The warm atmosphere and hearty portions embody mountain conviviality.
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Bellegarde-sur-Valserine sits at the confluence of the Valserine and Rhône, at the foot of the Jura mountains. The town developed around its strategic position and water power that attracted industries. The Pertes de la Valserine, where the river disappears underground before resurfacing several hundred meters away, constitutes a unique geological phenomenon. The surrounding gorges and caves fascinate with their wild beauty.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Pertes de la Valserine form a spectacular natural site where the river plunges into a chasm before resurfacing downstream. The water has carved impressive gorges through limestone rock, creating caves, potholes, and underground galleries. Footbridges allow safe observation of this geological phenomenon. The roar of water and wild setting create a striking atmosphere. Educational panels explain the formation of these natural curiosities.
⏱️ 105 min
3
Fort l'Écluse, perched on a rocky outcrop overlooking the Rhône valley, controlled the road to Geneva for centuries. The fortification, built in the 17th century and redesigned by Vauban, illustrates French military architecture evolution. The double fort, upper and lower connected by an underground staircase of 666 steps, impresses with its scale. Guided tours reveal siege secrets and daily life of garrison soldiers.
⏱️ 120 min
4
Walk through the old town where 19th-century houses bear witness to industrial prosperity. The Valserine waterfalls, in the town center, once powered mills and factories. Today they offer a refreshing spectacle and a pleasant walking area. The renovated quays invite strolling along the tumultuous waters.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Practice canyoning in the spectacular gorges of the Valserine. Local guides lead groups through natural slides, waterfalls, and natural pools. The activity, suitable for all levels with different difficulty courses, allows spectacular discovery of the landscape. Wetsuits and helmets provided ensure safety and comfort. The refreshing experience and aquatic playground nature delight adventure seekers.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Explore the caves and underground galleries dotting the region. Guided tours reveal impressive stalactites and stalagmites, underground rivers, and mysterious chambers. Constant temperature and humidity create a unique atmosphere. Some caves require crawling through narrow passages, adding adventure to the visit. Professional guides ensure safety while explaining cave formation.
⏱️ 150 min
→
Hike the Crêt de la Neige trail, Jura's highest point at 1720 meters. The marked path climbs through forests before emerging on alpine meadows. The summit offers a breathtaking panorama: Mont Blanc, Geneva Lake, Alps as far as the eye can see. The approximately 5-hour round trip is accessible to regular hikers. Diverse flora includes rare alpine species.
⏱️ 240 min

Discover

1
Nantua nestles along the shores of a glacial lake enclosed in a mountain cirque. The town developed around its Benedictine abbey founded in the 8th century. The lake, 2.7 kilometers long and 650 meters wide, offers enchanting landscapes with mountains reflected in calm waters. Nantua also played a crucial role during the Resistance, serving as a refuge for many maquis fighters.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Abbey Church of Saint-Michel, rebuilt in the 19th century in neo-Romanesque style, stands out with its two asymmetrical bell towers. The interior houses an exceptional collection of religious art including a monumental wooden altarpiece and remarkable stained glass windows. The crypt preserves vestiges of the primitive abbey dating from the 8th century. The building's history intertwines with that of the town over twelve centuries.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The Resistance and Deportation Museum pays tribute to Bugey maquis fighters who fought Nazi occupation. The collections present period documents, weapons, personal objects, and moving testimonies. Reconstituted scenes recreate daily life of clandestine fighters in the mountains. A section is dedicated to local heroes deported to concentration camps. The visit, both educational and emotional, keeps alive the memory of these dark years.
⏱️ 90 min
4
Walk along the lake on the promenade offering spectacular views of surrounding mountains. The tree-lined path connects beaches, picnic areas, and belvederes. Lakeside cafés invite you to enjoy quenelles de Nantua, local specialty with crayfish sauce, while admiring the landscape. Children's play areas and pétanque courts attract families. The peaceful atmosphere and natural beauty make this walk a privileged moment.
⏱️ 105 min
🎯

Activities

→
Taste the famous quenelles de Nantua, this pike dumpling topped with crayfish sauce that made the town's culinary reputation. Local restaurants all offer their version of this emblematic dish. The original recipe, jealously guarded, dates back several centuries. Some establishments open their kitchens to show preparation of this technical dish. The delicate texture and rich sauce delight fine palates.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Swim or practice water sports on the lake. The supervised beaches offer safe swimming in summer. Canoe, kayak, and paddleboard rentals allow exploration of hidden coves. Fishing enthusiasts can catch pike, perch, and trout. Pedalos invite families to gentle cruises while admiring mountain landscapes. The clear, fresh water delights swimmers.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Hike the Tour du Lac trail, an 8 km loop offering varied perspectives on water and mountains. The marked path alternates between lakeside sections and wooded passages. Belvederes reveal panoramic views of the cirque and town. The gentle slopes make this walk accessible to everyone. Picnic areas invite pauses to fully enjoy the landscape.

Cerdon clings to steep hillsides where vines produce a sparkling rosé wine with the ancestral method. The village developed around viticulture and copper craftsmanship that made its reputation in the 19th century. Narrow streets climb between golden stone houses to the heights dominating the valley. The Cerdon caves, spectacular reculée where a river emerges from rock, offer breathtaking landscapes.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Cerdon caves form an impressive limestone cirque where the river Cuisance springs from the mountain. The 80-meter-high cliffs shelter numerous cavities explored by speleologists. A marked trail allows discovery of this geological site while remaining safe. Educational panels explain the formation of this reculée and prehistory of occupation. The refreshing setting and spectacular views attract hikers and climbers.
⏱️ 105 min
3
The Cerdon Wine Museum, housed in an old cellar, recounts the history of this unique sparkling wine. Traditional tools, old bottles, and period documents illustrate evolution of production methods. Videos explain the ancestral method giving Cerdon its characteristic pearls. Guided tastings allow appreciation of subtle aromas of this light rosé wine. The museum also recalls the importance of viticulture in local economy.
⏱️ 75 min
4
Walk through the old village where copper craftsmen workshops can still be seen. This ancestral know-how, transmitted for generations, produced renowned pieces: alembics, kettles, decorative objects. Some craftsmen perpetuate the tradition and open their doors for demonstrations. The hammering sound and copper shine fascinate visitors. Shops offer quality artisanal creations.
