Grand Tour of the French Alps: From the Thousand Ponds to Mont Blanc

Three weeks traversing medieval citadels, emerald lakes, and towering peaks from the Dombes marshlands to the roof of Europe.

≈ 22
Days
645

📖 12 min read 🔄 Last updated: 2026-01-19

From the bird-filled wetlands of the Dombes to the eternal snows of Mont Blanc, this 21-day journey unveils the French Alps in all their splendor. Discover medieval villages, pristine glacial lakes, legendary mountain passes, and world-class gastronomy on an epic road trip through Ain, Savoie, and Haute-Savoie.

  • Medieval Pérouges and its famous sugar galette
  • The thousand ponds of the Dombes and world-class birdwatching
  • Lake Annecy, Europe's purest lake, and its Venetian charm
  • Chamonix and the breathtaking Aiguille du Midi at 3,842m
  • Royal Monastery of Brou, masterpiece of Flamboyant Gothic
  • Yvoire, fortified jewel on Lake Geneva

Perouges

Medieval citadel frozen in time where cobblestones whisper centuries of history

8 photos
Day 1
🔍

Discover

1
The fortified village of Pérouges stands atop a hill, surrounded by ramparts pierced by two medieval gates. Over eighty buildings are classified as historic monuments.
2
Pass through the Upper Gate to enter the town. The fortress-church of Sainte-Marie-Madeleine integrates its outer wall into the ramparts, with the patrol path running at roof level.
3
The Rue des Rondes encircles the village and reveals the most beautiful mansions: General Messimy's house with mullioned windows, the salt storehouse, and the 15th-century Valensot house.
4
Explore the House of the Princes on the street of the same name. This 14th and 15th-century dwelling housed representatives of the Dukes of Savoy and now contains the Old Pérouges Museum.
+3 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Pick up the walking trail map at the tourist office for routes winding through the verdant surroundings of the medieval town.
→
Taste the famous Pérouges sugar galette, a specialty created in the early 20th century whose recipe remains jealously guarded by local artisans.
→
Enjoy a glass of Cerdon, the sparkling rosé wine from Bugey that perfectly accompanies the region's sweet specialties.

Land of a thousand ponds where 3,000 birds from around the world take flight

Day 2

Discover

The Bird Park of Villars-les-Dombes brings together over 3,000 birds of 300 different species in a green setting of 35 hectares. Immersive aviaries allow close encounters with the rarest species.

Walk the interpretation trail at Chapelier Pond where two ornithological observatories offer prime viewpoints for watching the wild birdlife of the Dombes.

Notre-Dame des Dombes Abbey stands with its Cistercian buildings amid the ponds. The monks' footpath invites a meditative stroll through this landscape of water and sky.
4
Discover the educational farm where farmyard animals mingle with cows and horses. Children participate in daily care and bread-making workshops.

Activities

Rent bikes and ride the Fairy of a Thousand Ponds circuit, winding between bodies of water where clouds are reflected.

Take off in a hot air balloon to contemplate from the sky this aquatic checkerboard dotted with farms with red-tiled roofs.
→
Try canicross hiking with sled dogs who guide you along the paths bordering the ponds.

Chatillon Sur Chalaronne

France's most bee-friendly town draped in flowers and half-timbered charm

3 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
Châtillon-sur-Chalaronne displays its red bricks and half-timbering in the heart of the Dombes. France's first bee-friendly town, it proudly wears its four flowers and labels as a blooming city.
2
The 15th-century Market Hall hosts every Saturday morning the local produce market voted Most Beautiful Market in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region. Stalls overflow with Bresse terroir products.
3
The apothecary of the former hospital preserves a unique collection of 120 Meillonnas faience jars with blue and white decorations. 18th-century woodwork frames this pharmaceutical treasure.
4
Pass through the Villars Gate, vestige of the fortified town, to reach the ramparts of the old castle that have dominated the town since the 12th century.
+3 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
The municipal Arboretum gathers 342 exceptional tree varieties. The educational apiary welcomes thousands of children each year to explain the essential role of bees.
→
Push open the doors of art workshops where stained glass makers, bookbinders, and ceramicists perpetuate traditional crafts. The town is labeled a City of Arts and Crafts.
→
Sit down at one of the restaurants to savor fresh frogs' legs, PDO Bresse chicken, and carp from the Dombes ponds.

Former principality capital where artisans keep ancient crafts alive along the Saône

3 photos
Day 4
🔍

Discover

1
Trévoux, former capital of the Principality of Dombes, raises its medieval towers above the Saône. The City of Art and History label crowns this exceptional heritage.

The Castle has dominated the town since the 14th century. Its octagonal tower offers a splendid panorama over the Saône valley and the Beaujolais hills.

The Parliament of Dombes sat here until the Revolution. The restored rooms evoke the independence of this principality that even minted its own currency.
4
The Carré Patrimoines houses the apothecary of the former hospital, a remarkable collection of faiences and ointment jars in their period woodwork setting.

The Voie Bleue follows the Saône for 700 km from Luxembourg to Lyon. Hop on a bike to ride the section through the town.
→
The family activity booklet transforms the visit into a treasure hunt for children ages 6 and up.

Bourg En Bresse

Gateway to Brou's Gothic splendor and birthplace of the legendary Bresse chicken

3 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
The Royal Monastery of Brou is a jewel of Flamboyant Gothic built in the early 16th century. Margaret of Austria had this complex constructed to house the tomb of her husband Philibert the Fair.
2
The church displays its glazed roof with multicolored tiles and three cloisters of exceptional delicacy. The sculpted tombs rank among the masterpieces of European funerary statuary.
3
The 1496 Hugon house illustrates medieval architecture with its stone shop on the ground floor and half-timbered upper floors where the master and his workers lived.
4
Notre-Dame Co-Cathedral impresses with its 190 years of construction. Its 23-bell carillon rings through the town while its Flamboyant Gothic style rivals Brou.
+3 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
Pick up the discovery booklet at the tourist office for a self-guided tour of Bourg-en-Bresse's heritage.
→
The Bouvent leisure center offers 56 hectares of relaxation three kilometers from the center: swimming, paddle boats, climbing, bike park, and golf course.
→
Sit down to savor PDO Bresse chicken, the only poultry in the world to hold a protected designation of origin.

Emerald lake haven where the legendary quenelle sauce was born

6 photos
Day 7
🔍

Discover

1
Nantua nestles in a spectacular mountain pass, hemmed between vertiginous limestone cliffs and its emerald-green glacial lake.

Saint-Michel Abbey Church is the most important Romanesque building in the Ain department. Elder sister of Cluny, it preserves a Delacroix painting depicting the Martyrdom of Saint Sebastian.

Sculpted marble angels keep watch in the Romanesque and Gothic choir. The classified organ resounds during concerts that enliven the thousand-year-old nave.

The Museum of Resistance and Deportation bears witness to the commitment of the Ain maquis. This memorial honors the freedom fighters.

The turquoise lake invites swimming, canoeing, and paddleboarding. The limpid waters reflect the limestone cliffs that frame it.
→
Taste authentic quenelles with Nantua sauce, a specialty born on the lake shores. Pike and red-clawed crayfish once made this recipe famous.
→
Each autumn, the Quenelle Festival celebrates this local specialty in a gourmet and festive atmosphere.

Day 9
🔍

Discover

Belley was the historic capital of Bugey before giving birth to Brillat-Savarin, father of French gastronomy. Ancient alleyways preserve the charm of an episcopal city.

Saint-Jean-Baptiste Cathedral displays its 19th-century neo-Gothic style. The richly frescoed interior houses an 18th-century Virgin statue miraculously spared by the Revolution.

The 16th-century Bailliage Hotel now houses the Tourist Office. Push the door to admire its stone staircases and coffered ceilings.

Lake Aiguebelette spreads its turquoise waters in a preserved mountain setting. Smallest of the great Alpine lakes, it offers the greatest tranquility as motorboats are banned.
