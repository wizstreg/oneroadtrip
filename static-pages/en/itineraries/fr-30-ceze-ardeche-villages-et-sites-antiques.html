CÈZE VALLEY: ANCIENT GORGES AND PERCHED MEDIEVAL VILLAGES

Limestone gorges plunging toward the river, medieval villages defying gravity, Roman ruins and prehistoric caves. The Cèze Valley, history distilled.

≈ 20

Explore Cèze Valley and its limestone terrain. Cross Montclus perched above the river, observe Vallon-Pont-d'Arc's natural arch, then anchor the journey at Uzès and Nîmes where Roman heritage structures the landscape.

  • Montclus: clifftop medieval perch
  • Vallon-Pont-d'Arc: prehistoric natural arch
  • Uzès: roman Renaissance grace town
  • Nîmes: amphitheater ancient splendor

Fall (September-October): Harvest season, comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds, wine regions spectacular, excellent photography, good value.

Winter (November-March): Christmas markets, ski season Alps, fewer crowds, lowest prices, cozy indoor attractions, cultural experiences.
