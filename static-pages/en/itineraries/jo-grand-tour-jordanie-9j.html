Jordan's Ancient Wonders: Petra, Wadi Rum Desert & Dead Sea Relaxation

Rose temples emerging from canyon shadows, rust-colored desert towers piercing infinite sky, weightless floating on the Dead Sea. Jordan, a journey of initiation.

Float in the Dead Sea, then follow the canyons to Petra and its red facade rising from the rock. Push on to Wadi Rum for a night under the stars, to the rhythm of Bedouin fires and sand. Between mineral water, ancient city and desert, Jordan offers a striking crossing of landscapes and stories.

  • Petra: rose treasury at dawn
  • Wadi Rum: Bedouin stars, sandstone towers
  • Dead Sea: float in mineral stillness
  • Aqaba: Red Sea coral warmth

❓ Frequently asked questions

What weather should you expect?

Spring (March-May): Perfect weather with comfortable temperatures (18-28°C), minimal rainfall, and ideal conditions for exploring archaeological sites and desert regions. Excellent balance of pleasant weather and moderate tourist numbers with wildflower blooms in desert areas.

Autumn (September-November): Excellent temperatures (20-30°C) with clear skies, perfect outdoor conditions, and comfortable hiking weather. Second-best season with beautiful weather for Petra visits, reasonable prices, excellent photography lighting.

Winter (December-February): Cool temperatures (8-18°C) with occasional rainfall, fewer crowds, and budget-friendly rates. Some sites may close early, but excellent for cultural exploration and avoiding summer heat.

Summer (June-August): Very hot temperatures (30-45°C) making outdoor activities challenging during midday hours. Early morning and evening activities recommended, highest accommodation rates, extreme heat limits desert exploration.

How many days should I plan?

We believe you will enjoy this itinerary best by dedicating between 8 and 12 days. The next step will allow you to adjust the duration of your stay.

What to discover in Jordan?

Ancient kingdom with Petra wonder, desert landscapes, and warm hospitality. Experience archaeological treasures, traditional Bedouin culture, and authentic Middle Eastern experiences. The country offers historical significance with natural beauty and cultural depth. Jordan provides life-changing historical experiences with cultural authenticity and natural wonders.
