Alpine lakes shimmer in impossible shades of turquoise, Mont Blanc pierces the clouds with majestic indifference, glacial faces gleam brilliant white. Haute-Savoie is Alpine perfection distilled.

Annecy

9.2 Le studio d'Alery - au coeur de la ville - 16Bis

You can't miss this iconic 12th-century building perched on an island in the Thiou Canal, shaped like a ship's prow. Once a prison, mint, and courthouse, this architectural gem now houses a museum chronicling Annecy's fascinating history and unique architecture.

Rising above the old town, this fortress of the Counts of Geneva has watched over Annecy since the 12th century. Wander through medieval towers, explore galleries showcasing regional art and lake archaeology, then climb the ramparts for breathtaking panoramas sweeping across the old town, shimmering lake, and Alpine peaks beyond.

Regarded as Europe's purest lake, this 27 km² Alpine jewel dazzles with crystalline turquoise waters. Born 18,000 years ago from melting glaciers, the lake mirrors the majestic mountains that embrace it—a postcard scene that shifts with every hour of daylight.

Crowning the hillside above town, this Romano-Byzantine basilica built between 1922 and 1949 houses the tombs of Saint Francis de Sales and Saint Jane de Chantal. The terrace rewards visitors with a spectacular 360° panorama embracing Annecy, the lake, and the surrounding Alpine summits.

→

Glide across Lake Annecy on a 1- or 2-hour cruise. From the water, you'll witness the mountains framing the lake in dramatic fashion, picturesque villages dotting the shoreline, and elegant estates gracing the water's edge.

→

The Lake Annecy Greenway offers 40 km of safe, scenic cycling with stunning lake views at every turn. The flat, easy route welcomes riders of all abilities—perfect for families and leisurely explorers alike.

→

Try stand-up paddleboarding, rent a canoe or kayak to explore hidden coves, or take your first sailing lesson. The lake's calm waters create ideal conditions for water sports of all kinds.

Clinging to the cliff face 150 meters above the lake, this 11th-century hermitage offers vertiginous views over Talloires Bay. The steep 40-minute trail rewards the climb with a perspective that captures why monks chose this dramatic aerie for contemplation.

Talloires boasts several lovely beaches along its pristine shores. The municipal beach offers magnificent views of Duingt Castle and the mountains beyond. The crystalline waters beckon for swimming in an idyllic Alpine setting.

Set in a 17th-century former convent in Thônes, this museum brings to life traditional Savoyard valley culture. Discover the fascinating story of Reblochon cheese, born in this valley during the 13th century and still crafted using ancestral methods.

→

Drive up to Col de la Forclaz (1,150 m), which commands the finest panorama over Lake Annecy. The viewpoint offers a bird's-eye perspective plunging down to Talloires Bay—the postcard view that launched a thousand photographs.

→

Visit one of the local fromageries to witness the artisanal production of Reblochon AOP cheese. Sample this creamy treasure straight from the cheesemaker—it's a world apart from supermarket versions.

→

The 3 km trail winds through forest before reaching spectacular 60-meter waterfalls. A suspension bridge brings you face-to-face with the cascading torrents—thrilling and photogenic in equal measure.

This spectacular Alpine valley stretches between the Bornes massif and the Aravis chain. Limestone peaks soaring beyond 2,700 meters form a dramatic natural amphitheater—raw Alpine grandeur at its finest.

As the Reblochon capital, Thônes has preserved its traditional Savoyard market town character. Wander the historic center's narrow lanes and visit the 18th-century baroque Church of Saint-Hippolyte with its ornate interior.

This small neo-Gothic chapel from 1861 stands in magnificent Alpine surroundings at the hamlet of Les Confins. It marks the trailhead for numerous mountain hikes into pristine wilderness.

→

Hike from Les Confins hamlet to this charming mountain lake nestled at 1,430 meters. The easy 2 km trail meanders through flower-studded meadows and spruce forests—quintessential Alpine scenery without the crowds.

→

The Lake Annecy Golf Club offers an exceptional 18-hole course with stunning views across the lake to the mountains—where the challenge of the game meets breathtaking Alpine beauty.

La Clusaz

+2
Top hotels

A renowned ski resort that's retained its authentic mountain village soul, La Clusaz charms with its cluster of traditional carved-wood chalets and excellent gastronomic restaurants. This is Alpine living at its most genuine.

This 18th-century baroque church dominates the village center with its imposing onion-domed bell tower covered in wooden shingles. Inside, discover a gilded altarpiece and intricately carved woodwork that showcase Savoyard craftsmanship.

→

Ride the Beauregard cable car up to 1,800 meters. Multiple trails unfold from the summit, offering exceptional panoramas of the Aravis chain and Mont Blanc visible to the north—the Alps spread before you in all their glory.

→

Settle into one of the village restaurants for farm-fresh Reblochon fondue, authentic tartiflette, or diots sausages in white wine. Mountain farms produce Reblochon AOP right up in the high pastures—cheese that truly tastes of the Alps.

→

Ascend to the top of the Aiguille cable car to reach the highest point at 2,400 meters. The view sweeps across the entire Aravis chain and the Mont Blanc massif—a perspective that captures the full majesty of the French Alps.

Megeve

+2
Top hotels

Accessible by gondola, this calvary stands at 1,840 meters on Mont d'Arbois. It offers an exceptional 360° viewpoint over the Mont Blanc massif, where you can clearly distinguish the summit soaring to 4,809 meters—Western Europe's rooftop spread before your eyes.

The chicest resort in the French Alps, Megève has preserved its authentic Savoyard village character. The center clusters with superb wooden chalets sporting flower-laden balconies, while excellent gastronomic restaurants serve Alpine cuisine elevated to an art form.

→

Explore the flower-studded Alpine pastures surrounding Megève. Gentle trails wind through meadows and forests up to working mountain farms. Stop at the farmsteads to taste Beaufort, Tomme de Savoie, and Reblochon where they're made—cheese with a view.

→

Stroll the cobbled lanes lined with elegant boutiques and discover Savoyard specialties in the gourmet food shops: mountain charcuterie, aged Alpine cheeses, and artisan honey that captures the flavor of wildflower meadows.

Chamonix Mont Blanc

+2
Top hotels
9 Heliopic Hotel & Spa
9.3 Chalet-Hôtel Hermitage
9.1 Grand Paradis
9.1 MGM Hôtels & Résidences - Résidence Le Cristal de Jade

Board the cable car holding the world record for the highest vertical ascent: 2,807 meters in just 20 minutes. The summit reaches 3,842 meters with a 360° panorama over the Mont Blanc massif. Don't miss the Step Into the Void—a glass box suspended more than 1,000 meters above the abyss that tests your nerves and your Instagram game.

Wander the lively streets of Chamonix, the world capital of mountaineering. Visit the Saint-Michel church and admire the Belle Époque villas that recall the town's pioneering days. The Alpine museum traces the dramatic history of summit conquests that made this valley legendary.

The cable car's intermediate station at 2,310 meters, Plan de l'Aiguille serves as the jumping-off point for numerous summer hikes. This plateau offers unobstructed views of Chamonix's needle-sharp granite spires—the peaks that launched modern alpinism.

→

Experience a once-in-a-lifetime journey on this legendary cable car. The ascent unfolds in two stages: Plan de l'Aiguille, then the spectacular section that climbs the sheer north face. At the summit, explore the panoramic terraces and brave the Step Into the Void if you dare.

→

For experienced hikers, the Grand Balcon Nord trail links Plan de l'Aiguille to Montenvers station. The path traverses high above the valley beneath the razor-sharp granite needles that make Chamonix famous among climbers worldwide.

→

Explore the historic center, browse world-class mountaineering equipment shops, and soak up the unique atmosphere of a town where alpinists from every corner of the globe converge to test themselves against legendary peaks.

Samoens

Reach the Montenvers site aboard the little red cog railway in service since 1909. At 1,913 meters altitude, discover the Mer de Glace—the largest glacier in the French Alps, stretching 7 kilometers down the valley. Visit the Ice Grotto accessible via more than 400 steps carved into the glacier itself.

Discover Samoëns, a charming village classified among France's most beautiful flowered villages. The heart centers around the Place du Gros Tilleul, where a massive linden tree planted in 1438 still provides shade. Admire the Church of Notre-Dame-de-l'Assomption, completed in 1555—a monument to centuries of village devotion.

Explore this exceptional 3.7-hectare Alpine botanical garden, classified as a Remarkable Garden of France. Gifted in 1906 to her native village by Marie-Louise Cognacq-Jaÿ, the garden showcases over 2,500 mountain plant species along 1.5 km of winding trails. The Château Chapel offers panoramic views over the Giffre Valley.

→

Board the historic red train that climbs from Chamonix to the Montenvers site facing the Mer de Glace. The narrated journey reveals the dramatic history of mountaineering that transformed this valley—from 19th-century pioneers to modern alpinists.

→

Descend the metal staircase (more than 400 steps) to access the grotto carved fresh each year into the glacier. Marvel at the ice sculptures and translucent blue walls—a magical world deep within the living glacier.

→

Wander this unique Alpine garden where every sense awakens. The serpentine path leads through meticulously recreated Alpine ecosystems—from rocky outcrops to lush meadows—revealing how mountain plants adapt to harsh conditions with breathtaking beauty.

Evian Les Bains

+2
Top hotels
9.3 Hôtel Royal
9.5 Royal Park lake view apartment
9 L'Oasis
9 Appartement de charme sur les Hauts d'Evian

Explore this spectacular natural amphitheater in the Sixt-Fer-à-Cheval nature reserve. The cirque forms a 4-5 kilometer horseshoe with 500-700 meter vertical walls. More than thirty waterfalls cascade from its cliffs—particularly dramatic in spring when snowmelt sends torrents plunging down the rock faces.

Visit the legendary spring discovered in 1789 that launched Évian's worldwide fame. The magnificent Art Nouveau Cachat Pavilion, built in 1905, crowns the site with its glazed tile dome. The water flows continuously at a constant 52.9°F (11.6°C)—the pure Alpine water that became an international icon.

Marvel at this sumptuous building constructed in 1902 as a thermal spa. The former spa transformed into a cultural center in 2007 still impresses with its Belle Époque architecture—especially the immense glass dome soaring more than 30 meters high, flooding the interior with Alpine light.

Discover the former summer residence of the Lumière family, inventors of cinema, which became Évian's Town Hall in 1927. Rich in woodwork, bronze, and marquetry, the building can be visited freely during office hours—a glimpse into Belle Époque elegance.

→

Follow the family-friendly trail that climbs the valley floor from the parking area to the Bout du Monde (End of the World). This virtually flat walk traverses grandiose scenery beneath vertiginous walls and multiple cascading waterfalls—nature's drama on an epic scale.

→

Stroll the flower-lined lakefront promenade stretching nearly 3 kilometers along Lake Geneva's shores. Manicured gardens, fountains, and a music kiosk dot the route. The sunsets paint the lake and mountains in impossible colors—pure Alpine magic.

→

Ride the historic funicular, free to use and in service since 1907. This gem of railway heritage links the town center to the heights over 750 meters of track. At the upper station, you can visit the historic engine room—Victorian engineering still working perfectly.

Yvoire

9.5 L'Appart-MoH Yvoire

Explore this fortified medieval village founded in the early 14th century by Count Amadeus V of Savoy. The walled town has preserved more than 700 years of history intact—ramparts, fortified gates, and cobbled lanes cascading with geraniums. Classified among France's Most Beautiful Villages.

Admire the rectangular keep that has guarded the peninsula since the 14th century. Built between 1306 and 1311, the castle's mission was to monitor Lake Geneva's strategic waters. Owned by the d'Yvoire family since 1655, it remains a private residence and cannot be toured—but its imposing exterior dominates every photo.

Enter this unique 3.7-hectare garden classified as Remarkable by France's Ministry of Culture. Open to the public since 1988, this garden celebrates plants through the five senses in a labyrinth inspired by medieval monastery gardens. More than 1,500 plant varieties create a living tapestry of color, texture, and fragrance.

Stroll the picturesque harbor where boats of the Compagnie Générale de Navigation depart for Swiss shores. The port offers magnificent views of the castle, flower-draped ramparts, and the lake with the Alps providing a majestic backdrop—postcard Savoy in every direction.

→

Wander this garden-labyrinth where every sense awakens. Touch the soft textures of foliage, breathe in the perfume of roses and aromatic herbs, marvel at the colorful compositions. Children receive a discovery quiz to guide their sensory exploration.

→

Lose yourself in the maze of cobbled lanes draped with geraniums, admire the architectural details of stone houses, pass through fortified gates. Every corner reveals another photogenic perspective—medieval beauty preserved in amber.

→

Embark on a Lake Geneva cruise from Yvoire harbor. Several options: crossing to Switzerland, a circuit of the Petit Lac, or a narrated cruise along French and Swiss shores—a perspective on the lake that reveals why humans have prized these waters for millennia.
