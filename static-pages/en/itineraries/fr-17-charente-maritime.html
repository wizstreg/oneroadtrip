Charente-Maritime Road Trip: La Rochelle, Île de Ré, Fort Boyard and Roman Saintonge

Salt crystallizes in the marshes, oysters mature in the claires, medieval towers guard the harbor entrance. Charente-Maritime lives to the rhythm of the tides.

From La Rochelle's medieval towers to Île de Ré's Vauban ramparts, from Rochefort's Royal Rope Factory to Oléron's oyster shacks. Charente-Maritime unfolds its coastline between sleeping citadels, iconic lighthouses and villages perched above the estuary. A journey between maritime heritage and Atlantic gentleness.

  • La Rochelle: the three towers of the Old Port
  • Île de Ré: UNESCO Vauban fortifications and Baleines lighthouse
  • Rochefort: Royal Rope Factory and Hermione frigate
  • Oléron: oysters and Fort Boyard views
  • Talmont-sur-Gironde: Romanesque church on the cliff
  • Saintes: amphitheater and Arch of Germanicus

Discover

1
The three towers of the Old Port are remnants of La Rochelle's medieval fortifications. The Saint-Nicolas Tower (14th century), slightly tilted like Pisa, rises to 37 meters. A chain once linked it to the Chain Tower to close the harbor each evening – the original chain is visible at the tower's base.
⏱️ 75 min
2
The Lantern Tower, with its 55-meter octagonal Gothic spire, served as both lighthouse and prison. Some 600 graffiti carved by English, Spanish and Dutch corsairs adorn its walls – ships, figures, and even a locomotive. Rabelais mentions it in his Fifth Book.
⏱️ 45 min
3
La Rochelle's Old Port was the largest port on the Atlantic coast in the 15th century. Stroll along Cours des Dames and Quai Duperré, lined with café terraces. In the evening, the illuminated towers reflect in the basin – one of the most beautiful scenes on the French coast.
⏱️ 45 min
4
The arcades of the city center have sheltered passersby since the Middle Ages. Over 3 km of covered galleries line the shopping streets. Under stone and wooden vaults, half-timbered houses and Renaissance mansions tell the story of the wealthy Rochelais shipowners.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Visit of La Rochelle's three towers. A single ticket grants access to all three monuments. Climbing to the top of Saint-Nicolas Tower offers a complete panorama over the port and roadstead. Allow 2 hours for the full visit.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Walk along the rampart walkway between the Chain Tower and Lantern Tower. This section of rampart, called 'Rue sur les Murs', overlooks the Saint-Jean-d'Acre esplanade and offers stunning views of the port.
⏱️ 30 min
→
La Rochelle central market. Under the wrought iron and wooden halls, producers sell Marennes-Oléron oysters, Pineau des Charentes, mogettes (white beans) and tourteau fromager. Lively atmosphere every morning.
⏱️ 45 min

Discover

1
La Rochelle Aquarium is one of the largest private aquariums in Europe. Over 12,000 marine animals in 3 million liters of water. The shark tank and jellyfish gallery are spectacular. Allow 2 to 3 hours for the visit.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The New World Museum occupies Hôtel Fleuriau, an 18th-century shipowner's mansion. Collections on the triangular trade, the French West Indies and the slave trade. An unflinching look at La Rochelle's wealth built on slavery.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Gabut district, a former bastion converted into a colorful village. Brightly painted wooden houses, artists' studios, terraces facing the marina. An unexpected Scandinavian atmosphere on the Atlantic shore.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
The Sea Bus and the Ferry connect both sides of the port. A 5-minute crossing, a pleasant alternative to walking around. The electric ferry operates year-round.
⏱️ 15 min
→
Concurrence Beach, the closest to the city center. Fine sand, calm water, view of the towers. Lifeguarded in summer. The Mail promenade lined with tamarisk trees runs along the coast toward Les Minimes.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Yellow bike rental (Yélo) self-service. La Rochelle pioneered bike-sharing in France. Cycle paths lead to Les Minimes beaches and the marina.

Saint Martin De Re

2 photos
Day 3
🔍

Discover

1
The Île de Ré bridge, 2.9 km long, has connected the mainland to the island since 1988. Toll on entry (free on exit). The crossing offers a first view of the salt marshes and the black and white bell tower of Ars-en-Ré.
⏱️ 10 min
2
The fortifications of Saint-Martin-de-Ré, built by Vauban between 1681 and 1691, form a 14 km star-shaped rampart. UNESCO World Heritage since 2008, they could shelter the entire island population during an English invasion. Walking or cycling the ramparts is spectacular.
⏱️ 90 min
3
The port of Saint-Martin-de-Ré, nestled within the fortified walls, has quays lined with terraces. Convicts bound for Cayenne departed from here – the citadel served as a depot. Climb the church tower for a panoramic view of the island.
⏱️ 45 min
4
The Baleines Lighthouse, at the western tip of the island, stands 60 meters tall. 257 steps lead to the top – 360° view over the ocean and island. The name comes from whales that once stranded on this coast. The old Vauban tower (1682) and a lighthouse museum complete the site.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Île de Ré by bike: 110 km of cycle paths connect the island's ten villages. Rentals available in every village. The Saint-Martin → Baleines Lighthouse route (15 km) crosses salt marshes and pine forests.
⏱️ 180 min
→
Visit the salt marshes of Loix or Ars-en-Ré. Salt workers harvest fleur de sel by hand using age-old techniques. Guided walks in the marshes, explanations of the work and tastings.
⏱️ 90 min💡 Book guided tours in summer
→
Île de Ré beaches: fine sand on the south coast (Bois-Plage, La Couarde), wilder to the north. Fish traps, semi-circular stone walls, catch fish at low tide – an ancestral technique still practiced.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
The Royal Rope Factory of Rochefort, built between 1666 and 1669, measures 374 meters long – more than the height of the Eiffel Tower. This manufactory supplied ropes to the Royal Navy for two centuries. The International Sea Center presents the arsenal's history and seamanship techniques.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Hermione, replica of the frigate that took La Fayette to America in 1780, was rebuilt over 17 years in the Louis XV dry dock. The project brought together traditional ship carpentry skills. Visit the ship with explanations about life for the 300-man crew.
⏱️ 75 min
3
The National Naval Museum occupies Hôtel de Cheusses, Rochefort's oldest civilian building. Ship models built at the arsenal, navigation instruments, figureheads. The arsenal produced 550 ships between 1666 and 1927, including France's first submarine (Le Plongeur, 1863).
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Accro-mâts, in the Louis XV dry dock, recreates the Hermione's masts. A treetop adventure course at 30 meters height to experience the sensations of 18th-century topmen. Spectacular final zip line.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Stroll through the Arsenal gardens: the Garden of Returns, the Navy Garden and the King's vegetable garden. Palm trees, Virginia tulip trees, begonias – plants brought back from maritime expeditions. Peace and shade facing the Charente.
⏱️ 45 min
→
The Rochefort transporter bridge (1900), the last of its kind in France, connected both banks of the Charente by a suspended gondola. Listed as a Historic Monument in 1976, restored between 2016 and 2020. Gondola crossings available.
⏱️ 30 min

Marennes

2 photos
Day 5
🔍

Discover

1
Brouage, a citadel asleep in the marshes. Founded in 1555, it was Europe's leading salt port before silting up. Richelieu made it an impregnable stronghold between 1628 and 1633 under Pierre d'Argencourt, then Vauban reinforced the fortifications in 1685. Samuel de Champlain, founder of Quebec, was born here around 1570. Today, 500 inhabitants and an uncanny silence.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The Brouage rampart walk (2 km) offers views over former salt marshes turned bird reserve. Black-winged stilts, egrets and spoonbills nest here. The powder magazines, forge and restored church can be visited.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Marennes, oyster capital. The Marennes-Oléron basin produces 45% of French oysters. The Saint-Pierre church bell tower (85 m) is the tallest in Charente-Maritime. The Oyster City explains aging in 'claires', those brackish water pools that green and refine the oysters.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Visit an oyster shack. Producers open their establishments to explain farming and aging. Tasting on site with views of the claires – a glass of local white accompanies the oysters.
⏱️ 75 min💡 Prefer months with an 'R' for tasting
→
Walk in the Brouage marshes. Marked trails through former salt marshes. Binoculars recommended for watching migratory birds. The sunset over the pools is memorable.
⏱️ 60 min

Saint Trojan Les Bains

2 photos
Day 6
🔍

Discover

1
Île d'Oléron, the largest French Atlantic island (30 km long). The free viaduct (1966, 3 km) links it to the mainland. Varied landscapes: pine forests in the south, salt marshes in the north, fine sand beaches on the west coast.
⏱️ 30 min
2
The Chassiron lighthouse, at the northern tip of the island, has worn its black and white stripes since 1836. 224 steps to the lantern – views of Fort Boyard and Île d'Aix on clear days. The spiral garden at the lighthouse's base echoes oyster shell patterns.
⏱️ 60 min
3
Le Château d'Oléron, former stronghold at the island's entrance. The Vauban citadel guarded the estuary. Today, its ramparts house an artisan and local produce market (summer). The colorful port cabins have become artists' studios.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Fort Boyard, oval fortress built between 1801 and 1857 between Oléron and Île d'Aix. Never used militarily, it became famous through the TV game show. No visits inside, but cruises circle it – an iconic silhouette set on the ocean.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Cruise around Fort Boyard. Departures from Boyardville or Saint-Denis-d'Oléron. Full circuit of the fort, passing near Île d'Aix. Commentary on construction history and filming anecdotes.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Oyster tasting at La Cotinière port, the leading artisanal fishing port in Charente-Maritime and France's sixth largest. Auction in the morning, tasting shacks in the afternoon. Oléron's 'fines de claire' and 'spéciales' oysters have an incomparable briny flavor.
⏱️ 60 min
→
Île d'Oléron beaches: Grande Plage at Saint-Trojan, Les Saumonards at Boyardville, Vert-Bois in the south. Maritime pine forests stretch to the sand. The little Saint-Trojan train crosses the forest to Gatseau beach.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Royan, a seaside resort rebuilt after the 1945 bombings. The 1950s architecture, called 'Les Cinquante Glorieuses', now holds the Ville d'Art et d'Histoire label. Modernist villas, curved seafronts, luminous concrete.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Notre-Dame Church of Royan (1958), a brutalist concrete masterpiece by Guillaume Gillet. A 45-meter elliptical nave, inverted V-shaped vault, abstract stained glass. Controversial at its construction, it is now a listed Historic Monument.
⏱️ 30 min
3
Royan central market, a concrete and glass structure by Louis Simon and André Morisseau (1956). Undulating dome 52 meters in diameter, spectacular natural light. Seafood, orchard fruits, Charentes specialties. One of the finest covered markets in France.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Royan's seafront stretches across 5 beaches: Grande Conche, Foncillon, Chay, Pigeonnier, Pontaillac. Promenade along modernist villas, casino, congress center. Elegant seaside atmosphere facing the Gironde estuary.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
Guided tour 'Royan 1950s'. The tourist office offers architectural tours with commentary: the Parc district villas, congress center, Protestant temple, bus station. A fresh look at 20th-century heritage.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Grande Conche beach, 2 km of fine sand in an arc. Lifeguarded in summer, surf and sailing schools, terraces facing the ocean. The Cordouan lighthouse, 'Versailles of the sea', stands on the estuary horizon.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Royan-Le Verdon ferry. The 20-minute Gironde estuary crossing offers a unique view of both the Charente and Médoc coasts. Option to continue to Cordouan lighthouse at low tide (guided excursion).
⏱️ 90 min

Discover

1
Talmont-sur-Gironde, a village of 100 inhabitants perched on a rocky promontory. Listed as one of France's Most Beautiful Villages, it overlooks the Gironde estuary. Lanes lined with hollyhocks, white houses with blue shutters, artisan shops.
⏱️ 60 min
2
Sainte-Radegonde Church of Talmont (12th century), Romanesque Saintonge style, stands at the cliff edge. Its apse overlooks the estuary – the sea has eaten away the cemetery. Sculpted facade, single nave, golden light. One of the most photographed churches in France.
⏱️ 30 min
3
The carrelets of Talmont, fishing huts on stilts equipped with square nets. A traditional estuary technique for catching shrimp, eels and mullet. Some can be rented overnight – a unique sunset experience.
⏱️ 30 min
🎯

Activities

→
Talmont market (summer). In the village lanes, local producers and artisans. Pineau, cognac, jams, soaps. Festive atmosphere on summer evenings with concerts on the square.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Talmont cliff trail. 3 km walk along the estuary, between carrelets and vineyards. View of the Meschers cliffs and their troglodyte caves on the other side of the Gironde.
⏱️ 60 min

Discover

1
The Arch of Germanicus (19 AD), a two-bay Roman arch that marked the entrance to the bridge over the Charente. Moved stone by stone in 1843 to escape demolition. Dedicatory inscriptions to Tiberius, Germanicus and Drusus Caesar are readable on the attic.
⏱️ 20 min
2
The Gallo-Roman amphitheater of Saintes (1st century) could hold 15,000 spectators. Built into a hillside valley, it retains its tiers, vomitoria and elliptical arena. Summer concerts and shows in this ancient setting.
⏱️ 45 min
3
The Abbaye aux Dames (11th-12th century), the first women's monastery in Saintonge. Romanesque facade sculpted with grimacing heads and fantastic animals. Conical bell tower covered in stone scales. Now a cultural center and home to an early music festival.
⏱️ 45 min
4
Saintes old town stretches between the Charente and the hillsides. Saint-Pierre Cathedral (rebuilt after the Wars of Religion), 18th-century mansions, medieval lanes. Wrought iron halls for the market.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Guided tour of Gallo-Roman Saintes. The tourist office offers a complete circuit: Arch of Germanicus, Saint-Saloine baths, amphitheater, archaeological museum. Mediolanum Santonum was capital of the Aquitaine province.
⏱️ 120 min
→
Cruise on the Charente departing from Saintes. Traditional gabares or electric boats head upriver toward Cognac. Meadow landscapes, weeping willows, grey herons. Commentary on the history of cognac river transport.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Pineau and cognac tasting. Regional producers open their cellars for guided visits. Pineau des Charentes, a blend of grape must and cognac, is enjoyed chilled as an aperitif.
⏱️ 60 min
