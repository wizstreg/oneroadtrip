Haute-Garonne Road Trip: From Toulouse to the Pyrenees

Between pink brick and white stone, between the Garonne and the Pyrenees, Haute-Garonne unfolds its treasures: monumental cathedrals, fortified villages, and thermal spa towns.

Journey from Toulouse, the pink city, to the Pyrenees through medieval villages of the Gers. An itinerary blending UNESCO heritage, Gascon gastronomy, and Pyrenean peaks, from Saint-Bertrand-de-Comminges to the thermal baths of Luchon.

  • Toulouse: Romanesque basilica and Cité de l'Espace
  • Albi: Gothic cathedral with 18,500 m² of frescoes
  • Larressingle: 13th-century medieval fortress
  • Saint-Bertrand: medieval jewel on its rocky pinnacle

Discover

1
Place du Capitole has been Toulouse's beating heart since the 12th century. Its 135-meter neoclassical façade houses both city hall and the theater. The Occitan cross inlaid in marble on the ground celebrates the region's distinct identity. Step inside the Capitole for free to discover the Salle des Illustres, where monumental frescoes chronicle Toulouse's storied past across soaring walls.
⏱️ 75 min
2
Basilique Saint-Sernin stands as the largest intact Romanesque church in all of Europe. Built between the 11th and 13th centuries, this UNESCO World Heritage site served pilgrims walking the Way of Saint James. Its octagonal bell tower soars 64 meters above the city. Inside, carved capitals and a vast ambulatory still echo with the footsteps of medieval travelers.
⏱️ 90 min
3
The Couvent des Jacobins dates from the 13th century. Its southern Gothic architecture culminates in the Palm of Stone—a 28-meter column whose ribbed vaults fan out like a tree beneath the ceiling, one of medieval architecture's most stunning achievements. The peaceful cloister invites contemplation. Between 11am and 3pm, sunlight streams through stained glass, illuminating painted walls in shifting colors.
⏱️ 90 min
4
La Cité de l'Espace plunges you into the universe of space exploration. A full-scale Ariane 5 rocket commands attention at the entrance. Inside, the Planetarium and IMAX theater transport you to the heart of the cosmos. Interactive exhibits explain actual space missions and let you touch real meteorites brought back from beyond Earth.
⏱️ 180 min
+1 Places visited
🎯

Activities

→
The quays along the Garonne make for a lovely riverside walk. The 17th-century Pont Neuf is Toulouse's oldest bridge. At night, it lights up beautifully, and the view from the quays becomes magical as pink brick buildings reflect in the water. Walk or cycle while watching the city glow.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Marché Victor Hugo opens every morning except Monday. This covered market is a temple of Toulouse gastronomy where the region's finest producers gather. You'll find foie gras, cassoulet, local wines, and artisan cheeses. Upstairs, restaurants let you taste everything on the spot.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Try cassoulet, THE dish of Toulouse. This slow-cooked ragout of white beans, duck confit, and Toulouse sausages simmers for hours. It's a hearty celebration of Gascon tradition. Restaurants throughout the center offer their own recipes, each slightly different. Pair it with a Fronton or Gaillac wine.
⏱️ 120 min

Discover

1
Cathédrale Sainte-Cécile in Albi is the world's largest brick cathedral. Construction began in 1282, and it rises like a fortress with massive, imposing walls. Step inside and the contrast stuns: 18,500 square meters of frescoes make it Europe's largest painted cathedral. The deep blue vault dates from 1509-1512. The Last Judgment from the 15th century covers more than 200 square meters. The classical organ is one of France's grandest.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The Palais de la Berbie, the former bishops' palace, houses the Toulouse-Lautrec Museum. This is the world's largest collection of works by the artist born in Albi in 1864. You'll see his famous Moulin Rouge posters, paintings of Parisian nightlife, and intimate portraits. The museum holds over 1,000 works.
⏱️ 90 min
3
Collégiale Saint-Salvi is Albi's oldest church. Its 13th-century Romanesque cloister offers peaceful refuge. The church itself blends Romanesque and Gothic styles. The 12th-century bell tower leans slightly, lending it character and charm.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
The gardens of Palais de la Berbie descend in terraces to the Tarn River. The view over the Pont Vieux and old town is magnificent, especially at sunset. The formal French gardens are beautifully maintained and offer a quiet place to rest.
⏱️ 60 min

Wander through the UNESCO-listed episcopal city. Cobbled lanes, half-timbered houses, and brick buildings create a unique atmosphere. The 11th-century Pont Vieux lets you cross the Tarn while enjoying beautiful views of the cathedral rising above the city.

1
Larressingle is France's smallest fortified village, nicknamed the little Carcassonne of the Gers. This 13th-century fortress is surrounded by 270 meters of ramparts with eight towers. The bridge replaced the original drawbridge. Inside, a few medieval houses nestle against the walls. The bishops' castle of Condom (private) and the Romanesque church of Saint-Sigismond complete this remarkably preserved village.
⏱️ 90 min
2
The fortified Romanesque church dates from the 12th century. It has a peculiarity: it houses a statue representing Saint Sigismund that is actually a reduced copy of the Vercingetorix statue from Alise-Sainte-Reine. An ordering mistake by the 19th-century priest that makes the story amusing.
⏱️ 45 min
🎯

Activities

→
The medieval siege camp sits just outside the fortified town. From April to October, you can see and operate medieval war machines: catapults, trebuchets, and ballistas. Guides explain how they work and let you fire them. It's fun and educational for all ages.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Walk around the ramparts. The dry moats, crenellated towers, and arrow slits are clearly visible from outside. The village is quickly explored but the medieval atmosphere is genuinely preserved.

Discover

1
Sainte-Marie Cathedral in Auch is a UNESCO World Heritage site. This Flamboyant Gothic cathedral built between 1489 and 1548 measures 102 meters long. The 18 stained glass windows by Arnaud de Moles (1507-1513) rank among the finest of the French Renaissance. The 113 oak choir stalls (1510-1552) present over 1,500 carved figures. Two monumental organs complete this exceptional ensemble.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The monumental staircase completed in 1863 links the upper and lower towns across 35 meters of elevation. Its 374 steps (234 in the central flight plus double flights) alternate with terraces and fountains. On the first landing, Jaume Plensa's contemporary sculpture Observatory of Time evokes the 1977 flood. Lower down, the statue of d'Artagnan watches over the Gers valley. On clear days, the Pyrenees form a backdrop on the horizon.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The Jacobins Museum presents Gallo-Roman archaeological collections, medieval artifacts, and sacred art. Egyptian antiquities and pre-Columbian art are also on display. The former Dominican convent housing it is a fine example of medieval architecture.
⏱️ 75 min
🎯

Activities

→
Descend the monumental staircase to the lower town. Each landing offers a different view over the valley. The central fountain and terraced gardens bordered by vines create a pleasant atmosphere.
⏱️ 45 min
→
Thursday morning market spreads across several squares in the town center. It's one of the largest markets in the Gers with poultry, cheeses, vegetables, and Gascon specialties.
⏱️ 90 min
→
Wander the medieval lanes of the upper town. Rue Dessoles is the most commercial with its shops and restaurants. Half-timbered houses and private mansions testify to the past as capital of Gascony.

Discover

1
Sainte-Marie Cathedral dominates Saint-Bertrand from its rocky pinnacle. Built from the 11th century and transformed through the 16th, it blends Romanesque and Gothic. The 66 oak choir stalls (16th century) feature remarkable sculptures. The monumental organ, 16 meters high, is one of Europe's finest. It rests on five columns and its case depicts the labors of Hercules. The treasury displays embroidered copes gifted by Pope Clement V.
⏱️ 120 min
2
The 12th-century cloister has a unique feature: openings to the surrounding landscapes created in the early 20th century. This Romanesque gallery offers views of mountains and valley. At the center, the Evangelists' Pillar bears zodiacal sculptures on a Roman column recovered from the ancient site.
⏱️ 60 min
3
The ancient site of Lugdunum Convenarum extends across the plain. This prosperous Roman city had 30,000 inhabitants. You'll see vestiges of the forum, theater, baths, and covered market. Ruins of an early Christian basilica testify to the site's early Christianization.
⏱️ 90 min
4
The Basilica of Saint-Just de Valcabrère lies 2 km below. This 12th-century Romanesque church was built with stones salvaged from the Roman site. Its simplicity and Tuscan setting amid meadows create a peaceful atmosphere. It's also a UNESCO World Heritage site.
⏱️ 60 min
🎯

Activities

→
Wander the medieval lanes of the fortified village. Noble houses, vaulted passages, and ramparts tell 2,000 years of history. The three entrance gates blend into the rock itself.
⏱️ 75 min
→
The Festival du Comminges takes place in July and August. Classical music concerts resonate in the cathedral, showcasing the exceptional organ. The acoustics of the space are magnificent.

The thermal baths of Luchon rank among France's most renowned. Renovated in 2023, they treat rheumatological and respiratory conditions using the hottest sulfurous waters in the Pyrenees.
