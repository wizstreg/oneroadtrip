Wisconsin's Great Drives: Door County, Apostle Islands & Driftless Area

Sea caves glowing amber at sunset, cherry blossoms dusting peninsula villages, sandstone canyons hiding secret waterfalls. Wisconsin surprises.

≈ 23
Days
1855
Km

📖 11 min read 🔄 Last updated: 2026-02-03

Paddle through Apostle Islands' sea caves, sandstone arches set on Lake Superior, then drive to Door County between orchards and lighthouses. Stop at Spring Green to discover Frank Lloyd Wright's Taliesin, before wandering through Wisconsin Dells and their canyons. End at New Glarus, where Swiss heritage is savored at the counter.

  • Apostle Islands: sea caves, superior shores
  • Door County: cherry orchards, lighthouse trails
  • Wisconsin Dells: sandstone canyons, water fun
  • Spring Green: taliesin, wright's prairie vision

Summer (June-August): Peak season with warmest weather but highest crowds and prices. Excellent for northern regions, challenging in southwestern deserts, perfect for beaches and national parks.

Winter (December-February): Variable conditions from tropical Florida to snowy Colorado. Perfect for skiing, southern destinations, indoor attractions, lowest prices outside holiday periods.
