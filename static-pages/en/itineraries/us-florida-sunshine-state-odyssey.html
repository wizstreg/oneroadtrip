Day 24

Key West ★★★★

Discover
  • Key West - The Southernmost Point in the Continental USA: This legendary island 90 miles from Cuba has attracted pirates, wreckers, artists, and eccentrics for centuries, creating a culture unlike anywhere else in America.

  • Smathers Beach - Key West's Longest Stretch of Sand: This half-mile beach on the Atlantic side offers calm water, paddleboard rentals, and fewer crowds than downtown.

Day 25

Dry Tortugas ★★

Discover
  • Dry Tortugas National Park - America's Most Remote National Park: Located 70 miles west of Key West, this small chain of islands requires a ferry or seaplane to reach, rewarding visitors with pristine beaches, exceptional snorkeling, and a massive Civil War fortress.
  • Fort Jefferson - The Largest Masonry Structure in the Americas: This hexagonal fortress rises from Garden Key, its walls constructed from 16 million bricks yet never completed despite 30 years of construction. Its most famous prisoner was Dr. Samuel Mudd, convicted of conspiracy in Lincoln's assassination.
Activities
  • Snorkeling the Pristine Reefs - Caribbean Waters in American Territory: The clear, protected waters surrounding the islands offer some of the best snorkeling in North America, with coral formations and tropical fish rivaling any Caribbean destination.
  • Camping Under the Stars - Sleep in the Fortress Shadow: Limited primitive camping allows overnight visitors to experience the islands after day-trippers depart, with star-filled skies unspoiled by light pollution.
