Experience Guadeloupe's stunning diversity on this 10-day journey through the butterfly-shaped archipelago. From the volcanic peaks of Basse-Terre to the pristine lagoons of Grande-Terre, discover cascading waterfalls, world-class diving sites, and charming island villages that make this Caribbean destination unforgettable.

المحطات الرئيسية (6)
  • Dive the Cousteau Reserve's exceptional underwater world around Pigeon Islands
  • Hike to La Soufrière volcano summit for panoramic views across the entire Caribbean
  • Explore Fort Napoleon on Terre-de-Haut with its breathtaking bay views
  • Discover Marie-Galante's Gueule Grand Gouffre natural arch carved by Atlantic waves
  • Visit Deshaies Botanical Garden with over 1,000 tropical species and exotic birds
  • Swim in turquoise lagoons at Sainte-Anne and pristine beaches across three islands

Pointe-à-Pitre market — Historical context and standout views.

Îlet du Gosier floats 500 meters from shore. Its red and white lighthouse, built in 1930, dominates the island. Iguanas nearly 2 meters long still inhabit the area.

Schoelcher Museum — Local culture or botany with interpretation signs.

Sainte-Anne's artisan market offers jewelry, paintings, wooden objects and Creole textiles in a warm atmosphere. Particularly lively on Thursday mornings and weekends.

St-Pierre & St-Paul church — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

Bois Jolan beach stretches for 2 kilometers. Its white sand and shallow turquoise waters make it a family spot. Coconut palms and sea grape trees provide natural shade.

La Caravelle beach displays its brilliant sand and turquoise water. It's the postcard image you'll see everywhere.

→

Take the shuttle to Îlet du Gosier from Datcha beach. The crossing takes 15 minutes.

💡 Shuttle from 9am to 5pm, leave early to avoid crowds
→

Explore the Bois Jolan lagoon with fins, mask and snorkel. The shallow waters shelter corals, tropical fish and seagrass beds.

→

Rent a clear kayak in Sainte-Anne to glide over crystal waters and observe the seabed without getting wet.

Gosier islet — Historical context and standout views.

The central market hall, entirely metallic, was inaugurated in 1874. Its earthquake-resistant framework and cast iron pillars come from the Denonvilliers foundry.

Sainte-Anne craft market — Local culture or botany with interpretation signs.

The Schoelcher Museum displays objects that belonged to Victor Schoelcher, defender of the abolition of slavery. Housed in an elegant classical-style building.

Bois Jolan fringe reef — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

Saint-Pierre-et-Saint-Paul church, nicknamed the 'iron cathedral', features metallic architecture built in 1874 that has withstood hurricanes for over a century.

Place de la Victoire, lined with palm trees, displays busts of former governors and the monument of 100 chains. The old Renaissance cinema shows its orange-yellow Art Deco facade.

The Saint-John Perse Museum occupies a colonial house built in 1880. You'll see Creole costumes and documents about the Guadeloupean poet's life.

→

Stroll through the spice market in the morning. You'll find vanilla, cinnamon, turmeric, spiced rums and tropical fruits.

💡 Come early for the freshest produce
→

Spot street art murals in the historic district around the Darse. Powerful portraits and vibrant colors transform the walls into an open-air gallery.

→

Taste accras, bokit and local juices at the market stalls. The lively atmosphere reflects the island's Creole spirit.

💡 Try fresh sugar cane juice

Fort Delgrès — Historical context and standout views.

Deshaies Botanical Garden spreads over 7 hectares and houses more than 1,000 tropical species. Former property of comedian Coluche, transformed into a green paradise.

Basse-Terre cathedral — Local culture or botany with interpretation signs.

The beach of Grande Anse stretches at the foot of Deshaies village. Golden sand, calm waters and coconut palms make it a peaceful spot.

Old quarter arcades — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

Ferry Cove reveals its calm waters surrounded by lush vegetation. This small natural harbor sheltered colonial trading vessels.

The coastal trail connects Deshaies to Pointe Ferry through wild vegetation. Sea views and birdwatching opportunities along the way.

→

Walk through the botanical garden early in the morning. Flamingos, parrots and toucans wake up with the sunrise.

💡 Feeding session at 3pm
→

Snorkel at Pigeon Islands in the Cousteau Reserve. The protected underwater trail reveals corals, sponges and tropical fish.

→

Swim at Pearl beach sheltered by vegetation. Clear waters and shade under the trees for a relaxing afternoon.

Deshaies Botanical Garden — Historical context and standout views.

Carbet Falls cascade from the Soufrière volcano slopes. The second falls drop 110 meters into a natural pool surrounded by rainforest.

Grande Anse viewpoint — Local culture or botany with interpretation signs.

La Soufrière, the Caribbean's highest volcano at 1,467 meters, dominates the Guadeloupe National Park. Fumaroles constantly emit sulfurous steam.

Pearl beach lookout — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

Fort Delgrès stands on Morne Houël overlooking the Basse-Terre bay. This 17th-century military fortress played a key role in French colonial history.

The Basse-Terre cathedral, dedicated to Our Lady of Guadeloupe, displays 18th-century architecture. Its baroque interior houses religious paintings and sculptures.

Champ d'Arbaud Park offers walking trails under giant trees. This green space borders the old town with its colorful Creole houses.

→

Hike to Carbet Falls on the marked trail. Allow 30 minutes to reach the second falls viewpoint.

💡 Slippery trail after rain
→

Climb to La Soufrière's summit. The trail crosses a lunar landscape and offers views over the entire archipelago on clear days.

💡 Start early, often cloudy after 10am
→

Bathe in the Acomat hot springs at the foot of La Soufrière. Natural thermal waters heated by volcanic activity.

National Park HQ — Historical context and standout views.

Fort Napoleon sits atop Morne Mire at 114 meters altitude. Built between 1844 and 1867, it never saw combat but now houses a museum.

Saut de la Lézarde stop — Local culture or botany with interpretation signs.

Pain de Sucre beach displays white sand bordered by turquoise water. This sheltered cove offers calm swimming protected from Atlantic waves.

Maison du Cacao — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

The bay of Les Saintes is considered one of the world's most beautiful bays. Scattered islands create a natural harbor sheltered from ocean swells.

Terre-de-Haut village aligns its colorful houses along narrow streets. Flower-filled balconies and local boutiques give it a Mediterranean charm.

→

Climb to Fort Napoleon in the morning. The botanical garden and iguana sanctuary surround the fortifications.

💡 Panoramic view over the bay
→

Rent an electric scooter to explore the island. The 8-kilometer loop takes you to beaches and viewpoints.

💡 Book ahead in high season
→

Taste tourment d'amour, the local pastry filled with coconut or guava jam. Artisanal bakeries compete for the best recipe.

💡 Best fresh in the morning

Cousteau Reserve overlooks — Historical context and standout views.

Gueule Grand Gouffre reveals a 50-meter natural arch carved by the ocean. Atlantic waves crash against limestone cliffs in an impressive spectacle.

Malendure beach — Local culture or botany with interpretation signs.

Murat Habitation spans 16 hectares and houses the Ecomuseum of Arts and Popular Traditions. The former sugar estate shows 18th-century colonial life.

Pigeon islets view — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

The Père Labat distillery still produces agricultural rum using traditional methods. The watermill built in 1866 continues to crush sugar cane.

Grand-Bourg market displays tropical fruits, vegetables, spices and artisanal rums. The island's commercial heart beats on Saturday mornings.

The sea cliffs stretch along the eastern coast. Limestone formations sculpted by erosion create a wild lunar landscape.

→

Walk to Gueule Grand Gouffre from the parking lot. The trail follows the coast and takes about 10 minutes.

💡 Watch for sea spray on windy days
→

Visit the Père Labat rum distillery. The guided tour explains production steps and ends with tasting.

💡 Open morning only
→

Cycle around the island on the coastal road. The flat terrain makes a 60-kilometer loop around Marie-Galante easy.

Anse Canot beach stretches with white sand and turquoise waters. This remote beach offers tranquility far from the crowds.

Moustique beach displays turquoise water and sea grape trees. Shallow waters make it a family favorite.

Terre-de-Haut village lanes — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

The windmill trails cross cane fields dotted with stone mills. More than 100 mills remain from the sugar era.

Trou à Diable reveals a natural crevasse where ocean waves create impressive geysers at high tide.

Swim at Anse Canot where calm waters invite relaxation. Few tourists venture to this isolated beach.

Habitation Roussel-Trianon — Historical context and standout views.

Saint-Louis Church dominates Grand-Bourg with its neoclassical facade. Built in the 19th century, it shelters a painting of the Virgin.

Pointe Doublé lighthouse — Historical context and standout views.

Pointe Doublé lighthouse marks La Désirade's eastern tip. From its 25-meter height, it watches over the channel between the island and Petite-Terre.

Nature reserve tracks — Local culture or botany with interpretation signs.

The nature reserve trails cross unique xerophytic vegetation. Cacti, agaves and acacias compose this preserved arid landscape.

Baie-Mahault reef flats — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

The reef flats extend offshore from Baie-Mahault. These shallow coral formations shelter lobsters, conch and tropical fish.

Beauséjour village aligns its colorful Creole houses facing the sea. The peaceful atmosphere contrasts with the other islands' activity.

→

Walk the coastal trail on foot. The limestone cliffs offer spectacular views over the Atlantic Ocean.

→

Swim at Fifi beach. White sand, coconut palms and calm waters make it the island's family spot.

→

Pointe des Châteaux — Historical context and standout views.

Pointe des Châteaux projects its sculpted rocks into the Atlantic Ocean. A cross dominates the promontory and offers views of Marie-Galante, La Désirade and Les Saintes.

Saint‑François marina — Local culture or botany with interpretation signs.

Atlantic waves crash against the cliffs. Strong winds blow over this wild cape, Grande-Terre's southeastern tip.

Raisins Clairs boardwalk — Coastal scenery and trade‑wind vantage point.

Saint-François marina welcomes sailboats and catamarans. Departure point for excursions to La Désirade, Petite-Terre and Marie-Galante.

Raisins Clairs beach spreads its white sand bordered by coconut palms. Calm, shallow waters for peaceful swimming.

Saint-François night market sets up on Wednesday evenings. Local crafts, musicians and Creole specialty stands animate the waterfront.

→

Head to Anse à la Gourde beach at the foot of Pointe des Châteaux. Popular surf spot with great waves.

→

Taste grilled fish and accras at waterfront restaurants in Saint-François. Views over the marina and boats.

❓ الأسئلة الشائعة

ما الطقس المتوقع؟

موسم الجفاف (ديسمبر-أبريل): طقس مثالي، هطول أمطار قليل، درجات حرارة مناسبة، موسم الذروة السياحية، أسعار مرتفعة، الحجز المسبق ضروري.

موسم الكتف (مايو ونوفمبر): طقس جيد، حشود أقل، درجات حرارة معتدلة، أسعار معقولة، زخات مطر عرضية، قيمة ممتازة.

موسم الأعاصير (يونيو-نوفمبر): هطول أمطار أكثر، خطر العواصف، مناظر طبيعية خضراء، أسعار منخفضة جداً، سياح أقل، مراقبة الطقس ضرورية.

موسم الكرنفال (يناير-مارس): احتفالات نابضة بالحياة، مهرجانات ثقافية، أماكن إقامة مزدحمة، أعلى الأسعار، مطلوب حجز مسبق، أجواء احتفالية.

كم يوماً يجب أن أخطط؟

نعتقد أنك ستستمتع بهذا المسار بشكل أفضل إذا خصصت له بين 9 و 13 يوماً. الخطوة التالية ستمكنك من تعديل مدة إقامتك.

ماذا تكتشف في غوادلوب؟

جنة الكاريبي الفرنسية مع المناظر الطبيعية البركانية والشواطئ البكر والثقافة الكريولية. استمتع بالكمال الاستوائي مع المعايير الأوروبية والأجواء الكاريبية الأصيلة. تقدم الجزر التنوع الثقافي مع الجمال الطبيعي والمأكولات الممتازة. توفر Guadeloupe الرقي الفرنسي مع جنة الكاريبي والأصالة الثقافية.
