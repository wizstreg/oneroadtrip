A library of verified itineraries, not mass-generated content

Every itinerary on OneRoadTrip follows a strict verification protocol:

  • Information is gathered from web research; a fact is only included if confirmed by several independent sources
  • The itinerary is plotted on a map and the order of stops is corrected if the geographic logic doesn't hold up
  • A final automated pass detects any remaining inconsistencies (duplicates, abnormal distances, misplaced stops)
  • The photos for each location are selected based on the actual visits and activities offered there

The same place can appear in several different itineraries, each time with visits and activities matching the angle of the trip. A "Rome" stop in a cultural road trip won't offer the same content as a "Rome" stop in a culinary route.

Three ways to build your trip

OneRoadTrip lets you choose your method:

  • Start from an existing itinerary. Pick from hundreds of verified routes, on a map or through filters (country, duration, theme). Use it as is or customize it freely.
  • Build it step by step. Hand-pick the places listed on OneRoadTrip, one by one, and assemble your own route.
  • Use the Route Builder. Set a starting point and a destination, and the tool automatically suggests the highest-rated places along the way.

Everything is editable, down to the smallest detail

Once your itinerary is in place, you stay in full control:

  • Reorder stops directly on the map
  • Add or remove a place
  • Edit or expand the visits and activities at each stop
  • Request an automated review of your itinerary's coherence and interest
  • Paste your booking emails to integrate them straight into your travel notebook
  • Track your budget, calculated automatically as the trip takes shape
  • Generate a printable travel notebook in a professional format
  • Share your itinerary with your travel companions

A worldwide database of rated places

Every listed place receives a score built from objective criteria: presence of hotels and restaurants, historical sites, attractions, parks, UNESCO listings, depth of the Wikipedia page, and visits identified across the itineraries.

Each place is then compared, according to defined proportions, to other nearby places, then national, then global ones. A score out of 10 and a star rating are assigned.

This database means that, unlike other planners on the market, OneRoadTrip can automatically suggest the places that are truly worth a detour on any given route — without relying on a fixed editorial catalog.

Built-in bookings

From your itinerary, you can book hotels, activities, rental cars, transfers, flights and guided tours. Everything is centralized in a single dashboard, accessible on the road during your trip.

A fully free service

All OneRoadTrip features are free. No subscription, no paywall, no restriction on creating or editing itineraries.

OneRoadTrip earns commissions paid by hotels, car rental companies and activity platforms when you book through the site. You don't pay more than on the partner sites: they share part of their margin.
